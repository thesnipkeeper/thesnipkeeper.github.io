A Little Help from Friends

Luke stood in the middle of his apartment in a haze. He looked at the clock above the kitchen sink and was confused to find that he could hardly comprehend where the hands were pointed.

He shook his head, ignoring the onset of dizziness and brief wave of nausea.

The realization that he felt the need for caffeine hit him harder than either of those. Jesus, was he turning into Lorelai? Was coffee dependency contagious?

He groaned to himself and trudged down the stairs with extra care not to lose his balance. He headed straight through the kitchen, nearly overwhelmed by the smell of cooking. Caesar was dancing from foot to foot as he sang along to the radio, oblivious to Luke’s presence.

“Hey,” he said, getting his employee’s startled attention. “Thanks for opening, Caesar. Late night last night.”

Babette’s car had stalled at a gas station outside of town and Luke was the first person she thought to call. They were able to jump start it and get it running long enough to get the car to Gypsy’s, and by then it was already much later than Luke was used to being awake. He already knew that he’d be useless in the morning, lack of sleep usually resulted in botched orders and annoying/ annoyed (depending on how you looked at it) customers.

Caesar shrugged and waved it off before going back to the eggs on the griddle. “No problem, man! I love being here early!” Luke knew he was telling the truth, because Caesar suffered a power trip whenever he was left in charge for more than an hour.

“Hi Luuuke,” Lorelai sing-songed as he made his way around the corner of the kitchen doorway. He knew just by the sound of her voice that she’d already been served her morning coffee.

“Hi Lorelai, hi Rory,” he nodded to them each.

“Morning, Luke,” Rory mumbled distractedly.

Rory was seated next to Lorelai, blissfully quieter than her mother. She had homework in front of her and was writing rapidly, which reminded him that he needed to do inventory today.

“How’d ya sleep? I heard what happened with Babette last night...” she continued talking, but his attention faded there as his head started to ache.

Luke stared at Lorelai foggily, as if she were from another planet. He could hear her voice fluctuate as she spoke, and she gesticulated wildly about cars or Babette’s cat or maybe what she had for dinner last night, but he couldn’t understand any of it for the life of him. He thought he caught his name, and then Caesar’s and the word ‘coffee’ and ‘angel’, but none of it computed in the gummed up collection of gears that was his brain today, not over the suddenly excruciating headache.

He squinted up at the diner’s lights, bright and painful, and flipped the switch before he could think about what he was doing. The pain in his head was just pissing off his stomach again.

“Uh, Luke? Why are the light’s off?” Lorelai asked with a raised eyebrow.

The lack of light finally had Rory looking up from her schoolwork, brows pinched in confusion and annoyance. “Hey...”

"Oh sorry, wrong switch." Luke said lamely, before switching the lights on once again. Right, this was a diner. Customers needed to see. He hissed under his breath, his whole body tensing. He could turn them off again when the sun finished rising, but then he’d just have to deal with the sun, which was infinitely worse.

Rory breathed a sigh of relief and went back to her work, while Lorelai was busy staring at him with a weird smile and the same raised eyebrow.

He had to look down at the floor as a wave of dizziness hit him. It passed for the moment, and he was still being scrutinized when he looked up.

Jess emerged from the back room. He dragged his not-a-morning-person self over to them and set the inventory sheet on the counter in front of him. “All done,” he muttered. He seemed to notice that something was off about his uncle, or maybe he was just taking pride in his bewildered face. “Need anything else from me?”

Luke only shook his head, speechless from seeing his nephew finish work earlier than he needed to, and for fear of doing anything to upset his stomach more and humiliate himself in the process if he chose to open his mouth. He returned the sheet to its spot under the counter and went to the pantry to regain some semblance of composure.

 

Jess watched in confusion as his guardian wandered off without a word.

“He’s being weird this morning,” he muttered, sounding more concerned than he intended.

Lorelai waved dismissively. “I’m sure he’s fine. He had a lot of excitement last night, he’s probably just not used to sleeping in this late,” she said late with a chuckle, because of course it wasn’t late to them. But for Luke, it was wildly different than his usual waking-up time.

Lorelai still glanced at the back where Luke had disappeared to, with a look of worry that she wouldn’t be able to hide to save the world. God, those two were sickening.

 

Luke breathed slowly, in and out through his nose, eyes closed. He wasn’t sure how long he did that for before he walked back into the diner, not feeling better, but not any worse. He got himself a mug and half-filled it with what smelled like a fresh pot of coffee.

Jess had wandered off. From the voices in the kitchen, it sounded like he was just helping Caesar get set up for the day instead of doing his usual disappearing act. Huh, what was up with that?

He took a sip of the coffee and noticed belatedly both Rory and Lorelai watching him.

“You okay, bud?” Lorelai asked. He could hear the delight in her voice at witnessing him consume the substance he’d spent the past ten years warning her about and judging her for.

He took a couple more sips before finally putting the mug down. He leaned most of his weight against the counter, tried to make it look casual rather than the only thing holding him up.

Luke shrugged. “I’m a bit off today, no big deal. The coffee will take care of it,” he muttered, believing himself less and less as his stomach flipped angrily beneath the hand he’d unconsciously laid there. Lorelai noticed, clearly, because her amusement had faded.

“You look kinda sick, Luke.”

Rory nodded in agreement. “And you’re drinking coffee, which is just... I mean something has to be wrong.”

Luke shook his head. “I’m... I’m fine... Just a little tired from... last night,” he insisted, pausing between words to swallow hard, fighting his stomach’s attempt to show him the coffee again. He settled a hand on his belly, feeling it gurgle angrily.

“...Luke?” Lorelai asked cautiously, and by the tone in her voice, it wasn’t the first time she’d said his name.

He swallowed convulsively, eyes widening and his hand going from his stomach to his mouth. He was vaguely aware of Lorelai hopping off of her stool and dragging him to the diner’s bathroom.

He lurched over to the sink, breathing heavily through the nausea.

“Oh, Luke,” Lorelai muttered softly, placing a hand between his shoulder blades and rubbing gently. "Just breathe slowly. In through your nose and out your mouth."

Luke meant to shrug her hand off, but he didn't mind her touch so much as he braced his hands on the sink, trying to breathe instead of give into his stomach. It was a contact that wouldn’t have been allowed if it were anyone else besides Lorelai Gilmore.

She seemed to sense the terror, to know that Luke didn’t want what was about to happen to be witnessed and gabbed about all over Stars Hollow.

“Jess! Caesar!” she called out the bathroom door. “Can you guys handle things right now?” As if on cue, the bell signaled their first non-Gilmore customer.

Luke was too focused on his rebelling stomach to keep track of everything around him, but Lorelai’s hand left his back, and she and Jess talked in the hallway for a few seconds. Jess sounded annoyed, but he seemed to comply, and Jess was never one to do as he was told if he didn’t actually want to.

He heard Jess grumpily asking the new customer for their order, and Caesar making a new plan for his reign of the diner today.

She returned to him, gentle hand on his back once again despite him being drenched from nausea and illness.

“Luke, it’s okay. Stop fighting it so hard and just let it happen,” she said firmly, rubbing slow circles on his back.

It was as if that was all Luke needed, as he retched into the sink. Oh God, the sink? He didn’t even bother to make it to the toilet? He covered the bottom of the basin in murky brown liquid, barely having a moment to breathe before painting it more with the same disgusting liquid.

"That's it, get all that yuck out of your system," Lorelai encouraged as she grabbed a bucket from under the sink and dumped the cleaning products out of it. She waited patiently for him to stop heaving before she handed it to him. “You wanna get upstairs I’m guessing? Finish this adventure in privacy?”

He nodded and groaned. “God, yes.” They weren’t even out of the hallway yet when he gagged into the bucket, and it was just their luck that Jess had come back at that very moment to see what was going on.

“Oh, God...” Jess moaned at the sight of Luke vomiting up coffee and water. He rushed past them into the bathroom, and from the sounds of it, barely made it in time to puke.

“Rory, can you...?” she nodded back to Jess as she continued to lead Luke to the stairs.

Rory nodded and left her homework on the counter before rushing to Jess.

 

Lorelai escorted Luke to the apartment’s bathroom, before walking back to his bedroom area to get it set up for him. The bed wasn’t made, which seemed strange for him, but she left it unmade. She found a pair of comfy PJ’s and laid them on top, before setting a glass of water and tylenol on the nightstand. It was amazing that Luke even had tylenol.

He emerged from the bathroom, looking ten shades paler than he had earlier. He got changed back into pajamas, and huffed grumpily when Lorelai mentioned he should’ve just stayed in bed.

She wandered over when he was fully dressed to find him lying on the bed and looking miserable. “I think the meat we ate last night was... off,” he admitted, looking about to pass out. “That, or we both caught a bug.

“Jeez,” Lorelai sighed. “Maybe the diner should just be closed, then.”

Luke thought for a moment and nodded tiredly.

“Caesar’s gonna be devastated.”

“I’m alright with that,” Luke decided.

She helped him get situated under the covers before turning around in time to see Rory leading an equally pale Jess into the apartment. "Let's get you comfortable too, so you can sleep this off." she said.

Jess was less inclined to let either of them help him, and he wasn’t as sick as Luke... yet. Clearly, this came on fast.

They went down to the diner and cleaned it up once Jess was situated. They explained everything to a disappointed Caesar, emptied the diner, and cleaned up the situation in the diner’s bathroom. Or rather, Lorelai did, because Rory was still unequipped to deal with as much of someone else’s puke.

Instead, Rory and Caesar disinfected and put chairs back on the tables.

They heard vomiting when they returned to the apartment.

Lorelai walked in first, sighing softly. She could see Luke was in the bathroom puking violently into the toilet, and Jess was looking rather nauseated himself, curled up in bed with his arms folded across his stomach.

"You got Luke, I'll take care of Jess?" Rory asked uncertainly, glancing at the sick boy with thinly-veiled worry.

“Good idea,” Lorelai nodded. She walked to the bathroom, right as the door was slammed in front of her. "Luke?" she called softly.

"Don't come in here." Luke muttered out weakly, as the sounds of diarrhea and vomiting echoing. Vomit hit the bottom of the same bucket that she’d dragged him up here with. Jesus, the poor guy.

Jess wasn’t doing much better, from the sounds of the retching that was going on at the kitchen sink, and Rory’s panicked yet comforting words.

“Gotcha. Do you need anything, at least?”

There was a long, long pause, Luke cleared his throat gruffly. She was beginning to wonder if he’d heard her when, “A change of clothes,” he sighed out.

She wasn’t very fazed, and she would say that it was because she was a mom, but more likely because she was forced to handle her illnesses by herself since she was... well, born, really. She grabbed another pair of pajamas and a T-shirt that she’d seen Luke in before, and a pair of boxers hidden between the other two clothing items.

She knocked on the door. It sounded quiet, maybe Luke was feeling better, or maybe he just had nothing left in him. “I bring gifts,” she announced.

Luke sniffed. “Gimme a sec...” he said, sounding more sheepish than she’d ever heard him.

She waited patiently, moving her attention to Rory and Jess for the moment. Rory was in the process of leading him back to bed, an old mixing bowl pilfered from Luke’s kitchen in tow. Lorelai would break the news to Luke later, but right now he probably didn’t care.

Luke emerged after a long few minutes, looking washed out and all kinds of tired, but comfortable in clean clothes. He closed the door behind him with a sigh. Lorelai got a mere glimpse, enough to see that it was in a rough state.

“Do you need me to-?”

“No, absolutely not,” he said quickly. “I’ll... deal with it later.”

“If you’re sure. C’mon, get to bed before you pass out on me.”

Luke did so with no argument, but he asked a question as Lorelai helped him under the covers once again. “How’s Jess?”

“Sick. Being taken care of,” she promised.

Luke nodded. He knew what that meant, and he didn’t seem to mind. As unsure as the two of them had been about their respective teenagers liking each other, they were glad they had each other at the moment.

“Well, you two should uh...” his voice was hoarse from throwing up so much in such a small period of time. He waved, gesturing vaguely as if he couldn’t think of a word. “Go. Don’t want you getting sick.”

“Well, if you’re contagious, I think that ship has sailed, my friend.”

Luke sighed and gave her the most apologetic look. “Sorry.”

“No apologizing,” Lorelai scoffed. “You’d do the same for me, and you might still have to.”

That didn’t seem to comfort him much.

“Call us if you guys need anything.”

He nodded, smiling tiredly. “Thanks.”

She patted his leg and went to find her daughter.

She found Rory laying on the other bed beside a sleeping Jess, and reading a book contentedly. She smiled at the sight, knowing she would prefer Rory not be so cuddly with Jess, but if it helped, she wouldn't knock it... not today anyways. Maybe they can stay for a little bit longer, make sure the boys are alright.
