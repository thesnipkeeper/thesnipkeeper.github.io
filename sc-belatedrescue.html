Belated Rescue

“Hellooo?” Alexis called as she entered David and Patrick’s store. There was no one, customer or otherwise. “Um, hello?”

Finally, Patrick emerged from the back room. “Alexis hey, what’s up?”

“Umm, well, I was just wondering what your day was looking like.”

Patrick blinked. “Well, I’m working,” he said, gesturing to the empty store. If Alexis was being honest, he didn’t seem to be doing much work at all.

“Sure you are, but like...” she sighed. “Okay, can you like, pick David up after work and bring him home with you?”

Patrick chuckled. “He’s driving you nuts.”

“Yes, omigod,” Alexis gasped out, hanging on his shoulders. “I can’t take it anymore, Patrick. Take him away.”

He smiled and laughed again. “I think he wants me to anyway.” Just then, he tore from her grip and coughed harshly into his elbow.

Alexis jumped back in alarm and immediately gasped. “Oh my God,” she said with a gasp. “You’re sick too.”

“No, no I just had something in my throat,” Patrick said a little too quickly.

But Alexis knew that cough, and it was the same cough that she spent all of last night listening to from her stupid dramatic brother.

“No,” she shook her head, setting a single finger on his shoulder. “You’re totally sick.”

Now that she got a good look at him, his face was a little red, his cheeks, but also his nose. Just like David.

Patrick sighed. “I gave David the day off to recuperate. There wasn’t anything I could do about... this.”

Alexis nodded. “I get you. Better tell David, though, because I’m pretty sure that getting you sick was the only reason he didn’t ask you to come get him himself.

The smallest smile tugged at Patrick’s lips. God, he was adorable. “I’ll text him. Thank you, Alexis.”

“No problem,” she tapped his nose, and then winced when she caught a twinge of pain across his face. “Oof... sorry.”

Patrick held up a hand and shook his head. “It’s fine, that area’s just a little uh, sore right now... snf!”

-----

Alexis gently blew on her wet nails, and then rolled her eyes at the fiftieth sneeze that hour. David made sure to groan as dramatically as possibly before blowing his nose.

“Can you stop it, David?”

“Oh my God, shut up, Alexis!” David wasn’t threatening anyway, but he certainly wasn’t when he sounded like he had cotton stuffed up his nose.

She just huffed and shook her head. She couldn’t wait for Patrick to come rescue them both, David by taking him, and Alexis by taking David away from her.

Maybe she would finally be able to get a full night’s sleep after her brother was gone. She was so exhausted!

Finally, that blessed knock on the door came before they both called “come in!” and Patrick let himself in.

He looked so much worse than he had earlier. Specifically his nose, which was super red and his eyes looking like he’d been rubbing at them all day. Aww, poor Patrick.

“So, sorry that you had to work by yourself all day,” David said as he got up to greet his fiance. “We must’ve gotten sick at the same time.”

The two of them exchanged a kiss, causing Alexis to cringe in disgust and go back to her nails. “Thank you, Patrick,” she called as David grabbed his things.

“Wait,” David looked to Patrick, then to her. “Thanks for what, exactly?”

Patrick shrugged. “Nothing special. Come on, snf! Let’s go get settled for the night and chug some medicine.”

They were both about to walk out the door when something unfortunate got their attention.

Alexis sneezed.

The three of them went wide-eyed, exchanging looks.

Okay, so maybe there was a reason beyond David coughing all night for her to be tired. “Fuck,” she said simply.

David winced. “...sorry.”

“Just go away, David!” She snapped, hand rubbing at her nose as she searched for a tissue.

David and Patrick didn’t waste any time escaping her wrath.

She managed to find a tissue just in time to sneeze again, this time giving a similar groan to her brother’s before blowing her nose. Her sinuses were feeling increasingly stuffy by the second, and she knew that she was going to feel ten times worse tomorrow.

Alexis decided that sick or not, she was thankful to have the room to herself for a while.
