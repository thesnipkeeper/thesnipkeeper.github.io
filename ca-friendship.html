Title: Friendship
Fandom: Covert Affairs
Summary: A sick Auggie somehow becomes Annie's responsibility
Word count: 600

Friendship

“You might want to check on your friend.”

Annie starts, nearly knocking over a stack of papers that Jai swiftly sets his hand on to stop the awful from happening.

Jai chuckles. “Didn’t mean to freak you out.”

“It’s okay, I was just doing research.”

“For the last mission,” he says with a nod. It’s not a question.

“Yeah,” she breathes out. “Something went wrong, I’m trying to figure out what it was.” She runs a tired hand down her face with a sigh. “What were you saying about my friend?”

“Ah, just that Auggie’s been in the bathroom throwing up for a half hour and he wouldn’t let me near him.”

“Yeah, yeah... he mentioned having a bug.”

“Well, you’re the only one who might be able to talk him into going home.”

“I know he’s sick, but he’s done his job pretty well today. I don’t see why-”

“Annie, do you know what it looks like when something spreads through a whole department?”

“Uh...”

“Because I do, and trust me,” he clears his throat. “It’s not pretty. Auggie might still be able to do his job, but at what cost?”

Annie sighs. “You have a good point.”

So she finds herself standing outside the doorway of the department locker room. She can’t hear anything that sounds like gagging, but from what Auggie told her, it’s possible he doesn’t really have anything left to throw up after the night that he’s had.

“Auggie? Are you alive in here?”

A heavy sigh from the farthest stall answers her question. “Did Jai rope you into this?” the voice asks, echoing off of the outdated tiles.

“Yup!” she wanders further in until she reaches the end stall, and then props herself against the sink, doing her friend the favor of not facing him directly. “I’m supposed to talk you into going home so you don’t infect all of the department.”

“Of course you are...”

She huffs a laugh, and her smile quickly fades. “So, you okay? Do we need to take you to the infirmary and get re-hydrated or something?”

And then she hears him stand, and then a zipper pulled up, and-

Oh.

She swallows awkwardly. Well, she just committed one of the biggest invasions of privacy in the book, and she works for the CIA.

Auggie clears his throat. “I just need a minute. And I could... use a ride home.”

“Yeah, of course.”

-----

A hand finds her arm. “Ugh... Annie...”

Annie acts fast, grabbing the bucket and getting it under his chin just in time to catch an underwhelming amount of vomit. Each heave shakes his body, making his muscles tense, only for him to collapse when it’s all over with.

She rubs his back as he tries to catch his breath.

“You might not feel so terrible if you just took a sick day in the first place, you know...” she says with as much innocence as she can muster.

Auggie scoffs as he leans back against the couch and runs his sleeve across his mouth. “You’re gonna catch this, you know.”

Annie makes a faux-thoughtful sound. “I don’t think so. My immune system is pretty spectacular.” She pats his knee with one hand and takes the bucket with the other. “I’ll go clean this out.”

Auggie makes a face. “I can do that myself, you know.”

“Not as well, though.”

“Wow, is that a blind joke? Low blow, Annie Walker.”

“Um no, it’s a ‘you’re too sick to function properly’ joke, my friend.”

“Ah. I suppose that’s fair.”

“Mhm.”

When Annie returns, he’s dozed off pretty peacefully. She smiles and readjusts the blanket over him.
