Plethoric Responsibility

It was a long, long day. Not for Christopher, necessarily. He had a normal school day. But for Kade, on the other hand...

He’d been watching the boy. Sometimes he felt like the only person who noticed when Kade was stressed or tired, or stressed and tired. That wasn’t true, of course. But poor Kade looked like he’d had it today.

He broke up an argument between two new students who hadn’t gotten along since they arrived. Everyone had cut them some slack, knowing that the first weeks at the school weren’t easy.

Kade didn’t yell very often, but he was terrifying when he did, not unlike that of a fun parent who was on their last nerve.

He also seemed tired most of the day, like he’d been longing for sleep since he woke up. Christopher knew the feeling.

They were at lunch. Kade hadn’t eaten more than a couple bites of his sandwich, and he was now nodded off with his chin in his hand. Christopher had never seen him this tired before. There must have been even more going on today than he’d noticed.

He felt terrible for Kade, sometimes. So much responsibility on his shoulders must have been overwhelming. He was the one everyone went to for help, and when was the last time he asked someone for help himself?

“You have time to take a nap,” Christopher stated as he finished off his own lunch. He eyed Kade’s abandoned lunch, and thought better than to bother the guy with more than he needed to. “Free period, you know?”

“Mm,” Kade sat up and blinked drowsily. “Sorry, that was rude of me.” He sniffed, dragging a hand down his face as he sat up. Wow, he didn’t look good.

Christopher scoffed. Because table etiquette was always a concern at their meal times. Sure.

“Are you okay?”

“Just sleepy,”

“Well, I wasn’t kidding about the nap.”

“Honestly,” Kade yawned. “I think if I go to bed now, I’ll be out for the night. Probably better if I just wait.”

That made sense, Christopher supposed. It didn’t stop him from watching Kade the rest of the day. He spent free period playing his flute for a couple of mouse skeletons in his room when he heard the commotion upstairs.

He let curiosity get the best of him as he went up, and found Cora standing in the doorway of her bedroom as the voices went on across the hall.

“What’s going on?”

“Liam opened his suitcase,” Cora informed with a frown. She explained,

Liam was having a panic attack, and Kade was trying to calm him down.

Apparently Liam, one of the new students, had found one of his suitcases to be packed to the brim with floral patterns, something that was taboo in the world that his door led to.

Sure, they had plenty of worlds where plants didn’t exist like the way they did on this one, but that was the first any of them had heard of a world where plants were the most evil of enemies.

Kade sounded tired, but he was good at what he was doing, regardless.

“I can fix this up, no problem, alright?” He heard Kade promise. “That’s my job, after all!”

That was one of his many jobs, Christopher wanted to add.

After that, Kade sat with Liam for a while, and things calmed down.

Christopher and Cora found themselves chatting about nothing in particular. As much as he loved being in the basement by himself, he enjoyed her company. Cora was easy to talk to, and she was one of the more normal people at the school when it came to interacting with one another.

“Has Kade seemed off to you today?” He asked when the conversation came to a lull.

Cora was twirled a piece of blue-green hair in her fingers. “Maybe a little? It might just be one of those days, you know?”

“Sure,” Christopher agreed.

And that was around the time that the person in question darted out of the room like his life depended on it. Cora and Christopher exchanged a look as they heard the floor’s bathroom open and close. Liam stood in his doorway, looking confused as if wondering that he did something.

Christopher heard a cough and a sickening splatter against tile. He didn’t look to the other two as he made his way to investigate.

Kade hadn’t had a chance to close the door, so all Christopher had to do was nudge it open a little more. If Kade wanted him to leave, he could say so, but he had a feeling that that was the last thing on Kade’s mind right now and Christopher needed to make sure he was okay.

Kade heaved into the toilet. There was a puddle of vomit on the floor, indicating that he hadn’t quite made it. Jeez, poor Kade...

Christopher wasn’t particularly skilled in this area, but if he didn’t help, he wasn’t sure anyone would.

Sumi. Sumi would. But Sumi was also the last person Kade would want around while in his current state. And Kade wouldn’t be thrilled with him at all if he got Eleanor.

Kade had stopped throwing up for the moment. He inhaled and exhaled heavily, almost wheezing. It looked like it hurt, and Christopher imagined that puking hard with a binder on wasn’t particularly comfortable, but that was a concern for later.

He didn’t say anything as he took a washcloth and dampened it with warm water. He nudged Kade’s shoulder and the guy took it with a minute nod. Kade clumsily tried to clean his face of the goop.

Neither of them spoke, though Kade looked like he wanted to argue when Christopher started cleaning the floor. His stomach required his attention, anyway, as he started heaving again.

Christopher wasn’t too sure about this kind of thing, or what exactly he was supposed to do. But as he got his friend a cup of water to rinse his mouth, he noticed the subtle shudders in Kade’s limbs. His arms were also just shaking from the exertion of throwing up, but the shivers were different.

“Hey, is there a thermometer in here?” He asked, and it was the first time either of them had bothered to speak.

Kade thought for a moment and nodded toward one of the drawers in the sink cabinet. He knew where it was, just like he knew where everything was in this insane school. He was also the one who ended up checking temperatures when something was going around, once again taking care of everyone else.

Christopher held it out to Kade, who hesitated and slipped it under his tongue as he’s instructed others to do through flu seasons.

It wasn’t long before the thing beeped and Kade glanced at it wearily. Christopher leaned over to see the reading.

“Is... that bad?” It wasn’t like he knew much about fevers! Besides the fact that Kade obviously had one!

“It’s not good,” said Kade with a hoarse chuckle. “But I’ll probably live. I uh... could use some help standing, though.”

Christopher offered a hand and took most of Kade’s weight as he got to his feet. He wavered for a second, and then looked to have gained his balance enough for Christopher not to have to worry.

Kade sighed, cleared his throat, and nodded. “Think I will take a nap now.”

-----

Everyone else was getting ready for bed when Christopher went up to the attic. He walked carefully as he carried a plate of toast in one hand, and a cup of tea in the other. One of the women in the kitchen were happy to comply when he explained the situation. The faculty loved Kade, even if they didn’t know the school’s entire purpose or Kade’s true role in it.

The door was open, which usually meant that Kade welcomed visitors, but today it could’ve meant that he forgot to close it. Christopher gave a weak knock with his elbow before letting himself in.

Shit, Kade was asleep. Though, the waste bin that was usually by his desk was now next to his bed. Right, he didn’t really have a bathroom up here.

There was a shift in the bed immediately, and Christopher winced.

“Hi,” Kade propped himself up on his elbow, looking sleepy. His hair was a mess, but he didn’t seem to mind. He was in soft-looking pajamas, but kept the blankets over himself as he sat up.

“I didn’t mean to wake you up,” said Christopher. “Sorry.”

Kade shook his head, rubbing his eyes. “It’s okay, I wasn’t sleeping. Just... lying here.”

“Feeling any better?”

There was a smile and a small chuckle, as if Kade didn’t want to answer. “Not particularly, but that’s alright. What’ve you got there?”

“Oh,” Christopher set the plate and cup on Kade’s nightstand. “I figured you probably haven’t had much to drink or eat today, and Eleanor told me I should bring something up in case you felt like it.”

Kade’s face fell into a frown immediately. “You talked to Eleanor?”

Oh. Uh oh. Maybe he should have thought about that. “Yeah, but I told her that we’d make sure you were okay.”

“I’m fine,” Kade barked, almost defensively. “She doesn’t need to be bothered by something like this.”

“You’re not fine,” Christopher replied, and Kade’s mouth closed at that, because of course he couldn’t argue against it. “Look, you can’t get mad at me for telling Eleanor that you’re sick, okay? You just can’t. You’re one of the most important people at this school, of course I’m going to be worried about you.”

Kade sighed out through his nose. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I just don’t want her to have more on her plate than she already does. That’s all. I didn’t mean to snap at you like that.”

Christopher nodded. “It’s okay, just... let me help. Let everyone help. Everybody’s pretty worried about you since they heard what happened.”

Kade groaned, dragging a hand down his face. “That’s embarrassing.”

Christopher couldn’t help but laugh at that.

“How’s Liam?”

“He’s great,” Christopher said. “He’s got some issues to work out with the other new kid, though.”

And then Kade swore. “I was going to find him some new clothes...”

“He has other stuff to wear, don’t worry.”

Kade went to grab the cup of tea, and retracted his hand, blinking woozily. “Mm, yikes...”

“Dizzy?”

A nod.

“Here,” Christopher carefully set it in his hands and made sure Kade had a grip on it before he let go. “You probably need fluids, right? That’s what you’re always telling everyone else when they’re sick.”

Kade’s brows furrowed in a childish grumpiness that wasn’t usually found on him. “Don’t much appreciate having the tables turned, I’ve got to say,” he took a careful sip and called that good for now, letting the cup warm his hands.

“I figured as much.” Christopher watched him with a frown. “Are you gonna be okay up here by yourself? You seem like you can hardly move right now.”

Kade, to his credit, made it look like he was thinking about it for a second before he shrugged and said, “I’ll be alright.”

“I just... really don’t believe you.”

“Mmm...” Kade managed to put the cup back on his nightstand without spilling, and sank into bed once again. “Think I’m going back to sleep, whatever you decide.”

“If I decided to bring my pillow and blanket up here and sleep on the floor?”

A sleepy chuckle. “Go right ahead,”

Christopher did. He told the others the situation, where they could find him and that Kade was feeling pretty shitty.

-----

Kade wasn’t better in the morning, and morning came sooner than either of them would have liked.

Christopher slept like the dead on most nights, but he found himself being on high alert when he woke to his friend having a nightmare. He’d never really heard Kade so scared as he sounded right now. He forgot that Kade was capable of being that scared.

Christopher debated for a while whether he should wake him.

He decided against it just as Kade sat up and lunged for the bin next to his bed. He’d barely gotten to it in time before vomit pouring from his mouth. His breath was labored as he sniffed, eyes wide but still glassy with sleep.

He whined, just before heaving up another wave of puke. He looked shaky and exhausted.

Christopher got up rather slowly and sat on the edge of the bed, an attempt to be gentle in reminding Kade of his presence. He looked around and found a tissue box on the nightstand, pulling a couple to hand to Kade.

“I’m sorry,” Kade muttered tiredly.

“Shut up.”

Kade huffed out a laugh and did so.

It was late, or early, depending on how you looked at it. Either way, Christopher didn’t expect anyone to be awake while he went to clean up. It... probably could have waited until morning, but Kade seemed like he needed a moment and he wasn’t going to be able to do it himself in his state.

“Did Kade throw up?”

Christopher nearly jumped out of his skin. Of course, of course, Sumi was awake at this hour.

“Don’t sneak up on me like that!” Sumi seemed unaffected, so he sighed and said, “Yeah, he’s kind of in rough shape. I’ve never seen him like this.”

She frowned and hummed thoughtfully. “I’ll go see if he needs company.”

She walked off before Christopher could tell her to maybe wait a bit, but she wouldn’t have listened to him anyway.

He went back up to the attic a few minutes later, after re-lining the bin and grabbing a glass of water in case Kade felt like drinking anything ever again. The sight in front of him when he arrived was more adorable than he’d expected.

Sumi was seated in Kade’s bed, looking comfortable as if she’d been there all night. Kade’s head was rested in her lap, and she hummed a happy, but unrecognizable tune as she ran her fingers through his hair. He looked asleep, or almost asleep.

Christopher smiled. He set the stuff down and nodded to Sumi, who grinned triumphantly in return at having lulled their friend so easily.

-----

Dinner was the usual chorus of hungry teens having impossible conversations, and Christopher was perfectly content sitting alone with his flute and his half-eaten food. He wasn’t always that hungry by the time dinner rolled around, but he didn’t mind.

“Seat taken?”

Christopher couldn’t help but smile and gesture grandly to the chair in front of him. “Look who’s out of bed.”

Kade hummed and nodded. “I made it! Look at that.”

“How’re you feeling?”

“Alright, I think.”

He’d spent the better part of the past few days in bed, sweating and shivering and refusing to ingest anything that they tried to force on him. It was a comfort to see him dressed in his usual outfit, looking more like himself.

His eyes glanced at Christopher’s plate and back. “You gonna eat that roll?”

“No, go ahead, but you know that you can get your own plate, right?”

Kade shook his head. “I just want the roll.”

Christopher scoffed and nudged his plate in the guy’s direction. “Have at it. I’m just glad to see you eating again.”

“So am I, to be honest. I kinda miss food.” Kade began pulling the roll apart, almost daintily, and ate it tiny piece by tiny piece.

Ms. Eleanor walked into the dining room and began to explain the plans for the rest of the evening. As she passed their table, she leaned over and pressed a quick kiss to Kade’s head, and then continued talking about proper etiquette for movie nights.

Kade, surprised by it, smiled, and looked back down.

Christopher had forgotten about his friend’s weird reaction to telling Eleanor in all of the other commotion. And it was easy to tell that whatever feeling of anxiety that was there, hadn’t gone away.

He wanted to ask. He knew better than that. It wasn’t always a good idea to ask questions at that school. People had lots of feelings to hide, lots of feelings that they’d rather die than talk about it.

Especially Kade.
