Opening Up

Caleb had to admit, he never would have predicted he'd enjoy his life this much. That he'd meet so many assholes he cared about. It was... comfortable. Ah, yes... Caleb had a lot of time to think when he was shelving books. Bryce's warm voice could be heard complimenting a customer's choice of reading as Caleb shelved the last one from the stack. His eyes lingered on the last book as Caleb got lost imagining what his life would be like if Bryce had not given him a chance.

"Ready for more?"

Caleb startled and found Bryce behind him with more stacked books in their arms. "Ah, thank you, yes."

They nodded, smiling.

The rest of his shift went on without a hitch. More books were sold, more books were bought, more books were shelved. Caleb finally felt that he had a comfortable routine to latch onto, and he really was in his element here.

"See you tomorrow!" Bryce called as Caleb walked out the door. He waved a goodbye in return and headed to his car.

Rrrrrrrr

A sigh, and Caleb turned the key again.

Rrrrrrrr

He could feel a lump of anxiety rising in his throat. How was he supposed to get home if his car wouldn’t start? Was he stuck here until tomorrow? No, that was silly. He just needed to call someone. Caleb returned to the shop, reluctantly asking Bryce if he could use the phone. They didn’t refuse, in fact they seemed to look at him worriedly. Unprofessional, Caleb thought, to need his boss' help with something outside of work. He needed to keep this job.

The phone continued to ring for long enough that Caleb's heart was racing. What if nobody was home? What would he do then?

"Mr. Caleb?"

A hand on his shoulder and he nearly yelped.

Bryce held their hands up. "Sorry! I was just… are you alright? Do you need a ride home?" Their hands hovered with the desire to help him, and all Caleb could think about was that he was ruining his image in front of his boss. A few seconds went by before Bryce walked off.

No answer. Caleb breathed out, trying to steady the quick inhales that were making him lightheaded.

"Nothing?" Bryce asked. They sat a cup on the counter and dragged a chair to Caleb.

Caleb shook his head.

"Why don’t you have a seat and try again, alright?"

Caleb was too panicked to refuse anything right now. He blinked at the cup of water pressed into his hands. Ah, he understood now. Bryce was trying to short-circuit his panic attack.

He dialed again, leaning closer to the counter as to not pull the cord too tightly.

"Hello?"

That voice. That was…

"Mollymauk?"

One of two of the new roommates. Caleb didn’t spend much time talking to him, because frankly they were such different people that he didn’t see how it would go well. He was so… colorful and extravagant and outgoing, and Caleb- well, he wasn’t.

Seeing that he finally got a hold of someone, Bryce awkwardly pulled a piece of hair behind their ear and seemed to walk off to give him some space.

-----

Caleb climbed into the car and wordlessly buckled his seatbelt. He couldn’t bring himself to look Bryce in the eyes as he apologized earlier, and he couldn’t look at Molly, either. Ruining so many people's days today, Caleb.

"You look like you could use a good drink," Mollymauk said with a chuckle.

"Ja."

Molly started the car and a few seconds went by. Caleb could feel himself being scrutinized but he kept his head down. "I think I have just what you need back at the house."

A few minutes went by. Thankfully the drive wouldn’t be too long. Caleb was absolutely ready to drink.

"I'm sure I'm not the one you wanted to pick you up, but-"

"It's fine. I- thank you."

-----

The house was a large, five-bedroom bungalow that the seven of them had managed to squeeze into.

Nott and Caleb shared one room together and ended up quite comfortable in the space. They were used to staying in small spaces together, having basically lived in a van for a good time together before meeting the rest of the group.

Jester and Beau had the master bedroom. They got along as roommates more than most would assume.

And then there was Fjord, and the newcomers, Mollymauk and Yasha. Each of them had their own bedrooms. Yasha would come and go depending on her work schedule. None of the rest of the could figure out what her job actually was (Even Molly) but it must've been important.

Molly did indeed make a good drink for Caleb that evening, and for everyone else as they funneled in. They had good conversation, it being the first time all seven of them were spending time together. Molly told some ridiculous stories and then he and Jester did a ridiculous dance. It was a good night.

-----

He felt a little off that morning, but by the time afternoon came around, Caleb was coughing and sniffling and unable to stay upright for very long. He kept taking his coat off and putting it back on as things got suddenly chilly, and suddenly sweaty.

Caleb yanked his kerchief out just in time to muffle a sneeze. The force of it stung his sore throat and he winced.

"Alright, I believe this is the part where I send you home for the day," Bryce said with a sympathetic smile. "You sound awful."

"No, I am… snf… I can work."

A chuckle. "You can, but should you?"

The answer was no.

"I don’t want you to take offense to this, Caleb, but it sounds like you have the flu and I don’t want my store to require a quarantine," they patted his back as he pulled on his coat for the thousandth time that day. "Get some rest, don’t come back until that fever's gone."

Though he was disappointed, there was no refusing his boss, and being home in bed sounded divine.

"Caleb!" Of course Molly was the only one home. "You're back ear-" Molly's face dropped. "You poor thing, you look horrible. Like, super shitty."

Caleb nodded. "I feel super shitty."

Mollymauk settled him at the kitchen island before Caleb realized what was happening. "I have just the thing for you. Hold on."

Caleb could hardly follow what the tiefling was doing as he dashed back and forth in the kitchen, elegant movements paired with ridiculous ones as he boiled water and then poured a number of things into it. Before he knew it, a glass was slid into his direction. What little sense of smell he had picked up strong scents of cinnamon and alcohol as he leaned in and inhaled.

He took a sip.

"This is… good."

Molly made a satisfied little noise. "I'm glad you like it. Why don’t you take that to bed with you and get some rest?" he pressed a kiss to Caleb's forehead and walked off.

-----

Caleb stifled a cough into the sleeve of his shirt. It only figured that he'd catch the flu. He was always the one to get sick when he and Nott were on the road. He accused her of having a strange goblin immune system, and she didn’t exactly deny it. Still, he made arrangements for Nott to sleep in Yasha's room while she was away and until he was well, because sharing a bed with someone infected with the flu was just tempting fate.

He hadn't been paying attention to the TV. Focusing on things was hard when he was too busy shivering underneath a pile of covers. Everyone was keeping their distance. It was the weekend, and no one wanted to risk missing work on Monday, so Caleb promised to quarantine himself to avoid infection. Yes, they were actually calling it quarantine. He shifted in bed, trying to find a comfortable position despite the ache in his muscles.

Mrr? Came from a confused, rudely-awoken Frumpkin at the end of the bed. Instead of nipping at Caleb's intrusive legs, Frumpkin yawned and came up to curl against his chest.

He sniffled, and then coughed when it irritated his throat. Caleb curled up, stuffing his face into the cool pillow in lame hope that it would cool his overheated, yet somehow freezing skin. When that didn't work, he nuzzled Frumpkin's fur instead, listening to the quiet purrs.

"Wha- oh no. No way."

He stopped.

There was arguing going on outside the door.

"Into quarantine, c'mon!" Beau's voice. "I'm not getting sick this week."

"Sorry, Molly." Fjord.

Caleb raised an eyebrow. He was just beginning to consider calling out to see what was going on before there was a knock on his bedroom door.

"Yes?"

Fjord and Beau stood with Mollymauk, who was looking rather pale and sickly. Caleb had never seen a sick tiefling before, and it was rather interesting to see what discoloration did to lavender skin.

"Hey Caleb," Fjord said. "Do you mind if Molly stays in here? We're trying to… localize the germs."

Rather than his usual work clothes (colorful getup that had everyone gagging when he came downstairs for breakfast every morning), he was in plaid pajama bottoms and a hoodie with the zipper low enough to show the horizontal scars across his chest.

He looked miserable. Caleb suspected that he'd been woken from a nap

Caleb made his best attempt at a smile. He wasn’t fantastic at it on a good day, let alone when he was feeling awful.

"I… do not mind if you don’t, Mollymauk."

Molly smiled wearily. "I'm beyond caring where I am so long as these two stop pestering me."

Beau scoffed. To their credit, they made sure Molly was safely in bed and well taken care of before they left. Of course, the two of them could here hands being washed in the bathroom as soon as the door closed. Absolutely fair. Caleb wouldn’t wish this flu on his worst enemy.

And now he was sharing a bed with the one roommate he hardly spoke to outside of… well, he hardly spoke to.

Molly coughed. It sounded much worse than Caleb's own, deep and from the chest. "I'm sorry about this," He offered. His voice was hoarse and thick, different than the smooth chime that it usually was. "Those two are out of control."

"Always," Caleb mumbled. His eyes had slipped closed. Maybe he could finally get some sleep after the restless night he had before.

"You know," Caleb reluctantly opened his eyes as Molly started talking again. "To be honest, I've never really seen the inside of your room. I didn’t know the two of you shared a bed."

Caleb and Nott made a point to keep their door shut and locked as often as possible. They were cautious people, and always had been. Living in a house full of their friends wouldn’t change that.

"Ja. We are used to it."

"Because Jester and Beau, those two would kill each other if they shared a bed every single night."

"Ja."

"But you and Nott," Molly paused to rub his nose. He sniffled. "You two are like family. It's interesting." Molly sank down into the covers as well, resting his head on the other pillow. His teeth chattered, and Caleb could feel a rush of empathy. He'd been dealing with chills for the past two days, he knew how awful it was.

"We have spent a long time together. Haven't slept alone in years."

At Mollymauk's silence, Caleb looked up from where his face was half-buried in his own pillow. Molly was looking at him with an indecipherable expression. "So that's why you didn’t sleep last night."

It was true, Caleb hardly had an hour of sleep the night before. Between lacking his usual bedmate and all the achy symptoms that came with his illness, sleep was completely impossible. He wasn’t sure how in the world Molly knew that, though.

"I could hear you tossing and turning all night. And coughing, and sneezing… wow you sneeze quite a lot."

"Ah," Caleb cleared his throat and winced when it just made the pain spike. "I was trying to be quiet."

Molly scoffed, and then sniffled. "Well, dear, You couldn’t exactly h-hhhelp it-" he pulled his sleeve in front of his face in time to catch a sneeze, which then morphed into more chesty coughing. "Fuck…"

"When did you…"

"Woke up with it this morning."

"Ah." oops. "I suppose... I should have tried harder keep my germs to myself."

Molly giggled hoarsely before covering his mouth to muffle the cough that it caused. "You're sweet, but it was bound to happen," He said with a dramatic sigh. "My immune system is sort of shite. I suppose it's difficult to avoid, anyway, sharing a home and all."

Caleb nodded, eyes slipping shut.

"I'll let you rest," Molly said, voice full of fondness.

"You should rest as well," he mumbled as he got comfortable. Frumpkin repositioned and ended up curling up on his pillow.

"In a while. Sleep well, Caleb."

He did not. Caleb tried turning over, and then turning his pillow over. Frumpkin made an annoyed sound and went back to the foot of the bed. It was impossible to get comfortable, impossible to shut down his fucking loud brain. He turned over once again. He opened his eyes, maybe Molly was getting annoyed with his moving. But no, Molly was leaned against the headboard tiredly watching the TV.

"Having troubles?"

Caleb sighed. "Ja."

Molly cleared his throat. "Forgive me if this is too forward, but would it help if I was closer to you?"

Caleb raised an eyebrow.

"Because you're so used to sleeping with Nott. Perhaps your mind needs to be reminded that you're not alone?" Molly smiled, and then muffled a quick cough into his arm. He shook his head as if to rid himself of the fogginess, and zipped up his jacket all the way with a shiver. "Well, that and I'm very cold and could use a cuddle myself."

-----

Caleb hadn't realized he fell asleep until he woke to the bedroom door creaking open. The sun in the window was in a much different position, telling him it was nearing evening time.

Nott smiled, which faded into that of surprise when she saw that Mollymauk's arms were engulfing him.

"Oh my…"

"Ja." Caleb agreed.

"How are you? Do you need anything? Are you feeling any better?"

Caleb swatted away the hand that was feeling his face. He tried to carefully sit up out of Molly's grip, and the tiefling only shifted and coughed into his pillow. Caleb and Nott both winced at the sound. Still, he remained asleep.

He sniffled, grabbing his nearby handkerchief to wipe at his nose. "While I am not feeling my best, I think it is Mollymauk who needs the care."

Nott nodded. "He sort of looks like he's dying."

"He is right here, thank you very much," Molly cleared his throat, one more weak cough muffled into the pillow before he opened his eyes to reveal a less-vibrant red. "I'm not dying," he said, sounding defeated.

"That cough sounds rather nasty," Caleb pointed out.

Before Molly could bother arguing, Nott crawled onto the bed taking both of them by surprise. She pressed one hand to Caleb's forehead and one to Molly's. She frowned.

"You're both very warm. I'll get some medicine." and she was gone.

Molly caught the frown on Caleb's face. "What? What is it? Snf, you look more displeased than usual. Snf!... Are there any tissues around here?" He sat up slowly, propping himself on his elbow and looking around.

Caleb shook his head. "It's nothing."

Molly locked eyes on him and didn’t break it even as he assaulted his running nose. "It's something, my dear." Caleb sighed heavily, something he always did when he was annoyed. Molly pressed. "Oh come on, out with it. We have nothing better to talk about."

He was silent for a moment as if considering the pros and cons of opening up to someone for once.

"She worries," he said. "She worries too much."

"Ah." Molly still wasn’t sure he understood.

"I don’t want Nott to wear herself out worrying about a simple flu."

Molly didn’t see any of it as a big deal. The two of them were close, surely they'd seen each other sick before. Or perhaps that was part of the problem. He wondered what could have happened to make a 'simple flu' such a big concern.

Nott returned with tylenol and glasses of water, instructing each of them to take two and have some extra sips of water. She asked if they were hungry, they exchanged equally-nauseated looks at the thought of food. Their appetites were gone, unfortunately, which pained Molly because being served in bed sounded amazing.

In the end, Caleb had to ask her to leave, obvious worry for infecting her.

"Can goblins catch illnesses from humans?"

"I do not want to find out, Mollymauk."

-----

Molly, ever the cuddler, nuzzled underneath Caleb's chin. The TV was on to serve as a distraction from their misery but neither of them were paying attention. Both of them were feverish and exhausted, and Molly's coughing made it difficult for either of them to sleep.

"I'll try not to impale you with my horns," Molly mumbled before giving a contented sigh.

A scoff. "That would be appreciated, yes."

"So tell me…"

"Oh dear."

"You and Nott. What's the story there?"

Caleb cleared his throat and reached up to rub his nose with the hand that wasn’t pinned down by the purple tiefling. "Nott and I have lived together for many years. Before we came here and met the others, we were sleeping in a van."

"You were homeless?"

"By choice, perhaps. We weren't comfortable staying in one place for very long. Nott and I… we met in juvenile detention." He cleared his throat again, sniffled, and Molly had a feeling that it wasn’t just because of the illness. "We were just children, really. Idiot teenagers."

"Oh, darling… I had no idea."

"It's fine. You know, we've been on our own for so long, I apologize if we are hard to live with."

Molly giggled. "I think we all have our own little quirks in this house, don’t you?"

Caleb made a fond noise. "You're right."

A few seconds went by, Molly's curiosity getting the better of him. "Can I ask how-"

"I… I'm very tired, Mollymauk…"

Molly nodded as well as he could without jarring Caleb. "Sure. Of course."

Caleb's breath began to even out, telling Molly that he was finally getting the rest that he needed. Molly carefully lifted himself from Caleb's chest and looked down at the wizard. He lacked the usual wrinkle between his brows, face peaceful for once.

Of course that was when Molly felt a deep tickle in the back of his throat. He tried clearing it away, it wouldn't work. Not now, for the love of God. He took a deep, careful breath, a faint crackle as the air passed his airways. This was a coughing fit he wouldn't be able to stop.

Looking back at Caleb who was sleeping peacefully, Molly decidedly got up from the bed and left the room. It took more effort than he was hoping for. His body was tired and achey, completely weighed down by flu.

His breath caught the minute he closed the bedroom door behind him. The coughs were wet and gross, bringing up gunk he hadn't realized was in his chest. He headed to the bathroom and grabbed a wad of toilet tissue, hacking into it.

When the fit finally ended, he was breathless. He spat the gunk into the paper and threw it away, and then just sat on the edge of the tub. What little energy Molly had was sapped by a tiny bit of coughing.

Fucking pathetic.

"Oh..."

Molly realized he never shut the door when he saw Fjord standing there.

"You feeling okay?"

"Oh yes, I'm fantastic, thank you," he said dryly.

"Well I mean, you're not... Pukey or anything?"

The cogs in his brain turned rather slowly before he realized he was sitting over the toilet. He chuckled and sighed.

"No... No, I just needed a rest. This is fun and all, but you could just tell me you need to piss and make me get out if your way."

Fjord's eyes widened. "No, I don't... well I- I heard..." He cleared his throat. "What're you doin' out of bed?" There was a hint of concern in Fjord's voice that was absent when he dragged Molly into Caleb's room earlier in the day.

Molly sighed, and of course it ended with him coughing pathetically at the floor. "Caleb's sleeping." And he didn't want to talk, was probably sick of Molly and just didn't want to say anything.

"Ah."

Molly coughed again, this time pulling his sleeve up to cover. He stood, and immediately had to lean himself against the sink as his vision swam.

"Fuck, Molly!" Fjord hissed. He rushed to Molly's side, hands on his arm as the tiefling rubbed at his eyes.

"Lightheaded."

"Yeah, I can see that. Let's get you back to bed, huh? Yours or Caleb's?"

Molly rubbed at his temple, feeling a budding sinus headache from all of the movement. He didn’t want to think right now. Did he have to? Oh God, he felt awful.

Fjord nodded after a while of silence. "Caleb's. I don’t think you should be alone right now."

"Well," He breathed out, giving a humorless chuckle. "I think it would be best if he got some sleep without me around." though he was conflicted. What would happen if Caleb woke and found himself alone? Another cough rumbled out of him, he made an effort to turn away from Fjord.

Molly was in the middle of regaining his balance and deciding where he wanted to be when they heard a stifled cry from Caleb's bedroom.

They found him in a panicked state, eyes darting around the room as if to be sure of his surroundings before locking eyes on the two of them. He made an effort to look less frazzled, pawing at his nose more as fidgeting than in irritation.

Molly decided to stay.

-----

"Mollymauk," Caleb spoke out of the blue as the two of them were playing a very tame game of cards.

"Mhmm?"

The hands that had been shuffling cards paused. "If you would like to hear about Nott and I, I believe I am willing to tell the story now."

Molly looked up to inspect the wizard. His eyes were glued to the cards. Caleb was never the best at eye contact. Molly admired his ability to be completely unreadable when he wanted to be. "Oh Caleb…" he started, softening his voice. "It was all boredom and curiosity. You hardly know me, after all."

"No," Caleb sighed. "If you are curious, I do not mind telling you about myself. The others have not asked, so I haven't told them, but you…"

"Honestly, I'd love to know your life story. You're a very mysterious fellow."

Caleb nodded, obviously already lost in thought, thinking of how he should speak. His mouth opened and closed before he finally found how to begin it. "My parents considered me a magical prodigy, so I was sent to a wealthy home to live. My mentor taught me many things, and one of them was fire magic. He showed me that my parents weren't good people, bringing memories to the surface of them lying and hurting others. He showed me that I could use the pain and anger, harness it for stronger magic." Caleb gestured vaguely with his hands as he told the story, but then they went back to fidgeting with a stack of cards.

"Oh dear…"

"…So I did. My spells - I - became stronger than any of my classmates. Trent would reward me with special meals and extra privileges. But do something badly, and he would punish me far worse than any of the other children." He stopped and swallowed, cleared his throat, muffled a cough into his shoulder. "I was having trouble falling asleep one night, so I took a wander around the house. Trent was in his study, he was on the phone, telling someone how talented I was. I was so proud of myself."

Molly swallowed hard.

"And then one day he told us what the end of our program was. Our 'finals' he called it. He took Eodwulf, Astrid, and I, the three oldest and more prominent students, and explained to us what we needed to do with criminals like our parents." Caleb took a deep, shaky breath. He explained how the killed Eodwulf's parents, then Astrids, and Caleb ended the story with, "I could hear their screams as they burned… and I broke."

Molly's eyes widened. "I…" for once in his life, he was speechless. He even felt a pang of fear of the man next to him before he remembered what kind of person he himself was. He breathed out heavily, only managing to cough and ruin the tone.

"I was… the right age to be sent to a detention center."

"Did he go to prison?"

Caleb's jaw set. "Unfortunately, no. My punishment for- well, it was to take the blame for the 'accidental fire' that killed my parents." It was an anger Molly never thought he'd see on the man. Flames of resentment and guilt burning behind watery eyes. "The three of us… graduated the program, but he continued to teach students." Caleb sniffled, and once again Molly knew it wasn’t from illness. He scrubbed the heel of his hand into his eyes, and Molly's heart broke. He wasn’t sure who this man was, especially now, but he knew that he still felt for him. "They're…snf heh… they're monsters now, I imagine. But perhaps, so am I."

"Astrid and-"

"Oh, I don’t know about them. I- they were stronger than I was."

Molly reached up, hesitantly at first, and then cupped Caleb's face. He thumbed away the tears that were starting to drip down his cheeks, and then leaned in to kiss his forehead. He wasn’t sure if it was his imagination, or if Caleb leaned into it. "You're not a monster, my dear."

Caleb chuckled without any real humor. "But I am. And that is why you are now the only one who knows my story. You may think I am dangerous but… I'd appreciate it if you-"

"Don't worry about that," Molly said with a dismissive wave. "I'm not in the business of giving away secrets."

Caleb nodded and averted his eyes. He slid the cards back into the box and slid under the covers with a shiver.

Molly joined him, turning his way and propping himself on his elbow. "Caleb,"

"Mm?"

"Why did you tell me all of this if you were worried of what I might do with it? I mean, I'm new. You obviously don’t trust anyone, let alone the newcomer."

Caleb fidgeted with the hem of the blanket. "I… I'm not sure. Maybe illness has affected my judgement."

Mollymauk hummed thoughtfully. "Well, thank you for sharing with me. It truly means a lot, my friend."

"Mm."
