Fantastic

The cold wasn’t anything horrible. Sneezing and congestion and a few other minor symptoms, but it didn’t stop him from going to Magnus’ later that night.

He pulled away to sneeze, jostling Magnus who’d been cuddled up to him in the process. Alec sniffled and went back to watching the movie.

“Bless you. You’ve been sneezing a lot, are you feeling alright?”

“Yeah, I’m fine,” Alec dismissed. “It’s just a cold, nothing major.”

His eyes were glued to the screen until he turned to find that his boyfriend was giving him a rather strange look.

“What?”

Magnus shook his head as if to clear it. “A- a cold, hm? That’s too bad.”

Alec shrugged. “I’ve just been sneezing, is all.” But Magnus was suddenly acting uncomfortable. “Oh, if you’re worried about germs, I can-”

“No!” Magnus said quickly, gripping his arm. He cleared his throat. “No, it’s alright. I can’t get sick.”

“Really?”

“Well, not easily, anyway. No, I don’t want you to leave, Alexander.” Magnus’ gaze averted once again before he went back to cuddling. Alec couldn’t help but feel like he did something wrong.

“Okay...” he sniffled. That was weird.

But nothing must have been too wrong, because they proceeded to have a pretty fantastic night.

-----

The next time Alec had a cold, it wasn’t nearly as ‘fun’. He’d fallen asleep at Magnus’ with a scratchy throat, and woken up with a stuffed, runny nose, and the worst sinus headache in the world. His scratchy throat was now so sore he could hardly swallow, and he was quickly depleting Magnus’ tissue supply as he spent most of the morning getting ready sneezing and blowing his nose.

Magnus was sympathetic. He made him something warm and easy to swallow for breakfast, along with a cup of tea and a plea to take a sick day. But... he was still acting a little strange. Hesitant? Alec wasn’t sure, and to be honest, he felt too gross to try to figure it out.

As horrible as Alec felt, and as much as he wanted to take a day off from the institute, he couldn’t. They already had enough shadowhunters down with the same cold from hell. They needed all hands on deck.

“I can’t,” Alec said sadly as he pulled his jacket on. His voice was equal-parts hoarse and congested, and he knew it was only going to get worse from there. “You know I can’t.”

Magnus sighed. He looked like he was about to say something else to try to convince him to stay home, but Alec’s nose chose that moment to act up.

The same stinging tickle he’d been feeling all morning tortured him for a few seconds, breath hitching as he rubbed his nose to get rid of it. It was no use, the itch was far too deep in his sinuses. He muffled a pair of painful sneezes into the collar of his jacket with a groan and sniffled wetly. It might do him some good to pick up some tissues on the way to the institute, he thought.

“Bless you,” Magnus hummed as he snaked his arms around Alec’s waist. Before Alec knew what was happening, they were kissing far too passionately than they should be.

Alec had to pull away sooner than he’d like, both because he couldn’t breathe through his nose and because it was dangerous to get this worked up before he went to work.

He smiled, sniffled. “Whoa, what did I do to deserve that?”

Magnus shrugged, playful smile on his face. “I’m just going to miss you today. I do really wish you would stay home and take care of your cold, but... I understand,” he breathed out the last word dramatically. “Go save the world or do paperwork or whatever you do,” he said, finally releasing Alec from his grip so he could wipe his nose.

No matter how much he tried, he couldn’t get rid of the moisture that sat at the base of his nostrils. ugh. “I’m surprised you want to kiss me when I’m like this. I know you’ve said you can’t catch it, but...I’m kinda gross.”

Magnus shook his head. “You’re beautiful, even with a runny nose.” he booped said nose which prompted Alec to start rubbing it once again.

Alec scoffed and kissed him one last time. “I’ll see you later.”

-----

Alec hoped that working would distract him from his cold, but instead, as many had warned him, it made it worse. It was impossible to focus on anything besides the constant itch in his nose and the pain in his throat. He struggled to find something in the cafeteria that looked appetizing (struggled more than usual, anyway) and eventually settled on a cup of applesauce so he could just go back to his office and wallow in his misery.

Izzy wandered in to get a signature for a mission report and proceed to lay his hand on his forehead as he was telling her he had a cold and to keep her distance.

“You’re not warm,” she said, taking the report back. “That’s good.”

Alec nodded. “Yeah, I just feel gross.” He snuffled and dragged the back of his hand along his nose, only for Izzy to pointedly nudge a box of tissues closer.

“Well, you have Magnus’ to look forward to after you get off, right?” She said playfully. “I’m assuming you’re going back there tonight?”

Alec sighed. “About that...”

Izzy’s expression turned serious. “Uh oh.”

“No, it’s fine- I mean, I think it is. It’s just that he was acting kind of weird this morning?”

“Like... distant?” Izzy asked worriedly.

“No, he actually kissed me. Uh, really well.” Alec shook his head, a chuckled at the memory of that amazing kiss that helped jump start his morning. Izzy made a disgusted noise. “No, he was just acting like... like he’s afraid to overstep, almost? But it’s not like he’s afraid of germs.”

“Okay, first of all,” Izzy pushed the tissues closer. “Blow your nose because it’s starting to run and I can hardly understand you.” Alec had to agree and began to tend to his nose. “Second, it could be nothing, but you should talk to him about it.”

Alec sighed. “I know you’re right. But what do I say? It wasn’t like he was in a bad mood, I don’t want him thinking that I’m over analyzing everything he does!” he picked up the nearly-useless tissue he’s just blown his nose in, breath hitching, and muffled a sneeze into it. Izzy took a couple steps back.

She simply shrugged. “Maybe you are, though. Maybe it is nothing.”

Alec had to admit, she had a point. Maybe he was thinking too much about this. He didn’t have a lot of experience in relationships before he started dating Magnus, maybe it really was nothing.

-----

He walked in the door of Magnus’ apartment and dropped his things on the end table, very ready to forget about work for the day.

Magnus emerged from his apothecary with a smile, which soon faded when he got a look at Alec.

Alec hadn’t bothered to look in a mirror today, but he knew he must have looked awful. He’d been blowing and rubbing his nose non-stop, and his eyes felt watery and swollen from the congestion.

“Oh, Alexander, you look...”

“I know,” Alex nodded, giving a stuffy sniff. He let Magnus lead him to- to the dining room, apparently. He couldn’t smell a thing, but he could see something delicious simmering on the stove.

Magnus pulled out a chair for him, a silent order for him to sit as he magicked some dishes and silverware from the cupboards.

Alec sneezed, and groaned. That one hurt.

“Bless you, darling,” Magnus said, though his voice shook just a little, just enough for Alec to feel insecure about the whole thing allover again.

He remembered his conversation with Izzy, you should talk to him about it. Ugh.

Magnus ladled some amazing-looking soup into a bowl and set it in front of him before placing a kiss to his temple and taking a seat as well.

“You didn’t have to cook,” Alec said. “I’m not even sure I’m going to be able to taste it.”

“Oh,” Magnus chuckled. “You’ll taste it.”

Alec wasn’t sure what that was supposed to mean, but after running on nothing but applesauce all day (shit, he really should have eaten more, shouldn’t he?), he was quick to dig in.

It tasted good, even though he was too stuffed to sense all of the flavors. Alec knew exactly what Magnus meant, though, when his nose began to run and his mouth started to tingle. He took a few more spoonfuls.

“Oh!”

Magnus nodded. “Is it helping?”

Alec took an experimental breath through his nose and found that it was indeed helping. The wet gurgle that resulted was gross, but it felt good to at least have the option of breathing through his nose.

“Yeah definitely,” Alec said with a sniffle. And then a wet tickle made itself known, much closer to the surface than the perpetual one that he’d been dealing with most of the day.

He swore under his breath before the sneeze finally came. And another. He felt something warm and wet on his upper lip and immediately realized that he was not going to be able to remove his hand from his face until he... figured this out.

Magnus set his spoon down and sputtered for a second before finally landing on a simple question. “Tissue?”

Alec nodded, heat rising in his cheeks. Perhaps this was why Magnus was acting strange. He was disgusted, reminded how ugly human mundane illnesses were, how unattractive they could make people.

Alec offered a breathy thank you as he accepted the wad of tissues Magnus pressed into the hand that wasn’t being used to cover the snot on his face. He pushed away from the table just enough so that he could fully turn without Magnus seeing him.

“Sorry,” he said when he was finished. “I’m really sorry, I- Magnus?”

Magnus was looking at him wide-eyed. He cleared his throat, shook his head and chuckled breathlessly. “You don’t need to have to apologize for sn- It’s my fault, anyway. I should have warned you about the soup. I knew it would help clear you up a bit, but I didn’t think it’d be so um, irritating.”

Alec raised an eyebrow. Magnus seemed... rattled. Maybe he hadn’t done something wrong, maybe he was continuing to do something wrong that was making Magnus so uncomfortable. He cursed his sister’s insistence that he talk things out in his new relationship.

“Are you okay?” Was all he could think to ask.

Magnus laughed and continued to eat his soup. “Why wouldn’t I be okay?”

Alec shrugged. “I mean... you’ve just been acting a little weird today. You would tell me if I did something to upset you, wouldn’t you?”

Magnus sighed heavily. A spike of anxiety suddenly rushed through Alec. Had there been some obvious problem he’d just been oblivious to?

“You didn’t do anything to upset me, Alexander. I promise.”

“Then why do you look so freaked out right now?”

“I’m not freaked out,” Magnus said quickly. He cleared his throat, rolled his eyes at himself. “I’m just having a hard time balancing my attraction to you with the desire to take care of you when you’re not feeling well.”

Alec was relieved to find out that it wasn’t something he’d done, but it didn’t stop him from being confused. “What... exactly does that mean?”

“It means...” Magnus took a deep breath. “It means that...”

“...Magnus?”

“You’ve been... sneezing quite a bit today.” Magnus said, and Alec was prepared to say that he was very aware of that before Magnus continued. “It’s just getting me a bit worked up.”

Alec blinked. He thought for a moment. His brain was a little slow from the cold ravaging his system, but... “Are you- you mean- the sneezing...?”

Magnus closed his eyes and nodded sheepishly.

Alec couldn’t help but chuckle, and felt guilty when Magnus looked even worse. “I mean, you could have told me.”

Magnus made a small whining noise in the back of his throat. “The last thing I want you to think is that I’m enjoying your misery, Alexander.”

Everything made a little more sense now. Alec thought back to his last cold. Magnus was quite into their time together, even more than normal if that was possible. He assumed that it was just one of those nights where everything was great. The point, though, was that the sex they had that night was fantastic whether Alec was suffering from a cold at the time or not.

Alec laughed.

“Alexander, I’m kind of wearing my heart on my sleeve right now, if you could not laugh at me.” He looked very much like he was regretting his confession. Alec made a straight face yet again. Magnus was right, he was putting himself out there for him.

“I’m not laughing at you, Magnus, I promise. I just... I was thinking about the night we spent together the last time I had a cold.”

Magnus’ face turned a deep shade of red. He failed to bite back a smile. “Ah, yes... that night.”

“Yeah, that night,” Alec repeated. He sighed, giving a sniffle. Having an important conversation while your nose was running wasn’t exactly ideal. “Magnus, you should have just told me.”

Magnus nodded. “I know. I’m sorry, Alexander.”

Alec grinned. “If I’m feeling better tomorrow, maybe you can show me exactly what you love about all of... this.” He waved a hand in front of his face.

Magnus chuckled a bit nervously. “Only if you’re alright with that.”

“If it’s gonna be anything like last time, I’m more than alright with that. Tonight, though...”

Magnus shook his head and leaned over to kiss his cheek. “Definitely none of that tonight. You need to rest.”

“You going to be able to handle that?” Alec asked with a playful smile.

Magnus made a face at his teasing. Alec knew he was capable of controlling himself for one night, but this was going to be fun to tease him about for eternity. “Oh, I’ll manage.”

-----

“Are you sure you’re up for this?” Magnus asked. “I’d hate for you to make yourself feel worse.”

Alec sniffled. He, admittedly, did not wake up feeling better in the morning. The day of working too much and eating too little seemed to have had an effect. Magnus didn’t even have to try to talk him into staying home from work, because Izzy sent a text begging him to before Magnus had a chance.

Now, his boyfriend was sitting on his lap with a box of tissues and an eager look on his face.

“Yes, I’m up for this, Magnus. Just warn me before you-” he sneezed suddenly, only just being able to bring a hand up to shield Magnus from the explosion.

“Oh dear,” Magnus said breathlessly, not even giving Alec a chance to wipe his nose before he pulled him in for a kiss.

Alec made a noise that was a mix between a delighted chuckle, and a congested snort. He pulled away with a wet sniffle. By the way Magnus’ eyes were transfixed, his nose was visibly running now. “Sorry, had to breathe.” He snuffled again and went to rub his nose on his hand before he stopped himself. “Oh, did you want me to-?”

“I can,” Magnus said quickly.

Alec let him press a couple of tissues to his nose. It was strange to blow his nose when someone else was holding the tissue, but not entirely unwelcome. In fact, he sort of enjoyed it. It was a nice mix of being taken care of and watching his boyfriend get worked up out of his mind.

“Now what?” Alec asked.

“Now, if you’ll let me...” Magnus twisted a tissue into a point. “I’d like to make you sneeze.”

Alec raised his eyebrows, smile playing at his lips. “Oh boy.”

Magnus quickly pulled back. “Too much?” he asked with a grimace.

“No! Just... be ready. My nose is... itchy.”

Magnus’ face got redder. “I’m very ready.”
