Caring Too Much

Dr. Beckham was one of those people who was constantly a surprise. One would describe him as 'in rare form' except that it would be all the time because the man was just that weird. Great at what he did, though.

All of that to say that Eli was a little curious when she walked into therapy and Dr. Beck had to clear his throat about five thousand times before he was able to successfully ask her how she was. They went over the usual questions: How were things at the house? Getting along with everyone?

"Have you been feeling good lately?" He didn't seem to realize the irony of asking this before he had to duck his head to blow his nose. He really didn't sound that bad, but the stuffiness had become more clear in his voice as they'd gone on.

"Yeah, I think so." She smirked. "Have you?"

Dr. Beck sighed and rolled his eyes as he lowered the tissue, giving a sniff. "I think you know the answer to that one."

She scoffed. They continued talking until the time was up and Eli's brain was sufficiently shrunk for the week. She heard an itchy coughing fit erupt from the doctor as the door closed behind her, and Eli winced. Hopefully Dr. Beckham would rest tonight.

-----

"Have you seen my shoe?"

Lucas showed up in the doorway of the girls' room with one socked foot and one loafer. Eli was only half dressed, but it was hard to be modest at this point. They'd already lived together for months. All of them had seen much worse than each other's bras.

"Nope," she said, popping the 'p'.

"Did you check under the couch?" Anna offered.

Lucas scoffed and put his hands on his hips. "Uh, no, why would my one shoe be in my bedroom and the other one be all the way downstairs under the couch? Ridiculous."

Anna shrugged.

He sighed dramatically and left. Just a minute later, they hear a "What?!" from the ground floor.

They snorted. He'd found his shoe.

"Is Dr. Beckham still visiting today?"

"As far as I know. Why?"

"Dunno. No reason."

As if on cue, there was another series of sounds from downstairs. The door opened, closed, and then a horrible sneeze that had them both cringing.

They exchanged a look and finished getting dressed.

"Jesus, Dr. Beck," Eli said as they came down the stairs. What looked like a small cold at therapy a couple days before was now not so small. His nose was red as if it'd done nothing but get abused for those two days, and his eyes were red and puffy, and he sounded as if he couldn't breathe through his mouth.

"Uh uh, don't bring that in here," Lobo half-teased as she came from the kitchen.

Dr. Beckham chuckled, raising his hands in surrender even as one of them held the tissue he was about to use. "I'm not here for long, I promise. I just didn't want you all to think I forgot about you."

Lucas chimed in and made his presence known around that time as well. "Jeeesus. What happened to you?"

"Germs," Eli answered, crossing her arms and smirking.

"Well," Dr. Beck couldn't argue with that one. "Yes."

Lobo chuckled. "You look like you should be in bed. No one's gonna be heartbroken because you're not sharing whatever that is with everyone else."

He sighed, shoulders falling in defeat. The poor man hadn't even made it through the foyer and he was already being kicked out.

"Alright, point taken. I'm leaving."

He left, but not before giving a sniffle that signifies how stuffed his nose actually was.

Lobo shook her head in exasperation. "That man cares about all of you that much, you know that?" and she went back to the kitchen.
