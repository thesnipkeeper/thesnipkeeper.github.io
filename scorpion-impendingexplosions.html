Impending Explosions

Everyone around the garage had been getting sick. It was just a cold, nothing serious. Well, Toby never thought a common cold could be serious until Walter lost his balance and nearly face-planted.

It turned out that he hadn’t eaten or drank anything in a while, but the cold certainly didn’t help matters.

“I have work to do,” Walter protested weakly as Toby shoved him onto his bed. He practically had to drag his friend up the stairs in the first place, and not just because Walter didn’t want to go up. He sniffled and dragged the back of his hand against his nose. “Toby.” The intense look he was trying to give did nothing when his eyes were watery and his nose was running far more than he realized.

“Oh yeah, I’m sure you do,” Toby scoffed. “Too bad you’re gonna be up here resting like a normal person.”

“No one else is resting,” Walter muttered, sounding dangerously close to a pouting child.

“Well, no one else skipped breakfast and lunch and then drank a shit ton of coffee.” Was Walter even listening? Probably not because he was starting to get out of bed before Toby set a hand on his shoulder. “Ugh, Walter, would you just listen to me? You’re useless right now, your body is begging for sleep. Just- just a few hours! God!” Toby’s own frustration must have beaten Walter’s stubbornness, because his boss huffed and leaned back.

“You know, you’re not actually my doctor. Or a doctor.”

“Hey, I went to medical school and that’s good enough.” Toby threw a granola bar and a bottle of water at him on his way out. “Eat that, drink this, and then sleep until you’re not half-dead.”

Walter scoffed. Toby thought that he was in for another argument before he heard a quiet, “Thanks, buddy.”

So, Toby replied accordingly. “Don’t die!” Before going back downstairs.

The garage had been a cacophony of sniffles, sneezes and coughs for the better part of the week. Toby... may have been patient zero. And then Happy, Paige and Ralph...

Toby felt fine now, which he wasn’t sure was a good thing as the rest of the gang were not too appreciative.

“How is he?” Paige asked.

“Asleep, hopefully! Anywho...” He scanned the garage for a teammate to bother, and trailed off when he caught something unusual.

Sylvester, the last man standing, had been an anxious mess most of the week. His fear of germs had him skittering around the place like a scared squirrel, avoiding everyone and everything. He’d ask anyone who came near to keep their distance, and they knew enough to respect it.

This was more severe than Toby had seen in the past few days. He knew what someone - especially one of his friends - looked like when they were on the verge of a panic attack. Walter’s little episode must have exasperated things.

“Sly...” Toby said, stuffing his hands in his pockets as he made his way over to Sylvester’s ultra-sterile workspace. “You good?”

Sylvester looked up with wide eyes and immediately put on the most unconvincing smile. “Yeah! Yeah, I’m fine.”

He tensed up more the closer Toby got, so he stopped and looked over his friend from where he was.

“You sure? Because you look kinda like you’re-”

“I said that I’m fine, okay?!” Sylvester huffed and clumsily started doing equations that he probably didn’t have any need for.

“Look-” he tried to get closer, but Sylvester looked up once again, even more scared this time. Toby raised his hands non-threateningly and grabbed one of the many bottles of hand sanitizer scattered throughout the office. He made a show of scrubbing it over his hands, which seemed to calm Sylvester a little. “This situation might be a little stressful...”

“I’m fine.”

But his hands were shaking so much that he couldn’t hold his pencil steady anymore. God damn it, so this was how Toby’s day was going to be.

“Well, you’re obviously not,” Toby stated, gesturing to his hands. “Sly, why don’t you take a minute to- I dunno, relax? You seem like you’re about to-”

“Relax?!” Sylvester stood up. “How can I relax right now, Toby?”

His eyes were watering, lower lip trembling. About to explode Toby was going to say, but apparently he was too late.

Sylvester gave a choking sigh, a humorless chuckle. “Look, sorry, I... I just don’t want to talk about this. Any of this. It’s best for everyone if I just-”

“Ignore it? You know, ignoring your anxieties has proven to make things worse and cause-”

“Toby,” Sylvester begged. “I can’t. Please just leave me alone.”

So Toby did. He’d known Sylvester long enough that he wasn’t a stranger to one of his bad moods. It usually happened when he was particularly focused, but this could happen as well.

No one really talked about Sylvester’s phobias and anxieties anymore. It was sort of a thing that everyone knew about, no reason to discuss it. But watching his friend try to write with a shaky hand while holding back tears and trying to keep his breath steady wasn’t the easiest thing in the world. Sylvester’s panic attacks could usually be helped, but not when he was afraid of being around any of them.

Paige sneezed at her desk, Toby heard a pencil drop at Sylvester’s.

What was Toby doing? Oh, right, looking for someone to bother.

He leaned against the table that held a particularly interesting contraption Happy was working on. She was sniffling intermittently as she did calculations, muttering to herself rather adorably.

“Sylvester’s freaking the hell out right now,” he said.

Happy coughed and sighed. “He’s been freaking the hell out all week.”

“Yeah, but look at him.”

Despite her obvious reluctance, she did, craning her head past the stuff between their sides of the room. She frowned the moment she got a look at their friend. It didn’t take a professional behaviorist to see that something was wrong.

“Okay, not good,” she agreed.

“He’s like a bomb trying not to explode.”

“But he’s gonna.”

“You think I don’t know that? But it’s not like we can force him to let us help.”

“Well he’s not gonna let me anywhere near him. Or anyone else. You’re the only healthy person here. Got to hell, by the way.” Happy coughed once more.

Toby scoffed and outstretched his hands. “How many times do I have to say that I’m sorry?”

“Once would be a good start.”

“Psh, whatever. Anywho, I’m kinda worried about him.”

Happy spared another glance up at their friend with an anxious look of her own before going back to her work. “Has he eaten anything today?”

He shrugged. “Don’t think he wants to go in the kitchen. Common area.”

“Ugh, God. Poor guy.” She coughed, this time for longer. The sound was deeper, striking unwarranted concern in Toby.

“That cough doesn’t sound good,” he sing-songed.

Instead of arguing, Happy made a noise of agreement and spun around to her desk. “Think the medicine’s wearing off.” She went to grab a red bottle, but Toby beat her to it. “Okay, what the hell?”

“Taking constant cough suppressants can only make it worse. You’re supposed to hack all that gunk up to get the virus out of your system.”

“Gross. Give it to me.” She went for it, but he held it out of her reach.

“No can-do! It’s my job to keep this group healthy!”

“It obviously isn’t. Toby, give me the damn medicine! I’m not too sick to rip your head off.”

Toby found himself indeed dropping it. She still knew how to strike fear into anyone’s heart. “Fine, but don’t come crying to me if your cough gets worse and your cold lasts forever.”

“Trust me, I don’t plan on it.” She took a dose like a shot, and he had to give her props for not even wincing at the taste. “Keep an eye on Sylvester, okay? Before he... y’know...”

“Explodes.”

“Yeah.”

“I’ll sure try.”

First things first... get him to eat something without thinking about how many germs were on it. Packaged food was good, less-likely to freak him out, right? So Toby grabbed another granola bar from his stash. And a disinfectant wipe. God damn it, these were the good ones, the chocolate-dipped granola bars he hid from everyone else.

Oh well, going to a good cause, he supposed.

Unfortunately, the first thing Sylvester said when he set it on his desk was, “I’m not hungry.”

“I haven’t seen you eat anything all day, I disinfected the entire thing. C’mon, Sly.”

Sylvester was an easy person to break. He muttered a “fine” and carefully opened the wrapper before taking a bite. “Happy?”

“Ecstatic. Thanks, buddy.” He started to walk away with no true intention of leaving his friend alone, and turned around. “So, wanna go for a walk?”

“What?” Sylvester barely looked up from his work as he nibbled on the granola bar. “Why?”

Toby shrugged. “Could use some air, y’know?”

Sylvester’s movements were still those of a bundle of nerves. His pencil missed the cup when he tried to put it back and he gave a frustrated huff that wasn’t common for Sylvester Dodd. He narrowed his eyes at Toby for a split second before sighing.

“I- okay, fine...”

Toby nodded victoriously. “Let’s go!”

He thought that going for a walk would kind of help Sylvester’s nerves, get him a little calmer. Toby didn’t expect him to start tearing up the moment they walked outside. He swore.

“God, Sly, no one’s gonna be mad if you just go home. I know this isn’t... the easiest thing for you to deal with.”

Sylvester shook his head and sniffled. Tears were dripping freely down his cheeks as he was probably afraid to touch his face at all. “Sorry. I’m sorry. I just need like... a minute and I can go back-”

“No no... Sly...” Toby went to reach out for him, to offer some kind of comfort, but Sylvester moved away from him. Right. He didn’t want to be touched today.

His breath quickened, and Toby knew this was it. The explosion. The panic attack that was coming sooner or later because the poor guy had spent the past few days pushing it away and doing everything to stay strong. All Toby could do was be there.

So, he sat with Sylvester on the pavement. At least he got Sly to do that much before he started to hyperventilate and make himself dizzy.

“Hey, Sly...” he said carefully, getting Sylvester’s attention. “You wanna match my breath?”

He did. In and out. In and Out. Slow, deep breaths that would catch every once in a while.

Soon, they just sat quietly. Sylvester’s breathing was still quick and shallow, but the worst of it had passed.

“Wanna talk about it?” The question was usually used as a joke when Toby thought it would be fun to bug his friends with psychoanalyses, but it wasn’t often a joke with Sylvester.

Sylvester’s breath caught on a sob. “I just wish I could-” his face crumbled, and he took a moment to get himself together with a sniffle. “I should’ve been there was Walter before he made himself sicker, but I was too busy-”

“Hey, okay, first of all Walter isn’t your job,” Toby said steadily. “Second, he understands. We all do.”

Sylvester looked at the pavement and sighed heavily. “It’s just hard not to feel guilty when I’m... like this.”

“Buddy, there’s no reason to feel guilty. I mean, I’m the one who caused this whole outbreak, and you don’t see me feeling guilty, right?” Yeah, like that was going to help someone who was in the middle of an anxiety attack. “Look, I think you just need some time to cool down. We could stay out here for a while, or... I dunno, all day, if you wanted.”

Sylvester sighed, sniffled. “I don’t know. I don’t know what I want to do.”

“Or we could go get lunch somewhere. I’m sure getting away from this place for a while would help, right? Also, you didn’t even finish your granola bar earlier.”

He gave a watery chuckle. His fingers were tapping against his thigh, still filled to the brim with nervous energy. A quiet, broken response came, “I’m not the one who needs taken care of right now.”

Toby frowned. “Sly...”

“No, look, you should be here for Walter. And I can... I can just sit out here for a while.”

“Sly. Walter has a cold. He’s gonna be fine. And you know how he ended up where he is? By not eating anything. C’mon.”

Sylvester made an unsure noise. Toby stood up.

“Whatever! I’m leaving without you, then! Gonna go to that burger joint down the road!”

Sylvester looked up at him and swallowed. “The- the one with the bacon-wrapped-?”

“Yup! All by myself!”

“I guess I could...”

Toby clapped and cheered. “Woo! Let’s a’go!”

The day didn’t end perfectly. Sylvester still wasn’t solved in Toby’s opinion, but lunch at least gave him a chance to relax before they had to return to Scorpion.

He dropped Sylvester off at home that night. The ride was quiet, way too quiet, so Toby filled the silence with his own ramblings about a famous psychologist who didn’t know shit. Sylvester, though not enthusiastic, agreed and offered his own opinions. It wouldn’t surprise Toby if he was tired from a day - a week - full of anxiety, so he didn’t question the quietness.

“Thanks, Toby,” Sylvester said. “I owe you one.”

“The fact that you don’t is what I’ve been trying to get across to you all day, pal. Go take care of yourself.”

Sylvester nodded, smiled, and made his way into his apartment building. Toby tried to ignore the warm feeling in his chest that came from caring for his friends. He decided not to think about the psychological causes of it.

-----

Toby received a text the next morning.

I won’t need a ride to work today

He raised an eyebrow. Maybe Sylvester was finally giving in and hiding until the whole cold outbreak thing was over and done with. After seeing his state yesterday, Toby couldn’t exactly blame him.

But there was still worry nagging him in the back of his mind. So, instead of texting back, he called Sylvester.

“Hello?”

His voice sounded rough, like maybe he’d been crying again.

“Everything okay?” Toby asked as he got ready for work himself.

“Uh, yeah. Yeah, I’m fine, just... I’d rather not go in today. I can do work from here.”

Toby took in the tone of his voice. He was lying, at least a little bit.

“Mmm’kay. Feel better after yesterday?” Pants. He needed pants. Toby delved into the pile of laundry he’d need to fold at some point.

“Um...”

Toby paused. That was weird. Sylvester was being weird. “Why are you being weird? Should I be concerned?”

“No! I’m- I’m fine-”

And Toby might have believed him if that assurance wasn’t followed by the same kind of cough he’d been hearing from himself and the rest of his coworkers all week.

“Oh, Sly...” he sighed. “Sorry, buddy. You... need anything?”

Sylvester was quiet for a moment. “I- would you mind-?”

“Yyyesss?”

“Would you mind coming over just to make sure that- Well... I don’t know. I’m just a little anxious and I could use some reassurance. If! if you have time.”

“Sure,” Toby decided. “Aww, Sly, look at you asking for help. This is improvement!”

He could practically hear Sylvester rolling his eyes as he chuckled.

-----

Toby wasn’t sure what he expected, but it wasn’t Sylvester being as wrecked as he was. Yeah, fine, Sylvester freaked out over the tiniest of symptoms sometimes. But this cold clearly hit him hard.

His nose was already turning red, and his voice was wrecked from the coughing fits that had Toby wincing in sympathy.

“Thank you, thank you,” Sylvester said as he led Toby to the couch where he’d clearly been nesting. He must have slept there the night before.

“No problem. Anything to keep me from listening to Walter’s grumpy commands a while longer.” Granted, everyone had been rather grumpy. “Sit down?”

Sylvester did so, and offered easy access when Toby pressed the inside of his wrist to his forehead, and then his neck.

“Where’s your thermometer?”

“Which one?” Sylvester pointed to two different thermometers, sitting perfectly parallel on the coffee table. “What? I appreciate accuracy!” He defended to Toby’s weird look.

“Alright, whatever.”

He picked up the closest one and handed it to Sylvester. They waited awkwardly for the beep, and the resulting number was higher than Walter’s was the day before.

“Well, you must feel like shit.”

“What, why? Is it bad?”

He tipped the display toward Sylvester, who adjusted his glasses and leaned in with a squint.

“Oh. Oh God. I could have pneumonia, or I could have-”

“A cold, Sly. You have a cold. And you know why your body is working harder to fight? Because you’ve hardly eaten and you’ve been in a constant state of anxiety for days.”

Sylvester still looked concerned, but he closed his mouth as he thought about it. “Are you sure?”

“Positive. But...” he sighed. “I can check back later if you want me to.”

“You’d do that?”

“Are you gonna be okay here?”

He thought for a moment and sniffled uselessly. He must have felt miserable, but he seemed too concerned that he might be dying to actually notice his symptoms. “I think I’ll be better today than I’ve been all week, to be completely honest...”

“Okay.” he patted Sylvester’s shoulder, and this time he didn’t flinch. “Take it easy, buddy. And cut yourself some slack.”

He nodded. “Thanks, Toby. Um-”

Toby stopped on his way to the door, turning on his heel. “Mm?”

“Can you let Walter know that I’m not coming in? And- and tell him I’m sorry.”

“Walter’s not gonna be upset that you’re taking a day off.”

“No, I mean sorry for being...” Sylvester sighed and closed his eyes for a moment, before muttering out a “Me.”

Toby took a deep breath and nodded, offering a small smile. And then blurted, “Naw, I’m not gonna do that. Feel better!”

He left before Sylvester could worry any more.
