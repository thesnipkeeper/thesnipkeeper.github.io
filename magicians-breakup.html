Title: Breakup
Fandom: Magicians
Summary: Eliot has some things to fix, and one of those things might be Quentin
Word count: 3.1k

Breakup

The whole thing with Alice hasn’t gone over well. She's not speaking to Quentin, or any of them, really. Eliot doesn’t blame her. Quentin blames everyone, including himself. He loved her.

Quentin hasn’t left his room in days. Well, that's not true. He goes to class, and then comes back looking as horrible as he did when he left, and goes up to his room to hide until class the next morning. It's starting to get pathetic, and Eliot tells him this. He's rewarded with an eye roll and a door shut in his face.

New plan, then?

Eliot is suffering from his own, though, less serious misery. He woke up with the worst sore throat he's had in years and skipped class this morning, choosing instead to stay in the cottage and drink his favorite tea. His throat is still a little scratchy, but most of it has been replaced by all of the awful symptoms that one expects to come with an upper-respiratory infection. In a nutshell? He can't fucking breathe. He can't stop sneezing, or coughing, or falling asleep when he's studying for once in his life because that’s how bored he is when he can't drink.

He closes his textbook with a groan, feeling the telltale itch in the back of his sinuses. His nose won't give him a god damn break. The itch turns to an overwhelming tickle and Eliot tries his best to scrub it away, but of course it's not that easy. Eliot grinds out a quick sound of annoyance before managing to grab his handkerchief to muffle the sound.

"Jesus christ, El. I always forget how loud your sneezes are." Margo, beautiful as ever, confidently walks into the room as Eliot's wiping his nose. He needs to rid himself of some of the congestion, really, but his kerchief has had it for the day and he's not about to evacuate his sinuses in front of Margo. She raises an eyebrow at the contents of his desk. "Since when do you study?" she asks with disgust.

"Ugh," Eliot falls back in his desk chair, leaning his head back like a ragdoll. "There's nothing else. I'm too tired to do anything fun."

A small, cool hand drapes across his forehead, and Margo hums. "You're feeling worse." it's not a question, and she's right. He's gone from a sore throat and feeling a little sleepy to shivering, exhausted, and unable to go five minutes without sneezing or coughing.

He didn’t even stop to consider that he might have a fever on top of everything else, but it makes sense why the cottage feels either too hot or too cold, or too hot and too cold. He takes Margo's hand in his own and nuzzles her palm as if it'll fix everything.

"Talked to Q at all?"

"He's not gonna talk to me."

Eliot knows she doesn’t have remorse for sleeping with him, nor should she, really. But her refusal to give in clouds her judgement to the point where she can't see that he's suffering and not just angry that they had sex.

"Maybe I should check on him."

"Why bother?" Margo says with a scoff.

Eliot pulls himself out of his chair with great effort. A sinus headache starts to settle in from the change in position, but he chooses to ignore it. Margo pouts.

"Seriously?"

He nods before a tickle appears in his throat and he finds himself coughing into his elbow. "Seriously."

"It's not worth it, El."

"I'll decide that…" Eliot kisses the top of her head and walks off into the hallway. He realizes too late that he's wearing… less than amazing clothes, as he hasn’t needed to leave the cottage all day. His robe and a pair of lounge pants reserved for lazy days and hangovers. He pads down the hallway until he reaches Quentin's door, and wipes any possible wetness from his nose before knocking. The sleeves of his robe have probably had it by now as well, but he can't bother to care.

"Who is it?" Even the simple sentence sounds terrible. It's obvious that Quentin hasn’t used his voice in a few hours.

"Who do you think?"

A few seconds of silence go by, and Eliot wonders if he's going to be waiting for nothing to happen.

"Come in I guess."

That's probably as good of an invitation as he's gonna get, so he takes it, letting himself into Quentin's room and closing the door behind him. That's when he gets a good look at the room, at his friend. Quentin is in bed, flipping absently through a Fillory book that he surely has memorized by now.

"Heeeyy…" Eliot says softly, cautiously, as if any word is going to break him. "Haven't heard from you in a while. We live in the same house, you know."

Quentin glances at him and then goes right back to not reading his book. "You were right."

"Hm?"

"I am pathetic," the last word comes out tightly, and Eliot knows what could come next if he doesn’t tread carefully.

He closes his eyes and swears at himself for saying something so ridiculously insensitive. And he was calling Margo the one who's bad at emotions. Eliot carefully climbs onto the bed until he's sitting cross-legged over a tangled sheet. Quentin doesn’t kick him out, so he stays.

"I shouldn’t have-" a sigh. "I'm sorry, Q. That wasn’t what I meant."

The Fillory book falls shut, and Quentin sits up against his headboard, still not bothering to look Eliot in the eyes. "What the fuck did you mean then?"

"I hate- no, we hate seeing you like this. Margo doesn’t show it, but she's worried, Quentin." He gently lays a hand on Quentin's to get his attention, and finally red-rimmed eyes look up at him. "What's going on?"

"Can't focus on anything, think about anything… I just really, really hate myself. Why would I do something so awful to her, Eliot? She didn’t deserve that. She- oh God."

Eliot shushes him, rubbing his arm softly. It's like Quentin hasn’t bothered to cry since it all happened. He's isolated himself, used classes and homework as a distraction from it all. "Don’t you dare hate yourself."

A snort. "Thanks. I'm cured."

That sad little joke proves to Eliot that he's still good ol' Q, depressed or not.

"Oh, Q… you're more amazing than you'll ever know." Quentin shoots him a look of disbelief, and Eliot just grins. He pulls a piece of that long curtain of hair behind Q's ear, and… of course his nose ruins the moment.

The dry sting that's constantly there turns to an itch, and he can't stop the release. He quickly pulls his robe collar over his nose and mouth to muffle the deafening sneeze, and punctuates it with a congested snrf. He should have brought a handkerchief. Or tissues. Or something to stop him from being a biohazard when he's trying to be cute.

"Are you okay?" Quentin asks, brows furrowed in curiosity.

Eliot sighs dramatically. "I'm afraid I've been defeated by a simple virus. Say your prayers for me, dear Q."

Quentin finally cracks a genuine smile. "Jesus christ."

Eliot gives a small, unsatisfying cough into his elbow, and the sits back, silent, save for a few sniffles as his sinuses adjust to the new position.

Quentin eventually lies back down, getting under the covers and curling up away from Eliot.

"You and that beautiful curtain of hair is my favorite thing to see in the morning, you know."

"Oh yeah?"

"Mhm. You're one of my favorite people."

"That's a bad idea on your part. No offense."

"No no," Eliot sunk down into the bed and wrapped himself around Quentin from behind. "No self-deprecation."

"Telling me nice things isn't really going to fix anything, you know that right?"

"Of course," he says softly, tucking Quentin's head under his chin. "But I can sure try." The sentence was punctuated with a sniffle, and he hoped to God that his nose wouldn't start dripping on the boy he likes. "I just want to make sure that you know... You don't have to go through this alone. I'm here, even Margo, though she doesn't show it. You might blame us for your break-up, and I'm sorry if that's the case, but-"

"I don't." Quentin interrupts, voice full of defeat. "I was just mad at myself and I needed someone else to blame."

Eliot nods wordlessly. He can understand that.

"Look, El." Quentin breathes out. "I'm not worth all this." he makes no move to kick Eliot out or tell him to leave.

"You're worth everything. You make this cottage a better place by not putting up with everyone's bullshit. You- God, h-hold on..." He mutters shakily before an urgent intake of breath. He turns his head as far away as it'll go in his position and shields his hand on the side of his face, and the whole bed shakes with the force of the sneeze. He finishes of with a thick sniff that accomplishes nothing, grumbling a quiet, "Fuuuuck..."

Much to his delight, however, Quentin giggles. "Bless you." If he knew it would be that easy to make him laugh, he wouldn’t have been so reluctant to come and invade his space before now.

Eliot sniffles again and rubs his nose against the back of his wrist, wincing when he can see a shiny streak under the bedside lamp. He gives one final sniff before reclaiming his spot spooned against Quentin.

"What was I saying? Oh, yes, I was telling you how amazing you are."

Quentin's body shakes as he scoffs.

"Now now, I think you should listen to me very carefully," he leans in more and then feels guilty when he accidentally sniffles in his friend's ear. "You have more faith in magic than anyone else I've ever met."

"That's not always a good thing, is it?" It's so quiet, but Eliot isn't going to let it slide.

"All I know is that you refused to give up when the rest of us did. And you can lie in bed all day being upset about who you are - I'm serious, you can. You have the right and I'm not making fun of you. - but just know that you've done more than you'll ever know for this school." He tries to be quiet about clearing gunk out of his throat before adding, "And for me. And Margo, and...oh, Q. You're just such a sweet person," he presses a kiss to Quentin's head, and the other hums happily and leans into him more.

"So, okay wait," Quentin says as his mouth quirks up and he turns over to face Eliot, "When did Eliot Waugh get sincere?"

Eliot feels obligated to draw his face back slightly away from Quentin's, knowing his entire face is full of germs right now, and feigns offense. "Excuse me?"

Quentin chuckles and looks up at Eliot with that adorable puppy look that always has him weak at the knees.

-----

Eliot manages to get him out of bed the next day. Part of him wishes it isn't a weekend so he could at least get Quentin up for class so it would be easier to drag him out. Still, Quentin isn't in a terrible mood as Eliot throws a change of clothes at him and tells him to get dressed while he goes and takes an ungodly amount of decongestants. Quentin chuckles tiredly and starts taking off his gross shirt.

Though he usually prefers to smell of nice perfume or his expensive shampoo, Eliot needs to accept that today he's absolutely going to smell like cough drops. He pops one in as discretely as possible when he and Quentin head downstairs.

"Where is everyone?" Quentin asks, seeing the cottage empty besides a few studying first-years. They spare a quick glance at the two of them before going back to their frantic studying, and he's thankful that's all the attention he gets.

"There's a concert going on in the auditorium." Eliot says before running the back of his wrist under his nose. The decongestants are working. "Ridiculous magical band, in my opinion, but there isn't much entertainment to come by around here, so everyone's desperate for something new." There's a few sniffles betweens sentences. "Now, have a seat," Eliot pulls out a chair at the dining table and then leaves into the cottage's kitchen.

Quentin sighs tiredly. "What are we doing here, El?"

Eliot pointedly ignores the question and responds with his own. "When's the last time you ate an actual meal?"

The silence that follows confirms his suspicions. "Um..."

"Thought so! What are you hungry for?"

"I don't even... Cereal, I guess. It's morning " He shrugs.

The cottage has the basics when it comes to groceries, the school gracious enough to provide things that college students usually aren't able to afford. Eliot considers pouring the cereal himself, and thinks better of it when he remembers he's probably very contagious. So, he grabs a bowl, a cereal box, and a little carton of milk and sets it all in front of Quentin, who blinks in surprise.

"I think it's best if I don't touch anything you eat," Eliot explains, gesturing lamely at his current state.

Quentin cracks a little smile and nods. "Yeah... How- how are you feeling, anyway? I mean, no offense, but you kind of look like shit."

If Eliot's honest, he doesn't feel much better. The second day of his cold comes with a lot more congestion and mucus and coughs and everything horrible. His limbs feel like lead, his head stuffed with cotton. His ears pop when he swallows and they're not working exactly how they're supposed to.

Eliot chuckles and flops into a chair across from him. "I'd tell you that I didn't feel like shit, but that would be a terrible lie." He finishes with an embarrassingly wet sniffle.

-----

Eliot's trying to set up a movie night in Quentin's room, sniffling continuously. He doesn't know how Quentin isn't annoyed yet because he's annoyed with himself. All of the leaning over and moving around is just asking gravity to do it's thing.

"Snf! Ugh... Okay - snf! - hold on, I think I-"

"Eliot?" Quentin's got an adorable smile on his face like he's trying not to look too amused. "Need a tissue?" He asks, holding a box out. Eliot quickly accepts and presses one to his nose with a blush.

"Ugh, I'm a fucking mess," he says with a self-deprecating sigh. "Sorry, Q." He wipes his nose as thoroughly as he can without blowing it, because ew, Quentin probably thinks he's gross enough already.

"Wait, why are you sorry?" Quentin blinks in surprise.

"Contaminating your room, being disgusting, looking like shit..."

Quentin snorts, unable to hide his amusement this time. "Oh." He watches as Eliot goes back to setting up a TV, and adds, "I'm glad you care, El."

The sincere words make warmth swell up in his chest in a way he hasn't experienced in so long. Eliot bites back a smile as he looks back up at Quentin. "Love you, Q." It comes out before he can stop it, but Quentin doesn't look surprised one bit.

-----

Eliot finds himself unable to keep his eyes open halfway through the movie. His head ends up on Quentin's lap, getting his hair carded through in a way that seems to make him feel a little better. He hasn't really paid attention to how horrible he feels, but now he can't even imagine getting up and moving around like he was earlier.

"Eliot,"

Eliot jolts, and looks up to see Quentin, who has unfortunately stopped stroking his hair.

"It's okay if you like, need to go to bed. We can finish the movie tomorrow."

Eliot scoffs halfheartedly. "I don't need to go to bed."

"Yeah, well I mean, you were snoring."

Oh. Oops. The snoring is part of the reason Margo usually refuses to sleep in the same bed as him. Apparently it's "obnoxious as fuck" so he can't imagine what he sounds like when his sinuses are clogged.

Quentin's looking at him strangely as he sits up and rubs his eyes. He sniffles thickly and wishes he could blow his nose without sounding disgusting. "Quentin, I'm fine." The response is getting a hand pressed to his cheek.

"I just- hold on, I think I have a thermometer here somewhere..."

"Nooo, I'm supposed to be making you feel better." This entire day was supposed to be about helping Q forget about his snooty ex-girlfriend and distract him enough for him to forget about his depression. For once, Eliot can't let this night become about him.

Quentin scoffs as he digs through his things. "I'm fine." It's a lie, and they both know it. None of them were really "fine". A beep. "Here, put this under your tongue and keep your mouth closed 'til it beeps."

Eliot stares at the instrument, finding it kind of hilariously adorable that Quentin brought a thermometer to university. With a begrudging sigh, he takes it and slides it under his tongue just as he was told.

"Thirty seconds," Quentin informs.

He nods minutely, feigning inconvenience as he closes his lips around the thing, trying to sigh out through his nose. It comes out strangled and wet through his congested nasal passages, and he realizes he's unable to breathe without making gross noises that signifies how much gunk he has in him. His eyes widen and he reaches up to cover his nose as casually as possible.

"Oh!" Quentin looks around and finally finds his tissue box. "Here, sorry. Didn't think about that." He says, handing Eliot a handful of tissues like it's nothing. Is he really not fazed by any of this? Even Margo gets grossed out when he's sick.

The beep comes at a mercy. Eliot yanks the thing out and takes some deep breaths through his mouth while Quentin takes the thermometer from his loose grasp.

"100.4?" and then he presses the back of his hand to Eliot's forehead like that’s going to help. "Jesus, you should be in bed."

"I am in bed. In yours, admittedly, but-"

"I mean- you should be resting.  You should've been resting all day." He can see the guilt on Quentin's face a mile away so he has to step in.

Eliot sniffles uselessly (his nose is too congested to get anything up). "A little longer?"

Quentin thinks for a moment, then nods. "If you really want to."
