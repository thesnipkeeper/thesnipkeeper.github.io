Fandom: Shadowhunters
Summary: Alec doesn't find out that Magnus is sick until he's already at the institute
Word count: 2.6k

Spicy Pork

Alec woke when he went to wrap an arm around his partner and found that Magnus’ side of the bed was empty. He made a dissatisfied noise and took a few minutes to work up the will to get out of bed before finally wandering out.

Magnus was home, at least, hunched over a cup of tea at his dining table and looking more exhausted than Alec had seen him in a while.

“There you are.”

Only then did Magnus become alert to his presence, slowly looking up from his mug and offering a smile that didn’t quite reach his eyes. “Good morning!” he said, voice rough with sleep.

“I’d say the same, but it looks like you haven’t slept.”

Magnus took a sip of his tea before, “Should I be insulted by that?”

Alec chuckled. “No, I love that face.”

Magnus smiled a little more as Alec took a seat in front of him.

“Everything okay? You’re usually pretty adamant about not getting out of bed this early.”

“Everything’s perfectly fine,” Magnus assured softly. “I’ve just been busy. Work, warlock stuff. You know.” 

It wasn’t enough. Alec could see it. He couldn’t pinpoint it exactly, but there was something off about Magnus.

“Look, I know you well enough to know that something’s wrong.”

“Alexander, I’m fine.”

Alec wasn’t too sure about that. Magnus waved off his offer to make him breakfast, claiming that he’d already eaten even though Alec knew that that couldn’t be true. Magnus always started on his tea before breakfast. 

Magnus had yet to make a move to get ready for the day. His makeup was from the day before, his hair still had product in it but was a bit of a mess from the time he had spent in bed. 

Was something on his mind? Was that why he couldn’t sleep? No, Alec told himself. Everyone had an off day, even Magnus. Right?

Although  it was hard to keep his doomsday thoughts at bay when his boyfriend turned away when he tried to kiss him. Alec bit back the confusion.

“I love you,” Alec said with all of the sincerity in the world.

Magnus smiled. “I love you too.”

Alec went to work feeling... weird. 

-----

“He’s not going to break up with you, Alec.”

“I didn’t say that.” Alec spun, struggling to keep up with his sister as she buzzed around the workshop for parts and tools. He had to admit, this role fit her pretty well.

“But that’s what you mean, right? You asked me if you should be worried, Alec. The answer is no. Magnus loves you with everything he has.”

Alec nodded, breathing out a sigh that did little to relieve the anxiety in his stomach. “I just didn’t like the way he was acting. That’s all.”

“I get that,” Izzy assured. “But maybe don’t make assumptions quite yet. I’m sure he just had trouble sleeping like any normal person does sometimes.”

“Magnus isn’t exactly a normal person.”

Izzy scoffed. “If you’re so worried about it, why don’t you just talk to him?”

“I tried,” Alec knew his tone was getting dangerously close to a whine, which was something he didn’t do in front of anyone but his sister. “He wouldn’t- he wouldn’t talk to me.”

Izzy stopped her rapid movement to stand in front of her brother and stare at him until he met her eyes. “Everything’s going to be fine, big brother.”

Alec wanted to believe her. His sister was definitely more experienced in relationships of all kinds, ever the social butterfly. 

He tried to keep it in the back of his mind as he worked. It wasn’t easy, and he found himself thinking back to the few minutes before he left Magnus’ that morning, wondering if there was something else he should have said or done.

Alec groaned, propping his elbows on his desk and burying his face in his hands. He was so bad at this. The whole relationship thing... not just dating, but all relationships with all people. He just sucked at it.

The sound of someone clearing their throat had him sitting up straight immediately. Jace chuckled and closed the door behind him.

“Rough morning?”

“Uh, yeah. Something like that. What’s up?”

“We’ve caught wind of a warlock who’s doing some pretty questionable things.”

“Questionable things?”

“Like, turning someone into a bird? Turning a few people into birds, actually. It’s pretty bad.” Jace flopped onto the couch. “Anyway, we thought you could ask Magnus over here and see if he knew of them.”

Shit.

“Uh, yeah, sure. Yeah, I can do that.”

Jace raised an eyebrow. “Are you sure? Because you sound kinda like you don’t want to do that.”

No, he didn’t. He knew he needed to talk to Magnus, but this was too soon. He had to get his thoughts in order before he tried to have an important conversation. But this was work, this was his job.

“It’s fine,” Alec said with as much innocence as he could muster. He picked up his phone and pretended to be scrolling through his contacts when he was really just waiting for his brother to leave.

Jace stood up and leaned against the side of the desk, and Alec squirmed under his scrutiny.

“Something going on with you two?” His voice was full of concern, and it made Alec feel all the more guilty for lying. Although, was he lying if he didn’t know the truth for certain?

“No, nothing’s going on. I don’t- I don’t think so, anyway. I just-” Alec cleared his throat, shook his head. “I’ll call him.”

Jace nodded slowly. “Alright, well... let me know when he gets here. We also have a room full of birds we could use some help with.”

And he was gone.

Alec sighed. He was making this a bigger deal than he needed to.

Magnus sounded normal on the phone. Well, normal wasn’t right. He sounded the same as he had that morning--tired and a bit out of it, like he’d just woken up. But not upset or like he didn’t want to be talking to Alec, so that offered a bit of relief to Alec’s anxiety.

Magnus agreed to portal to the institute after he “finished something”. Alec assumed he was in the middle of making a potion or other warlock work.

He wandered into the main hub where Izzy was looking rather smug.

“You suggested Magnus, didn’t you.” It wasn’t a question. He knew his sister well enough to know that she was always ready to make him face his fear head-on. “Out of all of the warlocks in this city, you told Jace that we needed Magnus.”

Izzy shrugged. “He’s the best, isn’t he? Do you want those poor people to stay birds forever?”

Alec scoffed. “I love you, Iz, but you’re diabolical. I just want you to know that.”

“Love you too,” Izzy chuckled, gently punching him in the arm before going back to work.

Alec found himself watching the door nervously, waiting for his boyfriend to walk in. It had only been a few hours since they last saw each other, that morning was still so fresh in Alec’s memory. But it felt like he was waiting to see him for the first time in years.

The doors finally opened with a creak and in walked Magnus Bane... Looking very much like he didn’t want to be there. Izzy gave Alec a look as Magnus approached them.

His face lit up the moment he saw Alec. “I hear that you need my help with a bird problem?” there was a hoarse tinge to his voice that made Alec wince. He paused before they could answer, digging into his coat pocket with a strange expression.

Izzy looked as if she was about to ask if he was okay before Magnus twisted away from them and sneezed. And again.

“Are you sick?” Alec asked.

The bottom half of Magnus’  face was hidden by a rather elegant handkerchief as his eyes met Alec’s with guilt. He sniffed and gave his nose a quick wipe before hiding the kerchief.

Magnus cleared his throat. “Can we be a little quieter, please? I’d prefer if not everyone in the entire institute was aware.”

But everyone would know just by looking at him. His nose was pink, lips dry, and eyes watery.

By the Angel, he looked horrible.

“I...” Alec could feel Izzy’s gaze on him, one that probably said something along the lines of ‘I told you it was nothing’.

Jace thankfully took that moment to wander over, take a look at him, and join the conversation. “Magnus, if I’d known you were sick I wouldn’t have asked for you specifically. Sorry, man. I mean, Alec didn’t even mention it.”

Magnus and Alec exchanged rather awkward eye contact before turning away from each other.

He sniffed and pawed at his nose for a moment. “Oh, I don’t mind. Tell me about this fowl warlock.”

Jace grunted. “That one physically hurt. Come with me, I’ll tell you what we know so far.”

The moment they were out of earshot, Izzy turned to her brother. “Well.”

“I- he didn’t mention it, at all.”

“Bet it all makes sense now, though.”

“I guess.” Alec sighed. “I’d better go with them. They might need my help.”

Alec found them when Magnus seemed to be in the middle of coughing. Even Jace was watching him a little worriedly, like he didn’t know what to do.

“Sorry about that,” Magnus said, shaking his head as if to clear it. “Bit of a tickle in my throat.”

Alec frowned, biting back his concern for the time being. They had more pressing issues.

Which was really hard to do when Magnus coughed and sneezed his way through the whole process of turning the birds back into the poor people who’d been in the wrong place at the wrong time. By then, his voice was nearly gone as he tried to explain who he thought was behind it.

Magnus cleared his throat with a wince, hand going to his neck for a moment as if rubbing the tender area would help.

Alec tried really hard not to let his worry get in the way when Magnus took a seat on a bench in the hallway, mumbling something about needing to sit down for a moment, and don’t mind him.

There was a pink tinge developing on his cheeks, and Alec had cared for enough of his sick siblings enough times to know what a fever looked like.

“No worries,” Jace said, clapping a hand on Magnus’ shoulder before exchanged a look with Alec. “So that warlock from Mexico you were talking about...?”

“Yes, right.” Magnus muffled a quick cough into the collar of his coat. “I don’t know who else it could be. They’re rather eccentric in their-” his voice died out, and Magnus had to pause to clear his throat. “Their endeavors. I can’t tell you why this happened, but I imagine that the birds were just for a little fun.”

“Gotcha...” said Jace. “I’ll go let the team know, then.” He patted Alec on the back as he walked away.

Alec sighed, taking a seat on the bench next to his boyfriend.

Magnus gave a sigh of his own, but one that ended in a tickly cough. He sniffed, and a bit of liquid could be seen on the cusp of his septum, but Alec wasn’t about to mention it.

“I should have told you this morning,” Magnus said, just as much to himself as to Alec.

Alec couldn’t help but chuckle. The relief that his boyfriend wasn’t upset, but just sick... he almost felt guilty for being as happy as he felt.

“I thought I did something wrong. You wouldn’t even kiss me.”

Magnus winced at that. “Oh Alexander, I’m sorry. That wasn’t fair. I had a sore throat, and I knew that I was coming down with something, I just didn’t want to give you my germs.” He laughed a bit at their situation, but of course that ended in a cough as well.

He raised his hand, hesitated, and then pressed the back of it to Magnus’ cheek and neck.

“You have a fever,” Alec informed. “This is going to get way worse before it gets better, I think.”

Magnus rolled his eyes as if hearing that was an inconvenience. “I haven’t been sick in thirty years.”

“Really?”

Magnus paused. “Alright, maybe it’s closer to ten.”

-----

They made their way through the portal into Magnus’ apartment.

Alec had to catch him as he swayed dangerously, a bout of dizziness overtaking him.

“Whoa, maybe a portal wasn’t such a good idea,” Alec said. 

He kept a grip on Magnus’ arm until the warlock managed to regain his balance, blinking blearily.

“I think you’re right… I- oh…” he sniffed and rubbed his nose against the back of his hand. It did nothing to get rid of the glisten at the base of his nostrils. “Oh, this is the worst.

Alec had to bite back a grin. It was sort of adorable. He’d seen Magnus do plenty of human things, have plenty of human emotions, but there was something ridiculously endearing about seeing him deal with something like a runny nose. 

Alec pressed a kiss to his temple, earning a delighted noise from the sick warlock. “Why don’t you go get into sweats and lie down? I’ll make some tea and order lunch.”

Magnus didn’t make a move to leave. Instead, he was staring at Alec with a soft smile.

Alec laughed. “What?”

“Sometimes I forget that you grew up with three younger siblings.” Magnus shrugged. “Are you a secret mother hen?”

Alec scoffed. “Go lie down before you pass out.”

“Yes, sir!”

-----

Alec walked into the bedroom with the food, finding Magnus sipping at the tea he’d made him.

“I hope it’s not too terrible.” He sat on the edge of the bed and set the tray down next to him. “But it seems like it’s helping? You haven’t been coughing as much.”

Magnus nodded. “You did better than I expected. I’m just thankful that you didn’t decide to cook.” 

“Hey, I’m a competent cook, okay? I just happened to be craving chinese today.”

Magnus hummed noncommittally with raised eyebrows. “Well, it was nice of Jace and Izzy to let you come home with me.”

“Yeah, well…” Alec climbed onto the bed and sat cross legged before dividing up their lunch, handing Magnus the spicy stuff he could somehow tolerate. “I think Izzy was glad to get rid of me. I was kind of… on edge all morning.”

“Right.” Magnus cleared his throat, swiped at his nose as he sat up a little straighter. “I am sorry for scaring you.”

“No, it wasn’t your fault. I was overthinking things.”

“Still, that could have been avoided if I just admitted that I wasn’t feeling well.” He took a bite of his spicy pork, sniffling as it made his nose run. Alec caught a wince as he swallowed. Magnus made a displeased noise and set the food down. “Ow. Alright, maybe I’ll just settle for the egg drop soup right now.”

Alec nodded and handed him a container and a plastic spoon. “Did you sleep at all last night?”

“For a few hours. And then I woke up with a sore throat, and my head felt so stuffed…”

“I could have helped…” he said softly. “Or at least told Jace to find another warlock.”

“Well,” Magnus smiled. “I know that now.” he gestured to the food and tea. “You’re quite the caretaker.”

Alec chuckled and leaned over to kiss his cheek. “I try.”
