Then There Were Four

It’s a routine job. A hotel thinks someone’s hacking into their system and changing reservations. There doesn’t seem to be any pattern at first glance, but Toby’s sure that they’ll find the culprit eventually. They are Scorpion, after all.

Things get a little more complicated, however, when Walt mentions feeling weird while they’re going through the front desk surveillance feed.

“Weird? Weird how?”

Walter shakes his head. “I’m sure it’s nothing.”

An alarm bell goes off in Toby’s head when he adds up Walter’s admission to his slower movements and speech. Not to mention the fact that his face is red.

Toby’s first fear is some kind of poison. It’s not like it hasn’t happened before. But the rational, logical part of his brain reminds him that there’s probably a more common cause, like-

Toby doesn’t get to finish his thought before Walter makes a choking noise and clamps a hand over his mouth. Paige is the one who ends up dragging him out of the room to the bathroom, likely some motherly instincts coming into play when it comes to puke.

-like a stomach bug, Toby’s brain finishes as he sighs heavily.

“Not good,” Happy mutters without taking her eyes off of the screen.

-----

“You guys keep working, I’m gonna go check on Walter.”

They all see through Toby’s way of getting out of work, but no one argues. Walter does need to be checked on, technically, and if it also gives Toby an excuse to leave? That’s great too.

He finds his boss sitting up in bed with his laptop. Toby would lecture him about resting if he wasn’t helping get this job done faster so they can all go home. There’s a trash bin sitting next the bed just where they left it with Walter.

Walter sees him and sets the computer aside. “Are things going well?”

“Yeah, we think it’s an inside job.”

He nods. “This would be much more efficient if you’d let me have my comm in.”

“That’s a big ol’ no, buddy.” Toby sits on the edge of the bed without much grace. “We have absolutely no interest in listening to you puke in HD.”

Walter rolls his eyes. “That’s ridiculous. I’m not going to...” he pauses, winces, a hand going to his stomach.

“Are you gonna puke? I’d like a little bit of warning, Walt.”

Walter folds both arms over his middle with a grimace and shakes his head. “Just hurts,” he grinds out.

“Ah, that’d be the inflammation in your digestive system.”

“Yes, I’m aware, thank you.”

“Hey, no need to get testy with me!”

Admittedly, Walt doesn’t look well at all. Toby finds himself feeling the heat on his friend’s neck with a sigh. “Thought you might have a fever..”

Walter breathes out as the cramp eases. “I knew that already.”

“Right. Well, I’m gonna go back and help everyone. Maybe we can get this job done by sundown and get you home, huh?”

“Please.”

-----

They do finish the job and get paid handsomely for it, but the ride home isn’t nearly as much of a relief as they expect it to be.

Specifically when they’re parked in front of a gas station, after Walter nearly blew chunks in Cabe’s fancy government SUV.

Paige breaks their waiting silence with a question. “Is he going to be okay?”

“Is he contagious?” Sylvester asks.

“Probably, and most likely.” He can practically hear Sly tensing up, but he continues because since when has Toby known to stop talking anyway? “The chances of this being food poisoning aren’t very good since we’ve all eaten the same things the past few days. And he hasn’t crapped his pants yet, which is a very good indicator.” Cabe gives him a disgusted look. “What? Like you haven’t experienced intestinal stress after bad sushi?”

“I hate sushi.”

Toby sighs. “Of course you do.”

“Can we not talk about this?” Happy growls.

Toby’s just now realizing that she’s remained strangely quiet up until now, which isn’t normal for his wife while he’s divulging too much information about himself. He looks back and narrows his eyes at his wife, causing her to look at him intensely under his scrutiny.

“Happy?” He asks, taking in the tint in her cheeks and the exhaustion in her features. “You feeling okay?”

Sylvester gasps.

“I’m fine.”

“You’re lying!”

“Would you shut up?”

“Oh boy,” Cabe mumbles.

Happy groans, seems to fight with her options, and shoves past Paige out the door. She makes a dash for the gas station.

Cabe sighs heavily. “And then there were four.”

“This is... not great,” Paige says.

-----

Toby never thought that he’d be fighting to get his wife in bed with another man, and yet, here he was.

“Would you scoot over?”

“No, because it’s my bed.”

“You’re the reason I’m sick in the first place, asshole.”

“Technically, we don’t know that.”

“Arguing like a true divorced couple.” Toby’s remark gets him two separate glares, but at least it gives them a common enemy instead of each other. “Look, you guys are better off being together so we don’t have to run up and down the stairs taking care of you.”

“We don’t need taken care of,” Happy mutters.

“Bold words from someone who just needed to be carried out of the bathroom.” Well, it’s not very funny. That particular moment freaked him out so much that Sylvester even offered to help. Happy getting sick is not Toby’s favorite thing in the world. “Here take... take these...” he hands each of them a bottle of Walter’s electrolyte solution, which is truly disgusting. “Text or call any of us if you two need anything, okay?”

They each huff in their own stubborn way.

“Fine.”

“Cool.”

“Great!”
