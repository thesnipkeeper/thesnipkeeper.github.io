Unfinished Dessert

Something was wrong.

Aziraphale was quiet.

Crowley had agreed to go to lunch with his friend, had even taken a couple bites of Aziraphale's dessert when he didn't finish it. They spoke casually about what they'd been up to since the last time they saw each other.

Aziraphale hadn't rambled once.

Crowley was licking the remains of chocolate frosting off of his spoon as he glanced at the angel across from him.

Perhaps Aziraphale was upset for something Crowley had yet to learn he'd done. He thought they'd developed an understanding that as angel and demon they still had their respective jobs, but Aziraphale hadn't been this short with him in a long time.

He was frowning, deeply, like people do when they can't work up the will to put on a happy face. That wasn't like Crowley's angel friend at all.

He tried to talk about... Well, anything he could think of to either get a rise out of Aziraphale or simply a ramble. Or a smile. Or... something to tell him that he wasn't angry with Crowley. Despite all the attempts, they left the restaurant without Crowley knowing for sure what was wrong.

Why did he care this much? If the angel was angry, what could he do about it?

Aziraphale made noise, a hum in the back of his throat, getting Crowley's immediate attention. The angel's brows were furrowed and a hand was on his stomach.

"Y'alright?" Crowley found himself asking.

Aziraphale gave a half-nod and stopped himself. "I'm... Not sure," he admitted, and then cleared his throat. "Probably just a bit of indigestion. Nothing to worry about."

Perhaps the reassurance would have carried some more weight had Aziraphale not immediately brought his hand to his mouth with a panicked look in his eyes. Crowley knew that look very well - the angel had a habit of eating strange things in the past.

"Alright, this way..." He grabbed hold of his friend and led him to a hopefully empty alley.

As they were moving into it, Aziraphale gagged. Vomit seeped between his fingers, dripping down his poor vest. He only had time to lean against a wall and take one quick breath before the next wave came. It made a sickening splatter against the brick below. Crowley cringed and stepped back.

The angel vomited for a good ten minutes, and afterwards he was left with a mess on his face, his clothes... his hand. Crowley whooshed said mess away easily enough, but it did nothing to fix his friend's sad, distressed, and sick appearance.

Crowley softened. Relief that his friend wasn't angry with him... Probably not the best thing when the poor man was suffering, but he was a demon, after all. He laid a hand on Aziraphale's back, feeling him give an exhausted exhale.

"Let's get you home, Angel," he said softly.

-----

"I should have known," Aziraphale lamented, a now unusable bowl sitting in his lap. "There's an awful stomach bug going around..  I should have been more careful..."

"Ah yes, right. You are known for not being careful. C'mon, angel, everyone gets sick. It just happens."

Aziraphale raised an eyebrow. "Are you saying that this isn't your doing?"

"Course not! I mean, not this time, anyway."

Aziraphale didn't reply, because instead he was sick into the bowl.

It took a long time for him to stop throwing up. His stomach was still uneasy, but it was empty enough that he wasn't in immediate danger of ruining his favorite blanket.

"Crowley, will you stay with me? I don't want to be alone right now."

All it took was one tragic request for him to take a spot on the other side of the angel's bed. Aziraphale, who was snig and yet still shivering, gravitated toward him until he was leaning against Crowley and likely siphoning his body heat in the process.

He was warm. Much warmer than usual. His right arm was pinned underneath a miserable angel, so he reached across with his left hand to lay his palm against Aziraphale's.

"You're feverish," he said.

"Well, I'm aware of that," snipped the angel.

Crowley scoffed, and let himself sit with a numb arm as his friend started to snore.
