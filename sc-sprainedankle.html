Sprained Ankle

Everyone went silent when David hit the ground and didn’t get back up.

Patrick was still for a moment. “David?” No response.

And then he saw David’s breath hitch, and he was there in a second. He knelt down next to him, only to find that David’s face was scrunched in pain.

“What’s wrong?” Patrick tried not to sound too panicked.

“I think I twisted my ankle,” David said through tears and gritted teeth. He started to sob, and Patrick knew that he needed to get him out of there. Not just because he was injured, but because he would die before letting anyone else see him crying.

“Okay, okay, I’m going to help you up, alright?”

They held onto each other as they rose. David hissed in pain. “Ow, ow ow!”

“Can you lean on me?”

David gave a sniffly nod and held onto Patrick like his life depended on it. “It hurts,” he whimpered.

“I know, honey. Let’s just take it easy...”

He caught David looking back at everyone. The whole town was staring, chattering. Patrick desperately wanted to yell at them to go back to their game, but he just turned back and kept whispering encouraging words to David.

Alexis’ voice came as a rescue. “So um, are we going to keep playing, or what?”

They’d have to remember to thank her later.

Meanwhile, David had stopped holding back the tears now that they were far away from everyone else, and all Patrick could do was keep him steady and help him reach the motel faster.

“I told you I didn’t want to play,” David whined.

Patrick couldn’t help but chuckle. “I know, David. I’m sorry.”

He helped ease David onto his bed at the motel and went about grabbing various first-aid items from the two rooms.

David refused to leave the room for the rest of the day, partly because of his ankle, but mostly because he didn’t want to be seen by any of those people ever again.

“Humiliating,” he told Patrick.

“Yeah, well I’d like to see how any of them react if they sprained their ankle.” Patrick got onto the bed, taking up the rest of the small space. David leaned into him the best he could while also keeping his foot elevated. “I’ll never bully you into playing party lawn games again, okay?”

David looked up at him with a raised eyebrow. “Promise?”

“Promise.” He pressed a kiss to David’s head and sighed. “Now, don’t hate me, but I think we should probably head to Elmdale and get your foot checked out.”

David gave a dramatic groan, but ultimately ended up jostling his foot and crying out in pain.

So, that was that.

-----

“Could have done without a trip to the ER,” David muttered as Patrick helped him up the stairs of his apartment. He hissed in pain with each step. “Could have especially gone without watching that kid vomit repeatedly.”

“But now we know that it’s not broken,” Patrick said patiently. “And you have a prescription that should help you forget about your foot altogether.”

David scoffed. “Okay, that’s true.”

The trip up the stairs was painful, slow, and painfully slow. But they finally made it. Patrick got him settled on the couch and went about reading the directions on the bottle.

“One every six hours,” he informed before handing it over.

David dry swallowed the thing. Patrick decided not to acknowledge that.

Things were fine, and David spent the evening slightly high and but not in as much pain as he had been.

He started to complain about hurting again, and Patrick realized it was time for pain meds anyway, so he handed one to David and then sat next to him as he swallowed it.

David leaned against him. “God, I’m so tired.”

Patrick nodded. “It’s been a long day.”

The two of them sat like that for a little while before David started to shift and squirm. He’d been so still and silent up until then.

“You okay?”

“Yeah, I just-” David coughed, gagged, and clamped a hand over his mouth, eyes filled with terror before vomit spilled out through his fingers.

Patrick acted fast. He ran to grab the kitchen trash bin and got it within David’s reach just before the next surge of puke came up. David heaved unproductively, still bent over the bin. His foot was on the ground now, but that wasn’t the biggest issue at the moment.

Patrick had a bad feeling. While David was still hunched over, he pressed the back of his hand to his boyfriend’s temple.

He frowned.

David gagged again, bringing up a thicker stream of what they’d had for dinner. He   hoarsely.

“That fucking kid,” David hissed.

Patrick sighed. They were in for a fun couple of days.
