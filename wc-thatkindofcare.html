That Kind of Care

He could sense that something was wrong the moment Neal got into the car. His voice was different when he greeted Peter good morning. His movements were slow and rather clumsy compared to his usual smoothness.

The last piece of the puzzle appeared when Neal sneezed.

“Are you getting a cold?” Peter finally asked.

Neal sniffled and leaned back, closing his eyes. Yeah, he looked exhausted to add to all the other awful. “Something like that.”

Peter scoffed. “What the hell does that mean?”

“I dunno. I just don’t feel good.”

“Well, you could’ve told me, and y’know, stayed home.”

“Naw,” Neal said, making an attempt to perk up a bit. “I’m good.”

“Okay,” Peter sighed out.

-----

Neal was not good.

He was completely out of it the entire day. Peter was constantly having to repeat himself and regain Neal’s attention. It was only when they had to leave the office to talk to someone they thought might be involved in their current case that Peter realized his consultant was very not okay.

Neal had developed a persistent cough by the time they made it to their destination. He was looking increasingly pale.

Peter bit back his concern. Neal was a grown man, he could take care of himself.

At least, that was his thought process until his consultant grabbed his arm, blinking dizzily.

“Neal?”

Neal breathed out a, “I don’t feel so good,” before passing out right there.

-----

The sight of Neal Caffrey fast asleep on his couch was both adorable and... yeah, a little annoying. Peter couldn’t shake the thought that this was Neal’s only fault. He could have just stayed home and avoided all of this, but no, he had to pass out in the middle of a bowling alley.

He didn’t look any healthier than he had when Peter dropped him off, but he definitely looked comfier. Elizabeth seemed to have lent him some old clothes of Peter’s, and they fit pretty well.

But the next thing that caught Peter’s eye was the empty trash bin sitting next to the couch.

“Hey hon,” Elizabeth greeted him with a kiss as he removed his jacket.

“Has he been throwing up?” probably not the best question to greet his wife with.

She made a sympathetic sound, glancing at the sick CI on their sofa. “Just once,” she said as the two of them made their way to the kitchen. Once they were there, they didn’t have to worry about talking quietly. “He’s pretty sick. I’m surprised he was able to function at all today.”

“Well, barely,” Peter scoffed.

“Hon.” That was her scolding voice, the one she used when he was being irrational. He hated that he recognized it.

“Hm?”

“Try not to give him a hard time about this?”

Peter huffed. “I just don’t get it, El! It’s not like I’m working him to the bone! He had every opportunity to tell me that he was too sick to work.”

“I know...” she kissed his cheek.

Neal woke himself coughing. It was far worse than the cough Peter had heard earlier that day. It was painful and deep, and left Neal breathless and struggling to sit up. Peter rushed to his friend’s side before he even realized what he was doing.

Peter rubbed his back as Neal was hunched over, trying to catch his breath. It sounded like this had been going on for most of the day. He wasn’t sure if the coughing fit was going to end, and Peter was almost ready to panic when he didn’t see Neal inhale for a good few seconds.

Finally, it started to taper off just a little. Neal breathed, wheezed as Peter could hear it whistling through his airways. He found a half-empty glass of water on the coffee table and handed it to him, guiding it to Neal’s mouth as he took a few big sips.

“I’ll get you some tea, honey,” El said before disappearing into the kitchen.

Neal’s face was red, and now covered in a sheen of sweat. That fever must’ve broken for the time being. “Sorry,” he rasped before taking another sip.

Peter sighed heavily. “Oh, Neal...”

“Look, it sounds worse than it- than it is-” he barely got the sentence out before he had to cough again.

“Alright, okay...” Peter said, mostly to himself. His job as an FBI agent? Being close to death anytime he went out in the field? Never stressed him out. Being Neal’s handler and friend? Now that was a stressful job. “Why don’t you stay here for tonight?”

“What? Why?”

“Honestly? Because I have a feeling that you’re not going to take care of yourself if I take you home.”

Neal clenched his jaw, clearly lacking an argument against that that wasn’t a blatant lie.

“I’ll get the guest room all nice and set up for you, how does that sound?”

“You have a guest room?” Neal asked, chuckling a bit before it just made him cough again. All the coughing was starting to make his nose run, but Peter wasn’t sure how to tell him that.

“Well, it’s our office, technically, but it has a bed. Y’know. For guests.”

El returned with a mug of tea carefully handing it to Neal before taking Peter’s seat next to him.

“Thanks, El,” Neal said. “I’m sorry about all of this. You shouldn’t have had to-”

“Neal, you’re practically family. We’re always here for you.”

Peter decided not to feel all sappy. Instead, he made his way upstairs to the offi- guest room, and made sure the bed wasn’t covered in discarded coats and such. Thankfully, it was clear, and the room was- huh, it was actually a lot cleaner than Peter remembered it being...

Ah, Elizabeth. Bless her.

He gathered some of his own clothes, a couple more t-shirts, a hoodie, another pair of pajamas, for whenever Neal wanted to change. He set them on the bed, along with a towel and whatever else guests usually got when they came over. This wasn’t usually Peter’s department, but he felt that he was doing pretty good.

“Okay, the guest room’s all set,” he announced as he descended the stairs. He caught his wife’s eyes and smiled, she returned it with a much more knowing smile. God, he loved her.

-----

Neal tried to sleep.

He really, really tried to sleep.

He supposed that that was what he got for sleeping all day. He felt tired, but apparently that wasn’t enough.

Things only got worse when he started shivering again. Ugh, God, he was constantly either shivering or sweating, it wasn’t fair.

And coughing. Of course lying down made him cough.

Neal sat up and sighed. He wanted to cry, but he wasn’t even sure that he’d have the energy to do so.

This whole day had been terrible. From the time that he woke up and knew he was sick. Maybe Peter was right, maybe he should have just stayed at home, but... he wanted to be useful. And he really didn’t want to be alone.

He didn’t intend on being a burden to anyone, especially Elizabeth, who happened to be one of the sweetest people he knew. Neal tried to tell Peter to take him home after he passed out, but he was barely conscious at the time.

It was nice to have someone around. It’d been a while since he’d had someone to take care of him.

Neal sniffled and rubbed his nose. He took a tissue from the box that Peter had so graciously put on the nightstand and blew his nose, wincing at the sound. Ew.

He coughed again. Sniffled again. There was no way he was going to sleep any time soon, he decided. Maybe he could go downstairs and... sit? For a while? Ugh, this was terrible.

First things first, though, he needed to pee.

Neal got up, hanging onto the bed frame for support until he was sure that he wasn’t going to black out. Everything seemed good so far. He took a few slow steps to the bathroom. Darkness edged his vision, but he managed to get to the toilet before things got too bad. Getting down the stairs probably wasn’t a good idea if he could hardly walk to the bathroom.

Neal swallowed hard as he was washing his hands. The movement must have shaken him up, because nausea threatened him. Not too much, but enough for him to stand still for a minute and regain control.

He vomited before he even realized what was happening. The first wave wasn’t in the sink, but on the sink, and the floor, splashing his socks in the process. The second wave came immediately after, and this time he did make it over the sink in time.

He spat and tried to catch his breath.

Oh no, he... he had to clean this up. Seeing himself in the mirror, Neal ran the back of his hand across his chin, and got to work finding something to... to fix this.

He found paper towels, some spray... that would have to do. He didn’t really know what he was doing, it wasn’t often that he had to clean up vomit.

Neal was knelt down next to the bathroom cabinet, trying to be careful of the splash of vomit on the floor as he found everything he needed. It wasn’t until he went to stand up again that he realized things were bad.

Neal fell with a hard thump against the wall and floor as his vision went dark.

Now he definitely wanted to cry. He wasn’t going to let himself. He had other things he needed to do. If only he could get off of the floor.

“Neal?” the voice was muffled from outside the closed bathroom door, but it was unmistakably Elizabeth’s. There was a frantic knock on the door. “Honey, are you okay?”

The last thing he wanted was for her to see him in this state, after ruining her bathroom. Oh God...

“I’m- I’m good.” A blatant lie. “Sorry, I’ll be out in-”

El didn’t buy it for a second. “Neal, I heard a crash. Are you okay?”

He sighed heavily. He’d dealt with worse by himself. It shouldn’t have been an issue. And yet, he found himself giving a broken, “No...”

“Can I come in?”

“Yeah.” He prepared himself for the rush of embarrassment when Elizabeth opened the door and saw the mess.

Instead, she simply glanced at it and went right for him, kneeling down and taking in his appearance. “Neal,” she gasped, feeling his forehead. “Oh honey, you’re burning up.”

“What’s going on?” Peter’s voice, along with rush footsteps down the hallway. He appeared in the doorway, but Neal couldn’t bring himself to look up at him, or either of them, really.

“I think he fell,” Elizabeth said.

Neal gave a reluctant nod. “Lost my balance. I can- I can clean up, just need a few minutes to-”

“No no, we’re getting you to bed,” said Peter. “Don’t worry about that.”

“But-” any argument Neal had died on his lips as he found himself being hoisted up by Peter. He was useless, trying to walk so Peter didn’t have to drag him, but as it turned out, his legs weren’t working too well at the moment.

Peter got him seated on the bed, felt his forehead, then his neck, and sighed. “I’m gonna go get the thermometer.”

He seemed stressed, more stressed than Neal had ever seen him. And he was the one who caused it. He was the reason Peter and El were both up in the middle of the night, worrying and taking care of him and cleaning up after him.

Peter returned with the thermometer, and immediately set it aside and sat next to him with a, “Whoa, hey, Neal...”

He wondered what that soft tone was for at first, and then Neal realized that he was crying.

“What’s wrong?” Peter asked, squeezing his shoulder. “Are you hurt? Do you need something?”

Neal couldn’t help but chuckle through the tears. It wasn’t about him, it was about... “Just feeling a little guilty for this,” he gestured vaguely to his current state. “I’m so sorry, Peter.” What was it about being sick that made him so teary-eyed? Maybe this was why no one ever took care of him.

“No, none of that,” Peter said immediately. He stood up and found the thermometer once again. “Guilt isn’t a good look on you, Caffrey.”

Neal couldn’t help but agree. He must have looked awful. “ ‘m still sorry about the bathroom.”

“You know what?” Peter pressed a button on the thermometer and made it beep. “I think the bathroom is going to be okay. Put this under your tongue.”

Neal took it and did so, while Peter helped him get his legs on the bed. He even covered him up again, which made Neal feel a little bit like a kid.

When the beep came, they both read the numbers rather grimly.

“Okay,” Peter sighed, taking the thermometer and putting it on the nightstand. “Try to take it next time you remember, and please, for the love of God, wake us up if it gets any higher.”

Neal nodded, shuddered. “Thanks, Peter.”

Peter smiled, finally looking less stressed. “I’m gonna go help El. Try to get some rest, Caffrey.”

-----

Peter shouldn’t have been surprised when he woke up exhausted the next morning. That was what came with being woken up in the middle of the night, after all.

Peter and El were having a comfortable, quiet breakfast when they heard footsteps coming down the stairs.

Neal offered a tired smile. “Hey,” his voice didn’t sound particularly good. He didn’t look particularly good, either, but he didn’t seem in danger of passing out any second, which was a good improvement.

“Hi sweetheart,” El said cheerily. “How’d you sleep?”

Neal scoffed and took a seat at the table.

Peter chuckled. “Yeah, figured as much. C’mere,” he held a hand up, palm facing Neal. Peter really didn’t realize what he was doing, he was incredibly tired, after all! But then Neal leaned in and Peter felt his forehead just as he did when El was sick. “You’re still a little warm.”

Neal nodded, propping his elbow on the table and his chin on his palm. “My temp could be higher.” He cleared his throat and shifted. “I’m sorry for last night. You two shouldn’t have had to deal with-”

“Neal,” El cut him off, reaching across the table to lay a hand on his arm. “Don’t apologize. You’re family.”

Peter wanted to contradict her, to say that maybe the convicted criminal who made his life unbelievably difficult wasn’t exactly family. But his words died on his lips because he realized... yeah, El was right.
