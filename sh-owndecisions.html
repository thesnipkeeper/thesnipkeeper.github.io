Own Decisions

Magnus and Alec had very different morning routines. Alec would be up the minute the alarm went off (unless Magnus managed to entrap him for morning cuddles), and Magnus took a little longer to wake up.

Today, their routine was a little different.

Magnus gave a jaw-cracking yawn, cursing that the sun wasn't yet out and he was conscious to see it. He shook his head in a lame attempt to clear it and started on his makeup. And tried very hard to ignore the sounds of retching from the bathroom.

Poor Alec seemed to have succumbed to a stomach virus. If that wasn't bad enough, he still planned on going to work. Now, Magnus understood his work was important. But he'd been listening to him throw up since he woke darting out of bed to the bathroom.

He refused to let Magnus call in for him. Because obviously, he was in perfect health.

Magnus rolled his eyes at the mere thought before he finished his eyeliner. Perhaps that was all it would be today. It was too early for anything else.

There was the creak of the bathroom door, and Magnus turned around. Alec wandered out, pajamas rumpled, shirt sweaty with a small stain on the front that said he may not have made it to the toilet in time.

Magnus cooed at the pitiful sight. "Feeling any better?"

"Uh," Alex ran a hand through his hair, which was looking a little damp at this point. "Yeah, I think I'm good."

"You 'think' you're 'good'. That's not very comforting."

Alec, seemingly ignoring him, removed his shirt with a grimace and started to get dressed. Magnus didn't bother reminding him to shower like he usually did in the morning. He couldn't imagine that Alec felt like doing any more than the bare minimum.

"Still plenty of time to call the institute and take a sick day," Magnus said as he picked his jewelry for the day. "Plenty of time, it's quite early."

They'd been having this conversation off and on all morning, between Alec's violent vomiting bouts.

Alec shook his head. "I'm fine. And-" he cleared his throat. "Sorry for um, waking you up."

"That can be remedied by a midday nap. It will definitely be, actually," Magnus said with another yawn. He stood up, making his way to his boyfriend who was in the process of grabbing a shirt. He smelled like sweat and sickness, but Alec was remarkable at looking healthy when he wasn't without using a single glamour.

Magnus raised his hand and Alec sighed and leaned in, giving him permission to feel his forehead.

Sweaty, but not any warmer than usual.

"No fever," Magnus mumbled.

Alec smiled. "See? I'll be fine."

"Oh, Alexander," he said with a groan. "You and I have very different definitions of 'fine', but if you really want to go to work, I'm not going to stop you."

"I love you," Alec said, so impossibly sincere that it was hard to be upset with him for his terrible choices. "I'd give you a kiss, but-"

"Mm, yes," Magnus laid a finger on his chin and turned his head away from him slightly. "Please don't forget to brush your teeth."

Alec scoffed. And in the small moment of silence, a gurgle could clearly be heard from his boyfriend's upset tummy. Alec laid a hand on his middle and grimaced. He was a little reluctant to leave the conversation this time even though he obviously needed to.

“I um- I need to-”

Ah.

Magnus had a general idea of his boyfriend's body's demands and gently urged him out the door to deal with them. 

Magnus sighed in exasperation.

Fucking shadowhunters and their fucking sense of duty.

Magnus got to work mixing up a remedy for an upset stomach. By the time he was finished, Alec had emerged from the bathroom and the sun was finally starting to rise.

Alec, already looking extraordinarily pale and weak, leaned against the counter and blinked at the glass that was pressed into his hand. “What’s this?” Magnus couldn’t blame him for the look on his face. The stuff didn’t exactly look appetizing. Or taste appetizing.

“Don’t ask, just drink. Even a couple of sips will settle your stomach for a little while.”

To Alec’s credit, he didn’t ask any more questions before he drank it. 

Magnus smiled, smoothing the hair away from Alec’s sweaty forehead. He looked quite adorable when he was sick, thought Magnus would never tell him that. 

Alec handed the glass back with only half of the liquid left. “That’ll help?” he asked as he went to get his jacket.

“For a while.” Magnus repeated. “But it’s not going to solve all of your problems,” he continued, following Alec as he rushed around the loft to grab everything he needed. Magnus finally managed to catch him by the shoulders. “Alexander, promise me that you’ll take care of yourself.”

Alec smiled. “It’s just a bug, Magnus, not the plague.”

“Well… this is how many plagues started out.” Alec rolled his eyes. “Alright, go, do your heroic duty!” he said, waving his hand. “I’d give you a kiss, but I really don’t want to do that.”

Alec chuckled. “Love you!”

“Love you too, Alexander.”

-----

Magnus  went about his day, mixing potions people had ordered and doing various odd jobs that he’d be paid handsomely for.

He tried not to worry about Alec too much. He was a grown man, and had a lot of people at the institute looking out for him if something were to happen. But he still couldn’t shake the worry nagging at him. In a way, it was funny, because it reminded him how long it had been since he cared about someone this much.

Alec already hadn’t looked good when he left that morning, Magnus couldn’t imagine what he was going to look like when he returned. 

He shook his head and continued mixing his current concoction. Alec didn’t need him worrying about him every second of the day. He was strong, level headed, and-

Magnus lost his mixing utensil in the bubbling liquid below at the urgent knocking on his front door. He rushed over and opened the door.

He gasped. “What…?”

In front of him stood Jace with an arm wrapped around Alec. A very sick looking Alec. “Hey Magnus.”

Jace wasted no time dragging his brother inside and easing him onto Magnus’ sofa. Alec didn’t lie down, instead leaning against the arm like he didn’t want to fully give in.

Jace looked at Magnus and let out an exasperated breath. “He’s been throwing up like non-stop for two hours. Well, among other things. Which, to be honest, isn’t usually the biggest issue at the institute, but-”

“Passed out,” Alec muttered.

Jace nodded, rolling his eyes. “During the briefing.”

Magnus rushed to kneel in front of his boyfriend. Alec shifted uncomfortably, grimace on his face and an arm wrapped around his middle, but he seemed to relax just a little when his eyes reached Magnus’. 

Magnus frowned deeply as he gently rested the back of his hand to Alec’s temple. 

“Yeah,” said Jace. “He’s burning up.”

Magnus pressed a kiss to Alec’s forehead before standing up. 

“Thank you for bringing him back here.” He sighed. “He was a bit sick this morning, but he was pretty adamant that he was going to the institute.”

Jace scoffed. “Well, not your fault. He’s a stubborn asshole when he’s sick!”

“I can hear you,” Alec croaked.

“I know!” Jace said proudly.  “Anyway, he’s not doing so good. Take care of him.”

Alec made a choking noise that got both of their attention, and before they knew it he was stumbling to the bathroom, hand against the wall for support. 

Jace and Magnus exchanged looks of worry, looks that agreed this would be a long few days for Alec.

-----

“Mmph… Magnus-” Alec muttered, hand over his mouth as he swallowed compulsively. 

Magnus grabbed the bowl in a heartbeat, holding it under Alec’s chin as he choked up the water he’d had earlier. He traced careful circles on Alec’s back, feeling each breathless heave under his hand. 

Alec nudged it away when he was finished, wiping his mouth on his sleeve. He swallowed, and tried desperately to catch his breath. He squeezed his eyes shut for a moment with a grunt.

“What’s wrong?” Magnus asked.

“Head hurts,” Alec groaned. “I don’t- it didn’t hurt this bad before.”

Magnus had lived long enough to know what it looked like when dehydration was setting in. For Alec, of course, it wasn’t dangerous unless this went on for a while, but it was obviously making him feel worse than he needed to.

“Alright, we’ll let your stomach settle for a bit and try some electrolyte solution.” He said, running his fingers through Alec’s hair before pressing a kiss to his warm forehead and getting up to clean the bowl.

“I’m sorry you have to deal with this,” Alec said with a self-deprecating chuckle. “Usually it’s just Izzy, or Jace…”

Magnus got into bed, giving Alec the bowl to keep on his side just in case. They would have usually been asleep two hours ago, but Alec’s illness was making it hard for him to relax, and Magnus had long since accepted that neither of them were getting much sleep tonight.

“Well, then I’m glad they entrusted your health to me.”

Alec scoffed. “Yeah, Jace really pawned me off on you, huh?”

“No no,” Magnus said quickly. “Jace brought you here because he knew that I would take care of you.”

Alec smiled sleepily, sinking down into the covers. He was incredibly adorable looking up at Magnus. “You’re really good at it.”

“Well, thank you.” Magnus yawned. “Why don’t we both try to get a little sleep, I think the electrolyte solution can wait.”

-----

When Magnus woke, he felt like he was inside of an oven. He was wrapped in a stifling heat that just turned out to be his feverish boyfriend. As much as he didn’t want to wake Alec, he was far too warm for comfort. 

Magnus carefully pulled himself out of Alec’s embrace, inwardly cursing when the shadowhunter gave a questioning grunt. 

“Shhh,” Magnus soothed, running his fingers through Alec’s hair. “Go back to sleep, Alexander.”

Seeing a shudder run through him, Magnus pulled the blankets over his boyfriend and went to get the various supplies. 

The sun was up. Magnus smiled.

Alec slept through the whole night without throwing up. That was a good sign.

He mixed up an electrolyte solution that would hopefully taste better than the medicine he’d given Alec yesterday before he left for work. Along with some mundane fever reducer, because the poor thing didn’t need to be dealing with that fever all day.

Magnus had arranged everything on a tray and was prepared to take it back to the bedroom before he saw Alec wander out.

“Hey.” Alec smiled, though his voice was shaky from the chills. 

“Hey,” Magnus echoed. “I was about to bring some things to you. You didn’t have to get out of bed.”

Alec shrugged. “Just wanted to see where you went.”

Magnus set the tray back down and handed Alec the glass. He took a sip without question and took a seat in the kitchen. Magnus nudged the tylenol toward him as well and smiled as Alec swallowed them.

“How are you feeling?”

“Uh…” Alec thought for a moment. “Achy, and really gross. I think I might shower in a bit. But my stomach feels a little better, I think. Hopefully I’ll be able to keep this down,” he said, raising the glass.

“Your face is still flushed.”

Alec let out a shuddery breath. “Yeah, I’m freezing. The meds should kick in soon, though. Hey, Magnus…”

“Hm?”

“Thanks for… taking care of me.”

Magnus scoffed. “You don’t have to thank me for taking care of my own partner.”

Alec rolled his eyes and nodded. “Yeah, I know, it’s just… yesterday, Jace looked out for me when he knew I was sick, instead of trying to convince me to come here or go to my room at the institute. He like, made sure I was drinking water while I did paperwork, and… and you didn’t try to force me to stay here when I wanted to go to work...” Alec shook his head. “My point is, I’m glad that I have people around who both care about me and let me make my own decisions.”

Magnus was probably hiding his confusion terribly. He didn’t really think about those sorts of things, it wasn’t like he was making a conscious decision to let Alec do what he wanted. He supposed that was the thing about mortals trusting people they hadn’t grown up with.

“I…” Magnus cleared his throat and laid a hand over Alec’s. “You don’t need to thank me for that, either.” 

They found themselves lost in each other’s eyes for a good few seconds before pulling away, Alec going back to his drink.

“Well, shall we plan today’s itinerary? I was thinking movies, naps, and maybe some broth if we’re feeling up for it?”

Alec chuckled. “Sounds good to me.”

-----

Magnus woke with a groan.

“What’s wrong?” Alec yawned, rubbing his eyes and sitting up from where he’d fallen asleep on Magnus during the movie.

Magnus furrowed his brows. The movie was long over, the room had been silent, they’d both been asleep. What woke him up, then?

His question was answered when his stomach cramped sharply. 

He moaned, wrapping an arm around his middle and doubling over. 

Well, fuck.

“Magnus…?”

Magnus realized quickly that he couldn’t stay there any longer. He shot up from the couch and made it to the bathroom just in time to relinquish his stomach’s contents.

He heaved miserably over the toilet bowl, inwardly praying for the universe to show mercy and just end him right there. Was this how awful Alec felt? 

He was in the middle of being as miserable and self-pitying as possible when he felt a hand on his back. Alec knelt next to him, an impossibly soft presence in the midst of all of the horrible.

“I think you caught my bug,” Alec said.

Magnus couldn’t help but laugh.
