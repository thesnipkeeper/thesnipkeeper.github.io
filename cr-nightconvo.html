Night Convo

It was dark and silent when Vax woke.

He shoved the blankets away, what he was once desperately huddled in now causing a stifling heat. He stretched, yawned. Oh gods, his breath was awful.

Vax dragged a tired hand down his face as he pulled himself out of bed. He may want to change the bedding before he goes back to sleep, he thought. After spending the day going between sweating and shivering, the blankets were probably due for a wash.

The whole house was eerily silent as he padded down the stairs. He was desperate for a glass of water to wash out the awful taste in his mouth and hopefully help replenish what he’d lost that day.

“Percival!” He gasped. The reflecting glasses in dim lamplight caused him to lay a hand over his heart.

Percy gave a small chuckle. “Evening. Didn’t mean to startle you.”

“by the looks of things, it is certainly not evening.”

Percy shrugged and nodded. “Middle of the night, really, but who pays attention to those things?”

He nibbled on a piece of bread, a book in front of him that he looked to only be half reading. He watched passively as Vax filled a cup with water and sat at the table with a huff.

“Feeling better?”

“A bit,” Vax answered. “I was able to get out of bed on my own, so that’s a plus.”

“Indeed. You were in a bad way for a while there.”

Vax grimaced and nodded before taking a sip of the water. It was amazing. He was tempted to chug the entire thing, but practiced restraint as Keyleth had been instructing him to take small sips of things all day and there must have been a reason for that.

“So what is Percival De Rolo doing up at this hour?”

“Ah, I was in my workshop. Only just realized how late it was, and that I hadn’t eaten dinner.” He kept eyes on Vax for a moment, and just when Vax was about to ask what his problem was, Percy said, “We were all worried about you, you know.”

Vax chuckled. “Because of a silly stomach virus?”

“Well, I think the worry was from the fear of one of dying to something so mundane. We’ve been knocked unconscious by dragons and beasts and evil people, but it’s not often that one of us gets ill.”

With a scoff, Vax took a sip of his water. “Sorry I’m so weak, then.”

“Yes, it’s true, considering you’re the only one who succumbed to whatever plague this is.” He gestured vaguely toward Vax and sighed. “Do you need anything? Are you hungry? I could find a little-”

“No!” Vax answered quickly. “God, no. Not hungry.”

Percy laughed. “Understood.” The legs of his chair screeched against the floor as he stood. Vax winced, and that was how he realized he had a headache. It was hard to notice when he spent the day with symptoms ten times worse. “Apologies,” Percy said as he gathered his things. “Have a good night, Vax. Get some more sleep, alright?”

Vax nodded with a smile. “Thank you, Percy. Sleep well.”
