Problem Day

Cameron snuffled desperately and shoved the sleeve of his hoodie against his nose. There seemed to be no end to his drippy nose, and the skin above his upper lip was starting to get damp and sore from all of the rubbing.

He wasn’t paying attention to the teacher, not even close, but thankfully Mr. Matthew was as laid back as teachers came and seemed to understand that today was not Cameron’s day.

He sniffled again, running the back of his hand against his abraded nostrils.

Ugh.

“Cameron! Psst!”

Cameron nearly jumped when someone poked his arm. He pulled his hand away from his runny nose long enough to turn around.

It was just Af, holding a half-gone package of tissues out to him.

“You sound snotty,” they whispered.

Cameron gave a thankful nod and immediately pressed one to his nose. “Yeah snf!”

They just smiled, ridiculously innocent and adorable, and the two of them went back to paying attention to class.

-----

When lunch came around, Cameron was feeling particularly shitty. He wasn’t hungry, didn’t bother getting anything. Instead, he just sat at their usual lunch table and rested his head in his folded arms.

He didn’t bother reacting this time when he felt a hand on his back. He knew that delicate-yet-confident touch that only came from his best friend.

“Yeah, Af said you weren’t looking good,” Jamie said as they slid in next to him.

Cameron groaned.

“Why don’t you just go home?” Jamie ate their lunch ravenously. They must have woken up late and skipped breakfast again. Cameron had tried telling them not to get fucked up on a school night.

Cameron tried to sigh, but it just resulted in a ticklish cough bubbling up from his scratchy throat. Jamie rubbed his back with one hand and took a bite of their sandwich with the other.

“I’d rather be here, I think,” Cameron admitted.

Jamie nodded. “Fair enough, but they might send you home anyway if they hear your consumptive cough,” They chuckled.

Cameron scoffed and sank his head back down. Only for the table to start to shake as someone took their place across from him and Jamie.

“Hey Cameron, feeling okay?” Af asked.

“No,” he groaned pathetically. Jamie’s hand rubbed his back once again. He turned his head until one eye was visible, looking across to Af. “Thanks for the uh, tissues earlier.”

“It wasn’t a big deal. You sounded like you were having a problem.”

-----

"Thanks for letting me stay over, Jamie. I really don't feel like dealing with my parents today."

Jamie tossed him an extra blanket and pillow as Cameron made himself comfortable on the top of their covers. The three of them decided to study together, but it was really just an excuse to keep Cameron company when he wasn't feeling good.

Af sat cross-legged by Jamie's bed, Jamie sitting across from them as they each took out their homework.

Cameron, to his credit, got his textbook out all while sniffling and coughing. He wrapped the blanket around his shoulders, shuddering slightly.

"You know you can always come to my place too if you need to," Af said, in the middle of gnawing on their pencil as they tried to find their place in the textbook.

Cameron smiled. There was something about today that sucked and yet deeply warmed his heart. He knew Jamie was always there for him, but having someone added to the mix made him feel even more secure.

"Thanks man, I appreciate that." And then he promptly turned away and sneezed. "Eugh, Jamie, I'm going to germ up your bed."

"That's okay," they chuckled. "Worse things have happened in that bed."

Af made a noise of disgust. "Gross."

-----

Cameron was always amazed at how easily Jamie could fall asleep some nights. Their steady breath from the other side of the bed was the only sound in the otherwise silent room. Well, it would have been if Cameron wasn't trying desperately to get control of his nose.

Cameron struggled not to cough or sneeze. He considered moving to the floor. He'll probably tossing and turning all night and he didn't want to jostle the bed and wake Jamie. Unfortunately, getting out of bed and dragging blankets with him would probably also wake Jamie.

He scrubbed his sleeve against his nose, the fabric coming away wet and shiny with only a small amount of the slime filling his head. His shirt was losing its usefulness with each wipe, getting soggier and grosser.

Ugh.

Maybe he should have just slept at home. Sure, he would have had to deal with his parents, but he wasn’t afraid to blow his nose when he was in his own bed.

Then again, he probably wouldn’t be able to sleep anyway. Mom and dad were usually still arguing around this time of night.

Cameron snuffled uselessly. Mucus was perpetually sitting on the base of his nostrils and nothing could fix that until he dealt with his nose properly.

He decided that his only choice was to go to the bathroom, blow his nose, and hope he could sleep after that.

“Where you going?” Jamie’s figure shifted. They propped themselves on one elbow and squinted at him in the dark room.

“Bathroom.” Cameron couldn’t help punctuating the answer with a gurgly sniffle that probably sounded all kinds of disgusting.

“Cool,” Jamie yawned. They rubbed their eyes. “You okay?”

Cameron hesitated to answer honestly, but he knew they would see right through him if he lied.

“Not feeling so hot. Kind of hard to sleep.”

“Anything I can do?”

“I don’t know, Jamie...” Cameron sighed. He was so tired, he wanted to sleep this fucking cold off so badly, but that was impossible to do if he couldn’t sleep. “I’m just going to blow my nose and hope that helps.”

“Oh,” they looked around their room and nodded. “Just bring the whole box in here so you don’t have to get up every time.”

“Dude, I’m going to be making a lot of noise.”

“I can sleep through anything. You should know that by now.”

Cameron still blew his nose in the bathroom before he returned. It took an embarrassing amount of time to clear his sinuses, but when he was done- oh, it felt amazing to be able to breathe again. He toted the patterned box back to Jamie’s room and climbed back into the bed.

Much to his surprise (and delight), Jamie cuddled up to him the moment he got comfortable. They were warm; it was nice to have someone to snuggle with when he wasn’t feeling good. That was something he missed with Sasha.

“Comfy?” they ask, half asleep.

“Yeah. Night.”

“G’night, Cam.”

-----

Cameron woke up before Jamie, and his body offered enough annoyances that promised he wouldn’t be going back to sleep. So he got up and made himself a bowl of cereal.

Jamie had a thing for raisin bran (disgusting) and Cameron ate it, ignoring the way it scraped his scratchy throat. Having some kind of food in his body was bound to make him feel better.

He didn’t even pay attention to anything else in the house until he heard a sneeze from the other room. A moment later, Jamie made an appearance.

They sniffled and rubbed the back of their hand against their nose. “Thanks for this,” they muttered before opening the fridge and pouring themselves a glass of juice.

Cameron hissed in sympathy. “Sorry dude. But it’s kind of your fault for letting me sleep in your bed.”

Jamie shrugged. Couldn’t argue with that. They sat across from Cameron, sipping their juice with a kind of adorable glare.

Cameron chuckled. “Thanks for letting me stay here last night.” He cleared his throat. “It means a lot.”

Jamie smiled, more sincere than their usual. “Glad I could help. But also, fuck you.”

“That’s valid,” Cameron admitted.
