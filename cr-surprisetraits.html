Surprise Traits

This was possibly the most humiliating situation he could find himself in. Leaned against a brick wall, barfing in an alley of the sketchy tavern they’d been staying at.

Dorian knew it wasn’t the alcohol, because he hadn’t had any.

The weird looking stew that the place served when he was too damn hungry to refuse, on the other hand...

He gagged and brought up a slimy, chunky stream of half-digested vegetables and bits of whatever meat that was.

“Ate something weird, didn’t ya?”

Dorian allowed himself a dramatic groan as he wiped his mouth with a kerchief (not his sleeve, dear gods not his sleeve, no matter how much easier that would be). “Yep.”

“The stew?”

“... Yep.”

Ashton made an annoying ‘mhm!’ sound and wandered closer, getting way too close to Dorian’s former insides that were now pooled in front of him. “I did tell you-”

“Ashton, please shut up at this particular moment,” it was less angry and more of a desperate plea as he tried to gain control over his stomach.

Ashton did.

Dorian moaned pitifully as he felt his stomach cramp once again, and he heaved up another wave of gross. When would this end? Not now, apparently, because he vomited again, and then again.

It was only when he tried to catch his breath that he realized there was a hand on his back and another one holding his hair out of the way. He glanced at the ends of said hair and made a noise of dismay. It had clearly already gotten in the line of fire. How had he not noticed that?

“Oh no,” he sighed.

“It’s fine, it’s fine,” they assured him. “We’ll get you fixed up.”

“It’s... fixable,” he agreed hoarsely. “I- I think that I’m done.”

“For now,” Ashton offered, and it wasn’t a question. Dorian wondered how much experience he had with food poisoning.

“Shhh.”

The tavern’s washroom was... rough. Dorian had yet to get used to the unfortunate amenities that came with traveling, not that he’d ever say that out loud lest he become the token rich boy of the group once again. But Dorian couldn’t even begin to refuse the help as Ashton washed the disgusting section of his hair.

They hummed to themself as they did so, while Dorian was still getting himself together.

“You’re way too comfortable right now for washing vomit out of someone’s hair,” Dorian stated with some amusement.

“Eh, it’s nothing I haven’t done before.”

“That sounds terrible.”

“Well - and they’re going to kill me for talking about this, by the way - Milo had like, a terrible stomach bug a few months ago and I had to sit with them the whole time to make sure they didn’t choke on their own vomit or something. I’ve never seen someone so fucking miserable in my whole life.”

Right, Ashton had a whole other family besides them. Dorian smiled. “Well, thank you for helping me as well.”

Ashton hummed in acknowledgment. “ ‘s what I’m good for. I mean, besides punching the living shit out of people.”

“Huh.”

They followed behind him to his room, probably because he looked as shaky as he felt.

Ashton shoved their hands in their pockets and sighed. “You gonna be good?”

“Yes,” Dorian nodded. “Thank- thank you.”

“No problem. I’m gonna grab Orym, I think. You know, to make sure you’re actually gonna be good.”

Dorian snorted. “Good idea.”

Ashton squeezed his shoulder. “See ya.”

“See ya.”

Dorian laid back in the bed, feeling tired and washed out, and... not unhappy.

Huh. There was a lot more to these people than he was ready for.
