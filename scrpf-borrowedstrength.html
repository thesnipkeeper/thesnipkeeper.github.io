Title: Borrowed Strength
Fandom: Schitt's Creek RPF
Summary: Noah helps out a rather pitiful Dan
Word count: 1k

Borrowed Strength

Dan groaned and heaved. A trickle of bile went past his lips, but nothing more. It made sense, he couldn’t imagine that he had anything left in him.

God, he was so tired. And thirsty. And- cold. Really cold.

Going back to bed might have been an option a few hours ago, but now, after he’d spent the entire night vomiting in his hotel room, he couldn’t imagine having the strength to lift an arm, let alone his entire body.

He spat into the toilet and rested his head against the seat. That wasn’t something he’d usually do, but he was making an exception today. It wasn’t like he could get any sicker than he already was.

His stomach still hurt as much as it did when he first started puking last night, but now it was less crampy and more of an exhausted ache. As if his gut was begging him to stop puking and chill, except that wasn’t an option.

He was going to need to crawl out of the bathroom eventually, just to get his phone and let someone know that he wasn’t going to be able to do anything that involved eating or... standing.

Instead, Dan just stayed in that position and whimpered.

It was just his luck that someone knocked on the door.

He sat up, blinking woozily as it made his vision go dark for a moment, and scrubbed any nastiness from his face. Oh God, how was he going to answer the door when he could barely stand?

The door opened. Whoever it was had a key. Thank God.

But that meant that whoever it was was about to find him in this pitiful state.

“Dan? Are you home?” Noah’s voice, an edge of humor in the tone that he always had when he spoke while smiling.

Dan was tempted to close the bathroom door, but when the choice came down to moving or letting Noah find him like this, well... he didn’t feel like moving.

“Da- oh...”

Dan looked up just long enough to see his friend standing in the doorway of the bathroom, no doubt being absolutely disgusted at what was in front of him. He turned away to avoid the likely horrified expression on Noah’s face, only to be surprised by a hand on his back.

Noah knelt next to him, somehow not repeofkoen by Dan. Which was nuts, because Dan was pretty sure that he smelled awful, and looked awful. He would not be looking in a mirror any time soon.

“You sick?” was the first question out of Noah’s mouth, and Dan couldn’t help but chuckle despite himself.

“No, I’m perfectly healthy, thank you.” His voice sounded completely awful, which was probably about right for someone who’d been puking all night.

Noah laughed and finished it off with a sympathetic sound. He rubbed Dan’s back as he did so, even though his shirt was definitely soaked with sweat from the little while that he was sweating. Dan only wished that he could feel hot now.

“Well, I didn’t mean to barge in, but your dad wanted me to come get you. Cast breakfast, remember?”

Oh God. He groaned, whimpered. “I totally forgot.”

“Aw, Dan... buddy, you really don’t look good.”

He was aware of that, thank you. He went to respond with just that when his stomach jumped up his throat. He had a split second to get over the toilet, only to heave painfully with no real results.

Noah made a concerned sound and rubbed his back some more. “Okay, okay, take it easy...”

He was trying to. Dan breathed heavily when the heaving was over.

“Have you been like this all morning?” Dan shook his head, and Noah breathed a sigh of relief. “Thank God, I thought-”

“All night.”

Noah paused, quickly followed by, “Did you say- Dan! All night?!”

Maybe he shouldn’t have said anything. He just didn’t feel any need to hide how he was feeling right now!

“Buddy, you should have texted me. Or your dad, or Annie, or I don’t know, someone!”

“I know.” Dan nodded. He was still pitifully draped across the toilet. “Couldn’t get to my phone.”

“Oh, Dan...” Noah sighed in sympathy. “Well... do you- do you need anything? Really, can I do anything?”

“Dunno,” Dan moaned. “Jus’ don’t feel good. I’m tired. My head hurts. Can’t really stand up.”

“You can’t- Dan...” this was more of an annoyed sigh. Dan knew it came from a place of worry. “Do you want me to help you to bed?”

Dan thought about it for a moment and nodded. “I just don’t know if I can,” he said honestly.

“That’s okay,” Noah said. “I’ve got you, come on.” He gripped Dan’s arm and, with the help of the two of them hanging onto the wall and the sink, managed to pull Dan to his feet.

His vision immediately darkened. “Dizzy-” he quickly warned. “Noah, I can’t-”

“It’s okay,” Noah repeated. “I’ve got you.”

Dan wasn’t a small guy. He was very aware of that. So how Noah managed to drag him to bed was a complete mystery. All he really knew was that his head was on his pillow in the bed that he’d hardly had a chance to sleep in, and the room was spinning horribly. He closed his eyes as he waited for the vertigo to pass, and in that time, felt the inside of Noah’s wrist on his forehead.

Noah made a discontented hum. “Buddy, you’re really hot.”

Even in his current state and his eye closed, Dan managed a smirk. “Thanks. Means a lot.”

Noah scoffed. “I’m serious.”

Dan couldn’t bring himself to care about anything else now that he was in bed. “It’s just a little bug, I’ll be fine.”

“Wouldn’t call this ‘little’, but alright.” He heard shuffling, probably Noah shoving his hands in his pockets, and then a heavy sigh. “There’s vending machines down the hall, I’m gonna get a gatorade. I think you’re dehydrated.”

Oh, he was definitely dehydrated. It sounded a bit like he was talking to himself more than Dan.

“Can you let my dad know-”

“Yeah, don’t worry,” Noah said, voice soft. “I’ll be back, okay? Take it easy.”

After last night, no one would be able to stop him from taking it easy.
