Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Caduceus loses his spirit and Molly offers cuddles
Word count: 1.5k

Interesting People

No one likes it.

It really seems like the world is going to end when Caduceus Clay has lost his spirit. They don’t realize how much he’s the backbone of the group until he stops being carefree.

It really doesn’t seem fair that out of all of them, the proven assholes of the world, Caduceus is the one who ends up miserably sick.

It starts that morning when he walks outside, muttering something about needing some air. Jester goes out to check on him when he has yet to come back inside for breakfast, and returns with a sad expression and the news that Caduceus isn’t feeling well. The poor guy had thrown up.

When she leads him back in, he looks exhausted and out of it. Molly wants to give him a million hugs. Instead, Jester asks Caleb if Caduceus can borrow his room for the day so he doesn’t have to go back upstairs. Caleb agrees without a second thought, and the two wander off.

Molly bites his lip. He’s never even considered that Caduceus could get sick. The guy seems so resilient to everything around him.

Jester comes back, sadly informing them that Caduceus has a fever and he won’t let her heal him. It makes sense, they try not to use healing spells like that if they’re not in a dire situation, but that means that poor Caduceus is going to have to let it run its course the old-fashioned way.

None of them really know what to do from there.

Caduceus is usually the one who knows how to care for people, in his own special way. The rest of them didn’t really have that growing up like he did.

Molly sighs and shoves his remaining breakfast away. He feels like he should do something. He’s not sure what, but something. That’s what friends do, after all. Try to help, even if they don’t know how.

So when Fjord mentions that maybe someone should stay with Caduceus for the day to keep him company, Molly finds himself volunteering.

No one’s particularly surprised by that, or by anything Molly does these days.

He heads in the direction of Caleb’s bedroom. Caleb is usually not so ready to let people inside, despite obviously trusting them more than he used to. But old habits die hard.

He gently raps on the door. “Knock knock?”

“Hm?” Caduceus looks up, hair adorably ruffled from sleep. “Oh, Mr. Mollymauk. Hey.”

“Hi,” he says cheerily, closing the door behind him. “I thought that I’d stick around and offer a bit of company, is that alright?”

“Yeah. That’d be really nice, actually.” He smiles. “Apologies if I’m not very good company today.”

Molly laughs. “Oh, I think I can live with that.”

Molly sits on the edge of the bed, looking him over. He looks even sicker up close. He reaches up and brushes the long locks of pink out of his friend’s face, feeling the fever radiating from him.

Caduceus’ smile disappears soon enough as he sits back and grimaces, hand absently going to his stomach.

“Stomach ache?”

Caduceus nods.

“Do you mind if I...?” He hovers a hand over Cad’s stomach, and when the firbolg nods, Molly gently replaces his hand with his own.

Caduceus’ stomach is softer than before. A new lifestyle with new people seems to have done him some good, reminded him to eat regularly. Molly gently starts massaging his belly, careful not to upset it too much. He can feel and hear it gurgling under his hand, much to Caduceus’ embarrassment.

It doesn’t seem to do anything for his upset stomach, but Caduceus himself relaxes under his touch. He’s probably used to having people around when he’s not feeling well. Or he was used to having people around before his family left him at the temple by himself.

A few minutes of comfortable silence goes by before Caduceus makes a grunting sound. His eyebrows knit together, and he suddenly tenses up again.

“I... I think that I-”

Oh shit. Molly searches the room and is relieved to find that Jester had brought in a bucket merely for this purpose. He grabs it while helping Caduceus sit up with the other hand.

Caduceus makes a sound that sounds almost like a whimper as he leans over the thing. A few seconds go by as the poor guy waits miserably for his body to betray him yet again. Molly sits next to him, rubbing his back and making sure his hair isn’t in the line of fire.

Suddenly, a wet noise comes up Caduceus’ throat before a rush of vomit comes past his lips. He definitely whimpers this time, seeming so remarkably done with his body.

“Shh, it’s alright, love,” Molly mutters uselessly as Caduceus gags again.

This time he heaves up a trickle of liquid, and then continues dry heaving. Molly can hear his breath quickening as the time between bouts grows. He can’t possibly have anything left to bring up, but Caduceus isn’t comfortable lying back down quick yet.

He sniffs, tears prickling at the edges of his eyes that Molly knows has nothing to with having just been sick. Caduceus is crying. Caduceus doesn’t cry. He moves his hand from Cad’s back to his temple. He’s far too warm, and Molly has a feeling that the fever is partially to blame for the sudden emotions.

“I’ll be right back,” Molly says softly.

Caduceus gives a minute nod, still hovering over the bucket for fear that he could start throwing up again.

Mollymauk gets Beau’s help filling a basin with water and finding a cloth to use.

“How high is his fever?” she asks worriedly as they carry it from the kitchen back to Caleb’s room. “Like, ‘get Jester’ high?”

Molly sighs and shrugs. “More like, ‘making him emotional’ high. I think he’ll be okay, he’s just... miserable.”

It takes him a couple of seconds to realize that Beau has stopped. He turns around and finds that she’s staring at him with a raised eyebrow.

“What?”

Beau shakes her head and adjusts the basin in her hands. “Nothing. I’ve just never seen you be like this.”

“Like what, exactly?”

“Like... worried. About someone.” she clears her throat. “I mean, you care about everyone, that’s pretty obvious, but you don’t usually... get involved.”

Molly huffs. “Haven’t we all changed a little this past year?”

Beau shrugs. “Sure, I guess.”

He holds the door open for her as she carries it in. Caduceus is still bent over. He looks done, but tired.

“Fuck, hey Cad. How you feelin’?”

Caduceus sniffles, sitting up and scrubbing at his face. “Not well,” he admits after a while.

Beau lays her hand on his forehead and swears. “Want us to get Jester? She could probably help you out.”

Caduceus shakes his head. “I don’t want her to waste her spells. She could need them.”

Beau frowns. “You sure?”

A nod.

She sighs and pats his shoulder. “Whatever you want, dude.”

-----

Caduceus’ head is in Molly’s lap as he runs the cool cloth over his friend’s skin. It can’t be comfortable, Molly thinks, but he seems to prefer it. He hasn’t said a word since Beau left outside of a simple, quiet “thank you” when Molly started trying to cool him off.

And then the chills start. Caduceus shudders, and tries to pull the blankets over himself before Molly helps.

“Cold,” he mutters miserably when Molly tries to press the cloth to his face again.

“I know, I know...” he sets the cloth aside, choosing instead to run his fingers through Caduceus’ hair. He makes an attempt to de-tangle it as much as he can, humming softly as he does.

Caduceus continues to shiver, despite being covered up and next to another warm body.

“You know, Yasha did this for me once when I was ill.”

Caduceus’ mouth quirks up in a small smile. Good, talking helps.

“I wasn’t quite as put together as you at that time. I was still figuring myself out. But she was there. I’d never been sick before, had no idea what was happening to me. She just stayed with me until I was well again.”

“She’s very sweet,” Caduceus says through chattering teeth. “Yasha has an interesting side to her. Well,” he pauses, shuddering. “You’re all interesting.”

“As are you, Mr. Dead Person Tea.”

“Don’t see how that makes me interesting.”

“Of course you don’t,” Molly says fondly. “You know, if you let me, I’d love to braid your hair later. When you’re feeling up to it. I’d hate for it to get dirty or tangled.”

Caduceus nods against his leg. “That’d be alright.”

“I have one more request as well.”

Caduceus turns to look up at him a bit.

“Let Jester heal you.”

He hums noncommittally.

“It’d be understandable if we were in combat, but we’re here at our home, and you’re clearly miserable.”

“Fine.” Caduceus huffs out through his nose. “Later,” he says, before smiling again. “I’m sort of enjoying this.”

Molly laughs. “Alright, then.”
