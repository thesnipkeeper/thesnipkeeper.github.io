Title: Weird
Fandom: Kollok 1991
Summary: There was something to say about their lives when getting sick was the least of their problems
Word count: 600

Weird

Billy got like this sometimes. Quiet, weird.

There were times where he'd just stop talking and take in everything around him. Mickey enjoyed those times.

But today was different, because he had yet to speak. He had yet to say something obnoxious and stupid that made her want to run him over with her wheelchair.

Mickey always hated sitting at the end of the table at restaurants, but she didn't have much choice these days. So they wandered into the town's diner, she sat on the end while the rest of her friends slid into booths on either side of her. They ordered, all groaning when Billy asked for coffee.

He didn't defend himself or make some joke when Marcus made a comment about how he was going to stunt his growth, using an impressive old-person voice.

Billy wasn't talking. Mickey - damn it - was starting to worry. Something could be very wrong, and her brain ran through each possibility. With all of the craziness going on in their lives these days, any number of things could have driven Billy to suddenly act weird.

He leaned forward and rested his head in his folded arms.

“Okay, what the fuck is wrong with you today?” Skye asked, and of course she’d be the first to mention it. “You haven’t made me punch you once.”

Billy stayed there for a couple extra seconds before finally sitting up. They were all looking at him now, and he didn’t look too happy about it.

“What?” he snapped. “I’m fine.”

“No, you’re being kinda weird,” Marcus offered.

Billy scoffed. “No I’m not.”

Mickey sighed, wanting to argue but also not wanting to get involved today. Thankfully, the conversation was cut off when the waitress brought their food (and Billy’s coffee). She decided not to bother to think about it after that, no matter what weird thing could be going on.

And Mickey was doing really good at it until they were in the back of Marcus’ car. Billy whined. She could have sworn that he did.

She turned to him, expecting him to be pretending that he hadn’t just made a sound, but instead she found him doubled over with his arms wrapped around his stomach, face pinched in pain.

“Whoa, are you okay?” She asked before she could stop herself.

“What?” Marcus glanced back toward them. “What’s going on?”

“Nothing,” Billy ground out, still curled forward. “It’s just my stomach. It hurts.”

Marcus chuckled. “Maybe it was all of that coffee you had.”

Billy shook his head. “No, I-” His hand flew to his mouth, and Mickey knew exactly what that meant.

“Marcus, pull over!” she yelled.

Marcus looked back and, seeing the situation, swore and indeed pulled over. Billy stumbled out and made it a few feet away from the car before throwing up.

“Oh, gross,” Skye laughed.

“Oh my God,” Mickey mumbled, turning away.

Marcus sighed and leaned over Skye to grab a handful of napkins from the glovebox. Mickey and Skye both watched as he stepped out and went to hand them to Billy, who haphazardly wiped his mouth and spat on the ground.

Mickey had never seen Billy so out of it like that before, especially from just... being sick.

A conversation from the two of them faded in as they both got back inside.

“...ot some weird multiverse thing, is it?” Marcus asked.

“I’m fine,” Billy muttered as he climbed into the car. “My brother was sick yesterday.”

“Oh, thank God.”

The four of them found themselves chuckling.

There was something to say about their lives when getting sick was a relief.
