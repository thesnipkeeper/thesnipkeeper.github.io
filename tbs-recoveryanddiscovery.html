Recovery and Discovery

Joan woke up and immediately regretted it.

Bombarded with every pain, in her head, her stomach, her limbs. Discomfort despite lying down- where, exactly, was she lying down? She risked opening her eyes, assaulted by the bright morning light. Her groan of response must have been louder than she thought, because someone else was immediately at her side.

Owen?

Oh, God... what did she do?

“Joan, you’re awake!” he said, breathing out. “How do you feel?”

She swallowed, finding her mouth incredibly dry. “Um,” there was no good answer to that question and, even if there was, she couldn’t form sentences quite yet. “What...?”

He reached toward her, hesitant, and felt her forehead as quickly as possible with the backs of his fingers. He sighed. “You were a little out of it when you came over last night,” he explained, helping her sit up a bit against the arm of the couch. Ah, she was on Owen’s couch, then. That was... better. “You fell asleep at some point and I just couldn’t wake you. I mean, I could have, but I didn’t think it would be fair.”

Joan furrowed her brows as she tried to remember the night before. Right... right, she wasn’t feeling well the day before, she’d been dealing with a bit of a fever that evening. It was nothing that she hadn’t dealt with before, and she had papers to drop off at Owen’s, so she ignored it.

She’d had a run-in with a particularly draining Atypical at work that day. Embarrassingly enough, she passed out in the middle of a session and woke up in the infirmary. That was probably the biggest sign that she should take a break, not the fever.

“God...” she groaned, wincing in humiliation. “I’m sorry, I... oh, wow I don’t feel well.”

“I’m sure you don’t,” Owen said, while trying not to look like he was freaking out. He was good at that with everyone but her. “Macey sends her apologies, by the way. She felt pretty bad after the uh, incident in your session.”

“I hope it didn’t scare her too much.” And then she added, “It wasn’t really her fault in the first place. I should have known better than to go into a session like that when I was already getting sick.”

Owen blinked. “Joan...”

“Don’t. I don’t need the lecture, Owen, I-” she tried to sit up, right before the world decided to spin around her. Suddenly, she was lightheaded all over again. Right, that was the reason she’d lied down on Owen’s couch in the first place.

“Careful!”

“I should go home...”

“Well...” Owen had his worried thinking face on. “I can drive you home, or you can stay here until you feel well enough to drive yourself. Either way, I’m going to be leaving for work soon, and I’d like to know that you’ll be okay.”

She’d already caused more than enough trouble for him. She needed to go home and pretend that the day before never happened because God, was she ever going to forget how embarrassing all of that was? Fainting in front of a patient, in front of her coworkers, and now falling asleep on Owen’s couch?

“I’ll-” she tried to sit up again, and darkness crept over her vision. “Damn it,” she growled under her breath.

“You can always stay here and I can drive you after I get back?”

Joan’s illness-fogged brain slowly processed what he had just said. It wasn’t exactly hard to process, either. “I can’t impose more than I already have.” But she also wasn’t sure she’d be able to stomach a car ride right now. Or standing up.

“Not an imposition!” He said quickly, raising his hands. He offered a smile, a real smile, not one of those professional ‘talking to a new patient and trying to convince them that they don’t spy on people anymore’ smiles that he automatically wore at work. “Honestly, I’m just not sure that you should be driving right now, that’s all.”

“Yeah,” Joan agreed, dragging a hand down her face. “I don’t think so, either.”

It’d been ages since she spent this much time at Owen’s. Now she was going to be there for the whole day? Huh.

“I’ll head back home in a bit. You don’t mind if I stay until then?”

“Of course not. Here, I’ll...” he trailed off and walked away, returning with a glass of water. He set it on the coffee table in front of her, along with a bottle of tylenol. “And of course, there’s no need to call in sick because, well,” he gestured to himself with a chuckle.

Feeling awful and lacking any more words, Joan nodded her thanks, and he nodded back.

And he went to work, leaving her in his apartment.

Owen’s place was nice. Really nice. His job did well for him. She used to spend time there when she was tired of... well, being alone. He loved it.

She sighed heavily, taking it all in. The quiet apartment, the fact that she was there at all. The strange relationship between she and Owen now that the two of them were coworkers and... friends? Joan shook her head. She felt horrible, she was still recovering from the humiliation that was yesterday... this wasn’t the time to think about any of it.

She took a hesitant sip of the water Owen had brought, and then another when her stomach didn’t immediately object.

Had this been a year or two ago, being alone in Owen’s apartment would’ve given her the optimal opportunity to snoop. Instead, she went back to sleep.

-----

Joan woke with overwhelming vertigo, and nausea to go with it. She gasped, panicked, when she realized that she was going to vomit very soon.

The dizziness seemed like the last of her worries, surely she could get to the bathroom if she...

Joan sat up, the room spun. She tried to stand up, only for her vision to start going dark. She could cry right now if she wasn’t more focused on the fact that she was about to be sick. Actually, she might cry in addition to being sick.

“Joan, you’re-? Oh! Hold on, it’s okay, I-!”

Owen? Her eyes. were too foggy with sleep and tears to look at him or ask him when he got there. It didn’t matter, anyway, because suddenly there was the weight of another person next to her on the couch, and a bucket under her chin. She was throwing up.

Hard, painful heaves that didn’t bring much up but water and that morning’s coffee (ugh). It lasted far longer than she wanted to, and even then she still felt shaky and uneasy from the whole thing. Owen left with the bucket, presumably to clean it, and Joan definitely wanted to cry now.

As if things couldn’t get any more humiliating. Her ex-boyfriend, her boss...

“I’m so sorry,” she said, or tried to. It came out as more of a rasp; she hadn’t used her voice all day. She leaned forward with her head in her hands to hide the embarrassed, exhausted tears welling up. Just add to everything, why don’t we.

“Hey,” came Owen’s soft voice. Apparently he’d returned from cleaning. Fuck. “It’s alright.”

She laughed humorlessly and sat up to paw away the tears. “It’s really not. God, I- I’m sorry, Owen, I don’t even know why I’m crying right now, for fuck’s sake...”

“Joan,” he said. “You’re sick. You’re tired. You’ve had a long couple of days. It’s okay, really.”

She shook her head, wanting to argue with him. None of it was okay, and certainly not professional. Instead, she sniffled, cleared her throat, and tried to change the subject. “When did you get back?”

“Half hour ago.” He was always so determined to make people feel at ease, even when he could tell what they were trying to do, like change the subject. “I thought I’d let you sleep for a little longer. This might be a ridiculous question, but how do you feel?”

Joan snorted. “Oh, fantastic.” Like her head was going to explode, and her stomach wanted to turn itself inside-out. Getting the literal energy sucked out of her yesterday probably didn’t do her immune system any favors. “If-” she sighed. She couldn’t drive like this. “If you wouldn’t mind taking me home...”

“Of course! I can do that.”

-----

The car ride was mostly silent. Joan was just trying to keep her stomach contents where they were supposed to be, and Owen was good at respecting quiet. That was another thing she liked about him, he was good at talking, but he was also good at quiet.

“Thank you, Owen,” she said with a heavy sigh, breaking said silence. “Honestly, you shouldn’t have had to deal with any of this.”

“You know I don’t mind.”

She smiled.

“And by the way, if you ever need anything, you know that you can call Sam, or- or me, right?”

“It’s just a bug, Owen. I’ll be fine.”

“No, I know, It’s just-” he sighed. “What I’m trying to say is that you have people who care about you and want to help you. You have friends now, Joan.”

Oh God, she wasn’t ready to cry again. “I know,” she said. She thought back to the time when Damien, Damien was her closest friend. No one to count on, no one to talk to, just the man who pulled secrets out of her for fun. Owen was right, she was going to have to get used to having a support system. “You too, you know,” she said after a while.

“Hm?”

“You have friends too, Owen.”

He smiled. “Huh. That’s a nice feeling, isn’t it?”

She chuckled. “It is.” And things were quiet for a little while longer until Joan’s hand went to her mouth. “Owen, pull over!”

“Understood!” Owen pulled over.
