Title: Not a Cold
Fandom: Blindspot

Not a Cold

Rich moaned and wrapped his arms hard around his middle. Of course he'd get a stomach bug when he literally couldn't take a day off. It wasn't like FBI consultants got official sick days, after all. Especially consultants that were previously wanted for all the crimes.

He walked in pretending like he didn't immediately vomit up his breakfast that morning, and he'd been in his office dealing with a bellyache since. No amount of rubbing or doubling over relieved the pressure. His ailing body really had it out for him when he started shivering (and then sweating, then shivering again). His limbs felt weak. Just raising his arms to type on his keyboard took effort he didn't have.

His stomach gurgled, reminding him that it was only a matter of time before he threw up again. And again... and again.

Rich was about to head for the bathroom when his office was rudely invaded. Patterson threw a USB drive on his desk before he could process what was happening.

"I need you to look at the codes on this drive, okay?"

"Oh I see, the Great Patterson can't crack something so she needs me to do it. I knew this time would come."

Patterson huffed. She looked tired, now that he got a good look at her. It wasn't much of a surprise - she always looked tired.

"No! No, I just need a fresh set of eyes, Rich. C'mon."

He tried not to wince at the sudden nauseating ache in his belly. "Yeah, totally, I'll just do that and let you know-"

"Why don't you just do it now?" Patterson asked with confusion, and probably a little suspicion. Which, admittedly, was fair.

The nausea was rising to intolerable levels and he knew he needed to get to a bathroom before something gross happened. He did his best to swallow the collection of saliva in his mouth.

"Okay..." he drew out, earning a frustrated sigh from his coworker. "But first-"

"Rich! Stop stalling and do it already, please." She had that determined look in her eye that promised she wouldn't give up until he gave in. 

Rich chuckled, smiling smugly. "You still don't trust me?" The smile quickly faded. His belly complained, or warned, and he realized a little too late that he could have used the time he spent arguing with Patterson to run to the bathroom before the nausea got too bad. 

She growled. "Of course I trust you, but I need this done now and I don't understand why- Rich?"

The cramps intensified, and he found himself folding over in pain once again. He lifted a hand. "Fine. 'm fi- ohhh God..." a nauseating burp rose up from his throat, leaving a nasty taste in his mouth, and suddenly he had to clamp a hand over his mouth to stop himself from losing it right there in his lap.

No no no... this wasn't going to happen. Fuck this one virus in particular for humiliating him at work in front of the one person who sort of respects him.

"Are you gonna throw up?" Her voice was much softer now, he thought fondly. Awwww, she cared- oh fuck, bad time to gloat-

Rich nodded, stifling another burp into his hand. 

She shoved the waste basket, thankfully lined with a bag and unfortunately half-full of food wrappers that just added to his nausea. 

Rich gave an empty heave into the bin. He spat into it, hoping to rid his mouth of the gross taste. Not that it mattered.

His stomach fakes him out once more, and for a moment he thought he might be in the clear. Maybe it was a false alarm, and-

A violent wave of vomit came up. Following by another, much more productive heave. The sound of what was left of his dinner hitting the bottom of the bag was enough to gross him out and evacuate his stomach contents for a third time. 

He heaved a couple more times, but it felt like he was done. For the time-being, at least. This wasn't his first rodeo. Rich knew how the stomach flu worked, and he'd be puking for days even when he didn't have anything left to puke.

“Drink a little too much last night?” Patterson chuckled awkwardly.

He laughed despite himself and spat out the residual bile. He still hugged the bin to his chest, not willing to give it up quite yet, although seeing his stomach contents at the bottom didn’t do great things for the nausea, but still. 

Wow he felt awful. 

It took a good minute of awkward silence as he tried to get his breathing back to normal and make sure that there was no more vomit in his near future.

Patterson made a thoughtful noise in the back of her throat. “You know you can’t drink a night before work, right? That’s like, way against the rules.”

“I wasn’t drinking!” Rich argued. His voice was broken, and a little congested, as only then did he realize how runny his nose was. Patterson seemed to realize, too, because she nudged a nearby tissue box closer to him. He wasted no time blowing his nose and wiping the gross from his mouth and chin. “Ugh... God...”

“Okay...” she drew out. “Then why-?”

“I’m sick.” The only thing more painful than admitting defeat was the way his intestines were currently feeling after the forceful expulsion of its contents.

Patterson’s mouth pressed into a thin line, brows knitted together as she looked him over, trying to figure out if she could trust him. That was a convenient time for a shiver to wrack his body. Her eyes went to the sweater he was wearing.

“Ah,” she said. “Well, now I feel bad.”

Rich laughed. “You feel bad. Right.”

She huffed out a chuckle. “Okay okay, don’t get all dramatic on me. Here...” Patterson reached out toward him.

He narrowed his watery eyes at her, and then scoffed. “Gross. No. I’ll do it myself like a grown man.”

Patterson laughed. “Since when do you act like a grown man?”

“Fair point.”

She tied up the bag before taking the whole bin with her. Rich felt guilty for once, as well as ashamed and grossed out and if she hadn’t already seen him at his worst he’d probably be embarrassed. Well... probably not, but it was a nice thought anyway.

There were more pressing matters. The cramps had dissipated now that he’d gotten everything out of his ailing stomach, but they’d been replaced with a persistent, tired ache. 

He was free to be in pain now that he was alone. Rich pressed a hand into his belly, massaging it to alleviate the dull pain. There was no way this virus was done with him yet. It was only a matter of time before he was reduced to an achy ball of...sick. How was he going to get any work done today? Even when he wasn’t throwing up, he was exhausted, and even more so now that he’d puked twice that day and it wasn’t even noon.

He let himself double over and continue to press his arms into his soft abdomen, face squeezed in pain and discomfort.

“Jeez, are you okay?”

Rich looked up like a deer caught in headlights. Patterson was quick to close the door behind her and set the bin in front of him, probably concerned that he was about to throw up again.

He straightened up despite wanting desperately to ease the pain in his stomach.

“I might be dying, Patterson. Nope, wait,” he flopped back against his chair, looking up at the ceiling. “I’m definitely dying. Tell my story. Make me sound really cool and maybe don’t mention me getting caught by the FBI like three times.”

Patterson rolled her eyes. “Jesus christ,” she said with a fond chuckle. “What did I say about being dramatic?”

Rich moaned. Loudly.

“Look, I know you’re not...”

“You want your USB looked at, don’t you.”

“I sound like scum, I know. But seriously, Rich, I have no idea what’s going on with it and I could use your help.”

Rich reluctantly pulled himself upright, resisting the urge to massage his belly in her presence, and inserting the drive. 

He opened the first file and his eyebrow raised.

“Whoa, okay, when you said code I thought you meant like, alphabet or something.”

“Yeah, no, this is it.”

He was staring at a computer code. To a commoner’s eye it looked like a written program, but it didn’t make any sense. None of the words actually meant anything in any coding language. It was total gibberish disguised as some clever coding technique. 

“This is painful to look at.”

“Tell me about it.”

He examined it more closely. Somehow, his feverish mind managed to pick something up. Well, it might have been something, and that was good enough. “Wait, look at this. Every third word...”

Patterson leaned in and followed his finger on the screen.

“Oh my God.”

“A better hacker than you, even when I’m at death’s door- ow!” He rubbed the part of his arm that was rudely punched. It hurt more than it should have - his muscles were achy and his skin was probably a little overly-sensitive from fever.

Patterson made a regretful hissing noise. “...Sorry. But also, screw you, Rich.”

-----

It wasn’t long after Patterson left when he had to rush to the bathroom and vomit up what could not possibly have been left in his stomach. He drove a few FBI employees out, probably for the best, thank God for privacy. 

His belt was too tight on his tender belly, so he removed it and threw it with his things in his office. He was lucky his shirt hadn’t been puked on. Yet.

He wanted to go home, to take a sick day. 

So, he approached Reade in his office when the rest of the team had already left with the briefing.

“So, question.”

Reade raised an eyebrow. “...Yes?”

“I’m not feeling too great,” he shrugged casually. “Would it be possible for me to take the day off to, y’know, recuperate.”

Reade hesitated for a moment. “I... look, we kinda need you here. I’m Sure you can handle a little cold for a work day, right?”

“Oh, it’s not a cold. I had diarrhea like twenty minutes ago. It was super unpleasant.”

Reade winced and held up his hands. “Alright stop, I do not need the gory details. Just... would you mind staying? Patterson needs all the help she can get right now with that weird USB we found.”

Rich sighed. What kind of person was he to agree to stay and help people when it didn’t benefit him in the slightest? Well, that and he couldn’t make a fuss without his case being looked at again. He needed this, Rich wasn’t about to go back to prison because of a stupid stomach flu.

He’d just have to hold out for a couple more hours and help, that was all. No matter how much he wanted to sink to the ground and remain curled up in the fetal position until it was time to go home.

Rich wandered into Patterson’s lab, dragging his feet as much as possible. His stomach gurgled; he tried to ignore it. He had nothing left to puke up or shit out, if he was being honest. He just had to make sure he didn’t eat or drink anything today, because that’d just result in more pain.

No matter how fucking thirsty he was. (Wink wink.)

He shuddered. Whatever fever had been torturing his system was determined to get worse. Psh, whatever.

Patterson furrowed her brows at him as if trying really hard to figure out how he was feeling without asking in front of anyone. He shrugged carelessly and went on to explain to Jane and Weller that he figured the code out all by himself with no help whatsoever. 

Weller scoffed. “Yeah, uh huh. Patterson, what do we have?”

Patterson nodded. “So the words were a little... scrambled in places, but I managed to unscramble them, and here it is,” a vague sentence popped up on the screen. 

Rich used the opportunity to find the nearest chair and sit down. He wasn’t feeling too hot. He was feeling rather cold, actually. And lightheaded.

“Rich?”

Maybe skipping out on water wasn’t the best idea. But he didn’t want to puke again, at least not here. Would he rather feel dizzy and weak, or nauseated with debilitating stomach pain? The answer seemed pretty clear.

“Rich!” Patterson’s voice yanked him out of his thoughts. “Did you see anything else on the drive?”

“Oh, uh, naw. Nothing else, just the weird code,” he ran a hand down his face. He must look awful. It wasn’t like he was trying to hide being sick from Jane and Kurt, but that didn’t mean he felt like hiding when they gave him looks of both concern and confusion.

“Are you... okay?” Kurt asked, filling the silence in the room. He probably wasn’t looking for an actual answer, but Rich gave him one anyway.

“Oh, yeah, yeah I’m good. Just super tired, y’know?”

Patterson raised an eyebrow at him from behind the two agents.

“It’s two in the afternoon,” Jane said with amusement that was adorable on her face. “You shouldn’t be tired yet. We have a lot of work to do.”

Rich nodded. “Understood!” He pulled himself off the chair and grabbed onto the edge of a desk before he could sway too violently in front of his coworkers.

They hesitated before going back to discussing the case.

-----

Rich didn’t anticipate that they’d be working late. He should have, really. He wanted to leave early and sleep off the bug that was forcing him to run to the toilet only to puke nothing every hour, but he needed to stay. To prove that he was an asset. They needed him, like Reade said.

And then Rich Dotcom found himself hunched over the toilet. Empty heaves forced their way up his stomach, making him convulse uselessly. There was nothing left but bile and grossness, but that didn’t seem to matter. His body couldn’t stop trying to throw up. He couldn’t hold back a whimper as his stomach tightened.

“Rich,” a voice echoed off of the tile walls. He knew that voice. “Are you- are you okay?”

Rich groaned. “No.” It might have added to his case when he had to scramble over the toilet once again to gag with no results. The toilet was painted with yellow bile and saliva, but nothing else. He hadn’t eaten or drunk anything since that morning, and it came up long ago.

There was a knock on the metal door of the stall, and Rich was too defeat not to just reach up and unlock it. Moving was... difficult.

“Oh God, Rich...” Patterson gasped. She knelt down and hesitated only for a minute before laying a hand on his temple. “You’re burning up, and you’re not sweating, like at all.” She pulled her hand away, and he laid his forehead against the arm that was resting on the no-doubt disgusting toilet seat. “I’m getting medical.”

“No,” He grabbed her arm when she started to stand up. Patterson looked at him with wide eyes. “I can’t get sent home, Patterson, please?”

She frowned. “You’re really dehydrated.”

“I can’t be useful if they find out that-”

“Rich,” Patterson made straight eye contact with him. “You’re really sick and honestly? I’m kind of worried about you right now so just let me get you help.”

“No-”

“I will personally make sure that they don’t send you back to prison, got it? Rich?”

He snorted, smiling tiredly. “Got it. Thanks, Patterson. Love you.”

“Uck. Gross.” And she was gone.

The last time he was this sick was when he and Boston were on the run on vacation. 

They were in Australia, taking in the sun and eating the most stereotypical Australian foods when Rich caught the flu.

He was holed up in their hotel for five days. Too weak to go out and enjoy the food and sites with his boyfriend, and too sick to do anything but sleep and eat whatever food Boston managed to shove down his throat.

Boston would leave for a while, just enough to grab some essentials and probably have fun without his sick boyfriend weighing him down (Rich didn’t blame him in the slightest. He’d do the same. Well, probably worse than the same. He wasn’t great at caring for sick people.). He returned with food, medicine, once a thermometer even though Rich accused him of overkill on the whole caretaker thing.

After Rich ate, Boston let him rest his head in his lap and ran his fingers through his hair in the way that only he knew would tame him. And when his stomach started to cramp as it struggled to digest, Boston’s hands traveled down to his belly and rubbed it until Rich either felt better or started throwing up.

His hands were good at finding the perfect spot to massage, to relieve the pressure in his stomach. Boston was never as disgusted as he should have been when it resulted in a ridiculous amount of burping.

Boston’s played with his hair and feigned a disgusted noise. “You need to bathe. Your hair’s all greasy.”

“Mm, too much work.”

Boston scoffed. “I mean, I could help.”

When Rich looked up at him, he had a mischievous smile. He decided that being sick wasn’t so bad.

“Yeah, he’s in this one.”

The stall door opened and Rich found himself getting checked out by a man and a woman in scrubs.

“You’re right, he’s pretty dehydrated,” the woman said. “Sir, would you mind coming down to the medical facility?”

Rich groaned.

He heard Patterson scoff. “He’s like that.”

The nurses chuckled. “Here, we’ll help you up.”

Thankfully, he wasn’t forced into a wheelchair or any of that nonsense. Although, it would have earned him a little sympathy among the FBI personnel, but maybe that was for another day.
