Echoing Confession

She was ready for a good cup of coffee and a sprinkle doughnut. What she got instead was a reminder that life wasn’t fair, because Luke’s appeared to be closed.

Half the town crowded around the front of Luke’s, looking into the windows with a nosy, desperate air. Sure enough, she saw that there was no sign of life in the diner. The chairs were still up on the tables and all of the lights and machines were off.

"What's going on?" she demanded. “I need coffee, like now.”

"No sign of Luke anywhere," Kirk informed. "He was supposed to open three hours ago. I’m freaking out,” although the words couldn’t be taken too seriously in his deadpan tone.

It was strange that the place was closed with no sign explaining so, and even stranger that Luke’s truck was still out front.

Lorelai groaned loudly and pulled the key from the top of the door frame. Everyone gasped, despite the fact that literally the whole town knew that was where Luke kept his spare key.

“What are you doing?” Kirk asked, backing away as if he were afraid of being an accomplice.

“Breaking and entering!” Lorelai said as she let herself in. “I’m gonna make sure he’s not dead or something.” She’d hate to lose her coffee guy.

“You go, honey!” Babette cheered just before Lorelai closed the door.

She knocked, and then knocked again. “Luke? You alive? You’ve got starving people outside!” Nothing. She tried not to be disconcerted by the silence on the other side of the door. She failed. “Okay, you’re making me a little nervous here. Just let me know you’re actually alive.” The seconds ‘alive’ reference wasn’t as much of a joke as the first.

Lorelai pulled out her cell and called his phone. She listened to it ring a few times, and then she heard a sign of life. A groan. So maybe Luke wasn’t dead, but he could be dying.

“Hello?”

There was a bit of an echo as the tired voice came from both the phone and the apartment.

“Oh my God, were you sleeping just now?”

“Lorelai?” She heard him gasp, followed by the sound of rustling fabric as he got out of bed. “Jesus Christ, it’s...”

“It’s eight in the morning, Luke! Also, let me in. I’m at your door.”

“You’re...?” A sigh. God, he sounded fed up with not just her, but life. “It’s unlocked.”

She tried the handle, and sure enough... “Ah.” She ended the call and wandered around to find Luke sitting up (but barely) in bed, dragging a tired hand down his face. “Well you look...”

Luke coughed and climbed back under the covers as if her presence didn’t affect his new sleeping schedule. “Diner’s closed.”

“I think I got that now. Luke, are you sick?”

He sniffed. “Must be.”

She couldn’t help but lay a hand on his forehead. He didn’t even flinch. Well, that wasn’t good. What was even worse was that he was absolutely burning up. No wonder he seemed so out of it.

“Ah Jesus....” she muttered. “Sit tight.” As if he had any intention of doing anything else.

She wandered off to his bathroom. It only took a few seconds of snooping before she found the tylenol and extra washcloths. Luke didn’t seem like the type to take pain relievers of any kind, but the bottle was open and looked fairly new. It wouldn’t surprise her to find out that the guy dealt with headaches or something from the stress that he put on himself.

She ran the washcloth under cold water and found a glass to give with the tylenol. All the while, Luke lied in bed, sometimes when she glanced in his direction his eyes were open, idly watching her with a bit of confusion. Sometimes his eyes were closed as if he were desperately trying to get back to sleep despite the discomfort.

“Okay...” she sat on the edge of the bed and nudged his arm. It didn’t take much to get him to open his eyes when he wasn’t even asleep. “Here, meds.”

He swallowed the pills with little complaint, before lying back down. The poor guy, it looked like every movement took effort. Lorelai felt achy just looking at him.

She pressed the damp cloth in his hand. “And put this on your forehead, it’ll feel better.”

He did so, but not before his breath hitched at the cold against his burning skin. “You don’t have to-”

“Luke, I kinda do,” she said, voice soft. “I mean jeez, you really expect me to leave you here while your brain’s boiling? I can’t get coffee from you if you’re dead!”

Luke huffed out the smallest laugh, and then muffled a weak cough into his pillow. “Just don’t want you catching anything.”

“Yeah, about that...”

Luke’s eyes opened once again, an eyebrow raised.

“Think you got this from our house. Rory’s been sick, and you so sweetly brought that extra food over the other day...”

“Ah,” he murmured, giving a minuscule nod. And then, “Wait, is Rory okay?”

She chuckled. “Rory’s fine. She bounces back a little faster than us old folk. Pretty sure this is the flu, though, so you’re gonna be down for a few days at least. The joys of kids, I tell ya.”

“Mm.” He was falling asleep. It was adorable.

She should go.

“I’m gonna head out, okay? Water’s right here next to your bed. Call me if you need anything, Luke, seriously.”

“Mhm. Thanks, Lorelai.”

She smiled. “Talk to you later, Luke.”

“Mm. Love you.”

Lorelai’s brain short-circuited.

“What?”

But he was already asleep.

She ran into at least three pieces of furniture on her way out of the apartment.

“Well?”

Lorelai yelped and laid a hand over her heart as she exited the diner. Babette and the gang were still waiting for an update.

“Luke’s pretty sick. I don’t think he’s gonna be open for a while,” she informed, still distracted.

Everyone made dramatic noises of disappointment.

“What’re we supposed to do now?” Babette exclaimed, throwing her hands up in the air.

“Good question, Babette,” Lorelai glanced back into the diner. What was she supposed to do now? “I uh, guess I’m going to work.

Babette sighed as the rest of the town dispersed to find other breakfast sources. “Alright sweetie, have a good day! Hope Luke feels better soon, sheesh...”

Lorelai didn’t have enough function in her brain to respond.

The words “Love you”would be echoing in her head for the rest of the day. And then for the rest of her life.
