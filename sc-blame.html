Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Alexis' crazy driving is not the culprit this time
Word count: 1.1k

Blame

They drive over another pothole in the poorly-maintained country road. David closes his eyes and breathes through his nose.

“I don’t feel so good,” he mutters through clenched teeth. He snakes an arm around his stomach. It doesn’t actually do anything, but it makes him feel a little reassured.

Alexis sighs dramatically, and as if David can help feeling a little carsick. “Need me to pull over?”

David shakes his head and bites back the accusation that she’s driving like a maniac. He thinks for a few seconds. “Maybe soon, though.” He whines, squirms, his stomach getting more fluttery by the second. “Okay, very soon.”

Alexis doesn’t say anything, and for a moment he wonders if she’s even paying attention to his suffering. And then he feels the car slow to a stop and hears the sound of gravel under the tires. He opens his eyes, carefully at first, and realizes that they’re parked.

“You look terrible,” Alexis offers unhelpfully.

“Well, this is your fault. You drive like a- like a crazy person and expect me to-” He cuts himself off with a whimper, hand hovering over his mouth.

“Okay, fine whatever, it’s my fault. But just like, make sure you don’t puke in the car, David, because I think dad would actually murder both of us.”

David has to nod in agreement to that. “And mom would help,” he grinds out. David doubles over, hugging his stomach. He should have known not to eat a big breakfast before getting in the car with his sister.

Alexis sighs and opens her door. Before David can ask what she’s doing, she’s walked around to his side of the car. She reaches across him and releases the seat belt. He didn’t even realize how much pressure it was putting on his gut until it’s gone. Next, Alexis yanks both of his arms and manages to turn him so he’s facing the outside, despite his many weak protests.

She steps glamorously out of the line of fire seconds before he can vomit on her shoes.

He stops to take a breath between heaves, long enough to hear her going on about how she didn’t even want to come on this trip in the first place and why didn’t he say anything if her driving was making him sick in the first place?

He pukes again.

“God, David.”

David’s trying to catch his breath and figure out whether he done when he feels her arm brush his shoulder. She closes the glove compartment and shoves a handful of fast food napkins into his hand.

“You’re drooling and your nose is running,” she informs.

So he wipes his mouth, and blows his nose, and then sighs. “God. That was awful, oh my God.”

“Hey, can we go now?”

David pulls his legs into the car and nods.

“Only twenty minutes left, mm’kay?” she reassures. “And then you won’t have to drive with me ever again.”

To her credit, she does sound a little guilty for putting him in this awful situation. But still. Fuck her. Fuck her for doing this to him.

So he sulks. He offers one-word answers to all of her questions and ignores her attempts at conversation.

Alexis sighs. “You can’t actually be mad at me about this.”

“I threw up because of you. Of course I’m mad, Alexis.”

“Well if you didn’t have such a fucked up stomach-!” Alexis finishes her sentence with a frustrated groan as she pulls into the alley behind Rose Apothecary. They each grab a box of knit sweaters and trek into the store where Patrick’s dusting the shelves.

He greets them with a smile. “I thought you two decided to go on a road trip. That took a little longer than-”

“Alexis made me carsick!” David announces, plunking the box down with purpose. Alexis scoffs from behind him.

Patrick blinks. “What?”

“He threw up once,” she explains to Patrick. “Like, come on, David. You’re fine.”

“Well maybe now! But-”

Patrick holds up a hand to interrupt their sibling banter. “Okay, okay... David, maybe you should thank your sister for agreeing to go with you?”

David scoffs.

Patrick nods in a ‘fair enough’ manner, and turns to Alexis. “Thank you, Alexis, for helping. Our sweater lady really likes you.”

Alexis smiles, annoyingly proud, before booping Patrick’s nose. “No problem!”

The shakiness David feels from the drive hasn’t gone away, he realizes. In fact, he might need to throw up again very soon.

Alexis is in the middle of telling Patrick everything about the sweet knitting woman while David sneaks off to the bathroom. He winces, bracing himself on the sink. Carsickness doesn’t usually make him feel this weak, but Alexis’ driving can do anything, he decides.

He throws up immediately.

David rolls his eyes at his current situation as he blows his nose and wipes his face until he looks a little less terrible. His face is red, but there’s nothing he can do about that right now.

When he appears back into the main floor, both Alexis and Patrick turn to him, each making their own sympathetic face. Does he really look that bad? All he did was throw up a little bit.

Alexis clicks toward him in her heels and presses the back of her hand to his cheek before he can bat her away.

“You feel warm,” she states.

And suddenly there’s a second hand, this time on his forehead, and all David can do is roll his eyes. Frankly, he’s enjoying the attention, specifically from Patrick, and he does feel sick enough that he doesn’t have to milk it.

“Yeah...” Patrick mumbles.

David shoves his sister’s hand away so he can let Patrick from his face as he gets a good look at him.

“Your face is red. Are you sure that was carsickness?”

Alexis gasps. “It wasn’t me, then, is what you’re saying, Patrick?”

“Oh my God, seriously? I could be dying and you’re over there-”

“David,” Patrick says softly, guiding his gaze back to his beautiful face. “I’ll take you home. Alexis, don’t let anyone rob us while we’re gone.”

“Mhmm...”

-----

David has to admit, he isn’t thinking much about whether his sister is to blame or not when Patrick tucks him into bed and sets a bottle of water on his nightstand.

“I’ll be back after closing, okay? Maybe I can take you home with me and we can have a little movie night?”

David smiles, despite feeling like he’s going to vomit again very soon. “That sounds amazing.”

“Mhmm,” Patrick hums. “But get some rest first.”

He presses a kiss to David’s forehead, and David is surprised that he doesn’t melt right there.
