Title: Comparisons
Fandom: Gilmore Girls
Summary: Luke and Lorelai share a cold
Word count: 1.7k

Comparisons

“Luke.” Lorelai turned over on the couch so she could prop her head on the armrest, and looked at her partner pitifully. “Luke, I’m bored.”

“Drink your tea,” he muttered from his spot in the chair. He’d been flipping through some sort of auto magazine as the two of them began to wind down for the night. Well, as he began to wind down.

“I don’t like tea. You’ve been forcing that stuff on me for two days and I don’t like tea.” She sat up and pouted more intensely.

He chuckled, boring magazine abandoned. “It’s good for you!”

She scoffed in disgust. “I want coffee.”

Luke hummed. “I’m not stopping ya.”

“Yeah you are! You’re judging me!” she sniffed, an attempt to get sympathy that backfired when it turned out to spark an itch in her nose. She sneezed into her sleeve and huffed dramatically.

“Of course I’m judging you. It’s nine o’clock at night, you’re sick, and you want coffee. But since when has my disapproval stopped you from doing something?”

“Since... since I was so miserable and pathetic that you were more able bodied to make me coffee?”

He laughed. “Not a chance! You really want coffee, you drag your ailing self over there and make it your- y-...” Luke’s expression went hazy, and suddenly he sneezed. And again. He finished the pair with a quick snf! and a nose rub.

Lorelai narrowed her eyes at him, earning a ‘what?!’ look in response. “You caught my cold.”

Luke scoffed. “One sneeze doesn’t-”

“Not one sneeze!” Lorelai held up a finger to cut him off. “Two! That was two sneezes! Which either means there’s suddenly an awful lot of pollen in this room, or you’re getting sick!”

Luke was clearly intent on arguing. Which was why Lorelai watched smugly as he built up to two more sneezes instead. His eyes already looked a little watery in irritation, the burning kind of irritation that one felt in the back of their sinuses no matter how many times they sneezed, that Lorelai had been dealing with since she herself got sick.

“Bless you, babe,” she said with a grin.

He glared. “Don’t.”

-----

The Dragonfly was bustling that morning, with lots of guests checking in for the weekend and making plans for things to do around town. As nice as it was to have a couple of days off, Lorelai was glad to be back in somewhat-kinda-healthy form.

Sookie greeted her with a squeezing hug the moment she walked into the kitchen for her third coffee of the day. “Lorelai! I’ve missed you!”

“Jeez, I’m not returning from war, Sooks, but I’ll welcome the hug.” She grabbed a coffee cup the moment her friend released her and started to pour a generous amount. “Things go smooth without me?”

“We made it work,” Sookie shrugged modestly. “I saw Luke a few minutes ago. Is he finally doing those repairs?”

“E-yup!” Having obtained her coffee, Lorelai made her way out of the kitchen and it was no surprise when Sookie followed. She took a sip before she spoke again. “Apparently.”

“Apparently? What does that mean? Is he not doing the repairs today?”

“Oh, I’m sure he is.” Another sip as she casually as ever wandered into the inn’s library where the loose shelf doors had been irritating them to no end.

And there was Luke, sniffling incessantly as he tried to tighten a screw. His nose was pink with irritation, and Lorelai could see why as he scrubbed it against his shoulder. Lorelai knew the man well enough to see a sneeze coming from him. It happened when he started rubbing his nose like that. She stood there in silence and watched, and shushed Sookie when she was no doubt about to ask about his health.

The screwdriver clattered to the floor just before Luke shoved his arm up to his face and muffled not one, not two, but three sneezed into his poor flannel shirt. The third sneeze had a particular wetness to it, and Lorelai caught a glimpse of the mist in the morning light. Gross.

“Caught your cold, huh?” Sookie asked through the side of her mouth.

“E-yup.” Lorelai rolled her eyes. The man just spent two days forcing her to rest and drink tea and take care of herself, and he showed up sick as a dog to do some minor fixes. There was no talking him out of it when they left that morning, not even when he spoke between sneezes and coughs.

“Y’know Luke, that really doesn’t need to be done right now!” Sookie informed pleasantly. “The guests can handle some loose hinges for a couple of days.”

Bless Sookie for saying what Lorelai had been telling him all morning.

Luke sniffed liquidly and ran his sleeve under his nose. The poor man needed a tissue, and Lorelai wasn’t even going to offer until he admitted defeat. “Nah, it’s alright. Caesar’s got the diner today, I can use something to keep busy.” God, he sounded kind of hoarse. A sore throat, maybe?

Sookie nodded. “Right. Well... I’m going to go check on breakfast.”

She made her escape, leaving the two of them in the library.

“Okay,” she sighed heavily and sat her coffee on the nearest end table before making her way to him. She wrapped her arms around him from the side and kissed his cheek. “I so appreciate the work ethic from someone who’s not even getting paid, but whether you admit it or not, you’re sick, and you’re gonna infect my entire inn if you don’t leave right now.”

Luke huffed, and gave a congested sniffle. “What about you?”

“We agreed that I’m probably not contagious anymore, and- oh yeah, I don’t go around sneezing on everything.”

“I don’t-!” whatever argument he was about to make was nullified the moment he tore away from her to sneeze directly into the air.

Lorelai couldn't help but laugh. "And you serve food for a living!"

"It's just this cold," he defended, grumpily pawing at his nose. "They've been happening so fast..."

"Aw, Luke... go home. Take a nap."

He scoffed. "You know I'm not gonna do that."

"I know, but I thought I should try anyway." She propped her chin on his shoulder, arms around his waist. "Thanks for fixing the bookcases, babe."

He hummed in acknowledgment and turned to kiss her. He had to cut the kiss short to sniffle and clear his nose, but it was a good kiss, anyway.

"You really want me to go, don't you?"

"No offense, but very much so, yes."

-----

Lorelai was greeted with the sound of sneezing and coughing when she got home. Poor Luke, he sounded even worse than he had earlier. Luckily, she came prepared.

She was still recovering from the cold herself, and her body was ready for a restful night of eating and watching TV, and Luke’s had to have been as well, even if he didn’t pay attention.

She kicked off her shoes, threw her jacket in the general direction of the coat rack, and followed the sounds of misery that seemed to be coming from the kitchen.

He wasn’t.

“Are you cooking?”

Luke looked startled at her arrival, which was strange since she’d already made plenty of noise coming into the house.

“Uh, yeah,” he wrinkled his nose with a quick (and congested) snf! “Yeah, I figured you’d want something when you came home.”

Lorelai smiled. “We could’ve ordered in.”

“Ah psh,” he waved dismissively. “I’m fine.” Those words may have had slightly more merit if he hadn’t sneezed three times in a row immediately after.

Lorelai grabbed a napkin off of the table and held it out for him. He accepted it, and wiped his nose rather uselessly.

“Yeah, you sound super fine, Luke. Here, let me take over. You should be resting.”

“You’re still sick.”

“Not really. C’mon, scoot!”

“Lorelai Gilmore, I am not letting you near this stove.” He sniffled again and ran his sleeve under his nose.

“Luke Danes, you have no choice. Just tell me what to do.”

It was soup on the stove, fittingly. Luke had been making every delicious soup recipe he knew over the past few days, and as much as Lorelai had craved a burger, the soup was too good to complain about. It already smelled good.

Luke gave a resigned sigh, but didn’t sit down. Instead, he hovered behind her. “You just have to stir it until it starts to get a little thicker.”

“M’kay, m’kay...” she stirred. And listened to Luke give a horrendous cough behind her. “You should take something for that throat, hon.”

Luke grunted. “It won’t do any good.”

Oh, jeez. Was he really feeling that bad? Of course he was, Lorelai had just had the same cold. “Trust me, it will. Seriously, there’s some in the bathroom down here.”

He wandered off to look, which shouldn’t have surprised Lorelai as much as it had. Luke had to have been pretty sick to take any kind of medicine, insisting that your body fought off illness quicker if you didn’t take anything. Lorelai decided that she didn’t care about that.

“It’s not in here,” he called.

“Oh, hm. It must be in the upstairs bathroom!”

Luke coughed again.

“Come take over and I’ll go grab it!”

Luke was still coughing when he came back into the kitchen. He took the spoon rather eagerly, but not without a scoff. “You just kicked me off of stove duty.”

She rolled her eyes. “I know, but stairs make you cough more.”

“They do n-” he didn’t dare finish that sentence. Because he knew she was right. He’d dealt with a bout of bronchitis last year and Lorelai didn’t want to repeat any of that.

When the food was done and the meds were properly administered, they finally sat down. Not in the kitchen, in the living room.

Luke’s nose ran too frequently for him to really enjoy the food, and Lorelai had a feeling he couldn’t taste it anyway. Lorelai rubbed his leg, and snuggled up to him when she was done with her food.

“Aw, Luke...” she said a bit sadly as he wrapped an arm around her. Neither of them were really watching the TV, but it was nice to have something on.

“What?”

“You’re warm.”

“Oh,” he said. “Yeah. A little.”

“You’re taking the day off tomorrow.”

He sighed heavily, and she thought he might argue. She expected him to argue.

Instead, “Okay. You win.”
