Title: Low-Sodium Coffee AU: In the Diner
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly refuses to miss a date with his new boyfriend
Word count: 500

In the Diner

Jester has watched her coworker and favored customer grow closer every time she sees them. Why Molly wants to have dates at his own workplace, she’ll never understand, but it’s cute seeing him out of his work apron and giggling with his boyfriend like any other customer.

It’s no surprise when Molly walks in on a Saturday morning. It is a little different, however, seeing him look so tired. He plops into Caleb’s usual booth with a groan, letting his head fall onto the table.

"Jester. I’d like some very strong coffee, if you wouldn’t mind."

“A cup for Caleb, too?”

“Mhm,” comes the muffled reply before he lifts his head out of his arms.

“Molly, you look terrible,” she states matter-of-factly. The first thing she notices is his lack of makeup, and the second thing is the haphazard way his clothes hung off of him. She’s pretty sure he’s worn that sweater already this week.

"Ugh, I’m aware. I didn't sleep a wink last night,” he punctuates the sentence with a clogged sniffle that makes Jester wince in both sympathy and disgust. “My nose is- is being...” he pauses to rub at it aggressively as if that’ll do anything to stop it bothering him. “...Annoying,” he finishes with a defeated sigh.

“Molly...” Jester frowns. “If you’re sick, why didn’t you just stay home?”

The look he gives her says everything. Caleb. That’s why. He didn’t want to miss a date with his new love.

“It’s just a cold,” he says, waving his jewelried hand dismissively, regaining his usual smile.

Caleb’s sitting across from her friend by the time Jester returns with their cups and a pot of coffee. Caleb looks tired, but for an entirely different reason than Molly. She knows he doesn’t sleep well, the poor guy.

They’re smiling and laughing warmly with each other as she greets Caleb and fills the cups. The laughing stumbles into coughing, Molly bent forward and pressing a napkin to his mouth as he tries to muffle the small fit.

Jester gives him a sympathetic look before walking away, and she can hear a “You don’t sound very well.”

She hears a distinct sneeze from the other side of the restaurant as she refills someone’s drinks. She can hear Molly’s sniffles, wet and desperate as he tries to be quiet and still tend to his nose.

Jester grabs a stack of napkins to bring to their table when she catches a glimpse of something rather adorable: Caleb reaching across the table to feel Molly’s forehead.

A pang of happiness rises in her chest knowing her friend is being well cared for. And then she feels her heart sink at the worried frown appearing on Caleb’s face.

She puts the napkins down. Part of Jester knows it’s rude to be watching the two of them, but a much bigger part reminds her she’s never really cared about that.

Quiet conversation, worried expression remaining on Caleb’s face. Molly nods softly with a little smile, and he immediately catches Jester’s eyes.

She’s tempted to duck behind the counter when she’s caught, but he smiles knowingly and she realizes he just wants to pay for their coffee.

“Feel better, Molly,” she squeezes his shoulder, and does indeed find him a little warmer than usual. Jester wonders if he’s sicker than he let on. Either way, Caleb could tell.

“I’ll try,” Molly says, voice thick, but fangs peeking out of a smile. The two of them walk out of the restaurant hand-in-hand. 

Caleb is sniffling a week later.
