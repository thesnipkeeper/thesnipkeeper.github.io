Title: Dishwater
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David needs a hand
Word count: 300

Dishwater

David sniffled. And sniffled again. He’d been a little sick all day, but not enough to stay home from work. Nothing too bad, apparently until he started to wash his and Patrick’s supper dishes.

The steam from the sink was making his nose run, drip. He could hardly take it. And of course his husband had to come into the kitchen when he was struggling the most.

The liquid was reaching David’s upper lip, he could feel it. He just really hoped that Patrick wouldn’t notice. He tried to wipe it on his shoulder, but that did very little as it continued to run down his face.

“David?”

“Hm?” he sniffled as he tried to scrub a plate and keep his nose clear at the same time.

“Do you need a tissue?”

Another sniffle. Well, that was embarrassing “Maybe. I’m almost done with this, though... mmph!”

Before he could say another word, something soft was pressed to his nose. Patrick’s fingers were slightly squeezing his nostrils, effectively squeezing more gunk out. Oh God. How could he have let this happen? He wanted to complain, he was about to!

He sneezed instead. It expelled a fair amount of snot.

Patrick chuckled while David made a noise of embarrassment. “Bless you.”

“No one hears about this,” he said as Patrick wiped his upper lip.

Disgustingly, he then kissed David’s cheek. “Ew oh my God!” He splashed dishwater around in the kitchen in his hurry to move back. “Do you realize how contagious I am right now?!”

“David, we sleep in the same bed. I think I’m already doomed.”

David winced. He couldn’t argue with that. “Okay, well... please wash your hands.”

“Yup, on it!” Patrick called, already heading to the hallway bathroom.

He chuckled, sniffled, and went back to the dishes. He loved that man.
