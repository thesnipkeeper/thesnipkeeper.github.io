Outbreak

The town was too quiet for the number of houses and businesses around.

That should have been their first clue.

Few people could be seen wandering the streets. The Mighty Nein was used to getting strange looks, but these were different, as if no one really felt the need to pay attention to the colorful group of newcomers.

"Do you think there's somewhere to stay?" Beau asked as she eyed the tired old man walking past them.

"I hope so. This town's giving me the creeps, but... I mean, it's big enough. There's gotta be a place to stay somewhere."

They caught sight of a few buildings with "closed" signs on the front. Strange. It was still early in the day. The whole thing was creating a pit of anxiety in Caleb's stomach, and it grew with each closed building or strange person they came across.

Fjord finally managed to stop someone, an older middle-aged woman, about a nearby inn in town. She sent them on their way and then walked away with purpose.

"I don’t like this place," Caleb finally said.

"I have to agree," came from Caduceus behind him. "I don’t have a very good feeling about being here."

Fjord sighed. "Yeah, me either. But on the bright side, she said that the inn was one of the best places in town. It'll be nice to not have to camp somewhere tonight, won't it?"

Caleb truly hoped that that was true, because he was tired of traveling. Sometimes he felt like the only one of them who actually got worn out from all of their escapades. He wasn’t as physically fit as a lot of the others, after all, and he had trouble keeping up with everyone.

He followed behind everyone into an inn. The interior was admittedly very cozy but - once again - empty. Not a being in sight except for the innkeeper at the counter who was watching them enter with wide eyes.

"Well, aren't you an interesting bunch," he commented as he walked out from behind. "Are you looking for directions?"

"We need somewhere to stay," Jester said. "Can't we stay here?"

The innkeeper seemed caught off-guard, stammering and fidgeting with his hands. "I… yes, of course. I've got plenty of rooms available, I just wasn't expecting visitors."

Caleb wasn’t sure about any of this. "This is an inn, isn't it?"

"Oh, yes yes. Business has been quite slow since the wave of illness that came through town. Not many people want to settle here." The man looked at all of them uneasily and chuckled. "I may have said too much. How many rooms will that be?"

"What was that about a wave of illness?" Fjord asked, ignoring his question.

"W-well," he cleared his throat. "There's a bit of an outbreak happening here at the moment. Most people have chosen to stay home to try and avoid either getting sick or getting others sick. The only place open right now is the office of the town's doctor." He went behind the counter once more and pulled out a book. "And me, of course! You'll give me something to do!"

Caleb could hear a muttered curse word from Beau and a thoughtful, questioning "hm." from Caduceus.

He looked at Nott. /I knew something was wrong with this place, his expression said. She nodded back at him in agreement. He liked to think that the two of them were capable of communicating in that way often, because sometimes they just needed to understand each other without saying anything.

"Sounds serious!"

The man looked at Jester and seemed to realize that he'd dug himself a hole. "Oh, it's just a bit of flu going around. Nothing to be worried about!"

None of them liked the way he said it, but his prices for the night were significantly cheaper because of the unfortunate circumstances and his desperation for customers. So in the end, they rented rooms and decided to take the evening to rest up. They'd be back on the road tomorrow.

The rooms were much nicer than any of them expected. Caleb was used to roughing it, but he had to appreciate a nice environment when he saw it.

The group of them were eating in the tavern area, having awkward small talk with the innkeeper who had nothing better to do than hover over them and make sure they were satisfied. It'd been a while since they'd had a meal like this. Caduceus did what he could with what they had, but supplies and ingredients were always limited and Caleb sort of missed eating meat.

Their meal was interrupted when the same woman they'd spoken to earlier rushed in and pulled the innkeeper aside. None of them could hear exactly what was going on, but it was an intense conversation done by whispering and hand gestures. Caleb watched the innkeeper shake his head and shrug.

The woman sighed.

"I think something's wrong," Caduceus muttered without turning his head to them. His ears were perked up a bit, Caleb realized. He'd been listening for a while.

"They might be able to help." It was the first thing Caleb's own ears picked up before both the woman and the innkeeper approached their group.

-----

"So you're the doctor?" Beau asked as they followed the woman. "Then can you tell us what the hell is going on around here? Should we be worried?"

The woman slowed her walking a bit and turned to Beau and the others. She looked like she hadn't gotten a break in days, hair a mess and clothes wrinkled.

"It spreads the same as anything else does. Just don’t touch anything, and scrub your hands after you leave. We'll just be in my office anyway, so you should be alright."

"But is it deadly?" Fjord chimed in.

"Not… not so far."

Well, that was reassuring.

She led them into a small building. They could tell by the setup that it wasn’t meant for keeping patients, yet the lobby had several people resting on cots, nurses tending to them.

They followed her into an even smaller office, and it was almost comical trying to get all of them to fit inside. Caduceus was nearly as tall as the ceiling, and he shifted as he tried to get comfortable in the small space before finally deciding it was best for him to wait for them outside.

"Both very young and very old people are vulnerable to complications or passing away from this illness," She stated matter-of-factly. She opened a cabinet and pulled out a small glass bottle. "This medicine is the only thing that's kept us from worrying too much about that possibility, and our supplies are depleting fast."

She opened the cabinet door a little wide and sure enough there were only a few of the same bottles left.

"The next town over has offered us their own, but as I'm sure you know, no one wants to be here right now. Even to deliver the medicine for the very illness they're afraid of."

Just like that, they had a new job. Caleb was tired already.

They all made their way back out into the lobby, where Caduceus was kneeling next to the cot of a young patient, a little girl who looked like she'd been ill for a while. She squirmed in the lap of one of the nurses.

All of them watched her fussing dissipate as Caduceus gently laid a hand on her head. He muttered a few words before pulling back.

"It should ease the symptoms for a bit," he told the nurse.

"Thank you so much. She's been fussy all day."

"Deuces! Time to head out!" Caduceus looked up at Fjord, smiled and nodded.

The discussion began as soon as they left the office.

"We'll head out first thing in the morning, then?"

"Do we all have to go? I mean, it's not that far and they're already expecting us."

Caleb was tempted to volunteer to stay back. He could keep an eye on their things and… and rest for once. He was prepared for a day of travel tomorrow, but not another job. They probably wouldn’t have even taken the job if so many people weren't relying on them.

But they were, and apparently the Mighty Nein were good people now. Caleb reminded himself of the sick child at the clinic every time he started to question whether it was worth it.

"I'm not sure," Fjord said to Beau. "Probably not, but we might as well."

Everyone agreed. Caleb sighed.

-----

Caduceus went back to the clinic after a request from the physician, helping to administrate the remaining medicine and offer a little bit of relief to the patients' symptoms.

They congregated in the tavern the next morning, getting some final breakfast in their bellies before they were ready to head out. Caduceus had been the last to join them, and politely declined the breakfast he was offered, claiming that oversleeping made him feel sluggish.

Caleb felt how Caduceus looked this morning. Exhausted and not emotionally prepared for their trip.

"You're skipping out on breakfast?" Beau scoffed. "Are you feeling okay?" She jokingly reached up to feel the firbolg's forehead. Caleb watched a frown grow on her face as she pulled her hand back. "Shit… guys, I think Caduceus has a fever."

Caduceus looked at her like he was finding it out for the first time himself. "Oh… that makes sense."

Caduceus made a move to sit down before his eyes rolled back and suddenly he was unconscious on the ground.

Fjord knelt down next to him and swore. "He's burning up."

"Oh dear," the innkeeper muttered, rushing over. "Get him into bed right away, I'll fetch some cold water and a rag."

Jester and Fjord hoisted him up, each grabbing an arm and draping it over their shoulder. They mumbled something worrying about how hot and clammy he felt, and Caleb wondered how much worse this fever could get.

The Firbolg's eyes opened in slits, and he muttered something unintelligible before closing his eyes and allowing them to drag him upstairs. He was little help, but he did seem to make it easier for them than it would've been if he was dead weight.

"Someone should stay and keep an eye on him," Beau said as she watched Fjord help tuck Caduceus in.

"Sorry…" he mumbled. "Really don’ feel well…"

"We know, Caduceus. It's okay," Jester assured, petting his hair back. The firbolg closed his eyes and hummed at the touch. "Feel better, okay? You helped a lot of people yesterday, but now it's your turn to rest."

"Mm…" he nuzzled her hand sleepily before letting out a weak cough. Even in his fevered state, he tried to cover it. His hand didn’t make it to his mouth in time, and it wouldn’t have made much of a difference anyway. He furrowed his brows.

"I'll stay," Caleb said.

"Are you sure?" Fjord asked.

"Positive. Is it all right if I stay with you, Mr. Clay?"

Caduceus responded with loud snores. He always snored, but this time you could hear the gunk built up congesting his system. This virus wasn't going to be a pleasant one.

Jester gave his head one last pet before pressing a kiss to his forehead and walking out with the others.

Caleb looked at the sleeping firbolg and sighed. It'd been a very long time since he'd cared for a sick person.

The innkeeper arrived with a basin of cold water and a cloth. He didn't seem keen on sticking around.

"I'd rather keep my distance, but I'm happy to help in any way! Perhaps some soup for your sick friend?"

Caleb wrung out the cloth and laid it across Caduceus' forehead. The firbolg stirred and made an unhappy noise, but remained asleep.

"I'm not sure," Caleb said. "Perhaps, if he feels like eating."

"And you?"

Caleb's brows knitted in confusion.

"Are you hungry?"

"Oh, no. Thank you."

The innkeeper nodded and took his leave.

And then it was just them.

There was silence besides Caduceus' rather congested snoring. The quiet was wonderful. Caleb could get used to this.

-----

Caleb had his nose deep in a book when the lump in the bed shifted and began to cough.

Caleb didn't have time to react before Caduceus began coughing harder and harder until he suddenly leaned over the bed and vomited.

He made a whimpering choking noise, hand clamping over his mouth, looking confused.

Well, he was awake now.

Caleb went to work finding cleaning supplies from the innkeeper. Caduceus had cleaned up after him before, after all, and probably would do the same for any of them.

"You probably shouldn't be here," Caduceus said as Caleb cleaned up the puddle of vomit. "I'm... it's pretty contagious. I can-" he began to sit up, but only got so far before his head seemed to swim and he had to lie back again, blinking as if to steady his vision.

"Stay in bed please, Mr. Clay. Don't hurt yourself. You've cleaned up my vomit, it's only fair that I return the favor, isn't it?" He tried to offer a smile, but reassuring people had never been his strong suit.

Caduceus coughed weakly, but didn't seem to have the energy to argue. In fact, Caleb was pretty sure he was falling asleep again.

-----

Caduceus slept for the rest of the day. Caleb found himself dozing in the chair, book long abandoned. It was late at night when he woke

Laying a hand on his forehead, Caleb frowned. He was still burning up, but the fever didn't seem dangerous, just enough to make him feel tired and miserable.

The wizard wrung out the now-warm cloth once more and replaced it, cooling Caduceus' skin the best he could. He might think about asking for that soup. Caduceus would need something to keep his body fighting.

Just as he was wondering if he should wake Caduceus, the sick firbolg shifted and woke, eyes fluttering open. He gave a chesty cough and turned over, ultimately facing Caleb.

"How are you feeling?" Caleb tried.

Caduceus' brows furrowed as he considered the question. "Hurts..."

"What does?"

"Everything..."

Caleb bit back a smile. He shouldn't be enjoying this, but Caduceus was a bit entertaining when he was tired and feverish.

"Are you hungry? It's been a while since either of us have eaten."

Caduceus looked uneasy at the thought of food, but he nodded minutely against his pillow. "Should eat..." He muttered before beginning to sit up. By the looks of it, it took more effort than anything the guy had ever done. He blinked as his head swam.

Caleb nodded. "I'll be right back, Mr. Clay."

-----

"Hello! How's your friend?"

"I believe he should try eating now if you-"

"Oh, of course!"

Caleb nearly had to take a step back at the man's enthusiasm. It was unnerving, but at least he wasn't kicking them out for bringing this flu to his inn.

Still, he returned to their room with two bowls of stew. He set them down quietly, finding Caduceus having dozed off against the headboard. The damp cloth had fallen down onto his lap and Caleb took it and draped it over the water basin.

Caleb laid a hand on his shoulder and the firbolg woke with a snort.

"Dinner," he said, pressing a bowl into his hands.

Caduceus hummed, nodding a thank you.

He got through about half the bowl before he handed it away.

"Focus on keeping that down, ja?"

"Yeah... Yeah, thank you Mr. Caleb."

-----

He shifted in the bed and whimpered.

That was just the start. Caduceus began crying the moment that he was properly awake.

"Excuse me," Caduceus sniffed and rubbed the heel of his hand into his eyes to stop the tears. "I don't know what's wrong with me..."

Caleb was at a complete loss as what to do. He wasn't one to comfort people or make them feel better, really. He could hardly handle his own feelings let alone someone else's.

But.

But he knew who was.

"I ah... I think I have a way to help you feel better, Mr. Clay."

Caduceus looked up, curiosity temporarily distracting him from the tears. Caleb snapped his fingers.

Frumpkin seemed to have taken in the distress his master's friend was going through. A warm purring cat was in Caduceus' lap in no time, rubbing up against him.

"Oh..." Caduceus sniffled. He rubbed his eyes one last time and then ran a large hand down Frumpkin's back. "That's really nice..." He said as Frumpkin nudged under his chin. "Thank you, Mr. Caleb."

"He really likes you," Caleb said. "You're almost as soft as he is "

Caduceus chuckled. "I guess I am, yeah." A smile began to break out on his face as he continued to pet the cuddly cat. Frumpkin began to climb all over him in search of more pets, and Caduceus was loving it.

"Are you feeling better?"

Caduceus glanced up and then brought his attention back to the cat. "Yeah. Yeah, I think so. I'm not feeling quite as, um... tearful."

"Good, I'm glad."

"That doesn't usually happen," he added as he continued petting Frumpkin. "The- the crying, you know? I must be a bit worn out." Worn out was probably one word for it, considering he'd been nearly delirious with fever for the past day.

Caleb cleared his throat. "Then perhaps I should let you get some rest." He stood. "You can keep him here, it's alright. He's enjoying the attention."

Caduceus gave a teary smile as he continued to pet Frumpkin.
