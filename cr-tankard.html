Tankard

They were all pretty beat up when they returned to the tavern. Being covered in bruises and mud didn’t discourage them from staying downstairs and ordering a round of drinks.

Everyone was enjoying themselves save for Molly, who’d been hit pretty hard and was obviously trying to cover up the ridiculous amount of pain he must have been in. No one said anything, as alcohol was probably the best way to solve his problems outside of magic, but it was really weird that he hadn’t asked Jester to heal him. Molly wasn’t usually one to just tough through these things if he didn’t have to.

And Caleb, who... wasn’t around. Beau scanned the bar.

Nope, no wizard in sight.

Beau sighed.

The battle hadn’t been a particularly pleasant one. Well, none of them were, but this one went wrong in every way imaginable. They were all exhausted, having been woken up early in the morning by a band of large creatures that decided to attack them. 

Caleb wasn’t very aware of his surroundings at the time, Beau could tell. He sometimes got like that, so no one blamed him when Molly jumped in as one of the creatures took a swipe at him. No one blamed Caleb... except for Caleb, Beau guessed.

Apparently this wasn’t going to be the relaxing celebratory drink that Beau so wanted it to be. She had other things she needed to do. Fucking friendship.

So Beau went upstairs (taking her drink with her, because fuck she wasn’t going to do this sober) and found the room Caleb and Nott had claimed as theirs.

Beau knocked, but the door swung open at her touch.

She wasn’t sure what she expected, but finding Caleb sitting on the floor with his knees against his chest was sort of heartbreaking.

His eyes were glued to a tuft of fur he’d gathered from the creatures, probably some sort of spell component Beau didn’t understand. 

She groaned inwardly. This may be harder than she thought.

Beau stepped cautiously into the room and slid to the floor as well, a good two feet away from him.

“Hey uh, you okay?”

He didn’t look up. “It was my fault.”

Beau set her tankard down and crossed her arms, sitting against the wall. “Caleb...”

“How is he?” his head turned toward her. 

She blew out a heavy, non-committal sigh. “I mean, he’s hurting, but nothing Jester and a little sleep can’t fix.” 

He gave a minute nod.

“Why are you beating yourself up about this? He was just trying to-”

“Save me,” Caleb finished. “He was trying to save me, Beauregard, and he was hurt in the process. All because I wasn’t prepared.”

“None of us were.”

“But I...” he huffed a frustrated breath, which seemed to catch in his throat and make him cough. “I should have been. I’ve always...”

“Caleb, you can’t blame yourself for every fucking thing that goes wrong. Molly certainly doesn’t.” he was silent, and at a loss of what else to say, she lifted her half-empty tankard. “Here, have a drink.”

Caleb did, taking an impressively large gulp before setting it back down. “You said he’s hurting?”

“I mean, yeah, he hasn’t asked Jess to heal him.” 

“Strange.”

“Caleb,” Beau cleared her throat. “Do you want me to bring him up here?” He was more likely to agree to that than coming downstairs where everyone was. When Caleb didn’t answer, she added. “I think he could probably use a rest, anyway. Maybe he could stay in here tonight, Nott could sleep with us?” 

God, Beau hated being the problem-solver of the group, but she liked to think that she was improving. Caleb and Molly had had a... thing for a little while. The whole group could sense it, but none of them bothered to mention it.

Caleb was still and silent for a long few seconds, so long that Beau was about to say something to make things less awkward, when he finally shook his head.

“You su-?” She was cut off by a nasty, congested cough from her friend.

Caleb curled up tighter, if that was even possible, head between his legs until the fit was over. 

Beau frowned. He only gave a sigh when she laid the back of her hand against his temple, hesitated, and then went to Caleb’s neck.

“Wait,” she said. “That’s a fever. Caleb, what the fuck?”

Caleb shrunk away, a small cough bubbling up from his chest, eyes still glued to the floor. 

Her mind went back to how utterly exhausted he looked that morning, even before they had to kill giant furry monsters. How early he fell asleep, how distracted and out of it he had seemed.

Fuck.

“Caleb, how long have you been sick?”

Caleb shook his head, as if he didn’t know. But of course he knew, he remembered every fucking thing. 

“Started to come on yesterday,” he said quietly. “But it’s just a cough. A fever. Nothing.”

“Nothi- Caleb, this isn’t nothing. What if it was something that could have fucking killed you? Gods, you almost did get killed. People die of ‘coughs’ and ‘fevers’, you know.”

Caleb shrunk away more, arms wrapping around himself. 

Right. He knew all of this already. But for fuck’s sake...

Beau ran a hand down her face and groaned. “Sorry. Look... you- you can’t feel guilty just because you weren’t at your best. Because fuck, if that was the case then all of us would just feel guilty like, all the time. I’m going to arrange for Nott to move to our room and Molly to come up here. Is that okay?” She said the last three words sharply and clearly to urge a reaction out of him.

He swallowed, and immediately muffled another string of wet coughs into his scarf. A nod. “Ja.”

Beau softened her voice. “Cool. Now, the important question: are you gonna be okay if I leave you alone for a few minutes?” 

A nod. 

Beau started to stand up, grabbing the tankard and-

“Ah-” Caleb looked like he wanted to say something.

“What?”

“Leave the ale.”

Beau scoffed and set it on the floor next to him.

-----

Beau could catch Jester giving Molly concerned looks as she made her way to her friends’ table. Molly was slightly hunched over, hand over the seeping wounds, and Beau came to the realization tht Jester didn’t have any healing spells left.

These idiots refusing to ask for help, Beau grumbled inwardly. Fucking stubborn assholes.

The gash was bad, but not so bad that they needed to fear for his life or waste a healing potion. It should be dressed, though, and Beau decided that was her in.

She walked around to Molly and leaned over his shoulder. He immediately turned to her with a curious ear.

“Caleb needs you, and you should let me clean that up before it gets infected.”

Molly cleared his throat and nodded before plastering a smile on his face and bidding everyone goodnight. Beau managed to arrange everything else with a simple question to Nott and Jester, and boom, girls’ night? Sure.

“He’s ill,” Beau said quietly once they were out of earshot of the group. She watched Molly wince with each step up the stairs, hand not left his abdomen. 

“Wait, he’s ill? Why didn’t he-”

“I don’t know, Molly. Probably for the same fucking reason you didn’t ask for help when you’re obviously in pain.” She winced, a pang of guilt, and then she remembered that she was talking to Mollymauk and she didn’t feel so bad.

His mouth snapped shut, but of course he had an obnoxious smirk on his face. “Fair point,” he finally said. “I suppose that explains some things about him lately.”

“Yeah, I know.” Beau opened Caleb’s door, not bothering to knock this time.

Molly gasped at the sight of their resident wizard, immediately going to him and kneeling down despite the pain moving caused.

“Oh, Caleb...”

Beau bit her lip as Caleb finally made eye contact with someone - with Molly - for the first time all day. “Yeah, how about you both get on the bed. Molly, take your shirt off, I’ll get some supplies.” 

Molly was too focused on Caleb to even turn the context of that sentence around on her. The last thing she saw was Molly and Caleb exchanging soft words and Caleb giving a nod as he started to unfurl from his position. 

And when she returned, the two of them were in bed, both looking incredibly uncomfortable. Fair, as they were still covered in dried mud. When did this responsibility fall on her, Beau wondered. 

She sat on Molly’s side of the bed and began running an alcohol-soaked cloth over the claw marks that went from his stomach to his side under his armpit. He hissed his pain, but a cough from Caleb seemed to distract him as he began running a gentle hand along Caleb’s arm. 

Much to Beau’s surprise, rather than shrink away, the touch seemed to relax him, dissipating some of the tenseness he’d been giving off.

Beau helped Molly sit up when it came time to wrap the wound. His face scrunched up in pain, and Caleb tensed slightly once again.

“Okay, I don’t know when I became your mother,” she mumbled as she finished Molly’s wrapping. “But both of you have to rest. You both have healing to do, and we need you better as fast as possible.”

Molly pulled the blankets over himself and Caleb and leaned back happily. Pain was still evident on his face, but he seemed content. Strangely, so did Caleb.

“I think we can manage that.”

Caleb coughed again (it was the only sound he’d been making, really) and Molly frowned and felt his forehead. 

“We’ll have to keep an eye on your fever.”

Beau sighed. These next few days were bound to be awful.

-----

She was right.

With Caleb sick and Molly refusing to leave his side, that meant a sick Molly, as well. The two of them were bedridden, less because they weren’t capable of getting out of bed and more because they were trying to isolate the illness to the one room. 

Molly’s coughing exacerbated his wound as well, and every fit would be punctuated by a stifled groan of pain.

Still, it was sort of funny in a completely unfunny way how all of it went down. Of course these horrible things would happen to their group.

Beau leaned on the doorway as she watched Jester lay one hand on each feverish forehead. Molly was fast asleep, Caleb had been trying to muffle coughs.

“Can you do anything about this?” Beau asked. 

“I can heal one of them today, and one of them tomorrow,” she informed. “And I can fix Molly’s wound if he’ll let me...”

“Molly first,” Caleb rasped between coughs.

“Caleb, you’re a lot sicker than he is. And like, your fever’s higher.”

“Molly first, please.”

Jester and Beau exchanged a look, and Beau shrugged. This was obviously Caleb’s stupid self-sacrificing shit, but there was no reason not to listen to him. 

“It could help him heal on his own if he’s not coughing anymore,” Beau said. 

Jester nodded. “Okay, Molly...” She woke Molly more gently than Beau had watched her talk to anyone in a while. Apparently Jester did have bedside manner to some extent. 

He woke, blinking groggily. “Hm?”

“I’m going to cast a restoration spell on you so you feel better. Don’t move.”

Molly shook his head, cough bubbling up from his chest as he tried to sit up despite everyone telling him not to. “I’m fine,” he said, clearing his throat. “Heal Caleb, he-”

“Oh for fuck’s sake!” Beau groaned. “Just-”

But thankfully, Jester had already begun the spell. In a flash, alertness lit up in Molly’s face. He took a deep breath, delighted to be able to do so without coughing. 

His relief was short-lived, however, when he looked at his bedmate.

“Caleb...” 

But Caleb just shook his head and leaned against him, taking all three of them by surprise. It wasn’t like him to show affection so openly, especially toward the person everyone pretended not to know he was dating.

Molly grinned, humming happily and snuggling his partner.

“I’ll be alright, Mollymauk.”

“I know you will.”
