Word count: 1.8k

People Who Care

Oscar spat into the water below and flushed the toilet with a noise of distaste.

He hadn’t been this violently ill in a long time, and he wasn’t eager to do it again anytime soon.

“Oscar...?” Came the timid voice from the other side of the door. “Are you alright?”

He couldn’t help but chuckle as he assessed his situation. ‘Alright’ wasn’t exactly the word that he would have used, but he wasn’t in danger. He just felt ridiculously shitty.

“I’m... I’m okay.”

“Okay...” Sylvia said, sounding unsure. He didn’t blame her, he didn’t sound particularly convincing.

Oscar pulled himself to his feet, feeling shakier and weaker than he had before the most recent vomiting spell. He should probably try drink or - God forbid - eating at some point to keep his strength up. But for now, he wanted to rest.

He splashed some cold water on his face and avoided any glance at his reflection in the mirror in the process. There was no way that seeing what he looked like was going to make him feel better.

Oscar returned to his couch, which had become more and more of a blanket nest as the day went on. He didn’t want to go back to bed until night time. The living room had TV and distractions that didn’t require him to wonder when he was going to puke next.

The moment that he got comfortable was the moment that there was a knock on the door. He and Sylvia exchanged glances before she looked out the window, and then stuck her head through the door. Seconds later, a familiar redheaded werewolf sauntered in.

Cliff seemed to recognize that something was off the moment that he caught sight of the house, but he didn’t offer any question other than a raised eyebrow.

“Hey Cliff,” Oscar greeted. “This isn’t a great time, I’m... I’m pretty sick.”

Cliff scoffed, shoving his hands in his pockets. “So you’re just kicking me out?”

Oscar didn’t want to. He wanted another distraction, but, “I’m almost definitely contagious,” he said with a chuckle.

“Well-” Cliffs hands fidgeted in his pockets, he averted his gaze for a moment. “Can I stay anyway?” This wasn’t like him. Asking for permission? Since when?

“You really don’t want to catch this, trust me,” Oscar added, smile faltering as he looked over his friend a little more.

“Please?”

If nothing else was a sign that something was off about him, Cliff saying ‘please’ was a big neon sign screaming it.

“If... if you’re sure? Maybe don’t sit so close to me, though.”

Cliff flopped onto the recliner like a ragdoll and huffed. And then he didn’t say anything, giving the room an awkward air of silence.

“Anything exciting?” Sylvia asked before Oscar could form the words in his fever-addled brain.

“Naw. What are you guys up to today?”

Deflecting. The two of them could take the hint.

“Barfing, mostly,” Oscar said with faux pride, as if it was the best possible way he could be spending his Sunday.

Cliff cringed. “Gross, you didn’t say you were that kind of sick.”

Oscar laughed. “Yeah, well, I didn’t feel like broadcasting it.”

They spent the afternoon watching movies and chatting. It felt like any other day that Cliff was over, almost. Except that Oscar had to occasionally excuse himself to throw up. And that Cliff was distant like he often was after one of their insane, scary adventures. Oscar wanted to ask, but he was trying to keep a barrier up for his friend and... well, he wasn’t sure he was well enough for a deep talk right now.

“I’m heading to bed,” Oscar finally announced, after a long day of being sick and spending time with his friends.

“Can I crash here?” Cliff asked as Oscar started to get up. There was a desperate edge to his voice, which was the only reason that Oscar didn’t immediately say no.

Oscar and Sylvia exchanged worried glances for just a moment before, “As long as your parents are cool with it,” he said. “But the couch is probably covered in my germs right now.”

Cliff shrugged. “I can take the chair.”

Cliff texted his mom and wasted no time making himself at home. Oscar glanced up from gathering his blankets and pillow to see a wolf getting comfortable in his recliner.

“You’re lint-rolling that thing tomorrow,” he joked.

“Mmm, naw,” the wolf said.

Sylvia smiled. “Goodnight, both of you.”

“Night.”

“Goodnight, Sylvia.”

-----

Oscar didn’t have a chance to process what was happening before he was darting to the bathroom with a hand over his mouth, nausea overwhelming him. His stomach ached as he puked into the toilet, making it just in time.

When he was done, Oscar was left shivering violently. The mere idea of standing up exhausted him, and he wasn’t even sure that he was done throwing up, anyway.

What was an annoying low-grade fever the day before seemed to ravage his body now. Oscar shuddered, whimpered at the mere discomfort of it all. He leaned against the tub, huffing, catching his breath. God, this was definitely worse. He hadn’t realized it could get worse.

“Oscar...?” the same timid voice, but shakier with worry.

He hadn’t bothered to close the bathroom door in his rush to get there in time, so Sylvia stood right outside of the doorway as if wondering if she was allowed to enter.

Oscar opened his mouth to reply and found himself gagging instead. He clamped a hand over his mouth, rushing to get over the toilet in time to cough of a mouthful of horrible bile.

“I...” He breathed out. “I’m fine.”

“No, I don’t think that you are,” Sylvia said with a frown.

He made an attempt to wipe the drool from his chin and only succeeded in smearing it further. Oh well. He shuddered, now wishing that he’d brought a blanket or something with him. As if he would have thought of that.

“I really,” a tired chuckle. “Really hoped that I’d feel better after yesterday.” Or was it still yesterday? He didn’t really know what time it was at all.

Sylvia made a sympathetic noise and floated down to examine him more closely. “Oh, Oscar, you really don’t look well.”

Oscar gave a weak, empty heave. He hardly had the energy to throw up anymore. Huh. “I think that I’m just going to... hang out here for a little while,” he said, another humorless laugh as he draped himself over the toilet.

“Are you sure you don’t want to go back to bed?”

“Don’t think I could make it.”

Sylvia’s frown deepened. “I’ll check on you in a few minutes...”

He nodded. “Thanks, Sylvia.”

Sylvia managed to close the door behind her as she left. And Oscar was alone to wallow in his misery. His brain was goopy, all he could really focus on was how horrible he felt, how nauseated he was. He choked up liquid a couple times more before he felt truly empty and quite washed out.

And tired.

Oh God, he was exhausted. There was no way that he was going to make it to bed.

Oscar reached up and grabbed the nearest towel on the rack before wrapping himself in it. He was still shivering horribly, nothing was going to make the chills stop, but at least he could get comfortable enough on the bathroom floor to fall asleep.

-----

“He’s really sick, and-”

“Oz, wake up!”

Despite it being the last thing he wanted to do, Oscar did indeed wake up. He pried his eyes open and then immediately closed them when he was assaulted by the blinding light. A hand, not exactly the most gentle, pressed against his neck for a split second before he heard Cliff swear.

“Dude, we’ve gotta get you off the floor.” Cliff tugged at his arm, and Oscar reluctantly opened his eyes again. “C’mon, man, this is just gross.” The sound of the toilet flushing, but Oscar could only focus on so many things at once.

Oscar managed to take some of his own weight, but he probably wasn’t much help. Somehow, though, Cliff managed to get him into bed.

Maybe the sudden motion of being dragged and then dropped down was too much, or maybe it was just due at this point, but Oscar found himself feeling sick once again. He tried to warn someone, and when that didn’t work, he simply brought a hand to his mouth and swallowed compulsively.

“Quick, I think he’s going to-”

“Damn it, hold on, Oz...”

Oscar didn’t realize his eyes were closed again until he opened then and found his waste paper bin next to his head, being held by a frustrated werewolf.

He heaved uselessly at first, and then a second, more productive one. Cliff made a noise of disgust.

“Sorry...” he choked out, as if any of that could fix the position he put his friend in.

“Don’t-” Cliff sighed. “Don’t apologize. Are you even like, conscious right now?”

Oscar was pretty sure that he was conscious. Probably.

-----

He was exhausted.

After last night, even Sylvia must’ve been exhausted.

Oscar wandered out in his same bundle of blankets that he’d lived in the day before. Thankfully, he saw the sleeping werewolf on the couch before he flopped onto of him.

He took the chair instead. The stairs took a lot out of him.

Cliff woke up groggily, his eyes slowly finding Oscar’s.

“You look like shit,” he muttered as he sat, dragging a hand down his face.

Oscar scoffed. “Speak for yourself!”

Sylvia appeared in the living room and gasped happily. “You’re looking better!”

A chuckle. “I don’t feel as um, out of it.” He felt horrible, definitely, but it was better than being passed out on the bathroom floor. He’d probably spend the rest of the day napping, but thankfully he had nothing else to do today.

He looked to his two friends. They both seemed tired. Hell, Cliff looked almost as bad as Oscar felt. He wondered how late he’d kept him up. He certainly wasn’t aware of his surroundings for part of the night. Vague memories of Cliff and Sylvia sitting with him while he tried to keep tylenol down, just to lower his fever enough for him to fall asleep for a little while.

He smiled. “Thanks for keeping an eye on me last night,” he said to the two of them. “You shouldn’t have had to deal with that.”

Sylvia quickly shook her head. “You wouldn’t have done well on your own. I’m just glad that Cliff happened to be here!”

Cliff went wide-eyed at the acknowledgment. “I- I mean it was better than being at home!” he defended.

“Cleaning my puke was better than being at home? God, Cliff, there’s something wrong there.” He tried to keep his tone teasing lest Cliff start deflecting, but the random bits Cliff had mentioned about his home life didn’t make him feel better.

“Yeah well, what was I supposed to do? let you choke on your own vomit?”

He chuckled and shrugged. “I’m just glad that I have you two around.”

Cliff groaned.

Sylvia smiled. “We’re glad to have you around too, Oscar.”
