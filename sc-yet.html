Title: Yet
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's sick and Stevie accidentally comes to his rescue
Word count: 600

Yet

David winced at the tight, full pain in his gut, doubling over slightly and laying a hand on his stomach, as if that was going to do anything. Patrick was at home sick with a stomach bug, and David wanted to think that it was nothing when he himself woke up without an appetite for breakfast.

He left Patrick in bed with a packet of crackers and a bottle of water, kissed his forehead (he had no desire to kiss his husband on the lips after listening to him puke the entire night before), and went to work with a thermos of coffee.

The coffee did not help matters. And his stomach was killing him.

The doorbell dinged and he forced himself to stand up straight and smile at the incoming-

“Oh, it’s just you.”

Stevie scoffed and made her way to the shampoo. “Uh, thanks a lot.”

He sighed, shook his head, and returned to his previous position, leaning on the counter. Stevie grabbed a bottle and then paused, and picked up a can of lotion as well. He was still trying to will away the ache when she set her stuff on the counter.

“What’s up with you?”

“Stomach,” he ground out. The cramp intensified and he found himself whimpering. “Ugh, God.”

“Uh oh,” Stevie muttered. “Caught Patrick’s flu, huh?”

David nodded.

“Thrown up yet?”

He shook his head, squeezing his eyes shut. To her credit, she waited patiently the long few seconds it took for him to get himself together and ring up her purchase.

Nausea was clawing up his throat the moment she took the bag. He swallowed thickly, placing the tips of his fingers to his lips to stifle a wet burp. Oh God, this wasn’t going to be pleasant.

“...David?”

He darted to the bathroom, and made it in record time.

Coffee didn’t taste nearly as good coming back up.

He spat a mouthful of gross into the water below and flushed it away. He was finally getting his breath back when there was a knock on the door. “You okay?”

“Fine,” he muttered, even though he absolutely wasn’t fine. He knew that he was in for a miserable couple of days, he’d just watched Patrick with the same virus. “Just need a minute.”

As if the universe hated him, the moment he said that was the moment that he heard the door jingle. “Oh God...”

“I’ve got it.” Her footsteps faded away and he heard her greet the customer a few seconds later.

He breathed a sigh of relief. Eventually, he decided that he was done throwing up (for now) and stood up. David expected to see a hideous creature looking back at him when he looked at his reflection in the mirror, but no. Just him, looking slightly flushed. He fixed a single piece of hair that had gotten out of place, and nodded in approval at himself. He might have just been violently ill, but at least he looked good.

He waited until the customer left before he emerged. The look Stevie gave him was probably the most empathetic he had ever seen her.

“Go home to your husband.”

David blinked. “And leave you here by yourself? You’d actually do that?”

She shrugged. “It’s just a couple hours. Roland can handle front desk for that long.”

He grimaced. “Can he, though?”

“Honestly? No idea. But you should go home. Go! before I change my mind.”

David didn’t hesitate to grab his bag. “Thank you,” he said, sincerely.

“You’re welcome,” Stevie replied, somehow just as sincerely.

“A bottle of wine is yours.”

“Naturally. Bye, David.”
