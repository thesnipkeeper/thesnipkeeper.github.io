Title: Excuses
Fandom: In Plain Sight
Summary: Marshall's being weird, Mary calls him out and doesn't accomplish a thing
Word count: 1.2k

Excuses

Marshall burped. Mary knew he did, and yet... nothing. He lowered his hand from his mouth and continued typing. That was- that was weird.

“Since when do you burp without saying excuse me?”

Marshall’s eyes widened like a deer in headlights before looking directly at her. “Excuse me.”

“No, I don’t care about that. I care about why? Are you suddenly not your prissy self anymore? You said ‘excuse me’ for me last week when I refused to say it to the witness we were talking to.” And yes, she was still bitter about that.

Marshall shrugged. “I must not have really noticed.”

Mary narrowed her eyes at his faux-innocence. Something was weird. She didn’t know what was weird quite yet, but something was definitely weird.

They had a day full of processing paperwork. Mary hated it with every inch of her being, but she supposed it was best after the rough case they had the day before. Well, Marshall insisted it was best after the rough case. Mary didn’t care so much to be out of the action, but he certainly needed a break. She could spot the burnout a mile away, even if he couldn’t.

Marshall burped again. This time it was followed by a very pointed and enunciated “Excuse me” that had Mary rolling her eyes.

The third time, he simply brought a hand to his mouth like he didn’t want her to notice. Which was weird. Since when did he care? Mary didn’t say anything that time, just looked at him like a puzzle and then went back to her own work.

A half hour later, her partner shot up from his desk and darted off. She didn’t even have a chance to ask him what the hell his problem was.

She tried to keep working. She really did. But it was getting harder to do the more time that went by. Mary glanced at the clock. Marshall had been in the bathroom for twenty minutes, and normally that was not something that she would want to worry about.

There was a nagging in the back of her head that reminded her that something weird was going on. That nagging wasn’t going to go away until she went to see what his deal was.

So, Mary found herself wandering into the men’s bathroom.

“Marshall? You in here?”

“Mary?! What the hell-” the question was cut off by the horrible sound of heaving, followed by a groan.

As she walked further in, she saw a single stall locked, and the bottoms of two very expensive shoes.

“What’s wrong with you?”

Marshall gagged again, and brought up more than before from the sounds of it. “Sick,” came the croaked reply. “Some kind of virus, I think.”

Mary swore. “You gonna be okay?”

“Sure. And I’d love some privacy, if you wouldn’t mind.”

“Ah.” Right. Maybe interrogating the guy while he was puking wasn’t the best way to go about things. “Gotcha.”

-----

Marshall returned to his desk looking pale and worn out. He hung his jacket on the back of his chair, and sighed. “I should probably go home.”

“You think?” But the poor guy looked so pathetic that she almost felt bad for teasing him. Almost. “I’ll let Stan know I’m driving you home. I’ve been craving a coffee run, anyway.”

Marshal shook his head. “You don’t have to drive me.” His words were slightly slurred, like he was just that tired. Mary didn’t like it one bit.

“You can’t promise me that you’re gonna make it home in one piece without puking in your car.” She raised her eyebrows expectantly, and he sighed. “Thought so. I’ll just pick you up tomorrow. C’mon.” Mary grabbed her keys.

He was defeated enough to shut down his computer and gather his own things.

-----

Mary was thankful for her accidental hoarding of grocery bags in her car, because had it not been for them, she’d be hosing it out.

Marshall spat into the bag and averted his eyes from the contents. “God, this is awful.”

“Yeah, you’re telling me. I didn’t expect to watch someone puke in my car today.”

He chuckled. “I’ll owe you one.”

“Definitely.”

They reached his house without another incident. He very disgustedly tied up the bags. Mary wasn’t even going to risk looking at what he was holding as she walked him to his apartment. Because she was chivalrous, she thought with a chuckle.

-----

“Are you gonna be okay?”

Marshall nodded. He seemed a little out of it. Tired. Mary didn’t really want to leave him like this. She had to remind herself that he was an adult man.

“M’kay, well... call me tomorrow? Let me know how you’re doing, if you need anything. I’m gonna take a wild guess and say you won’t feel up to going into work in the morning.”

“Oh God no.” He scoffed. “Thanks, Mary.”

“Yeah, don’t mention it.”

-----

Mary woke up at some ungodly hour to her phone ringing. She was ready to give whoever was calling her at three in the morning a piece of her mind until she saw Marshall’s name on the caller ID.

“Marshall?”

“Remember when you said to call you if I needed anything?”

“What’s going on? Are you okay?”

“I could uh, use a ride to the emergency room.” His voice sounded even worse than before. She wondered how much time he’d spent puking since she dropped him off.

Mary swore, already getting out of bed and finding clothes to throw on. “What’s wrong? How much should I speed?”

She heard a light chuckle from her friend, which would have made her feel better if he didn’t follow it by saying, “There’s blood in my vomit. Fever’s getting kind of high too.”

“Oh my God...” she muttered, equal parts exasperated and worried out of her mind. “I’m on my way. Don’t you dare die before I get there.”

“Wouldn’t dream of it.”

-----

The bleeding turned out to be from inflammation caused by the virus coupled with throwing up so hard. Mary let out a breath she hadn’t realized she was holding when the doctor said that he was going to be fine. They put him on an anti-emetic and told him to get some rest.

The asshole had the audacity to laugh once again.

“What?” Mary demanded. She was sleep deprived and grumpy and not in the mood for his little secret thoughts.

“Nothing,” he said. “Just don’t see you like that very often. Guess I don’t have to get shot for you to worry about me.”

“Oh please, I always worry about you. I mean, look at you.”

“ ‘m gonna ignore that.”

Mary scoffed, and then heaved a deep sigh, setting her forehead on the edge of the hospital bed. “You scared me, okay? I mean, blood? Really? You just had to start puking blood, didn’t you.”

“Apparently.”

She shook her head, and yeah, found herself chuckling as well. “Hope you don’t mind, but I might head to work.”

He smiled. “I really owe you one.”

“Oh yes you do,” Mary said, before leaning down and pressing a kiss to his head. “Get some rest.”

“You too!”

“Definitely not happening thanks to you!”
