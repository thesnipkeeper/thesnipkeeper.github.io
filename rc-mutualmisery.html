Mutual Misery

He knew he should have known better than to let Ronan and his iron stomach decide which restaurant to order from on their way home. He should have known better than to order the weirdest-sounding burger on the menu and then eat the entire thing.

This is what Gansey considers as he lays curled up on the floor in the fetal position. Cramps shoot through his abdomen, reminding him of the meal that refuses to digest or come back up for whatever reason. He has chills, weirdly, like a fever brought on by his body in another attempt to throw a fit about what he put in his stomach.

“Whoa.”

Gansey looks up, only slightly, to see Ronan standing in the doorway of his room.

“What’s wrong with you?”

“Stomach,” he says, voice strained in pain. “I think I ate something bad.”

“That burger?”

“Mm,” he hums noncommittally.

“Do you need anything or should I just let you lay there for a while?”

In lieu of answering, Gansey finds himself curling up tighter and whimpering like a child. His stomach cramps worse than it has before, warning him of things to come. An ominous gurgle from deep in his intestines decides his fate. He uses all the strength he has to stand up and avoid eye contact with his friend as he nudges him aside and makes his way to the bathroom.

"Have fun," says Ronan, as unhelpful as ever.

Gansey closes the bathroom door behind him and yanks his pants down, sitting on the toilet with a mournful sigh.

It’s not pleasant, and it doesn’t make him feel better. Just when he thinks he’s... finished, his stomach gurgles again and he finds himself yanking an old bucket out from under the sink cabinet and vomiting forcefully into it. He heaves, gags, bits of undigested burger getting stuck in the back of his throat and causing him to gag harder this time.

He isn’t sure how long it lasts, but it’s too long. Gansey showers as thoroughly as he can in this state and shudders as he dries himself off. He’s freezing for some reason. The rush of cold air that hits his skin as he opens the bathroom door is almost overwhelming.

Ronan is reading, or pretending to, when Gansey drags his body to his bed and flops down without any grace.

“You look like shit,” and Ronan has about as much sympathy in his voice as he can muster, which is not a lot, but enough for Gansey to know that he must be pitiful right now.

“I feel like shit,” Gansey mutters. He shivers and grabs his blanket, completely unmaking his bed and not caring in the slightest. He wraps himself in it and it does nothing to make the chills go away.

“It’s not my fault, y’know.”

Gansey sighs irritably. “I never said that it was.”

“No, but you’re blaming me in your head,” Ronan almost sounds smug that he knows this.

And yes, he’s right, of course he’s right. That stupid restaurant was his idea and he was the asshole saying ‘oooh yeah order that one I bet you can’t eat the whole thing’. What an agent of chaos.

He feels the nausea begin to build again as a promise that his stomach isn’t done. Gansey groans.

Ronan sounds annoyed, maybe concerned. “What now?”

“I forgot to bring the bucket down here.”

“Would have thought you puked and shit everything out by now.”

Gansey ignores him, grabs a pillow, and hugs it to his stomach. Maybe he can just fall asleep and hope that this all goes away on its own. It’s a wonder what naps can fix, he tells himself.

He’s still trying his hardest to fall asleep when he hears a clunk of plastic against the old wood floor.

“Brought your stupid fucking bucket.”

“Thank you,” Gansey says with sincerity.

There’s quiet for a couple of minutes while Gansey closes his eyes and tries to let sleep take him with no avail. He doesn’t wonder what Ronan is doing during those couple of minutes until he hears the other guy shit from one foot to the other. He’s... still there for some reason.

“Ronan,” Gansey murmurs. “What?”

“Do I need to worry about you choking on your own puke or something?”

“It’s food poisoning.” That’s about all the answer he’s willing to give, because he feels like he may actually fall asleep now and escape the misery that’s befallen him.

-----

Gansey wakes up in the middle of the night. He must have fallen asleep, and then slept well, because it’s pitch black outside and there’s no signs of life anywhere else in the warehouse.

He sits up and immediately vomits over the side of the bed, but miraculously makes it into the bucket that had been conveniently placed there. He and Ronan have been friends long enough, apparently, that even his middle-of-the-night-vomit-zone is predictable.

He thinks, hopes, once and it’ll be over with, but he still feels nausea looming over him. So he sits up, properly now, shoves all the stupid blankets off of him (Jesus, he’s hot) and pulls the bucket into his lap.

He sits and waits for the impending bout. He’s desperate to get this over with, his body screaming for more sleep. Gansey’s exhausted, and he knows the second he lies down he’s going to wake and throw up, and there’s no guarantee that the next time is going to be so perfectly aimed.

And then he hears movement upstairs. Shit fuck, he does not need an audience right now!

Ronan comes out of his room. His footsteps are quick for someone who’s just woken up. Maybe he’s been awake and Gansey just hasn’t noticed, he thinks. He’s still hoping that he can just throw up and go back to sleep. Ronan doesn’t need to be worried about-

Shit.

Ronan’s throwing up.

The far too familiar sound of gagging from the bathroom. Jesus Christ.

Gansey comes to one simple conclusion, ‘this is not food poisoning’ before he heaves into the bucket.

-----

They don’t do much besides sleep the next day. Gansey in his bed and Ronan in his. He tries not to worry when he hears Ronan getting up to puke or go to the bathroom much more frequently than Gansey has. He tries not to worry, even when he starts to wonder whether Ronan’s eaten or drank anything to replace the fluids he’s lost.

Gansey glances at his own room-temperature Gatorade sitting on the floor beside his bed. It’s going to be his fault if something happens to Ronan, he realizes. He’s going to be responsible if the man dies of dehydration or neglects himself to death.

So he wraps himself in a blanket, shoves his phone in one pocket of his sweat pants and an extra bottle of gatorade in the other, and slowly makes his way upstairs to Ronan’s room. The stairs have never felt so treacherous before. He feels his vision clouding at the edges by the time he reaches the top step, as if his body is saying ‘enough!’. He takes a moment to catch his breath, to think about crying out of pure misery, and knocks on Ronan’s door.

“Jesus!” a hoarse, startled noise, which is fair because he probably doesn’t expect Gansey to be anything more than kind of conscious right now, let alone upstairs knocking on his door. “What?” Ronan sounds about as bad as Gansey feels. Maybe worse.

Gansey nudges the door and lets it swing open itself before he holds out the Gatorade for Ronan. Ronan takes it, sets it aside, a deeply sour expression on his face even as he says, “Thanks.”

Gansey nods. “I got really-” lonely, “bored down there.”

It’s all he has to say for Ronan to scoot to the side. Gansey lowers himself into his friend’s bed, and thinks he feels a little better already. Okay, maybe not physically, but still.

“How are you holding up, anyway?”

Ronan huffs, looking and sounding unbelievably exhausted. Maybe he didn’t get as much sleep as Gansey thought. “Food poisoning isn’t contagious.”

“Yes,” Gansey agrees stupidly.

“So you gave me some fucking bug-”

“Oh, fuck off, there’s no way you could have caught it that fast! We probably got it at the same time!” Despite how shitty he feels, Gansey isn’t about to let himself lose this argument.

Until Ronan clamps a hand over his mouth and rushes to get past Gansey out of the bed. It doesn’t even sound like anything comes up, and Ronan returns within minutes looking pale anyway.

“Drink something,” he says as Ronan gets back in bed. “Seriously.”

Ronan almost hesitates out of pure rebellion before he sighs and cracks the top of the drink, taking a minuscule sip and then another. And then he sinks down into the covers and shifts until his and Gansey’s arms are touching. It’s a bit too much for his fever-sensitive skin, but also the best thing Gansey’s felt this week.

-----

He doesn’t start to really worry until Ronan stops throwing up. Begins to feel warmer yet stops sweating through his shirts. He sleeps for hours at a time while Gansey’s itching to get better so he doesn’t have to be bedridden anymore.

Sure, Ronan sleeping more than he needs to isn’t exactly abnormal after everything he’s been through. And sure, he’s sick. But Gansey still freaks out a little bit.

He calls Adam. He babbles about Ronan and dehydration, and about the fact that he himself can barely get out of bed let alone go out and buy stuff that could help.

“I’d get whatever you want me to,” Adam starts, “but I don’t- I don’t have much cash. Or any, really.”

“You know where I keep my wallet, right? You can take my card.”

Adam sputters. “I- Are you- okay. Okay, I’ll be over in a bit, okay.”

-----

It feels wrong to even touch Gansey’s card. This thing has who knows how much money attached to it and Adam’s using it to make fucking drugstore run. Nevertheless, he gets everything on Gansey’s list and then just stuff he thinks Gansey might want for himself and not Ronan.

He’s not going to pretend like he understands the concern for Ronan. The guy puts himself in danger for fun everyday, a stupid stomach bug isn’t going to kill him.

And yet, Gansey is making it his problem. Once again, making everyone else’s problems his.

Adam certainly doesn’t expect to find the two of them snuggled up in bed together. When Gansey said he was in Ronan’s room, this wasn’t what he thought he meant.

“Hi, so I got everything,” he says, holding up the grocery bags.

Ronan’s awake, lying against Gansey, his signature tired RBF despite looking somewhat comfortable.

Gansey makes grabby hands. “Thermometer?”

Adam nods and pulls it out, and then takes the thing out of the packaging and hands it over.

“Here,” Gansey holds it in front of Ronan. “Put this under your tongue.”

“I-” Ronan looks like he’s trying to protest, and apparently his voice is just so pathetic that he gives up. Jesus, no wonder Gansey’s so worried. He slips the thermometer into his mouth. It makes him look significantly sicker.

“He could be dehydrated,” says Gansey.

“Well, that’s not the end of the world,” Adam says. “And I’m not trying to be an asshole when I say that, but we’ve got fluids and stuff.” Because they both know that Ronan would murder both of them before he lets them drag him to the ER.

Gansey nods, clearly needing the rational words to combat the scrambled concerns in his brain. It’s always Ronan, Adam thinks, that turns him into some kind of crazy person.

The thermometer beeps, Ronan pulls it out and reads it.

“102.7.”

Gansey sighs. Adam bites the inside of his mouth. Neither of them are surprised. Is that weird? Adam supposes it is. A simple bug shouldn’t hit anyone this hard, but Ronan always gets sicker than anyone else when something goes around.

A simple cold has him coughing and congested for at least a week afterward. Adam has never felt much sympathy for him. It’s his own fault. Ronan cares about himself just as much as he cares about anyone else. And yet.

And yet.

“I can stick around if you guys need me to,” he says.

Ronan scoffs. “Don’t need you to fucking babys- hngh.” Gansey elbows him, gently.

“Would you mind?”

“I’d love to be anywhere but home right now,” he laughs.

They all avoid eye contact for a moment. That’s the wrong thing to say. Oh well.

“So uh, do you guys want me to do something? Have you eaten? Because I could get some crackers or toast or something.”

“No,” they both groan in unison.

Gansey cuddles closer to Ronan, who already looks like he’s falling asleep again. They’ll have to keep an eye on his fever, and Gansey’s too while they’re at it.

Adam’s heart jumps a little when Gansey pats the empty spot in the bed next to him.

“Gross,” he says. “As if I want your germs.” Except he’s climbing into the bed even as he’s complaining.

Gansey hums happily, and Adam swears he can see a sleepy smile on Ronan’s face.

“You can stay here as long as you’d like.”

Adam chuckles. “Yeah, I know I can.”
