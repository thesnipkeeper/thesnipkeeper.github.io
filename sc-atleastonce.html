Title: At Least Once
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Things get awkward between David and Patrick
Word count: 2k

title

"I'm sorry, what happened?" Stevie asks with a level of amusement that's entirely uncalled for.

"Shhh!" David hisses. "No one can know, okay? Like, ever. Oh my God…" images of the day before flash in his mind and he buries his head in his hands. “This is a disaster.”

“So was it like ‘lovers’ curry’ bad?”

David whimpers at the table. “Yes...”

“Oh my God,” except Stevie says it with a chuckle, adding infuriating to the mix of emotions he’s been suffering from the past day.

David wants to cry. What does this mean for his relationship? He’s the person Patrick has sex with. Can that ever happen again? Probably not! He’s destined to die alone.

He hears Stevie sigh heavily and lifts his head to find her looking at him with seriousness. Well - genuine seriousness, he thinks. “David, he’s not going to break up with you if that’s what your doomsday thoughts are saying.”

How the fuck does she know what he’s thinking?

“Didn’t he puke on you like two days ago?”

He’s surprised she remembers that story as he just mentioned it in passing the last time they spoke on the phone.

“But this is so much worse and you know it.”

“I don’t see how,” Stevie says with a shrug. “And he’s not all freaking out right now like you are.” She waves a hand toward him.

David groans. She’s right. Patrick should be just as embarrassed right now and it isn’t fair that he isn’t dying of humiliation!

“Anyway, everyone shits their pants when they’re sick at least once in their life,” she says, less confident in the admission.

David shushes her more intensely. “We do not need the whole fucking town knowing about this!”

Stevie whispers back, “All I’m saying is that it was going to happen sooner or later!”

“Well I was hoping that we’d be old and disgusting with lost inhibitions when it did happen.” David huffs, flopping back against the booth and crossing his arms like a frustrated child.

“Guess you won’t be getting chinese at that place anytime soon, then, huh?”

“No, Stevie. No we won’t be,” David replies sharply.

He’s still in the process of wallowing in self-pity with no help from his best friend (Stevie’s in the process of scrolling through her phone the way she’s avoiding further conversation) when Twyla arrives with the food.

“Toast for David, and a taco salad for Stevie!” she smiles. “Anything else?”

David glares at Stevie’s food like it’s personally offended him, and it honestly has because if she had any sense of empathy she wouldn’t be ordering something that looks so good when she knows that he can’t stomach real food right now.

“No, we’re good, but thanks,” David sighs out.

“Okay!” and she’s gone.

“I tell you a horrifyingly-disgusting story about bodily functions you’re still hungry?”

“Starving. I have low food standards. And if I was still able to eat after the incident on our honeymoon night...” she chuckles to herself before dumping the little container of salsa on the thing and proceeding to stab it to death.

He frowns at the half-burnt dry toast on the plate in front of him. He had no intention of eating in the first place, but he had to order something. As hungry as he is, he isn’t sure his stomach could handle anything solid right now, and he isn’t willing to risk a repeat of yesterday to find out.

-----

The story went like this: Patrick picks up food from the new chinese restaurant in Elmdale two nights ago on his way home from a meeting with a supplier. David eats said food without question because he’s starving and it looks delicious.

What proceeds is an unfortunate series of events that involve Patrick waking up in the middle of the night, vomiting on himself and David, and apologizing profusely with a sense of distress David’s never witnessed before.

"I'm sorry, I'm sorry..."

"No no no, come on..." David shuffles him to the bathroom before the next wave hits, and then goes back to quickly strip and remake the bed.

Patrick spends the rest of the night throwing up while David sits at his side, not just to comfort him but because he’s sure he’s next and doesn’t want to be too far from the bathroom when the heaviness in his gut turns into nausea.

Patrick’s more of a mess than David’s ever seen him, tearful and clingy.

Eventually, he curls up on the bathroom floor with his head in David’s lap. It’s cute in a tragic sort of way.

And then David’s stomach starts to cramp. He regretfully has to move a sleepy, confused Patrick so he can double over and hope that’ll make the pain go away.

“Oh no,” Patrick mutters sleepily rubbing his eyes while David folds his arms over his stomach and whimpers. “It was the food.”

“Yeah,” David mutters through gritted teeth. “I think so.”

They make a day of living off of saltines, sprite, and whatever DVDs are within reach, cuddled up on the couch together. A bucket sits at their feet, but neither of them are desperate enough to be the first to use it.

David’s stomach gurgles.

Patrick sits up and looks at him. “You need to get up?” he asks.

He doesn’t feel particularly nauseated, just bloated and gross. Maybe his stomach is finally starting to digest correctly. David shakes his head.

Patrick shrugs and lays back against him. They’re both sick, but Patrick’s definitely had more of the rough night. He’s thrown up far more than David has, and he’s exhausted and worn out from a night of stress.

Maybe David’s stomach is just more resistant to food poisoning at this point. He’s spent his whole life eating weird things without question, so maybe he’s in the clear now.

It happens so suddenly.

David feels something in his stomach drop and suddenly he’s shoving the love of his life away from him and rushing to get off of the couch.

And that’s all he can do before the worst thing in his life happens right there.

-----

He cringes thinking about it.

He left Patrick’s last night with some excuse about Moira needing him to help with wigs or something and yeeted out of there the moment he... managed to change. They haven’t said so much as a word to each other today besides “feeling better?” and “where did you want the new hand cream again?”

Patrick hasn’t even been able to look at him today. David’s tainted his boyfriend’s image of him that badly. And David isn’t going to try a conversation when he’s still dying from embarrassment.

He sighs heavily and takes a bite of the toast without any joy. It’s gross, but if he doesn’t eat something he’s going to be adding ‘hangry’ to the number of emotions going on in his head right now.

“Maybe he’s just not talking to you because he doesn’t feel good, David. He did spend like, the last two days throwing up,” Stevie reasons when he brings it up.

But she doesn’t know him like David does. Patrick’s a different kind of quiet when he’s sick, and he’d do David the favor of warning him that he’s just quiet because he isn’t feeling good, because he knows how far David’s imagination can spiral when he thinks Patrick is mad at him.

This is different.

There’s been no acknowledgment of Patrick’s weirdness (admittedly, on either side, but...) and that’s no doubt because he can’t stand the idea of being in a relationship with someone who-

eugh.

“I don’t know,” David shrugs, pushing his toast toward Stevie. She makes a face and pushes it back. “I should probably get back to the store.”

“David...” but he’s already walked away.

-----

Patrick gives a quick glance from the counter as David enters the store. “Did you eat?”

David winces. “I had like, a bite. What about you? Are you going to-?”

“No,” Patrick says. He clears his throat. “No, I’m... I’m still not very hungry.” He chuckles, confusing David even more. “We should go on a celebratory dinner when we can eat again.”

“Celebrating what though?”

“Eating again.”

David snorts, and then nods, because that’s absolutely fair. “Sounds good to me.” He leans on the counter, where Patrick definitely has a spreadsheet open out of his own line of sight. He’s got his ‘nerdy thinking’ face on. “You um, still want to eat together, then?”

Patrick blinks, his thinking face fading into something completely innocent and cute. “Well- Yeah? Not any time soon, obviously. We should rush it and make ourselves worse. I’m not about to drag you to a restaurant tonight and make you eat, if that’s what you’re worried about.” Patrick looks a little worried now. “Is... everything okay?” It’s more nervous than Patrick’s usual questions.

David clears his throat, presses his lips together and looks up at the ceiling. “I um... I’d just understand if you didn’t want to- If you needed space-”

Patrick raises an eyebrow.

“After everything happened, after yesterday...”

“Oh God, David.” Patrick buries his head in his hands, making David that much more reluctant to finish what he’s trying to say. “I’m so sorry.”

David shakes his head. “I completely understand. It was, um, rough.” he wants space, then.

“Yeah,” Patrick breathes out, dragging a hand down his face. “I’m sorry, I should have warned you.”

David stopped. “wait...come again? What are you apologizing for exactly?”

Patrick swallowed, looking even paler than he had last night. He sighs. “I know that I’m a seriously emotional mess when I’m sick. I know that I’m needy and I can be a little too much, and you weren’t feeling great either so I shouldn’t have expected you to-”

“Hold on,” David lays a hand on his boyfriend’s arm, and Patrick’s mouth closes, wide-eyed. “What’s happening right now?”

Patrick huffs, almost looking frustrated as if David’s missing some big thing. He must be, though, because suddenly this conversation isn’t making sense. “David, I’m trying to apologize here okay?”

“For... being sick?”

“For being too much!” Patrick’s voice raises in intensity. “I know you left because you were a little too overwhelmed. I mean, you’ve said yourself that your mom would never let you touch one of her wigs. God- I even- I’m sorry for throwing up on you, too. I’m just- I’m sorry, David.” He sighs heavily and gives David an expectant look. As if David would know what to say after that.

“Mhm, right.” David nods, eyes fluttering closed as he tries to process what the fuck just happened.

“Please say something else, because I’m really embarrassed by this whole thing, and I thought you were mad at me.”

“You’re embarrassed?!” David exclaims.

“Yes!”

He gives something that is equal-parts growl and sigh. “Oh my God, Patrick. I left because I was embarrassed. I thought you never wanted to look at me again!”

Now Patrick has the audacity to look confused. “What? Why?”

“What do you mean why? I-” He can’t just say it, can he? Reminding Patrick of what happened is a surefire way to push him away, and he doesn’t want that! “After yesterday,” David emphasizes with purpose. He gives Patrick a look until Patrick’s face finally lights up with realization.

“Ohhh...”

David nods. “Yeah, oh.”

“That’s why you haven’t talked to me since you left yesterday? David, I thought you were upset with me! Jesus christ, I don’t care about that! It was food poisoning, it was bound to happen to one of us at some point yesterday.”

David covers his face and mutters out an, “Oh my Gooood.”

“So you’re not... upset? About how I was acting yesterday?”

“No,” and David can’t help but laugh out of pure relief. And embarrassment, but relief. “No, and the fact that you're clingy when you’re sick is incredibly adorable and something I’m definitely going to take advantage of in the future.”

Patrick scoffs. “Well, I’m glad we got this worked out,” he says, moving around the counter to wrap David in a tight huge. He clears his throat. “And I took your pajamas out of the dryer this morning, so if you want them back-”

“Burn them, please.”

Patrick chuckles. “Understood.”
