Old Habits

Patrick has heard David’s references to shame-eating. He still isn’t entirely sure what “shame eating” is, but he isn’t sure he should ask. David might be comfortable announcing it, but he admits to a lot of terrible things and passes them off as jokes quite nicely. David’s not an idiot, he’s aware that not everyone takes him seriously and he uses it to his advantage.

He uses a lot of things to his advantage. He relies on others tiptoeing around him so he can do as he likes without consequences.

Patrick’s phone buzzes. He’s not going to reply, not right now. He needs a moment to cool down before he tries to talk.

But the name on the phone isn’t David, but his sister.

so idk what happened between the two of u before david came home, but u need to come over and fix this patrick

Patrick sighs heavily.

I don’t think that’s a good idea right now

ok well thats like 100% ur choice and i totally get it

but like

all hes done since he got home is drink all of my wine coolers and eat and cry about how u guys might be over

He bites his lip, both out of frustration and worry. He knows he said some things David didn’t want to hear. But they were things he needed to hear if the two of them were really going to spend the rest of their lives together.

Although... he probably could have said them in a calmer and more productive way, when they weren’t in the middle of a heated argument about wedding costs.

we’re not over, he replies. i just need some time to regroup before i deal with him again

and did u tell him that?

Patrick thinks for a moment. The whole argument is a bit of a blur at this point, but he does recall saying something along the lines of “you’re too much for me right now”. David just gave a simple “okay” and left.

i think i did

The three dots pop up and disappear several times before Alexis finally replies

patrick i love u

but that was a total dick move

especially for u

u kno how he is with that kind of stuff

Patrick does know. He should have. He remembers when they were first together, not knowing each other quite enough to know where the line was when it came to teasing. Patrick made a joke about him being a handful and later found David crying in the cafe bathroom.

He knows why now. A lot of people left him because they didn’t want to deal with all of who he was. That’s not how Patrick felt, how he feels. He was just sick of hearing David tell him time and time again that he knew what he was doing when he clearly didn’t. He just expected Patrick to be okay with not knowing how much the flowers-

Fuck.

Was that whole argument about wedding flowers?

oh god i’m an idiot

i know but its ok

please just fix him before he eats my entire chocolate stash and/or starts puking

A few seconds pass before Alexis adds, he wont even let me touch him patrick

Patrick bites his lip, willing the guilty tears away. This is his fault. The two of them could have worked things out by calmly discussing the situation, but the whole thing just spiraled out of control. They didn’t mean any of what they said, they’ve just been so busy lately and it was late and they were both exhausted.

How could he do that to the love of his life? There’s nothing in the world that he can do to make this up to David.

But, he supposes, he can start by going to the motel.

-----

He takes a deep breath and knocks on the door of room seven. He isn’t surprised that Alexis is the one who answers.

She nods a gesture inside and closes the door behind him. David’s side of the room has a gaggle of glass bottles and plenty of candy wrappers, along with an empty bag of chips and a half-empty bag of cheez-its. But no David.

Alexis doesn’t look upset with him, thank goodness, but she does look a bit freaked out. Freaked out for her, anyway. He takes it that this doesn’t happen very often, which is a relief. The last thing he wants to find out is that this has been happening to David without him knowing for a long time.

“Where is he?” Patrick asks quietly.

Alexis sighs. “Um, you took too long, because he’s puking.” She throws her arm in the general direction of the bathroom.

Patrick makes his way over there and hears soft crying that sort of breaks his heart. Oh God, he really fucked things up, didn’t he? He hesitantly knocks on the door. “David? It’s me. Can I come in?”

A retched sob that has Patrick retracting his hand immediately. He looks back at Alexis, but she’s already on her way over. She taps Patrick out of the way and opens the bathroom door without bothering to knock. She closes it behind her, and Patrick takes another step back as to not invade their privacy.

He stuffs his hands in his pockets, feeling strangely out of his depth. He can hear quiet conversation on the other side of the door, Alexis saying something, asking something? And David replying with a croaky voice. The faucet runs for a second and Alexis says something else before she exits the bathroom and very pointedly closes the door behind her.

“So he seems to be doing better,” Alexis says. “Ummm... he’ll be out in a few minutes. He just needs to regroup.”

Fuuck. Patrick sighs heavily. “Alexis...”

She stops him with a finger on his shoulder. “Look, you don’t have to apologize to me.” She shakes her head and points her nose to the ceiling for a moment as she thinks of what to say. “And like, maybe David could take something like this a little better if he wasn’t already so stressed. I mean, he’s been impossible to live with this week. Like a big ball of anxiety, you know? He practically had a panic attack the other day when the caterers forgot to call him back. But like it turned out they were just closed for the day.”

“Wait, what?” That’s the first time Patrick’s heard about him being stressed. Well, more stressed than the two of them already were. He figured that they were both equally on edge as they’ve been trying to work a big vendor for the store and get everything ready for the visit. David has seemed his usual amount of stress.

Alexis’ eyes widen in realization. “Oh, you didn’t even... Patrick, ugh.” She taps his shoulder rapidly. “Look, it’s not your fault, but you should know that, like I said, David’s been a bit of a mess lately and he’s starting to go back to old habits and it’s freaking me out. He only panic-binges like this when he’s really upset, and as much as I respect his feelings, I kind of doubt that whatever argument you guys had was bad enough to freak him out enough for that.” She clears her throat. “Which means his threshold is lowering, which means he needs a fucking break and for you two to work things out.”

Patrick isn’t sure it’s his place to ask about what exactly “old habits” means, but he has a feeling that’s sort of David’s shame eating but on steroids.

Patrick is on full alert the moment the bathroom door opens. David’s in a t-shirt, his sweater draped over his arm. He drops it in the hamper next to the door before he meets Patrick’s eyes.

David’s eyes are puffy and red like he’s been crying for a long time. He looks awful.

“Hey,”

And sounds even worse.

“Hey, can we talk?”

Alexis clears her throat, backs her way to the door.. “Oof, wow, I’m really craving a milkshake from the cafe. Byye.”

Patrick can’t help but chuckle, and David rolls his eyes before wandering closer.

“Alexis texted me,” Patrick says.

David just nods and takes a seat on his bed. “I kind of figured,” he rasps. He looks like he could fall asleep right now, until he gets a look of the food wrappers surrounding his bed, and suddenly he looks just as worked up as usual.

A silence fills the room. Patrick hesitantly takes a seat across from David on Alexis’ bed. David’s eyes are fixated on the floor, he’s fidgeting with the rings on his left hand. Patrick can see his lip quivering, tears starting to drip down his face.

“David, I’m so sorry.” He doesn’t intend for his own voice to break. David’s tears fall faster. “I was angry. I didn’t know what I was saying.”

“You did, though.” David sniffles. “You knew exactly what you were saying, and you meant it.”

“David, you know I love you more than anything. I didn’t mean it like that, I think you know that.”

He wipes some of the tears from his cheek and gives a shaky sigh before he finally looks up to meet Patrick’s eyes. “It wasn’t just you,” he says quietly. “That upset me, I mean. Everything just piled up so fast and then we got an argument and that was just like, the icing on the cake of fuck-ups.” He chokes out a sob, a watery laugh.

Patrick doesn’t say anything. He just moves to David’s bed, sitting a safe enough distance away. That distance doesn’t matter, because soon enough David is closing it and leaning against him with tired sobs. And before he knows it, Patrick is crying too.

The two of them seem to come to the one silent conclusion: they’re both exhausted. They’d both showed up to the store early that morning to rearrange things before opening, and if David’s been as stressed as Alexis said he is, he probably didn’t get a lot of sleep the night before.

“Will you stay here tonight?” David asks quietly.

Patrick sniffles and nods. “I’d love to.”

They get comfortable, or as comfortable as two grown men can get in a twin-sized motel bed. Alexis is no doubt going to be home soon. There’s only so much someone can do at night in Schitt’s Creek.

They’re both emotionally drained. David doesn’t even bother cleaning up, which is something he’s probably going to end up doing the first thing he does when he wakes up in the morning.

“Alexis said she was worried that you were going back to ‘old habits’,” Patrick says, breaking the silence.

David huffs out a breath that tickles Patrick’s neck. “Yeah. Um... yeah.”

“You don’t have to talk about it if you don’t want to,” he says quickly, hearing the hesitation in his voice. “I just want you to feel... safe, I guess? Should I be doing something to help?”

David shakes his head as well as he can on the pillow. “It’s not a big deal. Not anymore. I just sometimes... drink a lot. And then I eat a lot. And then I get sick and puke it up.” He gives a humorless chuckle. “I’m a fucking mess. I was doing so good too. The last time it happened was when we-” he stops himself and continues to nuzzle Patrick’s neck.

Patrick presses a kiss to David’s forehead. “I’m sorry.”

David hums. “Me too.”

They fall asleep before Alexis returns. It’s only 8PM, but they’re exhausted.

In the morning, Patrick eats breakfast with the family, joking and laughing with Mrs. Rose and talking about sports with Mr. Rose.

He and David exchange a look, one that asks each other if they’re good. They both smile, David’s eyes crinkling at the corner in the way he insists isn’t cute, Patrick.

And Alexis texts them while they’re all at the table.

talked to stevie last night. were taking shifts at the store

u two get to relax today

She grins proudly while Mr. Rose is telling a golf story that no one is listening to, and the visible tenseness in David’s body disappears.

They’ll be fine, Patrick decides.
