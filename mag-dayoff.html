Day Off

Jon found himself dozing off until the incessant buzzing scraped through his ears.

He wasn't in bed, aa most people would be when their alarm clocks went off in the morning, but the bathroom floor where he'd spent most of the night.

Jon whimpered groaned and pried his eyes open, only to be met with the sharp, florescent light. The headache was back, then. He reluctantly pulled his fatigued, ailing body off of the floor, and stole a glance of himself in the mirror.

Good lord, he looked awful. He hoped none of his coworkers were feeling particularly perceptive today. He could probably just hole himself up in his office for the day if he needed to.

Jon's stomach gurgled. Oh... oh, he really didn't want to go to work.

BZZZ BZZZ BZZZ

And there was the pressing matter that was the angry alarm. That really needed to stop.

Jon moaned, running a hand down his face and swallowing back the nausea rising in his throat. He couldn't possibly have anything left to vomit up.

He appeased the alarm with a sigh of content. At least that was ove- urp!

Jon bolted into the bathroom on wobbly legs, barely making it to the toilet before he gagged bile and what remained of his stomach contents. He couldn't possibly have much left, could he?

He growled as his stomach cramped, kneading into it just enough to make him think that it's helping.

Getting dressed was one of the hardest things Jon had ever had to do. He peeled off his sweaty pajamas and replaced them with clean work clothes.

He... He should shower, but the mere idea exhausted him no end. Jon hoped he didn't look or smell too awful.

He grabbed a sweater for the chills and a thermos of mint tea for his stomach, and went to work.

-----

Martin tried not to act too excited when Jon showed up. Martin spent most days relieved that his coworkers had lived to see another day, with all that had been going on.

It wasn't unusual for Jon to come in sleep-deprived.

But this looked different, like he'd been running laps all night in lieu of sleeping. He got an annoyed growl in response to his morning greeting, though, so things must not have been too bad.

"Didn't sleep well?"

The way Jon massaged his temple tugged at Martin's heartstrings. He looked so tired, and it was kindofadorable.

Jon shook his head. "Was up all night with stomach flu," He stated.

Martin's face fell. He watched the man slump into his office chair and set his things down just like he did every day, but it was done with an air of exhaustion.

"W-what? Why did you come to work? Jon, you should be at home resting!"

"I don't have time," he hissed, though even his annoyance lacked its usual bite. "Elias wants these done by the end of the week." Martin followed his gesture to a stack of folders, statements.

"Yeah, but-"

"Don't you have work to do, Martin?" He sighed out.

Martin received the message loud and clear and went about his own business. Of course Jon didn't need him hovering. He was a grown man, a stomach bug wasn't the end of the world.

Jon was still nursing a thermos of some nice-smelling tea when Martin ran into him later that morning. He'd take a sip, and then lay a hand on his stomach with a bit of a wince when he thought no one was looking. He hadn't taken off his sweater, either, instead pulling it tighter around himself. Poor guy. Martin really hoped he'd be feeling better by the end of their work day.

Ha.

He was washing his hands in the bathroom when the door flew open. Jon burst in, walking quickly to the nearest stall.

Heaving.

Martin moved to the door, and hesitated as the heaving got more desperate.

Gagging, coughing, and liquid hitting liquid as that lovely tea came back up.

He was torn between giving Jon his privacy and staying to make sure he was alright.

"Jon?" Martin half-hoped he hadn't heard him. Maybe he should just go.

A sigh, and a hoarse voice, "What is it, Martin?"

"Are you... Are you alright? Do you need anything?"

Whether Jon was actually planning to answer or not, Martin didn't know, because he began vomiting again before he could.

Martin winced. He should just go. Jon probably didn't want an audience, right?

The gravelly groan caught his attention. "Are you still there?"

"Yeah, I'm still here. Sorry."

"No, I..." A sigh. The toilet flushed, but Jon made no move to leave the stall. "Could you... get me some water? Oh uh... Please?"

Martin let out a breath he hadn't realized he was holding at the relief that Jon wasn't mad at him.

"I'll be right back." He tried not to be too happy about feeling helpful. Martin... Did his best to look out for Jon without the man realizing it, but actually being asked for help (without the annoyed tone in his voice as he didn't bother to look up from his papers) was... almost nice.

The stall door was open when Martin returned with a styrofoam cup of water in his hands. Jon must not have locked it in the first place. It gave him a view of the ill archivist, back against the wall and legs folded. Is this the state he'd been in all night?

Watery eyes opened at Martin's "knock knock?"

"Thank you," he rasped, accepting the cup and immediately taking a drink.

"Ah, you should be careful drinking so soon after...erm, you know."

Jon took another sip in defiance. Fair enough.

He took in the ill man's appearance, and Martin sighed. "Go home, Jon..." He said gently. "I mean, you can't actually be useful like this, can you?"

Jon's eyes slipped shut once again and he leaned back against the tile wall. "You... Might have a point." He wrapped his arms tight around his middle. A few seconds went by of his uncomfortable shifting before he lunged for the toilet. "Oh God..." He moaned.

The water immediately came up, at least that was what Martin suspected. He turned away to give Jon some privacy, and when he turned back, the poor man was a mess.

Eyes watering from the force and nose dripping down his upper lip. He went for a wad of toilet tissue and cleaned himself up as best he could as he regained his breathing.

"Alright," he sniffled. "You win. I'm taking a sick day."

-----

It was an innocent suggestion, really. Martin just suggested that he could drive Jon home. He really wasn't in a state to be operating a machine, if his likely fever was anything to go by.

Jon sheepishly doubled-up some bags and put them in his pocket. He must've still been feeling sick, poor thing.

He was silent during the car ride, so of course Martin rambled on and on about his cat, what he saw on the news, whatever he could possibly think about to distract Jon from his illness and if anything make him get annoyed at Martin instead.

Jon was fast asleep the next time Martin looked over. His chest rose and fell steadily, breath forming puffs of fog on the window.

Staying up all night affected him on a good day, but Jon must have been exhausted after having been sick all night. It was good that he was getting some rest.

They pulled up to Jon's home, a small, rather sterile house. He was quite thankful Jon didn't have to make use of the bags in his pocket.

"Jon, we're here," he said softly, and couldn't help but smile when Jon remained peacefully asleep. Poor thing. "Hey, Jon..." He nudged the man's shoulder as gently as possible.

He was really warm. Martin figured he had a little bit of the chills, but this felt like a proper fever.

Jon came to consciousness, rubbing his eyes and taking a few seconds to remember where he was.

"Oh... Oh, thank you, Martin." He ran a hand down his face and unbuckled his seatbelt with a yawn. "I'll see you tomorrow, then."

Tomorrow? Of course Jon planned to work tomorrow.

"Do you mind if I walk you inside?" Martin was already getting out of the car along with the archivist. Jon's sigh seemed accepting enough.

So Martin followed behind him, helping to unlock the door when Jon's hands proved too uncoordinated. The door opened to a staircase in front and an archway to a living room on the right. Jon went for the living room, and Martin followed once again as the sick man sat down and buried his head in his hands.

"You're probably thirsty, I'll get you some water. Where are your-?"

"I'm sorry about all of this, Martin." He said, defeated. "You've done so much already, you don't- you don't  need to stay."

Martin frowned. "Jon, I- if you want me to go, I'll go, that's fine. But I think you could use a little extra help before I do."

Jon chuckled. "Alright. Just need a moment..."

"Sure."

Jon sighed, rubbing at his face, and then his stomach with a slight grimace.

"Bit sore?"

"Yeah," he admitted.

Martin felt a bit guilty for liking this version of Jon. Too tired to be stubborn and refuse help he clearly needed or- wanted.

Everything that Jon pretended to be crumbled the minute he was in a vulnerable position. His layer of irritability melted away to reveal the soft center of who he truly was.

So Martin's heart warmed a bit when he returned with a glass of water and Jon was in the middle of wrapping himself in the sofa throw. Cute.

"Before you drink that, it might be a good idea to- well, where's your thermometer?"

It took Jon a while to answer, either because his fevered mind had to process the question, or because he had to remember whether he owned a thermometer.

"Uh... bathroom, bottom drawer." With an added, "probably."

"Right."

He retrieved the thermometer in record time and gave it to Jon, who dutifully slipped it under his tongue.

The reading wasn't great, but it could've been worse!

Jon just sighed irritably and tossed it aside before drinking the water.

Martin stood awkwardly in Jon's living room for a few seconds before he finally decided to speak up.

"Are you going to be alright?" Martin asked. "You know, here, alone?"

"Of course," Jon muttered.

"You're going to take care of yourself?"

"I'm going to take care of myself."

"Promise?"

Jon opened his mouth, voice dying with a regrettable expression. He hesitated for too long for Martin's comfort.

Martin cleared his throat. "Would it be alright if I stopped by after work to check on you?"

Jon didn't meet his eyes as he nodded. "That would be fine."
