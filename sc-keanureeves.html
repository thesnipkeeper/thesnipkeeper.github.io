Title: Keanu Reeves
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick refuses to admit that he has a cold, David and Alexis think it's adorable
Word count: 1.8k

Keanu Reeves

“Are you sure that’s okay with Alexis?”

David quickly shook his head. “Alexis has plans tonight. And my parents are off on some stupid overnight spa trip, so we don’t have to worry about them making everything terrible.” 

Patrick chuckled, and then his face morphed into something indecipherable that had David worried that he’d somehow said something horribly wrong.

Patrick sneezed. Oh, yeah, he should have expected that.

“Bless you. Jesus, that’s like the third one today. Do you usually sneeze this much?”

“Allergies, I think,” Patrick said as he wiped his nose with his napkin. “My nose has been bugging me all morning.”

“Bless you, Patrick!” Twyla called from the other side of the cafe.

He laughed. “Thanks! snf! Anyway, I don’t mind coming over tonight to watch a movie if you and Alexis don’t. I really hope my new neighbors quiet down a little bit. I hardly slept last night...”

Patrick’s fucking neighbors had been moving furniture, talking loudly, playing terrible music, for days now. David refused to spend time there while all of that was going on, and they were both only thankful that Patrick lived next to them and not under them.

“Mmm, I thought you looked tired.”

“Thanks, David.”

“You know what I mean! And we have some stuff at the store that will make you look more alive.”

“Thanks, David.” Patrick made a point to sound exasperated, but he still chuckled a bit as he ran his sleeve under his nose.

David bit his lip. “So um, since when have you had allergies?”

“I dunno, it happens, you know?”

“Mm, sure.”

Whatever Patrick is allergic to must have been getting worse, because he proceeded to sneeze consistently throughout the work day. David tried not to comment, Patrick obviously didn’t want anyone to acknowledge it, but he really hoped that this was allergies.

They had a date tonight, after all.

When closing time came, David kissed him on the cheek instead of his lips. If he was getting sick, David decided he didn’t feel like tempting fate. 

“See you in an hour?”

Patrick nodded before swiping his sleeve under his nose. He smiled so sweetly that for a moment David forgot what they were talking about. “I’ll see you then.”

-----

David didn’t mind the sniffling in his ear as much as he thought he would, but he found himself being jostled as his fiancé let out another strong sneeze. He struggled to reposition himself again Patrick.

“Bless you.”

“David.”

“Mm?”

“It’s just allergies.”

“You’ve said that already.”

They’d been at it for a while. Patrick was clearly getting sick, why was he still denying it? 

Patrick sniffled, and sniffled again. David could hear the wet gunk in his sinuses that threatened to drip out, and he wasted no time reaching to grab a tissue from his nightstand and handing it to his fiance.

“Thanks,” Patrick breathed out before blowing his nose.

“Eugh, are allergies always so gross?”

Patrick gave a snuffly sigh and held David closer. David couldn’t even try to resist, he loved Patrick’s snuggly moods, whether he was lying about not being sick or not. 

Things were nice. The movie was good. They were comfortable.

Of course that was when Alexis had to walk through the door.

“Oh,” She said, pulling her bag off of her shoulder and going to throw it on her bed. “Well this is awkward.”

David growled and sat up so he could stare at his sister as intensely as possible. “I thought you had plans.”

“Um, I did, but Twyla couldn’t make it,” She sat on the edge of her bed and removed her shoes. “Something to do with her mom’s boyfriend stealing all of her stuff in the middle of the night. I dunno. So what’s up with you two?”

“Oh, we’re watching Point Break.”

“Swayze and Keanu? Yum.” 

Patrick and David made agreeing noises and then pretended they didn’t.

And then Patrick sneezed again. This time he’d managed to cover it with his hand, but David could tell by the look on his face that he didn’t plan on removing his hand any time soon. 

“Bless you, Patrick!” Alexis called from the bathroom as she was taking off her makeup.

“Okay, I’m going to get you a tissue so you can deal with whatever snotty situation is happening right now.”

Patrick rolled his eyes, but nodded gratefully when David pressed a wad of tissues into his hand. He turned away to spare David the sight of his mucus, for which David would be eternally grateful.

“Honey,” David said, stroking Patrick’s cheek as he finished wiping his nose. “Are you sure-”

And Patrick sneezed again, just managing to turn away in time. It didn’t stop David from yelping and flinching. 

“Jeez,” Alexis said. “That’s one hell of a cold.” She emerged from the bathrooms in pajamas and a face mask and climbed into bed to read a magazine that was probably stolen from their parents’ room.

Patrick shook his head. “Guys, I’m not sick. It’s just allergies.”

“Allergic to what, though?” David thought it was a pretty reasonable question.

“I don’t know, David. It’s spring, so maybe pollen?”

“Mmm.” David slid back down and leaned against him. “Whatever you say.”

The three of them moved their attention to the movie. David had missed enough that he had no idea what was happening, but Patrick helped explain enough. He’d definitely seen it like five times, and David had a feeling he knew why.

“Keanu bought me dinner one time.” Alexis flipped a page. “And like, a really expensive hat.”

David scoffed. “A hat?”

“Mmhmm. It was hideous, but I couldn’t really throw it out, right? Like, he was so happy to- Bless you!”

Patrick shook the entire bed with another sneeze. He sniffled. The sniffling had become constant at this point. David was sick of hearing the snot in his fiance’s sinuses.

“OhmyGod, Patrick. Just admit that you’ve caught a cold, and you can have the entire tissue box.”

“I don’t have a cold, David. snf! It’s probably just the weather or something.” 

“Except it’s not the weather, because you’re sick. And probably getting your gross germs allover my bed.”

“David, I-” Patrick scrubbed his nose hard, probably trying to avoid another terrible sneeze. 

David rolled his eyes and handed him the box of tissues. Just in time, too, it seemed because Patrick was immediately thrown into an unfortunate sneezing fit.

David and Alexis winced.

“So you’re not sick, then?” Alexis asked innocently. 

“No, I’m not si- hhh-” Patrick groaned after another sneeze.

“Bless you,” David cooed, rubbing circles on his back as he blew his nose. “Okay, well you have your tissues. Let’s just watch the movie, then.” 

And they did. Alexis and David went back and forth on their interactions with Keanu. 

“He told me my face was nice once, I had no idea what that meant but it lifted my self-esteem for months.”

“That sounds like him.”

Somehow, David stopped being the one being snuggled and ended up with his arms around Patrick. The poor guy was clearly miserable and struggling to get comfortable, but resting against David’s chest seemed to keep him from moving around too much as they finished the movie. 

When the end credits finally came after a disappointing and rather predictable ending in David’s opinion, Alexis and David could both hear snoring.

Patrick was fast asleep, having slipped down with his head against one of David’s pillows. His mouth was wide open, and liquid sat on the cusp of his nostrils.

David and Alexis exchanged smiles, and he pulled the covers over his sleeping fiance. He leaned down awkwardly to press a kiss to Patrick’s forehead, finding it a little warm.

“I think he’s sick,” Alexis whispered.

-----

David woke to his pillow moving quite a bit, and making noise. He whines until he realized the noise was coughing and the pillow was Patrick. 

“Sorry,” Patrick managed to get out between coughs.

David sat up and rubbed his eyes to get a good look at his fiance. Patrick’s nose was red just from the abuse it had taken the past twenty-four hours.

Patrick punctuated his fit with a thick sniffle that signified just how stuffed up he’d become. 

“Wow,” David muttered. “Allergy season is crazy this year, hm?”

Patrick sighed, which then prompted another horribly wet cough.

“Mm’kay, I’m going to go make you some tea.” 

This was the earliest David had gotten out of bed in ages, but his fiance sounded like he was dying of consumption and David could hear the mucus rattling in his chest. Patrick also looked thoroughly miserable.

David suddenly felt the need to do everything to keep him comfortable and get him feeling better again. Who was he? Since when did David Rose want to take care of people when they were so grossly contagious?

Those were the questions he asked himself as he filled Moira’s tea kettle, and he managed to answer them with “Love”. So ridiculously cheesy, but it was Patrick. Cheese was okay.

“David,” Alexis’ questioning tone of his name had him rolling his eyes as she made her way into the other motel room. “Is Patrick staying here today?” She tried to ask it quietly, but a quick

“No, I’m not!” came from the other room. Then the sound of the bed shifting.

“Stay in bed! I’m making you tea!”

“I don’t have time to stay in bed, David. The store opens in half an hour!” Followed by a very nasty coughing fit.

David sighed, mumbling an “Oh my God...”

Alexis bit back a smile, obviously enjoying whatever conversation was going on. 

“Obviously, I’m going to the store today and you are not.”

“It’s your day off.”

“Not anymore!”

The water was finally done, and David could make the tea so he could give it to his fiance and argue with him face-to-face.

Patrick was in the middle of pulling his button-down on when David gently pushed him back onto the bed and placed the mug of tea in his hand.

Patrick, though reluctant, seemed to be thankful for something hot to drink.

“Thanks,” he said with a sniffle. God, his voice sounded so thick and stuffed up. “But David, I can’t let you-”

“Um, you can. I would hope that you’d do the same for me if I was super germ-ridden on your day off. Yeah, I don’t think we want the customers to think that this,” he gestured at Patrick. “Is what we’re selling. Germs, I’m talking about germs.”

Patrick smiled just a bit. “Understood.”

“So you can stay here, or I can drive you to your apartment if you don’t think your neighbors are going to be performing an entire musical today.”

Patrick chuckled and shook his head. “Naw, I think they work during the day.” He sniffed. “Thanks, David.”

David smiled and leaned down to kiss his forehead. Patrick made a happy noise in return. “Drink your tea.”
