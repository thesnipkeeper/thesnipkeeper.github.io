Summary: Dirk's illness brings up some past issues and Todd helps
Word count: 1.2k

Reminder of Safety

Todd sat outside the bathroom door and listened to his friend throw up. It was exactly what he'd been doing for the last twenty minutes as Dirk wouldn't come out, or let him in.

Farah walked in, and paused what she was doing to register that Todd was on the floor. 

"Is he still throwing up?"

Todd nodded tiredly.

Farah frowned. "Was it something he ate?"

"I don't know, Farah, he won't even talk to me."

She raised her eyebrows. "Have you considered giving him some privacy?"

Todd felt incredibly offended at the suggestion, considering the other man had never given him privacy a day in his life. Before he could say something snarky, though, they heard a new sound from the other side of the door.

Soft hitching breaths and sniffs.

Dirk was crying.

They both paused.

Farah gave him a panicked look that said ‘Do something, idiot’. So Todd sighed heavily and tried the exact same thing he’d been trying since their friend locked himself in there.

“Dirk…” he started carefully. “Are you okay?” What a stupid question, but he wasn’t sure he’d get anywhere being more straightforward.

Dirk sobbed harder this time. “Go away, Todd. Please go away.”

Todd let his head fall back against the door with a thump. “It’s no use.”

Farah huffed. “Dirk, we just want to help.”

More crying, but this wasn’t just the sniffling they’d been hearing. These were near-hysterical, breathless sobs that ended up with Dirk heaving into the toilet once again.

Todd had seen this before. In fact, they both have. It never happened when Dirk was injured, or tired - though he was a different kind of difficult when those moments occurred - no, it happened when he was truly and properly sick. Todd had his guesses as to why. Something about the horrible childhood trauma and being treated like a lab rat for years, he was sure.

Farah’s jaw clenched the way it did when she wanted to solve a problem and didn’t know where to begin. Todd made a decision.

He opened the door and let himself in.

Dirk looked impossibly small when he was curled up on the floor. His face was red from illness and crying, he was shaking, and he had a shiny streak of saliva down his chin that wasn’t helped by how hard he was crying.

“Dirk, Dirk!”

He was hyperventilating now and Todd had the sudden fear that he’d witness his friend passing out and it would be his fault for barging in in the first place.

Against his better judgment, he dropped to the ground next to him and wrapped himself around Dirk. The dry warmth of a fever soaked through the fabric of his clothes, shaking from a combination of chills and whatever else was going on with him at that moment.

“Dirk, you’re okay,” he said, keeping his voice calm. After all they’d been through, this couldn’t freak him out more than any other state he’d seen Dirk in.

He relaxed in Todd’s arms.

Limp and exhausted and still shaking like a leaf. Quiet sobs into Todd’s shoulder, clinging to him like a lifeline.

Farah knelt down next to them. She wore a deep frown, saying nothing but exchanging a look with Todd once again.

They all stayed like that while Dirk’s crying tapered into sniffles and sighs.

Todd kept his voice quiet as he asked what they both needed to know. “What’s going on?”

Dirk sniffed and sat up, pushing off of Todd completely. He didn’t make eye contact with them, barely looked in their direction at all as he created space between them once against. He rubbed his eyes, shook his head.

“I want to go home.”

“Well, okay…” Todd nodded. “We can- we can do that.”

-----

Dirk barely made it through the door before he was rushing to the bathroom to vomit again. Todd swore and tossed his keys on the table.

Dirk was properly sick, then, and this wasn't going away anytime soon. He'd hoped his friend had already been through the worst of it.

Todd grabbed a bucket from under the kitchen sink and took it to Dirk's bedroom. He gathered various other things in a lame attempt to help, like a cup of water and some fever reducer when he could keep it down. He searched the apartment for a thermometer for a good while and came to the conclusion that they did not own one.

He made sure everything would be within reach when Dirk inevitably became bedridden for a few days. Throwing up always took a lot out of him, as did fevers. He just usually… worked through them. But he didn't have to anymore. For once, Dirk could rest.

Dirk wasn't in there for very long, yet he looked ten times worse when he emerged.

"Hey," Todd called. "I kind of set up your room. You should get some rest." 

Dirk's brows furrowed for a moment as his brain slowly processed what was being said. He took a few steps aside to peek into his bedroom, and then an indecipherable-but-not-good expression came over his face.

Tears began to form in his eyes.

"Shit," was Todd's first reaction. "What's wrong?" The fever and the fact that he'd done nothing but throw up all day was enough for him to cry, really, but something was triggering these sudden bouts.

"Don't make me go to bed," Dirk said, sniffling.

Todd couldn't help but laugh despite his confusion. "I'm… I'm not going to make you do anything. I just figured you'd be ready to sleep for the rest of the day."

He nodded. "I really am."

"But you don't want to go to bed?"

Dirk shook his head. "I can't bear the idea of being ill and cooped up and- and quarantined and I know that I should be but I can't, Todd, I can't."

"Quarantined," Todd repeated in realization. "Oh Jesus, Dirk..."

Dirk sobbed again. This wasn't like him at all, but then again he was never like himself when the institute was brought up.

Todd wrapped him in a hug.

Dirk melted into it.

"I'll move everything to the couch, okay?" he said, voice muffled against Dirk's shoulder.

Dirk nodded.

"Just go lie down. I'll grab you something to drink so you don't get dehydrated."

"That would be awful," he agreed.

-----

“You know, I’ve never expected to have a relationship like this, let alone two. Friendships come easy, of course, because people seem to adore my accent and my bizarre charm, but you and Farah… could be the first family I’ve had in a long time.” Dirk looked comfortable, wrapped in blankets on the couch, despite the million drinks next to him and the bucket nearby.

Todd frowned. “Does that have anything to do with how weird you are when you’re sick?”

“Yes, of course it does! I was always locked in my room at Blackwing, but at least they let me out for tests, or for sessions, but then I somehow caught something - which didn’t happen very often, of course - and I was quarantined. And then days or- or weeks of nothing and faceless people bringing my meals in protective gear and…” he ended the long ramble with a frustrated sigh, as if his traumatic childhood was something like an inconvenience.

“Dirk.”

“Hm?”

“I’m sorry. That you went through all that, I mean.”

He hummed.

“You’re safe here.”

“I know that,” he smiled. “In fact, I’ve never felt safer in my life.”

Then it was quiet, a comfortable silence filling the space between them.

Until Dirk’s stomach gurgled.

He groaned and brought a hand to his abdomen, brows furrowing. “If you’ll excuse me, though, I’m going to go vomit, like, a lot.”
