Personal Space

Caleb and his roommate were very different people.

It wasn't that they didn't get along, they were both very civil to each other and hadn't run into any large problems. But they also... Didn't have anything in common

At all.

He and Caleb had had very few real conversations since the school year began.

Mollymauk was an art major, for starters. Caleb couldn't even fathom what that meant, in all honesty. His own friend Jester also happened to major in art and she was always drawing or painting strange things for strange assignments.

Arcane practices, now that was something that Caleb could understand. The mix of math and magic was what he loved about it. The more you understood, the more you could alter and create your own magic.

Mollymauk’s side of the room looked like a war zone. A war thought was fought with a million different cultural objects. His clothes overflowed out of his closet and often made their way to Caleb’s own pristine side of the room. He didn’t pay it much mind. If Mollymauk was happy with it, Caleb was fine with it as well.

So, they got along fine.

Until Caleb came back from class one day, and saw that his desk had been rifled through. Beau liked to joke that he’d be able to notice if a single paperclip was moved, and she wasn’t wrong, but this wasn’t a single paperclip. He wasn’t overreacting this time.

His breath was shortening. His heart was beating faster.

Caleb turned to his roommate, who was lying on his bed with homework and seemed to be quite content with himself.

"You moved my things."

Mollymauk glanced up, a piece of purple hair falling in his face. "Oh, yes. I just needed to borrow a pen,” he said, lifting said pen in the air.

"So you came into my space and moved my entire desk around?" Caleb asked, agitated. There was a voice in the back of his head reminding him to breathe, but it wasn’t quite loud enough today.

Mollymauk’s expression changed. He slowly sat up without taking his eyes off of Caleb. "Alright, I'm now realizing I should have probably asked you first, but-"

"Yes, Mollymauk! You should have asked!" Caleb snapped, before going to rapidly rearranging the desk. Molly had hardly touched it, Caleb knew that in the back of his mind, but the mere thought that he'd come on his side of the room, his one personal space in the entire fucking campus, and messed with his things at all made an electric surge of anxiety in his chest, his stomach, his entire body. What if this wasn’t the first time? What if Mollymauk had been doing this all along? The mere thought of his things being rifled through more than once had Caleb’s heart beating even faster.

Caleb shook his head of the thought as he put a pencil back in its rightful place.

When Caleb looked up, he realized that Mollymauk was still. This wasn't how he’d known Molly to act. Quiet? He was wringing his hands and watching with a look of... Guilt? Since when did Mollymauk feel guilt for anything? He lived shamelessly, that was part of who he was.

"I'm so sorry, Caleb," Molly's voice was quiet, almost too quiet. He stood up and went to reach out to Caleb, but seemed to think better of it. "I honestly wasn't thinking."

Caleb shook his head and let out a calming breath. It wasn’t calming enough, but he supposed that it wouldn’t be. "I probably should not have ah... snapped at you. But please don't do that again."

"I won't," Molly quickly promised. “Let me make it up to you,” he held out the pen, and Caleb wordlessly took it. Alright, having it back made him feel a little better. “I’ll- I’ll buy you the book of your choice.”

Caleb stopped. He was still trying to get his stuff back in the order that it was in before Mollymauk messed with it, but the offer of a free book... well, he couldn’t just dismiss that, could he?

“Are you being serious right now?” He couldn’t always tell. Especially with someone who was getting through school by bullshitting.

“Oh, absolutely.”

Once glance at the tiefling’s face told Caleb that he was indeed serious. He chewed the inside of his mouth for a moment, heaving a sigh through his nose.

“I can’t let you do that,” no matter how much it hurt to refuse an offer like that, this was only happening because he snapped at Molly. It wasn’t right.

“Alright, well...” Molly sighed. “I’ll find a way to make it up to you, and I will start by not touching your things.”

Caleb nodded, and went back to reorganizing.

-----

Things between them the next morning were more awkward than they’d ever been. And Mollymauk wasn’t an ‘awkward’ type of person. Caleb made sure to leave the dorm before his roommate was awake to avoid any uncomfortable interaction. He wasn’t good at human interaction on a good day let alone when things were... weird.

He found himself telling his friends the story, as casually as ever, while he ate his lunch.

“He touched your things?” Nott fumed. She threw her fork on her plate with a fire in her eyes. “Want me to fight him?!”

Caleb chuckled a bit at his small friend. “I don’t think that’s necessary, but thank you.”

“God, I knew that guy was an asshole,” Beau huffed.

“He’s not that bad!” Jester defended. “Caleb said he apologized.”

Caleb nodded in agreement. “Ja, it’s all dealt with.”

“Yeah? ‘cause I haven’t seen you two around each other since,” Beau said with a raised eyebrow.

Caleb gave a heavy sigh, because that was true. “Things have been different since it happened.”

“Then it’s not dealt with.”

That was also true. He didn’t say anything after that, lacking anything substantial to really add. So he let the others continue with their own conversations while he quietly ate his meal.

As he let his mind wander, Caleb realized he hadn’t seen Molly since that morning. Sure, they had different classes and schedules, but he would have expected to see him around campus at some point.

“Has anyone seen Mollymauk?” He asked, and then quickly closed his mouth when he saw that he’d interrupted an unrelated conversation.

Jester frowned. “Actually... I don’t remember seeing him in class this morning. Did he say anything?”

“We-”

“Oh, right, you’re not talking.”

Caleb sighed and nodded.

“Maybe he’s just having a skip day,” Beau shrugged. “Everyone does it sometimes.”

“Except for Caleb,” Jester said with a giggle.

“Ja, well not everyone can be as adamant about education as me,” Caleb deadpanned before returning to his meal.

Perhaps he could go back to the dorm room. He had a couple of hours that he was going to waste in the library, but he wasn’t going to be able to forget what Jester said about Molly not being in class. He was curious now, he had to see for himself.

Caleb was just about to open the door to his and Mollymauk’s room when he heard a sound from inside. A cough. It didn’t sound like the type that came from swallowing the wrong way, but the sort of cough that came from illness.

It made sense that he’d been ill. That was a good excuse to not have gone to class. Caleb hadn’t even seen him awake that morning, perhaps he’d been sick all day.

His hand hovered over the doorknob for a moment. Should he let him rest? He asked Molly to respect his space, perhaps this would be a good way to return the favor.

Caleb, once again, let his curiosity get the best of him. He entered and closed the door behind him before he decided to look up. He was exceptionally good at avoiding eye contact with people, after all. When he did finally look towards Molly’s side of the room, he saw the tiefling sitting in bed, wrapped in a sweatshirt that Caleb was quite certain didn’t belong to him. It didn’t belong to Caleb either, though, so he didn’t bother wondering.

“Hello,” he said as casually as he possibly could. He quickly started rummaging through his bookshelf as if looking for something.

Mollymauk sniffled rather congestedly, which just confirmed Caleb’s assumption that he was sick. “How was class?”

“It was fine.” He fidgeted with a book about the practical applications of magic in urban areas, an interesting one that he’d already read through twice. “Are you not going for lunch?”

Molly chuckled and shook his head, unruly purple curls shaking as well. “As it happens, I’m not feeling so well today.” He sighed dramatically, or attempted to, but it seemed to just make him cough. He winced, hand going to his throat. “Perhaps I should find somewhere else to sleep. I’d hate for you to get sick.”

Caleb sat at his desk and began fiddling with the tip of a pen. “Germs don’t scare me.”

“Are you sure?” He asked, voice going up at the end with his accent. “It could be the flu. I was with Yasha all of last week when she was laid up with it.”

Oh, right. Caleb remembered hearing Beau mention that Molly’s friend was out sick. Very clearly, as that was one of the few times he’d heard pure concern in her voice. He wasn’t exactly sure how Mollymauk and Yasha knew each other, but they seemed to have a nice friendship.

“I am sure,” he affirmed. “There is a big list of things that bother me, and germs are very low on that list.” He offered a small smile, or tried to. Nott once told him that he wasn’t always actually smiling when he thought he was.

A corner of Molly’s mouth turned up in a sideways smile.

Caleb cleared his throat. “I should be getting to the library...” With the book that he now had to take with him. “Are you... good?”

“I’m fantastic!” Those words would have been a little more believable had Molly’s voice not died out in the end.

-----

Beau had wanted to study together, and Jester thought the group of them should go for a walk (“Just for something new, you guys!”) Caleb didn’t get back to the room until the time he usually went to bed. Which... wasn’t very late at all, if he was being honest. Molly was the night owl, though how a person could be both a night owl and a morning person was beyond him.

However, the room was dark when he entered. Caleb tried to step in as quietly as possible, only to trip on some clothing on the floor. Mollymauk’s clothing, of course. He caught himself before he fell, but it seemed the damage was done. Mollymauk stirred awake.

“Caleb?” His voice sounded far worse than it had before. Molly barely had a voice left.

“Sorry,” Caleb winced. “I was trying to be quiet.”

Molly gave a small cough and reached across his bed to turn on the light. He squinted, yawned. “ ‘s alright. I needed to wake up, anyway. Time is it?”

“Ten,” Caleb answered without having to glance at the clock.

Molly groaned. “I slept all day...”

“Probably a good thing?”

“Perhaps, if I’m able to sleep tonight at all. Fuck, I should’ve set an alarm.” He curled up in bed and muffled another groan into his pillow. After a few seconds, Mollymauk went to sit up, only to quickly close his eyes and lower himself back down.

“Dizzy?”

“Just a little lightheaded,” he said, eyes still closed.

“Have you eaten anything today?” It was a casual question. Caleb had a lot of experience with forgetting to eat.

“I’m not hungry. At all. In fact, vomit may be in my near future.”

Caleb frowned. He really was sick.

Caleb was still getting ready for bed when he heard an unfortunate cough coming from his roommate, followed by a pained groan as the cough must have torn at his throat. Poor Mollymauk, he found himself thinking.

-----

Caleb woke in the middle of the night to the sound of hushed whimpers. The sound was unfortunately recognizable as his roommate. For a moment, he wondered if he should just go back to sleep. Perhaps he shouldn’t be bothering Mollymauk. Some people just wanted space, right?

But, as he thought further, he never imagined Mollymauk being one of those people, and the crying seemed to get worse the longer Caleb waited to act.

“M-Mollymauk?” he said quietly into the dark.

The whimpering stopped. There were a few sniffles, and a shift in the other bed. “Sorry,” Molly said, breathless and broken and far different than he’d ever sounded like. “Sorry, Caleb.”

“Are you alright?”

“I-” a sob. “I don’t- I don’t feel well.” His voice broke at the end like he hadn’t used it in a while.

Before Caleb even realized what he was doing, he sat up and swung his legs over the edge of his bed. He was awake now, anyway, and he probably wouldn’t be able to go back to sleep knowing that his roommate was so miserable.

“Anything I can do?”

A teary chuckle. “Oh, don’t worry about me.”

“A bit difficult, now,” Caleb said in a teasing tone, or as much of a teasing tone as he himself could muster. He heard another laugh from Mollymauk, which made him somewhat optimistic that could help. “I have some medicine in my desk, I usually take it for headaches but it should help your fever.”

A few seconds of silence before a quiet, “That might be nice...”

Caleb padded his way to his desk, turned on the small lamp, and found the small bottle. He grabbed the water bottle on Mollymauk’s own desk and delivered them both to him.

Now, in the dim light Caleb could clearly see tear tracks down the tiefling’s face. His cheeks were bright red with fever, his nose pink from abuse.

Molly nodded his thanks and swallowed the pills, finishing his sip of water with a cough.

“Would you like me to stay up with you until the medicine kicks in?”

Molly smiled and laughed. “Honestly, I wouldn’t stop you if you did,” he said with a shudder.

Caleb nodded, rolling his desk chair closer to Molly’s bed and sitting with his folded hands in his lap.

They chatted for the next half hour or so until Molly stopped shivering.

-----

There was more than Mollymauk in the room when Caleb returned after class. He quickly identified the other person as Yasha, the quiet woman Molly was so close with. He wondered if they were dating when he first got to know them, but as he watched them interact, he learned that wasn’t the case.

They were in bed together, clearly a tight fit as Yasha wasn’t a small person, but Mollymauk looked content, and sound asleep.

“Ah, hello,” he said, nodding a greeting toward her.

She nodded back. “I hope it is alright that I’m here. I just wanted to keep Molly company.”

“You’re always welcome,” he told her before he even thought about it.

She gave a small smile. “Thank you for taking care of him.”

“Oh, I didn’t do that much,” Caleb said as he set his things down in their rightful places.

“You did, though,” she insisted. “Thank you.”

Lacking anything else to say, Caleb just smiled and nodded.

-----

Mollymauk missed classes for another day before he finally braved going out. It was clear that he wasn’t feeling amazing quite yet, but one could only take so many days off before they started feeling useless.

Caleb found himself getting back to the room before Mollymauk. As much as he liked his roommate, it was nice to have the space to himself. He sat at his desk with a content sigh, only to find something new in front of him.

Something gift wrapped?

There was a folded piece of paper on top, and he read it immediately.

Caleb,

Thank you for looking after me, and please accept this and a thousand apologies for upsetting you the other day.

Love, Molly

Caleb raised an eyebrow and inspected the package before finally unwrapping it. A book, a brand new book.

Spell Creation Through the Ages

Caleb could cry of happiness. He’d been wanting this exact book since he heard of its release, but he has so little disposable income these days to spend on brand new books.

He wasted no time opening it and starting the first chapter. Only a few minutes in, his phone buzzed.

Nott: did he give it to you?

Caleb: He did. Are you responsible for this?

Nott: all i did was give him advice when he asked for gift ideas!

Caleb smiled, found himself chuckling. This was certainly going to be an interesting semester.
