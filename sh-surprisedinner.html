Title: Surprise Dinner
Fandom: Shadowhunters
Summary: Alec is sick, Izzy is exasperated, and Magnus buys takeout
Word count: 2.8k

Surprise Dinner

It’s around the time that Alec is wearing two long-sleeved shirts and a jacket that he starts to realize it’s not the AC that’s making him feel like he’s freezing to death.

He shivers violently and zips his jacket all the way. It probably won’t do anything, but he’s desperate for some relief from the cold.

He’s been cold since late that morning. Is he getting sick? He felt fine when he woke up, if. not still a little tired, but it wasn’t a surprise considering how hard he’s been working.

Alec isn’t aware that his sister is in his office until she’s right up to the desk. She’s thankfully closed the door behind her. He’d hate for anyone to see him like this.

“Hey, Alec- whoa, what happened to you?”

Alec shakes his head. “I don’t know,” he says. “I’m freezing for no reason.”

“Are you sick?”

“Maybe,” he answers honestly. “Do I feel warm to you?”

Izzy bites her lip and presses the inside of her wrist to his forehead. “Yeah...” she says, like she feels bad being the one to break the bad news. “Yeah, you feel a little feverish. Why don’t you call Magnus and get an early portal home?” She props herself against his desk.

“I’d love to, but I have so much to get done.”

Izzy makes a sympathetic noise. She knows she can’t make his work go away, she understands it needs to be done. “Aw, sorry...is there anything I can help with?”

Alec’s sigh catches in his throat and he coughs instead. “Not really. I’m just having trouble concentrating.” He folds his arms in another attempt to retain his body heat. Although, if he has a fever, the last thing he should probably be doing is trying to retain his body heat. “What um, what was it that you needed?”

“Well... I was going to ask you to help me fill this out,” she holds a paper in front of him. It’s one of the newly-implemented mission sheets from the Clave. “But I think I can figure it out. Sorry.” She pats his shoulder before she leaves, again thankfully shutting the door behind her.

Alec muffles a cough into the collar of his coat and thinks about how nice it would be to be at Magnus’ home right now. Or even in his own room. He just wants to sleep, really.

But! He has work to do. And he can get it done. He’s done field work while he was sicker than this.

The memory of puking in an alley while Jace runs off to chase a demon is fresh in his mind. Jace didn’t appreciate being abandoned at the last second until he touched Alec’s arm and realized he had a fever.

Alec cringes at himself. Jace had been so soft when he felt Alec’s forehead. It was one of many things that made Alec stupidly-

Ugh. It’s in the past. The stupid fever is making him feel bad in every way possible, apparently.

Alec tries to fill out his paperwork with very minimal success. At the rate he’s going, he’ll have to stay late until the stack of papers is gone.

Izzy walks in a bit later. “Hey.”

As Alec looks up, he realizes that his eyes are watery, both from illness and being so focused on the work that’s hardly getting done. He blinks dazedly.

“Hey,” he says, dragging a tired hand down his face.

“You doing alright?”

No, no he isn’t. He’s still cold and there’s a constant tickle in the back of his throat that won’t be satiated no matter what.

“I...” Alec gives a raspy sigh. “I’ve been better.” He rests his chin on his elbow and yawns. “I think I need some coffee, actually.”

Alec goes to stand up and promptly sways.

“Shit-!” Izzy rushes to him and clasps onto his arm to help him regain his balance. “oh, Alec...”

He knew that he was a little weird from the fever, but he hadn’t realized that he was dizzy...

“Mmm,” he rubs his eyes. “Sorry. Got lightheaded.”

“Yeah, well I think you need to take a couple layers off before your insides start to boil.”

Alec wants to argue, but he knows that she’s right. Shadowhunters may have missed out on a lot of things growing up, but they did learn pretty extensive first-aid from a young age.

Meaning, he knows he’s doing things wrong. Or he should have. He must not be thinking clearly.

Alec gives a resigned nod, struggles with the zipper of his coat, and eventually lets Izzy do it. She lays her hand on his forehead once again, this time having to reach up to get to it, and sighs heavily.

“By the Angel, you’re burning up...” she mutters. “Where are you going?”

“Coffee,” Alec murmurs as he makes his way to the door, hanging onto the couch halfway there for balance.

Izzy, healthy as ever, is capable of running in front of him before he can reach it. “That can’t possibly be good for you right now.”

He nods admittedly. “I just need something to get me through the rest of the work.”

“Okay okay, then I’ll get it for you, but sit back down before you pass out.” She steers him back toward his desk.

“Thanks, Iz.”

Izzy sighs. “If anyone asks, I had no part to play in whatever coffee does to you. I’ll be back to help you, okay?”

“I already said-”

“I know what you said.” the door closes behind her.

Izzy follows through with her promise of caffeine. 

Alec drinks it. The whole thing.

He doesn’t feel much more aware, in fact he feels a headache coming on and his heart is doing annoying things. He doesn’t mention it to Izzy to save himself from the I-Told-You-So. 

Izzy pulls up a chair and does indeed start to help with the paperwork, or at least the stuff she’s authorized to do.

“Do you want something for the fever?” Izzy asks. He realizes she’s been watching him try to work for a while now. “You’re still shaky.”

Part of that shaking is the fever, but he knows the caffeine is to blame as well. He’s still so cold.

He continues to work with the headache and shaky hands and shivers, and sighs. Exhaustion is fogging his brain. Even with Izzy’s help the important papers won’t get done by tomorrow if he goes home now.

Alec coughs hard into a fist. He can feel it come directly from his chest.

“Yikes, Alec.”

“It sounds worse than it is.”

“Does it?”

Alec sighs, coughs. “No,” he admits. “I feel awful.”

“Hey, if you still want to call Magnus…”

“I might do that, actually.”

“Really?”

“Yeah, I’ve got to let him know I’m working late tonight in case he has plans.”

Izzy frowns. She sets the stuff she’s been working on down and leans forward until he’s forced to look her in the eyes.

“You know I’m not going to try to talk you out of working.” She knows how stressed he gets if he doesn’t have everything done when it’s supposed to be. The Clave is expecting these files tomorrow. “But I hate to see you work yourself sick. You know I can take care of all of this on my own.”

He can’t help but smile. “Thanks, Iz, but you don’t know how-”

“Excuse me, I watched mom do this kind of stuff our entire life. Of course I know how to do it.”

Alec chuckles. “I can’t let you stay here all night.”

“Okay,” Izzy says, biting her lip. “But if you change your mind, you know I will.”

Alec takes his phone and goes to stand. He grabs onto the desk for support waving Izzy off when she goes to help him, and dials Magnus’ number. He paces dizzily by the bookcases as he listens to it ring.

“Did you miss me already?”

“Magnus,” Alec can’t believe how much just hearing the other man’s voice improves his mood. He suddenly feels much better. “Hey, I um-”

“Are you alright, Alexander? You sound weird.”

“Yeah, I’m f-” Izzy shoots him a look from across the room as if she knows exactly what he’s going to say. “Actually, I think I’m getting a cold or something. It’s nothing, really.”

“I’m sorry to hear that. I’ll have some soup ready when you get here, then.”

Fuuuuck. “That’s actually why I called.” He sighs. “I have to stay late today. I put off a lot of paperwork that’s due tomorrow and now I’m paying for it.” Alec suddenly realizes how badly he wants to be with Magnus right this second, but he knows he can’t. 

“Ah.” The disappointment is clear in Magnus’ voice, but he brightens it up as quickly as he can. “Well, take care of yourself, okay? I know how hard you work.”

Alec can’t help but grin. How did he get so lucky?

-----

Magnus says goodbye to his boyfriend and ends the call with a sigh. He was going to take Alec to an entirely different continent for dinner tonight, something special since he’s been working so hard. He knows field work can take a lot out of any shadowhunter. It isn’t really something in Alec’s job description anymore.

But, he supposes, that will have to wait. His boyfriend is doing important things in this world, even if it’s paperwork.

So he decides to do the next best thing. Instead of taking Alec to India, he can bring India to Alec. In the most mundane way possible.

Magnus Bane buys indian takeout.

He totes it proudly into the institute, walking to his beloved’s office with purpose. Alec’s not going to remember to eat dinner if he’s busy. It’s a sad fact of life. And if Alec is working late and catching a cold, he needs to eat something.

The door is closed, so Magnus feels the need to knock in case he’s in the middle of some important shadowhunter business. He certainly doesn’t expect Isabelle to answer the door.

“Is Alec home?” he asks with a smile.

Izzy scoffs, and her eyes catch the bag of takeout held at his side. She smiles. “I’m gonna go get some coffee and- you know, check on-”

A hacking cough from the room gets both of their attention. Magnus frowns immediately. He’s about to ask about it before Izzy steps past him and leaves.

Magnus gasps the moment that he walks into the room. “Alexander…”

Alec’s shoulders are wrapped in the throw blanket that usually sits folded on his office’s couch for the nights he works so hard he’s too exhausted to leave. His cheeks glow bright red in contrast to his pale skin.

Alec also looks like a deer in headlights.

“Magnus! You’re- you’re here.”

“Mmhmm,” he hums, setting the food on the coffee table and moving to his boyfriend’s desk. “You know, just a few hours ago, I recall the words ‘It’s nothing’. Alexander, this isn’t nothing. You look miserable.” He feels Alec’s forehead and sure enough, it’s hot to the touch, and Alec is leaning into his hand like a needy cat. 

“Sorry. I didn’t want you to worry.”

“You didn’t want me to try to talk you into coming home,” Magnus corrects.

Alec’s embarrassed chuckle says it all, and Magnus can’t help but wince when that chuckle becomes a full-blown coughing fit that leaves him gasping for breath. Magnus magics water into the empty mug on Alec’s desk and presses it to his lips until Alec takes it and sips carefully.

“You sound awful. You shouldn’t be anywhere but bed right now.”

“I know,” Alec admits. “Izzy and I are almost done, though.”

“Can’t she take on duties for the night?”

“No, she- I mean, I guess she offered, but-”

“Well then, there you go,” Magnus gently guides him to the couch, masking both hands on his arm as affection when it’s really because he’s afraid Alec’s going to fall on his face where he stands. “We could go home right now and leave your precious sister to take care of things.”

“No, Magnus, I can’t do that to her,” he rasps. 

He shudders as they sit down, pulling the blanket tighter around himself. Magnus snaps more heat into the thing as casually as ever, but it doesn’t seem to help things. That’s fevers for you, he thinks.

“Izzy’s been working just as hard this week.”

“She’s capable of handling herself, Alexander.” He sets out the food and pushes it toward Alec. Give his boyfriend credit, Alec doesn’t even seem to think of refusing the food. He rubs gentle circles over Alec’s back. “And I think that you’d do the same for her in a heartbeat. Wouldn’t you?”

“Of course I would.”

The conversation quiets as they’re both more focused on their delicious meal. Not nearly as good as India, Magnus decides, but Alec seems to enjoy it. The shadowhunter shivers and coughs through the meal, and Magnus makes sure to pull the water within reach of him. Those breathless fits are scaring him just a bit.

“This is really good,” are the first words to come out of Alec in several minutes. “I never knew you to be the type to get takeout.”

Magnus chuckles. “I thought you would like it.” A thought finally occurs to him as he watches Alec quickly finish his food. “Is this the first time you’ve eaten today?”

Alec freezes. “It’s… It’s been busy here.”

“Not too busy for a cup of coffee, though?” Magnus asks smugly.

It’s a convenient time for Alec to start coughing, and Magnus just shakes his head fondly. This shadowhunter will be the death of his immortal body. He presses a kiss to Alec’s temple as the fit tapers off, and starts cleaning up. Or rather, starts moving his hands until the mess cleans itself up.

“I’m going to go talk to your sister, and then you and I are going to get out of here,” he says softly.

He expects an argument out of Alec, but instead he just nods. The poor thing looks so miserable, whatever he has has taken a lot out of him already. Magnus hopes this is as bad as it gets.

Izzy agrees to take care of everything before Magnus can finish asking the question.

-----

Magnus is quick to catch Alec before he can collapse to the floor with a fit of coughing. Alec hacks breathlessly, unable to get his balance as he tries to stop. Magnus coos sympathetically and takes most of Alec’s weight as he leads him to the couch.

“I should have warned you to hold your breath. It’s not usually a problem with portals…”

Alec shakes his head, tears built in his eyes from the force of coughing. “I’m fine,” he sputters. Eventually, he stops coughing, and he blinks dazedly. “Oh, ‘m dizzy…”

“Well...that’s concerning, considering you’re sitting down.”

Alec nods in agreement. 

Magnus frowns and kisses his cheek. He’s still impossibly warm. “Let’s get you to bed, hm?”

“Y-yeah.”

Alec continues to cough weakly as Magnus helps him get situated. He doesn’t bat an eye when Magnus helps him take off his clothes and get into more comfortable ones. It’s a little adorable, actually, how much he’s letting Magnus take care of him now that they’re home.

“I’m going to get you something for the fever so you can sleep better.”

Alec nods. 

Magnus considers various potions and components, but in the end he realizes that Alec doesn’t need magic to feel better.

Alec gives a surprised smile at the bottle he’s handed. “Tylenol? I was expecting-”

“As was I, but I think you’ll have an easier time taking this than an unfortunate-tasting potion. I’m afraid I shouldn’t give you anything for your cough, though,” he informs sadly. “Whatever you have probably needs to be coughed out.”

Alec winces. “Great.”

“You know,” Magnus begins as he sits on the bed next to him. “There’s nothing wrong with asking people for help. Especially when they’ve already offered twice.”

Alec laughs, and then coughs. “Izzy had something to say, huh?”

“Oh yes.”

“I know… I just- it’s not like no one else is having a hard time. It feels selfish to ask people to help me when I’m not the only one with problems.” Alec turns to look him in the eyes. “I think you can understand that, can’t you?”

Magnus nearly recoils. “Yikes, alright no need to make me feel so targeted. This was about you,” he chuckles, poking Alec’s chest with his index finger. 

Alec laughs again, coughs again. “Ugh, stop making me laugh.” He snuggles against Magnus, probably to sap his body heat more than anything else. “How do you ask for help when everyone else is struggling just as much as you?”

“Well, normally I wouldn’t know how to answer that,” Magnus admits. “But Izzy definitely wasn’t hacking up a lung today. So I don’t think she was suffering quite as much as you.”

“...You have a point.”

Magnus smiles. “We’ll figure it out together, I suppose.”

“Yeah, I guess so.”
