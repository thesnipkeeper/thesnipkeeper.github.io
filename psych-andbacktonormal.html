Title: And Back to Normal
Fandom: Psych
Summary: Shawn is still dealing with everything that happened, but his friends are there for him whether he likes it or not
Word count: 2.1k

And Back to Normal

They had one last night together, and Shawn woke the next morning to wrap his arm around her. He didn’t have to be psychic to know that he was never going to see Abigail again. He swallowed the lump in his throat that appeared as he watched her get ready to leave, as she gave him a sad kiss and walked out of his life for good.

This was what was going to make her happy, even if it tore him apart on the inside.

He went back to bed. He didn’t get out of bed for a couple of days.

Well, that may be an exaggeration. Feeling bad for himself didn’t stop him from getting hungry.

Shawn got up to grab a terrible burrito from the cheap Mexican place down the street, granted he didn’t bother to change out of his sweats or fixed his hair and probably looked a lot like a homeless person.

The person at the counter, a woman named Kris who was the sister-in-law of the owner (and - as far as Shawn’s psychic senses could tell over his many trips there - planned to buy the place herself once she had a chance), handed him a free churro before he left. He wasn’t about to argue, even if he despised the pitying look on her face as she did so.

God, he didn’t need to be treated like a charity case. He was, of course, thinking this while eating a free churro. He wasn’t about to let perfectly good food go to waste.

But seriously! Gus, his dad, even damn Lassiter looked at him differently since the night they went after Mr. Yin. Since the night Shawn nearly lost two of the most important people in his life. (He lost one anyway, but at least Abigail was alive).

He was stepping into his apartment and taking the last bite of churro (where’d the rest go? Damn.) when his phone buzzed. He sighed heavily with a glance at the screen.

“Hey, what’s crackin’, Gus?” He greeted, scrunching the churro wrapper into a ball and failing to throw it into the trash. He’d pick it up later. Probably. (Probably not.)

“You coming back to the Psych office today?”

Shawn set his untouched burrito aside. That churro was filling, huh? He pinched the bridge of his nose. “I uh, I’m kinda busy.”

“Busy doing what, exactly?”

“Oh, you know, stuff... things. Dad needs help with his computer again.” Was it technically lying if his dad probably would need help next time he went over there? Whose idea was it to let that ancient man get a computer in the first place?

“Sure, yeah. But I already talked to your dad today and he said he hasn’t heard from you since... you know.”

Shawn swore at the ceiling. “Yeah. Well-”

“I heard the chief might have a case for us, and honestly? We could use the money.”

Shawn scoffed. “We could always use the money. Who knew running a business was so damn expensive, huh Gus?”

“Pretty sure I’ve told you like a thousand times. Are you coming in today or not.”

He chewed the inside of his mouth for a second and said, “Not. Maybe tomorrow.”

There was a pause on the other end. “Okay, well... are you...?”

“Just one more day of R&R and I’ll be good as new!” He didn’t bother saying goodbye before he hung up. Gus was used to it by now.

It’d been a week since Mr. Yin, and only a few days since Abigail left for good. He felt terrible, and wasn’t he allowed to feel terrible after all that? Wasn’t he allowed to wallow, just a little bit?

Just one more day. That was all he wanted before he had to force himself back to normal again.

He glanced at the burrito and left it. It’d probably go bad if he didn’t eat it or refrigerate it right now, but he couldn’t bring himself to do either and went to bed instead.

-----

As Shawn found it that morning, it was easier to get out of bed. Whether he was better, or the commitment of promising to come in, he was motivated to shower and put real clothes on and (thank God) do his hair. He had to throw that burrito away when he woke up. He was tempted to eat it, but a past experience involving this exact situation told him that was a bad idea for his intestines’ wellbeing.

Gus wouldn’t have been able to hide his relief if he tried when Shawn walked in the next morning. To know that his best friend had been worried about him both annoyed him and made him feel... kinda guilty. Though, he wasn’t sure why he felt guilty for someone else feeling a certain way.

“I thought we could ride to the precinct together,” Gus explained. He was already getting ready to leave in an attempt to look busy when he’d obviously just been waiting to see if Shawn would actually show up.

So, they rode together.

The chief gave Shawn the same look of pity, mixed with sadness as she informed that she gave O’Hara a couple weeks off and she’d be back soon. Good. That was good. Juliet deserved as many days off as she could have. Shawn was the reason she went through what she had in the first place. Well, her association with him, was, anyway.

Jesus, he kind of ruined her life just by being the target of someone’s sick game. Was he supposed to feel guilty about that? Someone had to be blamed, didn’t they?

Lassiter didn’t make eye contact with him. Did he blame Shawn as much as Shawn did? No... no that was a different kind of avoidance. The same kind everyone else had around him lately.

“Thank you for coming, everyone.” Chief Vic folded her hands in front of her as she briefed them on the case. A kidnapping. A teenager girl, the daughter of a divorced CEO, had gone missing.

Something heavy dropped on Shawn’s chest and suddenly he wanted to run. He swallowed and kept his feet planted, instead, and made sure to make a few annoying quips in the meantime.

“Mr. Cartwright owns a good number of hotels and resorts in this area,” she explained.

“Ah, the hotel king!” Also an incredibly greasy businessman.

She nodded. “It’s likely that whoever took Alex is after some kind of ransom payout. I’ve convinced him to let us help for now, but he’s willing to pay as much as he needs to get her back.”

“Which is exactly what they want, right?” said Gus.

“Exactly,” Lassiter nodded, folding his arms. “And once these kidnappers know they’ve got a hold of him, he may never see his kid again.”

The thing on his chest got heavier.

“Well!” Shawn clapped his hands together and nodded, giving a wide smile. “We’ll save this hotel princess, and with enough time to grab tacos afterward.”

“I like the sound of that,” Gus agreed, while Lassiter just rolled his eyes.

“Mr. Spencer-” chief Vic stopped him on his way out of her office. Gus frowned when he realized that his friend wasn’t behind him.

“What’s up, chief?”

“This past week couldn’t have been easy on you,” she said slowly. Shawn really wished she’d get to the point so he could run out of there faster. “And I’d just like to remind you that the department has people you can talk to.”

Shawn cleared his throat. He found himself shifting uncomfortably. Did he really seem like he needed a therapist? “I- I wasn’t the only one involved in what happened, Chief.”

“Oh, I know,” she nodded. “I’ve already told everyone else the same thing. You, however, haven’t been easy to get a hold of.”

“Right,” he chuckled. “Right, thanks, Chief. I’m gonna go find us a hotel princess. Oh, hey-” he spun around on his heel in the doorway. The chief raised her brows expectantly. “My dad here today?”

“No, he’s coming in tomorrow.”

Shawn nodded and walked out. Thank God. He didn’t want a chance of running into his father, not while he was still suffering the post-breakup fog that his dad would just make worse.

-----

He’d never taken cases this seriously. Well, obviously he was determined to solve it quickly and efficiently, but seriously was not an adverb that needed to go with those two. Being quick and efficient didn’t need to be serious.

And yet he found his breath becoming shallow as they found dead end upon dead end. The girl’s boyfriend hadn’t heard from her and was broken up by her missing.

Lassiter was confused enough by Shawn’s lack of antics that it threw him off as well.

And suddenly, Shawn was poked in the arm. Gus handed him a bag of cheeto puffs.

“What’s this?”

“The machine gave me two by mistake,” Gus shrugged. The idiot didn’t even try to hide the fact that he didn’t have any cheeto puffs of his own.

Shawn ate them anyway.

-----

The chief called them when they were in the middle of interviewing one of Alex’s classmates.

The kidnappers had demanded ransom, and Mr. Cartwright was at the precinct begging her to let him bring the money.

They needed to find her now.

“I want my daughter back!” Mr. Cartwright demanded, as if he were demanding an extra chicken nugget for a long wait.

“We’re doing everything we can to make sure we get her back safely,” the chief explained with extraordinary calmness.

“It’s obviously not enough! You find my daughter, or I give them the money.”

Shawn wracked his brain, looking for information they’d gathered. There had to be something there!

“Alex kinda hates her dad,” her boyfriend had said.

“He refused to give her access to her trust! Like, who does that? Why does she have to work at that gross pizza place when there’s like, a ton of money right there already?” Her friend could hardly pause her clothes shopping to answer their questions.

“I know who the kidnapper is!” Shawn yelled, a hand to his head. He startled everyone in the conference room and decided that must have meant he was back on his game.

“Who, Mr. Spencer?” Chief asked desperately.

“Alex!”

Lassiter narrowed his eyes, and then scoffed. “She kidnapped herself?”

“Exactly!”

“Wait- !”

But Shawn was already out of there and dragging Gus along with him.

Alex was in the basement of the hotel she lived in. She was fine. More than fine, as she’d obviously taken anything she wanted from the hotel to make her stay down there more comfortable. Just like that, she and Mr. Cartwright began screaming at each other.

“She was trying to get her trust fund money early,” Gus said. “She did all this just so she could scam her dad out of money!”

“Jesus.” Lassiter sighed.

Relief washed over Shawn, seeing the girl safe and sound and bratty and terrible. Thank God. Thank God he didn’t have to be responsible for another horrible thing and-

And that heavy thing was back to sitting on his chest. This time it wasn’t going away. He excused himself, earning a confused look from Gus as he went upstairs to the hotel lobby and found the men’s room.

Shawn leaned over the sink and wheezed. Wheezed again. He couldn’t breathe.

He couldn’t inhale. He couldn’t-

A glance at himself in the mirror gave him terrible, disgusting news. He was crying. Jesus Christ, all because of some bratty teenager?

“Shawn?” How did Gus find him so fast? “Shawn are you-? Oh, damn...”

Shawn couldn’t get words out, but apparently he could sob like a pathetic loser. Gus laid a hand on his back rather awkwardly.

“L- look,” he sighed. “She’s okay.”

He wasn’t talking about the girl they just found.

“Just breathe, okay? Everyone’s fine.”

“I don’t know why...” he breathed, or tried to. “Why this is- now?” Why was this happening now, of all times? After he watched his loved ones get traumatized because of him, after the most perfect woman in the world broke up with him because of it. Why now?

“I don’t know, Shawn,” Gus said honestly. “Sorry, I shouldn’t have made you come back this soon. I just thought-”

“No-” Shawn shook his head and took another breath. He tried to straighten his face and dry his eyes on his sleeve. “No, I’m glad. I-” needed this. Somehow, he felt like this was good for him.

“Let’s go home.” And by home, of course, he meant the Psych office.

“Yeah,” Shawn nodded. “Sounds good.”

Maybe he’d take the chief up on her offer after all.
