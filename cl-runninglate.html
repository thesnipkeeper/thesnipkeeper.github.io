Title: Running Late
Fandom: Common Law
Summary: Wes wakes up and regrets it
Word count: 800

Running Late

Wes sighed heavily at the numbers on the screen.

He somehow hoped that he was just imagining things, that maybe this was his well-hidden hypochondria playing tricks on him. But no, it was real this time, and he didn’t have time for it.

He and Travis were getting closer to solving their latest case. They had an entire list of people they needed to interview, and how the hell was he supposed to do that if he was sick?

He set the thermometer aside and leaned on the sink to look at himself in the mirror. He could have looked worse, Wes decided. As long as no one else knew that anything was wrong, maybe he could still do his job.

No one else... specifically Travis. The two of them had been taking some new steps since they finished therapy, but Wes wasn’t ready to just be vulnerable for no reason. He could handle this on his own without his partner’s interference.

He shuddered and padded back to bed, flopping on top of the covers. It probably wasn’t the best idea, was he even going to be able to get up again? He just needed a minute.

-----

Wes woke to his phone ringing. The ache in his head intensified at the sound, and he answered it purely to make it stop.

“Hello?”

“Hey, where are you?”

Wes turned to his bedside table to check the time and immediately swore. “Oh my God...” he muttered, getting out of bed far quicker than his ailing body was ready for. “Ugh, fuck!”

Travis was giggling on the other end of the call. “Long night?”

“You have no idea.”

“Look, I can’t cover for you for long. You’d better get here quick.”

“Really? After all those times that I’ve reluctantly covered for you?!” Wes hissed into the phone while also trying to maintain his balance and pull on a pair of pants.

“Well- alright, good point. Hey, are you okay? You sound weird. Are you drunk or something?”

“No, I’m not drunk.” Wes zipped his pants and took the opportunity to sink back onto his bed until the room would stop spinning. “I’m fine, I just... slept in.”

“Even weirder.”

Wes sighed. “I’ll see you at the precinct.” he ended the call before Travis could ask anymore questions. This may not prove to be as easy as he thought.

Wes continued getting ready for the day, making sure to pull on a thicker jacket when he knew that the chills weren’t going away anytime soon. He stared longingly at the comfy hotel bed before heading to work.

-----

Travis climbed into the car with two paper cups and set them both in the heated cup holders that he was always so excited to use.

“Thanks,” Wes said, immediately taking a sip. He was eager to drink something hot to soothe his throat. He sputtered. “What the hell is this?”

“Tea. Lady at the counter said it was good for you,” Travis said innocently before taking a sip of his coffee. Coffee that he had and Wes didn’t.

“I don’t need tea,” he huffed. Really, he probably did. If he had been ordering instead of Travis, he would have gotten exactly what was in his hands. But that would require abandoning his pride and he just wasn’t ready to do that.

“Sure,” Travis shrugged.

Wes rolled his eyes, sniffling. Add runny nose to the problems he was dealing with, then. That was so fun to deal with while driving, too, Wes thought bitterly. Again, he would have gotten Travis to drive, but his pride was not going to be given up that easily.

-----

The timer on his phone went off and Wes popped another fever reducer into his mouth, washing it down with a sip of Gatorade. He could feel Travis’ eyes on him.

“You can stop staring,” he muttered tiredly.

“You look like shit.”

When he turned to look at his partner, Travis didn’t look smug. He didn’t look like he was enjoying this at all, actually. He almost looked concerned.

Wes gave a resigned sigh. “I... I feel like shit, okay? So, let’s just get this over with.”

Travis nodded. “You bet!”

The person that they were supposed to be interviewing wasn’t at their office. Or there house. Travis and Wes were about to give up until. They had given up, already headed to the precinct to research and see if they missed anything.

She was there. She was frantically telling the officers that her brother-in-law had tried to kill her.

“Guess we have a new suspect,” Travis said.

“Guess so,” but by that time, Wes’ voice was nearly gone. After a day of talking to witnesses (and Travis), his throat was so sore it hurt to talk. He didn’t try to hide the pain anymore, and Travis winced in sympathy.

“Look, I can... I can talk to her,” Travis said, casually as ever. “If you need like, a break or something.”

Wes coughed, and cringed in pain. “You don’t have to do that. I can-”

“Well I kinda want to.” Travis patted him on the back and sent him to the break room to make tea.
