Ending Early

"I cadn still mbake it," Taliesin rasped. "I just need an extra midute to get ready." he snuffled a bit, pawing at his nose as he sat up and began shoving blankets aside.

Matt shook his head, sitting on the edge and gently nudging his friend back onto the bed he was starting to escape. "You don’t actually want to play D&D for four hours straight, do you? You're gonna be miserable."

The sigh Taliesin gave was enough of an answer. He whined childishly and let himself fall back on the pillows. Matt chuckled and grabbed a pair of jeans and a t-shirt to change into.

"You're goi'g to slaughter Caduceus' voice," he groaned.

"Oh, totally. But so would you, to be fair."

"No way. I chose his voice just so I could do it when I'm sick. Like now."

"Just because you can do the voice when you're sick doesn’t mean-"

"I know, I know." Taliesin tried to clear his throat, and ended up yanking up his arm to cough into. Matt caught the wince.

"Throat sore?"

Taliesin's brows furrowed as he swallowed. "Mm," as if he didn’t want to completely admit to another symptom and further prove Matt's point.

He looked so miserable.

"Are you going to be alright by yourself?"

Taliesin chuckled. "It's the flu. I'm not dying."

Matt wasn’t convinced. Maybe they should just cancel the show tonight. Everyone was probably still tired from the week before anyway. "Yeah, but buddy… you can hardly get out of bed." He approached and laid the back of his hand on Taliesin's forehead. The other subconsciously leaned in. "And you're still running a temperature."

"Just the medicine wearing off," Taliesin shrugged. "I'll be fine, Matt."

Matt didn't like the heat that warmed his hand as he lowered it to cup Taliesin's cheek. He had to remind himself that Taliesin often ran fevers when he was sick, even with simple colds. But this wasn't a simple cold. This flu had knocked him on his ass all day, left him a coughing, sneezing, achy, bedridden mess.

But he had some DMing to do. And Taliesin was confident that he'd be fine. Four hours, maybe three if he could manage to end early, and then they'd be home to snuggle and care for their sick friend all night long.

"Are you sure you have everything?" Matt said anxiously. He knew he was getting on Taliesin's nerves with the hovering, but he was willing to deal with that for a bit.

"Yeah. Uh huh." He mumbled, eyes closed and head tilted back. He gave a sigh through his mouth.

Matt scanned the nightstand. "Water, Tylenol, tissues... Am I missing anything? Oh God, you need to eat at some point, hold on-"

"Matt!" He stopped him before he could run off to find some sort of food that could be accessible. "I'b not hungry. I've eatend today, I'll be fine for a few hours." His voice was going. Matt frowned.

"We'll stop somewhere and get you something fucking delicious on the way home, okay?"

"Againd, not hungry." He sniffled. He looked like he wanted to say more, and Matt eagerly waited for some instruction. "Lozenges, though. That would be nice."

"Definitely."

"The good kind."

"Of course," Matt smiled. He breathed out a sigh of relief. Everything would be fine. It was just a few hours. He gave Taliesin a quick kiss on the temple, mumbling an affectionate, "Love you, buddy."

Taliesin hummed happily. "Love you too."

-----

"Hey," he breathed out a sigh of relief when Taliesin answered, even though it sounded like he'd woken him from a nap. His voice was even rougher than before and laced with a thicker congestion.

"Shit, did I wake you up?"

"No- I mean, yeah, actually but that's okay," he heard movement, and a distant cough as Taliesin pulled the phone away. It sounded harsh and painful. "I'd rather be talking to you than sleeping."

"How's uh, how's it going?"

Taliesin chuckled awkwardly, which only made Matt more nervous. "I feel like shit. I'm sorry in advance if I don't stay awake for the whole stream, but ugh."

"Get as much rest as you need, okay? I can always catch you up later."

"Mmkay." Taliesin cleared his throat. "Are you still stopping for stuff on the way home?"

"Yeah, of course What do you need?"

"Mm, definitely lozenges and cough medicine. My throat is killing me. Flu medicine would be nice, something for the fever." A pause. "I feel so awful, Matt." There was a certain desperation in his voice that tugged at Matt's heart.

Matt frowned. "I know, buddy..." He sighed, wishing he could end the show right now. He was half tempted to, but they had people waiting for them to come back to the game.

"I'm fiiine," Taliesin insisted after a pause, drawing out the word dramatically. "I just feel like shit." His voice lit up a bit, easing some of the worry in Matt's stomach.

A call from the studio got his attention. Matt swore.

"Well, I have to go. Miss you. Love you. Take care of yourself!"

"I miss you too." A yawn. "Go finish the game so I can nap, and we'll talk about it when you guys get home."

-----

Matt was unfocused the entire game. He caught Marisha looking at him more than once whenever he stuttered or had to repeat himself more clearly. He so badly wanted to get it all over with and go home, and he was sure that was what she wanted too.

Finally, they ended the game. He told the audience that he didn’t want to do much more without Taliesin, which was true. But he also just wanted to go home early.

"He sounded worse when I talked to him," Matt told her as they wandered into the drugstore. "And he was running a bit of a fever before I left." a bit was an understatement.

"Poor Tal, of course he gets the flu at con. It can't be a cold or something."

"I know…" Matt sighed out, frowning.

They grabbed some throat sweets, cough medicine, and heavy flu medicine at Taliesin's request. They both supported his decision to medicate himself to oblivion until he felt less awful, but they also knew that he needed some good ol' fashioned TLC.

Like soup, and his favorite kind of herbal tea, and the kind of ice cream he ate when he was having a bad day.

Oh, and cuddles. Cuddles were good.

-----

Matt and Marisha set their things on the counter and both headed to the bedroom to check on Taliesin and change into PJs. Except Taliesin wasn't in bed.

That anxiety that had left Matt after Taliesin's phone call returned immediately when he saw the empty bed full of tangled sheets. The bathroom light off to the side of the room shined through a crack in the door. Matt and Marisha exchanged looks.

A dry heave, a cough, a groan. A flush. Matt knocked.

"Mm. Come in..." Came the miserable, stuffy response.

Matt slowly pushed the door open to find Taliesin looking ten times worse than that morning. He was even more paler than usual and covered in a sheen of sweat. By the looks of things, he'd been puking.

Taliesin wiped his mouth on the back of his hand and sniffled. He didn't bother looking over as Matt knelt down next to him.

"Been throwing up?" He asked, having a hard time tamping down the anxiety brought on by Taliesin's worsening illness, and the fact he was there alone for hours.

Taliesin sighed, closing his eyes. "Y-yeah."

"Think you're done for now?"

A subtle nod. Another sniffle. Taliesin looked miserable.

"Want me to help you back to bed?"

Another nod. Tired blinking. He scrubbed his nose with the same hand, and Matt figured this would be a good time to break out the new box of tissues.

"Hey Tal," Marisha said sadly as the two of then hobbled out.

Taliesin was unsteady and slow, but he didn't seem in immediate danger of falling or losing his balance. It seemed to be the fatigue and dizziness that was making it hard for him to move, and he probably would have been fine walking ten feet by himself. But Matt let him stay attached until they reached the bed.

"We might wanna change the sheets," Marisha said.

Matt had to agree. The bed was  looking a little damp and wrinkled, and probably wasn't super comfortable for Taliesin anymore.

He shook his head.

"You'll feel so much better," Matt tried. He pulled some blankets back to help Taliesin get situated, he nudged Matt's hand when he started replacing them. Right. He was probably feeling pretty hot and gross right now. No blankets.

"Plus, I don't know about Matt, but I don't wanna sleep in a sweat-soaked bed," Marisha said casually enough, removing her shirt from the stream and putting on a looser shirt and shorts.

"I- I know..." His voice cracked.  "Sorry."

It seemed to get Marisha's attention. She turned around looking horrified with herself. "Shit, Tal, it was just a joke I don't actually care that much!"

He sniffled more liquidly this time as his eyes began to water. Matt frowned. This teariness, it wasn't normal. And as Marisha sat next to their friend, apologizing and comforting, Matt laid the back of his hand against Taliesins forehead.

Hs furrowed his brows, shifted his hand to Taliesin's neck, and then the exposed skin of his arm.

Matt frowned grimly. "Yeah, alright... you're burning up. Take it easy, okay buddy? I'm gonna go get you some meds."

Taliesin nodded as he wiped at his eyes, letting Marisha rub gentle circles on his back.

"Sorry..." He sniffed. The tears seemed to be dying down. "I don't know what's wrong with me. Fuck."

"It's okay," she said. "You don't feel good. We're right here. Stop apologizing, seriously."

Taliesin gave a watery, self-deprecating chuckle.

He took the dose that Matt gave him, with the hopes that it would stay down.

They got him comfortable as the medicine kicked in. He became a little more level-headed, and instead of tearful and uncomfortable, he was just an exhausted lump in the middle of the bed letting Marisha run her fingers through her hair. The fever went down for a while.

"Were you puking for long?"

"Off and on."

Matt thought about how he sounded during the phone call.

"Oh, buddy... You were throwing up when I called, weren't you." It wasn't a question. Fuck, he should have known. He should have just cut the episode early.

Taliesin made a guilty noise. "Didn't want you to worry. I just- I started feeling gross and then it wouldn't go away." He swallowed hard, the thought of puking making him feel sick all over again, and then nuzzled into the pillows. Marisha leaned down to press a kiss to his temple.

It was late. They were all tired, and Taliesin needed a lot more rest.

Matt brushed his teeth, got into pajamas, and both his loves were fast asleep by the time he returned. He climbed in and wrapped an arm around Taliesin, sleepy smile tugging at his lips when he felt a wet nose nuzzle his collarbone.

-----

Taliesin tossed and turned most of the night, sleeping fitfully. His fever returned at some point in the night and he ended up curled up as close to Matt as he could, shivering until Matt managed to get him covered up in several blankets.

"Sorry, sorry..." Taliesin mumbled as Matt reached under the bed to grab another blanket.

Matt shushed him and kissed his forehead before draping the blanket over him. The shivering didn't stop, but he seemed to be more comfortable. Matt pulled him close and wrapped his arms around him as they fell asleep once again.

Matt wandered into the bedroom and was relieved to see that Taliesin was still asleep. It wasn’t exactly a peaceful sleep, but it was something. His body needed whatever rest he could get.

He quietly grabbed his sweatshirt and slipped it on before sneaking back out of the room.

Concern was nipping at him. "He hasn't eaten since yesterday, and he threw that up..." Matt muttered.

"We'll get some soup in him when he wakes up," Marisha said. She was always the level-headed one in these situations and he loved her for it. She was the one reminding him that it was just the flu, that Taliesin would be fine in a few days and not eating might make him feel crappy but it wasn't an actual danger to his health.

It wasn't hard for them to stay home. Matt had work to do for the campaign that could be done at home, and Marisha ordered some things for the studio from her computer as well as sending emails and making phone calls.

Noon came and Taliesin still hadn't woken up. They missed him, but it was comforting to know that he was getting some true rest after their busy schedules.

It was 12:45 when they heard the bedroom toilet flush, and Taliesin emerged a moment later with messy hair and rumpled clothes.

"What are you doing out of bed?" Marisha said accusingly.

"Wanted some tea," he said, voice rough from sleep and the sore throat that he was probably looking to solve.

"I'll make it! Sit down before you get dizzy."

He found his way to a chair in the living room where Matt and Marisha had been stationed all afternoon. He yawned and rubbed his eyes, giving a sniffle.

"How you feeling?"

"Shitty. Super shitty."

"Hungry?!" Marisha yelled. Then the sound of the faucet as she filled the tea kettle usually only Taliesin used. "We got some soup last night!"

Matt nodded. "It's the good stuff."

Taliesin looked uneasy. "I don't... I might just stick with tea right now but thank you."

"You really should eat something today," Matt tried.

"Yeah..." Taliesin hesitated. "Just, just not right now." He inhaled shakily and rushed to get his shirt collar over his mouth in time to cough. It sounded just as painful as it had. "Did you happen to get cough drops, though?"

"Yeah, yeah, hold on..." Matt dug grabbed the bag of things off of the dining room table and threw the package Taliesin's way. Impressively, he caught it.

"I fucking love you," he breathed out. He kept the package, not wanting to open it quite yet and ruin the flavor of his tea. He snuffled and ran his hand under his nose. "So, what happened in the game last night? What'd I miss after Liam saved us from prison?"

"Oh boy, here we go. Okay, be ready..." Matt said excitedly before explaining the crazy events of the night before.

He could tell that Taliesin was trying to listen, but his symptoms got in the way pretty frequently. He was constantly having to turn away to cough or sneeze, and he didn't ask for tissues even though the tea was obviously making his nose run. Marisha very pointedly set the box on his lap after a while, petting his hair as she did so to avoid a repeat of last night's emotions.

"Oh, thank you..." he said. He blew his nose pretty efficiently and continued to listen to Matt.

They quieted down, and Taliesin ended up on the couch with the two of them after the tea was gone. He laid his head on Marisha's shoulder, pulling away every once in a while to cough away from her.

And then he started shivering again.

Marisha's brows furrowed as she pressed a hand to his forehead. "You're super warm. Matt, can you-"

"On it!" Matt sing-songed as he retrieved their thermometer from the bathroom.

Taliesin seemed to know what to do with it once it was handed to him, somewhat reluctantly. They knew his sigh was directed at his current situation instead of them. He stick the thing in his mouth and it seemed to make him look even more miserable. He looked at the reading.

"Fuck."

Marisha looked over. "Aw, buddy. Why don't we get you back to bed and get you some medicine?"

"And soup," Matt tried.

Taliesin sighed. "Yeah, okay."

Though the fever came and went, they thought he might be getting better. He was able to keep down the soup they gave him, and he managed to stay awake long enough for a movie as they waited for the medicine to kick in.

As it usually went, though, it was worse at night. Taliesin didn't admit to feeling worse beside the mention of another headache as they got ready for bed, but he sounded stuffier, and he sheepishly asked to sleep on the end in case he needed to get up in the middle of the night.

He had a harder time falling asleep than last night. He was getting frustrated trying to breathe properly and dealing with the coughing fits that were more frequent tonight.

It was later in the night when Matt caught the sound of a quiet whimper, and he was immediately awake.

Taliesin snuffled miserably, seeming to get a little more exhausted and frustrated.

Matt gave a jaw-cracking yawn. Taliesin's constant movement and coughing had kept him up. Marisha, however, slept like a rock. "You okay?"

Taliesin gave a snuffly sigh. "Yeah, I'b just... I just want to sleep." His voice broke at the end, and Matt's heart tightened. "Am I keeping you up?" He asked, much quieter.

"Not a big deal. What's wrong?"

Taliesin huffed. "I dod't know... Head hurts, can't fucking breathe through my nose or stop coughing."

"Here, we'll do this..." Matt nudged him to sit up and arranged his pillows against the headboard. Taliesin muffled a cough before leaning back again.

He relaxed against the stack of pillows, still not looking horribly comfy.

"I know it's not great, but it might stop you coughing as much."

"Sorry."

"Was I complaining?" Matt chuckled. "Do you want some cough medicine? It might help knock you out."

Taliesin looked hesitant. Matt had a feeling he was only considering it for their benefit.

"That's okay. Let's just try to get some sleep, hm?"

Ten minutes later, after just a couple of stray coughs, quiet snores came from his bedmate. Matt fell asleep soon after.
