Title: Sweater Sleeve
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick isn't feeling well and makes some assumptions
Word count: 1.7k

Sweater Sleeve

At first David isn't sure what woke him. He can't imagine anything being able to wake him after the day he had yesterday.

As he becomes more awake, he also becomes more aware that the bed is moving, and his fiance is the one to blame.

Patrick squirms, curling up with his back to David.

If David didn’t know any better, he’d think he heard a whimper or a whine, but that’s not right. A nightmare? He’s never really known Patrick to have nightmares, at least not these kind.

David bites his lip and reaches over to lay a hand on Patrick’s shoulder. He clears his throat.

“Patrick...?”

Patrick groans.

So David gets closer and sits up to get a better view of his face. It’s pinched in discomfort. David tries to shake him awake more effectively, and finally Patrick’s awake.

It happens so fast. David doesn’t have a chance to get out of the line of fire before his and Patrick’s lap is covered in puke (and worst of all, the sleeve of his sweater). He nearly pukes himself, in all honesty, but it’s forgotten the moment he sees his fiance’s distress.

“David I-” his eyes go wide and he clamps a hand over his mouth.

“Okay, alright we’re going to ignore that mess for right now...” David mumbles before rushing to get the nearest receptacle--Patrick’s paper bin--in his hands. Just in time, Patrick gags sickeningly and another wave of vomit comes up.

David’s quick to get out of his now-soiled sweatpants and change his shirt, and then he flips on the lamp and looks at the state of the bed. He’d be doing a lot of laundry today, then. He shakes his head. Other things to think about, like the fiance who’s currently half-sobbing as he throws up.

David just stands there for a moment, completely unsure of what he’s supposed to be doing right now. Is he supposed to be comforting Patrick, or should he wait for him to be... done? Maybe he should be working on laundry right now.

Patrick gives a pained sob, and the answer is pretty clear.

David’s careful where he steps as he makes his way to his fiancé, and he sits on the edge of the bed. Slowly, he lays a hand on Patrick’s back, feeling him convulse with each heave. He has to turn away, of course, because he will absolutely vomit as well if he thinks about it too much.

David’s not sure how much time passes before Patrick finally breathes out. His eyes are watery from both crying and the exertion of throwing up, and there’s snot running down his face that David wants to scrub away himself so he doesn’t have to look at it anymore. Patrick has yet to look up at him, instead finding a spot on the floor rather interesting.

David sits uncertainly before pulling a tissue from the box on the nightstand and pressing it into Patrick’s hand--the one that isn’t still holding onto the bin.

Patrick nods his thanks, still not making eye contact, before going to work to dry his nose and eyes, and then wipe his mouth. He sniffs deeply, a sigh.

David thinks that he’s going to acknowledge what just happened. The crying--he’s never seen Patrick like that before. But instead, Patrick looks behind him and makes a displeased noise at the sight of their bed.

“Oh no, I need to-”

David shakes his head, interrupting him with a hand on his shoulder before he stands up. “Already on it, though, so don’t worry.”

Patrick frowns guiltily, eyes still on the soiled blankets as David pulls them off the bed one-by-one. “You don’t have to...”

“You kinda did this for me already so I think that I do.”

Patrick just nods. He stays sitting there for a while, even when David’s almost completely stripped the bed around him. He’s not sure what to do, usually Patrick’s the one who can handle things.

Patrick usually knows what to do, David isn’t sure how to react when he’s just staring off into space.

Finally, when the gross bedspread is in a heap by the door, David decides to be the one to initiate something for once.

Patrick’s still bent over the bin, one arm wrapped around his middle like he isn’t sure what’s going to happen next.

“Hey,” David says softly, sitting down and laying a hand on his back. “Are you still feeling-? Do you need help to the bathroom?”

Patrick swallows, less like he’s fighting nausea and more like he’s fighting the urge to cry. David’s heart breaks a little as he watches his fiance’s eyes fill with fresh tears.

“Patrick, can you look at me?” It’s a question Patrick always uses to pull David out of anxiety attacks, and it just seems fitting in the moment.

Patrick seems to understand the irony, because his mouth quirks up for a moment before he finally makes eye contact with David.

David smiles, finally getting to look at the beautiful dark eyes. “Hi, hello, there you are. I’m just going to...” he gently tugs the bin out of Patrick’s grip and sets it on the floor.

Patrick opens his mouth to protest, but doesn’t.

“You don’t have to stay,” is the first thing out of his mouth instead. He sniffles and scrubs the back of his hand against his nose.

David nods. “No no, I’ll get to the laundry in a second.”

Patrick looks at him, a look that David can’t decipher, and shakes his head. “You don’t have to do all of this, David. I can clean up after myself.”

David blinks. “I’m... sorry? Are you asking me to leave?”

“No! I just-” whatever Patrick was going to say is long forgotten when he swallows--definitely from nausea this time--and scrambles for the bin.

David rubs his back as he throws up, but can’t help but hope that it’s over soon so they can go back to their conversation.

When Patrick’s seemingly done, he makes a pained face and rubs his stomach. “I should probably...I don’t feel too well.”

“Meaning more is coming, gotcha. C’mon, then...” David nods and helps him to the bathroom, helps him kneel in front of the toilet.

Patrick leans against the wall and closes his eyes. “Seriously, David, you don’t have to be here.”

David blinks. He thought this was just Patrick being independent or whatever at first, but now he’s starting to wonder if something else is happening that he hasn’t noticed yet.

“M’kay, why do you keep saying that?”

Patrick’s eyes open just a bit. He huffs a sigh through his nose. “I’m really sick. I think...” a wince. “I think I have that bug that’s been going around? And...” he snakes an arm around his belly and stifles a moan. “And I’m probably going to be really sick for at least a couple of days.”

Patrick’s rational talk sounds much less believable when his voice is hoarse and stuffed from throwing up.

“Well that’s unfortunate, but I don’t understand what that has to do with-”

“-I don’t want you to feel obligated to take care of me just because we’re getting married.”

David blinks. Again. Did he hear that right?

“You don’t have to stay, David. I can take care of myself.”

“Is this about what I said after the um... incident?”

Patrick shrugs.

“Because that was a joke.”

“Yeah but... was it?”

David scoffs, if anything to hide the fact that his feelings are hurt. “You actually think that I’d just leave you here to like, choke on your own vomit?”

Patrick frowns as if he's just now realizing how awful it sounds. Good. David knows he can be a little bit of a spoiled bitch sometimes, but he likes to think that he’s still capable of caring about people.

“Fuck, Patrick...” David sniffs, willing the tears away. “Is that why you were so upset earlier? Because you thought- you thought I was leaving?”

“I’d just woken up, I wasn’t thinking clearly...”

“Uh, yeah, I’d hope so.” He sighs, taking a few seconds to gather his bearings before he joins Patrick on the bathroom floor. “I’m not going anywhere. Like, ever. Like, you’re stuck with me for good.”

Patrick chuckles.

“And I’m sorry, but you are not supposed to be the self-deprecating one in this relationship,” David says with a watery laugh. He reaches up to cup Patrick’s face, and then presses a kiss to his temple.

-----

David’s pulling on a fresh sweater just as his boyfriend’s coming out of the bathroom. The bedspread is in the washer, thank goodness, and until then they have spare blankets to use.

“Morning,” David says with a smile.

Patrick looks to the window. “Oh, it is light out, huh?”

“Mhmm. Feeling better?”

He chuckles and flops onto the bed. “Better? No, but my stomach is done ejecting things for now.”

“Ah, gross.”

“David...”

David gets a good look at him and finds Patrick looking at him so earnestly, the way he does when he’s about to bring up something important he knows David doesn’t want to talk about.

David shakes his head dismissively as he straightens his sweater. “We don’t have to...”

“I think we do, though.”

David sighs, getting in the bed and letting Patrick scoot closer to him. He’s warm--he should probably take Patrick’s temp at some point (does Patrick own a thermometer?).

“It wasn’t fair for me to expect you to just- just leave.”

David nods. “It definitely was not. Go on...”

“I just thought... I don’t know. I don’t want to put you in an uncomfortable situation because that’s what you feel like you have to do.”

He sighs. “That’s not what’s happening here. It kind of hurt that you thought I felt obligated before wondering if maybe I just actually cared.”

“I know, I know.” Patrick snuggles closer. “I’m sorry.”

“When I catch whatever death flu you have, though, I expect you to feel obligated to do the same.”

Patrick huffs out a weak laugh. “I suppose that’s only fair.” He frowns, turning to look up at David. “How’s your sweater?”

“Oh...” David clears his throat. “It’s... I’m not sure I’m ready to talk about that yet.”
