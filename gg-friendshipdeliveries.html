Friendship Deliveries

“Where’re you going?” Lorelai managed to stop him from disappearing in the back for the hundredth time since she’d shown up for lunch.

He’d been doing it constantly, consistently, and she knew there was no work to be done back there because he had one other customer besides her this late in the afternoon. It almost hurt, though she’d never admit it, because usually he’d have stuck around to chat with her during the slow hours, maybe judge her coffee consumption and the amount of syrup she was dumping on her pancakes (and the fact that she ordered pancakes for lunch).

But no, he just kept walking off as if he had something better to do than have a pleasant conversation. The ass.

“Dishes,” he answered, eyes darting to the back as if he longed to be there.

“There is absolutely no way you have that many dishes to do. You’ve been hiding since I got here, what gives?”

Luke clenched his jaw without looking at her. She almost thought he was going to snap at her or something (not that it would affect her any). He still looked back, nostrils flaring, before walking a few feet away and raising his arm in front of his face.

Luke sneezed.

“Oh my God,” Lorelai gasped, like she’d just solved the biggest mystery. “You’re sick, poor baby.” She almost felt bad teasing him. Almost. His eyes were watery after that sneeze, and he sniffed and wrinkled his nose.

“Don’t,” he growled, but it sounded more like a plea.

“Don’t what? Report you to the health department for serving germ-infested food?” The other customer glanced up with a raised eyebrow. Lorelai lowered her voice at Luke’s glare. “Have you been going back there to sneeze? Jesus, Luke, that’s a little extreme, don’t ya think?”

“Despite my feelings toward customers like you, I have no interest in serving germ-infested food.” He sniffed again. Lorelai had been a mother long enough to know the look when someone’s nose was running and they didn’t know what to do about it. “I go back there, I blow my nose, I wash my hands. Happy?”

“Sure,” she shrugged. “Are you?”

“Not usually.”

She snorted at that, and continued eating while he grabbed a napkin and disappeared once again.

He returned with a pinker nose and red eyes like he’d done nothing but try to rub the irritation out of them all day. Jesus Christ.

“God, Luke, do we need to get a hazmat team in here to escort you out?”

He smiled sarcastically. “You’re funny, Lorelai.”

“Thank you, I try. I’ve gotta go,” she hopped off the stool and grabbed her purse. “Rory and I will probably be by for dinner, so don’t die until then, m’kay?”

He didn’t respond with anything but some muttered words that she probably wasn’t meant to hear.

-----

Lorelai decided that it was time for her afternoon cup of sanity. It’d been a long day full of snarky guests and one employee who refused to do as he was told. She’d hardly had a chance to think between tasks and her coffee addiction was suffering for it.

“Coffee, stat,” Lorelai announced as she barged into the kitchen.

“Oh, hey!” Sookie was elbow-deep in a batch of dough that looked deliciously like chocolate chip cookie. Hmm. Coffee first. “Good timing, I just made a fresh pot! How’s your day?”

Lorelai threw her head back with a dramatic groan. “Busy,” she said.

She grabbed the first coffee cup she saw and filled it to the brim with the precious substance.

“Hey, you gonna share-” but a spoonful of cookie dough was alright shoved in her direction. “You are an angel. How the hell are you sane right now?” She shoved the dough into her mouth, rather than bothering to savor it. It was one of those days. “Like four guests sent their dishes back during lunch. That doesn’t bother you?”

“Well,” Sookie screwed up her face in thought, and then shrugged. “Of course it bothers me, but they’re not from around here.”

“Many of our guests aren’t,” Lorelai agreed with a slow nod.

“I mean, they don’t know what real FOOD is supposed to taste like, right? They sure didn’t go to culinary school for four years, or spend a year abroad in COUNTRY learning how to make FOOD perfectly, right?”

“...Right. So it doesn’t both you at all, is what I’m hearing?”

“Not at all!” Sookie decided.

“Mm’kay.”

“Hey, did you see Luke today?”

Lorelai raised an eyebrow as she took a sip of her coffee. “Uh, yeah, I went there around one. Why?”

“I picked up a late lunch just a little while ago, and he looked terrible!’ She shook her head in disbelief. “Poor Luke.”

“He’s fine,” Lorelai said with a scoff. “He’s a grown man with the sniffles.”

“I dunno, he didn’t look good. Or sound good. Gosh, his voice was nearly gone.”

Lorelai didn’t remember his voice sounding any different when she had gone for lunch. He was just sneezing a lot and being weird about it like men were prone to do. “You sure you’re not exaggerating?” She took another sip, and was about to ask for more cookie dough when a second spoon was handed to her.

“Since when do I exaggerate?”

“Ah, good point.”

“I think I might make him some soup later.”

“You really think that’s a good idea after last time?” Last time, years ago, when Luke insisted that he was not sick and proceeded to send Sookie away the moment she tried to give him some homemade soup. It probably had something to do with the fact Sookie had insulted his cooking skills in the process, but Lorelai wasn’t there to confirm.

“Who else is going to make him some good, healing soup, though?”

She had a point. People generally knew to steer clear when Luke was in a particularly prickly mood. “Alright, fair enough.”

“Do you think he has any medicine?”

“It’s a cold, Sookie, he’ll survive.”

She gave a non-committal hum. “I just worry about him. He’s such a loner since Rachel left and he doesn’t ask for help and-”

“I know,” Lorelai said with a heavy sigh. “Me too.”

“Hey!” Sookie looked at her with big eyes and an even bigger smile.

“Uh oh, I don’t like that look. That’s the ‘idea’ look.”

Sookie tried to make her expression a little more sly, but she failed spectacularly. “You get off in a couple of hours, right?”

“If all goes well, why?”

Sookie didn’t say a thing, just continued to smile.

“No! No, no way, I’m not going to be your soup delivery!”

“Lorelai, please! He’s sick, you should have seen him! He looked terrible, and he needs soup, and he won’t accept it from me but...”

“But what?”

“He might accept it from you!”

“What makes you think the king of stubborn would do that?”

“Well, because you’re the queen of stubborn, first of all,” Sookie explained logically. Lorelai couldn’t help but agree. “You’re the only one who can talk him into things, and-” she bit her lip before saying, “and he likes you! You’re his only friend, really!”

Lorelai sighed. She wasn’t usually interested in doing any more work than already necessary, but... “Ugh, fine!”

Sookie cheered in delight, clapping her gloved hands and getting cookie dough everywhere in the process. “Yay! Tell him I hope he gets well soon!”

“Yeah yeah, just make the dumb soup,” Lorelai said with a scoff.

-----

“Isn’t it kind of weird to be bringing in outside food?” Rory asked as they turned the last corner to Luke’s.

“Yup.” Sookie sent her with a cooler, with soup and ginger ale and some kind of sugary dessert that wasn’t healthy but tasted good. God, Sookie made the best sick day meals, it was the only good thing to happen whenever she or Rory got sick.

“So, what’s in there?”

“Nothing, just- just stuff from Sookie.”

“Why is Sookie sending us here with outside food? Aren’t we ordering dinner here?”

“Yup.”

“Mom, what’s going on?”

Rory stopped in front of her, cutting off her passage to the door to Luke’s. She folded her arms defiantly and raised her brows for an answer. She was far too good at that.

Lorelai sighed heavily. “Okay! it’s for Luke, alright?” She shoved past her kid, Rory making an annoyed sound as she did so. She tried to get the door open herself, but with her daughter having abandoned her, she couldn’t quite manage and ended up huffing dramatically in frustration until Rory rolled her eyes and opened it for her. “Sookie wanted me to bring some stuff, and I couldn’t say no because-”

Lorelai was cut off by a loud, miserable sneeze.

“Jesus,” she muttered under her breath.

Luke wiped his nose roughly with a napkin before tossing it and slathering sanitizer on his hands. His nose was bright red, like it’d been abused all day, and he was sniffling incessantly. It must’ve been driving the current customers insane, but no one looked like they wanted to risk saying anything.

“What is that?” He demanded, eyes immediately going to the lunchbox cooler in her hands. “Are you bringing food into my diner, Lorelai?” Sookie was right, he voice sounded horrible. She could hardly stand there and listen to him try to force words past what was likely a sore throat.

“Nope, I wouldn’t do something like that. Would I do something like that?” She asked Rory.

Rory raised her hands. “Don’t look at me. I’m just here to stave off post-school starvation.” She walked off, already finding a table.

Luke glared, though it didn’t have as much of an effect when his eyes were so watery and bloodshot he looked like he’d either been crying for days or inhaling pollen for days.

“Fine, here,” she shoved it toward him. He took it, looking weirdly at the cooler and then back at her. “It’s soup from Sookie. She was worried about you today, alright?”

He sighed and rolled his eyes. “I told her that-”

“Well, you’re not fine, you look like one of those gross contagious people from a disinfectant commercial who’s unknowingly spreading their germs everywhere until the people around them are force to purchase ungodly amounts of Clorox. Can you even breathe through your nose? Because I don’t think you’ve closed your mouth since we got here, and it’s not a great look for you.”

He wasn’t amused. “Take it back.”

She raised her hands to avoid all responsibility of the thing now that it was in his hands. “Nope, it’s not mine.”

“Lorelai, I don’t want this. Take it ba-” his breath caught, and suddenly he was shoving the thing back at her before she could refuse.

He held his elbow in front of his face, turned away from everything and coughed. It was possibly the most grating, most painful thing Lorelai had ever heard, and now he had the rest of the diner’s attention. Lorelai glanced back at Rory, and the two of them shared a sympathetic expression.

She patted his back unhelpfully as the fit came to a sputtering end and he was left looking more exhausted and drippy than he had before.

“Jesus Christ, Luke, close up and go to bed.”

“I’m fine.” He finished the sentence with a gurgling sniff that had her wincing at the gross sound. “No one takes a sick day because of a damn cold, Lorelai.”

“They might if they serve food to lovely townspeople.”

It wasn’t fair, she knew that. He was so careful working the diner when he was sick. He didn’t have anyone else’s help, and he prided himself on the fact that he was always able to make it on his own. But damn, he looked miserable, and it kind of made Lorelai feel guilty for even teasing him.

Not that she’d ever stop teasing him.

“Luke, c’mon, take the soup, go upstairs, go to bed.” She tried to hand the cooler back to him, he just pushed it away.

“No.”

“Luke-”

“Are you here to order or not, Lorelai? Or are you just here to bother me when I’m clearly not in the mood right now?”

She looked again at her starving child and nodded, with a heavy sigh. “Two burgers with as few germs as possible, please.”

“Fantastic.” He deadpanned before walking off.

“Jeez,” Rory said as Lorelai finally sat down at their table. “He’s grumpier than usual.”

Lorelai hummed in agreement. She plopped the cooler on the table in front of them and looked at it with a huff. “Sookie’s not gonna be happy, that’s for sure.”

“Right.” Rory didn’t say anything else.

-----

Lorelai was really ready for some pancakes that morning. She and Rory were having one of their rare mornings where everything went right, they left the house on time, and they actually had time to get breakfast together rather than each scarf a single pop tart on their way to work.

Of course it was too good to be true.

Luke’s was closed.

Lorelai was tempted to just drop in the middle of the sidewalk until someone gave her pancakes, but instead she just whined loudly.

“Not fair, oh my God! It was such a good morning, too!”

Rory scoffed. “You know, complaining about it is definitely going to change it.”

“Rory, don’t even joke about this, I can feel the pancake deficiency taking hold as we speak. What excuse could he possibly-”

Rory’s raised eyebrow clued her into the fact that she had forgotten something.

“Oh...” and then she laughed a bit. “Okay, but since when does Luke take a sick day? I mean, c’mon, he’s probably playing hooky or something.”

“Yeah, that sounds like Luke.”

Lorelai sighed heavily, tilting her head back to growl at the sky. She was so damn hungry.

“Okay, I’m gonna get something at school. Are you going to be okay on your own?” asked Rory with a hint of amusement. More than a hint, she was enjoying Lorelai’s suffering.

“Yeah,” Lorelai answered with a pout. “I’ll just... go back home... if I can make it there...”

“You can do it!” Rory called as she headed off for the bus stop.

-----

With Rory staying late at school for a project, Lorelai was once again left on her own to find food when dinnertime arrived. The search in the kitchen proved... unfruitful.

She wondered how Luke was doing. She told herself that the concern was purely selfish because she simply wanted to be able to go to the diner, and his poor health was preventing her from eating properly. But...

Luke taking a sick day? That was pretty concerning. How bad did he have to feel to close his restaurant for the whole day?

She fell into a chair with a huff and picked up the phone. She dialed Luke's number, one she had memorized for home maintenance and lack-of-diner-food emergencies. She tapped her finger against the table impatiently as it rang, and rang...

“Yeah?” The voice on the other end was raspy and unused. God, had he been sleeping? Was Luke Danes sleeping in the middle of the day? Lorelai could practically see pigs flying outside the kitchen window.

“Hey, are you open?” She asked, though she already knew the answer from the sound of his voice.

A pause, and Lorelai realized from the distant sounds that Luke had pulled the phone away to cough. “No.”

“Can you be open?” She had no real intention of eating food prepared by the germ-ridden man.

“Why?”

“Because I’m hungry and my only option is a couple pieces of sesame chicken I found in the back of the fridge. And - between you and me - I think it’s been there for a few weeks.”

“Jesus Christ,” he groaned. “No, the diner’s closed.”

“Fine,” she sighed out, and then hesitated. She and Luke had a really great sarcasm-all-the-time thing going on, and she wondered if she should use up one of their rare sincere moments right now. “How you feelin’?”

She heard Luke’s breath hitch, a small desperate sound she wasn’t used to hearing from him, before he sneezed. He sniffled thickly and sighed. “Feeling great,” he answered. “Might run a marathon later.”

“Aw jeez,” she murmured. “That bad, huh?”

The cough that followed answered her question quite nicely. It was so quick he hadn’t gotten the phone away in time, and she ended up pulling her own back instead. It sounded as if both of his lungs had just completely given up on functioning. The fit ended with a gurgling throat-clearing and a gross sniffle.

“Have you taken anything?”

“Like what?”

“Well, I hear that leeches are hot right now. Medicine, Luke, have you taken any cold medicine?”

Luke sniffed again, and Lorelai really really wished he’d blow his nose. “I don’t have any,” he admitted.

She sighed heavily. “Dumb question. You’re a single man, of course you don’t have cold medicine.” She tapped her nails on the table a few more times as she thought. Luke didn’t seem to mind or notice the stretch of silence, likely taking advantage of the moment to tend to his nose. “Okay, I’ll be over in a little bit.”

There was a pause, and then, “Wait, what?”

“I’m bringing you medicine, and the container of soup that’s been sitting in our fridge since yesterday, and you’re going to accept both of them from me because you feel like total crap and refusing help at this point is just self-sabotage.”

“Okay,” he sniffled again. “Fine.”

“Fine!” she said pleasantly before ending the call.

-----

She expertly balanced everything in her arms as she knocked on Luke’s apartment door.

“Come in, Lorelai,” Luke’s usual deadpan tone was really dampened by the hoarseness and congestion, Lorelai decided.

She let herself in, closed the door with a nudge of her foot, and found Luke in his kitchen doing dishes of all things. He wore sweats and a T-shirt old enough to have seen some things.

“Shouldn’t you be in bed?”

Luke sniffed, and then again, clearly struggling with a runny nose while he was elbow-deep in dishwater. “To be honest, I-” he rubbed his nose against his shoulder and - from the sounds of the sniffles that followed - it accomplished nothing. “Wanted to get these done before you came.”

“Aww, you’re cleaning for me?”

Luke gave a non-committal hum. “Haven’t felt much like doing my own dishes the past couple of d- hhh days...” he twisted away, managing to pull his arm up to shield his surroundings from a sneeze.

“Bless you, snotty. Why don’t you let me do that before you keel over and I have to drag your ass to the hospital.”

“Jesus, Lorelai, it’s a cold,” he finished the sentence with another sniffle.

“A bad one, my friend.” She poked the middle of his back, earning an annoyed grunt in response. “Go on, go sit down,” she continued poking him until he had no choice to comply. It appeared that he was too tired to argue today.

Luke didn’t stop glaring at her as he pulled out a chair and sat at his table. His nose looked sore, and even more so when he brought the back of his hand up to rub it. Maybe all hygiene was lost the moment he closed the diner.

She pulled the container of soup out of the pile of stuff she’d brought with her and put it in the microwave. “You know, the doors were unlocked when I came in. You inviting intruders now?”

Luke snorted. “I unlocked it for when you got here.”

Part of her was flattered by that. It was like he was looking forward to her arrival, unlocking the door, doing dishes, all preparations a sick person probably shouldn’t be doing.

“You didn’t have to do that,” she said. “I could’ve gotten a brick, smashed the window...”

“You’re right, what was I thinking?”

She turned her attention from the soup and finally got a good look at him. The poor guy was practically falling asleep at the table, chin on his palm, eyes half closed. There was a bit of wetness just at the base of his nostrils as if he was too tired to notice.

“I can bring this to you in bed,” Lorelai said as she poured the soup into the first clean bowl she found. “Do it for Rory all the time when she’s sick.”

Luke opened his eyes a bit wider and sat up with a gurgly inhale, as if it say I’m awake! “First you tell me to go here, now there...” but he still stood up.

“I know, I know,” Lorelai offered some teasing sympathy. “Life is hard, huh?”

He coughed on the way to his bed, not bothering to cover it this time. Lorelai caught to wince on his face afterward.

“Sore throat?”

“Eh,” he shrugged.

“Eh? What do you mean eh? Either your throat hurts or it doesn’t. That’s not really a maybe kind of-”

“Okay,” he growled. “Yes. Satisfied?” Well, if there was anything she was good at, it was annoying the truth out of people.

“I am,” she tilted her chin up in triumph. “Because the soup should help. It probably would’ve been even better if you’d just taken it from me yesterday, but...”

He huffed, which ended in a weak cough.

She went to set the soup in his hands, only for his eyes to widen in alarm as he inhale shakily and pulled his arm up to cover another cough. This one sounded dry and painful, like he’d been doing that all night.

She waited patiently, and maybe a little awkwardly before he was able to take the bowl from her.

“Sorry,” he rasped before clearing his throat.

“How dare you,” Lorelai deadpanned.

She didn’t bother to ask before she grabbed him a glass of water, along with the plethora of meds she bought. Sick people always needed water nearby, and he had none. She was willing to bet that her arrival was the only reason he bothered to get out of bed today.

She threw the pharmacy bag next to him on the bed. “Take a dose of like, everything.”

“You’re trying to kill me, aren’t you?” He was already starting to dig through them all to find one to take, and that was enough of a sign that he felt worse than he was letting on.

“Absolutely!”

Lorelai left him to eat while she went to finish the dishes he’d started. She didn’t usually volunteer to wash other people’s dishes, but this was different, apparently. She took his bowl when he claimed to be done. There was still a good amount of soup left, but by the pained look on his face as he swallowed the chosen meds, his throat wasn’t doing great.

Lorelai wasn’t sure what to do after that. Was she supposed to stay?

Luke looked like he wanted to go to sleep, but sounded like he wasn’t going to anytime soon. That cough was insistent, and painful. She wondered how well he’d slept the night before.

“I’m gonna head out, alright? Rory should be back by now.”

“Of course,” he cleared his throat. “Uh- thanks, Lorelai. You didn’t have to do all this,” he said, gesturing to all the medicine still scattered about his bed, and the lotion-y tissues she’d bought because his nose looked so horrible.

“You know that I did!”

He raised an eyebrow.

“What? The quicker you get better, the quicker I get to have pancakes in the morning again.”

He managed a raspy chuckle, and then winced when it hurt. “How kind of you.”

“I’m truly selfless,” Lorelai smiled. “Call me if you need anything. Seriously.”

“Will do.”

She hummed, with the knowledge that this stubborn man wouldn’t call anyone for help if he was on the ground dying, let alone suffering from a bad cold.

-----

She only had herself to blame when she woke up with a scratchy throat two days later. The headache didn’t help, nor did the chills deep in her bones.

“Shit,” she muttered at her ceiling.

Rory was already up and getting ready for school. There was the sound of the coffee maker and cereal hitting a ceramic bowl, and then the swearing when Rory inevitably remembered that they were out of milk.

Lorelai allowed herself to groan as she got out of bed. She considered calling in, but it wasn’t bad enough for her to stay home, not yet.

Now she understood how Luke felt.

She pulled on a sweatshirt before venturing downstairs. Rory was eating a bowl of dry Froot Loops and reading the newspaper even though Lorelai didn’t raise her to do either of those things.

“Morning,” she greeted, immediately grabbing the biggest coffee mug they had and filling it to the brim. Bless Rory for making a full pot this morning. She needed it.

“Morning. What’s wrong with you? You look... even less enthused than you usually do in the morning.”

“I feel terrible, I blame Luke, I may die today.”

“Oh no,” Rory frowned in sympathy. “You caught Luke’s cold, huh?”

“Think so,” Lorelai said with a huff before planting herself at the table with her precious coffee. “Remember me how I was, alright?”

Rory nudged the cereal box in her direction. “Will Froot Loops help?”

“Ugh, no. My throat hurts. I’ll just have coffee for breakfast, and probably lunch. Maybe dinner.”

“Okay then,” Rory shrugged. “I’m leaving early so Paris and I can work on our project. Are you going to survive on your own today?”

“You know... I’ll try.”

She chuckled and nodded. “Good. Feel better!”

Lorelai did not feel better.

It didn’t help that the inn was just one catastrophe after the other. Only half of their place cards arrived for a wedding that took place the next day, they were almost out of linens, and- and damn it, her throat was sore.

She felt the beginnings of congestion in her sinuses and a tickle started to develop in her chest, causing small, annoying coughs to bubble up and hurt her throat even more. Stupid Luke and his stupid cold.

She started shivering around lunchtime, and was thankful that she brought a sweater with her to work. Not that it would help, but it was nice to pretend.

She wandered into the kitchen for the lunch of coffee she’d promised herself. The pain in her throat spiked whenever she swallowed or spoke, and the hot liquid would hopefully provide some kind of relief.

“You look terrible!” Sookie said with a gasp.

“Gee, thank you,” Lorelai scoffed. She took a sip of coffee even though it was still a little too hot to drink. “Ugh, I think I’m getting what Luke had. Actually, I know I’m getting what he had, because he probably sneezed allover his apartment.”

“Oh you poor thing!” Sookie cooed, and she was one of the few people who could say that sincerely. “I’ll make you some soup, just let me finish up this chopping, and-”

Sookie didn’t get a chance to finish her sentence (or the chopping) before Michel stuck his head in. “There’s a lumberjack in the lobby asking for you, please make him go away.”

Lorelai raised an eyebrow. Coffee cup in tow, she made her way out of the kitchen.

She couldn’t help but laugh. “Luke, what are you doing here?”

“Well,” he cleared his throat. He set a box on the front desk, much to Michel’s chagrin. “Ran into Rory this morning, she said you uh, caught my cold. Thought I’d return the favor.” He gestured to the box, which contained a small container and the remaining medicine and cough drops Lorelai had brought him just a couple days before.

“Oh my God... that’s- thank you, Luke. That’s really sweet.”

He nodded, expression unchanging as he stuffed his hands in his pockets. “Well, so uh, so was what you did for me.” His voice still sounded rough, his nose was still a little pink, but he looked much better than he had. The first couple of days must have been the worst of it. “Call me if you need anything else,” he said.

She smiled and, before thinking too much about it, kissed his cheek. He froze in classic Luke fashion. Hey, it wasn’t like she could get him sick when he already was, right?

“Rory and I will probably be by later for dinner.”

Luke shook his head. “You’ll feel like death by dinner, trust me. I’ll bring it to you.”

“Luke, you’re amazing.”

“I’m aware. See ya!”

They said their goodbyes, and Lorelai’s new goal was to get as much daytime cold medicine in her as possible.

Though, she couldn’t help but feel excited to see Luke later that night, even if she’d probably be a mess by then.

Huh, excited to see Luke?

That was new.
