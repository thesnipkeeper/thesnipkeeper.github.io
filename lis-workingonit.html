Working on It

They made plans for Chloe to pick her up at Blackwell, and Max climbed into the old truck and offered a cute greeting just like always. But then she spent half the car ride to the Price house leaned against the window.

Max was acting weird.

Chloe brought it up, of course she did, but Max just shrugged and denied it.

“Are you sure?” The irritation remained at the edge of her voice no matter how much she tried to hold it back.

Thankfully, Max didn’t seem to notice, because Chloe probably would have felt more guilty.

“Yeah! I’m good.”

But something was off. Still, Chloe didn’t decide to push further. That... that was something that she was working on. Holy fuck it was difficult.

She raised an eyebrow at her friend who was half-asleep in the passenger seat. Maybe she was just tired. Chloe knew how Blackwell worked kids to the bone, after all.

“Mom’s making chili for dinner! And we’ve also got pie from the diner for dessert, because she’s so extra.”

Max seemed to perk up at that. There we go, just needed to talk about food. “Oh God, I’ve missed your mom’s chili so much.”

“Dude, me too. I think you’re the reason she decided to make it tonight.”

Max chuckled. “I must be special.”

“Oh, totally,” Chloe scoffed.

They talked about random things on the way there. Max talked about some of her insane assignments, some drama that was going on at Blackwell involving a love triangle where the girl the two guys were chasing was, in face, a lesbian. Max was still not as excitable as her usual self, but it was a relief to know that it wasn’t anything Chloe did. She knew she had a tendency to say hurtful things in the past, and she was really trying to fix that.

“We’re here!” Chloe called as they walked it. The two of them kicked off their shoes at the door (Joyce’s new rule, for some reason) and wandered in.

Chloe groaned immediately at the sight of her mom and her stepdad dancing to some ancient music in the kitchen.

“Get a fuckin’ room!” She said as she led Max past the kitchen to the dining room.

“Hey Max!” Joyce greeted, ignoring her daughter completely.

Max chuckled. “Hi,” she said, smiling. “Thanks for having me over!”

“Oh, psh,” Joyce waved dismissively. “You’re welcome here any time, sweetie. You should know that by now!”

As embarrassing as Chloe found her mom to be sometimes, it made her feel ridiculously warm and happy when she thought about how much she considered Max part of the family. Not that she’d ever admit that. Instead, she squeezed her friend’s shoulder before the two of them sat down.

Joyce brought out the pot of chili onto the table, while James set out crackers and everything else that absolutely needed to go on chili.

Max rubbed her face with a wince when she thought no one was looking. Chloe was looking.

“Hey,” she said quietly, leaning to Max while her parents continued to set everything up for dinner. “Are you sure everything’s okay?”

“Yeah, why?”

“You’re acting weird, man.”

She put on a smile once again. “Okay, I have a little bit of a headache, but-” Max stopped when the other two sat down at the table.

A headache. A headache would make sense. Chloe only wished that she could fucking do something to fix it.

“Shall we say grace?” Joyce said.

-----

Things only got more worrying when Max hardly ate anything. She loved Joyce’s chili more than Chloe did, why wasn’t she eating. Chloe could feel frustration bubbling up. Why wasn’t Max just saying what was wrong? Was it really just a headache?

Joyce glanced at Max’s bowl as she absently stirred the chili inside, met Chloe’s eyes with a worried look, but left it.

Max answered questions when she was asked, about school and her family. She laughed at Chloe’s parents’ lame jokes just like she always did for some reason, but she wasn’t present.

That was it, Chloe realized. She was just out of it.

Noticing that Max had no intention of finishing her chili, Chloe made an excuse for them to finish up.

“I think we’re gonna watch a movie,” she said. “Or two.”

“Oh, that’s nice! I hope you don’t mind if we join you,” Joyce grinned.

“Funny. No,” Chloe said.

She got roped into helping with dishes anyway, leaving Max to fend for herself.

“She’s actin’ a little strange,” Joyce casually mentioned as she rinsed a bowl.

Chloe scoffed. “You’re telling me.”

“She okay?”

“I don’t know, mom,” she said with a huff. It wasn’t fair, Joyce was just worried, but she didn’t have all the answers!

“Alright, alright...”

Max was still on the couch where Chloe left her, when the two of them were finished. Except, she was lying down, and upon further inspection, fast asleep. It would have been adorable if it wasn’t so worrying.

Joyce made a sympathetic noise. “I don’t think she’s feeling too good.”

Chloe frowned, and sighed. She didn’t have anything else to say to her mom before Joyce went upstairs.

She knelt down and woke Max with a gentle shake. Shit... she was kinda warm.

“Hm?” Max groaned.

“You okay?”

“Mhm.” Max blinked wearily and slowly sat up. Too slowly. Her face was red.

“Are you sure about that? ‘cause you kinda feel like you have a fever.”

Max furrowed her eyebrows and seemed to take a moment to evaluate how she was truly feeling before she groaned again and let her head fall back. “Fuuuck. I think you’re right.”

The fact that even Max didn’t realize that she was sick made Chloe feel better than it should have. That meant she wasn’t being too insensitive or missing too much. She laid a hand on her friend’s forehead just like her mom used to do when she was kid.

Admittedly, Chloe didn’t have a lot of experience with feeling other people’s foreheads, but that didn’t feel like a normal body temperature.

“Jeez, Max...”

She ran a tired hand down her face and sighed. “Sorry. I didn’t even think- I thought I was just sleep deprived or something.”

“Naw, it’s fine. Better here than at school alone, right?” God, Chloe had horrible memories of being sick at school. Thankfully she was able to live at home instead of on campus, but that didn’t make all of her responsibilities go away when she came down with something.

Max hummed, a small smile on her face. “I guess that’s true.”

“Okay, new plan,” Chloe decided. “We go upstairs and chill until you go back to sleep.”

Max bit back a smile, obviously fighting with the idea of going back to school before she nodded.

“Yeah?”

“Yeah, sure,” she said with a chuckle. “Chloe, I’m sorry...”

“Dude, stop apologizing. C’mon, upstairs we go!”

-----

Max started shivering not too long after they got settled in Chloe’s room. Chloe pulled out extra blankets to throw at her before feeling useless. She really didn’t know how to do this. She wasn’t good at taking care of other people. There was Rachel, sure, but she and Rachel had... a different dynamic.

“Should we like, take your temperature? Is that what people do?”

Max giggled. “Sure? I’m not really sure.”

“Okay, give me two seconds.”

Chloe found her mom in the bedroom folding laundry. “Hey,” she said, getting Joyce’s attention.

“Hey, how’s Max?”

“Where’s uh, where’s the thermometer?”

Joyce frowned, having gotten the answer to her question. “In the bathroom, bottom drawer.”

“Cool,” and Chloe was gone. She didn’t feel like talking about it, anyway. They could do that later.

-----

“That’s a fever,” she confirmed.

Max huffed. She looked like she was going to fall asleep any second, which was... incredibly cute. “This sucks.”

“Yeah,” Chloe agreed. She climbed onto the bed and threw an arm over her. You know, to warm her up. Actually, she probably didn’t need to be warmed up with a fever like that... but whatever. Max immediately cuddled into her. “But you can stay as long as you want. I’m not about to kick you out and send you back to the freezing dorm room.”

Max chuckled and smiled, eyes slipping shut. “Thanks, Chloe.”

Chloe scoffed. How dare someone be that adorable. “Go to sleep, hipster.”

As it turned out, both of them fell asleep. Because Chloe woke to the sound of a knock on her door, and then it was immediately opened because apparently no one knew how knocking actually worked.

She didn’t bother to look up at the intruder until she heard Joyce’s coo.

“Mom,” Chloe hissed.

Joyce hand a hand over her mouth, clearly covering a smile at the sight of the two of them, before she shrugged. “I just wanted to see how everything was going. I think I got my answer.”

Chloe rolled her eyes. “Okay, thank you bye.”

Joyce smiled and exited, quietly shutting the door behind her.
