Title: Best Friends
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Stevie ends up in the middle of a marriage dispute when they should really just communicate
Word count: 1.5k

Best Friends

The bell of the motel lobby door jingles, prompting Stevie to reluctantly look up. She can’t help but be a little confused to see David Rose wander in and proceed to flop on the sofa.

“Uh, you know you don’t live here anymore, right?”

David moans. “We got in a fight.”

“Ah.” So it’s going to be one of those days where she has to try to play emotionally-supportive best friend even though she has no idea what she’s doing. Stevie wanders out from behind the counter and sits next to him. “What happened?”

A sigh. David massages his temple. It’s been a while since she’s seen him get stress headaches. He’s usually good at managing them these days. Must have been some argument.

He shakes his head and waves a hand lazily in a ‘whatever’ manner. “It just... it started because I bought something for the store and apparently it was more expensive than we could afford right now, and-” He pauses, swallowing thickly. And the next words coming out of his mouth are broken, Stevie knows she’s about to practice the art of consoling. “I don’t know why I- I wasn’t thinking, and...”

“Okay,” she sighs, pretending not to notice the tears threatening to roll down his cheeks. “What’d he say?”

“He just- snf! He just got all quiet and he won’t talk to me and I tried apologizing but- fuck, what if I ruined the store? Or- or our marriage?!”

“David. Your marriage is fine. Your store is fine.”

David doesn’t look convinced. He sniffles and rubs his eyes and nose against the sleeve of his sweater. “This is just the last thing I wanted to happen today.”

“What does that mean?”

David shakes his head and rolls his eyes at himself, biting his lip for a moment as it quivers. “It’s stupid. I woke up and I wasn’t- ugh.” He sighs as if he knows what he’s about to say is stupid. “I think I’m getting sick.”

Now that Stevie gets a look at him, she realizes that his appearance is slightly more disheveled than usual. He’s wearing one of his older and more comfortable sweaters, and a skirt of soft fabric rather than tight jeans. What David wears says far more about how he’s feeling than anything else.

“So, you think you’re getting sick,” David nods. “and then you and Patrick got in an argument over something stupid.” Another nod. “Does he know that you ‘think’ you’re getting sick?”

David shakes his head. “I didn’t um, get around to mentioning that. Not that it matters,” he clears his throat, which by the wince he gives, is clearly bothering him. “I still did that stupid thing.”

“David, you’ve done a lot of stupid things. I don’t think this one is going to be the deal breaker.”

David closes his eyes and nods, because he knows she’s right. And he’d better. Stevie doesn’t just give out advice for no reason.

“So did you just leave him at the store?”

“I said I was going on a coffee run.”

“Okay, well, maybe you should tell him that you’re not feeling good.”

“What, so he’ll feel bad about getting rationally upset?”

“No,” Stevie says, patience wearing thin. “So he understands that you’re not as upset as he thinks you are.”

David blinks. “That’s the dumbest fucking thing I’ve ever heard.”

“Wow, okay. Just get out of here before he goes looking for you at the cafe. Maybe pick him up a tea?”

David nods and looks like he’s about to stand up before his breath catches. He has a second to turn away and pull his collar over his face before he lets out a coughing fit that has Stevie wincing in sympathy.

“Oh God,” she says.

He swallows with a pained look on his face, hand going to his throat. “Fuck,” he breathes out. “Do I...” David hesitates. “Do I feel warm to you?”

Stevie scoffs at first, because she’s heard that question from him a thousand times over that past few years, and it’s usually to get out of helping her with the motel. But as she reluctantly feels his forehead, she does find that he feels a little warmer than usual.

“Kind of,” she sighs. David groans. “But I think you’ll survive.”

He lets his head fall back for a moment and takes a deep, slightly crackly breath. “Okay,” he says decidedly. “I’m going to go apologize to my husband and convince him to give me the day off.” He stands up, fixes his sweater, and gives a wave.

“Good luck!”

“Thank you!”

-----

Stevie’s just getting ready to lock up the lobby for the night when her phone starts to ring. Seeing Patrick’s name, she really hopes that David didn’t drag her into the drama between the two of them when he showed up earlier.

She takes a deep breath and answers. “Hello?”

“Hey Stevie, I need a favor.”

Stevie blinks. “...okay?”

“Do you have a thermometer we could borrow? David’s got a temperature and it’s worrying me a little.”

Stevie’s already heading to the back and searching for the motel’s first aid kit. “You don’t have one? I would have thought between the two of you, you’d be prepared for anything.”

Patrick chuckles. “Yeah, I guess it kind of slipped our mind. We’ve just never needed one before this, but I just want to make sure I don’t need to take him to the hospital or something.” a distant, hoarse “nope!” can be heard in the background.

Stevie tries not to let the pang of worry take her over completely. She finds a blue plastic container in a cupboard and starts digging through until she sees the little case in which the motel’s old thermometer is kept.

“Found it. I’ll bring it over.”

Patrick breathes a sigh of relief. “Thanks so much. We owe you one.” followed by a “YOU owe her one, she gets nothing from me,” from her friend.

-----

Stevie knocks on the door. She’s holding the tan plastic case and wondering exactly how old the thing is. It still turns on, she checked before she left, and hopefully it’s still accurate.

How bad could David have gotten since she saw him earlier?

Patrick answers the door in his sweats and smiles before letting her in. Of course the first thing she sees is David on the couch in a dimly-lit living room, watching TV with an annoyed look.

“I’m not going to the hospital,” he says, probably to both of them. They’ve most likely been having this argument since Patrick called her. He definitely looks worse than before, and his voice is so rough it’s hard to listen to.

“It’ll just give me some peace of mind, David,” Patrick sighs, opening the case and handing it to his husband.

David is still glaring as he sits up and accepts it. “Has this been sanitized?”

“Yes.” She ran it under water, anyway, but she figures he’s not at risk of getting diseases from something that hasn’t been touched in a decade.

David winces at the reading it gives when it finally beeps, and hands it to Patrick. “It’s not that bad,” he mutters.

Patrick’s eyes widen. He breathes out and runs a hand through his hair. “Not hospital bad,” he agrees. “We’ll keep an eye on it.”

David huffs and goes back to tiredly watching TV in his couch nest.

Stevie thinks about staying just to bug him, but there’s a bottle of wine at home that she’s been looking forward to all day, along with the new season of a show on Netflix.

“I’m gonna head. Nice to know you’re not dying yet!” she yells to David.

“Thanks.”

Patrick walks her out to her car with his hands stuffed in his pockets the way he does when he wants to say something. So she waits, surprisingly patiently, pretending to struggle finding her car key when it’s the biggest one on the key ring.

“David talked to you today, didn’t he?”

Stevie clears her throat. “Uh, yeah. Yeah he did.” She leans against her car, watching Patrick fidget.

“We- we worked things out.”

“It’s none of my business,” she says quickly.

Patrick nods. “I know, I just... wanted to make sure you knew that things were good between him and I. I love him a lot.”

“I sure hope so. You married him, after all.”

Patrick smiles, impossibly sweet and stupidly heart-eyed as if he’s just remembered that oh yeah, he’s married to David. “Thanks for talking to him today.”

She decides that she’s not about to get sentimental this early in the evening. “Whatever,” Stevie says, unlocking her car. “I know you two are grossly in love, and that’s all I need to know. One argument doesn’t worry me.”

Patrick chuckles. “Thanks, Stevie. Oh! And we’ll get the thermometer back to you when-”

“Don’t worry about it,” she says over the car engine. “Take care of him!”

“I will!”

She knows he will.

Stevie drives away, chuckling to herself and feeling impossibly lucky to have these two morons in her life.
