Out of Town

Taliesin's phone vibrating startled him out of the light doze he'd dropped into while reading scripts in bed. His Roommates were long asleep by now, and he should be as well. He had to get to the studio early tomorrow and do some things for Marisha while she was out of town. The fact that someone was calling so late caused a pang of anxiety in his stomach. "Hello?"

"Hey Tal, it's Marisha."

"Hey! What's up?" he said, trying not to sound like he was nervous about her call.

Marisha sighed. She sounded tired. "Okay so I know it's late but is there any chance you can go over to Matt and I's place? Or are you like, in the middle of something?"

Taliesin sat up and swung his legs over the side of his bed. "I'm just reading scripts and shit. What's wrong?"

"It's not an emergency, but he's feeling pretty shitty. It's a stomach bug or something, and he claims to be fine but he sounds like hell. He didn’t want to bother you but he didn’t say no when I said I was gonna call you."

"Oh jeez," He stood up, already grabbing clothes. "Yeah, I'll get dressed and head over."

Marisha let out a heavy breath, sounding relieved. "Thank you. I just- it's making me nervous thinking about him being alone as unsteady as he sounds. And knowing him he'd spend the night on the bathroom floor then show up at the studio in the morning."

Taliesin chuckled. "Sounds about right."

"Thank you so much, seriously. You've got a key, right?"

"Absolutely."

-----

He thought about calling Matt on the ride there, but he figured Marisha would have let him know that Taliesin was on his way. It didn’t take him long at all to get there. Traffic was light that late at night.

Taliesin keyed himself into the house just as he'd done many times, and looking at the abandoned couch-nest, called out "Hello? Matt, you alive?"

It took a few seconds before he replied, "Yeah, I'm fine…" his voice rougher and far gone from its usual smoothness. Taliesin followed the voice to the bathroom and found his friend sitting on the bathroom floor, leaning into the corner next to the tub. His face was a sickly pale, hair stringy and damp, his t-shirt looked sweated through and his pajama pants were rumpled.

"Yeah, you look completely fine."

Matt groaned. "I'm sorry about Marisha calling you. It's too late for you to have to deal with this shit."

"I'm not sorry." Taliesin sat on the edge of the tub and reached out to pull some pieces of sweaty hair behind Matt's ear. His skin felt cool and clammy, and Taliesin tried to remember if he looked sick yesterday at the studio. "Did you feel sick this morning?"

Matt seemed to think about it. "A little bit, before Marisha left."

Taliesin made a troubled sound. "Why don’t we get you off the floor, hm? Do you feel like you're going to puke again?"

"I don’t… I don’t know." Matt croaked. He was completely defeated by whatever bug this was. "It hasn’t really… stopped."

"Hm, okay. Well, I'm going to help you up, alright? You'll be more comfortable in bed."

"But what if I-"

"It’s fine," Taliesin said with a wave of his hand. "I'll get a bucket or something."

He gave a slight nod, and allowed Taliesin to help him up. They got situated fairly easily despite the height difference and Taliesin's apparent lack of upper-body strength. Matt moaned quietly as he stood, put a hand on his head, and leaned more heavily on Taliesin.

"Headache?"

He nodded and dropped his head on Taliesin's shoulder. "Yeah," he rasped.

"You might be dehydrated… but we can deal with that later."

Matt hummed in agreement. "Can't keep anything down."

After a couple of steadying breaths, Matt stood up straighter and Taliesin led him out of the bathroom and over to the bedroom a few yards away. Matt put a hand on his stomach as he sat down on the side of the bed.

"Bucket?" Taliesin asked.

Matt shrugged unhelpfully, looking too nauseated to concentrate.

"Where is it, love?"

"Under…" he paused, swallowed. "Under the kitchen sink."

It was a trip downstairs and back up, but Taliesin managed to make it before Matt could throw up on the floor. He handed it over, and Matt gave an empty heave, and another. With a sigh, Taliesin carefully eased it out of his hands and set it on the floor next to the head of the bed.

He helped Matt pull his legs up and get comfortable, and covered him in a single blanket. He sat on the edge of the bed, frowning at the sight of his ill friend. Matt didn’t get this sick  often, he was usually the one taking care of other people.

"Does anything hurt worse than it should from just throwing up?"

Matt cracked his eyes open, a tired smile pulling at one edge of his mouth. "I don’t have appendicitis, buddy."

Ever since Taliesin's experience with appendicitis a few years back, he became a little freaked out whenever his friends threw up, eyes opening to the realization that vomiting could mean something serious. He knew it was irrational and he was being too much of a mom, but… he worried.

"Just humor me. Are you feeling… weirder than you think you should?"

A breathy chuckle. "I spent all day puking and shitting, so yeah."

At least he was feeling well enough to be annoying. "Don't you sass me, Mercer."

"I'm… no, I'm just sore and achy and…" he thought for a moment. "Bleh."

"Bleh, huh? That's pretty serious."

"They say it's uncurable."

Taliesin chuckled. "Alright, well now that I know that you're not dying, I'm going to go to the living room and let you get some rest, yeah? Shout if you need me."

Matt gave a slight nod. "Tell Marisha I'm okay."

"Uh-huh. Sure." Taliesin walked out and closed Matt's bedroom door most of the way then called Marisha.

"What's the situation?"

"Well, he told me to tell you he's okay. He's not, but he should be soon."

Marisha sighed. "I wish I could be there."

"I know, but he'll probably be feeling better by the time you get here." Taliesin wandered around in the kitchen in search for something he could give Matt.

"You don’t think he needs to go to an urgent care or something?"

"I… don’t think so. He mostly just seems worn out, so I'm going to stay and try to get some liquids in him at some point."

"You're staying? Thank fuck."

"I mean, he would do the same for me, and he might have to if this bug is as contagious as I've heard it is," It was half a joke, because of course he didn’t expect Matt to care for him. He had people at home to keep an eye on him. But their group did seem to share germs just like they shared everything else.

"You're literally the best and I love you," Was Marisha's response.

Taliesin snorted. "Well I love you too."

"Call me if anything changes?"

"I will, don’t worry."

"Thank you so much, Taliesin."

He smiled. "You're welcome."

As soon as she hung up, Taliesin went to work snooping about in the cupboard and the rest of the kitchen. His search was a success and soon he had a collection of crackers, gatorade, and a can of soup building on the counter. Matt wasn’t well enough to eat yet, but he would need to at some point, that and the gatorade should be able to save them an ER trip.

He was still looking around when he heard a round of gagging come from the upstairs bedroom. Fuck, Taliesin swore under his breath. He gathered the gatorade and crackers into his arms and made his way up the steps.

He found Matt looking utterly miserable, hunched over the bucket that had been on the bedside, trying to breathe and throw up nothing at the same time.

Taliesin dropped the things on the bed for the time being before sitting next to him. "Hey, hey," he soothed, hand very carefully stroking Matt's back. Matt gave a distressed whimper in return before finally gagging up a small stream of yellow slime. He heaved again, and - unsurprisingly - nothing came up. His stomach was obviously empty and it still insisted on making it emptier.

Taliesin was careful not to touch him too much right now, as more sweat had soaked through his shirt and caused a sheen on his skin. He reached for the bucket with hesitance and waited until Matt finally relinquished.

Matt eyed the bucket guiltily as Taliesin stood. "I'm sorry," it was barely above a whisper, and it broke Taliesin's heart. "You shouldn't have to-"

"Shh, none of that. This is what friends are for, right?"

Matt didn't say anything, but he seemed to offer a smile mostly for Taliesin's benefit.

It didn't take long at all to rinse the bucket. It wasn't like Matt had much in him to begin with.

Matt was idly scrolling through his phone when he returned. The frown on his face was too pronounced to be relaxed.

"Liam's sick too, just saw it in the group chat," he muttered. "guess something's going around the office."

That wasn't fun news to hear, but it wasn't surprising.

"He's got kids, though, so he was doomed from the start," Taliesin gathered up the crackers and gatorade and set them on the bedside. Matt looked nauseated just seeing them. "You don't have to eat the crackers, but try to drink when you think you can, alright?"

Matt took a while to process the sentence, and then nodded. "Just want to sleep right now, though."

Taliesin nodded, and hesitated before pressing a light kiss to Matt's temple. Matt leaned into it, smiling sleepily. "I'm gross."

"That's okay," Taliesin mumbled into Matt's hair, nuzzling him. "Get some rest. I'll be on the couch if you need me."

"Oh..."

Taliesin pulled away at the strange tone of voice to get a look at Matt. He looked upset.

"What's wrong?"

Matt shook his head. "Nothing, I just... I was going to say you could sleep in here, but you probably shouldn't. You could get sick and I'm not the best bedmate right now."

"I can certainly sleep in here. I just thought I'd give you some space."

"Space is the last thing I want right now."

And with that, Taliesin climbed into the bed. It felt strange, he'd never been in their bed before, or really spent much time in their bedroom at all. He got comfortable as Matt did as well, seeming to relax as soon as Taliesin joined him.

They both fell asleep immediately.

-----

Matt padded out to the kitchen with a yawn. He blinked at the kitchen until his eyes found Taliesin's.

Taliesin was sipping on a nice cup of coffee, something he needed if he was going to last the whole day at the studio after last night. He woke once in the night to Matt stumbling to the bathroom and didn’t go back to sleep for a while. It was nice in a way - the two of them had quiet conversation until they were both able to fall asleep again.

Still, Marisha and Matt had the good shit. Name brand coffee with an assortment of flavored creamers. He had a habit of taking advantage of their good coffee whenever he was over and today was no different.

"Morning," He greeted. "want some toast?"

Matt hesitated, and then shrugged. "Sure. But you can't be pissed at me if I puke it up."

Taliesin scoffed in faux exasperation. "All of my hard work gone to waste!"

He had Matt nibbling on toast by the time he was ready to leave for the studio, thermos of a second cup of fancy coffee in-hand. "Are you sure you'll be alright?"

"I think last night was the worst of it," Matt said. Taliesin wasn’t so sure he believed him. These bugs seemed to stick around longer than a day, and that toast would probably not stay down forever, but he didn’t argue. He had work to do and Matt was an adult.

"Just… just text me if you need anything, alright?"

"Promise."

"Good. Take it easy. I'm going to pick some things up from my house after I get done at the studio, and then I'll be back over if that's cool with you."

"Oh, you're coming back?" Matt looked genuinely surprised, possibly a little confused.

"I mean, yeah. I think Marisha would be more comfortable if she knew I was here with you until you were less in danger of passing out in the bathroom."

Matt chuckled. "Sounds good to me."

-----

Work seemed to go on forever. He had way too many things to oversee and to stop from going wrong. How on earth did Marisha do this every day? He was on his third cup of coffee when Taliesin realized that it stopped having an effect.

When Dani asked him about his lunch order, he was forced to turn it down because the coffee had already done enough to abolish his appetite. It wasn’t a good thing, but food just didn’t sound good to him. He'd get something later.

"Are you sure? You haven't eaten like, at all today."

Taliesin chuckled, checking his watch. "It's one in the afternoon."

"Yeah, but you're a snacker," Dani said with a shrug. "Eh, whatever. Just let me know if you change your mind."

And she was off taking everyone else's lunch orders.

Yeah, he was usually hungry around this time, wasn't he? No matter how much coffee he drank.

He was getting increasingly tired and the coffee was sitting like a pit in his stomach instead of giving him the energy he needed to finish out the work day. He forced his thoughts to stay on the task at hand, another thing on Marisha's list.

The pit in his stomach was turning to a bubbling, gurgling ache. Taliesin began to dread what it meant. He grunted, discretely pushing his hand into his stomach as if that would alleviate the pain. It didn't.

"Hey Taliesin," he pried his eyes from the computer screen to see Dani. Her eyebrows knitted together in concern. "Are you feeling okay?"

Taliesin stammered with the answer. How did he answer that when he wasn’t even sure himself? "I… yeah, yeah I'm fine. Why?"

"Okayyyy…" she raised an eyebrow. "Ard you sure you're not coming down with that bug that Liam and Matt have? 'cause I know that they started off being really tired and you've seemed super out of it all day."

Nausea suddenly hit him like a punch in the gut. He pressed a hand to his mouth with uncertainty as it began to over salivate. His gut gurgled angrily now, as if it were warning him for what was to come. He gave Dani an apologetic look.

Of course it ended with him puking in the studio's bathroom.

Dani sat with him until he felt well enough to drive to Matt and Marisha's. He didn’t have the energy to stop at home, he could do without a change of clothes for another night, and he could borrow Matt's phone charger if he needed to.

"I'm gonna… I should probably have a bag or something with me in case…" he didn’t finish before Dani was off on a mission. He had a feeling that he wouldn't make it without puking in the car, as much as he wanted.

She returned with a couple grocery bags and a sympathetic expression.

He thanked her, apologized, and reluctantly gave up the list of remaining tasks. "Please, for the love of God, Dani. Don’t catch this."

"I'll try. Feel better!"

-----

Matt had returned to his couch nest, and pretty much stayed there the whole day, save for the couple of times he had to get up to vomit. He'd really hoped that was the end of it, but his stomach was still unsteady and he was starting to feel feverish again.

He was bored, and just calculating how many more hours before Taliesin came home when - speak of the devil - he walked in the door.

"Hey!" Matt's smiled quickly faded. "You look like shit."

Taliesin's eyes were watery, his face red. His voice was nasally when he spoke, "Yeah, I kind of threw up on the way here. And at the studio."

"Fuck."

Taliesin scoffed in agreement.

"I'm sorry, buddy."

"Oh psh, you didn’t do anything," he clutched a tied bag in his hand and awkwardly hid it away. He pressed a hand to his stomach with a uneasy expression and disappeared into the bathroom. Matt winced, listening to the sickening gags.

He thought about going to check on Taliesin, but decided it might be best to give him his space.

He just... Wanted to do something to make up for this.

"I'm really sorry," Was the first thing out of his mouth when Taliesin emerged, looking miserable and exhausted. He moved over on the couch, lifting the blanket.

Taliesin gave a tired smile, taking the offer and laying his head on Matt's shoulder. He let the rest of the blanket on Matt's lap. Probably a good thing, because while Taliesin was sweating, Matt was shivering.

"It's not your fault, Matt." He sat up and looked him over. "Fuck, I forgot to ask how you're feeling."

Matt chuckled. "You were kind of busy when you walked in." He thought about it, taking in his achy stomach and his exhausted body. He doesn't feel in immediate danger of puking, but he knows that that could change any minute. "I've been better. Can't really keep food down right now. Sorry about your toast."

"Damn it, I worked all morning on that toast."

Matt snorted. "Maybe we should think about getting in bed for the rest of the day and try to sleep this off. Hm?"

-----

"Aw…"

They both woke to the voice, the sound of rustling. Marisha grinned as she looked at the two of them, and Taliesin realized that they must be quite the sight.

Matt was wrapped tightly in the blankets while Taliesin was too overheated to bother touching them. Still, they were cuddled together more for comfort than warmth.

"I kinda figured this is what would happen," She said as she sat on Matt's side of the bed. His eyes fluttered open as she brushed some hair out of his face.

"Hey," he croaked. "You're home already?"

"Finished early. Well, I convinced them to let me record my lines. How you feeling?"

"Mm okay. Taliesin's sick."

Marisha scoffed and she and Taliesin locked eyes for a moment. "Yeah, I see that. Nice going."
