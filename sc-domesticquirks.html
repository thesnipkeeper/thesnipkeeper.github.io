Title: Domestic Quirks
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David remembers that Patrick doesn't like being alone when he's sick
Word count: 1k

Domestic Quirks

David never thought he would be this lucky. He never thought that he’d be married to someone who loved him as much as Patrick did, to someone he loved as much as he did Patrick.

He never thought that he’d have a relationship in which he’d find it endearing when the other person was sick as a dog.

And yet, he found himself smiling warmly at his husband, who was struggling to get dressed while sniffling consistently and disgustingly. Patrick was weak and slow-moving, which made it easy for David to push him back into bed.

“You are not going to work like this,” David said with all of the love in the world.

Patrick gave a congested huff. He looked like he wanted to argue, but he must have given into his exhaustion as he smiled sleepily.

“Are you sure?”

“Yes, absolutely.” David wandered about the room, grabbing his shoes and socks. Patrick pulled his legs back onto the bed with a snuffle, and David took a seat by him.

“The store’s been busy lately...”

“I can handle it! And your whole... snot situation,” he said, gesturing to his adorably sniffly husband. “Is not really on brand.”

Patrick chuckled. “Yeah, I guess that’s fair.”

David smiled. He was so filled with warmth. This was his life now. Domestic and comfortable and all with the love of his life. Patrick must have seen the sappy look on his face, because he raised an eyebrow but didn’t say anything.

“It’ll be fine.” He cupped his husband’s cheek, feeling the heat to go with the flush. With a frown, he moved the back of his hand to Patrick’s forehead. “You have a temperature.”

Patrick’s brows knitted together in confusion before he sighed. “Great.”

David smiled and kissed his cheek. “No... housework or anything, okay? Just stay in bed and like, rest like you’re supposed to.”

Patrick didn’t look too thrill with the idea, but he nodded reluctantly. “I’ll miss you.”

That warm feeling in David’s chest just blossomed even more. “I’ll miss you too.”

-----

The store was busy. David hardly had a chance to sit down before the next customer was asking him a question. He supposed he should be happy - and he was! - that their business had become such a success. It was another thing David never saw himself as. The owner of a store in a small town.

He breathed a sigh of relief when the last customer of the rush finally walked out. They sold a lot of stuff today, but he was ready for a break. Only then did he decide to check his phone.

Two texts and a missed call from Patrick.

A pang of worry began to eat at him as he opened the texts.

Patrick: how’s the store?

Patrick: is everything going okay?

David chuckled. Of course Patrick would be worried about the store when he was at home sick.

The missed call was recent, just fifteen minutes ago. David decided to take advantage of the empty store and call him back.

“Hey?” Patrick sounded like he’d been sleeping, or trying to.

“Hey, how are you feeling?”

There was a wet sniffle. David was about to remind him to maybe blow his nose before he heard another sniffle. He frowned and stood up. His husband was crying.

“...Patrick?”

“Ugh...” he sniffed, and there was a distant rustling that sounded like he might be rubbing his face. “Sorry, hi. I’ll live.” His voice was rough, strained.

“Are you crying?” He didn’t give his husband a chance to answer before he was asking the next question. “Are you okay? Is something going on? Do I need to come home?” Or... few questions.

Patrick gave a watery chuckle. “No, I just missed you. Wanted to... talk to you. For a while.”

Oh.

Patrick didn’t get sick very often. He was in good shape, and David probably wasn’t prepared to deal with something gross like a cold early in their relationship anyway. But he did recall the last time Patrick was sick.

It was about six months ago, he’d finally come down with the flu that had been going around town. He wanted to be near David at all times, whether it be just sitting with him while he did his hair or talking him into watching a movie.

Patrick was clingy as fuck when he wasn’t feeling well.

How did David forget that? God, he was a terrible husband!

“Okay,” David smiled, sitting back down. “What do you want to talk about?”

“Are... are the new goat milk soaps selling?”

“Oh,” David scoffed. “Amazingly.”

They talked off and on for the rest of the day. David would say goodbye every time a custom walked in and call him back once they left.

Patrick slowly seemed to get less teary and emotional the more they talked. And eventually it was closing time.

-----

David walked in with containers of the diner’s finest soup. He nudged the door closed with his shoulder and made his way upstairs.

Patrick was up, reading a book with a tissue in his other hand as he sniffled, this time just from illness. His face was still red, and the bed was a mess of tangled blankets as if he spent the better part of the day tossing and turning.

“Welcome home,” Patrick smiled and closed his book as David kicked his shoes off and sat on the bed.

David was about to hand him his soup when he found himself engulfed in a tight, overly-warm hug. Patrick kissed his temple with all of the affection in the world.

“Wow,” David said with a laugh. “You must have really missed me.”

“I did.” Patrick opened his container of soup and accepted the spoon David handed him.

“And I now know not to leave you alone for too long when you’re sick.”

Patrick frowned, and suddenly David felt guilty. Maybe it was one of those things that you just didn’t acknowledge?

“I’m sorry about that...”

“No, no, don’t apologize.” David gave him a quick kiss on the cheek. “I mean are you kidding? I have so many stupid quirks, I think that you’re allowed to have one.”

Patrick chuckled. “Well, thanks for that.”

“Now, eat your soup.”

“Don’t have to tell me twice.”
