Reading One's Mind

“Bless you, Dr. Bright.”

Joan sighed in mere frustration at her body. She pressed a napkin to her nose and sniffled. “Thank you, Chloe."

They were supposed to be meeting Sam at a coffee shop to discuss the situation with Damien and Mark. Sam had just gotten back, and Joan was eager to see her again. It was likely that she’d leave early and Sam and Chloe would use that time just to catch up. (The two of them were actual friends, after all.)

She dismissed it when the sore throat from the day before turned out to be a full-blown cold that morning. At the time, it was a runny nose, a bit of a cough, some sneezes here and there. Of course the cold chose now, when she sat in a crowded cafe, to get worse.

“We’ll make this quick, don’t worry. You don’t have to stick with us all day.”

Joan nodded. She doubted they wanted her to stick with them all day, anyway.

“No, you’re free to if you want! It’s just... you don’t sound good. And I can hear you complaining about how bad you feel and how you wish you were at home right now...”

There was no hiding anything from a telepath. She sniffled wetly. At least she didn’t have to speak to Chloe, save her voice a little. God, was her nose ever going to stop running?

Chloe pulled some napkins from the table’s dispenser and slid them over with a smile.

Joan sheepishly accepted them. “Thank you,” she repeated. She was forced to blow her nose, which had a few people looking in their direction. Ugh...

As if things couldn’t get any worse, a sneeze crept out. Chloe winced, and Joan wasn’t sure if it was a reaction to the sneeze itself or other’s thoughts regarding it.

Colds didn’t used to be this bad. They... they were certainly easier back in the day when they could serve a purpose.

“So...” Chloe started, clearing her throat and suddenly looking awkward. “It’s none of my business, but... I’ve noticed something interesting about your... thoughts.”

Joan raised an eyebrow, mouth quirking up in a curious smile. Where was this going? “My thoughts, yes. What about them?”

“Well, every time you sneeze, you think of Agent Green.”

Joan froze.

“Like his face just flashes for a second and-”

No. No no. This couldn’t be happening. “Chloe, that’s not-” but of course the thoughts and memories were flooding her mind now. Because Chloe just had to ask.

Chloe’s eyes widened in realization. She gasped and quickly covered her mouth. “Oh! Oh, God, Dr. Bright, I- I didn’t mean to... shit...”

Joan sat there, horrified. Her heart pounded, her thoughts were racing (and by the look on Chloe’s face, she could tell). It took her a few seconds for the shock to wear off before she sat back and cleared her throat. “There’s just no keeping any secrets around you, is there?”

Chloe frowned. She did look remorseful, and Joan almost felt bad for making her feel bad. Almost. But it was hard to when a former patient had just learned one of the deepest parts of her... past relationships.

“Okay, you can’t exactly blame me! When I asked, I thought there was some kind of interesting explanation, not- not...” her eyebrow quirked up. She was listening again. “Wow, Dr. Bright,” she cooed, almost sounding impressed.

No no no, this wasn’t happening. “Chloe!” she snapped, face red. She’d probably regret it later, but right now she didn’t feel well and didn’t like her mind being probed against her will.

“Sorry!” Chloe raised her hands in a non-threatening manner. And then she leaned in closer to quietly say, “Look, I don’t kink shame, okay? I’ve seen much worse in people’s heads. This isn’t even top fifty.” And then her expression faded into sincerity, with an “Aww, no wonder you liked dating him. He was the only person that you could be with like that.”

Joan was, once again, horrified. “You just learned one of my most embarrassing secrets and you think it’s cute?”

“Kinda, yeah.”

She sniffed, rubbed her nose with a napkin that was already damp. “Let’s stop talking about this, please.”

“Uh-! Yeah, yeah of course.”

“I’m serious, Chloe, please.”

“I know!”

Part of her was thankful that it was Chloe who found out. The least judgmental person on the planet.

Also, the most talkative person on the planet.

She couldn’t help worrying about it.

Sam arrived a few minutes later.

The three of them talked, and Joan’s cold progressively got worse. It was hard for her to keep track of the conversation, and to avoid Chloe’s looks when she thought Joan didn’t notice. Every time she sneezed, the same look.

Sam looked between the two of them with a dramatic groan. “Okay, what is going on with you two? You’ve been acting weird toward each other since I got here. Did I miss something?”

Chloe cleared her throat and shook her head vigorously. “Nope!”

She wasn’t fantastic at being inconspicuous. Chloe glared at her for that thought.

Sam didn’t look convinced. “O-okay then.”

-----

Joan put the conversation with Chloe out of her mind, and kept it that way for good, long time. It wasn’t even something she thought about anymore, it wasn’t as if her sex life even existed these days, and-

And that wouldn’t change anything. Because Chloe was right, Owen was the only person. The only person who knew, who reciprocated like that.

That was in her past. In their past.

Until she started working with him at the AM.

She liked to think that there was nothing between them, because if there was one thing Joan Bright was good at, it was ignoring her feelings. But there were still those moments, where their hands would brush or their eyes would linger on each other for a little too long.

It went on like that for a good few months before they found themselves alone together in the break room.

Owen was nervous, she could feel it. He didn’t hide it well around her, she just didn’t know why he was nervous. “Joan, um,”

“Can you hand me the powder creamer?”

“Hm? Oh, yes,” he slid the container her way and she went to work putting it in her coffee. “Joan...”

She chuckled. “Yes, Owen, out with it.” It was amusing until she looked up and saw how terrified he looked. “Wait... what’s wrong?”

“Nothing! Nothing’s wrong!” He said immediately. “I was just wondering-”

“Owen.”

“Do you want to go out for dinner sometime?” But it was said so quickly that Joan had to take a moment to piece together what she’d just been asked. And then another moment to process what she’d just been asked.

“Um,” she cleared her throat of the shock, trying to continue to act casual despite the nervousness that flooded her. “Yes, that... that would be nice.”

Owen’s eyes widened even more than they already were. “O-okay. Awesome. Tomorrow’s Friday... which, well, you already know, of course, but-”

Joan couldn’t help the excited smile that came over her. “How’s seven?”

He nodded, the same smile tugging at his lips. “Seven’s perfect. I’ll pick you up.” He turned to leave.

“Owen!”

“Hm?”

“Your coffee...?” she pointed to the abandoned mug on the counter.

“Right! Of course...” he took his coffee and turned to leave once again.

Joan couldn’t stop smiling the rest of the day.

-----

They were trying to take it slowly, casually. Things weren’t going to go back to the way they were after a couple of dates. They still had things to talk about, about Mark, and the AM. Joan just wanted to enjoy the honeymoon phase for a bit.

No one could tell, at least she assumed that no one could tell. She hoped. Owen wasn’t the most subtle person in the universe.

It was nice. It was really nice.

And then one morning, Joan woke up with a cold.

She didn’t think about what it meant until she was already at work, sanitizer and tissues in tow, prepared to push through the whole day as normal.

“Bless you,” Sam said as they walked in together. “You sound like you’re catching a cold?”

“Oh,” Joan chuckled. “It’s thoroughly caught, I think.”

Sam hummed sympathetically. “Well, I hope you feel better. I have some cough drops in my desk, so you can always stop by if you need some.”

Joan nodded her thanks. “Right now it’s just... a lot of sneezing, apparently...” she trailed off, eyes going foggy, before muffling a sneeze into her arm.

“Bless you again.” Sam rubbed her back and squeezed her should before going about her own work.

Joan did her best to work through it. The cold was really more of a nuisance than anything else. She didn’t feel particularly awful, just annoyed. Annoyed at the liquid dripping from her nose and her watery eyes, and the constant itch that lingered in the back of her sinuses and warned her not to be too far from tissues.

So no, she didn’t realize the significance of a cold like this until, once again, she and Owen were alone in the break room.

She was in the middle of blowing her nose when he walked in with an empty coffee cup.

He cleared his throat and smiled softly. “Sam told me you weren’t feeling well,” he said. “I think she was worried I was going to give you too much work or something.”

Joan chuckled. “Even if you did, I think I’d live. It’s just a cold. An annoying one, but just a cold.”

He nodded. Owen set his cup under the coffee machine and turned it on. It buzzed, he leaned against the counter and waited. Joan herself had made a cup of tea that was doing absolutely nothing to help things.

Clearly, as she was still sneezing like her life depended on it.

“Bless-” Owen cut himself off, cleared his throat again. “Bless you. This is a little strange, isn’t it?” He asked, like she knew the answer already.

“What do you mean?”

“I think you know what I mean, Joan,” his voice was quiet, almost too quiet, as if he hoped she wouldn’t hear.

She was just thankful she wasn’t alone in the feeling.

Joan wasn’t sure she could do this right now. “It doesn’t have to be strange, Owen.” Her heart wasn’t in that denial. And her nose still itched. She sneezed again, another one following on its tail. She pulled a napkin from one of the dispensers and pressed it to her nose with an annoyed sniffle.

Owen averted his gaze to the coffee machine. He was uncomfortable, awkward, and it’d be entertaining if it wasn’t already... well, alright, it was strange. He was right.

“I should go back to my office...”

“Joan-”

Before Joan had a chance to ask him what he needed, they were kissing.

Joan only had to pull away to breathe. She looked up at him as his arms wrapped around her. She looked into his eyes for a few seconds before saying a quiet, “Oh boy.”

He hummed in agreement.

“Owen,” she said, setting a hand on his chest before he could kiss her again.

“Hm?”

“Your office has a lock.”

He nodded, giving a breathless chuckle. “That it does.”

-----

Sam had asked her to lunch, and after the busy past couple of days, Joan couldn’t help but agree to it.

“How’s your cold?”

“Clearing up finally,” Joan said.

Sam nodded. “You definitely sound a lot better. Your voice was hard to listen to for a little while there.”

Joan giggled. “Well gee, thank you.”

Sam laughed, the two of them went silent as they ate.

“So,” Sam started as she stirred her cup of soup. She got the same soup and sandwich every time they came here, Joan was thankful to have a friend who was so predictable. One thing in her life she could understand. “How long have you and Agent Green been seeing each other?”

Never mind.

Joan froze. “Excuse me?”

Sam’s eyes went wide. “Sorry, am I- am I assuming things?”

She thought about lying, telling Sam that she was just imagining things. But it was Sam. “N-no, I just... didn’t realize that it was that obvious.”

“It’s not!” Sam assured. “I mean, not a lot of people know that you two used to be an item. I guess I sort of figured it out when he came down with a cold a couple of days after you,” she chuckled. Joan’s face felt hot. “You’re usually so careful when you’re sick, I figured that if he caught it, then the two of you must be...”

Joan nodded, and couldn’t hold back the smile. “Yeah, yes, we uh... we’ve been seeing each other for a few weeks now.”

“Oh, Joan,” Sam said, with her own wide smile. “I’m so happy for you. But if he hurts you, I will hurt him ten times worse.”

“Understood!” she said with a scoff. “We’ll see. Right now it’s just-”

“Fun?”

Joan blinked. “Now you’re the telepath, huh?”

She shrugged, giggled. “Maybe I’ve picked some things up from Chloe. Whatever it is, I’m glad you’re happy.”

“Thank you, Sam.”

She was happy. And Chloe wouldn’t be finding out about this for a long time if she could help it.
