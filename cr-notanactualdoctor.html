Not an Actual Doctor

"You're certainly looking more like yourself!" Molly greeted from the kitchen island, wincing at the congestion in his voice. Caleb wondered how he'd recovered faster than Molly. Was that a tiefling thing?

"Ja, I feel much better. And you?"

Molly smiled. "Well, I'm not a hundred-percent, but-" He tore away just in time to give a few wet-sounding coughs. It went on for a little while before he was done. With a reluctant look, he tore off a paper towel and pressed it to his mouth to spit out whatever those coughs had brought up. "Fuck, my apologies."

Caleb furrowed his brows as his weary mind tried to work out what was happening. "Still have that cough, then? You should be taking it easy." he looked past the tiefling to the porridge on the stove, enough for all of them.

"Yeah, I can't seem to shake it." Molly said, clearing his throat. He waved his hand dismissively. "It's alright. If you can bounce back, I can as well!"

Caleb really hoped so.

But Molly coughed all through breakfast, sounding worse and worse by the minute. Everyone exchanged concerned looks with each coughing fit before Jester piped up.

"Molly, that cough sounds really bad."

Molly waved his hand once again. "It's nothing to worry about, really."

Jester didn’t look convinced, but she seemed to realize she couldn’t do much about it. She had made an attempt to use healing magic on the both of them when they were bedridden, and learned that - while it did wonders for broken bones and cuts - healing was useless on the flu.

"Will you let me examine you? I'm going to be a doctor, you know. It could be really good practice!"

Caleb saw right through her, and he loved it. Jester really was a precious soul when she wasn’t drawing dicks in his notebooks.

Molly, rather entertained by the idea, agreed to it.

Soon, Beau and Fjord had already gone to work and Jester was grabbing her medical bag. She had a brightly-colored stethoscope that went nicely with absolutely nothing but she loved it and that was all that mattered. Jester ushered Molly into his bedroom this time and Caleb could hear a bout of hacking once the two reached the top of the stairs.

He heard bits of voices here and there as he loaded their breakfast dishes into the dishwasher, and let his curiosity get the best of him. Caleb climbed the stairs, taking a deep breath at the top when his formerly flu-ridden body had had enough of the moving around.

Molly's door wasn’t closed. Neither tieflings had a sense of privacy on a good day, so he only felt slightly awkward stopping in front of the room to see what was going on. Both of them glanced up at the sound of his footsteps, and Molly - now shirtless and in nothing but his pajama bottoms - smiled.

He gasped as Jester pressed the stethoscope to his back.

"Sorry! I thought I warmed it up!"

"If you're sensitive to cold touch, perhaps you are still running a temperature," Caleb suggested to Molly.

Molly shook his head. "My fever broke yesterday. I'm not-" he stopped, eyes widening as Jester palmed his forehead.

She frowned. "Ohhh dear, okay, well… I'm going to listen to your lungs first, okay? And then you can put your shirt back on if you are cold."

Molly did as she instructed and took a deep breath in. Easy enough. She listened intently, the stethoscope pressed to his back. The exhaling seemed to be a bit more trouble. Jester recoiled when Molly stumbled into a coughing fit, struggling to turn away from her. He looked miserable, now that Caleb really saw. "Again?" Jester said, seeing as well how miserable he was and asking for permission. Molly nodded, and managed to inhale and exhale with success.

Jester winced. "You have a lot of icky stuff in your airways, you know."

"I'm aware, thank you," Molly replied breathlessly, muffling a weak cough behind his hand. He accepted the shirt Jester threw his way and was quick to slip it on.

Caleb leaned against the doorway and watched the blue tiefling dig into her bag, pulling out a case with a thermometer. Molly leaned in without a word from either of them and allowed her to press it inside his ear. He looked exhausted, unfocused eyes trained on the floor as they waited for the beep.

Jester's face fell at the reading. "Oh Molly, no wonder you feel so shitty."

"Is it that bad?"

"I mean, yeah, kind of." She put her things away while Molly grabbed the throw folded on the end of his bed and wrapped it around himself. Jester turned to him and sighed, shoulders falling as if she was about to tell him he was dying. "Okay, so I'm not an actual doctor yet and legally you shouldn’t take my diagnosis seriously…"

Molly chuckled. "Of course."

"Buuut, I think you have bronchitis."

"Not pneumonia, though?" Caleb asked.

"I don’t think so? He probably would have a much higher fever and stuff. But Molly, you should rest for a little while longer, okay?"

Molly looked defeated, and it was not a good look on the overly-confident man. He gave a gurgly sigh. "You're the doctor, I suppose."

"No, but almost!"

Molly was impossible to keep in bed. Caleb understood. The two of them had been bedridden for days already, and to be sick all over again must have been frustrating. Coughs could be heard throughout the house the entire day, fits that sometimes went on until it morphed into breathless gagging. It was when Caleb was at his desk writing that he saw Molly dart out of his room, still coughing, and into the bathroom. He heard hard heaves and the splash of liquid, along with coughing that still wouldn’t stop.

Ah, vomiting then.

Caleb mentioned it in passing when he went downstairs to make a sandwich for dinner, and immediately felt guilty when he heard Jester as he returned upstairs.

"Caleb said you threw up. Are you alright? Should I take you to an actual doctor?"

Cheeks burning with embarrassment and guilt, Caleb rushed into his and Nott's room before he heard Molly's response.

He didn’t agree to a doctor, Caleb knew that much, because Molly was home for the rest of the day. He wandered out to the living room as everyone had gathered to watch mindless television, looking disheveled and red in the face, most likely from coughing and fever both.

"Hey," Fjord was the first to greet him, the pleasant, gentle tone that seemed to accompany his voice. "Feeling any better?"

Molly groaned. "Worse. So much fucking worse." he turned his head to cough into his arm.

"What are you doing out of bed, anyway?" Jester asked.

"Well," Molly was already wandering away behind them. "I'm hungry!" There was clanking in the kitchen and the sound of pouring cereal.

"He's been coughing so hard he throws up!" Jester whispered loudly to the others.

"Yes, yes I have!" Called the purple tiefling. "Everyone should definitely know that!"

-----

The coughing persisted through the night. Nott and Caleb's room shared a wall with Molly's, meaning they could hear everything. The sputtering coughs, the violent tossing and turning.

Nott groaned, yellow eyes fluttering open and brows furrowing in annoyance. "It's impossible to sleep when he's coughing like that." she sighed. "But poor Molly, I supposed."

Caleb hadn't bothered trying to sleep. He felt creepy, but he'd been listening to their new friend for a while, wondering if he should do something.

"I should do something to help…"

"Like what?" Nott asked, voice still sleepy.

Making tea wasn’t a very complicated process. Caleb managed to get a nice cup made, ignoring the fact that he had work in the morning and probably shouldn’t be up and about this late in the night.

Knock knock

A quick punctuating cough, and a hoarse, congested, "...Yeeees?"

Caleb took a deep breath and opened the door. A bedside lamp flooded the room with just enough warm light. Molly sat up straight, eyebrow raised in amusement. He had a stack of pillows behind him, propping him up as Jester had instructed him to do.

"Oh. Well hello there, Mister Caleb. To what do I owe this surprise visit?" He sounded awful.

"It's very late, but I thought some tea might help with your cough. It's the kind that Jester drinks when she's not feeling well, I didn't think she'd mind if I borrowed some."

Molly hummed, a smile tugging at his lips. "I was keeping you up wasn't I?"

"Please, I've slept through your incessant coughing and sneezing for nearly a week. Nott, however, is less of a heavy sleeper."

Molly nodded. "Give her my- my apologies..." He said before hastily turning to cough again.

A voice sounded from the other room. "IT'S FINE JUST GET BETTER!"

Caleb stepped in awkwardly and handed the steaming mug to Mollymauk who accepted it with a smile. He took a sip.

"Wow, this is really good."

Caleb nodded. "I'm glad."

Moly scooted over and patted the spot next to him. "Come, sit, keep me company until I can breathe properly."

Caleb hesitated. "Ah... I probably shouldn't. I go back to work in the morning."

"And you will be slightly sleep-deprived because you were helping a sick friend."

Couldn't argue with that.

Molly snuggled against him, humming happily as they got comfortable. Caleb could fall asleep right there with the warm body next to him. The room was silent for a few minutes, save for Mollymauk's wheezing breaths. He managed to sip the tea, gradually draining the cup. The coughing had subsided a little.

"You know, you told me your whole story. Perhaps I owe you mine."

"That's alright, Mollymauk. You need rest and so do I."

He didn’t plan to fall asleep in Molly's bed. He certainly didn’t expect to wake up an hour before he needed to.

Molly sat up, having slid down horizontally in the night, and gave a wheezy coughing fit. He fumbled for a tissue and clamped it over his mouth, face going red. "So-… rry…" he choked out when he saw that Caleb had woken up, giving one more cough and trying discretely to spit the result of the fit into the tissue.

Caleb yawned and sat up. "Guten morgen."

Molly raised an eyebrow, and Caleb couldn’t for the life of him figure out what he'd done wrong until the cogs turned in his sleep-fogged brain. He shook his head of the cloud.

"Good morning, I meant to say." He had a habit of not speaking in the morning, Nott told him, and it sometimes came out in German before he realized it. That was part of the reason Nott knew the German that she did.

Molly smiled. "Good morning to you as well, Mister Caleb. I see you couldn’t get enough of sharing a bed with me."

Caleb rolled his eyes. "Apparently so. I'm going to put on some coffee and hope I can stay awake at work today."

"Give Bryce my apologies."

Caleb filled his body with caffeine and breakfast, conversing with the other as he did. Jester gave him waggly eyebrows when he mentioned falling asleep in Molly's room, and then mentioned that Nott ended up in her bed.

Bryce smiled warmly when Caleb walked into the shop. "Oh my, you look…"

Caleb scoffed. "Ja. I am aware."

"Are you still feeling poorly?"

Caleb shook his head. "My roommate. He offers his apologies for keeping me awake," Caleb couldn’t say it without a fond smile. "I ah… made him tea in the middle of the night."

Bryce chuckled. "He's forgiven. This wouldn’t happen to be Mollymauk, would it?"

"Yes, it would."

Bryce had a strange, knowing look before they started on their work.
