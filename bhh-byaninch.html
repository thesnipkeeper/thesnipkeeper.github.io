Fandom: Blake Holsey High
Summary: Marshall has a date tonight, therefore he is absolutely fine
Word count: 2k

By an Inch

Lucas threw his textbooks in his bag with a yawn. He was looking forward to getting some breakfast; maybe that would wake him up a little bit?

His groggy attention went to his roommate as Marshall emerged from the bathroom. He looked ten times more tired than Lucas currently felt.

"Whoa. You don't look so hot." 

"I don't feel so hot." Marshall wandered over like a zombie and sat on the edge of his bed. He didn't have the energy to do anything else but just stare at the floor and wish he hadn't just spent the morning throwing up. 

Lucas wanted to ask how he was, if he needed anything, but instead he decided on a more fitting, “Hope you’re planning on staying in bed today.” It was certainly a threat. Lucas hadn’t forgotten the last time Marshall was sick. Only Marshall could start an entire school outbreak just by being a computer nerd.

Marshall responded with the most pathetic, apologetic expression that had Lucas throwing his last book in his backpack. 

“Seriously?”

“Okay, look-!” Marshall stood up quickly and regretted it, swaying and closing his eyes until the dizziness passed. Lucas hovered in case he had to catch his stupid friend before he could fall and split his head open, but thankfully it didn’t come to that. “I can’t be sick today,” Marshall said with a desperate plea, as if either of them could do anything about it. His eyes darted around, and with a defeated sigh he sat back down. “It’s Friday, Lucas.”

“Yeah, so...?” Lucas paused. “Ah.”

Marshall nodded, groaned rather dramatically, and fell back onto his bed. Lucas resisted the urge to remind him that class started in ten minutes and he should really start getting dressed if he planned on going.

Instead, he sat on Marshall’s bed and gave a heavy sigh. “Dude, you can’t seriously want to go on a date.”

“I mean, I want to see him,” Marshall said quietly. His voice was hoarse from vomiting, but Lucas could have sworn that the way it broke for a moment wasn’t from being sick. “We’ve been so busy with midterms, and we haven’t really had a chance to hang out. God, this date was the only thing getting me through midterms!”

Lucas patted his leg. “Sorry, Marshall, but I think you’re going to have to cancel.”

“Maybe I’ll feel better by tonight.”

“Uh, yeah, I doubt that.”

Marshall huffed and sat up. “I have to be. Come on, Lucas, get ready! We’re going to be late.”

Lucas rolled his eyes and headed for the door, grabbing his backpack on the way. “Nope, you’re going to be late. Better get dressed!”

He was nearly out the door when he realized his friend hadn’t responded with something snarky, or anything at all. Lucas turned back and found Marshall with a hand over his mouth. 

He groaned and ran back to the bathroom, followed by the horrible retching that had kept Lucas up most of the morning. Lucas sighed.

-----

When the late bell rang, Lucas thought that Marshall might have finally come to his senses and stayed in the dorm. No, of course not. 

Professor Z had just started taking role call when Marshall rushed in.

“Sorry I’m late, Professor Z!” Marshall said. To his credit, he was dressed and his hair was styled as it usually was, but there was no mistaking the obvious illness and sleep-deprivation. No mistaking for Lucas, anyway.

Z nodded and smiled. “Just try not to let it happen again. Take a seat, Marshall.”

Lucas gave him a look that Marshall pointedly ignored. “Seriously?”

“I’ll be fine,” Marshall said without looking away from his textbook. And then his eyes went to the front of the classroom, where David was sitting.

Lucas shook his head and flipped to the page Z sent them to, ignoring the fact that he could hear his friend’s stomach churning. Marshall shifted uncomfortably throughout class. For a second, Lucas thought he was going to throw up in class, but instead, the bell rang and Marshall was the first out the door.

-----

The next period was breakfast. Josie, Corinne, Vaughn and Lucas were the ones at the table. David was still getting breakfast, and Marshall hadn’t been seen since he darted out of science class. 

Lucas was just starting to wonder if he should check on Marshall.

“Where is he?” Josie said, following Lucas’ nervous gaze to the door.

Lucas shrugged. “He... wasn’t feeling great this morning. His stomach might be bothering him.”

Josie winced. “Jeez, poor Marshall. Didn’t he have a date tonight?”

“Yup. Still does, apparently,” Lucas said with an exasperated sigh. 

He felt a little guilty for giving Marshall such a hard time about it, but it was only because he was being such a moron. David didn’t seem like the type to be sympathetic towards sick people, and Lucas had seen the many times he applied hand sanitizer throughout the day even when no one around him was visibly sick. Oh right, David.

“Guys, do me a favor and don’t mention that to David?”

“Why?” Josie mumbled. “If he’s throwing up he’s obviously too sick to-”

“We won’t say anything.” Corinne gave Josie a look. “It’s Marshall’s decision.”

Josie sighed with defeat and sat back, taking a bite of her cereal. 

Just in time for the end of the conversation, David Rose sat down next to the spot Marshall was usually sitting in by now.

“Where is he? He left class like, immediately.”

“He’s- um, probably just had some last minute stuff to do before our next class.”

David raised an eyebrow and nodded. “Alright...well, I hope he finishes it in time for breakfast.”

Lucas had to admit that it was sort of adorable the way David’s eyes lit up when Marshall finally made an appearance. Marshall’s face grew brighter as well, a change from the bleary tiredness pasted on his face all morning. 

“Hey,” David greeted, pulling out Marshall’s chair and rubbing his back as he sat down. “Don’t tell me you had homework after midterms. Who would do that?”

“Oh, uh-” Marshall glanced at Lucas, a thankful look, before shaking his head. “No, no homework, I just forgot one of my textbooks. You know how Mr. Charles gets if you forget something in his class.” He chuckled weakly and looked down at the single toast and jelly and took a careful bite.

Lucas winced. The last thing he probably felt like doing right now was eating, but he also shouldn’t go through the whole day without anything in his stomach, right?

Still, Marshall didn’t look too good when they left breakfast. 

-----

Lucas was convinced that his best friend was a crazy person. He looked horrible.

To Marshall’s credit, though, he made it through the whole day without alerting anyone to the fact that he was sick. He’d find impressively believable excuses to bow out or sit down. 

David was beginning to notice, Lucas could tell. 

He caught Marshall’s boyfriend with a confused frown more than once that day.

Marshall’s excuse was also being tired, not having gotten a lot of sleep last night. Lucas let him blame it on him being up late studying when it was actually Marshall who had kept them awake half the night.

David just rubbed his back, kissed his cheek, and continued their conversation.

“He looks like he’s dead,” Josie commented from behind him, arms crossed. 

“Yup,” Lucas agreed. “You know what’s even better? They’re supposed to go for dinner and a movie tonight.”

“Oh God.”

“Mhmm.” He sighed, watching David wrap an arm around Marshall as they talked to someone about how they thought they did on midterms.

-----

Marshall spent the evening puking up what little he’d eaten for lunch and dinner. Lucas checked on him every once in a while to make sure he hadn’t choked on his own vomit or something.

“Want me to talk to David?” Lucas asked. He was trying to be a good friend and save Marshall the tough conversation he should have had hours ago, but Marshall just shook his head. 

“I’ll be fine,” he said defiantly. “I just won’t... eat. Or drink. Anything.”

Lucas nodded. “So you’re going to go on a date. To a restaurant. And you’re not going to eat anything. You have to realize how stupid that-”

“Of course I realize how stupid it sounds, Lucas, but I’m not missing this! I’ll figure it out, okay?” 

Lucas’ mouth formed a thin line. This really was that important to him.

So Lucas didn’t say anything else as he watched his best friend brush his teeth rather compulsively and find the perfect shirt to wear. He paid far more attention to his hair than Lucas had ever seen.

“Do I...?” Marshall paused and sighed at what he was about to ask. “Do I look okay?”

The only answer he could think to offer was, “I guess so?” 

That seemed to be good enough for Marshall. Neither of them were exactly experts in the area, that was more of a Corinne question.

He watched as Marshall took an ibuprofen from the bottle on his desk (usually reserved for stress headaches) and blew out a calming sigh.

“What’s that for?” Lucas asked. 

“Oh, just... feeling a little weird.”

“I don’t think those will help your stomach.”

“No, it’s not-” Marshall sighed. “I might be getting a fever or something. Dunno.”

Lucas chose not to say anything. He was trying to be nice whether this was obviously a bad idea or not.

“Well! I’m going to go get him.”

“Have a good time,” Lucas offered with pure deadpan.

“Thanks,” Marshall echoed the tone, but he couldn’t hide his excitement.

-----

Lucas figured their date would go until it was dangerously close to lights out. 

So when someone knocked on the door not two hours after Marshall had left, he expected it to be Josie, or Vaughn, or Corinne...

Or anyone else besides David Rose with his roommate in tow. 

God, Marshall looked horrible. His hair had been perfectly styled before, was now a mess.

“Jeez, Marshall...” 

David nodded in agreement to Lucas’ reaction. “He threw up. He missed my shoes by like, an inch,” he told Lucas, cringing a bit as he steered his boyfriend to bed.

Marshall wandered dizzily to his bed with the help of David holding him steady. And David... he looked a little freaked out. 

“You should have told me,” David muttered, shaking his head in exasperation. 

“Sorry, I’m sorry,” Marshall looked so distressed, Lucas was afraid he was going to start crying right there. “Thought I’d be better tonight.”

“So you’ve been sick all day and you didn’t think to tell me that?”

Marshall sighed. “I didn’t want to miss our date.”

David looked like he wanted to chew him out a little more, but instead he just sighed dramatically and helped Marshall get his shoes off and situated into bed. “You’re ridiculous, and very lucky that you didn’t puke on me or I might have had to transfer schools again,” Marshall huffed out a chuckle at that. “But I’m flattered.”

“How long did it take before you puked?” Lucas asked with a smirk, breaking up the cute moment. Marshall glared.

“Maybe an hour.”

David nodded, absently running his fingers through Marshall’s hair as he said, “We were in the downstairs bathroom for a while before he felt well enough to come back here.”

“Did you even make it to the theater?”

“Mmmm... parking lot.”

They didn’t have to explain any further for Lucas to understand that Marshall started throwing up before they could even start the movie. 

David shook his head, rolling his eyes up to the ceiling with ultimate exasperation, before he leaned over and pressed a kiss to Marshall’s forehead. “I’m going to go before I get any more exposed to your plague. But I’m... I’ve missed you this week, too.”

Marshall nodded sleepily. “That’s fair. Bye, David.”

David bit his lip, “Bye.” He said quietly.

He waved a goodbye and mouthed ‘sorry’ to Lucas, who just chuckled, before David left.

Lucas waited until the door shut behind him. “Remember all those times that you said you’d be fine for your date tonight?”

“Shut up, Lucas.”
