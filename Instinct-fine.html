Title: Fine

Summary: Dylan is definitely fine
Word count: 1.5k

Fine

"Oh... oh, you look awful." Lizzie stared in awe at her partner looking unkempt for the first time.

He nodded. "Long night."

His voice was... Tired, with a miniscule slur like less effort went into it. Like how he sounded when he was up all night working on a case. It didn't happen often; he valued sleep as a tool to solving crime, claiming it kept the brain clear and functional. Lizzie argued that that was what coffee was for.

What in this case had kept him up all night? It was a simple case involving some poor sap who'd broken one too many hearts. She and Dylan were almost enjoying interviewing all the women he'd pissed off.

Lizzie's attention was brought to a bottle of Gatorade as he took a sip.

He responded to her confusion before she had the chance to say anything.

"Electrolytes," he said plainly. He set the bottle down and sat at his desk, booting up the computer for the day.

Lizzie blinked. "Electrolytes, huh? What about your morning coffee? You look like you could use it."

"Oh, probably..." He breathed out in feigned drama.

"Dylan, c'mon," Lizzie lowered her voice and perched herself against his desk with a playful smirk. "What kept you up so late, hm? 'cause I know it wasn't the case."

Dylan chuckled lightly, smile a little more timid than usual. He hardly spared a glance up at her before typing his password. "Well, you're being quite nosy today, detective." His smile faded away into an awkward stammer.

Lizzie suddenly felt like he was right - maybe she was pushing boundaries.

"I was up most of the night throwing up. The Gatorade is to replenish any fluids I lost," he answered matter-of-factly, like it was no big deal at all.

Lizzie's smirk fell. "What? Are you sick, or like, hungover?"

Dylan cleared his throat. "I might've come down with something, but it's nothing to worry about because I can still work. I need something to do, actually, after the night I had."

She wanted to reprimand him, but she and a lot of other officers worked when ill all the time. It wasn't exactly unheard of to get through a work day while sick. There were often more important things to worry about, and some of them preferred to be working than in bed thinking about nothing but how awful they feel.

She understood.

But she didn't approve. Dylan wasn't an officer.

"If you start feeling too sick, I'm sending you straight home," she informed, returning to her own desk.

"I'll be fine," he scoffed.

"You're pale as a ghost, Dylan."

Truthfully, he looked good for someone who'd been puking all night.

"Has our victim ever joined a dating app? I think we might have a new trove of suspects to delve into."

She let him change the topic, because yes, they needed to find a murderer.

-----

It didn't take long.

They were in the car for about ten minutes before Dylan got very quiet. She'd dealt with enough drunk people to know the signs of an impending vomiting fit. The swallowing, shifting... To his credit, he was trying very hard not to make a mess in her car.

She didn't think he'd last, but he was fine until the minute they pulled up to the convenience store their next suspect worked at.

Dylan threw the door open and vomited as soon as the car was parked.

"Thanks for sparing my car," she said.

He didn't reply. He spat into the puddle below him, breathing erratically. Dylan pulled a kerchief out of his pocket and wiped his mouth and brow, clearing his throat of the stomach acid as he did.

Lizzie sighed. "Why don't you sit in here and rest for a while? Let your stomach settle, you know?"

Dylan swallowed, pulling his body back into the car and closing the door. "Yeah, yeah... That might be a good idea. Just a moment."

Lizzie was chasing a college-aged girl down the street the minute she flashed her badge. The girl was much taller than her and seemed to run a lot faster. Luckily, her partner ended up with a head start when he flew out of the car after her as well.

Lizzie cuffed the girl that had just been tackled against the wall.

Dylan was breathing heavy and looked like he wanted to vomit all over again.  "I think...*huff* we found our new suspect." He said before promptly leaning against a wall and gagging up a mouthful of bile.

-----

"When most young girls find themselves in an interrogation room, they're terrified. But she just looks annoyed," Dylan pondered.

Lizzie scoffed at the girl. "Yeah, this isn't her first time. She's got a whole rap sheet."

"But for murder?"

"Mm, shoplifting to killing someone is a big leap. Still, a person's capable of doing anything if they're angry enough…. Are you sure you don’t want to go home?"

Dylan pulled his sweater tighter around himself. The chills started a while ago, he'd been shivering and trying to get warm. "You need a profiler."

"That's not answering my question."

Dylan walked into the room before they could further argue, and Lizzie huffed and followed.

The girl was no one important. She was a tinder match with the victim who ended up meeting up a few times afterward. It led to nothing, mainly because he was a scumbag and she was looking for something more, and the investigation led to nothing as well.

-----

They were looking for more suspects when Dylan made a noise. His brows furrowed as he place a hand on his stomach. He got up and walked off in the direction of the bathrooms, and hadn't returned since.

Should she check on him? Maybe it was an invasion of privacy, but if anyone was going to make sure he was alright, it would be her. 

Lizzie knocked. "Dylan? You okay?" stupid question considering the circumstances, Lizzie though as she listened to her partner gag uselessly. There was no way he had anything to puke anymore. A few seconds of silence went by. "Want me to call Andy?" No response. For a terrifying moment, Lizzie though he'd passed out. And then the heaving continued. "Dylan?"

"N-no," came the rough voice of her sick partner. "I'll be alright in a few minutes."

"Mm, yeah, I don’t think so. You've been saying that for a while."

Another gag. Lizzie sighed.

-----

She was relieved to see a tall guy with a visitor's badge walk in.

"Hey Andy," she said as went to meet him.

Andy nodded. He looked equal-parts worried and exasperated, which seemed to be the effect Dylan had on people. "Where is he?"

"He's been in the men's room since I called you," she sighed. "Take him home, okay?"

"Yeah, I think that's a good idea. God, he told me it was nothing to worry about, and I was running late so I didn’t even…" Andy cut himself off, massaging the bridge of his nose. "I shouldn't have let him come in."

"Because Dylan is someone who just lets people tell him what to do and isn't stubborn at all."

Andy scoffed. "You have a point."

She led him to the bathroom Dylan had been camped in, and a few minutes later Andy returned with his arm around her miserable partner.

-----

Lizzie took a deep breath as she knocked on the door. She had yet to decide whether this was a good idea or not. The antisocial part of her just wanted to get back in the car and drive away before they saw her, but the other part was telling her that this was a good thing that she was doing.

"Hey Lizzie," Andy's voice rose at the end in surprise. He didn't hesitate before letting her in.

"Heeyyy, I brought soup."

Andy smiled. "He's in the living room."

Lizzie was almost mad how together Dr. Dylan Reinhart could look while camped out on the couch sick. There was a lined trash bn nearby, she noticed, and about a million blankets scattered around the couch.

But Dylan had several papers scattered on the coffee table in front of him. He was still working, then. He greeted her with the same smile he always did. "Well, hello. It's been so long."

Lizzie snorted. "Way too long." She sat on the couch next to him, a safe distance away to give him space. She sat the container of deli soup on the table, along with a plastic spoon. "I know it's not much, but I just wanted to thank you for your help today."

Dylan smiled a bit self-consciously. "Oh, I don't think I was a lot of help."

"You really were, actually. We found the murderer. You played a big part in that."

"Well, that's good to hear."

Lizzie patted his leg. "Get some rest, Dylan. We need you back as soon as possible."

"Not too soon!" Andy called from the other room.

"Right," Lizzie nodded. "Get better first."

"I think I can do that."
