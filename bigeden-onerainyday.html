Fandom: Big Eden
Summary: Henry goes to the store for coffee, ends up becoming a delivery man for a sick store owner.
Word count: 1500

One Rainy Day

Henry shook out his umbrella outside the store and cursed the fact that he had to be out in the rain at all. He was out of coffee grounds, of all things, and reached the point of desperation during his first hour of painting when he realized that lack of coffee just was not going to work.

So here he was. He ran a hand through his hair to rid it of the raindrops and sighed.

“Morning, Henry!” Jim greeted without looking up from his newspaper.

“Morning, Jim.” Neither of them felt the need to converse on a dreary rainy morning, and that was all fine and good.

Until Henry walked in and found the place empty. Specifically lacking the store owner, but half the lights were turned off as well. Surely the store wasn’t closed if he’d just been able to walk in, right? But it was Big Eden, who locked doors?

Henry took a few steps back out into the doorway.

“Uh, Jim?”

“Hm?”

“What’s going on? Where’s Pike? Why is it dark in here?”

“Oh!” Jim threw down his newspaper and shot up as if he’d just remembered something. “Yeah, Pike ain’t here today. He’s out sick, but if you need me to help you out, I’m happy to. Told him I’d watch the place.”

“And you’re doing a fantastic job.”

“What can I do for ya?”

“I just needed coffee...” Henry trailed off as they walked back in. “Is he okay?”

“Yeah, just got a little bit of a bug, been laid up for a couple days now. I think he would’ve tried running the store today if he was actually able to get outta bed.”

Henry chewed the inside of his lip and grabbed a couple cans of coffee grounds off the shelf. “Well, tell him I hope he feels better.” of all people to get sick, Pike was the last person who deserved it. The poor guy.

They made their way to the cash register where Henry handed him a ten dollar bill and Jim counted the change on his hands instead of using the calculator. Pike didn’t really use the thing, either, to be fair.

Jim nodded, and then looked thoughtful. “Actually, would you mind doin’ me a favor?”

“Uh,” Henry glanced at the paper bag full of the one item that could pull him out into the rain.

Jim didn’t wait for a definite answer. “Could you take this to him?” He went to one of the beverage fridges and pulled out a wrapped sandwich that was definitely not for sale. “It’s mine, but I figure he ought to eat. I don’t reckon he’s done a lot of that lately.”

Pike wasn’t eating? Henry frowned and found, himself taking the cold sandwich. “Well, sure, but-”

“Tell him he’s gotta eat to get better!”

Henry had no intention of telling another grown man what to eat, but he could take the sandwich with him, sure.

“Why can’t you-?”

“So busy with the store, y’know?”

“Sure...”

“Go on, before the sandwich gets cold!” Jim laughed, and Henry found himself giving a chuckle as well.

-----

Henry knocked. Waited.

Maybe he was sleeping. Maybe he could just slip the sandwich into Pike’s fridge with a little note explaining where the hell it came from. Maybe-

Pike appeared at the door, but this wasn’t a version of Pike that Henry had ever seen before. The man before him looked like a sad, sick, being wishing that he was asleep instead of answering the door to a sandwich delivery.

That is, until he saw who the delivery person was.

Henry didn’t miss the small, twitchy smile that crossed Pike’s features for a heartbeat before his expression returned to that of the man who wanted to escape every social situation. He cleared his throat, wincing a bit as he did.

“Mr. Hart,” he breathed out, and Jesus his voice was rough. Weak like he hadn’t used it in a while, and hoarse like his throat was sore. “I... what-?”

Henry held out the sandwich with a smile. “From Jim! He wanted me to tell you to eat something to get better.”

Pike huffed out a laugh. “He couldn’t bring it himself?”

“Well, he’s got so much work to do, you know.”

He snorted. “I’m not sure when reading the paper was considered work.”

Henry shoved his hands in his pockets. “So have you been eating?”

Pike swallowed, averted his eyes.

“Ah,” Henry nodded.

“I really... haven’t done much at all.”

One glance at the place behind him made that clear. Dishes cluttered the kitchen and what served as Pike’s living room. Blankets strewn across one of the kitchen chairs as if Pike had been wrapped in it as he sat there and then forgot about it.

Henry cleared his throat as Pike hesitantly took the sandwich and looked like he had no intention of eating it.

“Look, would you mind if I came in for a minute? Just until the rain dies down a bit, you know?”

Pike looked like a deer caught in headlights. “I… the place is kind of a mess right now,” even as he said that, he opened the door wider for Henry to come in. “Haven’t really had the energy to clean,” he admitted.

“That’s okay, my place is a mess, too.”

Pike gave a small, awkward chuckle that resulted in a cough that had Henry wincing in sympathy. He saw Pike’s face pinch in pain, and heard the rattle in his airways.

"Jesus Pike, here…" he guided him to the kitchen table and Pike was powerless to fight against it as he continued to give little irritated coughs while Henry sat him down. "Let me make you tea or something. You know, to go with the sandwich." He was pretty sure he caught an eye roll from the man.

"I'm fine," he tried to clear his throat. It didn't work. "But thanks, really. And I'm really not hungry."

"You're not hungry or your throat hurts too much to eat?"

Pike didn't respond.

"Then just try some tea, and we'll see where we get after that, huh?"

A small smile. "Cabinet next to the sink, top shelf." 

Of course the man kept it on the top shelf where Henry nearly had to jump to get to it. He waved Pike off when he began to get up to help, and Henry eventually succeeded in doing it himself.

Pike sat at the table, patient and sleepy, petting Frances as Henry set the kettle on the stove. They listened to the rain beat against the roof, the window blow against the windows.

"You know," Henry began. "You don't have to sit here forever, you just looked like you were going to pass out or something if I kept you standing any longer."

Pike nodded. "I'm working up the courage to stand again."

"Okay, couch or bed?"

Pike paused for a second too long before he answered. "C- couch."

Yet another nest of blankets was found there. Poor Pike.

The large man practically fell onto the cushions, settled easily into the blankets, and Henry found himself looking away when he felt his heart do a weird fluttery thing. He busied himself with the tea, readying two mugs and setting them on the table before he returned to Pike's side.

Pike accepted the mug with a faint smile and took a sip. "Thank you," he murmured, looking up at Henry with grateful eyes.

Henry nodded, feeling his cheeks heat up. He cleared his throat and gestured to the sandwich he’d brought in with them. "You know, I'd feel better if you at least tried it." He unwrapped it and set it on the end table to make it as easy for Pike to attempt as possible.

Pike eyed the sandwich warily, but eventually picked it up and took a bite.

“How’d Jim do?”

Pike nodded. “It’s good.”

“Is it?”

Pike swallowed the bite with a bit of a wince and his eyes went to the remainder of the sandwich. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I really don’t have much of an appetite right now.”

“No, yeah, that’s okay.” He squeezed Pike’s shoulder. He sat at the other end of the couch. Pike’s living room didn’t offer another option, it was made for one man, after all.

It continued to rain. They sipped their tea.

Frances hopped up and curled up in the small gap of room left on the sofa, setting her chin on Pike’s leg. He smiled and began petting her head.

“Th- thanks for this,” Pike said, breaking the silence between them. He didn’t pull his eyes from Frances, never one for making eye contact. “You didn’t have to… it was nice. Thank you.” He nodded toward the mug of tea.

Henry hummed, shrugged, trying to seem nonchalant when the truth was that he couldn't fathom how adorable this man was when he wasn't feeling well. How could someone look so handsome while they're sick?

"Well, Jim seemed pretty worried about you."

"Jim's always looking for things to worry about." 

He took another sip of tea. It could have been his imagination, but Henry thought his voice sounded a little better since he started drinking it.

"Well… thanks for letting me hide out from the rain. We'll have to do this again when you're feeling better."

Pike froze up for a second and then cleared his throat. "Drink tea?"

"Sure. Would that be okay?"

"That would be… fine."
