Good Days

"Does this dress say 'I might like you but like, maybe not'?"

David sat up in his bed, blankets falling off of his shoulders and pooling in his lap. He shook his head the way he often did when he didn't have a true answer to a question. "Ugh, I don't know. Maybe."

Alexis groaned dramatically. "Okay, David, that's not really helpful. I don't want Ted to think that I'm coming with him to this vet thing because I like him!"

"Um, but you do like him?" His voice raised an octave at the end, but this time broke and died away.

"Yeah, but I don't want him to know that!"

David sniffled and ran the sleeve of his hoodie under his nose. "Okay honestly, he's Ted, Alexis. He's not going to be reading into anything. He has no idea you like him, and whatever dress you're wearing isn't going to- to…" He fumbled for a tissue and got it to his nose just in time to release a double of relatively disgusting and painful sneezes. He groaned as they scraped at his sore throat. "Oh my God…"

"Eww, David…"

"Don't 'ew' me! You think I don't know that this whole situation is 'ew'?!" He argued, gesturing wildly with the tissue in-hand at his current state, which was certainly a sight. He hadn't had the energy to go through his whole morning routine or even get out of his pajamas. He ran the tissue under his nose one more time before abandoning it to the trash bin next to his bed. "But my point is that you're overthinking this and Ted isn't going to care what dress you're wearing. But like…" A sigh. "I guess the peach one is a lot nicer for this time of year."

Alexis opened her mouth and looked like she was about to argue as she turned to the other dress draped over the sofa. Her mouth formed a thin line as she gave a reluctant nod of agreement and grabbed it on her way to change for a third time.

David coughed pitifully. Of course he'd wake up sick on his one day off, but then again, it was probably a good thing it happened today. It saved him from having to admit to Patrick that he was sick, because Patrick would probably show up and try to take care of him. The thought of his loving boyfriend seeing him in this state sent shivers down his spine.

His family was one thing - they've all seen him at his lowest points. But Patrick… Patrick was his boyfriend. Their relationship was partially based on their attraction to each other right? So if Patrick saw how gross he looked right now…

Yuck. No thank you.

Bzzzt.

Speak of the devil.

Patrick: Good morning. Have a good day <3

Ugh. He was so fucking sweet. David was in the middle of groaning when Alexis emerged in the peach-colored dress.

"What?" She asked in annoyance.

"Why does my boyfriend have to be so fucking perfect."

"Okay, I'm going to actually hit you right now."

David listened to his family leave the room, one by one, until he was left by himself. He’d planned on sleeping off this cold the moment they were gone.

Stupid plan.

He couldn’t get comfortable no matter the position he was in. And of course his nose would start dripping the moment that he thought he was comfortable. Or he’d end up in a coughing fit, because his lungs couldn’t handle him being horizontal for two fucking minutes.

He whimpered restlessly, throwing the blankets off of himself with a huff. This was the actual worst.

David’s nose started to run the moment he sat up, and he merely pressed the sleeve of his hoodie to his nostrils to catch the liquid. It wasn’t as if anyone was there to see it, and he would change out of his gross snotty stuff before his family came home.

He rolled his eyes at himself. As long as he stayed in the motel and no one saw him until this cold was gone, he’d be fine!

David nearly jumped out of his skin when his phone started to vibrate.

The screen read ‘Patrick’ and he knew he was in trouble. He sniffled deeply and cleared his throat to sound as healthy as possible.

“Hellooo?”

“Hey, do you wanna meet me at the cafe for lunch?”

“Oh, sure! Yeah, absolutely I-” His nose fucking itched. He pulled the phone away long enough for him to violently scrub it. “I’d love to.”

“Sweet! I’ll see you in a half hour?”

How dare Patrick think that he could be ready to go out in a half hour on a good day, let alone one where- Oh God, he looked awful.

David was out of bed the minute they said goodbye, ignoring the way his sinuses made his head spin when he stood up too quickly.

Shirt, he needed a new shirt. David chose one of his older, less expensive sweaters that could easily be cleaned if he got snot or sweat on it. He was pretty sure Patrick wouldn’t tell the difference, anyway.

Next, he fixed his hair in the mirror. That probably took the most effort. He skipped his shower that morning so it was greasy and gross and uncooperative, but he made it work.

David took a last look at himself in the mirror and sighed. He still looked tired, his eyes were red and puffy and his nose had a pink tinge to it.

Maybe he just thought he looked bad because he knew he was sick. Patrick didn’t really pay attention to those kinds of things anyway.

He made his sniffly walk to the cafe, wishing he’d asked Patrick to pick him up as he started to hack up a lung halfway there from the exertion. He took a moment to catch his breath and wipe his nose before he entered the cafe.

Patrick was sitting in the furthest booth back and facing away from the door, which gave him a little more time to make himself look healthy on his way there.

With one last sniffle and the hope that his nose stays clear for the entirety of lunch, David made his way to Patrick’s table.

Patrick’s face immediately lit up as David slid into the seat, and his heart kind of melted at the sight of his boyfriend. He didn’t realize how much he missed the slightest bit of affection during his... few hours on his sickbed.

“Hey,” Patrick greeted brightly. “How has your day been?”

“Oh, y’know, relaxing and uneventful.”

“Just how you like it, then?”

David bit back a smile and nodded. “Something like that, yeah.”

Patrick chuckled and reached for David’s hand across the table. And oh, how he desperately wanted to give in... but with how much he’d been rubbing his nose today, or even on his way here...

David cleared his throat as he pulled his hand away, and felt his heart fall at the confused look on his boyfriend’s face. He just couldn’t live with himself if he got Patrick sick. David tried to save himself by casually scratching at his face, but Patrick was still watching him with a raised eyebrow.

“How is your day going, though? A lot of customers?”

Patrick shrugged. “Enough. It’s been a pretty normal day. Oh! We did get a business card from a lesbian couple that wants us to look into selling their baked goods.”

“Definitely something to consider, then.”

“You hate it.”

David released a sigh he didn’t realize he was holding, along with a cough that he quickly had to disguise as a laugh. “I mean, Rose Apothecary isn’t a bake sale. But if they have something else they want to sell to us...”

Patrick chuckled. “We’ll call them later this week.”

David nodded in agreement. He shuddered, suddenly feeling chilly, and folded his arms to conserve his body heat.

“Do you think it’s cold in here? Twyla must have turned the AC up.”

“David, it’s summer. I don’t even know if the AC is on today. How are you cold?”

With one look, David realized Patrick had his sleeves pulled up and a sheen of sweat on his face. So apparently he shouldn’t be cold, which meant a fever, which was a little unnerving. He could freak out when lunch was over and he was back at home.

All he could think to say was “Oh,” and shrug as casually as possible. Patrick looked like he was about to question his weirdness, but to David’s relief, he thought against it and took a sip of his coffee instead. He was in the clear.

“Hey David!” Twyla chimed. “How’re you feeling? Alexis said you were home sick today.”

Motherfu-

“Oh I’m,” he cleared his voice of the roughness and winced when all that did was hurt his sore throat. “I’m feeling much better now. It was just one of those uh, four-hour bugs.” He tried very hard not to glance at his boyfriend who was now looking straight at him with furrowed brows and a ‘what the fuck, David?’ look.

Twyla scrunched her nose in thought. “Mmm, is that a thing?”

“Yeah, totally. it’s totally a thing.”

She didn’t look convinced, but she shrugged. “Well, I think I have a tea that might make you feel better if you’re interested. It’s really good for colds!”

David cleared his throat agai- ow, fuck, he had to stop doing that. “Thanks, Twy.”

She smiled. “Mhm! So what else can I get you two?”

The two of them ordered their food and of course Twyla walked off with the smile of someone who was way too happy for a full-time waitress.

David shrunk under Patrick’s sudden gaze, preparing for the lecture about not communicating or something. Instead, Patrick looked... concerned.

“I wouldn’t have dragged you over here if I knew you were sick,” Patrick frowned.

David huffed a halfhearted laugh and tried to pretend like he wasn’t freezing to death. “I wanted to see you, though, so I’m here. It’s nothing, anyway, just a really, really annoying c- cold...” The itch in his nose had returned with vengeance. “Jesus...” David muttered before pulling his arm over his face in time to cover the sneeze. He sniffled, and winced at the wet sound that signified how clogged his sinuses were.

There was a napkin hanging in front of his face as he looked up, as well as his boyfriend with an annoyingly-sympathetic expression.

"Bless you."

David had to keep his nose covered with one hand as he reached for the napkin, and cleaned his nostrils the best he could without disturbing the healthy patrons with gross sounds.

"I'm fine." He said when the whole debacle was done with. He sniffed and swiped at his nostrils with the back of his hand. So his nose was going to run now. That was great.

Patrick looked bewildered. "I don't get you. You whine about every single headache and leg cramp, but you have a full-blown cold and you’re just 'fine'? Why'd you try hiding it from me?"

"I'm sorry, do I have to give you every detail of my health, now?"

"No, it's just unusual, that's all." Patrick shrugged and took another sip of his coffee. "Doesn't bother me. I'm just not used to you being casual about something like this."

Lacking a proper response, David just made a 'hm' sound.

The sandwich that David usually ordered was difficult to swallow. He gave up on it after the third bite and decided to switch to tea for lunch. Twyla was right, it did help. His sinuses felt clearer and it appeased his sore, swollen throat. He had to ignore his stomach's beg for real food.

This was his least favorite part about being sick. Not being able to eat for one reason or another. He didn't do well with skipping meals.

He was mid-sip when he realized Patrick was watching him. "What?"

"You're not going to eat? Are you sure you're okay?"

"Yes, yes, I'm completely okay. Just don't feel like eating right now. Had a… big breakfast."

Patrick stared into his soul. David squirmed under his scrutiny. He hated that his boyfriend could figure him out so well. "You feel really terrible, huh?"

That was all David needed to let down his thinly-veiled misery. "My throat hurts so much," he whined.

"Okay, well we can fix that," Patrick said softly. He reached for David's hand again, and this time he didn't pull away.

"…And I can't breathe through my nose. And I might have a fever."

Patrick frowned, biting back worry. "I'll drive you back to the motel, we'll pick some stuff up on the way to help you feel better."

“Mmm... mhmm. Exactly how much time do you have for lunch?”

“Long enough.”

-----

David leaned into Patrick's cool touch on his forehead.

"I think you were right about that fever. Here," He reached into the pharmacy bag and handed David a bottle of tylenol. "This'll help."

David smiled. His bed was a mess of gross tissues and tangled blankets, and a part of him was terrified that something like this would somehow be the last straw of their relationship. But no, Patrick was too sweet and caring and precious. David didn't deserve any of it, but he'd take it.

"Thank you," David croaked. His voice was starting to go for good.

"Was there a reason you didn't tell me?" Patrick asked in that genuinely curious voice that tricked you into answering immediately.

"Well- Patrick! I'm gross!" He said as if Patrick should know this already. He should! It was obvious! "I'm so gross right now, and I'm snotty and germy and I couldn’t let you see me like this when-"

Patrick kissed him.

He fucking kissed him.

David pulled away as quickly as he could. "Wha- what are you doing?!"

"Proving to you that you're not gross."

"That's just not true. Oh my God, I'm dating a crazy person."

Patrick leaned in again, and this time David didn't resist as their lips made contact.

"You're going to regret this when you catch it," he mumbled.

"Small price to pay."
