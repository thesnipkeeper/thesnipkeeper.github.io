Fandom: Fantasy High
Summary: Gorgug has a bad day
Word count: 500

Whoops

Gorgug had been quiet. That wasn’t particularly unusual, sometimes he was just a quiet guy.

But Riz had to admit that it was a little weird. A different kind of quiet, where he was constantly spacing off instead of silently listening.

Riz took a moment where everyone else was talking to nudge him. Gorgug made a questioning, startled noise and turned to him.

“You good?” asked Riz.

“Yeah, yeah, I’m... I mean, I think so?”

Riz raised an eyebrow.

Gorgug sighed. “I feel kind of, weird.” He winced as he said it.

“Weird. What kind of weird?”

A shrug. “Dunno.”

Riz kept an eye on him. The others started to notice that he wasn’t himself as well. Fabian was the first to mention it.

“What’s with you today?” He demanded. “Like, you’re being weird.”

Gorgug already knew that.

“Sorry,” said Gorgug, while clearly not being sorry.

They continued to talk in the courtyard of the school, seated on one of the old picnic tables. Gorgug stopped talking at all. Kristin rubbed his back a little.

Suddenly, he made a strange noise that got their attention.

He was vomiting before anyone could figure out what was happening, including him. The sick dripped from his chin, down his shirt while he looked horrified at the mess before him. Whatever panic that was setting in had to wait, because he had to throw up again.

"It's okay, buddy..." Riz insisted, as Gorgug had a puke-covered hand clamped over his mouth. It was obvious in his eyes that the last thing he wanted to do was puke again.

He didn't have a choice, anyway, because it happened again. Only this time he leaned forward, letting it drip onto the grass where hopefully no one would be stepping anytime soon.

"Oh, shit..." said Fig.

"I..." Gorgug looked almost like he was about to cry. "I don't... I didn't even..."

"Yeah, we should go to the nurse," Fabian decided. He was already tugging at Gorgug's forearm until the half-orc stood up. "C'mon."

"I didn't bring a clean shirt..." He lamented as he clumsily let Fabian drag him away.

"That's fine," said Fabian. "You can borrow one of mine. They're probably much nicer, anyway." He managed to say it with such fondness. Good ol' Fabian.

The nurse was sympathetic, but tired. Apparently Gorgug wasn't the only stomach bug case that she'd gotten that day. She got him on a cot near the bathroom, set a bin next to him, and begged him not to use it.

She pressed a thermometer in his ear, and Gorgug just stared at the floor miserable as she did so.

"Yup, that's a fever," she said with a sigh. "I'll have the office call your parents."

"Oh, it's okay!" Fabian piped up. "I can take him home during lunch!"

She nodded, not in any state to argue.

"No," said Gorgug.

"What? Why?"

"On the motorcycle?"

"Oh," a chuckle. "Right. I'd rather my bike not get vomited on."

Riz scoffed. The nurse chuckled.

"We'll call your parents."
