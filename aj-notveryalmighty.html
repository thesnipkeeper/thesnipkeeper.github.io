Fandom: Almighty Johnsons
Summary: Dawn enjoys her boss' suffering just a little
Word count: 1.1k

Not Very Almighty

She felt guilty for being so smug about it.

Dawn grabbed the pink bottle from Anders’ desk and passed it to him as he exited the bathroom. The man looked exhausted, and of course anyone would be exhausted after being on the toilet all day.

But it was Anders. Anders didn’t get sick, he didn’t get colds or food poisoning. He was Anders the Almighty or however he thought of himself.

In reality, he just had a fantastic immune system and took more vitamins and supplements than Dawn bothered to take. She was still bitter whenever she came down with whatever seasonal illness and he remained completely healthy as everyone dropped like flies around him.

So fine, yes, she enjoyed this just a little bit.

“It should help,” she said.

He glanced at her and then the bottle tiredly before falling into his desk chair. Anders leaned forward and buried his head in his hands. “I feel like shit.”

Dawn bit back a smile. “It’s just a little tummy ache.”

“’s terrible,” he groaned. “Can you grab a water for me?” from the fridge that was five feet away from him.

Dawn did so, anyway. He cracked it open, took a sip, and seemed to hold it in his mouth for a moment as if swallowing extra slowly. He took another sip.

“No one’s forcing you to be here if you’re not well,” she reasoned. And the desire to have the office to herself to get real work done was very strong.

“No, I know,” he sighed, setting the water aside. “But I- I want to get this presentation done. The meeting is next week, I want to look prepared.”

It didn’t matter whether he was prepared. They would love him, anyway. They always did. Anders could have any roomful of businessmen hanging on every word that left his mouth.

Dawn went back to her desk. She started to get said real work done.

She’d replied to five emails, confirmed appointments, actually started to feel productive when her boss made a strange noise from his side of the room. She looked up from her computer to see Anders sitting statue still.

He pressed a loose fist to his mouth and made a sound that was something between a burp and a hiccup. Either way, he looked sick.

“Anders...?” she questioned uselessly.

Anders shook his head and stood up, his hand never leaving his mouth as he briskly made his way to the bathroom. There was the sound of a horrible heave, and another, and finally that of liquid hitting liquid. The poor man vomited for a good ten minutes before the sounds faded out.

And Dawn waited for him to emerge.

She waited.

He was fine. There was no way in hell she was worried about her asshole of a boss.

But... if he died, she wouldn’t get paid. That was worth worrying about, right?

“Anders?” she called. “You alright?”

His voice was muffled from behind the door, but she heard the distinct answer of, “Dunno.”

God. God fuck, she felt bad for him.

Dawn dragged a hand down her face in utter frustration before she made her way over there. She knocked, the knob turned. The door drifted open on its own to reveal her boss in a state of dishevelment that filled her heart with both delight and concern.

His tie and jacket were off, both carefully draped over the sink because God forbid they get wrinkled. His hair was damp with sweat, and so was his shirt for that matter. Holy shit, the man was a wreck.

“You’re a wreck,” also happened to be the first thing that came out of her mouth when she saw him.

He groaned and threw his head back. “Thanks. Thank you, for that, Dawn.”

“Anytime. Anders, honestly, go home.”

“Can’t.”

She crossed her arms over her chest and scoffed. "And why not?"

"Because I can't stand up. Tried twice already. My vision keeps going black before I can take a step out of this fucking bathroom." His voice was broken and hoarse from vomiting, which made it that much more tragic.

"God, Anders…" she muttered. "Here, need a hand?"

He looked at the proffered hand, sighed, and accepted it. They worked as he used both Dawn and the sink for support until he was on his feet. He smelled… not bad, necessarily, which was infuriating considering how sick he'd been, but he definitely smelled different. She hated that she noticed something as ridiculous as that, that she spent so much time around the man she could tell when he was sick by his smell. And as she wrapped an arm around Anders' waist, she felt his body heat roll off of him.

"You're running a temperature."

"Oh," Anders hummed thoughtfully. "That would explain why I was freezing earlier."

She rolled her eyes.

She managed to get him to the uncomfortable office couch, and set the bin from her desk next to him just in case. He clearly wasn't in any state to get up if he started to feel sick again.

"Alright. I'm obviously not going to let you drive home. Which one of your brothers do I call?"

Anders closed his eyes and draped his arm over his face, giving a miserable groan as he thought about it.

"...Mike." Just saying it seemed to cause him physical pain. "Call Mike."

Dawn did so.

Mike showed up, saw the state his brother was in, and heaved a heavy sigh. "Right, c'mon, let's get you home."

Dawn caught a glimpse of a grumpy teenager who didn't like being told what to do as Anders groaned and sat up. He let Mike help him to his feet with some reluctance, and only sort of pulled away when Mike laid a hand across his forehead.

"Christ, Anders…" he muttered. "We're stopping for a thermometer on the way home. Dunno if we have one, honestly."

"He's been vomiting and… other things all day," offered Dawn. "He's going to need some fluids in him."

Mike nodded his thanks. "I'm glad you called. He would've stayed here all day. I know you hate being alone when you're sick, Anders, but this is ridiculous."

Oh. Anders winced for a split second at the embarrassment. The man was so good at putting up a front, any sort of front, even while he was evacuating his stomach contents.

Huh. He didn't want to be alone. Dawn found it… fascinating. Not cute, exactly, because it was hard to find her manipulative asshole of a boss cute after spending every day with him. But it was certainly interesting.

She watched Mike drag his younger brother out of the office, and then let her shoulders fall with a sigh. 

Finally.

She had the office to herself.
