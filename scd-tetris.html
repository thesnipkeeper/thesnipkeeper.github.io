Fandom: Santa Clarita Diet
Summary: Sheila and Joel are reminded that hiding mass murder isn't the only obstacle in their lives
Word count: 800

Tetris

Joel sniffs as they're packing pieces of Nazi into the freezer. His hands are gloved, which makes dealing with a runny nose difficult. He can already feel the fluid making its way to his upper lip.

"Shit," he mutters, and then sniffs again. He pulls his arm up to wipe his nose on his shoulder. This shirt will probably be doomed by the end of the day anyway.

"What's wrong?"

Sheila's elbow deep in body parts as she tetrises the freezer to fit everything efficiently. She only looks up when he doesn't answer right away.

A prickle in his nose, and he rubs it more violently against his upper arm until it makes a gross wet streak on the fabric. He makes a noise of disgust that lasts about two seconds before the prickle grows into an overwhelming itch. He sneezes into his elbow, a moist sensation that leaves him sniffling even harder.

"Sorry, snf! I must be allergic to..."

"... to Nazi?"

"Sure. Maybe. I wouldn't mind that."

"Okay, well, you sound like you're getting a cold, honey."

He shakes his head with another sniff that does little to stop liquid from dripping down his face. He needs to take his gloves off and get a tissue before this becomes a humiliating disaster.

"I never get sick, sweetheart, you know that."

"'Never' isn't exactly the word that I'd use," says Sheila as she stands up, satisfied with her organization of the arms. "Remember that Christmas that you caught the flu? You could barely get out of bed."

He rubs his nose once more and winces at the memory of spending a Christmas coughing and sleeping.

"And what about the seedy chinese place outside of town that you swore you had to try on our way home?"

"Oh God, don't remind me."

"You slept in the bathroom for two days, Joel. I almost took you to the ER."

"I see your point," he sighs. "Fine, maybe I do get sick on occasion."

'No, my point is that when you do get sick, it hits you hard and fast. I think we should just throw the rest of this in the freezer and go home so you can rest."

Joel blinks. "But... your system! The arms and the legs so meticulously stacked-"

"I can work on it later." She pulls her gloves off and reaches up to kiss him. It can't be a good experience when his nose is so runny, but she doesn't seem to mind. “Let’s go home.”

-----

"Here ya go, sniffles."

He simply grunts at the nickname, and then hums happily when she kisses his forehead and sets a bowl of soup in his hands.

"Can't remember the last time I made this soup," Sheila says as she sits on the bed. "Maybe when Abby was sick."

"About five years ago." Joel drags the back of his hand under his nose when the steam makes it run.

"Five years? Jeez, why can't she get sick all the time like normal kids. I love how cuddly she gets." Sheila makes a happy noise and nuzzles his cheek. "And I know where she gets it from."

Joel goes to kiss her, and then has to turn away immediately to sneeze. And then again. He brings a hand to belatedly cover his nose when he feels warm wetness on his upper lip. "Snf! Sheila, snf! Can you grab me a-"

Joel doesn't finish his sentence  before the sleeve of his wife's hoodie is pressed to his nose. And she's still wearing it.

"Sheila!" He pulls away despite the snot situation on his face. A string inevitably remains connected to the sleeve before he pulls away further. "I can't blow my nose in your sleeve, sweetheart."

"Why not? You always said you love how soft this sweatshirt is!"

"Where in that sentence did I say that I wanted to blow snot into it?"

"It can be washed," she says, as if he's the one being ridiculous. "Come on..."

He's going to argue, and then his nose starts to itch again. Joel sniffles harshly, but it only exasperates the tickle and he's forced to press his face into his wife's sleeve no matter what his plan was going to be.

An explosion of gooey, contagious snot erupts into the fabric. And Sheila looks like she's done this a thousand times. She pinches a dry part of the sleeve around his nostrils to clean the excess.

"See? Wasn't that more pleasant?"

Joel sighs and nods. "Yeah..." he chuckles as she finally removes the now-disgusting hoodie.

Sheila crawls into bed with him, hands him the bowl pf soup and kisses his cheek.

"Kind of of weird, isn't it?"

"What just happened? Yes, I'd say that was rather strange."

"No, silly, I'm talking about you. Getting a cold." A fond smile before she nuzzles into him. "After everything that's happened, the sniffles is still... a thing."

He nuzzles her in return and ends up sniffling in her ear. She doesn't seem to mind. "Honestly? I'm not complaining."
