Title: Of Course
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David isn't having a good day
Word count: 1.6k

Of Course

The moment David wakes up, he knows that it’s going to be one of those days.

Everything feels wrong.

Everything’s annoying him.

Every sound and touch makes him want to throw himself out the window.

He has yet to figure out what causes it these days, exactly. Up until the wedding, he had a way of dealing with these days, and that was to sit calmly with music in his ears at a low volume. Or when he had work, like the professional adult he was, he would take a few minutes in the back alone, just to take a few deep breaths and gather himself.

But things are different now. Because he lives with the love of his life who has yet to deal with David on one of these days, at least without having a way for either of them to escape.

David growls under his breath and gets out of bed. He takes too long to make the bed, and even then it doesn’t look quite right and for some reason that takes his breath away for a moment. He shakes his head. Get it together. It’s just the bed.

He can hear Patrick starting the coffee maker downstairs. As amazing as coffee sounds at the moment, David knows that it’s not a good idea if he wants to stay sane through the whole day.

He gets dressed. Soft, breathable clothes that won’t suffocate him if he starts feeling worse.

Patrick’s pushing some toast down as David wanders out.

“Morning,” he says with a sleepy smile.

“Good morning!” God, how is he so cheerful in the morning? “Want your coffee in a thermos, or do you want to drink it now?” he asks, pouring himself a cup in the process.

“I’ll pass today,” David tries, as casually as ever.

Patrick nearly drops his own coffee in the process, looking at David wide-eyed. “I’m sorry, did you just say that you don’t want coffee? Are you feeling okay?” He lays a hand on David’s forehead before David can bat him away.

The answer is no, David wants to say. He’s not feeling okay. He wants to crawl out of his skin. He wants to hide in bed. Most of all he...

He wants Patrick.

He might feel horrible, but being alone will make it worse. Patrick makes everything better, even without realizing it.

David shrugs. “Just not feeling it today, y’know?”

Patrick raises an eyebrow, but dismisses it. “Sure. Toast?”

“Please.”

-----

David spends most of the morning trying to ignore the nagging voice in the back of his head. The nagging voice telling him to just give up for the day. Just go home and get in bed and sleep until he feels better.

But again, Patrick makes it better. Every once in a while he’ll press a kiss to David’s cheek as he walks by, or some other sweet gesture. And that’s just Patrick.

David’s never felt more lucky to have a husband like him than he does today.

Of course, things don’t stay better.

The store gets busy. Really busy. And of all days for a group of middle-aged woman to walk in, why did it have to be today? When the last thing David wants to do is deal with people?

Patrick would figure something out if David asked, but he’s not going to ask. This isn’t Patrick’s problem, and he’s already done so many nice things for him today that David doesn’t deserve. Toast, coffee (that he didn’t even drink), adorable kisses...

Ugh.

So instead David finds himself in a passive-aggressive argument with a customer he’s sure is named Karen while he struggles not to fidget too much in front of her, lest it be a sign of weakness. Patrick’s busy with another customer, he doesn’t even notice what’s going on until the woman storms out.

This is what the day has been leading up to, David thinks as he throws his hands in the air. He can feel himself starting to tear up from the sheer stress of it all. Stress he can usually handle pretty well, but of course it happened today...

He’s barely aware of himself rushing to the back room until he’s actually there, standing in the silence. The muffled voices of customers and Patrick remind him that he’s not safe from the anxiety, nor should he have any right to just walk away and leave Patrick with all of the work just because he can’t handle-

He can’t breathe.

Fuuuuck. This can’t be happening right now.

But it is.

David finds himself hyperventilating while tears completely cloud his vision and pour down his face. He can’t help but think that, God, he’s going to have so much touch-up work to do after this.

Why is this even happening? What the fuck is wrong with him that he’s crying in the middle of work on a perfectly normal day?

The shallow breaths start to make him feel lightheaded, so he sinks to the floor in a darker corner of the room, far away from the door and any possible gaze from a customer.

Patrick is doing all of the work himself right now. Because David can’t get his shit together.

He cries harder.

“David?”

David gasps and looks up to find his husband in the doorway. Patrick pulls the curtain closed while David tries to breath normally and dry his face. It’s no use, he looks horrible.

“Hey,” his voice is soft. Far softer than it should be after what David just did to him.

He looks worried, and he shouldn’t be. Instead of asking questions or trying to hold him, neither of which David would be perceptive to at the moment, Patrick just plops on the floor next to him.

“They left. Bought two-hundred dollars’ worth of products, though.”

David nods, sniffles. He runs the sleeve of his sweater under his nose. He’s going to have to wash it now anyway. He tries to swallow the remaining lump in his throat, to breathe normally again.

“Do you need anything?”

He shakes his head. “I’m fine.” Holy fuck, his voice sounds awful.

“Okay.” Patrick shifts, scooting a bit closer to him. “Do you just want to sit here for a while?”

“I’ll be... I’ll be good in a few minutes. I’m sorry.”

“David, hey, don’t apologize.”

David shakes his head, because he has to apologize for what just happened.

“I-” he huffs, sniffs. “I shouldn’t have even gotten out of bed this morning,” he says with a self-deprecating chuckle.

Patrick doesn’t say anything, but he digs into his pocket and pulls out a napkin he must have pilfered from the cafe at lunch and presses it into David’s hand. David nods thankfully and goes to work drying his face.

“I’m sorry.” Why is Patrick apologizing? “Have you been feeling like this all day?” David doesn’t answer, doesn’t plan to answer, but maybe that’s just as telling. “David...”

David shakes his head, looking up at the ceiling as he struggles to dry his eyes. “I’m fine. And it’s not- snf! it’s not your job to-” Patrick stands up and starts grabbing their jackets from the coat rack. “What...?”

“I think we can close a couple hours early,” Patrick says, handing David his jacket. “Family emergency, right?”

“No,” David says quickly. “No no, we can’t just-”

Patrick stops. David knows that look on his face, asking himself if he’s being too controlling or invasive again, like with the stupid olive branches. And suddenly David feels guilty for that too. He shouldn’t be making Patrick feel like he’s doing something wrong.

So, David stands up and starts pulling his jacket on. “Let’s go,” he says.

Patrick smiles.

-----

David finds himself being led to the couch and covered in the softest blanket they own (and they own many soft blankets). 

"I'm sorry," he says for the thousandth time, though he's getting a little tired of saying it, and just talking in general.

"Shhh," Patrick says. "Just get comfortable, and I'm going to make some tea."

David smiles, the sideways one he tries so hard to bite back. "Mm'kay, you realize that I'm not sick, right?"

"Yes," Patrick says, voice blessedly soft in contrast to the chaos going on in David's head. "But you're not feeling well either way. Sit tight!"

David watches his husband disappear into the kitchen, feeling so full of love. That feeling soon fades as his head starts attacking him again, reminding him that he's never done anything like this for Patrick.

Patrick deserves the world and David can't even be competent for his sake.

He sniffles and swipes at his eyes as Patrick comes back with tea. He has no reason to cry, and yet here he is.

Patrick just sits next to him and presses a kiss to his cheek while David sips the tea.

"David," he says gently.

David sniffs, feeling his eyes well up with tears yet again as he reluctantly turns to face his husband. "Hm?"

"I love you, you know that, right?" 

Despite what his head is constantly telling him during times like this, yes, David does know that. He nods. "Love you too. So fucking much."

Patrick smiles, hums, resting his chin on David's shoulder.

"We can't do this every time that I..." rolling his eyes, he gestures vaguely at himself.

"Maybe not, but it'll still be okay. For right now, why don't we just… relax, okay?"

David nods. "I'm sorry. I'm sorry that this is probably going to happen again, and-"

"Shh," Patrick turns David's face toward him once again and pulls him in for one of the softest kisses they've ever had. 

David's reminded how safe he is. Despite everything else, at least he's safe. And married to the most amazing person in the world.
