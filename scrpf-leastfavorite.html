Title: Least Favorite
Fandom: Schitt's Creek RPF
Summary: Dan finds out the hard way that it's kind of hard to work on a food show when you have the stomach flu, Julia makes things a little less horrible
Word count: 2.6k

Least Favorite

It starts with Dan showing up to the shoot with a different air to him than usual. He’s quiet, much less conversational than usual and sticks to sipping his coffee as they all get ready for the day. If Julia had to guess, she’d say that he didn’t sleep well last night.

He looks ready to be done already, irritably massaging his temple when one of the crew yells out instructions.

Hangover? That’s a possibility as well.

“Are you okay? You look a little pale.”

Dan smiles tiredly. “I’m good,” he assures. “I don’t know why, but I just woke up this morning and felt like I never went to sleep...” he ends his sentence with a jaw cracking yawn.

Julia makes a sympathetic noise and gives his back a quick rub, and he smiles appreciatively.

If only he remained just sleepy for the rest of the day, that would have been fine.

But instead, a worried frown grows deeper as the morning and early afternoon go by. Finally he leans over to her with a miserable expression and quietly says “I don’t feel so hot.”

He rushes off, and after a couple of minutes, she decides to go after him.

-----

She rubs his back in gentle circles as he gags unproductively. She can feel the warmth of a rising fever through his shirt, as if he needed anything else to worry about.

The poor thing had shown up that morning with every intention of toughing it out. He did a great job up until he ended up stuck knelt over the toilet.

This stomach bug isn’t being kind to him.

A noise makes its way up his throat before he heaves again, and again until another wave of vomit comes up and splashes into the water below. Dan gives a distressed whimper, and she can’t blame him. His body refuses to let him rest, even for a second.

“Take it easy, sweetheart,” Julia hums, wrapping an arm around him and rubbing his shoulder. She wishes she could do more to help him feel better, but instead he’s just stuck throwing up until his stomach decides that it’s empty.

He breathes heavily, panting as he tries to gather his bearings. His eyes are watering and his face red, both from the force of throwing up so much in a small period.

He whines, an arm wrapping protectively around his stomach while the other reaches up to flush the toilet.

“I don’t know what I’m going to do,” Dan says, shaking his head. “I can’t just leave.”

“You certainly can’t go around tasting desserts today, either.” She lays the back of her hand on his temple before replacing it with a kiss. “And I think you’re a little warm. Do you feel feverish?”

Dan spits into the toilet and swallows hard before he finally nods.

“Oh, Dan...” she says, at a loss of what else to say. It’s not like she can just fix this situation, but she can certainly be there for him if he tries to get through the rest of the day.

He finally sits back, taking his weight off of his knees and instead sitting cross-legged with a truly miserable look on his face.

He snuffles, runs the back of his hand against his nose. “Could you- ugh, thank you.”

Julia presses a wad of toilet tissue into his hand before he can finish his request. He dries his eyes and blows his nose, and then sighs heavily.

“I can finish the day,” he says, voice barely above a whisper after his throat has been wrecked by several bouts of vomiting. “I just- I just might need to bow out here and there,” he chuckles.

“If you really want to.”

Julia rubs his arm, frowning at her friend’s state. He looks awful. He says as much when he finally manages to get up and look at himself in the mirror as he’s washing his hands.

“Oh God, the makeup department is going to hate me,” Dan mumbles as he inspects his face. His eyes are still red rimmed and the color on his cheeks probably isn’t leaving anytime soon.

He scrubs his hands and does so while looking like he could fall asleep right then and there.

“Wash your hands, too.” He nods to the second sink, and she’s quick to start pulling up her sleeves. “This is almost definitely contagious, I don’t want you getting sick too.”

“Right, thank you for reminding me.”

They start their trek back to the break area where the crew spends most of their non-recording time.

“Are you dizzy?” Julia asks when she sees how slowly and carefully he’s walking.

Dan nods minutely and blinks. “Just a little lightheaded. I’ll see if I can find something with like... I don’t know, electrolytes or something?” His voice dies at the last word and he has to clear his throat.

Julia’s never seen him this out of sorts. Dan’s usually so well at putting up a facade that no one feels the need to worry about him. But as she watches him, massaging his head and breath still sounding awfully shallow, she knows that whatever he’s come down with is intent on ruining his entire week there at the show.

“Hey,” she says quietly, putting an arm around him and pulling him closer. “I have some ibuprofen in my bag when you feel like you can keep it down.”

Dan nods. “Thank you, but I have no idea when that’s going to be,” he admits.

She looks at him, brows knitted together. Nausea is remaining so clearly painted on his face, and his hand is on his stomach as if it will help anything. He’s still feeling sick.

“Maybe you should um, warn someone.”

“What?” Dan narrows his eyes at her. They take a seat on a couch in the lounge, and he immediately leans back as far as he can.

“I mean, in case you need to... you know, while we’re shooting. To have something ready?”

“Like a bucket?”

Julia shrugs. “Better than the floor?”

Dan groans, dragging a hand down his face and quickly stopping himself when he realizes that he probably shouldn’t be touching his face at all right now. He shakes his head, swallowing again in the way that makes her wonder how close the next bout really is.

“I haven’t puked in a bucket since I was ten,” he says, as if it’s some sort of accomplishment.

She makes a disagreeing noise, face screwing up in thought. “Don’t think that’s true, Dan...”

“Okay,” he holds out his index finger. “Being drunk does not count. I physically could not make it to the bathroom.”

“Because you drank almost an entire bottle of whiskey.”

She’ll give him some leeway, he was having a particularly bad week and they both needed to let off some steam. Granted, she didn’t realize how bad of a week he was really having until she saw not wine or beer, but whiskey make an appearance.

She ended up babysitting a drunk Dan Levy that night, but with the amount of times he’s held her hair back during one of her own bad days, she can’t bring herself to hold it against him.

Dan’s cheeks puff out in a huff. “God, yeah. I’m never-” he stops, swallows, clears his throat. “Never doing that again.”

They’re called back a moment later. She feels the need to hover as Dan starts to stand up, ignoring the fact that he’s easily twice her size and she could do very little if he were to fall.

“Are you sure you’re okay?” She asks.

He nods, and that’s all she gets.

-----

It’s clear to see his stomach is bothering him throughout the recording. He leans against the counter of one of the contestants, clearly trying to keep a straight face through a stomach cramp. There’s no hiding the strain in his voice, as the baker immediately looks up in concern as if wondering she’s doing something wrong. He just smiles and tells her that she’s doing fantastic, keep up the good work.

He stays a safe distance from all of the food. Julia is impressed on how well he manages to politely decline their offers of having him as their taste-tester.

And while he’s speaking to everyone, Julia talks to their producer.

He raises an eyebrow but shrugs and agrees when she asks to be the one to taste the desserts instead of Dan.

“Dan!” He calls.

Dan looks up from talking to someone having a rough time with their baking.

“Julia’s doing the tasting! You can do it next!”

Dan gives a wide smile and a thumbs-up, gaze going to Julia. Thank you, he mouths.

She winks at him and goes to find a baker to bother.

-----

“She’s doing great,” Dan says. They’ve been watching the same contestant for several minutes. She’d been strangely methodical in everything, and she seemed to know exactly what she was doing.

“I only hope it tastes good. It’s not an easy flavor to pull off.” When he doesn’t reply, she looks over at him. “Dan? You alright?”

He nods, but the way his mouth is closed in a thin line and his eyes are focused on a particular area on the floor says otherwise. His throat bobs as he swallows.

“Hey,” she lays a hand on his arm, finding him just as warm as before.

She catches a whimpered, “Oh God...” as he brings a hand to his mouth.

And Julia immediately realizes that they have very little time before something terrible happens.

“Bathroom,” she says quickly, grasping his arm.

He nods and follows her, but they don’t quite manage to make it out before he gags into his hand. Nothing comes up, thank goodness, but it gets everyone’s attention. Several contestants start asking if he’s okay, or asking each other if he’s okay.

She manages to pull him as far as the grass outside before he gags again. It drips between his fingers, and he’s afraid to pull his hand away. She offers useless words as she rubs the spot between his shoulder blades, feeling his muscles contract with each painful heave. This bout isn’t quite as productive as the last one, he has little left in his stomach after all, so instead he’s just left breathless.

“Fuck...” he groans hoarsely.

Julia has a feeling that the tears dripping down his cheeks are not just from the strain of throwing up. That suspicion is all but confirmed when he jerks forward with a sob.

“Shhhh, it’s okay...” She says gently, widening the circles on his back. “It’s alright.”

He shakes his head, sniffles, and heaves again. It brings up a small stream of bile that lands in the grass below. She lets him finish, just staying with him for a few minutes before the time between bouts lessens.

He’s left breathing heavily, eyes watering.

“Let’s go sit down, come on.”

Dan follows her as far as the stairs where he sort of seems to give up. She sits next to him, hand still on his back. This just seems to be their default position today.

“God...” he mutters tearfully when he realizes that his hand is still covered in gross.

“Here, I think I have... aha!” of all days to have stuffed a random napkin in her pocket.

He breathes out a grateful thank you and cleans up as thoroughly as he can. His eyes are still watering, fresh tears dripping down his cheeks as he sniffles uselessly.

“Hey, it’s okay.”

Dan shakes his head, sniffling again. “I know you keep saying that, but it really isn’t. Literally everyone witnessed that.” His voice has a desperate edge to it, one that she only hears when he’s about to lose his shit.

“Well, here’s what we’re going to do if you’re feeling up to it. We’re going to get you cleaned up, and then go back in there and you can watch me taste all of those delicious things that you can’t eat right now,” she finishes with a teasing nudge against him.

It gets a small smile out of him, enough so that he chuckles through the tears. “Literally the worst part about being sick is not being able to eat anything.”

“Of course that would be your least favorite part about throwing up.”

-----

She helps him get himself back together, and informs the crew of their situation despite how much he didn't want anyone to know. ("It's pretty obvious, now." "Ugh. Fine.")

Everyone calls his name when he finally returns. She has to admit, he looks good. The makeup department must have gotten a hold of him.

The contestants start asking him if he's alright, and he just dismisses it, waving his hand.

"Just not feeling great! So, should probably keep your distance," he chuckles.

He's still missing his usual energy, Julia notices, but it's good to see that he isn't going to remain completely defeated for the rest of the day.

She wraps an arm around his waist when she returns from talking to everyone. He's shaking. It's subtle, hopefully not obvious on camera, but she can feel his shudders come in waves. She takes the few moments of everyone working on their finishing touches to cuddle him a bit. He welcomes it, resting his cheek against the top of her head.

"You're shivering."

Dan nods against her.

Medicine would help if he could keep it down. But the moment he drinks anything, he's putting himself at risk of throwing up again and that's probably that last thing he wants. So, she just hugs him.

Julia follows the judges as they talk to everyone about their dishes and, of course, taste them. Dan stays safely behind the camera. She doesn’t think anything of it when she glances over and he's shifting uncomfortably.

Until he makes a choking noise that gets everyone's attention, and darts out of the tent.

They keep recording.

Julia wishes that the judging process would go faster so she can check on her friend. She fidgets impatiently, even exchanging looks with the bakers a few times. Everyone knows that something's wrong, but they can't stop shooting for one person who's not even on camera.

Finally, they're done with that segment. Julia rushes off.

-----

She runs her fingers through his hair as he shudders, his head rested on her lap. He hasn’t thrown up in a while, and that's something, but he's also been bombarded by a headache and miserable fever chills. He was slow moving when they left the shoot today, and now he can hardly move.

"I…" Dan's voice shakes as he tries to get his thoughts together. "I don't know if I can get to the shoot tomorrow."

Julia sighs, nods, continues petting his hair. It's been a while since she's seen him this sick. Sick enough to consider taking a day off without being forced.

"They'll understand. I already spoke with them and told them you might not make it."

"Going behind my back I see," the sentence comes out strained as he curls up. "God fuck, my stomach hurts."

Julia shushes him gently. "I know… try to rest."

Dan whines pitifully, taking a moment to wallow in his misery before he looks up at her. "You know you don't have to stay here. I'm probably going to be up like, all night."

"I don't want to leave you alone, Dan. I've never seen you like this."

"One of us has to be functioning tomorrow, though."

"I'll be fine," she hums.

He doesn’t seem to have the energy to argue with her. He relaxes under her touch as she continues to card her fingers through his hair in the way that always helps him when he's having a bad day.
