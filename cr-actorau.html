Title: Actor AU
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly mistakes the new writer as a homeless person
Word count: 2.7k

Actor AU

"Sorry, Jess. I've been blowing my nose all morning." 

"That's okay, I can make you super pretty again. Not that you weren't pretty before, but now you'll look less sick and snotty." 

Molly giggled. "There's not enough makeup in the world to make me look less sick." He could see himself in the mirror, after all. His eyes were watery and swollen, nose developing a pink tint. In a perfect world, he would have just stayed home and nursed his cold. But actors couldn't exactly take sick days. There were a hundred people depending on the actors to do their job whenever they needed to, which meant Molly was expected to show up to the set whether he’d been up all night with congestion or not.

It wasn’t that bad. The gunk in his head made him feel a little fuzzy and slow to react to what he was being told to do, but he could deal with it for a day or two.

And Jester, the makeup artist, was just that. An artist. She had a talent for making him look amazing no matter what state he was in when he showed up in the morning.

So as expected, she worked her magic (so to speak) and Mollymauk felt like a living person again. Well, looked like a living person again. The makeup didn’t stop him from breathing through his mouth and clearing his throat of the goop clogging his sinuses. But he looked hot, and that was all that mattered.

Gustav, the director, greeted him excitedly as he walked inside the studio. The green screen was nearly blinding, the bustle of crew members overwhelming for someone who only had half of their senses at the moment. This movie was cheap and ridiculous, but it was fun to film, and it paid a lot of people’s bills, so Molly wasn’t going to complain about the excessive green screen.

“Molly,” Gustav smiled. “I got you a little something.” 

Molly found a paper cup, full of warm liquid. Could it be...? He lifted it to his nose, but of course he couldn’t smell anything. Instead, the steam began to relieve his congestion, so he had to pull it away before his nose started to run again.

“Pumpkin spice latte, your favorite.”

“Mm. Why?”

“Can’t I do something nice for my favorite actor?” Gustav chuckled, rubbing the back of his neck sheepishly.

“Well, you’re the director, you’re not exactly expected to bring me coffee.” 

They worked together a lot. Gustav wasn’t the best movie director in the world, but he made enough money, and he was the reason Molly was able to start acting in movies at all. Molly was now a staple for most of the man’s movies, the cult fanbase would wait desperately to see what character he would play in Gustav’s new film.

He was a father figure of sorts. Molly didn’t have a childhood, and Gustav was the first to show him he didn’t need one to be a functioning person. 

After a long, silent battle, Gustav relented with a sigh. “Alright, alright. I have good news, and bad news.”

“Mhmmm.” Molly took a sip of the coffee, and realized with deep dissatisfaction that he couldn’t taste it. “Go on.”

“Good news is that the movie is still happening!”

Molly raised an eyebrow. “Was it... not going to?”

Gustav cleared his throat. “Bad news is, um... the writer and I had a bit of a tiff a couple days ago.”

“Oh God.”

“But!”

“More good news?” Molly sniffled. 

“I hired someone new. He’s not experienced in script-writing, but I figured, better than nothing, right?”

Molly couldn’t help but giggle. “I suppose you have a point!” he was going to add more ‘on the bright side’ stuff, before he ended up sneezing at the ground. 

Gustav raised an eyebrow.

“Sorry,” Molly said, giving a hearty sniffle to clear his nose. “Fighting a bit of a cold.”

“Oh my. Let me know if you need anything.”

“Won’t need to! Just a cold!...snf!” He patted Gustav’s back and went off to find something to blow his nose with before they started filming. Poor Jester would probably need to be on standby today. He couldn’t just not blow his nose, unfortunately. 

Molly caught site of a box of tissues on a table. They probably decided to scatter them around the studio as it was cold and flu season and a lot of the crew had been sniffling the past few days.

There was a tickle in his nose that wouldn’t go away no matter how many times he wriggled it, so he brought the tissue to his nose and waited for the inevitable sneeze to come.

He waited, holding it in front of his face as the itch rose and fell until finally, results.

If results were multitudes of snot shooting out of his nose and ruining the thin piece of paper that was the flimsy shield between his hands and the copious amounts of good.

Molly groaned, grabbed another tissue, and went through the painstaking process of wiping and blowing his nose without doing too much damage to Jester’s artwork.

“Bless.”

He turned on his heel to find a very... rough-looking man. Still handsome, though, even though he was wearing old clothes and his hair hadn’t been trimmed in years.

“Oh, thank you. snf! I’m afraid this cold is kicking my arse,” he said, giving his nose one final wipe on a third tissue, which he then stuffed into his pocket and hoped no one would notice.

“Well wishes to you, then.” The man spoke with a Zemnian accent, something Molly rarely heard in Zadash. 

Molly felt guilty for even thinking it, but... the man didn’t look like he belonged there. Often, it wasn’t hard for random people to wander in. They didn’t have as much security as the bigger productions around the area, so sometimes hooligans or bored homeless people would just show up and watch the show without anyone paying them much mind. Gustav didn’t crack hard on them, but others were a little more wary as it was a good way to get things leaked before the crew intended.

“I’m sorry but... are you supposed to be here?” Molly asked with a wince, just to convey that he felt bad for asking. The man chuckled. “Not that I mind, exactly, but some around here don’t like randoms walking in and-”

Molly cut himself off as the man reached a hand out toward him.

“Caleb Widogast, the uh, new writer.”

Oh. Oh Gods.

Molly swallowed his pride very quickly. “Alright, well, that’s humiliating.”

“I am used to it.”

“Obviously my disease-fogged brain isn’t exactly working on all cylinders. Oh-!” Caleb was still holding his hand out, and as much as Molly would have loved to, he held his hands up. “Speaking of disease, you probably don’t want to shake my hand...” he admitted sheepishly. He’d feel horrible if the entire crew got sick because of him.

Caleb nodded and stuffed his hand into the pocket of his jacket. “Understood. I will see you around, Mister Mollymauk.” 

Indeed he did.

Caleb was around a lot, especially for a writer. He made sure everything was done right, but without stepping on Gustav’s toes.

Molly watched him fondly as he spoke to one of the younger actors, so kind and soft. Gustav had nearly been forced to fire the poor thing as his nervousness got in the way of his acting, but whatever Caleb said turned him confident on camera.

Molly almost lost character when the kid spoke in his new self-assurance. And then he did lose character when he had to stop the scene to cough.

“Sor-ry...” he sputtered out, trying to rid himself of the tickle in his throat. 

“Everyone take five!” Gustav called out as he led Molly off the set. “I think we’ll end early today, hm?”

Once he got control of his lungs again, Molly scoffed. “No! It’s a /cold, Gustav.”

“Oh, I know it’s a cold. But we only have so many filming hours in a day and you’ve spent half of those sneezing and coughing.”

Molly blushed. “I...I tried to medicate myself, I swear.”

Gustav gave a hearty chuckle and patted him on the back. “Since Marius is doing well, why don’t we film his scenes today?”

-----

“Bless,” 

Molly looked up with wide eyes. He hadn’t realized anyone was around when he had that particular sneezing fit. He ran a tissue under his nose self-consciously, wincing at the dull lavender makeup that came off with every touch.

“Mister Caleb,” Molly sniffled. “You’re still here.”

Caleb nodded. “Ja. Waiting for Jester, actually.”

“Waiting for- oh! Do you two know each other?”

“I suppose we do now.” 

Molly had no idea what that was supposed to mean, but Jester emerged from the makeup trailer with a huge smile as if she hadn’t been working cramped in that space all day.

“Okay, I’m ready!” Molly nearly stepped back when she caught sight of him. “Molly! We’re going to the diner. Do you want to come?”

Molly giggled. “Oh no, I’d rather not intrude on your date.”

Caleb quickly opened his mouth, but Jester managed to speak before he did, giving a loud laugh. “It’s not a date, Molly. Caleb said that he didn’t know the city very well and I thought he might like to see it. You don’t have to come if you’re not feeling good, though. I know you’re a little sick.”

“I’d be happy to join you. If the two of you are okay with being in my diseased presence, that is.”

Caleb huffed out a laugh, quiet and breathy. “Ja. I think we will live.”

The more Molly listens to him talk, the more he realizes that there’s more to Caleb Widogast than just silent contemplation. He gave off an air of melancholy, trying to disguise it as awkwardness. It was like he had to try really hard to enjoy their night out.

Molly rested his chin on his hand, feeling the fatigue of his cold. He never got tired this early, and yet here he was. 

“Caleb, I’d really like to know. What did you say to little Marius? Gustav has been trying to work with him for days, and then you swoop in.”

“Pardon?”

“Marius, our teen lead, you spoke to him and then he acted better than he has since Gustav hired him.”

“Ah,” Caleb took one more sip of his coffee. “I was just telling him about where I grew up. He asked about my accent, and the Zemni Fields.”

Molly almost didn’t believe him. Why would telling Marius about his childhood make him do better? Unless, of course, it made him grateful for the job he had, and more determined to get it done right.

“Huh,” he said, before taking a bite of his food. “Interesting.”

“Marius is so annoying.”

“Jester!” Molly laughed. Everyone knew it was true. He was a bit whiny, but much less so than a lot of other actors they’d worked with.

“What? It’s true! He always wants me to do his makeup a certain way, and I’m all ‘um this is the way Gustav wants it though’ and he’s like ‘oh okay’.” 

“That was some story,” Caleb said with a ghost of a smile on his face.

Jester rolled her eyes and nudged his shoulder. “Shut up!”

Despite the bickering, the three of them had a great night. Although it was becoming clear to all of them that Molly should probably go home and take care of his cold before it had a chance to get worse.

Two weeks later, Molly woke to his phone ringing. Much too loud for however late it was.

“Hello?”

“Mister Mollymauk? I- I am sorry for calling so late.”

Caleb? Why was Caleb calling him? “Is everything okay, Caleb?” Molly asked, stifling a yawn.

“Ja- no... I’m... I’m not sure where I am, or how to get home, and-”

Molly furrowed his brow. He could hear people talking in the background, faint clinking of something as well. “Caleb, where are you?”

“A bar of some sort. The uh... ah, the Evening Nip.”

Oh yes, Molly knew where that was. He’d been kicked out of that bar a couple times after antagonizing some racist drunk men who didn’t like their fortune, accused of angering them on purpose. Granted, that was probably true, but fuck them.

“Sorry. I did not know who else to call.”

“I’m on my way,” Molly said, already working to find shoes and clothes. “Stay safe.”

“Danke.”

-----

Caleb wasted no time getting into his car When he pulled up. Molly offered a smile and pretended like he didn’t really want to be asleep right now. 

“What’s going on, my dear? How did you get lost in the city in the middle of the night?”

“Ah...” Caleb swallowed convulsively a couple of times. He looked... he looked bad. Molly realized that he was holding an arm protectively over his middle, his ribs.

“Were you... were you mugged?” Molly asked with wide eyes. Zadash wasn’t exactly a crime-ridden city, but those kinds of things weren’t unheard of in any big city.

“I’m afraid I was in the company of some bad people in the past. They found me, gave me what I deserved, I suppose.” He swallowed again. And then coughed into his hand. 

Molly caught sight of blood and made an immediate detour to the hospital.

-----

The emergency room wasn’t busy at this time of night, thank goodness.

‘This Ikithon man found you, then.”

“Ja. I don’t... I don’t know how. I changed my name, moved away...”

“I knew Widogast wasn’t a real name.”

Caleb huffed out a laugh and immediately winced in pain. “Ja.”

“Well, we’ll keep you safe, Caleb.”

“...Danke.”

-----

“How’s the writing going?”

“Bad. Terrible.” Caleb sighed. “I should stick to books.”

Molly couldn’t help but smile. Gustav had hired him to write another script for a future movie. He and Caleb had been meeting at a coffee shop every week. Caleb would write while Molly would drink coffee and talk his ear off and distract him from writing.

Their friendship was comfortable. Sometimes Jester would meet them between jobs, sometimes she’d bring her obnoxious friend Beau. Sometimes Molly would bring his roommate Yasha.

A sudden sneeze snaps him out of his reminiscence. Caleb rubbed his nose on a napkin that looked like it about had it. “Oh, Caleb,” Molly cooed sympathetically. “No wonder you’re having trouble writing. You’re sick.”

Caleb scoffed. “I have worked through much worse illnesses than this. If you can work with a cold, so can I.”

Molly shrugged and crossed his legs, leaning back in his chair with his expensive coffee. “If you recall, I didn’t work that day, because I was too disgusting for the camera. Not that you’re disgusting! Illness somehow makes you more handsome, actually.” Fuck. Molly hoped that that was another one of those things people would let him get away with saying because he was him. Wow, that was… embarrassing.

“Ja, I’m sure.” Caleb sniffled. “Nevertheless, I worked through worse than this when I was working for Ikithon. He wasn’t an easy person to please, either.”

“Well, I don’t know what you did for him, but I have a feeling it was much different than the creative work you do now.” Molly smiled. “This cold is going to gum up your brain, Caleb. C’mon, just have something and relax with me.”

He could see the adorable little quirk of Caleb’s lips that he loved. When he was trying not to smile, and couldn’t help it. 

“Ja, alright.” Caleb sniffled and ran the back of his hand under his nose. 

“Fantastic! I’ll order you some… you like herbal tea?”

“Ja, but you don’t have to-”

“Shh!”

Molly returned with a little pot of tea he had a feeling Caleb would enjoy, as well as a cookie for each of them. 

They indeed spent the afternoon relaxing, talking about their lives and its current goings-on. Jester joined them at some point.

“Hey guys,” she said cheerfully. She immediately pulled up a chair and set her phone on the table. “Remember that Trent guy?” 

Caleb chuckled awkwardly. “Of course I do.”

“Look at this.” She slid her phone toward the two of them, and they shared the screen, reading carefully.

Trent Ikithon Arrested, the article read. 

Caleb gasped for the first time since they’d known him. He laid a hand over each of theirs, breathing heavily. There was a little smile appearing on his face.

“It’s over,” he said to himself.

“It’s over,” Molly echoed.

“Completely,” Jester agreed.
