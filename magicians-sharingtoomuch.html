Sharing Too Much

"This is the most embarrassing moment of my life," Eliot croaked, bucket clutched to his chest. 

Margo rubbed his back, but he could also hear her giggling.

“I seriously doubt that.”

“No, it was pretty bad.” He swallowed hard. “I think I’m gonna have to drop out. They can wipe my memory, they can forget that I existed, but I can’t go to school here any lo- Oh God...” Eliot choked up another wave of vomit. Margo’s hands went up and down his back gently, reminding him that he wasn’t alone. 

This wasn’t new for either of them. They’d had countless times where one had to nurse the other through a truly awful hangover. With how much the two of them drank, vomit wasn’t a surprise between them.

“Is - ulp - is he out of the shower yet?”

Margo hummed. “Mm, not sure. Maybe. Want me to go see?”

Eliot thought good and hard about it. “No.”

Margo snorted. “He’s gonna come looking for you anyway, you realize that right?”

“Let’s just hope I’m done puking by then.” Of course, there was no guarantee of that.

“No, I mean if you dropped out. He’d do that Quentin thing where he gets fixated on something and decides he needs to do it. He’d find you, El.”

Eliot spat out the residual gross in his mouth. “It’s a nice thought, but I don’t think so.” Quentin was much more... mysterious than Eliot would ever admit. Maybe mysterious wasn’t the right word, though. Quentin didn’t intentionally keep secrets about himself, he just didn’t feel the need to divulge every emotion unless someone asked.

Eliot had yet to ask if he had feelings for him. They’d been dancing around it for a while. Both of them were affectionate people, they’d been rather content just flirting a little, cuddling a little. Like tonight when Eliot had gone out with a group of upperclassmen and come back to the cottage completely exhausted.

Quentin was studying in the common room, and he didn’t even bat an eye when Eliot flopped on the couch and shoved all the books off of Q’s lap so he could use it as a pillow.

“I mean, you could’ve just asked.”

“Shhhh....” He felt so full, even though he didn’t think he’d eaten that much.

Quentin scoffed. Eliot fell asleep to his crush carding fingers through his hair.

He woke to sharp cramps in his abdomen. In his sleepy state, Eliot didn’t stifle the whimper that came up his throat. 

“What’s wrong?” Oh, right, he was snuggling with Quentin.

“Stomach...” Eliot moaned. 

Quentin didn’t have a chance to respond, because Eliot promptly vomited all over his lap and down his pant leg.

Eliot gasped, sitting up immediately, covering his face not because he felt sick but because he just puked on his crush and it was every and Oh God.

“Oh shit, Eliot?”

“I’m sor- Oh my God...” Eliot couldn’t stop the burp that came up, signifying that more was coming and he needed to get away from this situation and fast. “Q, I-”

What followed was an incredibly awkward interaction involving Eliot running for the closest bathroom and leaving Quentin covered in puke. Eliot considered legally changing his name and moving to another country.

But for now, here he was.

He should’ve known not to eat at any restaurant Todd suggested. Eliot was going to have Words with him later, but for now he was left to puke until the infected food was out of his body.

Margo butted her head against his arm as he set the bin down with a heavy sigh.

“I’m gonna go check on him, see how he’s doing. Might need help getting regurgitated pasta sauce out of his jeans.”

“Margo!”

Margo chuckled and patted his head. “Calm down, I’m just gonna clean this out, okay?” True to her word, she took the disgusting bucket with her to Eliot’s bathroom where he was holed up before Margo managed to convince him to just get in bed.

His stomach still felt full. With as many times as he’d puked so far, not a whole lot came up.

Eliot knew he was in for a rough night, he’d been through this plenty of times before during his and Margo’s adventures.

His stomach gurgled, irritated by all the shit that was going on. He kneaded it with a wince, wanting to remove his shirt and get rid of the extra layer between his hand and his tender belly.

“Uh, hey.”

Eliot looked up with wide eyes. Quentin stood in the doorway and pulled a piece of hair behind his ear as they made eye contact. He was in pajama bottoms now, which wouldn’t have meant anything if it was just because it was night time, but it was hard to think like that when Eliot knew he had to change his clothes because they were vomited on.

“Q, I am so, so-” 

Quentin immediately started shaking his head. He had the audacity to chuckle. He closed the door behind him and made his way to the bed and sat down next to Eliot.

“It takes more than that to gross me out, just in case you wanted to know.”

Eliot raised an eyebrow. “Interesting.” 

“Are you feeling any better?”

“Did your jeans survive the assault?” Eliot deflected.

Quentin laughed. “I think they’ll be alright. There might be a recovery time, though. Do uh, do you need anything? I can run to the campus store and-”

“Q...” Eliot laid a hand on Quentin’s. “Listen to me very carefully.”

Quentin’s eyes widened. “Uh, okay...”

“Never look at me again. I’m hideous, and with any luck I’ll be living in New Zealand by next week.”

“What.”

“He’s being dramatic.” Much to Eliot’s embarrassment, Margo sat the bucket next to him on the floor. “Why don’t you hang out with him for a while, Q? I’m gonna go take a break from cleaning up vomit.”

“Yeah, sure.”

Eliot glared at her, she had no remorse as always. Quentin was completely oblivious to his friends having a silent conversation right in front of him.

Things were awkward for a good few seconds before Eliot’s stomach made a horribly-loud noise. He found himself wincing and wrapping both arms around his middle, present company be damned, he was in pain and he wanted it to stop.

He groaned, flopping back onto the bed and curling up in the fetal position.

“Stomach ache?”

Instead of answering, because that was rather obvious, Eliot sighed. His voice was tightened in pain, but he still felt the need to say it one more time. “I’m really sorry. I wouldn’t blame you if you never wanted to look at me again.”

Quentin laughed. “I think this is the most I’ve heard you apologize since I’ve met you.”

“Oof. Okay, point tak-ow-taken...”

Quentin pulled his legs up onto the bed. It would have had Eliot’s heart skipping beats had he not felt like shit.

The cramping in his stomach intensified and he squeezed his eyes shut, waiting for it to pass. There were all sorts of pains going on in his belly and he hated all of them. Fucking Todd.

Q reached over, and hesitated. “Do you mind if I...?”

Eliot wasn’t sure what he was asking, but no matter what it was, he readily agreed with a nod, face still scrunched up in pain.

Quentin gently pulled his arms away and rested on his stomach. Quentin started rubbing, gentle at first, and getting a little more  brave once he realized Eliot wasn’t stopping him.

Eliot moaned, this time in relief. He wasn’t sure if it was dissipating the cramps, but it sure felt good to have some sort of contact no matter how weird.

“Is this...?”

“Yes.” 

Quentin huffed out a laugh. “Gotcha.” His fingers expertly kneaded into Eliot’s stomach, finding the perfect spots to relieve his pain and help him feel better.

Eliot could kiss him, and he told him so.

“Maybe after you’re done puking,” Quentin said with a laugh.

The only way Eliot would have been happier about he and Quentin falling asleep in the same bed was if he wasn’t constantly getting up to puke and waking Quentin in the process.

He thought he’d feel better when he woke up, after getting all of the damned dinner out of his system.

He didn’t.

Eliot woke, shivering and feeling very sorry for himself. He could hardly get out of bed, even if he wanted to, and his limbs cried out at every movement. 

Maybe this wasn’t food poisoning.

He sighed irritably. He had shit to do, he couldn’t be sick right now. 

But there were good things to come out of all of this. He looked over to the spot next to him. Quentin was still fast asleep, looking adorably calm. The line of anxiousness on his forehead was absent, his chest moving up and down with each steady breath. Eliot smiled.

Good things indeed.

For a few minutes, things were perfect. He might have felt terrible, but he kinda-sorta spent the night with the guy he liked.  Ish.

Quentin made a noise, his brow creased and he curled up. Eliot wondered if he’d somehow disturbed him before he heard a familiar noise that didn’t come from his own stomach.

Quentin’s eyes fluttered open, squinting at the brightness of the day.

“Morning beautiful,” Eliot chuckled, trying his best to make his hoarse voice sound appealing. 

Quentin gave a slight smile before his eyes widened. He sat up slowly, hand going to his stomach, and then promptly rush to Eliot’s bathroom.

Eliot winced at the sound. 

Oops.

A few seconds went by as he tried not to feel guilty for the miserable sounds coming from his bathroom. He gave Quentin privacy for a while, but in the end found himself pulling his weak, shivery body out of bed to check on him.

Quentin, hunched over the toilet in a ball of misery, spared a glance up at him as he walked in. “Fuuuck you,” he moaned.

“Yeah... that’s completely fair,” Eliot decided. “I swear I thought it was food poisoning.”

Quentin sighed, until the sigh turned into a heave that led to nothing. “I know.” He gave a tight burp. 

Eliot could feel bile rising in his throat from the sick sounds. “I probably shouldn’t stick around here for too long but I’ll make all of this up to you, alright?”

“Please go away.”

Eliot nodded. “My pleasure.” 

He listened to Quentin throw up for the next half hour before the poor guy finally emerged. He was flushed, a thin sheen of sweat on his face and hair obviously damp. Eliot wasn’t sure he’d ever seen him so miserable.

He cleared his throat. “I’m gonna...” Quentin gestured vaguely. “My room...”

Eliot frowned. “You don’t have to...” he didn’t want him to.

“I don’t want to- to invade your space or whatever.”

“Are you upset with me?” Eliot decided to out and say it. He felt to awful to beat around the bush.

“No! I just don’t want to bother you!”

“Q, get in the bed.”

Quentin sighed like an angsty teen and did so. Neither of them were in a mood to snuggle, but it was nice being so close.

They stayed in bed most of the day, both trying to sleep off whatever this was. Margo delivered gatorades at one point so they didn’t waste away (her words).

Quentin nudged Eliot awake from a less-than-restful sleep.

“I need- can you hand me the-” 

It took Eliot’s sickness-fogged mind longer than it should have for him to realize Quentin was desperately asking for the bucket that was on his side of the bed. He grabbed it and got it to Quentin, who was propped up on an elbow with the other hand hovering over his mouth.

Quentin burped before bringing of a sickening string of bile. He groaned. “God, El...what did I ever do to you?”

Eliot snorted. “Loved me too hard, I suppose.”
