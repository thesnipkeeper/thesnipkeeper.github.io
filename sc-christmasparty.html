Christmas Party

David and Patrick were determined to make this the best Christmas party in Schitt’s Creek. Well, mainly David. Patrick considered himself “along for the ride”, because as long as he still got to spend the day with the love of his life, he was happy.

Originally it was supposed to be Christmas Eve. Patrick wasn’t entirely sure what happened, but he had a feeling it had something to do with a very upset-looking Johnny Rose who walked into their store a couple weeks ago.

The Rose family were still having their Christmas Eve party this year. Perhaps they didn’t want David and Patrick stealing their thunder on Christmas Eve (even though an apartment would be a better place to host a party than a motel room, but Patrick wasn’t getting in the middle of that). So, a week before Christmas it was.

Patrick left David to the party planning. Frankly, getting in the way of David Rose when he had a plan was a bad idea that could only result in petty arguments.

“Are you sure you don’t want me to do something before I leave?” There was no point in asking when David was already nudging him toward the door. There were several bags of brand new party supplies that David had been eager to use.

“Nope. You just go to the store and do work things. This place will be so fucking beautiful and Christmas-y by the time you get back.

Patrick stopped a foot away from the door and chuckled at his frazzled fiance. “I’m not going to lie, Party Planner David scares me a little bit.” and Jocelyn, and Mr. and Mrs. Rose, and probably just about anyone who worked for him back in New York. But credit where credit was due, he got stuff done.

“Good. Then it’ll get you out of my way faster,” David smirked. “Buh-bye!” He gave a cheesy wave goodbye, sending Patrick on his way to work.

They set the party date to a Monday partly because the store closed early on Mondays. Patrick would be back in a few hours to help David with whatever he needed (food. Cooking and preparing food was not David’s strength.)

-----

“Oh, David...” Patrick gasped. He could hardly recognize his apartment, gaping at all of the intricate decorations as he threw his keys on the counter. “This is amazing.”

David scoffed. “Um, I’m aware.” he certainly looked like he’d been working all day. While David wasn’t one to willingly make his vulnerabilities known, Patrick had developed the ability to read him pretty well. David’s mannerisms weren’t nearly as extravagant and wired when he was sleepy. And wow, he looked exhausted.

The apartment did look amazing, though. David had cleaned areas of Patrick’s home that he hadn’t even thought of cleaning, and Decorated parts that he never thought needed decorating. And it was all very pretty. “You did good.”

He hummed happily. “I don’t mean to put you to work the minute you get home, but um...” David nodded pointedly toward the kitchen where all of the new, unopened ingredients sat.

“Ah, yes. Artichoke dip and... peppermint bark, right?”

David nodded, and bit his lip sheepishly, even though he didn’t look to be suffering any real guilt. “I may have bought ingredients for extra peppermint bark? If you um... would be willing? To make extra? For us to hold onto after the party?”

Patrick laughed. “I think I can do that.” It was a cute quality. After learning all about David’s expensive taste, he didn’t expect for his love to fall for some peppermint bark they bought from a family at Elmdale’s annual Christmas market. David couldn’t get enough of the stuff since.

“I’d stay and help, but-”

“Yeah, please don’t.”

“Mhmm! Bad idea, yes, so I think I’m going to go lie down for a few minutes. Regain my bearings before everyone comes. That okay?”

“Go ahead. You worked harder than I did today,” Patrick smiled.

David breathed a thankful sigh and disappeared. From the sounds of it, he had immediately crawled into bed without so much as taking anything off. He must have been really tired, and he deserved a break with how good Patrick’s apartment looked.

Patrick slipped some headphones in and found the cheesiest Christmas playlist to listen to without disturbing David.

Patrick started on the dip, quickly mixing it together and getting it into the oven. With any luck it’d be done but still be warm by the time the guests showed up.

Then he started melting the chocolate. He expected David to be hovering over his shoulder and watching intensely by now. David wasn’t usually one to not try to sneak spoonfuls of chocolate when Patrick wasn’t looking.

Patrick didn’t give it much thought when David remained in bed as he poured the melted white chocolate on top of the hardened base.

He sprinkled some crushed candy canes on top, just enough for a crunch, which was how David decided he preferred it. The pan was huge. He could put half out for the guests and hide the other half for when everyone leaves and David would need a late-night snack.

He felt accomplished as he looked at the plate of sweet treats that would no doubt be rearranged later to David’s liking. The bark looked good.

He stopped his music. “David, I’m done! I think it’s time for a taste test, huh?”

No response. Patrick looked back but only found the curtain to the bed drawn closed.

Huh. He’d definitely been asleep for longer than a few minutes.

Patrick put a few pieces on a plate and sneaked to the other side of the apartment. He quietly pulled the curtain back, intending on waking his love gently.

Patrick frowned. Instead, of finding David peacefully asleep in one of his dead-to-the-world naps, he was curled up, eyebrows pinched together in discomfort.

He climbed into the bed while minding the plate of treats. Patrick didn’t even need to nudge David; the other woke immediately.

Patrick laid a hand on David, and his frown deepened.

“Hey, honey,” he said softly, abandoning the plate on the nightstand so he could wrap his arms around David. “You feeling okay? You feel a little warm.” ‘A little’ was an understatement, but Patrick had a feeling that “You have a fever David what the fuck?” would have been a little too overwhelming for someone who just woke from a nap.

David moaned and got as close as he could to Patrick, snuggling into his arms. “It just kind of hit all at once...” and finished the sentence with a rough coughing fit that shook both of them. He sniffled, whining and pressing his face into Patrick’s chest. “My head hurts...”

Patrick held onto him and ran his fingers through David’s hair as he thought about their situation. David wasn’t in any state to host a party. Patrick leaned back to kiss his forehead, and went back to hugging him.

“I’ll take care of everything, okay?”

“Mmm? No, no we’re not canceling the party.” Another cough, badly muffled into Patrick’s shirt. “Jus’ need a minute. snf!”

Patrick pulled away to get a look at him. “I think what you need is a tissue,” he said, vaguely indicating to David’s face where clear liquid was sluggishly making its way to his upper lip.

“Oh mby God, snrf! ew...” he pulled his sleeve over his hand and rubbed his nose.

“A tissue, David. I said a tissue.” He was joking, but David was starting to tense up even more. His breathing was getting a little quicker now. Patrick recognized those shallow breaths anywhere. “Okay, okay...” Patrick gently pulled David’s hands away from his face and wrapped his arms around him tighter, if anything to remind him that he was still there to help fix his problems. “This came on fast, it’s probably the flu...” Patrick said as lowly and carefully as possible. “So, we probably don’t want people coming over here.” He chuckled, jostling David a little (but he didn’t seem to mind). “The whole town would hate us if we were the cause for the next big flu outbreak.”

David huffed out what Patrick assumed was a laugh. “Maybe you’re right.”

“Don’t forget the garland!”

“We’re not going to forget the garland, David.”

David groaned in frustration. “Be careful!”

Alexis groaned louder. “Okay, seriously David, you need to stop talking because you sound like shit.”

Patrick chuckled as he boxed the last of their decorations. Alexis promised to do them justice. She had almost as good of taste in decorating as he did, David admitted. But it obviously still pained him to watch them take down everything he’d done.

“Alexis, don’t you dare mess this up for me. This is still my party.”

Alexis hummed faux-thoughtfully. “I don’t think so. Pretty sure it’s mine now. Bye!”

David looked like he was about to run after her when the door closed.

Patrick breathed out a sigh of relief. He loved Alexis, but she was exhausting.

David coughed, harsh and painful, and unhooked himself from where he’d been watching them over the back of the couch. He flopped back into his blanket nest.

He was still wildly feverish and looking two seconds away from falling asleep again, but he must have had just enough energy to pout.

“I know you’re upset,” Patrick started.

“Uh, yeah! I am!”

“But I have something that might make you feel better.”

Instead of scoffing in disbelief, David just raised an eyebrow from under his pile of blankets.

Patrick took the few steps to the kitchen and pulled out the plate of extra peppermint bark.

David gasped. “You know, I think I’m cured."
