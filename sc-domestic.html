Domestic

It became painfully clear by the time that they returned home that the “little cold” David had been dealing with wasn’t actually as little as they thought.

As was usually the case with being sick, it got worse at night. They both realized it the minute that they got in bed together. David coughed painfully for the third time since they laid down. He complained of not being able to breathe, and very much snapped at Patrick when he told him just to go to sleep.

They were tired, David was sick and cranky. Patrick managed to go to sleep despite his ailing boyfriend’s wiggling.

Only to feel the weight on the other side of the bed disappear. David got up, he assumed to blow his nose or use the bathroom. Instead, Patrick heard one of the dining chairs scrape along the floor. Fine, he decided. He’d give in and open his eyes.

David was on his computer, seemed to have taken the tissue box with him, as he had one pressed to his nose in the perfect picture of misery.

“David,” Patrick yawned. “What’re you doing?”

David shook his head dismissively. “Just going to wait until it’s impossible to stay awake. Don’t mind me.”

Fuck. Patrick couldn’t just let him wear himself out. From the looks of things, he’d done this plenty of times when he was sick. Now, though, he had someone to make sure he didn’t make himself sicker.

Patrick rubbed the sleepiness out of his eyes and checked the time. It was only eleven. It wasn’t like it was the middle of the night, they had time.

He’d gotten David’s attention as he swung his legs over the edge of the bed and got up. Patrick found the nearest jacket and pulled it on, and then grabbed his sneakers as well.

“Where are you going?” David asked, voice muffled by the tissue that was still pressed to his face. His eyes were watering from the congestion, and he looked like he desperately wanted to be asleep.

“CVS in Elmdale is twenty-four hour, so I’m going to grab some cold meds to help you sleep,” he informed.

David paused for a moment as Patrick finished pulling his shoes on. “You- you don’t have to do that. I’ll crash eventually,” David said with a shrug.

“I know,” Patrick pressed a kiss to the top of his head. “But you’re not feeling good and I want to help.”

“Fine,” David snuffled thickly, stuffed the tissue in his sweatpants pocket, and closed the laptop. “Then I’m coming with you. Maybe it’ll, I don't know, wear me out or something.”

So, they went on a nearly-midnight drive to CVS and wandered over to the cold and flu aisle. David had snuffled and sneezed his way through a handful of tissues by the time they finally decided on the right stuff to buy.

David sneezed productively when they got in the car.

“Bless you,” Patrick hummed, taking the box of tissues they’d bought and putting it in his lap for the drive home.

“Ugh, God...” David croaked. “I think I’d like to take the day off of work tomorrow.”

That sentence was used a lot during hangovers and a bad night’s sleep. He never really expected Patrick to agree, and Patrick knew he was fine working. But getting a look at his sick, miserably, and hideously contagious boyfriend, he couldn’t help but agree.

“I think that’s a good idea.”

-----

David felt only slightly guilty for finding his boyfriend so adorable when he was sick. There was just something endearing about the guy at the mercy as something as simple as a cold.

It was less adorable when he decided to ask a favor of him because of the cold.

“I’m sorry, you want me to what?”

Patrick sighed. “David, I can’t exactly make anything for others right now. The food safety inspectors would run down my door,” he chuckled which then stumbled into a horribly congested cough.

“Then tell them that you can’t make the food!” David threw his hands out in a ‘duh’ manner.

Patrick pressed his lips together with frustration. It only lasted a moment, though, because he was incapable of breathing through his nose. Those big brown eyes, pleading, red-rimmed and watery from illness.

“I already bought all of the ingredients,” Patrick said. “I just can’t make the salad myself because I’d probably end up sneezing on it or something.”

David went through a whole face journey as he tried to decide whether it was worth it. “Yeah, you probably would.”

“See? It’s just macaroni salad, okay? All you have to do is cook the pasta and mix some stuff with it.”

David growled under his breath, not unlike his sister, he realized, after living with her for years. “Fine.”

Patrick smiled cutely. “I love you.”

“Mm’kay. You probably don’t want to say that until you witness my cooking.”

So Patrick sat comfortably at the table as he read off the recipe to him. David had to admit, cooking was less stressful when it wasn’t his mother telling him what to do. And when all he had to do was make pasta.

Patrick sneezed and sniffled thickly.

“Bless you,” David sing-songed. “What do I do now?”

“Drain the pasta. snf!” Poor Patrick sounded so stuffed up. He snuffled and huffed out a breath before blowing his nose. “And let it cool for a minute.”

“Okay, okay...” David said to himself. He poured the contents of the pan through the drainer while Patrick muffled a cough behind him.

When the pasta was finally ready to be taste-tested, David nodded in satisfaction.

“How is it?”

“I... I think I did it?” He said with a proud smile. “It’s like, not disgusting.”

Patrick chuckled. “Good. Now um... would you mind being the one to take it to the potluck at the cafe?”

David hesitated. “Exactly... what is this potluck for...?”

“Well,” Patrick cleared his throat. “I told the jazzagals I’d make them my macaroni recipe in exchange for advertising the apothecary...”

David’s face went neutral for a moment. “You know I’m not going to make it out of there without at least my mother talking to me. They all want to chat with me.” He whined. “I don’t do small talk, Patrick!”

“Which is why I didn’t tell you who it was for until you made it?” Patrick said sheepishly.

“Patrick Brewer, that’s incredibly manipulative of you.”

Patrick smiled. “I’ve got to fit in with your family at some point, right?”

David huffed. But he had to admit he was a little impressed. There were very few people he would do this for. He rolled his eyes dramatically and snatched up the covered bowl.

“You owe me.”

-----

David leaned his head on Patrick’s shoulder and snuffled miserably. He was so remarkably stuffed up, and this was only the second day of the cold that was no doubt going to make his life hell for the rest of the week.

“Hi.” There was a fond smile in Patrick’s voice. He didn’t avert his eyes from the book he was reading.

“I feel like shit,” David announced. He sniffled, both out of necessity and example.

His cheek smushed against Patrick’s shoulder, and things were relatively adorable until his nose started to itch.

“Oh for fu-” was all he got out before he had to twist away to avoid spraying his fiance. “Ugh, God, Patrick-? Thanks...” Patrick already had the tissue box held out to him, and David snatched a couple to blow his nose. It hardly moved a thing in his swollen sinuses.

Patrick still didn’t look up from his book, but David could see the smile this time. “Bless you, sweetheart.” He’d been rather quiet all night. Not unkindly so, just quiet.

David wanted to say thank you, or maybe complain about how terrible he felt, but he couldn’t do either of those things because his nose demanded his attention a second time. And then a third.

“Bless you, bless you.”

“You don’t have to keep doing that.” David sniffled thickly and scrubbed at his nose. “It’ll get old for both of us.”

“Yeahh,” Patrick said. “You’re probably right about that.”

David coughed. Jesus fuck, his entire body was just bent on torturing him tonight.

“Oof, that doesn’t sound good.” Patrick finally pulled his gaze from his book to look him over. “Is this going to be one of those colds?”

“You mean the ones where I can’t stop coughing and neither of us sleep for a week? Maybe.” David sighed shakily, doing his best to avoid another cough. He folded his arms and plunked against the headboard. “In sickness and health, right?”

Patrick chuckled and nodded. “Yup. That’s what we’re both signing up for.” He kissed David’s cheek before pulling back with a frown.

David widened his eyes and sniffled. “What?”

Patrick’s brows furrowed as he brought a hand up to his forehead. His frown deepened. “You feel a little warm...”

“Warm? Like a fever?” David groaned dramatically, which did induce another cough. “What did I do to deserve this?”

“David, you’ll be fine.”

“I know I’ll be fine, but-” he whined. “We were supposed to have a date night tomorrow.”

Patrick kissed his cheek again. “We can do that any other night.”

David snuffled and dragged the back of his hand under his nose. He grimaced at the wet streak across his skin.

“I could just heavily medicate myself. Do you still have some of that cold medicine from last time I was sick?”

“Oh my God, David, you’re not drugging yourself up just so we can go one a date.” He wrapped an arm around David and pulled him close until the two of them were cuddling.

David nodded. “Probably not the best idea. But still. I’m capable of looking presentable when I have the plague. It wouldn’t be a problem.”

At the silence, David turned to look at his fiance who was wearing a strange, oddly guilty expression. Patrick cleared his throat.

“I’ve got to be honest with you, David.”

David felt himself tense. “What?”

“My throat’s a little sore.”

David swore. “Are we both just doomed to suffer this week, then?” he went back to snuggling. No wonder Patrick had been quiet tonight. He was getting sick. “Sorry I gave you my cold,” he said quietly after a few seconds.

“In sickness and health,” Patrick replied with a smirk. “This is probably just the beginning of married life. Sharing germs because we can’t keep our hands to ourselves and paying for it later.”

David scoffed, sniffled. “You know what? I think I’m okay with that.”

Patrick smiled. “Me too.”
