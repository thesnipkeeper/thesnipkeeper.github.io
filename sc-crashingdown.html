Crashing Down

It happens during their first month in Schitt’s creek.

It’s been years since he’s had to share a city with his sister, let alone a motel room. They somehow make it work, organizing shower times and ways of getting dressed without having any more humiliating walking-in-on-each-other moments.

But there’s no hiding their quirks when they’re around each other every hour of every day (not when they can help it, but still.)

For example, David finds out his sister talks in her sleep. A lot. He’s managed to throw a pillow at her a few times to get her to shut up, but his aim isn’t what it should be at two in the morning and he usually misses.

And Alexis finds out exactly how depressed and anxiety ridden her brother really is. Not by choice, of course.

His family has made countless jokes about how neurotic he is, about how none of his friendships or relationships last. He used to be okay with being a joke, because he hardly saw them. They didn’t have to witness his little... breakdowns.

Until tonight.

It’s two in the morning, Alexis hasn’t been talking in her sleep, that’s not what woke him. Well, nothing woke him up, because David hasn’t been to sleep yet.

He tried. Fuck, did he try.

Ever since the whole incident at the vet, he’s been doing good at keeping his anxiety in check. Stevie’s checked up in her own Stevie way. And while it’s going to take a long, long time to accept that he’s lost everything, he’s been doing better.

And then one of his New York “friends” texted him a photo of all of them attending the estate sale at their old house. They looked excited to see him having been knocked down a peg, enjoying his misery. And he... lost it.

So here he is, in the bathroom at two in the morning, crying hysterically while trying to keep quiet because his sister is sleeping in the other room and the moment she hears him, she’s going to make it her goal to find out what’s happening.

He sobs, uselessly scrubbing his face on the sleeve of his sweater. Every part of his brain is just telling him how useless he is, how useless he’s always been. He has nothing now, and he never had friends.

Is he really such an awful person that he deserves this?

knock knock

Fuck.

He sniffles, tries to quiet down.

“David, are you almost done?”

David chokes out a quiet sob and does his best to clean his face. He looks at his reflection, sees his red, puffy eyes and knows that it’ll be obvious.

He doesn’t say a word before he opens the door and finds himself face-to-face with his sister.

Alexis’ face falls. “Whoa, are you- are you okay? Did your favorite sweater unravel or something?”

David rolls his eyes, tries to laugh, and goes to leave. She stops him with a hand on his chest.

“David,” she says, more seriously. “What’s wrong?”

“What do you think is wrong? Look where we are, Alexis!”

“Oh my God, okay, and you’re just freaking out about that now?”

David’s about to argue, say something defensive or insult her intelligence in the way their parents always told him not to because just because his sister didn’t value her formal education didn’t mean she was dumb.

Instead, he crumbles, tears falling down his face once again.

Alexis swears and wraps her arms around him, letting him bury his face in her neck despite the fact that he’s probably getting gross tears all over her.

“Okay,” she says softly. “Is there like, anything I can do to help?”

David pulls away, sniffling wetly and drying his face with what are now pretty damp sleeves. “I don’t know,” he admits, shaking his head.

She looks unsure, not breaking her gaze on him for a moment. “O...kay. I have an idea? We could stay up and watch a movie? You know, something else to focus on or whatever, right?”

David’s tempted to refuse, to tell her to leave him alone and that he’s fine when he’s obviously not.

But...

This is the first time since they were kids that Alexis has been this... nice. It’s interesting. Different.

“Fine,” he sighs out with another sniff. “But I get to choose the movie.”

She thinks long and hard for a moment, and says, “Fine. Go start it or whatever, I still have to pee.”

It’s a few days after their first date. Things are good. Things are really good. David’s never felt this amazing, this happy, this carefree at the beginning of a relationship. There’s something about Patrick that offers a sense of comfort David hasn’t had anywhere else.

It’s the beginning of summer. The days are just starting to get hot enough to annoy a person. And the store’s air conditioning doesn’t work. Stevie graciously lent them a fan from the motel so customers don’t complain too much of the heat.

Patrick and David are cooking, however, as they’ve been restocking and trying to work despite the fact that they may very well die of heat stroke before closing time today.

Patrick’s already got his sleeves rolled up as far as he could manage, as well as a few unfastened buttons at the top of his shirt. He’s sweating. A lot. And David would find the sight of the guy he likes wiping his brow incredibly hot if he himself wasn’t suffering twice as much.

He isn’t taking off his sweater. He likes this sweater. He looks good in this sweater. It may, however, be the cause of his early demise if he doesn’t take it off. He growls under his breath and disappears into the back to remove it. Good thing he wore a semi-fashionable t-shirt underneath or his whole outfit really would be ruined by summer.

Patrick smiles in delight when he emerges. “I knew you were capable of getting hot like the rest of us!”

“Ugh, I know. I held out for as long as I could. It’s so fucking hot in here, though.” He growls.

He walks up behind Patrick at the register, and if it wasn’t burning like actual hell in there he’d consider doing something cute like giving him a kiss. But they’ve both agreed that affection might be off the table today, at least while they’re cooking to death in the store. Instead, he just reaches across his... co-worker, careful not to touch him too much, and grabs his water bottle that’s on the counter on the other side of Patrick.

David’s heart nearly stops when Patrick says, “What’s that?”

His eyes are locked on the inside of David’s arm, even when he tries to pull it out of sight. “Um, what?”

But it’s obvious that playing dumb isn’t going to fly this time. The sad look on Patrick’s face is akin to one of a puppy. Oh God. He hasn’t known Patrick nearly long enough to have this conversation. Granted, has he really known anyone long enough to have this conversation?

Except for Stevie and Alexis. Stevie knows. She might not know all of the details, but they have slept together which meant seeing a lot of each other’s bodies. And he couldn’t exactly hide it from Alexis when they were sharing a tiny motel room. That year wasn’t particularly pleasant for him.

David looks anywhere but Patrick as he crosses his arms over his chest, trying very hard to hide them.

He didn’t even think about the ramifications of taking off his sweater. God damn it, it’s just a sweater. He was just hot.

Patrick says his name, which has him looking into those big brown eyes allover again. “David-”

David shakes his head, finding himself giving a weak laugh. “Um. This is awkward, right?” he says with a grimace. Patrick doesn’t respond. David sighs, “They’re old.”

“But they are...?”

He nods quickly before Patrick can finish. “Yes. They’re- I did that to myself. But honestly, it’s just nasty-looking scars at this point?”

Patrick doesn’t say anything. He glances at David’s arms again, and oh fuck, he’s been gesturing, making the scars in plain view once again.

David goes back to crossing his arms.

He’s sure he knows how Patrick must feel. Patrick knew that he was a lot the moment they met, but it’s possible that he never considered how much David was until now. Here come those regrets, David thinks. No one wants the responsibility of dealing with someone like him on a regular basis.

Patrick’s frown deepens.

This is it. This is when he realizes who he’s really gotten into business with. Gotten into a relationship with. This is when he backs out.

David rapidly thinks of what to say to fix this. He just wanted to take his fucking sweater off God fucking-

Patrick hugs him.

Despite his confusion, David hugs back.

It’s warm, both in the nice affectionate sense and in the oh God it’s so fucking hot in this store this is awful sense. But neither of them really mind. They stay like that for a few seconds before Patrick finally pulls away.

“That was um, nice?”

“I’m sorry, David.”

David scoffs immediately. “Look, it’s nothing you have to worry about, okay? I promise.” He clears his throat. It needs to be said, he can’t drag some poor guy along just because he feels bad for him. Which he shouldn’t. “But if this changes things, I completely understand, just tell me-”

Patrick quickly shakes his head. “No, David! No, God no. This doesn’t change a thing!”

“Are you... sure?” David asks with a grimace. He’s had this conversation before, and it’s never ended well.

“Of course I’m sure,” Patrick says softly.

David tries very hard not to tear up, and he sort of succeeds. He can’t remember the last time someone said that to him, that they want to stay around him despite one of his many enormous flaws.

Patrick seems to have one more question, though. “When you say that they’re old, exactly how old...?” He clears his throat.

David nods as if to assure Patrick that it’s an okay question. From him, anyway. “Um, a few weeks.”

Patrick’s eyes widen.

“I- one of my exes came to town, and I wasn’t emotionally prepared for it at all.” He swallows. “Um, it wasn’t the best experience. Well, until that night, when I slept with him because he took pictures of my mom when she asked him not to and she would have died if anyone saw them. But also, it was really good sex until he saw the cuts and made some,” he clears his throat, willing himself not to get watery at the memory. “Back-handed comment about them.”

“Oh God, David.”

“Yeah,” David nods. “I know. I’m a mess.”

Patrick smiles so sweetly he thinks his heart is going to flutter out of his chest. “Well, I’m glad you felt comfortable enough to tell me. Sorry if I... overstepped.”

He shakes his head and presses a kiss to Patrick’s (albeit, sweaty) forehead. “Thanks for caring.” and he truly means it.

-----

He certainly didn’t expect to start crying in the middle of a quiet evening.

Alexis is at the table doing her nails, calming down after a lengthy argument about her tattoo. She’s still salty at him for bringing it up, but he doesn’t feel particularly bad. She’s always blabbing things around town anyway.

Thankfully not this particular thing.

Their dad probably wouldn’t have made that joke if he had known. But he still said it, and now David can’t get the hurt to leave. He’s suddenly reminded of everything wrong with him, as if these anxieties and thoughts were just waiting for the perfect moment to crash down on him.

It’s not exactly unusual for him to be in bed so early. He almost gets away with crying under the covers like an angsty teen until he chokes out a sob that has him crying harder.

He hasn’t taken the sweater off. He probably should, this one is too scratchy to sleep in. He still needs to start his skincare routine and brush his teeth and...

“David, are you crying?” Alexis asks from the other end of the room, trying to sound uninterested. She’s still mad at him, after all.

He doesn’t respond right away, struggling to fix his face before he sits up and lets her see him. He might still look like he’s been crying, but it won’t be quite as pathetic, right?

But he’s not even half done getting himself together before he feels a weight on the edge of his mattress. He emerges from his blanket to see his sister, and immediately sighs and falls back against his pillow.

“Is this about what dad said? Because you know-”

David sighs shakily, pulling himself to sit up against the headboard. Alexis still looks a little impatient, but it’s her choice to be talking to him right now, so he doesn’t care.

“It’s not just what dad said. I don’t know what’s wrong with me.” He shakes his head in exasperation at himself and drags both hands down his face.

Alexis groans. “David, I’m still mad at you. Don’t make me want to be here for you.”

“Oh my God, I didn’t ask you to be here for me!” He snaps, or tries to, but he finds himself laughing halfway through.

Alexis growls again, but with more frustration.

“And are you telling me that the look on mom and dad’s face when they realized their twelve year-old daughter got a tattoo without them knowing it for almost twenty years wasn’t hilarious?”

Alexis bites back a smile for just a moment before sighing and admitting, “Okay, that was amazing.”

“Thank you.”

She huffs. “Do you want a manicure while we exchange stories of mom and dad fucking up our childhoods?”

David only thinks for a second before he sighs, sniffles. “Fine.”
