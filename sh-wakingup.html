Waking Up

Alec woke with cramps in his stomach and an involuntary whimper. 

His eyebrows knitted together as he shifted, hand going to his stomach to quell the monster in his gut that was trying to claw its way out. It was no use, of course, and Alec opened his eyes with some reluctance to the pitch black.

His sleep-fogged mind started to tell him that he’d been poisoned, or there was some sort of invisible demon clawing at his abdomen. Either way, the ache was quickly morphing into an overwhelming nausea. 

He didn’t want to get up. He wanted to go back to sleep and ignore all of the awful feelings happening in his body, but Alec didn’t have a choice in the end, as he threw his blankets off and darted for the bathroom.

The communal bathroom was thankfully empty at this time of night, so he didn’t have to stifle the noises of discomfort. 

He really, really hoped that it would happen fast and he could go back to bed. No, instead he was leaned over the toilet for agonizing minutes waiting for something to come up. He tried to heave, to make it happen quicker, and all that happened was a burp that just reminded him of the turmoil happening in his gut. 

“C’mon…” Alec muttered impatiently. Something needed to happen. It had to. He just wanted to get this over with and go back to sleep.

Instead he found himself sitting in the bathroom stall for ten minutes, rubbing his stomach as if that would do anything to ease the nausea and gurgling cramps, before something did happen. 

It was so fast he almost didn’t have time to get over the toilet before he threw up. 

The contents floating in the toilet was a sickening reminder that he really hadn’t eaten anything yesterday besides a sandwich and a piece of chocolate Clary had offered him. Neither of which tasted nearly as good coming back up.

His stomach made a noise of complaint and Alec forced himself to heave again. He coughed up a stream of half-digested lettuce from dinner and moaned. Whether it was a good thing or not, the sight and smell had him vomiting again. He couldn’t manage to take a breath before it happened again. 

And then he waited. Drool dripped down into the water, he spat out the remnants, and he reached for a wad of toilet paper when he realized his nose was running, blowing halfheartedly.

He wasn’t sure how long it had been before he finally decided he wasn’t going to puke, at least not in the near future, but Alec flushed the toilet, washed his hands, and hoped no one heard him. He hadn’t tried to remain particularly quiet, after all, but it was like, three (?) in the morning. Everyone should have been asleep.

He felt shaky, like throwing up had taken all of his energy and he’d flushed his strength down with his stomach contents. No no no, Alec didn’t want to think about throwing up right now.  

He wandered into the institute’s kitchen, hoping for a glass of water. If he was going to actually drink it, he wasn’t sure, but he needed something to rinse the taste out of his mouth, and having some water near him would offer peace of mind so he didn’t have to worry about dying of dehydration alone in his room. 

The kitchen was empty as well. He was almost jealous that everyone else was getting a good night’s sleep and he was stuck in his current shitty situation. 

Alec swallowed shallowly and flipped on one of the less-intrusive lights in the kitchen, one that lit up the counters without shining directly in his eyes. 

He sipped the water and promptly spat it into the sink. Probably wasn’t the most hygenic thing to be doing in the kitchen sink, but he could hardly care right now. It just felt good to rid his mouth of the nasty sour taste. And he found himself taking another sip, and swallowing. God, he was so thirsty.

He sat at the island. He wasn’t ready to go to bed yet. It was comforting not to be confined alone in his room. There was something nice about the common areas of the institute, a strange sense of belonging and community that he’d managed to earn. 

Or maybe he was just tired and lazy.

The overhead lights turned on and Alec responded with a flinch. 

Jace raised his hands non-threateningly. “Sorry, I didn’t know anyone was-”

“Can you turn the light off?” Alec begged through gritted teeth, the lights searing into his brain and exasperating the headache he hadn’t realized he had in addition to everything else that seemed to be wrong with him.

Thankfully, Jace got the message loud and clear and the room was dim once again. He made his way to the fridge and opened it without any clear plan of what he was after. The two of them didn’t say anything for a little while as Alec sipped his water and Jace dug through the fridge for something without someone’s name on it.

Eventually, he seemed to settle for making a sandwich, and sat a seat away from Alec. Only then did he speak.

“Couldn’t sleep?”

“Something like that,” Alec mumbled.

Jace nodded, and then took a bite big enough that one would think he was starving. Alec tried not to relate it to the one that he had just puked up, but that wasn’t as easy as he had hoped. His stomach was still unsettled, possibly even more so. Maybe the water was a bad idea.

“Yeah, me either. And then I got hungry,” he said, gesturing to the sandwich that was already half gone. “I should’ve asked, do you want me to make you one?”

“No!” It felt like the fastest Alec had answered any question in his life. The mere thought of eating right now had his stomach turning. He closed his eyes for a moment, laying a hand on his stomach and carefully breathing through the nausea. “No,” he said again. “I um, I was just thirsty.”

There was a silence, and Alec really wished Jace would go back to eating his food so he could leave. Whatever was happening in his stomach wasn’t going to end well.

“Alec…” Jace turned in his chair, and Alec tensed under his scrutiny. Jace probably didn’t need to be his parabatai to know that something was wrong. There was no way he didn’t look like shit after spending who knew how long puking his guts out. “Hey, are you okay?”

Alec went to dump out the water. It was a bad idea, he should have known to be careful to drink something so soon after throwing up. The small movement from the island to the sink seemed to be his downfall, though. He grabbed onto the edge of the counter, willing the nausea to go away. 

He stood still for an awful few seconds, could vaguely hear Jace saying his name but far too focused on not throwing up. And it began to ease. Thank fuck. 

He was in the clear.

At least, that’s what Alec thought before promptly vomiting into the sink.

He heard the scraping of a chair against the floor like Jace had gotten up in a hurry, and then a hand on his back rubbing gently as he heaved up the water he’d managed to drink. 

“You’re sick.” It wasn’t a question. Why would it be? Alec had already humiliated himself, it was pretty obvious something was wrong. Still, he felt compelled to respond the only way he knew how.

“I’m fine.” He spat into the sink and haphazardly wiped his mouth on the back of his hand.

“You just threw up, I wouldn’t consider that ‘fine’.” His touch didn’t cease, even as Alec had to rinse his own vomit down the drain.

He really had to ignore the look of utter care and concern on his brother’s face. Nope, Alec couldn’t deal with this, not tonight. Feelings had to be on the backburner when he was already sick as fuck.

Alec’s stomach wasn’t cramping anymore, but the muscles in his abdomen ached as if he’d been at it for hours when in reality it was just a couple bouts of overly-intense puking. His body always did like to give him a hard time when he was sick, he just hadn’t had to deal with it in so long.

“Alec.” Jace said it in a way that Alec realized he’d probably said it a few times already. Alec met his eyes, Jace offered a small smile. “You want any help going back to your room?”

Alec shook his head. He felt shaky, but he could walk a little ways just fine. 

“Okay.” Jace cleared his throat. His hand was still on Alec’s back, like he was hesitant to let go. “Is it… okay if I come with you anyway?”

Alec swallowed. “Why- why would you want to do that?”

“Honestly? It’s been a while since any of us have been sick, and I just want to make sure you’re not about to die of poison or something.” He did look nervous. Alec wondered if it was about him or the institute as a whole. Perhaps Jace was just trying to be careful about an illness making an appearance. “Alec,” Jace reminded him to answer.

“I… yeah, that’s fine. I’m just going to go back to sleep, anyway, so you can… Yeah, whatever.”

Jace nodded. Alec started his way back to his room, eager to get in his bed, sleep, and pretend like none of this happened. He heard Jace filling another glass of water as he walked off, and pulling a few other things out of the kitchen as well. Alec was too exhausted to wonder what he was doing.

Being back in bed was utter bliss. Alec closed his eyes, and found it easier to ignore his body’s discomforts when he was finally in a place he could sleep it all away. Maybe this was just some sort of food poisoning and he would feel better by tomorrow. 

Just as expected, Jace knocked and made his way in, toting water and a package of crackers, and something unidentifiable in one hand and… ugh. A bucket, in the other. Alec couldn’t even argue that he didn’t need it, because it did offer some sort of peace of mind that he wouldn’t accidentally make a mess he would be too sick to clean up.

“I know you probably don’t want any of these things right now, but I figured I could bring them anyway.” Jace set the bucket on the side of the bed, and then the crackers and water on Alec’s nightstand. And Alec caught a glimpse of the other thing in his hand.

“Is that a thermometer?”

“Uh, yeah. Just in case you need to check later.”

“It’s probably nothing.”

“Probably,” Jace agreed. “But you don’t look good and I’d hate for something to happen because we weren’t expecting the worst.”

Alec sighed. He was right, yet again. “Thanks.”

“Are you going to be okay by yourself?”

Alec couldn’t help but chuckle. “I’m not five.”

Jace didn’t bother to argue. “Right. Then I’ll see you tomorrow. Night, Alec.”

“G’night, Jace.”

-----

Alec would have liked to sleep all night and wake up to birds chirping in the morning and his stomach not trying to turn itself inside out. Instead, he found himself lunging for the bucket two hours later and listening to his stomach contents splatter against the plastic bottom. Thank fuck for Jace, because Alec wouldn’t have been able to make it to the bathroom if he tried.

And thank fuck for Jace, because he was somehow in his room and at his side in a heartbeat. The mattress dipped under his brother’s weight as he sat on the edge, rubbing Alec’s back once again.

“You heard?” Wow, Alec’s voice sounded awful. It made sense, his throat was sore from all of the abuse it had gone through.

Jace shook his head. “I could feel it,” he said, gesturing vaguely to the area of his parabatai rune. “I think that’s what woke me up last night, actually. Here…” 

Alec was too out of it and miserable to question why Jace carried a kerchief like one of the ancient men that supervised the Clave, and he wiped his nose and mouth without much care. 

It wasn’t much of a surprise that Jace felt it. Ever since the ritual, they’d been able to sense each other’s pain or discomfort. It became particularly clear one day when they were teens, when Jace was down with some terrible flu and Alec couldn’t shake the underlying anxiety until he was well again.

“Time is it?” He asked, reluctantly letting Jace take the bucket out of his hands and set it far enough away that he couldn’t smell the bile anymore. 

“Uh, five in the morning.”

Alec moaned, burying his head in his hands. He should be up and ready for the day by now, and instead he felt like he hadn’t slept in weeks. 

“Don’t worry, I already told everyone you weren’t available for missions today.”

“You told the whole institute I was sick?” Alec demanded.

“No,” Jace answered patiently. “I told them you were busy with other work.”

“I can’t just take a sick day, Jace. No one can.”

“What are you going to do, puke on a demon? Come on. One day. Just one.”

And somehow, Alec found himself being tucked back into bed. 

“Going back to sleep? Or I can grab some books from the library if you want something to keep occupied?”

Alec shook his head. “Sleep.”

“Got it. Is it okay if I stick around for a while?”

Alec wanted nothing more, if he was being honest. He wasn’t ready to spend the day completely alone to deal with his own emotions and discomfort without work to distract him. He nodded, and Jace’s quiet “okay” was the last thing he heard before he fell asleep.

-----

The third time Alec woke up that morning was arguably the most miserable. He was freezing, shuddering, but that wasn’t the reason that he was awake. His stomach, of course, was what woke him. Alec made a noise that may have been a whimper and reached for the bucket, only to find that it was missing. 

His head pounded, only exasperating the nausea, as he sat up. He had to at least try to make it to the bathroom and hope he didn’t run into anyone on the way there. Except his vision was swimming and he wasn’t sure he’d make it anywhere before he-

Alec clamped a hand over his mouth as he gagged helplessly.

“Shitfuck, hold on,” came Jace’s voice, and then the door slamming and rapid footsteps.

And suddenly Alec wasn’t throwing up in his bed like he was afraid of, but in the elusive bucket that his brother was holding in front of him. Jace rubbed gentle circles on his back as he heaved, and Alec couldn’t help but lean into him. He was so cold and Jace was so warm.

A hand went to his forehead, and it was yet another warm thing to lean into. “Fever,” Jace said. He sounded like he had something else to add, but Alec heaved again, bringing up nothing but bile. “Alright, there you go…” Jace said softly.

Unlike the other times, Alec couldn’t tell when he was done throwing up. He couldn’t pick up any of his body’s signals, like all of him was scrambled by the illness. Still, Jace seemed to be able to tell, because he brushed Alec’s hair away from his face and took the bucket away to be washed.

He wished he could have fallen asleep while Jace was gone, but instead he was left to think about all of the stuff he wasn’t getting done. He trusted Jace to cover for him; what would people think if they found out he was brought down by a dumb mundane illness?

Jace returned before he could think too deeply about it.  He immediately took the thermometer from Alec’s nightstand, sat next to him. Alec heard a beep and then felt a pressure in his ear and an arm around his shoulders. The arm didn’t leave when the thermometer beeped, Jace staying close. He must have been able to feel Alec’s shudders.

“Fuck,” was the first thing that came out of his brother’s mouth at the reading. “Alec, you’re burning up. I’m going to get some meds, hold on.”

The warmth left his side, but returned in the form of a blanket as Jace helped him lean back and covered him. 

“Is it okay if I tell Izzy?”

“Yeah, ‘f course,” Alec said, words slurring together.

“Alright. I’ll be back.”

Alec wasn’t sure how much time passed. He drifted away off and on until both of his siblings entered, probably not as quiet as they were trying to be.

“Oh, Alec…” Izzy sat on the edge of the bed and ran her fingers through his hair. He was still shivering uselessly, even under the blankets. He really tried not to; he looked pathetic, but it couldn’t be helped. “Here, take these.”

He swallowed the pills pressed into his hand without a second thought, taking an extra sip of the water for good measure to remedy the dryness in his mouth.

“You should have told me sooner.” Alec was about to answer when he realized that Izzy wasn’t talking to him, but Jace. 

“I know, but he didn’t-”

“No, it’s fine. I get it,” Izzy bumped her forehead against his in a quick show of affection and stood up. She clapped a hand on Jace’s back, eyes still on Alec. “I have to go, they need me for a mission. Take fucking care of him, Jace.”

“I plan on it.”

“Feel better, Alec. I love you.”

“Love you too. Don’t get killed.”

Alec found himself drifting to sleep before his sister even left the room. The last thing he was aware of was a hand against his forehead and gentle words telling him to rest.

-----

Alec woke and found that he no longer felt half-dead. His stomach, while still feeling a bit weak, no longer showed any intention of turning itself inside out, and his head was no longer pounding mercilessly. The shakiness remained, but Alec felt human again.

He got dressed and wandered out of his room, various people welcoming him back and asking how his isolated work was. 

And then Jace, who was in the middle of instructing a first-year on some interesting combat techniques, found Alec’s eyes and immediately excused himself from the conversation.

“You’re looking better.”

Alec nodded. “I feel better.”

Jace smiled and wrapped him in a strong (almost too strong, in all honesty) hug. “I was almost worried, you know. It’s been a while since you were-” he lowered his voice. “Sick. Good to have you back.”

“It’s good to be back,” Alec returned the smile for just a moment before, “Time to get back to work, then.”

Jace sighed. “Yep, you’re definitely back.”
