Juno Steel and the Late Night

Juno snuffled. It did absolutely nothing to clear his nose.

He’d only been sick for a day and he was already tired of this stupid cold. He didn’t like his senses being so foggy when he was trying to, you know, work and do his job.

“Mistah Steel, I think you need a tissue.”

A box was nudged toward him before he had a chance to say no, he didn’t need anything but to solve this case and drink ungodly amounts of alcohol until he forgot that he was sick at all.

Still, he pulled a tissue and pressed it to his dripping nose. The sniffling must have been driving his secretary nuts, but she had yet to say anything. Maybe Rita was trying to avoid embarrassing him. It did help.

He was in the middle of trying to blow his nose when that somehow ignited a tickle in his throat. Juno coughed, the force of it scraping against his throat and causing tears to prick in his eyes. He couldn’t help but groan when it was over with.

“Are you sure you wanna be here, boss? I mean, it’s really just paperwork today, right?”

“I don’t really trust you not to burn the place down while I’m gone,” an absolute lie. What was he supposed to say? That he didn’t want to go home? That he’d just sleep here in the grimy office for the rest of his life if he could?

Rita scoffed. “Um, okay Mistah Steel, that was one time and I found the fire extinguisher in time! Listen, boss, you just aren’t lookin’ too good.”

“Really? Because I feel fantastic.” He promptly sneezed into the now-abused tissue.

Rita patted his shoulder, he growled but it did nothing to deter her. “Maybe we can watch a stream after I’m- we’re done logging everything, huh? A new one just came out about a princess on Neptune, and- bless you!”

To his annoyance, she managed to say it before he sneezed. How she saw it coming, he didn’t ask. Juno dragged the wad of tissue across the bottom of his nose as if that would solve anything. There was more snot than he could deal with right now.

He sighed heavily, if anything to hide the fact that it really sounded better than going home. “Sure. Whatever.”

“Awesome! I’ll- we’ll just finish up, and I’ll start the stream!”

Juno did very little work that day. Any time he tried to focus on something, he just ended up sneezing or coughing or struggling to breathe and having to blow his nose. The bin under his desk was nearly overflowing with tissues, and yet his nose wasn’t clear at all.

What was worse, however, was when he started shivering. It didn’t surprise him in the least. Any illness, even colds, seemed to hit him hard. Perhaps it had something to do with the fact that his immune system was weakened by his horrible eating and drinking habits, and general unhealthy lifestyle.

He groaned under his breath and ran a hand through his hair. Another weak cough against his irritated throat.

“How do you feel about starting that stream?”

“If you’re sure, boss! I thought maybe you’d changed your mind and wanted to go home.”

“Naw,” he said, and thought about adding more before he decided that it wasn’t worth it.

Rita didn’t mind his lack of words; she never did. She began explaining the whole plot to the stream that they were about to watch, how the main character was looking to avenge the death of her wife and that love was its own superpower.

He found himself being nudged toward the shitty old sofa in the office. While being pushed around like this would normally have him snapping at his secretary, this time he knew better. This time she really was helping. Not that he’d ever tell her than.

The plot was too complicated for his illness-fogged mind to grasp, but it offered itself as a distraction from how awful he felt.

Feeling an itch in the back of his nose, he grabbed a tissue and rubbed his nostrils.

“Bless you, boss!”

Juno sneezed. “Rita,” he paused to sniffle, because now his nose was running full-force. “could you please wait until I actually sneeze to say that?”

“Why? I may as well say it early if I see it comin’!”

He sighed. And then sneezed again.

“Whoa, bless you! Sorry, boss, I didn’t get that one early. You know, sneezes are actually the body’s way of expelling irritants? Like dust, or allergens, or, y’know, germs... pretty gross, right?”

Juno looked up from where his tissue-covered hands were steepled over his nose and mouth. He blew his nose, or tried to at least. He was too congested for it to do any real good. “That’s interesting.”

“I know! Sneezes are crazy things! Oh oh! Shush, my favorite part is comin’ up!”

Juno had no problem shushing. He tried to fix his nose, it was still somehow running despite the fact that he was so stuffed up. He ended up keeping a tissue pressed against it to just catch the liquid in lieu of trying to blow it.

Rita rambled on about the princess’ forbidden love for another person she met on her way to find her wife’s killer. It made no sense, at least not at the moment. Still, he listened.

Until, at some point, the monitor turned off and he felt Rita get up from the sofa. Juno opened his eyes, the room was dark.

“ ‘s going on?” He mumbled, dragging a hand down his face. He sniffled.

“You fell asleep, boss! I was gonna leave you here!”

“Oh,” another sniffle. “Right. Sorry.”

“Are you gonna be alright by yourself? ‘cause I can stay!”

“I’m- uh,” God, he couldn’t think right now. The only thing in his mind was ‘don’t leave’, and he couldn’t exactly say that out loud. “I should go home.” He should, shouldn’t he? But he really didn’t want to.

“Sure, mistah Steel.” She cleared her throat. “But! But if you wanted to watch one more stream, or y’know, play one more stream while you sleep...”

He schooled his features just so she couldn’t tell how nice that really sounded. “Fine. Let’s do that, then. It’s not like we have work tomorrow or anything.”

She ignored that remark and cheerily started the next show.

“Bless you, by the way!”

Juno sneezed. “Damn it, Rita...”
