Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David doesn't want to make Patrick's bad day even worse
Word count: 2.4k

Home to You

“I miss you,” David said pathetically to his laptop screen.

Patrick, the love of his life, still looked so beautiful, even when his face was pixelated from bad hotel wifi. He chuckled and the video took a bit longer to show the adorable smile on his face.

“I miss you too. God, I’m ready for this to be over with.”

“Well, if-”

“David.” Patrick scolded gently. “You don’t have to say something every time something goes wrong with my car.”

David scoffed. “It just seems like I do, because so many things happen to your car.”

Patrick shook his head with a laugh. “I just hope it’s out of the shop by the time I need to get home. I can’t wait to see you. It’s been two days but it feels like forever.” Patrick said it with such sincerity and desperation.

David tried and failed to bite back a smile. “I can’t wait until you come home, either, because I mean...” he grinned, and Patrick quickly returned it.

Patrick laughed. “Okay okay, well if everything turns out alright with my car, I’ll be back by tomorrow night.”

They chatted for a while and then said their goodnights. David put his laptop away and laid down in the empty bed.

Patrick was at a tax seminar two hours away. Of course his car broke down on the way there and he had to call a tow. Of course. David knew how tired Patrick was of being there and not at home.

He smiled and turned over to get comfortable. It was weird sleeping in Patrick’s bed without Patrick. He should be thankful that he was letting him sleep at his apartment while he was gone, though. As much as David loved his family, it was such a relief to get a couple of days away from the motel.

He struggled to fall asleep with the anticipation that he would be able to see Patrick again in less than twenty-four hours.

-----

When David woke up, his limbs felt heavy. It was like he never actually went to bed, which certainly wasn’t true. He couldn’t wrap his head around why he was so tired.

He shot out of bed after one glance at the clock. Oh God, he was going to be late, and this time he actually needed to be there on time. Still, David found time to make some extra strong coffee and fill a thermos to the brim before he left.

The morning went by rather slowly. For once, David didn’t mind how quiet the store was.

He found himself nursing a headache by lunch time. Maybe he shouldn’t have had all of that coffee. The caffeine was doing weird things to him, making him feel shaky and uneasy, and his stomach... his stomach felt weird.

The door chimed and in walked Stevie.

“Wow, why do you look like shit?”

“What?”

Stevie raised her eyebrows. “Um. Did you sleep last night?”

“Yeah, of course I slept last night. What do you mean I ‘look like shit’?”

“Nothing,” Stevie defended with a shrug. “How’s your um, day?”

David sighed. “Okay honestly? I’m really tired. I don’t know. And then I drank a bunch of coffee this morning and my stomach is...” He gestured toward his abdomen and shook his head. “I just want Patrick to be home already. I don’t know what’s wrong with me.”

Stevie lazily propped herself against the counter. “Well, what’d you have for breakfast?”

Oh. David made a face. “I...”

“Really?”

“I was running late!”

“Since when has that stopped you from eating breakfast?” She asked with a deadpan tone.

And she was right. Why didn’t he think to eat breakfast? “I don’t know, I guess I wasn’t hungry,” he said, throwing his arms out wildly.

Stevie gave a longsuffering sigh. “Well, I’m going to the cafe, wanna come with?”

David shook his head. He still wasn’t hungry, thanks to that fucking coffee that was doing weird things to his insides. “I should stay here, I’ll get something later.”

Stevie shrugged and left just when David’s phone started to ring.

“Patrick!” he greeted eagerly.

“Oh my God, David, I’m having the worst day.”

“Uh oh, what’s going on?”

“My car isn’t going to be done until a hour after the conference, so I have to wait until night time to drive back.” A sigh crackled through the speakers. “This is fucking ridiculous, I-”

“M’kay, Patrick? Can you just like, breathe for a second?”

He could hear Patrick’s teeming frustration. But he did take a deep breath, and let it out, albeit with a growl. “You are still coming home tonight, right?”

Patrick sighed, his voice a little softer. “Absolutely. I can’t stay here one more night.”

David smiled. “Good. Good, that’s great. I can’t wait.”

“I can’t either, David. I- I miss you.”

The warmth of those simple words had him more relaxed than he’d been all day.

-----

David grimaced at the distressed gurgle.

“Was that your stomach?” Stevie asked from across the table. She looked rather impressed, even when she was used to David’s insides making noise.

David nodded. “I really need to eat,” he decided.

“You should have come to lunch with me.”

“Mm’mm. I had too much work to do. And-” A cramp spiked in his belly and David only half-stifled the pained groan. He laid a hand on his middle with a wince.

“Whoa, are you okay?”

David nodded quickly. “Fine. Just need something to eat.”

He was hardly hungry for the sandwich and fries that Twyla delivered, and in the end he boxed it up and decided to take it back to Patrick’s apartment with him. Patrick would be home soon, anyway, and he could be there early so he didn’t miss him.

He was starting to get a bad feeling, and not just in his stomach, but... about it.

David was halfway up the last flight of stairs, breathing more heavily than he usually did at Patrick’s stupid third floor apartment, when he felt overwhelming nausea.

David keyed himself into the apartment as fast as he could. He dropped everything onto the table and darted into the bathroom with a speed he didn’t know he had.

He knelt down over the toilet and gagged, fruitlessly at first. The second heave brought up a wave of sour-tasting vomit that made a sickening splatter into the water below. David didn’t have time to react before he was throwing up again.

Everything suddenly made too much sense. He was tired, his stomach going all weird when he drank coffee, the shakiness that he thought was because of the coffee.

This was bad. This was so bad.

Patrick had been so stressed, so over it. The last thing he probably wanted was a violently sick partner that he had to take care of.

David was just starting to relax when a sudden gag had him lunging for the toilet once again. His stomach cramped, even as he was throwing up, and David couldn’t do a thing about it as he felt himself be sick from both ends at once.

He decided that this was as good of a time as any to let himself cry. He couldn’t clean up, he could hardly move he was so dizzy. God, he was so fucking pathetic.

The front door clicked. David made an effort to quiet down as Patrick’s footsteps came through the apartment.

“Honey, I’m home!” He said, so ridiculously cheesy that David found himself chuckling just a little bit through the tears. A pause. “David? Oh,” Patrick seemed to see that the bathroom door was closed, not a big deal in itself. In fact, he was probably going to dismiss it before David ended up sobbing once again.

A knock. “David, are you crying?”

David sniffled, trying desperately to wipe his tears away.

“Honey,” it came softer this time. “Honey, what’s wrong?”

“I’m fine.” He knew he didn’t sound fine, but maybe Patrick would leave him alone. He shouldn’t even have to worry about this right now.

“David...”

“I’m fine.”

David heard a heavy sigh on the other side of the door before he found himself heaving over the toilet again. His stomach still cramped horrible, and the force of throwing up had made his headache so much worse.

A few minutes went by as he tried to catch his breath.

And then another knock. “Are you sick? David, can you let me in? I can’t just...”

“No, I can’t,” David croaked. The door was too far away and he was... he was afraid to move. His stomach gurgled threateningly, and he- oh God...

“Okay, well if I get the key can I come in?”

David sobbed halfheartedly.

“What’s wrong?”

“I’m a mess right now. I don’t want you to see me.”

“Okay David,” he said softly. “Just let me know if you change your mind.”

-----

He spent far too long sitting on the toilet, trying to empty his stomach as much as possible so this wouldn’t happen again any time soon. David spent far too long trying to himself clean up, trying to shower away any remnants of sickness.

And then he went to work disinfecting the whole thing. His face was still wet with tears, and he had to take a break every once in a while when he’d get tired or dizzy again.

Another knock. “David, you don’t have to clean. I can do that...” Patrick said it with all of the softness in the world, and it made David feel that much more guilty for being sick the first night they’d been together this week.

“No,” David rasped. “No, I have to do this.”

When he was finally finished, he realized something. David wanted to cry allover again.

“Patrick...?”

Footsteps approached the door in a heartbeat. “Yeah?”

“I need- oh my God.”

“What, David?”

“I need a clean pair of pants.”

David waited for a laugh, or maybe an annoyed sigh, and in that time of waiting he hadn’t realized that Patrick had already run to grab some and come back.

He unlocked the door and made sure not to let Patrick see his face (or the rest of him, really) as he reached out. It seemed that he’d picked out David’s favorite pair of sweatpants. David hoped to God he wouldn’t ruin them.

He got dressed and mentally prepared himself. Patrick was probably already exhausted from today. He’d just go back to the motel and deal with this on his own.

David wandered out.

Patrick shot up from his spot on the couch, book he was reading forgotten as he rushed over.

“Did you just get sick? You sounded fine on the phone earlier,” he spouted out as he led David to the couch.

David sat a safe distance away from him. He didn’t feel particularly cuddly when he knew Patrick had heard him puking and shitting for God knew how long.

“It sort of came on fast,” he admitted quietly, folding his hands in his lap. He couldn’t even look Patrick in the eyes. “Like, right before you came home.”

“Oh David,” Patrick said sadly. “Do you feel any better?”

David shook his head.

“Can you maybe stop staring at the floor?”

David shook his head again, more violently this time.

“David, it’s okay.” There it was again, that soft voice that would usually make him melt. But now it was just annoying, because Patrick shouldn’t be comforting him right now.

David found himself shrinking away from the hand Patrick tried to lay on his back. He caught the confusion and hurt on his fiance’s face. “It’s not okay. It’s not okay, Patrick. I...” oh fuck, he was going to cry again, wasn’t he? His eyes filled with hot tears. Fuck fuck, he thought he was done with this. One ring-covered hand came to cover his mouth as he tried with all his might not to start sobbing again. “Oh God...” his voice was shaky as he tried to will back the choking sob. This time he didn’t shrink away when he felt a hesitant hand rubbing his back.

“Hey... maybe we should go to bed? You... you look tired.”

“N-no, no I think-” David cleared his throat and finally looked up to rub the tears from his eyes. “I think I’m going to go back to the motel. I don’t- I still don’t feel good and I’m not exactly going to be fun tonight,” the last two words came out in a watery, humorless laugh that just prompted more tears. “I know this isn’t exactly how you wanted to spend your first night at home.”

“Whoa hey, is that what this is about? Because you think you’re ruining my night?”

“I mean, I am, though.”

“David no, that’s not true.”

David accepted the wad of tissues that were pressed into his hand, trying desperately to dry up his face, Salt water could not be good for his skin right now.

Patrick sighed. “I know that I sounded annoyed on the phone earlier, but that was just because I was so eager to come home to you, and my car--yes I know it’s a piece of junk--was, you know, keeping me from doing that.”

David kept his face hidden by tissues with one hand while he wrapped an arm around his stomach with the other. It still ached horribly. He wasn’t done throwing up.

“Please stay? I want you here. In sickness or health, remember?”

David couldn’t keep himself from chuckling at that.

“This is humiliating,” he admitted.

“Well, if we’re going to spend the rest of our lives together, it was bound to happen eventually, right?”

“I... guess. Oh God.”

David decided he was completely powerless to stop him as he led him to the bed and started rubbing his belly. Patrick’s hands gently kneading the soft flesh of his tender stomach.

“That better?”

“Mhmm,” David said, completely blissed out. His stomach chose that moment to gurgle loud enough that both of them could hear.

Patrick chuckled. “Just so you know, I’m glad to be home with you no matter what. You deal with me on days that I’m particularly cranky, I can deal with you having a little stomach virus.”

“You say that now. I’m not exactly the most pleasant sick person.”

“That’s okay, neither am I.”

David shifted as a cramp made itself known in his gut. He made a noise and gently nudged Patrick’s hand away before pulling his shirt down.

“I have to... can you put headphones in or something?”

Patrick nodded, already grabbing his phone and opening youtube.
