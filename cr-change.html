Title: House AU 5: Change
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: The house is sick, Caleb has to handle some new surprises
Word count: 3k

Change

Caleb recognized the footsteps in the hallway. At first he thought that it was strange that someone was awake so late, and then he remembered that he’d simply gone to bed early before this... situation. Fuck, he didn’t even know what time it was.

Poor Caleb Widogast was currently on the bathroom floor, weakly draped over the toilet after having spent a good amount of time vomiting up what he’d eaten for dinner. He wasn’t even hungry, why did he eat?

Caleb was forced to accept the most difficult thing he’d have to do, at the expense of his pride.

“Fjord...” he called out, and only then realized how rough his throat felt, how bad he sounded.

The footsteps approached until Fjord’s voice came from the other side of the door. “Yeah? Caleb, that you?”

“I could use a hand,” he said before sighing heavily. He knew that he wasn’t getting off of this floor by his own strength. Asking for help was inevitable.

The bathroom door opened hesitantly at first, until Fjord saw what was happening. He swore with a deep frown. “Guess you caught Beau’s bug, huh?”

Caleb couldn’t help but chuckle just a bit. He must have looked so pitiful. Oh well, that was nothing new.

“Seems so, yes.” He tried not to be irritable at the one who he needed to get him to his feet, but he wasn’t exactly in a conversational mood.

Fjord reached over him to flush the toilet before pressing the back of his hand to Caleb’s temple. “Yeah, you’re burnin’ up. Need help to bed?”

Caleb nodded, lacking any more words. He was so tired.

“I’ve got you,” Fjord promised, before hooking a hand under each of his arms and pulling him up. Caleb’s vision went dark for a moment at the sudden movement, but he allowed himself to be manhandled to his feet until he had an arm draped over Fjord’s shoulders.

“Danke,” he breathed out.

“We’ll take this easy. And I’d appreciate if you didn’t hurl on me.”

“Can’t promise anything,” Caleb joked dryly. Well, he was kind of joking.

Fjord chuckled. “Lovely.”

The both of them, comically enough, were winded from the trip. Fjord likely wasn’t used to dragging someone across the house like a rag doll. He eased Caleb onto the bed before looking at the other side.

“Where’s Nott?”

“Out,” Caleb answered simply. She said that she’d been looking for someone, he didn’t think it was his place to ask what it was about.

“You good by yourself?”

Caleb thought for a moment, before giving a groggy nod. “I’m just going to sleep. Don’t need an audience for that.”

Fjord didn’t look too convinced, but he was never one to push boundaries. “Alright. Need anything?”

“Maybe a bucket. In case.” He was so tired.

“Gotcha.”

 

A bucket was a good idea, as it turned out, because Caleb didn’t have any energy to make it to the bathroom when he woke up in the middle of the night with overwhelming nausea. He’d deal with cleaning later.

 

Caleb woke late in the morning to footsteps in the hallway, different ones, faster ones, and then the bathroom door slamming. Beauregard was throwing up again, and it sounded unpleasant.

Caleb had hated seeing her so sick the past couple of days, and it seemed the others did as well. She was always so strong willed, seeing Beau broken down and miserable was... well, it was hard.

He decided to focus on his own misery, like the fact that he wasn’t sure he was ever going to be able to get out of bed again. He wasn’t sure how long it was before there was a knock on his door.

“Ja?” he muttered hoarsely. His throat was sore from a night of restless sleep and, of course, throwing up.

He expected the person who entered to be Nott or Fjord, but instead it was a very haggard-looking Beauregard. Her hair hung in front of her face, and she looked like she’d gotten just as much rest as he had last night. 

“Hey,” she rasped. “Fjord said you caught my bug, so, sorry.” She didn’t sound very sorry, but then again, Beau rarely did. That was just who she was.

Caleb shook his head. “It happens. How are you? You look like shit.”

“Well gee, thanks man.” And before Caleb knew it, Beau was flopping on his bed. “I feel like shit.”

He grunted at the sudden movement, briefly feeling like he was flying. She was at an odd diagonal angle with her legs hanging off the edge, staring at the ceiling.

“I’m so tired.”

Caleb hummed in agreement.

“Do you think Jester has anything to help with this? I don’t wanna like, die of dehydration.”

“Death would be a mercy at this point,” Caleb muttered dryly. They laid silently for a few... seconds? Minutes? Caleb wasn’t used to being so oblivious to the passing of time. He really, really hated being sick. His brain was the only good part of him, everything went to shit when he couldn’t think straight.

There was another knock at the door.

“Caleb! Caleb, you’re not dead, are you? Fjord told me you were sick!” Jester. “Caleb, have you seen Beau?”

“We’re both in here!” Beau called back.

“Oh! Hi Beau!” Jester grinned, letting herself in. “You guys, guess what? Nott’s back, and she said that she wants us to meet someone.”

Caleb suddenly felt uncomfortable. Those words, for some reason, sent a sense of anxiety through him. “What?”

“Come downstairs!” Jester said, excitedly gesturing for them to get up, as if that was such an easy feat.

“Jess, we’re pretty...” even miserable, Beau couldn’t straight admit to being sick, apparently. “Do we have to?”

“Well, I mean maybe. I guess you don’t have to, but-”

Caleb’s curiosity had already gotten the best of him. He sat up and pulled the blankets back, despite every muscle in his body screaming at him not to. He needed to know who... who Nott had been looking for. She’d been acting so different lately and he had no idea why.

He wanted answers.

“Caleb, you sure?” Beau asked after Jester had run off back downstairs. She seemed to gauge his determined expression, because the next question was, “...you okay?”

Caleb nodded. “Ja, I’m fine.”

“You don’t look fine.”

“Stop looking, then.”

“Oh my God,” Beau muttered under her breath, giving an exasperated chuckle. “Let’s see what the fuck is going on.”

Caleb and Beau, both still in their pajamas, sat in the living room in front of Nott and... a halfling man? He had bushy brown hair and big glasses, and he looked at Nott with a sort of fondness that she seemed to return. Was Nott dating now? Why didn’t she just tell them before?

And then Nott introduced Yeza as her husband.

Caleb felt like all the air had left his lungs. He tried to keep his expression neutral, but his eyes were wide. What was happening? He couldn’t understand, and his illness-fogged brain was making it even harder to try to put things together. There was something that he was missing, there had to have been, and if he just hadn’t been so sick maybe he could-

“Caleb?” Nott’s voice was hesitant, gentle, the way she spoke to him when she could see him spiraling. He looked up, meeting her eyes. “Caleb, I-”

“I don’t understand,” he said plainly. “But it’s very nice to meet you, Yeza.” If all of this wasn’t happening, he imagined that he and Yeza could get along well. He was an intellectual man - an alchemist, apparently. They probably had a lot in common. But all Caleb saw right now was a secret that his closest friend had been keeping from him.

Despite all that, Yeza gave a sheepish smile. “It’s nice to meet you too. Veth has talked about you a lot! You sound very cool.”

Veth?

Nott frowned immediately at the name. A look of recognition.

She cleared her throat. “I- I think that we should all talk.”

Yeza nodded quickly and smiled, looking rather awkward. Caleb enjoyed - despite the situation - how genuine he seemed. That was good. “I’ll leave you all to it, okay? Veth...” he kissed her cheek. “I love you.”

“I love you too,” she said softly.

Yeza left, and there was an immediate chorus of “Nott what the fuck”s in the living room.

Nott growled and nodded. “Okay okay okay!” She yelled, gesturing wildly for them to shut up. “I have a lot to tell you guys!”

“Uh, fuck yeah you do!” Beau hissed. “Nott, what the fuck?”

Nott sighed. “Alright, well, hello, my name is Nott, but a long time ago, people called me Veth Brenatto.”

Caleb felt a surge of anxiety coarse through him once again. He couldn’t look anyone in the eyes, not right now. He just listened.

Nott, or... Veth? Had grown up in a small town. She and her husband were attacked by goblins and soon she was turned into one as revenge for killing one of their elders.

She thought Yeza was dead, and then she found him.

Caleb breathed out shakily. He felt... nauseated. Well, it was likely that he’d throw up again at some point anyway.

“Caleb?” Nott- Veth? Said again, even softer than before. “Are you... alright?”

All of this had been going on and she didn’t tell him a thing. She knew everything about him, why- why didn’t she feel comfortable confiding in him after everything they’d been through?

“I-” and that was all he managed to say before he vomited down his front.

Nott felt his forehead as he wiped his mouth. He’d been camped in the bathroom for a while, now, after all of that. His vomit-covered shirt was gone, along with his dignity. He probably smelled like vomit, maybe he should shower. Oh, but that would require energy and balance that he didn’t have.

“You’re warm,” she said.

Caleb was freezing. He couldn’t stop shivering. “Doesn’t matter. When were you going to tell me?”

“Caleb, there’s more.”

Caleb didn’t look up at her. He couldn’t. He wasn’t upset, not with her, or anyone, but it was too much to process right now.

“I’m moving in with him. You know, a-again.”

Caleb breathed out. “Right.”

“I’m sorry.”

“You have nothing to be sorry about, Nott,” he sighed. “I just really don’t feel well.

Nott nodded. “I know.” She brushed some hair out of his eyes. “I’m still going to be around all the time, Caleb, I promise!”

Molly returned from his show, and immediately caught the stomach bug. Fjord was down with it now as well, leaving a lone Jester to keep an eye on everyone.

Caleb woke in the middle of the night and maneuvered around his friend in the bed to get up. He was so ridiculously thirsty. He’d hardly been able to keep anything down the past couple of days, but he needed water. His mouth was dry, it had an awful taste in it.

Mollymauk was sitting at the table when Caleb wandered downstairs. He looked exhausted, Caleb wondered why he wasn’t in bed.

“Caleb,” Molly greeted as Caleb made his way to the sink.

“Mollymauk, hello.” He clumsily grabbed for a class and filled it. Everything in him wanted to chug the whole thing in one go but past experience told him that would be a bad idea.

“Have you vomited as much as I have today?” Molly asked with his usual amused tone. Despite being horribly sick, he still managed to keep his jovial attitude, which would never not confuse Caleb.

Caleb gave a minuscule chuckle as he sat down with his water. “It’s very likely. How was your trip?”

“Oh it was quite nice! I wanted to stay a little longer, and now I think that I probably should have. No one warned me I was walking into a plagued house,” he laughed. He took a sip of some sort of tea.

“Mm,” Caleb nodded. “Our bad.” He leaned forward, head in his hand like he couldn’t sit up straight anymore. He was so tired.

“Caduceus gave us a very nice blend, in case you want some,” said Molly, gesturing to his mug, which was still over half full. “It’s supposed to be good for settling your stomach, but I think that I made some a little too late...” the end of his sentence was a bit trained. Molly made a small whining noise, hand going to his stomach. He stood up, nudging the mug over to Caleb. “I’m heading upstairs before something unfortunate happens,” he said. “Goodnight, Caleb!”

Even managed to be cheerful when he was about to vomit. Caleb chuckled and took the tea as his own. It wasn’t like he had to worry about germs.

The tea was nice, Caduceus was a true saint. 

Caleb finished it, and found himself exhausted. He looked at the long stairway, and decided that they were far too much work. He might wake Nott getting back into bed, anyway. So, Caleb flopped onto the couch. He was asleep in seconds.

“Shhh don’t wake him up!”

“I’m not the one being loud!”

Caleb turned over before opening his eyes. It seemed that his face had been smushed into the back cushion for most of the night, forming a patch of drool. He wiped his mouth and groaned.

“Shit. Morning, Caleb,” said Beau. “Sleep good?”

He sniffed, sighed. “Ja.”

“Sorry if we woke you up, Caleb,” said Jester. “We were trying to be quiet.”

Fjord scoffed.

Caleb sat up, dragging a hand down his face. Everyone looked a bit rough, eating single slices of toast for breakfast. It appeared that Beau and Fjord each had a half. Fjord appeared like he’d been up half the night, so did Mollymauk. Beau just looked plain fatigued. Jester, of course, looked perfectly happy.

He swung his legs over the side of the couch and just sat for a moment, waiting for his vision to be less blurry with sleep.

Wait. Where was Nott? Was she still asleep? Was she gone?

“Where’s Nott?” He asked. There must have been a hint of panic in his voice, because the tone of Fjord’s reply was gentler than usual.

“She just went out for some groceries. Gatorade and shit.”

“And shit? Ew.” Jester giggled.

Fjord scoffed and took a minuscule bite of his half slice of toast.

“Caleb, do you want anything? Do you feel like eating?”

Did he?

No, definitely not. But it seemed that he didn’t really feel like throwing up, either, which was an improvement. He stood, let his body get used to being upright for a moment, and went to join his friends in the kitchen. Jester was in the middle of feeling everyone’s foreheads, because of course she was, and Caleb found himself the next in line.

“Molly and Fjord, you guys both have fevers,” she informed sadly. “Do you want some medicine? I have some fever reducer in the bathroom cabinet!”

Molly moaned and dropped his head on the table. “Don’t think that I could keep it down. Goodness, I haven’t been this sick since-”

Molly looked up, Caleb averted his gaze.

Jester hummed thoughtfully. “I should ask Caduceus for more tea.”

“That stuff was real good,” Fjord agreed. He nudged his plate away with a sigh. “By the way, Beau, since we have a moment. Thanks for fuckin’ bringing this plague h-”

“Okay, fuck you, Fjord. It wasn’t my fault okay? You guys could’ve gotten sick in a million different ways and-”

“Guys! No fighting!” Jester scolded, like a disgruntled cat owner. “I know you don’t feel good and you’re all grumpy and stuff, but you can’t fight! C’mon!”

Beau and Fjord huffed, and didn’t continue their argument. All of them were too tired to keep up their usual banter, it seemed.

Molly sighed and giggled. “Today is going to be so interesting, huh?” He said, propping his chin on his palm.

“Maybe you guys will feel better when Nott comes back,” Jester said.

“Doubt it,” Fjord groaned. And then he looked to Caleb. “How’re you doin’ with all this?”

Caleb really didn’t want to have this conversation. It... it was far too much this early in the morning. Though, it probably wasn’t as early as he thought that it was. Who knew how long he’d slept on that couch, the droll stain was pretty big.

So, he was honest. “It’s a lot.”

Fjord gave an understanding nod, and that was the end of the discussion as no one wanted to talk about it. Caleb wondered if everyone else felt as he did. Betrayed... wasn’t the right word. But was everyone else also wondering why Nott never told them about her husband? He sighed and stood up from the table. He needed to be alone.

Caleb wasn’t sure how long he’d been sitting on the bed. He was going to change his clothes at one point, get into something cleaner. Instead, he found himself staring at the floor. He was out of it, he knew he was, and there wasn’t anything he could think to do about it.

“Caleb?”

Nott. She quietly closed the door behind her and sat next to him.

“Caleb, I know you’re mad at me.”

“No, I-”

“Whether you admit it or not, you’re mad at me, and that’s okay,” she said, pressing herself against his side. “I’m sorry that I didn’t tell you. I just... I was worried that by talking about it, I’d get my own hopes up and I’d never actually find Yeza.”

Caleb sighed out through his nose. “It’s alright,” he said quietly. “I will adjust, and we’ll have a new family member.”

Nott looked up at him, a smile, a look as if she was trying to see if he was telling the truth. Of course he was. Caleb was immediately wrapped in a warm hug. 

“Thank you, Caleb. Things will be okay.”

Caleb nodded, lacking any more words today. He was tired.
