Taking Turns

Between his roommates being out of town and catching a nasty stomach bug, he was out of options. He called his best friend.

There were very few people Taliesin would let see him like this. It was humiliating, after all.

Matt was one of those few people. He was proud to be. The two of them had been friends for long enough that they each knew how the other worked. 

For example, Taliesin was prone to motion sickness. He wouldn’t tell anyone that, of course, because he didn’t consider it important knowledge. But Matt made sure to keep an eye on him on long car rides and stop the others from talking him into going on roller coasters when he already said no.

Matt was nervous to find out that his friend had been alone sick all day and hadn’t bothered to tell anyone. Although, Taliesin had probably been sleeping most of the day. He’d probably been using it as an opportunity to catch up on shows. At least that was what Matt thought.

Which was why he was so alarmed to walk into a house that was nearly pitch black and hear distressed sounds from the bathroom.

Matt rushed in and found Taliesin sobbing over the toilet.

He swore and knelt down.

“Tal, hey…”

Taliesin did his best to swallow back the sobs when he realized that Matt was there, but his face was still wet with tears.

“God, I’m sorry for calling you so late…” his voice was nothing but a rasp from throwing up so much. He looked so miserable that Matt wanted to hug him right there and never let go. “I freaked the fuck out a little.”

Matt shook his head, moving his friend’s hair out of his eyes. It was slightly damp, but Tal’s skin was dry and warm. He was definitely running a temperature. 

“Buddy, don’t apologize. I’m glad you called.” 

Taliesin didn’t have a chance to respond before he was heaving into the bowl once again. Matt winced. From the sounds of it, he’d emptied his stomach a while ago, yet it still wasn’t done trying to evacuate its contents.

He rubbed Taliesin’s back, offering gentle words when he looked like he wanted to cry again.

Eventually, Matt grabbed a wet washcloth and handed it to Taliesin to wipe his face, rubbing his back as he recovered from the bout. 

“Thank you,” he breathed out, eyes still red and puffy. 

Matt pressed a kiss to his temple. “Do you feel like you’re done?”

Taliesin sniffled and nodded. “I think so. For now, anyway,” he said with a sigh.

Taliesin changed into clean, dry pajamas and got into bed. He sent a nauseated look at the bucket Matt set next to him.

“I’ll be on the couch if you need me, okay?”

“You don’t have to stay.”

Matt shrugged. “But I’m going to.” 

-----

Matt woke to the sound of retching and was immediately awake. He didn’t rush to the bathroom this time, wanting to give Taliesin some privacy. Instead, he waited until it sounded like his friend was done before he went and knocked on the door. 

“Tal? You okay?”

A moan.

“Can I come in?”

“Mm.”

Taliesin looked far worse than he had earlier. In fact, he looked like he’d been there for a while. 

“Oh, buddy…”

Somehow, they ended up with Matt sitting on the floor and Taliesin’s head in his lap. He was shivering, and Matt could feel the feverish heat against him. He was getting a bad feeling.

Tal fell asleep off and on, snoring each time. 

And suddenly he was back to throwing up. There was nothing but bile to come up, and Matt couldn’t help but wince at the sound of the fruitless heaving that must have hurt his stomach more than it already had.

“Okay, you’re going to hate me, but I think we should go to the hospital. You feel really hot and you keep throwing up and-”

Instead of an argument or an eyeroll, all Taliesin said was, “Please.”

-----

Taliesin took his hand and held it as they sat in the waiting room. It had only been a few minutes, and it would probably be many more before they saw a doctor.

It didn’t take him long at all to fall asleep against Matt’s shoulder, the warm weight pressed against him yet again.

Matt’s phone buzzed and he struggled to get it out with his left hand.

Matt frowned. He was exhausted, hadn’t even thought about it until now.

This certainly wasn’t the first time he and Taliesin had been to the ER together, though. He remembered the time he fell off of Laura and Travis’ deck and Taliesin was the first to offer to give him a ride. It took close to six hours for them to tell him that he had a broken arm.

Matt chuckled.

“What?” Taliesin mumbled, sitting up and running a tired hand down his face. 

“Was just thinking about the last time we were here.”

“Oh,” he snorted, a small smile on his face. “God, we spent that whole time making jokes about Travis’ shitty craftsmanship.”

“Mmhm, which--by the way--I still say was the reason I fell.”

“Oh, definitely.” Taliesin chuckled. “Guess it was my turn to drag us here, huh?”

“Guess so.” Matt smiled fondly. “Won’t be the last.”
