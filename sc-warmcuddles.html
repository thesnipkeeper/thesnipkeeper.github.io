Warm Cuddles

“Honey, I’m home!” Patrick called with a chuckle. He was giddy to finally be home after sitting in a seminar all day. A whole day in a seminar thinking about how much he wanted to be home with his husband. So, yeah, he was excited. “David?”

Patrick threw his keys on the counter and made his way further into the house. The place was dark, and there was no sign of David anywhere. Strange. Maybe he was out with Stevie? But he didn’t mention having plans this morning when they spoke.

He went upstairs, already unbuttoning his shirt as he was eager to get into sweats and relax for the rest of the night. He stepped into the bedroom, only to find his husband already in bed.

“Hey, what are you doing in bed this early?” he asked as he pulled his overshirt off, and then his jeans.

David was under all of the covers, idly scrolling through his phone until he saw Patrick. “Um, hi, how was the seminar?”

“Long and boring. Are you that tired?”

David cleared his throat and went back to his phone. “Don’tfeelwell,” he muttered.

“What?”

“I don’t feel well,” he said, louder and more whiny. “I think I’m sick.”

Patrick made a sympathetic noise and finished getting dressed before he went to climb in bed next to him. David immediately gravitated toward him, and Patrick could feel a subtle shiver.

“Do I feel warm to you?”

Concern nipping at him, Patrick laid a hand across his husband’s forehead and made a negative noise. “Yeah, kind of.”

David whined. Patrick cut him off with a kiss to his temple. “You need anything?”

“No...” David made it very clear that he didn’t want Patrick going anywhere when he latched onto him tighter. “Just missed you.”

“Well, I’m flattered.”

-----

David shook him awake. It was dark, how long had they been asleep?

“Patrick...” David sounded nervous, the tone was enough for Patrick to wake up completely and turn a light on.

They both winced at the sudden light, but now he got a look at his husband’s worried face. Something was wrong, then.

“What’s up?”

“I...” despite how upset he seemed, he didn’t seem to know the answer to that question “I don’t feel...” he whined, but this time it was a heartbreaking sound. While sputtering and looking more and more distressed by the second, he finally settled on, “I don’t know.”

Patrick had a hunch. He felt David’s forehead again, and it was far warmer than earlier. “I think your fever went up,” he said steadily. “I’m gonna go grab some tylenol.”

David tried to protest as he started to get out of bed, but it was a small walk to the bathroom and back. When he returned, though, his husband was on the verge of tears.

“Hey,” he said, sitting on the edge of the bed. “What’s wrong?”

“Nothing.” David shook his head. “I just really don’t feel well.” He sniffled and swiped away some tears on his cheek. “Sorry. I... I get like this, sometimes, when I...” he finished his sentence by lazily gesturing to himself.

Patrick shook his head and pressed a kiss to David’s temple once again. “Don’t apologize. I love you.”

“Love you too.”

“Now, let’s get some medicine in you.”

David nodded. “That sounds amazing.”
