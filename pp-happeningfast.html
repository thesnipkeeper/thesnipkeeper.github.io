Title: Happening Fast
Fandom: Penumbra Podcast
Summary: Juno is surprised to find out that isolation is the wrong decision
Word count: 1.2k

Happening Fast

If there’s one thing that Juno Steel hates, it’s getting sick.

What he hates more, however, is getting sick when he’s forced to be around others. Sure, he can lock himself in his room, but he’s still on a ship full of people intent on bothering him and questioning him and getting involved in his business.

Ugh.

He locks his door the morning that Juno wakes up with a sore throat. Better for him to quarantine himself now, lest the entire fucking ship get sick. Realistically, he remembers Vespa saying that people are contagious days before they show symptoms. Doesn’t matter, he’ll just... isolate for a while.

He coughs. And coughs, and coughs. Whatever this is is intent on settling in his chest. It shouldn’t be much of a surprise, colds often do.

Juno only just manages to cover a cough that results in a glob of gross ending up on his sleeve. He swear, grumbles, and goes to change his shirt and find some tissues to spit out future gunk.

His reflection in the bathroom mirror is what clues him into the fact that maybe this isn’t a cold. He looks... God he looks terrible! Thank goodness no one saw him today, they’d been appalled. Peter... Peter doesn’t ever need to see him this... sweaty, and tired and gross.

He ignores the reminder in the back of his head that says Peter has seen him in many different horrible states and this one wouldn’t be much different.

Juno coughs. He’s managed to curl up in bed for a good - a glance at the clock - two hours. Jeez, has it been that long?

He’s thirsty. He’s done nothing but cough and sniffle pathetically as his nose starts to block as well. Fantastic. He just needs some water, and...

Juno tries to get out of bed, only for the world to spin around him. He groans and lies back down until the vertigo and nausea eases.

Well, that’s not good.

He shudders, whines.

All he wants is a fucking glass of water and he can’t even get out of bed without passing out.

He’s tempted to call Peter, beg him to come take care of him and coddle him and make everything okay. Except, he’s supposed to be quarantining. He just didn’t realize that things would get so bad in one day.

He falls into an uncomfortable sleep before he can decide what to do about that water.

-----

Briiing

Juno groans. That’s the sound of his- where- ? Who-?

He goes to answer the call, sitting up slowly in his bed. The blankets fall off of him as he does so, and he shivers violently.

Juno doesn’t have a chance to answer before the movement becomes too much and he’s throwing up over the side of his bed. He gives something between a gasp and a sob as he dry heaves painfully. He can’t even think of answering the call right now, not when he has to clean up and-

Bang bang!

Juno startles.

“Juno? Are you in there? Please open the door!” Peter’s voice, but he sounds panicked. Juno overflows with guilt. He isn’t sure how long it’s been, but he should have just told Peter. Now his boyfriend is worried and scared and it’s Juno’s fault.

No, he can’t come in. This is definitely contagious.

“Juno, love, are you alright? Please, say something.”

Juno cries loudly instead.

Peter’s voice gets more desperate until it sounds like he’s about to cry as well. “Buddy! Can you open...” his voice fades out.

Juno curls up, closes his eyes. His face is wet with tears and snot and puke, but he can’t bring himself to do anything about it right now. God, he just wants Peter.

He doesn’t realize he’s fallen into an uneasy sleep until he feels someone shaking him awake.

“Sweetheart, you’re burning up,” Peter breathes out. Juno opens his eyes, only to see tears in Peter’s own. He turns around. “Buddy-”

“Don’t worry, Vespa’s already on her way.”

“Sorry,” is all Juno can think to say. Sorry for... forcing him to expose himself to whatever illness this is. Sorry for worrying him. Sorry for looking gross.

Peter just laughs, sobs, and lets his head drop against Juno’s shoulder. He can’t smell good right now, Juno thinks distantly.

He’s vaguely aware of someone taking his temperature, someone rough... Vespa, probably. Of someone cleaning his face with a damp cloth, long, gentle fingers.... Peter.

This is going to be humiliating to think back on, Juno thinks. But he can’t give a shit right now.

-----

As far as Juno knows, he’s not dying. So why Peter hasn’t left his side since he found him in his room... why he’s looking at him that way when the two of them are sitting quietly in the infirmary... he’s not sure.

He still feels out of it, tired, washed out. Vespa’s got him on an IV to hydrate him or something. He hates hospitals, being connected to things. It’s annoying.

Peter silently leans down and presses a lazy kiss to his shoulder, his hand.

“Okay,” Juno says, voice hoarse and throat sore from two days of coughing. Two days, because apparently he slept half a day away when it felt like a little nap. “What’s the deal? I mean, are you guys gonna tell me what’s going on?”

Peter sighs, looking as if that’s the last thing he wants to talk about. “We... found out that you and I were exposed to a type of... flu, I suppose, on the last job.”

Juno raises an eyebrow, urging him to continue.

“Vespa tested me, my bloodwork came back clean. I went to get you, and-”

“And I was...” he gestured lazily to himself.

Peter nods, swallows hard. “It’s known, apparently, for dehydrating you. Quickly.”

“And... being deadly?”

“Untreated, yes.”

Juno locked himself in a room while sick with an illness that killed when untreated. He really is a fucking crazy martyr, isn’t he?

“Buddy didn’t even find out about the exposure until Rita found someone discussing it in the texts, and by the time we realized, you were already so... Juno...” Peter says miserably. “Juno, that was awful. Don’t do that again.”

“Okay, in my defense,” Juno rasps. “I thought it was a cold. I didn’t want to get everyone else sick. Wait-” He went to sit up and ended up ridiculously dizzy instead. Peter sets a hand on his chest to stop him.

“Everyone’s fine, don’t you worry.”

“What about you, though?” Juno asks, because that should be an obvious question. “You’re right here with me!”

“Mm, I am, aren’t I?”

“Nureyev,” Juno hisses under his breath. “Don’t be stupid, get out of here before you get sick and die!”

“Hmm...” he screws his face up in thought. “No, maybe later.” At Juno’s horrified expression, he chuckles. “Vespa found some vaccinations. Everyone’s fine,” he repeats.

Juno sighs, and coughs instead. He’s still got an ungodly amount of gunk in his chest and sinuses that’s... ew, gonna have to come out. Before he can even get a word out, Peter presses a tissue to his mouth, muffling his gross cough with his hand.

Juno makes a noise, but he doesn’t have much choice. It’s that or cough up gunk into the air, and that’s much worse when his boyfriend is sitting right there. Peter’s even crazy enough to kiss his forehead afterward.

Juno blinks, furrows his brows at the man. “I’m disgusting right now.”

Peter chuckles fondly. “Yes, well, I’ve seen worse. Get some rest, love.”

Juno’s content to be coddled for the rest of his illness.
