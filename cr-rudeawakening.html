Rude Awakening

There are better ways to wake up, Percy decides. Better ways than vomiting over the side of your bed before the sun has even risen and scaring the living daylights out of your girlfriend.

“Fuck, Percy!” Vex exclaims, out of concern rather than scolding him for waking her. At least he hopes so, he thinks fondly.

Except Percy can’t respond, because he still feels sick, and all he can do is clamp a hand over his mouth and let her drag him to the bathroom. She’s asking him questions about his wellbeing that he clearly is not capable of answering right now, but it seems to make her feel better.

Percy vomits into the toilet. It’s not pretty, and it... doesn’t all make it in, but he’s just glad that he made it at all. Not everything is going to go perfectly, he supposes.

Vex rubs his back and mutters comforting words while he heaves up small streams.

With as much as he’s thrown up, he still doesn’t feel well, or done. And that, Percy decides, does not bode well. He’d heard talk of a bug going around the city, but he thought it was a cold or something, not a fucking-

Percy moans and wraps an arm around his stomach when a cramp made itself known. “Vex’ahlia, dear,” he says. “I’m going to need you to leave.”

“Are you sure?” She asks. She’s clearly concerned, but also clearly exhausted. Being woken up in the middle of the night to the sound of vomiting will do that.

“Very sure,” he says, pulling himself up. “Something unpleasant is about to happen and I’d prefer that there were no witnesses.”

She chuckles in realization. “Understood. I’m going to find someone to help clean the bedroom.”

He’s already fumbling with the waistband of his sleep pants as she leaves. Percival wraps both arms around his middle and hopes the cramps will subside. Perhaps he ate something bad and it just needs to... leave his system, or something. He can hear people outside scuttling around, likely replacing his and Vex’s bedding and fixing the damage that he’d done to the fine carpet that lay in the middle of his room.

Humiliating.

Even worse, not even shitting his guts out makes him feel better.

He isn’t comfortable getting up yet. He feels a bit lightheaded after the whole debacle, and liquid keeps leaving his body without him even trying to push. It’s probably for the best that he get it all out now, the last thing he wants is to have that sort of accident in bed.

The mere thought has him wincing in horror. He might want to talk to Vex about this.

A knock shakes him out of his fuzzy train of thought.

“Percy, I’m getting Pike.”

“Don’t get Pike...” he sighs out. “She’s asleep right now.”

“And do you know what kind of vengeance she will rain down if she finds out that you died of an illness she could’ve help with?”

“Well, I will have already been dead, so it hardly matters.” Confident words coming from someone who’s sitting on the toilet.

Vex chuckles. “Percival.”

“Alright, alright...” he sighs. “Probably for the best.”

“Mmhm.” Thankfully, Vex fetching their cleric friend gives him an opportunity to get himself together. He feels a little more human once he’s in less damp clothing and not locked away in the bathroom.

The bed is made up just as nicely as it had been before he and Vex got in the night before. The room is completely clean, looking as if it has hardly been touched let alone vomited in. He sits on the edge of the bed, shivers, and sighs.

Chills mean an oncoming fever. He’s been ill enough times to know that right away.

He wastes no time getting under the clean covers as he shudders, risking a small whine while no one's around to hear his misery.

He doesn’t realize that he's fallen asleep until he wakes to a cool, albeit small hand on his forehead.

"Oh, Percy…"

What he first notices is Pike standing in front of him with a sympathetic expression and his girlfriend behind her with the same. The second thing he notices is that he's incredibly cold. He shivers, teeth chattering.

Pike winces. "Sorry, I didn't mean to wake you up, you just looked like you might have a fever." A sigh. "Which… you do. I can't help right now, but…"

Percy shakes his head. "No, it's alright. It's just a bug, right?"

"Probably," she affirms. "I wish I could do more."

"Sorry for waking you," Vex said. "I think I… panicked a little bit."

Pike chuckles. Percy's eyes slip shut. "I can help tomorrow."

"Best you get back to sleep then," Percy mumbles, smallest smile on his lips.

"Yes, I guess so. I hope you have a… a good night, Percy."
