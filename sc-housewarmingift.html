Title: Housewarming Gift
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's sick and doesn't like having to be in bed all day, but not for the reason that one would think
Word count: 2.8k

Housewarming Gift

Patrick smiled as his fiancé flopped on the couch next to him. These were the best - he thought - the quiet nights at home after working at the store all day.

They’d both been working hard, especially David, and he’d understandably been running out of steam as the day progressed.

Now, both of them could finally relax, watch some mindless TV, maybe dig into their ice cream preserves later.

David’s head lazily rested on his shoulder. Patrick in turn rested his own on David’s head, a comforting position that usually happened when they both really needed to be close.

But today David felt unusually warm against Patrick’s skin.

Worry and suspicions prickling at the back of his mind, Patrick tilted his head until his cheek was against David’s forehead. David didn’t stir for a moment, probably falling asleep.

He was warm.

There was no way that this wasn’t a fever.

He should have noticed the signs sooner. David had been quiet most of the day, and that certainly wasn’t like him. He might have been tired as well, but he must have also been getting sick.

Patrick wondered if he even realized it between all the things they'd been working on.

He smiled sadly, aware that the coming week was going to be just as stressful for the two of them as this week was. But maybe this was David’s chance to finally get some rest between the store and the wedding planning.

He angled his head just enough to press a kiss to David’s forehead, earning a happy noise from his fiance.

“Don’t feel good,” David muttered sleepily.

Patrick cooed sympathetically. “I figured. You’re a little warm.”

“Mmm.”

He could tell just by the sound of his breathing that David was falling asleep. As much as Patrick was loving this moment, he’d just have to disturb David a few minutes later when he inevitably had to adjust or get up.

“Hey,” Patrick said softly, rubbing David’s arm until he lifted his head. “Why don’t you go to bed and get some rest?”

David groaned and rubbed his eyes sleepily. It was adorable.

“You’re probably right but... ugh...not how I wanted this night to go.”

“I know...” Patrick sighed. “Come on, let’s get you to bed.”

“Mmm, sexy.”

He scoffed. “I think you’d fall asleep before we could even get started.”

-----

He really hoped that this fever was some sort of fluke from the recent stress. Hopefully David just needed some extra rest.

But he fell asleep at six in the evening and slept well into the morning. Granted, Patrick could feel him occasionally tossing and turning that night. Maybe the long sleep wasn’t as restful as it should have been.

And then Patrick woke to being cuddled by a human furnace, and he realized this probably wasn’t just stress.

Patrick carefully sat on the edge of the bed and leaned over his fiance. He held a housewarming item his parents gave him that he hadn’t planned on putting to use this early. The device in his hand beeped as he pressed a button. Steady as he could, he slipped it into his sleeping fiance's ear.

David shifted and his brows furrowed, questioning noise bubbling up out of his throat.

Patrick shushed him, smoothing his hair back gently and David, half asleep, melted under the touch, no longer squirming against the sudden object.

The thermometer beeped, Patrick was slow as he took it out of David’s ear.

"Oh David…" he mumbled sadly at the numbers, before going back to running his fingers through his fiance’s hair.

David sniffled, nose having started to get drippy in the night. His eyes fluttered open, revealing to be adorably red-rimmed and sleepy.

Patrick pressed a soft kiss to his forehead.

“Morning, sunshine.”

David groaned and curled up, stuffing his face in the pillow. He gave a weak cough that seemed to bubble up directly from his chest. He sounded full of gunk already, and Patrick knew that he was in for a miserable few days.

“Feel gross, Patrick...” he whined.

Patrick hummed in agreement and proceeded to stroke his cheek with the back of his hand, enough to get David to stop curling up so tightly. “Your temperature isn’t great.”

David’s hand went up to his ear for a moment. “That’s what that was...”

“Mhmm. It’s funny, I told my parents I probably wouldn’t need it.”

“Course you did, though,” David muttered, eyes slipping shut.

“Don’t know what that’s supposed to mean, but I’ll let it slide.”

“Tired... going back to sleep...”

“David, hey, can you take some meds for me before you fall asleep?”

David furrowed his brows at first, and then seemed to think it over for a bit before finally opening his eyes again.

Patrick helped him sit up enough before handing him a couple tylenol and a bottle of water.

David swallowed them and immediately handed the water back and slid back under the covers.

“Th’nks.”

Patrick gave another kiss. “Get some sleep. I love you.”

“L’ve you more,” David snuffled. It wasn’t long before he was snoring peacefully.

David slept most of the day.

Patrick found ways to keep himself busy so he wasn’t checking on him all the time (he just needed rest, Patrick would remind himself). He cleaned the kitchen until it was spotless and tidied the rest of the apartment in a way that David would most likely correct if he wasn’t sick.

Patrick made sure to find a new thing to do every time he was tempted to wake David and force more meds or water into him. It wouldn’t actually solve anything, so Patrick cleaned the bathroom instead.

His ears perked up at the sound of a weak, sleepy voice coming from the other side of the apartment. Patrick, having been trying to read for the past few minutes, immediately set his book aside and rushed to the bed.

David shifted for a moment before his eyes fluttered open. Eyes that were usually so bright and alert were now clouded over with sickness, watery and pink.

“Hello, sweetheart.”

David groaned and reached up to clumsily rub his face. “Time is it?”

“It’s four. You’ve been sleeping for a little while,” he said softly, pulling himself onto the bed next to his sick fiance.

“Ugh, God...” David snuffled, and coughed as it triggered a tickle in his throat.

“Feeling any better?”

“No,” he answered quickly. He snatched Patrick’s arm with a pitiful whine until he was being cuddled to his liking. “Feel fucking awful.”

Patrick was relieved to find that he wasn’t as warm as before. He hummed in sympathy and pressed a kiss to David’s head. “You ready to eat something or do you want to go back to sleep?”

David sniffled, coughed. “I don’t think I can sleep anymore.”

“Dinner it is, then,” Patrick said with a nod. “I’m going to pick something up from the cafe and bring it home. Are you okay being here alone?”

David took long enough to answer that Patrick was starting to question himself. Maybe he shouldn’t have even asked; David was pretty sick.

“I’ll come with you.”

That definitely wasn’t the response he was expecting. “We can just order in if-”

“No, I want to get out of the apartment for a while. I slept all day, I feel useless, I need to do something.” There was a certain desperation in his voice that had Patrick worried for his fiance’s mental health more than physical at that moment.

“David, you’re sick. You’re supposed to sleep all day. That’s what sick days are for.”

“Mm’m. Nope.” David moved out of Patrick’s grasp and sat up, dragging both hands down his face. He threw his arms out, closed his eyes, and took a deep breath as if he was trying to calm himself. “Let’s go pick up the food.”

“David...”

“Patrick. I’m serious, I- oh God. I can’t believe I slept all day.” The more awake he became, the more anxious he seemed to get at the thought. Should Patrick have waken him all those times that he wanted to? But then David wouldn’t have gotten any rest. He made the right call. He hoped.

“Okay, David. Do you need help-?”

“No, thank you.” David grabbed onto the bedpost and pulled himself up, swaying slightly. Fuck, this was awful.

Patrick called the cafe and tried to act like he wasn’t watching him as he got dressed and fixed his hair. David was a hot mess, they both knew that, but he never expected sleeping in to give him anxiety like this.

David was unsurprisingly skilled at making himself look together despite being sick and feverish. Speaking of...

“Really?”

“Just do it for me? Please? I just want to know.”

That was all it took for Patrick to get him to sit docile on the couch while he got the thermometer. He pressed it into David’s ear and kissed his forehead as they waited for it to beep. The two of them looked at the reading together, both making negative ‘eh’ noises. Patrick smushed his nose against David’s temple, the other making a noise of faux-annoyance.

“Is that...” David cleared his throat. “Is that better than before?”

“Yeah, actually. Let’s make this trip quick?”

David gave a slow nod. He looked sleepy, that was for sure, but Patrick didn’t try to convince him to stay again. For some reason, David needed this.

-----

David took Patrick’s hand in his on their way into the cafe. He smiled a bit, and it reassured Patrick that he was alright. God, he shouldn’t be worrying this much.

“Hey guys!” Twyla grinned as they walked in. “Your order’s almost done, you can take a seat while you wait.”

They nodded, sitting at the small table closest to the door. David rested his chin on his palm, looking like he could fall asleep right there.

“You alright?” Patrick asked quietly.

David gave a minute nod. “Just not feeling great?” he said, scrunching his face. “My head hurts.”

“Ah.”

“I’ll take some meds when we get back.” He sniffled and scrubbed the back of his hand against his nose.

“There we go!”

Patrick got up and took the food from Twyla, thanking her and wishing her a good day. He laid a hand on David’s back as they walked out.

“Thank you for um, not trying to stop me from coming alone,” David said after a few moments of comfortable silence.

That was something Patrick had been working on; not deciding that he was always right. Especially if he was going to be married, he couldn’t force people to subscribe to his way of thinking.

“Can we talk about why you wanted to come, though?”

David’s face scrunched up which informed him that no, he really didn’t want to talk about that.

But unfortunately for David, communication was something else Patrick was trying to work on.

Patrick went through various ways to bring it up in his head. So many ways this conversation to go. In the end, however, Patrick decided on something simple. “Are you okay?”

David’s face un-scrunched. He turned to Patrick, brows turning up like he really hadn’t expected that. He searched Patrick’s face for a while, illness-clogged brain trying to work overtime to figure out if there was any hidden meaning behind his words.

And then a soft smile grew on his face. “I’m fine.” He cleared his throat. “Um, it’s just...when I stay in bed all day...it- it usually only happens on um- bad days.”

Patrick hated to hear about David’s “bad days”. He hated to think about David having “bad days”. He’d heard about them from David, but more from Alexis, and Mr. Rose, and Stevie, all people who knew how to deal with him when he was feeling down.

“So-”

“So like,” David continued, and it raised Patrick’s spirits just a bit to see him using his usual unnecessary hand gestures to speak. “Whenever that happens when I’m not having a... a day...”

“It makes you think about it.”

David nodded quickly. “Yeah, yeah. So. I can’t have whatever plague this is jump starting a stupid moodfest, okay?”

“I understand,” Patrick said.

David’s voice came out quiet. “...Thank you.”

“In that case, we will spend the evening together? Keep your mind off things until you feel better?”

David grinned. “That sounds amazing.”

-----

David was coughing more and more once they reached Patrick’s floor. Patrick ushered him to the couch and gave him a glass of water, all while David was still trying to stop coughing.

He plated their food, making it look a little fancier than usual, and set one on the coffee table in front of David before kissing his cheek.

David recovered from his coughing fit, clearing his throat. “You know what would make tonight really special?”

Patrick chuckled. “I don’t think I’m willing to do that while you’re sick.”

“Mm’m, no... wine.”

Patrick gave him a deadpan look. “David, the last thing you need right now is alcohol.”

“Is it, though?”

“Yes.”

He sighed. “Fine, fine. I guess I’ll drink water like some kind of simpleton.”

“Good,” Patrick shrugged. “Staying hydrated is important for fighting illness.”

David scoffed. “You’re the worst. I love you.”

“I love you too.”

Patrick handed him the bottle of tylenol once they were done eating, and then ran his fingers through David’s hair when he fell asleep on his lap. He was probably in for a few more awful days before he started to feel better, and Patrick was probably doomed to catch this bug, but right now, things were perfect.

-----

There were some incidents where Patrick had to pull David out of his own head.

The next day, he made sure to call David regularly while he was at the store, even when he was met with a grumpy, half-asleep voice on the other end.

It was late afternoon the next time he tried to call. He tapped his foot anxiously as he listened to the phone ring, and ring, and ring...

Patrick was seconds away from leaving the store unsupervised to check on David when he realized there were much better ways to handle this.

He dialed a number he didn’t often call.

“Patrick? Wow, hello!”

Patrick chuckled. “Hey, Alexis. Um, are you busy?”

“Ummm, not really? What’s up?”

“Would you mind going to my apartment and checking on David? He’s home sick and he’s not answering my call.”

There was a pause on the other end before, “I mean, you don’t think he’s just like... asleep?”

Ah. Patrick sighed. He’s an idiot. “That makes sense. Sorry for bothering you, I’m- I’m worried.”

“I can still check on him, if you want. I have literally nothing else to do today.”

Patrick smiled with relief. “That’d be great.”

“No problem. I know how David gets, he could probably use the company whether he admits it or not.”

Patrick couldn’t help but chuckle. “Let me know?”

-----

Thirty minutes later, David’s name appeared on his phone.

“David?”

“What have you done?”

“I take it Alexis made it, then.”

“Uh, yeah, Alexis made it, Patrick. Why are you sicking my own sister on me because I was sleeping?”

“I- look...”

“Mmm?!”

“David, I was worried. You weren’t picking up.”

“Because, again, I was sleeping, Patrick.”

“I know, I know. I wasn’t thinking clearly.”

“You know,” he could hear a smile in David’s voice now, and then Alexis in the background probably snooping around in the apartment. “The whole neurotic doomsday thinking is kind of my job.”

“David...”

A chuckle. “It’s kind of adorable how worried you get, you know.”

Patrick bit back a smile. “I’ll be home in a couple hours. Let Alexis take care of you, okay?”

“Fine, but if she tries to go through your stuff, I’m not going to stop her.”

“That’s fair.”

-----

The sibling banter could be heard before Patrick even entered his apartment. He never really paid attention to David and Alexis’ arguments, but from what he could tell, this one had something to do with a debate about Richard Gere or George Clooney. He shook his head fondly and keyed himself in.

Both Roses turned to him from the couch. David smiled brightly before giving an exasperate sigh.

“Can you kick her out, please? She thinks George Clooney looks old.”

“Eh, Alexis, you’re almost family, so you can stay.”

“Oh my God,” David groaned.

Alexis gave a smug grin as she stood up. “Well, thank you, Patrick. And I would like, love to stay-”

“Fuck-”

“No, you fuck off, David. I can’t be related to someone who thinks Richard Gere wasn’t super sexy in Pretty Woman, bye!”

Patrick didn’t have a chance to say anything else before she was out the door. He chuckled and threw his keys on the table.

“Sounds like you two had fun.”

“Never do that to me again.”
