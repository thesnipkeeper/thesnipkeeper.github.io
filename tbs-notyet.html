Not Yet

"Bless you. Goodness!"

"Sorry," Joan groaned and pulled another tissue from the box on Owen's desk. It was pretty much hers now. "I can't seem to stop sneezing today," she sniffled wetly, and then blew her nose. She was so beyond being embarrassed about it. Perhaps that was a testament to how much she shouldn't have been at work.

"You’re getting sick," Owen observed, though he'd clearly noticed ages ago when he nudged the tissue box in her direction after the third sneeze.

"Seems that way," she agreed with a sniffly sigh. She dragged the damp tissue across her nostrils to catch any visible wetness. Not that it mattered, her nose was just going to start running again, anyway. "But about our new patient? The one you wanted to talk about?"

"Right, right." Owen handed her a folder. "He's a telepath, but he's spent a good amount of his adult years in mental health facilities."

"Misdiagnosed as schizophrenia..."

"Precisely. He's not in a good place. Because of his... history, I'm not sure it's a good idea to keep him as an inpatient."

"I'm inclined to agree." She sniffled. Yup, her nose was running again. "He should be able to regain some sense of a normal life now that he's aware that the voices are real."

Owen nodded. "I'm glad that we can agree on something, then."

Joan's breath caught suddenly and she rushed to drop the folder and grab another tissue to sneeze into.

"Bless you, Joan," Owen said softly. "You really don't sound well."

"I'm aware," it was meant to come out as annoyed, instead she just sounded as tired as she felt. "But I can't go home just because of a cold."

"Unless you have a fever!” Owen added rather casually as he began collecting his papers.

"What?"

"The rule, you know, you're officially too sick to work if you have a fever. So, do-”

“No,” she sighed, sniffled, rested her head against her palm with an elbow on the desk. “No, I’ve already checked.”

Owen was quiet for a moment as if in thought. “Oh, Joan...”

Joan shook her head. “Don’t, Owen, I know what you’re going to say.”

“What? That you should take care of yourself?”

“Like I said, there’s no fever, which means I’m able to work today. Beyond that, you don’t get to decide.”

He frowned. “I... I wasn’t trying to be pushy, I just-”

“I know,” she said, standing up. “We’re just not there yet, okay?”

Owen looked like he wanted to argue more, but instead just nodded. “Thank you for your uh,” he cleared his throat. “For your time, then.”

Joan offered a smile and left his office. The guilt hit her instantly.

Their conversations always used to end like that, back when he worked for the AM and she didn’t. It’d been a while since that happened again. She wasn’t sure what happened; maybe working together was going to take some time to get used to.

“Hey!” Sam caught up with her in the hallway. “I was wondering if you were going for lunch today? Thought maybe we could- wow, are you okay?”

Joan was so caught up with replaying her and Owen’s conversation in her head that it took her a moment to realize what Sam was talking about. “It’s a cold. Jesus, do I really look that bad?”

Sam gave a confused chuckle. “I take it that I’m not the first person to ask. This makes sense, though. You were acting kind of weird yesterday, thought you were just tired, but I guess...” she gestured toward her. “Anyway, lunch?”

Joan sniffed. Thankfully, her nose had decided to give her a break for a moment. She still felt gross, but standing seemed to better prevent the damn waterfall on her face. “Sorry, Owen just asked me the same thing... kind of, but when he does it, it’s more-”

“Owen-y?”

She laughed. “Sure. Let’s go with that. Um...” the idea of going out in public right now didn’t seem like the best idea, she’d probably just end up humiliating herself further than she already had today. “I think I’ll be working through lunch, sorry Sam.”

“That’s okay! But really, you sound... not good.”

“I’ll live, I promise.”

“Well, I’d hope so.” She smiled sympathetically, which made Joan feel equal parts annoyed and flattered that multiple people were so fixated on her ridiculous head cold. Someone caught Sam’s attention, “Oh, I need to help with that. See you later!”

Joan sneezed. Into her hands. Gross, Oh God...

Today was going to be a long day.

-----

The day was a series of blunders and unfortunate situations that could have been avoided. Yet, they all decided to happen today. Perhaps the universe was using Joan’s bad karma against her. Perhaps it was about time.

Two patients scheduled for the same time slot, one having to frustratingly re-schedule.

But... of course it had to happen today of all days. Why wouldn’t it? And what was ‘it’, one may ask? A later scheduled appointment with a new patient, just his second session. A temperature manipulator.

He was able to freeze things or melt them in a matter of seconds. He affected a good ten-foot radius around him. He, like Joan, was also having a bad day.

He had a panic attack. The room was suddenly freezing.

“Ethan, you need to calm down,” she didn’t mean to sound as urgent as she had. Her job was to stay calm for the patients. But her teeth were chattering and it was as if all warmth had been siphoned out of her.

Ethan’s face fell in realization when he saw what he’d done to the space around hi.m. “God... Dr. Bright, I’m so sorry, I-”

She held up a hand to stop him, because the room started to get cold once again with every panicked word that came out of his mouth. She took a deep shuddering breath, arms wrapped around herself. She was so cold. “It’s alright, Ethan. It’s nothing I haven’t dealt with before. Are you okay?”

He nodded, eyes still wide. “W-what about you?”

“I’ll be fine,” she said, though it was hard to sound confident when her voice was shaking with the rest of her. She cleared her throat, sniffled. Oh, her nose was running. Badly.

The session ended not too long after that. Joan reached for a tissue from her desk and tried to be casual as she wiped her nose and upper lip. The two of them stood up, Ethan significantly calmer after a few minutes of talking.

“I’m sorry again, Dr. Bright.”

She shook her head. “My office has suffered quite a few fates, frost isn’t even among the worst of them.”

He laughed. “If you say so.”

“I’d shake your hand but,” she gestured to herself. As embarrassing as it was, there was no way that he couldn’t tell that she had a cold. Everyone in the building could probably tell by now.

“That’s cool. Next week?”

“Next week,” she agreed, suppressing some shivers that were trying to creep up.

Ethan left, she blew her nose.

She cringed at the sound and feeling of all of the mucus escaping her body. And of course not just one tissue cut it anymore. Joan shuddered. Her skin was still cold, and she couldn’t get warm no matter what. She pulled the extra sweater from her office’s coat rack, it didn’t matter.

Perhaps she should wander off and let the office warm up a bit.

She tossed the soiled tissues and drowned her hands in sanitizer before heading out. A trek to the break room might make things better, she decided.

As if today couldn’t get any worse, they were out of tea. With her arms still wrapped around herself and involuntary shivers still making its way down her spine, she groaned.

“Everything okay?”

Joan wasn’t sure that she could deal with Owen right now. He was just... too helpful sometimes. And yet, out of all of the people who could’ve seen her like this, she was strangely glad it was him.

“Yeah,” she said, turning to face him and shoving her hands in her pockets in an attempt to casually keep them warm. He frowned. Joan sighed. “We’re out of tea.”

“Ah,” he said, brows turning up in amusement, or concern. Possibly both. It wasn’t like her to get frazzled over tea. “Sorry about that, I guess we haven’t restocked in a while.”

She shook her head dismissively and made her way to the Keurig. “Coffee’s fine!” Her voice cracked. She’d been talking all day and it was absolutely ruined. She may have to take the day off tomorrow just because she won’t have a voice left.

“Joan, you’re- you’re shivering.” Owen tried his best to make it sound less like concern and more like an observation, but...

“Yes, well, Ethan got a little worked up during our session today.”

She sniffled in an attempt to keep the stuff running out of her nose from reaching her upper lip. She only half-succeeded, because while it cleared her nose, it also irritated it. She rushed to push the final button on the coffee machine before burying her face in her arm. The first sneeze scraped against a scratchy throat that she hadn’t noticed until now. The second sneezed teased her for a good few seconds, and when it finally surfaced, it was... productive. She couldn’t pull her arm away without revealing the horrible, contagious mess underneath.

“Bless you,” came the soft voice of her coworker. Held in front of her was a handful of tissues.

“Snf! Thank you.” She must’ve looked ridiculous standing there with her arm over her face until she replaced it with the soft material. As if things couldn’t get any more humiliating, the touch of the tissues prompted a third (albeit much weaker) sneeze.

Owen blessedly went about his own business in the break room while she handled her situation. Coffee suddenly didn’t sound like it was going to fix anything. Except her throat, maybe. Wow, her throat was sore.

Speaking of her throat, she coughed. As if she already didn’t sound like a walking plague.

This time it was Sam who showed up. “What’s going on in here?” It was a playful question, but by the look on Sam’s face, she realized that that was not the atmosphere.

“Dr. Bright’s not feeling well, I was just about to send her home,” Owen said.

Joan scoffed, though that just triggered another cough. “I don’t need to go home.”

“Are you sure about that?” Sam raised an eyebrow. “Because you look... chilly.”

“Well, I am, but not because of this.”

“It’s been a bit since your last session and you don’t seem to have warmed up at all,” Owen commented.

Joan ignored them as she took her coffee cup and prepared it to her liking. They might’ve been out of tea, but they weren’t out of the impossibly-sweet dessert flavored creamer that she couldn’t get enough of. It was Sam’s sympathetic hum from behind her that made her realize she was sniffling repeatedly. Had she sounded like this all day?

“Aw, Joan, you should really go home.”

She chuckled hoarsely before taking a sip of her coffee. Couldn’t taste a thing. Damn. “You two are ganging up on me! I’m fine, really. I’ve worked through worse than this!”

“Well, of course you have. You’re you. But you shouldn’t have to!”

“Not to mention,” Owen interjected. “I’m your boss, technically. So...” he cleared his throat. “Go home, Joan.”

She opened her mouth to argue, but he lifted a hand and continued.

“Even if you’re well enough to work-”

“Which you’re definitely not,” Sam offered.

“You’re still contagious. you do remember what happens during cold and flu season around here? I know you’re careful about those sorts of things,” of course he did, he’d suffered through her daily sanitation of her (and sometimes his) apartment during that time of year. She’d always refuse to let him come over when she was sick (he came over anyway. She didn’t mind).

She closed her eyes and sighed. “Alright. I see your point.”

“Tomorrow, too.” He added.

She was going to argue again, and realized that oh yeah, she wouldn’t have a voice tomorrow. At least not one fit for talking to patients.

“Okay,” she nodded. “I’ll go home. You two win, and I hope you’re proud of yourselves.”

They looked quite proud of themselves.
