Title: Differences
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick learns that he prefers David to be whiny when he's not feeling good
Word count: 1.4k

Differences

The first time Patrick saw David sick, his attitude toward the whole situation didn’t surprise him a bit. David was dramatic and whiny, constantly begging Patrick to end his suffering.

“Ugh,” David complained after blowing his nose. “This is the worst, and I need it to end, like now.”

Patrick just chuckled, rolled his eyes, and told his ailing boyfriend to get back to work.

To David’s credit, he did get to work, although with some sniffles, sneezes, and complaints mixed in.

He conked out the minute they got to Patrick’s place later that night, and he was feeling better within two days.

-----

Today was different.

David was quiet. It was obvious that he wasn’t feeling good, and he hadn’t mentioned it once. Only showed up, threw his things in the back, and went on with his day. Patrick would have just assumed that he was in a bad mood, had a rough morning, except that would have never swayed him to refuse a kiss on the lips. Instead, David guided Patrick’s face to his cheek as he did when he had a cold, a silent way to say 'I love you but you don't want my germs'.

David didn’t say a thing. He’d respond when Patrick asked questions, or laugh halfheartedly at any bad joke, but the store missed his usual ramblings. David Rose wasn’t exactly a person known for his silence, and yet...

Patrick bit the inside of his lip, watching his boyfriend go about their work much slower than usual.

Patrick finally decided to speak up. "Hey, David," wide, brown eyes immediately looked up at him. "Are you okay?"

David looked up and furrowed his brows as if Patrick was being completely ridiculous. "Uh... Yeah." His voice was rough with disuse. David's brows furrowed in apparent discomfort this time as he cleared his throat, and when that didn't work, a handful of coughs bubbled out of him. "Jesus, sorry..." David thumped his chest. "Yeah, I'm fine," he said with a dismissive wave. "I'm just not… feeling great? But it's, you know, it's nothing. Like a cold or something."

Well, if David, dramatic of all dramatics was saying that it was nothing, then Patrick had no choice but to believe him. He could understand that maybe a little sore throat could cause his boyfriend to be quiet, and maybe he was just acting tired because he stayed up late the night before.

Patrick didn’t give it a second thought.

David’s coughing had become more and more constant, and by lunch he was having to turn away every five minutes. It was nipping at Patrick’s worry, but David continued to insist he was fine and Patrick was inclined to believe him. Because he’d be whining an awful lot if he wasn’t fine, wouldn’t he?

Until the next coughing fit. It left him breathless, gasping for air as he was doubled over behind the counter.

Patrick frowned, laying a hand on David’s back. The warmth that met his hand was enough to make him recoil for a second.

“David!” He didn’t mean for it to sound so accusatory, but it was too late now. David, breathing hard and eyes red from the hacking, looked up like he was ready to argue back. No, this wasn’t an argument Patrick wanted to have. He lowered his voice and tentatively laid the back of his hand on David’s forehead. “Honey, you’re burning up. Why didn’t you say anything?”

David rolled his eyes. "We have, like, a lot of work to do. And I don’t have time to be sick right now."

He was right. They'd been needing to restock for a while now and today was finally going to be the day that they got it all done so their customers had something to buy. Patrick sighed. Sending David home was the right thing to do, technically, but Patrick alone couldn't finish all of the work.

He pressed a slow kiss to David's forehead, and tried not to let his heart completely melt when David leaned into the touch like a needy cat.

"Go lay down in the back. I'll finish everything out here."

David was already shaking his head. He rested an arm on each of Patrick's shoulders and smiled softly. "I'm fine. Honestly, I can finish work. I've done it before when I was in much worse shape."

Patrick looked in his eyes and sighed. "That's not reassuring, David."

"Well, It's the truth. I'm full-capable of functioning no matter how sick I am!"

David turned to go back to work, but Patrick wasn't going to let him off that easily! He snaked a hand around David's waist before he could get away, David making a noise that was equal parts delighted and begrudging.

"What?" He asked, throwing a hand out.

"I have an idea."

David narrowed his eyes. "…Okay…"

"We take your temp, and if your fever is too high, you don't work today."

David bit his lip, weighing his options. He cleared his throat, which ultimately resulted in a harsh, chesty cough that had them both wincing. "Fine," he finally said.

-----

"Keep this under your tongue for one minute."

David hopped up on the counter with far more effort than usual. "Uh, yeah, I know how thermometers work."

It was kind of adorable how docile a fever caused David to be, or it would be adorable if Patrick didn't know how miserable he was feeling. He rested a hand on the back of David's neck and pulled him forward to kiss his forehead.

The thermometer beeped, Patrick got to it before David did. Admittedly, the reading is underwhelming. David had a fever--absolutely--but it was the same kind of fever one could expect from a bad cold.

David snatched his wrist and turned in so he could see the numbers. "Okay, well that's not nearly as bad as I feel, so I'm fine."

Patrick stared. "Not as bad as you feel, huh?"

"Fuuuck…" David mumbled, eyes rolling to the ceiling. "I mean-"

He shook his head. "Nope. You're going home, or lying down in the back, or calling someone to pick you up, but David, you are not working today."

"Okay, but like, I'm technically your boss, so-"

"Not today you aren’t."

David smiled fondly, which made Patrick feel like less of a dick for bossing him around. "You're so cute when you're worried."

"David…"

"Right, right!" David jumped off, only to sway on his feet. He closed his eyes for a moment until the dizziness passed, and made his way to the back room. "Resting!" he called.

"Good!"

-----

Patrick finished closing up completely before he went in back to disturb the snoring that was happening. David was stretched across their old couch, legs dangling off the side because he was such a big guy he couldn't really stretch out on any sofa.

He looked comfortable enough, though.

Patrick sat on the edge and gently rubbed David's back until he woke with an undignified snort. "Hey."

David yawned. "Hey." he snuffled and scrubbed his nose. " 't time is it?"

"Little after five."

David's eyes widened momentarily when he realized that he'd been sleeping for hours. "God… did everything go okay?"

"Everything was fine. I mean, I did have a particularly tense conversation with a Karen who was upset that we were out of lavender soap, but I'm sure she'll be back."

A sleepy, huffed out chuckle as David continued to wake up.

"Can we talk about…"

"I'd rather just go home and sleep for a month."

"David, you and I have talked about sharing our feelings with each other."

"Right…" David said with a careful nod.

"I think that should include physical feelings. You know, like being horribly sick."

"I tell you when I'm sick, though."

"When you have a cold, yeah, but this… hey, I just want to know when you're not feeling good, okay? Whether we have work to do or not." He reached to brush some stray hair out of David's face, his product long since stopped working. He felt warmer now, although that could have just been from sleeping.

David seemed to realize what this meant to him, because he nodded without another argument. "Alright, I will tell you every time I don't feel one-hundred percent. And you'll have to deal with it."

Patrick chuckled. "Sounds good to me." He stood up and grabbed his jacket from the coat hanger, letting David slowly get up on his own. "My place or the motel?"

David was in the middle of dragging a hand down his face when he looked up. "Are you sure you want my germs allover your apartment?"

Patrick shrugged. "I probably have your germs allover everything else by now."
