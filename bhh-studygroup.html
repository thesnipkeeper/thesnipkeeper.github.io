Study Group

The group of them thought that it would be a good idea to study together. They told themselves it was just because they needed to study, but the truth was that they’d been so busy that they hadn’t really had a chance to hang out together for so long.

All five of them gathered in Corrine and Josie’s room with their books, bringing snacks along with them.

They probably wouldn’t get much studying done, Corrine lamented. She already had plans to study on her own later, because she knew her friends and that they would want to do more talking than actual schoolwork.

The others were gossiping about a new possible couple in the school. Usually Marshall enjoyed getting in on the juicy discussion, but tonight he was strangely quiet.

It wasn’t until he refused the junk food Josie offered him that any of them realized that something was very wrong. It wasn’t like Marshall (or... any guy, really) to say no to junk food. Josie and Corrine exchanged confused looks.

“Are you feeling okay?” Corrine asked. She instinctively reached up to feel his forehead, something her parents always did when she was acting weird. She didn’t expect him to actually feel warm.

He made an annoyed sound and moved away, but it was too late.

“Marshall!” Corrine said. “You have a fever!”

“I do not,” Marshall chuckled as if that was the most ridiculous thing he’d ever heard. But getting a look at her face, he frowned and reached up to feel it for himself (which wouldn’t work, but Corrine wasn’t going to tell him that.) “Do I?”

“Now that I think about it, you’ve been acting really weird today,” Lucas said, narrowing his eyes.

Marshall looked confused as if realizing it himself, but he soon put on a smile. “Guys, I’m fine. I’m sure it’s nothing.”

It wasn’t nothing. Corrine wasn’t sure exactly what was wrong, and she was doing her best not to automatically diagnose him, but he was acting strange all night.

It wasn’t long before he said that he was tired and should probably go to bed early for class tomorrow. Corrine didn’t miss the wince that flashed across his face for a split second as he stood up.

“Sorry, guys...” he said.

“Go rest,” Corrine said.

“See you later, Marshall,” Lucas said.

Marshall waved as he headed out the door.

“That was weird,” Josie muttered.

Lucas shrugged. “He’s probably just tired.”

Corrine knew that wasn’t true. She knew how Marshall acted when he was just tired and that wasn’t it. The others should have been able to notice as well, but maybe they were too distracted by everything else. “He wasn’t just tired, he was running a fever.”

“Are you sure?”

Corrine blinked. “Yeah?”

“I mean,” Josie shrugged. “He was wearing long sleeves. Maybe he was just hot.”

Corinne shook her head. “No, that wasn’t it.”

Josie bit her lip. The others went back to studying. Corrine spent the whole night wondering if she should go check on him.

But if he left, maybe that meant that he wanted to be alone.

-----

She and Josie were both in bed, lights finally off, when there was a knock at the door.

They both sat up and looked at each other. Who could that be...?

Josie nodded toward the door, and Corrine sighed and got out of bed. She wasn’t sure who she expected, but it certainly wasn’t Lucas.

“Lucas, what...?”

Lucas sighed heavily, his shoulders falling. “It’s Marshall.”

“What about him?” Josie asked, now fully awake.

He looked to Corrine. “He’s... there’s something wrong. He’s- he’s fine, I guess, but I thought you would be better at handling...” he gestured vaguely. He seemed already unsure of what to do. As if Corrine knew.

Still, she nodded. “Okay... um, I guess I’ll...” she scooted past him and headed to their room, trying not to let herself worry too much.

It wasn’t until she arrived and saw her boyfriend’s face pinched in pain that she really started to worry.

“Marshall! What’s wrong?”

“Nothing, it’s just my ear,” he ground out. Sure enough, she realized that he had a hand protectively over his right ear. “I’m sorry, I didn’t want to-”

She shook her head. “How long has it been hurting?”

“Um,” Marshall swallowed. “Probably since... since study group.” He winced more, but this time in fear of a lecture instead of pain.

Corrine gave him a look and sighed. “Okay, well that would have been nice to know.”

“I know, I’m sorry... can you fix me?”

“Fix you? No. But help a little bit? ... maybe.”

Corrine (quietly) rushed back to her room, finding Lucas passed out on the floor, makeshift bed of spare blankets and pillows. She scoffed and grabbed her bottle of ibuprofen before going back.

“Here,” she said, shaking out two pills. “These should help the pain enough for you to sleep. But you need to tell someone in the morning. This is probably an ear infection, and you’re going to need actual medicine to- what?”

Marshall was giving her a weird look. He was smiling.

“Nothing,” he shrugged. “Thanks.”

She found herself smiling as well. “Well, you’re welcome. Um, do you mind if I crash in here tonight? Lucas is passed out on our floor.”

Marshall snorted, and then winced when the sudden movement hurt his ear. “Yeah, obviously.”

Corrine couldn’t help but kiss his cheek before she started making her own makeshift bed. “Feel better.”

“I think that I will.”
