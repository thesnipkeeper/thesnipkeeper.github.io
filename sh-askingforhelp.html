Asking for Help

Jace had a habit of hiding injuries. He constantly argued that it wasn’t a big deal, that he used an iratze and it’d heal in no time.

And he was usually right.

But it didn’t stop Alec from worrying when he saw Jace wince when he thought no one was looking. He’d been acting off all day, quieter and less arguing with Alec’s way of doing things. Alec thought it was suspicious, that he was up to something, but no. He was just in pain.

Alec was a mix between concerned and annoyed. Concerned because his brother was hurting, annoyed that he hadn’t acknowledged it once.

Jace squeezed his eyes shut for a moment. He was breathing unsteadily, which went well with the strange glazed-over look in his eyes.

He was going to say something, Alec decided. Not as Jace’s brother, but as the head of the institute and his boss. (he would make an effort not to use that exact phrasing as the conversation probably wouldn’t go well from there.)

So when everyone was walking off to do their jobs after the briefing, Alec grabbed Jace’s arm and pulled him aside. He tried not to think about how strangely easy it was to move him against his will.

“What’s up with you?”

Jace’s entire being turned defensive. “What do you mean ‘what’s up with me’?”

Alec sighed. Jace’s boss, not his brother... he needed to do this the right way or it wasn’t going to go anywhere.

“You’ve looked like you’re in pain all morning. Did you hurt yourself in the field yesterday? Because you should have at least told someone if that was the-”

“I’m not injured,” Jace stated. The determined look on his face remained for a few seconds as Alec stared at him for an explanation. It quickly faded, like whatever was hurting him was persisting as he argued. The crease between his brows disappeared and he sighed, crossing his arms over his chest. “It’s stupid. I’m... fine.”

Fine, huh? Alec wanted to snap at him, but he managed to keep his cool and calmly say, “I need to know what’s going on with you if I’m going to send you out on a mission.”

Jace huffed, a flash of pain on his face. “I-”

“Jace.”

“It’s my ear okay?”

Alec blinked. “Your ear...?”

“It hurts,” Jace hissed quietly. “It might be infected. But that’s all it is, and I’m fine to go out in the field.”

“You’re sure?”

“Yes!”

And Alec decided to trust him.

-----

Jace was not fine.

Alec walked into his office only to find two of his siblings already in there. Jace looked... frustrated. Sick. Izzy was in the middle of feeling his forehead when Alec walked in.

“Jace has a fever,” she said, to which Jace just groaned.

“It’s nothing, Alec. She’s just overreacting. Ow!”

Izzy showed no remorse for smacking him. “It’s not nothing if it’s a fever. You wouldn’t have agreed to come here with me if you were feeling fine.”

Alec sighed and went to lay a hand on his brother’s forehead. It was warm, a low-grade fever at least, and the fact that Jace was seething with frustration and yet still letting this happen was enough in itself to tell him that he wasn’t feeling okay.

“He got dizzy in the middle of training,” Izzy explained.

“Did you take anything yet?”

Jace sighed. “I took some mundane pain relievers this morning, but they might have worn off by now...”

“I’ll call Magnus. I’m sure he can do something, at least help with the pain.”

“You don’t have to do that,” Jace said quickly, shaking his head. “Look, okay fine, I’ll admit that I’m not feeling my best, but it’s not that big of a deal. You definitely don’t have to bother Magnus when he’s probably busy working on some overpriced potion.”

Alec smiled fondly at that, because Jace was probably right. “Okay. Well, if you want to take the day off, you can, or-!” He started before Jace could object. “Or you can just do desk work for the day. Those are your choices.”

The last thing Alec expected was for Jace to take a sick day, and of course he chose not to.

With his brother safely (grumpily) at a desk to observe security footage, Alec went back to his office sat down to do his own work.

Alec took a bite of the sandwich he’d brought up from the cafeteria as he filled out the latest mission report. He must not have heard the door open, because suddenly his sister was in front of his desk with her own food. She sat down and sighed.

“How is he?”

“Sick. I think he’s skipping lunch.”

Alec sighed. “I don’t know why he doesn’t just take a day off.”

“You know exactly why, Alec,” Izzy said in a heartbeat.

Ans she was right. He did know why. They, along with most shadowhunters, were raised to be strong, intimidating, to be able to take care of themselves with no problem. To not let something simple like a mundane illness effect them.

Their family was the best example of the Clave’s style of upbringing. The Lightwoods. Of course their parents loved them and cared for them, but they weren’t the only adults in their lives, training them and teaching them.

It wasn’t until they met people like Clary and Simon that any of them realized how strange and messed up their childhoods were compared to those of mundanes. It wasn’t until they met them that they learned it was okay to show some sort of vulnerability. So really, the three of them had been getting collectively better at it.

Alec and Izzy took a few minutes to chat about upcoming missions and perhaps gossip about a couple of unpleasant members of the Clave before they both had to go back to work.

-----

Jace was grumpy.

Alec was sure he knew why. He was sick, hadn’t eaten, and one of those two things alone was enough to put any shadowhunter in a bad mood. He didn’t push anything, just sat some pills and a bottle of water on Jace’s desk and watched him take the medicine after he walked away.

A lot of good that did, apparently, as Alec heard from someone that Jace had thrown up minutes afterward. They mentioned it with a chuckle, assuming Jace was hungover or had eaten some bad food.

Jace returned to his desk of exile looking out of it and dizzy, his face red and his eyes hazy.

Alec and Izzy exchanged a look and silently decided not to say anything.

“Come in,” Alec mumbled. He’d been fixated on his work for a while, determined to get done on time so he could be home on time. When he finally looked up, he smiled. “Hey Jace.”

Jace nodded minutely and sat down at the desk. “Um.”

Alec raised an eyebrow. He looked tired, worse than he had earlier. Alec wouldn’t have even given him the option of working if he looked like that this morning.

“You okay?” Alec asked after a few seconds of Jace’s silence.

He hesitated, very un-Jace like. “Do you think Magnus would still be willing to come here?”

Concern, heavier than he’d felt all day, settling in him. Since when did Jace ask for help?

“I’ll text him now.”

“Thanks,” Jace nodded. “Um, don’t tell anyone.”

“I wouldn’t do that,” Alec assured.

He stood up and led Jace to the couch in his office. “Lay down until he gets here?” Much to his surprise, Jace complied without a problem. It was a true testament to how he was feeling that he wasn’t fighting Alec on every single thing.

Laying down was a problem, it seemed, as Jace’s ear started hurting tenfold the moment he tried to. His face was still red, and when Alec felt his forehead, it was far warmer than it had been when he checked earlier.

Jace let him sit next to him, though. Let him drape a throw blanket around his shoulders and rub his arm as he tried to breathe through the pain and possible nausea.

“Heard you threw up earlier.”

“Fuck,” Jace muttered. “How many people know?”

“Not sure, but I don’t think anyone particularly cared. You know, that’s around the time that you should come to me and tell me that you’re too sick to be working.”

Jace sighed heavily through his nose. His jaw was clenched through a wave of pain. “I’ve gone out in the field with the flu, Alec. I don’t think a little earache was going to be the end of me.” Alec decided not to argue that it obviously wasn’t ‘little’.

Alec blinked. “When did you have the flu?”

Jace’s eyes widened. “Uh. Don’t worry about it.”

Magnus portaled in just in time to save Jace from further interrogation. The warlock’s expression grew surprised and a bit grim at the sight before him.

“I hear you’re not feeling well,” Magnus said, approaching Jace cautiously like a wounded animal that could snap at him at any moment.

Jace sighed. “Thanks for coming, Magnus.”

Magnus’ expression warmed. “What exactly are your symptoms? It’ll make it easier for the magic if I know what I’m doing with it.”

“Um,” Jace cleared his throat. “Pain. Dizziness.”

“And,” Alec added. “He threw up earlier and he’s also running a fever.”

Magnus blew out a breath. “Oh boy, that’s a lot. Alright, this is going to feel strange at first, but it should hopefully make things a little more tolerable.”

Blue magical energy sparked at his fingertips as he waved his hands as if to gather it, and then hovered them over Jace.

Alec felt some of the tenseness in Jace’s body disappear. The pained crease in his forehead went away, along with the small shudders from chills he’d been denying before Magnus arrived.

Magnus smiled proudly when he was done. “How does that feel?”

Jace breathed a sigh of relief. “Better. Thanks, Magnus.”

Magnus squeezed his shoulder and turned to smile at Alec. It was as if even he knew what a big step asking for help was in their family.

“No problem. I know it doesn’t fix everything, but I did my best. It’ll wear off in about twenty hours, though, so have Alec bring me here if you’re still feeling bad by then.”

“Thank you for what you did for Jace today,” Alec said as they pulled the blankets back together.

Magnus scoffed. “It was nothing, you know that. He should have said something sooner, but I’m glad he did at all.”

Alec nodded. “Honestly, I was surprised when he even admitted anything. He’s so stubborn.”

“Mm,” Magnus hummed. “Good thing I don’t know anyone like that,” he said, biting back a grin.”

“Okay, well he admitted to going on a mission with the flu before this, so I’m not that bad.”

Magnus giggled and got under the covers, Alec following. “I seem to remember a certain day when you went out with food poisoning.”

Alec made a noise of disgust at the memory of puking in the middle of an alley a foot away from a demon corpse. “Yeah, okay. Yeesh, that was a rough day for everyone involved.”

“You’re telling me.” Despite the discussion they just had about Alec’s last bout of food poisoning, Magnus still seemed to gravitate toward him in bed until they were cuddling. He kissed Alec’s head, and Alec hummed happily.

“So maybe Jace and I both have things to work on.”

“Maybe. To be honest, though, I think we all do.”
