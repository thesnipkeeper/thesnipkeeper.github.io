Gettin It

There was one thing David was looking forward to after work, and that was spending the night with his boyfriend. The two of them had been so busy lately they’d hardly had a chance to... connect, and it was clear that they were both getting antsy. Even just spending time together would be enough.

The time finally came where David didn’t have to help Alexis with singles week and Patrick didn’t have baseball practice. They were going to close a half hour early, grab some food, and go to Patrick’s apartment.

So, of course, this was the day that David woke up with the cold from hell. He thought he felt a little weird the day before, but attributed it to just needing some sleep.

He spent the work day hiding in the bathroom to blow his nose and being casually ignorant every time Patrick tried to kiss him. Halfway to closing, his voice took on an awful nasally sound as his sinuses congested. It was almost a relief when Patrick called him out.

“I think the new sweaters should go on this side, snf! so if we can just move these to the other end,” David mumbled, carefully moving the folded sweaters.

Patrick nodded and started to help. “That’s a good idea, actually... hey, are you getting a cold?”

David froze at the sudden change in conversation. “Um,” he sniffled and gave his nose a quick rub. He needed a tissue again, for the thousandth time today. He nodded slowly and closed his eyes for a moment as he answered. “Yes, I think I am.”

Patrick breathed out a sigh. “So that’s why you haven’t kissed me. I was worried that I did something.”

“Oh God,” David dragged a hand down his face, feeling guilty for making Patrick worry. “No, no everything’s fine, my body is just full of germs right now and...” he stopped himself and gave his boyfriend the most pitiful look. “I was going to tell you, later. We can  have a date night later, right?”

Patrick looked disappointed but nodded. “We don’t have to do anything if you’re not feeling up to it.” He cleared his throat. “But, and I don’t want to freak you out, we did kiss yesterday. And the day before that. So if you’re afraid of getting me sick, I think that ship has sailed.”

David grimaced. He was right. He was always telling Patrick that people are contagious before they realize that they’re sick and that’s why he doesn’t share food and drinks with many people.

“So what you’re saying is...” he gestured for Patrick to continue as his snot-fogged brain tried to process their conversation.

Patrick nodded slowly. “What I’m saying... is that I want to be with you, David.”

“Are you sure, though? Because I don’t want you blaming me in a few days and saying I talked you into this and-”

David’s crazy rambling was cut off when Patrick kissed him. It was a good kiss, a long kiss. David hated that he had to pull away, gently setting a hand on Patrick’s chest to signal him to move away. Patrick took a step back just in time to avoid getting sneezed on.

He chuckled. “Bless you.”

“snf! Thank you,” David muttered, dragging the back of his wrist under his nose. “snf! I- I need to...” he didn’t bother to finish, instead simply gesturing toward the bathroom where the dwindling tissue box was with one hand and covering his runny nose with the other.

Patrick nodded in understanding, soft smile on his face that turned David to butter.

“Hope you’re still ready for tonight!” David yelled from the bathroom as he... fixed his snot situation.

“Can’t wait!”

David rolled his eyes. Tonight would be just fine, even if it would definitely result in germs being spread.

-----

Patrick turned to his husband, who had been sniffling for the past half hour without acknowledging it. He assumed that it was just because they had come inside from the winter air, but that didn’t seem to be the case.

David had been a little quiet all day, and it made sense. He’d been working hard the past week and was probably getting a little burnt out.

That was why Patrick suggested they go see a movie in the first place. Something relaxing but still fun for them to do together. He hadn’t said anything, but David was obviously not in the mood to go anywhere.

The movie didn’t start for another few minutes as people shuffled into the small theater.

“Are you getting a cold?” Patrick asked as David sniffed and wiped his nose on his sleeve. Things must have been dire, then, because David usually refused to abuse his sleeves like that.

He paused rubbing at his nose to look at Patrick, side-eyed like a guilty puppy. “Maybe,” David said slowly. “But, I mean, it’s just- it’s nothing.”

Patrick frowned. “Okay... if you’re sure.”

“I am. But um...” David sighed. “You wouldn’t happen to have any tissues on you, would you?” he drew out the question as if he felt deeply ashamed for even asking

It’d be a ridiculous question if it wasn’t allergy season. Patrick’s allergies weren’t terrible, but he did keep a few tissues or pilfered napkins in his pockets in case he was struck with a runny nose at an inopportune moment.

Sure enough, he had a few crumpled in his jeans pocket. They were clean, but David was so quick to accept them that that didn’t seem to matter to him at the moment.

Patrick caught a glimpse of the liquid at the base of his husband’s nostrils before David separated one tissue from the rest and tried to discretely dab away the wetness. It didn’t look like it helped much, and Patrick felt a twinge of sympathy for him. There was no way David was every going to blow his nose in public. Hell, it took him months to do it in front of Patrick, and only came about when he and Patrick were sharing a cold and were both so miserable they abandoned etiquette around each other.

Patrick wrapped an arm around him as the lights dimmed, pressing a quick kiss to his temple that had David making an annoyed sound that insisted I’m fine.

David obviously was not fine. The only thing worse than having a cold in a public place was having a cold in a quiet public place, and David was struggling to handle his running nose. He sniffled desperately, pressing the tissue to his wet nose several times. He was breathing through his mouth now, obviously needing to blow his nose. Patrick could see tears built up in his eyes from the congestion as well. He looked awful.

Patrick decided enough was enough. There was no way that David was enjoying the movie, or even able to pay attention to it. It wasn’t that good, anyway, but the Elmdale theater didn’t have many options for them to choose from.

“I’m kind of hungry,” Patrick whispered, catching David’s attention from his dripping nose. “Why don’t we just go grab food and head home?”

David’s eyebrows turned up guiltily, looking like he wanted to argue. Instead, he closed his mouth for a stuffy sniffle that didn’t seem to do anything to clear his sinuses, and nodded. “Yeah.”

“Yeah?”

“Please.”

He led David through the tiny theater lobby and out to the parking lot. David immediately blew his nose when they were finally in the car. He breathed a sigh of relief afterward.

Patrick chuckled. “Feel better?”

David nodded. “So what was that about food?”

“Yes, right, you choose.”

David’s face lit up and then morphed into suspicion. “Isn’t it your turn, though?”

“You’re sick, so I’m graciously letting you decide.”

David grinned, and then wrinkled his nose with a sniffle. “That burger place down the street.”

Patrick sighed heavily, knowing that David had been dying to go there for a while, but Patrick hadn’t been a huge fan.

“What? You said I get to pick! I’m sick, I could waste away any day now!”

The two of them ended up laughing as Patrick started the way to the burger joint.
