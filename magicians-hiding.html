Hiding

Eliot goes to his room early. He's felt off all day, but now his stomach is beginning to complain about all of the alcohol and junk food that he's had. Not a good sign. He likes to think he's known for his exceptionally strong stomach. Everyone gives him strange looks as he heads to the stairs.

"Where are you going so early?"

"That's no one's business," Eliot tries slyly, but he just sounds tired. It doesn’t matter, they know not to bother him. Probably.

He climbs into bed, trying to ignore the uneasiness in his stomach.

Eliot stirs awake with a groan. It's still dark out, and the red numbers on his clock tell him it's only three in the morning. The house is silent - everyone must have gone to bed. He doesn’t have a chance to wonder why he's awake.

In his sleepy state, Eliot whines and curls up, rubbing his sour stomach. He should get up. He never does. Vomit rushes up his throat and cascades down his chin, his bed, onto the floor. Something catches in the back of his throat and suddenly he's throwing up again. The bitter taste of alcohol burns his throat and makes him gag.

He has to be sure he's done before he strips his bed and removes his shirt. It's sweaty, and spotted with puke. Eliot bites back tears as he inspects the ruined fabrics. He isn't sure why, he just feels terrible and he can't muster the strength to magically clean anything, so he wipes up his mess, covers his bed the best he can, and goes to sleep.

-----

"Can you open the door? Please?" Quentin huffs at the closed, warded door in front of him. Eliot had locked himself in last night, rumors spreading that he was dead. Todd dispelled them when he confessed he heard Eliot throwing up early in the morning, and now - knowing Eliot's self-destructive tendencies - they had a whole new set of concerns.

"Why me?"

Margo rolls her eyes. "You gonna make me say it?"

Quentin shrugs.

"He opens up to you, Q. Literally."

So here he is, continuously knocking until he can annoy his friend enough to-

"Oh God. You look like shit," It comes out before he can stop it, but he means it.

Eliot, peaking out, sighs irritably. "What do you want, Q?" His speech is slurred the way it does when he's tired, or really, really high. His mouth forms a thin line as Quentin scrutinizes what he can see of his friend. The dark circles under his eyes, greasy, unkempt hair, but most of all - is Eliot wearing a hoodie?

"Just… making sure you're not dead, I guess. You're not, right?" It's a joke, which is why Quentin worries when Eliot hesitates.

"I'm… not dead."

"Well, Margo and I were worried. You usually spend Saturdays, uh…"

"Drunk?"

"…Yeah." Quentin raises an eyebrow. "Are you? Drunk, I mean?"

Eliot's eyes close slowly, and he leans his head against the frame of the door as if he can't keep it upright another second. It’s kind of adorable. "I wish."

Quentin bounces on his heels, fidgeting awkwardly before finally, "Can I come in?"

A dry laugh huffs out of Eliot. "Q, trust me when I tell you that you don't want to come in right now." and the door closes.

Quentin rolls his eyes. "You're being dramatic, Eliot," he says to the door. "I'm not leaving until I know you're okay. I have, like, no plans ever so I can stand here bugging you all weekend."

He hears footsteps coming back toward him, and Eliot seems to simply unlock it and turn the knob, leaving the actual opening part to Quentin. By the time Quentin walks in, Eliot's scrambling to grab a bin by his bed and starts to heave. He urps some cloudy water and continues to heave as if he can't stop even though his stomach is empty.

"Oh, El…" Quentin coos as he looks at his friend, and then the rest of the room.

It’s a mess. Clothes and bedding are piled in the corner of the room next to the laundry hamper and a subtle acidic smell finds itself to Quentin's nose. The bed is a mess of tangled blankets, and Eliot sits on the edge, hugging the bin and gagging uselessly.

"Jesus Christ, you should've-" Eliot gags, Quentin continues. "You should've told someone."

"No point in that, is it?" His voice is hoarse and painful now. He looks up with tear-filled eyes, face red from the force of vomiting (or trying to). Quentin wanted to say that he's never seen his friend look so awful, but he'd be lying. Eliot's kind of a mess. "Actually," he stops to spit into the bin, before grimacing at the contents and setting it next to the bed. "I'm pretty sure I'm very contagious, so if you don't mind…" he tries to wave Quentin away.

Quentin crosses his arms. "I'm not leaving."

"Q," Eliot growls with a tired eyeroll. He pulls his legs onto the bed and struggles to get the under the tangled mass of sheets and quilts, more covers than he usually has on his bed. "I don’t- ugh, what the fuck?"

"Here, let me help…" Quentin says softly, approaching slowly at first, but Eliot doesn’t fight him. He lies back with reluctance and allows Q to tuck him in. Still adorable, he thinks, as Eliot rolls over and miserably hugs a pillow to his abdomen.

"Leave," Eliot looks up, resembling a sick puppy, "please."

"You've never been this eager to get me out of your room before."

"Well," He starts matter-of-factly "this is the stomach bug that's been going around campus, and I was trying to quarantine myself before you showed up." Quentin just rolls his eyes at the pointed look, because Eliot doesn’t exactly look threatening at the moment. "Do you know what it looks like when the entire cottage is sick at the same time? Because I do, and it's not pretty. Margo will agree with me."

Admittedly, the whole building catching a stomach bug doesn’t sound fantastic. But there's more to it, because when is Eliot courteous of those around him?

No, he's just self-destructive, and prideful, and doesn’t want help even when he's drowning. They both know that.

Quentin sighs, adding his weight to the bed as he sits down. He runs a hand up and down Eliot's back, and realizes he feels warmer than usual. Eliot lets out a low whine as Quentin's cold hand presses against his cheek.

"Okay, you definitely have a fever."

"No shit."

"Come on… what can I do?" Eliot doesn’t respond. "Fine. How about I start on laundry."

Eliot's eyes widen in panic, and he sits up. "Do not do that.."

"I don’t mind-"

"Do you know how many things I puked on last night?" it starts as a warning, but Eliot's voice is filled with defeat by the end of the sentence.

Quentin stops dead in his tracks and turns, giving a rare and dangerous look. "Why didn’t you get someone? Jesus… if you were that sick…"

"I could handle it."

"Obviously not!"

They stare, challenging each other for a good few seconds before Eliot flops back down and Quentin shakes his head. "Whatever."

It's a simple spell to clean the bedding and clothes. Quentin can't help but wince at the stains before he makes them disappear. There's a sweet aroma of rosewater engulfing the pile by the time he's finished. Eliot's asleep by the time he finishes.

-----

"How is he?" Margo asks, flipping a magazine.

Quentin shrugs. "He's got some kind of stomach virus."

"Oh, Jesus. I thought he was just-"

"That too, I think."

He can't quite figure out the look on Margo's face, but he knows the two of them well enough. She's had to have seen him at his worst, right?

Eliot doesn’t come out of his room for the rest of the day. He's asleep or maybe trying to every time Quentin or Margo check on him, huddled under blankets, or having kicked them in a pile at the end of the bed. Quentin covered him when the chills returned and he was too out of it to cover himself back up.

He brushed Eliot's cheek with the back of his hand, feeling the heat roll off of him.

-----

"Oh shit okay, uh..." Quentin jumps off the bed and rushes to the other side where Eliot is gagging painfully into a bin. He coughs up a mouthful of yellow fluid, his stomach having emptied a while ago. The only thing he can do while Eliot is dry heaving is drape a blanket around his shoulders to keep him warm.

It doesn't stop, even when Eliot's stomach is worryingly empty and he's unable to keep down water. He ends up walking to the infirmary to pick up an electrolyte solution that they were giving to students.

"If he doesn't keep this down, you may want to bring him in." Professor Lipson said in passing as she tended to a number of other infected students. The outbreak must have started with the herbalism students, as most of them are from the treehouse they shared. Quentin has vague memories of Eliot hooking up with a nature student.

So Quentin leaves with a bottle of unappetizing-looking enchanted liquid that's supposed to help ease inflammation in the digestive system (but only if it could stay in the body long enough).

Eliot raises an eyebrow at the stuff. "That looks terrible." His voice is completely hoarse from throwing up all day, hair messy from sleep and facial hair growing out.

"Yeah, I know," Quentin says in a 'yes and?' voice. "But it's going to make you feel better. You're gonna get... I don't know, dehydrated or something."

Eliot's lip curls in distaste before he takes the bottle from Quentin's hands.

"Ah... drink it slow," Quentin adds as Eliot inspects the stuff inside.

And he takes one big sip. He looks as if he's about to gag, and Quentin can't blame him, but he manages to swallow it.

"Holy fuck that is the most vile thing I have ever tasted in my life."

"Yeah... Lipson told me not to ask what's in it. But it should help."

"Is it medicine?"

"More like a magical electrolyte solution with benefits?"

-----

Quentin is rubbing Eliot's back and feeling his neck for the fever as he gags into a waste bin. Nothing's coming up, not really. Sometimes bile, if Eliot can manage it, but the stuff Lipson gave them is long gone. He didn't keep it down nearly long enough.

He's looking pale and fragile and... Well, before he just looked sick, but now he looks like a corpse.

"Jesus."

Both Quentin and Eliot look up to the source of the voice. Margo leans against the doorway. She looks more upset than she usually does, a look she only ever gives Eliot.

Eliot goes to say something, at least he looks like he's going to, but he just ends up heaving urgently into the bin in his lap.

"Honey," Margo says softly as Eliot spits out leftover bits from his last bout. "You look awful."

"Okay, rude." It'd be funny if his voice wasn't so torn from throwing up non stop.

"Margo's right. We should think about getting you to the infirmary."

"No! No, I don't-"

Quentin interrupted him with an exasperated scoff. "I mean... you're dehydrated, and you haven't stopped puking all day, and Jesus, Eliot, you're scaring us."

Eliot had the decency to look at least a little guilty.

Margo scoffed. "Let's go, El."

"You two don't get it."

Oh, Quentin 'got it' just fine. And he wasn't letting Eliot have his way. From the look on Margo's face, she wasn't planning to either.

"Look, if you won't go for yourself, go for us."

Eliot looked up at Quentin, and then Margo, eyebrows knitted guiltily as he weighed his options. They gave him a look to ensure he knew he had none.

-----

Eliot made an impressive way on his own before he finally had to rest and ask for help from the hovering Margo and Quentin. Well, Quentin was hovering, and Margo was amusedly watching his drunken walking.

Professor Lipson caught sight of the three of the and quickly began ordering people to help.

"Bed 3 is vacant!" She told a healing student.

With the help of said healing student, they got Eliot onto the hospital bed. He looked even more pale and sickly in the light of the infirmary, and he allowed himself to be helped.

They went to work putting him on an IV and asking about his symptoms. Quentin felt a soft hand rubbing his arm and turned to find Margo with a reassuring look. He knew he shouldn't be worried about something as minor as a stomach bug, but seeing Eliot attached to machines and looking like a corpse was freaking him out. He wondered if Margo was as freaked out as he was, and just better at hiding it.

Eliot's eyes widened from the foreign metal object in his mouth that was the thermometer. The student hummed thoughtfully at the reading.

"How do you feel?"

"Awful."

Quentin chuckled.

"Nausea? Chills?"

"Mhm," Eliot is already looking like he wants to fall asleep, and soon his eyes are closed. Still, the student continues asking questions.

"Have you kept any food or water down?"

"N-no, he hasn’t." Quentin answers, absently running his fingers through Eliot's hair. "Not even Lipson's stuff."

The student doesn’t look too worried. He nods, "This bug is a bad one. It's probably a good thing you brought him in."

Quentin looks down at his friend. He's in a feverish sleep, and doesn’t look too comfortable in the strange bed. Margo stands on the other side, holding his hand.

"We should let him rest, I guess?"

Margo nods, and they head out.

-----

After a day of obnoxious healing students and no privacy, Eliot is eager to get out of the infirmary. He's aware no one will be at the cottage at this time of day, it's only ten in the morning. So, he plans to stroll in and take his free day off of class to do nothing but relax and maybe drink what alcohol he couldn’t have when he was sick.

Eliot ends up lounging on the couch and falling asleep instead of anything else. He only wakes up to the sound of the door creaking open. Classes are done? Has he been asleep that long?

"I really think you should go to the infirmary..."

The familiar voice catches his attention enough to make him look up and see what's going on. Margo's walking in with her school bag over her shoulder, but someone else's as well. A brown messenger bag?

She holds the door open, and in walks a positively green-looking Quentin. He walks slowly, keeping his head low and his arms wrapped around himself. Eliot knows that look very well. His little friend is about to have an awful few days

"I'll be fine, I just need some... Rest. Maybe."

Margo scoffs. "'Rest.' You've already hurled twice."

Fuck.

"Oh, Q..." Eliot stands and walks over to them. Quentin looks startled but relieved to see him up and walking around. "I got you sick." The back of his fingers brushes against Quentin's warm cheek, feeling the budding fever that'll no doubt make him utterly miserable later.

Quentin leans into the touch, but only slightly. He doesn't seem prepared to say or do anything.

"I'd hate to cut this short, because it's nice to see you alive, El, but I don't think he should be far from a bathroom right now."

The two of them help Quentin upstairs, or watch him very carefully as he unsteadily makes his way up on his own while insisting he doesn't need help.

After the slow trip to his bedroom, it startles them both when Quentin bolts for his bathroom. The sounds of his stomach contents splashing into the toilet has Eliot's sensitive stomach turning in sympathy. Margo pats him on the back as if to say 'it's okay if you need to go,' but he doesn't. Quentin retches painfully and lets out a breathy whimper that has Margo rushing in to make sure he's alright.

"Q?"

"I'm good..."

Eliot can't help but huff out a laugh.

"I'm gonna get you some water, okay sweetie?" There's something so special about hearing Margo talk to someone else like that and Eliot not get jealous. It's because, he realizes, Quentin is the only one he talks to like that as well.

Margo emerges from the bathroom and grabs a water bottle sitting on Quentin's desk before going back in. "You'll probably want to rinse your mouth when you're done."

"Thanks Margo," and it's so strangled and pained that Eliot wants to go in there and hold him until he feels better, but he isn't sure he has the energy to take care of anyone right now.

-----

Eliot smiles sadly at his friend, watching as Quentin shivers desperately and struggles to get his blankets around him. Eventually, Eliot intervenes and tucks him in and it's rather adorable the way Quentin just accepts it. It doesn't stop the chills wracking his body.

Without hesitation, Eliot crawls into the bed behind him and wraps him in his long limbs, hoping to warm him a little.

"th-thanks. Where's Margo?"

"She went back to class. You're stuck with me," Eliot murmurs into his hair. "Sorry for infecting you."

"I mean, you did warn me not to come in."
