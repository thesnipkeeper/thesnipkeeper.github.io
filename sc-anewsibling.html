A New Sibling

Alexis shudders. That should have been the first sign. She’s never cold!

And now her nose is running, and her head hurts, and for once, she doesn’t feel like being at a party right now.

Jocelyn decided to throw a town-wide Halloween party at the cafe, and Alexis was thrilled to be able to dress up and have fun.

Maybe she shouldn’t have written off every single one of her body’s warnings that she was getting sick.

She inconspicuously presses one of the cafe napkins to her nose and sniffles when it does little to nothing. Her nose is starting to get sore, too, which wouldn’t be such a problem if she wasn’t worried about it becoming noticeable. Ugh, and along with her watery eyes, her makeup must look awful.

Usually she’s in the middle of the action at parties, drinking and having a good time and entertaining everyone else with his beauty, but tonight it hurts to talk. It hurts to do anything, really.

She shivers again, sniffles again, and then sits on one of the stools at the counter and pouts.

“Having fun?”

Alexis turns to see Patrick with his cute smile, wearing some dollar store sunglasses that he bought for his and David’s Blues Brothers costumes.

“Um, yeah!” She says, trying not to make her voice sound as scratchy as it feels. “Are you having fun, Patrick?”

“Oh, yeah yeah, it’s a pretty nice party. I’m not used to you sitting on the sidelines, though. Everything okay?” Of course it’s Patrick who can tell that something’s wrong. She loves him, but she doesn’t need to be found out right now.

“Yeah!” She says, maybe a little too quickly. “Yeah, everything’s like, totally fine.” Alexis goes to pull her hair behind her ear, and realizes too late that she’s wearing an extravagant headband for her goddess costume that makes her little nervous tick impossible.

Patrick nods, but in the way he does when he doesn’t actually believe someone. “M’kay, well can I get you anything? Some snacks or drinks, or...” he trails off with a shrug.

Alexis shakes her head. “No, I’m-” fuck her nose itches. She tries to scrub at it, but it’s not enough to smush the tickle. She sneezes, sudden and high pitched and enough to raise Patrick’s tiny eyebrows. She doesn’t like that knowing look on his face one bit.

Alexis sniffles and wipes her nose with the abused napkin in her hand.

He clears his throat and takes a step closer. “You know, there’s been a little bit of a cold going around.”

She growls under her breath, and finds herself shivering afterward. “Okay, can we like, not talk about that right now? I’m fine, Patrick, jeez.” She thinks it’s funny that she does find herself talking to him like another sibling. Even if she’s annoyed with him right now.

Patrick is silent for a few seconds before he asks, “Do you want a ride back to the motel?”

Alexis sniffles pathetically and nods.

“Alright. Here,” suddenly something warm is being carefully placed over her shoulder and Patrick isn’t wearing his jacket.

“Awww,” she says. “You’re sweet.” She goes to boop his nose, but thinks better of it and sighs.

He chuckles. “Any time. I’ll just let David know I’m leaving.”

She smiles as he walks away, realizing that she indeed has another brother. A much nicer one.
