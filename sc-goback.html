Title: Go Back
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Alexis has the flu, David's worried, and Patrick thinks it's cute
Word count: 600

Go Back

David opened the door with a smile. "Hi, um, come in."

"Hey," Patrick greeted. "Are you… ready to go? The game starts in fifteen minutes." As he walked in, he realized the curtains were shut and not only was Alexis home, but she was in bed.

"Yes! Yes I'm almost ready, I just need to get my shoes on, and my jacket, and…" David turned to his sister.

He caught on immediately. By the tissues scattered on the bed, the various bottles of medicine on the nightstand, and the flush on Alexis' face, he could see that she was sick. David fluttered around, moving a cup of water an inch closer to her and fussing with the blankets when they didn't need to be fixed.

"Okay, don't get out of bed while I'm gone."

"Okay, but what if I have to pee, David?"

David growled under his breath. "Fine, but be careful!" he hissed. "I don't want to come back and find you bleeding out on the ground."

"Oh my God, David…" was the muttered reply as Alexis turned over. "Ugh, I'm cold again."

"Is everything okay…?"

David nodded quickly. "Yeah, everything's um, everything's fine."

Patrick didn't say anything as David finished getting dressed and grabbed his bag. Before he joined Patrick, though, he went back to his sister.

"Here, look at me." Alexis did, with a begrudging noise. David laid a hand across her forehead and sighed. "Your fever's up again…" He bit his lip. "You have to wait another three hours before you can take anything else. For the love of God, please don't overdose on medicine."

Patrick could tell his mind was elsewhere as they got in the car together. For one thing, he was quieter. It was otherworldly being in a car with David Rose, and everything being silent.

"So is… Alexis okay?" Patrick tried.

“Hm? Oh, yeah, she just has the flu and mom and dad are gone for the weekend so I’ve been making sure she doesn’t like, die or something.”

Patrick nodded. “David, if you want to go back to the motel...”

“What? Oh my God, no, this is our date night. Why would I want that?”

“You’re worried about Alexis.”

“I’m not,” David scoffed. “I’m not worried. Alexis doesn’t need me to take care of her. She’s like thirty years old.”

He shrugged innocently. “I think it’s sweet.”

David made a face and huffed. “It’s fine.”

They pulled up to a stop sign and Patrick took the opportunity to turn to him and smile.

David looked at him with a grimace. “What?” he asked, shaking his head wildly.

“We can go back.”

David went through an entire face journey before sighing heavily. “It’s just... the last time she was this sick I ended up driving her to the hospital. Granted, the doctor said that it probably wasn’t necessary and she would be fine, but...”

Patrick turned a corner that would lead them back to the motel. “Your big brother instinct is one of the sweetest things about you, you know.”

“Ew. Stop.” His eyebrows raised when he saw that they were no longer going to the baseball game, but David didn’t say anything.

“I’m just saying, not a lot of people care about their family as much as you do.”

“Excuse me, have you met my family? They are all terrible people.”

Patrick chuckled. He wasn’t exactly in a place to agree with him, he was about to be part of the family, after all.

He pulled into the motel parking lot and smiled at David. “Well, that was a good game, huh?”

David laughed. “Thank you, dad. Great joke.” He bit his lip for a moment, eyes darting around like he wanted to say something. “Um, thanks for understanding. Sorry for dragging you all the way here.”

Patrick shook his head and leaned in to kiss him. “Go take care of your sister.”
