Title: Will Be Fine
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Alexis has to step in when David needs help and Patrick isn't around
Word count: 1.4k

Will Be Fine

It was the morning after David and Patrick’s weird little fight, and Alexis was feeling particularly unhelpful.

Her father had reminded her countless times over the past day that this wasn’t about her, but she begged to differ. Her brother was currently a huge wet blanket on her mood, just lying there and crying and sleeping and- ugh. It was so annoying. She hated it.

Staring at the lump of blankets that was her brother, still in bed at eleven in the morning, she waved her hands through the air in hopes her nails would dry faster and she wouldn’t have to be there anymore.

“David,” she tried. The lump didn’t move. She really hoped he was alive, because they didn’t need another corpse situation in the motel. “Do you want to like, go to the cafe with me?”

“No.”

Well, at least he wasn’t dead.

“Um, are you sure? We could get like, waffles, or...”

“Alexis,” he growled. “Can we not talk about food right now?”

Alexis scoffed. She was trying to help, and that was how he talked to her? “Fine, David. Ugh!”

David huffed and she watched the lump curl up into a big, pitiful ball.

Whatever. She rolled her eyes and went back to drying her nails. Said nails were about ready to go to the cafe when Alexis was startled by her brother jumping out of bed, quicker than she’d ever seen him move in her entire life.

It made a little more sense when she heard her brother vomiting. He didn’t even close the door! And of course that was when Johnny came in to investigate the sudden movement.

“What’s going on?” He asked, brows furrowing.

“Um, David’s puking?”

Johnny became aware of the sound, and winced. “Well, he did eat... well, everything, last night.”

Oh, that was true. No wonder he didn’t want to talk about food.

“You okay in there, David?” their dad called unhelpfully.

“Peachy!” He bit back.

He wasn’t peachy, because he was in there for a half hour, and more infuriating - Alexis realized - was that she hadn’t left yet because of her idiot brother. Apparently she was worried.

She was scrolling through nonsense on her phone when he emerged, red faced and tired. He immediately flopped onto his bed once again.

“You ate that much last night?” Alexis asked.

David gave a long groan. “Apparently,” he muffled into his pillow.

“So that’s like, a total no on lunch at the cafe, then?”

“Alexis, I swear to God.”

She smirked, feeling better now that she knew he was alive and conscious. “Cool. Well, I am going-” she didn’t even get to finish her sentence before he gasped and ran past her into the bathroom once again, hand clamped over his mouth.

Just like that, he was throwing up again. She muttered a few colorful words under her breath, looking up at the ceiling as she did. Because, as much as she did want to escape this dreary and tragic situation, she also felt the obnoxious need to... be there for her brother. She almost missed when the two of them didn’t give a shit about each other. At least that was easier.

Except they did give a shit about each other, or at least he did. She huffed and dragged a hand down her face as she remembered all the times he saved her from getting held hostage by whatever palace she was in that day.

Maybe she owed him, or whatever.

So, accepting that her nails had been dry for a while, Alexis got up and put in one of David’s favorite movies. Pretty Woman. Alexis didn’t really see the appeal of anything that was made before the twenty-first century, but he had a thing for early-nineties Richard Gere and that was something she could support.

And when David came out looking even more exhausted than he had before, a hand over his stomach and a miserable expression on his face. He sat on his bed with a sigh, looked at the TV, and blinked.

“Wha- you’re watching Pretty Woman?”

Alexis shrugged. “I mean, why not?”

“You don’t even like this movie. And I thought you were going to the cafe?”

“I mean, I was, but like, do I really need to, you know?”

David raised an eyebrow. “What?” he said, shaking his head as he did.

Alexis groaned. “I didn’t want to leave you alone, okay?!”

He widened his eyes and furrowed his brows. “I’m not alone! Mom and dad are in the next room!”

“Yeah, but mom and dad aren’t-” weren’t what? But David seemed to know what she was trying to say, because he sighed and nodded.

“Fine,” he said, waving a hand before getting back into bed. “Whatever.”

She sat on her bed and decided that maybe she could be okay with spending a day at home. David still didn’t look good, even if he wasn’t puking all the time.

After the second time he told her to pause the movie and went to the bathroom, he returned with a different expression. He looked freaked out, and she could hear his breath getting quicker like it did before an anxiety attack.

Alexis sat up, suddenly on high alert. “What’s wrong?”

He shook his head, taking loud, shallow breaths through his nose. She watched him dig through the drawer in their nightstand and pull out a white case. “I feel weird,” he said, sounding near tears. “I have like, a really bad feeling.”

Alexis watched him sit on the edge of his bed and take his temperature, breath still ridiculously fast. Oh, no wonder he was throwing up so much. He was sick.

He looked at the reading and immediately started crying. Hand over his mouth as tears started dripping down his face. Alexis had never seen him react this badly to having a fever. Sure, she’d seen some exceptionally freak-outs, but not ones this... tearful and quiet.

She wasted no time moving to his bed and wrapping her arms around him. He smelled kinda gross, and he was uncomfortably warm, but she just sort of held onto him as he cried.

“It’s fine, David,” she finally tried.

“It’s not fine,” he said with a sniff. “My boyfriend probably hates me, and now I-”

“Okay, okay...” Patrick didn’t hate him, of course he didn’t! But saying that probably wouldn’t help anything right now. “Just... deep breaths, David,” she instructed. She stopped hugging him and instead rubbed his back as her brother tried to do as he was told. It didn’t work out well, because each breath would just end in a pathetic sob.

She hated this. She hated this so much. Part of her wanted to blame Patrick for what he did, but it wasn’t all his fault. Some of it definitely was, but...

David sniffed and dragged the sleeve of his hoodie (oh God, he was wearing a hoodie. That should’ve been the first sign that something was wrong) over his face. His sleeve-covered hand didn’t leave his face, instead remaining over his mouth.

Alexis immediately took all hands off of him with a grimace as he rushed back to the bathroom once again. She sighed, listening to her brother vomit for the thousandth time, and got out her phone.

Alexis: david’s sick

Patrick: sick? how sick?

Alexis: like gross pukey sick

Patrick: what can I do?

-----

Alexis felt her phone buzz and didn’t even have to check it. She looked toward the bathroom, where David had remained for a good while, and left the room.

She could have cried out of gratefulness when she saw her favorite button with grocery bags in-hand.

“Is he okay?” Patrick asked as he handed her the bags. They had to do this quick, the last thing David needed was another trigger.

“I mean...” she sighed. “No, not really.”

Now Patrick looked like the one who was about to cry. His concern did make her hate him a little less, though.

“He’s just like, upset, Patrick.”

He nodded, lip quivering before biting it. “I know. I-”

“Oh God! I wasn’t trying to make you cry!” She said in distress.

He gave a teary chuckle and shook his head. “No it’s- it’s not you.” He sniffed, rubbing the tears from his cheeks. “Jeez, just everything’s been making me cry since last night.”

Alexis frowned, and gave him as much of a hug as she could with grocery bags in her hands. “I hope you two work things out,” she said quietly. “I kinda think you need each other.”

He laughed again. “I think so too.”

Alexis gave him a quick boop on the nose before disappearing into the motel once again. She couldn’t help but smile.

Things might have been shitty right now, but she had a feeling that they would be just fine.
