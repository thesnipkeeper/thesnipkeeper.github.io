Fandom: Magicians
Summary: Quentin just wants to go to bed
Word count: 2k

Bedroom

"Freshers' flu" is what they called it. First week of school, everyone was stressed, trying to get their shit together, in a new environment and around new people. Getting sick was the last thing Quentin was worried about between studying for tests and researching the beast.

Alas, his worry was misplaced.

"Quentin!" He looked up, belatedly realizing that Alice had been whispering to him for a while.

"Oh…thanks," he whispered back. He sniffled shyly and accepted the proffered packet of tissues. He didn’t waste any time pulling one out to wipe his nose. He was trying not to be too loud during class, but his nose kept running and he couldn’t breathe and-

Alice offered a sympathetic smile, "Don’t mention it." he wasn’t sure where the two of them stood quite yet, but it was reassuring to know that he at least had an ally. A friend? A cute friend.

He usually tried to head to the library and do his work as soon as class was over, but today he spent the class looking forward to going back to bed. The buzz on his phone told him neither of those things were happening.

'Party at the cottage. Be there or be a fucking square.'

Quentin sighed heavily. He was ninety-percent sure he was coming down with something, but Eliot was… insistent. And enthusiastic.

'Raincheck? Now's not a great time' he thought, and then added, 'Also, it's Monday' Quentin really didn’t want to, not today. Especially when Eliot and Margo would probably end up quarantining him anyway.

"So you finally caught it, huh?" Alice smiled a little.

Quentin shrugged, stumbling over his words. "I mean… yeah, I guess so."

"Well, you're in for a shitty couple of days."

"Fantastic."

His phone buzzed once more and Quentin had to stifle a groan as he pulled it out.

'Nice try. Let me rephrase: participate or we kidnap you in the name of having fun for once in your miserable life.'

He certainly had the miserable life part right.

-----

Eliot's grin grew as he opened the door to the physical kids college. "You actually made it!"

Margo, as always, appeared at his side. "I told you he would."

"Did I have much of a choice?"

Eliot chuckled at his muttering and wrapped an arm around his shoulders. People around them were drinking and dancing, and some poor souls were trying to study in the middle of all the chaos. Quentin cleared his throat, wincing at the growing soreness. He felt chills under his skin that wouldn't go away despite his heavy jacket. He should've just said no and hid at the library until it was safe. On the other hand, maybe Eliot was right. Maybe he could use some fun.

"Come along, my young grasshopper. I'm gonna make you our signature cocktail." Eliot chimed, steering him toward the plethora of drinks and bottles. The thought of alcohol made Quentin uneasy, but he didn't refuse when Eliot pressed a strange blue drink into his hand. The taste made his stomach turn, bringing yet another symptom to his attention. His nose was so stuffed he couldn't taste anything besides the alcohol.

"Hm," Eliot said. "You don't like it."

"N-no. It's really good." Quentin took another nauseating, tasteless sip to prove his point.

"No, it's fine," Eliot said with a dramatic sigh. "Not everyone has the refined taste that I do."

Quentin offered a sheepish smile, not ready to reply with anything to entertain Eliot's drama. "Did you... Need me for something?"

"Hm? No no, just for you to live it up. Margo said you were looking stressed, and Q let me tell you, no one should be stressed on the weekend."

"But unwinding on a Monday is fine, right?"

"Exactly. Life is short." He punctuated the sentence by lighting a cigarette.

The smoke was inhaled before Quentin realized it. He hastily set his drink down on the end table and buried his face in his sleeve to muffle a cough. It went on long enough for people to start looking at him, and Eliot was watching him with mild concern (interest? Amusement?) in his eyes.

"Sorry," Quentin croaked out as he cleared his throat. "My throat..." He trailed off.

Eliot nodded, picking up his drink and pressing it to Quentin's lips before he had a chance to protest. "Here, drink."

Quentin sputtered and coughed but the liquid did seem to calm his throat a little.

-----

Quentin sniffled. His nose was sore from wiping it all day, and probably making him look more diseased than he already did. Part of him wanted to go to bed and say fuck you to all of Eliot's efforts, but that was just the thing - Eliot was making an effort. An effort to help him unwind, meet people, have fun. He wasn't just being difficult, he was trying to help in his own special way.

"You don’t look like you're enjoying yourself."

Quentin blinked up at the tall figure towering over him with a newly refilled drink. Eliot looked legitimately disappointed - probably in himself for having an unhappy party-goer.

"It's fun, really!"

Eliot didn’t believe that for a second. Quentin sighed.

"Sorry, I swear I'm not trying to bring the mood down. I just- just-" he hurried to get a tissue out of his pocket before muffling a sneeze into it. Oh God, he felt awful. He shivered pitifully, still not having taken off his jacket.

Eliot's eyes widened in realization, and his lips stretched with a knowing smile. "Ah," he said with a nod. "Freshers' flu."

Quentin nodded. He rolled his eyes at his own awful situation as he wiped his sore nose yet again. He sniffled, and an irritated cough followed. He shouldn’t have been surprised when Eliot hummed and pressed the back of his hand (the one not holding his drink) to Quentin's forehead. His hands were soft and cool, and Quentin leaned into the touch.

"Okay… you're hot, and not in the good kind of way." Eliot's brows furrowed.

Quentin huffed out a laugh, but had to hastily pull away from Eliot to muffle another cough. Eliot frowned, looking around and weighing his options.

"I'm just going to upstairs and lie down…"

"Ah, yes, the room full of angry magical bugs."

Quentin groaned, letting his head fall back on the sofa. He'd completely forgotten about last night's adventure, that involved summoning bugs from another world and then not knowing what to do with them. He and Eliot had to get them somewhere quick, and Quentin's room was as close as they could get. He remembered now, sleeping on the couch last night.

Eliot sighed. "Alright, you look like a sick puppy and I'm not about to let you walk out that door without anywhere to rest."

Eliot grabbed his arm and pulled him off the sofa. The movement was too quick, and Quentin's vision swam as he stood. He could feel Eliot's steadying hands as he regained his balance, and then let himself be led. It wasn’t until they reached the stairs that Quentin realized what was going on. He skidded against the floor as Eliot started ascending the staircase, losing his grip on Quentin's hand in the process.

"Wait, what's- where are we going, Eliot?"

"You'll see. Come along, child." he said with pointed hand waving until Quentin finally relented, realizing he was too tired to argue.

Eliot once again took his hand, long fingers entwining with Quentin's as they walked down the hallway of the cottage. He stopped at a door and turned the knob before gesturing dramatically into the room.

"What's this?" Quentin asked, coughing into his collar.

Eliot snorted. "Uh, my bedroom. Go on, lie down, take a nap. Sleep here all night if you want, just know that I'm a cuddler."

Quentin blinked. He wasn’t feeling well, maybe his brain was slowing down. What was happening?

"Wait, I'm sorry, what… what's going on right now?"

Eliot giggled, and then cooed, cupping Quentin's face and stroking his cheek with his thumb. Quentin had to admit, it'd been a long time since someone had shown this kind of physical affection toward him, but it was different. It was Eliot. "You're sick. I'm giving you my bed to sleep in, because you need the rest and I feel a little guilty for dragging you here and putting alcohol in you." He leaned in and Quentin caught the slightest bit of hesitation before pressing a kiss to his forehead.

"If I sleep in your bed, you're going to get sick for sure."

"Mmm'nope. Wrong." Eliot pushed him into the room a little more.

"Wrong? What do you mean 'wrong', Eliot?"

Eliot sighed, with a eyeroll to boot. "Okay, you want to know a secret? Freshers' flu only happens to you first-years because… well, it's freshers' flu. It's a one-time thing that you build an immunity to after you contract it the first time."

"Are you… telling me I have the Brakebills equivalent of chicken pox? How is that even possible?" Even as he spoke, he let Eliot help him out of his jacket, and watched as the older hanged it with his own collection.

"Decades ago someone thought it would be funny to create a magical illness to dick with the first-years," he patted the edge of the bed and helped Quentin remove his shoes. "And it never went away."

"Shit…" Quentin breathed out. This flu, how shitty he felt - was him being pranked by some douchebag from the 1950s. Anytime he forgot how fucked up this school was, there was always something to remind him.

Eliot pulled the covers back and this time Quentin didn't fight it. The bed looked too inviting and his fatigue was taking a toll. He climbed in and let Eliot tuck him in like he was a kid or something. It was almost sweet. Even more so when the older pressed one more kiss to his forehead.

Eliot's voice softened, "I was serious when I said you could stay the night, you know."

Quentin nodded, a small smile. "Okay."

-----

"Well you look like shit." Was the first thing Eliot said when he returned to the room. Quentin had been asleep for a couple hours after an uncomfortable while of tossing and turning. He was still trying not to infect Eliot's bed despite the fact that he wasn’t supposed to be contagious.

"Thanks. Thanks for that," he muttered, pulling himself up as Eliot settled next to him. Q could feel a layer of sweat covering him. He went to sleep freezing, and woke up drenched.

He nearly jumped at the coldness on the back of his neck, then forehead. Then he realized how nice it felt.

Eliot gave a low chuckle. "There we go..." He said softly, running the cool cloth down Quentin's face and neck. "Y'know, Q, You're fucking adorable when you're sick."

Quentin scoffed, eyes still closed as Eliot cooled him down. The coolness disappeared, cloth losing it's effect, and Eliot folds it and puts it off to the side. Quentin hated that he couldn't appreciate this moment more, not when his brain was so fogged with fever.

"So," Eliot began, flopping against the headboard and lounging enough that Quentin doesn't feel as uncomfortable in the foreign bed. "There's a spell that can ease some flu symptoms..." His hands came toward Quentin, and then stopped. Quentin wasn't sure he'd ever seen that kind of hesitation from the upperclassmen. Quentin offered a slow nod, curious as to what was going to happen. "It's only temporary, like medicine," Eliot continued as he laid one hand on Quentin's forehead and the other on the back of his neck. "But you'll feel well enough to sleep through the night."

Quentin couldn't help but close his eyes as the pressure in his head dissipated, and the nagging ache in his muscles that made it impossible to sit still. His congested eased the slightest bit and he could finally breathe.

His eyes fluttered open when soft lips pressed against his temple.

"Feel better, Q. Get some rest."
