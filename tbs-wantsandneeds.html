Wants and Needs

Another shitty day in another shitty hotel room.

He wanted to leave, but only when Damien was out. And by that time, what would he do? Where would he go? He didn't even know where he was half the time, just let Damien do his thing until they got another room to live in.

Not to mention the fact that his body still didn't work as it should have. He still ached, his legs could only take him so far before he needed Damien for support. He was weak.

The whole thing just got shittier when he woke them both up in the middle of the night with a coughing fit.

Shifting in the other bed, a grumpy groan.

"Y'okay?"

"Yeah," Mark said instinctively, before actually thinking about the answer. "...No, I don't know. Think I'm getting sick."

"Shit."

He scoffed. "Yeah." Maybe it was a fluke. Maybe he'd wake up in the morning and his scratchy throat and headache would we gone.

They fell asleep.

They woke up. Or- Mark woke up.

Damien was shuffling quietly about the room.

Mark let out an involuntary groan as he opened his eyes. He was tempted to go back to sleep, but Damien had already heard him, and he wanted Mark awake. So Mark stayed awake.

"Good morning, sunshine," Damien said through a mouthful of toothpaste. He disappeared into the bathroom to spit.

"No, it absolutely is not," Mark decided.

"How you feelin'?"

"Like I got hit by a truck and then trampled by livestock." He draped his arm over his eyes to shield himself from the morning light trying to make its way through the hotel curtains.

"More than usual?"

Mark nodded wordlessly.

"Jesus, that sucks."

"Yes, Damien. It sucks."

"You... I dunno... need anything?"

Mark shook his head. "Think I'll just try to sleep-" He was cut off by a coughing fit, wet and crackly, like it's pulled directly from his chest. He sat up urgently, desperate to get a breath in between the ripping coughs.

"Mark?" Damien stood a few feet away, mouth still covered in toothpaste. To the guy's credit, he looked concerned (which wasn't a look Damien wore often).

Mark rubbed the irritated tears from his eyes and sniffled. His whole body ached. Not the soreness that came from being in a coma for two years, but from illness ravaging his body and rendering him more useless than he already was. “I’m good, I’m good,” he rasped.

He was not good.

All he’d done all day was cough and sneeze and nod off and shiver and... yeah, he didn’t feel well. It didn’t help that his legs didn’t work great on a good day, let alone when he was clearly feverish.

“I’m getting lunch,” Damien said after a while. Was it lunch time already? “What do you want?”

Mark groaned and pulled the covers over his head. “I’m not hungry.” Just the thought of eating made him aware of nausea he hadn’t even realized was there.

“Really?” Damien scoffed. “Okay then.”

“Damien, wait...” He peeked out, and then coughed. “Can you get some like... I don’t know, medicine or cough drops or something?”

Damien shrugged. “Hell, I could get ya the prescription stuff if you wanted me to.”

It was tempting, but in the end Mark shook his head. “Over the counter is fine.”

“You’re feeling really shitty, huh?”

“I mean, what do you think, Damien?” he growled. “God...” he flopped back on the bed with a dramatic flourish. “On second thought, just kill me. Just end it right here.”

Damien chuckled and sat on the edge of the bed. He patted Mark’s leg, a look of amusement on his face. “I’ll get you the good stuff.”

Mark listened to him leave, and let out a big sigh. There was a relief that always washed over him whenever Damien was out, like a heavy weight he didn’t know was there was taken off of his mind and letting him think for himself. Clear. Sober. Of course, that all changed whenever Damien came back and reminded him that he didn’t want to leave.

Mark wasn’t sure where he could even go right now. His body barely worked on a good day, let alone now.

But, he had to pee. And possibly vomit. So Mark let out a loud groan and shoved the blankets to one side. Cold air immediately assaulted him and he shuddered, teeth chattering. Jesus, even the air hurt, his skin was so oversensitive right now.

His legs were ready to give out by the time he got to the bathroom. He managed to take care of things, but then there was the nausea nagging at him and showing no intention of going away. Well, it wasn’t like he had anything better to do, or the strength to make it back to bed.

Mark sat on the edge of the tub and waited.

At first he thought maybe he wasn’t going to throw up at all, maybe it was just his brain thinking of the worst possible thing that could happen when he already felt like shit.

Maybe he should try to make it back to bed. He could wait for Damien to come back and help him, or he could... fuck, crawl or something. He probably didn’t have the strength to do that either.

His stomach growled angrily before he had a chance to think about it anymore. He swallowed compulsively.

Just let it happen, right? That was what you were supposed to do?

A sickening burp bubbled up from his throat, and what followed was a rush of last night’s dinner of cheeseburgers and a shake. Neither tasted as good coming back up. The sight of it had him throwing up again. If things couldn’t get any worse, his nose was dripping all over his face as well, because obviously he needed more slime on his face.

The actual puking only lasted a few minutes. After that he was able to clean his face a little bit, and finally blow his nose. But he still found himself dry heaving, perhaps just because he kept thinking about what just happened. The muscles in his abdomen ached from the force of it all, and his throat was sore.

Water... oh wow, he hadn’t had any water recently. God, why didn’t he think to do that?

The sound of the door being unlocked caused him to freeze. He hadn’t bothered to close the bathroom door, not that it mattered.

“I’m back! Hope you’re dec- Mark?”

“In here,” he rasped.

Damien threw a variety of plastic bags on one of the beds and appeared in the doorway of the small bathroom. “Christ,” he muttered, shoving his hands in his pockets and leaning against the frame. “You look... I mean, Jesus.”

“Mind helping me back to bed?” Mark asked, ignoring Damien’s exclamations. “I- my legs are kind of tired.” his everything was tired.

“Yeah, sure.” Damien draped his arm over his shoulders, just like they’d done many times before since he rescued (kidnapped? Mark wasn’t sure) him from the AM. “Okay, you’re like, really hot,” Damien said as he helped him back into bed.

Even in his miserable, sleepy state, Mark smirked. “Well gee, you’re a great guy and all, but-”

“Oh ha ha, I mean that you-”

“I know what you meant.” Mark huffed, struggled to get himself thoroughly burrowed under the covers once again. “Everything feels terrible.”

“I bet,” Damien scoffed. “Listen, I got you some of the heavy cough medicine. Should help you sleep tonight. And me, so I don’t have to listen to it.”

Mark groaned in relief. “Bless you.”

“Mhm, look over here for a sec.” Mark did. Damien sat on the edge of the bed and went to feel his forehead.

Mark wanted to resist at first for some reason, feeling weird about it, and then he didn’t want to resist anymore. He did make a noise of discomfort, though. “Your hands are freezing!”

“It’s cold as fuck outside, okay?”

“Ugh.” Mark turned away from him.

“Here,” the crackling of plastic bags and then one placed on Mark’s bed at his feet. “Do with it what you please. I think I got pretty much everything you’ll ever need. If anything, you’ll get so high on meds you’ll just forget that you’re sick at all,” he chuckled.

“Thanks,” Mark muffled into his pillow.

Things were silent for a few seconds, and Mark wondered if he was expected to say something more. Then Damien spoke, voice softer. “You uh... gonna be okay?”

Mark turned back to him to see... was that concern on Damien’s face? Huh. That was nice. Weird, uncomfortable, but kinda nice. “Yeah, I’ll be fine,” he said, in the same tone he always used with Sam when she expressed worry for his mental wellbeing. “It’s just the flu or something, right?”

Damien nodded, cleared his throat. “It’s just... we can’t exactly go to a doctor.”

“Wait, why not? I’ve been to doctors before. Being Atypical doesn’t always mean that there’s some weird biology behind it.”

“No, but do you want the AM to find you? And your sister? Because that’s what’s gonna happen if we just go walking around giving people our names and-”

“Okay, you know what? I’m still not convinced that my sister is involved in any of this.”

“Oh yeah? Then how do you explain her working for the people who fucking tortured you, then? Huh?”

Mark couldn’t. But he also couldn’t see his sister involved in something so horrible. “Look,” he said, biting back his anger and wondering if that was his want or Damien’s. “I’m tired. I don’t need a doctor right now. I just need sleep, okay? So just- just let me sleep. Please.”

Damien set his jaw, brows furrowed. “Fine. Whatever. I’m just saying that we don’t want to risk making a trip right now."

He was right, Mark didn’t want to.
