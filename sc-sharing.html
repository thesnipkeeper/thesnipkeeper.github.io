Title: Sharing
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David and Patrick are good at sharing early in their relationship
Word count: 1.2k

Sharing

David tapped his foot nervously. He didn’t know why he was nervous exactly, he just saw Patrick a couple hours ago. Of course he was going to show up to a simple dinner date at the cafe.

But then... where was he?

It wasn’t like Patrick was often late, not like David. So either something bad had happened, or Patrick finally came to his senses and skipped town before he and David got serious.

David’s heart rate started to skyrocket at the self-created thought of someone else leaving him, so it was a good thing Patrick finally walked in when he did.

David shook himself out of his stupid thoughts and found himself grinning brightly as Patrick sat down. The grin soon faded when he got a look at his face.

“Wow, why do you look like you haven’t slept in years.”

Patrick chuckled, and David couldn’t help but recoil when that chuckle morphed into a weak cough. “Didn’t get a lot of sleep last night. You look amazing as always, by the way.”

David winced. “Sorry, you know I think you look great. And,” he wiggled energetically in his seat, “It’s nice to see you. Finally. Again.”

“Ah, yes, it’s been so long,” Patrick said, ridiculously dramatic with one of those adorable smiles David loved. “Yeah, sorry I’m late. I laid down to take a nap the moment I got home and I slept through my alarm.” He sniffed.

David narrowed his eyes, causing Patrick to squirm uncomfortably under the scrutiny. He did look like one would if they’d just woken from a nap. David didn’t remember Patrick looking that awful earlier, but then again, the store was so busy today that he hadn’t really had a chance to be close to Patrick, which was why they made dinner plans in the first place.

“Are you okay? Because you look kinda...” he finished his sentence with a grimace.

Patrick sighed, and sniffled. For a moment David thought he was crying, but... no. “I-”

“Oh my God, you’re sick.”

Patrick shook his head. “It- it’s just a little cold!” he said quickly, setting a hand out as if to diffuse a situation that didn’t need diffusing. “It’s nothing, r-hhhh-really-” Patrick had a truly impressive sneeze.

David bit back a smile. There was something endearing about seeing Patrick Brewer with something as simple as the sniffles. He pulled a napkin from the dispenser on the table and slid it over. “Bless you.”

“Snf! Sorry, thanks.”

They ordered their food, and thanked Twyla as she brought it. Patrick sniffled his way through his sandwich and fries.

“You should have said something this morning if you weren’t feeling good,” David said matter-of-factly.

Patrick gave a resigned nod. “I thought I might’ve been coming down with something, but I wasn’t really sure until I woke up from my nap.” He offered a self-deprecating chuckle and then ate a fry.

“Mmm,” David hummed. “Well, I can cover for you tomorrow if you need a day off.”

“David, tomorrow’s your day off.”

“Yeah...” Dave drew out. “But you’re obviously sick.”

Patrick chuckled. “Thank you, but I’ll be okay. I’ll make sure to keep my germs to myself, but it’s just a cold.”

-----

David woke up far earlier than he ever planned to an obnoxious ringtone. It took Alexis chucking a pillow at him to make him realize that it was his phone that was ringing.

“David, answer the fucking phone!” Alexis growled before covering her ears with her remaining pillow.

David groaned and somehow managed to coordinate his hands well enough to slide the green symbol across the screen.

“Hello?”

“David,”

Patrick, this early? David was both happy and bemused. “Hi, what um, what do you want this early?”

“David, it’s like eight- never mind, I-”

David had to pull the phone a few inches away from his ear as Patrick hacked horribly. “I was wondering if you were still willing to cover for me at the store?” he paused to sniffle thickly, David winced. “I’m not feeling too great.”

David was much more awake now at the sound of his business partner’s misery. He sat up and scrubbed at his eyes. “Um, yeah. Are- are you... okay?” He asked cautiously.

Patrick gave a positively disgusting, snot-filled snuffle. “I’m fine, David, I just... I don’t think I should let customers see me like this. I’m probably very contagious.”

“Oh God, yeah please don’t be patient zero.” David threw his blankets back and pulled his slippers on. “I’ve got it. Take care of yourself, please.”

A soft, congested chuckle. “I will. Thank you so much, David. I owe you.”

“Oh, absolutely.”

-----

The day was relatively uneventful.

David spent the store’s hours fixing displays and finding other busywork when there weren’t customers around.

He was eager to close up and pick up some things for Patrick. Mainly soup and tissues. Poor Patrick sounded miserable on the phone, and the idea that he was sick and alone (at Ray’s, no less) made David want to go hold him forever.

Twyla happily handed him his order the moment he walked in, and then David was on his way.

He managed to sneak by without getting spotted by Ray. Oh God, he couldn’t deal with that man right now. David gave a courtesy knock before stepping into Patrick’s room and closing the door behind him.

Patrick looked up from his book with an amused grin.

“Well, hello.”

“Hi. I probably should have told you I was coming, but-”

Patrick shook his head and sat up with a sniffle. He set his book to the side, scooted to the edge and patted the empty space next to him.

“I brought soup,” David said as he sat down, handing the container to Patrick. “Thought you probably haven’t eaten yet?”

Patrick’s smile grew wider. “I don’t think I’ve gotten out of bed today, if I’m being honest.”

“Well then, eat up.”

Patrick leaned over until they were shoulder-to-shoulder as he ate his soup. David would occasionally hand him a tissue when his sniffling became frequent and particularly unpleasant to listen to.

Soon, the soup was all gone.

David wrapped an arm around Patrick’s shoulder and pressed a kiss to his temple with a smile.

“Are you going to be okay if I leave? I’m supposed to meet my family for dinner at the cafe.”

Patrick chuckled. “I’ll be fine, David. snf! Thank you.”

David bumped his forehead against Patrick’s for a moment. He was a little warm, the best thing for him was probably sleep, and David’s presence would just be getting in the way of that.

-----

David woke more sluggishly than usual. His head felt heavy, and his limbs took a bit longer to coordinate.

He didn’t think too much of it until he went to work. Patrick was still sick, but his cold’s disgusting phase was gone enough that he could at least work in the back without customers fleeing in disgust.

David took a sip of his coffee as he booted up the computer. He stopped, cleared his throat. Huh, a little scratchy-

Fuck.

He heard a sneeze from Patrick in the back, and - as if the universe was playing with him - David echoed it a mere seconds later with his own sneeze.

Footsteps came up behind him and arms wrapped around his waist. Patrick propped his chin on David’s shoulder.

“Bless you.”

David groaned dramatically.

Patrick sniffled and hugged him tighter. “We’re getting really good at sharing, huh?”

“Ugh.” David rubbed his nose. “I suppose so.”
