Being a Trigger

He couldn’t stop.

He hadn’t even felt sick until a couple hours ago and now he couldn’t stop puking.

So much for getting a good night’s sleep. So much for sleeping this off. So much for making himself the least of the house’s problems.

Luke moaned, or tried to before he was cut off by another horrible heave, and he was bringing up the remaining contents of his stomach, mostly-digested lunch because he’d skipped dinner.

He heard a knock on the door.

“Luke? I’m coming in.” The tone in Lobo’s voice, the combination of fear and sadness. He tried to clean his face before she entered, but he knew he still looked like shit. “Oh…” she breathed out.

Lobo knelt next to him as he tried to catch his breath.

“I’m not-” but he heaved again. A trickle of bile fell into the water below. She didn’t say anything. “I’m-” he spat.

“Luke,” she said, voice quiet and careful. “It’s okay.”

“I’m sick,” he chuckled. He scrubbed the back of his hand across his chin which was probably disgusting but he wasn’t in a position to care.

Lobo got a look at him and frowned, brows furrowed. She felt his forehead and breathed out what could only be a sigh of relief.

“Your first clue should have been that the door wasn’t locked,” he offered. Although, maybe it should have been. He wouldn't want someone besides Lobo walking in on this.

Lobo scoffed. “Sit tight, I’ll get the thermometer.”

-----

Eli stared at the empty chair next to her for longer than necessary. The early-morning fog made it longer to process what was confusing her about that chair.

No Luke.

She ignored the food on the table. Nothing was new about that except that she was more distracted than anything else.

Finally, "I didn't see him after dinner last night, either," Pearl offered, after his absence had gone unspoken for a while.

"Neither... did I," now that she thought about it. He liked to chill by himself sometimes, that wasn't new, but now it felt weird that he hadn't made an appearance at all.

Everyone had to sit down for meal times. That was the rule. Even Eli followed the rule. Where the hell was he?

A surge of fear that he somehow packed up and left without saying goodbye, without anyone noticing, ran through her. What if he was gone? What if he just decided that he was done, that he was healthy, that he didn't need them anymore?

She shook her head.

She managed to eat a few pieces of cereal, drank some wild-tasting tea, and got up to go in search of her friend.

She caught Lobo in the stairway. "Hey-"

"He's in his room. He wasn't feeling well last night." Lobo sighed, her shoulders falling as she said it. "But I don't know if he wants visitors."

Eli laughed. "Why not?" It was Luke. Of course he wanted visitors. Of course he wanted someone to torture with Broadway musicals or his refusal to read social cues.

Lobo shook her head. "He didn’t want me to tell everyone, alright? But just respect his decision if he doesn’t want to hang out right now. Okay, Eli?”

Eli furrowed her brows, feeling equal parts confused and defiant. “Uh… sure? Fine.” She started up the stairs and then stopped again to turn around. “But he’s okay, right?”

She smiled. “He’s fine."

The way she said that didn't give Eli much confidence.

She always associated the words "Not feeling well" with bad days. The days before she'd get driven to the hospital when her stepmom would tell someone she "wasn't feeling well" or as an excuse when the whole thing first began, when her friends wanted to go out but she hadn't eaten in two days and her head hurt and she hardly had the strength to stand let alone go to a party.

So she imagined the worst for Luke. What if he was regressing? The cheerful asshole who actually wanted to get better, what if he gave up?

She stopped just before his doorway and tried to listen for movement. From that position, a lump of blankets were visible in the bed. She heard a sound, a sniffle, like he was crying, which would be unlike Luke, but then again, she'd only ever seen him when he was on the mend.

"So you're just going to lurk like a pervert, then?"

Eli sighed.

The lump moved as Luke sat up and leaned forward to peek out.

"Knock knock?" Was her sheepish greeting as she leaned against his doorway.

Luke looked all kinds of shitty. His hair was a mess, and he was lacking any of his usual accessories, a hat or a necklace or anything to make himself stand out. A white t-shirt and a pair of sweatpants were in place of the extravagant outfit of a dancer. His face was pale, or paler than normal. Paler than what was healthy.

"Please, come in." He gestured a dramatic welcome. Exhaustion took from the grin that usually lit up a room.

She wrapped her arms around herself and tried not to look as uncomfortable as she was. "Lobo said you might not want visitors."

He hummed. “I... I just didn’t want everyone tripping over each other to get their noses in my business.”

“Because your nose is never in anyone else’s business.”

“You may have a point, but I’d say this is different.”

“Luke, c’mon,” Eli stood up straight. “It’s me. What’s going on?”

Luke looked down at his lap. He sniffled again, his mouth still open like he couldn’t breathe through his nose. He found her eyes once again, though looking weirdly nervous. “I just don’t feel well.”

Eli stared at him to elaborate.

“I get the flu once a year, and it tends to make me very nauseous, and I threw up an awful lot last night. Lobo found me puking my guts out in the bathroom and- God, the way she looked at me, I thought she’d... start crying or something before I told her that I was sick.” He cleared his throat, and then coughed into the crook of his arm when that didn’t work. His voice was hoarse when he spoke again, “Just didn’t want anything triggered by my shitty immune system.”

“So, you have the flu.”

“I have the flu, probably,” he confirmed. “Although, it could just be a really bad cold and my body just gave up, because that could happen. A rexy’s body isn’t known for its strength.”

Eli set her jaw as she thought of what to say or do next. She considered how serious Lobo was as she told her to respect Luke’s decision. How upset Luke looked as he told the events of the night before. The idea that someone thought he’d relapsed hurt him.

“Do you want me to fuck off?”

Luke’s eyes widened with an expression that was indecipherable at first. “I really, really don’t. I’ve spent the entire day being bored as shit...”

She frowned. “But?”

“But I don’t think I’m done throwing up. I don’t want you to- to feel-”

“Jealous?”

Luke laughed at the absurdity of the situation. He was good at that, just taking it all in and accepting it and laughing at the trouble life sent their way. He lifted his shoulders in a floppy shrug.

“I don’t-”

“I know you don’t,” Luke insisted. “But that doesn’t always matter.”

“No, look, I sit with my sister when she’s sick all the time and it doesn’t bother me, okay? If you want me to go, that’s fine, but I should be allowed to decide whether I need to be protected from a fucking trigger.”

He smiled as if that was what he wanted to hear.

“We are two very fucked up people, you know that?”

“Yep. Scoot over.”

Luke does. He smells different, not bad necessarily, but sick.

“You know, you’re almost guaranteed to not vomit if you catch this. Just thought I’d let you know, in case that was what you were hoping for.”

It crossed her mind.

“I don’t throw up easily.”

“Hm,” he hummed. “Learning something new about you everyday.”

Eli snorted.
