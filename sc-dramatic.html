Title: Dramatic
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's sick, Patrick apolgizes for not taking him seriously
Word count: 1.2k

Dramatic

Patrick leans in, only for David to take a step back.

“Uh, can I have a kiss?”

“Oh, better not,” David says regretfully. “My throat’s a little scratchy, and I’d hate for you to get any germs I might have,” he rubs his throat for effect.

“I think I’ll survive.” Patrick leans in again, but David shakes his head and sets a finger on Patrick’s chin to stop him.

“Mm’m. Nope.”

Patrick sighs. “Alright. Are you sure you’re sick, though? Sometimes scratchy throats happen from like... the weather or allergies.”

“Positive.” David assures. “And because I’m so positive, I’d like to take tomorrow off.”

“David...”

“No, you don’t understand! I’m getting sick, Patrick.”

“Are you, though?”

David gapes in unbelief. “Oh my God, really? You’re going to be like this?”

“It’s just that you happen to be getting sick on inventory day so yeah, I’m being like this.” He smiles, because he’s not trying to argue! But David’s not having it.

“Is this because I won’t kiss you?!”

Patrick scoffs and David’s expression gets even more exasperated the way it does when David realizes he’s not being taken seriously, so he straightens his face. “No, David. I just- sometimes you have the tendency to be a little dramatic...”

“Well, yeah!” He flails. “I have a tendency to be dramatic, yes! I mean, have you met my mother?”

“David-”

“But if you said that you were getting sick, I’d believe you!”

Patrick frowns. If he ever mentioned not feeling good, David would be there in a heartbeat to care for him in his own David-ish way. But a scratchy throat doesn’t exactly qualify as being sick.

“And you know what? I will come into work tomorrow if it’s so important to you.” David puts his hands on his hips with a huff.

Patrick waits for a few seconds, watches as David starts to relax his stance, and then quietly says, “I’m capable of taking care of the store if you need a day off.”

David swallows, and Patrick catches the wince that follows. He closes his eyes and shakes his head, wringing his hands under the long sleeves of his sweater.

“Don’t worry about it. Ignore me, you’re probably right, I’m just being dramatic.”

Fuck.

Patrick feels even worse now that David’s not arguing with him anymore. And the worst part of it is that he doesn’t even seem angry. He’s accepting it.

-----

Patrick doesn’t think much of it when David doesn’t show up after ten minutes of the store being open. He still finds himself anxiously checking the time. He doesn’t like how they left things. He doesn’t like that David was so willing to give up on a simple argument, and he doesn’t like that he won.

He takes a swig of his coffee, nervously tapping his foot. David’s coffee is sitting on the counter, a size bigger than usual as some sort of apology.

Patrick doesn’t even think about David’s scratchy throat from last night. He still believes it was just a fluke and an excuse for David to get out of doing inventory.

Until David shows up, that is.

His eyes are shadowed and his nose is red like it’s been rubbed raw. His face is more neutral than Patrick’s ever seen it - tired, and if the way he’s breathing through his mouth is anything to go by, the congestion kept him up most of the night.

The stubbornness in Patrick keeps him from acknowledging his boyfriend’s current state and instead he picks up the coffee.

“Got you something,” Patrick says.

David smiles a bit. “Th-” he stops and clears his throat. “Thanks. That’s really nice of you.”

Patrick can’t help but wince at the sound of his voice. The normal smooth tone when David speaks is completely taken over by something gravelly and thick.

David takes a sip and enjoys the way it feels on his throat. He snuffles, clears his throat again. “Sorry I’m late. My alarm didn’t- well, actually it did, but I kind of ignored it for a half hour? And then Alexis finally threw a bunch of things at me until I turned it off,” he says with smile and a dismissive shrug.

Patrick waits for him to rub this in his face.

He doesn’t. At all. David doesn’t say a thing when he has to blow his nose for the fifth time since he arrived, or when a coughing fit turns into a wheezy fight with his lungs.

Patrick wants to do something, but in order for that he’d have to admit that he was wrong and well... he can’t do that.

So instead, he watches his boyfriend sneeze miserably and then slather hand sanitizer on his hands before going back to work. Is this his way of proving that he was right? Because coming into work when he’s obviously not feeling good is way more dedication than Patrick would expect, even from him.

David catches a sneeze in his hands, and then another, and his eyes are red and watery when he finally looks up. His hands are still steepled over his face and he looks around rapidly as if deciding how to solve his situation.

Patrick swallows his pride to come to his boyfriend’s rescue. He picks up the tissue box from the counter and brings it over to him.

David doesn’t take one at first. Is he really going to be like that? But then Patrick hears the quick intake of air, a hitch in his breath, and David’s eyes close in anticipation for another sneeze.

This one sounds even worse than the others.

David blushes and keeps one hand up to shield his face as he pulls a tissue from the box Patrick is holding in front of him. And then another, and another.

That must be quite the situation.

He spends a good amount of time turned toward the corner of the store as he blows and wipes his nose. Patrick sets the box down and finds a bottle of one of their new lotions particularly interesting until David’s finished. The pang of guilt in his stomach rises all the while.

David could have said something, another part of him thinks. He doesn’t have to be here.

Patrick doesn’t realize David’s done (and that he’s been quietly staring at the floor) until his boyfriend speaks.

“Is everything okay?” David asks with another sniffle.

“Yeah, yeah, I just...” Patrick clears his throat. This is going to be harder than he thought. “Wanted to apologize about yesterday. Sorry I didn’t-” Ugh. Why is apologizing so hard? “Sorry I didn’t believe you. I see now that you were in the right,” he finishes with an awkward chuckle.

David blinks and shakes his head. “I’m sorry, did that just happen? Did you just admit that you were wrong?”

A sigh. “Yeah. I did.”

David grins, looking wildly smug for someone who’s face was covered in snot just a moment ago.

“I believe you now, though, so I can handle things if you want to go home and rest.”

“Oh no, I’m not doing that. Mom and dad are home, Alexis is also working from home today. I’m safer here.”

“Okay David, well you sound a little like a plague victim, so I’m not sure you want to be serving customers.”

David nods. “That’s true, because I feel like a plague victim right now.”

“Why don’t you just go in the back and... work?”

“Good idea.”
