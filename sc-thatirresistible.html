That Irresistible

They’ve slept together almost every night that week, which is why Stevie is a little alarmed to receive a text that says David “can’t hang out tonight”. She immediately starts to wonder if she did or said something to make him hate her. She isn’t usually one to worry about those kinds of things, but maybe something happened the last time that they had sex that... turned him off?

She huffs and rolls her eyes. No, she’s not going to let this bother her today. Since when has a fucking boy been this prominent in her mind? The last thing she’s going to do is give him that much credit.

And it’s not as hard as she thought it would be, to get David out of her mind. She goes on with her day, checking in guests and receiving complaints from those checking out with a straight face and sarcastic remark they don’t catch on to.

Things are okay. Until Mr. Rose asks her to bring towels to David and Alexis’ room.

Suddenly, all of those thoughts she’s been ignoring rush back. She doesn’t want to see David, not right now, not when he just told her that he didn’t want to see her.

But, this is her fucking job. And if she doesn’t do it, one of the Roses will find a way to get what they want, and it’ll probably be by bugging her a lot.

Stevie goes upstairs and grabs a stack of towels. They’re clean - technically - although they seem to have developed a mildew-y smell since they’ve been sitting up there for who knows how long.

She takes a deep breath and knocks on the door of room seven.

Alexis answers the door with a “Thank God, Stevie.” She takes the stack from Stevie’s arms immediately and leaves the door open while she goes to put them in the bathroom. There’s no sign of David, thankfully.

Stevie stands awkwardly for a moment, wondering whether the open door was somehow telling her that she needed to stay for something else, or just a result of Alexis’ forgetfulness. She’s just about to walk away when she hears a high-pitched sneeze and David emerges from the adjoining door a second later with his nose buried in a tissue.

Things suddenly make a lot more sense. And Stevie now has a whole new set of problems.

David pauses in his tracks and quickly stuffs the tissue in his pocket. He clears his throat, takes a few steps toward her and crosses his arms.

“What are you doing here?” He asks, genuinely curious.

“She was bringing towels, David,” Alexis says, emerging from the bathroom once again in an entirely different outfit than before. “Okay, move!” She nudges him out of the way of the dresser where she stuffs her clothes in there without so much as folding them, and David - to Stevie’s surprise - just blinks and indeed moves out of her way instead of biting back.

He shakes his head as if to clear it and walks outside, closing the door behind him. The two of them are face-to-face, and Stevie is suddenly much more uncomfortable than before.

“Um,” he clears his throat again, and swipes at his nose. “I’m sorry about tonight,” he says, rolling his eyes. “I’m kind of sick. I thought it’d be best for everyone if I didn’t... do anything. Anywhere.”

Stevie nods. “Yeah, I see that now. You look great.”

David groans. “Oh my God, don’t. I spent like an hour this morning just trying to look less germy.”

“It didn’t work,” she informs with as much deadpan as she can muster. She bites hard on the inside of her lip as she looks at him up close. His eyes are red (probably something that’s driving him nuts) and his nose is taking on a sore pinkness.

“Thank you, yes, I- hhh... I’m aware of th- hhhh...”

She can feel her heart begin to beat faster as his breath catches, expressive nostrils twitching as he rushes to get the crumpled tissue out of his pocket in time to catch the sneeze.

David groans, eyes even more watery than before as he wipes his nose and sniffles miserably.

Stevie finds herself taking a step back in some lame hope that being a little further away will make her less readable. It probably doesn’t matter anyway, because he seems too preoccupied with his own situation to notice hers.

Stevie crosses her arms over her chest, finding anywhere else to look besides David’s face.

“Hey,” David says.

She looks up, swallowing hard. His nose is a deeper shade of pink now that he’s been rubbing it more.

“We’re... good, right? Because when I texted you this morning, I didn’t mean-”

“We’re good.”

David blinks, taken aback. “Okay, I’m just a little confused because you’re acting really weird, Stevie.”

Stevie scoffs. She uncrosses her arms and shoves her hands in her jeans pockets instead. “No I’m not.”

“Um, yeah you are.”

“How am I acting weird, David?” She asks, basically accusing him of being the crazy one in this conversation when she knows that it is - in fact - herself.

He sighs, shaking his head and waving his hands dismissively. “Whatever. My throat hurts. I’m going back inside.”

Stevie growls inwardly at herself. The... sexual part of her is going to hate her if she doesn’t at least- “David, wait-”

David twists around on his heel and throws his hands out questioningly, looking a mix of annoyed and confused at what is happening right now. “What?”

“Did...” God, Stevie, pull it together. “Did you just not want to hang out tonight because you were, I dunno, embarrassed, or...?”

He raises an eyebrow, sniffling and swiping at his nose once again. “I mean, yeah. I kind of figured it’d save me some dignity if we didn’t do anything tonight. Um, why?” He sniffles again. His nose is clearly bothering him again, he probably needs a clean tissue from inside, which makes Stevie feel both guilty and excited. Wow.

“Well,” she shrugs as casually as possible. “I don’t mind. If you- I mean, if you still wanted to.”

The confused expression on David’s face slowly fades into that stupid sideways smirk that Stevie hates so much, especially when he looks sick at the same time.

“Are you sure?”

“Yeah, why not?”

He narrows his eyes, and she finds herself shifting under his scrutiny. And then his eyes widen for a split second as if he thinks he’s realized something. Stevie really hopes he hasn’t.

“So I’m just that irresistible, huh?” He asks playfully before rubbing his nose again.

Stevie scoffs. “Your nose is running.”

“Ew, oh my God,” he scrubs it, harder this time as if trying to squash a growing itch.

David groans a few seconds later and reluctantly pulls out the damp tissue before pressing it to his face. He sneezes harshly, and Stevie goes back to biting the inside of her mouth. He looks up at her, embarrassed, eyes now red and watery and nose probably equally so. After a few seconds of tending to his nose, though, he gasps, any sign of embarrassment gone from his face.

Stevie really doesn’t like that look.

“Oh my God,” he repeats. “Okay, I might just be assuming things, but-”

“You are!” Stevie counters far too defensively.

David pauses for a moment, closes his mouth for another smirk, and continues. “Am I, though? Because I think I know what you look like when you’re-”

Stevie shakes her head and throws her hands in the air. “Mm’kay, fuck you!” she says as she walks away.

“Yes! Should we say eight-o-clock?”

She huffs and disappears into the motel lobby.

David looks significantly worse when they meet up at the abandoned storage room. His nose looks sore to the touch now, and his voice is hoarse. He wasn’t lying when he said that his throat was hurting.

Stevie suddenly feels slightly guilty. But she knows that he wouldn’t have agreed to spend the night together if he didn’t want to.

Despite how terrible he feels, David still manages to smirk knowingly at her as he enters the room. “So, there’s something about this?” he asks, gesturing to his face. “Is it the fact that I look seconds away from death?”

Stevie shakes her head. There’s literally no reason to hide it, especially not from David who’s probably been with a million people with weirder fetishes. “It’s the sneezing.”

David’s mouth forms a thin line just for a moment before he has to open it to breathe. He snuffles congestedly and nods. “I thought so. Um, so how do you want to do this?”

“Let’s just do this.” David nods. “But don’t...” she sighs. “Don’t... turn away?”

His eyes widen. “Wait, so are you asking me to sneeze on you? Like, during sex?”

Oh God, he’s right. She sounds like a crazy person. “Forget it,” she says, throwing her hands in the air. “It’s stupid. It’s really stupid, isn’t it?”

“Well, no, not necessarily,” David pauses to sniffle again. Where his nose was simply running earlier, his sinuses are obviously swollen now. “I just meant that I’m contagious. Like, super contagious, probably.”

“That’s... fine.”

David chuckles, grins, in that same way that she hates so much. “Okay then.”

She huffs. “Can we please-”

“Yes,” David says with a nod, before pulling her in for a kiss.

They don’t usually actually sleep together. They usually go back to their respective homes when they’re done and then do it again the next night. But... last night was different, and the first sign of that is that David is snoring, mouth wide open.

He sounds completely blocked up, and Stevie thinks his face might be a little red. She would feel guilty, except he made it very clear the night before that he had fun. But I’ve never enjoyed it that much.”)

He smirks. “Mm’kay.”
