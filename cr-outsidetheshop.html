Outside the Shop

There was a nasty stomach bug going around in Zadash and they hoped they weren't around long enough to catch it. Just a few days in town to stock up on supplies and rest was all they needed. 

From what they'd heard, the bug acted quickly. You're fine one minute and puking your guts the next.

Which was precisely what was currently happening to Molly. 

Mollymauk wasn't an awfully talkative person when he didn't need to be, but he was too quiet today. No snide remarks or inappropriate laughter. And then the tiefling leaned on the outside brick of the invulnerable vagrant and unleashed a bout of sickness that had everyone groaning in disgust. Strangers gave a wide berth around him as he convulsed.

He was pale and sweaty, looked like he had been for a long time and they just didn't notice.

Fjord had to act quickly in order to stop him from collapsing in his own sick, and ended up holding poor Mollymauk up while he brought up waves of bile and partially-digested food. 

The others watched with worry frowns.

“Ugh, gross!” someone said, and Beau gave the stranger a look that made them walk faster. 

"Aw, Molly..." Jester frowned.

“Let’s get him inside,” Caleb suggested. 

Fjord nodded and helped Molly regain his balance. “Maybe Pumat has something to help.”

“Ugh…” Molly groaned as they made their way inside. It was empty, thank goodness. 

“Oh my, what do we have here?” Came the deep voice that they all knew and loved. Pumat Sol, goggles on his forehead, shuffled past a couple of his duplicates, immediately realizing that this wasn’t like their other visits. His eyebrows knitted in concern, looking to someone for an answer.

“We were on our way here and Molly got really sick, do you have anything that could help him?” Jester hardly took a breath between the words.

“Well, uh, we don’t have any illness-curing potions if that’s what you’re asking for,” he moved to pull back the curtain to the workshop. “But we have a couch. Why don’t you let your friend lie down for a little bit?”

They led Molly back without another word, and Pumat followed. By the looks of things, he was in the middle of sewing a bag of holding. Funny, to think of a big guy like that sitting at a sewing machine.

Molly looked so out of it and tired as Fjord helped him easy onto the sofa. The thing was old and covered in dust, obviously having been back there for a long time, but it looked comfortable enough. 

Instead of lying down, Molly stayed sitting and hunched over with his arms wrapped around his middle. His hair was damp now, face covered in a sheen of sweat. He swallowed convulsively. 

“Feelin’ a little queasy there, huh? Let me grab something just in case…” they watched him walk off and return with a waste bin. “Must be that flu going around, a nasty thing, hm?”

“Sorry for bringing this into your place,” Fjord said. “We were just about to come in, when…” he gestured to Molly, who by this time was hunched over the bin as it sat on the ground.

“Not a problem. As long as I can help you feel better, Mister Mollymauk.”

“I’ll be fine in just a moment.” it was the first thing Molly had said since he vomited, and it came out scratchy and hoarse. “I just- ohh..”

Everyone winced as he gagged and brought up what was left in his stomach. He really hadn’t eaten much that morning.

“Why don’t we give him some privacy,” Fjord whispered to everyone else. Mollymauk probably didn’t want an audience for this. They made their way into the main shop. “And in the meantime, we should probably make a plan for what to do if Molly isn’t better by tomorrow. Can’t exactly travel when he’s this sick.”

“We’ll figure something out.” Beau was still distracted by everyone going on. 

In fact, all of them were a little freaked out. It was their first time seeing Molly in such a bad way. 

They left the moment Molly was feeling well enough. Caleb washed out the bin without any complaint. 

Molly unconsciously laid a hand on his stomach as they walked. He looked so tired, so worn out, and by the time they reached the inn, he was shivering. Fjord lent him some looser, more comfortable clothes and helped him get situated in bed.

Molly shuddered, pulling the blankets around himself. “Well, this isn’t exactly how we wanted this week to go,” he said with a chuckle. 

“Better that it happened here and not while we were traveling, you know.” Fjord shrugged. He sat on the edge of the bed while Molly sank under the covers and got as comfortable as he could. 

They’d put a bucket next to the bed for him just in case, but it was doubtful that he had anything left to vomit. 

“You need anything?”

Molly sighed and shook his head. “Not unless you have a cure.”

Fjord scoffed. “Can’t help you there, sorry. I’ll be back up in a while. Think you’ll be hungry for dinner?”

Molly’s reply was a halfhearted giggle. 

“Yeah, figured. Get some rest.”

-----

Molly was fast asleep when Fjord returned to their room from dinner. He was exhausted. Today was exhausting. He climbed into bed, deciding he didn’t have anything else he needed to get done, and fell asleep.

Fjord woke to vomiting hours later. But not Molly’s - his own. He threw up over the side of the bed before he realized what was happening. It was a strange sensation puking something other than seawater, he thought to himself before the second wave hit.

Fjord heard little movement from the other side of the room, as he was a little busy at the moment, but he felt a weight next to him and then a soft hand on his back. 

The first thing Molly said when FJord was done vomiting was, “Well, fuck.”

Fjord was inclined to agree. 

They were forced to stay at the inn an extra night just to gather their bearings. Jester wasn’t very good at looking out for sick people, but- well, none of them were. Still, Jester kept them company, and Beau checked in on them every so often.

Caleb was the one checking temperatures and making sure they were alright, though.

Molly seemed to enjoy the human feeling his face for a fever, while it was a little more awkward for Fjord.

“You’re very cool,” Molly said blissfully, nuzzling the human’s hand. 

“No, you’re very warm,” Caleb stated. 

There was something going on there, but Fjord wasn’t in a state to question it. Either way, no matter how much of a mess their group was, they were a good family.
