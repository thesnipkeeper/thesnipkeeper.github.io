Distress

David dragged himself out of the bathroom and flopped onto his bed. He immediately curled into the fetal position and whined loudly in self-pity.

"My stomach is killing me." He needed to be at the shop in a half hour and all he'd done was feel awful and wallow in self-pity.

"Oh my God, just tell Patrick that you can't go into work, David."

"No, never. Because he's going to ask why, and I'm going to have to tell him, and then I'll die of embarrassment and you'll have to keep my cause of death a secret from everyone at my funeral."

Alexis rolled her eyes. She re-dipped her nail polish brush and moved on to her right hand. "Or maybe he'll understand. Everyone gets-"

David sat up and pointed a finger at her threateningly. "Don't say it. Don't you dare."

Alexis sighed. "Just tell him you're sick, David. I don't see what the big deal is."

"Mhmm, and what if he like, shows up with soup or something and finds out?!" David groaned at his unfortunate situation and grabbed the nearest pillow to hug, because life really wasn't fair. He couldn't help but wonder if this was the universe getting back at him for being a piece of shit.

"Okay, David, then why don't you just tell him that you have diarrhea and you haven't been able to leave the bathroom all night?" Alexis shrugged.

David knew his sister was biting back a smirk without even looking at her. She was enjoying this. Even when he broke the news to her that she'd have to use mom and dad's bathroom until he was better and their toilet was disinfected.

"Well, it's not like it's only been d- um, lower intestinal issues." He sighed and stuffed his face in the pillow with a muffled, "'m just sick. Would you tell Ted if this," he used one hand to lazily gesture to himself, "was you?"

"I mean, yeah. He's already combed lice out of my hair, so I think we're past that boundary."

David's face screwed up in distaste. "That's somehow more disgusting."

Alexis scoffed. "I'm just trying to help."

"Well quit it. I can still work, okay? Just with more... breaks. Oh, God..." His stomach started to cramp once more, and he knew he didn't have time to put off getting out of bed. He threw the pillow away from him and laid a hand on his stomach as he headed for the bathroom yet again.

"Whatever you say, David!" Alexis called. He heard her flip on the TV and turn the volume up an extra notch.

It was the agreement they'd come to when the other was... taking care of business, because sharing such a close living space didn't make that sort of thing easy and the last thing either of them wanted was to hear what the other was doing in the bathroom.

David felt significantly more awful when he emerged from the bathroom for the millionth time. He was thirsty, and achy, and cold but sometimes hot, and...

Tired.

So. Fucking. Tired.

Because he'd been up half the night running to the bathroom with cramps, constantly unsure of which end it was going to come out of this time. He would cry if he had the energy. His stomach wouldn't stop hurting.

"Seriously, David," Alexis looked worried. He must be dying. "Just call Patrick and tell him you can't come in." She blew gentle puffs on her freshly-painted nails as he made his way back to bed.

He sighed. "Fine." And reached for his phone on the nightstand, a difficult feat when he never wanted to move ever again. He found Patrick's name in his contacts and took a deep breath as he hit 'call'.

He ended the call the moment it started to ring. "Maybe I'll just go in and see how it goes. I'll just get dressed, and... I don't know. It'll be fine, right? A little stomach flu never killed anyone... right?"

Alexis narrowed her eyes in thought. "Mm. Not sure that's true. David-"

Bzzzzzt. Bzzzzzt.

The name Patrick <3 appeared on his phone screen.

"Fuck..." David took a deep breath and brought it to his ear. "... Hello?"

"Hey, did you just call me?"

"What? Oh, yeah, um. I just..."

Alexis gave him a stern expression. Tell him, she mouthed.

"Um, Patrick, I'm not feeling great and I don't think I'll be able to make it to the store today and I'm really, really sorry."

"You're sick?"

David's heart tightened at the concern in his boyfriend's voice. "Yeahhh..."

"Aw, sorry. Yeah, I can handle the shop without a problem. Feel better, okay? I'll stop by after closing with the most amazing soup you've ever tasted."

David swallowed hard at the idea of eating anything in the near future. "Oh, honey, you don't have to do that..."

"No, really! It clears the sinuses right up. Did wonders last time I was sick."

David couldn't help but smile. Patrick was so eager to help. He couldn't just... tell him!

"I... thank you, Patrick. I love you. Like, a lot."

"I love you too. Take it easy, alright?"

David dropped the phone on his bed and sighed as dramatically as possible. "He's going to bring soup after work."

"Awww."

"No, not 'awww', Alexis. He can’t be here right now! What if- what if I- what if he…???"

"Oh my God, calm your tits, David. Just don't barf it up and you'll be fine."

"Oh God, I'm totally going to barf it up."

Alexis hissed in sympathy. "Yeahhh… " She turned around in her chair and looked him over. "Are you going to be okay when I go out? I can always stay and hang out with you…"

David scoffed, eyes closed as he tried to will the stomach pain away. "No no, don't go using me to try and get out of your plans." He reluctantly opened his eyes to situate himself under the covers.

He could just hear her pout. "No, I mean, I'm totally looking forward to hanging out and stuff, but like, if you need me to stay here and make sure you're okay..."

David must have been really sick, because he honestly was considering asking his sister to stay home and keep him company. Being sick was always so lonely before they moved there, and he still wasn't quite over it.

But also, he knew Alexis wasn't exactly looking forward to hanging out with Ted's friends. She was looking for a way out, he wasn't about to give it to her.

"Go on, leave me to die. Natural selection is merely running its course…"

Alexis scoffed. "If you insist." She checked her nails one last time before grabbing a jacket.

"Have fun," David said, mustering up just enough energy to smirk.

Her mouth gaped in realization. He wasn't dead set on her staying or leaving, which made teasing her all the more fun. "You know there's nothing stopping me from just like, staying here and caring for my poor, sick brother."

A chuckle. "Maybe, but-"

"My weak, pathetic, somehow older brother…"

"Okay okay, don't go too far!  I'm sick, I'm emotionally sensitive right now," He huffed.

Alexis just scoffed and rolled her eyes. She pulled out her phone, probably texting Ted to let him know she was on her way.

"But seriously… it's probably best for you to be gone for as long as possible before you have the chance to catch this." He sighed. Sharing germs between them didn't used to be an issue. Their house was huge, they hardly saw each other. But now they shared a full living space. "Maybe you should stay at Ted's tonight. I don't…" he sighed, feeling disgustingly serious. It was a lot easier to be sarcastic when he wasn't seconds away from passing out. "I don't want you to get sick."

Alexis gave a non-committal hum. "We'll see how the game night goes. I might need you to call me later. Look for an SOS text."

"Well at least this time I won't be rescuing you from a hostage situation."

"Like I said, we'll see." She checked her hair in the mirror one last time. "Byyye!"

David basked in the silence that filled the room once the door closed behind her.

For two minutes. It was quiet, almost eerily quiet. Normally he would be watching TV, but the remote was so far away and he was so weak.

His stomach wasn't telling him to immediately evacuate its contents any time soon, and David was exhausted. Maybe he should be taking the opportunity to get some sleep while he could. Who knew when he'd get a nap next, or whether he'd get some sleep that night without having to get up to rush to the bathroom.

Though his hair was still damp and sticky with sweat, he could feel himself starting to shiver again, just like it’d been all night. Hot, then cold, then hot, then cold...

David huddled under the blankets, wrapping them tight around himself and ignoring his stomach’s angry sounds as apparently moving in bed was too much for it. He got the last blanket situated, one from Alexis’ bed that she let him borrow during his bout of chills early that morning.

He looked around the room one last time to make sure everything was good. There was an old cleaning bucket by his bed Stevie had delivered when she heard he was sick. He didn’t plan on using it, God no, but it offered some peace of mind in case he woke up with nausea.

Just a little nap until Patrick showed up, to make him look more alive. David took a deep breath. He settled back, closed his eyes, and let himself drift to sleep.

He stumbled half-awake to the bathroom and put an arm on the wall over the toilet to steady himself in lieu of kneeling in front of it. If he got on the floor, he might not get back up again for a very long time.

His stomach rumbled angrily before bile rose in his throat, and David found himself gagging over the toilet once more. This was his life now, apparently, because it was beginning to seem more like an inconvenience than anything else.

Just when he didn’t think he had anything left to puke up, gross yellow liquid fell into the water below as his stomach contracted in a heave.

At least it was over fast.

He couldn’t even bother to be disgusted as he wiped the saliva off of his chin and blew his runny nose.

His reflection made him want to vomit allover again. Dark rings under his eyes, hair a total sweaty mess. His shirt had a stain from when he threw up on it early that morning and didn’t bother to change it.

At least that was something he could do to make himself look less horrible.

He removed his stained, sweaty shirt and threw it on the floor with a grimace, and slipped on a hoodie that would certainly make him sweat and feel gross until the chills hit once again.

God, he was a mess.

Hopefully he could go back to sleep and make himself a little more together. Maybe he could just sleep off this entire stomach bug before Patrick showed up.

Yeah, David thought as he shoved away all the blankets but one, sleep was supposed to be good for you, right? Maybe it would cure him completely. He just had to take another nap, and-

He nearly jumped out of his skin when there was a knock at the door.

David grabbed his phone and frantically checked the time.

5:30. Jesus, how long was he asleep?

Another knock. “David? Are you home?”

Oh God. He... he looked awful. Patrick couldn’t see him like this. He wasn’t prepared to pretend to have a cold, either. He needed more time to... to... well, there wasn’t much he could do but let Patrick in and hope he didn’t run off at the sight of his plague-ridden boyfriend.

David quickly pulled their room’s tissue box closer to him and- the bucket! “Fuck shit fuck...” he turned it over and shoved it under the bed.

Patrick knocked once more. “...David?”

David groaned inwardly and finally said, “Come in!”

God, he sounded so sick.

Patrick entered slowly as if he wasn’t sure if David was actually ready for him (he wasn’t, but Patrick didn’t need to know that).

His sympathetic smile kind of made David melt and want to be sick forever if it meant seeing that adorable look on his boyfriend’s face.

“Hi sweetheart,” he sat a paper grocery bag on top of the mound of blankets. “How are you feeling?”

Patrick leaned in for a kiss and David found himself flinching. Instead of the offended look he expected to see, Patrick just huffed a gentle laugh and kissed his temple.

“Oh, I’m... you know, just um,” he waved a hand in front of his face. “Stuffed up, and like, sneezing a lot...”

David nodded slowly. “Ah. So you know...?”

“Yes, David, I know.”

“Oh God.” He buried his head in his hands. “Oh my God, why would she tell you that?!”

Much to his dismay, Patrick laughed. David felt the mattress sink next to him under Patrick’s weight, and then a hand on his back, rubbing gently. “You could have just said that you didn’t have a cold.”

“Mm, yes, but then I would basically be admitting to you that I spent all night glued to the toilet in one way or another and that wasn’t something I was willing to disclose to my boyfriend.” He sighed. “And instead, my own sister betrayed me. Well, that happens like twice a day, but still.”

Patrick wrapped an arm around him and laid his head on David’s shoulder. There was no possible way David smelled fantastic right now, he realized with disgust, and yet Patrick insisted on being close to him.

“Okay, but stop kissing me at least, because I’ve been sweating for almost a full day."

David’s mouth was dry and he knew his body desperately wanted to drink something, but he feared something coming back up while Patrick was around.

He really, really liked glacier cherry gatorade, though. And he was so thirsty…

With a sigh, David cracked the bottle open and took a big drink. It was the best thing he'd experienced all day (though that wasn't saying much). Having liquid going in instead of out was a welcome change from the past day's events.

"Careful, don't drink too fast or you'll just make yourself feel worse."

David chuckled as he recapped the bottle of the blessed drink. "Not my first time puking my guts out. Although, I guess it was usually because I was super drunk." He laid a hand on his stomach, and then folded an arm all the way around it as it gurgled in irritation from the sudden stuff entering his body
