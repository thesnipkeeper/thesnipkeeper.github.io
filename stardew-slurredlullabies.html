Slurred Lullabies

He really, truly, did not believe Sebastian when he claimed to be too sick to hang out.

Sam had had to drag his friend out with them hundreds of times before, and the guy always had a good time. He was just introverted and easily-exhausted, and needed a little nudge sometimes. Sam couldn’t imagine what state his friend would be in if he isolated himself as much as he wanted to.

So just like any other time he was given a lame excuse, he went to Sebastian’s.

Sam greeted Robin with a kind smile and a wave as he made his way down to Sebastian’s depressing-ass bedroom. She was used to him letting himself in at that point, and seemed to be thankful that her son had someone he’d actually talk to.

“Knock knock!” Sam announced as he loudly let himself in.

There was an immediate groan from the lump in the bed. Sebastian turned over and pulled the covers over his head. He groaned a little louder when Sam flopped on his bed, causing the cheap frame to creak.

“Fuck off, man...” he muttered without any venom. His voice was muffled into the bedding.

“Nah, no can do, my dude. It’s Saturday and I literally refuse to let you spend it in bed.” Sebastian constantly said how weekends didn’t really matter when he worked from home, but Sam was determined to make at least one day of the week special in his eyes.

“I’m not doing anything today.”

“Come on,” Sam complained playfully, poking his friend in the shoulder. “If you don’t hang out with me, Abigail’s gonna get me playing her video games until dark.”

When Sebastian didn’t respond, Sam did what he did best and annoyed him more. He poked the guy and then pulled the blanket from his grasp.

Sebastian groaned again. His hair was all kinds of messed up as he sat up and huffed at Sam. “I really, really hate you.”

“I know,” Sam smiled proudly. “Now, we-” he set a hand on his friend’s shoulder, and frowned. “You’re- you’re hot.”

“Thanks, I guess.”

“No, why is your skin so hot?”

Sebastian only made a halfhearted attempt to dodge as Sam pressed his hand on various parts of his face, finally landing on his forehead. He looked at Sam like he was an idiot.

“I literally told you I was sick, what did you expect?”

“... oh.”

He gave a breathy chuckle and shook his head. “Sorry, I was looking forward to going to the lake today.”

“No, it’s fine, I just thought you were being a little shit again.”

Sebastian shuddered and grabbed the blanket that Sam realized he’d been holding hostage. He wrapped it around himself, looking chilled and weak. Sam always forgot how scrawny he was under those hoodies. He always got cold easily, sick or healthy.

“Dr. Harvey been by?”

A scoff. Sebastian leaned against the wall as if he couldn’t hold himself upright anymore. “I don’t need a doctor.”

Fair enough. Though, that argument would have had more weight to it if he didn’t look to Sam with wide eyes all of a sudden and say, “Can- can you hand me the-” A hand was hovering over his mouth, his other arm wrapping around his stomach.

Sam looked down and realized he was referring to the bucket that had been next to his bed. It was clean, thankfully, but Sam imagined that he’d set it there the minute he started to realize he might throw up. He was quick to hand the thing over, mostly in determination to not get barfed on.

Sebastian nodded his thanks, breathless as he held the thing between his knees and swallowed. “Ugh...”

He leaned over it spitting and whimpering at the aches in his stomach, waiting and waiting for the nausea to peak. Sam got up, but only to sit next to his friend and rub his back. Sebastian didn’t tense under the touch.

A strange sound came from the back of his throat before a trickle came down from his lips and pattered into the bottom of the bucket. And the floodgates were open.

Sebastian vomited, hard and forceful. He could hardly get a breath in between each heave. Sam couldn’t do anything but rub his back and say something comforting like “it’s okay” even though it obviously wasn’t okay, because he was in the process of puking his guts out.

The heaves tapered off after a while, and Sebastian looked even more exhausted than he had before. And with that, he slumped against Sam’s shoulder. Jeez, he felt warm. Sam wondered what his temperature was at, or whether it should be a concern. It wasn’t like he knew about those things.

“Maybe I should get your mom.” It felt like such a stupid thing for an adult man to say.

Sebastian shook his head against him. “She’s got three different jobs today.” He sat up, rubbing at his eyes and nose. “I’ll be fine. You should go before I get you sick or something.”

Sam gaped, and then laughed. “Uh, I’m not leaving. Not if you’re going to be alone.”

“Dude, I’m just gonna sleep.”

“And puke.”

A sigh. “And puke, probably, yeah. You really want to be around for that?”

“Hey, I’m just concerned for my best friend’s wellbeing.”

Sebastian didn’t look like he wanted to argue much. “Fine, stay. We’ll watch a movie or something. Just give me a sec to-” he began to get up, bucket in hand, but Sam stopped him.

“I’ll take care of it.”

The look Sebastian gave him was that of confusion and exasperation. “Dude.”

“Dude.”

Just the year before, Sebastian had ended up cleaning up after an insanely-drunk Sam following a festival. Sam hoped this would be returning the favor, although he could do nothing to make up for the fact that his friend had to listen to him drunkenly sing lullabies with incorrect lyrics until he finally passed out.

So, he took the bucket to the cold, concrete-floored bathroom in Sebastian’s basement room, scrubbed it clean, and returned it. Sebastian looked asleep at first, and his plan was to grab one of his friend’s books and pull up a chair.

He stopped when a muffled “Thanks” came from the bed. Sebastian’s eyes were closed, but he smiled a bit.

“No problem, bud.” And against his better judgment, Sam leaned down and pressed a kiss to his friend’s forehead.
