Title: The Ruins
Fandom: Stargate SG-1
Summary: Daniel's sick on a mission and still determined to do what he loves
Word count: 3.4k

The Ruins

Daniel looked up from his coffee, realizing that he was being talked to. “Sorry, what was that?”

Sam chuckled, pulling out a chair and flopping down across from him. “I asked you if you slept well, but I think I know the answer to that now.”

A sigh. That was only confirmation that he looked as bad as he felt. “Yeah...” he pulled off his glasses and ran a tired hand down his face, as if that was going to do anything to improve things. “Yeah, I had a little trouble falling asleep last night. I think I’m still getting used to all of the noise or... something?”

Sam made a sympathetic sound before opening her yogurt cup before digging into it hungrily. “You’ll get used to it.”

“Hopefully,” he said with a yawn. “Anyway, I’m interested to see what’s in store for us on the new world.”

“Mm,” in the middle of another spoonful of yogurt, Sam nodded. “The rover didn’t show any signs of intelligent life, it looks like it's just woods. This job might be more of an off-world camping expedition.”

He’d heard about the rover’s readings. There were no people from what they knew of, but there had been glimpses of man-made art and possible ruins on the camera that made Daniel’s archaeologist heard skip in excitement. There had also been discussions about possible new plant life to study, which he knew Sam’s own scientist heart was looking forward to.

“Camping expedition with ruins,” Daniel grinned. “My kind of thing.”

Sam laughed. “You bet’cha. Are you gonna eat something?”

Daniel made an ‘ugh’ sound and went back to rubbing his face. It wasn’t helping. “I’m not very hungry.”

“Daniel.” When he put his glasses back on, his teammate was giving him a serious scolding look that he never liked coming from Sam. “Eat something. Do you want me to grab you something? Cereal?”

She wasn’t going to let this go, and he didn’t really blame her. He’d probably force her to eat if she tried to skip breakfast the day of a mission too. So, Daniel sighed and shook his head, getting up and finding that his body had lost any energy it had in the small amount of time that he’d been awake.

“Naw, I’ll get something. If you’re going to be like that.”

She nodded victoriously. “Good.”

He ate half of a bagel. It certainly didn’t improve things. The other half ended up being slid to Sam’s side of the table and dropped his head on his folded arms on the table.

“Are you gonna be okay?” Sam asked.

Daniel sat up too fast and tried to ignore the way his vision swam for a second before he answered, “Yeah, of course!”

Sam didn’t look too sure, but she went back to finishing his bagel.

-----

He suffered a terrible bout of vertigo the moment he stepped out of the gate. He didn’t realize how bad it was until Teal’C was holding onto him. Oh, he’d nearly fallen on his face. Well, that wasn’t embarrassing at all.

“Sorry, Teal’C,” he shook his head as if to clear it, and it did the exact opposite. His friend didn’t relinquish the grip until Daniel was standing steadily, and the moment the world wasn’t spinning was the moment Daniel saw that the others were staring at him. Oh boy. “God, that was weird.”

Sam looked more worried than the others, looking at him intently.

“Look alive, Danny Boy,” Jack said with a sigh before turning around to lead the group. “Don’t want the trees to think that we’re vulnerable.”

Daniel scoffed. “Oops.” Teal’C was still hovering near him, while clearly trying to pretend that he wasn’t. Daniel nodded him his thanks, Teal’C nodded back. “I really should have gotten more sleep last night, I guess.” After nearly passing out at the gate, it might have been worth looking into getting something from the doctor. They wouldn’t have been able to afford a blunder like that if the stakes were higher.

“Well, maybe we can turn in early tonight.”

It was a quiet world, a relaxing quiet as opposed to the eeriness that they sometimes came across. The group of them wandered further into the woods, and all Daniel could think of were the ruins that he could study (and the breakfast that was sitting like a pit in his stomach) “If there were people here, what happened to them?”

“Dunno,” Sam shrugged. “Goa’uld?”

“It is possible,” Teal’C said. “Perhaps the Goa’uld did not need them anymore.”

“Well yeah, but wouldn’t they have just left the people here to fend for themselves? The Goa’uld don’t usually bother to kill their... failed experiments.”

Jack sighed. “Those guys aren’t exactly known for their moral standing, either.”

“Teal’C?”

Teal’C looked unsure, or as unsure as he could ever look. “I have heard stories,” he said after a few seconds. “But I cannot be sure that this is the world.”

“Oh boy,” Jack breathed out. “I guess we’ll find out more when we get to the ruins, eh Daniel?”

Daniel nodded, but didn’t answer immediately. He was far too out of breath than he should have been. “Can’t wait!”

-----

They didn’t manage to reach the ruins before night time set in. It must have only been late afternoon on earth, but Daniel welcomed the early nightfall. It meant going to bed, something that he’d wanted to do since he woke up that morning.

Jack took a sip from his canteen and sat on a nearby fallen tree. “Look, I know there’s no one here, but we should probably have someone on watch. Just to be, y’know, safe.”

Sam nodded. “I think that’s a good idea, sir. I can take first watch.”

“Naw, I’ve got it,” Jack said. “I’m not really tired yet.”

“I guess that makes sense. What time is it on earth? About four?”

“Yeesh, dinner time,” Jack chuckled. Sam chucked a protein bar his way. “Oh, thanks.”

Daniel knew that he should be eating something, but all he really wanted to do was go to sleep.

And go to sleep he did. He was the first one to fall asleep, before the others had even gotten their sleeping bags out. The last thing he heard was Sam mentioning that he hadn’t slept well the night before.

-----

Daniel woke up with an intense cramp in his gut. He tried to ignore it first, desperate to go back to sleep and deal with it later. But alas, his body wouldn’t let him have that. He moaned quietly, wrapping his arms around his middle.’ It was no use. Maybe he should have eaten, then. Fine, he’d admit it.

Daniel lay there, exhausted, simply trying to will away the pain before he felt something else. He barely had time to scramble out of his sleeping bag before he was vomiting onto the forest floor.

“Whoa!” Jack’s voice exclaimed from somewhere close by. Daniel heard footsteps approach him, and he tried to say something, something to make this somehow look better than it was, but he only ended up gagging again. “Hey buddy, why don’t we get you a little away from camp...”

Right, he was still right there, throwing up. His feet were still tangled in the sleeping bag, and he let Jack help him stand up and get his footing, only then seeing that his sleeve had been in the line of fire. Jack half-dragged him further away from the camp and the light of the fire. He tore away from his teammate when his stomach gurgled and more of its undigested contents made their way up.

Jack made a disgusted sound laced with sympathy as he patted Daniel’s back. Which really wasn’t helping, but Daniel appreciated the attempt all the same.

“Okay... tell me you just ate a bad burrito or something and don’t have some kind of alien plague.”

Daniel groaned and spat into the muddy puddle below him. “I think it’s just a stomach bug,” he admitted.

“And that’s why you’ve been acting all strange? Damn it, we need to know about these things, Daniel!”

Daniel really wasn’t in the mood to be scolded. “Jack, I didn’t know that I was getting sick. If I had known, I wouldn’t be here. Trust me.”

Jack sighed. Daniel couldn’t see him too well, but he was pretty sure that he was massaging the bridge of his nose the way he did when he was extra annoyed. Although, the annoyance was probably more at the situation than Daniel. Probably.

“We’ll head back first thing tomorrow. Can you survive until then?” He was only half-joking.

“No, Jack, I can do this. Sending me back is just going to waste time that we could be using to get to the ruins.”

“Sure you can, but I can’t, in good conscious, let you do this.” He set a hand on Daniel’s back, leading him back to camp. Daniel wasn’t so sure he wouldn’t have to run off and puke again, but he did want to be back in the light.

“Jack.” His voice was more of an intense whisper now that they were in proximity of their sleeping teammates.

“Daniel.”

“Come on, I’ll survive. I’m the only one that can know anything about these ruins and it’s going to go a lot smooth if I’m...” he paused, pressed a hand to his mouth to stifle a sick burp. “If I’m here...”

In the warm glow of the campfire, he watched Jack’s face go through an entire journey before, “Fine! Fine, but you’re my responsibility and you do what I tell you to, understood?”

Daniel really wasn’t a soldier and didn’t officially take orders from Jack, but if that was what it took, then... “Deal.”

He got up twice in the night with those same cramps to sleepily stumble into the woods and vomit, Jack didn’t say anything. He felt awful, that was for certain, but he wanted to see those ruins. Dealing with a miserable stomach bug was a small price to pay when he could understand yet another alien civilization.

Thankfully his stomach had settled a bit by the time Jack woke Sam for her shift. Daniel did his best to fall back into sleep, an uneasy sleep as opposed to the heavy sleep he was hoping for after the night before. But it was sleep.

-----

Daniel was the first to wake up in the morning. He stumbled past Teal’C, who Sam must have decided to wake up instead of Daniel, and vomited onto the ground. Teal’C stood immediately.

“Are you ill, Daniel Jackson?”

“Uh,” Daniel ran the back of his hand across his mouth. “Yeah.”

Teal’C’s ever-undecipherable face looked him over. “Do you need assistance?”

“No,” Daniel breathed out. No, he wasn’t sure exactly how Teal’C could help, even though he was shaky and shivery for no reason at all and his stomach felt like it had turned itself inside-out during the night. “Don’t worry, Jack and I already uh... discussed it.”

Teal’C nodded minutely, and sat back down.

If this was how the day was going to go, Daniel was not looking forward to it.

-----

“You okay, buddy?” Jack called as Daniel was leaning against a tree not too far away from them.

He’d walked off with that same feeling in his stomach, heaved, but nothing came up. He was starting to get more frustrated with his body than he already was. He almost preferred the shivery feeling from earlier, because now he was cooking in his gear, feeling sweat drip down his back.

“Great!” He called back with a croak.

“Uh-huh.”

Daniel gave up and returned to the group.

“You need a minute?” Sam asked, looking at him with the caution that she’d given him yesterday when he lost his balance at the gate.

“I already had a minute, didn’t I?”

Sam rolled her eyes. “I meant-”

Jack groaned loudly and started walking in the direction that they’d been heading. “Let’s just get this over with!”

Daniel couldn’t help but agree. The ruins were the only thing he had to look forward to. He tried not to think about the impending trip back to the gate where he’d no doubt feel worse by then.

But he found himself gasping at the sight, illness momentarily forgotten. He could already see the rich history among the ancient rubble. “Oh my God...” Daniel muttered, practically running toward it. “This is... this is amazing. It- Teal’C?”

The three of them turned to their friend, who wore a deep frown as he took a step onto the ground of what must have been an ancient temple and looked over their surroundings. “I believe that this is the world I have heard stories of,” he stated.

“Okay,” Daniel drew out. “What... what were the stories?”

“There are tales of a world that learned too much of Goa’uld technology. They had begun planning an overthrowing, and were found out and killed.” He took a few more steps around.

Daniel was blinded by the sheer beauty of the ruins, but looking at them with new eyes, he saw signs of battle. There were marks and broken areas that proved the structure hadn’t just been brought down by nature, but also by staffs like Teal’C’s, and other weapon Daniel couldn’t even identify.

“Oh no,” he muttered as he stood back up. “They were too smart for their own good.”

Teal’C nodded. “Perhaps.”

“The Goa’uld killed them because they were a threat,” Sam said.

“Or would have been,” Jack added. “God damn it.”

“I do not believe that we are in danger.”

Sam stepped onto the patterned floor and wandered around. “Well, that’s a relief. Maybe we can find something about the technology that they were developing.”

The ruins suddenly became much less exciting now that they were the remnants of mass murder. Daniel’s stomach chose that time to actually relinquish its contents, and he had a split second to avoid vomiting on the ancient building. He stumbled over a pile of stone, and Jack rushed over in time to keep him from landing on his face.

“Okay, I think it’s time to take a break,” Jack said, still holding Daniel up. “A quick rest and recharge before we find out exactly what these people were doing.”

Daniel nodded.

“Sounds good,” said Sam.

-----

Daniel was tired. Exhausted, actually. His brain wasn’t working as fast as it usually did, but that didn’t stop him from inspecting every inch of the temple that he could get to.

“It looks like they worshiped a different god. A sun god of some kind...” he said, mostly to himself as usual.

“So, not a Goa’uld, then.”

“No, no I don’t think so... Teal’C, do you know anything about this?”

Teal’C knelt down next to him and inspected the same intricate designs on the wall. “I do not,” he admitted. “But it appears to be highly-exalted among the people.”

“Yeah, maybe they got sick of being told that the people who abandoned them were their gods, and started to worship their own.”

His stomach gurgled, cramped and Daniel winced. He laid a hand on his abdomen, pressing down in some attempt to ease the pressure. Teal’C, in turn, set a hand on his shoulder, a silent offering of help. Daniel couldn’t keep himself from accepting it immediately, letting his friend help him up and away from the temple. Jack watched them as they passed.

“You know, I’d hate to say it, but I think that you should try to eat something.” He had the audacity to say it as Daniel was heaving up bile onto the forest floor. Asshole.

Daniel groaned. “Don’t mention food, please.”

“Well not right now, but you can’t go two days without eating, especially when we’re going to be traveling all day tomorrow.”

He had a point. Daniel knew that he had a point, but God damn it, eating was the last thing he wanted to do right now. He wasn’t hungry, of course he wasn’t hungry! Daniel gagged and yellow-orange bile dripped from his lips. It tasted awful, probably looked even worse to the others. He was thankful that it was Teal’C with him, as his alien friend was probably less-likely to be disgusted by something like this.

Daniel sighed heavily and took a few steps away to sit on the edge of the temple floor. “I’d hate to slow us down tomorrow.”

“Oh, you’re gonna slow us down no matter what,” Jack said helpfully.

Sam chuckled from the other side of the temple, still turned around to take pictures of the various carvings in the floors and pillars.

“Great,” Daniel breathed out. He reached for his canteen, and rinsed his mouth out a bit before taking a couple of careful sips. He shuddered.

Ah, so the chills were back too. That was... ugh.

He took as many notes and photos as he could. If his brain wouldn’t work on all cylinders right now, at least he could get as much as possible to study later when his body wasn’t trying to kill him.

He shivered again.

“I’m freezing,” he complained, because he thought he maybe have earned the right to complain at that point.

He wasn’t even aware that someone was nearby until he felt a hand, a big one - Teal’C’s - on his forehead. A gesture that Daniel certainly didn’t expect from him out of everyone there, but it made sense. Teal’C probably didn’t even think anything of it.

Teal’C hummed thoughtfully. “You have a fever.”

Hearing this, Jack called a “Fantastic!” from the outside of the structure.

“Oh...” Sam said sympathetically, making her way toward he and Teal’C. “Are you gonna be okay?”

“I’ll be fine,” Daniel breathed out. “I just...I’ll be ready to rest when we get back home.”

Sam snorted at the understatement.

-----

Despite his entire body crying for him to stop during their trip back, Daniel found himself being excited to study what they’d found of the world. Sam carried a piece of material that they were unable to identify, hiding her giddiness.

The travel back to the gate seemed much quicker, though near the end of it Teal’C stayed by Daniel’s side after a couple of unfortunate stumbles and losses of balance. Daniel decided that he’d already abandoned his dignity the moment he puked in front of Jack not two feet away from his bedroll.

They’d stop and rest, and get back to walking.

“You sure you don’t want a... nap, or something?” Jack asked, while Sam raised her eyebrow at the interesting phrasing of the question.

“Definitely,” Daniel answered, even while Teal’C was practically holding him up. “I’m just ready to go home.”

-----

The infirmary wasn’t quiet by any means. In fact, it may have been louder than the area of the base that Daniel’s quarters were located. But he didn’t have any problem resting now, after two days of exertion and... throwing up.

Dr. Frasier had set an emesis bin on the table next to his bin with the instruction not to try to get up. He was tired enough and connected to enough things that he wasn’t even going to try.

He really should have been focusing on resting. That would have been best. But all of the photos and notes that he took off-world were sitting in his office waiting for him to go over it.

“When are you gonna release me?” He asked after Dr. Frasier finished taking his temperature. By the look that she gave, it wasn’t as good as she would have liked.

“Whenever you’re fever-free or stop vomiting,” she said, popping the cap of the thermometer into the trash and wiping it down. “Whichever comes first, Dr. Jackson.”

“Oh boy.”

She laughed. “I know that you’re eager to get back to work, but your body really needs some recuperating right now.”

He nodded, sighing. Realistically, he might not even be able to stay standing long enough to get to his office. Although, the meds Janet had given him were really doing wonders...

No, he had to wait.

“You really shouldn’t have gone on that mission,” she said. Or, repeated, really.

“Look,” his eyelids were starting to feel heavy. “I didn’t know- I thought I was just tired.”

She hummed. “Well, your team certainly took care of you.”

He chuckled fondly. “They certainly did. I think I owe them one.”
