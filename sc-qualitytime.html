Title: Quality Time
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David learns that he could, in fact, feel worse
Word count: 1k

Quality Time

When David woke with an earache, he knew it was going to be a long day.

It sounded like Patrick was already downstairs making breakfast (And coffee, oh blessed coffee), so David somehow worked up the will to get out of bed and start getting dressed.

He gave a congested sniffle that did nothing to fix his runny nose and everything to spike the pain in his right ear. He whimpered and winced, standing impossibly still for a moment as he waited for the pain to pass.

Definitely a long day.

He’d been dealing with a cold for the past couple of days, and by the time and he and Patrick went to bed the night before, David felt like he was finally over the worst of it. It was now clear that that wasn’t the case.

He did an abbreviated version of his morning routine, the same abbreviated version that he’d been doing. He was going to have to give his skin and hair some good pampering when this cold was gone, they both deserved it.

David made his way downstairs and found his husband fully dressed, dancing around the kitchen to Christmas music.

“Absolutely not,” David announced himself. “No Christmas music before December in this house!”

Patrick laughed and made no effort to change the music. “Sorry, babe! I need my Christmas music!”

David huffed, but he couldn’t stay mad for long as his husband pulled him in by the waist and began coercing him into the most uncoordinated dancing David had ever done. He giggled, only for it to turn into a gross, wet cough that had him pulling away and burying his face in his sleeve.

“Ohh,” Patrick cringed. “That doesn’t sound too good.”

“Ugh, I know,” David agreed. “But it’s better than yesterday because my throat isn’t trying to tear itself apart.”

“Ah, good,” Patrick said with a grin. “That means that you can eat whatever you want again!”

The thought made David smile. If his sore throat was gone, he could indeed eat again without being in excruciating pain. Except...

“Except I can’t taste!”

Patrick made a sympathetic sound that was quickly interrupted by a chuckle. “Sorry.”

“Mm, I’m glad my suffering amuses you.”

“Not your suffering specifically, just you,” Patrick promised before pulling him in for a kiss. “Want some pancakes?”

It was only then that David realized Patrick was actually cooking breakfast instead of eating a bowl of cereal or a granola bar.

“Wow, what’s the occasion?”

Patrick shrugged. “You’ve been feeling so crappy this week, I thought it would help cheer you up.”

Oh God, he was adorable. David couldn’t bite back the smile, the idea that someone had thought of them, even if that someone was his husband who’d already professed his undying love multiple times.

“I would love some.”

He had to blow his nose between bites as sitting down somehow made the congestion worse. After three day of this, David was beyond feeling self-conscious about gross sickness habits. He knew Patrick didn’t mind, and Patrick was probably going to be in the same state in a few days anyway since the mad man refused to stop kissing him while he was sick.

David swallowed, and winced as it, combined with his stuffed nose, caused another surge of pain in his ear. He couldn’t stop the grunt of discomfort as he put his fork down, hand going to his ear.

“What’s wrong?” Patrick asked, the same tone of worry he had when David tried to eat with his sore throat the day before.

David shook his head. “Nothing. Just uh- chewed wrong.”

Patrick didn’t question how one could hurt themselves by chewing pancakes wrong, and instead gave him a quick rub on the back and went back to breakfast.

Ugh, he didn’t want to be sick all over again. He was so over it.

-----

His ear only started to hurt more as they went to work and began their day at the store.

The increased coughing - which he originally just thought was his body’s way of recovering - was getting... concerning. It felt like it was coming from his chest as opposed to just his airways. It was sort of hard to deal with customers when he had to constantly hold off a consumptive cough that would surely scare everyone away.

It was only around lunch that David started to slow down. He was tired, he was - admittedly - a bit grumpy. He didn’t realize why, outside of generally not feeling well, until he was fussing over the lotions and there was suddenly a hand on his forehead.

Patrick removed his hand and pressed a kiss to David’s cheek. “You haven’t had a fever in two days, why is it back now?”

David sighed, which just irritated his chest and forced him to give another terrible cough. Which then hurt his ear. His hand went up to his ear once again as he whimpered pitifully. “I feel terrible,” he said in the most pathetic voice he could muster.

“Your ear?”

David nodded.

“Aw, David, I’m sorry.”

“No, it’s fine, the universe just hates me!”

Patrick laughed. “Maybe that’s it. We can head to the doctor tomorrow if it gets worse, how does that sound?”

“Good,” David agreed. The itchy feeling in his chest that he’d become so familiar with had him raising his arm over his face to muffle another cough. And another.

Patrick gave a concerned hum. “That cough doesn’t sound great, either.”

“And it doesn’t feel great, but-” David sighed. He was starting to get achy, and with that achiness came an irritability that Patrick didn’t deserve. “Okay,” he said decidedly, raising his hands. “I’m done whining, I promise!”

Patrick chuckled. “Don’t make promises you can’t keep.” He turned David toward him and, for whatever reason, still kissed him on the mouth.

David made a noise of disgust. “You are very much going to regret kissing me.”

“Mmm, I don’t know if that could ever happen.”

David scoffed. He could already see his husband sneezing and sniffling and blaming him for it, but that was alright. He wasn’t going to complain. Well, he was, but not about this.

“Do you want to stay here? You could always head home and rest,” Patrick said softly, arms still wrapped around David’s waist.

“Honestly?” David said. “I think I’d rather be here with you.”
