Title: Still Here
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David doesn't want Patrick to see him while he's disgustingly ill
Word count: 2.6k

Still Here

David blinked at the steaming mug set in front of him. "What's this?"

"Um, it's tea."

"Um, I can see that. But why are you giving it to me?"

Alexis gave a twitchy, suspicious shrug. "I just thought I'd do something nice for my super sick big brother."

"Mhm. Did a bug fall in it or something and you don't want it anymore?"

"OhmygodDavid just drink the fucking tea. I'm trying to be nice."

"Okay, fine!" He picked it up and took a big sip. It was quite nice on his throat, even though he couldn’t taste anything. The flavor would forever remain a mystery. He gave a reluctant, “That’s nice, thank you.”

“Are you still bummed that you had to cancel your date with Patrick?”

He let out a deep sigh which just turned into a cough at the end. He rushed to cover his mouth with the sleeve of his hoodie. As much as his sister got on his nerves at times, he didn’t want her getting sick.

“I mean, yeah. But it’s fine. Just the first date we’ve been able to go on in a long time because we’ve been so busy with the store, but whatever.”

Alexis crossed her legs and leaned against the table he was currently sat at with his laptop. He was looking at some things to buy for the store, with the promise to Patrick that he wouldn’t take the business down with expensive decorations.

Also, online shopping made him forget about the inevitable demise that this awful cold was going to bring.

“David,” Alexis sighed. “Why didn’t you just invite him over?”

“Because, Alexis, we’ve never seen each other sick before and I refuse to be the first one to be snotty in front of him.”

“That’s the dumbest thing I’ve ever heard. He’s not gonna care.”

“Um, do you recall what happened with Emily?”

Alexis looked up, face screwed up in thought for entirely too long. “You mean that girl you met during our vacation in England? The one with all the rose gold piercings and cute dresses?”

“That was the trip that-”

Alexis was already nodding and holding her hand up to stop him finishing his story. “That you had a cold the second night you hung out. She went to the bathroom and never came back. Yes David, I remember, because you wouldn’t stop talking about it that whole night. You ordered like, every dessert on the room service menu and then complained that you couldn't taste any of them.”

“And that is why,” he said, holding out a hand as an example, because that should be enough to convince her that Patrick seeing him at his worst this early in the relationship was a bad idea. Well, maybe it wasn’t so early in their relationship. They’d been together for nearly a year now, but still...

“’kay David, but she was also kind of a bitch.”

“Why do you think I asked her out?” he said, biting back a smile.

“Ew. I’m just saying, Patrick’s different. And I think you know that.

He did. It didn’t make it any easier to shake the underlying fear that if Patrick saw him tonight, he wouldn’t show up to work the next morning. He could disappear from the face of the earth just like Emily, or tell him he needed to look less disgusting just like Sebastian.

He didn’t feel well enough to go out tonight, and when Patrick offered to come over and hang out with him and Alexis instead, David found himself insisting that he don’t come over. Patrick was reluctant to agree, but he was too respectful of David’s space to argue.

David missed him. Alexis could tell.

He gave a hoarse groan and then sneezed, just managing to cover it with his arm. “Ugh.”

Alexis nudged him with the tissue box and he accepted it with an eye roll.

“Then again, you are pretty gross right now.”

“You’re cruel,” he mumbled with a thick sniffle.

Alexis smirked and looked like she was about to say something along the lines of ‘thanks I try’ before their door opened. David was in the middle of wiping his nose when he looked over and saw both his parents in the doorway between their adjoined rooms.

“Uh, hi?” he said after a couple seconds of them not saying anything.

Johnny looked to Moira, who shook her head and took a step back like the nurturing mother she was. He rolled his eyes and entered the room. “David, we’re heading to the cafe. Do you want any soup or something?”

David thought for a moment. “Some cake would be nice?”

“Cake, huh?”

“I mean if you want to get me something to help me feel better...”

Johnny chuckled. “Alright, fine. I’ll get you cake.”

Alexis gave him a look of utmost pity. He wanted to slap it off of her but held back. Yes, fine, he was planning to eat his feelings just a little bit, because guess what? He missed his boyfriend.

“Are you sure you don’t need to go to the hospital?” Moira called from the other side of her room.

“It’s a cold, mother!”

“Are you sure you should be up and about?”

“Oh my God, I can’t just do nothing because I’m a little sick.” Well, normally he would. No matter how sick he was, he’d stay in bed and milk it for all it’s worth. But he needed to keep busy this time, and he was quickly learning that not every cold required him to stay in bed and wallow in self pity. Unfortunately.

The two siblings listened to their parents leave the motel, and Alexis turned to him.

“You can’t eat your feelings this time you know!” She sing-songed. “Because this time is different and Patrick loves you so much.”

“He might not after I refused to see him tonight...” David sniffled, coughed, and wiped his nose.

“Then why don’t you just invite him over?!”

“Because then he might not love me after that either!” David said, exasperated. “I can’t win! The universe is after me, okay? Maybe I was destined to be single my whole life.”

Alexis scoffed and rolled her eyes. She made a show of standing up and walking away from him.

David just scowled at his computer and closed it. This was fine. Whatever happened between him and Patrick was something that he just had to accept. He angrily scrubbed at his nose, which was giving off more liquid that he would have liked.

“Gross David, use a tissue.” Alexis sat in front of him once again with a handful of nail polishes. “Give my your hand.”

“Uh, no, you’re not touching me right now when I’m literally covered in germs. You just watched me wipe my nose.”

“Okay, then go wash your hands and then give me your hand.”

David rolled his eyes. But something compelled him to do so. Maybe it was because he was soft on her tonight. Or maybe it was because he promised her ages ago that she could practice nail techniques on him and he had been ‘too busy’ every time she mentioned it. There was no excuse now. He was as not-busy as he would ever be, aside from the coughing and sneezing.

He took another sip of the tea before offering his right hand. It was the only thing soothing his scratchy throat, and he hoped that it would make him sound better before work tomorrow when he’d have to talk to customers.

“You know he’s not going to break up with you for being sick, right?”

“Emily did.” Alexis gave him A Look, and yeah, he knew it was a cheap defense. Give him a break, he was sick.

She pointedly ignored it and unscrewed the cap of the clear undercoat, beginning to apply it to his nails. “But Patrick isn’t Emily. Nor is he Sebastian, or Riley...”

“Okay well, we don’t need to mention Riley. Or Sebastian, for that matter.”

Riley, the person who turned out to have been cheating on him for weeks, and broke up with him when he accused them of it, even when he caught them in the act. They manipulated him into thinking he did something wrong. Convinced him that he deserved to be cheated on.

Sebastian, the person responsible for all of his walls. All of the crazy walls Patrick had to work to break through because Sebastian made David think he needed someone to tell him what to do all the time.

He was careful who he let in, and part of him felt that same caution in his relationship with the man of his dreams.

“My point is,” she grabbed his hand for better access to his thumb, “That he’s the one. And you worry too much.”

“Worrying too much is part of my adorable charm.”

“Shut up,” Alexis rolled her eyes. She moved on to his left hand. “Has he texted you?”

“Yeah, like a million ‘get well soon’s and stuff.”

“Mm, yeah he must hate you.” He didn’t much appreciate the sarcasm dripping from her voice, but decided to ignore it while she was in charge of making his nails look good.

The manicure went by in relative silence, besides the occasional sniffle or sneeze from David. Blowing his nose with wet nails was becoming a learn skill.

He sniffled wetly, trying to keep his nose under control while Alexis dug through her bag of polish.

“What color?”

“Black.”

He realized Alexis had already grabbed the black. “Glossy or matte top coat?”

“Snf! Matte.”

She gave a high-pitched “Cool!” And began painting his nails the deep black color he enjoyed since he was in high school. At least black nails were back in style.

“Left hand,” she instructed.

He gave her free reign to move and turn his hands however she liked to get the job done, and David had to admit that they were looking pretty good. Much better than the job she did when she was ten. She guilted him into letting her paint his nails back then too.

He sniffled, and suddenly his breath caught at a sudden, overwhelming itch in his nose. He scrunched it up, sniffed again, but it didn’t do a thing to abolish the tickle.

“Alexis... snf!”

He broke her concentration, she looked up and growled. “Ew David, don’t you dare.”

“Then give me my... snf! hand back.” He sniffled, liquid threatening to drip onto his upper lip and he was not about to let that happen. He minded the damp polish on his right hand as he scrubbed his nose, making gross wet noises in the process that signified how full it was. “Ugh.” The itch died down a bit, becoming a bothersome tingle in the back of his sinuses but nothing of immediate concern.

“If you sneeze on me-”

“I’m not going to sneeze on you. Gross. I have a little more self control than that.”

“Sure, whatever you say,” she scoffed, and went back to painting his nails.

They both paused at the sound of a door opening, along with a sing-songed “We’re home!” that could only belong to Moira rose.

“And we brought you something!” Johnny added.

Thank God. David nodded. “You can just leave the cake right- what?” he followed Alexis’ wide-eyed gaze to the space behind him. “Oh my God.”

Standing in his bedroom was none other than his boyfriend, who looked a little sheepish and guilty, but happy to be there nonetheless.

“Uh, hi.”

Patrick chuckled. “Hey.”

Moira patted Patrick’s shoulder. “We found poor Patrick dining alone. Thought he could use the company.”

“I- I don’t have to stay,” he said quickly. “I just thought I’d come say hi,” he leaned forward into David’s ear. “And your parents are hard to say no to.”

David snorted, and realized that was a mistake and quickly covered his nose. Oh God, of course Patrick had to show up right now when he hadn’t blown his nose in a while.

Patrick set a takeout container down in front of him, along with a single packaged fork.

“I thought I’d take care of the cake so your parents didn’t have to.” They both glanced back as the conjoined door closed. Patrick pulled up a chair, Alexis welcoming him with a smile. “Wow, what’s going on here?”

“David owes me,” she answered simply.

That must have been enough for Patrick, because he just nodded. “Well, they look great.” He cleared his throat. Patrick still looked wildly uncomfortable, like he didn’t know what he should be doing. David knew he was afraid of crossing the line.

“Oh, thank you.” Alexis said proudly. “I can give you a little manicure too if you wanted. Apparently I have nothing better to do tonight.”

“As much as I appreciate that... offer? I’ll probably have to leave soon, and nail polish does not last very long on my hands.”

David wanted to question when exactly Patrick’s painted his nails, but instead he found himself saying, “You don’t have to leave.”

Patrick chuckled.

“I- I mean, if you need to then whatever, it’s fine. But you can stay if you-”

“I’d love to.”

“Hey guys, this is super awkward right now, being here for this.”

David sent a glare his sister’s way and sniffled. He pressed the back of his hand to his nose, suddenly realizing that things could turn very quickly if he had to sneeze in front of Patrick.

“Oh my God...” He snuffled thickly, wincing at the gurgle in his sinuses. Alexis winced as well, which didn’t make him feel any better.

“Don’t sneeze on me, David!”

“Ugh...” The itch intensified once again. “snf! Calm down, I’m not going to sn- sneeze on you...” it crawled its way through his sinuses until he did indeed end up sneezing.

And when David looked up from the explosion, he realized there was a tissue pressed to his nose that no-doubt caught an embarrassing amount of snot.

And then he realized it was Patrick’s hand holding the tissue to his nose.

“OhmyGod,” he went to take over with his own hand, and growled in frustration when Alexis stopped him.

“They’re still drying, David. It’s fine.”

It wasn’t fine. And yet, Patrick looked so comfortable as he wiped underneath his nostrils like he’d done it before when he most certainly hadn’t because David never would have allowed that in a million years.

“I’ve got it,” Patrick said, pulled the tissue away, folded it, and used it to wipe up any residual. “There. Better?”

David froze. “Whatthefuck,” he muttered.

“What? It’s not a big deal, David. It’s just a little-”

“Um,” he stopped to sniffle, and found that his nose was clearer than it’d been in a while. Patrick had done a thorough job. “It absolutely is a big deal. I’m going to go die in a pit of embarrassment oh my God. Go wash your hands, Patrick!”

He nodded, biting back an amused grin and holding his hands up non-threateningly on his way to the bathroom. He pressed a kiss to David’s cheek on his return like a crazy person.

“Sorry, I just thought it’d be a good idea to protect your sister from sudden death.”

Alexis giggled. “Well, thank you Patrick.”

What the fuck.

David wanted to-

He wasn’t sure what he wanted to do. Anyone else would be running for the hills at this point. Sebastian would be convincing him to go out even though he was sick as a dog, Emily would be- well, Emily would be gone.

But Patrick was still here.

“What color, Patrick?”

“Oh, I guess I’ll take a blue if you have it.”

Alexis nodded. “And you know, I could give you an extra top coat so it doesn’t chip as fast if you want me to.”

“Sure?”

“Cool!”

Patrick was still here, getting his nails done by David’s sister while David was home sick.

Maybe Alexis was right.
