Familiar Falling

Joan was greeted with the very last name on her caller ID that she wanted to see. It wasn't that she and Owen were necessarily... arguing. But they'd found some differences in their working together quickly.

It wasn't usually this bad. They'd talk it out, work it out, and everything would be back to normal.

This time was a little different. It involved the treatment of one of Joan's new patients.

So, no, she wasn't thrilled to see that he was calling. He probably wasn't even going try to convince her to agree with him, which almost infuriated her more. Instead, he was most likely calling to tell her that she was right and they could do things her way.

That was the thing about Owen. He was the hardest person to argue with because at the end he'd just tell her she was right and apologize when it wasn't his fault. Joan hated that more than anything.

"Owen, I'm kind of busy," she said, even though she wasn't busy.

"Dr. Bright, Joan, I..." He sounded rough, like he'd just woken up. She was too much not in the mood to notice.

"What is it, Owen?"

"I could... use a favor."

"A favor," she repeated. "What would that be."

"I..." He swallowed, breathed hard. Alright, things were decidedly weird. "I've found myself in a... situation." She was about to complain about his vague explanation when he continued. "I'm very sick, I can't... stand?" A self-deprecating chuckle. "And I..." he trailed off. He didn’t sound well.

Joan sighed inwardly. She knew just how Owen was like when he was ill. Needy, sad... he hated nothing more than not being at his best. She remembered a particular bout of flu, while they were dating, that ended with a whimpering Owen with his head in her lap. It was... rather adorable at the time, if she was being honest.

Fevers really took hold of him. She ended up dragging him to an urgent care during that same bout of flu when his fever reached 104.

"What do you need me to do?" She didn't mean to sound so reluctant, so inconvenienced, but... she was, wasn't she? She had things to do just like everyone else.

She felt guilty, though, when Owen’s voice got quieter. "I'm- I'm sorry, Joan. This isn't your job, to-"

"Owen," she interrupted, and continued, softer. "What do you need?"

"Something for dehydration, please. I've... lost a lot of fluids the past couple of days."

"Is that all?"

"Yes," he said. "I just... can't make it to the store at the moment," he laughed, clearly making an attempt not to sound as miserable as he was.

“When’s the last time that you’ve eaten?”

“Wha...”

“Food, Owen.”

Another laugh, just as equally sad. “Good question?”

She sighed once more. “Okay, I’ll be over in a little while.”

-----

Owen’s place wasn’t tidy, never had been. What it was was organized. That was always the difference between the two of them. Joan liked cleanliness, order; Owen knew where everything was, even through the stacks of books and papers scattered about his apartment. There was a certain system she could never quite grasp, and it worked.

Same place, same Owen. She tried not to let her nostalgia get to her. She wasn’t staying.

It certainly didn’t help, however, that she still had a key to his place.

“I’m here!” Joan called, setting the bags on the counter. She rounded the corner to find her coworker passed out on the couch in a sad nest of blankets. There was a waste bin next to him which thankfully looked unused.

Joan considered leaving a note next to the groceries and going. It’d save them some awkwardness.

Instead, she gently shook his shoulder until his eyes opened. His brows furrowed in confusion for a moment as his brain tried to process why there was another person in his apartment. And then his face lit up with recognition, (and then fell with embarrassment at the realization of why she was there in the first place).

“Hi, hey, sorry.” He sat up slowly, blankets falling into a pool on his lap as he did. “I didn’t hear the door.”

“I figured. I let myself in,” Joan admitted. “Hope that’s alright.”

“Yeah,” he rubbed his eyes. “ ‘s fine. How are you?”

Joan couldn’t help but laugh. “Oh, better than you at the moment.”

He chuckled. “I’m sure. It really looks worse than it is, though. Just a, you know, a stomach bug.”

“Alright, well on the phone you told me that you couldn’t stand, so I’m thinking that it’s actually worse than it looks.”

Owen swallowed, didn’t argue. Joan took the opportunity to retrieve the stuff from the counter and set it on his rather cluttered coffee table.

“I bought saltines, minute rice, and three different flavors of Gatorade. Hope that’s okay?”

He smiled brightly and nodded. “Thank you so much, Joan. How much do I owe you?”

Joan almost forgot that they were coworkers for a moment. Not significant others, not even friends. ‘what do I owe you’ is a normal question for a coworker, isn’t it? “Nothing,” she shook her head. He opened his mouth to argue (she knew the ‘about to argue’ face on Owen). “Really, I didn’t mind, it was an excuse to get out for a little while.” Okay, she did mind a little. She stopped her own research to run this errand, but perhaps she needed the break. Sam would certainly say she needed the break.

“If you’re sure. Thank you, Joan, things were getting... grim.”

“Grim,” she repeated with a raised eyebrow.

He chuckled, another one of those ‘trying not to break’ chuckles she heard over the phone earlier. “I forgot how much I hate getting sick.”

“No one likes to get sick, Owen.”

“I know, of course not, it’s just... harder when I’m alone.” He averted his eyes quickly, now aware that he couldn’t take it back. “I- I mean-”

“It’s okay, I know what you meant.” Joan sighed. The two of them were really the only people who could deal with them when they weren’t feeling well. Joan still hadn’t quite found someone who could understand how she was when she was ill, or upset. Not completely.

It didn’t take Owen long when they were dating to realize that he couldn’t force her to rest when she was ill. She was going to work, to continue checking on patients over the phone and e-mail. He learned to keep her comfortable and to not make herself worse as she did so.

In turn, Joan understood that his... fragility during illness wasn’t an irrational part of him that she should brush away or try to therapy out of him. The therapist in her did sometimes wonder what brought it on, perhaps something in childhood. She didn’t plan on asking.

“Have you taken your temperature lately?”

Owen thought for a moment and shook his head. Joan found the thermometer in the same place it always had been.

Owen complied, slipping it under his tongue and looking far too heart-eyed for her at the moment. She ignored it. Not today, not right now. The last thing she needed was another complicated relationship to figure out.

“Not great, but... could be worse,” she said at the number on the little screen.

“Joan, really, you don’t have to stay,” he said, while looking like all he wanted was for her to stay.

She nodded. “I do have to get back to work, but...” was she going to regret this? “Call me if you need anything else, alright?” Just because they weren’t dating anymore didn’t mean that he had to be completely alone.

“I’ll hopefully be back to the AM tomorrow, if I manage to keep food down and my fever breaks.”

Joan stared at him, dumbfounded. “You are not coming to work tomorrow!”

He scoffed. “Well, you’re hardly my boss.”

“Owen-” no, nope. This wasn’t the time to chew him out. Let him figure it out on his own. So, she swallowed whatever lecture he didn’t need to hear right now. “I should go.”

“Thank you, Joan. Honestly.”

“You’re welcome. Get some rest.”

-----

“Joan! Joan, hey, good morning!” Sam caught up with her as she walked in the building and signed it. Her morning chipper-ness made Joan smile, even before she’d managed to get to the break room for morning coffee.

“Good morning to you too,” Joan offered. “What’s up?”

“Oh, well, it’s just the two of us today. Owen was actually just here for like a half hour ago, and-”

“Let me guess,” Joan sighed. “He went home sick.” She should’ve known he’d actually do it. Of course he did.

Sam nodded. “Yup.”

“Well, that’s fine. We can take care of things on our own today.”

“Yeah, of course,” Sam smiled, but it faded by the time Joan gave another glance to her as they weaved through the hallways.

“What’s wrong?”

Sam scrunched up her nose and sighed. “Just... he didn’t look great.”

“I’m sure he didn’t. He’s had a stomach bug all weekend and shouldn’t have tried to come into work in the first place.”

“Yeah, he seemed really sick. But also kind of sad. I mean, I was the one who had to suggest that he go home. He was so out of it.”

She was afraid of that. That was the problem that Joan had hoped she wouldn’t have to acknowledge. She nodded. “He... gets like that, I think.”

“What do you mean?”

“I-” maybe it wouldn’t be fair to him to say anything. Then again, he wasn’t a patient, was he? He was her ex, Sam already knew enough. “I’ll call him later, make sure he’s alright.”

Sam nodded slowly. “Okay, sure...”

-----

Joan reluctantly dialed his number the moment that she got to her office. The phone rang, and rang... she wondered if he was asleep. Or not near his phone. Or-

“Joan?”

“Hey, Sam said that you were here earlier and you didn’t look well. Are you alright? Well, besides the obvious?” They both knew what she was asking.

“I’m okay,” he said with a broken voice that didn’t sound very okay, and yet it went up at the end as if he were trying to convince himself of that.

“Okay,” Joan cleared her throat. She’d tried so hard to fight back the worry. They weren’t anything anymore. “Do you need anything?”

“Oh, goodness... no, I wouldn’t ask you for anything after you just-”

“Owen, have you eaten?” The line was quiet. Joan smiled smugly; she’d clearly gotten her answer. “What if I picked something up after work and brought it over? Something easy on the stomach, soup or broth?”

“I-” a chuckle. “You don’t have to do that, really...”

“You need to eat. You know that.”

There was a pause, and then, “Alright.” She could hear his smile. God, he was so smitten.

And apparently, so was she.

“Alright,” she echoed. “See you later, then.”

“See you, Joan.”

She ended the call with a smile and some excitement in her stomach that she couldn’t tamp down. She was falling right back into it, wasn’t she?
