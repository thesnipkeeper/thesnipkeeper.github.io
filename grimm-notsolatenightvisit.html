Not-So-Late Night Visit

Monroe was more than happy to take a sick day. That was one of the many nice things about working from home, he could work on clocks for as long as he felt like it, and then turn in early without anything to stop him.

He didn’t want to turn in this early, though. His eyes weren’t quite focusing anymore, and his sweater wasn’t quite warm enough. Maybe it’d be a good idea to get, like, a ton of meds in him and go to bed. Whether he could actually fall asleep, who knew. All he’d done when he tried to lie down earlier was cough and fail to breathe through his nose.

He’d just turned off his worktable lamp when there was a knock on the door. Who the hell would visit at this- oh, it was only seven. Damn, he must’ve been sicker than he thought. He felt like he’d been awake for two days at least.

Monroe’s muscles complained as he stood up, a combination of how long he’d been sitting and the damn flu that had taken hold of him since that morning. The room swam just a bit, his head feeling light when he finally reached the front door. A rush of cold air hit him immediately.

“Oh, hey Nick.” He leaned against the frame with a sigh and let the Grimm inside, eager to close the door as soon as possible.

“Hey, I-” Nick stopped and raised an eyebrow. He got a look at Monroe. “You okay? This a bad time?”

“No,” Monroe said and then, “... Maybe. I was about to go to bed, actually.”

He chuckled. “This early? Did you really have that many clocks to work on today?”

The two of them wandered into the living room, and Monroe plopped onto the couch without much grace. That was where he’d been napping between making himself work, and it’d been a good system. “I’m sick, give a guy a break,” he muttered, massaging the bridge of his nose.

“Oh,” Nick frowned. “Jesus, sorry. And I just showed up.”

“Nah, it’s fine. I should be used to it by now.” Nick snorted. “What’d you need, anyway?”

Nick sat down next to him, casually shoving aside a pile of blankets. “Do you know anything about a Wessen that eats livers?”

Monroe’s stomach turned. He took a moment to swallow away any nausea that chose now to make an appearance. “Come again?”

“We have two different victims, both of them are missing their livers and nothing else. Uh... pieces were found nearby.”

“Pieces... of the livers?”

Nick winced and nodded. “Yeah. It kind of reminded me of, you know, when you give a big piece of meat to a dog...”

Monroe swallowed thickly an closed his eyes. “Nick.”

“Okay, right, probably should be less graphic.”

“Please. Or at least, you can’t blame me if I barf all over you.” He felt a tickle in his throat and braced himself to cough without jostling the nausea that was still very much present. Nick tried to hide his wince at the sound.

“My point is that the ME found every other organ intact for the most part. This person went straight for the liver.”

“Jesus Christ, okay, let me think...” he took extra thinking time to try not to puke, and then it hit him. “I think I’ve heard of people who believe that eating the organ of another will heal their own. But like, animals.”

“So we’re looking for someone who has a bad liver?”

“Maybe,” Monroe shrugged. He coughed again and wished he’d taken the medicine before he sat down. Then at least it could start working. “Not that I don’t enjoy your company, but...”

Nick was already standing and zipping up his jacket. “Yeah, I’ll get out of your hair. Thanks, Monroe. I owe you one!”

Monroe snorted. “You always do, Nick. You always do.”
