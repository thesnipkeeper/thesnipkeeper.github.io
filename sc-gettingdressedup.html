Getting Dressed Up

“Are you sure you’re okay to go?” Patrick bit his lip as his husband stumbled through a routine that usually came easy to him. “We can always-”

“No!” David said, stopping him with an outstretched finger. “No, I’m fine.”

“Okay, it’s just that you sound like you might be getting sick.”

He’d sounded like that all day. David had been snuffling and blowing his nose throughout work. His voice was deeper and thicker like he was stuffed up, and Patrick had a feeling that it could only get worse from there.

But no. Apparently he was fine.

He didn’t blame David for wanting to go, the two of them had been looking forward to this dinner for weeks. David made the reservation in advance while telling Patrick all of the amazing food they served. It was fair that he didn’t want to miss out. Patrick just couldn’t imagine this going well.

David was in the middle of pulling his shirt on when he sneezed, immediately followed by a congested groan.

“Bless you,” Patrick said as casually as possible.

David rolled his eyes and started to sniffle.

“Need a tissue?”

“Shh. I’m fine,” he said, right before going in search for tissues.

Patrick heard an unfortunate noseblow from the bathroom that signified how much David should not be going out tonight. But maybe he wouldn’t have tried to argue that fact again if he hadn’t heard the coughing that soon followed.

“Okay...” Patrick said to himself with a heavy sigh. “David? Love of my life?” He called.

David finally wandered out of the bathroom with a wad of tissues pressed to his nose. His eyes were starting to get red from the irritation, the poor guy just looked awful.

“Whad?”

“We’re not going out tonight.”

David rolled his eyes once more. “Okay, I-”

“You are not fine!” Patrick couldn’t help but laugh as he said it, because it was so very clear that David wasn’t fine in anyway. He decided to soften his approach just a bit, walking up to his husband and wrapping an arm around his waist. “Can you really tell me that you don’t want to stay home?”

David looked up at the ceiling, whining. “I’ve- we’ve beed looki’g forward to this for...”

“I know, but you’re not feeling good and I don’t think you’re going to have a very good time.”

David groaned, and promptly had to twist out of Patrick’s grip to muffle the cough that it prompted.

Patrick rubbed his back. “Get comfortable and I’ll order something?”

David smiled and nodded, finally giving in. “Fide.”

“And maybe blow your nose again.”

“Oh my God...”

It wasn’t long before the two of them were out of their fancy clothes and into PJs, comfy on the couch with a pizza.

David was sounding more and more miserable by the minute. His nose now demanded his constant attention, and his coughs were getting more frequent and now coupled by whines as they irritated his sore throat.

“Fide,” David said with a thick sniffle. “I’m glad you talked be out goi’g out todight. I cad’t ibagide goi’g od a date like this.”

“Uh oh, you must be dying if you just admitted that I was right.”

David groaned. “Shut up,” he said, right before flopping against Patrick, probably rubbing his nose on his shoulder in the process.

Patrick could immediately feel the heat from David’s skin seeping through the fabric of his shirt. He frowned and laid a hand on David’s forehead, his husband not even bothering to move away despite it being in his way of the movie they were watching.

Patrick dropped his hand with a sigh. “You’re kind of warm.”

“Cold, actually...” David whined.

Patrick kissed his head. “Want me to go pick up some medicine?”

David shook his head against him.

“You sure? It might help you sleep tonight.”

“Later...” a sniffle. “You’re warbm.”

-----

David gave off an air of misery that was probably clear to all patrons at the store. Patrick didn’t know why he insisted on coming with him, except maybe that he... that he didn’t want to be alone. He knew he was in for a few days of a clingy and miserable David, and Patrick decided he was prepared.

David was wrapped in his warmest sweater, his arms crossed across his chest to retain as much heat as possible as he followed Patrick around the store.

He coughed, pulling his collar over his mouth as he did so. Patrick grabbed some cough medicine, along with a couple of other things that should help him feel better. That may have included alcohol.

David grabbed a bag of chips on their way to checkout and threw it with the various cold supplies on the counter. Of course, Patrick thought fondly.

When they got back in the car, David gave a coughing fit that he must have been holding back.

“You sound terrible,” Patrick said.

“Ugh. Thadks. Thadks for that. Love you too.”

He chuckled. “Let’s get home and get you medicated.”
