Title: Working
Fandom: Queer Eye RPF
Summary: Jonathan is sick and the gang helps out
Word count: 2.9k

Working

If there was one thing Jonathan was not, it was quiet. So why - in the world - wasn’t he talking. No one said anything, but they were all giving each other looks, silently wondering what was up. Was he upset?

Jonathan shifted, bumping his colleagues in the process. He couldn’t get comfortable no matter how hard he tried. He made a noise the others could only describe as a whimper, and folded himself over his stomach.

"Bobby, could you pull over for just a second?"

"Why? Everything okay?" Bobby didn’t bother looking back

"Please…"

Antoni looked at him. "Uh, better do it fast. I think he's carsick."

Bobby quickly pulled over and looked back at Jonathan, who was still completely curled in on himself. He held his eyes shut as if he was trying to stave off the inevitable.

Slowly, he pressed his hand to his mouth and opened the door with his other. He took a few steps into the grassy area next to the road before losing what little he had eaten for breakfast. Everyone looked away with a wince.

"Poor thing…" Tan muttered in sympathy.

"Maybe too much travel in one day," Antoni added.

"I dunno. He seemed off this morning," Karamo said.

They watched as Jonathan fell to his knees and dry heaved.

-----

"Got quite a fever..." Tan frowned as he felt Jonathan's forehead. He ran his fingers through his friend's hair as a comfort, and Jonathan leaned into his touch with a little smile.

"Oh, I'm sure it's fine." He dismissed with a little hand wave.

"I'm not." Bobby frowned.

As the evening went on, Jonathan got even quieter. They'd assume that he'd fallen asleep and then he'd shift or let out a whimper.

Tan kept feeling his forehead, frown getting deeper each time.

Meanwhile, Antoni and Karamo were out getting supplies. Everyone feared the worst, that Jonathan would still have to film the rest while he's this sick, and they needed to help him feel better any way they could until then.

"Okay," Antoni announced their return. "I got lots of gatorade. And lots of food."

"Of course you did." Tan chuckled.

Jonathan didn't say anything, only smiled. His smiles were getting more exhausted, that wasn't good.

"And Karamo bought a thermometer."

"And medicine," He said as he followed behind Antoni. "Because we need Jonathan's brain not melted."

-----

Jonathan went to bed far earlier than the rest of them, and with good reason. His stomach seemed to have settled, and now he was just left being worn out and feverish.

"Are you sure you're alright?" Tan had asked worriedly as he watched Jonathan stand up from the sofa. They'd been having a little movie night, a night to relax and spend time together, and make sure Jonathan was alright.

He looked back and gave a tired smile. "Yeah, I'll be fine! Just really sleepy."

Everyone nodded, saying goodnights and sleep wells. Jonathan dragged his body into his room. They heard the door click shut and all shared concerned looks.

"Is he really alright?" Bobby asked.

Karamo shrugged. "It's hard to tell."

Tan stayed silent and hoped Jonathan was being honest with them. They could never really tell when he was sick, or upset... He was too good at hiding his feelings beneath a thick layer of cheer.

It wasn't long before everyone had gone to bed too.

Tan woke up an hour later to lots of movement going on in the room next door. He didn't think anything of it until he heard a whimper, and then a badly stifled sob.

Tan threw his blankets back and shot out of bed. He gave Jonathan's door a courtesy knock before opening it right up and rushing to his friend.

The poor man was curled up holding his stomach, and looking quite embarrassed now that someone had heard him. An empty trash can was next to the bed, telling Tan his stomach ache hadn't entirely been taken care of.

"Jonathan..." Tan breathed out as he sat on the edge of the bed. He desperately wanted to pull his friend into a hug and hold him until he felt better, but Jonathan didn't look like he'd be down for that at the moment.

He put on a smile through the tears, still holding his stomach, and gave a self-deprecating chuckle. "Sorry, I'm sorry. It- it hurts..." The last part was lost in a low groan as a new cramp showed up. His eyes squeezed shut and Jonathan finally let himself lie in Tan's arms.

They stayed that way for a while. Tan hummed quietly, holding his friend. Jonathan's body was far too warm... Maybe he should get the thermometer again? Tan wasn't sure. He considered waking Karamo - he had kids, he knew how to care for people.

Eventually, Jonathan had ended up with his head in Tan's lap. He was nearly asleep, practically purring as his friend ran fingers through his hair. He suddenly shot up. His head barely made it over the bin before he was puking forcefully. There went everything Antoni and Karamo talked him into eating, there went the ginger ale Jonathan swore he could stomach.

"Oh, sweetheart..." Tan shook his head, rubbing Jonathan's back. He did his best to keep Jonathan's long hair out of the way.

He stayed leaned over the bed for a few seconds afterward, spitting into the bin and... Oh no, he was crying. Quiet sobs, little tears...

"I'm sorry," Jonathan muttered. "I thought-"

"Jonathan, you're sick and you've nothing to apologize for."

He chuckled despite himself, rubbing his eyes.

Tan patted his back. "You're just tired." He stood and grabbed the trash bin. "Think you're done?"

Jonathan hesitated, and then nodded. "Yeah. I don't think there's anything left…" He croaked.

 Tan smiled reassuringly. He took the bin and washed it, then returned to Jonathan's room. He was snuggled under blankets and still shivering like he couldn’t get warm.

"Thanks for… heh, everything. I love you."

"I love you too," Tan said with a little amused smile. He was about to leave when he heard a small noise from his friend. Tan turned to find Jonathan looking hesitant.

"Could you..."

"Mhm?"

"Naw, nevermind. Night, Tan."

"No no! Don't be like that. What did you want?"

Jonathan shook his head. "It was nothing."

"Want me to stay?" The look on his friend's face said it all. Tan felt warmth well up inside of him.

"You probably shouldn't. Germs and stuff, y'know?"

"I think we're well past that, don't you? Considering I just rinsed your vomit out of a bucket."

Jonathan cringed. "I am... So sorry."

Tan chuckled. "Scoot over."

-----

Tan woke later that morning feeling as if he hadn't slept at all. It took a while to actually fall asleep, although he was comfortable with Jonathan snuggling up to him. He was just on edge, constantly making sure that his friend was okay, that his fever wasn’t rising or that he wasn’t going to be sick again. The different bed, different room… it made things difficult.

Jonathan was fine through the night, Tan was a bundle of nerves.

His friend woke with a groan, curling in on himself and rubbing his eyes. He turned over to see Tan. Jonathan blinked.

"Oh. Hey," he said, giving a pleasant if not sleepy smile. He giggled hoarsely. "I forgot- I'm sorry about last night. You shouldn’t have had to…" A sigh. "But I'm glad you did."

Tan smiled. He was about to say something along the lines that it was no trouble helping his friend, but something got their attention.

"Oh!" Antoni stood in the doorway with a rather amused smirk on his face. "I was just gonna see if Jonathan wanted breakfast but uh… heh, I'll just…"

"Wait!" Tan said. "We weren't- oh God."

More giggling from the other side of the bed. Jonathan was starting to sound more like himself. "Oh well!"

-----

The day started by Jonathan struggling to keep breakfast down. He tried, he really did, but within an hour Antoni's omelet was being vomited into the toilet and they were left worrying as they listened to their friend whimper in discomfort.

"Jonathan?" Antoni called.

Bobby frowned. "Honey, are you okay?"

"I'm-" another gag, and liquid hitting liquid. The poor man couldn’t catch a break. "Give me a sec…" He croaked.

They decided to give him some space, instead waiting anxiously for him to emerge. Karamo came in from the balcony minutes later with a grim look on his face.

"What's up?" Tan asked.

"I uh, I have some bad news."

Tan could only guess what it was.

"They still want us to film today."

Antoni ran a hand down his face. "Oh God."

Bobby scoffed. "Did you tell them that that's not happening?!"

"I tried, alright? Look I don't think it's a good idea either, but it's just some intros and stuff and It won't take that long. If we get in and out as soon as we-"

"He can't even keep food down, let alone ride in a car!" Tan didn’t get irritated often, but…

"I know!" Karamo yelled back. "You think I didn't tell them that? We don’t have a choice!"

Antoni sighed heavily, leaning his elbows on the counter and hiding his face in his arms.

Bobby was livid. "This is ridiculous."

"I should go."

They all turned to Jonathan who was leaning against the doorway. His face was flushed with fever and exertion, and he look exhausted. Yet, he smiled brightly.

Antoni shook his head. "No, we can call the producers or something and postpone. There's no way they can make you go when you're this sick!"

Jonathan shrugged and padded into the room. He quickly seated himself on their sofa and got as comfortable as he could. "I mean, I feel horrible! But this is kind of what we're supposed to do, right? Our responsibility. Karamo's right anyway, it shouldn’t take… take long…" His eyes got wide, a hand slowly going to his mouth. Another arm snaked around his stomach.

Tan heard a muttered "uh-oh" from Antoni and knew the impending danger.

"Bathroom?" He asked. Jonathan quickly nodded, allowing himself to be lifted from the couch and led back to the bathroom.

They exchanged looks. It was going to be a long day.

-----

Jonathan ran to the studio bathroom for the second time since they arrived. He was looking worse and worse as time went on, and they weren't even halfway done recording.

Still, he insisted he could make it through the day.

Tan himself was nursing a pretty annoying headache. Tylenol helped for a little while, but not long enough. Last night's sleep was getting to him.

"Alright, now we'll wait for Jonathan get his makeup ready." The director was insistent that they finish this.

Jonathan returned, looking pale and worn. He sent a quick, desperate glance Tan's way before he went to get his makeup reapplied.

Tan sighed and walked over to him. "Do you need anything? I think Antoni brought a bottle of Gatorade in case you wanted it."

Jonathan huffed through his mouth. "Naw, I'll be okay." for now, Tan wanted to add.

He was okay for a while. His stomach had settled for the time being, but that could have been because there was nothing left.

Jonathan took frequent breaks, with his body being so fatigued and his muscles weak. Antoni had managed to pull him aside and get some fluids in him. Everything was going pretty well.

And then Jonathan nearly ended up on the floor when he tried to stand up. He would have if Karamo hadn't have caught him and helped him get his balance back.

"Alright," Karamo pressed his palm to Jonathan's forehead as he helped him sit back down. "I think you're done for today."

Tan insisted he go to a doctor. Jonathan probably wasn't in any immediate danger, getting dizzy was a normal thing when you were sick. But he wanted to make sure, and they needed to know they were doing things right.

Tan followed in the exam room while the rest stayed out. He dragged behind more than he was willing to admit. Today's events and his unfortunate sleep was really getting to him.

The nurse took Jonathan's temperature, his pulse, and everything else. If Jonathan was feeling himself, he'd probably be making some jokes about being touched so much.

"I understand you're not feeling so great?" The doctor said.

Jonathan chuckled. "I'm /so/ healthy right now."

The doctor smirked. "Oh yes, I see that."

In the end, he was dehydrated. Not too badly, thank goodness. But the doctor instructed Tan to keep giving him fluids.

The other three immediately stood up when the saw Tan leading their friend out of the exam room.

-----

Tan usually didn't mind all of them being squished in back. It was fun, and he'd learned he found it a little comforting.

Not today.

The only thing that got him through the stifling closeness was Jonathan laying his head on his shoulder. Tan was pretty sure he was asleep, and made a mental note to give him some Gatorade and tuck him in bed when they get home.

The worst of it seemed to be over for him. He'd spend a few days not so sure about food, and constantly ready for a nap, but Tan had a feeling he was done throwing up non stop. Hopefully.

Wow, throwing up was definitely not something he wanted to think about right now.

Tan kept his mouth shut in a thin line, realizing his stomach was really unhappy with Karamo's driving today.

His head complained about the noise of cars honking outside, and Bobby and Antoni's conversation about their most recent client.

Jonathan stirred, and his eyes slowly fluttered open. Head still on Tan's shoulder, he looked up.

"You good?" He asked quietly.

Tan just nodded. He didn't trust himself to speak right now. Not with the rising nausea and his angry stomach. Jonathan didn't look too convinced, either.

The minute they walked in the door, he was rushing to their loft's bathroom. He heard a "he must really have to go" joke from Antoni. Tan didn't bother explaining himself.

He stood in front of the toilet for a few miserable seconds, waiting. He hated this so much.

Finally, a heave brought up the half-digested omelet he'd eaten for breakfast. Oh God. He coughed, spat, and gagged once again. Seeing his stomach contents just made him feel more nauseated.

A few minutes pass afterwards, trying to make sure he's actually done for now.

With a defeated sigh, he flushed the toilet, washed his hands, and went to the kitchen to get a bottle of water.

That was where everyone was looking at him worriedly. Tan was sure he looked awful now.

"Sounds like the plague's spreading," Bobby joked half-heartedly.

"I think- I don't-" he couldn't deny it. Tan felt awful, and his stomach was still doing backflips. Bobby raised an eyebrow, obviously waiting for him to finish an excuse. He never did.

"Right. Well, I think it's time for the two of you to take it easy."

Jonathan, who was in the process of pulling on a looser t-shirt, stood in his doorway and looked at Tan with a guilty frown.

"Honey, I am so sorry..."

-----

The boys had managed to get both of them snuggled in bed together. Jonathan looked completely exhausted, and now guilty as well. No matter how many times Tan told him it wasn't his fault and these things happen, Jonathan would just smile, nod, and go back to being upset with himself.

Tan was having to make frequent trips to the bathroom to vomit, or unfortunately to hastily pull his pajama bottoms down. His stomach was always finicky when it wasn't hundred-percent. After the third time he'd vomited, Karamo was tired of seeing him nearly pass out in the hallway and sat the waste bin next to his bed.

Jonathan would rub his back comfortingly whenever he returned, knowing exactly how miserable this bug was.

Tan sighed at yet another stomach cramp. He sat up and pulled the covers back with an exhausted whimper.

"Hm?" Jonathan had finally started taking a nap. Now Tan felt guilty for waking him.

"Sorry, I didn't mean to wake you." He tried not to sound in pain. By Jonathan's sympathetic look, he was bad at it. "Stomach ache," he explained.

"You know, Karamo put a bin-"

"Not that kind of stomach ache, I'm afraid." Tan blushed furiously, and ran a hand down his face as he stood.

Jonathan just nodded in understanding, that same sympathetic look. Ugh, he was too good.

Tan left, and returned minutes later feeling ten times worse, which was saying something.

"You okay?" Jonathan asked, eyeing his friend as he got into bed.

Tan sighed and nodded. "IIt'll pass."

Tan was so tired, and his stomach refused to let him sleep. He was eager to get back into bed before his body fought him once again.

Jonathan draped an arm arou Ind him, humming contentedly. "I'm sorry about this."

Tan snorted. "Small price to pay for helping my friend!"

They ended up snuggled together, and falling asleep. Tan could vaguely recall someone feeling his and Jonathan's forehead, whispering about comparing their temperatures.

He remembered being woken up to drink, and Jonathan talking to someone about his condition.

Tan really hated being sick, but it was worth it.
