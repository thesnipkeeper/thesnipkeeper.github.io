Nice to Tea You

Jester had some fun, bouncy music playing, and she danced to it as she spread peanut butter on her toast. “Morning, Molly!”

“Goood morning, Jester!” He kissed her cheek as he passed her in the kitchen. “Are you excited?”

“Ohmigod, so excited! I’m going to buy so much food. What are you going to buy, Molly?”

“Mmm,” Molly screwed up his face in thought. “We’ll see. Part of the fun of farmer’s markets is people watching.”

The two of them had been planning to go to the farmer’s market together, to have a fun day amongst Jester’s studying and Molly’s rehearsals. Mostly Jester’s studying. Gustav let Molly get away with missing rehearsal all the time, bless his heart.

They danced together in the kitchen as they got their coffee and breakfasts prepared, giggling like children. Eventually, a disheveled Caleb wandered in. They each offered a cheery greeting, to which he winced at. Caleb wasn’t a morning person.

“Morgen,” he muttered, immediately gravitating toward the coffee maker.

“Are you sure you don’t want to come with us today, Caleb?” Jester asked between bites of toast.

He nodded as he filled his cup. “Ja, I am sure. Thank you, though.”

“Markets aren’t exactly your thing, hm?” Molly smiled, watching the sleepy Caleb find his way to a stool.

“No, but I hope the two of you have fun.” He sipped his coffee even though there was no way it was cool enough yet.

-----

The pair of tieflings happily wandered about the little farmer’s market. Jester had already purchased a plate of cookies from a nice, elderly gnome woman when they arrived. They’d made their way through half the plate.

“Ohh! Molly, there’s a tea stand.”

Molly followed her gaze and saw that there was indeed a little booth with different teas, and cute little mugs to go along with it.

“We have to go over, don’t we?”

Jester nodded with an affirmative sound.

Sitting at the booth was a firbolg man with pink hair. He was thin, almost alarmingly so. And of what Molly knew about firbolgs, he was sitting because he wouldn’t be able to stand in the low tent. Firbolgs were fucking tall.

“Hey,” he greeted, sitting up straight as they approached.

“Hellooo! What kind of tea do you have?”

“Oh, all sorts.”

“You know, his last name is Tealeaf,” Jester informed as she looked through all the different bags, throwing a thumb over her shoulder at Molly.

The firbolg blinked for a moment, gaze slowly going to Molly, and smiled. “Ah, that’s pretty cool. Hello.”

Molly smiled, nodded. “So you grow all of this yourself? That’s pretty amazing.”

“Yeah, it’s a nice hobby. Want to try some?” He was already pulling himself up, careful to remain hunched over in the tent. Molly wondered why he bothered getting a tent in the first place.

“Absolutely,” Molly said, at the same time Jester gave an enthusiastic “Yeah!”

The firbolg set out two sample cups and filled them each with tea from a thermos. He handed one to Molly, one to Jester, with a proud smile.

“Whoa!”

It was amazing tea. Molly told him as much. “Your hobby? What in the world are you doing for a job that isn’t selling this fine stuff?”

The firbolg sat back down. “Well, I help with the family business. The tea is just a way to relax, really.”

“What family business?” Jester asked.

“We own the cemetery outside of town.”

Jester choked on her second sip of tea. “O-oh!”

Molly cleared his throat. That wasn’t what he was expecting.

“Yeah, it’s nice and quiet there.”

“Dead people are very quiet,” Jester agreed.

“Usually,” the firbolg said. “I’m Caduceus, by the way.”

“I’m Jester! And this is Mollymauk Tealeaf.”

He chuckled and held out a hand. “Molly to my friends. Hello there, Caduceus!”

Caduceus’ hand was large, wrapping around Molly’s own as he shook his hand, and then Jester’s. And just like that, they’d made a new friend.

-----

Molly wasn’t sure how Jester managed to talk Caleb into coming with them to the market a week later. Although, she did have a way with people. It may have helped that Molly and Caleb were... together...ish? He wasn’t sure, and he didn’t mind that much.

But somehow, Caleb was walking around with them, looking incredibly uncomfortable. He had an expression that reminded Molly of a dog that was being held for too long and desperately wanted down. Caleb wanted to go home, but he was trying. The effort was what was important.

“Where is this firbolg you told me about, Jester?”

Or maybe he was just fanboying over a rare race that he didn’t see often. So that was how Jester convinced him.

“He was right over here last week, c’mon Caleb.” She grabbed onto his wrist and he let her drag him to the far side of the row of booths.

Molly followed behind the two with a chuckle.

Caduceus was indeed there, sitting in his booth. He seemed to be sipping some tea today, with a different air to him.

“Hi Caduceus!” Jester greeted. “This is our friend Caleb. He wanted to meet you!”

One edge of Caduceus’ mouth curled up in a lazy smile. “Hey-” he went to stand up, and bumped his head against the bar on the tent. Molly hissed in sympathy as they firbolg groaned and sat back down, hand to his head.

“Oh my gosh, are you okay?” Jester rushed back behind the booth without a second thought. “Can I look at your head? It’s okay, I’m a doctor!”

“Medical student,” Caleb coughed out.

Caduceus made a dismissive sound. “That’s not the first time that’s happened today, but sure.” He let Jester tilt his head up and inspect the growing bruise.

“Oh my,” Molly said, trying not to chuckle. “Maybe not the best place to be sitting, then.”

Caduceus nodded. He furrowed his brows for a moment, taking in a quick breath before turning far away from Jester and the table, coughing.

Jester pressed the back of her hand against Caduceus’ temple and frowned.

“Aw, you’re not feeling good, are you?”

Caduceus shook his head as he cleared his throat, and looked back up at Caleb with the same smile a before. “Sorry, Mr. Caleb. I’m Caduceus Clay, it’s nice to meet you.”

Caleb nodded a greeting, “You as well, Mr. Clay.”

Jester and Molly exchanged concerned looks. And if Jester was worried, that firbolg must’ve had some fever.

“Are you sure you’re alright?” Molly asked, folding his arms over his chest.

“Just a little cold,” Caduceus dismissed. “Nothing a little tea can’t fix. Or, a lot of tea, really,” he chuckled and took another sip.

Jester bit her lip, a fang peeking out as she did so. “Maybe you should go home and rest. You’re really warm, Caduceus. You know, last time Molly had a fever like that, he-”

“We don’t have to bring that up, do we?” Molly had spent ages trying to forget about his awful bout of bronchitis.

“Well, my point is that you could get sicker working out here.”

Molly couldn’t imagine anyone taking the advice of stranger, but clearly Caduceus wasn’t anyone. He hummed thoughtfully and nodded.

“That might be a good idea.” Molly wondered if he’d been having the same thought all day. He just needed a little push to go home.

“Good!” Jester said. “Do you want to tell us where you live so we can bring medicine later?”

Molly snorted. Caleb’s eyes were very wide.

Caduceus... nodded?

Man, they really attracted the adorable weirdos, huh?

“Sure.”

And just like that, they were friends with the firbolg from the cemetery.
