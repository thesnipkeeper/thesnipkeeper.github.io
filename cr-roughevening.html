Rough Evening

Both of them skipped lunch.

Caleb had seen Bryce exhausted plenty of times. The bookstore would be so busy some days that neither of them would get a chance to eat lunch. His body was used to skipping meals, but they made more of an effort to be well-nourished, skipping meals slowed them down. Exhausted, hungry, Caleb had seen them... But this time was different.

Bryce didn’t take the lull in customers as a chance to get a bite. Instead, they excused themselves and returned a few minutes later looking flushed and voice sounding thick.

Caleb questioned it, as causal as possible, and Bryce just smiled. "A little tired today."

Caleb didn’t catch on until he saw his boss rushing off to the back with a hand clamped over their mouth.

"You're sick, then," he stated when Bryce returned with red-rimmed eyes and a runny nose.

Bryce nodded, not even bothering to hide it. They grabbed a tissue from the counter and wiped their nose, sniffling.

In the end, Caleb insisted Bryce rest in the back and let him do most of the work. They closed the shop an hour early that night and both went home.

The phone started ringing that evening. He was getting some late-night reading done before going to bed. Jester and Beau were camped in the living room as well, Fjord and Nott having already gone to bed and Molly and Yasha out working at the carnival.

"Hello?" Caleb answered hesitantly, unsure of what a phone call at this time of night could mean.

"Ah, Caleb, you're awake! It's Bryce."

Caleb was relieved to hear his boss' name instead of that of a stranger, but Bryce still had a tone in their voice that kept the pang of anxiety lingering in his gut. They sounded sicker than before.

"Well, it's good to talk to you, but I can't help but wonder why you're calling me so late."

There was a throat clearing. "Caleb, I have a favor to ask." Caleb swallowed. "I'm... I haven't been feeling well this evening and I'm not sure how I'll be feeling by tomorrow. Do you think you could open the store tomorrow, handle it without me?"

The anxiety grew, forming a stone that began to rise in his throat. "I..."

Bryce stopped him. "It's alright if you're uncomfortable with it. I could probably figure out-"

"Ja, I'll do it." He needed this job, he needed to do this. Bryce needed to be able to rely on him. How bad could it be anyway? And he thought of Bryce working earlier that day, how out of it they were.

A pause. A sigh. "Thank you, so much, Caleb. Do you mind if I stop by and drop off the key tonight?"

Bryce was even more pale than before. They looked exhausted like they'd been sick constantly since they'd gotten home.

"Thank you so much, Caleb." Bryce looked as if they wanted to hug him, so much that Caleb braced himself.

He took the keys with a smile. "Of course." A pause. He should say something. "Are you feeling any better?"

Bryce hesitated, and it gave just enough time for Jester to pop up.

"Are you sick, Bryce? I could help! I'm almost a doctor, you know."

"Do not let her help."

Bryce huffed out a tired chuckle. "It's..." A sigh. "...been a rough evening." They smiled. "I'm just hoping it's a twenty-four hour bug."

Jester frowned sympathetically. "Hope you feel better, Bryce."

They said their goodbyes and well wishes as Bryce left. Caleb looked down at the keys in his hand.

This was definitely going to be a new experience.

-----

The day went by without a hitch. Caleb was surprised how much he enjoyed himself. Though he missed Bryce and having someone else to work with, he was comfortable in the store by himself. Things were easy and he knew the routine and processes by now.

He was just finishing with a customer when the door opened with a jingle.

"Look at you," Molly grinned, fangs showing. He was still in his performance clothes, a mess of patterns and colors. "You seem to be enjoying yourself."

"it's been a good day."

Molly hummed happily and leaned over the counter, getting close enough to Caleb to cause him to instinctually take a step back. "Just thought I'd stop by and see how things were. Jester said you looked a little freaked out last night. But you seem to be in your element." he looked around. "This is a cute little shop, isn't it?"

Caleb made a noise of agreement. "You've never been here?"

"Well, Yasha and I haven't lived here for very long." Molly shrugged. "Anyway, I'm not much of a reader."

Now that Caleb thought about it, he really had never seen Molly read a book or newspaper. He knew that some people just didn’t enjoy that sort of thing, but it was hard for Caleb to grasp sometimes.

"I could find a book you might enjoy, if you'd like?"

Molly shook his head. "No, that's alright."

As conversation faded, Molly held up a brown paper bag. "You haven't eaten lunch yet, have you?" it was nearly closing time, but no, Caleb hadn't. He didn’t have his boss reminding him to take a lunch break. So they ate together.

And then the phone rang. He and Molly were just finishing up when Caleb shot up to answer, giving the shop's official phone greeting.

"Caleb, it's Jester," the familiar accent had Caleb giving Molly a wide-eyed look.

What? Molly mouthed.

"I'm at the hospital and Bryce is here and I think you should probably come see them because no one is visiting and they drove themselves and I don’t think they have any friends and I feel really bad and-"

"Bryce is in the hospital?"

Molly stood up.

"Yeah, are you done at the shop yet? Because they look lonely and like, super sick."

"Jester," Caleb said. "Are they alright?"

"What? Oh, yes of course. They're fine."

Caleb sighed and gave Molly a nod.

"Well, they're fine now, but like, they had an appendectomy earlier."

"Appendicitis? What-"

Molly yanked the phone from his hand, earning himself an annoyed glance from Caleb. "We'll be there as soon as possible."

-----

Jester met them at the door, looking rather cute in her uniform, and led them to a hospital room.

Bryce looked rough, but they smiled as soon as the two of them walked in. They all talked for a while until Bryce started nodding off.

"We should let you get some rest, I think."

Bryce smiled sleepily. "Thank you for watching the store, Caleb. And thank you both for visiting."

"We're always here if you need anything, you know," Molly said with a softness that Caleb loved.

With as much fun as Caleb had watching the store that day, he froze when he was asked to do it for a week. Molly seemed to see the look on his face.

"Do you mind if I help a bit? Without pay, of course."

And they spent the week running the bookstore together.

It was perfect.
