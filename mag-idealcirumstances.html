Ideal Cirumstances

Martin knew it was going to be a long day when he woke and immediately vomited over the side of his bed.

Maybe it was just something he ate, Martin thought. Except, he knew the truth. A nasty stomach bug had been going around, and of course he’d catch it because the universe hates him.

He cleaned up the best he could without triggering another bout of vomiting and finished getting ready for the day, taking extra care to brush his teeth.

He skipped breakfast entirely.

Driving to work did not sound ideal, but he didn’t have a choice. He wasn’t one to use sick days, not since... well, not since Jane Prentiss. It didn’t feel right. So, Martin grabbed the small trash bin from his living room, lined it with a bag, and decided to take it along with him. At least then he’d be prepared if something unfortunate were to happen.

Martin’s stomach gurgled angrily with every turn he had to make on the way to the Archives. He winced, allowed himself to whimper as long as he was alone. 

His insides were cramping like nothing he’d felt in years. He’d probably need to use the restroom soon, Martin realized with disgust. 

Maybe taking a sick day would have been a good idea, but it was too late now, wasn’t it?

“Hello, Jon!” He greeted his boss as they passed each other in the hallway.

“Martin,” was Jon’s only greeting back. That was alright, it was early, and Jon wasn’t much of a morning person. Or afternoon person. Or a night person. 

Still, Martin smiled and went to set his things down in his office. The bin under his desk offered a little peace of mind, that would be for emergencies only. 

Martin really, really hated stomach bugs. Colds he could deal with. You might be sneezing and sniffling non-stop, but at least you can still function without periodically evacuating your stomach contents.

Martin got to work immediately. He enjoyed researching, it gave him an opportunity to focus on something and feel truly useful in a job he was unqualified for.

At some point, he needed to stop. Martin doubled over, folding both arms over his abdomen and pressing them in, hoping that would somehow ease the pressure in his gut. He couldn’t tell if he was nauseated or if he needed to... go. Possibly both. 

The cramps got lower. The answer was clear what he’d been doing in the bathroom first.

Martin winced as he stood up. He hadn’t realized how nauseated he truly was until he started moving. Dizzy too, apparently. Fuck.

So that was where Martin found himself an hour into work. Doubled over on the toilet while he waited for his stomach to evacuate everything inside of him.

Martin tried not to make noise this time, but the pain was unbearable. It was just a matter of time before he started throwing up as well. 

He was dreading it.

His stomach gurgled angrily, and the cramps dissipated for a bit. Martin was very much trying not to cry. He felt awful even when the pain was gone.

This certainly was going to be a long day.

-----

Martin retreated to the bathroom every hour or so, whenever he felt like he was going to throw up, or felt an urgency in his lower intestines. He was thankful that Jon’s office door was closed whenever he passed it. This was embarrassing enough without his boss knowing exactly how many trips to the bathroom he’s taken.

It was still manageable, thank goodness. He was capable of working, at least. Maybe he could convince Jon to let him go home a little early, but Martin wasn’t going to count on it. He’d survive the work day. It may not be pleasant, but he’d survive.

Martin took a sip of tea, a nice ginger tea he’d found in the break room cupboard. He wasn’t sure if it was actually helping, but the placebo would be good enough.

Until that tea wanted to come right back up not a half hour later.

Martin whimpered and clamped a hand over his mouth, walking briskly to the restroom. He barely got in front of the toilet before he was throwing up the sickly-colored liquid. Could he ever drink tea again after this?

The first bout was over, and he didn’t feel quite... done. Martin sank to the floor and leaned his back against the cold metal wall. He was tired. Throwing up took all the energy he had, and the exhaustion made him realize how awful he really felt. 

Martin groaned, hugging his stomach as it cramp. Come on, just get it over with already...

It was getting there. Not quick enough, but he could feel the nausea building and now he just wanted to throw it all up until there was nothing left to disturb his work.

Martin was in the process of feeling very bad for himself when the bathroom door slammed open and someone rushed into the stall next to him. The sounds of a belt unbuckling and pants unzipping were almost too quick to catch, and they sat down.

The sounds that followed were the sickening sounds that were familiar to Martin by now. Whoever it was was most likely just as sick as he was.

Wait...

He recognized those shoes. 

Martin wondered briefly if he should say anything, but... no, that’s a ridiculous invasion of privacy. He’d just leave without saying a word, and-

Martin rushed to get over the toilet. A mix of tea and bile made its way up his throat and splashed against the water with a sickening noise. 

“...Martin...?” Jon’s voice was a miserable mix of confusion and embarrassment.

Martin coughed, gagging up one last bit before his gut decided it was done for a bit. 

He had a feeling Jon hadn’t realized anyone else was in there.

“Mm,” was Martin’s only reply as he wiped the back of his mouth.

Jon sighed heavily. Martin was inclined to agree. 

Martin left the bathroom first, retreating back to his office. He heard the bathroom door open about ten minutes later, and soon an exhausted-looking Jonathan Sims was standing in the doorway.

He set a green bottle on his desk, and looked sheepish when Martin furrowed his brows in confusion.

Jon cleared his throat. “Ginger ale. Um... I’m aware that these aren’t the most... ideal circumstances.”

“But you need me to work,” Martin finished.

Jon sighed and nodded. “There’s... there’s a lot to do, and I can’t make you stay when you’re ill.”

“You shouldn’t be here either, Jon.”

“Yes, well, I have a lot of work to do.”

Martin wanted to argue with him, to insist that a sick person needed rest. Martin was used to working through illnesses. He couldn’t always afford to take sick days in the past, and he’d learned the process to deal with it well. He wondered if Jon had as well.

“I apologize about, er, about earlier. I didn’t realize you were-”

“It’s a bathroom, Jon.”

He swallowed, looking quite nauseated. “Right. Well, this is incredibly awkward so I’m going to go back to my office now. Think I left the tape recorder on...” That last part was mumbled under his breath as he left.

Martin huffed out a chuckle. it was terrible, but seeing Jon so embarrassed and vulnerable was enough to make him feel better.
