Title: Clingier
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick likes seeing the "big brother" side of David
Word count: 1k

Clingier

David had mentioned it, and Patrick had noticed it himself. Alexis hated being alone.

It was obvious to pretty much everyone that she’d been having a hard time since she and Ted broke up. She was... “clingier”, as David had put it, and Patrick was inclined to agree.

David showed up to work one morning looking particularly exasperated.

“Alexis is probably going to show up at some point today,” he said with a heavy sigh, throwing his things in the back room. “She’s a total mess, I don’t know what’s up with her.” He waved a hand lazily through the air, shaking his head.

“I hope she realizes that we’re going to be putting her to work,” Patrick chuckled.

They had a big delivery of new products that they were planning on setting out after closing, and they’d agreed that if Alexis came while they were trying to work, she would now be helping. Admittedly, it was because they thought she needed a distraction. They didn’t really need the help at all, but watching her get all in her head as she hung out was rough.

“Oh my God, she’d better.” David had been acting all tired of her, but Patrick could tell that he was worried. He’d been on edge ever since her little... rebound guy situation, and had been staying at the motel far more than usual.

It wasn’t until David had just flipped the sign from ‘open’ to ‘closed’ that his sister walked up and waved at him through the window.

She looked... slightly wrong? Her hair wasn’t as styled as usual, she wasn’t wearing heels, and she was wearing a sweater over a dress. Like she’d just gotten dressed to come to their store.

Alexis greeted them and proceeded to start telling them about something stupid Roland had said at the cafe. Her voice had a certain thickness to it. Had she been crying?

“M’kay, that’s nice, but can you take this?” David set a box in her arms and walked away.

“Ugh, David...” but surprisingly, she started going to work setting them out. Not the right way, but at least she was trying. “So anyway, I don’t think I ever want to eat again thanks to him.”

Patrick laughed. “He seems to have that effect on people.”

“Ugh, right?” She sniffled and dragged the side of her hand under her nose.

At first Patrick thought she was crying. He prepared to run and get David, not sure exactly how to handle his fiance’s sister quite yet.

“snf! Oh my God...”

Patrick couldn’t help but chuckle to himself, mostly out of relief. She wasn’t crying, she just had a runny nose.

“Getting a cold?” he tried.

Alexis growled. “Yes, it’s the worst. Oh, thank you...” she accepted the tissue box Patrick sat next to her and immediately pulled out a handful to blow her nose.

Of course that was when David finally emerged from the back room.

“I’m sorry, did I just hear that you’re sick?” He asked with disgust. Alexis still had tissues pressed to her face, but the eyeroll was very obvious. “Get your germy hands off of my merchandise!”

Alexis growled, snuffled. “Oh my God, David, I’m trying to help.”

“You’re not helping if you start an entire pandemic in this town!” David said, gesticulating wildly. “And since when do you want to help anyway?”

Alexis sneezed, David recoiled.

“Okay,” he turned to Patrick. “We have to disinfect everything.”

“David, I think it’ll be fine.”

“No, it won’t!”

Alexis sighed and threw her hands up in the air. “Fine! Fine, you know what? You don’t need my help!”

Patrick realized one thing, and that was that this was the time to step away and let the siblings squabble. He continued his work with his head down.

“Okay, you didn’t even put them facing the right way...” David muttered, taking a tissue from the box and turning everything without having to touch them.

When Alexis didn’t respond, Patrick looked up again, and saw that she was crying this time.

David caught his eyes first, and followed his worried gaze to his sister. He gasped, and then sighed.

“It’s- it’s not even that bad. We can fix this tomorrow...” he tried, but Alexis was already tearing up and wow, Patrick had no idea what to do right now.

It completely warmed his heart, however, when Alexis leaned in, and David immediately wrapped her in a hug.

“O-okay...what is up with you today...?” he mumbled, shaking his head to himself as Alexis cried into his shoulder.

Alexis responded with a muffled, “I don’t know.”

And they stayed like that for a while, David occasionally rubbing her back and looking a little helpless.

Patrick smiled.

Eventually, they pulled away, David laying the back on his hand to her forehead. “Oh my God, did you seriously walk here with a fever?”

Alexis shook her head and scrubbed at her eyes and nose with the wad of tissues in her hand. “I’m fine, it’s not that bad.”

“Um, I think it is.” He looked to Patrick, and Patrick just smiled and nodded. They could come in earlier tomorrow and finish this (and inevitably end up disinfecting the box of products, because David definitely wasn’t going to forget about that).

“M’kay, I’m taking you home. Come on.” He started to walk away, backtracked to grab the tissue box, and shoved it at his sister. “Don’t get germs in the car, though, I swear to God.”

Patrick watched the two walk out, and chuckled. He didn’t understand how Alexis worked like he did his fiance, but he had a feeling that he would, in time. David seemed to understand her more than most people.

He grabbed the disinfectant wipes from under the counter and went to work. Less work for David in the morning, and admittedly, Patrick didn’t want to end up with germs all over the store either.

Not that it mattered.

The next morning David would show up with a runny nose, and he’d complain about his sister the entire day, and inevitably go home early when he can hardly get anything done between sneezing and sanitizing his hands.

Patrick would bring soup and cold medicine for the two siblings and never mention how cute his fiance looked with a red nose.
