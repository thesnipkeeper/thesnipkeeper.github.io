Title: Need Anything
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Alexis is sick, David worries
Word count: 1.6k

Need Anything

David woke to sudden movement on his sister’s side of the room. He groaned initially, because how dare she wake him up when it was still dark! Why was she awake when it was still dark, anyway?

He tried to go back to sleep, and he almost succeeded until he heard a terrible gagging sound from the bathroom. He unfortunately knew what his sister sounded like when she threw up, maybe it was part of being siblings, who knew, but he couldn’t tamp down the concern.

God, couldn’t David just be annoyed at his sister for waking him up instead of... worried? Ugh.

Accepting that he wasn’t going back to sleep any time soon, David grabbed his phone and started scrolling idly. He hoped Alexis would come back out after a couple of minutes. Maybe this was some one-time thing that wasn’t a big deal at all. It wouldn’t have been the first time the cafe’s horrible leftovers would strike them with food poisoning.

Alexis didn’t emerge. It had been nearly an hour, David realized. An hour that he hadn’t gotten to sleep, and an hour that Alexis had spent throwing up. There was no going to sleep. He had to- ugh, he had to check on her.

David sighed heavily and got out of bed. Alexis’ bed, he realized, was more messed up than it usually was. She seemed to have gotten one of the spare blankets, except it was kicked to the end of the bed while the duvet hung off. She must not have been feeling well for a while.

He bit his lip and knocked on the bathroom door. “You okay?” he asked rather awkwardly. It wasn’t as if he expressed concern for her so readily, or as if they had conversations with each other while the other was in the bathroom.

“Yeah,” came the croaked reply. She cleared her throat, but it didn’t help. “Yeah, I’m good.”

“Are you sure? Because it seemed like you’ve been puking a lot in the past hour.”

“Ew David, were you listening to me?”

“You woke me up! I couldn’t go back to sleep!”

He heard a heavy sigh on the other side of the door that morphed into a groan. And then there was a click as the door swung open. She returned to her apparent spot, sitting on the edge of the tub, leaning over the toilet.

“I don’t feel good,” she whined, as if that wasn’t abundantly clear already. “David...” whatever she was going to whine at him was cut off by a small burp, followed by a gag and a trickle of liquid into the water below her.

He cringed in disgust and took a couple steps back. “Okay, alright, um... is there,” he sighed. He was going to regret asking, he knew already. “Is there anything that I can do?”

“Can I have some water?” She sounded so pathetic, and some of that was definitely an act, but not all of it.

David wordlessly nodded and retrieved said water. He returned to Alexis in the exact same position, doubled over with her arms wrapped around her middle. She was sweating, which was probably why the blankets were all messed up on her bed.

“Can you sit with me, David?” she asked, patting the edge of the tub.

“Ew, and watch you vomit all night?”

“Please? I don’t want to be alone right now.” Oh God, she was so tragic.

He gave a loud, heavy sigh. “Fine, whatever.” Before taking a seat next to her. “You should really be careful with that water, by the way, because it could make your throw up more.”

“Ugh, I know, David.”

She didn’t take his advice, of course, because she was puking it up within a matter of minutes. David winced, wanting very much not to be there but also wanting to for some reason? Ugh.

She whimpered, heaving uselessly. It was pretty clear that there was nothing left, at least for the time being.

“I think you’re done for a while,” he offered, and she nodded in agreement.

“Will you carry me back to bed?”

David scoffed at that. “Absolutely not, no.”

“Ugh!”

“Quit whining! I will help you back to bed. I refuse to carry anyone.”

“Because you can’t?” she asked, giving a tired smirk.

“Shut up, of course I can. Go- go brush your teeth or something.”

Alexis rolled her eyes and did so, moving slowly from the tub to the sink. David used his foot to flush the toilet, making an effort not to look at the contents.

And then he did indeed help her to bed. He wasn’t about to carry her, hadn’t done so since they were kids, and even then, all she really wanted was to be tossed onto the couch like a crazy child.

He set the trash bin from the bathroom next to her bed, determined to not have to deal with cleaning up vomit or convincing anyone else if she couldn’t make it in time.

“Get some sleep,” it came out far softer and kinder than he intended.

“Thanks, David.”

-----

She slept in the next morning, no surprise there when she’d been up sick half the night. David was tired, tempted to sleep in as well, but he figured that he should probably... do something helpful. God, who was he?

He wandered into the lobby where Stevie was working hard at reading a novel.

"Do you have any Gatorade or something with like, electrolytes I can borrow?"

She raised an eyebrow, giving a chuckle as she set her book aside. "Ummm, I can check the fridge in the back, maybe I have something back there." Stevie blinked. "…Why exactly do you need it?"

David cleared his throat and averted his eye. “So, Alexis is pretty sick, and I just thought that she should probably drink something substantial before she dehydrates or something.” He was talking with his hands, and he couldn’t stop himself, even when he knew how nervous it made him look.

He didn’t miss the flash of concern on Stevie’s face before it went back to the usual apathy. Something was going on with the two of them, he wasn’t sure what, but he could see it when they interacted. Alexis was charming, and Stevie was... possibly falling for it.

She disappeared into the back and emerged with a green can that she set on the desk. "Sprite. That's the best I can do."

David took it. “Thanks so much.”

“We have a vending machine, you know,” she muttered as she returned to her book.

“Yeah, but like, those cost money.”

“Ah,” she scoffed. “My mistake.” David was about to leave when he heard a, “Tell Alexis that I hope he feels better.” It was just as deadpan as anything else Stevie said, but David couldn’t help but grin as he left.

-----

There was one thing the two of them had a blast doing together, and that was Buzzfeed quizzes. Alexis and David each sat cross legged on their beds, incredibly focused as they answered questions to see what color represented them the most.

Focused until there was a knock on the door. “Come in,” David called without taking his eye from his phone.

“I bring gifts!” Patrick announced. “Well, mostly sports drinks and saltines.”

David smiled, relieved to see his boyfriend. “Thank you,” he said before exchanging a kiss. Patrick sat the grocery bag on their table and began unpacking it.

“Patrick!” Alexis squealed. “Look at you, being so sweet like always.”

Patrick chuckled. “Thank you thank you, I do try. Here,” he took a blue bottle and cracked the lid before handing it over. “That’s the best flavor.”

“It totally is,” Alexis agreed after taking a sip. “So are you guys like, going somewhere?”

“Naw,” Patrick shrugged. “Thought I’d come and hang out with you two tonight if that’s okay?”

“Um, of course!”

David smiled. He warned Patrick how needy and annoying his sister was when she was sick. More needy and annoying than usual, anyway. But he swore that he didn’t mind. He was going to regret that later, but for right now...

What a sweetheart.

“So what are you up to?” Patrick asked as he plopped down next to David.

“Oh, well I’m about to find out which Friends character I am,” David informed.

“Ah,” Patrick nodded with a smirk. “Important stuff.”

“Oh yes.”

He looked up at Alexis, “And how are you feeling?” he asked it so casually, as if he’d been around the two of them for ages. David both loved and hated how comfortable Alexis was with his boyfriend. It was definitely going to result in some schemes.

“Not great,” she admitted with a wince. “But I’m sure that I’ll feel better tomorrow.”

“Yeah,” Patrick nodded. “I uh, wouldn’t count on it.”

She raised an eyebrow.

“I’ve heard people talking around town, and it sounds like the bug going around lasts like a few days.”

“Oh my God,” she groaned.

“But! But, you get an excuse to relax, which is pretty nice, right?”

“Not helping,” David whispers.

“I hate that! Ugh!” Alexis huffed and took an angry sip of gatorade.

Patrick chuckled. “If you need anything, just let me know.”

David wanted to warn him not to say that, but it was too late. Oh well, he would learn eventually. He sighed and leaned against his boyfriend as Patrick and Alexis talked, mainly Alexis complaining about how awful she was feeling.

His attention was captured when there was a hand on his forehead and a worried hum. Patrick pulled away from him, much to his disappointment, and looked him over.

“You’re kind of warm,” he said, this time setting the inside of his wrist against David’s forehead.

That was the first time that he realized he did feel a little... cold and shaky. He ached a little bit, but he just figured it was sleep deprivation from being up the night before. But it was all but confirmed that whatever Alexis had was contagious.

David closed his eyes and growled. “Fuck.”
