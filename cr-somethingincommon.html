Something in Common

Jester settled into her beautiful bedroom in Caleb’s tower. She took a deep breath, smiling, and sat on her bed with her eyes closed.

“So, traveler, what have you been up to today?” He didn’t always respond, and now she knew that it was because he was sometimes busy.

That was fine, it just felt good to pray- talk to him sometimes. She felt a light gust of air that signaled the arrival of someone next to her, and suddenly there was a second person’s weight on the edge of the bed. She gasped happily and turned to him.

“You came!” She exclaimed.

Artagan chuckled and pulled his hood down to reveal his unruly red curls. “I did. Hello, Jester.” His voice sounded different than usual. He sounded tired.

“Hello! Are you alright? You sound weird.”

Another chuckle. “I’m perfectly fine, just a little tired. Had a lot to do today.”

“Oh really? Like what?”

“Oh, all sorts of things. I was rather busy!” His jovial grin faded for a moment into exhaustion.

It bothered her seeing him like this after knowing the elusive and strong figure she’d grown up with. At the same time, Jester’s heart warmed that she was the one person he was willing to let down his facade around.

With a sympathetic smile, she laid a hand on his back and began rubbing gently. It was so strange feeling his body heat. It was warmer than other people, but perhaps that came with being as amazing and skilled as he was.

He hummed, and then sighed. “I’m sorry, Jester. I can’t stay long. I must...”

Jester gasped in horror as she watched her god stand up and immediately crumple to the ground. “Traveler!” She rushed to his side, flipping him on his back and cupping his cheek. His face was hot, cheeks deep red. Fever. He had a fever! Why didn’t she see that sooner?! “Oh fuck oh fuck oh fuck...” she mumbled. “Please wake up. Please, please...”

His brows knitted together as he gave a discomforted groan, but his eye remained closed.

Jester struggled to hold back tears. This wasn’t the time to cry, this was the time to get help. “Caduceus!”

-----

The Traveler looked so small and fragile when he was asleep and ill, Jester thought. She watched Caduceus lay a hand over Artagan’s forehead, and then the back of his fingers against Artagan’s neck. He hummed thoughtfully.

“Yeah,” he said with a deep sigh. “That’s a fever. That’s a really bad fever. We should keep an eye on him through the night.” He didn’t sound worried, but Caduceus rarely did.

“I don’t have any spells left...” she lamented.

“Me either,’ he said. “But that’s alright. Rest is the best medicine.”

The Traveler moaned and shifted. He was shuddering violently underneath the blankets. His breathing was quick and shallow. He wasn’t resting. He wasn’t comfortable in the least.

Jester bit the inside of her mouth when her lip started to quiver. Suddenly every fear rushed her mind, fears of losing her longest and - for most of her life - only friend. A large hand squeezed her shoulder, and she looked up to see Caduceus offering a kind, warm smile.

“Really, he’ll be fine. He’s got two amazing clerics, after all.”

Jester chuckled a bit before wrapping an arm around his waist and hugging him as tightly as possible. “Thank you, Caduceus,” she said with a sad sniff.

-----

Caduceus dabbed the man’s forehead with a cool cloth, shushing him gently as he shivered. “It doesn’t feel good, but it’ll help you feel better,” he promised.

“Hey,” Beau greeted as she walked in. “Jester told me what happened.”

Caduceus nodded, and then sighed. “Yeah. How is she?”

She shrugged, unable to take her eyes off of the Traveler lying in Jester’s bed. He didn’t blame her, it was a strange sight. “I mean, she’s pretty upset, but she’s doing her thing where she pretends like she’s not crying...” a sigh. “I told her to get some sleep, that we’d take care of everything.”

“Good, good...” Caduceus hummed.

“So uh, how long have you been at this?”

“Oh, I don’t know.” He wasn’t the best at tracking time, after all. “Since Jester left, at least.”

“Deuces, that was two hours ago.”

“Yeah?”

“Yeah, let me take over for a while.”

Caduceus looked at the feverish man before him. Maybe Beau had a point. He couldn’t exactly help Artagan in the morning if he didn’t get some rest.

“Okay,” he said.

She patted his shoulder and he stood up from his seat next to the bed. “It’ll be fine. I’ll just ask the cats if I need anything,” Beau said with a chuckle.

-----

Beau wasn’t sure how long she’d sat next to the bed, watching Artagan stir and whimper and... lots of vulnerable things she never would have expected from him. Could fake gods get sick? Well, apparently they could.

He moaned, brows knitting together as he stirred. His face was covered in a sheen of sweat, Beau hoped that meant the fever had broken. Still, she continued to dab at his forehead and neck in a hope that it would do any different at all.

His chapped lips parted for a weak, “J’ster?”

“ ‘fraid not, man.”

Artagan’s eyes opened, immediately finding her. He huffed out a weak sigh and looked away. “Beauregard.”

“Yup.”

“Is she alright? I think I may have scared her.”

“Yeah, you totally scared her,” Beau snapped, and then softened just a bit. Hostility wasn’t necessary when he was the one laid up. “But yeah... she’s fine.”

He struggled to keep his eyes open, but did so enough the green slits were looking at her. A smile, tired smile on his lips as he said, “Are you going to kill me?”

Beau laughed. “Yeah, I mean I’ve been sitting at your bedside all this time just so I can kill you when you wake up.”

Artagan chuckled, or tried to, but it resulted in a weak cough. He frowned. “I... think I was dreaming about cats.”

It was comedic timing that one of the tower’s many servants chose that moment to make its appearance with a glass of water. Artagan’s eyes widened as Beau thanked the cat and accepted the glass.

“Don’t worry, they’re supposed to be here.”

“I’m not hallucinating?”

“Naw... damn it, it would’ve been funny if I said you were.” She mentally punched herself for not thinking of it.

Another weak laugh. “Next time.”

He blinked wearily at the ceiling. They had a quiet few seconds before Beau cleared her throat. “So, you should probably sit up and drink some water. You’re sweating a lot.”

He nodded and went to sit up, but the Traveler stopped just as his head was off of the pillow before lying back down. “Ohhh...”

“Dizzy?”

“Dizzy, weak...” He muttered with a frustrated huff.

Beau sighed. “Okay fine, let me help.”

Every fiber of her being was telling her not to help this guy. He was a certified asshole. But he was Jester’s friend, and as much as Beau hated the Traveler, she loved Jester far more. She gripped his forearm and helped pull him up until his back was against the headboard of the bed.

“Tha-” his voice died, and he had to pause to clear it, “Thank you.” He still looked so sick and out of it. Beau almost felt bad for him. Almost.

“No problem. Here,” she held the glass out in front of her until he took it in his own shaky grip. She thought maybe her job was about done until the glass started to slip from his hand. “Fuck!” she managed to catch it just in time, but Beau could swear she saw an embarrassed flush on the Traveler’s face that had nothing to do with the fever.

She wasn’t actually going to... help him, was she? Sure, it was easy when he was asleep and helpless, but her feelings toward him had certainly come back when he woke.

Still, he needed to drink.

For Jester, Beau reminded herself.

So, she set her hand underneath the bottom of the glass, effectively keeping it steady as he guided it to his mouth. She helped him to tilt it just enough to drink, though some dribbles did make their way down his chin and shirt. He made an annoyed noise, but kept drinking. Apparently the thirst brought on by the fever was more important than his dignity right now.

He gently nudged the glass away, so Beau set it aside.

Things were silent again, and Beau had something to say now that he was conscious.

“You really freaked her out, you know.”

“I can imagine.” His expression changed to something sadder and more aware. “I must have... overexerted myself by the time she called me.”

Beau huffed. “Look man, I don’t know what she’d do if something ever happened to you.”

Artagan remained silent, eyes going to his lap where he seemed to be fidgeting. The big powerful god fidgeting. Weird.

He finally spoke, voice still broken from illness. “And I don’t know what I would do if something happened to her.”

Ugh. Fuck. Beau didn’t want him to make her like him.

“I care about her so much, Beauregard. I know you don’t like me, but we have one thing in common: our love for a glittery blue tiefling.”

Beau couldn’t help but chuckle at that. “I mean, yeah. But what I’m saying is- ugh. You better not let this happen again, man. Because if you die, if you die in front of Jester, I’ll fuckin’ bring you back and kill you again.”

He grinned. “I suppose that’s fair. Is this your way of saying that you care about me?”

“Fuck no. But like you said, we have one thing in common.”

A minute nod. His eyes were slipping closed again.

Beau sighed. “Go back to sleep, I’ll make sure that Jester’s here when you wake up.”

“Thank you, Beauregard.” He started to get settled back under the covers.

Beau scoffed and patted his arm as she stood up. “Fuck you, Traveler,” before leaving to inform her friend that he was going to be alright.
