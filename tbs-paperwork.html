Title: Paperwork
Fandom: Bright Sessions
Summary: Joan goes to Owen's and ends up caring more than she expected to
Word count: 1.1k

Paperwork

Joan knocks on the door with a bit of a huff. She doesn’t want to be here. She doesn’t want to be here.

So, why is she?

For some reason, when Owen Thompson asked her to bring work to him, she couldn’t say no.

She stands for a long, long thirty seconds waiting for him to answer the door. His neighborhood is safe enough, but it gives her the creeps standing outside in the dark any longer than she needs to.

“Coming! Sorry!” comes the panicked voice from inside the door.

She hears thumping. Is he running to the door?

Joan tries not to chuckle, because Owen... ugh, Owen’s ridiculous when he’s not at work trying to be professional. He's always been good at making her smile.

He opens the door, breathing heavily and looking horrible. His hair is messier than she’s seen it in a long time, he’s wearing pajamas, he- wow, he doesn’t look good.

“You clearly weren’t expecting me,” Joan observes.

He sighs regretfully and gestures for her to come in. “I’m sorry! I-” a sheepish chuckle. “I overslept.”

Sleeping in the middle of the day? That’s new.

“How are you feeling?” She asks, rather clinical instead of friendly like she knows she should sound.

“Oh, you know...” he clears his throat more out of necessity than anything else. “I keep sneezing, and coughing and it’s annoying but- but you’re not here for that. Thanks for coming, Joan.”

She nods curtly and hands him the folders. “Not a problem.” No, she’s not here for that.

He sniffles and hurries to put them down to run his sleeve under his nose. “I didn’t think that it’d be a good idea to go to work today...”

“No, probably not.”

If it were just a few years earlier, she’d be pushing him to the couch and telling him to hydrate. The two of them had a whole thing going whenever one of them got sick, Joan almost misses it.

The awkward silence becomes so much more awkward when she looks at his face and realizes that he’s thinking the exact same thing. She’s not a mind reader, but she is a therapist, and Owen isn’t the hardest person to read for anyone, let alone someone who dated him for three years.

“I shouldn’t expose you to my germs any longer than I need to, so I’ll let you go,” says Owen with so much reluctance that he’s terrible at hiding. "I'll see you tomorrow!"

“Alr- wait, tomorrow? You’re coming to work tomorrow?”

“I’m sure I’ll be feeling a bit more like myself by then.” That sentence might mean a little more if he didn’t crumble into a coughing fit immediately afterward.

“Owen...” she sighs out.

Owen pulls the sleeve of his sweatshirt over his mouth and continues to cough harshly. He grabs onto a nearby chair for support while Joan stands uselessly thinking of all the things she could do to help if they weren’t just coworkers.

“Sorry,” he sputters, voice hoarse. He clears his throat and shakes his head of the fog, giving another small cough. His brows are still pinched in discomfort, just slightly. “Whew! Wow, um...” he sniffs, rubs his nose.

“You really think you should go into work tomorrow?” Joan asks with as much judgment in her tone as she can muster.

Owen hums non-committal. “There’s so much to get done.” He gestures toward the folders that Joan dropped off, and yes, there’s a lot of work that he needs to do before tomorrow, even on a sick day.

Joan clenches her jaw for a moment as she considers. “Let me help with that, then, and I’ll take care of things tomorrow.”

Owen’s eyes go wide before, “Joan, I can’t let you-”

“Not all of it, but enough to keep things from piling up when you get back.”

He closes his mouth and looks at her, reading her, searching for a motive or reason that he’s too sick to think so much about. He gives in quickly with a heavy, crackly exhale as his shoulders fall. He’s definitely not feeling well if he’s alright with letting someone else take the reins.

He sniffles wetly, paws at his nose. “F- fine. Yes, I- I guess it wouldn’t be a good idea to take whatever this is with me to work.”

Joan nods. “Exactly.”

Another sniff, and he’s looking around. “Oh God...” he mutters, scrubbing his nose again. “Where did I put the...?”

Joan’s confused at first, and then realizes what he’s looking for. She spots the box of tissues on the dining table just to the right of the front door, and gets it back to him in record time.

“Th- thank you, snf!” and that’s about all he gets out before he sneezes. And sneezes. Four consecutive until he’s breathless and sniffly and looking even more miserable than he already did.

“Let’s get to work,” Joan says as he’s busy wiping his nose. “The sooner we can get these done, the sooner you can go to bed and start recovering from your plague.”

Owen gives a laugh and nods in agreement. “Sounds good to me!”

-----

The paperwork doesn’t actually take that long to go through, or it normally wouldn’t. But tonight they’re having to pause every few minutes for Owen to sneeze or cough or blow his nose.

Joan’s thankful that he’s not going to work tomorrow, he’d probably get the whole institute sick.

“I believe she’s a pyrokinetic? She’s been involved with - ahem - quite a few arson cases.” His voice is going, and it has Joan wincing in sympathy.

The case they’re going over right now is a woman named Susanna Castro who’s currently on trial. Someone had brought the case to them, someone who’d apparently refused to give a name. They’ve had a few possible patients brought to them in recent months, all by the same person matching the same description.

“Have you spoken to her?”

Owen clears his throat with a wince. “I contacted her lawyer, we might be able to set up a meeting if she agrees to it.”

“Good,” Joan decides. “Owen, can I get you some water or something?”

The offer seems to throw him off a bit. Maybe it is weird, maybe they’re not there yet.

Instead of looking weirded out like she half expects him to, he smiles. “Sure, yeah...” he gives a liquid sniff and paws at his nose. “Thanks, Joan.”

Joan finds herself content to stay there for the rest of the evening. There’s something about looking out for a sick Owen (even if it’s only slightly) that makes her miss the old times between the two of them. Huh.
