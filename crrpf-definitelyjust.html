Definitely Just

They all wince at the loud sneeze that sounds from the other side of the studio. Taliesin emerges into the lobby from the bathroom, a wad of pilfered tissues in his hands as he uses one to scrub at his nose.

"Wow, you look fantastic," Liam exclaims unhelpfully.

"Yeah," Marisha chimes in. "thanks for like, showing up and trying to get us sick too. That's great," though it's not mean-spirited teasing. She's the last person to care about germs.

Taliesin shakes his head as he squeezes any excess moisture off the end of his nose. "I'm not sick. It's just allergies." He finishes right before he's thrown into another wet sneezing fit. "Pollen count today, super high."

"Doesn't matter what it is," Liam comments with a wince. "You obviously don't feel good. Go home."

"I'm fine, really. It's just the sneezing that's...th-" he buries another sneeze into a tissue and then gives a sniff.

"You need drugs?" Travis asks, poking his head around the corner. "Cause I have drugs."

Taliesin chuckles congestedly. "Sure, as long as it's not going to make me pass out."

"Oh no don't worry, I've got the good stuff."

Taliesin takes some of Travis' Claritin and continues with his day.

He's still sneezing and runny-nosed. Eventually Taliesin just grabs the tissue box from the bathroom and sets it next to him as he works.

"Jesus," Liam says after a rather impressive sneeze. "I think Travis must've given you a crappy placebo."

Taliesin laughs, or tries to before it faded into an itchy coughing fit. Yeah, the medicine didn't help at all. Liam is looking at him with concern, and then goes about his business.

Liam pats his back. "I'm heading out. Feel better, buddy."

As the day goes on, Taliesin develops a cough. It's nothing major, just a little tickle, but it tips them (including Taliesin) off that this probably isn't allergies.

Watery fluid is dripping out of his nostrils, the kind that won't go away no matter how many times he blows his nose. Sneezing is a whole different ordeal.

He muffles a quick, embarrassingly loud sneeze into yet another tissue, fading into a blow to get the gunk out while it was loose.

"Okay," he hears from behind him. "I'm kicking you out. Go home. This isn't allergies."

"It's just a cold," he argues.

"You sound like shit and you're gonna get everyone sick." Marisha perches next to him on the arm of the couch, rubbing his back as if it'll make any of this better. "And you're getting literally nothing done."

Staring at his work, Taliesin knows she's right. He's no good to anyone today.

"Alright, alright. You win. I'll go home and do things that sick people do."

"Good."

And he does. Taliesin packs up his things and heads home. He still has some cold medicine from his last ones he takes a dose and climbs into bed for a nap.
