Fandom: To the Bone
Summary: Luke is bummed. Eli realizes he needs some cheering up
Word count: 1k

Cheer Up

Luke had been battling a cold for the past couple of days. How did Eli know that? The same way everyone else did: he was loud as fuck.

Luke could talk anyone's ear off and make them enjoy it. He sang in the shower until people banged on the door and begged him to give it a rest.

It would make sense that he'd be just as loud when he was sick.

A loud sneezer. A loud cougher.

And when the man blew his nose… well, everyone fucking heard it. Gross.

She listened to a horrible fit of sneezes coming from downstairs. One after the other, tumbling over each other. Luke followed it with a groan. Eli sighed, setting aside her sketchbook and hopping off her bed.

She found Luke stretched across the sofa in the living room, looking particularly tragic.

He was in the process of wiping his nose in a frustrated fashion, abusing the thing as he tried to rid his body of the snot clogging it. It wouldn’t work, Eli knew, because the snot never ended when this cold was hers.

"Have you come to keep me company in my last days?" He dramatically draped an arm over his head as Eli sat on the other end of the couch and scoffed.

"Nope, I've come to tell you to quit spreading germs all over the place."

Luke huffed. "It was everyone else's cold first."

"Okay… true, but no one else was as obnoxious as you're being right now."

The house was full of the sounds of sniffling for a good week or so, but nothing as bad as whatever Luke was doing right now. They’d been telling him to suck it up, even Lobo who didn’t feel the need to entertain his whininess.

“I can’t help that I suffer more deeply,” he smirked.

“Jesus Christ.”

“But if you prefer to go to my room and die alone…”

“I would prefer that, actually.”

“Ugh,” Luke groaned, a sudden change in vibe but not in dramatics. “Please don’t make me. I don’t want to be holed up in my room all day.”

Eli laughed. “I’m just sick of hearing you sneeze. You sound like you’re dying of the plague.”

“I clearly am, and I’d appreciate some sympathy in my trying time, thank you very much.” He turned up his nose as if to appear unwilling to listen to reason, and it gave Eli a view of wet nostrils that had her cringing.

“You need a fucking tissue.”

Abandoning the hilarious facade, he chuckled and sheepishly covered his nose with one hand while grabbing a tissue with the other. He sniffed lightly before giving an unholy nose blow. Eli herself had had the same cold, but she didn’t remember being quite that... mucus-y.

He finished with a small sigh, a congested exhale that wasn’t anywhere near as dramatic as his others.

“In all honesty, though,” he said. “I really don’t feel good.”

Eli hummed. “Yeah, I can kind of tell. Colds always hit you this hard?”

A shrug. “Didn’t use to. And then they did.”

Another sniff before he let his head fall against her shoulder. That couldn’t have been comfortable, but he stayed there. Eli tried to remain as still as possible, he seemed to be enjoying himself. And then she heard even, congested snores of a plague victim currently using her as a pillow.

“Dude, c’mon,” she said, shrugging him off her shoulder. It resulted in a snort and another groan. “You should go to bed.”

“Don’t wanna.”

“Come on, Luke.”

“Dinner!” They heard from the kitchen.

From all the time she knew him, Eli had never seen Luke look crestfallen at the word ‘dinner’.

-----

Lobo found her in the kitchen helping Anna with dinner dishes. “You were right! He’s got a fever.” She said this as if Eli had won some kind of prize. “He’s moping now, might need some cheering up.”

Anna shook her head. “I can’t believe he’s this sick from a cold.”

“Luke was sick every other week when he first came in. Came a long way since then, after getting healthy. So, this is probably bumming him out, like a lot.” She smiled at Eli. “Go see the poor guy when you’re done.”

Eli scoffed. “I’m his keeper now?”

Lobo shrugged. “Luke can’t make his own happiness right now, he needs someone else to do it for him.”

So Eli finished the dishes, dried her hands on her sweatshirt, and made her way up the stairs. Luke did look sort of bummed out, and currently had some old music playing on his speakers as he read a book on his iPad. One hand held the iPad while another had a tissue clamped over his nose.

It was perfect timing that she knocked the same moment he began gearing up for a sneeze. 

He squeezed his nose in the wad of tissue and pitched forward in a sneeze, and then another, bending him at the waist and leaving him breathless for a moment before he tried to give his nose an ineffectual blow into the poor tissue.

“Wow.”

Luke jumped. “Jesus Christ! Snf! Warn a guy.” He tended his nose in more of a panic now, but Eli still caught a glimpse of wetness on his upper lip before he finally resorted to his sleeve.

“Sorry,” she chuckled. She flopped on his bed and looked up at him. “Lobo wanted me to come cheer you up.”

He sighed out a laugh. “Didn’t realize I needed cheering up. ‘S just a cold.”

“I don’t know about you,” she muttered, rolling over to face him. “But being sick always makes things ten times worse.”

He nodded, silent for a few seconds. “I could tell.”

Her eyes found his for a split second before she had to look away. “I mean it’s- it’s hard to eat when your throat is sore and you’re not even hungry anyway.”

“Mm. No, I get it.” He turned away to cough into his elbow. “You still feel poorly at all?”

“A little tired, maybe.”

“Well, then let’s both get better together.”

She snorted. “Whatever you say.”
