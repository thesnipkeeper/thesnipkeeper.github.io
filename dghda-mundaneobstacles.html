Mundane Obstacles

Todd left the pharmacy with two measly objects in a plastic bag. He would’ve gotten more had they been a bit richer and not had a limited amount of cash for this impromptu road trip. He climbed into the car, frowned at the man next to him, and emptied the bag.

“This first,” he said as he began ripping open a plastic package.

Dirk watched him with the same confused, intrigued eyes even through the watery fog.

“Here, put that under your tongue until it beeps.”

Dirk did so, but not without inspecting the thermometer first, out of mere habit. He took it out at the beep and squinted at the reading, then turned it toward Todd.

Todd sighed. “Shit, Dirk.”

Dirk laughed, or it started as a laugh before he was thrown into a fit of coughs that sounded downright painful. Tears pricked at his eyes as the coughs tore against his sore throat, and Todd just sat there uselessly until Dirk was done.

Todd had never really considered that Dirk could get sick. It wasn't that he thought of the man as invincible, rather he didn't think of him as a person. A normal person who could catch normal illnesses. Shouldn’t Dirk be immune to things like the flu just because he’s Dirk?

“I have a great idea,” Todd began as he pressed a bottle of water into his friend’s hand. “Let’s go home.”

Dirk furrowed his brows, managing to look somewhat annoyed even with his nose and eyes both running down his face. “Go home? How are we going to solve the case if we just go home, Todd? That’s ridiculous!” It was the first time Todd had heard him speak in a while, and his voice sounded worse than it had before. He finished his irritated rant with a thick snf! And dragged the back of his hand under his nose. It left a streak of wetness behind, but he went at it again with his sleeve. Todd didn't blame him, they had few tissues to their name, and by tissues that meant a stash of fast food napkins. Despite their limited stash, Todd grabbed one and handed it to him. 

Dirk pressed it to his nose with another sniffle, and then found himself coughing harshly into the napkin. "Ow!" He whimpered, hand going to his throat with a pained wince.

"I just mean that with a fever like that…"

"... We get this done now before it gets worse?"

"That's definitely not what I was going to say.”

“You’d might as well, because that’s my plan anyway,” Dirk said with a shrug, with more of an informative tone than a sassy one. “I will be completely useless in a day or two. Better to get everything done now.”

“You’re speaking from experience, I guess?”

“Unfortunately, yes.” Dirk shuddered, either from the thought, or from chills. "I'm no stranger to going delirious from fever on occasion.  Sometimes things go a bit tipsy when I'm ill." He flipped his hand in a way to convey whatever 'tipsy' was supposed to mean. 

“That’s- wow, that’s kind of terrifying.”

“It’s not so bad. Really, a fever can give you a different perspective on things.”

“Sure. Do you want something to bring yours down, though?” He asked with a chuckle.

“Oh, please.”

Todd shoved a dose of Tylenol into his friend’s hand and winced in sympathy as Dirk choked in the middle of swallowing the pills, coughing once again.

Dirk glanced at him weirdly as he wiped his nose, and then looked away.

Todd narrowed his eyes. "What? What's that look?"

"You should keep your distance. I'm definitely contagious beyond belief."

"Well, we're stuck in an enclosed space together, so that's gonna be a little difficult."

"Oh God," Dirk threw his head back and sniffled. "You're right. Stupid Dirk, can't even keep your germs to yourself…" He huffed grumpily, and then turned back to Todd, brows turned up in sudden concern. "What if it makes your attacks worse? Can that happen with pararibulitis?"

"It…" Todd stumbled. "Amanda was definitely more… on edge when she was sick," he admitted with a sigh. Images of his sister, exhausted and burning with fever and unable to sleep for fear of everything around her, flashed through his brain. "But! But that's not anything to worry about, because I'll be fine. Just rest for a while, Dirk."

Dirk looked uneasy, but leaned back and got comfortable.

He was asleep within an hour of their drive, snoring softly while continuing to look miserable.

Todd sighed.

He should have just... not agreed to this. Dirk told him he was coming down with something even before he told Todd that they needed to drive to a city four hours away. Dirk’s cough was already bad, he already sounded like he’d spent the night awake struggling to breathe through his nose. His cheeks were already kind of red. Todd should have said no.

Except, that wouldn’t have made a difference, because Dirk would have just found another way to get to his destination. He always did.

He drove.

Dirk woke himself with a coughing fit, and a pitiful whine when it scraped his throat. “Are we there yet?”

“You haven’t actually told me where ‘there’ is, Dirk,” Todd explained.

“It’s ‘there’. You’ll know when we’re ‘there’.”

A groan. “No, no I won’t. You’ll know when we’re there if you’re- if you’re conscious enough.”

A scoff. “Of course I’m-” Dirk paused. “Stop.”

“What?”

“Stop the car.”

“Oh, are we finally ‘there’?” he asked mockingly.

“No, I-” Dirk pressed a hand to his mouth with a look of panic. “Just-!” he used the other hand for a ‘hurry up!’ gesture as something gurgled from his throat.

“Oh, shit...” Todd started to pull over, but not before Dirk gagged into his hand.

Todd was pretty sure that vomit was leaking through his fingers. Yet, the poor man still managed to open the door and aim for the outside before the bout fully hit. He choked up a mouthful of whatever he’d actually managed to eat that morning, the sound of liquid splattering against the ground below. He moaned miserably, one arm wrapped around his stomach while he struggled to stay upright.

It didn’t last long, but it was enough to take everything out of the detective (which wasn’t much). Todd grabbed a napkin from their collection and nudged his friend as Dirk got back into the car. He frowned as Dirk clumsily wiped off his face of the saliva and snot and tears that had accumulated.

Dirk sniffed, and gave a restrained cough as if he were afraid to cough fully and risk puking again.

“We can still go back,” Todd offered as he rubbed Dirk’s arm.

Dirk turned to him with the most exhausted-yet-stubborn look and said, “We’re almost there.”

Todd muttered, “Jesus Christ.” and they were on their way.

The client was looking for her granddaughter. It wasn’t the kind of strange case that they normally took, but Dirk agreed before anyone could discuss it. Todd trusted it, whatever his hunch was.The woman’s son had moved away without so much as a goodbye and taken his kid with him. Their relationship was shaky. She’d contacted Dirk after she learned that her granddaughter was no longer living at home, but with a group of people whom she described as ‘vagrants’.

They weren’t looking for the girl so much for the grandmother as they were for the girl’s safety. But damn, there had to be easier ways to find someone than drive in one direction for hours. The internet, for example. But Dirk would hear none of it.

Todd kept driving, until Dirk sat up, more alert than he had been all day, and said, “Look, there’s a thing!”

Todd stopped and pulled over despite the vagueness of that sentence. “A thing, huh?”

“Yes, look at that thing,” Dirk pointed across the street from where they had parked, to a small town monument. “That statue is wearing a hat.” And indeed, someone had managed to get their old baseball cap on the statue’s head. “Statues are definitely not known for wearing hats.” Dirk released his seatbelt with a small cough. His voice still sounded terrible. “Let’s go over there!”

“No, wait, I’ll go check it out. You’re- you should stay here and rest. You don’t look good, Dirk.”

“I’m aware of that, thank you,” he huffed. “But I am the detective here, am I not? I have to actually do the detecting in order to be the dete- detect-...” He scrubbed at his nose with everything he had, sniffed urgently until his breath caught. Dirk groaned in annoyance and searched for their collection of napkins or anything until Todd finally managed to get one in his hand. Dirk got it over his nose just in time to save the car from being pelted with germs by the series of sneezes.

He gave a wet sniffle that made it clear how much the fit had stuffed him up, and then groaned once more as his face fell and breath hitched once again. One last, soaking sneeze into the napkin before he made a lame attempt to blow his nose. Dirk used what was left of the paper to wipe his nostrils somewhat dry, and then he shook his head like a dog. "Bleh. God, snf! That's awful. Anyway, let's go!"

Todd didn't have time to argue before the crazy British guy was out of the car and crossing the road illegally to get to the statue. He followed.

It sat on the pavement in front of an old building, one that contained a bar on one side and a boutique on the other.

Dirk hopped up and grabbed the hat. Doing so seemed to just irritate his airways as he muffled a cough into his shoulder and tried to inspect the inside of the hat at the same time. Dirk stood there for a while. He looked back and forth between the two doors.

He chose the boutique.

It was small and cramped and carpeted. There was a woman folding clothes near the counter. She look familiar.

"Helen Cartwright," said Dirk without a moment of hesitation.

She looked like a deer in headlights. "Shit. What? What do you want?"

"I have your hat!" He held it up with delight and she snatched it away. "You must have been expecting… someone?"

She took the hat and glanced out the window at the statue. She sighed. "Whoever you are, tell them that I don't want them involved in my life, not anymore."

Dirk raised an eyebrow. "And by 'them', you mean…?"

"My dad, my grandmother, whoever hired you to find me or to give me something or whatever. I'm not interested. Just go." She flicked her hand in the direction of the door and continued working.

“Look…” Todd began. “We were hired to find you. I don’t think we can just leave without knowing what’s going on.”

She looked back at him and scoffed. “Really?”

He and Dirk nodded in unison.

“Since my mom died, my grandma and father have fought over the proper way to raise me or whatever. I moved out of dad’s the second I turned eighteen. I’m done being their example of themselves. So,” she gestured around her. “I got a job, and a place, and now they can’t say that I belong to them anymore.”

“Jesus,” said Todd.

They called Helen’s grandmother and told her that they found her, and that she was okay.

“Well?” the woman said. “Where is she?”

Dirk cleared his throat, more out of necessity than anything. “Well… she’s asked that we don’t tell you. But we can assure you that she’s safe, and-”

“Where is my granddaughter? You expect to be paid for this?”

“Well, not anymore,” Dirk admitted. And hung up.

Helen smirked at the phone. “Thank you, seriously.”

“No problem. You’re grandma sounds…”

“- crazy.”

They didn’t have a chance to say anything else before Dirk started to cough. He gripped Todd’s shoulder for support and Todd in turn held onto the man as he hacked into the crook of his arm. His skin was burning, almost overwhelming to touch.

“Okay,” he said, rubbing Dirk’s back while the coughing subsided. “I think the Tylenol’s wearing off. We should go.”

Helen frowned. “Do you guys need anything before you leave?”

“Have you got anywhere to take a quick nap?” Dirk asked hoarsely.

-----

Dirk ended up on an old couch in the back room of a small town boutique. He fell asleep almost immediately after he flopped down on the thing. Helen Cartwright just laughed and made Todd a cup of coffee.

"You guys are the weirdest PI's I've ever seen. Granted, I haven't met any PI's before, but I imagine that they're not usually like you."

Todd snorted and took a sip of his coffee. "We've got our own brand. Well, Dirk has his own brand." He sat on the floor in front of the couch and laid the back of his hand on his friend's forehead. Dirk was right, this virus was going to be brutal. "He'll work through anything apparently, even the flu. It shouldn't surprise me. He once solved a case with two arrows sticking out of him."

"Damn."

Dirk groaned in his sleep, folded in on himself and rolled over. He coughed into cushions of the couch rather grossly and then made a more pathetic whimpering noise. This nap didn't seem very restful, but it was better than he was going to get in the car.

"He really doesn't seem like the type, you know, to… keep going after getting shot."

Todd hummed thoughtfully. "You know, I don't think he is. Or, I mean, I don't think he would be if he wasn't the way he was."

Helen blinked. "What?"

Todd shook his head, cleared his throat. "Dirk's just- he's just weird. Thanks for the use of your couch. He's been pretty worn out today."

She shrugged. "Thanks for not telling my grandmother where I live." Helen sat down at an old desk and woke the computer, probably getting minimal work done while she was back there.

"Yeah, if you don't mind my asking… is she that bad?"

"She's not abusive, if that's what you're asking. Not in the traditional sense, anyway. She's just a raging bitch who thinks she gets to control my life. I don't think my mom could stand her, either."

"Jeez, sorry."

"It's fine. Just- thanks."

They sat in a comfortable silence for a while as Helen typed on her computer and Todd finished his coffee. Dirk rolled over again and made another noise of discomfort. Todd reached up to rub his friend’s arm in some hope that it would help.

But it didn’t. Dirk kept whining, whimpering, curling in on himself even tighter. At first Todd thought he was having some nightmare, and maybe he was, but the true source of his discomfort became clear when his breath caught in his throat with a horribly wet sound.

“Shit,” Todd muttered under his breath as Dirk started to wake. “Helen, do you have a trash can or a bag or something...? I think he’s about to-”

She managed to get the trash bin from beside the desk, which still contained wadded up papers and old pieces of gum, passed it to Todd, who was able to get it under Dirk’s just in time for him to lean over and vomit horribly.

His eyes were open now, though whether he was truly awake was debatable. Todd sat on the edge of the couch and rubbed his back as he heaved again. There really wasn’t much to bring up, it wasn’t like they’d eaten or drank anything since he puked on the side of the road.

Todd dropped his head with a sigh. “Sorry about this...”

“It’s all good,” she promised. “You guys are the most exciting thing to happen in a while.”

That was probably true, Todd thought with a bit of a chuckle, considering who they were.

“Todd...” Dirk swallowed thickly. He didn’t trust himself to turn away from the bin quite yet, so his hand searched until it found Todd’s. “Feel sick.”

“Yeah, I know, buddy.”

“Home.”

“Yeah, we’ll head home.”

Helen frowned. “Are you sure you guys want to drive when he’s- you know, like that?”

From the way Dirk was talking earlier, their situation wasn’t going to be better any time soon. Dirk was not going to be better any time soon.

“We should probably get back before he’s completely out of commission.” Too late.

Todd cleaned up, got his friend medicated once again, and finally helped Dirk to his feet.

Helen gave them each a hug while all three of them refused to acknowledge the strangeness of their meeting.

Todd put the detective in the passenger seat with a handful of grocery bags in case he needed to vomit again. It wasn’t the most pleasant solution, but they’d get home faster if they didn’t have to stop.

Dirk was so quiet that Todd had assumed he'd fallen asleep again. Until, that is, he heard sniffling. He was about to pass over another napkin until he glanced at his friend and realized that he was crying.

"Dirk?" He asked, keeping his voice soft. "What's wrong?"

Dirk breathed out, sniffled, scrubbed at his eyes and then his nose. "Don't want you to get sick."

"This again? That's not something that you should be worried about right now.

"You're going to have an attack and it'll be my fault!" He sobbed.

"Oh God…" Todd muttered sadly. "That's not… I'll be fine, buddy." Would he? He'd never been sick since his pararibulitis made itself known. "And I think you're just freaking out about this right now because your temperature is insane."

Dirk nodded, giving another tragic sniff. "Probably."

"We'll get home and get you to bed."

"Told you. Fevers, me, crazy," he flipped his hand about his head.

"That's okay."

They reached the apartment in one piece. Dirk did throw up again, but it was blessedly ten minutes away from home where they didn’t have to deal with the gross bag contents for too long. He stumbled inside, upstairs with some help from Todd.

When he went to lie on the couch, Todd tugged on his sleeve toward the bedroom and met with resistance when they reached the doorway. Dirk had stopped like a stubborn dog.

“What?”

“Germs.”

“We already shared the bed last night,” Todd reasoned with exasperation. “I’m sufficiently germed by now.”

Dirk made a whining noise but allowed himself to be nudged further into the room.

Todd helped him out of his jacket and shoes, and threw some sweatpants and a t-shirt in his direction to change into while he left to get a cup of water and the bottle of fever reducer that was probably about to be Dirk’s new best friend this week.

Dirk was out by the time he returned, in sweatpants but shirtless, the shirt he was intending to put on still sitting in the mess of blankets. Todd chuckled and found himself yawning from the long day that they’d endured. Case solved, kind of.

He climbed into the bed and Dirk immediately clung to him.

-----

Todd was exhausted.

Dirk was exhausted.

It was four in the morning and the third time that Dirk’s coughing had woken them. He seemed so out of it as he took the cup of cough medicine Todd had found in the bathroom cabinet. It… may have been old, but they were too tired to care.

He wordlessly traded Dirk the medicine cup for the thermometer. The detective’s cheeks were bright red, though that could have just been from sleeping. Not that either of them had done much sleeping.

Dirk took his temperature with an exceptionally miserable frown. It hadn't changed much, maybe slightly higher but other than that he was just… sick. They both crashed not long after that.

-----

Todd woke with a headache.

He turned to find that the bed was empty.

"Dirk?" He called.

"Bathroom," he heard back from the hallway. "Out in a moment!"

Todd got out of bed and winced as he muscles complained at the movement. Eight hours in a car could do that, he figured. He considered getting right back into bed when his phone rang.

"Hey,"

"How'd it go yesterday?"

"Oh, God!" Todd groaned and pinched the bridge of his nose. He ended up lowering himself back on the edge of the bed. "I totally forgot to call you. Farah, I'm so sorry. There was so much going on."

"Yeah, I wondered. That's okay! So how'd it go? Did you find Helen?"

"We did…" Todd said slowly. "She kind of didn't want her grandmother to know where she was, so now we're probably not getting paid."

There was a pause on the other side of the line. "O…kay."

"Look, I'll- I'll tell you everything. It's- she was really nice. Dirk's sick and he ended up sleeping on the couch where she worked," he snorted. 

"Wait, Dirk's sick?"

"Must be the flu. He's been coughing and throwing up, and his temperature is really high…" Todd sighed. "Kinda freaked me out, to be honest."

She made a sympathetic noise. "Poor Dirk…"

"Yeah, he-"

There was a loud thump in the bathroom followed by a "shit!"

"Farah, I need to call you back," he rushed to end the call before he dropped the phone and ran to the bathroom. "Are you okay?" He called. He tried the door and it opened, but he suddenly felt like he shouldn't be barging into the bathroom. He did anyway; there wasn't much he hadn't seen at this point.

Dirk was on the floor rubbing his back with a look of annoyance.

"What happened?"

He huffed. "I seemed to have lost my balance. Apparently I can't stand up on my own long enough to brush my teeth, which is quite ridiculous, if you ask me."

Todd sighed. "Here," he held out a hand. Dirk accepted it and managed to get on two feet. "Are you feeling.any better?"

He nodded. "Think it's the fever reducer to tell you the truth. I'll take it, though! Think I'll go find something to eat."

"You're hungry?" Asked Todd in disbelief. "You coughed yourself sick last night and you want to put more in your stomach?"

"Well, isn't lacking anything in my stomach a good reason to eat?"

"Yeah, I guess. Go sit down, I'll get you something, okay?"

Todd fixed a PB&J at Dirk's request. He was just glad to see the guy awake and aware. Meanwhile, Todd had begun to feel more exhausted by the second, as if the energy were being siphoned out of him. 

It was when a sudden chill hit him that he knew he was doomed.

“Are you okay?” Dirk was looking at him and probably had been for a few seconds now.

“Yeah,” he cleared his throat, finding it scratchy. “Yeah, just tired.”

Dirk frowned, swallowing another bite of his ‘breakfast’. “I should sleep here tonight.”

“Dirk, I’m not making you sleep on the couch when you have the flu,” he chuckled. “We’ve both agreed that we sleep better in the same bed.”

“Right, but that was before I-” he stopped himself and pressed his lips into a thin line.

“Dirk?” but Todd knew that look well enough now. He scrambled for a bucket under the sink and ran to get it in Dirk’s hands in time. The quick movement caused the room to spin and he haphazardly seated himself next to Dirk before he fell over in a less graceful way. 

Not that his friend would have noticed; Dirk swallowed thickly as he held the bucket, determined to keep the food in his stomach. Todd rubbed his back.

“Take it easy,” he soothed. “Just let it happen.”

“God…” Dirk breathed out, before he gave an unproductive heave. His brows furrowed as he took another breath and tried to gather his bearings. And then he shrugged away Todd’s arm, not unkindly. “You’re too hot,” he complained before vomiting his breakfast.

“Sorry, sorry...” so Todd just sat there feeling useless while Dirk suffered more of the misery of his illness.

He should have probably said something when he realized it, if anything to not freak Dirk the fuck out when he was already sick and had already expressed some pretty intense anxiety regarding Todd’s whole ‘thing’. But that was also a good reason not to say anything for as long as possible, right? That was something Todd was fantastic at.

So he took the bucket to clean it. Looked at his tired reflection, and dragged a hand down his face with a long sigh. The thermometer wasn’t far, they’d kept it nearby for Dirk, of course.

He thought about it.

It would have been a good idea to see if he actually did have a fever, or if it just part of his imagination. He didn’t really sleep the night before, neither of them had. But the longer he looked at himself in the mirror, the more he realized how red his face was.

How red his... his neck was. His... arms?

Perhaps part of his imagination, but his body took imagination seriously.

That was the last coherent thought Todd had before his skin burst into flames. Chest first, which then spread to his arms and head and then his legs all the way down.

It was breathless panic at first. And then he screamed out in pain and horror as the scent of his own charred skin hit his nostrils, and the knowledge that he would be ash by the time anyone-

“Todd!”

He felt a hand on his arm but saw nothing but flame when he tried to open his eyes. Everything burned, and the flames continued to consume him.

”Oh my God, oh my God, oh my God...” He heard drawers opening, cabinets slamming shut, and the rattle of a pill bottle.

Something was shoved into his burning lips and Todd’s reflexes were enough to swallow the pills.

The flames died away.

Coherence must have come into his eyes, because Dirk breathed a sigh of relief. The detective had tears forming in his eyes. His hand was still on Todd’s forehead from holding him still long enough to get the meds into him.

“You are burning up, Todd,” he said, accusatory. “Quite literally, apparently!”

Todd tried to breathe, to make the remnants of panic fade away, and pulled himself up to sit against the bathroom cabinet. “That’s... that’s not funny... yet. Give me a few minutes,” he huffed out a laugh, which triggered a cough. The cough only frustrated Dirk and sent him further into madness.

“I did tell you! I told you and you didn’t listen!” Now tears ran down his cheeks, he ignored them. “And now you-! Well, look at where we are, Todd!” He shook his head. “No... no, this is my fault. I should have insisted on the couch-”

“Dirk.”

“... and I shouldn’t have just let you get in the car with me for hours. I could have driven myself! I’m a grown man, after all!”

“Dirk.”

Dirk’s mouth snapped shut. He looked back to Todd, clearly seconds away from losing it.

“I probably caught it from you before we even knew you were sick. We live together.” He sighed. “But I- should have told you I was getting sick. I didn’t want to scare you, but-”

Dirk scoffed dramatically. “Oh, really.”

“Yeah, that didn’t work out too well.”

They both went quiet, both coming down from different kinds of panic. Dirk sniffed, dried the tears from his face.

"Look…" Todd sighed out. "We're both aware that this is… bad. We can't do this on our own. Not if I'm going to-" have an attack every hour or who knew when.

Dirk nodded, sniffed. "Right. I'll call Farah."

"Wait…" except Dirk had already gotten himself onto his lanky legs and was making his way to wherever he left his phone. He cringed at the idea at first, but then remembered that she'd seen him at much worse.

-----

Dirk whimpered. The whole-body ache had returned as he was between the miserable time that his meds wore off yet it was too early to take more. He snuggled into Todd.

Todd found himself in pain at every movement. It felt nice to have real pain, he found, as opposed to imaginary fire shooting out of him

He felt a cool hand on his forehead, turned over, saw there was a similar one on Dirk’s. Farah sighed.

“You’re both burning up.”

“Poor choice of words,” Dirk mumbled.

“What?”

Todd shook his head lamely. “Don’t ask.”

“Okay, well, I’m not leaving this apartment today.”

Dirk coughed and curled up until his long body was much smaller. “So you’ll stay?”

“That is what not leaving means, yes,” she said with a small laugh. “Get some rest, dorks. I’ll be here.”

Todd and DIrk found themselves gravitating toward each other as they fell into a feverish sleep.
