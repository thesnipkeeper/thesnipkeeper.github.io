Henry's Mask

Jo expected to be bombarded with a crazy theory about a body’s death when she walked into the morgue. Lucas would say something awkward, Henry would say something weird.

She knew that they wore masks when they worked with the bodies, but as she talked to Henry for the first couple minutes she was there, he had yet to take his mask off.

“Henry, what’s with the...” she waved a hand around her face.

“Ah, yes, w-well-” the terrible, long coughing fit that followed told her everything she needed to know.

Her expression must have looked as concerned as she felt, because Lucas nodded in agreement from behind him while Henry’s fit sputtered out.

“Bronchitis. I’ve been trying to talk him into going home all day,” he shrugged.

Jo sighed. “Henry...”

Henry rolled his eyes. “This is hardly a reason to call out sick. Don’t worry, I’m keeping my germs to myself.” He cleared his throat and led her to the body.

She was a realtor. Her body had been found in one of the homes she was trying to sell. It... had been there for a while. Their biggest suspect was the woman’s estranged daughter. Or rather, Jo’s suspect. Henry didn’t think that the girl was guilty of anything besides using colorful language to describe her mother.

Needless to say, Henry was determined to be right.

“The body is quite decayed,” Henry paused to muffle a cough into his shoulder. “But we managed to find something interesting in her left arm.” He picked up a small white rod with tweezers. 

“Birth control.”

“Mhmm. I don’t think- oh, excuse me...” Jo tried not to wince at the gurgle in his breath as he inhaled and coughed harshly into the bend of his arm. “As - ahem - as I was saying, I don’t think Gretchen Andrews was as much of a single recluse as her daughter claims.”

“So there was someone else,” Jo said. 

Lucas added, “Or maybe multiple someone elses.”

“Are you willing to admit that I might be right, detective?”

Jo chuckled “That’d sound real smug if your voice wasn’t half gone.”

Henry gave an admitting nod. “You have a point.”

-----

She wasn’t sure how she let him talk her into bringing him with while she investigated. But somehow, they ended up at a realtor’s office, interviewing coworkers that hardly knew anything about their victim. The woman was so closed off, they didn’t even know she had a daughter.

Henry removed the mask before they headed in as to not draw too much attention to himself. Jo had to admit, he looked worse than he thought he did once she saw the rest of his face. His cheeks were flushed and he looked... tired. Granted, he usually looked tired, but this was a different sort.

“I didn’t know anything about her, really.” the receptionist was a young twenty-something. She seemed pretty freaked out that her boss had died suddenly. “She didn’t talk about her personal life. Didn’t really like us to, either.”

Jo nodded and thanked her for her time. Henry tried to as well before he was nearly doubled over in a coughing fit. 

That was their last interview. They didn’t know of anyone else who had spoken to Gretchen in the past year. Her coworkers certainly didn’t know of any boyfriends or sexual escapades.

“Did you sleep last night?” Jo asked as they got into her car. 

Henry rolled his eyes and gave a deep sigh, or would have had he not ended up coughing instead. He seemed irritated, not with her but with his situation. She had a feeling he wasn’t used to being less than his best.

She nodded. “Thought so. You look tired.”

“I’m exhausted,” he admitted. “Was up coughing most of the night.”

“Then why don’t you just go home, Henry?”

“Need I remind you that there’s a killer on the loose?”

“Need I remind you that I’m with the police and we’re generally known for solving crimes without know-it-all M.E.’s following us around?” Jo replied with a chuckle.

A smile tugged at the edge of his lips. “I wouldn’t have guessed.”

Jo rolled her eyes. “Do you at least want to stop somewhere and get cough drops or something?”

Henry hmm’d. “I doubt cough drops are going to help with th-this...”

Jo couldn’t resist patting his back as his coughed. They were getting deeper and more breathless. Her brows furrowed. She pressed the backs of her fingers to his cheek as he sat up. 

“You feel kind of warm.”

“Yes, I’m - cough - aware.”

“Henry...”

“I’ve worked through much worse, detective.”

“Doesn’t mean you have to.”

“Says the woman who needed to be dragged home when she came to work with influenza and passed out not even two months ago.”

Ah. Checkmate. “Fair point,” she said with a sigh. “How about some coffee? I’m buying.”

“Sounds wonderful.”

-----

The warm drink seemed to help at least a little with his cough. Movement made it worse, though, as evidenced by the fit that he had when the two of them were forced to chase down a fleeing suspect not ten minutes after they finished their coffee.

“You okay?” Jo asked as she pulled the handcuffed suspect to his feet. He was swearing colorfully at her, but she was too busy watching her colleague hack up a lung. 

The coughs sounded like gags. She worried for a moment that he was going to end up puking onto the pavement.

“Henry?”

He held up a hand and gave a less severe cough as the fit died down. “I’m- I’m fine.”

His face was red with the force of coughing, his breath still heavy and ragged. 

-----

It was late. Later than they should be working, anyway. There were a few people still sticking around at the precinct. Jo and Henry had a number of their victim’s client’s files to go through, so they settled themselves in the conference room with a pot of coffee.

He was coughing wetly into a tissue when she returned with their dinner for the night, takeout from the mexican restaurant down the street.

“You’re sure this isn’t pneumonia?”

Even with a red face and watery eyes, the look he gave her was clear. Jo chuckled. 

“Right. Mr. Genius Doctor.”

“Ah,” Henry smirked. “I rather like that title.”

“Funny,” she chuckled, handing off his food. “You can head home whenever you need to, by the way. I can take care of whatever’s left.”

Henry nodded absently. He was already focused on one of the files. 

“Henry.”

“Mm?”

“I’m serious. Don’t feel like you need to stay here. It’s not like you’re getting paid to be here. You obviously need rest, anyway.”

“Yes, well...” he picked up another folder from the stack. “I’m not too keen on going home at the moment.”

Jo raised an eyebrow at that. He didn’t offer any more of an explanation, and she didn’t fish for one. They had work to do.

Henry was still coughing, of course, and then drowning his hands in sanitizer whenever he thought he needed to. The way he tugged his sweater around himself told Jo that he was suffering from chills of a low grade fever. She tried offering him meds earlier, but he refused, claiming that he didn’t like taking anything. Because of course he didn’t. That’d make him feel better.

After an hour of reading file after file, Jo felt as tired as Henry looked.

“Maybe I’ll finish this tomorrow when my brain is working on all cylinders again.” She yawned and closed the folder.

“No need...” Henry said absently. He handed her the folder he’d been reading. “I think I found our killer.”

-----

The victim’s son. An eighteen year-old high school dropout with a history of mental illness. It was... it was sad. His mother pretended like he didn’t exist, even more so than she already did with her daughter. 

He blamed her for a lot of things in his life, but mainly leaving him to be raised by a neglectful father, one of her old clients.

Jo arrested the kid at one in the morning. 

Henry looked like death warmed over, no pun intended.

“I think it’s time that both of us get some sleep,” Jo said. “Thanks for all of your help on this case, Henry.”

He smiled. “It was my pleasure.”

She pulled her keys out of her bag. “I’ll take you home, now.”

His smile disappeared immediately.

That was it. She was done with the secrets and the avoidance and- “Okay, what’s your issue with going home, Henry? Are you and Abe not getting along or something?”

“No! No, I...” Henry sighed. “It’s a lot harder to keep your germs from someone who shares the same living space everyday.”

Ohhh. “You don’t want to get him sick, so you decided to stay out and work yourself to death instead.”

Henry winced. “The less I’m at home, the better.”

Jo was about to tell him how ridiculous that was. She may not have known Abe as well as he did, but she knew that he wasn’t the type to care about those things. She was about to tell him so when her phone rang. 

“Hello?”

”Is Henry with you?”

Speak of the devil. Jo made full eye contact with her colleague. “Oh hi Abe.” Henry’s brows raised. “Yeah, he’s right here.”

“Thank God. He sounded like hell this morning and I was afraid something awful happened to him. Irrational, I know, but-”

Jo couldn’t help a smile. “He’s fine. Well- he’s not dead, anyway.” She wasn’t sure what she said to warrant a hearty laugh from Abe, but she continued. “You want to talk to him?”

“Yes please.”

She watched Henry attempt to stammer out a few words into the phone, along with having to pull away to cough a couple of times. Whatever conversation was happening there was more one-sided, and she could only imagine what Abe could be saying to make Henry Morgan speechless. 

“Yes- I... I know! But-” a scoff. “Abraham, I’m- alright. I’m on my way. Good lord.”

Jo took the phone back with a smug grin. “Your place?”

Henry nodded with an exasperated sigh. “If you wouldn’t mind.”
