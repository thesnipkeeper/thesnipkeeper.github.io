Brewer's Visit

Mr. and Mrs. Brewer visiting them was supposed to be a happy time. But David knows that things are about to be significantly less fun when he wakes up to his husband blowing his nose and still struggling to breathe through it.

“Told you that you were getting sick,” David mutters sleepily as he sits up. He rubs his eyes. Patrick’s already fully-dressed, though looking a bit more disheveled than usual.

“I’m not sick,” Patrick says in probably what is the most congested voice David has ever heard.

David gives an exasperated sigh as he gets out of bed. Usually he’d linger for a while, but by the way things look he’s already slept in enough and they have somewhere important to be. Patrick tosses the tissue in the trash can, which appears to have accumulated several others.

“Your eyes are puffy, your face is red, and your nose is running. You’re sick.”

“David,” Patrick breathes out. “It’s allergies, okay?”

“Oh, sorry, I didn’t see the cat that we must have adopted in the middle of the night. My mistake.” It has a fifty-fifty chance of either making Patrick laugh or upsetting him more, and thankfully, he cracks a smile.

“Seriously, I’m fine. Hurry up, we’ve got to be at the airport in an hour!”

David nods and goes through his morning routine as quickly as he can. All while listening to his ailing husband in the other room. Marcy and Clint didn’t have a chance to leave this town healthy if Patrick didn’t admit to being sick soon.

He emerges from the bathroom, and Patrick is about to ask whether he’s ready to leave when David holds up a finger to stop him.

“One more thing.”

“Okay...” he’s annoyed, but he’ll thank him when David’s finished.

Probably.

David sprays disinfectant allover every surface of their house. Thankfully the guest room has gone untouched by Patrick, as David had taken it upon himself to get it cleaned up, organized, and decorated correctly.

“David, what are you doing?”

“Cleaning so your lovely parents don’t get sick!”

“I’m not sick!”

“Okay!” He smiles as he catches up to Patrick, who’s already nearly out the door.

-----

Patrick spends the drive battling with his runny nose and losing terribly. He takes advantage of red lights to wipe his nose, but that’s not nearly enough.

Clint and Marcy smile brightly when they catch sight of the two of them. A part of David is happier to see them than he expected to be. The in-laws visiting, he imagined, would be more nerve wracking, but now he’s... excited, apparently. Huh.

They each exchange hugs with one another until Patrick has to step away to sneeze harshly at the ground. David sighs and rubs his husband’s arm, giving the Brewers a Look while Patrick’s still tending to his nose.

“Oh, honey, are you catching a cold?” Marcy asks with all the sympathy in the world.

“Naw, I’m fine. Let’s go.”

“He’s not fine, he’s been sneezing all morning,” David informs. He smacks Patrick’s hands away and takes Marcy’s bags.

Clint chuckles. “Uh huh, sounds about right.”

Patrick rolls his eyes and turns back to his parents. “So we thought the four of us could stop at the cafe for breakfast before you see the house?”

“That sounds nice!” Marcy chimes.

Clint nods. “Sure!”

David silently holds out a hand for the keys. Patrick complies. Neither of them want a snot incident, especially with his parents in the car.

They’re only a few minutes into the drive when David looks over to find his husband sniffling desperately. David wonders why he’s not just using a tissue when he realizes that Patrick must have used the rest of them on the way there. Patrick turns to him with a hand over his nose and a pleading look before David nods toward the glovebox. For once, their fast food habit has saved them, because they have enough napkins stashed for the ride home.

He turns back to the road and tries to think of something to keep attention off of Patrick while he fixes his face.

“So um, how was your flight?”

“It was good!” Marcy says. “Went by quick.”

“Good! That’s- that’s good.” God, why is he such a terrible conversationalist? These are his in-laws! “Well, we’re going to have a great time this weekend. Glad that you could join us for the spring festival.”

Clint hums. “We’re excited for that, but honestly, it was just a good excuse for us to come visit.”

David bites back a smile. So many people have done crazy things to avoid spending time with him, and then the Brewers came along.

They chat about nothing in particular until David pulls up next to the cafe. Patrick sneezes.

“Bless you,” they all say in unison, which prompts a growl from him.

Marcy giggles, going to wrap an arm around him as soon as they’re out of the car. Patrick, of course, can’t help but smile.

-----

Patrick sneezes twice, in succession, only just managing to cover his mouth and turn away. David passes him a napkin and pats his leg affectionately as the Brewers bless him and continued their story about a cruise that they went on.

He’s losing steam fast. David can tell when his husband’s tired, even when it’s still morning.

He doesn’t eat much, and David catches a quick wince as he swallows a sip of his coffee. Sore throat. He’s really not feeling well, then. He still makes an attempt to contribute to conversation, but he sounds so awful it’s hard to listen to him.

Marcy and Clint must be able to tell that he wants to go home, because they eat faster than David expected. David has no problem scarfing his food down to leave quicker as well.

“Let’s go see the house,” Marcy says excitedly. “I can’t wait to see what you two have done with it.”

David chuckles as they exit the booth. “Well, it’s a work in progress...”

“Oh, I’m sure it’s beautiful.”

-----

Marcy insists on cooking dinner that night. David and Patrick don’t even try to stop her. One, because she’s Marcy Brewer, and two, because neither of them have cooked real food in weeks and they really looked forward to it.

David is less sure of the idea when she insists he help her. “Oh... oh, I am not the best cook...”

“I won’t make you do too much work, promise. You and I don’t get to spend enough time together!”

Patrick and Clint both chuckle as she drags him with her to the kitchen.

Is this it? Is he about to get The Talk from his mother-in-law? Is he going to have to spend the whole weekend sucking up to them? He hasn’t had to do that for anyone in a while, since he moved to Schitt’s Creek.

Marcy must notice his nervousness, because she lays a hand on his arm and looks up at him with a sweet smile. “You have nothing to worry about, sweetheart. I just wanted an extra hand, and Patrick...”

As if on cue, there’s a loud sneeze from the other room. They both exchange a wince, and David nods.

“Yeah, he will not be touching any of my food anytime soon.”

She giggles. “That poor kid. Colds always hit him so hard.”

“Right? I would’ve never expected it from someone so... strong.”

“I think that’s why. He works so hard all the time, on everything he does, and it backfires. Let’s see what you’ve got in here...” Marcy goes to open the fridge, and only then does David remember that it’s practically empty.

“Um-”

“Oh dear.”

David hissed apologetically. “Uh, sorry, should have warned you. We... eat out a lot.”

Marcy still retains her cheerful spirit. “I should’ve expected nothing less, especially from my son. We’ll figure something out.”

Somehow, they do. David watches her pull random thing from their cupboards and put together a meal that’s not only edible, but delicious.

Patrick doesn’t eat much.

-----

They retire a little earlier that night. They’re all tired from a day of excitement, and Patrick’s already started falling asleep on David’s shoulder in the living room.

He’s sniffling consistently as they pull their covers back and get into bed.

“Hey,” David says, pulling him in for cuddles. Real cuddles that they couldn’t have with his parents around. “How’re you feeling?”

Patrick sniffles thickly, and from the sounds of it, it helps nothing. “Awful,” he says on the exhale. “I don’t know how I’m going to sleep tonight. snf!”

David kisses the top of his head despite the germs that must be radiating off of him. “Sorry.”

“Naw, I’m snf! I’m fine. Just a little stuffed. Hey, can you hand me a- yeah, th- hhh thanks-” he barely gets the last word out before he sneezes in the tissue David manages to get to him in time. He gives a miserable blow that can’t possibly have shifted anything.

“Mm’kay, I know that I’m not exactly a health expert, but I think you should consider taking tomorrow to rest and recuperate.”

“David...” Patrick sighs out. “My parents are here. I can’t just stay in bed all day.”

David nods, affectionately laying a hand on the side of his face. “You’ve done nothing but sneeze and cough and spread germs all day, and I love you dearly but you’re also starting to feel a little warm and I’m worried.”

“It’s a cold.”

“This morning it was ‘nothing’. And I know how you are with colds. You’re going to feel even worse tomorrow. You know this.”

Patrick looks like he wants to argue, but he doesn’t. Because he knows that David is right. “We’ll see how I’m feeling tomorrow,” he says, sniffling and turning off his bedside lamp.

“Deal.”

Patrick barely sleeps that night. Lying down seems to make him cough more, and makes it impossible to breathe through his nose. David imagines that he’d be snoring an awful lot if he could fall asleep at all.

After a particularly rough coughing fit that keeps them both away, David sits up and yawns. “Okay, sit up. I’m getting the cough medicine from the bathroom.”

“Hate that stuff...” Patrick’s so sleepy, apparently, that he thinks he can complain about medicine when he’s spent all night coughing up a lung. David decides to keep from rolling his eyes and kisses Patrick’s forehead instead. He’s too warm, but that’s a problem for later.

“I know, but I want at least some sleep tonight and I think you do too.”

He retrieves the medicine and measures out a... generous dose before handing it to his husband.

Patrick stares at the goop in distaste.

“Down it all at once,” David instructs. “You know, like a shot.”

“More of a beer guy.”

“Not tonight you aren’t.”

Patrick chuckles. A look of determination comes across his features before he does, indeed, down the whole thing like a shot. A disgusting, slimy shot. David can’t help but wince as Patrick coughs, more out of disgust than from illness. He pat’s Patrick’s back.

“Good job.”

“Thanks,” Patrick deadpans.

“Hey, lean forward a little? Maybe I can...”

Patrick does as he’s told, and David tries to rearrange the pillows behind him so he can sleep sitting up at least somewhat comfortably. A cough bubbles out of him, but not as harsh as the fits before. Hopefully the medicine is starting to kick in.

“Thanks,” Patrick repeats, sincerely this time. “Didn’t mean to keep you up all night, David, I’m sorry.”

David hums. “I have an idea,” he says, getting Patrick’s interest. “Maybe we can make tomorrow a relaxing day.”

“What do you mean?”

“Like, movies and popcorn or something. Your mother and I could even get some real groceries and cook dinner again.”

“Whoa, excuse me, did I marry a master chef?”

“Oh God no, but she’s... I like spending time with her.”

Patrick makes a happy noise. “Good. I’m glad. She likes spending time with you, too.” he sneezes, just missing David.

“Bless you, please keep your germs to yourself.”

Patrick gives a wet sniff and drags the back of his hand under his nose. “We’re sleeping in the same bed. I’m sorry to say it, dearest, but I think you’re doomed.”

David groans in disgust. “Eugh. Don’t say that.”

-----

David wakes up far too early. He can already hear Marcy and Clint up and getting ready for the day. It’s the only thing that keeps him from going back to sleep - he should be a good host and... be around, or something.

Patrick is still sound asleep, snoring just as loudly as he usually does when his nose is stuffed. David can’t resist giving a peck on his temple before he gets out of bed.

He pads to the bathroom with a yawn, flips on the light, and recoils from the brightness like a vampire. It agonizes a headache he didn’t realize existed, and-

David sneezes.

His eyes widen.

“Dav’d?” The sound of Patrick sitting up in bed.

David sighs, sniffles. “Morning,” he says, before sneezing again. He turns around into the bedroom and glares at his half-asleep husband. “Thank you for your germs.”

“Aww, David...snf! ‘m sorry. C’mere.” He pats the bed and opens his arms like a needy koala, and David sits down and lets himself become wrapped in his husband’s arms. “Maybe we should put mom and dad up at the motel.”

David sniffles and sighs. “Probably a good idea.”
