Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick has a cold and David's acting weird
Word count: 1.6k

Driving You Crazy

Patrick has a cold.

It’s nothing, really. A few sniffles and sneezes, but he feels perfectly capable of working without worrying about spreading his plague to the customers.

He’s in the middle of a sneezing fit when the door jingles. Walking in the door is none other than David, ten minutes late as usual.

“Hey,” Patrick greets as he wipes the underside of his nose.

David’s eyes go wide. “Um, hey? What was with all of... that?” he asks, waving a hand in Patrick’s direction.

Patrick clears his throat. “Just fighting a little cold.”

If it’s possible, David’s eyes go wider. “Ah. Um, are you okay?”

Patrick waves a hand dismissively. “Yeah, I’m fine. Just a little sniffly.”

David disappears and drops his bag haphazardly in the back. Patrick’s sensing... something. He knew David’s weird about germs, but it’s never been a big concern between the two of them before. David usually ends up making an exception when it comes to Patrick. He won’t even share drinks with his own sister, but he agrees to it with Patrick without a second thought.

Maybe this is his breaking point? Where he realizes that actual germs are involved? Patrick should have still taken David’s anxieties into consideration before he came to work.

“Are you okay?” he asks David, who’s still looking very tense and wide-eyed next to him.

“What? Oh, yeah, of course.” David clears his throat and claps his hands together. “Time to get to work, right?”

“...Right.”

Patrick catches David sending glances at him far more than usual, looking nervous, and goes back to shifting from foot to foot as he goes through the inventory list.

“Excuse me?” A customer, a young woman, says as she walks up to the counter. “Do you carry shampoo?”

Patrick thinks. “I believe we do, actually. There should be some right over here...”

She follows him to a corner of the store where there does happen to be a type of shampoo made from all sorts of things David insists is good for hair.

Her face lights up. “Sweet!”

“No pro-hhh-blem...” Patrick brings his elbow to his face and turns away before sneezing. He recovers with a sniffle. “Whew, ‘scuse me.”

She’s in the middle of blessing him when a series of clatters get their attention on the other side of the store.

“Sorry, clumsy me,” David chuckles as he bends down to pick up all of the bottles he knocked over.

Patrick lets him go to the cafe for lunch. Well- he insists. David can probably use a break from whatever anxieties he’s is dealing with. Patrick only wishes he would say something about it. It’s painfully clear that something is bothering him.

“If you’re sure,” David says, swallowing hard.

“Totally sure. Yeah. Go ahead.”

David almost looks reluctant to leave, which isn’t something Patrick anticipated. He should be relieved to get away, right?

Patrick takes a deep (snuffly) breath the moment David leaves. He needs to think of the right way to bring this up. He and David have been dating long enough that they should be able to have conversations like this, and Patrick’s fine with being the one to initiate them.

-----

“Stevie, this is not funny.”

Stevie doesn’t stop laughing. “Oh, I think it is. I can’t believe you haven’t told him, David. He’s your boyfriend.”

“Well I’m not exactly known for being open with the person I’m dating,” David admits, or argues, really. “This isn’t something you can just tell someone.”

“You told me, though.”

David sighs. “You’re you.”

“I don’t know what that means, but I’ll take it,” Stevie says before shoving a fry in her mouth.

-----

Patrick looks up at the sound of the door. “David, I- oh.”

Alexis’ eyebrows raise as she walks in, heels clicking against the wood floor. “I’m definitely not David, sorry.”

“Yeah.” Patrick sighs. He sniffles and grabs a tissue to wipe his nose. “He’s at lunch. I’m just waiting for him to get back.”

“Ooooh,” Alexis leans against the counter. “Trouble in paradise?”

Shit. He needs to be careful what he says to the gossip of the town. Still... Alexis might be the town gossip, but she’s also David’s sister.

Patrick clears his throat. “Alexis, can I ask you a question. About- about David?”

She practically jumps with delight. “Absolutely, you can.”

Patrick’s regretting this already. “He’s been acting... ugh, hold on.” An itch makes itself known in the back of his nose and he pulls up the same tissue to muffle the sneeze. Alexis’ eyes go about as wide as her brothers' were that morning.

“Bless you. Are you sick?”

“Yeah, just a little cold. Snf! Anyway-”

"Oh my God, David must be dying right now,” says Alexis with a worryingly-big smirk.

Patrick raises an eyebrow. "What? What do you mean?"

Alexis scoffs. "You know exactly what I mean," she says with a few added winks.

"I don't think that I do?"

Alexis face lights up in realization. “Oh my God, you really don’t know, do you?”

“No, I don’t. Would you mind enlightening me?” his annoyance goes completely unnoticed by her.

Alexis giggles as if Patrick has told him something juicy when he hasn’t even had the chance to explain the problem he wanted advice on.

“Okay, so,” Alexis taps a finger on his hand with each word. “David has this thing for um... sneezing? And honestly, the only reason that I know is because I found some really weird porn open on his computer one time and he swore me to secrecy in exchange for one of his diamond bracelets. Which went with the house so that agreement is now nullified.” She says all of this while flailing a hand wildly, looking very proud of herself for being the one to divulge this information. “Anyway, one time his girlfriend stayed at our house for Christmas and she had a cold and I’m pretty sure David wanted to die and it was so fucking funny.”

Patrick blinks. “I’m sor- David’s...? I mean, since I have a cold...”

Alexis is already nodding. “Basically, you’re driving him insane. But like, in a good way. But I have to go now because talking about this kind of stuff about my brother is kind of disgusting.”

Patrick doesn’t even pay attention to Alexis as she leaves. He gapes. So David’s been... turned on this whole time. He finds it funny how much of a relief it is that he hasn’t been in a constant state of anxiety since he came to work. Just...

Patrick chuckles.

They need to talk about this. Absolutely.

But first... he can have fun with this.

-----

Patrick lets out the sneeze he's been holding the moment David's back at the store. He watches his boyfriend freeze in his tracks and swallow hard.

Patrick isn't sure why he didn't see it earlier. It's so clear now that this is nothing close to anxiety.

"Have a good lunch?" He asks as he wipes his nose.

"Mhm, yep," David nods. "Brought you some soup? That's what you're supposed to do for sick people, right?" He sets a styrofoam container on the counter.

"Aww, you're sweet."

"Oh, I know." David clears his throat as Patrick opens the container. "So um, how are you feeling?"

This is it. This is Patrick's chance. "Oh, you know, I'd be feeling fine if my nose wasn't bothering me so much."

David's eyebrows raise. "Really? That's… that's too bad…" he says, uncharacteristically conversational. "Do you um, need anything?"

"No, I just wish it would stop itching." Patrick sniffles deeply with purpose, and he catches David's eyes go straight to his nose. How did he not catch this sooner? David isn't exactly the most subtle person. Patrick feels a well-timed tickle in his nose, and he sniffs quickly before turning away to sneeze.

David’s face is bright red. “Bless you,” he says, clearing his throat again.

“Ugh, snf! Thank you.”

He decides not to deliberately mess with David for the rest of the work day. They have stuff to do after all. But when they lock the doors...

Patrick’s not feeling particularly great, and he wishes that he was putting on dramatics as he’s sneezing and sniffling non-stop. His nose won’t stop running and it’s constantly requiring his attention.

The great thing, though, is that David is a mess.

The moment they walk into Patrick’s apartment intent on having a movie night, he lets loose.

It’s during a particular sneezing fit in the middle of the movie that Patrick is met with messy results.

“Hey, can you pass me a tissue?” He asks.

David pauses as if his brain needs to process the question, and he grabs the tissue box and hastily sets it between him and Patrick.

Patrick intends on blowing his nose, but instead he ends up sneezing again. His nose still itches... so, he decides to irritate one more sneeze out of himself.

David freezes.

Patrick can’t hold back a smirk this time.

David gasps. “Who told you?”

“Told me what?” Patrick asks with an innocent shrug.

“Was it Stevie? I’m going to actually kill her.”

“Wait, Stevie knows and I didn’t?”

David gasps again, even more scandalized. “Alexis?! Ew, oh my God... I’m stealing one of her bracelets back.”

Patrick chuckles, and then sniffles. “David,” he says, voice thick with congestion. “You should’ve just told me.”

“Do you know what happened the last time I told someone I was dating? They called me a freak and left.”

“Okay, well... snf! I’m definitely not going to call you a freak.”

David shifts. His eyes go to Patrick’s nose again, and then the rest of him.

Patrick finds himself in the middle of a deep, passionate kiss. This isn’t how he expected his night to go, but he’s perfectly fine with it.
