RIP De Rolo Shoes

His stomach started feeling weird a while after dinner. He knew it wasn’t the food, it wouldn’t have been fair to the kitchen staff to try to blame them. He’d been strangely tired all day, so the fact that he was getting sick made more sense than Vax would have liked.

He certainly didn’t intend to vomit in the hallway of the castle as he was walking with Percy. He heard a gasp, a swear, before he felt another rush of vomit come up and pass his lips.

-----

“I am sorry, Freddy.”

“Mhm, I know you are.”

Percy somehow managed to exude the aura of a disappointed mother. Vax wasn’t even sure why he cared. It’s not like it was his fault that he was sick, right?

“I mean it!”

“I know, Vax,” Percy muttered as he reached into a closet and pulled out an old blanket that looked like it hadn’t been used in centuries.

“Is that my punishment? A dusty old blanket?”

The slightest of grins appeared on Percy’s face. “Perhaps. Take it or leave it, we honestly don’t have a lot of spare blankets around the castle.”

Vax did indeed take it. He was beyond caring at this point. “It’s not like I was aiming for you, Percival.”

“And yet,” Percy sighed out. “My shoes will never be the same. Would you like some water?”

Vax rolled his eyes and nodded. “Please.”

As Percy left, he couldn’t help but chuckle. Being sick was awful, but vomiting on Percival De Rolo made things just a bit better.
