Title: Chronologically
Fandom: Shadowhunters
Summary: Jace and Magnus each have their own person to care for
Word count: 2.6k

Chronologically

It starts during lunch, though he doesn’t notice. He isn’t hungry, assumes that he’d eaten a big breakfast, and just nibbles on his sandwich.

He starts to realize that something is wrong by the time he returns to his office. It isn’t like him to feel as tired and out of it as he does at the moment.

He winces as he sits down, every movement a greater effort than it should be. His entire body aches.

Alec swears. It’s time to accept his fate.

Alec should have known better than to think he could escape the flu that’s going around the institute.

Angel blood certainly boosts their immune systems, but illnesses still spread through the place like wildfire, especially during cold and flu season.

He grabs a bottle from his desk drawer and downs two ibuprofen. Realistically, he has a lot of work that can’t wait. But he also... he really shouldn’t be around anyone else if he’s contagious. He doesn’t want to contribute to the local pandemic. He’ll just go-

Fuck.

He can’t just go to Magnus’ and expect him to take care of him. What if he was busy? Alec sighs. He’ll just stay in his room, just like he would if he and Magnus hadn’t planned a night in.

“You look awful,” is the first thing Jace says as he walks in. If it’s that obvious, then Alec should definitely not be working.

Alec nods, setting his elbow on the desk and laying his head in his hand. “I think I’m getting the flu. I was about to call Magnus and cancel our date.”

Jace makes a sympathetic noise. “So you too, huh?” he continues when Alec raises an eyebrow. “Clary’s pretty sick, too. Woke up with it this morning. Izzy’s doing the work of like, ten people right now.”

Alec sighs. “Maybe I should stay, then...”

“Naw, we’ll figure it out. You shouldn’t be working if you’re as sick as everyone else is.”

“I’m not,” Alec says, and then, “Yet, I guess. Okay, I know you’ve got Clary, but would you mind making sure the place doesn’t burn down or something?”

Jace scoffs and nods. “You don’t have anything to worry about.”

Alec calls Magnus the moment Jace leaves, eager to get everything cleared up so he can just go to his room and sleep this thing away. He’s feeling worse with every passing second, his muscles making him feel like he’s been hit by a truck.

“You poor thing,” Magnus says. “You didn’t mention that you were sick yesterday when I was there.”

“I don’t know what happened. I felt fine this morning, and then it just... hit me,” Alec admits. He holds the phone with one hand as he straightens and gathers his paperwork with the other. He makes the mistake of looking up too quickly and finds himself swaying dangerously. Spots prickle at the edge of his vision. “Whoa...” he thinks he says it quietly enough, but Magnus must be on high alert.

“Alexander? Is something wrong?”

Alec blinks dazedly until the room stops spinning. “I’m fine.” He sniffles, and apparently his nose is running now. Great. “Just got a little dizzy.”

Magnus makes a discontented hum on the other end of the call. “I hope you’re going to be resting and not working.”

“Yeah.” Alec clears his throat. “I’m taking the day off. Gonna go to my room and sleep for eternity.”

Magnus chuckles. “Hopefully not that long. Do you have someone to keep an eye on you? Jace? Izzy?”

“Um,” Alec has almost all of his work gathered when he starts to feel lightheaded again. He carefully sinks back into his desk chair to let everything settle. “Jace is taking care of Clary, and Izzy’s taking care of... well, literally everything else. I’ll be fine. I’ve dealt with the flu before, Magnus.”

“So you’re going to be alone?”

“It’s not a big deal. I-” The most inopportune time for Alec to sneeze, he thinks.

“Bless you, darling. I’m on my way.”

“Magnus, you don’t have to-” but he’s already ended the call, and Alec can’t bring himself to care. He’s not sure he wants to care. He’s just tired, and he’s quickly realizing that he really doesn’t feel good.

Not thirty seconds go by before a portal forms in the middle of his office and his beautiful boyfriend steps out.

“Oh Alexander...” Magnus sighs the moment he lays eyes on him. Alec hadn’t even considered that he might be running a fever until Magnus presses the back of his hand to his forehead and finds his boyfriend’s skin blissfully cool against his own. “Well, you’re definitely coming down with a fever. Let’s go get you in bed before you’re too sick to move.”

“It’s not going to get that bad,” Alec says with a sideways smile as he uses Magnus’ arm to pull himself up to stand once again. Magnus’ concern is sort of adorable, he has to admit.

“You haven’t had the flu in a while, have you?”

“I-... have you?”

Magnus opens his mouth to answer, and then closes it. “Just take my word for it.” He reaches up to press a kiss to Alec’s cheek, and they both walk through the portal.

-----

Clary certainly didn’t want to look like this when Jace returned to her room, but here she is, knelt over the toilet when she hears the door open and Jace’s worried voice.

“Clary?”

She spits the remnants of illness into the water below her and flushes it away. Standing on shaky legs, Clary swears and grabs onto the wall until she’s balanced enough to walk out without worry of falling on her face.

Jace rushes over to her the moment she opens the door, but Clary raises a hand to insist that she doesn’t need help. He backs off and lets her get into bed before sitting on the edge and pressing the inside of his wrist to her forehead.

“Jeez, you’re burning up,” he mutters before shifting his hand to her neck. It’s cold, despite the fact that she is also cold.

“Really?” Clary asks with a shiver. “Because I’m freezing.” It’s supposed to be lighthearted, but Jace’s worried frown makes her realize that he might need more comforting than she does. “Hey,” she brings a hand up to cup his chin, attempting a bright smile. “I’m going to be fine, Jace.”

Jace nods, heaving a sigh. “I know. Logically, I know, it’s just hard seeing anyone I love sick.”

Her heart warms. There’s something so sweet about it, even though she doesn’t want him worrying himself to no return just because she has the flu.

He clears his throat. “Anyway,” he shakes his head as if to clear it, and Clary continues getting comfortable in bed. “Turns out that Alec’s sick too, so Izzy and I are going to have our hands full this week. But I’m going to make sure I’m still here if you need anything,” he assures.

“Poor Alec. Why do I feel like he’s taking this about as well as you are?”

Jace chuckles. “I was pretty surprised that he decided to take the day off without someone having to force him. A year ago he passed out in the middle of a mission. We found out later that what he’d been calling a cold was actually pneumonia.”

“Oh God,” Clary gasped, and then smirked. “Seems like Magnus is doing him some good then, huh?”

“I think so,” Jace agreed. His phone buzzed and he sighed heavily at the text. “Izzy needs my help with something. Text me if you need anything, okay? Or if you’re feeling worse, or-”

Clary lays a hand on his. “I’ll be fine. I’m just going to sleep, anyway.”

“Okay,” Jace nods. His gaze stays on her for a second longer before he makes his way to the door. “Anything!” He repeats.

“Got it!” Clary yells back, though her voice isn’t nearly as strong as his right now. She chuckles to herself and sinks deep into the covers.

-----

Magnus was right, Alec realizes, because the moment he’s in bed, he isn’t sure he’s ever going to be able to get out again. He shudders under the covers. The medicine must have worn off, because he can’t get warm even though the thermometer informed them that his insides are nearly boiling.

He coughs as Magnus walks into the room.

Magnus makes a sympathetic noise. “That doesn’t sound good,” he says. In a swift move, he sets a tray on Alec’s lap and presses a kiss to his temple.

“What’s this?” Alec looks down at the

“Dinner,” Magnus says as if Alec should know that already.

“It’s like two in the afternoon.”

“Mhmm, it is, but you said that you didn’t eat lunch and your body can’t do a thing for you if you don’t eat.” He sits on the bed next to Alec and affectionately nuzzles him. As much as Alec loves it, he can’t help but lean away, much to Magnus’ disappointment.

He bites back a smile. “Didn’t you say you’ve been sick before? Magnus, if you can get sick you shouldn’t be so close to me.” He sniffles.

Magnus chuckles. “Well, you are in my bed, and I do plan on sleeping next to you tonight, so I doubt it matters.” He gives Alec’s cheek a sneaky kiss and grins.

“Yeah, but should you be tempting fate?” Alec asks, even as he’s enjoying the love.

“I’m going to make sure that you feel taken care of, alright?” Another kiss. 

“O...kay?” This time Alec leans into the kiss with a smile. “I’m glad.”

“Now, eat your soup.”

Alec does make an effort, but he can’t even finish half of it before he sets it down and gives Magnus an apologetic look. Magnus just nuzzles his temple before taking the bowl to the kitchen. 

He’s tired, and thinks he might finally be able to sleep despite the aches and chills. So Alec sinks into the covers, and he’s asleep before Magnus returns.

-----

Magnus does his best to focus on the potion he's making and not on his sick boyfriend. Although, he does decide to leave the bedroom door open in case Alec needs anything.

He quietly sits in his living room and uses magic to stir the mixture so he doesn't have to bother. The sound of Alec's congested snores make him chuckle, the poor guy is going to wake up feeling so much worse than he had when he went to sleep.

Magnus knows there's more symptoms to come. He's seen the flu run its course hundreds of times, both in himself and in others. But he can't help but feel a strange sense of… contentment. Taking care of your boyfriend when he's home sick, how ridiculously domestic and normal. It's been decades since he's had this. He's determined to make Alec feel as loved as possible.

He's preparing to add a third component to the bubbling cauldron when his phone buzzes on the coffee table.

"Jace," Magnus answers with a mix of confusion and delight. As much as he likes Jace, he's not used to seeing the man's name appear on his phone. 

"Hey, I figured I'd call you instead, in case Alec was sleeping."

"Ah, he crashed the moment I finished getting some food in him."

"How is he?"

"He's.. he's sick," Magnus answers plainly. "It's… too soon to know, I think. I'm just glad I got him to eat something."

"Thank the Angel. He hardly ate anything today."

"So I've heard," Magnus sighs out. 

"So he hasn't been like, throwing up or anything?"

Magnus pauses. "No…?"

"Good." There's a strange breathiness to his voice that Magnus can't quite pin down. "I just uh, Clary's been throwing up and it's starting to freak me out a little bit."

"Oh, poor biscuit," Magnus says. "Tell her I hope she feels better. Rest and fluids are the best you can do for this sort of thing."

"Will do. She seems more okay with it than I am, to be honest. Uh hey Magnus-"

"Yeah?"

"Thanks for taking care of him. He needs someone looking out for him the same way he does everyone else." Jace clears his throat. "Don't tell him I said that."

Magnus smiles wide. "I wouldn't trade taking care of Alexander for the world."

"I know."

-----

Jace somehow manages to get two meals on one tray. He’s been waiting for dinner for a while, not because he’s hungry, but because he’s ready to sit down and quietly eat with his girlfriend, even if she’s sick.

Clary’s in bed reading a book, and it’s a comforting sight after the day he’s had. 

-----

Magnus quietly goes about getting ready for bed. He even uses magic to make simple things like brushing his teeth and changing his clothes completely silent to avoid waking the dead-asleep shadowhunter in his bed.

In the end, it doesn't seem to matter.

Alec shifts and makes a noise of discomfort, getting Magnus' attention immediately. 

"What's wrong, darling?" Magnus asks, quietly in case Alec is still asleep. In that case, though, this could be a nightmare, and is that more concerning?

After a few more seconds of shifting, Alec's eyes finally open. Magnus is about to welcome him back to the conscious word when he makes a strangled sound and darts to the bathroom.

Magnus winces at the retching that echoes from the bathroom. Seems that Alec only managed to close the door halfway, and the blankets he was covered in are trailed across the floor from his haste to get there. Magnus picks them up and puts them aside, and with a snap of his fingers, the sheets are clean and free of a day of being slept and sweated in.

Alec wanders out a mere few minutes later and hums appreciatively as Magnus helps him to bed. “Sorry,” he says. His voice is raw from vomiting. “You didn’t exactly sign up for this tonight.”

“I did, though.” 

-----

The two of them get comfortable, or at least, Magnus does. Alec seems to be having a harder time. He tosses and turns, making tiny annoyed grunts that he probably thinks Magnus can’t hear. 

“Alexander,” Magnus mumbles into his pillow. “You’re moving too much.”

Alec sighs. “Sorry. I’m trying not to…” He continues to wriggle around, and Magnus finally opens his eyes and moves closer to his sick boyfriend. 

Magnus can hardly see him in the dark, but now that he’s close, he can feel the heat radiating off of Alec. Magnus lazily feels his forehead and frowns. 

“Your temperature is higher. No wonder you feel so uncomfortable.”

Alec huffs. 

“Alright,” Magnus moves to rest against him, effectively holding him down. “How about I stay awake with you?”

“You don’t have to.” 

“But you want me to?”

“...Yeah. I’m sorry Magnus, I don’t feel-”

“Shhh, it’s okay.” Magnus presses a kiss to his shoulder, the closest part of him he can get to. 

They lay quietly for a while. Alec’s still achy and feverish and all sorts of uncomfortable, but he seems to enjoy the contact. Magnus thinks he’s falling asleep when he hears a very clear,

“You’re good at this.”

Magnus huffs out a lazy laugh. “Thank you.”

“No, I mean…” Alec sighs. “Did you have someone to take care of you? When you were sick?”

Oh. “Not… always. It’s not that hard to manage on my own, especially with magic,” although the use of magic was sometimes part of the reason he was so sick in the first place. “Sometimes I called Catarina, when things got particularly… scary.”

Alec stays silent. Magnus feels the need to continue, fill the quiet in the air. 

“Which is why I’m determined to make sure that you feel taken care of. I was alone all those times, and yes, it was a little miserable. I imagine you’re not much different when your siblings aren’t around.”

Alec chuckles. “Yeah.” But there’s something to his voice. He’s still thinking about it. Magnus nuzzles his neck.

“Get some sleep, Alexander.”
