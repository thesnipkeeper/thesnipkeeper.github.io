Self-Importance

Ted didn’t show up for work.

It was concerning, of course it was, because for all the man’s faults, he was a punctual person who despised the idea of being late for anything, ever.

And checking on late employees really, very much, was not Rebecca’s job. She was the literal boss and she had better things to do than take a car to Ted Lasso’s apartment.

But there was that nagging feeling that something was horribly wrong. The panic attacks hit him hard and fast, could knock him out of commission for a good day or two afterward. She was worried.

She checked her phone. No text, no call.

“Leslie, have you heard from Ted?” she asked Higgins as he came to give her some more godawful paperwork.

He shook his head. “No one has, actually. The team is rather worried!” That... that wasn’t comforting. Higgins waited a few seconds before asking, “Shall I-?”

Rebecca stood up. “I’ll take care of it.”

“Oh,” his eyes widened as he nodded. He’d caught onto her concern, apparently. Good old Higgins. “Alright, please let me know if things are okay?”

“Of course,” she agreed kindly as she grabbed her purse.

Apparently Rebecca didn’t have better things to do than take a car to Ted Lasso’s apartment, because that was exactly where she found herself.

She knocked. No answer. She knocked again.

When that failed, she grabbed her phone with the intent of calling him. If she could hear his ringtone from inside, then she could be worried. The door opened before she reached his name in her contacts.

“Oh... Ted...” she said with nothing but sympathy. He looked... he looked awful! Rebecca had never seen the man’s hair so messy, nor had she ever seen him in sweatpants and a plain T-shirt. He didn’t look like coach Lasso at all.

“I’m sorry,” he said quickly. “I’m so sorry, boss, I- I forgot to set an alarm, and I- think I’m comin’ down with somethin’ to tell you the truth. Brain melted a little last night.” He waved a hand around his temple, gesturing vaguely to his head as if that meant anything.

Rebecca found herself sighing in relief. “Ted, I thought you- I thought you were-”

“Havin’ a bad day?” He finished, offering a goofy smile that had no place in this conversation or while he was in this horrible state.

“Yes,” she confirmed. “Yes, I was worried.”

“I’d say. I should be flattered, the boss herself showing up to drag me to work.” He muffled a cough into the crook of his arm as he found his work bag and began throwing things in it.

“No, that’s not- you can take a day off, Ted. It’s alright.” Rebecca stepped into the apartment and closed the door behind her. “You look terrible.”

He chuckled. “Gee, that’s a little hurtful. And to think I was just about to tell you that you looked super in charge today!”

“Appreciated, and I’m sorry that you feel that way, because it’s the truth,” Rebecca said playfully. “Ted...”

He was still trying to get ready for work in the most inefficient way possible. Rebecca had no intention of bringing the clearly-infectious man anywhere near her place of work.

She wasn’t sure exactly what to do about this until he wavered and ended up dizzily lowering himself onto a couch that looked like it’d been occupied at least part of the night. Another weak cough bubbled up from his throat.

Rebecca took the opportunity to lay a hand on his forehead. She gasped and sat next to him as she said, “You’re burning up. There’s no way I’m letting you go to work today.”

“Look, I... it’s just a little temperature. Nothing I can’t handle.”

“There’s no argument here, Ted. You’re staying home.”

His shoulders seemed to relax a bit. He did look like he’d had a rough night, and that cough probably had something to do with it.

“I didn’t mean to make you come all the way here.”

“You didn’t,” she assured. “I’m checking on a friend. That’s what friends do, right?”

He smiled. “Sure is. Thanks.”

“I’d stay longer,” Rebecca said as she stood up, giving his shoulder a squeeze and ignoring the feverish heat radiating off of him. “But I have work to do, and I also would rather not catch whatever you have.”

A chuckle, that ended in a dry cough and had Rebecca wincing in sympathy. “Fair enough, boss. See you tomorrow.”

“See you when your fever’s gone,” she corrected.

“Jeez... alright.”

“Good-bye, Ted. Feel better.”

-----

A work day just wasn’t the same without Ted Lasso around to- well, to be himself. She missed the sudden intrusions into her office with a terrible joke and a sunny tone. She missed... God, she missed the biscuits.

“Ted’s ill,” she informed Higgins. “Tell the others that they’re on their own today.”

He nodded, and then frowned as he processed what she said. “He’s ill? As in, properly ill?”

“Yes, probably flu or something.”

“Oh, that’s too bad. But, to be honest, I sort of feel terrible for-”

“For being relieved?”

“Well... yes,” he admitted. “But I’m sure Coach Lasso can handle a flu much easier than anything else.”

Rebecca wasn’t too sure. Lasso wasn’t exactly an example of “self care”.

-----

She had some groceries sent to his place that night.

She didn’t intend on doing any more, but... he entered her thoughts yet again as she ate a late dinner in front of the TV. Ted probably wouldn’t have gone out to get anything, he may not have been in a state to.

Her phone buzzed as she finished her last bite and she swallowed quickly before she answered.

“Coach Lasso,” Rebecca greeted.

“Hey boss, just wanted to thank you for the stuff. That’s real nice of you!” He tried so hard to sound like his usual enthusiastic self, but his voice was hard to listen to. He sounded equally hoarse and congested. Rebecca imagined that he hadn’t done much that day besides sleep and cough.

“You’re very welcome. I wanted to make sure that you were...” taken care of, but that seemed like the wrong thing to say when he was alone. “Alright. Are you alright?”

“Sure! Yeah. I mean, I’m a little chilly sometimes and it’ll take me a bit to fall asleep tonight, but I’m hardly on death’s door, y’know?” he chuckled. She didn’t.

“Please take care of yourself?” she found herself begging.

“Don’t you worry!”

She did anyway.

Ted called out sick the next day. Thank God. She was worried that she’d have to get security to drag him out if he tried to show up looking the way he had yesterday.

She and Higgins weren’t quite sure what to think when they got through an entire meeting without the American barging in to say something ridiculous.

“He didn’t sound well when I spoke to him last night,” she mentioned.

“I can’t imagine he feels well, either. Flu is terrible.”

She considered sending him a text to see if he was alright, and decided against it. The man would drop anything to reply to a text, he didn’t need to be bothered.

-----

Ted came in to work two days later, announcing himself with a terrible cough as he made his way through the halls.

He didn’t look better.

His hair wasn’t done quite right, and Rebecca watched as he had even less coordination than he usually did.

He sounded worse than he had on the phone a few nights ago.

“Ted,” she caught up to him as he made his way down to the pitch. They let the team go ahead of them as the slowed down.

“Yeah, what’s up?”

Rebecca and Beard exchanged looks as he passed them. His face begged her to do something.

Was this a normal thing for Ted? What was she thinking, of course it was a normal thing for Ted to come into work before he was well. The man would work through anything if people let him. That was how he measure his... usefulness, in a way.

“Are you sure you’re ready to be back to work?” She wasn’t going to beat around the bush. She never had enough time these days.

He muffled a cough into his sleeve, and seemed to be surprised by how painful it was and how breathless it left him. Rebecca bit the inside of her mouth as he went to answer.

“I know I don’t sound better,” he said, clearing his throat. “But it’s just some residual stuff, y’know? Think some fresh air will do me some good!”

Oh, Jesus. “Ted, it’s cold outside. And that cough-”

“I’m all good, boss! Promise!”

She stopped, prompting him to stop as well, and narrowed her eyes. “Don’t you dare lie to me, Coach Lasso.” The threat was partially a joke, because they were friends and she could get away with it. But not completely. The words “I’m good” weren’t super trustworthy coming from the man who’d give someone the shirt off his back in the middle of a snowstorm and willingly die of hypothermia.

He lifted his hands and leaned back. “No lying here. I just wanna get back to work!”

She sighed. “Alright. But if you start feeling like you need to go home-”

“I’ll do so, right away!” he coughed again.

God, Ted. He wouldn’t let any player near the pitch if they sounded like he did right now. But she supposed that he wasn’t going to be nearly as physical as the players were.

They reached the pitch before she realized it, and she found herself following him out. It was freezing outside. Everyone, including Ted, was bundled up.

“Let’s get to work!” Ted yelled hoarsely.

She needed to get inside, Rebecca decided. It was too damn cold for her to be hanging around out here. She didn’t turn around quick enough to miss the look of disappointment that Beard sent her way.

Rebecca shrugged helplessly at him behind Ted’s back. He nodded, and went back to work.

Beard was an expert on communicating non-verbally, Rebecca was not. What had they just said to each other?

-----

Rebecca got a lot of work done that day. A lot of e-mails sent, a lot of calls returned, a lot of papers signed. She’d completely forgotten about the players outside her window as she and Leslie went over the schedule for next week.

Suffice to say, of all the contacts in her phone, she didn’t expect “Beard” to be the one to show up when it started buzzing. She and Higgins exchanged raised eyebrows. She chose to put the call on speaker for the sake of finishing the rest of her cursed paperwork.

“Yes, Beard, what is it?”

“So, we’re at the hospital.”

Rebecca stopped what she was doing. Higgins’ eyes widened. “Excuse me?”

“Would’ve told you sooner, but it happened kinda fast and we forgot-”

“Beard, what happened?”

“Ted passed out on the pitch.”

“Oh my God...” Leslie mouth to himself.

“He- he fainted? Is he alright?”

“Define alright,” Beard said, and as if sensing her eye roll, he continued, “He’s got a pretty high fever. They’re thinking pneumonia.”

She buried her head in her hands and gave a mumbled, “Jesus Christ.”

“Think he’s gonna need a few more days off.”

“Tell him that he’s not to come back to work until he’s gotten authorization from you,” damn, that was a good system. Maybe that was one she needed to use more often.

Beard huffed out a chuckle. “Gotcha.”

-----

Rebecca found herself, yet again, at Ted’s flat. The only difference was that he knew that she was coming this time. He said he’d leave the door unlocked, and she was thankful to find it so lest she have to knock and force him out of bed.

The place was boiling.

“Ted, I’m here,” she called.

Rebecca glanced at the thermostat and swore. Was he trying to cook in here?

She reached his bedroom and nudged the door open with her free hand. The other hand was occupied by a container of the best soup she could find in the area. The poor man probably hadn’t felt like eating today.

Ted mumbled a tired greeting as he sat up in bed. She let him get his bearings as he rubbed the sleep out of his eyes and yawned. “Hey boss!” he finally got out. He still sounded like shit, he still looked like shit, and he probably would for the next couple of weeks if pneumonia was like Google said that it was.

“Delivery,” she said lamely, holding up the soup.

“Oh...” he winced, tugging a blanket around his shoulders. “I...”

“You have to eat something.” She shoved some tangled blankets aside and sat on the edge of the bed. “I know that it’s probably the last thing that you feel like doing right now, but...”

“But I’ve gotta keep my strength up,” Ted nodded.

“Exactly.”

He accepted the container and the accompanying spoon with shaky hands. His breath wheezed as he tried to sit up a little straighter. “You make this?”

Rebecca laugh out loud. “Oh God no. But it is the best soup that money can buy, I assure you.”

He smiled. “Sounds good to me.”

“Now Ted, do you mind telling me why it feels like an oven in here?”

“What’re you talkin’ about?” He began stirring the soup in lieu of eating it. “It’s freezing in here!”

“No,” Rebecca informed. “It’s not.”

He looked at her for a moment with a blank expression as the cogs turned in his head. “Ah.”

She felt his forehead, receiving no resistance in return. “Your fever clearly hasn’t gone away.”

Ted nodded. He swallowed a minuscule bit of soup. “Doc said it’d take a while for the meds to kick in.”

“I see,” she said. As he ate, she got up and turned the heat down, and couldn’t help tidying the living room a bit while she did. When she returned, he was coughing horribly into a tissue with the soup abandoned on his nightstand. His bedroom was a mess as well, but it probably wouldn’t do any good to try to clean up, nor did she want to.

His cough got worrying fast as it turned into breathless wheezing. His body was so desperate to get rid of the gunk inside of him that it didn’t let him fucking breathe. Rebecca rushed to his side, and then felt useless.

She tried rubbing his back, and he didn’t seem to mind, so she continued as the cough tapered off.

“Is that how you sounded before your hospital trip today?” she asked, slightly accusatory.

“That was an isolated incident. Cold air.”

“Mm.”

“Yes, you were right.”

“I know that I was, but it feels fantastic to hear it,” she chuckled as she handed him a glass of water. He took small careful sips, still trying to get his breath back from the fit. “You need to take care of yourself more often, Lasso. I don’t know what the team would do without you.”

What would she do without him? What was her life before the personification of obnoxious sunlight walked into her office?

“Understood,” he coughed weakly.

She rubbed his arm and sighed. “Now, I don’t like leaving you alone.”

Ted shook his head. “Beard’s on his way over. Don’t you worry, ma’am. I’m in good hands.”

She nodded approvingly. “Alright, then. Take care of yourself, Ted.”

“Will do!”

He wouldn’t. He already proved that he wouldn’t. But Beard would, and Rebecca would. And that was good enough for now.
