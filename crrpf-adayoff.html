A Day Off

Anyone could see how stressed Matt was today. He was distracted, constantly stumbling over his own words and forgetting what he was doing. The crew had noticed and were going easy on him when it came to how many questions they were asking. 

Taliesin ran into him in the kitchen when he was grabbing a bottle of water. 

“Did you sleep last night?”

Matt chuckled. “Who has time for sleep these days?”

“Fair enough.”

Matt hadn’t closed the fridge yet, in fact he hadn’t moved at all. He stared at nothing for a good few seconds and it was starting to freak Taliesin out.

He reached out hesitantly and placed a hand on Matt’s shoulder. “Matt?”

Matt exhaled noisily, but it didn’t stop there. He sounded short of breath, shallow inhales and exhales. The fridge door drifted closed. 

When Matt finally looked up, his eyes were red and watery and he was still trying desperately to get his breathing back to normal. Taliesin wasn’t used to this, but he knew a panic attack when he saw one.

“Do you need to sit down? Come on, let’s go…” he led his friend to a nearby couch while trying to keep his own nervousness at bay. Matt complied without a word, and sat down with his eyes still glued to the ground.

He buried his face in his hands and began to cry.

"Matt? Oh, fuck, c'mere..." Taliesin gently pulled him into a hug and wrapped his arms around his friend’s shuddering frame.

A wet patch formed into Taliesin's shirt as Matt sobbed into his shoulder. His entire body was wracked with erratic breathing, the kind that came with hard, panicky crying. All Taliesin could do was hold him.

“You’ll be okay…” Taliesin said softly. “Just relax, I know…”

Eventually, things quieted down. 

Footsteps caught Taliesin’s attention, and perhaps Matt’s as well, though he didn’t bother to look up, and Liam walked in with his coffee cup in-hand. He looked like he wanted to make a joke before he Taliesin’s grim expression and realized this wasn’t the time.

He okay? Liam mouthed.

Taliesin nodded and continued to rub his back. He will be.

Should I get Marisha?

Taliesin thought for a moment and nodded, and then Liam was rushing out.

Marisha rushed to him the moment she walked into the kitchen. She spoke to him with such gentleness and softness before he nodded and sat up.

“I’m good,” he breathed out. “I’m fine now. Sorry,” Matt looked toward Taliesin sheepishly.

“Matt…” Taliesin frowned. “Maybe you should take a day off.”

Marisha nodded. “That’s a good idea. I’m taking you home.”

“What? No, no no, guys I’m fine!”

“You need a break,” Taliesin decided.

-----

Marisha was more than ready to work from home so she could stay with Matt, but she was a vital cog that kept the studio running smoothly and Taliesin could tell that the crew was nervous about being left to their own devices.

So he volunteered instead. He had some script work to do that he could take with him to the Mercers’ house.

“Wanna watch-? Matt!” 

Matt was a deer in headlights, in the middle of pulling his work computer out of his bag. “I have work to do, Tal. So do you!”

He had a point. If Taliesin was gonna work, then what was stopping Matt.

“No, you’re right,” Taliesin sighed out. “Fine, neither of us will work today.”

“Wait, Tal, I-”

Taliesin looked him in the eyes. “Please, just relax for one day. You didn’t have a panic attack at work for no reason. You need a break.”

“Everyone needs a break. Doesn’t always mean that they’re gonna get one.”

“Well you are today.”

He somehow managed to get Matt comfortable on the couch. The two of them watched a terrible home remodel show on Netflix and scrolled idly through their phones.

“I’m sorry about today,” Matt broke the silence between them. 

There was genuine guilt in his expression.

“No, don’t. I wouldn’t have helped you if I didn’t want to. You know that, right? I mean, you gave me an excuse to take a day off too, so…”

“You really needed one too, in all fairness.”

“Naw…”

“Tal, I’ve been watching you this week. You haven’t been going home until we lock up the studio.”

He didn’t really consider that being busy. The studio was sort of his second home by this point, but he realized that Matt might’ve been right. Staying at work way past the time he needed to probably wasn’t the healthiest. Now that he thought about it, he has been drinking more tea to keep up with the tiredness.

“Okay, so I guess we both need to learn to take breaks sometimes.”

“We’ve got each other’s backs, huh?”

“Absolutely.”
