Title: Hangover
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David thinks he's just hungover, and he's very wrong
Word count: 4.3k

Hangover

David’s nursing a headache and a cup of mediocre cafe coffee when Alexis walks in. She presses her lips together in a tight smile and widens her eyes when she catches sight of him.

“Black coffee, huh?”

"Mhmm." He takes another slow sip. He’s still wearing his sunglasses, and honestly they’re probably not coming off anytime soon. A single bit of light is going to pierce through his eye sockets and into his brain, he already knows it.

She flops into the booth. “So you and Stevie got super drunk then, huh?”

“Mhmmm.”

He and Stevie had one of their alcohol-filled movie nights, the first one in a long time. His body doesn’t seem to be handling the movie nights as well as it used to. He spent the morning almost throwing up, and then absolutely vomiting into Stevie’s kitchen sink while she accused him of being a lightweight (and then promptly threw up herself).

Alexis chuckles menacingly. “Think I’ll order something very heavy this morning. Maybe like, lots of eggs? Oooh, and bacon!”

David clears his throat and tries not to gag at the thought of egg yolks running across a half-washed restaurant plate. He swallows thickly and glares at his sister through his glasses.

“You know, I don’t remember inviting you to sit at my table. What are you doing here, Alexis?”

“Rude David. Ugh, I was supposed to meet Ted here but he texted me when I was halfway here and said that he was sick.”

“Okay, well I’m meeting Patrick, so why don’t you...” David’s in the middle of a shooing motion when his boyfriend appears and slides into the booth next to him.

“Hey,” he greets with a smile that’s way too sweet for how early it is. “Alexis, I see you decided to join us.”

“Yeah, it wasn’t a problem,” she says, waving her hand dismissively as if anyone wanted her there in the first place.

“And wow,” Patrick’s eyes finally land on David. “Looks like you and Stevie had quite a night.”

“Yep, mhmm. I’m never doing that again.” He takes another sip of his coffee and winces at how it feels going down. As if his stomach isn’t even trying. Actually, he’s probably going to need to excuse himself pretty soon. “My head feels like it did after my first rave,” he groans.

“Oh, honey,” Patrick doesn’t sound too sympathetic given the situation David put himself in, but still rubs his back while David guzzles down the rest of his coffee.

“I might feel too bad to work today,” David tries.

“Unfortunately, we don’t offer sick days for hangovers,” Patrick says before pressing a kiss to his temple and opening a menu.

Shortly after they order, David starts to squirm. His stomach gives a sickening gurgle he can only pray Patrick can’t hear, and Alexis smirks. Glad she’s heartlessly enjoying his misery, then.

David clears his throat and taps on Patrick’s shoulder. “Can- can you- yeah, thank you.”

Patrick gets the message in record time and lets David out of the booth (although he and Alexis are exchanging a smirk now). David tries very hard not to gag as he presses his hand to his mouth and speed-walks to the bathrooms in the back of the restaurant.

What proceeds is an unpleasant experience and a worsening headache afterward.

It’s going to be a long day.

-----

He’s exhausted. David fights the temptation to go in the back and nap on the futon until it’s time to close the store. The only thing that stops him from falling asleep at the register is when the door jingles.

Seconds later, there’s a bottle sitting in front of him.

“Thought you could use some water,” Patrick says, patting his back as he moves behind the counter next to him.

“Mm. Thank you.” David sighs, lazily reaching until he captures the bottle. “I’ve never been hungover for this long before. I don’t know what alcohol could still be in my system at this point.”

Patrick chuckles. “Well, I imagine you two had quite a lot.”

He’s not wrong, but David feels incredibly judged. Patrick knows that their movie nights are just an excuse to stay up late and get drunk. Granted, not usually that late or that drunk.

David pouts and takes a sip of water. Patrick kisses his cheek.

-----

“Here, have some more water,” Patrick says as David returns from the bathroom, shoving the half-drunk bottle into his chest without so much as a glance.

David sighs and takes the bottle, but he doesn’t dare drink anything when his stomach just tried to empty itself once again. Nothing but dry heaves, and the overheated feeling that usually came at the beginning of drinking, not ten hours afterward.

-----

It’s the moment that they walk into Patrick’s apartment that David makes a beeline for the bathroom.

His stomach twists itself in knots even though there’s really nothing to throw up but a few sips of water and a bite of his sandwich from lunch. It’s been cramping since before they closed, but he thought it was just because of all the throwing up. Turns out it’s the other way around.

He’s starting to think that this is not just from drinking too much. Which means he could be contagious.

“David?” Patrick asks, panicked as David locks the door behind him. He’s not about to let Patrick witness this.

He has a split-second before he goes from fidgeting with the lock on the bathroom door and rushing to get over the toilet in time to bring up that bit of water and lunch.

He feels significantly awful, and even more self-pitiful now that he realizes it.

David grabs the sides of the bowl with a white-knuckled grip and gags unproductively. The intensity makes his headache worse, and now his eyes are watering and-

ah, and now he’s crying. Because of course he is.

He heaves one more time before his stomach decides he’s done for a moment, and David takes the opportunity to blow his nose and feel sorry for himself. And feel humiliated for the fact that he let this happen in his boyfriend’s home.

He hears footsteps fade away, and he’s satisfied to know that Patrick’s not about to break down the door.

David starts to sigh, but it turns into a muffled sob halfway through. His head hurts, his stomach hurts, and he’s... suddenly freezing for no reason. David curls up until the next bout of vomiting starts.

-----

"David, please open the door…"

It’s been an hour. No doubt that Patrick’s heard him crying.

"Can’t...” David croaks before spitting into the water below. “I keep throwing up.”

"Honey, you probably have that flu that’s going around,” Patrick says as reasonably as ever.

“Then I am definitely not letting you in.” David’s voice is half gone at this point, and his nose is stuffed both from puking and crying. He’s a complete mess, snot on his face he hasn’t had the motivation to fully wash away, a small puddle of puke next to the toilet where he missed in a rush to get back to it in time, and just... his existence as a whole--at this point--is a mess.

Maybe it’s time to leave, David thinks. Maybe he can just sneak back to the motel without Patrick noticing.

A sigh on the other side of the door, followed by a strange sliding sound. It takes David’s illness-addled brain too long to realize that Patrick is now sitting on the floor. Thump as Patrick’s head rests against the flimsy wooden door.

“Please, David...” and he sounds so desperate. “Let me help.”

Maybe it’s not worth it, David thinks, to hide if it’s still going to cause problems. But he feels so awful and he looks even worse and he needs to clean and disinfect this entire bathroom before Patrick even thinks of doing anything in here and-

David unlocks the door.

Patrick scrambles to his feet in the haste to get inside, and rushes to David with a sympathetic coo that’d melt David’s heart if he felt he deserved it.

Suddenly, he’s wrapped in a tight hug and he’s taking a hiccuping breath. David tries to stop the tears, he really does, but he finds himself crying without restraint for the first time today.

“Oh, David...” Patrick shushes him, holds him. “It’s okay. Everything’s fine.”

But everything’s not fine, because he has to throw up again and this time Patrick’s here to witness it.

Patrick must be able to tell somehow. He’s quick to help David back over the toilet, giving a quick glance at the vomit that David hasn’t managed to clean up quite yet.

David’s forced to stop crying as his body heaves violently without his consent. A warm hand moves up and down his back as he brings up a small bit of thick vomit and spits into the water.

He sniffles, sobs tiredly, because that’s what he is. Tired. He and Stevie were up late last night, and David’s learned from experience that being sick and sleep deprived is not a great mix.

Patrick leans away from him for a second and suddenly his hand is on the nape of David’s neck and something soft is pressing against his nose and chin, drying his face of the grossness that’s accumulated.

David makes a noise of protest and tries to move away for a second, but Patrick just shushes him and gently dabs at his nose.

He throws the gross wad of paper into the toilet and flushes it.

And then they just sit. David leans into him and shudders.

A cool, soft hand brushes the sweat-damp hair from his forehead, Patrick hums.

"Last I checked, hangovers don't come with a fever."

David gives a teary chuckle.

Patrick’s embrace is warm and welcoming, but David can’t stop thinking about how he must smell like sweat and vomit. How he needs to start cleaning because this is the only bathroom in the apartment and it’s covered in germs and Patrick’s going to have to use it at some point.

“Honey,” Patrick says softly.

David realizes his breath has quickened, he’s tearing up again. He’s surprised he has enough liquid left in his body to cry any more. All he manages to say, “My head hurts,” he breathes out, broken.

Patrick strokes his arm and presses a kiss to his gross, sweaty hair. “Everything’s okay.”

“I’m sorry,” he says, barely above a whisper.

Patrick pauses. “For what, David?”

“Your bathroom,” he sniffles.

Much to his confusion, Patrick chuckles, jostling David as his chest moves in a huffed laugh. “I think my bathroom will be just fine.”

“Mmm,” David protests. He starts pulling himself up. “I,” he pauses to sniffle. “I need to clean-”

“No- David,” Patrick gently pulls him back to his chest. “That’s not happening. It’s fine for now.”

“Mm’m. No it’s not. It’s-”

“I’ll take care of it,” he soothes. “Now, I’ve got some ginger ale and saltines with your name on it. Do you feel like you’re ready to get up? We can get you set up on the couch, I’ll even watch that terrible Sandra Bullock movie you like.”

Two Weeks' Notice is not a terrible movie, David wants to argue. You just have to understand where it’s coming from!

But instead he clears his throat. “Probably gonna throw up again, though.”

“I’ll get a bucket.”

“Ew. No.”

Patrick sighs. “David, I’m not letting you camp out in here. You’re going to make yourself feel ten times worse.”

-----

David watches with utter distaste as his boyfriend pulls a gray bucket from under the sink and sets it beside him. He barely registers the glass of water and pills pressed into his hands a moment later.

“Let’s see if those stay down?”

David downs them. Anything to make him feel a little better, even just for a while.

He snuggles more efficiently under the warm comforter Patrick dragged over from his bed, leaning back against the pillow Patrick also dragged from his bed.

He checks his phone for the first time in hours and finds four new tests.

Stevie: please tell me you’re sick too

Stevie: because there’s no way I’m suffering when you’re not

Stevie: because I’ll drag you down with me, david rose

Stevie: I’ve never thrown up so much in my entire life

David frowns.

David: ok, who got who sick?

David: did you do this?

David: did you invite me over when you already knew you had the plague?

David: I could have died stevie

David: my immune system is VERY sensitive

Stevie: ugh

Stevie: i don’t know. My brain hurts.

Stevie: let’s call this a truce.

David shakes his head, puts his phone away, and groans. Only after the groan does he notice the sound of sprays from... ah, from the bathroom. Patrick’s cleaning, then. He feels like he should feel guilty, but he can’t bring himself to when he feels so awful. Guilt is an emotion for later when he's not on the verge of coughing up his digestive system.

Patrick scrubs his hands (good. David was about to remind him.) before he comes out to join David on the couch.

They end up with his head on Patrick’s lap as they watch the movie. And--yeah--David has to admit that it’s not as good as he remembers it, but Hugh Grant makes any movie ten times better.

“I mean, he’s okay,” Patrick says. He goes to run his fingers through David’s hair as he’s been doing before David shifts to look up at him.

“Okay?”

“He’s kinda old,” Patrick mutters through the side of his mouth.

“Old? No no, Betty White is old, Patrick. Hugh Grant has aged gracefully.”

“Mmm, whatever you say, David.”

David finds himself dozing off halfway through the movie. Patrick’s fingers carding through his hair and lightly scratching his scalp is enough to put him to sleep immediately. And he would have been asleep immediately had it not been for how terrible he feels.

His stomach starts to feel unsettled again, like another vomiting fit is in his near future. He shifts to accommodate for the bloated feeling, lying on his back as to not put any pressure on his tender stomach.

“You okay?”

David gives a slightly pained, “Mhm...”

“You’re not going to puke on me, are you?”

David furrows his brows. “I will literally die before that happens.”

Patrick huffs out a laugh and continues petting David’s hair. And things are nice for a while.

They get through ten more minutes of the movie before the unsettled feeling in David’s stomach turns to a crampy pain. He makes a noise and shifts to his side again before wrapping an arm around his middle.

“I don’t feel good...” he mutters. He starts to sit up and raises his hand to his mouth to muffle a hiccup. “I think, oh God, Patrick-?”

“Yup, on it...”

David’s too preoccupied by the nausea to pay attention to when Patrick leans over to grab it, but suddenly there’s a bucket in front of his face and- fuck no.

“David, where are you going?”

David sheds the blanket he was covered in as he shakily stands from the couch and rushes to the bathroom. Oh God, Patrick just cleaned, too...

“I can’t. I’m sorry, I’ll--urp--I’ll come back. Can you pause it?”

David hears the movie stop right before he gets over the toilet. He gags and heaves for a good couple of minutes until something finally comes up.

He shivers. Should have brought the blanket with him.

“Honey? Do you need anything?”

“A new digestive system would be fantastic,” David mutters.

“Mhm, anything I can give you now, though?”

David sighs an sits up to flush the toilet. He pulls himself to his feet, achy limbs complaining the whole way, and washes his hands before opening the door to face his boyfriend.

He must look as tired as he feels, because Patrick just nods as if David said something.

“Wanna turn in early?”

David thinks about it, and nods. “As much as I want to finish the movie, I don’t think I’m going to be able to stay awake for much longer.”

He watches with alarm as Patrick starts moving the blankets and pillow back to his bed. “I’m sorry, do you um, do you expect to sleep in the same bed as me after I’ve been horking my stomach contents all day?”

Patrick considers. “It’ll be a lot more comfortable than my couch. Unless you want to be closer to the bathroom, which-”

“I’m contagious, Patrick.” When he thinks about it, sleeping on the sofa all night sounds... unappealing, especially when he hardly fits on the thing.

“Yeah, and I’ve spent the whole day with you, so I think it’s just a matter of time before-”

“Oh my God,” David mumbles, burying his head in his hands. He should have just gone back to the motel. Being coddled by his boyfriend is amazing, but it isn’t worth the guilt he’s going to feel if Patrick ends up sick. Patrick cleaned up his vomit. He’s right, he’s doomed.

“David, hey, it’s fine.”

“It’s not fine.”

“Okay, I know you might not understand this but part of relationships are sharing everything. Including--you know--germs.”

David shakes his head in disbelief. “That’s the most ridiculous thing I’ve ever heard.” He clears his throat. “But um, I’m inclined to believe you because you’re the most trustworthy person I’ve ever dated, so...”

Patrick chuckles. “Let’s go to bed, David.”

-----

David wakes and immediately knows why. His stomach is cramping again, acting likes it’s full when he hasn’t eaten anything besides a saltine in twelve hours. He carefully sits up and pulls the blankets away, but the motion causes the nausea to increase tenfold. David clamps his hand over his mouth in a panic, but it’s too late to stop himself from gagging. He wants to cry when he feels the slimy vomit seep between his fingers.

David doesn’t have time to panic before he’s forced to heave again, except this time he manages to grab the bucket Patrick set on his side of the bed. (“Gross, no” “It’s just in case, David”)

He hugs the thing to his chest and holds on like his life depends on it as he gives a couple more empty gags, followed by a sickening belch.

He freezes at the sound of shifting behind him, trying to keep his ragged breaths low and his snuffling to a minimum.

“David?” Patrick says, so sleepy and slurred and adorable that it’s absolutely unfair that this is the situation they’re in right now. “You okay?”

David wants to answer, he plans to answer, but instead he brings up another wave of bile...

...and starts to cry.

He’s exhausted and his stomach feels awful and everything aches and he’s gone from cold to hot in the past two minutes. He can’t stop the thoughts that are telling him that he’s selfish for staying here and forcing Patrick to put up with him.

And the gentle hand on his back just proves that his boyfriend is way too good for him.

“Hey, David,” there’s moving sounds, and the mattress dips next to him under Patrick’s weight. Patrick reaches up to caress his face-- hesitant at first as to make sure David’s alright with it, like the ridiculously thoughtful person he is--and then uses the knuckle of his index finger to wipe away a stray tear.

David instinctively fights the tugging at the bucket (what if he’s not done?) but in the end he gives up fighting. Patrick sets the thing on the floor far enough away from them, and then wraps an arm around David.

It’s hot and stifling, David’s already sweating so much, but he still finds comfort in the closeness.

Patrick’s hand pauses at his forehead, and the look he gives David is so soft David thinks he’s not sweating, but simply melting. “I’m here.”

And David’s sobbing allover again. Patrick doesn’t even know why he’s acting like a mess and he still decides to hold him.

Because he’s so fucking perfect.

He leaves at some point to clean out the bin, giving David a kiss on the forehead as he walks away. It gives David some time to calm down, catch his breath, and wallow in self-pity.

He’s back in bed by the time Patrick returns, and David turns over to face him.

“Why are you putting up with this?” he mutters, admittedly already half asleep.

Patrick chuckles as if that’s funny. “David, I don’t know if I’ve told you this before, but I’m very much in love with you. A little puke isn’t going to dissuade me.”

“Hm.”

“Just don’t breathe on me too much. Your breath is... not fantastic.”

“Oh my God.”

They fall asleep, and when David wakes up to Patrick’s alarm blaring, he realizes that Patrick is not, in fact, in bed to turn it off.

“Patrick!” David groans, trying to filter out the awful noise. “Your phone... turn it off...”

“Kinda busy right now!”

David furrows his brow at the muffled voice and finally gives up and opens his eyes.

The bathroom door is closed, which wouldn’t be such a concern if David couldn’t hear sickening gags under the angry alarm. He bites his lip and reaches over to turn the noise off. The sound is obvious now--Patrick’s throwing up. David sits up with a groan.

Ugh.

His whole body hurts. his whole body feels completely exhausted from the day before, but at least his stomach isn’t forcing him to relinquish whatever could be left inside.

He pads to the bathroom and knocks.

“Can I come in?” he asks sheepishly.

“Mm- hurp- hmm...”

The sight that greets him is Patrick draped inelegantly over the toilet, sweaty and pale and looking like he’s been at this for long enough that he doesn’t care that his head is resting on the toiler seat. He’s shirtless and David understands why when he notices white fabric piled in the corner of the bathroom with an obvious stain on it.

Patrick swallows thickly and pulls away from the toilet a bit, but not too much. “I’ll be done in a sec. Do you need-?”

David shakes his head and kneels down. “No, I’m fine for now. I’m more concerned about your-” he waves a hand over Patrick’s form, “-situation.” Patrick smiles sleepily. “I think you caught my bug,” David says, acting as if he’s revealing some horrible news that Patrick hasn’t figured out yet.

Patrick huffs out a weak chuckle. “Yeahhh, you know, I think you might be right.”

David wets a washcloth and carefully starts wiping away the gross on Patrick’s face. He rinses it, and then drapes it over Patrick’s neck.

“Feels nice.”

“Mhmm.”

Patrick doesn’t have a chance to say anything else cute before he’s rushing to get over the toilet. David has to look away, suddenly feeling queasy allover again when he hears his boyfriend’s stomach contents hit the inside of the bowl.

“You don’t have to stay,” Patrick croaks before spitting. He makes a pained face and lays a hand on his stomach. “I’m probably going to be here for a while.”

“Yeah, but you were there for me the whole time, though, so-”

“Ah, David...” Patrick clears his throat. “Trust me when I say that you don’t want to be here the whole time,” he says, wrapping an arm around his lower stomach.

Ah. David nods, “Gotcha.” he stands up.

“Didn’t you say Stevie was sick too?”

“Oh, right...” David thinks about it. He’s had Patrick, and now he and Patrick have each other. Who does Stevie have? “I should call her, then.”

-----

“You cried all night, huh? Sounds about right.”

“Wow, Stevie, wow.”

Stevie’s far better than either of them. She sounds just like David does after probably also puking all night, but she also seems to be in high enough spirits, happy to have a day off of the motel to stay at home by herself. Exactly how she likes it, David thinks, besides the constant puking.

“It was so bad, I’m a fucking mess.”

“Everyone knows that, though.”

“Again, wow.”

Stevie chuckles. “I’m glad to know that you two aren’t dead yet. I’d hate to show up to the store and find it completely neglected because you’re both rotting in his apartment.”

“Ew, oh my God,” David has to swallow back a rush of bile at the thought. “And I guess I’m happy to find out that you didn’t spend the night crying like I did.”

“Some of us are just stronger than others,” Stevie says, and David can hear the sarcastic smirk.

He’s about to tell her off when Patrick emerges from the bathroom, face read and hair damp, and looking incredibly exhausted.

He gives Stevie some less-than-well wishes and hangs up.

“I’d hate to be the voice of reason here,” David says proudly as Patrick flops against him. “But I don’t think we should open the store today.”

Patrick nods, hair brushing against David’s chin. “Probably a good idea not to infect our customers with an overly-contagious stomach flu.

When David calls his sister to ask her to look after the store, he’s met with an unenthusiastic, “Hello?”

“Hey, so, what are your plans for today?” David tries not to smile as Patrick snuggles against him even more, because it’s really fucking adorable.

“Ummm, I dunno, sleeping? Ted and I are super sick.”

David makes a face. “Ah.”

“Why?” she asks, sounding bored and done with their conversation.

David clears his throat. “Um, no reason, I was just trying to catch up.”

“Okay, well we saw each other literally a day ago but that’s fine I guess.” She sounds less annoyed and more confused and slightly delighted that he’s checking up.

He says his goodbyes and gives Patrick a regretful look. “So, I think everyone’s sick.”

Patrick sighs. “Guess we’re just closing the store for a day, then. Probably won’t matter much if the whole town has the flu.”

He has a point.

“So we just get to spend the whole day cuddling?” David says, trying not to be too happy about both of them being grossly sick.

“Unfortunately!” Patrick says with a sleepy smile.
