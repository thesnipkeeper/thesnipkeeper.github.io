Title: Dramaticism
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Taryon is pitiful and Percy has so much pity to offer
Word count: 1.2k

Dramaticism

With Vex off on a trip with new trainees, Percival found himself incredibly bored. And lonely.

He had plenty to do, sure, but there was only so much work one could do before they craved some sort of person. Percy had noticed that change over time, as he got used to having all of his friends around.

Percy wondered what Taryon was up to today.

He was in the middle of cleaning Bad News, letting his mind wander, when he stopped.

He hadn’t seen Taryon in at least a day, since Vex left.

Well, he decided, maybe he could pay his friend a visit and see what he was up to.

Percy tidied his workshop, set Bad News on its dedicated spot on the wall, and grabbed his jacket. It was starting to get cool out, which meant winter, the toughest time of year, was quickly approaching.

He wandered to Vex and Taryon’s home and knocked. No answer. Percy knocked again.

Surely Taryon was home? Where else could he be? The bakery, perhaps...

Percy was just considering heading to the bakery when he heard the rather defeated voice of Taryon Darrington yell.

“Come in...”

Raising an eyebrow, Percy let himself in and closed the door behind him. The entire place was dark, the curtains drawn and no lamps or candles lit. He scanned the room before seeing Taryon’s bedroom door open.

“Hello-? Oh, Taryon...” he breathed out at the sight of his friend.

Taryon was in bed bent over a bucket, clutching it like his life depended on it. Drool was dripping down his chin and tears from his eyes. He’d clearly been vomiting, and by the state of the room, things had been this way for a while.

If that sight wasn’t already sad enough, Taryon started sobbing.

Percy swore and rushed to his side, trying very hard to ignore the smell of vomit. He’d dealt with much worse, much much worse in his days of adventuring. He pulled some loose pieces of hair off of his friend’s damp forehead behind his ear.

“No, don’t... I’m surely contagious. I must have the plague...” he sobbed again.

Percy chuckled and wrapped an arm around him. “You do not have the plague, I’m sure.”

Taryon’s breath caught in his throat, and he quickly shoved Percy away before vomiting into the bucket. Percy winced and averted his eyes while he rubbed his back. It didn’t sound like much had come up, likely because anything that was in his stomach was already in the bucket. Awful.

Percy gathered his hair and pulled it out of the line of fire, which didn’t seem to do much good at this point. It clearly had already been in the line of fire, poor thing. If he wasn’t freaking out about that, then he must’ve been really sick.

“Take a deep breath, my dear,” he instructed as Taryon heaved uselessly.

Taryon cried again, more tears dripping down his cheeks. “I’m so sorry, Percy, you shouldn’t have to see me like this. I’m hideous!”

“Yes, but we’ll fix that later if you’d like,” Percy said fondly.

Things quieted down as Taryon was reduced to pitiful sniffles.

“I’m just going to take this...” he carefully, very carefully took the bucket from Taryon’s grasp and set it far away from them on the floor.

Taryon dragged the back of his hand along his mouth and nose, only managing to further smear the gross across his face. This seemed to upset him more, and luckily Percy had a clean kerchief in his pocket that was usually reserved for cleaning his face after having been in the workshop.

He handed it to Taryon and sat patiently while he wiped his face.

And with that, Taryon finally took a deep breath and sighed. “I’m sorry.”

“Don’t apologize.”

“I’m a mess, my room is a mess...”

“Absolutely. How would you feel about getting cleaned up?”

“Oh, please,” Taryon said immediately. “I feel disgusting.”

Percy chuckled and nodded. “Come along, I know that Vex has a very nice tub.”

“She really does!”

-----

“Now, I was going to avoid telling you this, but I do believe you have a little erm... in your hair,” Percy said. His back was turned from Taryon as he undressed, but he could still hear the horrified gasp. Percy winced.

“Oh gods!” Taryon exclaimed, sounding like he may start crying again.

“It’s alright! We can fix it!”

Things were better once Taryon was in the bath. He was relaxed, and looked significantly less sick now that his face wasn’t covered in snot and tears. He looked rather adorable, if Percy was being honest with himself.

Especially with the sense of bliss painted on his face as he was getting his hair washed. It was out of necessity of course, but he still seemed to be enjoying it quite a bit.

“We won’t be telling Vex that we’re using her bathing things, correct?” he asked with a hint of fear that was absolutely warranted.

Percy laughed. “No we will not.”

“Thank goodness.”

Percy left him to dry off while he went to find some comfortable clothes for him. Something more comfortably than the fancy pajamas he was wearing before.

He sighed as he looked around the room. There was a pile of blankets in the corner than he suspected had been vomited on. There were also blankets on the floor that must’ve been kicked off the bed while Taryon was asleep.

Percy frowned as he imagined his friend sick and alone all day. He should have come over sooner. There was no way Taryon was used to being alone while he was sick, he had a lifetime of being cared for by servants, or by Doty.

Percy set the clothes on the floor in the hallway and called “Pajamas are in front of the door when you need them, dear!”

“Thank you!”

He went to work gathering the blankets from the bedroom floor and adding them to the pile in the corner, along with the sheets from Taryon’s bed. Percy knew where Vex kept her extra bedding, and he had only a few seconds of hesitation before delving into the linen closet. Hopefully she wouldn’t mind. Taryon was one of her closest friends these days, after all.

He had just finished nicely making up the bed when Taryon came in. He looked comfortable, which was different than Percy had ever seen him, but his face was red which might have meant an oncoming fever. That theory was only proven by the quick shudder Percy saw run through him.

He realized that Taryon had stopped just inside the doorway. “You made my bed?” he asked, softer and much less dramatic than usual.

“Er,” was that strange? Percy didn’t even stop to think that maybe it was a weird thing to do. “I did, yes.”

Taryon gave a little chuckle to go along with the small smile now gracing his face. “Well, that’s very nice of you, Percy. Thank you.”

He nodded. “It’s my pleasure.”

There was an awkward few seconds of silence before Taryon said, “Will you stay with me today? Please, Percy, I don’t want to be alone!” There it was. Good old dramatic Taryon.

“Weren’t you just worried about giving me the plague a few moments ago?” Percy asked with a chuckle.

Taryon winced. “Right. Um. Well...”

“I’m kidding, of course I’ll stay with you.”

He let out a sigh of relief. “You’re a great friend!”

“I know, I know.”
