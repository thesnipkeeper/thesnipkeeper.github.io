Title: Carpet
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's getting sick, Stevie cleans
Word count: 2k

Carpet

To say that David is exhausted would be an understatement.

His limbs feel like rocks. No - like they’re being weighed down by rocks. In a deep body of water and the rocks are anchoring him further and further down into the abyss.

Work gets to him quickly. Maybe it’s because he stayed up too late, or maybe it’s because he didn’t have time to eat breakfast that morning, but he’s dragging by lunchtime. He snaps at a few more customers than usual. (and quickly apologizes and gives them a compliment to make it better. He needs that job.) Wendy offers him a coffee from the cheap machine in her office (does he really look that tired?) and he’s desperate enough to accept it.

Not even caffeine can save him today, it seems. The coffee sits like sludge in his stomach, refusing to turn into the fake energy he desperately needs.

“See ya, David!” Wendy calls as he’s walking out the door, and he just waves back at her.

He spots Stevie’s car, thanks the universe that she’s on time for once, and falls heavily into the passenger seat.

“Whoa, bad day?”

“Not exactly,” David admits. Because being tired doesn’t make it a bad day, does it? He’s had plenty of bad days, and today just went by too slowly. “I’m just so tired. I don’t think I should’ve skipped breakfast.”

Stevie gasps in faux-surprise, sparing a glance at him as she pulls onto the empty road that leads to Schitt’s Creek. “Since when do you skip breakfast? Actually, since when do you skip any meal?”

“Excuse me, I-” It’s not worth arguing that he’s absolutely capable of going a few hours without eating! But he doesn’t have the energy for his usual dramatics. Talking with his hands feels like an impossible feat. David sighs. “I slept past my alarm, my choices were skincare or food, and I wasn’t super hungry, so I chose skincare.” Also, he can’t bear the thought of skipping that part of his routine.

“Damn. That sucks.”

“Oh my God, I know.” David agrees. He hopes his lack of enthusiasm doesn’t discourage her, and then he remembers that it’s Stevie and he doesn’t care about other people’s feelings anyway.

The drive is silent. Not unusual for the two of them. He’s tired, Stevie prefers to avoid conversation, and...

At some point, David finds himself drifting off.

-----

“-vid!”

David wakes to being violently shoved. He groans and shoves her back.

“Hey! Jesus, you are hard to wake up. We’re here.”

He begins the hard job of opening his eyes, finds them covered in a disgusting film that only naps seem to offer. Rubs it away with an displeased noise.

Stevie scoffs. “I’ve got to get back to work. You’re free to... stay, or whatever. Just remember to lock my car.”

“No, no, I’m getting out,” he groans.

He isn’t truly sure how he manages stay conscious enough to unlock the motel door, or close it, or get his shoes off. He falls into bed when anything that would be ruined or wrinkled is off his body, and he’s out.

-----

It’s cold. It’s really fucking cold. He can hear Alexis changing in the bathroom - she must have turned the heat down when she got home from work.

David grabs a blanket from under his bed and haphazardly throws it over himself.

He goes back to sleep.

-----

His stomach hurts. Probably should’ve eaten before he went to sleep.

Probably should’ve had something besides coffee and a doughnut for lunch.

Oh well.

-----

“David, have you seen my curler?”

He doesn’t open his eyes. Too tired to yell at her for waking him when he was obviously sound asleep. “Was in the way...put it on your desk...”

“No, you didn’t, David. It’s not- Oh, nevermind.”

His stomach hurts. He’s not hungry. Sleep will fix it.

David turns over and goes back to sleep.

-----

Stomach hurts.

Something’s wrong, David realizes. He feels wrong.

He feels- he feels- sweaty.

David opens his eyes for the first time in- (it’s 7pm? that’s all?) three hours.

Everything’s foggy.

Something’s wrong.

His stomach hurts. It gives a sickening gurgle, and he curls up once again, squeezing his eyes shut.

Alexis’ music is too loud. She’s wearing headphones, but he can still hear Britney Spears’ less popular album blasting through them.

Music would help, he thinks. His headphones are on his nightstand. They’d help him fall asleep.

Yeah, sleep. Sleep will make him feel better. He’s having an off day, that’s all, and he’s trying very hard to ignore the feeling of his stomach contents rolling inside of him.

Be easier to ignore if he’s asleep.

-----

David wakes up with a whimper.

Stomach hurts.

“Oh my God!” Alexis is so loud.

He’s not entirely awake until he opens his eyes as sees a puddle of vomit on the carpet.

Something’s caught in the back of his throat. He’s going to- nononono...

“EW OH MY GOD!”

David gags. And gags again. That’s when he realizes how cold he is again, and how his body still feels unbelievably tired.

When David looks up, wiping his mouth uselessly, Alexis is running into their room with Johnny Rose following behind.

His dad’s uncoordinated hand finds itself on his forehead and halfway covering his left eye.

“Thank God your mother’s not home, she’d have you quarantined,” Johnny mumbles. “Uh... here.”

David finds a tissue shoved into his hand. He uses it to wipe his mouth and chin, and then he realizes that there are very real tears in his eyes. He’s crying.

Ohhh God.

“Alexis, get the thermometer out of first-aid kit. Your brother’s got a fever.”

“Okay but like...” Alexis’ voice fades away as she goes into their parents’ room, and fades back in when she returns. “-going to clean that up?”

Whatever she’s asking about, it makes Johnny sigh heavily.

-----

“I’m only doing this because it’s my job and I think I have a much higher gross-out threshold than your family.”

“Mmm. That’s definitely true. Yep.” David can’t help but watch anxiously as Stevie scrubs the patch of carpet by his bed. He’s sitting cross-legged, unable to get comfortable as long as someone is cleaning his puke out of something. Unable to get comfortable as long as his stomach is still threatening to torture him again.

Stevie sighs, using the part of her arm that’s not covered in rubber gloves to wipe her forehead. “So, are you feeling better now?” she asks, pointedly nodding toward the soap-covered stain on the carpet.

David closes his eyes, smiling sheepishly, “Nope,” he admits.

Stevie huffs out a chuckle. “Well, at least now you know why you were so tired this afternoon.”

“Mmhm.”

“David?”

“Hm?”

“Are you... good?”

David looks down at her and realizes he hadn’t opened his eyes again. “Yeah. Yeah, I’m fine. I just,” he clears his throat. “It’s been a while since I’ve...” he gestures from his mouth to the air with a silent ‘blegh’. Stevie’s eyebrow shoots up, and he stumbles over his words to add something. “I-In front of my family. Or. Anyone.”

“Ah,” says Stevie with a knowing nod. “So you’re embarrassed.”

No. Not about that. “I mean, always, but um-” He hasn’t been vulnerable in front of his family for a very long time. Especially the sick kind of vulnerable that couldn’t be helped or avoided or hidden. But that isn’t the problem. He doesn’t care what Alexis or his parents think. They’ve seen him at his worst, or at least Alexis has. “I don’t know,” he finishes with a shrug.

Stevie just nods and goes back to work.

He knows she’s not bursting to ask about his personal life. But... hey, he’s sick, probably still feverish. She’s washing his vomit out of the carpet. He has nothing to lose by adding onto it.

“Back in New York,” he swallows hard, realizing that he can’t back out now. “Back in New York, I couldn’t exactly... be sick in front of anyone. My friends weren’t the most sympathetic.”

“Shocking.”

“They would’ve dropped me so fast if they saw me like- well, like this,” he gestured to himself, his current state. They did, he thinks, once. He was out with a group of newer performance artists who made excuses to leave when he admitted to having a sore throat. He saw them together on their snapchat stories two hours later. That he could scoff away and never talk to them again.

The time Sebastian left him sobbing in the bathroom after a bad pizza however, was... well, that left a mark.

“Mm. Well, as disgusting as this is right now, I’m not going anywhere,” Stevie reassures.

It’d be comforting to hear if he was honest with her. He isn’t afraid of losing her because he’s sick. He’s afraid of losing her because he’s a mess and still nauseated and if he- while she’s still here...

“Where’s your family, anyway?”

He shakes the thoughts of doom out of his head. “Alexis is on a date. Mom and dad are at a dinner party at the Schitt’s that they couldn’t get out of.”

Stevie scoffs. “Nice. You’re here by yourself then.”

“Mmhmm,” David hums, eyes closing again. He leans against the headboard and tries to ignore the increasingly-distressed noises his stomach is making. Stevie had put a bucket on the other side of the bed for him (“so I don’t have to clean anything else”) but he’d really prefer not to use it.

His stomach hurts.

His mouth is starting to salivate disgustingly.

He begs his body to give him a break, just end it now. A particularly jarring gurgle in his stomach tells him that his body isn’t listening.

“Do you want me to stay?”

When David opens his eyes, Stevie’s already gathered her supplies. There’s a towel over the spot now, and she’s stomping on it periodically as any cleaning professional would.

No, he doesn’t. Because he’s going to be a panicky mess and no one should have to witness that. (Yes, he does. Because he feels awful and he’s scared and he wants someone there with him.)

David shakes his head, putting on a soft smile. “I’m-” Ulp.

Stevie raises an eyebrow. “Uh...”

He carefully brings a hand up to his mouth, carefully getting out of bed, and runs to the bathroom. Nonono please...

But his stomach doesn’t care.

He manages to close the door despite Stevie’s words of concern.

David isn’t sure how much time passes, but at some point he goes from quiet crying to sobs, and can’t even explain why. Stevie’s probably gone by now, so he doesn’t bother holding back.

He hates it. So. Much. His throat is sore now, along with his stomach.

“I’m coming in. You better be decent.” His heart nearly stops at the voice. Has she been there the whole time? Fuck.

David sniffles pathetically and rushes to flush the stool before the door opens. He knows how bad he looks. He’s been crying for- well, for however long he’s been throwing up.

The sound of running water. An alarmingly-cold washcloth in his hand.

Stevie seems to see his confusion. “For your face. You have... goop everywhere.”

Ah. David does. He sniffles again, wants to cry again, really, but he won’t.

Stevie flops next to him on the floor, leaning against the door frame. “You wanna talk about that?”

“And by ‘that’ do you mean the hysterical crying over a pretty normal bodily function?’ David doesn’t say. “No,” he says instead.

“Good,” Stevie breathes out. “Neither do I.”

If anything, that gets a chuckle out of David through the residual tears.

“Are you good, though? I mean, you just threw up a ton so obviously you’re not good but- should I... be worried?”

David sighs. He’s tired. Again. He’s cold. Again. But the panic has passed. “I’ll be fine. I might need help getting back to bed, though.”

Stevie nods. “I can do that.”

They don’t talk about it again. They’re not exactly the most talkative of friends, anyway.

But Stevie always happens to be there if he’s sick, or drunk, or ate too much.

David realizes that she’s not leaving.
