Fandom: Penumbra Podcast
Summary: Peter introduces Juno to his favorite calming technique
Word count: 1.3k

Juno Steel and the Magic Hot Cocoa

“Ow! What happened to ‘do no harm’?!”

Vespa growled, sounding seconds away from losing the cool that she’d never had. “For fuck’s sake, Steel, you have to let me look at your ear if you want to know what’s wrong with it!”

“No, no, you know what? I changed my mind. I’m fine on my own!” He went to hop off of the exam table, except Vespa grabbed him and held him in place.

“Juno, please,” came Peter’s voice, the one rational tone in the room. “You’re the one who wanted to come here. Let dear Vespa help. You’re hurting.”

Juno sighed heavily. He was hurting. And Vespa wasn’t exactly being gentle! Still, he slowly sat back while continuing to eye her.

She huffed and forced his hand away from his ear before looking inside once again. Juno hissed in pain, and squeezed his eyes shut. Peter took his hand and held it. It made things a little better.Finally, the device disappeared from his ear.

“Middle ear infection,” Vespa informed gruffly. “You need antibiotics.” She pulled her gloves off and started going through a specific cabinet.

“Ear infection,” Juno huffed. “Out of all the stupid shit that’s happened to my body, it’s an ear infection that takes me down. Figures.”

Peter chuckled fondly and pressed a kiss to the hand that he was still holding. “Don’t fret, my love. I’ll take care of you.”

“Well... that’s- that’s nice of you, but I don’t need taken care of. It’s just-”

“Actually,” Vespa interrupted. “Looks like your fever’s rising. This is not gonna be fun for you.”

“That news wouldn’t be quite as bad if you weren’t smiling, Vespa.”

She laughed. “Get out of my infirmary, lovebirds.”

 

“I’m grounding you for tomorrow’s job,” Buddy informed him as he and Peter made their way into the common area.

“What?! That’s not-”

“It’s necessary because I need you to be at your best, and if you’re not, then you’re-”

“A liability?!”

Buddy sighed heavily, and to her credit, looked a bit guilty. That was something. “Please, Juno.”

Juno scoffed. “Ridiculous.”

Peter had been gentle with him all day, far sweeter than he needed to be. Maybe that was one of the many reasons that Juno was so... angry. So, fine, he was in a bad mood. But who wouldn’t be? His ear heart, his- his body hurt, and now he was too useless to do his job and protect his family.

And things only got worse when he and Peter went to bed that night.

He couldn’t get comfortable no matter how much he tried. He was exhausted, sure, but he knew that sleep wasn’t coming anytime soon. His ear still hurt, the painkillers Vespa had given him were wearing off too soon and he knew he’d have to wait before he could take more.

And he was cold. Chills had been biting at him for the past hour or so.

Everything was worse, being in bed with no distractions, forced to focus on his misery and aching and cold.

Juno huffed.

“J’no?” came the sleepy voice next to him. Peter turned over to face him, still half asleep as he narrowed his eyes at Juno. “Wha’s wrong?” He yawned, dragged a hand down his face.

Juno swore. “I didn’t mean to wake you. Go back to-” he hissed as the pain in his ear spiked, hand going to it as if that would help.

“Oh, Juno...” Peter hummed sympathetically. He sat up and turned the lights on a low setting. By the way he frowned, Juno must have looked as bad as he felt. “What’s wrong?” He repeated.

“I’m just having trouble sleeping, that’s it.”

“Anything I can do?”

“I mean... no, not really. I just-” he gave an exhausted sigh. “I just don’t feel good.”

Peter nodded. “Well... you know what always makes me feel better when I can’t sleep?”

Juno smiled a bit, willing to humor him. “What?”

“Hot cocoa.”

“Oh. Wait, really? You just... go around drinking hot cocoa when you can’t sleep.”

He laughed. “Well, when it’s available! Wait here, I’ll go get us some.”

“No,” Juno said, making Peter pause as he started to get up. “I’ll come with you. Think I wanna get out of here for a little while.”

Peter smiled and nodded. “Alright.”

The two of them sneaked into the kitchen like rebellious children being out past their bedtime. Though he was tired, and movement seemed to make his ear hurt worse, Juno had to admit that this beat lying in bed and thinking about how awful he felt.

Peter poured some milk in a pan and set it on the tiny burner that they called a stove. He started pulling things out of the cupboards that Juno didn’t know they ever had. Then again, he was never really looking for cocoa powder or sugar when coffee and Vespa’s cookies existed (she didn’t know that he was the one who stole them, and she never would.)

“What’s your chocolate to sugar ratio preference?” Peter asked, pausing his delightful humming.

“Honestly? Tooth-rotting.”

“Ah, excellent, me as well!”

Juno gave a fond chuckle. He watched his boyfriend, still in pajamas and still looking amazing at shit-o’-clock at night, dance around the kitchen, humming once again, and of course talking himself through the process. The pain in his ear seemed to fade away into the background while he watched.

“Alright, it’s warm, now we add the cocoa...” he wasn’t talking to Juno. He might have pretended that he was, but he was definitely not. Adorable.

“You’ve got this down to a science, I see,” said Juno, setting his chin on the palm of his hand as he leaned on the table.

“Absolutely I do,” Nureyev said proudly. “Do you like cinnamon, love?”

“Yeah, sure. We... have cinnamon?”

“Well, someone’s got to buy the tasteful stuff around here.”

Ah, so he did his own private grocery shopping for nights like these.

Peter whisked some things in the pan, pulled some cups out of the cupboard as he waited for it to get hotter. Juno stopped paying attention after a while. Not from boredom, but from the fever deciding that it was time he space off. The exhaustion didn’t help, either. Maybe he’d be able to sleep after this.

He was snapped back to the present when a steaming mug of something that smelled delicious was set in front of him. Peter to the seat on his right, where his good ear was, and kissed his cheek.

“Enjoy,” he murmured.

Juno smiled. “Looks good.”

It tasted good, too. Juno relished every sip. The warm liquid made him feel more tired by the second, calmer. No wonder Nureyev swore by this stuff.

“You know,’ Peter started, dragging his hand across his own upper lip to wipe away a cocoa mustache. “Buddy didn’t mean anything when she took you off the next job.” He was tired as well. His eyes were half-lidded, he leaned against the table, still sipping the last of his mug’s contents.

“Do we have to talk about this now? C’mon, we were having such a good time.” Peter’s frown, the kind that pleaded him to do this for him, made him cave immediately. Juno sighed. “She benched me and I hate it. I’m no use to you guys if I’m just here doing nothing.”

“Oh, Juno,” Peter hummed. “Being sick doesn’t make you useless.”

“Oh yeah?”

Another frown. Peter kissed his temple and sighed. “I know that you’re not easily convinced of your own worth, my dear, but you mean more than a job to everyone on this ship. Even Vespa!”

Juno snorted.

“How was your cocoa?”

He huffed, looking at his empty cup. “It was... amazing,” he sighed out. “Thank you.”

“Think that you can sleep now?”

He nodded. “I think so.”
