No Grilled Cheese

Patrick has been watching him carefully all day. David isn’t a subtle person by an means, and is therefore terrible at hiding things. The one problem, however, is that Patrick doesn’t know what he’s hiding.

Patrick watches with raised eyebrows as David meticulously spritzes the plants and arranges and rearranges the products on the other side of the store.

He’s been acting weird all day.

Patrick warned him when he arrived that morning that they shouldn’t kiss since he woke with a little bit of a cold. He thought he was at least going to get a hug, or something, but instead David just smiled and nodded and went to work.

Something’s wrong.

They haven’t spent a lot of time together outside of work the past week. Patrick’s been helping Ray stage some homes that he’s determined to sell. He wouldn’t normally agree to it, but the extra cash Ray was paying him was nice, and David was already busy helping Alexis and his mother plan an event that he didn’t feel like he was taking time from their relationship.

They still see each other every day for eight hours at the store. They still get to flirt with each other disgusting amounts when there aren’t any customers around.

So why is David being so quiet all of a sudden?

Patrick tries to think of anything he’s done wrong lately, because this has to be intentional on David’s part. The ‘maybe he’s tired’ excuse doesn’t work on someone who’s known for their nervous rambles. There’s a reason his boyfriend’s gone quiet, and Patrick can’t stand a problem going unsolved like this.

Patrick sniffles as David straightens the candle display for the third time.

“Maybe you’ll actually fix those this time,” Patrick says with a chuckle as he pulls a tissue from the box next to him and presses it to his runny nose.

David glances over and quickly turns back. “Yeah, heh. I guess they look okay.”

“Do you want any help? You’ve been working over there for a while.”

“Hm?” David looks like he’s only just joined this conversation, and Patrick has to tamp away the annoyance.

“Do you need help?” he asks again.

“Oh! Oh, no I don’t think-” David scans the area he’s basically lived in since he showed up to work. “I don’t think so. Everything going super well over here, but thanks.”

“O...kay?” Patrick says, voice slightly muffled by the tissue in his face. He blows his nose, and relishes in the ten seconds that he can actually breathe through his congested sinuses. “Oh my God, we might have to grab some more tissues for the store if things keep going like this,” he jokes.

David doesn’t even turn to him this time. “Hah! Yeah, I’m- I’m sure we are. Oh boy.”

Oh boy?

Patrick shrugs. Something’s rattling him, and he’ll figure it out sooner or later. Ideally, David will come to him, but he doesn’t see things happening like that.

Patrick sneezes for the millionth time in the hour. He blows his nose for the ten-millionth time. This fucking cold. While he doesn’t feel awful, it’s obnoxious having to constantly worry about his nose.

He checks his watch when his stomachs starts grumbling.

“Do you want to go for lunch first? I can finish restocking the stuff.” Patrick makes his way over to the side of the store David’s been working. He perches himself on the center table, only knocking over a couple things in the process. “Or we could close the store for lunch and both head over together. You know, spend some time together for the first time in a decade.”

David still hasn’t looked up at him. He’s quite focused on which direction the labels are facing right now. He clears his throat.

“Oh, you can uh... go ahead. I’m not very hungry.”

Any concern Patrick has increases tenfold at those four words. “I’m sorry,” he chuckles. “but since when are you ‘not hungry’?”

David shrugs and makes a non-committal noise. “So much work to do. Like, sooo much. And I like, can’t bear the thought of just leaving the store on a busy... day like this.” Yeah, the store is empty, and David realizes it the moment he starts to gesture to the space around them.

“Right.”

Patrick clears his throat and snuffles. He’s probably going to need to blow his nose soon, but that’s going to have to wait because David is being weird and he’s determined to fix it.

David’s gone back to adjusting a specific canister of body butter until the label is facing just right.

“David, is something wrong? Like, did I do something to upset you, or...?”

David starts shaking his head before he even finishes the sentence. “No. Nope, everything’s perfectly fine here. Why don’t you go to lunch?”

“David-” he’s about to say something meaningful and reassuring, but instead a tickle in his throat gets the better of him and Patrick has to turn away to cough.

David’s looking tense, like he does right before he blows up and has one of his known freak-out sessions. Patrick can’t imagine why asking if something’s wrong would upset him so much. His asking questions has never upset David like this before.

David looks up and then somehow seems more tense. “Go on and get yourself some lunch! I swear nothing’s going to fall apart while you’re gone!” The light tone would be a comfort if David’s smile wasn’t so obviously forced.

Patrick hopes that things can be settled when he comes back.

He can’t even consider sitting in the restaurant alone while David’s at the store dealing with whatever issue, so he orders two sandwiches to go and hopes that they can talk this out over lunch.

He’s learned in the past months of their relationship that David’s a lot easier when he has food in him. Easier to talk to, easier to get along with, just... easier.

He ends up returning to the store in record time. David’s moved to the opposite wall of the door, now apparently rearranging their decorations. He almost looks startled when Patrick’s entrance is announced by the bell on the door.

“Surprise! I got you a grilled cheese.”

“Oh, I... I said I wasn’t hungry.”

“I mean, yeah, but you were lying.”

David doesn’t laugh, scoff, or fondly roll his eyes. He doesn’t even go to Patrick to retrieve his food.

“Okay... I guess I’ll put it in the fridge and you can heat it up later.”

That’s it. He’s tired of this. It’s one thing for David to be in a bad mood, but he doesn’t appreciate being completely ignored for no reason. He’s not just going to spend the rest of the day pretending that nothing’s wrong. He...

He has to sneeze. Again. Patrick can’t help but roll his eyes as he grabs yet another tissue. He muffles a loud, forceful sneeze, and then another one, and another...

CRASH

Patrick startles. He looks over the tissue to find a broken pot on the ground. Dirt and pieces of ceramic scattered across the floor with one sad little aloe lying amid the pile.

He finishes wiping his nose as he stands from the stool behind the counter. “I’ll get the br- David?”

David is standing still, head in his hands. At first Patrick thinks he’s just embarrassed about breaking something, and then he hears a shaky breath.

He’s crying. Tense body shaking, not even making a move to get away from the dirt-covered floor that could very well ruin his shoes.

“David?” Patrick tries again, approaching slowly. “Honey, what’s wrong.”

David brings down one hand, revealing more of his face.

“I can’t do this,” David chokes out through the tears. He repeats it over and over, quietly to himself.

“What are you talking about?”

His crumpled expression and streaming tears is enough for Patrick to want to hold him for eternity. Instead, he settles for laying a gentle hand on his arm.

David flinches. “Don’t-!”

“Okay, okay...” Patrick lifts his hands non-threateningly and bites his lip. “What can’t you do? What’s wrong?”

David shakes his head and starts crying harder. “I’m sorry.”

“You have nothing to be sorry for. I just want to know what’s wrong so I can fix it.”

“You can’t-” a sob. “You can’t fix this.” David gestures vaguely.

Patrick has to cough once more, and when he turns back David’s breathing has gotten quicker and more shallow. He worries that he’s going to make himself lightheaded, and there’s nothing he can do.

“I’d like to try.”

David shakes his head and covers his face again. “I’m sorry.” he breathes out. “I just need- I need a m-minute...”

“Sure, honey.” Patrick reluctantly moves out of his way and watches his boyfriend escape into the back room. He lets out a shaky breath of his own and wills any threatening tears away.

He hates this. David wouldn’t even let him touch him. He knows that’s normal when David’s in the midst of a panic attack, but then Patrick wants to know what the panic attack was about.

But David wants space, and it wouldn’t be fair to bug him for answers when he very clearly wants to be alone.

So Patrick reluctantly nibbles on his lunch while he can hear his love crying in the back room. He’s only glad that the sobbing dies off quickly, because he’s not sure how much more he can listen to before he starts crying too.

He’s in the process of wiping his nose (almost crying has made it runnier, it seems) when footsteps sound from behind him. David emerges from the back room with puffy red eyes, looking tired and timid.

“So, I...” he clears his throat, and closes his eyes for a second as if he’s thinking hard. “I may have some... explaining? To do?”

Words start coming out of Patrick’s mouth before he realizes it. He’s been waiting all day to fix this and he just can’t stop himself fast enough before he lets out all the questions he’s been holding.

“Was it something I said?”

David’s mouth shuts abruptly, and then he sighs. “No, Patrick-”

“Did I upset you this morning? I just didn’t think kissing would be a good idea because I’d hate for you to-”

“Patrick!” David holds his hands up to stop him. “It wasn’t the kiss. I mean- it kind of was? But it wasn’t- ugh...” He buries his head in his hands.

“What did you mean when you said ‘I can’t do this’? You’re scaring me, David, I need to know what-”

“You’re sick.”

Patrick’s eyes widen. “I... yeah? And that... bothers you?”

David fidgets restlessly, eyes darting around the room like he’s rethinking this conversation. There’s a second where Patrick half-expects he’s going to run out of the store.

He sighs heavily and gives one of his careful nods. “It does, yes. I have a thing with um... germs.”

Oh. “I may have been able to figure that out.”

“Usually I can like, emotionally prepare myself for this kind of thing,” David gestures to Patrick. “But it caught me off guard and I didn’t get a lot of sleep last night and so I’m probably more emotional than I should be and I’m really sorry for freaking you out when you’re already...”

“So my having a cold made you...” Well now they’re just gesturing at each other uselessly.

David shrugs. “It’s not your fault. It’s just the sounds and the idea of...” he clears his throat and runs a hand through his hair. “I’m really really sorry.”

Patrick clears his own throat, and regrets it when David takes a step back. “Well, what now?”

“That’s a good question.”

Patrick comes prepared the next day. He wears a mask, one of those things that people with allergies usually wear, despite how ridiculous he must look. He buys a large bottle of sanitizer for the counter (probably a good idea in general, now that he thinks about it). And he now knows to escape to the bathroom whenever he needs to take care of his nose. It’s not a perfect plan but it’s as good as he’s going to get.

The door jingles open just as he’s booting up the computer and getting ready for the day.

“Oh my God.” David takes his sunglasses off to squint at Patrick. He raises an eyebrow. “Uh, hi.”

“Hey!” Patrick smiles and then remembers that David can’t see him smile under the mask. “How are you doing? You sounded kind of shaken up last night.”

“You’re the sick one and you’re asking me about my well-being?”

“Yep.”

David chuckles awkwardly. “I’m... fine. How uh, how are you?”

“Also fine.”

“Very prepared, I see.”

Patrick nods sheepishly. “Does it help a little?”

David clears his throat. “I uh... no, I’m sorry Patrick but you have to take that off right now before we start getting customers.”

Patrick can’t help but laugh, but he complies. The laughing triggers a coughing fit, though, and he rushes to get the bend of his arm over his face. David tenses.

“I’m just trying to help,” Patrick says as he clears his throat. “Look, I’m determined to make you comfortable.”

“No, no no,” David shakes his head and moves a bit closer, throwing his bag on the ground. He goes to put his hands on Patrick’s shoulders and hesitates for just a moment before he does it. Maybe he really did just need time to emotionally prepare himself. “This isn’t your problem! It’s my weird stupid issue, not yours, and I don’t want you worrying about something that neither of us can-”

“But we can try! Also, you might want to move, because I have to sneeze.”

David’s hands are already off of him and Patrick pretends like he doesn’t notice David trying to stealthily close his ears. He sneezes into a tissue and fixes himself as well as he can before pumping a generous amount of sanitizer into his palm.

David lowers his hands the moment Patrick looks back at him, apologetic expression on his face.

Patrick chuckles. “Sorry, that one happened fast. But I promise I’m going to do my best to keep from upsetting you again today, alright?”

David frowns, which isn’t exactly the reaction Patrick was hoping for. “You don’t have to do all of this...” He sighs. “I’m honestly not worth this kind of effort. And like, you’re already sick and now...”

“Well guess what? I’m gonna make this work, and I’m going to keep my germs to myself, and we’re going to figure this out.”

David bites his lip. “This isn’t going to be easy, you know. I mean, I don’t even want to touch you right now. And like, my immune system is super weak because I haven't slept well in two days.”

“Guess I’ll just have to get better fast then.” He sniffles, goes to wipe his nose on his hand and thinks better of it (and sees David's horrified and anxious face). "I need to go blow my nose, and I promise I'll stay out of your way today."

This time the look on David's face is indecipherable. "I can handle the store today. I've, y'know, done it before."

"David…"

"No, like, you look really awful and I'm not sure our customers would be into…this." He waves his hand a safe distance from Patrick's face.

"I guess I'll be in the back working on the budget today." He heads for the bathroom instead of the back, now in desperate need of blowing his nose.

David nods approvingly. "Good. Good idea. I love you."

"I love you too!"

The next time Patrick gets sick is a little different.

It’s early in the morning, and David is fast asleep on his shoulder when he feels his gut rearrange itself.

He felt a little weird when he went to bed last night, dinner not quite settling the way it should, but he chalked it up to eating too much too fast. Now, he’s wondering if he was too quick to dismiss it.

His stomach gurgles angrily, this time accompanied by cramps that force Patrick to grunt in pain. He tries not to jostle David too much as he brings his hand to his stomach. He can feel it bubble under his skin in a sickening way.

And the nausea soon follows.

Fuck.

A stomach flu has been slowly making its way around town. David’s been sanitzing the store whenever its empty and he has a moment to do so.

Patrick can hope that he just ate something weird all he wants, but the fact of the matter is that he’s starting to feel achy and sweaty, and his belly is making increasingly distressed noises.

He sits still, swallowing convulsively to keep his stomach contents where they should be. David’s going to wake up the moment he moves, and the apartment isn’t exactly big; he’s going to hear what’s going on in the bathroom.

Patrick might feel worse about subjecting his boyfriend to this than how he feels physically.

The nausea spikes, and he knows that he’s run out of time. He nudges David off of him as carefully as possible, earning a sleepy “mm?” in response.

He feels weak and a bit disoriented as he gets out of bed. Patrick rushes into the bathroom, props himself in front of the toilet, and-

and nothing.

False alarm?

His stomach is still gurgling angrily and he still feels ominous muscle aches that threaten the beginning of an unpleasant illness.

He sighs and sits on the edge of the tub, head falling in his hands.

He really feels awful, now that he thinks about it. He hasn’t had a stomach bug since he moved to Schitt’s Creek (Thank God. He wouldn’t have been able to handle that while living with Ray).

In his unwell state, Patrick can’t help but startle at a knock on the door.

“Everything okay?” David’s nervous, over-cautionary voice that he uses when he’s pretty sure everything is not okay.

“Yeah, just washing my face.”

“Okay! I mean, you got out of bed kinda fast to wash your face, though. I was having a great dream, too.”

“Sorry, David.”

“Patrick? You’re scaring me just a little so can you open the door?”

Patrick realizes that hiding could be causing just as much distress for David. Except, when he gets up to open the door, his vision swims and his stomach lurches. He finds himself falling on his knees and barely making it in time before he chokes up a wave of last night’s dinner.

“Oh my God, Patrick?”

The door opens, and Patrick can’t bring himself to do anything about it when he’s busy gagging over the toilet.

He expects David to turn around and get as far away as possible. Instead, he feels a hand on his sweaty back.

Patrick makes a distressed whine right before the next rush of vomit passes his lips, hitting the water below with a nauseating splash. He barely registers the sound of running water.

Suddenly, something cool is dabbing at his forehead and neck.

He wants to protest that he’s sick and definitely contagious and probably the last thing David wants to be touching. But a selfish part of him is enjoying this, and that part wins.

David holds the cool cloth to the back of Patrick’s neck when he’s finished throwing up.

Neither of them say anything for a long time.

“David...”

David cuts him off with a quick, “Sh.”

“No, David, I’m sick.”

A chuckle. “I can see that, thank you.”

Patrick isn’t sure what’s going on, but he doesn’t have the energy to argue.

After a few more minutes without puking, David stands and lays the washcloth on the edge of the sink.

And somehow, Patrick lets David convince him that he should go back to bed. David grabs the bathroom waste basket on their way out.

“You don’t have to stay,” Patrick mumbles, even as David is the only thing keeping him from falling on his face. He’s not dizzy, but throwing up has made his whole body feel shaky. He doesn’t trust himself to make it anywhere without David’s help.

“I’m going to pretend like you didn’t just insinuate that I’d leave you here alone while you’re on your deathbed. Well,” he helps Patrick lower himself onto the edge of the mattress. “Now you’re on your deathbed.”

“I’m sick.”

“Mmhm, and you keep saying that like it’s not painfully obvious.”

“David,” he doesn’t mean to take that tone, but David’s not listening! Then again, maybe that didn’t come out how he thought it did, because David doesn’t look bothered in the slightest by the tone that usually makes him go defensive. “I’m sick and probably contagious. And I don’t want you getting sick too, or... I mean, I know this kind of thing bothers you and I don’t want to make you uncomfortable or...”

What would normally come out as a reasonable plea does not have the same affect when his voice is ruined by an hour of puking. He doesn’t have the chance to make it sound better before David’s nodding and trying to shush him.

“Okay, Patrick, I thought this subject might come up again, so listen to me. I can’t promise you that I’m not going to disinfect literally everything in this apartment every hour.” He clears his throat, “But... I’ve been working on my little issues since that uh, last time? I want to be able to take care of you when you’re not feeling good. I want to be able to be there for you no matter what.”

Patrick frowns. “David...”

“No, stop. I know what you’re going to say. And you need me. So. Here I am. Don’t be ungrateful.”

Patrick isn’t sure when he started tearing up, but he sniffles. “I don’t want you to-”

“Shhh,” David wraps his arms around him and pulls him into a hug. His sweatshirt is big and warm and Patrick could stay here forever. “Wow, you’re really out of it...” he hears David mumble.

“I love you.”

David’s chest expands with a chuckle, jostling Patrick only slightly. “I love you too.”

“You’re going to change your shirt, right?”

“Oh, absolutely.”

Patrick huffs out a watery laugh.
