Word count: 1.4k

Ouch

Magnus was rudely awoken that morning when Alec coughed and as a result, made a terrible and disruptive pillow. He groaned, turning over onto his actual pillow and rubbing his eyes.

“Sorry,” Alec chuckled. “It came out of nowhere.”

Magnus watched as his boyfriend brought his legs over the bed and hunched forward for a moment with a powerful yawn.

It was early. Too early, but Alec liked to be at the institute before everyone was up so he could go over paperwork and read mission logs before everyone started coming to his office to ask questions. It was commendable, but boy did Magnus miss sleeping in.

Alec stayed hunched over for a bit, as if fighting with the idea of being awake himself, and then finally stood up. with a grunt. He looked as tired as Magnus felt, which may have been unexpected but completely understandable. The sun was barely out, after all.

He wanted to go to sleep, but he also wanted to watch his beautiful boyfriend get dressed.

Alec grabbed the first t-shirt within reach and pulled one of his button-downs out of the closet. He got his arms through the sleeves, but didn’t bother to button it just yet, resulting in a very nice look if Magnus did say so himself.

Alec stopped, sighed heavily. Magnus smiled.

“You look tired.”

“Yeah,” he breathed out. He dragged a hand down his face with a quiet groan. “It’s just gonna be a long day. I’ve got field work.”

“What?” Magnus sat up with a frown. “I thought you were done with all of that nonsense now that you’re the... head honcho, or something.”

Alec scoffed and unfortunately continued to get dressed. “I thought so too! But there’s been a big influx and everyone could really use the extra help. Anyways, I’m going with Izzy and Jace, so maybe it’ll be a bit like old times.”

“Well, you have fun with that.” Magnus laid back down and got rather comfortable under his fine down comforter. “I’m going back to sleep.”

Alec smiled fondly as he buttoned his shirt. “Good. I’ll see you tonight, okay?”

“I’m very much looking forward to it,” he hummed.

“Mm, I would hope so.”

The last thing he felt before he went back to sleep was a hand run through his hair and a kiss on his temple. Magnus knew that this was going to be a good day. He could feel it.

-----

Well...

Apparently, his feelings were wrong.

It was an hour before Alec was due to come home.

Magnus’ heart nearly jumped into his throat when he got a call from Izzy telling him that Alec had been hurt out in the field.

“He’s going to be fine, Magnus, I promise. It’s just a couple fractured ribs. He got hit pretty hard.”

Izzy’s reassurances did help ground him a bit. He wasn’t quite used to having someone so close to worry about. Most of his close friends were powerful warlocks as opposed to... squishy humans.

“Okay,” he breathed out. “Do you need my help with anything? Healing magic...”

“Well...”

“Isabelle, why do I get the feeling that there’s something you’re not telling me?”

“Okay, Magnus, it’s not a big deal... we’re just trying to figure some stuff out.”

“Stuff...? What stuff?”

“We tried healing him already,” Izzy said. “It wasn’t... well, it wasn’t working.” Magnus’ brain went from worried buzzing to problem-solving mode in a heartbeat. He tried to think of a cause for that sort of thing, but Izzy continued before he could blurt out his first guess. “I think he’s getting sick and just hasn’t said anything,” she sighed.

“Ah...” that made sense. It would explain why he was tired this morning, and why he rudely coughed Magnus awake. It also meant that healing magic couldn’t work, at least not as it usually did. “Damn it, Alec.”

Izzy hummed in agreement. “So I hope you’re ready for him.”

“Always am!”

-----

Getting Alec threw a portal and to bed was a feat in itself.

He hissed in pain as he lied down. “I’m sorry. This isn’t... exactly how we wanted to spend tonight.”

“Don’t be sorry,” Magnus assured.

Alec had yet to acknowledge why he wasn’t healed at the institute. It was like the two of them were playing a game of chicken, and Magnus probably wouldn’t win.

He sat on the edge of the bed, making sure that Alec was comfortable enough and had everything in reach. “I was told to keep you in bed for at least two days.”

Alec groaned. “I’m not that bad off, I swear.” Those words may have had a bit more weight if his voice didn’t break off into a cough.

A flash of pain went across Alec’s face, more intense than it had since Magnus picked him up. That was when Magnus truly realized how awful his boyfriend’s situation was. Being sick was one thing, being bedridden with an injury was another, but he was doomed to spend the next few days dreading each cough or sneeze.

“Ohhh darling...” Magnus cooed in sympathy.

Alec shook his head, face still pinched in pain as he cradled his ribs. “I’m-”

“You’re not fine. But maybe that’s a conversation for later.”

Alec gave a small smile, eyes lighting up in the realization that Magnus knew, and relief that he didn’t have to admit to it.

The two of them spent the rest of the evening in bed watching movies and joking. Alec was still coughing every once in a while, and his voice had taken on a thick quality like that of someone with a stuffed nose. Even just a cold was going to make a painful few days for him, but Magnus hoped that that was all it was.

Alec finally dozed off around eight, snoring even louder than usual, and Magnus carefully sneaked out of the bedroom. He had a couple of potions he could work on while Alec was asleep, and maybe something medicinal as well.

And that was fine for a while. He set the liquid to stir itself and watched with a fond smile as he thought about how thankful he was to have Alec in his life. And even more thankful that he wasn’t more seriously injured. His job was far more dangerous than Magnus liked, but he wasn’t exactly one to talk.

Things weren’t perfect though, Magnus was reminded this when he heard coughing from the bedroom. He wasn’t going to go check on Alec at first. His presence could distract Alec from going back to sleep and getting the rest his body desperately needed. But the coughing went on far longer than it should have, and the sound was different.

It wasn’t the ticklish coughs he’d been dealing with all day, but something came directly from the chest and refused to let up anytime soon. Unfortunately for Alec, his chest was broken at the moment.

Magnus was immediately up when he heard a wheezy cry of pain. He rushed into the bedroom and saw his boyfriend struggling to take a breath that didn’t hurt while coughs still tried to bubble up from his chest. Magnus couldn’t do anything but stay with him until it passed.

Alec’s eyes were teary, his face still red from the coughing, and nose running slightly.

He hated seeing Alec so distressed or in pain, and it wasn’t usually the result of a simple injury like this.

He tried to move, only ending up groaning and muttering an “Ow...” as he carefully laid an arm across his ribs.

After a few moments, Magnus risked laying a hand on his boyfriend’s forehead, Alec not even doing anything to move away.

“You’re a little warm...”

Alec sniffled. “It’s not as bad as it looks,” he said, voice hoarse and tight. “Or... sounds.”

Magnus hummed and moved closer to Alec, careful not to jostle the bed too much and cause him even more pain.

“I honestly hope so...”

Alec cleared his throat, sniffled again. “Do you have any painkillers? I don’t... I don’t think my iratze is gonna work right now.”

Magnus pressed a kiss to his temple. “I can go out and get some if you’d like.”

“Thank you,” Alec rasped.

Magnus didn’t remember ever having to go shopping at an actual, mundane pharmacy. The place was tiny and cramped and full of people who sounded about as sick as Alec did. It didn’t take him long to find the stuff that Alec had asked for. He grabbed some a weird-looking bottle that was under a cold and flu sign, and then threw a box of tissues onto the counter for good measure.

Soon he was headed back to the apartment.

The medicine seemed to help dull the pain enough for him to go back to sleep. Magnus had a feeling that neither of them would be sleeping too much tonight, but that was alright.
