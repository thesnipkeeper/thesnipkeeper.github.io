Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Caduceus has some good friends
Word count: 1.4k

More Sleep

“How you doin’ Deucey?”

Fjord sat on the edge of the bed by his friend’s feet and looked him over.

Caduceus shrugged, making a non-committal noise. “Tired,” he said. “I didn’t get a lot of sleep last night... nor did anyone else, I think.” He frowned guiltily.

“Don’t worry about it.” Poor Caduceus spent the whole night coughing, Fjord didn’t imagine that he’d gotten any sleep at all. Jester and Beau had created their own sound-proofing measures, and the others were so accustomed to sleeping under any circumstance that they weren’t disturbed.

“Feeling up to some breakfast? I think Jester’s trying her hand at baking.”

Caduceus’ ears perk up, just slightly, at the mention of food. Fjord knew how to get to the guy, and it was through his stomach, just like Caduceus did with everyone else.

“I think that I could manage,” he said with a sheepish smile. He took a moment to assess, and made a noise of disbelief. “Wow, it’s been a while since I’ve eaten.”

“Yeah, yeah... I wasn’t gonna say anything, but we were pretty worried for a while. Your fever was way up there, and you weren’t letting us feed you, you were barely drinking...” Fjord let out a shaky sigh. Yesterday was scary, and it was probably even harder for Jester, as she didn’t have any healing magic left.

“Oh...” Caduceus’ brows furrowed as if trying to remember. It was no use. “I didn’t mean to worry you all. I... run a little warm when I’m sick.”

Fjord nodded and patted his leg. “Well, you seem like you’re feelin’ better.”

“Definitely,” Caduceus agreed, right before his breath caught and he doubled over in a fit of coughs.

He clamped a hand over his mouth, face turning pink from the effort as he hacked. This was about how it’d been the past couple of days up until he was bedridden the day before. Fjord stood up and moved to help Caduceus sit up as he coughed more.

It was turning into breathless coughs, almost heaves that had Fjord wondering if he should find a bucket or something. Caduceus hadn’t thrown up yet, but only because he hadn’t had anything in his stomach.

“Excuse me...” Caduceus said breathlessly, thumping his chest. He gave a couple more small coughs as he caught his breath. His eyes were watery, adding to his general misery.

“Alright,” said Fjord. “I’m gonna go get you some breakfast, and maybe some of that Xhorasian tea that Essek dropped off and see if it helps you breathe a little easier.”

“Mr. Fjord-” Caduceus stopped him before he could leave, and when Fjord turned around, the firbolg was sitting up and swinging his legs over the edge of the bed. “I think that I’ll come to the kitchen with you and eat with the others.” He muffled a cough into his sleeve with a wince.

He didn’t look well, sure, but Fjord didn’t see any reason for the guy not to get up if he wasn’t in immediate danger of passing out. Again. It might calm the others’ nerves a bit to see him feeling better.

Fjord kept a careful eye on him as they walked side-by-side to the kitchen and dining area. The place was bustling with activity, mainly Beau and Jester bickering over breakfast pastries and which recipe to make next. It resulted in Jester getting flour blown in her face and Beau getting immediately attacked.

“Morning, Caduceus!” Nott was the first to see the both of them wander in. “You’re looking better!”

Caduceus offered a small, sweet smile. “Yeah, I feel a lot more... aware? I think.”

“Do you want some pastries?” Jester asked as she shoved an angry (but laughing) Beau away.

“Oh, please!” He sounded far too excited, but that was par for the course for Caduceus. Everything was new and exciting for someone who’d spent their whole life in a graveyard.

They get some breakfast and tea in front of him.

The whole eating together thing was something that Fjord would always secretly love. Even when his sick friend was struggling to stop coughing. He patted Caduceus’ back, and of course that didn’t really help, because why would it?

“Here, uh,” Fjord nudged the cup that had gone relatively untouched (along with the food, now that he realized it) closer to Caduceus. “Try some tea, Essek says it’s supposed to help.”

Caduceus did so, and hummed at it. Was that a good hum? It sounded like a good hum.

“What kind of tea is this?” Caduceus asked before taking another sip. “It’s... sweet.” Apparently that was a good thing, because he took a third sip before setting the cup down.

“Yeah, he said that it’s some kind of plant that they grow here, mixed with, I don’t know, another plant?”

Beau scoffed, but she didn’t have anything else to offer. “How is it?”

Caduceus nodded. “I’d love to get some leaves from Essek before we... leave again.”

“I think we can do that,” said Fjord.

Caduceus only took a couple bites of his food. He didn’t push his plate away or make any indication that he was finished, but looked guiltily at Jester when she asked if he was going to eat any more.

“That’s okay, Caduceus,” Jester promised, giving him a squeeze before taking his plate. “I know that you’re not feeling good.”

Caduceus smiled. “I’d love to try your pastries when I’m feeling better,” he promised. He inhaled, but his breath caught and resulted in another coughing fit, this one rougher and dryer than the last.

“Take it easy, Caduceus.”

The poor guy couldn’t stop coughing for a good couple of minutes. He took another sip of tea as soon as he could take in a breath in the hopes that it would help.

Surprisingly, it did. Or at least, it seemed to, and that was enough.

He cleared his throat, once again looking exhausted and so incredibly over this illness.

“Want help getting back to bed?” Beau offered.

“Oh, I don’t need to go back yet, do I? Well, I guess there’s the concern of... germs...” he frowned a bit at the realization.

The others, all at once, immediately reassured him that they didn’t mind one bit. How could a group like them mind, really?

“We’re just makin’ sure that you’re not straining yourself for our sake,” Fjord said.

Caleb made a noise of agreement. “If you want to join us for whatever we do, Caduceus, we’re happy to have you in our company.”

Cad’s ears perked up just a bit. He nodded and smiled. “Well, thank you.”

Fjord knew for a fact that the girls had a plan to go out and get some supplies that they needed (and didn’t need), and Caleb was going to work on his books (whatever that meant). Fjord was planning to go say hello to the neighbors, possibly get more of a feel for Rosohna. And yet, they all insisted that they had nothing planned for the day, because Caduceus was miserable and lonely and longing for people to be around.

It made sense, Fjord realized. He grew up in a big family and in close quarters. He was probably used to having someone to snuggle up to when he wasn’t feeling well, just like he was doing with Jester and Nott right now. (Caduceus was an adorably snuggly individual.)

They goofed off, played some games, told stories, and eventually tired themselves out. They dwindled down to simple conversation, especially when they heard snoring coming from their big firbolg friend. He was rested uncomfortably on Jester’s shoulder, and she somehow managed to move his head to her lap without disturbing him.

She hummed contently and ran her fingers through his hair.

“Fuck,” Beau said. “We’re gonna have to wake him.”

“We don’t have to... I can sit here for a while. He’ll wake himself up at some point, anyway.”

They all chuckled. Caduceus was in for a rough few days, from what Essek had told them about the illness. But they had tea, and they clearly had enough cuddly people to tide him over.

“You sure?” Fjord asked.

Jester nodded quickly. “He didn’t even sleep last night because he kept coughing. If we wake him up now he might not be able to go back to sleep.” She continued gently detangling his hair.

Beau shrugged. “I can chill here for a little while longer.”

A few of them ended up falling asleep right there, Fjord included. They’d all wake up to Caduceus coughing, but until then, they were all together.
