Title: Not Like Before
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David does something unspeakable
Word count: 700

Not Like Before

Patrick doesn't even bat an eye as his fiance flops on the couch and puts his head in his lap. David hadn't slept well last night, or at least that's what he's assumed. He's always extra quiet and clingy when he hasn't slept.

He runs his fingers through David's hair, lightly scratching his scalp as David sighs contentedly.

"Sleepy?"

David nods against his leg. "Feel a little weird."

"Weird?"

"Dunno." David yawns, nuzzles his thigh. "Just... tired but like, weird tired."

Patrick hums, but doesn't think much of it. They remain in that position for a while as Patrick reads and David's breath slows. He thinks maybe his fiance has fallen asleep until David makes a noise and stirs.

"You okay?" Patrick questions as he flips to the next page of his book.

David makes another discontented noise and starts to sit up. "Patrick, I think-" and promptly vomits on Patrick's lap, and down his pant leg.

Patrick swears and grabs a hold of David's shoulders to help him sit up. The poor guy has his hand clamped over his mouth, desperately trying to hold back the next wave of puke. He looks horrified at what he's just done, and Patrick knows that they're going to have to have a conversation to repair that damage, but later. Right now he needs to get David to the bathroom before he throws up again.

“Do you want me to stay?” Patrick asks softly.

David only shakes his head, refusing to look at him. So Patrick carefully closes the door behind him.

The mess on the floor reminds him how thankful he is that his apartment doesn’t have carpet. He goes about cleaning, while he listens to David heave. It’s going to be a rough couple of days for them both, then.

-----

Patrick knocks on the bathroom door and holds the folded clothes in his arms. “David? I brought some stuff for you to change into just in case your clothes got dirty.”

He hears a shaky sigh, and waits a few seconds before the door opens. David’s shirtless, which must mean that his poor sweater must have indeed taken a hit.

He doesn’t make eye contact with Patrick at first as he takes the clothes. Patrick thinks he’s just going to turn around without another word, but thankfully he says something, even if it’s, “Was it really bad?”

“The- the mess?”

David nods.

“It’s taken care of.”

David’s mouth forms a thin line as he sighs out through his nose. “I’m so sorry,” he finally says, looking more apologetic than Patrick’s ever seen him. “Like, I’m so sorry, I’ll buy you new-”

“David,” he holds a hand up to stop his fiance from whatever he’s about to offer. “It’s not a big deal!”

“Um, it is, though.” David shudders, and huffs before he begrudgingly starts pulling on the sweatshirt Patrick brought him. “I vomited on you. You don’t think that’s a big deal?!”

“Well, it was pretty gross.”

David groaned and buried his head in his hands. “Oh my God...”

Patrick chuckled. “David, it happens! It’s fine!”

Except it’s not fine, because when David looks up again, his eyes are watering. He’s crying. Fuck.

“Hey,” Patrick hesitates, and then pulls him into a hug. David wastes no time leaning into it, giving shaky breaths as he tries not to cry. “I’m serious, sweetheart. I don’t care.”

David exhales, breath warming Patrick’s shoulder. “Are you sure?”

“Positive!”

Patrick sees it now. The fear that he’s going to leave him like plenty of others in his life have. Probably for just as stupid reasons. David deserves so much better, and Patrick’s going to give it to him.

“Now,” he says, pulling away and laying hand on David’s neck. “You feel kind of warm, so maybe we should take it easy for the rest of the night.”

“Please,” David breathes out. He’s still shaky, probably a mixture of the fever and having spent so long throwing up.

The two of them get settled in bed, and prepare themselves for a long night. David snuggles into him once again, overly-warm body pressed to Patrick’s.

“Might be a long night,” Patrick says quietly.

David nods against him. “Yeah,” he groans. “Think I’m getting sick.”

“Really? How could you tell?”

“Oh my God, shut up.”
