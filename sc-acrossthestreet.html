Title: Across the Street
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: The day the plumbing is out is the perfect day to get the stomach flu
Word count: 1.2k

Across the Street

They were sorting through several boxes of product, arranging them by date just how David insisted they did.

David shifted, a twinge of discomfort on his face. "I'm going to um," he swallowed and gestured toward the door. "-going to head to the cafe and use the restroom."

Patrick chuckled. "Why don't you just use the drain? No one will know."

David opened his mouth to reply, closed it, and winced.

Ah. Patrick cleared his throat. "Ohhh. Yeah, go ahead."

He nodded and quickly set his work on the counter.

With the store’s plumbing on the fritz (this time without Ronnie’s help), their choices were to sneak into the cafe several times a day, or give up coffee and tea until the bathroom was fixed. David and Patrick weren’t prepared to make that sacrifice.

One more day until the plumber arrived, Patrick and David reminded themselves.

David returned, sheepishly walking through the door. He picked up the box that he left and once again began putting everything out, refusing to look at Patrick at all.

Patrick didn’t think anything of it until David made a face and laid a hand on his stomach.

“You okay?”

“Hm? Oh,” David removed his hand quickly and nodded, putting on a smile. “Yeah, yeah I’m fine. Everything’s fine. Just a little bit of a um...” He cleared his throat and winced as he said, “Stomach ache?”

Patrick grinned. “Did you eat an entire box of cinnamon buns again?”

“No,” David answered quickly, and then cringed as if having flashbacks. “Nope, I only made that mistake once and it’s definitely never going to happen again. Uh no, it’s probably just a little indigestion, as my mother continues to nag me about it.”

Patrick knew David was prone to stomach aches. It probably had something to do with his wildly-varied diet and inability to go an hour without eating. He’d also witnessed David overeat countless times.

A specific moment he remembered very clearly was before they were officially dating. David walked into the store with a bag of chips and container of ice cream, and proceeded to eat both before it was time for dinner. Patrick didn’t find out until a later conversation about past relationships that Sebastian, David’s ex, had been visiting town around that time.

This was different, Patrick knew, because he wasn’t even sure he’d seen David eat yet today. Didn’t mean he hadn’t, of course, but he often brought snacks to work.

They were nearly done with the first box when David stifled a groan. “I’ll be right back,” there was a tightness in his voice, and Patrick didn’t have a chance to question it before he was walking back to the cafe once again.

Patrick sent anxious glances out the window until David finally returned again.

He didn’t say anything, just went back to work. Patrick pretended not to notice the sheen of sweat on his skin or the fact that David’s sleeves were pushed up.

-----

The pained moan from his boyfriend didn’t go unnoticed.

David hunched over, both arms wrapped tightly around his middle. This time when he saw Patrick looking at him, he didn’t seem to care.

His face was flushed now, and David looked in a constant state of discomfort.

“Everything alright?”

“Mhm,” David nodded, even as his eyes were squeezed shut and his hand was kneading into his soft stomach. He cleared his throat and started for the door. “Just need a minute... sorry.”

An apology for leaving work? Well, that wasn’t like him at all.

Patrick finished up the sorting and restocking before David got back, which left him to nervously double-check his spreadsheets and make sure he entered everything correctly the day before.

He did, of course he did, but he needed something else to think about.

The bell rang, announcing David’s return.

He looked worse. “Are you sure this is just indigestion?” Patrick asked.

David gave a nauseated swallow and hesitantly shook his head. “Nope. I don’t...” he groaned as his stomach cramped, and Patrick thought he could even hear David’s belly making noise. “I don’t know what’s wrong, but it needs to stop like, now.”

Patrick frowned. “Hey,” he laid a hand on David’s arm, “why don’t you go home for the day?”

David bit his lip. “I don’t want to leave you with all of this work...”

“Yes you do.”

He snorted. “You’re right, of course I do.”

“And it’s probably a good idea for you to stay close to a bathroom that isn’t across the street.”

David’s face became ten shades redder. He cleared his throat awkwardly, slowly shifting away. “Ew,” he said. “But yes, I will take the day off.”

-----

David texted him an hour later, asking him to pick up some ginger ale and saltines and drop them off before he went home. When asked if he was okay, he just said 'sick. Very sick' and left it at that. That was enough for Patrick.

Patrick knocked on the motel door, only to be greeted by Alexis.

She smiled the same way she always smiled at him. "Are you sure you want to be here right now? It's very… pukey, in here," she said it lowly, like she didn't want her brother to hear.

Patrick just chuckled and made his way into the motel. It turned out she wasn't whispering to hide from David, but because he was asleep. He was curled up in bed, looking rather uncomfortable. Patrick nearly tip-toed through the room and pushed a few bottles of nail polish aside to set the pharmacy bag on his and Alexis' table.

Alexis made a noise of delight and began digging through the bag. He wasn't too worried about her running away with the ginger ale and many flavors of gatorade.

Despite his efforts to keep quiet, David shifted and made a sleepy, questioning noise. "Mm?"

Patrick sat on the edge of the bed and rubbed David's back through the blanket. "Hey, I brought some stuff for you."

Sleepy, dark brown eyes looked up at him, and David gave the smallest of smiles. "Thank you." He pulled himself up to sit against the wall, running a tired hand down his face as he woke up. "I think I might be dying," he informed with a wince.

Patrick chuckled. "Well, if that happens, I promise to say something very nice and heartfelt at your funeral."

"Um, I would hope so."

Patrick couldn't help but wince at the croaky quality of his boyfriend's voice. He reached up to run his fingers through David's hair, both because it was a mess, and because he wanted to gauge any current fever.

"You're a little warm."

"I'm very cold, actually."

"I took his temp earlier," Alexis said. "He wasn't running a fever then, but he's vomited like, a lot since then."

David made an ugh noise at the thought. "My stomach is trying to kill me, Patrick."

"I think you just have a virus, honey."

David made an even more displeased noise and curled up again, pressing his arms into his belly. "Either way, I'm sure I only have moments to live, so I think you should probably stay here."

Patrick chuckled. "I can do that."
