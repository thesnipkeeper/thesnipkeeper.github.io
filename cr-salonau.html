Title: Salon AU
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly crushes on his newest client
Word count: 1.1k

Salon AU

Mollymauk had a thing about interesting people. He met plenty of them. He cut hair for a living, after all. There was Caduceus Clay, who would get his hair dyed one specific color of pink, his side shave touched up, and the occasional trim (his hair was getting quite long and Molly gushed verbally about it each time he came).

Fjord, who was rather simple (and adorably graying).

Beau, who constantly threatened him if he messed up her hair (she was never serious, and he would always respond with an equal threat before starting on the trim).

And then there was Jester, who sometimes just came for a wash so she could talk to him.

Now, what was important about Jester was that she started telling her friends to get their hair done by Molly.

Specifically her one friend, Caleb.

Caleb had red hair that went well-past his chin. It was sometimes greasy, and Molly noticed that Caleb was a little quieter on those days.

He never missed an appointment. Always showed up right on time, even when Molly wasn’t ready for him yet.

Never missed an appointment, even when he was afflicted with a miserable cold.

He didn’t mention it, but it wasn’t hard to tell when his nose was pink and he was sniffling constantly.

Molly combed his hair quite contently as Caleb snuffled and pressed a kerchief to his nose.

“How is work going?” Molly asked easily, tilting Caleb’s head forward a bit to begin cutting. Caleb was a professor at the same school Jester taught art. He was rather surprised when he’d first heard about it, Caleb didn’t seem the type to have a job that required... constant communication, but he supposed that he did look the type.

“It has not been too bad lately,” he said with a voice that told Molly his voice was sore. “But there is a cold going around in my class and...” he gestured to himself at Molly’s reflection.

Molly made a sympathetic noise as if it were a surprise and he hasn’t been feeling the warmth of a fever since he’d been touching Caleb’s skin. “Well, I’m sorry to hear that. Are many of your students sick as well?”

Caleb made a confirming noise in the back of his throat. “Ja, attendance has been rather thin recently. I’ve considered just giving them some days off to recover and work on their other classes.”

Molly smiled. “Oh, your students must love you.”

“Ah, some of them. Others like to...” he paused to sniffle, Molly pretended not to notice the wetness building on his upper lip. “Others like to cause trouble. I’ve suffered a few pranks, a few angry students who didn’t like their grade.”

“Caleb, I’m just now realizing that I don’t know what you teach,” Molly admitted.

Molly caught a glimpse of a soft smile before Caleb raised his handkerchief to tend to his nose. “History,” he answered plainly.

“Just ‘history’, then?”

“I ah, snf! Specialize in the history of the arcane and its effects on today’s society, but general history as well. The college isn’t exactly well-funded enough for more than one history professor.”

“That’s quite interesting!” Molly didn’t give much thought to arcana. The practice of magic had quite died out hundreds of years ago, unless you were a race that had magic in your blood (like a tiefling). He could imagine it would be fun to learn about such a thing.

Caleb had developed a cough by the time Molly was done cutting his hair.

“Caleb,” Molly said softly, leaning over the counter after Caleb had paid. “Please take care of yourself. You sound awful.”

Caleb nodded quickly. “Ja, I will try. Thank you, Mollymauk.”

“I told you, you can call me Molly.”

Caleb just smiled and exited the salon.

Goodness, Mollymauk thought. I’m smitten, aren’t I?

And that, that was just the beginning.

-----

The next time Molly saw Caleb wasn't at his salon but rather, the drugstore.

Molly wandered in looking for an inexpensive mascara when he heard the wracking cough from the other side of the store. Whoever was coughing like that was obviously not having a good week, and he frowned in sympathy. 

He spotted a color of nail polish he didn't own yet and threw that in his basket as well before making his way to the register. 

There was a miserable, muffled cough from the person who had taken their place behind him in line. Molly stole a glance at the poor person, and gasped.

"Caleb, hello!" He greeted, perhaps too cheery for someone who looked so sick.

Caleb certainly didn't seem surprised to see his face, although he probably identified Molly a few seconds before bd just didn't say anything. Mollymauk knew he stood out particularly well no matter where he was.

"Hello," Caleb offered in return.

Molly set his basket on the counter, offering the cashier a sincere greeting as well. 

Before he walked out of the store, he curiously stood at the doorway and  watched Caleb set his things on the counter. Tissues, cough suppressants of all sorts, and a fever reducer. Oh, the poor thing. 

Caleb's eyes widened when he saw that Molly was still there when he took his bag.

"You look worse, Caleb. Much worse."

Caleb nodded. "Developed a bit of a chest infection," he admitted with another wet cough. They walked out of the store together as Caleb muffled coughs into his sweater.

"Oh Caleb, did you take my advice when I told you to take care of yourself?"

Caleb huffed out a chuckle that seemed to crackle in his chest. "Honestly, I did not. I've been busy, and I've had to work harder as a result of this cold."

Molly sighed. "Sorry. That's awful." 

Caleb made a noise of agreement. He coughed again. He stopped in front of a car that must have been thirty years old. "It was nice seeing you again, Mister Mollymauk."

"You as well, Mister Caleb!"

-----

The next few months went by uneventfully.

Save for Caleb's increasing visits.

Getting more frequent trims were definitely doing good for his hair, though Mollymauk couldn't help but wonder… no, he was just being lovesick and silly.

“You know,” Jester said one day as Molly was drying her hair. “You should ask Caleb out.”

Molly’s eyes widened. “What?”

“Don’t you like him?”

“Of course I like him, I-”

“No.” Jester quickly shook her head. “Like like him.” she said with waggly eyebrows.

Molly giggled. “Is this primary school?”

“Molly, come on, I know you totally have the hots for Caleb!”

Molly scoffed and guided her toward the register. He was quick to finish her transaction and escort her out of the salon, all while she smugly informed him that he’d picked a good one. Molly didn’t realize until after she left how warm his cheeks had become.
