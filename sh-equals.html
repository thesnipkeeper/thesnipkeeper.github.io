Title: Equals
Fandom: Shadowhunters
Summary: Alec gets food poisoning and Magnus accidentally enjoys it
Word count: 700

Equals

Since Magnus had lost his magic, he was the one who'd needed the comforting and coddling. The help with normalcy.

He was starting to feel a bit invalid in a sea of shadowhunters who were now stronger and more powerful than he was.

So Magnus felt slightly guilty for the sense of relief he got when Alec came down with a bad case of food poisoning.

It was just nice to not be the one who needed to be taken care of.

Alec had mentioned a bit of a stomach ache as they got ready for bed the night before. His stomach woke them both before the sun was up. Alec hadn't intended to wake Magnus, but it was clear that the stomach cramps were severe with the way he was shifting and whimpering.

"Stomach?" Magnus slurred, still half asleep as he reached up to rub Alec's back.

"Yeah," Alec's voice was strained, sounding more pained than he had when he'd gotten stabbed a few days ago. "Sorry, I didn't mean to wake you up." He moaned, curled up tighter. "I don't feel so good."

Magnus made a sympathetic noise and bumped his forehead against Alec's back in a sleepy show of affection.

Alex continued to whimper and shift uncomfortably until he finally sighed. Magnus propped himself up on his elbow as Alec pulled the blankets off of himself.

"Do you think you're going to throw up?" Magnus asked worriedly.

"Um," Alec started. "I'm not sure, to be honest, but I should probably go to the bathroom in case..."

Magnus' sleep-fogged brain caught on. "Ah."

"Go back to sleep, Magnus. I'm fine."

He was not fine, and Magnus did not go back to sleep.

Alec had been in and out of the bathroom all morning. He ended up nothing but a shaky mess under his covers, tired and dizzy from throwing up and losing so many fluids.

Magnus returned to the room from the cafeteria with tiniest bits of what he remembered being good for an upset stomach.

" 'm not hungry..." Alec muttered, even as Magnus coerced him to sit up and placed the tray in his lap.

"Have some water, at least," Magnus said softly, pressing a cup of water into his hand. Alec looked at it cautiously, as if it might bite him. "Just a some small sips, my love."

He raised the cup to his lips and took a few careful swallows before setting back on the tray. "I can't eat right now, Magnus. 'm sorry."

"That's okay. We'll try when you're stomach's settled a little more."

The poor thing seemed so out of it. Part of it was illness, but the other part was probably just exhaustion from having gotten such little rest since all of this started. He... Yeah, he felt guilty for finding a sick, miserable Alec so endearing. In no way should he have been enjoying his love's suffering.

"What?"

Apparently he'd been looking at him strangely.

"Sorry," Magnus shook his head to clear it. "I've just never seen you like this."

Despite looking like death warmed over, Alec chuckled, mouth quirking up on one side the way Magnus loved. "Thank goodness for that. I'm surprised you're still sticking around after everything this morning."

"Are you kidding? You've stuck with me through my worst, I'll stick with you through yours."

Alec chuckled and took another sip of water. "That's love, I guess."

Magnus hummed thoughtfully.  "You know, Alexander, I won't lie. This," he waved his hands around, "situation has certainly helped my um... Fractured ego."

Alec raised an eyebrow. Magnus bit his lip. Maybe that was one of those things one shouldn't say out loud.

"Okay. I'm not sure whether that's a compliment or an insult," he said with a confused smile.

Magnus shook his head and took Alec's free hand, pressing a kiss to it. "Sorry. No, I've just had a rough few days of... Worrying about my place in your life these days."

Alec frowned immediately. "Magnus, I love you more than anything. You have to know that."

"I do. I just worry, Alexander, that's all."

"No worrying," Alec said decidedly. "It'll take you a while to get used to this, I know, but you will."

He sounded so sure, as if he actually knew their future. And Magnus found himself believing it. He smiled a bit sheepishly and covered Alec's hand with both of his own.

"Well, I'm sorry for enjoying your suffering."

Alec scoffed. "Thanks."
