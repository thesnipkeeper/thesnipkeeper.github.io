Caring for Your Firbolg

It was a long day. They were forced to travel in the rain, trudging through mud and deep puddles until they could finally find an inn. This was, of course, after they’d gotten the shit beat out of them by some surprisingly skilled thieves.

The thieves didn’t steal anything, except for maybe the group’s pride.

All in all, Caleb was ready to call it a day. They all paid for their rooms and made their way up the stays, shivering and grumbling as they did. Caleb was ready to get out of his soaked clothing and finally be able to sit down and comfortably read, ideally with some alcohol in-hand.

His ears perked up at a ticklish-sounding sneeze from one of the other rooms. Huh. He didn’t recognize that particular sneeze.

He got out of his wet clothes, mentioned to Nott that he might get a drink, and the two of them went downstairs.

The other members of the Nein trickled down as well, finally followed by Fjord and Caduceus. Caduceus always looked comfortable when he was out of his armor, all of his clothes were loose-fitting and nice. He didn’t look quite as comfortable tonight.

The firbolg had his arms folded over his chest, and he still seemed to be shivering from the cold despite the fact that he was completely dry. It was strange to see him differently, but it didn’t surprise Caleb to find out that Caduceus got cold easily. He was quite thin, after all, probably didn’t retain body heat very well.

Caduceus didn’t join in any of the conversations for the rest of the night, and no one really paid attention to it until he made a shuddering sound that got everyone’s attention.

“You sure you’re feelin’ okay?” Fjord asked, and from the sounds of it, they had already had that conversation before.

Caduceus nodded. “Just a chill, you know?”

“Sure...” Fjord said with suspicion.

But Caduceus seemed to be getting sleepier as time went on. He was out of it.

“Mr. Clay,” Caleb said.

Caduceus’ eyes widened a bit, and he seemed to realize that he had been half asleep. “Hm?”

“Would you...” he was not so great at this, but he wanted to do something. “Would you mind coming upstairs with me? I forgot one of my books, but I do not trust this town enough to go alone.”

“Yeah, of course,” Caduceus said with a lazy smile.

Caleb nodded and the two of them made their way upstairs. Caduceus was moving slower, and looking back at him, Caleb noticed that his cheeks had a pinkish hue that definitely didn’t bode well. When he finally reached the top of the stairs, he looked at Caleb expectantly while the wizard stood there.

“I must admit something to you, Mr. Clay. I lied about the book.”

Caduceus’ brows knitted in confusion.

“You looked like you were not feeling so well, I thought you might need an excuse to come back here without suspicion.”

“Oh,” Caduceus chuckled and cleared his throat. “That’s really nice of you, but I-”

“You’re not fine,” Caleb said, more of a guessing statement.

Caduceus winced a bit. “Head’s a little fuzzy,” he finally admitted. “Maybe I will lie down for just a little while...”

Caleb watched the firbolg head to his and Fjord’s room, only for him to stumble. Caleb gasped and ran to help (even though there was no way he could catch a seven-foot tall firbolg), but Caduceus seemed to catch himself on the frame of the door. He made a discomforted sound in the back of his throat.

Caleb followed him into the room and helped Caduceus onto the bed. He was blinking hazily, trying to get his vision to focus.

“You look dizzy.”

“Mm,” Caduceus gave a minute nod. He got settled on the bed, not exactly in the bed. He didn’t cover up, or even really lie down, just rested with his back against the wall.

Caleb hesitated, opening his mouth and then closing it again until he finally asked, “Is it alright if I feel your forehead?”

Another nod, and a small smile that he chose to ignore.

Caleb laid the back of his hand against his skin. That was a fever without a doubt, but that wasn’t cause for immediate alarm. He didn’t like it. Jester was tapped out, and if he knew Caduceus, he wasn’t going to cast anyone on himself. He expressed his concerns (or rather, mumbled his concerns to himself because he wasn’t good at not talking to himself).

“Best to let the fever run its course,” Caduceus said with a dismissing sound.

“Alright,” Caleb nodded. “But that means that you are going to be miserable.”

“Yeah, it’s fine.”

Caleb sighed. What a strange man, but he supposed that he was one to talk.

-----

It was the middle of the night when Caleb and Nott heard a knock on their door. Nott immediately grabbed one of her daggers and his behind the bed.

Caleb was certainly on guard as well as he opened the door, but it was only Fjord.

“Caleb,” Fjord breathed out. “Caduceus is burnin’ up and he’d only let me come get you.”

“Me?”

“Doesn’t want everyone knowing he’s ill,” Fjord said. “I don’t know, he just... he’s out of it.”

Caleb nodded and made his way to their room. Caduceus was wrapped in blankets and shuddering heavily. He looked far worse than he had before.

“There isn’t a lot we can do without a healing spell...”

“He just needs to get through the night, that’s all.”

“He’s not dying, Fjord.”

“Wha-? Oh, no! That’s not what I meant! I just mean that he needs to be kept comfortable until the fever breaks.”

Caduceus shivered again. “Didn’t mean to be a burden.”

“You’re not anyone’s burden, Deuces.”

Just like that, Caleb stayed in their room the rest of the night as they checked Caduceus’ fever and distracted him from his misery.

-----

The next morning, Caleb was exhausted. Fjord looked the same. But Caduceus had gotten through the worst of his fever for now and that was all that mattered.

“Wow, you guys look like shit,” was Beau’s lovely comment as they came down for breakfast.

Jester hummed and nodded. “Nott said they were together with Caduceus all night,” she made sure to add some waggly eyebrows to the mix.

Caleb’s eyes widened in the realization. With a glance at Fjord, he had the same expression.

“Now, hold on-” said Fjord, but Caleb stopped him with a hand on his arm.

Caduceus didn’t want anyone to know that he was ill, they had to keep their promise. “Ja, it was a very nice three-way. Fjord is very skilled in the bedroom.”

Beau gagged on her bacon, while Jester snorted mid-sip of her milk.

Fjord sighed heavily. “Yep. Fantastic,” he deadpanned.
