Getting Involved

Percy had spent most of the day being either asleep or mostly asleep. Every so often someone would check on him, bring him water or something to eat. It was probably Cassandra, but he never bothered to open his eyes to find out.

He was determined to sleep off this horrible plague and get back on his feet as soon as possible. His body was more determined to keep him bedridden, Percy realized, when he nearly passed out as he got ready to leave with his friends that morning.

The idea of all of them out, adventuring, getting things done without him was maddening.

They’d been hired for a rather mundane job by someone in town. They owed the man a thank you, and he asked for help when some thieves had ravaged his home and taken something important to his family.

Kill the thieves, take their belongings, return the priceless heirloom. It was mundane, but it sounded like good fun.

Percy huffed. He decided that he was allowed to be upset about this. He hated being left out, damn it, especially when it came to serving a little bit of justice.

He was nearly asleep again when he heard his friends returning. Percy’s ears perked up, expecting to hear everyone laughing and basking in post-battle glee.

Nothing.

A few quiet voices, some footsteps, but nothing that really signified that the job had gone well.

Percy swore tiredly. He should go out there, he thought, and make sure that everything was alright.

Footsteps ascended the stairs, getting closer to his bedroom door.

“...to check on Percy...” Keyleth’s voice, but something sounded off.

No response.

Percy did his best to try to sit up, but he could hardly lift his head off the pillow before there was a knock at the door.

“Come in,” Percy rasped. Gods, he hadn’t used his voice in hours.

The door slowly opened, revealing Keyleth who smiled sheepishly and closed it behind her. “Hey, how’re you feeling?”

“Oh, awful,” he said with a sleepy smile.

She chuckled, but her own smile didn’t quite meet her eyes. Something was definitely wrong. Keyleth wasn’t exactly the best at lying or hiding her emotions, which - granted - was something he loved about her. One less person he had to try to figure out.

“How was the job?”

“Oh it was... it was good...” she nodded.

“It doesn’t sound like it,” Percy muttered skeptically. He tried to sit up once more and failed.

“No, no, it went...” she let out a shaky breath, and Percy watched her entire facade crumble. Keyleth sniffled, tears running down her cheeks despite how hard she was trying to sweep them away.

“Oh, Keyleth...” he frowned.

He had a feeling that she’d been waiting to let it go. Keyleth was good at avoiding her emotions until her breaking point.

She sniffled and scrubbed at her eyes. “I’m sorry. I just...”

“Don’t apologize. Here, help me up so I can give you a hug?”

Keyleth offered a strong grip for him to hold onto and assistance as he finally managed to pull himself up and sit against the headboard. His vision swam for a few seconds afterward before he breathed out. Keyleth immediately wrapped him in a hug, and Percy did his best to reciprocate despite his weak limbs.

She sobbed quietly into his shoulder for a long while. When she finally pulled away, her face was flushed and wet with tears.

“What happened?” Percy asked, careful to keep his voice steady. “Is everyone alright?”

Keyleth nodded. “I just- I fucked up, Percy. Everyone’s upset with me. Gods, I wouldn’t be surprised if Vex never spoke to me again...”

“Alright,” Percy stopped her before she could spiral any further, but without any plan of what to say next. His fever-addled brain tried to think. This was one of the things he hated most about being sick, his brain didn’t work as fast as it should. He wasn’t sure if he should ask Keyleth for more information, or simply let her cry it out.

She crossed her arms tightly and hunched over, eyes trained on the bed as she tried to stop crying.

“Can... can I ask what happened?”

Keyleth huffed, sniffled. “I took too long to cast a spell, and one of the guys got to Vax, and- and stabbed him.”

“Is Vax upset with you?”

“I don’t know,” she admitted. “I didn’t want to be out there with any of them anymore. I’m sorry. Vex hasn’t looked at me since we got back, and no one’s said anything...”

Percy stretched out an arm in invitation until she accepted and leaned against him. They seemed to be holding each other up at this point.

He wasn’t great at this. But he knew one thing he could do for his best friend. “Well, if it helps, you can stay here at long as you’d like.”

Keyleth breathed a sigh of relief, her body relaxing a bit. “Thanks, Percy.” she sniffled and pawed at her nose. “I love you. You know that, right?”

“I love you too, Keyleth.”

The two of them ended up cuddled up together. It was nice to have another person next to him when Percy’s chills returned. Keyleth’s panic slowly dissipated, gradually unfolding herself until she was comfortable. She was still curled up a bit when she fell asleep. Percy thought about warning her about germs beforehand, but he doubted that she cared.

With his friend asleep, he decided it was time to fix some things.

He threw the blankets back, moving slowly both to avoid waking Keyleth and to keep his vision from going black.

Getting to the door was a true feat, but he managed to get out to the hallway and down the stairs without passing out. Hey, maybe he was getting better.

The group was scattered about in the kitchen, eating, drinking, or cleaning weapons.

“Does someone want to explain what the fuck happened on that job today?” he put on his best intimidating voice despite how hoarse he probably sounded.

“What? What do you mean?” Vax was the first to speak.

“We killed some fuckin’ thieves, that’s what!” Grog announced proudly.

Vex shrugged. “It wasn’t anything special.”

“Right, well Keyleth came up to my room and had an anxiety attack earlier,” he said matter-of-factly. He took a seat at the table and sighed. When he looked up again, Vax was staring worriedly.

“Is she still in...?”

“Yes,” Percy said. “She’s asleep, but I think she’d appreciate...”

Vax was up and heading to the stairs. Percy didn’t try to stop him. Hopefully it’d help Keyleth to know for sure that at least one of them wasn’t pissed at her. He might have had a bit of a competitive streak with Vax, but the guy cared for his friend as much as he did.

“Vex, darling,” Percy sighed out. “What happened?”

She groaned. “Some unkind things may have been said, but she was- she was scrambled. It was getting annoying.” Vex sat across from him and frowned. “I didn’t realize that I’d upset her that much. She usually gets over it pretty fast!”

Percy didn’t want to get in the middle of it. Vex was just that way and he loved her for it. But Keyleth’s distress today was... more than usual. She could take a lot more than people gave her credit for, but then why was she so upset today?

The slow gears in Percy’s head turned slowly, until he let out a heavy breath. “That’s alright, I think I know what’s going on.”

“Do you need help back upstairs?” Vex asked, even though he was sure she knew the answer.

“...Possibly.”

When they got back to his room, Vax was sitting on the edge of the bed, talking to Keyleth who was sitting up and looking... yeah. He should have realized. Vax was in the middle of laying a hand on her forehead when Percy and Vex entered.

“She’s got a fever,” Vax informed sadly.

“I don’t, though,” Keyleth argued, but not too strong of an argument when she was obviously sick. And then her eyes caught onto Vex behind Percy, and Keyleth sat up. “Vex, I’m so-”

“No,” Vex sighed. “I’m sorry for yelling at you. We’ll... we’ll talk later, though. I think you both need rest.”

She nodded at her brother, who kissed Keyleth’s forehead and offered quiet well-wishes. “Get some sleep, love.”

When the twins were out, Percy got back into bed and turned to his friend. “I may have gotten involved,” he said apologetically.

Keyleth smiled. “That’s okay. I probably would have done the same.”

“I know you would. Come on.” He adjusted his pillow. “Let’s get some sleep.”
