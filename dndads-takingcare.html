Summary: Henry sees the Worried Dad side of Glenn
Word count: 1.1k

Taking Care

Of course Henry doesn’t expect to get a phone call in the middle of the night at all, but he certainly doesn’t expect one from Glenn.

Mercedes groans, stirs as Henry answers the phone, already going through the possibilities of why his friend is calling so late. Knowing Glenn, it can’t have been anything good. He just hopes the guy isn’t in any kind of danger.

“Hello?” says Henry, willing his voice not to shake with concern.

There’s a heavy exhale. “Hey Henry. Uh, what’s up?” The question would irritate anyone else, but Henry just can’t ignore the strange, new tone in his friend’s voice.

Henry blinks. “Well, I was sleeping pretty soundly before you called.” He slowly gets out of bed as to not disturb Mercedes further, makes his way through the kitchen and steps outside onto the deck. If the kids know he’s awake, they’re going to want to get up and goof around instead of going back to bed.

There’s a light (too light) chuckle on the other end. “Yeah, that’s my bad, man.”

And then silence.

Henry frowns. “What’s going on, buddy? Everything okay?”

“Actually uh,” Glenn swallows, and his voice is pinched as opposed to its usual ‘chill vibe’. “Nick is kinda sick. We’re at the emergency room right now. I don’t know why I-... I guess I just wanted to...” A sigh. “I don’t know, man.”

The hospital. No wonder Glenn sounds so terrible. Everyone knows that Glenn doesn’t do hospitals, even if no one really knows why.

Glenn and Nick have been fighting colds for the past week or two, but he has a feeling that that’s not exactly what Glenn’s referring to.

“He’s sick?”

There’s a sniff, a shaky breath. “He was, like, coughing a lot earlier? And he couldn’t sleep so I was just laying with him and we were watching dumb Youtube videos and stuff, kinda trying distract him or wear him out so he could fall asleep, y’know? But then he had this really bad coughing fit and he couldn’t fucking breathe and his lips were turning like, blue or something.”

Oh. Oh, poor Nick. And poor Glenn.

“I guess it’s pneumonia, but they said that it-” a humorless chuckle. “It could be worse. And I mean he’s finally getting some sleep, so...”

“Glenn...” Henry says quietly. “I’m so sorry. But he’s going to be alright?”

“Yeah, he’s on meds and oxygen and they just- hooked him up to all sorts of shit...” Another shaky breath, this one doesn’t quite stifle the sob he’s been holding back.

Henry makes his decision right then and there. Glenn doesn’t cry, nor show any kind of emotion at all, ever. He’s clearly not doing great. “I’m on my way. Okay, buddy?”

“Yeah, man.” No insistence that he’s fine, that he doesn’t need someone there right now. Glenn clears his throat. “I’ll meet you in the waiting room. They only let family back there at this time of night.” His voice is breaking, like he’s rushing to end the phone call so he can hold back the tears. Henry lets him.

“I’ll see you soon.”

Henry hangs up and takes a deep breath. Glenn needs... well, he’ll need food at some point, and he’ll probably demand coffee eventually. But right now, Henry will just bring himself. What Glenn really needs at this very moment is a hug.

“What’s wrong with Nick?”

Henry turned to the smaller voice to find Lark and Sparrow, staring sleepily but not any less interested than they usually were when they eavesdropped on his phone calls.

“Nick’s not feeling too great, but he’ll be alright.” Henry smiles rather apologetically as Mercedes makes her way through the two. “Sweetheart, I think I need to be with Glenn.” He says as she wraps an arm around him and nods.

“It sounds important,” she agrees. “Take care of him.”

“I will,” he says.

He instructs the boys to listen to their mother when she tells them to go back to bed. They roll their eyes, but do end up listening to Mercedes when she sleepily shoves them back to their bedroom.

-----

Henry steps through the automatic doors and immediately spots the pajama-clad man who looks more unkempt that usual. Glenn is leaned over, elbow on the armrest of the chair, chin propped on his palm, eyes closed.

At first, Henry’s afraid to bother him. This might be the only sleep he gets tonight. But that position can’t be comfortable, right?

He wakes Glenn rubbing his back and then pressing a kiss to his temple as he groggily remembers his surroundings.

“Hey,” he says, voice rough with crying and sleep and the cold that he’s still getting over.

“How’s Nick?”

“Still asleep,” Glenn says, rubbing his eyes. They’re red-rimmed, still a bit teary.

Henry’s heart hurts looking at him. Glenn is usually so closed off, impossibly to console because he ignores all of his negative feelings. It’s not healthy! Henry’s told him that time and time again.

“Thanks for coming, man.”

Henry continues to rub his back. “I’m glad you called me, Glenn! You need someone in times like this.”

“Ugh, don’t make this weird.”

“Gotcha.”

They spend a good couple of hours talking, and then sitting silently as the two of them get sleepy. They do kind of nap, though their necks are going to be horribly sore for the next couple of days.

Glenn practically forces himself awake the moment that visiting hours are back up. He takes Henry’s hand and squeezes it, a silent request for him to follow back to Nick’s room.

Henry’s never seen his friend like this before, jumpy and nervous and scared. Glenn’s normally a “chill dude” (as he calls himself) but this is something completely different. He forgets, sometimes, that Glenn is Nick’s father. Not his brother or his friend, but the man responsible for his upbringing and well being.

A terrifying thought for everyone, really.

Nick is awake when they walk in. There’s a nurse taking his vitals, who smiles at the two of them and wishes them a good morning.

Glenn’s at his son’s side the minute that she rolls her cart of the room.

Nick smiles sleepily, though it quickly disappears into a look of confusion when he sees Henry.

“Wha- hey?” He says, voice hoarse and hard to understand through the oxygen.

Henry waves. “Hi Nick! How are you feeling?”

Nick scoffs. “Shitty. But-” he glances at his dad, and looks down at his lap. “Better than last night.”

Glenn sighs out shakily. “Yeah, let’s uh, never do that again.”

“No way.”

Glenn still looks like he could sleep for hours, and so does Nick.

“I’m gonna leave you two to get some rest.” He gives Glenn a side-hug and finds that it lingers a bit as Glenn takes a while to let go. “Take care of yourselves, okay?”

“Definitely,” says Glenn. “See ya, Henry.”

Henry heads out with the knowledge that the two are probably going to need a little help with the taking care of themselves in the future.

And honestly? He’s alright with that.
