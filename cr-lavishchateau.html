Title: Lavish Chateau
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Marion is sick but Jester's visit makes things better
Word count: 700

Lavish Chateau

Jester was always excited to see her mom. She loved any moment where they could take the time to visit her. She practically bounced into the Chateau, clad in a human disguise, but the moment she got upstairs (leaving the others downstairs to drink), she dropped the spell.

Bluud sat up immediately, expression unchanging from the usual stoicism besides some raised eyebrows. "Miss Jester, it's nice to see you."

Jester giggled. "Hi, Bluud, can you tell my mom I'm here?"

He hesitated. "Of course, but..."

Jester cocks her head. "Oh, is she busy?"

"No, no your mother is ready for you." But he hesitated to open the door, and added, "She's not feeling well." Any smile disappeared from Jester's face. But the slightest smile could be seen on his. "She'll be happy to see you. Go on in." 

Jester opened the door more quietly than usual, and poked her head in. 

"Mama? It's Jesterrr!" She sing-songed.

The room was dim, curtains drawn and candles lit. She took a few steps in until she saw her mom bundled up in bed. She definitely looked sick, but her face lit up

"Little sapphire!"

Jester smiled sympathetically and didn't waste time climbing into her mom's bed with her. "You sound awful, mama," Jester said fondly. 

Her hair was braided out of her face, and there were bags under her eyes. Marion pressed the pretty handkerchief in her hand to her nose and sniffled a bit before giving her daughter a hug.

Marion laughed, and then coughed. "Oh, I know. I was supposed to sing tonight," she gave another self-deprecating chuckle, hugging Jester tighter like she never wanted to let her go. "I'm so glad you're here, Jester. I was, snf! Really missing you today."

Jester realized that her mother's sniffles weren't just from illness, but that she was starting to cry.

Marion pulled back, wiping at her eyes.

"Oh, mama! Don't cry!"

"I'm sorry, I don't know what's gotten into me."

"Do you want me to get you anything? Like food, or tea, or-"

Marion shook her head with a tearful smile. She cupped Jester's face and stroked her cheek with a thumb. "Nadine has been taking good care of me. I would just like to spend some time with you, is that alright?"

Jester quickly nodded. "Yeah, yeah, mama!"

Marion kissed her head and snuggled her. "Whenever you got sick as a little one, I wouldn't sleep. I wanted to keep you with me the whole time, keep you comforted and happy."

"Really? I don't remember that."

Marion hummed, sniffled. "You were very young, and quite fussy when you weren't feeling good. All I wanted was to help you feel better." She paused to cough into her kerchief. "Bluud would always remind me to get some rest, or find someone else to watch you. It was… very hard the first time. Trying to rest when I could hear you fussing in the other room…" her eyes started to water again, so Jester decided to interject.

This visit was supposed to make her mom feel better, not worse! 

"Mama… I was okay!"

"Oh, snf! I know, I know." Marion pulled the blankets up to cover both of them more thoroughly. 

Jester remembered the two of them couldn't be separated when one of them was sick. Her mom would do her best to keep her away when she wasn't feeling well for fear of spreading something to her daughter, but the maids and nannies couldn't do a thing to stop the little mischief from cuddling up to her mom

She might've broken a lot of rules, but it always made Marion feel better in the end.

The last time it happened was when Jester was a teenager. Her mother had brushed the fever off as nothing, but Jester could tell it wasn't nothing. Bluud let her in without a word, and she'd never seen her mother.look so relieved to see her. Until now, that is.

"Are your friends here?" 

"Yeah, they're downstairs having dinner." 

"Why don't you go join them? I'm sure you haven't eaten yet."

"Do I have to?" Jester said, sounding like a child again.

Marion gave a watery giggle. "No, you can stay right here with me if you wish."
