Right Here

Happy woke shivering.

If the fact that she was cold wasn’t a sign that something wasn’t right, she was suddenly aware of every other wrong thing in her body.

Everything ached. Her head was swimming. A fluttery feeling in her stomach warned her not to move too fast.

None of this would have been a problem if she could have just gone back to sleep. But, of course, that wasn’t happening.

Happy gave herself the freedom to whine as she flipped her pillow to the cool side. Her face felt hot, and yet she was still shivering.

Not good.

She curled up, stretched out, turned over... nothing helped. She wasn’t falling asleep any time soon.

With a heavy sigh, Happy sat up as slowly as she could without agitating either her head or her stomach. She sat on the edge of the bed, and stayed there for... well, she wasn’t sure how long. Time seemed to be going by a little differently. Eventually, she stood on shaky legs and grabbed a hoodie hanging on the bag of her door. The fabric rubbed against her oversensitive skin, making her wince until the thing was finally on and doing a terrible job of keeping her warm.

She wasn’t even sure what she was going to do. She was just sick of lying in bed.

Happy wandered into the kitchen, turned the light on, and gasped.

Apparently she was sick enough to forget that someone else lived there too.

“Sorry,” he winced. “The doc said that fluorescent lights suppress melatonin production, whatever that means, so I thought it was best to sit in the dark, y’know?”

“Cabe,” she breathed out. “What’re you doing up anyway?”

He shrugged and nodded toward the mug in his hands. “Warm milk. Couldn’t sleep.”

“Mm.”

Warm milk sounded good if she knew she could stomach it right now. But her entire body felt uncertain about everything.

“What about you, huh?”

Happy growled under her breath and got herself a glass of water. “I also couldn’t sleep.” she cleared her throat. “Headache.” It wasn’t a lie, just omitting the truth.

“Ah, sorry. Have you taken anything?”

“Not yet.”

She took a couple of sips of water and decided that was enough. Her stomach was feeling more unsteady by the second. Actually, her everything was feeling more unsteady by the second. Happy sighed and sat at the table across from Cabe.

It was a tiny table she originally had just for her before he ended up living there. She even had to pilfer an extra chair from the garage so the two of them could both sit together.

It was late, so they had no problem sitting in silence. Until an itch made itself known in the back of Happy’s throat. She turned away and coughed, and it was the perfect moment to find out that her throat was sore as well. Fantastic.

Cabe made a disapproving noise. “That’s not a headache.”

Happy shook her head. “I’m fine. Just feel a little weird.”

“Weird? You sound like hell.”

“I know, I know... ugh, God... we have a job tomorrow.” She buried her head in her hands and thought that maybe she’d just stay like that forever. It was comfortable enough, she was exhausted.

“Hey, kid, let me feel your forehead.”

Anyone else with that request would probably find themselves unconscious, but Cabe was different. She lowered her hands and leaned forward slightly as his calloused palm touched her forehead.

Cabe sat back and sighed. “Now, that’s a fever. Might wanna call the doc in the morning.”

“It’s a cold,” she muttered. “He’s got better stuff to do.”

Cabe shrugged. “Just saying.”

-----

When Happy woke up, it wasn’t the next morning but rather the next afternoon. She must have slept a lot. That was a good thing, right?

Wrong. The first thing she noticed was that she felt even worse. In the mere few hours, whatever plague she had was now thriving in her body. Her throat felt like needles and she couldn’t breathe through her nose. And she felt the perpetual need to cough, which just made tears form in her eyes from the pain and force of it.

Great. Fantastic. There was no way that she could go to work like this and be useful.

Happy huffed, coughed, and pulled her blankets up to her neck. Only then did she stop and realize that there were sounds coming from the kitchen. Cabe shouldn’t be home. No one should be in her apartment.

Despite her body’s relentless protests, Happy got up to investigate.

“Toby?” Oh God, her voice sounded terrible.

Toby Quinn was dancing around her kitchen in an apron that he definitely brought from home. Something was cooking on the stove, but her sense of smell was far too gone for her to know what it was.

Toby turned around and gasped. “What are you doing out of bed. Back! Go!” He tried to usher her away, but even in her weakened state she wasn’t about to budge.

“What are you doing here?” Happy asked after he gave up.

Toby sighed. “Cabe said you weren’t coming to work today, and I could tell that he was worried about you, which made me worry. And I asked if I should come check on you and he said yes so here I am! Will you please go back to bed? Your lunch is almost ready!”

Happy raised an eyebrow, speechless. “I... thanks?”

He smiled a little too proudly. “You’re welcome.”

“But I think I’m gonna camp on the couch. I’ve been in bed all day.”

“Fine fine, just go rest. You look like you’re about to keel over.”

He may have been right about that.

Happy sat on the couch, legs crossed under her. Soon, she found herself getting buried in pillows and blankets from her room, and then finding a bowl of soup in her hands.

Toby plopped down next to her and urged her to try it.

“Okay, I’m gonna warn you now, I’m not super hungry, so don’t take offense if I don’t eat this whole thing.”

Toby hummed thoughtfully. “Loss of appetite. Sounds like the flu.”

“Feels like the flu,” she grumbled. Still, she took a hesitant spoonful. It was good. Probably. “I can’t taste.”

“That’s okay, it’s got nutrients you need. Just eat a little bit more before you give up.”

Happy sighed and shrugged. She ate a little bit more before her stomach decided that was enough, and set it aside.

“You want me to leave?”

“Hell no,” she said, before she could even think of an answer that would preserve her dignity. Instead, she leaned against him. “I want you right here.”
