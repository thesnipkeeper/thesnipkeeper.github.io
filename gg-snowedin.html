Snowed In

Lorelai hummed contently.

These were the nights she loved the most. Everyone could say what they wanted about winter, but there was nothing more beautiful than a nighttime walk in gently falling snow. Especially on your handsome boyfriend’s arm.

A thin blanket of white already covered the park, the lights on the gazebo shining fuzzily through it. Lorelai couldn’t have been happier.

She smiled and held Luke’s arm a little tighter, cuddling up to him and pressing her cheek to his shoulder.

“It’s so nice out.”

“If you say so,” Luke muttered, but he had that adorable smile on his face that he was never good at hiding. He pulled his arm out of her grip and wrapped it around her shoulders instead. “Aren’t you cold?”

She scoffed. “No, of course not.” As if she’d even notice being cold when there was snow to enjoy! Ha! But Luke chuckled and nodded, instead of calling her an insane person or something else she’d expect. “Why?” she asked after a few seconds. “Are you?”

“No! No, I just thought you might want my coat or something.” But it took him a split-second too long to answer.

“Well, thank you but I do have my own coat.” If Lorelai was being honest, she probably would have been cold had it not been for Luke’s body heat. He was always a nice, big warm body to snuggle up to. Very convenient to have during winter.

“Right, right.” He cleared his throat.

Lorelai raised an eyebrow. Was he bitter about being dragged out here? He did complain, but he only complained loudly when he didn’t actually mind. The shutdown quietness was more of a concern.

“Luke,” she tried to keep her upbeat tone, despite being worried that he was upset. “We can go back inside. I’m sure we’ve seen enough snow for one day.”

“No, I like this.” He pressed a kiss to her head, and they went back to cuddling and walking and not a lot of speaking.

He sniffed, using the hand that wasn’t on her shoulder to scrub at his nose. Lorelai only heard a quick intake of breath before he muffled an earth-shattering sneeze into his glove. And then another. And... yep, there was the third one. Just like clockwork. Huh.

“Bless you.”

He nodded his faux thanks as he scrubbed at his nose a little more, effectively abusing the thing with the rough fabric of his dollar store gloves. The sniffles got a little more frequent after that, which wasn’t exactly unheard of since they were walking in cold weather.

Luke got quieter and quieter as they walked, and soon Lorelai made the decision. “Mm’kay, I’m ready to go back so you can make me hot chocolate and then judge me for the amount of whipped cream I assault it with.”

“Sounds good,” he agreed. “We’re gonna need marshmallows, though.”

Lorelai gasped and grabbed his arm. “Oh my God, you’re right, we’re out of marshmallows! Luke, you’ve saved the day! What would we have done without marshmallows?”

“Oh, the world would have ended,” he said dryly. “Should probably get extra chocolate chips, too, just in- just in cahhh...” Luke rubbed his nose vigorously, making a damp crackling noise that signified how runny it had been. He fully twisted away from her this time, muffling a pair of sneezes into his elbow. His poor nose needed a break, hopefully it was warm in the store.

“Bless you again,” she offered. “C’mon, maybe Doose’s has those chocolate-peanut butter chips.”

-----

Their shopping trip was more than successful, with all sorts of sweets that Lorelai begged her partner for. He reluctantly and fondly agreed to all of it.

The snow was falling much heavier by the time they got back to the house. The weather forecast called for snow, snow and more snow, and there was every kind of advisory out. Lorelai heard none of it, of course, because it was the first big snow of the winter and she felt nothing but joy over it.

She turned her nose up at the naysayers on TV and turned the thing off.

“Don’t forget the peanut butter!” she called as she excitedly jogged into the kitchen where Luke was stirring the chocolate-y goodness on the stove.

“If you add peanut butter to hot chocolate, then it’s not hot chocolate, it’s hot peanut butter!”

Lorelai scoffed. “Purist.” She leaned against the counter, happily watching him do all the work for something delicious. He wouldn’t let her help if she offered, so they came to an understanding years ago that Lorelai would remain the cooking spectator for eternity.

Luke sniffled and rubbed his nose on his shoulder. His brows knitted in annoyance, and then his face slackened as he let go of the spoon he’d been stirring with and twisted away from the stove. Luke sneezed three times in quick succession, and then a fourth when the first ones didn’t do a good job of dislodging the itch in his sinuses.

Lorelai frowned. He’d been a little sniffly since they got home, but so had she. She really just thought it was the cold weather. But now her shivers and sniffles had gone away, and Luke’s only seemed to have gotten worse.

His eyes were red and water when he recovered from the fit with a snf! His nose had already turned a shade of pink from the abuse it’d suffered.

“Honey, are you getting sick?”

Luke had just started to continue stirring when he looked up with a bemused expression.

“What? Why?”

“Well,” she crossed her arms over her chest and looked him over. “You just seem a little more tired than usual.” she waited, before adding “And you only ever sneeze more than once at a time when you’re sick.”

Luke blinked. “What? That’s not true...”

“It’s one-hundred percent true!” She insisted. “Rory can back me up on that! We used to count your sneezes at the diner to figure out whether you had a cold or allergies.”

“Oh my God,” he winced, feeling a little violated. “I’m fine. Get some mugs, the cocoa’s ready.”

-----

He’d resisted her idea of taking the stuff to bed with them (“You’re gonna spill it” “I would never be so careless with this liquid gold!”), but he gave in, and Lorelai decided that it was the best idea she’d ever had. The two of them got comfortable under the blankets, surrounded by the million pillows on their bed that Luke constantly argued they didn’t need.

The TV played a special Christmas episode for some kind of baking show, though neither of them really paid attention. Lorelai, because she was loving the hot chocolate too much (he agreed to the peanut butter, it was delicious) and Luke, because he’d been sniffling wetly and repeatedly since they laid down.

Lorelai wasn’t going to say anything, she wasn’t! There wasn’t a point to it. He’d just get grumpy.

And then Luke sneezed. Lorelai caught a glimpse of the clear snot that had gotten shot onto his upper lip. He didn’t seem to notice, however, because he had a cold, and therefore was about to sneeze again. His brows knitted together in annoyance at his uncooperative body, before his face went slack and his breath hitched. He looked at a loss of what to do, so Lorelai jumped in by setting her own cocoa aside and taking his. Just in time for him to catch the second sneeze, which sounded even snottier than the first.

Luke sniffled desperately, but didn’t dare remove both hands, keeping one to shield the sight from Lorelai while he searched for something to help with this situation.

“Wait, don’t move, I’ve got this,” Lorelai was reluctant, but left her warm cocoon to grab the tissues from the bathroom. The box felt light, but she didn’t worry too much about it. He looked at her thankfully as she climbed back into bed and placed the box between them.

He gave one long liquid snuffle before taking a tissue and blowing his nose, and then another one to layer over the first when one didn’t cut it. He finished by wiping his upper lip with a sniff, and Lorelai took the opportunity to press the back of her hand to his temple and neck.

“Lorelai...” he said with warning. “I’m fine.”

She hummed. “You’re not too warm.” She was really hoping for a fever, some reason for her to force him to accept that he’s properly sick.

“Yes,” he gave a slow nod, then a sniff. “That’s because I’m fine.”

“I mean, your whole face was Niagara Falls a couple minutes ago, so I don’t know if fine is the right word for it.”

He waved dismissively after abandoning the tissue wad on his nightstand. “Probably just need some sleep.”

He always said that, and he always woke up ten times sicker the next morning because that was just how colds worked. Especially Luke’s colds.

“If you say so. Here,” she went to hand him back his mug, but he shook his head.

“That’s alright, I think I’m done.” The throat clearing that followed had her suspecting a scratchy throat that he hadn’t admitted to, but she just shrugged and downed the remains of her own.

“Usually I’d jump at the chance to drink the rest of a poor, abandoned hot chocolate, but considering this one’s been sneezed in...”

Luke sighed heavily.

She chuckled and kissed his cheek. “I’ll go down and wash these.”

“Wait, what?”

She paused on her way out of bed. “What?”

“You’re doing dishes?”

“What? You always hate when we have dishes up here.”

“Yeah, because they attract bugs and get all gross,” he sniffed, dragged his hand across his nose.

“Well, I’m doing them!”

Luke narrowed his eyes. “You just want a snack, don’t you?”

“Doesn’t matter!” She announced.

Lorelai made her quick escape. She left the mugs by the sink to be dealt with tomorrow and began digging through the cabinets for something to satiate her nighttime hunger. A single pop tart would do, she decided. Luke hated when she left opened packs in the box, but she did it anyway. She wasn’t hungry enough for two, after all.

The world went dark.

The muffled voices of the TV upstairs was replace by the hissing wind outside, and the branches of a nearby tree bumping the window. Lorelai didn’t move.

“Uh... Luke?” She yelled.

It wasn’t long before she heard the thumping of Luke running downstairs, followed by a “God damn it” when he likely stubbed his toe on the banister yet again.

Lorelai was still frozen in fear when he found his way to the kitchen. She was so afraid, in fact, that she’d already eaten half the pop tart.

“The power went out.”

“No, duh,” she sighed.

The cold air had already begun to seep into the house without the luxury of the furnace. Fantastic. She didn’t get a chance to acknowledge it before Luke huffed and grabbed the flashlights from the kitchen junk drawer.

“Here,” he handed one to her. “I’m going to start a fire before we freeze to death.”

“Oh God...” Lorelai moaned. “Is that a possibility? Will Babette just find icicle corpses when she inevitably comes over to talk about the new cat?” She followed him as she blabbered on, gesticulating wildly and causing her flashlight to shine every which way as she did.

“Chances are that power’s out for the whole town, so Babette would also be an icicle corpse,” Luke deadpanned.

“Oh God!” she repeated.

Luke managed to get a nice fire going. If anything, this power outage gave them a reason to use the fireplace for the first time in a century.

Lorelai went to grab their blankets from the bedroom when it was decided that the two of them would be staying down there by the fire until the power went back on. She was determined to make it as comfortable as possible. She caught a glance at the tissue box on the bed, tangled in the blankets, and grabbed that as well. What a time for Luke to have a cold. What a time for Luke to have a cold that he actively denied.

Speaking of Luke’s cold, he muffled a tickly cough into the collar of his sweatshirt and sniffed. He saw her armful of blankets and comforters and immediately went to help, but not without, “I’m surprised you didn’t bring the mattress, too.”

“I’ll go back for that later. C’mon, we’ve gotta stay warm, right? We’ll be all nice and snuggly!”

“Mmm,” he acknowledged dryly, but he smiled as he gestured her toward the couch.

The two of them got situated with their respective blanket mountains.

“Honey, are you sure you don’t want the couch?”

“Stop it,” Luke scolded, as if that was the most ridiculous thing he’d ever heard. Darn these chivalrous men.

“Just asking!”

To his credit, he did look comfortable on the floor with his million blankets. Lorelai and Rory had accumulated plenty of blankets and throws over the years, a few of them being Hello Kitty and Barbie, of course. The house was well stocked for a town-wide sleepover when it came to blankets.

He would have looked more comfortable if he wasn’t so obviously sick. He sat with his back against the couch, sniffling just as bad as he had been when he was in bed.

Lorelai shrugged as she fixed the couch for herself. The tissue box fell out of the blankets in the process, so she made sure to toss them in his direction.

He hummed in acknowledgment once again, but didn’t put them to use quite yet. Of course not, he had pride and all that. And pride meant letting snot run down your face until you couldn’t take it anymore.

She sighed. She didn’t like this arrangement, sleeping in different places. It was too weird.

So, as soon as Lorelai got comfortable, she shook her head at herself and started throwing things down on the floor next to Luke.

Luke was in the process of rubbing his nose when he looked over with the classic ‘what the hell are you doing, Lorelai?’ look that he sported so well.

“What are you doing?”

“I wanted the floor too.”

“Well, I’m not taking the couch if you’re on the floor.”

Lorelai chuckled as she laid a blanket across the floor and made herself comfortable once again. She finished by pressing against his side and relishing his warmth. “I know. It’s important to share body heat, though. Hypothermia is no joke.”

He scoffed, but he clearly didn’t feel the need to argue against that. “Whatever.”

They sat quietly for a bit, mesmerized by the crackling fire in front of them. It was nice and warm, and the sound drowned the whistling wind outside.

The peace was interrupted when Luke tensed under her. “Lor-” and that was all he managed to get out before he sneezed. Lorelai got a glimpse of the slime that burst from his nose in the process, just before he pulled a tissue to his face and sneezed a second time. He winced after each one, as if it hurt his throat, or maybe his head. Maybe both... the guy didn’t look so good.

“Aw, Luke...” she cooed. “That sounded painful,” it was a lame attempt to get him to admit to something, which was why she was so surprised when he nodded.

 

His expression went cloudy just before a third sneeze tore through his throat. The mere pathetic-ness of it all had Lorelai petting his head and kissing his cheek as if that’s going to help the poor man feel better.

“Jesus Christ,” he muttered as he dragged the now-damp tissue across his nostrils and sniffed. The sniffling did nothing to rid his nose of the liquid that continued to creep out.

“Okay,” Lorelai sighed loudly. She sat up and patted her lap. “C’mon, get comfortable.”

Luke’s hesitation was adorable, because she knew he wanted to, and yet, “I don’t know if lying down is a good idea right now.”

“Just for a few minutes. I promise, I won’t be mad if you get snot on my twenty year-old pajamas. Please?”

That was all the convincing it took. Luke laid his head on her lap, a too-warm weight that reminded her to check his temp later when it wasn’t freezing outside of their blanket nest. He was adorably cuddly at the moment, and Lorelai wanted to take full advantage of it.

She began to massage his temples, and his face.

It was the same thing she did when Rory was four and had the worst sinus infection Lorelai had ever dealt with. The kid would cry every time she sneezed or coughed or moved, and eventually Lorelai had reached her breaking point and called the doctor in tears of her own, begging the man for something she can do to make her kid feel better and make it so at least one of them could get some sleep.

That was how she became the self-proclaimed sinus massage expert. And that title still held up, apparently, because Luke looked pretty blissed out at the moment.

His eyes were closed. His mouth was open as there was no other way to breathe right now, and his nose was wet with snot that would certainly start dripping out the minute he turned his head.

“ ‘s nice,” he muttered sleepily, eyes still closed.

“Is it helping?”

“Dunno. Sure feels good.”

Lorelai smiled. “You’re very cute, do you know that?”

“I’m gross.”

“Well, yes,” she moved her thumbs in circular motions under his heads. “But you do it cute. The whole snotty thing kinda works.”

He scoffed softly, which just succeeded in pushing watery goop out of his nose. His eyes finally opened, and he started to sit up to grab another tissue before Lorelai gently pushed him back down.

“I’ve got it.”

Luke’s breath hitched. He sat up again.

“Oh! Oh, time sensitive, gotcha!” She leaned over as far as she could to grab the box, and managed to pull one free rather easily before pressing it to his face.

Luke couldn’t have resisted if he tried. The sneezes came whether he had a tissue or not, and it just so happened that Lorelai was also an expert sneeze-catcher.

The gush of dampness hit Lorelai’s palm with both sneezes as he pitched forward. Luke was no longer blissed out, just annoyed at his body once again.

“Need to blow your nose?” She asked.

He glared at her over the tissue with watery eyes, and then did so, clearly desperate to rid his nose of the junk clogging it. When it was over, Lorelai balled up the tissue and tossed it elsewhere, to be dealt with when the lights came back on.

“Feel better?”

“Jesus, no,” and he does sound more congested than he had before, as if those sneezes brought the next phase of the cold into being. He sniffed and scrubbed at his nose with his hand. “Ugh... it still itches.”

“Need another tissue?” she didn’t wait for him to answer before she went to grab one, except...

Empty.

Well, damn.

“Okay, scratch that, there are no more.”

Luke was already gearing up for another sneeze, although he was actively denying it by abusing his poor nose. He sniffled urgently a few times, but doing so just exacerbated the tickle.

Lorelai pulled her hoodie sleeve over her hand and pressed the fabric to his nose.

“L- Lorelai-!” but he was powerless to stop it.

And yeah, her sleeve ended up a little slimy. So she used it to clean him up, and the took the thing off and wrapped herself in another blanket instead.

“Here,” she set the sweatshirt in his lap. He just looked at it strangely. “Use that.”

“Your Yale sweatshirt doesn’t deserve this,” he said, even as he pressed the thing to his nose. “We have toilet paper, towels...”

“Yes, but those aren’t within reach right now and I don’t wanna get up.”

Luke scoffed. “Yeah, me either.”

“C’mon, cuddle me more. You nice and warm.”

Luke complied, the sweatshirt still in his grasp. The two of them ended up lying down eventually, content and sniffly and warm.

She smiled as Luke’s breath evened out, and he started to snore. He was finally getting some rest. She hoped that he’d feel better in the morning, but that wasn’t likely. So, she just continued snuggling against her partner.

It was late, and Lorelai was almost asleep when she was assaulted by light, along with the sound of the furnace coming back on.

“Oh my God...” she breathed out in relief, sitting up to look at the newly-bright house. “Luke-!”

She stopped herself. He looked peaceful, exhausted, and sick. She couldn’t wake him, she just couldn’t. So instead, she laid back down. The lights could be turned off tomorrow, and she wasn’t about to go back upstairs when she had a warm, cuddly guy already comfortable next to her.

Lorelai went to sleep, knowing that in the morning, she’d get to see the town covered in snow.
