Royalty

Julia was the reason he was there in the first place.

"Come on, Q. It'll be fun!"

He didn't say no because he wasn't about to spend Christmas with his mom's family. Julia knew he needed to get away, and he knew Julia needed to get away. So there they were, settling into a surprisingly nice hotel room that Julia's family had scored for them. He sank onto the edge of the bed and admired how nice it was. It sure beat sitting in his room and being depressed, now he could do it in a fancy hotel.

"That's not even funny, Q," Julia warned when he said that out loud. She threw her suitcase on her bed and glanced at him with worry. Quentin looked lost. Just as lost as he had been. Julia made the decision to not let him leave her sight the entire trip, it may have been obsessive but she couldn’t stand the thought of something happening while she wasn’t paying attention. She flopped onto his bed and yanked him down with her so he was lying on her stomach. "This will good for both of us. We'll go to museums, go shopping, do… tourist-y things. You know?"

Quentin's mouth quirked up in a smile. "You're right, Jules. I really appreciate this, just don’t expect me to…" He didn’t need to finish it. Julia had learned back in high school that Quentin's depression couldn’t just be fixed with a good time. But a good time could certainly help, to offer a distraction. And Quentin owed it to her to at least try.

She pressed a kiss to his cheek. "How about we finish unpacking and then go out for dinner? Maybe even explore a little bit."

Damn her knowing his weakness. "Sounds good."

They managed to unpack, sharing drawer space as efficiently as possible and Julia cracking some jokes about him bringing the first Fillory book with him. ("I almost forgot that you were a nerd for a second" "Well, I can't let that happen, can I?") And then they walked out of the hotel, and walked in any direction. They probably could have rented a car or called a car service, but the two of them found it was so much more fun to walk and get lost in it all.

"This," Julia picked a hat from one of the craft market stand they were browsing, "is the hat for you."

Quentin looked in the mirror. "A fedora. Very funny, haha." Julia smiled proudly. "Hey, I'm gonna go sit over there for a while and read," he gestured toward a bench that looked terribly cold. It was winter, there was snow everywhere, but if he wanted to read she wasn’t going to stop her. He was probably getting tired.

"Okay, I'll finish up here and we can go back?"

"Yeah, sure."

She understood that he needed space, and Quentin was thankful that he was able to retreat without question. He had his destination in sight and headed toward the bench without a second thought, already pulling out a paperback version of Fillory and Further, because he didn’t trust himself not to lose the original book he was so attached to.

A warranted concern, apparently.

Suddenly, someone knocked into him. Quentin gasped as his book fell into a pile of snow, and the stranger didn’t even bother looking back. "Hey!" he yelled. Because yelling at strangers with their hoods up in a strange country was always a great plan, right? He didn’t care, because his book was probably ruined and he wanted to read that on this trip!

Quentin was never known for his self-preservation instincts, and he realized it when he chased the stranger into an albeit kind of hipster-y alley. The stranger looked back and found he was being followed, so he stopped.

"You ruined my book," Quentin accused, holding up the evidence that was soaked pages and running ink.

The stranger furrowed his brows at him, looked him over, and then removed his hood. Quentin tried not to swoon over the douchebag that just fucked his book, but he was tall, and handsome. He could only hope the man had a shitty personality, but not so shitty that he was a serial killer.

"You should have held onto it better." He was in the process of catching his breath, but still managed a charming smile that made Quentin want to punch him in the face.

Quentin blinked. "Excuse me?"

And just like that, the stranger walked off to some dark corner Quentin wasn’t about to follow him into. He sighed, frowning at the sad state of his book. Oh well, there were other Fillory books out there. He walked off in another direction than he came, deciding a little exploring would be more therapeutic anyway.

"Hey, nerd boy."

Quentin couldn’t believe he actually responded to that name. But there stood the stranger and a brand new Fillory and Further book one. He gaped as it was pressed into his hands. "Oh… thanks."

"Yes, well, I wouldn’t want to have someone's dead book on my conscience. A friend of mine loves these books, and she'd kill me if she found out I was responsible for… well, you're welcome, I guess." He smiled.

Quentin took a closer look at the book. "Wait this…" the cover was quality, definitely not a paperback. "This is a collector's edition from the rerelease in '82. This had to have cost-" Just holding something so nice and valuable made his heart race and his palms sweaty, so he shoved it back at the handsome man who accepted it with an oof. "I can't accept this."

He looked at Quentin, confused. "But you- I mean, I should do something. I totally ran into you like a neanderthal."

"It's- it's okay."

The stranger chuckled. "No, it's not, or you wouldn’t have chased me down. Look, you won't take the book so at least let me repay you with a nice cup of coffee. I know a good café."

Quentin swallowed. Coffee sounded great. Coffee with this hot guy sounded great. But he was a stranger in a foreign country and Quentin was told to be careful about impulse decisions. "I'm not great with spontaneous plans."

"Alright," another lazy but charming smile. "How about dinner tonight. Less spontaneous."

Quentin swallowed. "Sure."

And that date was all that he could think about for the rest of the day.

"He asked you out?" Julia was more excited about it than he was. "Are you going to go?"

"I don’t know, Jules! He's… and I'm… ugh, you know me. How do I know this date isn't going to end in a total disaster?"

Much to his offense, Julia laughed. "You don’t. But some people are worth the risk, so…" she took a bite of her pastry. "Is he?"

-----

"He should've been here by now…" Quentin looked around anxiously, his mystery man nowhere in sight.

Julia rubbed his arm. "Sorry, Q."

They were about to walk back to the hotel when a tall figure came towards them. "So sorry I'm late. There was some… family business I had to tend to. Hope I'm not too late?"

"Uh-"

"Nope!" Julia answered before Quentin could get words out of his mouth. "You two go ahead."

He looked at her with a raised eyebrow and Quentin quickly stepped in. "Uh, this is my friend Julia."

"Ah, here to make sure I'm safe?"

"Yes," Julia said unapologetically. "But you seem alright to me." she sent a wry glance in Quentin's direction. "Have fun, you two." Quentin desperately wanted her to come with them, but he knew it would be weird to bring a friend on a date, so he let her walk off with plans to text her updates if he decided he wanted out. Which he probably wouldn’t, because wow this guy looked even better.

He wore a vest and dress shirt that hugged his waist nicely, and a long coat on top of that. Quentin almost felt underdressed. Their eyes caught as Quentin was checking him out, and his date grinned knowingly before gesturing ahead of them. "Restaurant's this way."

So they went that way.

"The name's Eliot, by the way."

Quentin shook the proffered hand, feeling surprisingly rough skin for such a delicately-dressed person. "Oh, I'm Quentin."

"Quentin," Eliot repeated. "That's a nice name."

Well, Quentin's heart nearly stopped right there.

-----

The host at the restaurant went pale as soon as they walked in. Quentin's anxiety went up the wall.

"Y-you can sit anywhere, your h- um, Eliot." so they did.

The meal was great, the coffee even better. Eliot grinned. "Thank you for agreeing to dinner. This is fun." Even as he said it, he looked a mile away. There was something sad in his eyes, not obvious like Quentin's. Pained, deep, like a sadness he couldn’t escape. Quentin wondered if the two of them were in the same boat.

"Are you okay?"

Eliot looked up from his dessert and gave a shy smile as if he'd been found out. "Oh yes. Just had a… stressful meeting earlier."

"The family business you had?"

Eliot nodded. "Had a," he cleared his throat, "fun conversation with my mother, but it's all over now."

"Family, huh?"

"You can say that again. She doesn’t understand a lot about me, or my wishes. Just wants me to be my father."

"I hear you, except my mom would do anything to keep me from being my father."

Eliot chuckled. "Family."

"Mhm."

"So! What brings you here?"

Quentin's eyes went wide. He wasn’t ready to talk about what brought him there, specifically. "My friend Julia thought that we needed to get away for the holidays. I'm along for the ride."

"Getting away, that sounds nice," He said whimsically, propping his chin on his hand. "How do you like this boring little country so far?"

"It's beautiful."

Eliot raised an eyebrow. "Really?" He said with disbelief.

Quentin learned that Eliot had a little more to him than just his looks. He was sweet underneath all the sarcasm, and he knew how to take charge without being a dick. Quentin was in love. After the date, Eliot and Quentin wandered outside. Eliot had offered to walk him to the hotel, and Quentin quickly accepted.

"So when can we do this again?" Eliot asked with the same lazy grin that made Quentin weak at the knees.

"I'm… not sure. Julia has a lot of things planned, so I don't exactly…"

"Why don’t I check in with you tomorrow, then?"

Quentin smiled. "Sure."

-----

"Quentin!" He woke up to Julia calling his name from the door. She wore a robe, her hair stringy and damp as she stood in the doorway. "Did you order room service?"

"No?" He rubbed his eyes, padding to the door tiredly.

The man smiled. "This is a special gift," he handed Quentin a card with his name on it, and Quentin knew exactly who it was from.

"O-oh. Thank you." He opened the envelope and read the contents. "if you're free at ten or any time after, let my friend Rafe know and he will organize a car for you. Join me on an adventure. Sincerely - Eliot"

Julia's eyes were wide. "Oh my God. Q, you have to go."

"No, I don’t," he waved his hand. "I don’t even really know this guy."

"Didn’t you say that you've never bonded with someone so fast? Q…"

"I don’t know… I have to think about it."

"What is there to think about?" Julia said, sitting at the table. "He sent food."

She had a point.

-----

"I know it sounds stupid, but I'd always wanted to be an author. The Fillory books… they changed my life, and I want to do that for someone someday."

Eliot hummed softly in acknowledgement. He had such a way of looking interested and lost at the same time. "Quentin, don't take this the wrong way, but that sounds nerdy as fuck."

Quentin snorted. "You sound like Julia."

He shrugged. "But I support anyone's artistic endeavors. I've heard the Fillory books are good."

Quentin stopped. "Wait a minute," he grabbed Eliot's arm. "you've never read them? Eliot, what the hell?"

"Does it really surprise you that much?"

"I mean, you seem all… cultured and shit."

"Oh, thank you. I definitely try to look cultured and shit. But reading is kinda boring." Quentin kind of wanted to cry, but he ended up laughing instead. What a strange person to fall in love with.

They walked down the trail, stopping at a snow-dusted bridge. The snow was falling lightly and slowly, looking like powdered sugar covering the ground. For once, Quentin didn’t mind the cold. He could stay out here all day if it meant being with Eliot.

"So, what's your story?" Eliot asked, leaning over the bridge in a way that made Quentin force his eyes up.

"My story?"

"Well, I don’t mean to pry but- actually, who am I kidding? I always mean to pry. Something brought you and Julia here. This," he waved a finger at Quentin. "This isn't just a vacation. It's an escape."

Escape. The words hit Quentin hard, because he knew it was true. All he'd ever wanted was an escape so yeah, sure, he agreed to go on a long trip with Julia when she asked, because he could run away from his problems for a while, ignore anything in the real world. Now the real world was crashing on him fast. He really hated thinking.

"Jesus, sorry…" Eliot's eyebrows furrowed in worry and he quickly stood up and rushed to Quentin. "I didn’t mean to- I… are you okay?"

Quentin took a deep breath and had only realized that he was having trouble breathing at all. He wiped the building tears from his eyes and felt a rush of humiliation. Eliot's hands hovered around him as if he didn’t know how to fix whatever was happening, and Quentin found it adorable.

"Fuck," Quentin sniffled, and huffed out a self-deprecating chuckle. "Sorry. Uh, heh, my story…"

"Oh God, you don’t have to answer-"

"My dad died."

Eliot's lips formed a thin line. His hands returned to his sides and he looked at Quentin with all the sympathy in the world. "That's… that's awful. Fuck."

Quentin shrugged even though it wasn’t a shrugging matter. "A month ago. Julia was worried about me, and she had her own stuff to get away from, so she organized this whole trip. This is a lot of baggage for a date, huh?"

"No fucking way. You don’t know what kind of dates I've been on."

Before Quentin knew it, he had strong arms wrapped around him, and his face pressed into Eliot's chest. He smelled good, different, expensive. He hadn't been hugged like that in a long time.

And then a phone start ringing.

"Fuck." Eliot swore to himself before having to pull away and answer his phone. "Oh, I lost track of time. I'll be there as soon as I can." He sighed as he hung up.

"Ah, the real world." Quentin smiled.

"Yes, I'm afraid so. I need to go. Thank you for this, Quentin. I hope to see you again very soon."

They were touring the museum on the history of the country. Quentin and Julia, both nerds in their own right, loved it to death. They spent the whole afternoon looking around and learning new facts. In the end of the museum, was a big room with the history of the royal family. Quentin saw old items that belonged to the ancestors. Julia was already looking at the portraits, she was a fast reader than he was.

"Q, come here."

"Why?" he didn’t bother looking up from the antique hairbrush in front of him.

"You may want to see this."

So he went over to her, and found himself in front of a very familiar face. "Eliot?"

The elegant picture hanged on the wall, taking up plenty of room next to his parents' portraits. He looked fierce in the photo, annoyed. It was a good look.

-----

Eliot pulled up to the hotel in a normal-looking car. "Hey, ready to go?"

"Absolutely, your highness," Quentin was proud of himself for saying it without missing a beat.

"Great, I-" Eliot's eyes widened. "You know."

"Julia and I went to the museum today."

He sighed. "The portraits."

"Yeah, the portraits."

Quentin felt betrayed, stupid, ridiculous. He shared things with this man, he cried in front of him, and none of it was real. He couldn’t believe he let himself get tricked like that. Still, he let Eliot lead him to a closed part of the hotel and explain to him what was going on.

"The real you?" Quentin scoffed. "This isn't the real you. Even when you weren't royalty to me, you were still hiding parts of your personality."

Eliot swallowed. "I know."

"I want to know who you really are, Eliot."

"Then you will. Just give me another chance, alright?"

Quentin did.

"An apology date?"

"An apology invitation." Quentin confirmed. He was throwing clothes allover his bed looking for something to wear, something that didn’t make him look like the depressed doofus he was. Alas, there was nothing. "At the fucking castle. Why don’t I have any-"

"Q, Q…" hands on his shoulders steadied him. Julia looked into his eyes, looking worried herself. "Calm down, alright? It'll be fine. It's just a party."

"A royal party, Jules. And I have nothing to wear to a royal party. Why did I accept?!"

"Jesus okay, let's just go buy something."

Quentin hated that he didn't have a choice. He hated buying clothes, but he kind of had to. Or so he thought.

There was a knock on the door, and Quentin opened it to Rafe.

"His royal highness felt you may need some help preparing for the party this evening." Was all he said before wheeling in a rack full of nice tuxes.

"Holy shit," Julia gaped. "He's a keeper."

And with Julia's help Quentin managed to find the perfect tux. They all fit him perfectly, he found out. He wondered where they came from, but they looked expensive so maybe he didn't want to know. But as it led up to the time for the car to come, Quentin waiting anxiously at the hotel entrance, he could feel his head threatening to conk out on him.

"Q," Julia said softly, rubbing his back. "I know I've been encouraging you and stuff, but you don’t have to go if you don’t want to. You know that, right?"

"Yeah, I know," Quentin breathed out. "It's fine. It'll be fine."

Julia smiled and patted his shoulder. "Have fun. Looks like your ride's here!"

Quentin took one deep breath and ventured out to the fancy car before him. The hotel staff recognized the royal car and hovered until he was safely inside. The driver greeted him and then started for the castle.

Eliot was descending a grand staircase to the left when Quentin walked in. His eyes locked onto him, the only familiar thing in range that could ease his anxiety just a little.

Eliot's usual stone look morphed into a smile as soon as he saw Quentin. He looked him over, eyes scanning Quentin slowly, looking quite thirsty enough for Quentin to blush. His smile turned to a smirk.

"I knew you'd pick that one."

Quentin scoffed. "I wish you would've told me. It took Julia and I an hour to decide."

Eliot chuckled. "You look... Ssssuper hot. Also, why don't you give this nice man your coat?"

Sure enough, there was a castle staff looking anxious to take Quentin's coat, as if his entire job relied on it. "Oh, sorry, yeah," he shrugged off his coat with the help of Eliot who seemed to have no problem helping him remove a piece of clothing. "I uh, I've never been to something so formal before," Quentin admitted.

"Ah, a learning experience then." Eliot must have caught sight of Quentin's anxiety, because he added, "You'll be fine. As long as my mother doesn’t-"

"Eliot, are you going to introduce me to your guest?"

Eliot's eyes widened bigger than Quentin ever thought possible. An elegantly dressed woman descended the same staircase.

"Mother, this is Quentin. Quentin, this is my mother. The queen."

Quentin stammered for a good while deciding how to greet a queen. She finally allowed him a handshake.

Dinner was brutal and awkward. The first conversation topic was the upcoming coronation of which Quentin knew nothing about. He looked at Eliot when his name was mentioned along with the word 'king'. Eliot offered a sheepish smile.

"Perhaps you all can settle something for me," Eliot chimed into the conversation for the first time that night. Everyone looked at him eagerly. "What is your opinion on a coronation opened to the public?"

Eyes went from Eliot to the queen. She looked livid. Quentin wanted to sink away.

"I should've known better than to throw you into that garbage fire of a conversation."

Quentin nodded a thanks as Eliot helped him into his coat. "It's fine, I just… I think I made a bad impression."

Before Eliot could agree with him (probably) a woman came running up to the, as fast as she could in her heels. She had been sitting on the other side of the table, mouthing off at the queen like it was her job. Quentin couldn’t believe it, no one else dared to say something out of turn, but this woman was all for it.

"You're the Quentin I've heard so much about," She said, looking him up and down. "Nice."

"Be good, Bambi."

"Oh, I'll be good. He had the balls to say that royalty was obsolete and then compared this country to Fillory." She winked at Quentin. "Couldn’t have said it better myself.

Eliot sighed, but didn’t exactly look inconvenienced by her presence. "Quentin, I'd like you to meet my friend Margo. She's the one who reads all those books I tell you about."

"Oh! Hello!"

"Margo is the only reason I'm still living in this God forsaken castle. That's why my mother keeps her around."

Quentin was beginning to put all the pieces together. Eliot wasn’t just some straight-forward prince, and he didn’t seem excited to become a king.

"Okay, well there's no way everyone hated you."

Still, she flopped on the bed next to him, still in her coat having returned minutes after he did. "He's really hot though."

Quentin scoffed. "He's so hot."

Suddenly it was midnight and he'd been staring at their window from bed for two hours. Julia was fast asleep - she never had trouble falling asleep and Quentin always envied her for it. He hadn't been able to sleep. He was too busy thinking about the events at the party, and the jetlag wasn’t helping either. The country was six hours ahead of their own and it was certainly taking a toll on his awareness and - though he wasn’t going to tell Julia - his mental health. Sleeping eased his mind at the end of the day. If he didn’t have that, what was he supposed to do?

Thump.

A strange sound on the window. Possibly a bird?

Thump.

Quentin furrowed his brows and sat up in bed to get a good look. Two round spots of snow stuck to the window.

Thump.

He crawled out of bed, curiosity getting the better of him, and looked out the window. Eliot? Quentin opened the window, finding it old-fashioned enough to swing open.

"Let's go for a walk." Was the first thing Eliot said. He was in more casual clothes than the party, and bundled up for once.

Quentin couldn’t stop an excited smile. He felt like he was in high school again, sneaking out. But his smile soon disappeared when the events of the night became clear in his head once again. "Are you sure?"

Eliot's brows furrowed in confusion. "Of course I'm sure."

"I… cause a lot of trouble for you."

Quentin chuckled. "Give me a minute."

"Sorry I forgot to call you after the party."

"Kidnapping me is much better, anyway." Quentin joked. He held a hot chocolate in his hands from the late-night coffee shop around the corner. It was mostly inhabited by half-dead university students, which made it all the more funny when their prince strolled in and asked for two hot chocolates.

"I just want you to know that I- um," he cleared his throat. "Well, it was fun."

"Fun," Quentin said dryly. "That's one word for it."

"Look, my mother can be abrasive. Actually no, she's a complete bitch to anyone I date who isn't also royalty."

Date. Quentin liked that word.

Eliot smiled fondly. "I like being with you, though. I get to be myself. Not… future King Eliot."

"You'll be a great king, Eliot."

"I'm glad you think so. Everyone expects me to be like my father."

"You don’t like that, huh?" Quentin cleared his throat and took another sip of his hot drink.

"He was an asshole."

"And people… liked him?"

Eliot chuckled. "He was an asshole to me. He was a great king, apparently. But the man hated my guts and he was constantly threatening to disown me. As if that stopped me from doing anything. Can I tell you a secret?"

Quentin muffled a cough into the crook of his arm before looking up.

Eliot's mouth quirked in a strange smile Quentin had never seen before. "I was almost relieved when he died. No more fights, no more anger, no more being told I wasn’t good enough. It sounds selfish, I know. But… Jesus, we hated each other. My mother might not be great, but at least she can stand to be in a room with me without telling me off for looking too uninterested."

"Oh. Shit." Quentin looked straight ahead, not daring to look at his date. "Sorry, Eliot."

"Oh, it's okay. It happened three years ago."

Quentin cleared his throat once more, and found that the cold air was making his nose run. He wiped it with his glove and took another sip of his hot chocolate before stuffing his freezing hands into his pockets. He wanted to keep them there, but another itch in his throat begged his attention and he stifled an itchy cough.

"You okay?"

"Hm? Oh, yeah yeah, just cold."

Eliot hummed thoughtfully. "I should get you back before you get hypothermia or something. Can't have that on my conscience!"

Quentin desperately didn’t want to go back to the hotel just to lie awake for another night, but he knew he should.

This is vacation." he tried to sound enthusiastic, but his voice cracked at the end because why wouldn’t it fuck him over one last time?

Quentin did manage to nap for a little while before he was woken by a knock at the door. He pulled himself out of bed, hoping whoever it was could handle the sight of his wrinkled pajamas. His shirt probably had dried snot in it by now, but he couldn't be bothered to look.

A familiar face peeked in.

Quentin snorted. "Thank you. I'm fine."

Eliot looked reluctant, like he wanted to stay all day. "I... Need to go. There's a meeting at the castle about the coronation and..."

"Go on, be a prince," Quentin smiles proudly.

"O-okay. Jules and I were gonna get dinner..."

Eliot was proud of himself.

-----

Days passed, Eliot leaving Quentin a couple of days to rest and spend time with Julia.

"So, Margo is his cousin?"

"Distant cousin, I guess?" Quentin thanked the person at the counter and took his plate, a delectable slice of cake he couldn't pass up. He and Julia find a place to sit down.

"She's kinda hot."

Quentin glanced up from his cake, eyebrow quirked up. "Excuse me?"

Julia shrugged. She glanced around, taking in the cute little bakery. They'd been meaning to visit it for a while, but Quentin insisted they wait until he had his sense of taste back. Julia smiled fondly, but something caught her attention. The local paper.

"Prince Eliot dating tourist?"

"Q… look."

Quentin followed her gaze to the paper stand, and his eyes widened in horror. "What the fuck."

They fled back to the hotel room, and Quentin nearly burned a hole in the floor he was pacing so much.

"I can't believe this. Oh God, Julia, what if-"

"No what if's, not right now."

-----

Quentin watched Eliot pull out a fancy handkerchief and wipe his prominent nose for the third time since they started their walk. It wasn’t nearly as cold out tonight, the snow melting and turning to mush.

"Did you catch my cold?"

"Hm? Oh, no no, I just…" Eliot cleared his throat, wincing as he did. "It's just cold out."

"Not… really."

The next day Eliot's nose was a shade of red Quentin never thought possible. His voice was hoarse (because a cold wasn't going to stop the man from talking if he wanted to talk) and he was wearing an extra layer. He offered to take Quentin and Julia out for dinner to apologize for the paparazzi.

"You must be Julia," Margo had been invited along, and definitely not because Quentin told Eliot he should bring her because he's definitely not one to meddle in people's lives. But Julia was there in the first place because of her bad breakup with James, so…

Julia looked at her like she'd never seen anything more beautiful in her entire life. It was adorable.

Their food arrived and it looked amazing. Quentin wondered what it would look like if they hadn't arrived with the prince, but he'd take it. They all began to dig in when Eliot turned away to sneeze.

Margo rubbed his arm absently before taking a bit of her pasta.

"Eliot," Quentin said.

The rest of Eliot's face was hidden by another handkerchief as he looked up. "Hm?"

"You okay?"

"I'm fantas-" a cough interrupted him, and the other three exchanged looks.

"El's fighting a cold. Can't begin to imagine where he picked it up from," Margo said with a wry look in Quentin's direction.

"It's nothing… ahem. Why don’t we just eat? Everything looks amazing."

Quentin and Julia exchanged looks when dinner started coming to a close. He'd received a phone call that morning from his mother, asking him what the hell he was doing in Europe with a prince. She also had a request (order) for him. Quentin knew he needed to tell Eliot, Julia knew he needed to tell Eliot. But… God, he didn’t want to.

-----

For the first time since Quentin met him, Eliot was crying. Unshed tears rested in his eyes, looking unbelievably hurt and upset. He sniffled, and this time it probably wasn’t from his cold, and swiped at his nose with the back of his hand.

"You're leaving?"

Quentin felt his own tears running down his cheeks. He hadn't even realized he was crying. "I have to. I'm sorry. My mom wants me to pack up dad's things and-" he sniffled.

"When?"

Quentin could hardly stand to look at him. "Two days. Day of your coronation."

Eliot's bottom lip quivered, but he refused to break down. Maybe he really was ready to be king.

"If I had a choice… "

"I know," Eliot said with another sniffle. "I just… I don’t know what I'm going to do without you."

Three weeks. Was it possible to fall in love in three weeks? Apparently so. Quentin choked out an ugly, "I'll miss you, El." Eliot bit back any sob or whimper and wrapped his arms around Quentin instead. He could smell that same scent on Eliot's clothes as he pressed his nose in his chest. Quentin wanted to remember. He never wanted to forget these amazing few weeks.

"You're the reason I'm crying for the first time in years," Eliot said with a sniffle.

They stayed that way for a good while, Eliot pressing soft kisses into Quentin's hair, Quentin listening to his breathing and heartbeat. Eliot's breath hitched, and suddenly he was pulling back to cough away from Quentin.

They looked at each other, and started laughing despite themselves. Eliot cleared his throat and swiped at his nose one more time. The crying had seemed to make his runny nose so much worse. He patted his pockets for his handkerchief, but Quentin nudged him with the tissue box before he could find it. Eliot nodded his thanks.

"You'll have that to remember me by, huh?"

Eliot chuckled before blowing his nose. "Guess so." was the congested reply.

They ended up cuddling in Quentin's bed, almost a last moment of closeness before they're separated. Eliot started shivering near evening time, eventually climbing under the hotel duvet to warm up. Quentin frowned before placing a hand on his forehead.

"I think you have a fever. Shit, are we sure this is what I had?"

Eliot smiled sleepily before nuzzling a pillow. "I always run hot when I'm sick. It's fine, really." still, he was coughing an awful lot, and struggled to breathe through his nose while lying down.

Julia and Margo returned soon after with a few shopping bags. They were giggling and joking around, and Quentin was so thankful that Julia found someone too. Eliot was fast asleep, cuddling Quentin and snoring congestedly. Margo aw'ed like he was a kid.

-----

Quentin stepped out of the car breathing that familiar air. Everything looked different when it wasn’t covered in snow. He saw the hotel he and Julia had spent so much time at two months before. Speaking of Julia…

"Where are you going?"

Julia had her purse and nothing else on her shoulder. After their last stay, the hotel insisted they handle all of their luggage and such, so they were free to roam.

"Oh, just gonna get a coffee. Go do your thing," she said as she waved him off. So he did.

Pictures of Eliot in his crown were all over, the country still celebrating their new kind. Quentin was reminded how much Eliot fought the title at first, and now he looked like he was born for it. Well, he was.

It was February now. Cool out, but snowless and easy to get around.

"Excuse me?" Said an unamused royal employee at the castle.

"I'd like to see the king?" Quentin replied, less confident than before. He should've thought this through.

The woman chuckled, muttering an "Americans..." Under her breath. "One cannot just show up and visit King Eliot. He's got more important things than-"

"Yes, but they're so much more boring." The familiar voice made Quentin's heart stop. The two of them turned and saw the king himself descending the grand staircase. Eliot looked exactly the same, save for slightly longer hair and even nicer clothes. He grinned like he knew how well he looked. "Quentin here has permission to show up whenever he wants. Understand?"

"But majesty-"

"You're dismissed."

She looked like she was about to argue, but didn't dare. As soon as she was out of sight, Eliot looked down at Quentin, eyes full of love.

"You're... Here."

"Yeah. Yeah, I am."

"Your dad's-?"

"Everything's taken care of. My mom is appeased." He didn’t bother to mention that going through his father's things was the hardest thing he'd ever had to do. Long nights going through box after box, Julia calling and visiting to check on him. He could tell how worried she was, and he tried so hard not to worry her.

It ended with her getting a call from him in the middle of the night telling her that he was scared and he didn’t want to be alone.

Julia stayed with him the rest of the night, the two of them sleeping in the living room of his dad's house.

-----

Much to Quentin's relief, they didn't stay in the palace to talk, and Eliot enjoyed having an excuse to get out of there for a little bit. He looked worn, but happy.

"I should've told you I was coming, but... I dunno, guess wanted to surprise you."

Eliot snorted. "Mission accomplished."

"How's things been?"

"Good, good. We've got a charity foundation started along with other members of royal families. My mother still thinks I'm incompetent but you know, I don't care that much now that I'm a king." He grinned proudly and unapologetically.

"Wow, that's great, El."

"What about you? What have you been up to?"

Quentin swallowed.

-----

Quentin linked his hand to Eliot's arm. It wasn't as... Intimate as he had hoped for, but it was better than nothing after two months of missing him.

"I really think I can make a difference in this godforsaken country."

"Don't let your citizens hear you say that."

"Never. But any population that has faith in me deserves some credit for my success."

The arrive at side-doors of the palace, the closest doors to the dining room and least-guarded of all areas. Eliot swallowed, suddenly looking nervous. He turned the door handle and looked at it sheepishly, hesitating. "Listen, Quentin... There's one more thing I haven't mentioned, but-"

Quentin's anxiety spiked. "What is it? What are you-?"

Just then, a cheerful little voice greeted them in their entrance. "There you are! We've been wondering when you'd get back."

She was small, long brown hair in intricate braids. Her clothes were casual enough for the palace, and she grinned with an adorable smile when she saw them. She was currently seated at the small dining table with several papers in front of her, but she quickly stood to rush to them.

"Uh," Eliot cleared his throat. "Quentin, meet Fen."

Quentin shook her hand before he knew what was happening.

"My... Fiancee."

Quentin froze.

Everything froze.

"Your... I-"

The adorable smile on her face disappeared. "Oh no, you didn't tell him? Eliot, you said-"

"I know, I know... Quentin, let's go talk, alright?"

Quentin tried to swallow the anger rising in his throat, he really did. "Talk? We've been talking all day and you didn't bother to mention that you were getting married. What the fuck, Eliot."

Fen retreated back to her paperwork, while Quentin let himself be led to a courtyard.

"I didn't have a choice."

"What do you mean? You could have told me. What did you expect me to-?" Quentin sighed.

Eliot sighed and turned to look him in the eyes. "Look, it's not like she and I are in love."

"You're getting married."

"Yes, and it was arranged by my mother to create an alliance with Fen's kingdom. We aren't in love, Quentin, and we both know that this marriage is just a formality."

Quentin was skeptical.

"We have separate bedrooms."

He scrutinized Eliot for a good few seconds. He took in the desperate look in the prince's eyes, the absolute… - oh God - love.

"How is this going to work?" Quentin said with a self-deprecating chuckle.

Eliot hugged him, and then kissed the top of his head. "We'll figure it out."

That was good enough.
