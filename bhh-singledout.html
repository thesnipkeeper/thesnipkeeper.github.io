Singled Out

Lucas jumped at the sudden sneeze from the other side of the room, although he should have known not to be startled by that particular sound by now.

"Bless you."

Marshall sniffled thickly. "Thadks."

"Still think you don't need to be in bed?"

"Snf! I'b fide."

"It might be easier to believe you if you blew your nose," Lucas advised dryly.

Marshall took the suggestion without a second thought. It sounded like the end of a smoothie through a straw, and once definitely wasn't enough, but Marshall was too focused on his paper to notice that his nose was still stuffed beyond reason.

Lucas scoffed and shook his head. The homework Z gave them was easy enough, just worksheets that they needed to fill out with their own answers, at least they were easy enough for Marshall. Lucas found himself continuously stumped.

"What did you put down for 3b?"

"Hm? Oh, snf!" Marshall ran his sleeve under his nose while using the other hand to scan through a stack of papers non his desk. He finally pulls out a green notebook. "Here, you cad just read mby class ndotes."

"Sweet."

Marshall sneezed as he passed it over, and Lucas quickly pulled his collar over his nose and mouth. It didn't really matter, he was living with the germ infested guy anyway, but it made him feel better.

"Bless you."

Marshall shook his head, eyes squinted and watery. "Not...done..." The next sneeze had him doubled over. He grabbed a tissue, and the nose blow that followed sounded miserable.

"Bless you again. Are you sure you can't take anything?" He remembered his mom having an entire pharmacy in their house whenever he was sick. Maybe Marshall would feel better if he just took some cold medicine.

But Marshall shook his head as he wiped his nose.

"The ndurse said she cad't give me adything stro'ger thadn Tylenol without mby parents' codsent. And they're out if the coudtry for a busidess trip, so ki'da hard to contact."

"That sucks, man."

Marshall sniffled. "Tell mbe about it."

"How are either of us going to get sleep tonight then?"

"Dod't plad od sleepi'g. Snf! Found out we get extra credit if we turn ind our LA papers early."

Lucas gaped, staring at his roommate until Marshall finally looked up. "Seriously?"

"I kdow, that's what I said!" He inhaled quickly and had to turn away to cough. Even his coughs sounded stuffed, Lucas realized with disgust.

"No, you're seriously considering that when you're this sick? Marshall, are you crazy?"

Marshall shrugged. "I just wadt a good grade."

Lucas scoffed. "Like you don't already have the highest in our year."

The sounds of snuffling and snorting became mere background noise as the two of them continued to do their homework. But soon it was accompanied by a long, shaky breath. That was new.

Lucas looked up curiously to find his roommate zipping up his hoodie and shuddering with chills.

“Marshall,” he ground out.

“Hm?” Marshall questioned as if nothing was wrong.

“Go to bed.”

“You’re still up.”

“But I’m not dying of the plague!”

Marshall chuckled. “I told you, it’s just a c-cold-” he twisted away to sneeze at the ground. Lucas covered his face again.

“A bad one.”

Marshall sighed--or tried to--but it came out as a wet cough that had him grabbing a tissue to hack into. “Fine. I’ll go to bed after I finish this.”

“Thank you.”

Of course, rest didn’t come easily for either of them. Marshall was making an effort to be quiet, but Lucas could tell that his nose was still bothering him.

He’d hear his roommate toss and turn every once in a while, and then shiver, and then cough. He sounded miserable.

Neither of them got much sleep that night.

-----

“Rest is important in every way. Your mind benefits, your body benefits, and,” Professor Z threw his notes onto the desk that was currently being used by two sleeping students. Or rather, one sleeping student and one almost-asleep student.

He had a feeling what exactly was happening. The two were roommates, and Marshall was pretty obviously not feeling well.

Marshall startled, sitting up immediately with a sniffle, and Lucas shot up from using his arms as a pillow.

“Rest,” Z continued pointedly. “Prevents you from falling asleep in the middle of class and getting singled out in front of your peers.”

The rest of the class giggled while Lucas and Marshall gathered their bearings. Marshall had spent most of the class having claimed the tissue box as his own. He was obviously sick, but Z couldn’t pick on him for that too much this time as he was obviously making an effort to keep his germs to himself by keeping his area tidy. This time, anyway.

He chuckled. “Dismissed. Don’t forget to turn in your worksheets!”

The scuttle of the non-science club students leaving made enough noise that the sound of Marshall blowing his nose was almost drowned out.

“Sorry Professor Z,” Lucas said, dragging a hand down his face. “Marshall’s cold kept us both up.”

“Ah,” Z nodded. “I don’t suppose you two got your homework done in the midst of all of that?” Marshall and Lucas tiredly pulled their worksheets out of their folders and slid them over to him. He shouldn’t have expected any less. “Wow, nice job!”

“Mhmm.” Lucas let his head fall back onto the counter.

“You should be in bed,” said Josie as she less-than-gently felt Marshall’s forehead. Corinne nudged her out of the way and did so as well.

She turned to Z with an exasperated sigh. “Josie’s right, he has a fever.”

Z looked at the two students that were currently fighting to stay awake. It was obvious that Marshall wasn’t feeling good at all, and if Z knew anything about how germs spread, he had a feeling Lucas was well on his way to catching it as well.

“I think both of you should be in bed, actually.”

Lucas was alert, wide-eyed and looking wronged. “Why me?”

“I’b fide, professor,” Marshall insisted.

Josie scoffed.

“You, need to rest to get better,” he said, pointing to Marshall who was currently dragging his sweatshirt sleeve under his nose. (Vaughn made a face and casually nudged the tissue box closer to him with his pencil). “And you,” Z turned to Lucas, “need to rest to prevent getting sick, or get a head start on the cold you’re probably starting to catch so you don’t end up as sick as your friend.”

Lucas looked like he wanted to argue for a moment, and then he groaned in frustration. “We can’t just miss class…"

“I’ll let them know you’ll be absent today, does that work?”

Lucas and Marshall exchanged looks and then nodded reluctantly.
