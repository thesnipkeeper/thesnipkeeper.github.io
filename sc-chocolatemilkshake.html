Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David feels awful for reasons besides having a cold, Patrick attempts to make things slightly less awful
Word count: 3k

Chocolate Milkshake

Patrick didn’t think anything of it when David mentioned coming down with a cold. It was the only reason he decided not to give him crap for coming to work late the third day in a row. They’ve had colds before, they’ve worked through colds before with no problem, so Patrick couldn’t figure out why David seemed so... upset?

He thought upset was the right word for it, but the truth was that David just wasn’t nearly as talkative as usual. Or as flirty or as anal about the placement of the new lip balm tins.

The thing about this, Patrick realized, was that this wasn’t the first time he’d come into work acting a bit off. It was just far more noticeable today.

David sneezed and then huffed in annoyance, as opposed to the dramatic noise he usually enjoyed making when he was sick.

“Bless you!” Patrick called across the store.

David rubbed his nose. “Thanks.”

It was the same exchange they’d been having since David came into work that morning. He’d been sneezing non-stop, Patrick had made sure that there were tissues in easy reach (and not visible to the customers, as David has continued to insist. Visible tissues when one of them is sick is, apparently, incorrect.)

David was only ever quiet like this when he really wasn’t feeling good. Maybe he was just that sick? Maybe he was just playing off his cold as nothing when he actually felt horrible. His voice had seemed a little scratchy, maybe his throat was sore.

Patrick shook his head. He was reading too much into this.

David did his work just as he would have any other day. Being sick didn’t stop him. In fact, he seemed to have been working harder. Patrick smiled as he approached his boyfriend, only for his smile to fade when he got a look at David’s face.

He knew that look. It was the expression David wore when he was fixating. Fixating so much, it seemed, that he didn’t realize his nose was running despite the constant sniffles.

“David,” Patrick said softly, a change from the cheery greeting he was going to give him. “Are you okay?”

“Hm? Oh, yeah, I’m fine. I told you it’s just a cold,” he said with a smile that didn’t quite reach his eyes. He crinkled his nose with a sniffle.

That wasn’t what Patrick meant, but he couldn’t bring himself to correct his question because David immediately went back to organizing products.

-----

“Patrick, hi,” Alexis slipped into his booth the moment she saw him sitting alone in the cafe. “What are you doing here all by yourself?”

Patrick chuckled, tried to smile. “David wanted me to get lunch while he finished up.”

Alexis narrowed her eyes. She took a long time to inhale through her nose before breathing out an, “Mm’kay,” her index finger lands on his hand, “You don’t seem super thrilled about that. Everything okay?”

Patrick sighed. He didn’t want to discuss his concerns with others, and would the fact that she was David’s sister make it better or worse? In the end, though, he knew that he’d need to talk to someone about this or he’d just end up trying to fix a problem that might not have been there.

“David’s been really... focused on work today. And I know that he’s sick, so maybe he’s just not feeling well and he wants to get it all done? But if that was the case, then why didn’t he just stay home and-” he was cut off when Alexis’s face lit up with some sort of realization.

She started nodding and tapping her finger on his arm. “Right. Yeah, that must be um, like really tough? Um, for him? And you, obviously.”

Patrick raised an eyebrow. “I mean... did you talk to him this morning? Did he seem like he was too sick to be coming to work?”

Alexis cleared her throat and took a moment to break eye contact with him to fix her hair. “I’m sure David’s fine,” She said in possibly the most untrustworthy way. “Um, I will admit,” Alexis tapped her nails against the table. “He didn’t mention having a cold? But that makes sense now considering he was sneezing all morning,” She laughed.

“Alexis, what do you know?”

Alexis’s eyes widened, and she made a frustrated sound in the back of her throat. “Look, okay, I can’t-!” she huffed, taking a calming breath. “If there was something to say, I wouldn’t be able to tell you, because David would have told me not to tell anyone and I’m working on not gossiping about him without his like, consent or whatever?”

Patrick cleared his throat. “And you’re doing a fine job.”

“Shut up! Patrick, I can’t say anything.” she hissed in sympathy. “I’m sorry!”

Patrick shook his head and sighed. “No, it’s fine, Alexis. Thanks for letting me... ramble, I guess.”

Alexis smiled, and yep, her finger was back to tapping against him as if she didn’t already have his attention. “Talk to David! It’s fiiine! Just talk to David!”

That conversation probably should have offered some sort of relief, but now he was just on edge about the fact that there was something he needed to talk to David about, and that it didn’t have anything to do with him being sick. He bit his lip, deciding what to do next.

Alexis was right, (Patrick never thought that was something he’d admit) he just needed to talk to David.

He ordered lunch to go instead, getting a favorite of David’s and a chocolate milkshake that he sometimes craved.

Alexis patted his shoulder as he was on his way out. “You’re a good guy, Patrick. I’m glad David has someone like you around.”

When Patrick entered the store, David was nowhere to be found. Patrick paused, listening for him in the back room. He set the food and plastic cup on the counter and went in search for him.

He went to turn into the back room and ended up face-to-face with David on his way out.

David blinked. “Hi. You ate fast.”

Patrick shrugged, followed him back out to the floor. “I thought I’d bring you something too and we could eat together. What were you doing back there?”

“Nothing,” David dismissed, sniffling and grabbing a tissue to wipe his nose. “What’d you bring?”

“Well, first of all...” Patrick slid the cup to his side of the counter and David gasped.

“Oh my God, I’d kiss you but-”

Patrick chuckled. “Yeah, please don’t. I just thought you could use a pick-me-up.”

David blinked, halfway into taking a sip when he pulled his mouth off of the straw. “Why- why? Why did you think that?” He asked, eyes suddenly darting nervously and hand going to set on his waist.

Patrick sputtered. Had he said something wrong? “You’ve... just been quiet today. I figured your cold was getting to you.” He should’ve just been asking what was wrong. Swift and clean. But Patrick found himself nervous enough to dance around the subject.

“Ah. Okay, um...” David sniffed and took another drink of the milkshake. “Thank you.”

They ate for a bit, David mentioned a sore throat, but he continued to eat anyway. Patrick guessed his stomach won the war over his cold.

“So um,” David closed his eyes for a moment, scrunching his nose and turning up to the ceiling as he always did before saying something he was conflicted about. “I- I don’t want you to think that I’m like, in a bad mood.”

It caught Patrick off-guard. “I know you’re not, David,” he assured. “You get... focused sometimes. I know that.”

David nodded quickly. “Okay, just making sure. Because I hope you know that I love you so much, no matter what mood I’m in?”

Patrick gave an awkward chuckle and moved closer to him. “David, I think I’ve seen every side of you at this point.”

“MMmmhmm...” David cleared his throat. “I wouldn’t quite say th- You know what? Thank you so much for lunch. It’s very delicious.”

David Rose, expert at changing the subject. Patrick decided not to push it, so they both continued to eat.

He should say something. Ask David if something else was up. Patrick was working up the courage just as the front door jingled. He sighed and got up to help the customer and leave David to finish his shake.

-----

When work ended, David announced that he was going back to the motel.

“I don’t want to like, germ up your house and give you whatever this is,” he said, waving his hand in front of his face. “So I’ll see you tomorrow?”

Patrick blinked. “I- yeah, sure. Are you feeling okay? If you need meds or anything I can go to the pharmacy and drop them off later.”

“No, that’s okay,” David said quickly. “I’m just going to, y’know, sleep it off.”

Patrick frowned when David walked out the door. Alexis’ voice echoed in his mind. Talk to David.

He’ll wait until tomorrow, and if David’s still acting weird, he’ll talk to him then. No need to make a big deal of one extra-weird day, right?

Still, there was something strange about the amount of heart emojis David sent not long after he must have made it back to the motel, along with a “Sorry again if I was too much today.”

Patrick couldn’t think of a single moment that David would have been too much. If anything, he was too little of whatever David was worried about.

His phone buzzed as he was walking into his quiet, empty apartment. He missed David when he wasn’t staying at his apartment, though he would never tell him that for fear of making him feel guilty. He knew David secretly enjoyed living with his family. He wouldn’t still be living with them if he didn’t.

Alexis’ name was in his notification hub, and under it was the preview of a message: u didnt talk to him

no, i’m going to tomorrow

The three dots of Alexis typing appeared and disappeared several times before her next message finally sent.

ugh. fine.

Patrick raised an eyebrow. He was questioning his decision now more than ever.

-----

David arrived even later than he had the past few days. He offered a halfhearted smile and good morning as he walked in and disappeared into the back to set his bag down.

“How’s the cold?”

David’s nose had a tender pink tone to go with his overall exhausted appearance.

David nodded. “Um, fine, yeah. It’s fine.”

Patrick cleared his throat. He should do this, right? He eyed the window to make sure there weren’t any customers on the way in. “David... if you’re not feeling well, you don’t have to be here.”

David blinked. “I know that. I’m fine, Patrick. I keep telling you that it’s just a cold. Need I remind you that you worked through food poisoning two months ago?

“No, you really don’t.” Patrick winced. “I’m just worried about you, okay? You can take a day off, or-”

“I’m not going home,” David snapped. He closed his eyes and took a deep breath. “I’d rather be here. It helps keep my mind off of... things, okay? And I’m sorry for making you worry. I don’t want you to have to worry about me all the time just because I’m acting a little weird.”

None of that answered his question, and none of that was what Patrick wanted to focus on right now, not even the ‘things’ he was referring to. “David,” Patrick said quietly. “Why do you keep apologizing? That’s like the fifth time since yesterday. That’s more than you’ve apologized in your entire lifetime, I’m pretty sure.” Probably could have done without that last joke. Ugh.

Patrick expected him to be defensive and annoyed at the question, or maybe to play it off as a joke. He didn’t expect his boyfriend to start tearing up. His own expression immediately went soft as he laid a hand on David’s arm, rubbing in earnest.

“I’m sorry,” David sputtered out, voice broken from both the cold and the crying. “I just don’t want you to-”

“Don’t want me to what, David? Come on, please talk to me?” He begged. He was begging.

David shook his head and looked up at the ceiling. He sniffled and bit his lip as tears began to slide down his cheek. “Can we- can we go in the back?” He asked, voice so vulnerable that Patrick’s heart tightened.

Patrick nodded and quickly led him away from the main store, hand on David’s back, running gentle circles that seemed to do nothing to dissipate the tenseness in his boyfriend.

David was close to crumbling, he could tell. He led him to the old couch in the back and slowly sat down next to him.

“What’s going on?” Patrick asked gently.

And there it was. David gave a heartbreaking sob and buried his head in his hands. He tensed slightly when Patrick went to comfort him, so Patrick retracted his hand, feeling helpless. He sort of wanted to cry now.

David took a couple of minutes to recover enough to lift his head. His nose was starting to run, exasperated by his cold, and tears were still falling down his cheeks. Patrick told him to sit tight and ran to retrieve the tissue box before placing it in his lap. David quickly took one and did his best to clean up his face, although it didn’t do much when he was still crying.

“I’m a lot to handle, Patrick,” was the first thing he said.

“What...?”

David sighed, sobbed, laughed... Patrick wasn’t sure. He sniffled and shook his head. “Things are usually... really good. Especially since I met you. But sometimes... sometimes things aren’t good. I just...” He chuckled humorlessly. “I hate myself a lot, sometimes. I thought it was like... over with since I-” He looked up at Patrick with watery eyes. Ah, since he met Patrick.

“David-”

“No, I-” he swallowed. “I have to say this. Alexis- fucking Alexis told me I needed to say it all at once. I- I thought I had stopped getting these... moods when we started dating. And then I woke up the other day and literally the only thing that got me out of bed was the thought that I’d be useful, and I’d... see you. And then I realized...” Another chuckle. “I didn’t want you to see me like this. I thought maybe I could hide the fact that I was so exhausted and depressed. And then this fucking cold happened and it made everything so much worse.”

Patrick tried to wrap an arm around him, and this time David didn’t pull away, but leaned in. He pressed a kiss to David’s temple and held him as tightly as he could.

“I had no idea,” Patrick admitted. “David, I would never blame you for having a few off days. Sure, you’re responsible for your actions, but you can tell me.”

David sat silently save for a few sniffles. When he finally spoke, it was so quiet Patrick had to strain his ears to hear it. “I didn’t want you to leave me.”

“I’m not going to leave you for feeling depressed, David. Why would I do that?” David’s silence answered his question quite nicely. It wouldn’t be the first time. “Look, you don’t have to, but going back to the motel and resting might actually do you some good.”

David sat up and scrubbed at his face with his sweater sleeve. “It’s just... I sort of associate being in bed in the middle of the day with... these bad days. I don’t know if I can relax.”

“You can try? Call it a... self-care day?” Patrick tried.

David snorted with laughter, and then winced. “Ugh, my throat.”

Patrick kissed his forehead. “Just for today. I’ll come over after I close the store.”

-----

Patrick had only just gotten out of his car when Alexis Rose emerged from room seven. She trotted over to him in the heels that she was still wearing for whatever reason.

“Thank you,” she said, biting back a smile.

“For...?”

“For talking to him. He’s been moping around the motel for days, and he was all ‘what if Patrick never talks to me again’.”

Patrick scoffed, because the idea was ridiculous.

“It’s just that,” Alexis continued. “You’re kind of the first person to stick around after witnessing David’s... bad days.”

“Is it that bad?”

She shook her head. “He just gets... tired and like, self-deprecating? Not anything like dangerous, if that’s what you’re wondering. Not anymore, anyway.” She pulled him into a hug before he realized what was happening. “Thank you for being there for him. Anyway! I bought ice cream, we’re going to watch a movie, he doesn’t know you’re here yet so like, you have to wait a few seconds before you come in.”

She poked his shoulder and then disappeared inside the room. He heard a quiet, questioning voice. David. And then Alexis’ voice. He did wait a few seconds just as she said, and then walked in.

David was in bed, but he smiled brightly, if not a little sleepily, and then immediately sneezed.

“Ew, David.”

“Oh my God, shut up.” He made grabby hands for Patrick, who sat on the bed. David clung onto him and cuddled as close as he could. “Glad you came. I missed you.”

Patrick chuckled and pressed a kiss to the top of his head. He was relieved that David was back to his usual clingy-when-sick self. Their conversation earlier must have made them both a little more comfortable.

“I missed you too.” He pulled away a bit to get a look at his boyfriend’s face, earning a dismayed whine from David. Patrick took in the flush in his cheeks before laying his palm across his forehead. “You feel a little warm...”

David sighed.

“Uh, yeah, that’s what I told him earlier, and he was all ‘it’s ‘cause I’ve been under the blankets all day’. Like yeah right, David, you’re probably running a fever.” Alexis snipped as she put in a DVD.

“Oh my Goood.” David groaned into Patrick’s shirt.

Patrick chuckled, and made a sympathetic noise at his boyfriend. “It’ll be fine.” he nuzzled him affectionately as they started their movie.

“I know,” David said. “You’re here.”
