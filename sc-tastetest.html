Fandom: Schitt's Creek

Taste Test

“Ugh... God...”

Patrick winces, hearing the sound of coughing followed by the splattering of liquid coming from the living room. He looks down at the soup he’s currently stirring on the stove. It... probably wouldn’t be eaten any time soon. Instead, he turns off the burner and grabs a bottle of water from the fridge.

Patrick makes a sympathetic noise at the sight of his husband hugging a bucket to his chest. He looks... he looks pathetic. And Patrick would be lying if he said it isn’t kind of amusing to see David Rose with drool down his chin and snot running out of his nose. He’s usually so well put-together, this must be driving him nuts. Or it would if David cared what he looks like right now. The fact that he doesn’t is arguably worrying.

He sits next to him on the sofa and adjusts the blanket covering David’s broad, hunched-over shoulders as his husband tries to catch his breath. “Here,” he says gently, cracking the top of the bottle and handing it to him. “Don’t drink, just rinse your mouth.”

David nods, taking it with a shaky hand. Shakier than Patrick would like, but that’s sort of what happens to the human body when it’s been puking non-stop for almost two days. David takes a gulp and spits it into the bucket with a ‘euck’.

It’s becoming a bit of a routine. David started feeling sick the morning before, and by noon he was puking up his breakfast and begging Patrick to perform a mercy kill. Patrick refused and instead went out to buy some gatorade and saltines.

“When is this going to end?” David mutters, voice hoarse.

“Not sure,” Patrick admits. “Here, I’ll take- yeah, there we go.”

David hands over the bucket, averting his eyes with an expression of defeat. He argued about Patrick cleaning up after him at first, but he’s beyond caring at this point.

Patrick starts to walk off before he stops himself. “Oh, I uh,” a chuckle. “I heated up some soup, but-”

David shakes his head without bothering to look at him.

“Thought so. I’ll be back!”

He finishes cleaning out the bucket with a heavy sigh. He’s made an attempt to keep spirits high despite things, but it’s clear that David doesn’t have much spirit left to lift. Patrick isn’t sure what else he can do to make things better. But he needs to make things better.

Oh... yeah, it’s thinking like that that causes David to accuse him of being a serial problem-solver. He can’t help it! If he can do something, why shouldn’t he try?

Well, for one, because he’s tired.

Neither of them got much sleep last night. David was getting in and out of bed regularly, and had one unfortunate incident where he couldn’t get out of bed quickly enough. Only then did Patrick understand for the first time how thankful he was that David fought for hardwood floors as opposed to carpet in their new house. He’d heard David crying a bit at one point, but he was half-asleep and didn’t register it until morning.

Poor David.

Patrick washes his hands with the name-brand antibacterial soap David was very adamant that they buy, and tries to avoid his reflection in the mirror. He looks about as good as David does, he thinks with a chuckle.

Come to think of it, when’s the last time he’s eaten? Or drank anything? He even skipped morning coffee. Patrick exhales noisily through his mouth. It might be time for what David calls ‘self care’.

Patrick grabs the now-sterilized bucket and brings it out to where David’s been camped out for most of the day. He’s laying across the couch, the blanket half-hanging off of him. He’s not asleep, hasn’t been able to sleep properly since he started getting sick.

Patrick sets the bucket on the ground next to him and then leans down to kiss his temple. David whines and turns his head into the pillow.

“Don’t do that. ‘m disgusting,” comes the muffled complaint.

“Oh, I think I’ll live. How’s your stomach?”

A sigh.

“Got it. Hey, I think I’m going to eat something. Do you want me to keep it in the kitchen?”

“Please.” David finally pulls his head out of the pillow and huffs as he tries to get his hair out of his face. “I don’t think I can handle the smell of food right now.”

A nod. “Understood.” He starts to make his way to the kitchen before he’s stopped.

“Have you eaten at all?”

Patrick turns around guiltily, like a kid who almost got away with something they shouldn’t have. David’s propped up on his elbow, looking at him expectantly. “I- okay, don’t look at me like that! I had a granola bar this morning! I think!”

“You think? Patrick, staying healthy is the only way that you’re going to avoid catching my plague. You can’t just-” He stops abruptly, hand hovering over his mouth for a moment while he tries to figure out whether he’s about to throw up. Patrick winces, but David swallows thickly and sighs. “Don’t stop taking care of yourself. because you’re taking care of me.”

He can’t help but smile. “I’m going to eat just now. Promise.”

David nods approvingly and goes back to being a ball of misery.

The soup needs to be heated again by the time Patrick gets to it. He turns the burner back on, grabs a bowl, and waits. Maybe he can eat, and then cuddle up with David and take a nap. Or just go to bed and take a nap in the likely scenario that David isn’t feeling particularly cuddly.

Patrick ends up stirring the remaining soup with disinterest. Despite how little he’s eaten today, he feels full, and the last thing he feels like doing is finishing his dinner. So, he dumps it and puts what remains in the pan into the fridge for later.

“Can you bring some gatorade when you come back?” David calls.

“What kind?” He calls back as he scrubs the pan.

He grabbed a bunch of different flavors when David got sick, unsure of how long the bug was going to last. It’s better that they be prepared, he told him.

“The red... stuff?”

Predictably, David hasn’t spent a lot of time drinking gatorade in his life, but that’s okay because Patrick knows that he’s going to love the red stuff. Maybe they can make their night into a stupid taste test while they work on rehydrating David.

Patrick’s finally feeling optimistic about how things are going.

And then he throws up in the dishwater.

He gasps, and that’s all he has time to do before it’s happening again, this time loud enough that David has to hear him.

“Patrick?”

Yep. There it is.

This was bound to happen, Patrick tells himself as he wipes his mouth. But it could have waited a couple more days. And just when he thinks that things couldn’t get any worse, he realizes that he’s going to have to stick his hand in the water to unplug the drain. It doesn’t help his nausea.

“Patrick? Oh God...” That voice isn’t as distant as it was. Sure enough, Patrick turns around and finds David rushing toward him. “Did you just-?”

He nods, pressing a hand to his mouth. “Bathroom...” he grinds out. Thankfully, David’s quick enough to step out of his way as he rushes down the hall. He makes it just in time.

David hovers awkwardly in the bathroom doorway as Patrick throws up. “Do you- do you like, need anything?”

He wants to help, do something. Patrick can sense the nervous energy coursing through his husband even while he himself is still knelt in front of the toilet. Patrick shakes his head, running the back of his hand across his mouth.

“Go lay back down. I’ll be...” he gulps. “I’ll be out in a few minutes.”

“Are you sure?”

“Yeah, David.”

David hesitates for a few seconds before leaving.

-----

David is indeed feeling cuddly, which is good because that’s all Patrick wants. They’re both shivery and uncomfortable, and there’s the bucket sitting on the floor waiting for one of them to inevitably need it.

“I’m sorry,” David says as they watch someone on Great British Bake Off scramble to get their cake done on time. “I should’ve been more careful.”

“You sanitized the entire house after you got sick.”

“Yeah, and then we slept in the same bed.”

“Oh,” Patrick chuckles. “Yeah. Oh well, that’s marriage, right?”

A scoff. “Um, I guess?” David snuggles up to him more and sighs contentedly. That lasts all of two minutes before he sits up again with a hand over his mouth. “Can you-” he nods a thanks as Patrick hands over the bucket.

This time, Patrick has to look away as David throws up. It doesn’t help, or maybe his and David’s stomachs are getting on the same wavelength, because Patrick has to dart off to the bathroom.

It’s going to be a long night, he decides.

-----

It is a long night. They bring the stupid bucket with them when they go to bed in case anything unfortunate happens again. Patrick’s feeling worse by the minute, and David’s quickly losing any semblance of himself.

Falling asleep doesn’t come easily for either of them. And it apparently doesn’t last long, either.

Patrick wakes up in the middle of the night with a groan as his stomach cramps. He knows from experience that it’s better to just get up now instead of waiting for the nausea to follow, so he makes his way to the bathroom and sits on the edge of the tub.

It doesn’t take long for him to throw up. He gags uselessly at first, and then brings up a wave of what has to be the end of his stomach contents. Instead of deciding that he’s done for now, he chooses to simply lean against the shower wall and let the tile cool his forehead.

He doesn’t realize that he’s falling asleep until the bathroom door opens and he startles awake. David rushes in and makes it to the sink before he vomits.

“David?” Patrick slurs sleepily. “You okay?”

David takes a moment to catch his breath and swallow. “Uh... Uh-huh.” Then he turns to Patrick guiltily. “Sorry. Didn’t want to use the fucking bucket.”

Patrick chuckles. “Think we’re going to get any sleep tonight?”

David scoffs loudly.

-----

“You two are pathetic.”

“Your bedside manner could use some work.”

“I’m doing this for free, so my bedside manner is fine.”

“I’m sorry, I didn’t realize that I had to pay you to be a good friend.”

“Children...” Patrick mutters. “Trying to sleep.”

When the two of them had yet to function by morning, Patrick decided that they needed reinforcements in the form of Stevie. The worst person for the job and yet the only person they can stand to have around while they’re suffering.

Despite their argument about bedside manner, the three of them are in the living room, David and Patrick on the couch. It’s not super comfortable, but neither of them could stand being in bed a minute longer.

Stevie sighs heavily, making sure that it’s loud enough for them to hear. “You guys probably need some kind of fluids. The last thing I want to do is drive you to the hospital.”

“So kind,” David mumbles.

Patrick, his cheek smushed against David’s shoulder, manages to get out a, “Gatorade in the fridge.”

Stevie leaves, and seconds later yells, “Which kind?”

“All of them.”

He can feel David looking at him. “What?”

-----

They needed something to get their mind off of being sick, Patrick found a solution. And despite what David says, he’s clearly enjoying it. It’s nice to see him complaining about the flavor, or complaining about anything. That’s how Patrick knows he’s feeling better.

“I recommend this one,” Stevie pours a little bit of Glacier Cherry into each of their glasses.

David takes a sip and makes a delighted noise. “A nice uh, body.”

“Careful,” Patrick says. “I’m right here.” He leans against his husband, savoring his body heat. The medicine’s beginning to wear off, his chills are back, but David certainly makes things better. It helps that he’s big and warm.

David wraps an arm around him as he continues sipping the various flavors.

“You know if I get sick, you guys owe me.”

“Ah, true friendship.”

“We really appreciate this, Stevie. We’ll make sure you’re okay.”

Stevie smiles, scoffs. “I know. Anyway, I’m gonna open the weird green bottle.”
