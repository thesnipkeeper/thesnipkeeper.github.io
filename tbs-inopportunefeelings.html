Title: Inopportune Feelings
Fandom: Bright Session
Summary: Owen has a cold and he and Joan are professional about it
Word count: 1.6k

Inopportune Feelings

Joan’s alerted to a knock on her office door.

“Come in,” she calls, not immediately looking up until she hears her friend’s voice.

“Joan?” Sam asks, taking cautious steps inside.

“Mhm? What’s up?” When she finally looks up at her, she finds Sam looking more nervous around her than she’s been in a good while. Any number of reasons Sam is in her office start spinning in her head, and none of them are correct.

“So, I don’t know if you’ve talked to Owen today...”

She shakes her head. “I’ve been in here most of the morning, why?” They’ve had so much work to do that they’d hardly seen each other. So, just their normal work/dating relationship. She and Owen try their best to keep their relationship professional.

Sam bites her lip, thinks for a moment, and sighs. “I think it’d be a good idea if you sent him home.”

Joan raises an eyebrow. “Sent him home?”

“Okay, he’s clearly super sick and he’s not even acknowledging it and I’m worried that he’s either going to work himself to the bone until he passes out or something, or that he’s just going to get the whole facility sick and honestly? I don’t feel like dealing with an outbreak right now and I doubt that either of you do as well, so-”

Joan blinks. “Sam, whoa,” she holds out her hands to stop her. “Owen’s sick?”

Sam nods. “It’s a cold or something, but it’s like, a pretty bad one? I don’t know how he’s still up and doing things, honestly, because he sounds terrible.”

Joan sighs heavily, dragging a hand down her face. She forgot this little quirk of Owen’s, they hadn’t worked together for years. The man never takes a sick day. Owen Thompson could be seconds away from dying and he’d hold it off until his paperwork was finished.

She can’t count the number of times he’s made himself sicker by doing so, but he never even noticed when it did happen.

“Okay,” she says. “I’ll talk to him. Thanks for letting me know, Owen and I haven’t even had a chance to talk this past week with all the new intakes...”

“He does this a lot, huh?”

“Unfortunately, an aversion to sick days is something Owen and I have in common.”

Sam chuckles. “It’s almost scary how alike you two are.”

“Oh, don’t even start.”

-----

Owen’s in the lobby with a patient, in the middle of blowing his (alarmingly red) nose and then using a horrendous amount of hand sanitizer.

He looks tired. Oftentimes Joan is the only one to notice, so the fact that his cheery facade has dropped enough for Sam (and likely others) to notice is... concerning.

Nevertheless, he smiles and wishes the patient a good day as she signs herself out and waves goodbye. He waits until she’s out the big double doors before he buries his face in his arm and gives an itchy-sounding cough.

“Owen,” Joan greets, keeping her voice light as to not alarm him too much. It doesn’t work, he knows what she sounds like when she’s about to say something he doesn’t like. Doesn’t stop him from playing dumb.

“Hello, Dr. Bright!” God, his voice sounds terrible. “How’s your day going so far?”

“Oh, I can’t complain.”

They walk off in the direction of the elevator together, Joan pretends she can’t hear him struggling to sniffle against a runny nose.

She waits until they’re in the elevator together before she turns to him. “I’ve been asked to send you home before you infect the whole building.”

Any smile on his face fades immediately. “I’m being careful, Joan. You know me!”

“Right, right, how about for yourself?”

He blinks. “What?”

“You don’t think that maybe you’re pushing yourself too far? You know, like you always do?”

He clears his throat, likely out of necessity than awkwardness, and the pulls a neatly-folded tissue from his pocket to wipe his nose with a liquid snf! “It’s not that bad,” he mutters. “Really, it’s just a cold. The sniffles, that’s all.”

She lifts a hand and instructs, “Come down here for a second.”

He stares, confused at first, and then understands. Owen leans down until Joan can reach his forehead. A gesture the two of them would never do at work had they not been in the elevator.

Joan gasps as she pulls her hand away. “Owen!” she says. “You’re burning up! What are you still doing here?” It’s meant to sound like a scold, but honestly, she’s more surprised and worried than upset with him.

Owen doesn’t have a look of shame as if he’s been caught, but furrows his brows instead. “Am I?” he tries to feel his own forehead and face, to no avail.

“Oh my goodness...” she mutters. “You’re unbelievable.”

Maybe Owen is going to defend himself, maybe he’s going to admit defeat, but the elevator doors open and they’re forced to step out through a crowd of new hires who really shouldn’t know how close the two of them truly are.

“So Sam thinks that I’m a health hazard?” he asks, somewhat amused, as they make their way down the hall.

“I never said that it was Sam,” Joan scoffs. “But yes. There was also something about worrying you were going to pass out.”

“I’m not going to-” he sniffs sharply, and the sudden noise gets Joan’s attention.

She watches his eyes go cloudy as he scrubs his nose and digs in his pocket for another tissue. Apparently, nothing can appease the itch in his sinuses long enough for him to get to a tissue. He sneezes in his hand. Joan winces.

“Bless you,” she mutters. “And if you won’t go home, I at least think that you should quarantine yours-”

Owen sneezes again. That one sounded... productive. She tries to ignore it. None of that at work (not today, at least). He finally manages to find a tissue and presses it to his face, and for a quick moment between his hand and the tissue, Joan gets a glimpse of the mess on his upper lip and around his pink nostrils. For fuck’s sake...

She takes a deep breath. He glances at her over steepled hands, and she can see his eyes crinkle with a realizing smile.

“Joan...”

“I know, we have work to do...”

“No, I- well, yes, but I was going to ask if,” he clears his throat. “If you wanted to come over. You know, after work. Shouldn’t waste an opportunity like this, right?” he finishes with an awkward chuckle that’s unbelievably adorable, even when his voice is almost painful to listen to.

Joan feels the heat rise to her cheeks. She takes another breath. “That’d... be nice.”

He nods, shoving the soaked wad back in his pocket. Joan winces at that.

“You need to sanitize your hands before you touch anything else in this building,” she says with a no-nonsense look. It does well to distract them both from what was on their mind.

“Oh! Right, good idea!”

-----

She doesn’t see him much after that. She does, however, hear some sneezes from down the hall whenever his office door is open. He sounds more and more miserable by the second, and she can’t bring herself to enjoy it when she’s too busy worrying that he’s too sick to do much of anything tonight. Maybe she should just make him soup and put him to bed. He has a temperature, after all.

She gets a text around the time Owen usually takes his lunch break (which is at least an hour after everyone else, as they like to make sure things are taken care of). Joan is almost afraid to open it at work, but the text is a simple, “Headache. Need your help please?”

Ah. Joan smiles, and makes her way down to Owen’s office.

He does indeed look in pain, rubbing his head with a deep frown. His nose has taken on a bright red appearance now, and his temperature is likely a steady heat that’s making him even more uncomfortable than he already was.

She frowns sympathetically.

He looks up and offers a sheepish smile. “Do you mind?”

“Of course not, here...” she weaves around his desk and instructs him to face her.

She lays a hand on each side of his face and begins moving her thumbs in a circular motion over his cheeks and forehead. Owen gives a sniffly sigh of relief as the massage begins to ease the pressure in his head.

“You’re really warm,” she says softly.

“ ‘s not that bad.”

“Oh, Owen... go home,” but she continues to relieve his headache. They had this down to a science back in the day, and Joan prided herself on being exceptionally good at it.

He sniffs, though the sound is congested and she doubts he’s getting any air through at all. They’re silent for a few seconds as she strokes his face and he closes his eyes.

And then a, “You can hold down the fort for the rest of the day?” breaks the silence.

Joan can’t help but smile at that. She cups his cheek and presses a kiss to his forehead. “I’d like to think of myself as competent, yes.”

He huffs. “A-alright. If you’re sure.”

Either she wore him down, or Owen really must not be feeling well. Perhaps a little of both.

“Get your stuff,” she says before giving another kiss, this time on his lips. “Get out of here. I’ll be by after work with dinner.”

He nods and smiles too excitedly for someone who’s down with the cold from hell.
