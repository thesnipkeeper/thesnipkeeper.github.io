Yay Field Trip

They haven’t been to the history museum since middle school. Mostly because no one goes to the history  museum after middle school. And yet Cinderbrush High decided it was the perfect field trip for a group of upperclassmen. 

Jamie didn’t mind initially. It was time that they would otherwise be spending in class, after all, and wandering around a museum and making sarcastic remarks when the guide wasn’t paying attention sounded much more appealing.

Now, though, they’re very much regretting agreeing to this. 

They thought they were just a little tired the night before. It was the first time in months that they’d gone to bed before midnight. Now they know that not only were they tired, but they were coming down with something. 

Literally the last thing they feel like doing right now is climbing on a crowded bus full of loud teenagers, and yet...

They take the inside of the bench by the window before Cameron asks. They can’t handle the thought of touching knees with someone today, and no one really knows how to mind their own space on a school bus.

Cameron would usually at least question it or argue before letting Jamie just have it, but he knows Jamie isn’t feeling good, they complained about it in a text before either of them even got to school, so today he just sits down next to them.

Jamie sniffles and wipes their nose on the sleeve of their kimono. The fabric is rough against the tender skin. Ugh, they should have brought tissues. They didn’t even consider it, having spent most of their getting ready time this morning blowing their nose and doing their makeup, they didn’t have enough foresight to have things prepared for the rest of the day. Like tissues. For their incessantly drippy nose.

Jamie groans inwardly and lets their head bump against the window. It’s blessedly cool, the only thing keeping them from going insane with the noisy, humid hunk of metal they’re currently trapped in.

They try to sniffle as quietly as possible, but it just ends with having to swallow the gunk and irritate their throat. Apparently they had a sore throat now. Fantastic.

Cameron’s talking to someone about the next football game, or basketball game, or something Jamie doesn’t care about enough to follow.

They’re just beginning to doze against the window when the bus stops with a jolt.

Jamie wakes with a pathetic moan and rubs their eyes (carefully. don’t wanna ruin the makeup they worked so hard on). Cameron just chuckles and pats them on the back as they both hop out of the bus.

Going from sitting in a bus to walking around in a matter of seconds has Jamie’s head pounding and their runny nose now deciding that it’s clogged instead. 

“Hey,” Cameron whispers, nudging them as they’re in the middle of pawing at their nose. He pulls a plastic package out of his pocket, and Jamie gasps the moment it’s in their hands.

“Oh my God, you’re a lifesaver.”

“I know,” Cameron smirks.

Jamie wastes no time opening the thing and blowing their nose. “So you’re just walking around with an entire packet of tissues in your pocket?”

Cameron shrugs. “I have allergies.”

“No, you don’t,” Jamie scoffs, shoving the rest in their own pocket, along with the gross tissue that they might be able to get a second use out of. (Yuck, colds can really slaughter a person’s sense of personal hygiene)

“Yeah, you’re right. You said you were sick and I figured I’d grab one of these on my way out. You always forget tissues when you have a cold.”

“Do I?” 

Cameron chuckles.

An overly-cheery tour guide greets their group as they walk in, Jamie’s eyes are watering and probably smudging their makeup.

Jamie decides that it’s going to be a long day.

-----

“Oh, bless you!”

Jamie could kill the tour guide as she drew attention to them with a tissue over their nose. Cameron just laughs and makes a joke about the medieval-era diorama that had the entire group laughing.

Thank fuck for him, Jamie thinks, as it gives them time to blow their nose without worrying about being gross.

“I wish I brought my components,” Jamie mutters under their breath. “A silence spell would do her some fucking good.”

Cameron scoffs. “You’re telling me. It wouldn’t be worth it, though.”

Jamie snuffles, scrubbing their palm against their nose. It still itches, but they can’t let themselves sneeze after that embarrassing situation. They can only rely on Cameron to distract the others so many times. 

Jamie has little interest in the World War II exhibit, and even if they did, their head feels so fuzzy that they wouldn’t be able to pay attention to anything anyway.

There’s a bench by the entrance to the room and they immediately take everyone else’s fascination in the maimed helmet as an opportunity to sit down. 

Their nose itches, and they don’t have a chance to grab a tissue in time. Ugh, that one scrapes against their tender throat. They’ve been reluctant to swallow anymore, because it’ll just result in horrible pain.

Someone sits next to them on the same fucking bench as they’re tending to their nose. Jamie’s ready to make themselves sound even more contagious than they already do just to make this person go away. But- oh, it’s just Cameron.

He smiles sympathetically and carefully wraps an arm around them, just loose enough that they can shove him away if they want to. They definitely don’t. 

Instead, Jamie leans into him and gives a pitiful sniffle.

“How’re you feeling?”

“Like shit, mostly.”

Cameron chuckles and nods. “Sorry, man. I wish I could do something.”

“Are you kidding? Without these tissues I’d be fucked.”

“Yeah, I guess you would, huh?”

Jamie wants to stay like that forever, but a tickle in their throat demands their attention. They turn away from Cameron and muffle a painful cough into the too-thin fabric of their kimono. They should have just worn a hoodie like everyone else does when they come to school sick, but no, Jamie thinks bitterly, they still had to look good. 

They’re miserable enough that looking good definitely doesn’t matter to them anymore.

The cough, of course, is followed by a sneeze. And another cough. Fuck, they’re such a mess.

“Jesus,” Cameron mutters. “That sounded like it hurt.”

Jamie groans. “Yeah,” they say with a wince. Their hand goes to their throat, as if rubbing it is actually going to do anything.

Eventually, the teacher hails them over as the class starts to move on to another exhibit. Cameron lays a hand on Jamie’s back as they follow, Jamie trying to sniffle enough to clear their nose. 

The 1950s exhibit is also not something Jamie cares about, surprise surprise. But they find themselves trying to pay attention simply because they need something to distract them from how horrible they feel.

Cameron pats their back whenever they cough. It makes them laugh, which in turn makes them cough again. Cameron always does it, just to be funny. 

Jamie sniffles their way through the whole exhibit. They find themselves following Cameron around, possibly to hide behind him in their horrible state.

When it comes time to eat lunch, Jamie of course hasn’t brought anything to eat. Packing a lunch was the last thing on their mind this morning. 

They try to refuse Cameron when he offers half of his sandwich. 

“My throat feels like sandpaper,” they admit. “I can’t eat anything right now.”

Cameron just sits the half on the bag it came in and slides it over to them. “Just a couple bites? It’ll make me feel better.”

Jamie rolls their eyes. “Shame on you for trying to manipulate someone who’s horribly ill,” they say, right before taking a bite of the sandwich.

Cameron shrugs innocently and scarfs down the rest of his lunch. 

“There’s a cafe here. I might get a coffee before they need us back again, you want anything?”

Jamie narrows their eyes. Cameron doesn’t drink coffee on school days. He learned a while ago that it fucks with his anxiety to no end. “No, thank you.”

He nods, leaves, and returns with two cups anyway.

If Jamie could smell anything right now, they’re pretty sure they’d be smelling the sweetness of hot cocoa coming from their friend’s cup. They scoff and sip the warm liquid. Tea, green tea. It soothes their throat, and in the process makes their nose run, which is fine, because at least they can breathe again.

“Thank you,” they say quietly. They don’t just mean for the tea. Everything. They’re talking about everything Cameron’s done for them today, even when it’s just a stupid cold. And Cameron knows exactly what they’re talking about.

“Hey, anything to help you feel better. It’s nothing you haven’t done for me before. Remember when I got food poisoning when my parents were gone?”

“You poisoned yourself,” Jamie laughs hoarsley. 

Cameron starts laughing as well. “Hey! How was I supposed to know that leftover chinese would be bad by then?”

Jamie snorts. “Oh God, you were such a mess.”

“Yeah, and I’m trying to return the favor. Now that you’re a mess.”

Jamie shakes their head, sips their tea. 

Eventually, they’re rallied to join the rest of the class for two more exhibits. 

Jamie’s feeling significantly more awful by the end of the trip, especially after they run out of tea. Their nose is clogged and they feel like their entire head is underwater. The headache is still going strong.

The group is near the end of the last exhibit when Cameron wraps both arms around them and presses a soft kiss to their head. They feel so miserable that it’s actually a comfort to be hugged out of nowhere. Only Cameron could ever get away with it, or make them feel better by doing it. 

Jamie finds themselves shuddering, even in his arms. “Do I- do I feel warm to you?” they whisper, glad to be in the very back of the group where no one’s paying attention to them.

Cameron bites the inside of his mouth, lays the back of his hand on their cheek just to be sure, and nods. “Yeah, you feel like you have a fever?”

Jamie sighs, sniffles. “Unfortunately. God fucking damn it, there’s a party tomorrow.”

“Oh my God, calm down. You can miss one party.”

“I need cash, Cameron.”

“Cash isn’t gonna do you any good if you die from pneumonia or something.”

Jamie scoffs. “Alright, whatever.” They can’t quite shake the sense of dread for tomorrow. There’s nothing worse than going to a party when you’re sick, surrounded by everything loud and obnoxious, things you usually enjoy just getting on your nerves and making you want to die. But, maybe Cameron is right.

They’re still wrapped around each other when the tour guide’s eyes meet theirs for a moment. Cameron and Jamie both fear they’re about to be called out, but instead she just smiles and goes back to talking about history stuff. 

Maybe she’s not as bad as they thought.

-----

The group is loud as they file back onto the bus.

By that time, Jamie is feeling all sorts of horrible. They can’t wait to get home and sleep until school tomorrow. Maybe they can even take a sick day and forget that homework exists. 

Psh.

Jamie flops into the bench and muffles a truly horrific cough. 

The minute Cameron sits now next to them, they lay their head on his shoulder with a defeated sniffle. They’re half asleep, but they think they feel another kiss against their head as they shudder against their friend.

They don’t realize until they’re very comfortable that it’s probably not the kind of weather for snuggling. “Sorry if I’m making you hot.”

“You’re good,” Cameron says. “I can feel you shivering.”

“Yeah, I’m freezing,” they admit. 

Cameron wraps an arm around them just to bring them closer, and Jamie is quick to take advantage and snuggle as close as they can.

Jamie hums happily, and has no idea that they start to snore the moment they fall asleep.
