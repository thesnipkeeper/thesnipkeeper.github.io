Spicy Cheetos

Shane was perfectly comfortable on the bathroom floor, using a towel as a pillow. Unfortunately, his stomach cramped yet again and he knew he had mere seconds to react.

With a groan, he dizzily sat up and got himself over the toilet. He swallowed hard, which only seemed to exasperate the nausea. A tight burp, followed by a stream of something bright and awful-tasting fell into the water before.

Shane grabbed a handful of toilet paper to clean himself up with, but it hardly mattered. He was a mess whether he wiped the drool off of his chin or not. He was currently shirtless and uncomfortable, his t-shirt now a sad heap in the corner of the bathroom. It had received the brunt of the first bout of puke and probably smelled terrible. Shane was far beyond caring. He just wanted to be able to go to bed without worrying about puking in it.

A knock on the door startled him. 

"Whoever's in there, are you almost done?"

Shane sighed. Michael. "Nah."

"Okay, well... I was gonna brush my teeth. Are you... Almost done?"

"Nah," Shane repeated. He was still leaning over the toilet, not entirely trusting of his stomach. Which was good, because he ended up heaving once again.

The door opened. Michael must have decided that privacy was for chumps.

"Damn," he said. His voice was strained, and Shane had a feeling he knew why. It probably smelled strongly of vomit in there. Not that he himself could tell anymore after being camped in there for the past two hours. "Are you sick?"

"Sure looks that way," Shane muttered pitifully.

Michael was quiet for a few seconds as if trying to figure out what to say. Shane had all the patience in the world. He wasn’t going anywhere, after all.

“How long have you been like this?”

“Oh, I dunno,” he sighed out. “A few hours.”

“Jesus Shane. You could’ve come gotten me. I was just downstairs.”

“And what would you have done?”

“I-” Michael nods in defeat. “Fine, whatever. You need help?”

Shane considered the question. How exactly could Michael help when all he was doing was throwing up? He just wanted to be left alone for the night.

... of course, that could prove problematic come morning when Eve would be ready to fight him for the bathroom.

“Ugh... I guess I can’t stay in here all night.”

“Yeah,” Michael agreed.

“But I don’t think... ugh, my stomach still feels weird.” He let himself whine, because he felt shitty enough that he should have that, and wrapped an arm around his abdomen, curling up.

“I help you out a little. Come on...” Michael leaned down and gripped Shane’s arm, pulling him up.

Shane... Shane was a big guy, he knew that Michael wouldn’t be able to get him off the floor without help. But he realized as he tried to stand up that his legs were shaky and he was far dizzier than he thought he was. He swayed, grabbing onto the sink for support. Michael’s grip tightened.

“Whoa, okay. Take it easy.”

Shane nodded. He was sure trying to take it easy. They waited an awkward few seconds until he got his balance back.

“ ‘kay, think I’m- mph-!” And Shane promptly puked into the sink. Michael swore, but kept him upright when Shane’s body wanted to force him back down to the floor. He heaved again, but he couldn’t possibly have anything left to puke up.

“Alright, just take a breath,” Michael soothed. He rubbed Shane’s back with the hand that wasn’t completely holding him up. Shane would appreciate the affection from the friend that usually shied away from any emotional bonding, if he wasn’t... you know, in the middle of vomiting.

One issue that he ran into was that he couldn’t take a breath. His stomach was so determined to get rid of something that wasn’t there that he was just leaned over heaving for what felt like an eternity. When it finally ended, he realized that he was leaning heavily against Michael. Michael didn’t say anything, just reached across to rinse out the sink and the grabbed the bin from under the sink.

“Let’s go, huh?”

Shane nodded.

-----

“You don’t have to stay with me all night,” Shane said as he watched his friend grab a chair. He plopped into it with a book as if he’d already made up his mind about being in someone else’s room.

“I mean, I can leave if you want me to, but I-”

“Naw, it’s fine. Stay.” Things had been weird between them recently. Shane was skilled at ignoring awkwardness, and Michael was skilled at not noticing it. But there was definitely something weird.

Michael did stay. And he was there to help him out when he started throwing up again. To give him a bottle of water to rinse his mouth of the gross stuff, and to-

Jesus, did he just feel Shane’s forehead? Shane froze, blinked, and realized he didn’t hate it. And also that he’d been shivering for a good while.

“Thermometer would be useful right about now,” Michael muttered. Their house wasn’t exactly packed to the brim with essentials, but if they needed multiple brands of chili powder, they were set. “I mean, it’s probably fine, but I just don’t want to have to drag you to the ER.”

Shane scoffed. “I’m fine. Actually, no I’m not, but I will be... eventually... at some point.” He paused for a dramatic sigh that had his friend rolling his eyes. “Hey, thanks for- you know, taking care of me tonight.”

Michael gave a small smile. “No problem. I’m always here for you.”

“Well, history says that that’s obviously a lie.”

“Okay, what did you expect to happen when you ate a giant bag of spicy Cheetos in one sitting? You didn’t deserve help.”

“Wow.” Shane chuckled, and then winced when he remembered what his digestive system went through after the Spicy Cheeto Incident. His stomach didn’t cramp as much now as it just ached from all of the puking. If he needed an ab workout, well he got it. A shiver ran through him as he tried to get comfortable in bed. “Hey...” he ground out through the shudders and aches.

Michael looked up from his book. “Sup?”

“You can like, sit on the bed. It’s probably more comfortable than that old-ass chair.”

Michael raised an eyebrow, looking from Shane to the empty spot he was patting next to him. Shane could have sworn his heart was going to jump out of his chest. That was way too forward. He must have misread some signals at some point, or-

“Sure.” Michael closed his book, using a finger as a bookmark as he got up and got into Shane’s bed.

Shane froze. He didn’t think he’d get this far.

“I...” he cleared his throat. “Thanks, man. I owe you one.”

“Oh, totally,” Michael agreed with a chuckle.
