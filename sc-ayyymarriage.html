Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David sleeps in, Patrick gets annoyed until he realizes why
Word count: 1.4k

Ayyy Marriage

Patrick wakes to the alarm with a groan, dragging a hand down his face while the other goes to turn off the awful noise.

The moment he’s awake, he remembers what today is, and why he’s awake so horribly early. They’ve got a big delivery from one of their vendors coming in before the store opens. As much as they didn’t want to agree to have it delivered so early, David and Patrick both knew that it would be nice to have it all stocked today.

Patrick is very much rethinking that when he realizes that the sun hasn’t even risen yet.

It’s no surprise that David is still fast asleep, lying on his stomach and hugging his pillow and looking generally adorable.

“David,” he yawns, patting his husband’s back. “Time to get up.”

David groans in response and curls up.

“C’mon, we need to be at the store soon, remember?”

A grunt. David turns over away from him.

Patrick sighs. He knows better than to force a David Rose out of bed. He values his marriage and his life, for that matter. So he gets up and starts finding some clothes for the day, careful to be quiet.

Except it gets to being a half hour before their vendor is scheduled to arrive, and David is still in bed. Annoyance nips at Patrick. It was David’s idea to have everything stocked before they opened, after all. One would think he could get out of bed for it.

“David,” Patrick says sharply. The lump in under the blankets shifts, makes a grumpy sound. “What are you doing? We have to leave soon.” He makes an attempt to be playful and nudge David’s feet (or where he assumes David’s feet are), only for his hand to get kicked.

Finally, a voice comes from the duvet. “Don’t feel well.”

“Like, sick?”

David makes a non-committal noise, but Patrick takes that as a yes.

“Would have been nice to know earlier, I could have called her and rescheduled.”

David makes an entirely different noise and shifts more than he has since they woke up. A whimper?

“David...?”

Before Patrick can say anything else, his husband is throwing the duvet to the end of the bed and darting (stumbling) to their bathroom.

Ah. When he said he didn’t feel well, Patrick assumed a sore throat or a headache, but this is not what he expected. So starts the first time ‘sickness and health’ is applicable in their new home.

“You okay?” he calls.

A few seconds go by before a miserable, “Sure.”

He hadn’t bothered to close the door, so Patrick goes and leans on the doorway. David’s sitting on the edge of the bathtub, arm wrapped tightly around his stomach with a miserable, nauseated expression.

Patrick makes a sympathetic sound. “So, sick, then?”

David looks up, but doesn’t reply. In fact, he seems to be afraid to move, let alone open his mouth. A David who’s too sick to talk is a sick David indeed.

Patrick sighs and sits next to his husband. The room is silent (David is silent), and he swears he can hear David’s stomach gurgle and slosh sickeningly.

David raises a hand to his mouth and muffles a small burp. And little time passes before he burps again, this time closely followed by him lunging for the toilet and barely making it.

Patrick can’t help but wince at the series of gags and heaves, and looks away in time to hear the sound of liquid hitting liquid. David whines hoarsely and then heaves again, as if trying to get this over with as soon as possible.

“Hey, take it easy...” Patrick soothes, running a hand up and down his back. He can feel the quick, ragged breaths under his hand.

David burps again, bringing up a small stream of vomit with it. He takes the opportunity to catch his breath and spit away the strings of saliva hanging from his mouth. He makes a grateful sound when Patrick hands him a wad of toilet paper to wipe his face.

“Fuck,” he whispers. “I don’t-” he swallows thickly and exhales. “Patrick...”

“Hey, it’s okay.” Patrick runs his fingers through David’s unstyled hair before his husband reaches up to flush the toilet. “I’ll take care of everything.”

David nods. “I’m sorry.”

“Shhhh.”

“You need to go. Can’t be late,” he breathes out. He props his elbows on his knees and buries his head in his hands.

“Are you sure you’ll be okay?”

David nods slowly. “Mmm’yeah. It’s just my stom... Ugh, God...”

Patrick frowns as his husband starts throwing up once more.

“I’ll call later, okay David?” He sighs. The last thing he wants to do is go to work and leave David at home to be sick alone. “Feel better. Love you.”

“Love you too,” David croaks as Patrick leaves the bathroom.

Patrick finds his car keys and sighs, looking back to the stairs. He's tempted to call the vendor and reschedule last-minute, but he knows that wouldn't be good for business.

-----

He gets the merchandise moved into the storage room, but decides to wait until David's back to stock everything. He'll need help for that much stuff, and David will definitely have an opinion on where it goes and what direction it faces.

With the store open and quiet, he decides to call his husband.

"Heyyy," a croaky voice greets him.

"How're you feeling?"

"Ugh," Patrick hears shuffling. He's in bed now, good. "I mean, not amazing, obviously. But I think I'm done throwing up for now, so that's good?" He chuckles . "I'm just going to sleep forever, now."

Patrick's glad to hear that he's alright. He's witnessed David horribly sick before, enough to kill his spirit completely and turn him into a bit of a zombie. But today he's just tired.

"Okay," Patrick says. "I'll let you get some sleep, then."

David hums. "I love you. Like, a lot." The smile is evident in his voice, and Patrick can imagine him shaking his head like he does to emphasize things.

"I love you too."

-----

Patrick throws his keys on the kitchen table, finding the house to be just as quiet as he expected. He’s glad to be home. It’s been a long, boring day at work. The store is much livelier when he has David around.

He ascends the stairs and quietly enters the bedroom. David’s asleep, snoring softly. The bathroom door is open, the light pouring in through the dark room as David never opened the curtains.

He thinks about waking him. But it’s probably better to let him sleep, Patrick decides, so he tiptoes around the room to change out of his jeans and get into sweats. He’s just in the middle of unbuttoning his shirt when the bed shifts.

“Hey,” comes the sleepy voice. David sits up and rubs his face, looking rather adorable. “You’re home.”

“I am,” Patrick says. He sits on the edge of the bed next to David’s legs and looks him over in the glow of the bathroom light. “Feeling better?”

David smiles and nods. “Not going to be eating a five-course meal any time soon, but I haven’t puked in a while, so I’ll consider that today’s accomplishment.”

“I’m glad. I missed you today. Have you eaten?”

He shakes his head quickly. “God, no. I might be done puking... for now... but I’m not hungry. I may never be.”

“Never? Whoa, David, that’s a long time.”

David scoffs. “I know. Very tragic.”

It’s nice to see him well enough to joke around. Still, he makes a face, grunting a bit as he wraps an arm around your stomach. David turns on his side and curls up as tightly as he can.

“Stomach still bothering you?”

A wordless nod.

“Want me to-?”

“Please.”

Patrick smiles and nudges David’s arm away from his tender belly. He begins to gently knead the soft flesh. He’d never tell David this in a million years lest his husband die of embarrassment, but he admires the bit of pudge on David’s stomach just a bit.

David’s breath starts to even out as he relaxes under Patrick’s touch. They’ve done this plenty of times. It usually happens when David’s eaten something he shouldn’t have, which happens more often than he’s ever willing to admit.

“Better?”

David nods again. His eyes are closed, looking adorably blissed out.

Patrick chuckles and leans over to press a kiss to his husband’s forehead. “I’m going to make you some toast.”

“Love you.”

“Love you too, David.”
