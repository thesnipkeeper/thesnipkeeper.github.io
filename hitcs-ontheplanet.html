On the Planet

"This was to be expected, was it not," Linus said, rather unhelpfully, as he dabbed the back of his fiance’s neck with a cool cloth.

It wasn't that he didn't have sympathy for the man, of course he did. But Arthur had spent the past few days insisting that he be the one to stay up with the kids every night. Zoe and Linus both tried to help with that side of things, but Arthur wouldn't hear of it.

"No sense in all of us exposing ourselves to this," he'd said.

No, Linus supposed, there wasn't. But in being the lone caretaker, Arthur had all but guaranteed himself that he'd catch what the kids had.

And that was how the two of them ended up on the bathroom floor while poor Arthur Parnassus heaved up anything he’d remembered to eat that day (which wasn’t much). Linus offered comfort, rubbing his back and making sure his clothes or hair wasn’t somehow in the way.

“It’ll pass,” Arthur rasped while looking like death warmed over. “I’ll be right as rain once this passes.”

“Of course you will, dear,” Linus sighed. “Unfortunately, I don’t think this will go away for you any quicker than it did for the kids.”

Arthur sighed out, or began to before his breath caught in his throat. He pulled himself over the toilet once again and gagged uselessly. It brought up a stream of bile and what little water he’d been able to drink since this all started.

“The children...”

“Are fine. Zoe is entertaining them with quite an imaginative bedtime story in the common room.”

Arthur smiled tiredly. He didn’t say a lot after that. Eventually he flushed the toilet and slumped back against Linus, feeling sweaty and warm and like a heavy weight that Linus wasn’t sure what to do with, exactly. So he just sat there and let Arthur rest.

The man’s head ended up on his lap, and Linus was pretty sure he’d fallen asleep.

It was when he started shivering, not too long after, that Linus decided it was time for them to go somewhere with less tiled floor.

“Darling,” Linus hummed, brushing Arthur’s hair back to wake him. The man opened his eyes, and his brows furrowed, either in annoyance at being woken or confusion at where they were. “I think it’s time to go to bed, don’t you?”

“Ah,” Arthur sat up rather slowly and dragged a hand down his face. “Yes, I suppose that’s a good idea.”

He was slow, and slightly unsteady as he stood and made his way back to the bedroom. He didn’t look in immediate danger of falling, but that didn’t stop Linus from hovering anxiously. He’d never seen Arthur this weak or worn out, and the past few days of little sleep surely didn’t help anything.

He hardly had a chance to get Arthur under the covers before he started falling asleep.

“Alright,” Linus said fondly. He leaned down and pressed a kiss to Arthur’s temple, earning an affectionate hum in response. “You rest. I think you’ve earned it.”

He made sure to wash his hands thoroughly. Not that it mattered much, he and Zoe were probably both doomed to catch this at some point.

Speaking of Zoe, the woman was in the middle of describing a ship full of deadly pirates as he made his way downstairs. He thought he heard the name “Glitterbeard the Pirate”, but-

“What color of glitter?” Chauncey asked, eyes wide and leaning toward her with great interest.

Zoe thought for just a moment before, “Pink and purple, I think, although nobody can say for sure. Glitterbeard defeated anyone in his path far before they could make record of his appearance.”

Linus snorted, getting all of their attention in the process.

“Linus!” Chauncey greeted.

All of the kids looked to be doing well. He knew children well enough to be aware that they weren’t at a hundred percent quite yet, but their joy and energy was coming back.

“Is Arthur dying?” Lucy asked. “He looked like he was dying.”

“Arthur is not dying,” Linus assured. “It’s nothing to worry about, he just needs some rest.”

“Sounds boring.”

“Indeed, I think he’ll have something similar to say about it tomorrow,” Zoe said. “Alright, everyone go get ready for bed.”

“Can we go adventuring tomorrow?” Talia asked hopefully.

“We’ll see how you’re all feeling tomorrow,” Linus promised.

They made their way upstairs, and Zoe let out a heavy sigh.

“Were they awfully rowdy?”

“Oh, not really,” she waved it off. “Although, I think we’ll have a riot on our hands if we don’t let them outside tomorrow.”

Linus nodded, because he knew she was right. They’d been stuck inside for a good few days, they were antsy to do anything that didn’t involve sleeping or reading or drinking broth.

“Theodore still had a fever this morning, Sal still looks exhausted,” Linus said with a frown. “With as wild as they all can be, I don’t know if taking them on an adventure would be wise.”

Zoe nodded, setting her hands on her hips. “Agreed. Time to think of a new activity, then.” She began folding the various blankets scattered around the living room, some draped over the couch and others on the floor.

“Yes, I think- oh,” he sighed. “You mean me.”

“Indeed I do, Mr. Baker,” she looked back at him with a grin. “But for now, you make sure Arthur stays in bed.”

“Oh, by the way he crashed when I put him to bed, I doubt that will be a...” he trailed off at the sound of a man’s voice upstairs, informing Lucy that his usual pajamas were in the wash, and wishing Sal a good night, telling him to get as much rest as he needs. “What...? How in the world...?” He was asleep! Deeply asleep, after nights of not having any sleep at all!

Zoe chuckled and squeezed his shoulder. “This is as good of a time as any to tell you that Arthur Parnassus is the worst patient on the planet.”

Linus groaned. “Excuse me while I bully him back to bed. Goodnight, Zoe. Thank you for keeping the children occupied.”

“Always a pleasure,” she said with a nod. “See you tomorrow.”

Linus couldn’t bring himself to interrupt Arthur as he help the kids to bed. He hardly looked able to stand, but there was no stopping him from asking each of them how they were feeling. Linus just shook his head in exasperation and waited for Arthur with a cup of water and some pills that he’d hopefully be able to keep down.

He handed it to the man wordlessly as he returned. Arthur took them without question.

“What are we thinking for tomorrow?” he asked. “It’s Saturday, after all.”

“I will find something to keep the children occupied. You will be in bed.”

Arthur chuckled. Chuckled! “I’ll be well enough tomorrow.”

“No, no no...” Linus shook his head as he pulled the covers back. “You need rest, Arthur. What have you been telling the kids? Ah, yes, don’t push your body too hard while it recovers. And they’ve been rather good at taking your advice.”

Arthur sighed. “I can’t stay in bed all day. I just can’t.”

Linus frowned. “Well, I believe that. You couldn’t stay in bed for five minutes while I went downstairs.”

Arthur smiled guiltily.

“Sleep in, at least? Can we agree on that?”

“Sure.” Linus didn’t believe him in the slightest.

-----

The next morning, Linus slipped out of bed carefully, and got dressed at quietly as possible. He made his way down the stairs, finding that a few of the children were already awake, though looking sleepy.

“Good morning!” Chauncey greeted.

“Good morning,” Linus returned with a smile. “How-”

“Arthur!”

Linus blinked, and followed the kids’ gaze to the man descending the staircase. How had he managed to get dressed so fast? He looked- well, he looked perfect!

“Are we going on an adventure today?” Talia asked immediately.

Linus still stared at him, trying to figure out exactly what kind of magic he was using to look as not-dead as he currently looked. The man was just that beautiful, apparently, even when ravaged by flu. Or perhaps he really did recover quickly. Was it a phoenix thing? Perhaps.

Arthur wandered over to him and draped an arm around his shoulders, pulling him into a lazy hug. “I think that an adventure may be a little too exciting at the moment,” the kids groaned. “But we’re certainly due for some time outside. How about a picnic?”

-----

Arthur shivered.

The children had long since abandoned the whole sitting-down-and-quietly-enjoying-nature part of the picnic. They chased each other around in a game that may have been tag, but Linus had yet to figure out what the rules were. They watched from the front steps of the house, neither of them interested in sitting on the ground any longer.

And Arthur looked cold.

Linus scooted next to him and pressed against his side, pretending to be affectionate but he really just wanted to help his fiance get warm. It wasn’t going to help, of course, but he could try. Arthur leaned into it and wrapped an arm around him. Now Linus could feel the shudders, and the fact that he was far warmer than usual (in human form, that is).

Arthur’s arm retracted as he gasped and wrapped both arms around his middle.

“What’s wrong?” Linus asked, rather panicked.

“I... it’s nothing, just a stomach ache.”

“Well, that makes sense, seeing as though you’re ill and all...” maybe he shouldn’t be teasing him. Arthur looked miserable, and Linus could swear he saw the man’s face turn grayer every second.

Arthur clenched his jaw.

“Arthur, why don’t we-”

He clamped a hand over his mouth and rushed back inside. Linus followed.

“Oh...” he sighed out in sympathy. The downstairs bathroom wasn’t too far away from the front door, but Arthur hadn’t quite made it, if the vomit on the floor was any indication. And next to it, the man heaved desperately into the toilet. He looked more distressed than Linus had ever seen him.

It didn’t take long for Arthur to allow himself to be led upstairs. Linus tucked him in just as he had the night before, only this time he would probably be less inclined to get up seconds after.

“You’re warm,” Linus said. He smoothed Arthur’s hair back, feeling the fevered heat radiate from his forehead.

“It happens,” Arthur admitted. “Fevers are my specialty.”

Linus chuckled and pressed a kiss to his head. “I’ll go get things straightened up before the children come inside.”

Arthur frowned immediately at the realization of what mess he was referring to. “Oh, I’m... I’m sorry...”

He shook his head. “I won’t hear of it. It’s only fair that I do the cleaning up after your sacrifice this week.”

-----

“How is he?” Linus looked up from his tidying to find Zoe with a sleeping Theodore in her arms. The kids were outside doing their own individual activities now, apparently having gotten tired of arguing over whether someone had been tagged or not.

“He’s... sick,” Linus shrugged. “The poor thing worked himself too hard this week.”

“As he always does,” she said with a sigh. “I’m surprised he’s still rest-”

Zoe didn’t get to finish her sentence before someone came down the stairs. Arthur, for God’s sake...

“What in the world are you doing out of bed?” Linus squeaked in exasperation.

Arthur looked rather sheepish between the two of them as he said, “I... got lonely.”

Linus sighed heavily, Zoe chuckled. “I’ll go see what chaos is happening outdoors. Feel better, Arthur.” She made her way out, leaving Linus to give his fiance the look that didn’t dare him to finish the last two stairs.

“You could have called for me,” he said, making his way up the stairs and shuffling Arthur back up as well.

“I didn’t think yelling would be appropriate.”

He couldn’t help but laugh. Linus put the man to bed once again, but he knew not to leave him this time. Arthur looked exhausted, and the stairs probably didn’t help, but it just made him more cuddly when Linus lied on the bed and let the man snuggle up against him.

Linus hummed in content and ran his fingers through the golden hair that will need a wash at some point. “Rest, darling. I’m here.”
