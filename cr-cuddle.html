Title: Cuddle
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly can't sleep
Word count: 300

Cuddle

Mollymauk had been sneezing and coughing all day. More of an annoyance for him, than anything else.

As happened with most illnesses, though, it got worse at night.

Caleb was up late reading when there was a soft knock on his door.

"Ja, come in."

Molly walked in, shutting the door behind him and plopping down onto Caleb's bed. He sniffled pitifully.

"Can't sleep. I feel awful," Molly moaned. He grabbed a pillow to hug, giving another clogged snuffle.

Caleb closed his book, stood up, and sat on the edge of his bed to get a closer look at the sick tiefling. He absently stroked Molly's hair when his hand was met with the heat of a low-grade fever.

"What's wrong?" He finally asked.

Molly sighed. "I can hardly breathe through my nose, my head hurts, I can't stop coughing and sneezing..." Another sniffle, and a weak cough when that irritated his throat. It was truly a pitiful sight.

Caleb only nodded as he gently brushed his fingers against the base of Molly's horns. And then he leaned down to kiss his forehead.

"I wish I could fix you, Mollymauk. Caduceus or Jester might be able to help you feel a little better, though."

Molly propped himself up on his elbow. "No..." He sighed out. "I already told them I don't want them wasting their magic on a head cold. I just... Ugh, I don't feel good. And I want to sleep."

"You're free to sleep in here if you think it will help."

Molly's eyebrows raised. "Really? You mean that?"

"Ja. I'm going to be reading for a while, so you can have the bed."

Caleb saw an expression flicker across Mollymauk's face. Disappointment?

"Oh..."

"What?"

"I was just hoping that you'd... You know, it's sometimes easier to fall asleep when you've got someone to cuddle with." A bit of a cheeky smile, groggier than normal but definitely there.

Caleb sighed. "Ja, alright."

Molly grinned.

"Let's get off the top of the covers, shall we? Come on, Mollymauk."

They did manage to get comfortable, eventually. Molly buried his face in Caleb's chest with a sniffle, no doubt using his shirt as a handkerchief.

"Thank you, Caleb."

"Anytime," Caleb mumbled, enjoying the warmth of his friend's feverish body. Molly was right, it was easier to fall asleep when you had someone to cuddle with.
