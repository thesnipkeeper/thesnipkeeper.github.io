Title: Gator Aid
Fandom: Sleepy Hollow
Summary: Nothing can stop Ichabod from solving mysteries, not even the stomach flu
Word count: 1.7k

Gator Aid

She wanders into the cabin, heaving a sigh as she sets the various grocery bags on the table. She wasn't sure what to prepare for when Crane said that he'd come down with something, so Abbie brought... Everything. It'll be nice to have some cold medicine and stuff around the place anyway, she decides.

"Crane?" She calls.

There's no sign of her favorite colonial friend anywhere. No sign of the usual couch nest that happens when most people are sick. Of course, Ichabod Crane isn't most people.

It's possible that he fell asleep again after calling her, despite his insistence that he'd be fine by the time she arrived to pick him up. (He sounded awful. She never had any intention of actually bringing him to work, but she wasn't going to tell him that.)

The bed is empty. Abbie starts to worry, until she hears painful coughing from the bathroom and realizes that the blankets are draped off of the mattress and trailed in that very direction.

Abbie smiles sadly at the sight. Her partner is sick as a dog, leaned weakly over the toilet and breathing heavily.

Ichabod is still in his pajamas, the loose sweats she bought for him swearing that they'd be more comfortable loungewear for summer ("loungewear, leftenant?").  His t-shirt is as drenched in sweat as the rest of him, and has an unfortunate stain going down the front.

"Damn, Crane..."

He looks up, hardly managing to be surprised to see her, before his eyes widen and he's scrambling to get over the toilet in time to throw up.

She winces and looks away for a second, and then just sighs and kneels down next to him.

Ichabod gags up a thin stream, looking so impossibly miserable and tired as if he's been doing this for a while.

"Apologies, leftenant," he croaks. He snuffles and runs the back of his hand against his mouth and nose. "I...oh, I don't feel very well." He swallows thickly, a hand going to his tender stomach. "I can- urp- I can be ready, just give me a few moments to-"

"Uh uh, you're not going anywhere. Not like this." She pulls a loose piece of hair behind his ear as he tries to catch his breath, and then reaches up to flush the gunk away before the sight makes them both sick. She hands him a wad of toilet paper to clean himself up with, and he nods thankfully. "Feel like you're done for now?"

Ichabod thinks for a few seconds before rolling his eyes and sighing. "I don't- I don't know, if I'm being honest. It hasn't really ceased since this morning."

Abbie frowns. "Crane, how long have you been throwing up?"

"What time is it?"

"It's nine."

His eyebrows go up a bit. "Ah. Well, that would make it four hours, then. Erm, off and on."

Abbie swears. “Okay, I brought some stuff that might help. Let’s get you to bed, huh?”

Ichabod looks uneasy about the idea, but the thought of being back in bed seems to win, because he nods and lets her help him up.

“Careful, if you fall I am sure as hell not carrying you.”

He gives a breathy chuckle as he braces against the sink. Ichabod blinks blearily and sighs.

“Good?”

A nod.

“Alright...”

She hovers as the two of them head to his room, watching each unsteady step he takes. Abbie leaves and grabs the small waste basket from the bathroom and lines it with a new bag before returning to him slipping on a clean shirt.

He huffs at the bin as if it’s personally offended him.

“Just in case,” Abbie assures. “But to be honest with you, I really don’t feel like cleaning up puke today.”

Ichabod makes a disagreeing noise at the thought. “I can assure you that that won’t be necessary.”

“Well, don’t make promises you can’t keep,” Abbie chuckles. “These stomach bugs can get pretty bad.”

-----

Bad indeed.

She’s never witnessed Crane this much out of commission.

“Whoa-! Careful, Crane...” she manages to grab his arm before he loses his balance completely and gets him back onto the bed.

He makes a sound, something between a grunt and a whimper. “I-”

Abbie shushes him and nods. “Yeah, I know, here...” she says setting the bin in his lap.

She can see him inwardly fighting with his pride for a few seconds before he has no choice. That waste bin is put to use far sooner than either of them would have liked.

She winces when she notices that a piece of his hair had been in the line of fire.

“Oh, oh Crane, you have some in your hair, hold on,”

He swallows thickly and rolls his eyes as she takes a tissue and runs it over the spot.

“This is humiliating.”

“Yeah,” she agrees. “But I think we’ve seen each other at worse.”

Abbie takes a hair tie off of her wrist and doesn’t bother to ask before she starts pulling his hair back. He nods appreciatively when she’s finished, running the back of his hand against his mouth.

“I am sorry about this, leftenant. There’s no reason for you to stay,” he croaks.

“Naw, I’m not going anywhere. I’m off work today and I don’t know if I want to leave you alone like this.”

Ichabod clears his throat, still recovering from the bout. “I have been ill before, you know.”

“Mhmm, and how do I know you’re not going to go find some... leech doctor or something?”

He rolls his eyes in lieu of an actual reply, and she takes the bin.

-----

“Gator-aid?”

“Gatorade, Crane. It’ll help replenish any fluids that you lost. I don’t want you getting dehydrated.”

Ichabod inspects the bright red liquid with a raised eyebrow. “I should think water can do that just as well.”

“Maybe, but this is better for right now.”

“I don’t understand-”

“Just drink the damn juice, Crane.”

He gives a tired glare before cracking open the bottle and taking a sip. It seems to go down well, because he takes another.

A calming breath. “Thank you, leftenant.”

“Mhmm, good right?”

“Delicious. And it will be nice not to die of thirst.”

She laughs. “Okay, I’m just-”

Both of their attention goes to Abbie’s phone. Jenny’s name pops up, and they exchange a quick look before Abbie answers.

“Hey, what’s up?”

“So, I dunno where you guys are, but there’s a lot of weird stuff happening at the Morrison farm, and I think you’ll want to look into it.”

“What kind of weird stuff?” Ichabod raises his eyebrows, urging for information. She waves him off and earns an annoyed look.

“All of their goats were massacred last night.”

“Goats.”

“They were drained of blood. I think whoe-whatever did this is working up to something big.”

Abbie sighs. “Look, Crane’s pretty sick and I don’t know if I can leave him alone, so-”

Ichabod is muttering something at her, eyes wide in frustration, but she just turns so she doesn’t have to pay attention.

“Oh wow, okay, I guess I could probably-”

Abbie has a feeling her sister is about to offer to drive up to the cabin, but she’ll never know, because the phone is snatched out of her hand before Jenny can finish. “Damn it, Crane-!”

“We’ll meet you at the archive, Ms. Jenny.” He ends the call before Abbie can do anything about it.

“Seriously.”

Ichabod sighs and nods, at least showing a little remorse for what he’s done. “This is more important than my illness.”

“You don’t even know what it is.”

“But we will!”

Abbie sighs.

They stop a couple of times on the way there when Ichabod is feeling particularly nauseated, but thankfully the drink seems to stay down.

She shoves him in his chair before he even has a chance to greet Jenny.

Jenny laughs at the sight. “Jesus, you look terrible.”

Ichabod snorts. “Thank you, Ms. Jenny.”

To Jenny’s credit, she’s managed to collect a fair amount of research on the strange event that helps them figure out what’s going on.

The three of them make a great team until Ichabod falls asleep.

Abbie chuckles and covers him with a nearby blanket. They keep a lot of comfort items in the archive these days, they spend enough time in here.

Noticing the flush of his cheeks above his beard, she carefully presses the back of his hand to his forehead, finding his skin dry and sickly-warm.

“He’s really not feeling great, is he?” Jenny observes.

“No,” Abbie sighs. “He spent most of today puking.”

Jenny swears, massages the bridge of her nose. “Then why did he-?”

“I think he wants to feel useful,” she says. “I think Crane isn’t sure what to do when he’s not fighting demons.”

A scoff. “Yeah well, I can relate to that.”

Abbie rolls her eyes. “Come on, let’s keep reading while he naps. I have a feeling he’s gonna want to go home again when he wakes up.”

-----

He looks well enough by that night when she takes him home that she’s comfortable leaving him to take care of himself. He thanks her for her help, and immediately falls asleep with the (thankfully capped) gatorade on the very edge of his nightstand.

A couple of days pass when Abbie returns to the cabin.

Crane is sitting cross-legged on his bed, surrounded by books that she could have sworn were at the archive.

“Ah, leftenant,” he greets with a smile.

“Hi?” Abbie picks up one of the books and inspects it. “Is Jenny enabling you? She brought these, didn’t she?”

Ichabod nods reluctantly. “She did. I just required some reading material to pass the time, and-”

“And you knew I wouldn’t let you work while you were sick.”

“Exactly.”

Abbie scoffs and drops the book back on the bed. “Okay, fair enough.” but she notices a few other things. Specifically empty bottles of gatorade flavors she hadn’t bought him. “Looks like she helped out, huh?”

“She did, yes.”

“Are you feeling better?”

“Oh,” he laughs. “Much better.”

“How’s the fever?”

“Are you my physician now?”

Abbie rolls her eyes and sits on the edge of the bed, shoving some books out of the way in the process.

Ichabod caves and answers. “A little bit of the chills remain, but I’m no longer wishing for a second death, so I believe I’m on the mend, leftenant.”

She smiles. “I’m glad. I never wanna see you that sick again.”

“And I never want to be that sick again.”
