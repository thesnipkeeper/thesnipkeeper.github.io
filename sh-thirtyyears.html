Thirty Years

 The New York institute was down a third of its shadowhunters. The defenses could be used to ward off demons and downworlders with ill-intent.

But they seemed to be completely powerless against a stomach flu. The infirmary could only do so much with mundane illnesses once someone already had it. Angel blood helped prevent illness, but that was about it. Having a good immune system didn’t always work.

Alec spent the past week doing the work of ten people, returning to Magnus’, and crashing before they could even exchange words. He’d actually woken up on the couch the night before when Magnus could bring himself to wake him and put him to bed.

Alec knew things were only going to get worse when Jace mentioned feeling off. He sent his brother to his room before anything bad could happen and began reworking all of their field plans.

Magnus’ eyes widened at the papers strewn across his coffee table. “Goodness, when you said that you were bringing work home, I didn’t know you meant all of your work.” He set a cup of coffee on one of the few spots of visible wood and kissed Alec’s cheek.

Alec scoffed. “If only this was all of it. Sorry, Magnus, this’ll all be over in a few days.”

“I hope so,” Magnus sighed. “I’ve been getting good business lately, but this is tiring.”

“It’s going around in the downworld too?”

“Mmhm.” Magnus huffed out a breath and leaned against Alec, his cheek smushed against his shoulder. Alec wrapped an arm around him without pulling his focus from his papers. “I’m exhausted. One can only make so many immune-strengthening potions.”

“You can do that?”

“Mm. They’re not very good for you, and I’ve warned everyone who’s come to me, but no one cares if it can keep them from getting sick.”

“How is an immunity potion bad for you, though?”

Magnus took a moment to yawn, and Alec realized that his boyfriend had been just as busy as he had. He’s been so focused on work that he didn’t notice that Magnus was exhausted as well.

“The immune system is supposed to learn to fight things on its own. I wouldn’t even take the stuff myself, if I’m being honest. Any potion that cures illness is going to have the nasty side-effect of getting sick more.”

“Sounds like you learned that the hard way.”

Magnus chuckled. “Oh yes. But us warlocks have naturally strong immune systems, anyway.” He sat up and dragged a tired hand down his face. “Well, I’d better go to bed before I fall asleep right here.”

Alec hummed happily when Magnus pressed another kiss to his cheek. “I’ll be here for a little longer. Sleep well!”

“Oh I will!” Magnus called as he disappeared into the bedroom.

Alec chuckled to himself and went back to work. He liked this. To be able to come home from work and have the domestic life he would have never imagined for himself.

A glance at the clock told him that he’d been working far longer than he’d planned. But, he supposed, the bright side was that it was finished, and hopefully several people would be back to work tomorrow.

He yawned and gathered his papers, putting them in a nice stack to be grabbed on his way out in the morning.

Alec was asleep the moment his head hit the pillow.

-----

The weight on the other side of the bed shifted. Alec opened his eyes, only to see that Magnus was getting up.

It was still dark out, he was probably just using the bathroom or something.

Alec went back to sleep, or had started to until he heard a cough from the bathroom. Except it wasn’t a cough. Magnus was throwing up.

Apparently not even the High Warlock of Brooklyn was immune to the stomach virus that was going around.

Alec got up, sleep forgotten, and retrieved a glass of water from the kitchen.

He hesitated before knocking on the bathroom door.

“Can I come in?”

A shaky sigh could be heard right before the door drifted open on its own.

“Oh no, Magnus...”

He took in the sight of his boyfriend miserably curled around the toilet, drool on his chin and eyes watering from the force of throwing up. Alec set the water on the sink and knelt down.

Magnus didn’t even have a chance to react to his presence before he was gagging weakly into the water. It brought up a small stream of vomit. He heaved again and again before finally another bit came up. From the looks of things, he’d already succeeded in emptying his stomach before Alec made it there.

Alec rubbed his back, finding it damp. “Take it easy,” he soothed. “Just take a breath... there we go...”

He’d done this plenty of times with his siblings. Though, admittedly, it had been a while since he was needed. Most of them were adults now and they could take care of themselves. (or that was what Jace said yesterday when Alec spent an hour sitting with him during a bad bout of sickness).

Magnus’ breathing began to calm, the muscles on his back relaxing before he flushed the toilet and sat back.

He gave a raspy thanks as Alec handed him a wad of toilet tissue to clean his face with.

“Sorry for waking you up,” he croaked. He still looked shaky and uncomfortable. Alec wanted to do anything to make him feel better again, but he’d witnessed this virus enough to know that it was just going to have to run its course.

“This came on quick,” Alec said. He rubbed Magnus’ arm, not sure how much contact he would want at the moment.

Magnus sighed. “I think I know why I was so tired today.” He swallowed hard, wrapping an arm protectively around his stomach. “My stomach’s been cramping for the past hour, I was hoping it was just...ugh, I don’t know.” His eyes slipped shut, but he search for Alec until he managed to urge the shadowhunter closer.

The moment Alec was close enough, Magnus leaned heavily against him and groaned. Alec wrapped both arms around his and kissed his head.

“Want to go back to bed?”

Magnus shook his head with a miserable expression. “I don’t think I’m done.”

“Gotcha.”

“You don’t have to stay. I think I’m going to be here for a while.”

“I’d rather not leave, if that’s okay.”

Magnus made a fond noise. “That’s perfectly fine.”

They sat silently and as comfortably as they could on the bathroom floor before Magnus whined and sat up. He doubled over with both arms pressed into his middle, face scrunched in pain. All Alec could do was rub his back as they waited for the cramp to pass.

When it did, of course, Magnus was back to throwing up again. He made a distressed whimper between heaves that had Alec at his side in an instant.

Magnus was completely worn out by the time the bout was over. Alec flushed the toilet and handed him the water to rinse his mouth out.

“Bed?”

Magnus nodded and started to get up, only to sink back down to the floor. “I- ohh,” he gave a self-deprecating chuckle that really didn’t work in his favor when he was sick. “My legs don’t seem to be...”

Alec didn’t let him finish the sentence before he was scooping him up and taking him to bed. Magnus smiled as Alec set him down.

“My hero.”

Alec scoffed. “Not too sick to make jokes, huh?”

“Never.” Magnus curled up under the blankets and gave a shaky sigh. “I’m sorry that you’re stuck taking care of me and the institute.”

“I wouldn’t trade it for the world.”

Magnus giggled sleepily. “We’ll see what you say in a couple days when you inevitably come down with the plague.”

“I’ll be fine,” Alec smiled. He squeezed Magnus’ hand and got up to grab a few things that they’d need.

Magnus, who appeared to be sleeping up until Alec returned, glared at the bucket that had been placed on his side of the bed. “You’re kidding.”

“It’s just in case,” Alec assured.

“I think I’ll be just fine without it.”

With a sigh, Alec took it and put it in the bathroom. It’d be close enough for him to grab in case Magnus changed his mind.

He climbed into bed with Magnus after everything was ready for a day of a sick boyfriend and smiled as the warlock immediately latched onto him.

-----

Magnus wasn’t in quite as good spirits the next day. He’d been either throwing up or sleeping most of the morning and early afternoon. And yet he had the strength to insist that Alec go to work despite the fact that he looked like death warmed over as he said it.

Alec answered his phone the moment he saw the name on the screen.

“Hey Izzy, everything okay?”

“Everything’s fine. A lot of people came back to work today.”

“How are you doing?”

“I can handle being the head of the institute for one day, Alec. How’s Magnus?”

“He’s not feeling too great,” Alec admitted. He held the phone on his shoulder and opened his laptop. He might as well go through his email, get a little work done.

Izzy made a sympathetic noise. “Poor Magnus. Tell him I hope he feels better.”

“Yeah, I will. He’s sleeping right now, though. Didn’t get a lot of sleep last night.” Alec yawned.

“Guessing you didn’t either?”

“I could probably go for a nap soon, but I'd rather wait until Magnus is doing a little better, you know?”

Izzy made a sound that Alec couldn’t quite decipher. “Don’t wear yourself out, Alec.” Ah, concern. “He doesn’t want you worrying about him, he just wants you.”

Alec knew she was probably right. They talked for a little while longer, chatting about work. Apparently Jace was working again, no surprise there. The guy needed to be tied to his bed to take more than one sick day.

Magnus wandered out of the bedroom, rumbled PJs and wildly unkempt hair, looking so unbelievably not-Magnus and yet absolutely adorable.

“Hey sweetheart,” Alec greeted. “Magnus is up, I have to go.”

Magnus gave a tired smile and flopped next to him on the couch. “Was that Isabelle?”

“Yeah. She’s sending well wishes your way.”

“Ah, that’s nice of her.” Magnus leaned all of his weight into Alec’s side with a soft huff.

“So how’re you feeling?”

“Awful.”

He was warm, Alec realized, and shuddering just a little. He pulled the throw off of the back of the couch and wrapped it around both of them, earning a delighted hum from Magnus.

“Thanks. I don’t know why I’m so cold all of a sudden.”

Alec laid a hand on his forehead. “The fever might have something to do with it.”

“Fever? Huh, haven’t had one of those in a while.”

“Yeah by the way, when’s the last time you’ve been sick?”

“Like this?” He bit his lip in thought. “It must have been thirty years ago. It’s not often that I spend so much time with others when my magic is depleting. Well, besides you. One of my clients must have been carrying something.”

“Or I was carrying something.”

Magnus shrugged. “We’ll never know.” He shifted against Alec before pulling his legs onto the couch. They managed to maneuver until Magnus’ head was resting comfortably on his lap. “Can I tell you a secret, Alexander?”

“Oh boy.”

“I’m glad that you stayed home today.”

Alec couldn’t stop the smile that spread across his lips. “I’m glad I did, too. You gonna get some more sleep?”

Magnus nodded against his leg. “I think so.”

Alec somehow managed to use his computer with a sleeping warlock in his lap. Having the warm weight against him sure helped with any work anxiety he felt as he answered badly-written emails from old members of the institute.

He started feeling weird halfway through the third email. It was probably nothing.

He realized that it was not nothing around the same time that he had to wake Magnus so he could get up.

Breakfast made a reappearance. He heaved up everything he’d eaten today. Did he forget to eat lunch? He may have, he hadn’t been very hungry.

“Uh oh.” Magnus was leaned against the door frame, biting the inside of his lip. He was wrapped in the throw from the living room, still shivering.

“I’m fine,” Alec croaked. He tried to pull himself up but he just ended up gagging again.

Magnus laughed. “Oh right, my mistake. You’re fine.” He wet a wash cloth and slowly sank to the floor, handing it to Alec to wipe his face. Magnus sighed heavily. “I think we need reinforcements.”

Alec swore. “You might be right.”

-----

Izzy laid a hand on Alec’s forehead, and the other on Magnus’.

“Well, you’re both sick.”

Magnus huffed out a laugh. “Thank you, doctor.”

She smiled. “I don’t have a problem sticking around if you guys are alright with it.”

Alec moaned and curled up against his stomach. “You don’t have to stay. You’ll just get sick.”

“Mmm, yeah, probably. But I know you two well enough and you’re going to need someone to stop you both from trying to take care of each other.”

“She has a point,” Magnus sighed out.

Alec couldn’t even bring himself to argue, especially not while his stomach was cramping.

“Thanks, Izzy.”

“Not a problem.”
