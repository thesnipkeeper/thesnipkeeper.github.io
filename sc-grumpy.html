Title: Grumpy
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick's handling being sick less gracefully than David
Word count: 1.1k

Grumpy

The day starts off rather unfortunately.

The two of them spend them spend the morning sniping at each other while they great ready.

Of course they both had to wake up with colds, because why wouldn’t they?

“You should eat something,” Patrick says as he slips on a sweater in lieu of his usual button-down. The reminder is much less fond than usual, and more annoyed as if David not having eaten yet has personally offended him.

“Mm, I’m just like, not very hungry right now.” It’s a lie. He’s so hungry. He’s always hungry. But his throat hurts and he probably wouldn’t be able to taste anything anyway.

David’s sitting at the table with a cup of coffee, scrolling through his phone and echoing Patrick’s sniffles with his own. He’s wearing his usual loose-fitted garb, and sweating horribly in it.

He desperately doesn’t want to go into work, and it’s pretty obvious that Patrick doesn’t want to either. But David supposes that part of running your own business is having to work no matter what.

Patrick sighs irritably. “Okay, but you’re going to get hungry later when we’re in the middle of work.”

He can’t help but roll his eyes. It makes sense that Patrick would be the type to be grumpy when he’s not feeling well, but he shouldn’t be acting like he’s the only one suffering here.

“I mean, normally I’d agree, but-”

“David-!”

David recoils, eyebrows raised, and does himself the service of looking offended.

Patrick runs a hand down his face, giving a sigh that turns into a painful cough. “Please eat something? I don’t think I can listen to you whine about being hungry today.”

David blinks. “Wow, okay then.”

Patrick at least looks like he wants to apologize, but instead he just sniffles and grabs a tissue to blow his nose while David makes himself a bowl of cereal.

“Um, are you going to eat?”

Patrick rolls his eyes, and doesn’t respond. David wants to push it so badly, but he sees the exhaustion on his boyfriend’s face and decides not to.

-----

David coughs, causing the pain in his throat to spike, enough to make his eyes tear up when he tries to swallow. He rubs his neck as if it’s going to somehow help, mumbling a, “Ugh, my throat hurts.”

“Well, mine does too, David. Doesn’t mean we can’t get work done,” Patrick snaps from the counter.

David rolls his eyes. “Okay, I wasn’t trying to start a competition.” He looks over where Patrick is flipping through their inventory list.

Patrick sniffles and swipes at his nose. David would remind him to use a tissue or wash his hands if he didn’t know he’s be snapped at immediately. He sneezes into the collar of his shirt and sniffles again, brow furrowed in annoyance.

David ends up echoing it with a sneeze of his own as well, and the whole show is just in time for the front door to jingle.

They’ve been doing their best to quiet their symptoms when they have customers. looking and sounding like germs in human form isn’t exactly good for business.

Thank God it’s just Stevie.

“Jesus, you two sound really healthy. Good to know all this organic, locally-sourced stuff is working.”

Patrick scoffs, and then sniffles.

Stevie and David exchange looks that say something along the lines of “Wow he’s grumpy today” and “I’m well aware”

“Lot of customers today?”

David shrugs. “The usual amount, really. Um, aren’t you supposed to be at the motel?”

“I just told your dad I was going for lunch. Wanted to come see how your guys’ day was going, but I think I got my answer.

David nods reluctantly, rolling his eyes back with a sigh. “Ugh, yeah,” sniffle, “We woke up sick this morning.”

“Yeesh, that’s gotta be rough.” Stevie shoves her hands in her pockets. “Well, I’m heading to the cafe. You guys can join me if you want.”

“I think I’ll stay here,” Patrick mutters. “You two can go.”

David’s annoyance faded to sympathy. Patrick sounds so tired.

“Cool. David?” Stevie asks, already halfway out the door.

“Um, yeah...” he follows her out, but not without sparing a glance at his ailing boyfriend on his way. Sure, Patrick can get cranky, but this is the first time he’s seen him like this.

-----

“Ugh, God,” David mumbles as he blows his nose into a napkin. “I don’t know how you can eat when this,” he gestures to himself, “is sitting right in front of you.”

Stevie shrugs and stuffs another fry in her mouth. “Pretty easy. Don’t forget that I’ve seen you much sicker,” she says with a smirk.

“Okay, don’t make me think about that or I’m not going to be able to eat.”

“So Patrick’s pretty pissed today, huh?”

“Mhmmm,” David answers quickly. “Very much so. Who knew he gets so grumpy when he’s sick.”

“Guess it makes sense though,” Stevie says through a mouthful of food. “Patrick’s all Mr. Control Freak. This is probably killing him.”

“Oh my God, Stevie,” he frowns, looking at her horrified. “Do you think that’s it?”

“I mean maybe. I’m not the Patrick Brewer expert here.”

It makes sense. Patrick gets so annoyed when unexpected problems arise. When he doesn’t see something coming, when he can’t prevent an issue.

He’s not just grumpy because being sick makes his mood drop.

He’s grumpy because he’s sick.

David drags a hand down his face. “I was trying to give him like, space so he didn’t feel smothered or something.” It’s possible that it just added to his bad mood. Maybe that’s the opposite what David should be doing. Maybe he needs to be trying to help. Maybe he should be smothering Patrick.

He orders a cup of soup to go, and a slice of Patrick’s favorite pie from the cafe (along with a slice for himself for late), and goes back to the store.

Patrick’s muffling a cough into his sleeve when David walks in, inventory clipboard in the other hand. His face is red when he finally looks up.

“Heyyy,” David greets with a sheep smile. He steps closer and holds up the paper bag. “I brought you some lunch. It’s soup. That’s what sick people usually eat, and you skipped breakfast, so...”

Patrick sniffles. “Thanks, David.”

David nods. “How um, how are you feeling?”

“Fine,” Patrick says with a shrug.

“Well, that’s obviously not true...”

“I just-” Patrick sighs, and David prepares to be snapped at again. But then Patrick looks at him, completely resigned. “I don’t feel great, David.”

“I know,” David hesitates before pulling him into a hug. To his delight, Patrick leans into it and wraps his arms around David’s waist.

They finally pull away when David has to sneeze. He accepts a tissue from Patrick with a sniffle.

“So you got me soup?”

“Mhm, and pie.”

“You’re the best.”

“I’m aware, thank you.”
