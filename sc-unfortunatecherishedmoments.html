Unfortunate Cherished Moments

It wasn’t often that he saw his husband this out of sorts. David - admittedly - cherished the moments that he was reminded Patrick wasn’t perfect.

Tonight he couldn’t quite enjoy it, though, because Patrick was clearly miserable.

He’d started complaining of an upset stomach at the store. He went to the bathroom three times before closing time, and started throwing up just minutes after they came home.

David had been taking care of him all night. It was hard to do anything else when he knew his husband was camped out in the bathroom.

Which was where he was now, curled up on a pile of towels on their nice tile floor, waiting for the next bout of vomiting. David reached over to lazily brush some hair off of Patrick’s forehead. He never thought he’d willingly sit with someone who was throwing up, yet here he was.

“ ‘t time is it?” Patrick asked, well, more slurred. His energy had left him hours ago.

David checked his phone. “Twelve-thirty-two.”

Patrick groaned. “You should go to bed.”

“And what? Just leave you here like this?!”

Patrick let out a sleepy huff that David thought might’ve been a laugh. “I’ll be fine. ‘t’s just a stomach bug. You should get some rest so you’re not tired tomorrow.”

David bit his lip. “I don’t know.” He shook his head. “I don’t like this. You can barely stand.”

“But...” Patrick sounded like he might’ve been about to argue again, but instead he gulped sickeningly and started to sit up.

“Alright, okay...” David muttered as he helped his husband get over the toilet. “I don’t think I’m going anywhere.”

Patrick gave a wet, queasy burp before he heaved. With a hand on his back, David could feel his muscles tighten with each one. He winced and had to look away when there was a splash of liquid against liquid. They’d been doing this for hours, so he knew the drill, grabbing a wad of toilet paper and passing it to Patrick to wipe his face with.

The poor guy looked so tired and washed out, David wondered if he should start worrying about dehydration.

“Mm’kay,” David said softly, petting his husband’s head as he spat into the toilet. “Can you possible have anything left in you?”

“I don’t know...” Patrick breathed out.

“Why don’t we both go to bed and try to get some rest?”

Patrick looked unsure, but it was clear that he longed for sleep somewhere more comfortable than the bathroom floor. He gave a dazed nod.

“Alright, up we go, that’s it...”

David held tightly onto Patrick as the two of them stood. He watched his husband blink dizzily and stumble for a bit before he regained his balance. They made their way to the bed, and David sat him on the edge before going to Patrick’s dresser to find an appropriate replacement for the shirt he’d been sweating through all evening.

“I’m sorry you have to deal with this,” Patrick mumbled as he started to pull his shirt off.

“Shhh,” was David’s only response as he handed him a clean shirt and pressed his lips to Patrick’s forehead. He was sweaty, gross, but Patrick seemed to appreciate the affection.

“I don’t think I can go to work tomorrow.”

“Um, yeah,” David laughed. “I kind of thought that was a given.”

Patrick managed to get the clean shirt on before immediately getting under the covers. He looked rather adorable now that he was comfortable, David thought.

“Let’s just both try to get some sleep,” he said as he turned the lamp off.

Patrick was asleep within seconds.

-----

David woke to sudden movement. It took his sleepy mind a while to realize what was happening, but as he woke up more he realized that Patrick was rushing to get out of bed.

From the splattering sound he heard in the hallway a second later, he didn’t quite make it.

David rubbed his eyes and sat up, knowing that they were in for another few hours of this. It was still dark out, by the clock on the bedside table they had barely been asleep for three hours.

And then he heard another sound, a new one. Sniffling, whimpering...

Patrick was crying.

David got out of bed faster than he ever thought possible.

Patrick didn’t cry, that was just a fact. David cried over lots of things, but when Patrick cried, something was terribly wrong.

He found his husband standing over a puddle of vomit in the hallway, struggling to fight back tears.

David took a careful step over the mess and laid a hand on Patrick's back.

"Hey..." He said gently.

Patrick sniffed, scrubbing his face and then shaking his head. "You can go back to bed, I can take care of- take care of..." His lip quivered, which made David close any distance that remained between them.

"Uh-uh, absolutely not," he said. "I've got this," he gestured at the floor, and then cupped Patrick's cheek. "You just go back to bed."

The tears weren't something David was okay with, as they meant that Patrick was just that worn down from illness and exhaustion.

Patrick grimaced. "David, I don't think that I'm... done."

David was already nodding and leading his husband back to the bedroom. Patrick was indeed looking queasier by the second.

"Just lie-... there we go," he said, approvingly tapping Patrick's shoulder as he got back into bed, clearly too tired to argue.

David retrieved the trash bin from underneath the bathroom sink and set it on the floor next to Patrick's side of the bed without a word. His heart tightened with sympathy when his husband immediately picked it up in set it in his lap.

He went back out to observe the damage of the hallway floor. It wasn't as much as it could have been, he thought, and then grimaced when he realized how normal this all was to him now.

The last thing he would have volunteered to do when he first moved to Schitt's Creek was clean up someone else's vomit, and yet here he was.

He slipped on the gloves they kept for cleaning (an absolute must in David's opinion), and went to work. He soaked it up with paper towels, wincing and trying to ignore the smell, before finally spraying disinfectant over the area. The sound of breathless gagging cane from the bedroom as he was putting everything back where it belonged.

Patrick was clutching the bin for dear life as he coughed up a stream of vomit. He whimpered between heaves, and David rushing to his side must have opened the floodgates.

Patrick sobbed.

David shushed him gently, only really having the energy to rub Patrick's back.

When the heaves taper off, the two of them are left in a silence only occasionally broken by a sniffle from Patrick.

"Are you... hurting?" David tried, hoping that the crying really was just from being worn out.

Patrick shook his head and then dragged a hand across his cheek to dry it. "Just tired."

David pressed his forehead against Patrick's shoulder and nodded. "M'kay." He sat up and took the bin out of Patrick's grip.

"David, I-"

"Sh, no. No way." He ran a hand through Patrick's hair. "Go to sleep," David instructed sternly, earning a small smile from his husband.

David got the bin cleaned out while giving the occasional yawn. It was late, or rather, early. He wondered how many hours of sleep he could get before he had to get up and open the store. Maybe it wasn't even worth going to bed, David thought with a chuckle, remembering back to his times as a teen raver with too many drugs and to much money.

But once he was done, his body was absolutely crying for sleep. He flopped back into bed, cuddled up to his husband's feverish body, and closed his eyes.

-----

Morning came, and David didn't even feel like he had slept. Thankfully, Patrick was still fast asleep despite the blaring alarm.

David moaned and turned it off. Usually he would take this time to complain about how much he didn't want to get up until he absolutely had to, but he didn't have that luxury today. He needed to make sure Patrick was okay, and then make some breakfast and coffee (oh God, lots of coffee) and open the apothecary.

David was careful not to jostle the bed as he got up.

He made sure water and medicine was on Patrick's nightstand and within reach, as well as the trash bin.

Patrick groggily accepted a single slice of dry toast. He nibbled on it before deciding to go back to sleep. David smiled fondly and went to make his own breakfast.

He knew Patrick would be alright for a few hours. It was just a stomach bug, after all.

Despite everything, today probably would have gone alright had he not vomited in the sink as he was rinsing his breakfast dishes.

“David, I’m fine...” he Patrick muttered sleepily. “You didn’t have to stay home.”

David bit the inside of his mouth and continued to usher his husband to the couch. Patrick had refused to stay in bed any longer, so they compromised. Having the TV could help distract him from the dumb virus anyway... and distract him from the fact that David was slowly deteriorating.

He’d watched Patrick come down with this thing. He knew what he was in for. He just had to make sure that Patrick didn’t find out, at least for a little while, because there was no way he was going to let David take care of him if he knew.

So, David ignored the dull, nagging headache and the unsettled gurgles in his own stomach as he got his husband comfortable on the couch. He made sure the bin, likely now designated the vomit bin for the rest of their lives, was nearby.

“Do you think you can keep tylenol down?” He asked, brushing some unruly curls away from Patrick’s sweaty forehead.

Patrick nodded. “Yeah, I think so. Seriously, David, go to work.”

“I don’t want to leave you here alone.” And he knew he wouldn’t make it through work. There was no way. “And Stevie’s got a sign taken care of, so we’re just going to rest and prepare ourselves for a fantastically miserable day.” For both of them.

Patrick sighed, knowing that this was a fight he couldn’t win. He didn’t usually give in that easily, but he must have been too tired to try to argue. And, David thought, maybe he did want the company.

“I guess I kind of kept you up last night, huh?”

“Mhm, and not in a fun way this time.” David chuckled, and then forced a smile when a cramp overtook his gut. It took everything he had not to curl up in the fetal position. Ugh, God, who was he kidding? “I’ll go get the tylenol,” he said before hurrying away.

The moment he was far enough up the stairs that Patrick couldn’t see him anymore, David hunched forward with a whimper. He held onto the railing and waited for the feeling to pass. The feeling did pass, but what replaced it was sudden, overwhelming nausea.

David’s eyes widened, clamping a hand over his mouth as he ran the rest of the way up the stairs and to the bathroom.

He stood over the toilet, bracing himself. David spat the excess saliva into the water below as he waited for the inevitable. Oh God, what if Patrick heard him? He reached over and turned on the faucet, hoping that that would be enough noise to cover any sounds he was about to make. That was all he had time to do before he started vomiting.

David found himself trying to hurry it along. He was just supposed to be upstairs to grab the medicine, Patrick would start to get suspicious. He choked up a mouthful of his undigested breakfast, or the part of it that hadn’t already left him.

He struggled to catch his breath when it was all over with. Ugh, that was awful.

David brushed his teeth, grabbed the tylenol, and went back downstairs

Patrick looked as comfy as he could, curled up on the couch, fluffy blanket covering his lower half as he watched TV. He glanced up at David when he returned.

“Hey, what were you doing?”

“Oh, brushing my teeth. I forgot to do that this morning. You know, like, change of routine and everything,” he said with a dismissive hand wave. And then he realized that it was true. He did forget to brush his teeth. Gross.

Patrick just gave an acknowledging hum and accepted the bottle. He swallowed two pills with a gulp of gatorade and went back to being comfy and adorable.

“So um,” David sat at the other end of the couch by Patrick’s feet, thankful to be able to rest after the whole situation upstairs. He tugged at the collar of his sweater. Was it hot in there? “Anything fun on TV?”

“Nope,” Patrick said with a chuckle. “That’s fine, though, I’ll probably fall asleep in a few minutes.”

“Ah,” David nodded.

Patrick looked up at him. “Honestly, you’re gonna be bored staying here all day.” Apparently he hadn’t entirely dropped that argument

“I’m not going to work, okay? You need me here. What if you need help getting up or something?” At Patrick’s look of ‘c’mon’ he held up a finger and shook his head. “No, I’m staying.” And suddenly, he felt guilty for making himself sound so selfless when that wasn’t the case. He added, “If anything, this gives me an excuse to take a day off.”

A sleepy giggle from his husband. “Ah, there it is.”

“Mhm.” He patted Patrick’s leg and stood up. The world spun a bit, but he grabbed onto the arm of the couch until things got less swirly. “Get some sleep. I think I’m going to do some laundry.”

It didn’t take long for Patrick to start snoring.

David went back upstairs and gathered all of the towels from the bathroom floor. He stripped the bed, and piled everything in the corner of the room. The thought of going down to the basement and back up all day to do laundry didn’t sound like a fun time, but the least he could do was prepare everything. Though, now that he thought about it, it might be better to get everything done before he was totally out of commission.

David was in the middle of loading the laundry into the washer when a fresh wave of nausea hit him. He took a slow, deep breath and continued, pressing his mouth into a thin line. David had never started laundry that quickly before.

He hated stairs on a good day, and today they were torture. David hardly had a chance to catch his breath from the climb before he was darting to the bathroom.

By the time he’d vomited everything he’d eaten in the past decade, David was exhausted. He didn’t want to leave Patrick alone, he might need something, but... David might not be any use if he was this tired. So, he trudged up the stairs (ugh) and flopped onto the bed.

Naps hated him, apparently, because David felt ten times worse when he woke up.

He sat up with a groan. The room spun, his stomach complained. Actually, his entire body complained. His headache was now very present. Oh God.

Despite all of that, David slowly got out of bed. He could hear Patrick in the kitchen, and he probably needed to steer his husband back to the couch to rest before Patrick tried to cook a four-course meal in his state.

He held onto the railing for dear life as he made his way down the stairs. The lights were on, it must’ve been dark outside, and Patrick was indeed putting something on the stove.

“I could’ve made you dinner!” David said, and then winced at the sound of his voice. Sleep and vomiting both made him sound like a dying raccoon (Whatever that sounded like).

Patrick looked up and smiled. He still looked tired, but he had some color to him now, and he also didn’t seem in immediate danger of throwing up which was always great.

“Naw,” he said with a dismissive wave. “I wasn’t going to wake you up for a can of soup.”

David scoffed. “That’s what I’m here for, isn’t it?”

Patrick chuckled and rolled his eyes. “Did you have a nice nap?”

“Not really,” David admitted. “I stripped the bed, so I had to improvise. Patrick, seriously, let me-”

“Nope!” At David’s pout, Patrick sighed. “I need to do something to stay sane, okay? I’m not used to being in bed all day.”

David raised his hands in surrender. “Fine! Fine. I’ll just sit here and watch you then.” He sat on one of the stools at the island, pretending that that wasn’t the only thing he really felt like doing.

The smell of the soup on the stove was getting to him. He definitely wasn’t hungry, that was now clear. He felt the sting of bile in the back of his throat as a tight, sickening burp made its way up. David leaned against the counter and did his best to covertly press his fist to his mouth.

“...David?”

From the annoyance in Patrick’s voice, that wasn’t the first time he’d said his name. David quickly retracted his hand and fixed his face despite the sour nausea in his stomach.

“Hm?”

Patrick raised an eyebrow. “I asked if you wanted any soup.”

“Oh, yeah, because I’m absolutely going to eat something prepared by someone who’s spent the whole day-”

Patrick hissed with regret. “Okay, you have a point.” He poured a single ladle-full into a bowl. He must not have been very hungry either. He sat next to David and stirred the soup without immediately eating it.

The closure proximity to that smell... David felt the nausea building.

“Have you eaten today?” Patrick asked.

David didn’t trust himself to open his mouth, so he just shook his head.

“You okay?”

David hesitated, and then shook his head. That was about all he could do before he was rushing out of the kitchen. It wasn’t until he was done throwing up that he realized Patrick was in the doorway, leaning against it with crossed arms and a sleepy smirk.

David groaned. “Stevie.”

“E-yup!” Patrick chuckled. He knelt down next to him, grabbing a wad of toilet paper and wiping David’s face like a little kid. David pulled away a little too late.

“Stop, I’m supposed to be-”

“Resting. Because you’re sick.” Patrick nuzzled his temple in lieu of a kiss, because frankly they probably weren’t going to be kissing each other for a while. “I saw her text when I woke up.”

David whimpered. He felt himself starting to cry, and he was powerless to stop it. Patrick’s hands appeared between his shoulder blades, rubbing in slow circles.

“You should have just told me.”

“It didn’t seem important,” David said with a teary eyeroll. He sighed. “Can we go lie down?”

“Absolutely. I’ll put the soup away.”

“Please.”

Going to lie down wasn’t the easiest thing to do. Their bedding still needed to be dried. Instead of making it up like normal, non-lazy people, they threw random blankets on the bed (including the ones Patrick had on the couch) and decided that that was good enough.

The stupid bin joined them, sitting on Patrick’s side.

But, it was comfortable. Cozy. David was glad to be able to cuddle up with his husband after the terrible day. He couldn’t quite shake the guilt, though.

“You’re important,” Patrick said sleepily. “You’re so important, David.”

David smiled. “Thank you.”

“I mean it. Don’t do that again. If we’re sick together, we’re sick together. We can figure things out.”

David bit his lip. He felt too awful to argue, especially when... when Patrick might be right.

“Okay,” he said, before the two of them fell asleep.
