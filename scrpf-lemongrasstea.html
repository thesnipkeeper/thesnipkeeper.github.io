Title: Lemongrass Tea
Fandom: Schitt's Creek RPF
Summary: Noah lets himself in
Word count: 1.8k

Lemongrass Tea

The sound of the microwave beeping is enough to exacerbate the headache pounding in Dan’s skull. He’s quick to pull out his mug and stop the incessant noise. Oh God...

He sniffles thickly. He needs to blow his nose, but that can wait until after he gets the tea brewing. His scratchy throat takes priority, he decides.

He finds the box of lemongrass tea in the back of his cupboard. It’s exclusively for when he’s sick, Dan’s not a huge fan otherwise, and it’s not often that he even gets sick. Dan finds himself huffing when he’s reminded that he’s managed to catch a cold for the first time in a year, and drops a tea bag into the steaming mug.

He scrubs his nose with a frustrated sniff, and then sighs. Out of all the times he could have caught a stupid traveling bug, he supposes now was as good of a time as ever. He has a few days off and it’ll give him enough time to recover and be back on his feet without having to work himself worse.

Doesn’t make the cold much better, though, because he still can’t breathe through his nose, and his throat still hurts, and his sinus headache refuses to go away no matter what he takes.

Dan gives a thick sniffle once more and realizes that he still needs to blow his nose. He snatches a tissue from the box on the counter-

And his phone starts ringing.

He answers the thing without even looking at the name on the screen. Anything to make the loud noise stop!

“Hello?” he breathes out in annoyance.

“Hey!”

Dan isn’t ready for the kind voice on the other end of the call. He finds himself smiling. “Noah, what’s up?”

“So, I’m in town, and I thought I could come visit?”

Dan closes his eyes and turns his head to the sky, silently swearing.

“Dan?”

“I would love to have you over, but- honestly, I’m not feeling well and I think you would be better off not coming over when I’ve been sneezing allover the house.” Fuck fuck fuck. Dan’s missed him so much, why does this have to happen to him now? Does the universe just hate him? Is that it?

“Oh, I guess I should let you rest.”

“Less about resting and more about not wanting you to catch the plague.” And between the wrinkled pajamas and chapped nose, he does not look or feel particularly human right now.

“Well, alright. Sorry to hear that. Listen, I’ve got to go, but I’ll talk to you later.”

Dan sort of wants to cry. “Right. Sounds... sounds good.”

He ends the call and growls loudly. All it does is hurt his throat and wake Red from a lazy nap. He finally wraps the tissue around his nose and gives a truly productive blow that he’s glad no one’s around to hear, and grabs his tea.

This is awful. He desperately wants to call Noah back and tell him to come over anyway. Dan hates being alone when he’s sick, but it doesn’t quite come up against the thing he hates more, getting other people sick when he himself is a germaphobe.

Dan accepts his fate of spending the day alone watching Netflix and trying every remedy to clear his sinuses. Things aren’t so bad, he decides, when Red hops onto the couch with him and curls up by his legs.

-----

Dan doesn’t realize that he fell asleep until he jolts awake with an undignified snort. His nose has plugged up on the side he was laying on, and begins to drip as he sits up. He groans and drags a hand down his face. Netflix is asking him if he’s still watching, and who knows how long that message has been up there.

knock knock

Ah, so that’s why he’s awake.

Redmond barks and runs to the door as Dan gathers his bearings. He shudders as the throw blanket falls off of his shoulders, and the room spins for a seconds when he stands. Dan snuffles and runs his nose across the back of his hand to rid of any visible wetness before he goes to tell whoever it is to go away and he’s not interested in buying any.

“Oh my lord,” he gasps. In front of him stands Noah Reid, with a plastic grocery bag in his hand and a sheepish smile, looking incredibly adorable in his casual outfit.

“Hey, can I come in?”

“I- um, sure.” One doesn’t really notice how messy their house is until they have company over. And in this case, it’s the snot-filled tissues littering the living room around Dan’s sofa nest.

“I hope this is okay,” Noah says. “I just really wanted to see you, and who knows when I’ll be back in America.”

Dan doesn’t bother biting back the smile. “Well, I’m glad that you did. I wasn’t kidding, though.” He looks anxiously at the horrible state of his living room and tries to stand in front of it to block Noah’s view. “This is basically a petri dish right now.”

Noah just chuckles. He’s never cared about that kind of stuff nearly as much as Dan did, which always confused him, because how could one not care about germs?

“I don’t think you realize how clean your house is compared to mine.”

“Visually, maybe, but you can’t see germs.”

Dan follows him as he makes his way to the kitchen and sets the bag on the table. He pulls out a bottle of cold medicine and a can of Campbells and Dan sort of wants to kiss him.

“It’s a can because I’m a terrible cook.”

Dan can’t help but laugh. “Why did- why?”

“Well, you said that you were sick! I was going to come anyway, but I didn’t want to show up empty-handed!”

Laughing causes his nose to run even more. Dan resigns himself to getting a tissue and pressing it to his nose while Noah goes to work heating up the soup.

“Sorry,” he says from behind the tissue as they make eye contact. He must look so disgusting right now.

“Yeah, how dare you,” Noah says as seriously as he can muster. He smiles rather cutely after, which causes Dan to smile in return even though his nose is currently trying to drip down his face.

He doesn’t have a chance to say anything before he sneezes suddenly. Thank fuck he had a tissue.

“Bless you,” Noah says, voice soft and genuine. “Why don’t you go sit down and I’ll bring the soup out when it’s done? You look tired.”

Dan wipes the underside of his nose. The couch does sound good, and he’s eager to wrap himself up again after the chills are running through him.

“That’s a very nice way of telling me I look like shit, thank you.”

“Well!” Noah throws his hands up in surrender. “I mean... you look tired, Daniel. Go on, go rest like a proper sick person.”

“I just woke up from a nap. I don’t need-” Dan sighs. “Aren’t we supposed to be catching up? And instead you’re making me fucking soup.”

“Maybe I’m making you fucking soup because I really wanted soup and my hotel room doesn’t have a kitchen.”

“Considering the number of restaurants in this neighborhood alone... un- hhhh- untrue-” He has to twist away to avoid sneezing on the kitchen, or Noah.

Noah laughs. “Just go, go on. Before you infect the food!” he makes a shooing motion with his hands, and Dan is powerless to fight against the idea of sitting down again.

He tries not to think about how much Noah can hear from the other side of the room as he sniffles and coughs, blowing his way through a million more tissues. It’s a true symphony of illness.

Dan coughs into his arm before accepting the bowl of soup. “Thank you,” he rasps. His voice is... leaving him. Not a great deal for someone who’s career depends on talking.

“I also brought this,” Noah says, setting the little red bottle on the coffee table.

They eat and chat, and Dan’s voice really starts to go.

He brings a hand to his throat and winces. “God, sorry...”

Noah frowns. “Yeah, you don’t sound too good. Want me to make some tea?”

Dan clears his throat. “I can make myself some tea.” he doesn’t give Noah a chance to protest before he throws the blanket off of himself... and promptly shudders. “Fuck... it’s cold.”

“Dan,” Noah says his name just to get his attention. There’s a hand on his forehead the moment Dan turns to face him. “You feel kind of warm.”

“What, like a fever?”

Noah worries his bottom lip. “Yeah...here, take some of this and I’ll go make you some tea, okay?”

“You don’t have to do that...”

“I know, but I want to.” his hand travels down to cup Dan’s cheek, and he offers possibly one of the cutest smiles in the entire world. That’s the thing about Noah, he’s so impossibly genuine that it’s hard to argue with him.

So Dan reads the dosage on the bottle while he listens to Noah putter around in the kitchen.

“What kind of tea would you like, Mr. Levy?”

Dan chuckles. “Lemongrass, please.”

“You got it!”

Dan downs the medicine with a grimace. He tries to sniffle, but his nose has decided that it’s completely clogged now so it really doesn’t do anything.

A sympathetic sound gets his attention, and he looks up to see Noah with a steaming mug in his hand. “You’re shivering...”

“Ugh, yeah... look, you don’t have to-”

“Dan,” Noah interrupts softly. He sits on the couch and is careful as he hands him the tea. “I think now is the time for me to admit that I’ve been a little homesick while I’ve been here...”

Dan sniffs, the steam making his nose run. “Oh. That’s... that’s kind of adorable.”

Noah rolls his eyes. “So yeah, that’s why I wanted to be here so badly. Seeing someone from home definitely helps.”

“I see.” Dan smiles. “Well, I’m enjoying this, so...” He leans against Noah, shuddering and preparing to sap his body heat. “Your ulterior motives are fine with me.”

He ends up with his head in Noah’s lap, feeling fingers card through his hair as a movie plays that neither of them are really paying attention to.

“Bless you,” Noah says.

“snf! thank you, sorry.”

“Need a tissue?”

“Oh God, my nose is running, isn’t it?”

Noah bites back a smile. “Little bit, here...”

Dan takes the tissue and presses it against his nose without bothering to blow. He’s so congested that it probably wouldn’t help anyway.

“You know you’re going to catch this, right?”

Noah scoffs. “Small price to pay,” he says, pulling the blanket further over Dan’s shoulders as he starts to shiver again.

“Well, you say that now.”
