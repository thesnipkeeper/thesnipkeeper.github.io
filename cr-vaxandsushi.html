Title: Vax and Sushi
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Vax eats sushi.
Word count: 400

Vax and Sushi

Vex didn’t even bother to knock before she let herself in and took a sit on the edge of the tub, smirking at her brother who was currently hunched over the toilet. His pajamas were sweated through, damp hair tied back in a lazy ponytail. 

“I did tell you not to eat that shit,” she said smugly.

He groaned and heaved up another mouthful of bile into the bowl. “I know,” he croaked. “But you’re not in charge of what I put in my body.”

Vex hummed. “But perhaps I should be,” she chuckled. Though it was a humorous situation, she couldn’t help but worry as her brother sat there, unmoving except for the heavy breathing. “Are you alright?” she asked more seriously.

Vax sighed and backed away from the toilet to lean against the wall. He folded an arm over his cramping middle. “Yes,” he drew out, as if that were a ridiculous question. “Gonna be… yeeting my stomach contents for a while, though.”

Vex snorted. 

“I’m very thirsty…”

“You shouldn’t drink until you’ve stopped throwing up,” Vex said regretfully. “It’ll be harder on your stomach.”

Vax groaned. “Fine.” His stomach gurgled, and he winced, hunching over until both arms were pressed into his abdomen. “Er… Vex… do you mind getting out of here?”

At first she was confused, she recognized the look on her brother’s face and immediately understood. 

“Of course. I’ll be out here if you need anything.”

“New intestines would be nice,” he muttered, already untying the waistband of his pajama bottoms.

Vex turned on the TV, putting the volume up a little louder than usual to give her brother some dignity. It wasn’t as if they hadn’t witnessed each other in the worst conditions possible, but it didn’t make it less embarrassing.

Vax emerged a half hour later. He was even more pale than usual, and looked completely exhausted as he flopped onto the couch next to his sister. 

“Feeling any better?”

Vax shook his head. “Everything hurts. And I’m cold.”

Vex hummed thoughtfully. She went to his room and pulled out a clean blanket from his closet as opposed to grabbing one off of his bed. They were still damp with sweat, she should probably change his bedding completely at some point.

She threw the blanket over him, and he pulled it away from his face and snuggled into it. “Thanks.”

“So, will you listen to me next time I tell you not to eat something?” 

Vax rolled his eyes. “Probably not.”

“Fine, be that way, but I will always be right.”

A scoff. “You keep thinking that, dear sister.”
