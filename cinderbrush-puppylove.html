Puppy Love

Cameron strolls into the bathroom, hands in the pockets of his jacket. The sound of retching is clear as soon as he steps in. He sighs and locks the bathroom door. People might be pissed, but he doesn’t particularly care right now, or ever.

He sees the bottoms of heeled boots as he looks under the one occupied stall. He had an idea of what to expect when Jamie asked to be excused from class as quickly as they did.

“Are you drunk?”

Jamie spits loudly before responding. “No, I’m not drunk.”

They don’t even sound surprised that Cameron followed them into the bathroom. The two of them have been through this enough times. Jamie’s shown up to school fucked up before, but Cameron isn’t one to judge. He’s just there to make sure his friend doesn’t choke on their own vomit.

“Okay,” Cameron says, folding his arms and leaning against the wall. “Then why are you barfing?”

Instead of an answer, Cameron hears a forceful heave that must have been painful, followed by the trickle of liquid hitting liquid. Jamie spits again.

“Fuck...” they mutter. They tear off some toilet paper, likely to wipe their mouth. “I think I’m sick. Some kind of... some kind of bug.”

“Oh,” Cameron stands up straight and furrows his brow. Out of all the reasons for Jamie to be puking, why was that the last thing he would have thought of? Now that he thinks about it, he has heard of a few other people getting sent home. Af had missed a couple days of school last week because they puked in class. “Damn, sorry man.”

Jamie pukes again. Cameron winces.

“Want me to fuck off?” Cameron asks after a few awkward seconds of listening to his friend vomit.

A chuckle. “Doesn’t matter to me. You-” they cut themselves off with a low moan. Their voice is a little more broken when they say, “My stomach’s killing me.”

Cameron frowns. The whole situation is a little less amusing once he knows that Jamie is in actual pain, and from a stomach bug of all things. “Hey man, I can go get the nurse or something...”

“Oh, she’s not gonna help me.”

Cameron thinks about it and realizes they’re right. Everyone knows that Jamie Wrenly is involved with drugs, even if not all of them know how. The nurse would draw her own conclusions fast and then be the least helpful person on the planet.

Also, Cameron has a feeling that Jamie wouldn’t be cooperative. At all.

So, he waits for a few minutes until Jamie stops throwing up, and by then there are a couple of insistent knocks on the door. Cameron simply unlocks it and people are apparently so desperate to piss that they only make one comment about it.

“You two getting it on in here or something?” a guy mutter as he hurries in.

Cameron follows the guy’s quick glance toward Jamie, who’s stepping out of the stall. Their makeup is almost non-existent at this point, especially their lipstick, and they look rough. Their face is red, clothes wrinkled, and they’re clearly exhausted.

-----

“You didn’t have to drive me home,” they say, though the relieved smile is evident on their face now that they don’t have to walk home.

Cameron shrugs. “Eh, it was an excuse to get out of class.” School was almost over, anyway. “And an excuse not to go home yet.”

Jamie nods and Cameron can feel the meaningful glance that has him shifting under the scrutiny. It isn’t fair that Jamie can be so good at reading people when they’ve got a bag in their lap in case they puke (Cameron is suddenly thankful he didn’t clean his car when his mom told him to or he wouldn’t have any random grocery bags around).

“You know, you can stay at my place if you need to.”

“Thanks, but I don’t think that dad would-” he stops. Dad’s on a business trip. Simply remembering it brings a wave of relief over him. “Oh shit, dad’s not home. Yeah, sure.” Mom won’t be thrilled that he’s staying at Jamie’s, but she’s less likely to murder him over it.

Jamie smiles. “Sweet. I could use the company.”

They get set up on the couch, and Cameron wastes no time dragging the nearest trash bin next to his friend.

“Ew, what?”

“Dude.”

“I am adult, I can make it to the bathroom on my own.” The wave of regret the moment that they say that is apparent on their face, and Cameron just smirks.

“Yeah, ‘cause I never had to scrub puke out of my carpet after your drunk ass-”

“Look, that was one time.”

Jamie had climbed in through Cameron’s bedroom window of all places in the middle of the night. They were ridiculously wasted and out of it. Cameron spent the rest of the night trying to help his friend sober up while trying not to make enough noise to wake his dad. They both somehow managed to get through the night without being found out, and Jamie somehow managed to go to school the next morning as if they hadn’t been drunk out of their mind the night before.

“But it happened, so don’t complain about the fucking puke bucket.”

Jamie, surprisingly, does not, which is a true testament to how awful they must be feeling. Instead, they fold their legs under them and then wrap their arms around themself. They’re shivering. Cameron wordlessly takes the throw blanket from the armchair and throws it to them.

Jamie moans as they wrap themself up in the blanket that doesn’t seem to be doing much. “I feel like shit.”

“No way,” Cameron deadpans.

They scoff. “Ugh, fuck you.”

Cameron chuckles and flops onto the couch next to them. “I think Af had this thing too, so it must be making its way around school.

Jamie simply nods, a strange expression on their face without making eye contact. Cameron narrows his eyes at them and sits up to lean in close enough to make them uncomfortable.

“You and Af, huh?”

“No,” they scoff again, but its followed by a not-so-confident smile. This bug is wearing them down enough for Cameron to get the juicy stuff, apparently. “Maybe. I don’t know. I just brought them their homework last week and then their dad asked if I wanted to stay for dinner and damn it, have you met Af’s dad?”

“He’s the best,” Cameron agrees.

“Exactly, even I can’t say no to someone so nice.”

“I would’ve thought you’d have something against him for, you know...”

“Nah,” they sigh. “He didn’t- well, he did do something, but... I don’t know.”

“So,” Cameron clears his throat and fails to bite back a smirk. “Since when do you bring people their homework? Since when do you care about homework at all?”

Jamie rolls their eyes. “Stop.”

“I’m just saying that-”

“Cam-”

“You two-”

“Cam-!”

“No, c’mon, you obviously have a soft spot for Af and I actually think it’s kind of-”

“Cameron I need the fucking bucket now!” They ground out in a panic, clamping a hand over their mouth with wide eyes.

Cameron immediately gets it in their lap, and then they immediately vomit up a stream of something that looks like last night’s dinner.

“Oh God...” they mutter, dragging a lazy hand across their mouth. “Oh Goood... I hate this...”

“So you two hung out or something?”

“Really?” Jamie rasps. “While I’m throwing up?”

Cameron shrugs innocently. “Doesn’t stop me from wanting to know what the fuck happened between the two of you.”

Jamie groans as their forehead bumps the edge of the bin. “We watched a movie together.”

“Oh wow. Sounds like a date.” At this point, he’s just egging them on for his own entertainment.

“It wasn’t a-!” Jamie spits into the bin and sets it down, wiping their mouth once more. “It wasn’t a date. They asked if I wanted to stay and watch Terminator and I- well, for some reason I couldn’t say no.” The last part is a little bit quieter, an admission that has Cameron grinning.

The idea of Jamie sitting and watching a movie with Af is rather adorable, Cameron decides.

“Huh.” He sits back.

Jamie hums in agreement. They shudder and pull the blanket tighter around themself. A couple minutes of comfortable silence go by as they both get comfortable, Jamie against Cameron’s shoulder.

“Did I ever tell you that we made out?”

Cameron freezes, processing the words that just came from his friend. “No, what the fuck?”

Jamie chuckles and nods against his shoulder. “Yep. The party, that night it all went to shit.”

“When were you gonna tell me that?”

“I mean,” they sigh. “I wasn’t sure if it mattered. We were both high at the time, and it’s not like I haven’t done that before.”

But it does matter, because they’re telling him about it. He grins, and they can’t even tell because they’re half asleep against his shoulder.

“Huh,” he repeats.

-----

Neither of them realize that they’ve fallen asleep until they wake to a knock on the door.

Jamie moans. They feel a little warmer now, like maybe their fever’s up, or maybe it was just because the two of them were all snuggled up for - Cameron glances at the clock - an hour. Alright, not too long.

“I’ll get it,” he says, because Jamie barely looks aware of what’s happening. They also look kind of close to blowing chunks again.

Cameron is ready to look like his most proper, respectable self for whoever is knocking on the Wrenly house. He doesn’t expect Af to be standing in front of him.

Their eyes widen. “Cameron! Heyyy...” They laugh awkwardly and fidget with the handles of a canvas bag in their hands. “Uh, is... Jamie home?”

Cameron opens his mouth to answer, but his friend’s voice cuts him off.

“Af?” Jamie is standing behind him with a raised eyebrow and an almost anxious (has Jamie ever been anxious?) air to them. “What...?”

“Uh!” Af panics. They step in and let Cameron close the door behind them. They quickly shove the bag toward Jamie, who just stairs at it in confusion. Chances are, Jamie’s brain is still foggy from sleep and illness and their crush showing up to their house. “I brought some- some stuff ‘cause I heard that you were puking at school and like, I had um, gatorade and stuff leftover from when I was-” they sigh, shoulders falling. “I’m sorry, man! I really didn’t mean to get you sick.”

Jamie stands there for a good few seconds with a blank expression before Cameron sees the smallest of smiles tug at their lips. “Uh,” they chuckle. “Thank you, I think? Hey, if you wanna stay... I mean, I’m just going to be throwing up all night, but-”

“Oh, yeah, totally! That’d be awesome!”

Cameron bites back a smile. “I’ve gotta head home, so...”

“I’m not kicking you out,” Jamie says.

“Nah, I’ve got homework. And dad’s not home, so I should enjoy that while I can. You guys have fun!” He squeezes Af’s shoulder with a smirk before walking out.

He’s never seen Jamie fall this hard.

 

The four of them end up at the diner a few days later. Cameron an Sasha are sharing an order of chili fries while Jamie keeps their eyes averted from the table at all times.

They still aren’t feeling a hundred-percent, but they couldn’t stand being stuck in the house another minute. So, when Cameron mentioned that he was going to the diner with Sasha and Af, Jamie jumped on the train immediately.

Jamie’s still looking pale and tired, and Cameron knows that their stomach is still sensitive, but they do look better. And... and much closer to the werewolf sitting next to them.

Jamie swallows hard and Af turns to them. “You okay?”

A nod. “Just tired.”

“Do you want me to stay over again tonight?”

Cameron and Sasha exchange raised eyebrows. Again, huh? He grins.

“You two have been spending a lot of time together lately, hm?” Sasha hums, lazily stirring her shake and not bothering to make eye contact with either of them. She has that knowing smile on her red lips that says she already knows the answer.

“Oh God, don’t even,” Jamie hisses.

“I mean, not a lot of time!” Af defends.

She giggles and sets her chin on her palm, looking them over as if calculating. Finally, she says, “I think you two make an adorable couple.”

The next ten minutes at the diner is spent listening to Af and Jamie talk about how much they are not a couple. That lasts up until Jamie stops talking altogether to bring a jewelried hand to their mouth and nudge Af out of the booth. Cameron knows that look well, and so does Af, apparently.

“Oh shit, Jamie?”

Jamie gives a near-silent whine as they remove their hand and slowly get out of the booth.

“Dude, are you okay?” Cameron asks.

Jamie nods while not looking okay at all, raising a find before heading off to the bathroom.

Af frowns, chewing their lip. “I feel like shit,” they say.

“Please don’t tell me that you’re going to puke too,” Sasha says.

“No! No, I mean, because they caught it from me and it’s lasted way longer for them than me.”

“I feel like it should surprise literally no one that you’re healthier than the local drug dealer,” Cameron deadpans. “They’re fine, Af.”

Af bites their lip again, eyes going to wear Jamie had rushed off to.

Cameron sighs. He’d be lying if he said that he didn’t feel just as useless. And this time Jamie’s illness wasn’t even their fault. “Look, don’t tell them that I told you this,” Cameron says. Af perks up curiously. “They really fuckin’ hate being alone when they’re throwing up.”

“Oh thank God,” Af breathes out. “I was scared that I was bugging them.”

Jamie isn’t one to get emotional, but he’s seen them wrecked enough times to know.

Af gestures to the other side of the diner. “I’m gonna... sorry guys, I think I’m gonna drive them home as soon as they...”

Cameron and Sasha nod. “Go on, little pup,” Sasha says.

They exchange smiles.

“This is going to make senior year interesting...”

“Totally,” Cameron agrees.
