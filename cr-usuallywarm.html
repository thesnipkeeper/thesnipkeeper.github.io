Title: Usually Warm
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Caduceus outs his relationship with Pumat, but it's worth it
Word count: 800

Usually Warm

“We were hoping you could tell us how much something like this would be worth,” Fjord said, pulling the magical cloak out of the pink bag on Jester’s back. He laid it on the counter in front of Pumat prime. 

“Oh, would you look at that - ahem - one moment...” he inhaled shakily and scrambled to pull a rather colorful handkerchief out of his pocket, just in time to muffle a barking cough. 

The group exchanged glances and winced. 

“That sounds really bad,” Jester said with a frown. “Are you okay, Pumat?” 

Pumat furrowed his brows for only a second, cleared his throat and stuffed the kerchief away. “Oh, I’m just fine. You’re just catching me on the tail-end of a cold.” 

“You’re not contagious, right?” Beau asked, crossing her arms. 

Pumat shook his head, giving a hoarse chuckle, much less hearty than usual. “I’ve had it long enough, it’s probably just some residual, ah...” He waved a hand vaguely in front of his face. “Anyway, let’s take a look at this nice piece of tailored magic!” 

They browsed the shop for a while as he inspected the cloak, first casting an identifying spell on it as they expected, and then looking carefully at the linings and stitching. Caduceus had a feeling that those didn’t have anything to do with its magical properties - maybe he just appreciated well-tailored clothing.

Everything was alright until a sputtering sounded from the counter. Pumat had his face stuffed in a kerchief, eyebrows furrowed as he coughed and coughed. It didn’t sound like he was getting a breath in between, Caduceus worried. People needed air, people needed to breathe...

Finally, Pumat breathed. He patted his chest and shook his head as if to clear it.

“Apologies,” he said, clearing his throat excessively. “I don’t know where that came from at all...” Pumat turned his head and muffled one last cough into his sleeve. It was hard and painful-sounding.

Caduceus frowned. He remembered a cough like that. He was a child and his father had gotten sick. It started out as a tickle in his throat, until Cornelius was bedridden and unable to do anything else but cough.

So Caduceus didn’t hesitate before walking up to the counter and placing a hand on Pumat’s forehead. The other firbolg raised an eyebrow at first, but he leaned into the touch before long. 

Caduceus hummed. “You’re really warm.” He kept his voice low, some people were embarrassed by such things being broadcasted, he’d learned, and he admired Pumat enough to want to be as kind and discrete as possible

Pumat smiled. “Well, respectfully Mister Clay, I’m usually warm.”

Caduceus shook his head. “No... I’m pretty sure this is a fever.” Thankfully, Pumat allowed him to feel his neck and cheeks. It was definitely a fever, Caduceus realized, and with a cough like that… “I’d like to cast a healing spell on you, if that’s okay?”

Pumat suddenly looked alarmed. “I’m not that bad off, am I, Mister Clay?”

“Not sure, but it would sure offer me some peace of mind if you allowed me to help.”

Pumat hardly took a moment to think. he shrugged. “Well, alright! If you think it’ll help, pumpkin.”

Caduceus felt heat go to his face even as he tried desperately not to blush. The pet names got him good. He looked around to make sure none of the other group were listening to the conversation.

Nott and Fjord were teasing each other, Caleb was petting Frumpkin, and Jester and Beau were looking at a set of enchanted armor.

No one heard.

Caduceus nodded, and realized it was hard to be discrete when you were loudly saying the words to a spell. Still, he tried not to think about that as he waved his hand the way he learned to do when he was younger, and spoke as quietly as he could. He laid a hand on Pumat’s arm, feeling the healing energy go from his hand to his firbolg friend’s body.

Pumat breathed a sigh of relief. “Wow, you’re right. I feel a lot better. Thanks to you, Mister Clay!”

Caduceus smiled. “Oh, it was no problem.” He blushed as Pumat leaned over the counter to press a kiss to his cheek, turning his head to allow the other easier access when...

Oh. 

Everyone was watching.

They all awkwardly went back to what they were doing. It wasn’t as if Caduceus was trying to hide his and Pumat’s... relationship from the others, he decided. It was alright if they knew. He just wasn’t prepared for the questions that he’d be receiving their whole walk back to the inn.

“I can uh,” Pumat chuckled. “I can offer you five-hundred gold for this cloak. Is it a deal?”

Fjord broke his ‘busy’ facade and  walked over. “Yeah! Yeah, that should work. Thanks, Pumat.”

And the six of them were off. The first couple minutes of their walk was silent, and Caduceus really hoped that they’d forgotten when they’d seen, or decided not to acknowledge it. He wasn’t great at answering questions about himself.

It was all very nice until Nott piped up. “So you and Pumat, huh? Honestly, it makes sense.”

Caduceus chuckled. Here they go...
