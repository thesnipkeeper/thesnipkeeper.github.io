Title: House Call
Fandom: Gilmore Girls
Summary: Luke is completely out of his element when Rory calls him for help, he does pretty good anyway
Word count: 2.6k

House Call

Luke found himself bored at the end of the day. It was a strange feeling, really, because he'd often end a day of working at the diner with a heavy sigh and sometimes a beer. Instead... He couldn't get himself to sit down and do nothing.

Part of it may have had to do with the fact that Lorelai Gilmore had been at a conference all weekend, recommended for inn employees.

Not once had he gotten to tell someone that what they were ordering was supremely terrible for them, or have ridiculous, complicated banter that'd inevitably end in one of them scoffing and leaving the room.

Sure, he could do those things with someone else, but Lorelai was one of the few people he was able to enjoy it with.

He shook his head at the thought of missing her, because he didn't, and finished up the last table of the night. 

He was just ready to go upstairs and grab a beer when the phone rang.

"We're closed."

"Luke? Hey it's… It's Rory."

Luke frowned immediately at the sound of her voice. Something was wrong, and she was home alone.

"Rory, hello. Everything okay?"

"Um," not a great start to the answer. "I'm sorry, Luke.  I was freaking out and you're the first person I thought to call but I can just call Babette because she's next door and she probably-" her voice tightened and cracked near the end of the ramble.

"What's wrong?" He asked, hiding the panic in his voice.

"Nothing, I'm just sick and I think I have a fever and I’m really dizzy and I tried calling mom but her phone isn't on."

"You're sick," he said, more to himself. His brain was already going through ways to handle this situation. "Okay, uh, listen Rory, I'm gonna drop some stuff off and when I get there, I'll see I can get a hold of your mom."

She breathed a shaky sigh of relief. Jesus, the poor kid was crying. "Thank you, really, I didn't mean to bother you..."

"You're not bothering me, Rory. Are you okay until I get there?"

"Y-yeah, I'm just on the couch."

"Alright, stay there."

“Not a problem. I’m kinda stuck here.”

They said goodbye. Luke felt a swell of worry in his stomach, the idea of Lorelai’s kid being alone and miserable. And then a moment of terror when he realized that he'd just made himself responsible for Lorelei’s kid, and if he somehow screwed up and made things worse, he wouldn't live to see the next day.

He took a second to think about what to bring to the Gilmore house, and another second to realize that he had no idea what he was doing.

-----

Luke knocked on the door, and then swore at himself when he remembered that she wouldn’t be able to answer the door. He tried the handle, however, and found it unlocked. That was a concern for another day.

“I brought... stuff?” he announced, knocking on the open door as he walked in.

The TV was on, playing some infomercial for a cooking appliance only stupid people bought. He balanced the box on one arm as he closed the door behind him. Rory’s head popped up over the arm of the couch and smiled.

“I’m sorry,” she said again. “I freaked.”

He wanted to tell her that he was literally the last person who should’ve been called. Anyone else in the town besides Luke Danes would know how to help a panicking sick teenager better than he did. But instead, he shook his head and set the box down on the coffee table, sliding some empty mugs aside in the process.

“No apology necessary. How’re you feeling?”

The amount of blankets she’d wrapped herself in and the bright red patches on her cheeks should’ve been an answer itself. The poor kid looked terrible, and the worst part was that she was still trying to look like her usual lively self.

“Um... I dunno. Sick. Bad. I feel bad.”

He nodded. “That’s pretty obvious. Have you been able to get a hold of your mom yet?”

She shook her head, and then coughed suddenly, pulling her arm to her face to cover it. That wasn’t the kind of cough that came from a cold, Luke thought. This kid looked like she had the flu. “I haven’t tried in a while, though.”

“Gotcha,” he cleared his throat, setting his hands on his waist as he decided what in the world to do now. “U- um, I brought soup? And ingredients to make homemade electrolyte solution. You’ve been drinking water, right? Staying hydrated?”

“Tea?” she asked with a wince.

Luke raised an eyebrow. “What kind of tea?”

“Herbal?” she looked hopeful.

He nodded his head sideways in indecision before, “Yeah, alright. You hungry?”

Rory glanced at the soup as if it’d murdered her family. “Not really.”

“Stomach’s feeling weird, huh?”

“A little, yeah.”

What did he do now that she wasn’t going to eat? He tried to look like he wasn’t as lost as he was, but Rory could clearly read him from where she was laying. He tried harder, nodding decidedly.

“In that case, I’m gonna grab a bucket and then try to call Lorelai.”

“I don’t need a bucket,” she practically laughed. “I just don’t have an appetite, Luke, it’s- it’s fine.”

He narrowed his eyes and hummed in thought. “Alright, if you’re sure.”

He left her to get comfortable on the couch once again while he dialed Lorelai’s cell and took the box to the kitchen.

“Hey, sorry I didn’t call back sooner, but I got roped into a conversation with the most boring man on the planet and he held me hostage with all these stories about kinky customers and I immediately had to go take a shower in my room just to wash off the thought of any of it and-”

“Lorelai,” he said, probably louder than necessary. He lowered his voice in case Rory was sleeping or something.

“Wha- Luke? Is that you?”

“It’s me,” he confirmed as he put the container of soup in the fridge. The very bleak fridge, but that also was a conversation for another day. “Look, Rory’s really sick and she called me all freaked out.”

“What?”Lorelai gasped. “Rory’s sick? How sick?”

“Think it might be the flu,” he admitted. “Few people around town have been down with it.”

Lorelai made a sympathetic noise. “The one weekend I’m not there, and she goes and gets the flu.”

“The nerve,” he deadpanned. “I think she’s doing that thing she does where she pretends she’s fine, but-”

“-but she’s having a total meltdown on the inside, yeah. And I’m sure it’s kinda warranted this time. Ugh God, my poor baby. Has she eaten anything?”

“I brought soup, she says she’s not hungry.”

Lorelai made an ‘m’hmm’ noise. “Get a bucket.”

“She said she doesn’t-”

“Get a bucket. That kid doesn’t know crap about her sensitive stomach when she’s sick. If she feels weird, she’s gonna puke. Trust me, I have made the ‘no bucket’ mistake and paid for it horribly.”

Luke winced. “Uh, got it.”

“Do you know if she’s taken her temp yet?”

“Not sure,” he said. God, was that something he was supposed to ask? Of course he was, that’s what you did when someone was sick, you took their temperature. “I’ll do that.”

“Hey Luke,” Lorelai said, softer.

“What?”

“Thanks. For taking care of her. I know this isn’t really your area of expertise.”

“Yeah well...” he sighed heavily, and kept his voice low as he said, “I don’t know why she called me of all people.”

“She trusts you! It’s sweet!”

He wasn’t sure what to think about that.

“Okay, let me talk to my kid.”

“Oh, right...” he rushed to the living room and gently nudged Rory’s arm with the phone. “ ‘t’s your mom.”

She sat up groggily, looking at him and then the phone. Luke realize with horror that there were tears in her eyes. Oh God, he wasn’t ready for this. She was crying!

“Mom?” the voice that came out was much more broken than it was a few minutes ago.

Luke suddenly felt uncomfortable being there, so he put himself to work grabbing the bucket from under the kitchen sink (he sat it in the living room, but just under the arm of the couch so Rory wouldn’t immediately spot it) and mixing up the electrolyte drink.

He squeezed some lemon juice into a glass before filling it with water. He added the honey, and then the salt, and then more honey in case Rory was able to taste the salt (and there could never be too much honey in a case like this). It could be in the fridge for later, and hopefully it would help when it was needed.

He’d spent so long using that drink to remedy hangovers and get himself through rough days at the diner when he was sick, he never considered making it for someone else.

There was a sob from the other room, and a sad “I miss you,” that made Luke’s heart clench. He stood awkwardly in the hallway as Rory finished the phone call, scrubbing tears from her already-reddened face in the process. “Okay...” she said to the phone before looking back at him and holding it up. She opened her mouth to speak, and then seemed to just give up and hand it to him as words wouldn’t come out properly.

He took the ‘Lorelai wants to talk to you’ message anyway and took the phone from her before turning to give the poor kid some privacy.

“Hey.”

A sniff. Damn, now Lorelai was crying too. “God, I hate that I’m not there.”

“She’ll be fine, Lorelai, I promise. You’ve got an entire town willing to make sure she’s okay.”

“I know, I know...”she cleared her throat. “And I know that she’s sixteen and all, but I’m just not used to- to not being there.”

“Everything’s fine,” he assured. “I’ll call Sookie, and she can-”

“No. God, no... I love Sookie but she’s terrible around sick people. Can’t you just stay there?”

“Me? No no, I’m- I’m a moron, Lorelai. When it comes to this stuff, I mean. You know that.”

“But she called you, and I think I’m beginning to understand why.”

“Oh yeah?”

“Luke, you’re the least excitable person on the planet. She doesn’t want someone freaking out about her, she just wants someone to be there.”

He sighed out through his nose. “I’m not good at this. I- Jesus, I don’t even know what to do with her now.”

“Watch TV with her, make sure she eats something, and drinks fluids, and all those normal sick-person things. I’m finding a way home now, you won’t be on your own forever.”

The call ended with Lorelai once again assuring him that she believed in him. (Pressuring. It was pressuring). He was convinced now more than ever that she would murder him if he screwed this up.

Okay... alright... it was only a couple hours with a sick kid, not even a kid, really. She was sixteen, all he really had to do was make sure she didn’t get hurt or something.

The sound of movement got his attention back to the couch.

“Whoa, hey what’re you doing?” He asked helplessly as Rory started to sit up. She wavered, even as she was still on the couch.

“I... I need to-”

He was thankfully able to recognize the look on her face, and grabbed the bucket from the side of the couch as Rory brought a hand to her mouth.

She shook her head at the think. “No! No, I don’t-” but talking didn’t come easy as she tried to hold down what was attempting to come up.

“You wanna try to get upstairs? Or to the kitchen? ‘cause I don’t think you’re gonna-”

That was all he got out before she lunged for the bin. She gagged, bringing up a rush of liquid, hitting the bottom of the bucket with a sickening splatter. He ignored the splash back on his hand, to be dealt with when he wasn’t holding a puke bucket for a sick teenager.

He found himself offering words of comfort as Rory heaved and hoping they did something. She was completely exhausted afterward, and rightfully so. And now she was crying, actually crying, with choking sobs that made Luke feel like the most useless person on the planet.

“H-hey...” his hand hovered for a moment before settling on her back. “It happens, Rory. Honestly, it’s nothing.”

She wouldn’t look at him, instead sniffling and running her sleeve across her face.

“Oh, here, uh...” he looked around until he spotted the tissue box among the rest of the clutter on the coffee table and handed her a couple. He sighed to himself, patting her back as he stood up.

-----

“It goin’ down okay?”

Rory gave the drink another careful sip and nodded. “I think so, yeah. Where’d you learn how to make this stuff?”

“Oh, y’know, life.” Liz getting wasted every weekend when she was a teenager, begging Luke to help once again. He always came to pick her up when she called, and he was always prepared to help her with the worst hangovers of her life with the electrolyte drink and a smoothie or two.

This, now, was a better use of his skills.

Rory curled up against the arm of the couch, pulling the blanket up to her shoulders. She blinked slowly, looking as if she could fall asleep at any moment. Luke was surprised that she hadn’t yet.

The TV was the only thing making any noise as they both quieted. Maybe the poor kid would finally be able to get some sleep.

And then the sound of a car door came from outside, and Rory was wide awake. Luke smiled.

“Mom!” Rory sat up quickly before a hand went to her head, dizziness reminding her that she wasn’t in a state to get too excited.

“Hey, careful,” Luke said out of both worry and fear that Rory would hurt herself while she was still technically on his watch. “Make her come to you, huh?”

Rory groaned and blinked away the woozy feeling just in time for Lorelai to make her grand entrance. The woman rushed to hug her kid over the back of the couch, peppering some kisses in her hair before turning to smile at Luke.

“Hi,” she said.

Luke nodded, smiled. “Hi.”

-----

They didn’t bother dragging Rory to her bedroom, even after Luke offered to carry her. Instead, the three of them ended up in the living room together, Rory asleep with her head on Lorelai’s lap. She finally looked like she was getting some real rest, and Lorelai looked like she was winding down from a stressful drive back home.

Lorelai sighed heavily as she ran her fingers through Rory’s hair. “Thank you for this, Luke.”

He shrugged. “I didn’t do much.”

She scoffed. “You did everything to make sure that she was okay.”

He wasn’t sure what to say. He’d felt like he was flailing all not, shooting in the dark to find the right thing to do. But if Lorelai was displeased with how he handled the situation, she would have said as much.

“I... tried.”

Lorelai chuckled, looking at her sick daughter and back up to him. “You know you’re gonna catch this, right?”

“Oh,” he gave a dismissive wave. “I’ll be fine.”

“Mhm.”

He got sick the next day, and decided that it was worth it.
