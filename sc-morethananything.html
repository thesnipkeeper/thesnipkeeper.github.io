More Than Anything

A nasty stomach virus has been making its way through Schitt’s Creek. David’s heard far too much about the thing that he’s become determined not to get it. He’s decided to do everything in his power to prevent him and Patrick from falling to the town plague.

Patrick seems determined as well. When he usually just chuckles at David’s obsessive disinfecting of the store, he’s now helping. It’s one problem with working with the public, David thinks, is so many people coming in and out and bringing their germs with them.

He buys some multivitamins for the both of them, probably something they should be taking anyway, Patrick says. They’ve been good about staying healthy through this cold and flu season, even if cold and flu isn’t what they’re worried about.

David and Patrick exchange disgusted looks as Roland from the booth next to them loudly announces what every single symptom of his bout of illness was, in horrifyingly vivid detail. They order their food to go. Twyla understands immediately.

They spend the next week disinfecting everything and using hand sanitizer. By that time, it seems that the virus has done its rounds throughout the town. They think the whole thing has blown over.

David’s sluggish in the morning, or more so than usual. He does his best to get ready for the day, but it feels like he’s wading through molasses. He’s doing his hair as he whines about wanting to go back to bed, and Patrick just laughs and kisses his cheek.

“I told you not to drink coffee that late at night.”

David makes a complaining noise. “Okay, but how am I supposed to eat chocolate without coffee though?”

Patrick shrugs and walks off to get his tea. “Looks like you’ll just have to live with your bad decisions, David!”

David rolls his eyes and continues his usual morning routine, perhaps more half hearted than usual as his body is crying for another hour of sleep. They’re supposed to be able to sleep in on their days off, but David begrudgingly agreed to make today the day that they get all of their errands done. He has to admit, if he has to be stuck running errands with anyone, he’s glad it’s Patrick. He knows that today will be fun.

Even if he is bone-tired for no reason.

It starts with an ominous gurgle in David’s belly as he and Patrick are waiting in line at the grocery store. He grimaces, stifling a burp into his hand. Patrick turns around with a look of amusement. David mutters an “excuse me” quietly enough that hopefully no one else can hear him.

He shifts uncomfortably, his insides feel like they’re tightening. A sudden, sharp cramp makes itself known and David can’t help the soft gasp of pain that escapes his lips.

Patrick turns back once again, this time his eyebrows going up in worry. “What’s wrong?” he asks.

David shakes his head. “Nothing.” There’s plenty of people in the line who would be able to hear him if he admitted what’s really wrong.

He needs the bathroom. Very soon. His stomach growls and cramps angrily. He doesn’t remember eating anything particularly... heavy for breakfast today. A fruit cup, one of Patrick’s bacon slices when he regretted ordering a fruit cup.

He presses a fist to his mouth to muffle another burp. The line is long, and they’re far behind everyone else. And of course the bathroom, in this small town grocery store, is on the other side of the register. Of course!

Another cramp, this time lower in his intestines, making him want to double over in pain until it passes. He settles for leaning against the shelf displaying the day-old pastries. He needs to tell Patrick before this goes in a direction no one wants to witness. Not that Patrick can do anything about it, but... moral support?

He goes to tap Patrick on the shoulder before a tiny, pained whimper escapes him instead. He lays a hand on his belly, trying to be discreet as possible, when the noise gets Patrick’s attention.

“David? You okay?"

David swallows. “I um...”

Patrick nods, urging him to continue. He leans in when he sees that David is about to say something that he doesn’t particularly want everyone else hearing.

“I need to use the restroom,” it’s barely above a whisper.

Patrick takes in his current situation, the look on his face, and nods. “Ah. Um...”

“No, no it’s fine,” David waves off. And it is fine for a few more minutes, when they finally make their way to the register with a week’s worth of groceries.

And then he has to struggle through the gas cramp that only causes the painful pressure to worsen.

David’s not sure how he makes it all the way through the transaction without making his situation obvious to everyone else at the store, but Patrick wordlessly stops their cart of groceries in front of the bathroom. David nods and tries not to completely dart in, even when it feels like he’s about to lose control.

What happens next is... disgusting and uncomfortable. He takes an embarrassingly long time before he finally emerges again, and by the look on Patrick’s face, he must look awful.

“I’m sorry,” David says once they’re in the car. He should feel better, right? But instead his stomach is still complaining, just in a different way. “Ugh, God.”

“David, it happens.” There’s a hint of amusement in his voice that has David immediately scowling at him, even in his exhausted state. “Probably has something to do with having coffee and chocolate before you go to bed.”

“Mmm’kay, it was like three hours before bed and-” David sighs. “And you’re probably right.” He gives a pathetic whine and shifts as much as he can under the seat belt. “And you might want to drive faster.”

Patrick chuckles, nods. “Got it.” and to his credit, he does drive faster.

It’s after David’s second bout of diarrhea since they’ve returned to Patrick’s apartment that he realizes something is very wrong. This isn’t indigestion.

Patrick gives him a sympathetic smile after he wanders out of the bathroom and sits on the couch, far enough away that Patrick knows he’s not feeling particularly cuddly at the moment.

“I’m getting sick, aren’t I,” David says before whining pitifully and going limp against the couch. He should have seen the signs sooner, he’s no stranger to the stomach flu. Although, he’s no stranger to run-of-the-mill indigestion either.

“I think so,” Patrick replies sadly.

David gives a long sigh, taking a few seconds to relax after the rather unfortunate couple of hours he’s had. And then he sits up, getting Patrick’s attention.

He manages to stand despite his legs which are now shaky. “I’ll head back to the motel. I... I disinfected the bathroom, so you shouldn’t have to worry about-” Patrick grasps his wrist before he can walk off to gather his things.

“You’re not leaving just because you’re sick,” he says in that voice that promises he’s being the rational one. David’s learned in the past to listen to that voice, as it’s the only thing that’s kept him from spiraling out of control at all hours of the day.

David sighs, not sure what to do with his hands at first before settling for crossing his arms. “Patrick, this is contagious.”

“So you’d rather get your family sick?”

David looks up to the ceiling for a moment, thinking. “That’s a good point,” he admits quietly. And being sick with his family around doesn’t sound particularly pleasant. They aren’t the most sympathetic bunch. His mother would probably send him to be quarantined. His gurgling stomach reminds him to make a choice, because if he wants to make it through the short drive home, he needs to leave like, now.

An urgent noise from David’s stomach prompts him to dart to the bathroom, and this time he finds himself kneeling in front of the toilet and gagging up waves of his breakfast.

-----

A knock on the door drags him back to a reality that isn’t just the toilet bowl and drool. He groans weakly and pulls himself away from the thing.

“David, how’re you holding up?”

David can’t bring himself to answer. After throwing up off and on all afternoon, all he feels like doing is curling up on the horrendous tile of Patrick’s bathroom. That’s the position Patrick finds him in when he opens the door.

“Oh sweetheart...” Patrick mumbles, kneeling down next to him and letting David rest his head in his lap

David whimpers as Patrick’s hands run through his hair, pulling it off of his damp forehead. He must be a pitiful sight indeed. He vaguely wonders why Patrick is still with him at all.

“After you stuck with me through my bronchitis last year? We’re in it for the long haul.” Patrick laughs softly and continues petting his head. So he said that out loud.

David isn’t sure how long the two of them are on the bathroom floor together before Patrick tries to talk him into going to bed. The thing about bed, David thinks tiredly, is that it’s on the opposite side of the apartment. Getting there is one thing, but his stomach’s going to inevitably demand his attention again and he’s going to need to get there quickly. Patrick seems to understand his reluctance.

“I’ll be right back, okay?”

David nods, whimpers as Patrick moves out from under him. He thinks he hears lots of moving and rustling, but David doesn’t give it much thought until Patrick returns.

“Nnngh...” David groans as Patrick pulls him to his feet.

“I know, I know, we’re almost there...”

The couch is comfy. Comfier than usual. David looks around him, even as he’s being covered in a blanket, and realizes that he’s on top of two other blankets. Despite both of those things, he finds himself starting to shiver.

“I’m still cold,” he whines.

There’s no response from Patrick at first, and then he hears him hum thoughtfully before he rests the backs of his fingers to David’s forehead. Patrick sighs.

“I think that’s because you have a fever, honey.”

David whimpers, curls up as well as he can on the couch, and shudders more. If he’s already going to be pathetically sick, he may as well own it.

Patrick’s talking to himself (or David?) about something, but David can’t be bothered to stay awake long enough to figure out what he’s saying.

-----

David bats at whoever is touching him. Don’t they know that he’s trying to sleep? Whoever it is bats his hand right back.

“Knock it off, David.” That’s certainly not Patrick’s voice.

He opens his eyes rather reluctantly and sees Stevie knelt in front of his with something in her hand.

“Patrick called and asked if I had a thermometer he could borrow,” she explains at his confused look. “You’re lucky your dad decided to get one for the first aid kit at the motel.” She looks over him, presumably to Patrick. “So you just have to like, press this button and then press it again when you’re done. Super easy.”

David groans and turns to lie on his back until he’s leaning against the arm of the couch. His stomach aches and his muscles cry out in pain. He certainly doesn’t feel better since he’s slept.

“Okay, sit still,” Stevie instructs before pushing a button on it and pressing it into his ear.

A hand starts petting his hair once again, and he looks up as well as he can without angering Stevie to see Patrick standing over him.

Stevie looks at the reading and turns the screen to the two of them.

“I’m no doctor, but that looks like a fever,” she says with a long sigh. She pats David’s stomach as she stands up, much to his dismay. “Don’t fucking die, got it?”

David moans. “I’ll try.”

Stevie scoffs. Patrick thanks her, and she leaves.

Patrick leans over the couch and kisses his forehead. David makes a disgusted noise, but doesn’t bother to shrink away.

“Ick. I’m like, sweaty,” he complains.

Patrick chuckles. “Now that you’re awake, do you want something for dinner?”

“Dinner?” David mumbles, rubbing his face. “How long have I been asleep?”

“Long enough.”

David thinks for a moment, pulls himself out of his post-nap fog to assess how he feels. The answer is horrible. He feels horrible. More than horrible. And he can tell by the way his stomach is cramping that it’s not done torturing him.

“Better not,” he grumbles, turning over once again to hug his pillow. And with a sigh, he gets up and tiredly goes to the bathroom while Patrick does whatever in the kitchen.

There’s a plate with dry toast on the coffee table when he returns. He sits down and stares at it, and then the cup of something carbonated that’s set in front of him as Patrick sits down as well. He has a plate of toast, although his has peanut butter on it.

“You don’t have to eat it,” Patrick says, noticing the uneasy look on his face. “It’s just in case you get hungry.”

David finds himself smiling at the toast, and then of course at his fiance. “Thank you.”

“It’s just toast, David.”

David scoffs. “You know what I’m trying to say. Stop making this harder!”

Patrick grins and wraps an arm around him. “Yes, I know exactly what you’re trying to say. I love you too, David.”

David nibbles on the toast. He desperately wants to be able to eat, this is one of the worst parts of being sick. But in the end, it sits like an indigestible rock in his stomach and he can’t finish the slice. Patrick kisses his temple and takes their plates to the kitchen, to be dealt with later.

If only that’s the end of the story. If only Patrick hadn’t told David that he loved him hours before they went to bed, hours before David wakes them both up by puking on the duvet.

Patrick’s awake immediately. There’s a bucket next to the bed that David hadn’t been fast enough to get to. He wants to cry, but he’s too busy heaving up a second wave of vomit while Patrick gathers the comforter. He’s exhausted, David can see it on his face, in his mannerisms, and yet he still makes an effort to gently assure him that it’s okay, it happens, it’s not hard to clean.

He disappears for a while to do the laundry while David’s stomach settles. Patrick carefully takes the bucket out of his grasp when he returns, and David makes a noise of protest. He struggles to find words in his tired, miserable state, but he sputters something out before Patrick can take it away.

“Oh my God, Patrick. Don’t- I can do it-”

Patrick shushes him. “I’ll be back in a minute.”

He returns with a freshly cleaned bin, and climbs into bed with a yawn. David hesitates before cuddling next to him.

“Still love me?”

Patrick’s chest rises with a huffed laugh. “More ‘an anything. Go to sle’p.”

-----

David wakes up to the sun shining in his face. It’s later than they usually wake up, then. He groans, stretches, and finds the other side of the bed empty.

“Patrick?” David yawns. He feels a little better today. His stomach still seems to hate him, and he’s still tired, but the fever and muscle aches have gone for now. And he can get up without feeling lightheaded.

He assumes that Patrick is showering when he sees that the bathroom door is closed, but then he hears the unmistakable sound of gagging. He knows, he’s been making those sounds for a whole day.

His heart tightens.

“You okay?” He calls to the bathroom door, not bothering to announce his presence.

A heave, the sound of liquid hitting liquid. “Not really. Give me a sec.”

David does, taking a seat on the couch and fidgeting with his rings until Patrick comes out.

“Think I caught your bug,” Patrick says with a self-deprecating chuckle. He flops on the couch next to David.

The two of them exchange looks.

And find themselves laughing.

“So is this what married life is going to be like?” David asks, biting his lip.

Patrick lets his head fall against David’s shoulder. He’s stiflingly hot, but David isn’t about to stop him from being cuddly. “I think so.”

-----

“Patrick, I told you to stay still,” Stevie bites before putting the thermometer in his ear. 

“Sorry, sorry!”

She sighs at the reading. “Okay, well now your fever is higher than David’s was yesterday.”

“What?!” David shrieks. 

“I mean, I’m sure it’s fine…”

Patrick nods. “Yeah, I run kind of hot when I’m sick,” he admits. “Used to scare the shit out of my mom.” he turns over and buries his face in David’s shoulder. “Thanks, Stevie.”

“Don’t mention it. But uh,  if I catch whatever hellbug you two have…”

“We’ll make sure you don’t choke on your own vomit,” David offers sleepily.

“Uh, I was going to say you owed me a bottle of wine, actually.”

“That too,” says Patrick.

She leaves shortly after, and the two of them get comfortable. David wraps an arm around Patrick’s waist, and this time it’s his turn to lay on him. That is, until he hears Patrick’s stomach start to gurgle. He hopes it’s nothing for his own sake, because he’s so comfortable, but Patrick makes a pain noise and shifts.

“David…I- I need to go to the bathroom.”

David sighs and sits up. “Got it.” 

He hugs his pillow instead, and can’t help but giggle. He’s not happy that Patrick’s sick, not at all. But he realizes that, despite what a mess he’s been, Patrick stuck around long enough to get sick. No one’s ever done that for him before, no one’s ever tried to take care of him when he’s hideously sick, outside of Adelina, Alexis at one time, and maybe his parents.

And now Patrick.
