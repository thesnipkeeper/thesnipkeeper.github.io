Client Trip

With Patrick out on a conference, that left David to tend to the client meeting and hopefully pick up some home-spun yarn in the process.

He had managed to drag Alexis with him for the little trip by promising to buy her dinner at the cafe afterward. Honestly, she was bored enough to agree.

“She spins her own yarn? That’s a little extra.”

“Of course she spins her own yarn. That’s her whole business. I’m hoping to be able to get some of her sweaters in the deal, too. But she’s old, Alexis, so you can’t be too... trendy.”

“You’re one to talk, David!”

He shrugged. “Whatever.” He took a hand off of the steering wheel to cover a quick cough.

“Ew, are you sick?”

“No, I’m not sick. It was just a cough.” His throat had been a little scratchy since the morning, and it took him a little longer to get up and ready that morning, but the scratchy throat was probably just a change in weather, and he stayed up a little too late last night, and none of it had anything to do with the fact that Patrick was just getting over a bad cold before he left.

David cleared his throat and shook his head to clear it, blinking.

Everything was going to be fine.

-----

The woman was far nicer than David expected, almost too nice for his liking. She offered them coffee and snacks and sat them down on old floral furniture to talk to them.

She also adored Alexis.

It would be cute if David didn't spend the whole time holding back coughs and trying to keep control of his increasingly-drippy nose. Alexis, bless her, knew exactly when to ask the woman about a the ceramic angel on her mantle so he could make use of his sweater sleeve.

He couldn't believe this was happening.

The one day that Patrick was gone, and he had to come down with the cold from hell.

He watched Patrick deal with this cold for a week; David knew what he was in for.

David sniffled, and then sniffled again. He brought a hand to his face to discretely hide any visible runniness. This was bad. Alexis gave him a look and bit her lip, knowing exactly what his current issue was. If they were anywhere else she probably would have called him out with an 'ew David!'.

"Um, do you mind if I use your restroom?"

"Go right ahead! Down the hall and to the right."

David nodded a thanks while still trying to hide his face. He found his way into a hideously-pastel, wallpapered bathroom and risked a peek at himself in the mirror.

That was a mistake.

He gasped, taking in his shadowed eyes and flushed features. David brought a hand to his face and gaped at how awful he looked. How could Alexis let him leave home looking like this?!

Unfortunately, there were somehow more pressing matters to deal with, like the snot dripping out of his nose, and the fact that he needed this deal to work out.

Well, he really wanted this deal to work out.

Same thing, really.

David snatched a good handful of toilet paper and blew his nose with disgusting-yet-relieving results. He rubbed, ignoring the beginning of soreness around his nostrils that signified the beginning of a gross, "obviously contagious" look involving a red nose and chapped lips and everything that was difficult to remedy with a plethora of skincare products.

He sniffled, wiped his nose one last time, and decided it felt amazing to be able to breathe for the first time in so long (like two hours). David washed his hand thoroughly and joined the client and Alexis.

They discussed the store and everything that would happen if she signed on with them, and Alexis helped to smooth it over with her charm that David had never been able to nail down, let alone when he felt like he may have been dying.

Of course he sneezed suddenly, prompting the woman to quickly bless him.

“Are you getting a cold?”

“No! No, just allergies,” he lied. He felt Alexis set her hand on his shoulder as she continued to talk to the woman.

Finally, they left with a box of yarn and a promise to meet again in a month to discuss the sales.

David let his sister usher him to the passenger side without complaint before she took the box and put it in the back seat.

He sighed, sniffled, as she started to car.

“Thank you.”

“Yeah, well I’d hate to see you ruin a meeting just because you’re disgusting.”

David chuckled.

“Also,” she added. “You owe me.”

A groan. “Fine.”
