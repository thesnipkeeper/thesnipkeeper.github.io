True Family

“That’s it, take it easy...”

Isabelle’s soft touch and soothing words were the only things keeping Clary from completely losing it as she heaved up another trickle of vomit.

She wasn’t planning on going to anyone when she started getting sick. Shadowhunters were supposed to be strong, right? So a mundane illness was probably the last thing they cared about. She was wrong, of course, because Izzy rushed to her side the minute she saw Clary was throwing up.

Clary had finally managed to catch her breath after the violent and unproductive bout. Izzy stood for just a moment to wet a washcloth. She knelt next to Clary once again and gently turned her chin to face her. She wiped the remnants of vomit off of Clary’s chin and then her upper lip where her nose had dripped. She folded the cloth and began dabbing at her forehead and neck.

Clary had a feeling that Izzy had done this before.

“Sorry,” Clary breathed out. “I’m kind of gross.”

Izzy chuckled and shook her head. “Definitely not. I’m just trying to help.” She reached up to drape the washcloth on the edge of the sink. “Do you think you’re done?”

Clary shrugged. “I- I can’t tell,” she admitted.

“Okay,” Izzy said easily. “Well, if anything, I could get a pillow and blanket so you’re more comfortable.”

Clary nodded, giving a self-deprecating laugh. “Sure.”

She listened to the rustle of blankets before Izzy returned with a pile in her arms. She helped Clary get comfortable, or as comfortable as she could be on the bathroom floor. Clary forgot, sometimes, that she was someone’s sister. She probably took care of Max and Alec and Jace plenty of times.

“Hey, are you okay?”

“Hm? Yeah, why?”

“You’re getting a little teary-eyed. Is your stomach hurting?”

“Oh,” Clary rubbed her eyes. “No, sorry. I was just thinking. It’s nice to have someone around. Someone like family.”

Isabelle smiled, sweet and sad and full of sympathy. She sat by Clary’s head and gently brushed her hair away from her forehead. “This is your first time getting sick since... since everything?” the since your mom died, went unsaid. Since your life did a one-eighty, since you found out your father was a manipulative nut-job.

Clary nodded. “Yeah.” She made a noise of discomfort and shifted. Her stomach was bothering her again, and Clary was starting to realize that this was going to be an all-night thing for sure. Why couldn’t it have been a cold or something? “You don’t have to stay, though. I’m probably just going to fall asleep.”

“If you want me to go, I’ll go,” Izzy said softly, still playing with Clary’s hair in such a comforting way. “But I kind of want to stay and keep you company.”

Clary couldn’t help but smile. “That’s fine.” And things were very sweet for two minutes until she started throwing up again.

Isabelle eventually managed to get her to bed when she’d stopped throwing up for a while. Clary found herself dreading it, honestly, because she didn’t want Izzy to go. She liked having someone, someone like a sister around when she wasn’t feeling good and needed someone to cling onto.

Which was why she was so surprised and delighted when Izzy grabbed a spare blanket and pillow from the closet and started making her own bed on the floor.

Clary fell asleep faster than she had in weeks.

-----

Clary moaned and curled up in the fetal position. She was freezing and- oh, her stomach was churning sickeningly. Did she even have anything left to throw up?

A sudden audible gurgle had her throwing her blankets off of herself and dashing to the bathroom.

“Clary?” A tired voice from her bedroom. Right. Izzy. She must have woken her up.

Izzy thankfully gave her space as she threw up. And then Clary went through her normal morning routine of brushing her teeth, washing her face... anything to feel a little more human after a night of puking.

Izzy was in the middle of a yawn, the blankets she had slept with now neatly folded on Clary’s bed.

“Sorry. Not the best way for you to wake up...” Clary said sheepishly.

Izzy chuckled. “Honestly, I think I slept way later than usual. How are you feeling?”

Clary’s embarrassed grimace said everything.

She smiled. “I kind of figured. Well, I’m going to let Alec know that you’re not available for missions, alright? Oh, and Jace should know about-”

Clary gasped. “Magnus’ party! It’s tonight...” She dragged a hand down her face with a groan, haphazardly sitting on her bed. “I promised Jace that I would go with him.”

“You’re sick, Clary.” Izzy sat next to her and wrapped an arm around her shoulder, pulling her close. “You really want to go to a party?”

“I might feel better by then.” Clary shrugged. “Anyway... thanks for staying with me last night. It... it meant a lot.”

Izzy smiled. “Well, you’re never alone. Don’t be afraid to let me know next time you need help. Or company.”

-----

Alec and Jace both turned to Izzy as she walked into the center hub.

“Where have you been?” Alec asked. “Where’s Clary?”

“I can answer both of those by saying that Clary was sick all night and I stayed with her.”

Jace’s face dropped, and Isabelle realized she probably should have let him know alone first. “Clary’s sick?”

“I- yeah, I went to get her opinion on an outfit last night and she was throwing up.”

“Well, is she okay?” Jace demanded.

“Jace,” Izzy laid a hand on his arm and looked in his eyes. “She’s fine. It’s probably just a stomach bug.” Izzy sighed. “You might want to talk to her, though. She’s still planning on going to Magnus’ party tonight because she wants to be there for you, and I’m not sure she should be going anywhere today.”

Jace sighed. “I’ll be back,” he said, clapping Alec on the back before walking off.

Izzy smirked. Of course she didn’t want Clary to go out tonight when she was so miserable, and of course she hoped Jace would be able to talk her out of it, but... “He’s not going to be able to convince her to change her mind one bit.”

Alec scoffed. “Nope.”

-----

Magnus still often had to push away the concern of having shadowhunters and downworlders in the same room, but he did his best to make his parties as enjoyable as possible. Sure, he couldn’t please everyone, there would be some sort of disagreement or awkward interaction that would make a person want to leave, but all in all, things always went well.

Magnus was unused to his guests looking unhappy before they even finished walking through the foyer, though.

Clary and Jace walked in, less arm-in-arm and more attached to each other, Jace’s arm around her and what looked to be his jacket draped over her shoulders. It was adorable, Magnus decided, even if they didn’t look happy to be there.

The conversation he overheard above the rest of the chatter had him curious as well.

“Are you sure you’re up for this?” Jace asked, rubbing her arm as if he was trying to warm her up despite the jacket she had yet to take off.

“I told you, everything will be fine,” said Clary, with her usual delightful sweetness. But there was an edge to her voice that seemed to say the exact opposite.

Magnus did indeed wonder why there was a question as to whether everything would not be fine, but he brushed off the little eavesdropped snippet and went back to entertaining guests.

Clary didn’t remove the jacket off of her shoulders for the rest of the night. Magnus was almost tempted to say something, ask if it was too cold. He could warm the place in a heartbeat, after all. But he left it, because Jace stayed by her side the entire time as they took a seat on the couch that had been pushed against the wall, out of the way of the other mingling guests.

The two of them caught his attention once again when Clary finally did take the jacket off with a strange look on her face and handed it to Jace. He frowned, concern painted allover his face and said something Magnus couldn’t hear over the noise, but whatever it was, she shook her head at it and walked off down the hallway.

Strange.

Alec was behind him talking on the phone, of all things to do at a party. Magnus was about to ask about Jace and Clary as Alec ended the call, but instead his boyfriend brushed past him and went directly to Jace.

Jace’s frown deepened, suddenly looking distressed at whatever Alec was saying to him.

Magnus pretended not to have been watching them when Alec finally came to talk to him.

“Everything alright?” Magnus asked innocently.

Alec sighed. “Actually, no. Jace and I just got called in on a mission.”

“That’s not something your sister can take care of? I don’t want you to leave...” Magnus couldn’t help but whine a bit.

“Sorry,” Alec smiled and gave him a kiss. “Izzy’s already out on patrol on a different side of town, and Clary really shouldn’t be going anywhere.” He looked back at Jace, sighed again. “I should go.”

Magnus gave his most heartfelt goodbye with the hopes that it would somehow convince his boyfriend to stay. Alec left, Jace came out from the hallway and followed with obvious reluctance. The moment they were out the door, Magnus’ thoughts switched from lamenting his boyfriend leaving his party and went back to the curiosity that was Clary Fairchild.

Clary shouldn’t be going anywhere? What did that mean?

Magnus wandered down the hallway she’d disappeared in so long ago. He wasn’t sure what he was expecting, but finding the bathroom door shut and hearing unfortunate gagging from inside wasn’t it.

He frowned deeply and knocked. “Are you alright, biscuit?” He tried to keep his voice down, the last thing Clary probably wanted right now was for attention to be drawn to her.

“Yeah,” came the croaky, miserable reply. The toilet flushed, there was a pause, and then he heard the faucet turn on for a moment.

Magnus bit his lip. “I hope-”

The door opened, and Clary seemed to nearly recoil in embarrassment. Her cheeks were bright red, her eyes as well, and her nose was running just a little. At first, Magnus thought she’d been crying.

“You poor thing, is everything alright?”

“U-um...” Clary gave a self-deprecating chuckle. “Actually I’m kind of... not feeling well.”

“Oh no, I hope it wasn’t the food?”

“No,” Clary said quickly. “No, I was sick before, I just thought I’d be feeling a little better by tonight.” Her words were shuddering just as much as she was.

Magnus made a sympathetic noise and palmed her forehead. “You’re burning up.” He looked back at the party, the crowd of people. It was probably the last thing she wanted to deal with right now. He simply steered her a few feet in the other direction to the spare bedroom. “Come along, you can lie down in here until you’re feeling well enough to go back to the institute.”

Clary didn’t waste any time getting on the bed and curled up. She shivered miserably, and Magnus risked helping pull the blankets over her.

“Sorry about this,” she said. “I... Jace didn’t want me to come with him tonight, but I wanted to. He hates- I mean, he prefers to go to parties with someone.”

Magnus chuckled. “Well, it seems both of our loves left us high and dry for the night, hm?” It was a joke, of course, and he hoped Clary knew that. He knew Alec’s job was unpredictable most of the time, as was his own these days.

Clary cracked a smile. “Guess so.”

“I’m going to get you a glass of water for you to sip on, and then you can rest until you’re feeling well enough for a portal, alright?”

“Thanks, Magnus.”

He managed to evade most conversations with people on his way to the kitchen and get the water back to Clary in record time. The poor thing was still curled up and shuddering miserably. She didn’t look prepared, he had a feeling that this fever was a new development.

He offered a hand to help her sit up and gave her the glass, carefully guiding the bottom of it until he was sure she had a grip.

“Small sips, my dear. You don’t want to get dehydrated, but you also don’t want it to come back up later.”

Clary grimaced, but did take a few careful sips after the initial gulps.

“Hope I didn’t... ruin your party,” she said as she handed the glass back.

“Nonsense, everyone is having a great time. Well... almost everyone.”

Clary laughed. “Yeah, guess so.”

“You’re free to stay the night, by the way. I said until you felt better, but if that means tomorrow morning, that’s alright with me.”

“Thank you, Magnus. I- I really appreciate this.”

Magnus could see the tears building in her eyes, and in a moment of weakness, he pressed a hesitant kiss to her forehead.

She gave a watery smile.

“Get some rest, biscuit. I’ll text Jace and let him know that you’re alright so he doesn’t worry.”

-----

Jace was a rather adorably-worried boyfriend, asking question after question. Many of them had typos, as he was probably in the middle of kicking a demon to Timbuktu.

The last (or, second to last) guest left, offering well-wishes, and Magnus cleaned his loft with the snap of a finger. Things were in their proper order, that glass someone broke and tried to hide was back in one piece. Very nice.

He was in the middle of pouring himself a post-successful-party drink when he heard movement from the other room. Blankets rustling, a cough, and a splatter of liquid against the hardwood. He grimaced and set his drink down.

By the time he went to check on her, the bathroom door was closed. With a single glance into the bedroom, he did indeed catch sight of a puddle of vomit on the floor and partially on the bedspread. Once again, the whole thing was clean with a snap of his fingers.

He waited for a few minutes before knocking on the door.

“Do you need anything?”

“No...” the poor thing barely had a voice from throwing up so much. He’d have to see about a potion to help her feel better. “But I- puked on-”

“Already taken care of,” he assured. “Warlock, remember?”

A hoarse chuckle. “Right.”

She emerged a few minutes later, and Magnus realized something. She was still wearing her dress.

“Do you want something a little more comfortable to wear to bed?” He offered. “I’m sorry, I didn’t even think about that.”

“Sure,” Clary said. “Seriously, Magnus, thank you.”

He smiled. “I don’t know what this is all about, but you don’t have to keep thanking me, biscuit. You’re practically family, in a strange way.”

She returned the smile, and he caught her eyes watering once more. There was certainly something else going on, something she’d been thinking about, but whatever it was seemed to be a good thing.

She sat on the bed, arms folded over her stomach while he found some sweatpants and a T-shirt.

“There you are!” he said. “Sleep well, don’t be afraid to come get me if you need anything else. Anything at all.”

-----

Clary wasn’t sure what time it was when she woke up, but it was late enough that the sun had completely illuminated the room.

She felt better. She still felt off, and she probably would for a few more days, but Clary was just thankful that she no longer felt in danger of throwing up.

At the sound of various familiar voices outside, she climbed out of bed and wandered out.

Jace stood up and rushed over to her, pulling out a chair at Magnus’ breakfast table and urging her to sit down.

“I’m so sorry I left you last night, I-”

“It was my fault,” Alec cut in. “I got the mission, I didn’t realize how long it was going to take.”

Izzy was leaning over to feel her forehead. “Do you feel any better?”

Clary had to bite back a smile. She wasn’t expecting this at all, but it... it was nice. She never realized that having a gross stomach bug was going to show her who her family was, but... here they were.

“I’m fine, you guys. I feel a lot better, actually.” She leaned into Jace as he wrapped an arm around her, savoring his warmth.

“Morning,” Magnus greeted as he wandered over and sat a plate in front of her. “I made you some banana toast. Something worth nibbling on if you’re feeling up to it.”

And surprisingly, she was.

“Thank you,” she said, and she hoped they knew how much it meant.
