Pals

Sam and Taliesin didn’t spend all that much one-on-one time with each other. They liked each other just fine, of course, but they had little in common besides their friends.

Which was why Taliesin had to do a double take when he looked out the window at the sound of a car door and saw Sam Riegel walking up to his door with a mysterious grocery bag in his hand. Had Sam ever even been to his home?

He wondered for a moment if he was delirious from fever, blinking dazedly through the window, cup of tea in-hand. The only reason he’d ventured outside of his room in the first place was for tea.

Taliesin was yanked out of his daze when a knock at the door reminded him that, oh yeah, he had a visitor. He cleared his throat of the gunk that had settled from disuse, made an attempt to fix his hair despite the fact that it hadn’t been washed in days, and opened the front door.

And there was Sam’s gigantic smile, though it was a bit more sheepish than usual.

“Heyyy,” He said.

Taliesin chuckled and then sniffled when simply laughing apparently made his nose run. “Hi. Um.”

Sam cleared his throat. “So buddy, I heard that you had the flu, and I think I’m to blame?” he said, face pulling back in a grimace.

Taliesin blinked. “What?”

Sam nodded and continued, ready to elaborate more. “My kids got over the flu last week, and you and Matt visited to help me with my VO project, and...” he drew out the last word and gestured toward Taliesin. “Matt’s not sick, but when he said that you were, I needed to apology immediately, because I should have warned you or had you over another day, so...”

Taliesin’s brain was too gummed up with illness to process exactly what was happening, but he watch Sam pull something out of the bag and he hesitantly held his hands out. It was a container of soup, still quite warm.

“That’s soup,” Sam stated. “And this bag,” Taliesin accepted the pharmacy bag in his other hand, carefully getting the handle of his cup of tea. “Is full of flu medicine and cough drops and packets of tissues and pretty much everything else.”

Taliesin looked down at the things in his hand, and smiled. After spending the day in bed feeling sorry for himself and too sick to buy any of these things himself (too embarrassed to ask anyone else to), it was nice. Sam, of all people, he thought.

“Thank you,” he said, voice thick with congestion and hoarse from all of the coughing he’s been doing. “You didn’t have to do all of this just because your kids were sick.”

Sam shrugged. “I wanted to. Feel better, okay buddy?”

He nodded. “Okay.” He’d certainly feel much better after eating some soup and taking some strong flu medicine.

Taliesin chuckled. What a strange man he was, but, he supposed, they were all a little strange.

Taliesin didn’t really notice it at first. His whole head hurt to some extent. This cold had not been kind to him, and he’d spent days being too congested to sleep, head aching, throat sore, nose red and raw.

He had gotten over it for the most part by the time Laura and Travis’ barbecue came around, and Laura had graciously given him permission to come now that he was most likely no longer contagious. The lingering cough was all that remained, and that could be remedied with a pocketful of cough drops.

Well, the lingering cough and the soreness in his ear were all that remained. Probably from the congestion, Taliesin decided.

He arrived at the party, relieved to see his friends and be over the awful cold so he could see them again without worrying about getting them sick.

And then he started to feel off.

He’d been roped into a game of volleyball, and it was going well for someone who didn’t consider themselves a particularly sport person. He moved quickly to hit the ball, too quickly apparently, as the world was suddenly spinning and he was sitting on the grass with his hand to his head. The ache in his ear seemed to have gotten worse and he just hadn’t noticed.

“Taliesin?” Laura, who’d chosen him for her team, rushed over to him.

Everyone else either chuckled, cracked a joke to lighten to mood (“too much to drink, huh?” came from Liam, and he would have laughed had he not been dealing with the humiliation of falling), or just stopped and watched.

Laura knelt next to him as he blinked blearily, trying to clear his head. He still found himself moaning as the pain in his ear spiked, hand going over it protectively. Laura didn’t miss a beat, clasping both hands around his other arm and leading him far away from the... sportsy part of the yard. “Come on, let’s go sit down. Keep playing, you guys!” she called to the others.

Taliesin tried to keep himself from looking or sounding too pathetic. He began to realize that he really didn’t feel good. It was like he was catching the same cold all over again, but worse.

Laura sat next to him on the picnic table bench. “Hey, are you feeling okay? Your face is all red.”

Taliesin nodded immediately. “I’m fine,” a lie. But he didn’t want her to think that he’d lied about feeling better. He had been feeling better up until now.

Laura laughed. “No, you’re not. Tell me what’s wrong. Maybe I can help.”

Taliesin sighed. He considered continuing to lie. But in the end, he didn’t have the energy. “I’m not feeling well,” he admitted. Laura frowned immediately, eyebrows turning up in worry. “I’m not- I don’t think I’m contagious or anything,” he said quickly.

“Taliesin, I don’t care about that... I mean, I do, obviously, but that’s not my biggest concern at the moment. What do you mean when you say you’re not feeling well?”

He hesitated, weighing his options for telling the truth once more before, “It’s my ear. I think it’s infected.”

Laura made a sympathetic noise. “Ronin had one of those a couple months ago.” Taliesin didn’t expect her to feel his forehead, but it wasn’t unwelcome. “You’re kind of warm, but it’s hot out here so... let’s go inside, away from all the craziness.” She nodded pointedly toward the rowdy volleyball game.

She sat him at the dining room table, disappeared, and returned with a bottle of tylenol. She filled a glass of water and slid it toward him.

He didn’t have to be instructed before he took the meds. He’d lived on the stuff through his cold, not wanting to take heavy meds since he had work to do.

“You can lie down in our guest room, if you want.”

Taliesin raised an eyebrow. Oh. He’d ridden there with Matt and Marisha.

“I mean, I’m guessing that they’ll be ready to go when they find out you’re sick, but I’m sure it’ll be nice to have somewhere to chill until then.”

Taliesin smiled. “Thank you.”

Laura returned the smile, only more proudly. “You’re very welcome, Tal.”

Taliesin was starving by two o’clock. 

He informed Dani that he was just going to head out and get chinese from the place down the street.

She grimaced, the kind of face she made when she knew something he didn’t.

“What?”

She shrugged at first, and then hesitated before speaking. “Are you sure? That place looks... sketchy.”

Taliesin chuckled. “I don’t care. I’m starving, chinese sounds so good right now.”

Dani made a face but didn’t stop him as he headed out.

Lunch was delicious. He was just happy to have something to eat, honestly, after forgetting breakfast and working all morning and early afternoon. 

He scarfed the whole thing in record time and went back to work.

Except… as time went on, he realized that his stomach wasn't digesting as well as it should. It was chinese food, he justified. It was never meant to be digested well, just to be tasty. But it felt like a rock in his stomach, stubborn and starting to make him worry just a bit. 

He winced as he worked, laying a hand on his stomach as if it would ease the painful cramping. He brought his hand up to muffle a burp. Taliesin was beginning to realize that this wasn’t run-of-the-mill indigestion.

Okay, Taliesin thought, maybe ordering from that place was a bad idea. He folded both arms over his stomach and doubled over, hoping that it would somehow ease the pressure in his gut. It didn’t, of course, but it still helped him feel better in a way.

It wasn’t long before Taliesin found himself on the floor of the studio bathroom, sweating and sick, waiting for the next bout of vomiting to start. He’d been through this enough times in his life to know it wasn’t going to stop until his stomach was empty. Until then, he just had to wait and hope that death would somehow fall swiftly upon him.

It became clear that his stomach wasn’t quite ready to relinquish all of its contents. He was hogging the bathroom, so he decided just to sit nearby in the lobby and try to… relax.

The moment he did, a can was set in front of him on the coffee table. Dani smiled. “Ginger ale. I told you.”

Taliesin sighed heavily and leaned back. “I knowww. I should have known better than to not listen to you.” It was true, honestly. Dani was the one who, no matter how busy, checked in with everyone and made sure they’d eaten, taken a break. He really should have trusted her advice.

Dani giggled and sat next to him. She rubbed his arm comfortingly before cracking the can open and pressing it into his hand. “Small sips. If anything, it’ll make it easier to puke up whatever you’ve got left.”

Taliesin made a noise of disgust, but sipped the drink. He had to admit, it felt good to have something to wash the awful taste out of his mouth. 

“What would I do without you?”

Dani scoffed. “I honestly have no idea.”
