Keyleth is Cute

Percy was working peacefully on a new project (or three) when he nearly jumped out of his skin at a sudden voice.

"You're still awake?"

"Keyleth!" He breathed out, laying a hand over his heart. "Don't do that!"

She chuckled a bit. "Oops, sorry," Before walking in and taking a seat on one of the more comfortable chairs in the workshop (which wasn't saying much). She was wearing her nightgown, long hair braided down her back to be kept out of her way. She slouched forward, propping her chin in her palm. Keyleth certainly looked tired.

"What are you doing awake?"

Keyleth pulled her dressing gown tight around herself. "I couldn't sleep," she sighed. "I don't feel very good."

Well, that had Percy's attention. "Don’t feel good? How do you mean?"

As if answering his question, Keyleth gave a deep cough into the sleeve of her robe. Oh. 

"Ah," he frowned. Though, he couldn't help but feel a bit relieved that it wasn't something weird and magical going on. "Feeling a bit sick, then?"

Keyleth nodded, giving a miserable snuffle that signified how full her sinuses were. He winced in sympathy. “I think I was keeping Vax up. Can’t stop coughing,” she groaned, draping herself dramatically across the chair. “Do you care if I hang around here for a while?”

“Not at all,” Percy smiled, and pretended like it wasn’t incredibly nice to have some company for once as he worked. 

Keyleth talked with him about nothing in particular, occasionally giving a cough or a sniffle, or on one occasion, an unfortunate sneeze. She had to borrow a kerchief from Percy to blow her nose, which was getting redder by the second from the constantly rubbing.

After a while, Percy found himself getting tired. His vision fogged when he tried to read any of the plans he’d drawn up, and he couldn’t think straight anymore. It was time for sleep, then, before he passed out in his workshop. Again. 

“I ought to go upstairs and get some sleep,” he said as he put his various tools and parts away to be worked on when his brain was working again. Keyleth looked upset by this, and understandable, too. 

Her cough had gotten worse, he knew she was reluctant to go back to bed with Vax when she was making so much noise. 

“Really?” She asked, disappointed. “Don’t you have stuff to finish? You were building that- that thing. Don’t you need to finish it?”

Percy did have to resist the temptation to get back to work until he truly couldn’t keep his eyes open anymore. “I- I should probably sleep. Sorry, dear.” She still looked unsure of what to do with herself. He wished Pike were here. She would know what to do with a sick Keyleth. Hell, she could probably cure a sick Keyleth. “Why don’t you come to the kitchen with me and I’ll make you a cup of tea?”

“Oh! You don’t have to-” she started coughing once more, not even able to finish her sentence as the coughs bubbled up from her chest. 

“I think that I do. It might help your cough!”

She considered it, and then gave a hoarse, “Alright.”

Soon, the two of them were seated at their kitchen table with tea. While it was doing wonders to warm Percy and get him more sleepy, Keyleth was next to him shivering. 

“It’s so cold in here…” she mumbled.

Percy frowned “It really isn’t…  do you mind if I...?” At Keyleth’s nod he pressed the inside of his wrist to her forehead. She was burning up just as he suspected. “You’re running a temperature, Keyleth… you should be in bed.”

“Nooo…” she argued, and then gave a couple sputtering coughs. “I’m not gonna be able to sleep, then Vax won’t be able to sleep, and then-”

“Keyleth?” the sleepy voice game from a gangly figure in the doorway that proved to be Vax’ildan as he stepped into the dim lights of the kitchen candles. “I woke up and you were gone.” His sleepy gaze eventually found Percy.

“And that’s my time to go,” Percy decided, standing up. “Take care of her, she’s got a bit of a fever,” he said to Vax before leaning over to press a kiss to his friend’s head. “Feel better, darling.”

“Thanks, Percy,” she said, smiling. 

The last thing Percy saw was Vax leading her up to their bedroom and coddling her the entire way. Percy’s heart filled with warmth, reminded how beautiful their- their family truly was. With Keyleth being well taken care of, Percy made his way to his own bed. Or rather, his and Vex’s bed. 

She gravitated toward him the minute he got under the covers. “Keyleth alright?” Vex asked with a yawn. “I heard her coughing in the hallway.”

Percy nodded. “She’ll be fine.”

He woke himself with a sneeze the next morning, scraping against a throat that felt like he’d been swallowing nails the night before instead of nice herbal tea. Fantastic.

He’d been up late, very late, so of course Vex was awake and doing whatever she did in the morning. It wasn’t like him to sleep in no matter how long he’d been up, and he couldn’t bring himself to want to get out of bed. 

Harsh hacking sounded from down the hall, and this time Percy echoed it with his own fit. His head, his chest, felt completely full of gunk that it was trying to expel in any possible way.

“Percy?” He looked up as his coughing died down and he was able to take a breath. Vax was watching him with disgust and possible sympathy. “You alright? You sound about as well as Keyleth does right now.”

Percy sighed and sniffled. He ran the back of his hand under his nose, something he wouldn’t usually do, at least not in front of someone. “Ugh.” 

“Yeah, sounds about right.” More coughing from down the hall, and a frown appeared on Vax’s face.

“It might be best for the two of us to be quarantined. Can’t have everyone else coming down with this…”

Vax rolled his eyes. “Don’t be dramatic, Whitey. We’re not quarantining you.”

“I’m not being dramatic,” Percy hissed. “What will happen if all of us get sick? We’d be vulnerable to any number of-”

“Percy, calm down.” The tone of Vax’s voice wasn’t its normal obnoxiousness. He’d stepped further into the room, now standing right next to the bed and looking alarmingly serious. “That’s something to worry about later. Right now, though, we’re going to get you two feeling better.” He turned to walk out, but before he did, “and I’m not quarentining my fucking girlfriend, you imbecile.”

Right, of course. He needed to keep Keyleth in mind when he was trying to plan something. Thinking clearly was so difficult when his head was full of ick.

He sighed to himself. He should probably get out of bed. Sick or not, Percy still had projects to finish before their next adventure.

Getting out from under the warmth of the blankets was a rough transition. He managed, eventually, with a snuffle as the change in position did strange things to his sinuses. Percy muffled a hacking cough into his sleeve as he made his way out the door in his dressing gown. 

The cough from the room he passed had him backtracking. Keyleth was snuggled up in bed and yet not looking comfortable at all, in the middle of a harsh fit. 

"I'm getting a little breakfast," Percy said, choosing his words carefully as to not give away his congestion. "Would you like anything?"

Keyleth smiled, eyes still watery. "Oh, no thank you. Vax is fetching things, I think he's trying really to be useful." She giggled.

Percy chuckled. "Fair enough."

“So how’re you feeling?”

Percy frowned and took some steps into the bedroom. He chuckled awkwardly. So much for hiding it. “I’ll survive.”

“Sorry for getting you sick, Percy.”

Percy smiled, sitting on the edge of the bed next to her. “Come here,” he said with a sniffle, gesturing for her to lean closer. When she did, he pressed a kiss to her cheek and pulled a piece of hair behind her ear. “Feel better, alright? I’ll be downstairs if you-”

“No you’re not!”

“...Pardon?”

“Percy, you’re not working while you’re sick! If I have to stay in bed all day, so do you, damn it!”

“Keyleth-” But she was already shuffling over in the bed and pulling the covers back.

“Come on,” she said as she patted the spot next to her. “We’re going to have a fun little sick day together and neither of us are going to work on anything.”

He wanted to argue, but she sounded so determined he couldn’t say no. Percy climbed into the bed and let her rest her head on his shoulder with a snuffle. 

Vex and Vax waited on them the entire day, smothering them with attention and love. Percy pretended that it wasn’t the best day he’d had in a while.
