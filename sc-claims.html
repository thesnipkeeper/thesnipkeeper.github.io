Title: Claims
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Patrick will believe David the next time he claims to be sick
Word count: 300

Claims

“You’re not that sick, David,” Patrick says with a scoff.

Alright, so admittedly he’s been trying to milk the ‘might be getting sick thing’ all day, but the truth is that he now feels terrible.

But Patrick won’t believe him. Because he doesn’t have any obvious symptoms. He’s unnaturally tired, he has a chill that won’t go away, and every inch of his skin is achy and oversensitive (he had to go for the least-scratchy sweater this morning when none of them were comfortable enough).

But Patrick doesn’t know all that. And David’s going to sound even whinier if he tries to list it off, so instead he just rolls his eyes and decides to move on with the day. The quicker they can get inventory done, the quicker he can talk Patrick into letting him go home. Except...

There’s one thing he hasn’t tried that just might work. The one obvious symptom if he just...

David sets his clipboard down and walks up to Patrick.

“Do I have a fever?”

“David-” Patrick rolls his eyes, but lifts his hand anyway and- “Actually you do feel kind of warm,” he says, furrowing his brow, and then bringing his hand from David’s forehead to cheek, then neck. “Okay,” Patrick sighs, concern now evident on his face. “Okay, so you’re officially abolished from doing inventory.”

His hand hasn’t left David’s skin, now gently cupping his cheek and feeling exceptionally cool. David can’t help but lean into it.

“You’re really warm, actually. Are you sure you don’t have the flu?”

David scoffs. “I’m sure it’s nothing.”

It’s not nothing.

Patrick has to call Alexis to pick him up when he nearly passes out carrying a box from the stockroom.

But damn if he doesn’t believe David next time he claims to be sick.
