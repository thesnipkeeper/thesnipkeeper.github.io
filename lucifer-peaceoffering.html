Peace Offering

Lucifer has learned that bringing a hot beverage to someone in the morning is a fantastic way to stay in their good graces.

Which is why he's standing here right now, at the detective's door, with two coffees in-hand and a wide smile on his face. He gives a quick, non-threatening knock and waits.

The person who opens the door is not Detective Decker. It can't be. The person who opens the door looks like they haven't slept or showered in days, dressed in an old stretched-out T-shirt and a pair of sweats with a frayed drawstring.

"Lucifer, what are you doing here?"

"I brought you a coffee!" He says grandly, trying his best to ignore her horrendous state. "A peace offering, if you'll accept-"

It's snatched out of his hand before he can finish that sentence, and Lucifer watches as Chloe gulps half the cup in one go.

"Oh my, well-"

Chloe sighs, regaining her composure. "Sorry. Look, thank you for the coffee. Seriously. But you don't need to be here. And you probably shouldn't be here. I took the last two days off because Trixie's sick, and Dan's got this big case, and..." she breathes out a heavy sigh and runs a hand through her greasy hair.

Lucifer blinks, at a loss of what to say at first. He really wasn't expecting this.

"What?" she spits, adding a sharpness to the 'T'.

"Well, I..." he chuckles. "I just assumed that you were giving me a classic silent treatment. But now I know that you haven’t called because you haven't been working! What a relief."

"Oh," she huffs out a laugh. "Oh no, I'm still not happy with you."

Their last interaction involved a lot of yelling, and ended with "Just go home, Lucifer."

She went radio silent on him. Lucifer's never found himself to care whether people are upset with him. It usually means that they just don't know any better. Still, he hadn’t been able to get those words out of his mind, the way she looked at him...

Hence, the coffee.

She takes another sip, and Lucifer smiles.

“Yet, you drink my peace offering.”

Chloe looks at the cup and nods as if just realizing that she’s still drinking it. “Okay, fair point.”

“So, the offspring is ill?” he begins, as he lets himself in and closes the door behind him. Chloe is clearly too engrossed in her caffeine intake to either notice or do anything about it.

Chloe sighs out and glances toward the couch. Lucifer follows her gaze to see Trixie fast asleep, snuggled under a blanket. There’s a bucket next to the sofa that has him cringing.

“Oh,” he says with unhindered disgust.

A scoff. “Yeah. Dan’s going to come help out tonight so I can finally... breathe, a little.”

"I see."

The bundle on the couch stirred.

“Lucifer? Lucifer!” the offspring lifted her head to look at him over the edge of the couch. She gasps excitedly and moves faster than Lucifer expects as she hops off and runs to him. He finds himself engulfed in a hug before he knows it, just as it always seems to be the case with the small creature.

Lucifer reacts with an oof, and a “Hello.”

“Honey, let's not smother Lucifer in germs, huh?” Chloe says, effectively peeling her off of him. “Go lay down, I’ll get you something to eat, okay?”

Trixie does so, but not before giving Lucifer one last goodbye. The sound of cartoons starts up once she returns to her nest.

He brushes his clothes of the child grime and straightens himself. "Let me cook for you both. You clearly haven't eaten anything of proper nutritional value in days."

He's already making his way to the kitchen, opening cupboards and cabinets and pulling random things from the fridge. Chloe seats herself at the island and rests her chin on her hand.

"Usually, you fight me a bit more," he says. "Are you sure you're not getting whatever the plague rat has?"

She chuckles and shakes her head. "I'm immune to Trixie's germs. Dan always hated that he'd get knocked on his ass every time she brought something home. Guess that's just my superpower... Lucifer, what are you making?"

"Peanut butter banana toast for the offspring," he says proudly. "Scrambled eggs for you."

"Scrambled eggs?"

"Don't judge until you've tasted it! You've got a well-stocked kitchen here, detective. I admire it."

"I've been trying to cook more. Besides grilled cheese and spaghettios, I mean. Obviously hasn't happened lately."

"Well, prepare yourself for the most fantastic and fulfilling meal you've ever tasted."

"Again, scrambled eggs?"

Lucifer grins.

The look on Chloe's face as she tastes the perfectly-seasoned eggs, with chopped vegetables added as well to prevent the detective's body from failing despite the amount of vending machine food she eats on a daily basis, is clear in his mind. He feels... well, he feels good. It makes him feel good to help, he finds. Even the offspring seems to enjoy her meal, and Lucifer doesn't hate that, either.

-----

It starts with a dull ache in the pit of his stomach.

The true pain he experiences is when he dumps the rest of a perfectly good drink down the drain and refills it with water.

He thinks that something is strange as the night goes on. He doesn't feel the usual exuberance, the endorphin rush that comes with being in the midst of the excitement in the club.

"Lucifer!" A beautiful brunette in a tight black dress sing-songs her way to him. "How free are you tonight?"

"Oh," he chuckles. "I'm not quite sure, Anne. What did you have in mind?"

"Well," her hands caress him in a way that reminds him of the last time they slept together, and there's nothing he'd rather do.

Until her hand runs down his abdomen at the exact same moment that that dull ache turns into an overwhelming pain and a sense of urgency.

Poor Anne did not expect to get shoved off of him so suddenly, and probably didn't expect for him to run off without any explanation.

He darts into his apartment and runs in the direction of the bathroom, fiddling with his belt in a panic. This can't be happening. This doesn't happen.

It does. He sits on the toilet doubled over in pain, whimpering like a damn child as his intestines evacuate the expensive dinner he'd treated himself to just two hours before.

He thinks it's over when the cramps subside. But the pain is replace by nausea before he can get himself out of the confines of the bathroom and he finds himself vomiting a lot all at once.

So this is happening. He can't recall ever feeling this miserable.

He runs a hand through his now-damp hair and removes his clothes when he begins to cook in them. The desire to lie in bed and sleep this off is strong, but the motivation to get to the bed isn’t there. He falls onto the sofa with zero grace, and Lucifer is overcome by his body’s demand to rest.

-----

She's a little worried.

It's alarming, actually, if Chloe's being honest, when she texts her partner and doesn't receive a text back. And then when her call goes to voicemail. Maybe he's trying to ghost her like she'd done to him, but that wouldn't make much sense, either. He knows that she hadn't done it entirely on purpose.

Chloe taps her fingernail on the back of her phone and sighs as she decides what to do.

In the end, Chloe comes to the conclusion that she needs his help. She also comes to the conclusion that she's irked that he's ignoring her and a sense of satisfaction might come from going to his home to chew him out properly.

I really need your perspective on this one, she texts. And then, Put some pants on. I'm coming over.

-----

The place is dark. There's no sign of Lucifer, no sign of another unfortunate sexual encounter for Chloe to walk in on.

He's clearly not home.

Clearly, until she spots a lump in the couch. No way.

Lucifer Morningstar would not be napping on his sofa in the middle of the day.

Chloe approaches the sofa and a sour stench reaches her nose. She winces, walks closer, and gasps.

"Oh, Lucifer…"

Huddled beneath three blankets, but now sweating so much that his hair and what she can see of his shirt are both freshly damp. He takes quick, shallow breaths in a restless sleep. He looks horrible.

And then there's the puddle of vomit on the floor next to him. It's as if he had the energy to do little but simply lift his head and lean over to throw up.

Chloe almost feels bad waking him, but she can't let him sleep like this. He's going to burn up or dehydrate or both.

She carefully steps around the mess and lays a hand on the side of his face. He's warm.

"Hey, Lucifer?"

"Hm?"

"Wake up for a second, okay? You're sick."

Lucifer's lashes flutter as he wakes. His brows furrow, he gives a low groan of misery while he shifts uncomfortably beneath the pile of smothering blankets. Eyes open, and widen when they land on her.

“Detective,” he gasps out. Lucifer becomes aware of his current state, even while he’s clearly feverish and out of it. He sits up against the arm of the couch as the blankets fall off his bare shoulders. Chloe hopes to God (or... whoever) that he’s wearing pants under there. Not that it’s anything she hasn’t seen before, unfortunately. “This... this is all your fault...”

“My fault?” she spits. “Why-... Oh.”

He looks up at her with a look that’s both exhausted and vengeful. “Yes. Oh.”

“I didn’t make you stay-” she shakes her head. “You know what? That’s not important right now. I’m- I’m gonna go get you some water and clean up a little bit.”

“Wh-” his tired gaze falls to the mess on the floor and horror hits him. He closes his eyes and sighs out through his mouth as if thinking “This isn’t happening to me”.

She ignores his humiliation. “You should have called me.”

“Mm,” a nod. “That didn’t come to mind.”

He shoves the blankets to the other end of the couch and stares at nothing for a couple of seconds. And he’s wearing boxers. Thank Whoever. He looks like he could fall asleep all over again, his body hasn’t had near enough rest.

The first thing Chloe does is get a bottle of water from the fridge. (It’s well-stocked and she's shocked up until she remembers the number of houseguests he has on the regular). She cracks it open and brings it to him without the lid in the hopes that it forces him to drink a little. That doesn’t seem to be a problem, because he takes it and gulps down half the bottle before Chloe can stop him.

“Your stomach isn’t in the best shape right now, in case you haven’t noticed. Probably shouldn’t be bombarding it all at once.”

“I appreciate the water, Detective. Since I wouldn’t have been likely to retrieve it on my own.” He swallows. Chloe winces. “But you can go now.”

“I remember this exact conversation from a couple days ago, and it ended with you staying and making me eat the first real meal I’d had that week.”

“I am begging you, do not cook for me right now.” He swallows again, drags a hand down his face.

Chloe snorts, but... sympathetically. “No, just... lay down.”

“Actually,” Lucifer takes a deep breath. “I think that I might throw up.”

“You mean chugging half a bottle of water wasn’t a good idea?”

He glares at her right before panic crosses his face and a hand goes to clamp over his mouth in a last-ditch effort not to lose his meager stomach contents on the floor allover again. He goes to stand, nearly falls over and has to catch himself on the arm of the couch before Chloe intervenes.

“Okay, alright, let’s go.” She holds onto his arm. She isn’t a fan of the warmth radiating off of him, or the amount of his weight that she’s actually holding up right now because his legs can’t seem to do it. He blinks rapidly as if getting lightheaded. “Almost there, hang on...”

The bathroom looks like it’s been camped in already, towels on the floor as if he slept there.

Lucifer collapses to his knees as his body finally gives out on him. He barely gets a hold of himself to get over the toilet in time, but its not like there’d be much of a mess if he hadn’t. What comes out of him is water and nothing else.

Chloe rubs his back. “You gonna be okay for a second while I go take care of your floor situation?”

“No, Detective...” Lucifer breathes out, shaking his head insistently. “Don’t do that. Let me keep some of my dignity, I beg of you.”

“You’re draped over a toilet seat like a renascence painting and you think you still have dignity?”

He furrows his brows in annoyance and huffs. “That was uncalled for.”

“Lucifer, I spent all of last week cleaning up puke. One more time isn’t gonna kill me.”

He doesn’t have the energy to argue with that.

She makes sure he’s in bed, with a waste bin next to him and several bottles of water, along with a package of crackers that he glared at when she set it on his nightstand.

“You don’t have to eat until you’re ready,” she says. “But you’re not going to want to get out of bed for a while. Trust me.”

His expression goes blank, as plain as she’s ever seen that stupid face. Like he’s thinking, worrying over something.

She tries to gain eye contact from him once again. “What’s wrong? Or are you just tired?”

He shakes his head. “No, I... well, yes, I’m disgustingly exhausted, but...” a sigh. “I... haven’t been ill in... thousands of years? I have to say, I don’t like it.”

“Well, I’d be worried if you did.”

“Yes, well... thank you for this, Detective.”

“I’d say sorry for getting you sick, but I literally told you to leave. Like, multiple times. I warned you.”

He smiles a bit. “But you didn’t want me to go.”

“No,” she sighs out, sitting on the edge of his bed. “Of course I didn’t. It was nice talking to an adult and it was nice eating food that didn’t come from a microwave.”

“Is your offspring alright now?”

“She’s fine. She bounces back pretty fast.” Chloe looks him over. He does look like he’s about to crash, could probably use some peace and quiet after the horrible night he had. “I need to go work on this case. It’s the whole reason I came over here, actually. I wanted your opinion.”

Lucifer’s eyes had fallen shut, but then they’re open once again. “Really? What is it about? Details, Detective.”

Chloe shakes her head. “Nope, sorry, I’ve gotta let you sleep. Text me if you need anything.”

Lucifer frowns childishly as she stands up. “I have absolutely nothing to do.”

“Sleep, that’s what your body needs right now!”

“Ugh.”

-----

Chloe gets as much work done as she can. She is going to want to talk to Lucifer at some point, but it can wait until tomorrow. They're not completely lost without him.

She’s just in the process of packing up her things for the night when her phone starts to buzz and Lucifer’s name pops up on the screen.

“Hey Lucifer, you alright?”

“Detective, I’m... quite sick.”

The tone of his voice gives her pause. It’s rough around the edges, thicker and a bit slurred. He sounds even worse than that morning.

“As in, sicker?”

“I don’t know,” he replies lowly. “I don’t know, I just can’t... can’t stop throwing up.”

He sounds sad. That’s the only way Chloe can interpret it. Sad.

Guilt tugs at her.

Oh, Lucifer...

“Hey, I’m on my way, alright? Just take it easy until I get there.”

“Don’t intend on doing much else, Detective.”

-----

He’s curled up in bed, shaking like a leaf with the trash bin in his lap. He’s in the process of dry heaving, and it looks painful and miserable and she can’t help but rush to his side and rub his back in comfort. His tense muscles seem to relax just a bit under her hand.

Chloe brushes the damp hair off of his forehead. “I shouldn’t have left you like this," she mutters.

“I don’t know what’s wrong with me,” he rasps. “This... this doesn’t just happen.”

“Unless you’re around me, I guess.” Chloe sighs.

“Detective...” his grasp is too weak to fight it as she takes the waste bin from his hand and sets it aside. She ignore the tiny slosh of liquid as she moves it and sets it down. “I... really don’t feel well.”

“I know, I’m sorry.” Chloe bumps her forehead into his shoulder in some attempt at affection that can’t be interpreted incorrectly later. “You wanted me to stay with you earlier. I completely missed it.”

He hums. “You needed to get back to work.”

“Still, I could have... checked in on you, at least.” She ends her pity party when she gets a good look at him. There’s no way he’s gotten enough sleep, or maybe any. “Have you been able to drink anything?”

He shakes his head, looking particularly miserable. “Won’t stay down.”

“Okay, that’s okay.”

“I’m going to trust that you mean that, because right now I feel like I may die.”

She chuckles, and then sees that he’s genuinely nervous about his current state. It makes sense. If she had the stomach flu without having ever experienced it before, she’d probably wonder if she was dying, too. The fever can’t be helping his emotional state.

“The first night Trixie was sick last week, I almost took her to the ER. She couldn’t keep anything down and her fever seemed like it was getting higher and higher. But in the end she was just fine.”

Lucifer furrows his brows at her.

Chloe sighs. “My point is that you might feel like hell,” he chuckles at that. “right now, but you’ll be fine, alright? I’m here. And I don’t think that taking you to the hospital would be a good idea. They’d probably never let you out.”

Lucifer lets out a whimper. He lays his head on her shoulder, so defeated and tired and with fever. Chloe wraps her arms around him in the tightest hug she can muster.

“Did you sleep at all today?”

“Hour or so.”

“Okay, well,” she pulls away gently. “Lie down and rest. I’ll be here.” Chloe kicks her shoes off and pulls her legs onto the bed as if to prove it. Even as sick as he is, he stares as if he can’t believe that she’s voluntarily putting herself in his bed.

He lowers himself under the covers, still shaking and shivering. She’ll have to see if she can find some meds of some kind for him. Can he even take meds? Would he have any?

“How about that case of yours?”

“Lucifer...”

“Please, give me something to think about for a bit.”

“Okay, okay... well, we found a body chained up to a set of train tracks. Well, more like two pieces of a body. Ugh, sorry, that visual probably isn’t helping your stomach.”

Lucifer scoffs. “On the contrary, gory details don’t deter me.”

“Right. We think he was being interrogated somehow.”

“Not an execution?”

“Maybe, but there were signs of torture on... on both halves of him. He was an accountant for a firm by the...” Chloe trails off when she hears snoring.

Lucifer is sound asleep.

She hums.

Before she can stop herself, Chloe leans down and kisses the top of his head.

“Sleep well, Lucifer.”
