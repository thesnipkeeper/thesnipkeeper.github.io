Weary Memory

Where on earth was that man?

Stede thought that Ed would be on the ship, entertaining the crew with some insane story of piracy or divulging true knowledge of sailing from his vast experience at sea. It wasn’t just that he wasn’t on deck, but that no one else had seen him all day, either.

Stede thought about the last place he’d seen Ed as if Ed were a misplaced shoe.

Oh, his quarters.

They’d spent the night together, (snuggling, rather). He supposed that Ed was still there waking up when he’d gone out to see how the crew was doing.

“Ed, you in here?”

It didn’t take long for him to see the figure on his sofa.

It was an Edward he’d never quite seen before. The man was wrapped in the same blanket he’d hogged during the night, having very clearly dragged it from the bed.

“What are you doing? Adventure awaits-!”

Ed’s large brown eyes looked up at him as he shuddered. The man was practically shivering hard enough to shake the whole ship. He held the blankets around him like a lifeline.

“Oh, Ed...” Stede frowned and sat on the edge by his legs.

“Can’t- can’t get warm,” he whined. “I’m all- I’m all dizzy and weird. I don’t know what the hell is wrong with me.”

Stede laid the back of his hand to Ed’s forehead and nodded. “That’s certainly a fever,” he informed.

“Impossible.”

“I assure you that it’s not. Come on, back to bed with you.”

Ed shook his head, even as he let Stede help him sit up. “No, no I haven’t been ill in years.”

“Mm,” hummed Stede as he helped Ed to his feet. He took his hand and most of his weight along with it as they made their slow trek back to the bed. “I’m not sure that’s possible.”

“What d’you mean?” His slurred speech was rather adorable. And also the fact that he let himself get tucked in like a child. He was very cute in all regards, really.

“Well, you’re a pirate. You travel the world terrorizing other people. You come in contact with illness and bad food and all sorts of awful disgusting things. So I’m not sure I believe that you haven’t been sick in years, Ed.”

Edward huffed, which incited a sudden, congested cough. “Well it’s true.”

“Alright, alright. Do you want something to drink? Perhaps some tea?”

He thought about it, and nodded. “Tea... would be nice.”

Stede smiled and patted his leg. “I’ll be right back.”

-----

“That’s ridiculous.” Izzy sharpened his knife in a manner that had Stede taking a step away with each swipe. “He’s been ill dozens of times. Won’t stop putting strange food in his mouth. The man’s addicted to new experiences or whatever the fuck.”

“I see...” Stede furrowed his brows. “I’ll make sure he rests and recovers properly.”

Izzy snorted. “Good luck with that, mate,” with all the sarcasm in the world that Stede didn’t understand. “He’ll rest when he’s dead and no sooner.”

Stede got about the same thing from the rest of Blackbeard’s crew as well. Everyone else claimed that he got sick just as much as any other person, and possibly more because of the way he insisted on living. He thought about it as he retrieved the tea and brought it back to the man shivering in his bed.

Ed was in the middle of a coughing fit, curled in on himself and head lifted just slightly as his boy tried to rid itself of the irritants. Stede sat on the edge of the bed and laid a gentle, careful hand on the man’s back. He still felt the fever, even under the pajamas he never changed out of.

“Easy, easy...” he soothed.

Ed took a shaky breath and coughed again.

“Here, sit up and have some tea. It’ll help.”

Ed tried to suppress the fit, and failed spectacularly when he ended up coughing on Stede’s arm. Stede was far from caring about that sort of thing, now that he was a pirate and all.

Ed took a moment to gather himself before accepting the tea. “You should to get out of here before you get sick.”

“Well, you’re in my quarters so I won’t be doing that,” Stede informed with a soft smile. “I did share a bed with you last night, anyway. I’m not sure it’s worth retreating now.”

Ed harrumphed and sipped his tea. His nose ran a bit from the steam. Stede went and found a stack of handkerchiefs, setting them next to him without a word. Ed glanced at them sheepishly and took one to press to his nose with a damp snf.

Stede took the empty teacup out of his unsteady grip when he was done, and laid the back of his hand against the man’s warm, dry forehead.

“You ought to sleep off that fever, hm?”

Ed frowned, brows furrowing in annoyance. “Don’t know if I can fall asleep at the moment. Everything hurts.”

Stede made a hum of sympathy. “That’s alright, I can keep you company.”

He seemed to like that, because he moved over and let Stede settle in the bed with a book. The heavy weight of his head fell on Stede’s shoulder while Ed hummed happily.

Izzy had given him the impression that keeping Blackbeard in bed for a sick day would be impossible. But Stede had seen nothing besides a sleepy, feverish version of the insane man who was now his closest friend. He ran his fingers through the tangles of Ed’s hair and made a note to Future Stede to do something about that mess. One thing at a time.

He read for a while. Ed had slowly fallen down until his head was on Stede’s thigh, hot, congested puffs of breath hitting Stede’s hand on occasion. He tried to cover his coughs and sneezes, to his credit, but one or two sneezes did sneak up on him. He made a grumpy apology and sat up for a moment to blow his nose.

He sniffed liquidly. “Would you... mind passing me another kerchief?” he asked, looking rather embarrassed but also desperate to fix his nose before it could drip down his upper lip.

Stede did so with a cheery, “Here you are!”

“Thank you. snf! God... my fucking nose.”

Stede made a noise of sympathy as he hands went to pet the man’s hair. “You know...” he began, letting the book fall open in his lap. “I spoke to Izzy, and the rest of your crew.”

“Mm?”

“You’ve no need to hide things from me to try and seem... invulnerable, Ed. I’m a bit hurt that you felt the need to.” Edward sat up - fully this time - and looked at Stede with furrowed brows. He attempted to read Stede’s face for a few seconds before the man continued. “I’m not upset or anything! Just confused, I suppose.”

Ed blinked, crinkled his nose with another sniffle. “What in the hell are you talking about? What the fuck did Izzy say?”

He should have left it alone, shouldn’t he? But he was in it now. He chuckled, because it really wasn’t that important! “Everyone I’ve spoken to tells me that you’ve been ill plenty of times! That’s all, no big deal.”

Ed narrowed his eyes. “No I haven’t.”

“Edward...”

“I haven’t been ill!” His tone wasn’t even defensive, rather... insistent and a bit confused. “The last time was- was-”

“Food poisoning from badly-fermented fish in a tavern?” Fang had divulged that one. As well as, “Or when you nearly died from influenza?”

Ed’s mouth closed slowly. His eyes drifted down as if the memory were just coming to him. “Huh.”

“Fucking scurvy, Ed, really? You’re telling me that none of those things happened?”

“I...” why did he look so confused? “I don’t- damn,” he breathed out a chuckle. “You’re right. You’re absolutely right. Why didn’t I remember that?”

“I can’t begin to guess,” Stede replied honestly.

“Huh,” he said again. He fell back onto the bed and sniffed again. “That’s strange, isn’t it?”

“Well, of course it is! That doesn’t concern you?”

“Honestly, there’s so little that I care about at the moment.” With how miserable he looked, Stede supposed that was fair.

“Alright...” Stede sighed. He picked up the book when he accepted that he wasn’t getting anything else on that topic. “Shall I continue.”

“Please!”

Ed was asleep within minutes, and so was Stede.

-----

Stede woke up the moment the other man started coughing. He wasn’t sure how long either of them had been asleep, but it couldn’t have been long.

Poor Ed was back to trying to get his lungs under control, his nose running freely as his attention was taken away from it. He was trying to cough away from Stede, but the bed was only so big.

A particularly deep cough caught Stede’s attention. Neither of them had any warning before Ed vomited on the bed, and Stede’s lap, and himself.

“Oh goodness!” Stede exclaimed.

Ed had stopped coughing, but now he held a hand over his mouth with wide eyes. The first thing Stede could think to grab was the water basin on top of his bureau. It was empty, thankfully, (Stede had learned his lesson about leaving open containers of water on a ship) but probably wouldn’t ever be used for its intended purpose again as he watched Edward heave miserably into it.

The poor man didn’t have much to bring up besides tea and water.

He laid a hand on Ed’s back and offered comforting words that felt useless at the moment. He realized too late that Ed’s hair had been in the line of fire the first time around, but still tried to hold it back.

“There we are...” he said softly. “Take a breath, that’s it, love.” The breath he attempted turned into another forceful heave. Ed whimpered. “Shhh, I’ve got you.”

Edward hunched forward, taking deep breaths as he tried to decide whether he was done. Stede took the opportunity to get a kerchief and wipe his mouth and nose. Ed made an annoyed noise but didn’t bother turning away.

“I’m not a toddler,” he muttered.

“Of course you aren’t,” Stede assured, carefully releasing his hair and inspecting the damage that had been done to the bed and, well, their clothes. “But anyway, why don’t you have a bath while I clean up a bit?”

“I tell you that I’m not a toddler and then you try to bathe m-” That drew Ed’s attention to the mess. He looked sheepish. “Y- yeah... my bad.”

“No worries at all!” there were a few worries about the stains setting in, but he decided to keep that to himself.

-----

Stede had gotten the great Blackbeard to agree to a bath. Ed looked in bliss the moment he sank into the warm water. His nose started running a second later and he scrubbed it in frustration.

“Better to let it all come out, I think,” Stede offered, pressing a kiss to his forehead.

Ed harrumphed. “Gross.”

“You’ll be alright for a few minutes?”

Edward sniffled wetly and dragged his hand along the underside of his nose. “Yeah, I’m alright,” he said quietly. “Thanks.” He gave the smallest, most adorable of smiles as Stede left the room.

Stede went to work cleaning. And cleaning, and cleaning. His heart ached as he heard his friend, his love, sneezing and coughing harshly in the bath. The poor man wasn’t in for a good time, that was certain, but Stede intended to make it less miserable than any other time he’s been ill. And perhaps to prove to Izzy Hands that he was capable of taming the beast that was an Ill Blackbeard who had yet to give him any trouble.

The sounds of Ed’s misery quieted. Stede went to check on him, hoping that he hadn’t fallen asleep or something.

No, he was awake.

And Stede was pretty sure he was crying.

“Oh no,” Stede cooed, hurrying to his side and announcing his presence in the process.

Ed, embarrassed, tried to wipe his face dry and failed because his hands were indeed wet from bath water. He snuffled uselessly, liquid building on his upper lip and eyes red with tears.

“Goodness, Ed, what’s wrong?” Stede asked tenderly. “Does something hurt? I can-”

Ed shook his head and took a long, shaky breath to calm himself. “Don’t know what’s wrong with me,” he sniffed. “I’m just frustrated is all.”

Stede hummed sympathetically. “Being ill is no fun. Mind if I wash your hair?”

Ed simply tipped his head back a bit and let Stede begin. “No, not-” he huffed, sniffed sharply as if to hold back more tears. “Just don’t understand why I didn’t remember being ill all of those other times. Not until you mentioned it. What the fuck does that mean? Am I losing my mind?” His voice broke at those last few words.

Stede tutted as he massaged soap into his hair. “You’re not losing your mind, Edward.”

“snf! You don’t know that.”

“I do, because I know you, and while I’m not sure any of us are truly sane, you’re of just as much sound mind as I am.”

Ed had melted, his breathing slowed gradually as Stede washed his hair.

Ed snorted. “Great. We’re a couple of nuts, then.”

“Oh, sure, we can say that. But just because your memory was a bit cloudy at first doesn’t mean there’s something wrong with you.” He dipped a cup in the water and began to gently rinse the soap out, combing his fingers through as he did. “It’s quite possible that you weren’t too aware of it in the first place. You’re easily-distracted, after all. Constantly have a thousand things on your mind, lots of pirate-y things to do. Especially back then.”

Ed made a hum that sounded vaguely like agreement. “Guess so.”

“And look at you now, being all sweet and cuddly. Can’t say I don’t enjoy that.”

He chuckled, a small smile tugging at the edge of his lips. “Well,” he said. “I’ve never been cared for quite like this. ‘s nice. To have someone.”

Stede thought of all the times he himself had craved care and love. He grew up gravitating towards the first person to show him kindness no matter how many times he got fucked over. One would think he was insane for trusting anyone ever again, but perhaps it was the idea that someone had to be trustworthy and he just had to wade through the rest to find it. Now he had a crew full of people willing to do (almost) anything for him.

Ed seemed to have gone the other way. Not having grown up with as much love as he deserved, he’d decided that he didn’t need it at all.

Stede intended to show him that he did need it, and absolutely did deserve all of it.

They sat in a comfortable silence for a good while as Stede combed his fingers through the thick locks of hair. It would need a proper brushing, and perhaps put up and out of the way to keep it from getting too tangled if Ed were to remain bedridden.

“Best we get you out of this tub before the water goes cold, I think,” Stede said, keeping his voice gentle to not break the silence too violently. “Then I think I’ll do something to your hair, if that’s alright with you.”

Ed nodded, sniffled. “ ‘s fine with me.”

-----

The sun was particularly bright that morning, and the air was strangely dry for it being, well, ocean and all. Stede had to clear his throat several times, and the salty air still stung for some reason. Still, he remained on deck, giving orders and directions to the crew for their next venture (food run).

“Oi, captain!”

Stede folded away the handkerchief that he’d had pressed to his nose most of the day, slipping it into his pocket. It was quite cold out, his poor nose had been running since he arrived on deck.

“Oluwande! What a fine morning, no?”

He shrugged and nodded. “Sure.”

“Bit chilly, though, don’t you think?”

Olu raised an eyebrow and cleared his throat. “Not really. How’s um, how’s Blackbeard fairing, flu and all?”

“Oh, he’s recovering nicely.” Ed had gotten tired of being in bed, so he moved to the couch that morning with a stack of books. The man must have been quite bored if he was delving into Stede’s library. “Very kind of you to ask!” Stede took a moment to lean against the edge of the boat, feeling a bit lightheaded. He should have eaten breakfast, why did he skip breakfast?

“Yeah, well...” Olu sighed heavily, and Stede began to realize that something other than a pleasant conversation was on its way. “Listen captain, we appreciate your dedication and all...”

“Thank you...?”

“... but maybe you should be in bed as well. You don’t sound too great.”

Stede furrowed his brows and tried to figure out what in the world the man was talking about. “I’m sorry, Olu, I don’t quite follow.”

Olu chuckled. “C’mon, captain, you’ve obviously got what he had.”

Stede blinked. Oluwande frowned, eyes widening when he realized that Stede didn’t actually understand what he meant.

“You’ve- you’ve been sneezing all morning...? And you’re breathing real hard, and it’s actually pretty warm out here, but you’re wearing layers.”

Stede frowned. The headache, the sore throat, runny nose, loss of appetite...

Ah.

“Huh. I... I hadn’t noticed.”

Olu nodded. “You ought to be in bed. I can ask Roach to make you up some of that tea that Blackbeard liked so much.”

It was mostly alcohol. Stede didn’t care.

“... sure,” he said, fully aware that it was admitting defeat. “Alright, thank you, Olu.”

“No problem. Hope the two of you feel better, captain.”

Stede didn’t feel the true weight of his illness until he stepped foot in his quarters. Suddenly, he was exhausted. He sniffled thickly, his nose had filled up again since the last time he blew it, and the sniffle ignited a tickle. Stede hurriedly pulled out his handkerchief and muffled three awful, wet, grating sneezes into it.

“Whoa,” Ed had been sitting against the arm of the couch fully engaged in a book up until that point. “You sound like shit.” His own voice was still a little rough.

Stede sniffed pitifully and went to flop on the other end. He found himself wrapped up in a blissfully warm embrace that still did nothing to calm his shivers. “The crew made me leave,” pouted Stede.

Ed pressed a stubbly kiss to his cheek. “Well now you get to hang out with me all day, so it’s not all bad.” A pause, and then, “Sorry. Didn’t mean to infect you.”

“Don’t you apologize.” Stede shook his head. “I’m just glad I get to spend more time with you.”

“Yeah?”

“Mhm. You know, it’s funny, I had no idea that I was ill until Olu pointed it out.”

“Hm. Maybe that’s more common than we thought.”

“Pirate captains are rather busy, after all.”

“Indeed we are.” Ed kissed him once more and let the man lay against him as he read.
