Word count: 1.1k

With Someone Around

Taliesin sighed and closed his computer. He’d been trying to do work for the past hour and it just wasn’t happening. He felt... weird. Everything felt wrong and he had so much to do and yet none of it were things that he could stand to do right now.

Often when he felt like this, he’d call Matt and Marisha and invite himself over. It was nice to be around them... they... they calmed him.

So, he called, his knee bouncing anxiously as he was apparently incapable of sitting still at the moment.

Marisha answered, and he was so relieved to hear her voice.

“That’s totally fine,” was her response, but then she hesitated. “But uh, just like, a warning, Matt’s been sick all day.”

Taliesin frowned. “Is he okay?”

“He’s just throwing up a lot,” Marisha said with a chuckle. “But if you’re not worried about germs...”

“I’m not,” he replied quickly. “I just... I just want to be around people, you know?”

There was a pause on the other end. “Are you okay?” Marisha asked, a bit of teasing in her voice though Taliesin knew the question was sincere.

“Yeah! Yeah, I’m fine. Like I said, I just want to be around people.”

“Mm’kay! Well, you’re free to come over. Matt’s not gonna mind.”

Taliesin wasted no time saying goodbye and getting ready to head out. He was eager to get there and hopefully relieve some of his weirdness.

-----

Marisha was in the kitchen when he walked in (he was beyond knocking at that point). She smiled and ran to give him a big hug.

“It’s nice to see you,” she said, while still hugging him tightly.

He chuckled. “You...too?”

She finally let him go after a few seconds. “Matt’s in bed if you wanna go say hi. I think he could use a little cheering up.”

“Rough day?”

Marisha nodded, making a sympathetic sound. “He’s been puking since two this morning. Now he’s kinda having trouble falling asleep.”

Taliesin frowned. So he was really sick, then. Oh, Matt...

“Not to mention,” Marisha added, lowering her voice a little. “He keeps trying to do work. Like, c’mon man you have a 102 fever, maybe just relax for a day.”

“102? Jesus.”

“Yeah,” she scoffed in agreement. “Go on, go say hi. Give him some love.” She nudged him toward the hallway and he continued down to the bedroom.

Matt was half-sitting up, laptop on his chest over the blankets that he was wrapped in. He looked awful. He was pale, his hair was not nearly as neat as the Mercer hair often was, and he just looked exhausted. So, of course he was trying to do work.

Matt didn’t even seem to notice him until Taliesin gave a scolding, “Whaaat are you doing?”

Matt looked up, and smiled so adorably it made him look less miserable. “Hey! I forgot Marisha said you were coming over.” His voice was hoarse and weak, and had Taliesin wincing.

“You sound awful,” Taliesin informed with a sympathetic chuckle as he sat on the edge of the bed.

“Ugh, I know.” Thankfully, he closed the laptop before Taliesin forced him to.

Taliesin took the computer and set it aside before laying the back of his hand on Matt’s forehead. “Jesus Christ, Marisha wasn’t kidding about your fever.”

Matt whined. “It’s not that baaaad.”

“Uh huh!”

He chuckled, reaching up to take Taliesin’s hand between his own. Even his hands were impossibly warm. “ ‘m just glad you’re here. Marisha said you sounded weird on the phone.”

Taliesin sighed and nodded. He hadn’t realized it, but he probably did. “Just felt like being around someone. You know, like you guys.”

“Well I’m glad you came over.”

“Me too!”

-----

They ended up in the living room. Matt still hadn’t been able to fall asleep, so Marisha suggested a movie night while the three of them were together.

Matt sat between the two of them on the couch, wrapped in a blanket and looked rather comfy for someone who’s spent the past twelve hours vomiting. He leaned his head on Taliesin’s shoulder and sighed contentedly.

Taliesin smiled and pressed a kiss to his rather greasy hair.

They were only twenty minutes into the movie when Marisha and Taliesin heard snoring. Matt had fallen asleep.

Marisha scoffed. “I hope you know that you’re stuck there for good. You wake up my husband and you die, Tal.”

“Oh boy,” Taliesin said. He had to admit, having the warm weight pressed against him was sort of comforting in a way.

They finished the movie with a sleeping Matt, and had only begun to quietly discuss the next one when he stirred awake.

Matt whined sleepily as he sat up. “Marisha, I-”

Unfortunately, he didn’t have time to say what he was going to say before he vomited down the side of the couch. Marisha and Taliesin both swore and rushed to help him.

And he was back to throwing up, apparently, camped in the bathroom where Taliesin imagined he’d been for a good portion of the day before he arrived. Marisha sat next to him on the tile floor, rubbing his back and looking almost as exhausted as he was. Taliesin couldn’t imagine that she’d gotten much sleep either.

“I’ll take care of the mess,” he said, grabbing some supplies from under the sink.

Marisha looked up, slightly horrified. “God, are you sure?”

“Guys, I can take care of it...” came the croaky voice of Matt Mercer.

“That’s big talk from someone who’s bent over a toilet now, buddy,” Taliesin said, giving his shoulder a quick squeeze. “Yeah, it’s fine. I want to be useful right now.”

Marisha looked hesitant, but it was clear that she had enough things on her mind besides trying to talk him out of it.

-----

In the end, Taliesin had cleaned and sanitized everything, and he’d started a load of laundry and remade the bed with nice clean sheets.

“Tal...” Matt said with an embarrassed smile as he climbed into said bed. “You didn’t have to do all this.”

Taliesin pressed a kiss to his temple as he and Marisha helped the poor guy get situated. “Like I said, I wanted to be useful.”

“God, you’re amazing,” Marisha muttered, absently running her fingers through Matt’s hair.

Taliesin smiled. “Hey, why don’t you both try to get some sleep?”

“Naw, I’m fine,” Marisha said.

“You look exhausted.”

Matt turned to her and immediately looked guilty, like he hadn’t realized it until now.

She sighed. “Okay fine, a like small nap.”

Taliesin kissed the both of them on the forehead and made sure they were adorably tucked in.

He had more laundry to do, anyway, which was apparently what he did now. Other people’s laundry. He chuckled at himself. It’d be weird if it wasn’t Matt and Marisha.

And as he did continue to clean their house, he realized that he felt far better than he had earlier.
