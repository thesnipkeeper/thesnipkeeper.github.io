Title: Delivery
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David and Patrick have the flu and decide that they need outside help
Word count: 2.3k

Delivery

David was a surprisingly attentive caregiver. When Patrick woke up with a fever and a cough, he expected to be quarantined. What happened instead was David shoving medicine at him and reminding him to stay hydrated.

"Are you sure you can do this on your own?" Patrick asked as David gave his hair some last-minute fixes.

He scoffed and seated himself on the edge of the bed. "Um, I’ve worked my store alone before. Believe it or not, I know what I’m doing.”

"I know, but we've got the big delivery coming in..." Patrick mumbled. His voice sounded awful, hoarse and full of congestion. It was a wonder David was letting him stay in their bed all day with how contagious he felt.

David leaned forward and gave him possibly the softest kiss in the world on his forehead. “I’ll handle it.”

Patrick wasn’t sure how much he believed him, but he felt too awful to question it.

-----

Stevie sauntered in just as David was opening the third box of product. He’d be pricing and restocking all day, but at least that meant Patrick wouldn’t have a lot of work to do when he came back.

“Hey,” she greeted.

“H-” David stopped to clear his throat. “Hey, what’s up?”

Stevie shrugged. “Motel things were slow, your dad gave me the rest of the day off. He said I ‘looked tired’ and I’m not sure how to take that.”

With a nod, David scoffed. “He didn’t understand why I used to freak out when he said that to me. So, what are you doing here?”

“Thought you could use some help. I remembered you saying you had the new product coming in, and I know how long it takes you to get it put out with Patrick around, so...”

David blinked. “I’m sorry... you got a day off and immediately came over here to work? For free?”

“Yeah. I mean, that and Patrick texted me and asked if I could come help you.”

“Nice to know he has faith in me, then,” David mumbled, clearing his throat again. He would need to get a drink of water at some point, it was so dry in the store today. And hot.

Stevie scoffed as if that was the most ridiculous thing she’d ever heard. “He probably just didn’t want you stuck here all day, David.”

“Oh. That too, I suppose.”

Stevie immediately grabbed a box and went to work. He watched her carefully to make sure she was doing it right, and much to his surprise, she made sure to turn the labels in the right direction.

David sneezed.

“Bless you.”

“Ugh, thank you. snf!” He ran the palm of his hand over his nose just as he had been reminding Patrick not to do for most of the morning.

David yawned.

Unpacking was a lot of work, and he was too busy taking care of his boyfriend to eat a proper breakfast. Not to mention they were both woken far too early by Patrick’s cough.

“Ugh,” David said, sniffling and wiping his nose on his sleeve. “I’m so tired today for some reason. I totally forgot to get coffee this morning,” he lamented.

"Are you sure you're not catching Patrick's flu?" Stevie asked, as casual as ever.

"snf! Definitely not. No time for that. Nope." No. No, right? That wasn’t possible, Patrick had only been sick for a few hours. Although... people were contagious before showing symptoms, and he and Patrick had also been together for most of the week so it was possible they could have both caught it from the same place...

Stevie raised an eyebrow. “David, you look really pale. And sweaty.”

She motioned toward his sweater sleeves, that he’d pulled up without even thinking about it. He didn’t usually believe in pulling his sleeves up. It could create ugly creases in the fabric and if he wanted a short-sleeved shirt then he’d wear one! But Stevie was right, he felt... sweaty.

“I’m fine,” this time he said it more to himself than Stevie. “I’m fine, I’m fine... not sick. Just super tired because I spent all morning taking care of my ailing boyfriend and stuff.”

“And stuff,” Stevie repeated, amused. “Right.”

David unpacked the next half of the hand creams before his head started to swim. Black spotted his vision more and more by the second and he was becoming increasingly aware that he wasn’t going to be standing for long. His body was giving him every warning sign to sit down, so he found one of the various chair scattered about the store and plopped down without any grace.

The strenuousness of walking across the room had him breathing heavily, which set off a coughing fit. It sounded about like the one he woke up to that morning.

When David opened his eyes, the room was still, and Stevie was standing next to him.

“David, go.”

He whined in protest, but that just prompted another pathetic cough. “Can’t close the store. Customer loyalty or... whatever,” he said with a lazy wave of the hand.

Stevie groaned. “If I promise to stay here for the rest of the day, would you go back to Patrick’s apartment?”

David looked up at her in disbelief. “Um... I- would you actually-?”

“I’ll be taking a bottle of wine home, but yes.”

-----

Patrick was fast asleep when David returned to the apartment, snoring softly and looking like that's all he'd been able to do today.

David immediately removed his shoes, put on some sweatpants, and climbed into bed with him, wrapping his arms around Patrick like a lifeline. Of course he had to cough as soon as he was somewhat comfortable. David tried to turn away, there was no point in covering when they were both sick.

His boyfriend made a questioning noise and turned over to face him. Patrick's eyes fluttered open.

Patrick snuffled, voice congested. "Why aren't you at the store?"

"Why do you think?" David sighed, or tried to, before he started coughing.

Patrick frowned. "Fuck."

"Mm. Shh. I think you and I should both try to sleep this off."

"Did you lock up?"

David sniffled and ran his hand under his nose, which was getting runnier by the minute. "Stevie's there. She'll close up."

Patrick's brows raised to his hairline. "You left Stevie with the store?!"

"She volunteered! Well, for a bottle of wine. I guess she cares about us or something dumb like that," he said with a sleepy smile. "Can we go to sleep now?"

David was finally getting comfortable again, which was why he felt the need to give the most pathetic whine when Patrick moved out from under him.

"Gotta pee."

"Ughh. Can't you hold it?"

Patrick scoffed as he slowly sat up. "No, David, I've already been holding it for a couple of hours."

"Oh my God, what?" David had lectured him time and time again on not holding his pee to avoid bladder infections. Patrick never really took him seriously, but he was determined to get through to him someday.

"I was tired," Patrick shrugged. He got his legs over the edge of the bed in one swift movement and stopped. David watched him lean over, head in hands.

David lifted his head from his pillow, giving a concerned, "Honey?"

Patrick didn't say anything for a few seconds, sitting still and breathing steadily. Finally, he lifted his head and sniffled. "'m good, just got super lightheaded for a second."

David worried his lip. "Do you… need help?"

"No," Patrick said as if that was an insane question to ask someone who'd been nearly incapable of getting out of bed all day.

David watched warily as his boyfriend stood on unsteady legs and started his way toward the bathroom. "Are you sure…?"

Patrick gave him one of those Looks that told David he didn't feel like talking, and David felt a little offended considering he was just worried.

But he stayed where he was and tried not to watch too closely. Instead, he curled up, deciding to get comfortable without his overly-warm pillow. His whole body was starting to ache, and a bone-deep chill had taken hold of him, causing him to shudder.

This was going to be their life for a few days, then.

He heard the bathroom door close, and the sink running a minute later. And then David started to doze off. The exhaustion hit him like a truck the minute he walked in. He planned on sleeping until this flu was out of his system.

THUMP

David’s eyes shot open.

“Patrick?”

Silence.

David’s stomach turned with anxiety as he sat up and ran to the bathroom with a kind of energy that came out of nowhere.

“Oh my God!” David knelt down where his boyfriend lay on the floor, blinking blearily. Patrick’s brow furrowed as he started to pull himself up. “Easy...” David said, lowering his voice. “Oh my God, are you hurt?”

Patrick shook his head. “Lost my balance. Sorry.”

David felt himself tearing up. He felt completely unqualified to deal with a situation like this when he was sick as well. Adrenaline had been enough to get him to the bathroom, but he had a hard time believing that either of them would get back to bed anytime soon.

“David, are you crying? Hey, I’m fine,” said Patrick sleepily. He let his head fall back, leaning against the wall and also looking like he had no intention of leaving.

“You scared me,” David snuffled and scrubbed his cheeks and nose. Even as he was crying, he knew how ridiculous it was, but he just couldn’t stop it. “You said you didn’t need help!”

“I know...” Patrick’s hand gravitated toward David’s own, grasping it relatively weakly. “I’ll ask for your help next time I need to pee.”

David laughed through the tears, and then of course ended up coughing. Patrick echoed it with a sneeze. “God, we’re a mess.”

-----

A beeping reminded David that he was taking his temperature, and he anxiously removed the thermometer. He frowned.

“What is it?” Patrick asked.

“I might be cooking from the inside,” David sighed. “I’m freezing, though.” He snuggled closer to Patrick, who wrapped an arm around him with a mutual need to share body heat. David took the opportunity to shove the thermometer in his boyfriend’s face. “Now it’s your turn.”

Patrick groaned. “David, we already know that-”

“Please. Do it for me?”

He gave a resigned sigh and slipped it under his tongue. They quickly started to doze before the beeping had them wide awake. Patrick squinted at the reading.

“Oh.”

David grabbed hold of Patrick’s wrist and turned it toward him. “That’s higher than mine,” he said with a hint of panic.

That was when David decided they couldn’t do this alone. He was too sick to give Patrick the care that he needed, and he’d never be able to forgive himself if Patrick got hurt. The man needed someone to stop him from being independent at all times.

They needed reinforcements.

“Stevie’s busy, David.”

“Stevie can close the store an hour early.”

“Stevie’s not going to leave the work she was doing for free to come catch the flu from us.”

“Well, when you put it like that...” David bit his lip as he scrolled through his phone contacts. Mom? Fuck. No. Dad? ...maybe, but that sounded humiliating and the man was incapable of being normal about any situation.

Patrick gave an annoyed huff. “What about your sister?” He seemed to have accepted that he couldn’t change David’s mind about dragging someone over there, so he was reluctantly offering help instead.

“Alexis?”

“That is the sister I was referring to,” Patrick mumbled, snuggling David and stuffing his face into his sweatshirt (He had a habit of being unbelievably cuddly when he wasn’t feeling good. David loved it).

Alexis.

David hated that he didn’t hate the idea.

His sister had seen the worst of him throughout their lives, specifically the past couple of years since they were forced to move to Schitt’s Creek. She watched him fall apart and build himself back up again.

She told him to shut up those first few months on the nights he cried himself to sleep mourning the loss of the life he knew and everything he valued in it.

And then one night he had a panic attack and suddenly she was just sitting on his bed and keeping him company until he could breathe again. They watched late-night infomercials and made fun of them until David finally crashed.

Yeah, she would do.

The question was, would she agree?

-----

“Just so you know, I expect full bedside service when I get sick.” Alexis called as she filled a glass with juice and brought it to Patrick.

Patrick chuckled. “We’ll owe you. Thanks.”

David hummed faux-thoughtfully, scrunching his face. “I don’t think I owe you anything after everything I had to deal with when you were a kid. If anything, this is just you repaying your own debt.”

Alexis glared. “Okay, I can leave like, right now.” She looked conflicted for a moment, and then let out a frustrated groan. “But apparently I care about you guys or whatever.”

Patrick snuffled and sipped his juice, leaning against David to remind him that this wasn’t a fight to be fought right now when Alexis had nothing to lose by leaving them to their own devices. David’s body was pleasantly warm, nice to snuggle up to on a good day, let alone today when they were both running high fevers and using each other’s body heat to stay warm.

He was very alert when he felt a hand on his forehead. Patrick looked over and saw that there was one on David’s as well. Alexis stayed that way, looking thoughtful as she gauged and compared their temperatures.

David shoved her hand away. “Get off of me!” but Alexis wasn’t bothered.

“Mm’kay, I’m gonna force drugs into you guys. You can take ibuprofen, right Patrick?”

“Mmhm,” Patrick said, still comfortable against his boyfriend. The rattle of a bottle, and then two pills placed into his hands. He opened his eyes once more to swallow them. “Thanks Alexis.”

David gave a resigned sigh. “Yeah, thanks...”

Alexis shrugged. “You owe me!”
