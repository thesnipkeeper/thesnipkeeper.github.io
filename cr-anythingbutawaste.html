Anything but a Waste

Settling in a town that wasn’t known for its tourists, they managed to get what was less of an inn room and more of a bunker. None of them really minded, they were still beds, after all. It gave them the opportunity to watch the nighttime or morning routines of those that they didn’t often share rooms with.

Or check on each other while pretending to be grabbing something out of their things, like Mollymauk was now doing. He’d watched Caleb retire rather early, simply leaving the table without bothering to tell anyone where he was going. Everyone else found it weird, and Beau and Nott were both showing concern, acting as if they wanted to go check on him. They seemed to calm when Molly announced that he was going to.

He expected to see Caleb with his nose buried in a book, or perhaps counting or sorting through his things like he did sometimes. Instead, he was in his bunk, a bed underneath Nott’s.

“Huh! It’s not like you to be in bed this early.” Molly went for his coat that was hanging on his and Jester’s bunks and put it on, doing his best to make it look like that was his intention.

Caleb cleared his throat and nodded. What was he doing? It looked like nothing at all. “Been a long day,” he said.

That was fair enough, Molly thought. Caleb had used a lot of magic today, he had every right to be tired. But then he got a proper look at the wizard and narrowed his eyes. “Caleb... why is your face so red?”

Caleb’s hand went to his cheek as if he hadn’t noticed the heat in his face himself. “Ah, well...”

Molly, never one for minding others’ personal space, immediately laid a hand on Caleb’s forehead. He gasped and pulled back. “Caleb!” He said, almost scolding. “You’re quite warm.”

Caleb sighed. “A bit cold, actually...”

“Oh, Caleb...” Molly was sort of at a loss of what to do. He never had to care for sick people at the fucking circus! “I can get Jester for you?”

“Nei- no,” he said, clearing his throat. “I don’t want her wasting her spells on me. Just a bit of rest will do me some good.”

-----

It didn’t. The group woke far earlier than they’d planned the next morning when Caleb started coughing. And couldn’t stop.

Nott scrambled from her bed down to his in a matter of seconds as the others were groaning awake. Molly watched her feel his forehead just as he’d done last night, and she had about the same reaction as well.

Well, except for the scream of “Jester!”

Jester, of course, perked up in a matter of seconds. “Does he have a fever?” she asked, rushing over.

“Yes!” Nott said.

Caleb tried to deny it, but he wasn’t able to before he started coughing again. He must have despised having yet another person feel his face.

Jester gasped. “Caleb, you’re really sick!”

“I... I’ll be okay,” he tried.

Fjord made an unsure noise. “A cough like that, I don’t think you will.”

Caleb couldn’t argue with that.

“Jester, you can fix that, can’t you?” Molly asked, even while Caleb gave him a watery-eyed glare.

“Yeah! Of course!” She prepared to cast the spell, before Caleb stopped her with some stammering.

“Don’t waste your spell,” he said quickly. “There are better uses for your magic.”

The mere sentence sort of broke Molly’s heart. From the noise everyone else made, it broke all their hearts.

Jester’s voice got soft as she said, “Caleb, you’re absolutely worth this.” Before casting the spell and taking his fever away.

-----

Caleb had nothing but a residual scratchy throat for the rest of the day. Molly could tell, because he kept clearing it and coughing just slightly. But he was clearly better. Physically, anyway. He hadn’t said much all day, just held Nott’s hand when she offered it.

Molly slowed his pace until he was on the other side of Caleb, who was walking a bit slowly.

“You know, Caleb,” he said lowly, though he could tell the wizard heard him by the change in mannerisms. “You are always worth it. And, sometimes it’s more selfish to refuse help.” He shrugged.

Caleb clenched his jaw for a moment before giving a minute nod. “I am learning, Mollymauk.”

Molly nodded. “I know.” He patted his friend on the back before walking away.
