Title: Sibling Energy
Fandom: Shadowhunters
Summary: Jace has the flu, Alec and Izzy look out for him
Word count: 3.4k

Sibling Energy

Alec knew something was wrong. He wasn’t sure exactly what was wrong, but his parabatai rune felt tender and he felt... off. It could be any number of things. Jace could just have broken a glass in the kitchen and was pissed about having to clean it up, or maybe someone hit him too hard in the sparring ring.

Either way, he had to find Jace. Alec wasn’t going to rest until he knew that his brother was okay.

“Has anyone seen Jace?”

A roomful of young shadowhunters shook their heads and went back to work.

Alec tried to tamp away the worry nagging at his insides. Jace could be anywhere, doing anything. He hated the idea of his brother being in distress and not even knowing where he was.

“Did you check his room?”

Alec turned to Izzy. “No, not yet. But why would he...?” He shook his head. “I’ll go check.”

Izzy nodded and clapped a hand on his back. “He’s probably fine, Alec.”

“No, he’s not,” Alec said quickly, hand going to his abdomen where the rune was still sore.

Izzy’s eyes followed, and she bit back a frown. “Let me know if you find him.”

“Yeah.”

Jace’s door was still closed. Much to Alec’s relief (and confusion) he could hear snoring on the other side. Thank the Angel that he wasn’t missing, but why was Alec’s rune telling him that something was wrong?

He quietly opened the door and saw that his brother was asleep in his bed. So he was safe.

And then Alec saw the signs, the reason his rune was giving him that feeling of unease. Jace looked awful, pale and sweaty, breathing through his mouth like his nose had stopped working. Alec didn’t have to wake him up, because a hacking cough gave Jace the rude awakening instead.

He propped himself on his elbow and leaned to the side as he gave a series of painful-sounding coughs. He moaned, and Alec thought this was a good time to announce his presence.

“Hey.”

Jace startled, eyes wide as they found him in the room. “What are-” he paused to clear his throat. It didn’t help that much. “What are you doing in my room?” Although, with a stuffed nose, it was harder to understand him.

Alec hesitated. This... was a little embarrassing. But he just lifted his shirt to show where the symbol was glowing, and Jace relaxed.

“I...” Jace struggled to sit up. Alec had never seen him so weak and tired-looking. “I’m fine.”

“That’s obviously not true.”

Jace’s face went through a few different expressions as he settled against his pillows, and the one he finally landed on was discomfort.

“I don’t- I’m not feeling too great, Alec. I don’t know what’s happening.”

Alec approached, slowly at first, and sat on the edge of Jace’s bed. “What’s wrong?”

Jace blew out a heavy sigh, which resulted in a cough. He shrugged. “My entire body aches. And I’ve been freezing all night, but then I’m not, and I-” he stopped to sniffle, and Alec could hear the thickness in his sinuses. “I can’t fucking breathe.”

Alec frowned. “That sounds like the flu.” He’d nursed his siblings through the flu plenty of times and knew the difference between that and a cold, or any other number of illnesses.

Izzy always lost her voice when she was sick (go figure). Max always refused to rest until he made himself ten times worse. Jace...

With Jace, the fevers always hit him hard. They weren’t always higher than the others’ necessarily, but he had a harder time dealing with them.

It made sense now, why Alec’s rune was bothering him when all Jace was doing was sleeping. He felt horrible, and he was- scared? No, just upset.

Jace groaned, head going back as he closed his eyes. “I can’t deal with this right now. We have shit to- to do-” Alec could hear a crackle in his airways before he was lunged into another coughing fit. Alec couldn’t do anything but wait for it to be over, and uselessly remind Jace to try to breathe. His face was beet red by the time the fit had tapered off, his eyes full of unshed tears from the force.

And then he shuddered and grabbed one of the many blankets he’d previously kicked away in his sleep to pull over himself.

Alec frowned and laid a hand on Jace’s forehead. Normally he’d be nervous about touching someone when he wasn’t sure he should, but this was normal to both of them. Alec had been feeling his forehead for years.

“Looks like your fever’s back.” Alec sighed.

Jace swore under his breath. “This is gonna be one of those flus, huh?” He said with a halfhearted chuckle.

“I sure hope not.” He couldn’t afford to have Jace bedridden for long, and Jace would certainly go insane being stuck in his room for days. He moved his brother’s damp hair out of his eyes. “I- I have to get back to work. Are you going to be...?”

“Yes,” Jace answered quickly. “I’m just... gonna try to sleep. Thanks, Alec.”

Alec nodded. He tracked down a bottle of tylenol, grabbed a bottle of water from the fridge. Jace was asleep when Alec returned to his brother’s room, although it didn’t look exactly peaceful when he was shuddering with every breath and struggling to breathe through his nose.

Alec didn’t want to leave him, but Jace wouldn’t want him hovering over him all day.

-----

Izzy caught his eyes as he walked back into the main work area. She was going through some truly boring footage of a rogue vampire.

“Hey, did you find him?”

Alec sighed. “He has the flu.”

Izzy swore. “That’s the last thing he needed.”

“Yeah,” Alec agreed. “He’s sleeping right now, but I’m going to keep an eye on him. You know how he gets when...”

Izzy nodded, concern painting her features. “I can help, if you want me to.”

“Oh, you don’t-”

“He’s not going to want to be alone, Alec.”

It was true. Jace hid it well, but they knew he craved human contact when he was sick. But the thing about Jace, of course, was that he was terrible at asking for what he wanted. He’d spend all day insisting that he was fine when he wasn’t.

Alec found himself caught up in duties that he was unable to pull away from that he did ask Izzy to check on him and bring him something for lunch (“Just please don’t cook it” “Excuse me? My cooking is- yeah, alright”). She texted him to say that he took a few bites and went back to sleep. The rune, though still reminding him that Jace wasn’t okay, had stopped bugging him, and Alec took that as a good sign.

He found himself more and more restless as the day went on. Not because of the rune, but because his brother was sick and he wasn’t there.

And then it was the rune.

Alec had to grab onto the edge of the nearest table as a rush of vertigo hit him like a truck. Izzy had a cautious grasp on his arm in a heartbeat.

“What’s wrong? Are you okay?”

“Jace...” Alec said, blinking until the room stopped spinning. “I’m- I should go check on him.”

Izzy looked reluctant to let him go after his sudden dizzy spell, but she nodded slowly and loosed her grip on him as he walked off.

When Alec entered Jace’s room, the first thing that caught his eye was the empty bed, and the second thing was his brother on the floor next to it. Jace sat on a pool of blankets like he’d dragged them down with him.

His eyes were squeezed shut as he rubbed them.

“Jace, what happened?”

“Hm?” Jace seemed much less alarmed to see him in his room this time. “Oh, I just got really dizzy. Gimme a sec.”

Alec knelt down next to him. He waited patiently for Jace to open his eyes again when they were finally able to focus.

“What were you doing out of bed? Come on-”

“No, I-” Jace weakly pulled out of his grasp as he tried to help him back onto the mattress. “I have to piss, Alec.”

“Oh.” Alec swallowed. “Do you want... help?”

Jace sighed in frustration, coughed. “Just help me to the bathroom?”

Alec did so, taking most of Jace’s weight as they made their way to the bathroom. He waited patiently, Jace giving a reluctant nod as he emerged and let Alec help him back to bed.

Jace coughed weakly into his fist, looking so very over it. “I’m guessing that you... felt it.” He gestured lazily to his own parabatai rune.

Alec nodded. “Something changed, I got a little nervous.”

“I’m not sure which would have taken more of my dignity, crawling to the bathroom, or my brother having to carry me.”

“Oh, shut up. I don’t care.”

“Mmm, but my pride does.” He made a mock-pained sound and then sniffed deeply.

-----

Alec brought dinner into Jace’s room and ate with him. Usually Jace was the first one to scarf down his food (followed by getting seconds... and thirds) but he struggled through each bite.

Jace sneezed and coughed his way through half of a bowl of soup before he decided that he was tired of trying. And tired in general.

“Sorry,” he said as he handed the bowl back to Alec. He promptly turned away to sneeze, which turned into a cough.

Alec patted his leg. “I’m just glad you’re eating at all.”

“I’m really not hungry,” Jace admitted. “But you and Izzy are hard to refuse.”

“Good. Is your stomach bothering you?”

Jace took a bit to answer. “I can’t tell,” he said honestly. He winced, leaned back, and draped an arm over his eyes. “Head hurts, though.”

“Here,” Alec set the bowls aside and grabbed the pill bottle he’d brought in that morning. He shook out two and handed them to Jace along with a glass of water. “I think this should help for a while.”

Jace didn’t question a thing before he swallowed them. “Thanks, Alec. You don’t have to stick around. I think I’m just gonna go back to sleep.”

Alec figured that was probably Jace’s way of politely asking him to leave. He nodded and stacked the dishes in his hand.

“Text me or Izzy if you need anything, okay?”

Jace nodded sleepily.

-----

Alec didn’t expect him to text at all, truth be told. Jace was usually far too independent to ask for help when he needed it, but he was also a grown man and Alec knew he shouldn’t be checking on him every hour just because he had the flu.

He was combing through more vampire footage, exhausted, thinking about retiring for the night. And his phone buzzed.

J: hey u busy?

A: not really. everything ok?

J: woke up. cant go back to sleep. wanna come hang out?

Alec was out of his chair the moment he read the text. That was Jace’s way of saying he wanted someone around, and Alec knew he must have been feeling awful if he was comfortable asking for it. He grabbed an extra box of tissues and another bottle of water on his way to Jace’s room.

“Oh no, Jace...” was the first thing that came out of his mouth as he entered.

Jace was shuddering with chills. The meds must have worn off, because now he looked even more miserable than he had that morning. Still, he offered a halfhearted chuckle that really did nothing to dissipate Alec’s worry when his teeth were chattering.

Alec set the things aside and sat on the bed, this time pulling his legs up with him as well. Jace leaned against him, just enough to leech some of his body heat. He could feel every shiver that ran through his brother.

Alec felt his forehead and a pang of worry grew in his gut. “You’re burning up.”

“I’m f-freezing.” He pressed himself closer to Alec until Alec’s arms were wrapped around his brother. “Fuck, I j-just wanted to sleep this off.”

Alec just held him through the bout of chills.

Jace had stopped shivering, but Alec could tell he was still achy and suffering every other result of a fever. He carefully pulled away and sat up.

“Want to try some more medicine?” Alec asked, reaching across Jace for the bottle on his nightstand.

Jace looked uneasy. “I don’t know, Alec.” He sniffled, and then coughed as it irritated his throat. “I feel weird.”

“Weird?”

“Like, I don’t want to try swallowing anything right now,” he admitted. “I’ll... I’ll be fine. I can deal with it.”

“Okay,” Alec said. “Do you um, think you’re going to throw up?”

Jace shook his head. “Naw, just feel weird.”

So they laid there, and Alec was relieved to hear his brother’s breaths even out and eventually turn into snores. Sleep was what he really needed, Alec decided. Not sleeping was probably part of the reason he’d gotten so sick anyway.

If only it could have stayed that way.

Alec hadn’t realized he was dozing off as well until he felt his brother shift. Jace made a strange noise as he woke, and then sat up.

“Alec-!” but it was far too late. Vomit rushed up his throat and onto his lap, pooling in the blankets.

Alec was wide awake now, rushing out of bed and grabbing a bin from under Jace’s desk. He didn’t quite get there in time to catch the second bout, but he still handed it to Jace in case there was more.

“Fuck, fuck fuck...” Jace mumbled between heaves.

Cleaning up was a process, but Alec liked to think he managed it pretty well. Jace had thrown up a couple of times while Alec got the laundry worked out.

“You didn’t have to do that...” Jace mumbled. He looked exhausted from puking, and though he was trying to hide it, Alec could tell he was starting to shiver again.

Alec shook his head as he laid a new set of blankets over him. “Get some sleep, okay?”

“I’ll sure fucking try. God, the flu didn’t seem this bad when we were kids.”

“Well, we also felt personally victimized at being told we had to stay in bed and do nothing.” Their poor mother.

Jace scoffed. “I can’t believe Maryse put up with us.”

-----

The next day went about the same, with Jace sleeping off and on, and Izzy and Alec convincing him to eat and take medicine between his naps.

It was night time when things got hard. Alec saw it, the teariness in his brother’s eyes. Jace didn’t cry, not really, but all bets were off when he was sick. 

Alec silently wrapped an arm around him, careful at first, before Jace leaned into him with a sniffle. And Alec was holding him, both arms hugging him tightly as he shuddered.

“We’ll never speak of this,” Jace said, voice so hoarse it was nearly a whisper. 

Alec scoffed. “Got it.”

-----

Alec woke with a cough.

He wasn’t stupid, he knew that spending so much time with Jace was going to get him sick sooner or later. But it usually happened later, when his siblings were well enough that they didn’t need him to care for them anymore. 

As Alec started to sit up, he realized that it wasn’t just a cough (No, that’d be sort of convenient and something he could work with). His vision swam as he tried to move, and it felt as if all of the energy had been sapped out of him. 

So, he took a little while longer to get out of bed. Waiting patiently for his head to reorient itself with every change in position until he was out of bed. He’d be fine as long as he wasn’t out in the field. He’d just stick to paperwork and camera footage and it would all be fine.

However, “What’s wrong with you?” was the first thing his sister said to him as he came to do his work.

“Nothing,” Alec said, as if she was crazy for even asking. It was a low blow, but he wanted to hang onto his dignity for as long as he could.

“No no, your face is red.” 

Was it? 

Alec was powerless to stop her as she turned his face toward her, and of course that was the moment he felt a tickle in his throat. Izzy’s eyes widened at the sound of his cough, and she quickly reached up to feel his forehead.

“By the Angel, you’re burning up. Come on, Alec.” She took his wrist and started dragging him away.

“No, Izzy, I-” he stumbled along until his vision began to swim once again. “Izzy, stop.” Alec braced himself against a nearby wall and closed his eyes. “Ugh, I don’t feel so good.”

“Obviously.”

Not even five minutes at work and he was already regretting this. He was miserable. He was useless.

“Alec, seriously, you’re gonna get the whole institute sick,” she laughed. “What were you thinking?”

“I… wasn’t.”

Alec let her lead him back to the rooms, except they didn’t stop at his. She brought him to Jace’s.

Jace woke groggily as they came in, and then swore the moment he got a good look at Alec. “Okay,” he rapsed. “That was fast.”

Alec found himself forced to sit on the bed. Jace simply moved over to make room, but Alec wasn’t done with their sister. 

“Izzy, I don’t-” want to be sick, to be stuck in bed, to share a bed with Jace because he was still recovering from the embarrassment of all of the stupid feelings.

Izzy seemed to understand all of it. Or none of it. He couldn’t tell. Still, she nodded.

“It’ll be easier for me to keep an eye on both of you if you’re in the same room, okay? Just try to get some sleep.” 

Alec gave a resigned sigh as Izzy walked out. He removed his shoes and jacket and climbed in, staying on his side of the bed. It was somehow more awkward now, even though they’d been cuddling off and on for two days. Maybe it wasn’t the bed he was feeling weird about, but being sick when Jace was still sick.

“Guess I gave you my plague,” Jace mumbled, eyes slipping shut once again. “Sorry.”

“I’ll be fine.”

“Oh, I’m sure.” Jace cleared his throat. “Hey um, thanks for… being here. Last night.”

Last night, last night...oh. 

“I always will be, you know.”

“Yeah, well… I guess I should say I love you and stuff.”

Alec huffed out a laugh, only to find that his nose was fantastically stuffed. “I love you too and stuff. Go to sleep, ‘m tired…”

-----

Jace was finally starting to be his old self by the next day. He still had a lingering cough (Alec knew this because it kept him up half of the night) but he was able to get out of bed without passing out, and they considered that an improvement.

“ ‘d you take your meds?” Alec mumbled into his pillow.

“Yes, mom, I took them. Did you take anything?”

Alec chose not to answer.

“Okay, in that case…” 

He heard the rattling of pills in a bottle before something cold touched his arm and forced him to open his eyes. Jace kept nudging him with the water bottle until he finally started to sit up. He managed to swallow them, despite the way his ears popped as he did.

“How you feeling?”

“Fine,” Alec answered. “Jus’ tired.”

“Alec, you’re not fine. Your temp was at like 200 degrees this morning.”

“Not possible.”

He felt his brother’s rough hand against his forehead, and it was followed by a heavy sigh. “Look, I know you see it as your responsibility or whatever to take care of us, but we’re fully-grown adults. You know that, right? I mean, except for Max.”

Alec coughed.

He thought Jace wanted to add onto it, but instead he said “Izzy, what-?”

Then promptly a third body’s weight was in the bed.

Alec finally opened his eyes to find their sister scrambling under the covers. She sniffled thickly and coughed.

Ah.

“I don’t feel good,” she finally said.

Alec watched as Jace took her temperature and gave her medicine just like he’d been doing for him the past few days.

Maybe Jace was right, they were fully-grown adults. But not one of them was the responsible one now. They could  all take care of each other pretty well.
