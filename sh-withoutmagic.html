Title: Without Magic
Fandom: Shadowhunters
Summary: Magnus has trouble dealing with his newfound mortality
Word count: 2.3k

Without Magic

Magnus learns more and more of what not having magic means every day. Every time he has to change clothes or put on makeup (fucking hell, painting one’s nails is a nightmare), he’s reminded that he’s mundane now.

He’s reminded even more so when he wakes up feeling like death. He forgot what it feels like to be sick, but he’s certainly reminded now, as he lays in bed and tries not to think about what’s happening.

Alec’s peacefully asleep, the warm glow of the sun coming in through the stained glass casting a beautiful light on him. It’s nice to see him getting some rest after the stressful week he’s had.

The seelies have been putting up a bit of a fight regarding their place in society. They never appreciate the Clave telling them what to do. Alec’s been working tirelessly to regain control of the situation.

Magnus really hates politics, and what they’re doing to his boyfriend.

What he also hates, however, is how he’s feeling at the moment. He goes to turn over, and can’t stop a quiet gasp as the room spins the minute he tries to move.

His limbs feel like lead, his head is starting to hurt the longer he’s awake, and he can feel an ominous itch in the back of his sinuses like sneezes and coughs are in his near future.

Alec’s alarm goes off, and he wakes with a start. His sleep-tousled hair hangs in his face a bit as he rubs his eyes. Finally, he looks to Magnus with a sleepy smile.

“Morning.”

Magnus gladly returns the smile. “Good morning.”

Alec sits up with a groan and immediately starts to get dressed. Magnus wants to pull him back into bed, and normally he probably would, but he can hardly muster up the strength to get his head off the pillow right now.

“I’m not ready for today,” Alec sighs. “We’ve got the others coming over to discuss their side of things. It’s... not going to be fun.”

Magnus hums sympathetically, or tries to. It seems his nose is starting to congest. Fantastic. He saves himself by cutting himself off and saying instead, “I hope you’ll remember to eat breakfast today.”

Alec’s halfway through pulling his shirt over his head when he stops to look at Magnus. “That was a one time thing, okay?” the rest of his sentence muffled as he finishes getting dressed. “I was really busy yesterday. I barely had time to shower.”

“Mm, and today?”

“I’ll eat breakfast. Promise.”

Magnus smiles up at him. He thinks about telling Alec he isn’t feeling well. He knows Alec would call everything off in a heartbeat to make sure Magnus was okay.

But he has a job to do, and he’s already done so much to help Magnus. There’s other things for him to be worrying about right now, much more important things than his now-mundane and useless boyfriend.

Magnus has to admit that there’s some pride in the decision as well. He’s tired of all of these shadowhunters taking care of him. Most of them don’t even want him to be there. What a sight it must be to see one of the world’s most powerful warlocks now sharing what is ultimately a dorm room with his boyfriend.

So he doesn’t tell Alec he’s sick.

He feigns being too dramatically tired to get up and give him a kiss before he leaves. Alec just leans down a kisses him instead before telling him to have a good day and he can go back to sleep now without being bothered. Magnus doesn’t bother to tell him that he was awake before Alec. It’d just spawn questions that he’d have to lie to answer.

Magnus does go back to sleep, immediately. Perhaps that’s all he needs. Rest is the best thing for sick people, after all. Maybe it’ll fix him.

-----

He wakes up feeling even more like death than he had before.

He shivers. Ah, the beginnings of a fever, then. Fantastic. Exactly what he needs on top of the pressure in his skull and the ache in the rest of his body.

Everything hurts. He doesn’t want to feel self-pitiful but... yeah, everything hurts and he hates this. How do humans deal with this on a regular basis?

An itchy cough bubbles out of him, weak and unproductive and painful. There’s probably plenty of mundane medications that can solve his problems, but finding them would require getting out of bed.

Instead, he just lies there and wishes that he wasn’t a burden on everyone he loved. That’s probably a bit dramatic, but he can’t bring himself to care.

Magnus shudders, curling up as tightly as he can and pulling the covers up to his neck. Each movement hurts and requires far more effort than he has.

He snuffles, feeling his nose start to run. Well, now he’s getting germs allover his boyfriend’s bed. The last thing Alec needs right now is to get sick as well.

Lying in bed and wishing for death is a fantastic strategy until his bladder demands attention.

Magnus sits up slowly, carefully, because by the dots edging his vision, he knows that he’s going to black out if he moves any quicker. His teeth chatter as the blankets fall off of him and pool in his lap, cursing the decision to wear a sleeveless shirt to bed.

It takes about five minutes to walk from the bed to Alec’s bathroom, having to pause to stop his vision going black every once in a while. He pees. And spends more than five minutes getting back to bed, even with the help of the walls and other furniture.

He all but falls into bed and gets halfway under the blankets before his body officially gives up on him. He’s still absolutely freezing, but all of his strength is gone. He wants to cry.

Magnus vaguely hears his phone before he falls into a chilly, uneasy sleep.

-----

He comes to wakefulness with a moan.

He winces, curls up. His arm snakes around his stomach before he opens his eyes.

And as he comes to full consciousness, Magnus realizes he only has moments before he vomits in Alec’s bed.

The rush to the bathroom is a blur and he’s not sure how he manages besides sheer will to not make Alec’s life harder than he already has.

Magnus gives a sickening gag into the toilet, and then another. Nothing comes up, but he feels tears sting at the corner of his eyes that aren’t simply from the exertion. He’s tired, and miserable, and a little scared that he’s going to be alone in here forever. Magnus heaves and brings up a wave of last night’s dinner, a horrible tasteless soup and simple sandwich from the institute cafeteria.

It’s not long before he’s finished. His body seems to be content with what he’s expelled for now.

Magnus finally lets himself cry.

He curls up on the bathroom floor, managing to weakly pull a towel down to offer a bit of warmth. He sniffles pitifully as tears run down his cheeks, and continues to shiver.

He should go back to bed. He tries to at one point, but his body is so tired from throwing up and running in here that he can’t seem to sit up without his vision going dark.

He wants Alec, he thinks miserably, which just makes him sob harder.

-----

The door to Alec’s room opens. Magnus is barely awake, barely aware of it, but just enough to hope that it’s Alec back from a long work day. Magnus could use some help back to bed, after all, and hopefully he can just go back to sleep and hope this was all a terrible nightmare.

“Magnus?” Izzy. Ah. What’s she doing here?

“Magn- by the Angel!” Izzy’s at his side, he realizes, laying a hand on his forehead and swearing colorfully.

She taps his cheek and he groans. His skin is so oversensitive that even that hurts.

“Hey, Magnus, can you hear me?”

“Mmm,” he responds, brows furrowing as he’s once again aware of how much his entire body hurts. Talking is too much effort.

“You’re burning up...” she seems to be saying it more to herself than Magnus, and that’s okay. He’s not much of a conversationalist right now. “Magnus, can you sit up for me?”

“Mm’m,” he shakes his head. “Dizzy. Tired.”

Izzy swears. “Okay, okay,” she pulls her phone out of her pocket and texts expertly with one hand while the other pets his hair. It’s nice, more comforting than he’ll ever be willing to admit. The affection does remind him of one thing, though.

“Alec...”

Izzy continues running her fingers through his hair and shakes her head. “Alec’s out in the field on an emergency, but Jace will be here to help, okay? I’m afraid I can’t carry you to the infirmary by myself.” She chuckles, and then sighs. “By the Angel, Magnus...”

He’s vaguely aware of a male voice - still not his boyfriend unfortunately - and of being hoisted up and carried bridal-style.

“Jace, why are you carrying me?” It’s the first sentence he’s managed to get out, and he feels that it’s a fair question.

Jace just laughs. “I’d like to see you walk, Magnus. Hold on tight.”

Magnus does not. He tries, but it’s impossible, and that seems to be alright because Jace is- wow, Jace is strong. The first couple of steps jostle Magnus’ stomach and he groans.

“Magnus, if you puke on me...”

Instead, he ends up coughing weakly into Jace’s shoulder, not having near enough strength to bring a hand up to cover it. Jace pauses for a moment, sighs heavily, and continues.

A little more time passes, Magnus keeping his eyes shut to avoid getting dizzier than he already is.

“Damn, he is burning up,” Jace says, Magnus feeling his voice vibrate in his chest.

“I told you,” Izzy replies. “I think it’s the flu? He must’ve been throwing up earlier.”

“I hope that’s all it is.”

A fresh wave of anxiety goes through Magnus when he realizes that this could be something much worse. Is it because he lost his magic? Is his body giving up on him now?

He doesn’t realize he’s made any sort of worried noise until Jace’s grip on him tightens and he says, “Hey, it’s okay. We’ll get you figured out no matter what it is.”

Magnus coughs again.

“Damn it, Magnus.”

Izzy laughs.

Several faces he’s seen around the institute but never spoke to make themselves known in the infirmary. He wouldn’t have guessed that any of these people were doctors or healing experts. All of the shadowhunters gave off the same young-and-ready-to-fight-vibe.

A gentle woman sticks a thermometer in his ear and frowns at the reading.

Magnus hears bits of conversation after that. He tries very hard to fall asleep and forget that this is happening, but alas his worries and get the best of his body’s desire to rest.

“...probably the flu, but we’ll keep an eye...”

“...heard from Alec?”

“...on his way back...”

Despite not wanting to appear as weak as he is in front of anyone but Izzy and Jace, Magnus finds himself shuddering pitifully. He’s still so fucking cold. There’s a thin blanket over him that does nothing, and if he had his magic he could make it warmer himself.

If he had his magic, he wouldn’t be in this mess. He wouldn’t be this weak, susceptible to a mundane illness that causes him to rely on everyone around him.

“...him another blanket?”

“...unfortunately, the fever...”

He hears a heavy sigh from Jace and opens his eyes to see him and Izzy both looking concerned. Is he on his deathbed? Why are they so worried about him? They’ve only known him for a few months.

“Magnus!”

Now that, that is Alec’s voice. Magnus is sure he’s not hallucinating.

Alec runs into the room and Magnus finds himself breathing a sigh of relief as his boyfriend wraps him in a hug. Alec presses a kiss to his forehead, and his cheek, and hugs him again.

Magnus is sure to grab hold of his hand as he turns to talk to the doctor. He’s unwilling to give up the contact now that he finally has Alec back, can’t have him walking away for even a second.

Alec kisses the back of Magnus’ hand a keeps it in his grip.

He seems to ask a lot of questions that Jace and Izzy have already been asking, but the doctor answers them patiently and offers the two of them the room.

Alec sighs heavily the moment that they’re alone, and turns to Magnus with a look of utter concern and relief.

“Why didn’t you say anything this morning? Damn it, I thought you felt warm when I kissed you but I just figured it was because of the blankets, and we’d just woken up...”

Magnus coughs weakly, struggling to cover it and protect Alec from his horrible mundane germs. “I’m fine,” he says lamely.

“Magnus. Izzy said she went to look for you when you weren’t returning her texts and found you on the bathroom floor?”

Magnus closes his eyes and sighs. That wasn’t his best moment. “She needed help?”

“She just had a question about someone in the downworld, nothing- Magnus, you had a fever of one-hundred and three.”

“Is that bad?” It’s been so long since he’s had to gauge a temperature.

“Yes,” Alec answers definitively. “Yes, that’s bad, Magnus.” He breathes out a calming sigh, and Magnus feels his forehead press against his own. “Don’t do that again. Please.”

Magnus really hopes that he can promise he won’t, but the idea that there’s even a possibility of again, that he’ll get sick like this in the future, is overwhelming.

He misses his magic.

He misses being the one in the relationship who does the looking-after.

“I love you,” he says simply. He’s half asleep already, doesn’t have the strength to say much more.

Alec chuckles, his breath tickling Magnus’ neck. “I love you, too.”

“I’m really tired. Sorry.”

“Get some rest. I’ll be right here when you wake up.”
