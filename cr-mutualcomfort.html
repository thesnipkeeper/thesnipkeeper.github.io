Mutual Comfort

She was having a hard time with it all. Jester wasn't sure how everyone else was dealing with it. It was like no one missed Yasha. 

She pressed herself to Caduceus' side, able to feel the uneven breathing and periodic sniffles. He'd been fighting a cold for the past few days with no sign of true improvement. She'd learned that being miserably sick didn't change him at all. He was quieter, but he'd mentioned having a sore throat which probably had something to do with his silence. 

"Hey," he greeted, voice even deeper and more gravelly than usual. 

"Hey." 

"What's up? You seem a little down." 

"I miss her, Caduceus." 

A pause, "Oh." He went quiet for a moment. "Sorry, this can't be easy for you." 

"Well apparently I'm the only one bothered by it," she could hear her mom's voice in the back of her head, telling her to stop pouting. She didn't listen, of course.

"Now, that's not true. That's not very fair to everyone else when they're dealing with the same loss as you are." Caduceus sniffled and ran the back of his hand under his nose, brows furrowed as if he was annoyed with his body. "Everyone grieves in their own way." 

She trusted him. He lived and worked in a graveyard his entire life, after all.

"I know..." Jester sighed. "I just wish they acted like they cared." 

“Well,” Caduceus cleared his throat and sniffed again. “When have you known anyone else in this group to openly express their feelings?” he said good-naturedly, a little smile tugging at his lips.

He had a point.

“Don’t worry about things you can’t control,” Caduceus added, and then promptly turned away to sneeze. It was sudden enough to jostle  Jester off of him. 

He recovered with huff, wiping at his nose with more aggression than she’d ever seen from him. Moisture glistened at the base of his nostrils even as he tried to snort up the mess expelled by the sneeze. Jester realized how sore his nose must’ve been if he’d been treating it like that for the past three days.

“Whew… ‘scuse me.”

“...You really don’t feel good, do you?” She asked, previous conversation momentarily forgotten.

Caduceus hummed thoughtfully and wrinkled his nose with another sniffle. “My head hurts a little, but it’s nothing that can’t be dealt with.” They relaxed into each other again once they were sure he wasn’t due for anymore house-shaking sneezes. 

He was warmer than usual, had been for a few days. The little fever wasn’t enough to keep him in bed, but he couldn’t have been feeling great.

“With any luck, we’ll get Yasha back. I may not have known her for as long as you have, but she seemed very strong-willed. She can’t stay under Obann’s control forever, can she?”

“I really hope not, Caduceus. I mean, she never talked about her past. What if Obann is a friend?”

“I think he’s more like a slaver.” Caduceus inhaled deeply, breath crackling before he coughed harshly into his handkerchief. Jester could hear the gunk in his chest.

Before she could think of something to say, Fjord appeared in the room, Beau following behind

“How you feeling, Dueces?”

“Oh, I’ve been better,” he replied with a lazy smile.

Jester was admittedly confused when he started getting up, gently nudging her off of him. There went her source of heat.

“You ready to go?” Beau asked with a raised eyebrow as she scrutinized him. 

Caduceus nodded in lieu of a verbal confirmation.

RIght. Someone had asked them to case a shop for anything illegal, suspecting that it was a front for something more sinister. Caduceus was good at catching inconsistencies, Beau was good at searching for stuff, and Caleb  and Fjord were able to look for strange magic.

Caduceus rubbed at his temples with a quiet grunt. By the look on Beau’s face, she caught it as well.

“You okay, big guy?” She asked.

“Hm? Oh, yes,” his voice came out scratchy, and he cleared it with a wince. Jester frowned. “Was kind of… woozy for a second.”

That seemed to catch Fjord’s attention, as well as everyone else’s. “As much as we want you with us for this, maybe it’d be best if you stayed and held down the fort.”

Caduceus shook his head, even as he was wiping his nose on his handkerchief. “Naw, no need for that.”

-----

The shop was small and dingy. The prices were too high and the items were terrible quality. The real crime was the rip-off, Jester thought. 

The owner, a stout drow woman, greeted them with a lazy grin and asked all of their misleading questions.

Fjord bought one of the badly-made candles to throw her off any scent and distract her while Beau went snooping. Jester watched Caduceus the whole time, scanning the interior with watery, irritated eyes. 

He looked like he needed to be in bed. Probably everyone knew that by now.

“Oh, that cup comes with a set. Ten gold for the lot!”

Caleb raised an eyebrow. “Ten gold for a tea set? Don’t you consider that a bit steep?”

The drow woman furrowed her brows. “Excuse me, but if you don’t like my prices, then you can leave. I have customers in everyday who are willing to pay full-”

Crash!

All heads turned to the noise. Caduceus leaned on a shelf in the middle of the room, obvious haven shaken it and knocked a few things onto the ground as he fell.

“Sorry,” he blinked blearily, massaging his head once again. “Got a little dizzy…”

The woman harrumphed. “I hope you know you’re paying for anything broken, over there!”

“Yes yes, absolutely,” Caleb got her attention once again, pulling out his coin purse. “Will ten gold plus that tea set cover everything?”

A chuckle bubbled out of her. “No.”

“Caduceus,” Jester said quietly, making her way over to him. He was still leaning on the shelf, his nose was dripping a bit but she didn’t think this was the time to tell him. He still looked lightheaded, like he didn’t trust himself to stand upright, but when she felt his forehead, he didn’t feel much warmer than before. “What’s wrong?”

He shook his head and knuckled his nose, wincing when his hand came away wet. “Don’t feel very well all of a sudden… I think- oh…” the firbolg pressed a fist to his mouth and swallowed hard. He was puking before Jester realized what was happening.

The vomit splashed onto the wooden floor, getting everyone’s attention once again. Jester grabbed his arm, hoping to help keep him upright before he fell into his own sick. 

“What the- excuse me!” The drow woman marched out from behind the safety of her counter. “Get out of my shop! You’ve made a complete mess that I’m forced to clean up. Go! Now!” 

“Oh come on!” Beau hissed. “Seriously?!” she looked ready to fight the woman.

Caleb, much to everyone’s confusion, started shuffling everyone out along with the woman. “Yes, yes we are on our way out. Thank you for your um… we’re going now.” 

Jester helped Caduceus regain his balance and go out the door. He looked like he was feeling better once they walked out.

They walked for a good couple of minutes before Caleb started to explain.

“Whatever magic in there was reacting badly to our-” he held up the emblem around his neck and gesture to theirs. “A little ‘fuck you’ to the government, I think.”

“Caduceus must’ve gotten the brunt of it,” Fjord said with realization. “Since he was already sick.”

Caduceus made an unhappy noise in the back of his throat, furrowing his brows. “I really don’t feel well…” he mumbled. 

“Yeah, we’re taking you home. And calling Essek. I think I know what’s going on there. She had a fuckin’ huge stash of drugs in the back,” said Beau with a chuckle. “If we weren’t doing this for the dynasty, I would’ve just snatched it.”

“Classy as fuck,” Fjord deadpanned.

“Always.”

They returned to the house in no time, and deposited Caduceus onto the one sofa he could actually fit on without his legs hanging off the edge. He finally got out his kerchief, thoroughly abused by now, and gave his nose a good blow before wiping any remaining wetness away.

Nott sat on the floor next to him, keeping him company while Jester messaged Essek.

Heyyyy, so there’s something really weird going on at that shop and you should come talk to us about it. Also, can you bring soup?

I’ll be on my way, then. Soup…? I’ll see what I can do?

“Did you seriously ask him to bring soup?” Beau said with an amused grin.

Jester shrugged. “I thought I might as well, right?”

Fjord chuckled. “That guy must hate us so fucking much.”

There was a knock at the door far sooner than they expected. Essek did indeed show up, and - surprisingly - with a covered pot in his hands. 

He handed it off to Fjord. “Not entirely sure why you needed this, but- ah,” Essek’s eyes caught onto Caduceus, half asleep, running his fingers through Frumpkin’s fur as the cat lay on his chest. “I see you’ve come down with the cold that’s been going around.” 

Beau nodded. “Yeah, the magic at the shop made him feel shittier, so we thought it was best to bring him home and call you here.”

“Ah, my apologies for that then, Mister Clay.”

“Oh…” Caduceus sniffed, and tried to pull himself to sit up without jostling the purring cat. “I was happy to help. I suppose I was the detector of sorts,” he added with a hoarse chuckle.

They filled a hefty bowl with soup and let Caduceus carefully sip it, sniffling and leaning against Beau on the sofa, Frumpkin asleep on his lap. They told Essek their findings, and he was thankful for the clear information.

“I’ll be on my way, then,” he said. “Feel better, Mister Clay. I can have a physician or healer look in on you if needed. Just let me know.”

Everyone thanked him in one way or another.

Essek smiled, for once seeming happy to help, and left the pot of soup. They didn’t find out until they tried to return it to the dynasty guards that it belonged to Essek personally.
