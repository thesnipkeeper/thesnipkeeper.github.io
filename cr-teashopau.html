Title: Tea Shop AU
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly stops planning crimes to rescue his ailing husband from work
Word count: 1k

Tea Shop AU

Mollymauk loved his husband more than anything in the world. 

He and Caduceus both had unconventional upbringings. 

Caduceus grew up living above a funeral home next to the cemetery that had been owned by his family for generations.

Molly didn’t remember his childhood, but Gustav had practically adopted him as an adult when he and Molly ended up sharing a hospital room. The circus owner brought him home when he was released and Mollymauk never stopped being a performer, even when he moved away to pursue his own, less moral career as a conman and thief. Gustav didn’t have as much of a problem with it as he probably should have.

Of course Caduceus was already awake when Molly woke. He was always eager to start the day with a good tea blend and a healthy breakfast. 

Molly grinned at the sight of his sleepy husband sipping from a teacup that was a bit too small for his firbolg hands.

“Morning,” Mollymauk greeted. 

Caduceus gave his usual warm, lazy smile. “Hey, good morning. Did you sleep good?”

“Slept amazing. And you?”

Caduceus made a non-committal noise in the back of his throat that made Molly look back at him as he poured himself some tea. “Not well enough, I think. Kind of tired this morning.”

Molly frowned. That wasn’t normal at all. Caduceus was very much a morning person, and he slept like a log.

They had a breakfast of oatmeal and tea, Molly scrolling through his phone while Caduceus flipped through one of his new cookbooks. Eventually, it was time for him to head out.

Caduceus owned a small tea shop. It was his pride and joy, and he was good at all of it. He was an easy person for customers to talk to and his tea was excellent. He spent a lot of time working, but he did have one other employee - Caleb Widogast. Molly had met the man a couple of times, but hadn’t really ever spoken to him.

“Have a good day at work,” Molly said fondly. He pressed a kiss to Caduceus’ cheek before he could stand up and Molly wouldn’t be able to reach his face anymore. Molly paused. Caduceus did as well, despite Molly’s weird behavior. “Why are you so warm, love?”

“Hm?” Caduceus furrowed his brows as if he didn’t know what Molly was talking about.

“Your face is really warm,” Molly said, pressed his hand to Caduceus’ cheek, and then his neck.

“Oh, I think I bundled up a little too much last night.”

Molly frowned, but he knew Caduceus had a habit of being too comfortable in too many blankets. He was thin enough that a big body like his didn’t retain heat very well. 

“Alright, if you say so.” they exchanged a quick kiss before Caduceus was off.

-----

“There’s no fucking way this is gonna work,” said Beau, Molly’s newest partner in crime. She was more capable of fighting than anyone he knew, a great asset to have. Didn’t stop her from being a douchebag.

“Beau, you need to trust me. That place has terrible security. I talk my way in, distract them, you swipe it and maybe have to knock one person out in the process.”

Beau narrowed her eyes and looked like she was about to argue once more when his phone started to ring.

Molly smiled and kept full eye contact as he answered. “Hello?” he said, voice all the more brighter with Beauregard glaring at him.

“Hello, is this Mister Mollymauk?”

Molly’s smile faded. He knew that accent anywhere. Caleb Widogast.

“Yes…”

“This is Caleb from the tea shop. Mister Clay is ah… I think you should come pick him up. He’s not feeling very well.”

Molly frowned. “I’ll be right over,” he was already getting up to grab his coat when he ended the call.

“What the fuck?” Beau demanded. “You’re leaving? I just got here. I had shit to do, Molly, but you insisted-”

Molly rolled his eyes. “Calm down. My husband’s sick. I need to go pick him up from work.”

Her mouth closed at that, and she didn’t seem to have any more to say. Apparently his new partner was a romantic, because after a pause, she asked, “You need a ride?”

Mollymauk grinned a little too widely. The Done look on her face, he thought she was about to renounce her offer. 

-----

“Do you have a way to get home?”

Molly nodded. “Caduceus has his car. I owe you for this,” Molly pressed a kiss to her cheek before she could punch him.

“Yeah you fucking do, Molly!”

Every time he walked into the shop, he was greeted by the chime of the bell above the door. It reminded of the first day he met Caduceus, when he walked in and a strange firbolg greeted him in the warmest voice he’d ever heard.

A redhead emerged from the back. “He’s back here,” Caleb kept his eyes on the other customer and his voice low.

“Oh…” 

Caduceus sat in the small back room, chin rested in his palm on the table where he and Molly would sometimes have their lunch together. He looked exhausted and woozy, with a box of tissues placed next to him.

He wasted no time rushing to his husband’s side and caressing the backs of his fingers to Caduceus’ face.

“I think I’m coming down with something,” his voice was lower and scratchier than usual. He snuffled, the base of his nostrils damp and pink like he’d been dealing with it for a while. “Got really dizzy all of a sudden…”

Molly only chuckled. “I think you’ve very well come down with it, my dear.”

“I can handle the place, Mister Clay,” Caleb said. “You should be in bed.”

“I’m inclined to agree,” Molly sighed, rubbing Caduceus’ arm. “Come on, let’s get you home.”

Caduceus nodded. Both Molly and Caleb were needed to make sure he didn’t pass out as he stood up, and they got several looks from people as they slowly made their way out the door.

Caduceus was snoring by the time they got home.
