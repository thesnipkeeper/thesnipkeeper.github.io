Title: History
Fandom: Blake Holsey High
Summary: Marshall tortures himself because of history class, Lucas and Z help
Word count: 2.4k

History

It was the night before their big history test. Lucas had no problem with it, but history, Lucas knew, was Marshall’s worst subject. Science and math, he was practically a prodigy. But history... stressed him out.

The two of them had spent most of the evening studying. It was better to just get this test over with.

A small pained noise got Lucas' attention, and he saw his roommate doubled over at his desk with his arms wrapped around his middle.

"You okay?" He asked before going back to chewing on his pencil.

"Yeah, my stomach just hurts a little. It's… it's probably nothing."

"Dude, did you eat the mystery meat in the cafeteria again?"

"What? No! Give me a little credit!" Marshall answered with a scoff. “I only made that mistake once.”

Marshall mentioned a stomach ache a couple more times that night, only casually, but Lucas could feel worry creeping up. It could have just been a run-of-the-mill stomach ache, but he had a feeling that something bad was going to happen.

They both went to bed.

-----

Lucas woke far too early for his liking. The sun wasn’t even up yet, and his clock read 3:26AM. Too early. He groaned, wondering what could have woken him up this time.

As he became more aware, Lucas realized that his roommate’s bed was empty, and the bathroom light shone in the crack under the door.

And those were the very distinctive sounds of puking.

Lucas rubbed the sleep out of his eyes and threw his blankets to the side. By the look of Marshall’s side of the room, blankets trailed across the floor, he got up in a hurry. Maybe that was what woke Lucas in the first place.

He knocked. “Marshall?”

A whimper.

Lucas sighed. So this was the kind of day it was going to be. He carefully turned the doorknob and found it to be unlocked. Locking the door was probably the farthest thing from Marshall’s mind in the moment.

Marshall was curled in a sad heap over the toilet. His hair and pajamas were both damp like he’d sweat through them and then stopped.

Lucas swore under his breath and leaned against the wall, 3AM sleepiness not quite gone.

“...You okay?”

Marshall spat into the water and lazily reached up to flush the contents away. “Do I look okay?” he croaked.

Lucas couldn’t help but chuckle. “Good point.” He left for a moment and returned with Marshall’s water bottle. He set it next to him. “So you can rinse your mouth out when you’re done.”

Marshall groaned, and then it cut off as he gagged again.

“...Which obviously isn’t going to be any time soon.”

Marshall threw up a couple more times before he finally seemed confident that he was done for now. He rinsed his mouth out and let Lucas help him back to bed.

By the time Marshall was in bed, he looked like he could pass out right there.

“Are you gonna be okay if I go back to sleep?”

Marshall gave a minute nod, eyes already fallen shut.

Lucas woke an hour later to his roommate scrambling back to the bathroom.

It was definitely going to be one of those days.

This was generally how it went. When one of them was sick, neither of them got sleep. They’d stopped blaming each other for it long ago; they’d been roommates for long enough.

Lucas groaned. It was five in the morning. He might as well get ready for class and use the extra time to study.

“Need anything?” he called as he grabbed a uniform from his closet.

A sniffle. “No...” Marshall said. His voice was hoarse, almost painfully so, and it sounded like he might have been crying.

Lucas was almost done getting dressed when the toilet flushed and Marshall emerged, looking even worse than he had earlier. He padded to his bed and sat down. Just as Lucas suspected, his eyes were red and watery, and he knew it wasn’t just from throwing up. He did Marshall the favor of not mentioning it, he’d probably deny it anyway.

Lucas finished buttoning his shirt, and when he looked over, he found Marshall with his face in his history textbook.

“Dude, you should be resting.”

Marshall rolled his eyes. “The test is today. I can’t just not study.”

“You’re still going to class? Marshall, you hardly slept last night.”

Marshall nodded. “I know, mom. It’s not like I want to go to class today! But I have to get that test done.”

Lucas had to admit, he couldn’t argue against that. It wouldn’t be the first time a student had gone to class sick. The school took education seriously, and outside of Professor Z, most of the teachers weren’t as lenient when it came to being absent from class.

Marshall flipped a page and then swallowed hard, bringing the back of his hand to his lips. Lucas was ready to grab the trash bin from under his desk.

Marshall shook his head and breathed out a sigh of relief. “False alarm.”

-----

They were about to head out the door when Marshall grabbed a sweater and quickly put it on.

“What?” he said to Lucas’ raised eyebrow. “I’m a little chilly.”

“Because you have the chills, Marshall. You’re probably running a fever.” He rolled his eyes.

They made their way out to the hallway, and Marshall didn’t respond with anything outside of a shrug.

“...sorry.” Lucas sighed. “I know it’s not your fault.”

“Dude, I’ll be fine. Seriously.”

Lucas didn’t believe that for a second.

Professor Z greeted them from his desk as they walked in and took their seats. And when he stood up and actually got a good look at them, his eyes widened. “Marshall, you look...”

“He knows,” Lucas mumbled.

Marshall buttoned his sweater and crossed his arms as if he was trying to conserve as much heat as possible, despite the fact that his face was red and his body was obviously warm enough.

“I was kind of... up all night,” He cleared his throat. “Puking?”

Z frowned. “Why aren’t you in bed?” He asked, a bit of scolding at the edge of his voice.

“We have a big history test today,” Lucas said, because Marshall’s brain seemed to be working much slower today.

“Oh my God, I’m dreading that,” was the first thing Josie said as she walked in, followed by Corinne.

Z rolled his eyes. “It’s not hard to get a note from the nurse to say you aren’t fit to attend class, I’m sure.”

“Yeah, but...” Marshall paused, hand going to his mouth for a moment. “I just want it to be over.”

“We all do,” Josie agreed. She threw her bag on her desk and came back to lean on Marshall and Lucas’. Her eyebrows knitted in concern. “What’s wrong with you?”

“Sick,” Marshall admitted.

“What are you doing-? Oh. The test.”

“Mm.”

“What a time to get sick,” Josie said sympathetically.

Z looked tempted to send him out of class, hesitating before going to the front with a sigh. The bell rang just as the last couple of students filed in.

“Alright everyone, take your seats!”

Lucas found it difficult to concentrate, which was why he was so proud of himself when he managed to answer one of Z’s questions correctly.

“What’s another example of that, Marshall?”

Lucas, along with the entire rest of the class turned to him. He was fast asleep at the counter.

“Marshall!” Lucas whispered. He went to nudge his friend awake, but Z just shook his head and turned to someone else.

“Corinne?”

Corinne, of course, had the correct answer immediately, distracting the rest of the class from the fact that Marshall was napping in the middle of science. Z seemed to understand.

Lucas could see him sparing glances at Marshall (who was still dead asleep) throughout class. He knew and didn’t acknowledge it.

In the end, the bell was what woke Marshall.

He sat up with a groan and rubbed his eyes as everyone else filed out.

As he became more aware, his brows furrowed in confusion. He looked around before he realized that class was over.

“I- did I sleep-?”

Lucas nodded.

Marshall turned to Professor Z. “I’m so sorry, I don’t know how-”

Z held up a hand, shaking his head. “From the night that you had, I thought you could use the extra sleep. Just for today.”

“Of course, thanks,” Marshall muttered, and then groaned. He stood up, and that was as far as he got before he had to lean against the desk for support.

“Whoa, easy...” Lucas said.

“I don’t... I don’t feel so good,” rubbed his head, eyes squeezing shut.

“Like you’re going to throw up?” He helped Marshall to a nearby chair that was closer to the ground than those stools were. “Because a little warning is nice.”

“No, like... I’m kinda dizzy? And I don’t know... I feel... shaky.”

Z hummed thoughtfully. “Okay, sit still, I’m going to get something from my desk.”

The second bell rang, and Marshall went to stand up before Lucas nudged him back down again.

“Don’t worry! I’ll write a note having your absences excused,” Z assured as he dug around in various desk drawers. “When’s history class?”

“Next period,” Lucas said as he rubbed his friend’s back. “What are-?”

“Alright... aha!” Z returned with a small plastic case, and pulled something out of it to reveal a thermometer.

“You just have one of those lying around?” Lucas asked while the two of them watched Z press various buttons and place a plastic cover on the tip.

“believe it or not, there’s one in every classroom for situations just like this.”

Marshall turned his head and Z carefully pressed the thermometer into his ear. It beeped a moment later.

Z frowned. “That,” he said, flipping it around for Marshall and Lucas to see the reading. “Is why you’re feeling dizzy and weak. Two symptoms of a high fever.”

“He had chills this morning, too,” Lucas offered.

Marshall huffed.

“Well,” Z knelt down so he was in Marshall’s line of vision. “I hate to say it, but I can’t let you go to class like this, Marshall.”

“I’m fine. I’ll be fine.”

“I’m afraid the school has rules about fevers this high. And if you’ve been throwing up as well...” he ejected the thermometer cover and then placed it back in the case.

“Please don’t make me miss this test, Professor Z.”

Z sighed. “Why do you need to get to this test so badly, Marshall? You can make up for it later.”

Lucas knew the answer before Marshall could give it. “Our history teacher is... well, let’s just say that missing a test - sick or not - has... repercussions.”

“I don’t think anyone’s going to punish you for being too sick to attend class.”

“She would,” Marshall muttered. “She doesn’t say it, but the kids who miss class always get extra assignments in their make-up work.”

Z frowned. “Really?”

Lucas nodded. “Yup.”

“Alright, well... I can take care of that.”

Marshall and Lucas both looked at him curiously.

“You... can?”

“If that’s what it’ll take to get you to go back to your room and rest. Leave it to me, Marshall.”

-----

Lucas sighed and threw his bag on his bed, before flopping on it himself. “That test was the worst.”

Marshall sat up, apparently just having been woken from a nap. “Really? Wow, if you’re saying that then I guess I’m glad I missed it.”

Lucas changed out of his uniform immediately. “So... feeling any better?”

Marshall shook his head as he yawned. “Not really... I threw up after you left.”

“Did you drink anything?”

“Mhm. Josie dropped off a sports drink between periods.” He gestured toward the blue bottle on his nightstand. He chuckled fondly, “And then Corinne left some tylenol.”

“Nice.”

“Hey!” Lucas and Marshall both looked toward the door to see Josie. “Principal Durst wants to see you in her office if you’re feeling up to it.“

Marshall was suddenly alert. “What? Why?”

Josie shrugged and walked in to plop into a desk chair. “Don’t know, but Professor Z is in there too.”

Marshall and Lucas exchanged looks.

Marshall shrugged, got out of bed, started to take his shirt off before looking at Josie who was currently playing with a puzzle game she found on Marshall’s desk, “Could you turn around please?”

Josie scoffed. “I’m not even looking at you!” But she did anyway while Marshall changed out of his gross shirt and put on a pair of jeans over his pajamas.

He cleared his throat. “Do um, you guys want to come with me? In case I’m in huge trouble?”

“Why would you be in huge trouble?”

“I don’t know, but I don’t want to go to Durst’s office alone!”

-----

“Apologies for bringing you out of your room, but I felt this couldn’t wait. Professor Zachary has brought to my attention that you attended class while you were sick, is that true?”

Marshall swallowed. “Y-yes?”

“And you intended on going to all of your classes in the state that you’re in?”

Josie opened her mouth to defend him, but Lucas stopped her.

“Yeah,” Marshall admitted with a sigh. “I didn’t want to miss our history test.”

Durst nodded, expression as undecipherable as always. “So Professor Zachary has told me. He has also volunteered to be the one to do your and other students’ make-up test so Ms. Laurence can further focus on building her curriculum.”

All three of them turned to Z, who smiled and nodded.

“Now, we don’t want this happening again, understood? We can’t have sick students wandering our campus and spreading germs because they’re concerned about missing a test.”

Marshall paused, and then nodded. “Understood.”

“So, the next time you intend on going to class when you’re ill, don’t. It will be taken care of. Now, if you’ll excuse me, I have a conversation to have with Ms. Laurence in the history department.”

All three of them were left wide-eyed as Principal Durst left the office, and they all turned to Z.

“And that, I believe, is her way of apologizing for the way things went. Or- almost went.”

“Professor Z, what’d you do?” Lucas gasped.

Z shrugged. “I told you I’d take care of it.”

“Thanks, professor,” said Marshall. “That... thanks.”

He smiled. “No problem, just stay awake in my class next time, deal?”

“Deal.”
