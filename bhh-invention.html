Title: Invention
Fandom: Blake Holsey High
Summary: Marshall's newest business idea has its setbacks
Word count: 1.5k

Invention

“He’s working on his newest business idea for his college fund,” Lucas says as they ascend the stairs.

“What is it?”

“No idea. I just hope that this one doesn’t get him in any trouble like... well, all of his other ideas.”

Just as expected, Marshall is hunched over his desk working on... something. A small, strange-looking device with wires coming out of each side. Lucas can’t even begin to guess what it does, he’s never seen anything like it before.

“What...”

“Oh,” Marshall laughs excitedly, and seems to do so so hard he begins to cough. “Hey guys, what’s up?”

“You weren’t at dinner,” Josie says, throwing her books on Lucas’ bed and leaning against the wall to face Marshall.

“Oh, yeah, I wasn’t super hungry and I’m busy perfecting my newest invention.”

“So...” Lucas says. “What is it?”

“I don’t know,” Marshall says smugly because he absolutely does know. “Why don’t you ask Corinne?”

“Corinne? She’s sick. She’s been stuck in our room all day. When did you talk to her?”

“Not anymore,” Marshall proudly declares.

“...What?”

“Ladies,” and nods to Josie, and then to Lucas, “and gentlemen, I have created a device that cures colds.”

Josie and Lucas burst out in laughter.

“No way. C’mon, what’s it really do?”

“I’m serious, you guys! Corinne’s fine now! Last I knew, she was going to the library to study!”

They exchange looks. “How...?”

“It’s simple, really. I just-”

-----

Lucas walks into the student lounge just in time to see another happy customer of Marshall’s he’s spent the whole Saturday ‘curing’ sick students.

“You’re still at this, huh?”

“Yeah, of course. I’m charging twenty-five bucks for every person!”

Lucas takes a seat next to him and leans in close. “Be honest. This doesn’t actually work. It’s just a placebo.”

Marshall looks genuinely offended. “What?!”

“C’mon Marshall, you’re almost literally selling snake oil, admit it.”

Marshall pulls the device close and shields it with his arms like a parent covering their child’s ears. “How dare you! This isn’t snake oil, this is science. And no one asked you-” Marshall’s breath catches in his throat and he turns away quickly, crushing the sleeve over his hoodie over his mouth to muffle a harsh cough.

Lucas leans back and looks him over. Marshall looks tired, and if he didn’t know any better, he’d think that Marshall was sick.

“You okay?”

Marshall slowly lowers his hand and clear his throat. “Yeah! Yeah, psh, I’m fine.”

“Are you sure? Because you kind of sound like you’re getting sick.”

“Are you kidding? That’d be ridiculous.

-----

Sunday comes along, and Lucas now knows that Marshall isn’t fine. He has yet to get out of bed. Granted, he has no real responsibilities today, but Lucas expected him to be in the lounge curing all ailments by now.

It’s ten when Marshall coughs himself awake. Lucas winces.

“Whoa dude, you okay?”

Marshall sits up. “Yeah, I’m good.” but he sounds awful. His voice is rough and thick, and he looks like he hasn’t slept all night.

“O...kay? Well, I’m going downstairs to study with everyone. You can like... join us if you want.”

Marshall snuffles. “Thanks, but I think I’m going to make some more money today.” Even though he sounds like death, he still manages to sound like a skeezy business man.

Lucas chuckles and shakes his head. “Whatever. See ya.”

-----

“He’s sick?” Josie’s eyebrows raise to her hairline as if she can’t believe it. “Doesn’t he have his weird cold cure machine?”

“Yeah, that’s the weird part.” He sighs and takes a lazy bite of his sandwich. It's awful just like school food always is, but he should probably bring a tray back to the room. Marshall would most certainly forget to eat and let himself starve.

Corinne shrugs. "That thing actually works, too. He cured me somehow."

"Something weird is definitely going on here," Josie mutters.

-----

Lucas hears a harsh, rattling cough from just outside the door. He bites the inside of his mouth and mutters something along the lines of "idiot", gripping the sides of the tray harder as he walked in.

He certainly doesn't expect Marshall to not be working on his new invention. Instead, Marshall is sitting cross-legged on his bed, tissue pressed to his nose as he flips through a textbook in a lame attempt to study.

"Dinner," Lucas says shortly before setting the tray on the spot in front of him on the bed.

"Oh, snf! Thadks, but I'b ndot very hungry." Another sniffle, followed by an irritated cough.

"Dude, do you need to go to the nurse or something?"

"What? Oh, no. No way. I'b just tired frob working all day. You kdow how mady kids have beed sick odn campus this week?"

"Can't imagine."

-----

"Marshall, I know you have my- whoa." Josie stops in the doorway. She takes a few slow steps inside Marshall and Lucas' room.

Marshall is asleep (at least, he looks asleep) with Lucas leaning over him, feeling his forehead without much grace.

"What's... going on?"

He shushes her. "He's kinda been asleep all day. Off and on, I mean."

"Wha-"

Lucas leads her out to the hallway and quietly closes the door behind them. "He's sick. Like, really sick."

Josie looks back at the door with a frown. "Something's wrong, then."

"Uh, yeah. I think so."

"Does it...have something to do with that thing?" Josie nods toward the device, Marshall's new invention on his desk.

Cogs begin to turn in Lucas' mind. "...Someone showed up here right before lights out last night. He was like, really sick and he begged Marshall to hell him because he had a big test tomorrow."

Josie blinks. "So maybe he... Caught it?"

Lucas shakes his head. "That guy left totally healthy."

"Oh… my God."

They both turn to Marshall, who's sleeping fitfully. He's a total mess, face pinched in discomfort and nose very visibly running. Lucas sighs.

Josie growls in frustration. "He must know, right?"

"Maybe not at first, but now... Yeah, he definitely knows." No wonder he didn't mention getting sick. Marshall realized what he was doing, and he wasn't willing to stop.

-----

"Guys, come on!" Marshall chases them as fast as he can in his current state. "Don't do anything impulsive, please!"

People stare at the strange scene as the group runs by with Marshall's little device. They rush into the science room, Marshall chasing them right in.

Z looks to be in the process of building a model when he looks up.

"Hey, what's-" He evaluates the situation before him. All of the students look in various states of distress, and Marshall--whom he was told was sick--is fighting to get something out of Lucas' hands. He raises his hands. "Alright, alright, what's going on?"

"Marshall built this thing, and it's what's making him sick!" Corinne blurts.

"Wha-"

"And he refuses to stop using it," Lucas adds.

Z turns to Marshall, who once again doesn't look like he should be out of bed. "It's… making you sick? How is that?"

Marshall slows the fighting with his friend and shrugs hesitantly. "Dunno, it just… happened. But Z, people are paying a lot of money for what it does and I need to save as much as I can-"

Z raises a hand to stop him. "What exactly does it do?"

Marshall doesn't answer.

Josie sighs. "He's taking other people's colds and stuff."

"Okay, that's not good." Z frowns. He wants to question exactly how that happened, but knowing this school, and his science club, it probably wouldn't help. He clears his throat. "Marshall, the human body can only take so much stress at once, so you're really not doing yourself a favor by taking on various illnesses…" Z takes a step closer to the group and extends a hand to Lucas. "May I?"

Lucas nods carefully and hands the device over.

He turns it over in his hands, inspecting each part of it. It's… something. "Well, it's possible that-"

Josie chimes in. "I think we should smash it."

"Oh no, I don't-"

But they're already taking it and placing it on the closest desk.

"What?!" Marshall says. "Guys!"

Z isn't exactly sure where Josie found the hammer, or how she knew how to find the hammers in the science room, but in one swift move, it's a pile of misshapen parts.

-----

Vaughn, being a Pearson, can get away with much more in the school. Like asking the kitchen ladies for things outside of meal hours--like tea.

"I guess I should… thank you guys for this. I'm feeling better already," Marshall takes the cup of tea from Vaughn. He sniffles.

"You're a moron," Josie says.

Everyone nods in agreement.

Lucas scoffs. "I can't believe smashing it even worked."

"It was pretty cathartic." Josie gives Marshall a very pointed look that has him averting his eyes back to the hot tea. "Pull something like that again, Marshall, and next time it'll be you I'm smashing with a hammer."

Marshall clears his throat. "Understood."
