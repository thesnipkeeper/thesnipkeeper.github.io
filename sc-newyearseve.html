Title: New Year's Eve
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's not big on celebrating holidays, but Patrick makes it better
Word count: 1.3k

New Year's Eve

It’s Patrick’s idea to go to the new year’s party at the cafe. David would have preferred to stay home, do nothing, and watch the ball drop on TV. But Patrick’s trying to involve himself in the community, and it’s kind of cute how excited he is for a party that will mostly consist of drunk townies.

David’s already exhausted when they arrive. He isn’t sure exactly why, maybe it’s all the extra work they did at the store beforehand so they could have everything done early, but he finds himself wanting to go home the moment they walk in the door.

The sight of various people already downing drinks, the sound of Ronnie in the middle of one of her nonsensical stories, ugh.

“I’ll go get us some punch,” Patrick says, squeezing David’s shoulder as he walks away.

David tries to smile as brightly as possible.

Patrick’s done so much for him. For once, David’s going to put up with something for Patrick’s sake.

Patrick returns with two cups of no-doubt repulsive party punch and hands one to David before taking a sip of his own. “Looks like half the town’s here,” he says. “Do they do this every year?”

“I wouldn’t know,” David scrunches his eyes shut and wrinkles his nose. “I’ve always spent November-through-January at the motel in a fugue state.”

Patrick raises an eyebrow. “Oh, David...” he says with pity. “You and I are going to spend a lot of time doing obnoxious holiday things from now on,” he chuckles.

David clears his throat, and winces at the dry feeling. Breathing the cold air on the way here has really taken a toll, then. “I’m so excited.”

“I can see that.”

-----

Everyone’s sitting down comfortably and chatting. The front of the restaurant is covered in groups playing cards and board games, rather loudly.

David feels a headache coming on. The dryness in the back of his throat is starting to turn into a scratchy feeling that he can’t smooth over with drinks or subtle coughs.

He’s beginning to wonder if he’s getting sick.

Normally he’d use it as an excuse to escape the crowded social setting, but Patrick’s having fun. He looks way too happy to be here amidst the drunk patrons.

He leans against the corner where the booth meets the wall, while Patrick chats enthusiastically to his father about some sport involving a ball. He could fall asleep right here.

“David.”

“Hm?”

“Are we boring you?” Patrick asks with a chuckle.

“No- well, yes, but I’m just really tired.”

Johnny hums disapprovingly. “I’ve told you that you should be getting enough sleep, son. You’re not going to feel as young as you do now in a couple years...”

“A couple?” David growls under his breath. “Well, thank you dad, but I got plenty of sleep last night.”

Patrick nods in a agreement. “Yeah, he fell asleep in the middle of our movie. I practically had to drag him to bed. And then he slept in an hour late.”

That is true, David realizes. He shouldn’t be tired at all. Fuck.

The conversation fades back into sportsball while David yawns. Patrick doesn’t even turn to him as he wraps an arm around David and lets him lean into him.

-----

“Hey, it’s snowing!” Twyla announces.

David looks up from his overfilled plate of snacks to see the there are indeed fat snowflakes floating down.

He pulls his collar away from his neck in an attempt to get away from the stifling heat. He’s sweating. Sure, it’s snowing, but does that mean that the heating has to be on so high?

“So are you having fun?” Alexis asks, setting both hands on his arm to get his full attention.

“Uh, sure?” he clears his throat. It hurts, but his voice is starting to sound noticeably off and he can’t have that. He huffs and sets his plate down. “Why is it so hot in here, though?” He irritably rolls his sleeves up and tugs at his collar yet again.

Alexis looks at him like he’s insane. “I thought it was a little chilly in here actually.”

“Mm’kay, but you’re wearing very little clothing.”

“Yeah, well if you’re hot maybe you should take off one of your three-hundred layers, David. Jeez.”

She has a point.

David rolls his eyes and pulls his sweater off with only a little difficulty. he’s wearing a long-sleeved t-shirt underneath and he supposes that having it exposed isn’t the worst that could happen if it means being a little more comfortable.

“Ugh, it’s still hot...” He groans.

Alexis raises an eyebrow. “Um, are you feeling okay? You’re like, always cold.”

David nods before she even finishes talking. “I’m fine,” because he is. “I’m just going to go get some fresh air.”

Alexis doesn’t say anything else as he makes his way out the door of the cafe. He can feel the bite in the cold air, but it feels blissful on his skin. David smiles, sighing in relief at finally being away from the stifling heat, the stifling people...

He sniffles. He’d like to think that the runny nose is a result of being outside, but it’s probably just another symptom of the plague that he’s slowly coming down with.

“David?”

David startles. He hadn’t even heard the door jingle when Patrick came out.

“Oh, hey!” he smiles, opening an arm to bring Patrick closer.

“What are you doing out here? It’s freezing.”

“Just getting some fresh air. It was getting a little too warm for me in there.”

Patrick pulls away slightly and furrows his brow as if trying to figure something out. David shifts under his scrutiny. And Patrick finally looks as if he’s going to dismiss David’s weirdness.

And then David coughs. Of course the first cough has to happen in front of Patrick. Of course it’s the cold weather that initiates it.

Patrick’s eyes widen in realization. “Are you coming down with a cold?”

David sighs and drags both hands down his face. “Or something,” he admits.

“Well that’s probably why you’re so hot,” Patrick says, concern edging his words. He reaches up and lays a hand against David’s neck. He frowns, hums thoughtfully. “Yeah, you feel really warm.”

David sniffles and drags his sleeve across the underside of his nose. “Fuuuck. Why now, of all times-”

Patrick nods, “I know. Wanna go back inside?”

He seats David at their booth, kisses his temple, and walks away. David thinks he hears him having a conversation with Twyla, but he’s not paying enough attention.

And then a mug appears in front of him. Patrick slides in with an identical drink. “Twlya helped me out a little.”

David raises an eyebrow and leans in to take a careful sniff. “Is this a hot toddy?”

“Yeah, yeah it is. Not actually good for sick people, I’ve heard, but it’ll help your throat, and I didn’t think you’d want to be done drinking yet...?”

David shakes his head. “I absolutely did not, thank you.” He nurses the drink over the next few minutes. Patrick has to pass him some napkins for his increasingly-runny nose at one point.

They both startle at the countdown.

“TEN, NINE-”

“Oh...” David realizes right then and there that he’s not going to get to kiss his boyfriend on new year’s for the first time.

“-SIX, FIVE, FOUR, THREE, TWO...”

Patrick kisses him.

David’s eyes slip shut as his leans into the kiss, Patrick’s hand along the nape of his neck to pull him in even closer.

They pull away slowly, the cheers filling the restaurant mere background noise as they stare into each other’s eyes.

“I’m probably contagious,” David informs. He’s unable to bite back a smile, though.

Patrick bumps their foreheads together and chuckles. “I think I’ll live.” He leans in for one more kiss before, “Happy New Year, David.”

David grins. “Happy New Year, Patrick.”
