Day Off

David woke that morning and immediately regretted it. His entire body ached, muscles screaming at him when he tried to turn over. In the end, he only had enough energy to make it on his back before he gave up. A nagging headache throbbed just above his eyes even before he opened them, and he pulled the duvet up to his neck to stop the strange chills.

David whimpered quietly - or tried to - and then he gave a scratchy cough. This was unfair.

Patrick stirred. “Morning,” he mumbled, sitting up and rubbing his eyes. When he looked over at David, who was currently on his back huddled as far as he could under the blanket with an unhappy expression, he raised an eyebrow. “What’s up?”

David shook his head, cleared his throat. He’d have to admit it to Patrick at some point, but maybe that time would come after they had celebrated their day off. “Nothing’s up!” David said, forcing himself to sit up, granted he did it very slowly. His limbs hardly wanted to work, his energy was gone despite the fact that they went to bed relatively early the night before. He leaned over and nuzzled Patrick’s neck in lieu of a morning kiss. “Are you excited for today?”

Patrick hummed happily. “I really am. We finally get to spend some time together outside of work.” He took in a breath and furrowed his brow at David with a scrutinizing expression. “Are you feeling okay? Your voice sounds a little scratchy.”

David swallowed experimentally and found that his throat was indeed scratchy. “Just using it for the first time this morning. I’m sure that it’ll... sort itself out.” He said, clearing his throat. “I’m going to go take a shower? And then we can talk about what we’re going to do today.” Showering was the very last thing he felt like doing. No- leaving the apartment and doing things were the last things he felt like doing. Everything hurt, even more so when he swung his legs over the edge of the bed. He tried to suppress the shivers now that he was no longer under the warmth and safety of Patrick’s bedspread.

“Okay, but are you sure...?”

“Mhm, I’m fine.”

He went to stand up, and the room immediately began to swim before he was even up at all. David groaned and brought a hand to his head, quickly grabbing the frame of the bed to help ease himself back down. His legs wouldn’t support him. The headache worsened, and a pang of nausea made itself known the more he moved.

“David!” Patrick laid a hand on his arm, paused, and then his cool fingers rested against David’s temple. He sighed. “I’ll get the thermometer.”

-----

Patrick gently removed the thermometer from his boyfriend’s ear. “No wonder you don’t feel like moving,” Patrick said before pressing a kiss to David’s forehead. “Get comfortable, I’ll start on some soup for lunch.”

“Can you help me, though?” David whined before he could walk away.

“You want me to tuck you in?”

“I’m just... I’m so weak...”

Patrick chuckled, shaking his head fondly. He adjusted the pillows and pulled the blankets over David. David’s eyes were already slipping shut, the fever sapping all of his energy. He cupped David’s cheek.

“You going back to sleep?”

David nodded.

“Okay, David. Want me to wake you up when lunch is done?”

David thought about it for a while, and then nodded again.

He was asleep before Patrick even made it to the kitchen.

-----

“I feel terrible,” David muttered.

“You’ll feel a little better in a couple days, I’m sure.”

“No,” David pulled the blanket around himself, struggling to get comfortable on Patrick’s couch. “I feel terrible for ruining our one true day off. We were actually going to do things. We never do things.” He coughed into his pillow and then shuddered again.

“We’ll have other days off.” Patrick weaved around the couch and tapped David’s legs until he managed to sit up. He helped David get the blanket situated around him before handing him a bowl of steaming soup.

It smelled good, and it looked even better. But David didn’t feel like eating, or moving, really. He looked at it regretfully.

“Okay, if you refuse a meal then you’re officially scaring me, David.”

David groaned, though it was less enthusiastic than his usual one. “I’m scaring even me,” he admitted. “I don’t feel good. Can we save the soup for later?”

Patrick looked down at the soup and back at his boyfriend before taking the bowl out of his hands. “Well, I want you to eat a little bit at least... I think I have an idea.”

David was only half-conscious, practically rag-dolling against the corner of the couch, until he felt soothing fingers running through his hair, and then something prodding at his lips.

“Oh my God,” David pulled back. “Are you trying to feed me?”

“Maybe,” Patrick answered innocently.

David opened his mouth, and tasted some of the most delicious soup he’d ever had. Granted, maybe it just tasted so good because his loving boyfriend made it from scratch just for him. David found himself opening his mouth expectantly, and Patrick chuckled and complied.

David managed to swallow a few more spoonfuls before he realized he couldn’t stomach anymore, even as much as he was enjoying the attention from Patrick.

“No more,” David mumbled hoarsely.

Patrick nodded. “Okay, David.” He placed the bowl on the coffee table and wordlessly helped David lie down once again.

-----

David was feeling exceptionally disgusting by the end of the day. After spending most of it either in bed or on the couch, switching between sweating and chills, he was gross. His hair was greasy, his clothes were damp, and his nose was crusty from a day of wiping and blowing it.

He was feeling a little better, maybe he could handle a shower before bedtime.

“I think I’ll take a shower,” David said as he sat up.

“Ah. You... think that’s a good idea?”

“I can handle bathing on my own, Patrick.” David scoffed.

“Okay, I’m just a little worried that you’re going to pass out or something. You’ve gotten really lightheaded whenever you tried to stand up, remember? And maybe you should conserve your energy?”

David gave him a Look, and then relented with a pitiful whine because Patrick was probably right. “I just hate feeling gross. You know this.”

Patrick nodded. “I know.”

-----

David sat on the lid of the toilet as Patrick helped him remove his sweater and undershirt. He’d have to get David some clean, dry clothes to change into later.

Patrick left David to remove the rest of his clothing while Patrick took off his own, starting with his sweatpants.

“Oh,” David chuckled, biting back a smirk. “I don’t think I’m in the mood for that tonight...”

Patrick rolled his eyes fondly. “I’m just going to help you. Give me this for peace of mind.”

“If you insist,” David said with a smile.

The two of them managed to squeeze in without issue. Patrick began to wash David’s back when David whimpered.

“That doesn’t feel good...”

Patrick stopped. “What, the washcloth?”

David nodded.

“Okay, give me a sec.” He squeezed some soap onto his palm and started spreading it across David’s back, gliding across the overheated skin. David relaxed under his touch.

He was getting sleepier by the minute, though he would never admit it. Patrick knew David didn’t have enough energy to stand this long, but of course...

Patrick kissed his shoulder as he reached across David to grab the shampoo.

“Mmm...” David hummed happily.

Patrick massaged the soap into his hair, gently at the scalp just the way David was constantly reminding him.

For a moment he wondered if David was asleep, standing up in the middle of the shower.

“You still with me?”

“Mhmmmm.”

It was a nice moment until David took a gurgly breath and had to abruptly turn to cough away from Patrick, harsh and wet.

“Just spit it onto the drain.”

David whined, but had no choice but to do so. “Eww. We were having such a nice moment. snf!”

Patrick laughed and kissed his cheek. “You might as well blow your nose too.”

David tensed and gently nudged him away. “Oh my God no, you don’t get to kiss me and be all cute and then say things like that.”

“I’m just trying to help! Your nose is very clearly running, David.”

David blushed and brought a hand up to his nose. “Okay, but you have to leave first.”

Patrick nodded. “I’ll get you some clean clothes while you take care of that, then.”

He left the shower and dried himself off as David’s snuffling got more desperate. He closed the bathroom door behind him just as he heard an unfortunate sneeze and a series of wet gurgles. Patrick winced.

He grabbed a pair of flannel pajama bottoms he bought David for Christmas last year, and then a long sleeved T-shirt that was light enough he wouldn’t get overheated.

The shower turned off, and he knocked before going in to deliver the clothes.

David’s face was red, he was rubbing his nose, but he looked like he felt better.

“Thanks,” David said, taking the stack of clothes.

-----

He emerged minutes later, comfortable in the clothes Patrick chose for him. He cleared his throat, eyes going anywhere but Patrick as he got back down on the couch/blanket nest.

David gave a reluctant smile, looking up at the ceiling. “You didn’t have to do all of this.”

“Well, I think I did. If you passed out in my bathroom I’d be liable.”

“Oh my God no, I mean-”

“I know what you meant, David.” He leaned in and pressed a gentle kiss to his lips.

David’s eyes widened. “Are you insane?”

Patrick ignored it. “I hope you know that I want to take care of you.

“It is pretty nice...” David admitted. He forgot how nice it is to soak up attention when you’re sick. Especially from someone you love so much. “No one’s ever really... I mean, back in New York...”

“Well, fuck them, right?”

“Absolutely,” David agreed. “Just... thank you. So much.”

Patrick kissed him again.

And it was no surprise when Patrick woke up in the same state the next morning.
