Title: Uncaring
Fandom: Jeremiah
Summary: Markus is sick, Jeremiah gets him
Word count: 1.4k

Uncaring

Jeremiah wasn't sure what to expect when Erin told him and Kurdy that Markus was ill, but it wasn't this.

He always saw the man as... Well, a little invincible. He was an asshole, but he was strong, smart, competent. So to see him lying in the infirmary, pale with a vomit bin sitting next to him... It was weird.

He smiled as Jeremiah entered, something that Markus seemed to be good at doing no matter what. What he had to smile about in this day and age, Jeremiah was never sure.

"Jesus Christ," Jeremiah said, taking in his employer's appearance.

Markus chuckled as he pulled himself further up into a sitting position. "Naw, just me."

Jeremiah scoffed. "You look like shit."

"Well, that might be because I feel like shit. How was your trip?"

Jeremiah sighed heavily. It hadn't gone as planned, but then again, when did they ever? He told Markus the whole story about the community in which he and Kurdy were incredibly unwelcome.

Markus was falling asleep.

His eyes would slip shut, and he'd blink blearily and shake his head a bit.

Jeremiah couldn't help but smile. He patted the man's shoulder.

"Get some rest, Markus."

Markus groaned and dragged a hand down his face. "Sorry, I didn't even realize that I was... We'll pick this up later."

Jeremiah laughed. "No problem."

-----

The next time Jeremiah went to visit him, later that day, Markus was bent over the vomit bin he’d seen earlier.

“Just take it easy, Markus.”

“Oh great id- hgh-”

Erin patted his back as he heaved uselessly. Jeremiah got close enough just in time to see a trickle of bile drip from the man’s lips. He cringed, but he couldn’t be too deterred. He’d seen far worse in his lifetime.

Markus and Erin both looked up at the sound of his footsteps.

Markus gave a tired smile and chuckled, before running the back of his hand along his mouth. “What are you doing here?” asked a hoarse, broken voice that’d clearly gone through some abuse since Jeremiah had last spoken to him.

“Oh, just wanted to make sure you were still alive and kickin’,” Jeremiah said, stuffing his hands in his pockets and wandering closer.

Markus sighed, spat, and then swallowed thickly. “Feelin’ great.”

“He can’t keep anything down,” Erin reported.

The look on Markus’ face wasn’t unlike that of a kid who’d just been told on. “Erin’s trying to get me to take the rest of the week off.”

By the way his words slurred when he spoke, Jeremiah didn’t think that sounded like a bad idea. He looked terrible, sounded terrible. He likely hadn’t eaten anything substantial in a long time, but as Jeremiah thought about that further, he was reminded how long people could truly go without eating. Markus would be fine.

“You really think that’s a bad idea?”

Markus groaned. “Not you too. Listen, I- urp-” Erin helped him get back over the bin in time to cough up nothing at all. It seemed that his stomach was already pretty empty.

“What was that?” Jeremiah asked with a smirk. Markus wasn’t amused.

Erin sighed, brushing some damp curls from Markus’ forehead. “I think I’m gonna leave you with Jeremiah for a while. Maybe he can talk some sense into you.” The last part carried some venom, but Markus only chuckled.

“Thank you, Erin.”

Erin scoffed and left the infirmary.

That was when Markus’ facade fell, just a bit. It was as if a wave of exhaustion came upon him the moment that Erin was out the door.

“Nothin’ wrong with taking a day off,” Jeremiah said, finding a cup and filling it with water.

Markus gave a raspy chuckle and sat up straight. “You’re one to talk.” He accepted the cup of water, swishing some around in his mouth before spitting it into the bin. He winced at the contents and looked away, nausea sweeping his face allover again.

“Eh, I guess you’re right. Hey Markus, where you going?” He folded his arms and raised an eyebrow, watching his colleague swing his legs off of the edge of the bed. He wasn’t in as much of a hurry to intervene as he was to watch Markus shakily get on his feet.

“Bin needs to be cleaned,” he dismissed, making his way to the sink.

Jeremiah leaned his head back and swore. Markus hadn’t gotten that far, it took only a few swift steps to stop him. “I think Erin made that my job, buddy.”

It didn’t take as much convincing as Jeremiah expected to get Markus back in bed. He cleaned the been, making sure to look away most of the time to avoid having to use the thing himself.

When he turned around, Markus was watching him.

“Didn’t have to do that,” he muttered. Just like earlier, he looked seconds from falling asleep, or would have if he wasn’t constantly shifting and wiggling around.

“No, I didn’t,” Jeremiah agreed, smirking. “You’re welcome.”

Markus scoffed. “I am capable of taking care of myself.”

“Well, practically everyone alive right now is capable of that,” Jeremiah countered. “Not to mention, you take care of everyone else on this base.”

Markus gave a tired smile. “I try to, anyway.” He suddenly winced, making a grunting sound that was suspiciously like a whine as he pulled himself further up against the pillow.

“What’s wrong?” Jeremiah asked, probably with more annoyance than necessary. It wasn’t as if he spent a lot of time taking care of sick people. “If you’re gonna puke again, you will be the one cleaning it up.”

Markus shook his head. He shifted his legs a bit, and sighed. “It’s nothing.” The raised eyebrow Jeremiah gave him urged him to elaborate. “I just ache... everywhere.” He finished with a frustrated huff.

Jeremiah hummed thoughtfully. “That could be dehydration setting in.”

“Probably,” Markus agreed. “I’ll try some water in a little while.”

His eyes fell closed for a moment, before his brows furrowed in pain and they opened again. The poor guy’s body wasn’t going to let him rest. That became even more clear when Jeremiah saw a shiver wrack his frame. Markus huffed again.

Jeremiah was great at telling people off, scolding them and giving them tough love and unwanted help. He wasn’t quite as good at comfort. He found himself standing awkwardly as Markus pulled a thin blanket over himself.

Maybe he should go.

“Jeremiah...”

“Yeah?”

“Your trip... remember when I said that we’d pick it up later?” He chuckled to himself. “Do you mind finishing it?”

Jeremiah was about to tell him that they’d already briefed Erin and that there was no need. But the cogs in his brain turned just fast enough for him to realize that Markus was asking for a distraction. That was something he could do.

“Yeah, I can do that.”

-----

Markus was in his office the next day, sipping on a cup of tea and looking like death. There was a bed just feet away, but instead he was hunched over his desk.

“I guess Erin didn’t accomplish her mission, then,” Jeremiah said, leaning in the doorway.

Markus looked up, eyes wide at first, before crinkling with a smile. “No she did not. She’s...” he cleared his throat. “... not happy with me today.”

“I can imagine. How’s the tea?”

“Oh,” he breathed out. “Not great.”

Jeremiah hummed.

“Look, I know that you’re probably not happy with me either, but I have people counting on me, Jeremiah. It’s not that I don’t trust everyone else to pick up the slack, it’s just that we can use all the help we can get, and so taking more than one sick day just isn’t-”

“I know.”

Markus blinked, letting out the breath he’d been holding for the rest of his ramble. “What?”

“I know,” he repeated. “You’re a productive guy. You’re getting things done. You’re helping people. I get it.”

Markus gave a smile. “I have to admit, I’m surprised that you’re not on her side.”

“Well,” Jeremiah shrugged. “She’s only taken that stance because she cares about you so much and doesn’t want to see you hurt yourself.”

Markus’ smile widened into one a bit more knowing. “So, what you’re saying is that you’re objective because you don’t care about me.”

Jeremiah smirked. “Yup, sounds about right.”

A chuckle. “Well, thank you, I suppose. For not caring.”

“It’s my pleasure.”
