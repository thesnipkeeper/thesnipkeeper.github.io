Title: Regrets
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David regrets eating that.
Word count: 1k

Regrets

David winces and discretely presses his hand into his belly as if that will solve anything.

It was the food. It was the fucking food. Patrick told him that this would happen. Did he listen? No, because he was hungry, and how bad could that restaurant really be?

But he will literally die before admitting that his stomach is starting to hurt.

He muffles a burp into his fist, shifting uncomfortably. He really doesn't feel good, and if past bouts of food poisoning told him anything (and there were a lot of them), it’ll only get worse from here. But damn it, he’ll hold out for as long as he can maintain his pride and dignity.

So he tries to ignore the full, bloated feeling as he and Patrick start winding down for the evening. He stifles a whimper as a particularly sharp cramp makes itself known, and wraps both arms around his middle. Nothing is relieving the pressure in his gut.

“David?”

David looks up from his computer where he’s been putting in data for the store and definitely not online shopping. Patrick seems to have already said his name before he heard him.

“Hm?” He raises his eyebrows innocently.

“You okay?”

“I’m fine, why?” He straightens his posture, despite the way it just worsens the cramping. God, his stomach hurts so much he wants to cry.

Patrick shrugs. “You looked like you were hurting for a second there. Are you getting a headache?”

That too, David thinks. “Um, yeah. Yes, I am.”

Patrick makes a sympathetic noise, and they eventually go back to their quiet evening in.

Until David’s stomach gurgles. Loudly, in the near-silent living room. If that wasn’t bad enough, he also feels a heaviness in his gut that wasn’t there before.

Patrick laughs. “Was that your stomach?”

David wants to lie, but how does one exactly lie about that? Instead, he just closes his eyes and nods. “Um, you may have been right. About the burger joint in Elmdale.” He prepares himself for a hearty ‘I told you so’ and maybe a few jokes to go along with it.

Patrick’s smile fades. He gives a quiet, sympathetic, “Oh no...”

David can’t even think of hiding the next wave of cramps that hit him. He moans pathetically and doubles over, both arms pressed into his belly. He knows that he’s dangerously close to crying now. It hurts!

“Ow! Fuck...” he whimpers, squeezing his eyes shut. This time the cramps don’t go away on their own. They’re constant now, and the pain just keeps getting worse.

Patrick scoots closer to him on the couch and lays a hand on his back. No physical contact feels good right now. David’s sweating, and his stomach hurts even more.

Fuck. He needs to leave.

He shrugs off Patrick’s hand as gently as possible.

“David, you okay?”

“Yeah, but can you- can you turn on the TV or something?”

Patrick looks confused at first before he nods. “Gotcha.”

-----

It happens a few more times. David hopes that every trip to the bathroom is the end of it, maybe his body finally got rid of everything it needed to, but then he just ends up rushing back there again when his stomach cramps or the nausea gets worse.

Eventually, he decides that he’s just going to live there, now. 

He’s shuddering against the bathroom wall when Patrick knocks.

“Are you still throwing up?”

“I don’t fucking know,” David huffs, the annoyance with his body loud and clear. He's exhausted. It has to be nearing their usual bedtime, and he finds himself getting upset that he might not even be able to sleep tonight.

Patrick chuckles. “Can I come in?”

David makes a non-committal sound and lazily reaches up to turn the doorknob. It drifts open, and Patrick along with it.

Patrick sets a glass of water on the floor next to him, and then runs a washcloth under the faucet. David carefully sips the water as his husband presses the cool cloth against his forehead and neck, making a noise of delight when he realizes how nice it feels.

"I was mostly joking when I said that about the restaurant," Patrick said, running his fingers through David's damp hair. "I mean, it has a reputation, but I didn't actually think…" he has the audacity to giggle.

David glares at him. "Are you enjoying this? How dare you revel in my misery!"

Patrick laughs and presses a kiss to his gross forehead. "No, David, I'm not enjoying this. It's just funny."

"Funny," David scoffs.

"Hey," Patrick's hand glides to the nape of his neck. "Why don't you come to bed?"

David hesitates. "I don't think that's a good idea."

"Hey, if you'd rather spend the night in here, go for it. But I think you'll be a little more comfortable in our nice, warm bed…"

Fuck. "Fine," David croaks. He feels too awful to argue with the person who's just trying to take care of him. And being in bed does sound pretty nice, he thinks.

He curls up immediately, arms around his achy stomach. Where it was hurting because of nausea, his stomach is now just sore from all of the abuse it's been put through in the past few hours.

David forgets about his misery as his husband climbs into bed next to him and offers one of his adorable smiles that reminds David everything is okay.

“Um, needless to say, I’d rather not go to work tomorrow.”

Patrick chuckles. “I kind of figured.” He turns over to shut off the bedside lamp and goes back to smiling at David.

“What?”

“Nothing,” Patrick says innocently. “I just love you. A lot.”

David sighs. “Mm’kay, that’s kind of a weird thing to say after I’ve been in the bathroom the whole night, but I guess I love you a lot as well.”

Despite everything horrible that has happened today, he’s glad that it can end like this. That every day is going to end like this.

Patrick gives a happy, sleepy hum and presses a kiss to his forehead. “Let’s get some sleep.”
