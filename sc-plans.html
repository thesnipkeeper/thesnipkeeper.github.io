Plans

Alexis is sick, and David has somehow taken the responsibility of keeping her alive. With Moira in Bosnia, and Alexis not really wanting Johnny to hover over her, she texted him to come straight home after work.

David hasn't taken care of a sick Alexis since she was eleven and returned from shooting a sitcom with an airplane flu. He wasn't exactly a caretaker back then, either.

He vaguely remembers pacing in front of his bed while his sister keeps coughing herself awake, worrying that he'll have to take her to the hospital because what if she can't sleep? What if her fever goes up?

Thankfully, Adelina arrived in time for him to leave and have a panic attack in peace.

Since then, he's accepted that he's responsible for her. Reluctantly, of course, because she's incapable of staying out of trouble (or knowing when she should go to the doctor, which he pretends doesn't terrify him).

"David," comes the whiny voice from a pile of blankets. "I'm cold."

David sighs. "Mm'kay, but I can't do anything about that."

"What about another blanket?"

"Nope. That will make your fever go up."

Alexis groans and curls up. She shudders pitifully.

As annoying as she's been, David can't help but still feel sympathy for her. She's had a pretty miserable day, and with Ted out for a vet conference, there's no one else she'd want looking after her.

He's cleaned up her puke before, mainly the first time she came home drunk at 14, and he knows that he'll continue to do it again for some crazy reason, he thinks as he glares at the recently-sanitized bucket next to his sisters bed.

David spares another glance at her to make sure she doesn’t need anything else at the moment before flopping down in his own bed and turning the TV on. There's nothing good on, of course, but he needs some sort of noise to combat the silence in the room with his normally-loud sister asleep.

Or so he thought.

"Hey," the lump of blankets moves until Alexis emerges, sitting up and brushing her hair out of her face. "I thought you were going out with Patrick tonight."

David shrugs. "We decided to do it a different day."

"Not 'cause of me though, right David?"

David gives her an uncertain look. "Um, why?"

"I mean, I'll be fine here if you want to- if you want to go out..." She swallows and presses a hand to her mouth, and in a flash the pile of blankets is on the floor and his sister is darting for the bathroom.

He winces and turns the volume up higher. "I just wanted to chill here tonight!" He calls in her direction.

"Yeah but-! Oh, God…"

David rolls his eyes and grabs a book from his nightstand. He tries to tune out the noise as he reads, and then turns the volume down once again when his sister emerges.

Her face is red, he realizes. Her fever probably has gone up.

She groans and gets back in bed, and doesn't cover up quite fast enough before David manages to grab the heap of blankets and throw them on the futon.

"Hey!" Alexis protests.

"Sorry, it's for your own good! Here," he picks up one blanket and gives it to her. "You can have that one."

"Ugh, David." She doesn't argue nearly as much as he expects. Huh. It's almost as if she knows he's right. Still, it's a little sad and pathetic when she curls up with her one blanket and shivers.

David sighs. "Have you taken any fever reducer?"

"You think I haven't tried?! Won't stay down," Alexis grumbles.

"Ah."

She shudders, making a distressed whine that almost makes him feel bad for her.

"David," she whines. "David, I'm cold."

David rolls his eyes. "Okay, well there's nothing I can do about that."

"Can't you just stay here?" Much to David's horror, Alexis pats the bed as if there's any room for him. "C'mon David, please?"

David huffs and rolls his eyes. "Fine, but if you puke on me I'm locking you in the bathroom until you're better."

Alexis doesn't seem to have a problem with that plan, so David climbs onto the other side of her bed. It's a struggle to manage to get both of his legs to fit, but it happens eventually. He tries to keep some distance between them  because even though he agreed to this, he's still thinking about how contagious she probably is.

Alexis quickly closes the distance between them, resting her head against his arm and getting far more comfortable than he is.

"Thanks, David. You're really warm."

"You're actually the one that's warm here because of your elevated body temperature but you're welcome."

David watches TV while his sister falls asleep against his arm, effectively pinning him in place and making it so he can't feel his arm anymore.

Still, Alexis looks more comfortable than she has all evening. She should get as much rest as she can. If David knows anything about stomach bugs, things can seem fine and then fuck you over quickly. If David knows anything about his sister, she'll be just fine.

He could probably still go out with Patrick if he wanted to.

But there's still some annoying older brother part of him that's telling him to stay and make sure she's okay.

Alexis can take care of herself, but she shouldn't have to all the time.

Eventually, she stirs and ends up cuddling a pillow instead of him. He gets feeling in his arm again and he takes the opportunity to pull out his phone.

David yawns. He supposes falling asleep against Alexis' headboard is among the least weird places he's passed out. So he closes his eyes, leans his head back.

Everything's peaceful.

He falls asleep.

-----

It's dark when David wakes up. He doesn't remember turning the lights off (dad must have, and David has a sinking suspicion that he didn't do it without taking a photo first). He groans, post-nap grogginess really getting to him. Well, is it a nap if it's technically bed time? It has to be by now.

David drags a hand down his face and realizes that he has full movement of all of his limbs. He squints in the dark to find that Alexis has curled up away from him and is shivering pretty terribly. He does his best to pull the covers over her shoulders without waking her, and carefully gets up without jostling the bed.

He pees, washes his hands, and looks at himself in the mirror. Good God. He still feels pretty gross from having fallen asleep in such a weird position. It's times like these that David's reminded how old he's getting.

He takes some ibuprofen to ease the headache and he's ready to go back to his own bed when he starts to feel significantly grosser.

David winces, laying a hand on his stomach. At first he thinks it's his imagination. He has a habit of going full hypochondriac when he's around someone sick. But the unsettled gurgle in his stomach tells him that that's not the case this time.

-----

David can't believe his stomach can still have anything in it. Yet, he can hear its contents churning as he tries to get comfortable on the gross tile floor with his crampy belly. David startles at a knock on the door.

"David? Can you piss faster? Think I'm gonna throw up again," comes his sister's urgent plea.

David rolls his eyes and reaches up to turn the doorknob, just enough for the door to drift open itself. Alexis stops and peeks in for a moment. She makes a disgusted noise at him (or at herself, David can't tell) and promptly pukes in the sink.

David can't even bring himself to be grossed out.

Alexis whines, gagging uselessly into the sink and finally spitting the remainder of the gross in her mouth. So much for letting Alexis get some rest, then. She hangs on the rim of the sink for a while before halfheartedly rinsing everything down the drain.

“Heyyy,” David draws out, his voice croaky from throwing up.

Alexis turns to him and groans. “Is this seriously happening?” she doesn’t bother to ask before sinking to the floor next to him.

“Mmm. Like, we might die here.”

Alexis scoffs. She pulls her knees to her chest and shudders, David takes pity on her and does her the favor of not shoving her away when she leans against him.

“You’re just going to sit here all night?”

“Well, I’m definitely not done.”

“Oh okay, well,” she pauses to inspect her nails. “Do you want me to like, stay in here for a while? Or are you about to tell me to fuck off?”

David shifts and whimpers as his stomach cramps. He can’t imagine how he can feel so full when there’s so little left in him. Alexis is still sicker than he is, she needs the rest. He hates that he cares about his little sister so much.

But he also really, really doesn’t want to be alone right now.

David gives a heavy sigh. “You can stay, just don’t get mad at me if I like, vomit on you or something.”

“Gross, okay.”

A few minutes go by, and David’s pretty sure Alexis has fallen asleep against him. Which makes him feel all the more guilty when his stomach chooses then to rebel. He moves out from under her, earning a confused noise from his sister before heaving into the toilet.

He forgot how unpleasant this was.

Things get caught in the back of his throat, triggering his gag reflex allover again until he’s vomited so much he can hardly breath. David moans, realizing that he’s been hanging onto a motel toilet seat for dear life for close to ten minutes now.

“David,” Alexis says quietly. “David, go back to bed.” She says, patting his back to get his attention.

“NNgh. But-”

“You’re done. Nothing else is coming up.”

He shakes his head. His stomach is still churning. He’s not done, and he doesn’t want to leave until he’s sure he’s not going to throw up anymore. David is not about to throw up in anything but the toilet if he can help it, Alexis has to know that.

He doesn’t leave. Neither does Alexis.

“You’re the one who should be in bed,” he argues before spitting into the water and flushing the disgusting contents.

“I’m fine.” No, she’s not. Because David’s been watching her throw up all evening. “I mean, physically I’m not fine, like, obviously,” ah. “But I know that you... you’re...” She doesn’t finish the sentence.

She doesn’t have to.

David knows he’s a little less okay with being sick or in any sort of physically distress. He’s just- he’s not okay with unexpected things going wrong with his body. He’s not okay with not being able to eat or drink without worrying whether it’s going to come back up. He still has memories of flu season back in New York, spending days alone in his apartment, breaking down because he had no reason to keep it together when there was no one around.

“I’ll be fine,” Alexis repeats. She sits with him again for a while. She’s still shivering, but the last thing David feels like doing when he’s sweaty and gross is touching another person. She seems to understand.

They both huff out a sigh.

“Maybe we should get dad,” she says.

“Dad’s going to hover, and try to help, and it’ll be way worse than this.”

Alexis doesn’t bother to argue, instead nodding. “Yeahhh.”

knock knock knock

They’re both suddenly on high alert at the knock at the door.

Who the-

Alexis starts to get up, but he stops her. “Who is it?!” David yells from the bathroom.

They don’t answer. In fact, they have a key. Stevie?

“Hello?”

Patrick.

Fuuuck. David groans inwardly and knocks his head against the bathroom wall.

“Oh,” is the first thing he says when he pokes his head in. “Hey. I guess it’s not just Alexis who’s sick, huh?”

-----

“What are you doing here in the middle of the night?” David asks as Patrick pulls back the covers on his bed and ushers him in. Alexis is already half-asleep in her own.

Patrick raises an eyebrow. “David, it’s eight-o-clock.”

Oh. “Right. Kind of... napped. Lost track of time... are you sure that you want to be here?”

“Hm?” Patrick looks up from tucking him in rather adorably.

“You’re going to get sick,” David says more clearly.

Patrick shrugs. “Chances are you were contagious before you left work today, so...”

“Oh God.”

“David, it’s fine. Hey-” A hand comes up to cup David’s cheek, blissfully cool. Oh, the fever must be coming on. “Want me to stay here? Make sure you two are okay?”

David can’t help a smile. It’s the sweetest offer, but it’s not necessary at all. He and Alexis can manage a random stomach virus. He thinks about it for a moment and shakes his head.

“No, but thank you.”

Patrick nods. “Want me to get your dad?”

“No!” David and now fully-awake Alexis say in unison.

David clears his throat, “That’s really not necessary.”

Patrick chuckles and nods. “Okay, well I guess I’ll head home, then. I love you,” he leans in and presses a kiss to David’s forehead. “Feel better Alexis!”

“Thanks, Patrick,” comes muffled from a pile of blankets.

Patrick leaves with some reluctance and Alexis emerges from her pile with as smug of a grin as she can muster.

“What?” David demands.

She shrugs innocently. “You’ve got to keep him around.”

David smiles. “I think I plan on it.”
