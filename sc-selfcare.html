Self Care

“You’re sure you don’t want anything?”

David shook his head, eyes still trained on the table.

“Not even some toast?”

“Patrick...”

“Sorry,” Patrick said, raising his hands defensively. “My husband hasn’t eaten in two days, I think I’m allowed to be worried.”

David groaned. Patrick was right. He just really, really didn’t want to talk about food right now.

He’d spent the past two days throwing up from some kind of stomach bug. He finally felt like he was on the mend, but that didn’t mean that putting anything solid in his stomach was a good idea. He wasn’t exactly nauseated anymore, but he was unbelievably tired.

“Maybe a slice of dry toast, Twyla?”

“Sure!”

David hadn’t even realized that they were ordering. Whoops. When he looked up, Twyla was giving him the most sympathetic smile. Ugh. He hated when people looked at him like that, and he’d been getting that look from everyone today.

Somehow the entire town knew that he’d been sick.

“Tea?”

David nodded. “Please.”

He sighed heavily and ran a hand through his hair as he sat back. He was tempted to rest his head against the wall and close his eyes, but then he’d definitely fall asleep.

“Aww,” Patrick cooed.

“What?”

“You don’t have to come back to work yet, honey.”

David didn’t want to go back to work yet, but he did own half of the business. And he was tired of lying in bed thinking of all the delicious food that he couldn’t stomach. “I mean...” he sighed. “I’m not really sick anymore,” he said with a shrug, rolling his eyes.

Patrick smiled. “Up to you! But I can drop you off at home if you need me to. Nothing wrong with taking care of yourself.”

He had a point. David had been learning the concept of self care, true self care, since he moved to Schitt’s Creek.

He groaned and leaned on the table, dragging a hand down his face. “I just... ugh I just want to be useful.”

“You’re useful being my motivation to get through work so I can come home.”

“Oh my God,” he’d been grumpy all morning, but he couldn’t help but laugh at the cheesy line. “Okay, if you insist, I guess I can stand to stay home and lie in bed for one more day.”

Patrick grinned, which in turn made David grin just as wide.
