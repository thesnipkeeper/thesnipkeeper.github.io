Title: Dishes
Fandom: Morganville Vampires
Summary: Michael's being a grump and they find out why
Word count: 1.5k

Dishes

The three of them got along relatively well, all things considered. Sure, there was the occasional regarding the only bathroom, or Shane’s new, godawful chili recipe. But they always solved their conflict in the end. It was nice.

Eve would be lying if she said she wasn’t more comfortable having two male roommates. After hearing so many stories on the news about kidnappings and murders and home invasions, she felt safe living with men who were both twice her size.

Even if they were, on occasion, complete morons.

She descended the stairs of the giant house to find Michael and Shane bickering like an old married couple. Shane was in the process of making eggs and bacon while Michael stood next to him, making comments that Shane clearly wasn’t in the mood for.

“What the hell?” Eve muttered, more to herself than to them, as she grabbed a juice from the fridge.

Shane huffed and angrily prodded at the eggs in the pan. “Michael’s being an asshole.”

“Okay, all I did was ask you to do the dishes when you’re done.”

“Did I say that I wasn’t gonna do the dishes?!”

“Well, you didn’t need to if the past is any indication. It’d just be nice if you cleaned up, just once.”

Shane rolled his eyes and gave Eve an exasperated look, gesturing toward their friend with the goopy spatula. “Are you listening to this?”

“Oh my God, you idiots, I’ll do the dishes.”

Michael didn’t like that idea, apparently, because he immediately pushed off the counter and huffed. “No! No, he should be responsible for his own-”

“But he’s cooking for us, right?”

Shane nodded silently without taking his eyes off the pan, raising his eyebrows as if to say ‘he’s your problem now’.

“Then I’ll do the dishes.”

Michael sighed, shaking his head and stalking out of the kitchen.

“What’s with him?” Eve asked as she pulled herself onto the counter. “Did you do something to him?”

Shane scoffed. “As if I’m gonna poke the bear first thing in the morning! I’m not an idi- well, I am, but not that kind of idiot.”

Eve took a sip of her juice and listened to Michael’s video game start up in the other room. Good. Maybe shooting zombies would help him cool down. “Weird.”

“Dude woke up on the wrong side of the bed,” Shane muttered as he plated the bacon. “That’s all I’ve got to say.”

Another thing about their particular group Eve loved, was that no matter how young an stupid they might have been, they some how managed to eat at the table together against all odds. It was something they started doing when she first moved in, and Michael thought it would be nice for them. It was.

Michael hardly ate a thing. She and Shane exchanged looks as he took a half-unfinished plate to the kitchen to rinse it off. Maybe he was being petty about Shane somehow, but that wasn’t usually Michael’s style.

“Why is it so cold in here?”

Shane scoffed. “It’s like, seventy-five in here, dude.”

Michael rolled his eyes. “It is not seventy-five degrees.”

“Uh, yeah it is.”

Michael shivered. Eve knew what she saw. And he was even wearing a hoodie. Shane seemed to notice the same thing a second later.

“Since when do you get cold? You feeling okay?” He said with an obnoxious chuckle. He stood up, his chair scraping against the floor, which had Michael wincing. Eve feared for Shane’s life as he went to Michael and laid a hand on his forehead.

Michael tried to dodge it and failed. He gave a grumpy, resigned sigh around the same time that the smug look on Shane’s face disappeared.

“Oh shit, you’re really hot.” And if Shane wasn’t even going to follow it up with a joke about calling his friend hot, something must have been really wrong.

Instead of denying it, Michael’s brows furrowed as Shane took his hand away. “Am I really?”

“Eve, c’mere.” Shane waved her over.

Michael had to lean down a little bit for her to get to his forehead, but she found that Shane was right. “I’m no doctor,” she said. “But that kind of feels like a fever?”

“No wonder you’ve been such a grump this morning.” Aaaand Shane was back to his annoying quips. “Go back to bed, man.”

“Can’t,” Michael said, almost regrettably. “I have class.” They couldn’t exactly argue with that. School meant a lot to Michael, more than it did to her, if she was being honest.

“Fine, whatever, don’t die.” Shane walked off to do the dishes. And thank God, because Eve wasn’t actually planning on doing them.

-----

Eve was spending her day off enjoying a new CD and catching up on reading. No work, no school... it was nice. She could vaguely hear Shane downstairs violently killing something on TV. The house was noisy, they usually had to be quiet while Michael was studying, so they valued the time when they didn’t have to.

The house was so noisy, in fact, that Eve didn’t realize her phone was ringing until it had apparently already rang a couple of times. She paused the CD as she looked at the name on the front of the phone, and then quickly answered.

“Hey, what’s up?”

“Would you mind picking me up?” Michael didn’t sound like Michael. His voice was rough and low. Hoarse? And not nearly as confident as usual.

Eve immediately started searching for her shoes. “Uh, yeah, sure. You okay? Something wrong?” It was definitely not time for his last class to be over, and he drove himself there.

“I just don’t feel great and I don’t really trust my driving right now. A little dizzy.” He cleared his throat, and then coughed. Eve winced. “I’ll owe you.”

“Totally will. I’m on my way.”

“Thanks, Eve.”

She went downstairs and grabbed her keys. “Michael needs to be picked up,” she informed Shane, who was in an intense game of something shoot-y. “He just called.”

“What happened to his car?”

“Nothing, he just said that he was dizzy and didn’t want to drive.”

That got Shane’s attention enough for him to pull his eyes from the screen. “Damn,” he said worriedly. “I thought he was just getting a cold or something.”

She shrugged. “Dunno. We’ll be back soon.”

-----

She pulled up in front of the school. If she sat there long enough, someone would tell her she couldn’t park there, but thankfully there wasn’t enough time for that to happen. Again.

Michael wandered out, still wearing his hoodie and looking totally exhausted. “Hey. Thanks.” As he got in, she noticed how pale he was, and that his nose had begun to get a little pink like he’d been rubbing it all day. The moment she started to drive, Michael closed his eyes and leaned his head back against the seat.

“Wow, you really aren’t feeling good, huh?” She offered a glance his way a few minutes into the drive. He hadn’t moved.

Michael shook his head minutely. “Naw.”

“Still cold?”

“Yup.”

“Is this like... doctor worthy?”

“Naw.” Michael sat up, but only to muffle a cough into his sleeve before leaning back once more. “Must be the flu or something.”

“Flu in summer, lucky you.”

“Mhm.”

Eve couldn’t believe her eyes as they walked through the door. Shane had made up the entire couch, complete with pillows and blankets from Michael’s room. He presented it with grand hand gestures and led Michael over to it. Eve couldn’t help but wonder if he was feeling guilty for arguing with him that morning.

“Need anything?” Shane asking. “Tylenol, Gatorade, an enema?”

Eve groaned. “Oh my God.”

“I’m just going to sleep,” Michael said, already getting comfortable. “Maybe forever.”

Shane scoffed. “Good luck with that.”

-----

She tried to be extra quiet as she went downstairs for a cup of milk. Turned out that it didn’t matter, because Michael was awake and watching TV. It took him a while to notice she was down there because he was too busy sneezing and blowing his nose.

He finally looked at her over the tissue. “Oh, hey,” came the muffle greeting. He sounded even worse than he had early.

“How you feeling?” She asked from the kitchen. “Less dead?”

“Not... really.” He called back.

Eve appeared in the living room with her glass. “Do you care if I sit with you?”

“Not if you don’t,” he said, shoving a pile of blankets aside to make room for her.

She set the glass on the coffee table and went to feel his forehead. He even leaned over for easier reach. He was still just as hot, and by the dampness of his skin, Eve figured he’d probably been sweating off and on all night.

She drank her milk while Michael gave the occasional cough or sneeze. Eve was about to get up when their ears perked up at a sound from the stairs.

A cough, followed by Shane walking in.

He looked awful even in the dull glow of the TV.

Shane stood there just to glare at Michael “Hate you.”

Michael grimaced apologetically. “Sorry, man.”

“Great, the plague is spreading,” Eve mumbled. “I’ll get my hazmat suit.”
