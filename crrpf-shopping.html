Title: Shopping
Fandom: Critical Role RPF
Summary: Taliesin and Marisha go shopping
Word count: 400

Shopping

Taliesin is the fun person to bring when you want to go shopping. He's helpful and he makes things fun and he encourages you to make poor financial choices in the name of being happy.

Marisha asks him a week before, and he readily agrees, saying that she’s going to regret asking when he ends up keeping her at the store for hours. She just scoffs and says, “ditto.”

When the time comes, however, Taliesin doesn’t look like he’s enjoying himself at all. He makes an effort to laugh or joke every once in a while, but Marisha watches the smiles disappear from his face just as quick as they come. She wonders for a moment if he never really wanted to do this in the first place. Naw, she knows better than that.

Things become abundantly clear when his voice dies in the middle of a conversation. He has to pause and clear his throat, pain evident on his face as he does so.

“You sound kind of rough. Are you feeling okay?”

Taliesin hesitates before finally answering, “I... woke up with a scratchy throat. Hoping it’s nothing.” he shrugs.

Marisha isn’t too sure it is nothing, but if he isn’t worried about it, she’ll try not to be either.

And then he sneezes while they’re looking at shirts. That seems to open the floodgates, because suddenly Taliesin is snuffling and scrubbing at his nose.

“Ugh,” he says. “I think I’m getting sick.”

Marisha chuckles. “That’s a fair assumption. You sound like shit.” She puts away the blouse she was looking at and turns to him. “You wanna go home?”

“Naw, I’m fine.”

“Okay, but tell me if you’re ready to leave?”

“Of course.”

Five sneezes and a soiled sleeve later, Marisha decides it’s time to go. His nose is turning red just from rubbing it and he looks so tired. Yet, he continues to shop like nothing’s wrong. It’s a bout if sniffling that finally gets Marisha to lay a hand on his arm and make him look her in the eyes.

“You’re sick. We’re going home.”

Taliesin scoffs. “It’s a cold.” his voice sounds thick, like his head has already filled with gunk.

And yeah, Marisha gets it. A cold isn’t a reason to drop everything and go to bed. Especially when you work nonstop and have countless responsibilities.

“Yeah, but we can do this any time,” she decides. “So we’re going home.”

Taliesin opens his mouth to argue, and then closes it, sniffles, and nods.
