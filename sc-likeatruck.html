Like a Truck

The two of them had a lot of obvious differences, but this one was a little more subtle.

When David got sick, it happened slowly. His facade would fade over a day or two until he completely mellowed out and it became obvious to everyone that he wasn’t feeling good.

Patrick, on the other hand, suffered differently.

Illness hit him like a truck.

Every fucking time.

He’d be fine one second, and miserable the next. He remembered going to school as a kid, feeling just fine, and then being sent home with the flu a few hours later.

Or when he and David picked up chinese from the new place in Elmdale. David complained of a stomach ache the rest of the night until he finally started throwing up. David swore that something was wrong with the food (grumpily, as he was bent over the toilet), but Patrick argued that he ate the exact same thing and he felt fine!

And then, of course, he woke up in the middle of the night with the worst stomach ache of his life and proceeded to spend the next eighteen hours unable to leave the bathroom, either shitting or puking, while David made sure to inform him that he was right and Patrick was wrong.

Some would argue that he just didn’t pay attention to his body’s signals. That was a definite possibility.

All Patrick really knew was that he was fine when he and David went to bed last night and now he couldn’t even lift his head off of the pillow.

“I hate being sick,” he lamented as he watched his husband pull some things out of the medicine cabinet in their en suite. His voice was hardly there, not so much because his throat was scratchy and more because he was just too tired to talk any louder.

“Well, I don’t think anyone loves being sick,” David replied fondly. He placed some things on the nightstand, making sure they were within Patrick’s reach, and sat on the edge of the bed.

It was a side of David Rose that few would ever witness. The nurturing, caring side that wanted to help fix someone when they weren’t at their best. He was strangely good at taking care of sick people.

Patrick coughed, only half-bothering to cover it. And instead of scolding him, David just smiled, leaned over, and pressed possibly the softest, warmest kiss on his forehead. Patrick could feel his hand cradling the back of his head, rings an alarming cold against his feverish, oversensitive skin. Despite how awful he felt and how grumpy he probably seemed, Patrick couldn’t help but smile.

“Are you going to be okay by yourself?” David asked, running his fingers through Patrick’s hair (he hadn’t admitted it, but Patrick knew he was enjoying the curls as David couldn’t stop touching his hair since he decided to grow it out).

Patrick nodded against the pillow. “I’ll be fine. Think I’m just gonna sleep all day.”

David smiled, but almost looked disappointed to not be needed today. “Okay, well... I guess I’m gonna go, then. There’s medicine next to you, and water. I can bring something from the cafe for lunch.” He looked Patrick over once more and sighed heavily. “I don’t want to leave you. You look pathetic.”

Patrick scoffed. “Thanks, David. That’s very nice of you to s-” he coughed, only just managing to twist away to avoid coughing on his husband.

The concerned look on David’s face only intensified. “I love you, so much. I’m also going to have to go to the store and sanitize everything that you’ve touched in the past day so we don’t start some kind of outbreak in the town.”

“That’s fair. I love you too.”

David kissed his forehead, and then his temple, and then his cheek. “Just... be careful, okay? And take care of yourself, don’t forget to hydrate, and-”

Patrick laid a hand on his to stop him before he started radiating anxiety. “David.”

David took a deep breath and nodded. “Right. I love you, I’ll bring lunch, um...”

“Go on, David,” Patrick chuckled.

“I’m going! Goodbye!”

-----

Patrick woke to the sound of the front door opening. He checked the time, definitely not five yet, not even close. The jingling that he recognized specifically as his husband’s keys hitting the bowl next to the door.

Brows furrowed, he pulled himself up with as much strength he could muster, curiosity getting the best of him. He’d just managed to get his legs over the edge of the bed when David came in.

“What are you doing?!” David gently pushed him back, but not before setting Patrick’s pillows up so he could sit against them.

“Just wanted to see what you were doing home.”

David gestured to two takeout containers on the end of the bed. “Lunch, obviously. But um, I may have also closed the store?”

Patrick gasped, but only ended up coughing again. The bottle of water was pressed into his hand before David started getting their food together.

“I know what you’re going to say, and just so you know, we only had like two customers since I opened because it’s so fucking cold out, so... I’m here. And I’m not leaving. And you’re very sick.”

Patrick opened his mouth to argue that he really was fine, but David handed him his food and a fork and slid into bed next to him. He didn’t realize how much he missed his husband’s presence until he was leaning against him and scarfing down a chicken salad.

“Thank you, David,” he said softly.

“What,” David said, mouth still full. “You’re not going to lecture me?”

“Naw.” Patrick laid his head on his shoulder, food forgotten in his lap. He wasn’t hungry at all, but he was happy to have David. Even though they were both missing an entire day of work. “I’m glad you’re here.”

David made a concerned noise in the back of his throat and set his fork down to press the back of his hand to Patrick’s forehead. “I think your temp is higher.”

“Mmm,” Patrick hummed. No wonder he felt all... sentimental and stuff. “Can we worry about that later?”

“Yes, I think we can.” David kissed his temple and went back to his food. “Are you going to eat?”

“Probably not...”

“Okay, then I will put this in the fridge for later.” David didn’t seem very surprised. He boxed Patrick’s food back up after he was done with his own. “I say that we spend the rest of the day watching movies and being lazy.”

“Oh my God, please.”

David chuckled. He went downstairs to put the food away, and left Patrick to think about how much he loved his husband.
