Bouts

David was admittedly not in the best of moods. He snapped at Patrick a little too harshly minutes after he arrived to work, and then proceeded to fuck up his inventory count and have to redo it.

Part of this foul mood was just the fact he hadn’t eaten breakfast. He wasn’t really hungry at all, but it was part of his routine and everything felt wrong without having done it.

Another reason, however, was that his stomach didn’t feel good. He wasn’t sick--it wasn’t that kind of issue--but he felt so full and bloated and after spending nearly ten minutes this morning trying to-

Ugh. He was not in the highest spirits. And he couldn’t even explain to Patrick why, because that was humiliating and for fuck’s sake, this was a workplace!

David winced, a sharp pain making itself known in his stomach as he knelt down to count the items on the lowest shelves. He couldn’t even do anything about it, nothing would help. He just had to put up with it all day, and hope that things would sort themselves out by the time he got back to the motel.

Alexis was used to it at this point, he didn’t have to hide it around her. He couldn’t if he wanted to, actually, because they fucking shared a room.

Patrick was annoyed with him, his stomach felt gross, inventory was fucking him over, David didn’t think his day could get any worse.

And then his mother walked in.

“Hello Patrick, David,” she said, nodding at each of them before removing her sunglasses. “I’ve come to deliver some special news.”

“Oh, I’m sure you have,” David muttered. He winced as he stood up, the pain in his stomach spiking with movement. David leaned against a nearby table. “Well, what is it? What’s your news, mother?” his annoyance flared as Moira casually as ever took a can of lip balm and slipped it into her purse.

“Mrs. Rose, do you want to pay for th-?”

“I’m here to invite the two of you to a barbecue we’re throwing this evening. Your father and I thought it would be nice to have some, quality family time,” she announced proudly.

Though barbecue was usually one of his favorite things to hear, David cringed at the thought of putting heavy foods into his body right now. “No thank you,” he said with a cheerless smile.

Patrick gave him a confused, slightly annoyed look, but David didn’t relent. He didn’t feel like hanging out with his family tonight, and it was even better if didn’t join them and he just had the motel to himself for a little while...

“David, that actually sounds like it could be fun,” Patrick said, in that tone he used when he was trying so hard to convince him of something.

“Mhmm, then you can go. I’m not feeling that... kind of thing tonight.”

Moira looked between the two of them, watching the way they were now communicating with various annoyed expressions before she piped in. “Oh dear. If I didn’t know better, I’d suspect a little trouble in paradise?”

Patrick chuckled and cleared his throat. “I think David’s just having a bit of a day.”

David very pointedly dropped his clipboard on the table and gave Patrick a ‘seriously?’ look. But honestly, it wasn’t Patrick’s fault. For all he knew, David just didn’t get a lot of sleep last night, or his favorite artist released a bad album.

“Okay, I’m doing fine, thanks.” David hissed.

But his mom was looking at him in the way that told him she could see straight into his soul.

“David, what’s wrong? When are you the type to pass on any sort of free meal?”

“I just... don’t feel like going to a barbecue with all of you crazy people tonight, okay?” he said, waving a hand toward his mother. She stepped closer, still scrutinizing him.

"Oh, David,” What was she-? “are you having one of your bouts again?"

"Bouts?" Patrick questioned.

David glared wide-eyed at her. "Hey, mom, can you not be here right now please? Thank you so much."

Moira frowned in sympathy, or whatever Moira Rose’s version of sympathy was. “There’s nothing to be ashamed of. I’m sure Patrick can buy you some medicines to remedy the whole thing before tonight, isn’t that right, Patrick?”

Patrick wore a confused smile. “I’m sorry, what? Medicine?” He looked to David for answers.

David just rolled his eyes back and groaned at the ceiling.

“I don’t want to talk about this right now.”

“David, I’ve told you countless times that your poor diet was going to continue to catch up to you-”

“Mother-”

“And now you can’t even spend time with your family and your precious beau because you’re-”

“Nope!” David threw his hands in the air. “I’m not doing this. I’m leaving, and if I don’t come back,” he pointed a finger at his mother. “It’ll be your fault.”

Moira showed little remorse, and David stormed out wishing that his stomach would decide what it was doing.

He huffed, walking down the street, burping into his fist. Maybe it was good that he left so Patrick didn’t have to witness that, then.

He knew that his mother was right. He had a habit of eating a lot. A habit of eating a lot at once. And fast. But that was no reason for her to come out and say it in front of the person he was going to be sharing a life with within the next year. He dreaded the thought of Patrick finally finding out about David’s various stomach issues, because it was going to come up eventually and God he didn’t plan for it to be this soon. Or within this decade.

-----

Patrick smiled more sweetly than he had all day when David walked through the door. His mother had thankfully left, but he didn’t like the look his fiancé was giving him one bit.

“What did she tell you?” David demanded. He should have known better than to leave those two alone.

Patrick shrugged innocently. “She just mentioned that you sometimes have... intestinal difficulties?”

“Oh my God. I have to leave the country. Can you run the store by yourself from now on?”

“David, it’s fine.”

“No, It’s not fine, Patrick.”

“We’re getting married, I was going to find out sooner or later, right?” He moved out from behind the counter and took David’s hands in his.

David was still feeling rather tense, but he allowed the contact simply because the look Patrick was giving him was ridiculously soft for the conversation they were having.

“So um, you’re not feeling great? Is that why you been cranky all day?”

David sighed. “Maybe.”

“Okay, well that can be fixed, right?”

“Um, yeah. Obviously.”

“David...” Patrick cleared his throat. “Do you want me to run out and get you some stuff?”

“No.”

Patrick sighed and nodded. “Alright.”

And David spent the rest of the work day with unrelenting gas pains in his gut. Which he was going to do anyway, he told himself, so it didn’t matter. Patrick had no business buying him constipation medicine.

-----

When the barbecue came around David found himself sitting between Patrick and Alexis as he nibbled on one of the cookies Ted had brought.

He was hungry, but his stomach was still gurgling, and he realized that the bext hour wait about to be very unpleasant. Patrick and Alexis made a point to continue their conversation about ideas for the store as David excused himself.

He went to the bathroom, and

Nothing.

Of course. It wasn't going to be that easy, right? To just end all of it and relieve his pain?

David sighed.

Patrick gave him a look as he returned to the table, and David just shook his head.

-----

"Want to stay at my place tonight?" Patrick asked as he helped David clean off the table.

David looked up rather alarmed. "You really think that's a good idea?"

Patrick cleared his throat. "Hey, since I learned this very personal thing about tyouou today," David winced. "Would it help if I told you something embarrassing?"

David raised an eyebrow and set his stack of paper plates back onto the table. "Go on."

"Well, I- maybe it's better if I just show you. Come over tonight?"

"Maybe."

-----

David did not feel particularly like cuddling, but he still leaned against Patrick as he got into bed.

"Still not feeling good, huh?"

"If you asking me if I've um... the answer is no." David hissed. It was further evidenced when his stomach gurgled angrily. "But please, I'm very eager to hear about this incredibly embarrassing thing."

Patrick chuckled, and proceeded to show David the mouth gaurd and "nose thing" he'd been dreading to tell David about.
