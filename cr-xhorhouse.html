Title: Xhorhouse
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Caduceus isn't feeling good but his pals have his back

Xhorhouse

The 'Xhorhouse' is bustling with activity as everyone gets up and moving that morning. Some are making plans to shop while others are training and practicing their skills for the next job they're given. Voices and movement fill the interior while everyone grabs a breakfast of fruit or bread and goes about their own things, weaving through each other to get where they need to be.

Jester sits amid the chaos and cheerfully nibbles on a cinnamon-y pastry (one of many she'd bought the day before)

"Has anyone seen my bracers?!" Beau calls out with a mouthful of bread.

Caleb, who is full dressed and sipping on some crudely-made Xhorhasian coffee, mumbles a, "Have you checked under your bed where most of your things end up?"

Beau lets out a dramatic sigh. "Caleb! Seriously, I-" she stops. "Wait, that's actually a good idea." before stuffing the rest of her breakfast in her mouth and running off.

The little taste of domesticity has seemed to be good for them. Having a real home to come back to after everything is awful, somewhere they can come down from the excitement for a little while before someone in the town commissions them for another job, it's probably one of the best times their group has had.

Just yesterday they'd gotten back from the retrieval of some priceless artifacts. A simple fight with thieves had turned near-deadly when one of them turned out to have some magical abilities. What was supposed to be a quick job for a little extra coin had turned into the worst day they've had in week.

But, they were able to return, relax, and take care of each other in their own home.

Jester takes another bite of her pastry before her eyes catch onto Caleb's drink across the table. "Hey Caleb, can I dip my last bite in your coffee? Please? It'll taste super good."

"My coffee or your breakfast?"

"Both, probably."

Caleb looks down at his mug, and gives a half-hearted nod. "Ja, okay," he says as he pushes it across the table. Jester gets a quick dip and it tastes just as amazing as she'd expected.

She can tell by the look on his face when he takes another sip that it's improved the flavor of the coffee as well, and Jester smiles. It's easy to tell that Caleb's not completely recovered from the day before. He looks tired and a little out of it, but that's to be expected. She's surprised he even came out of his bedroom this morning; maybe he actually wanted to be around everyone for once. An improvement!

So, Jester finishes her breakfast and heads to the common area to see if everyone's still around. Nott and Yeza have already disappeared into their lab, and Beau is probably started training. Others are taking a well-deserved lazy day. She greets those lounging around as she tries to decide what she's doing today. She can ask around and see if someone wants to go out with her for the day.

Something dawns on her.

"Has anyone seen Caduceus?"

Caleb shakes his head, Fjord shrugs. "Not since we got back last night. He's probably just sleeping in."

"Caduceus doesn’t sleep in," Caleb says it before Jester has a chance.

There's a few early-risers in their group, but Caduceus is definitely the… earliest riser. He enjoys being up and ready before the others, and sometimes making extravagant breakfasts when he's feeling particularly fancy. Jester loves to join him in the kitchen when he's cooking. There's a sort of solitude there that she doesn’t realize she enjoys until she's around her cleric friend.

"Maybe he's working in the garden," Jester thinks aloud, scanning the room around her as if he's going to appear. She ends up wandering to the stairs before she gets any response from Caleb and Fjord.

The garden's warm lights remind her how much she loves it up here. The tree forms a ceiling of leaves over the top of the tower, creating one of the coziest spaces in their house.

"Caduceus?" No sign of the firbolg working on anything.

The garden is silent save for the sounds of wildlife and… snoring? She immediately turns to his little sleeping area, a shelter made from stone with a mattress shoved inside. Jester kneels down and peeks in, and sure enough, Caduceus is still fast asleep. Fjord was right. The more civilized part of her brain tells her that it'd be rude to wake him, but she easily ignores that part and crawls in.

"Caduceus, wake up! it's morning!"

He wakes with an undignified snort. "Hm? Oh, oh my…" he rubs at his eyes and yawns. "Is it late?"

"Not very late, no. But everyone else is up, so I thought you might want to be too," and maybe she also wanted a possible partner in crime for whatever shenanigans could happen today. "You slept a lot!"

"Mm," Caduceus agrees, blinked owlishly as he continues to fully wake up. "I was pretty beat last night. All of those spells took a lot out of me, I think." There's a hint of thickness in his voice that makes her wonder if it's congestion or just the result of waking up.

Jester winces. "You don’t sound very good. Are you feeling okay?"

Caduceus' brows furrow as he takes time to process the question. He sits up, running a hand down his face with a thick sniff. "I'm not sure? I feel…" a pause. He sniffs again, experimentally this time, "…stuffed."

Jester frowns and brushes some hair away from his forehead to feel his temperature. Caduceus has generally always been a big, warm body to snuggle against, but this is too hot, even for him.

"You're really warm," she informs sadly. She's about to pull her hand back when she feels him leaning into it. She must feel rather cool compared to him right now. "Caduceus," Jester says, lowering her hand to cup his cheek. His eyes close just for a second at the soft touch. "I think you're sick."

Caduceus sighs as if he's just been told that his favorite teacup has been broken. "Well, this wasn’t exactly how I wanted to spend our day o-" he breaks away from her with a fast inhale and a deep-sounding, chesty cough. He exhales, and Jester's pretty sure it's the first time she's heard him honest-to-gods irritated.

 Caduceus worked hard to keep everyone in fighting condition yesterday. Now that she thinks about it, he did seem pretty tired last night. The exhaustion has probably gotten to him.

"We have plenty of days off to do things! This is just the first!"

He smiles, sleepier than usual. "Yeah." Caduceus yawns and sinks back into his bed, getting comfortable once again. Jester thinks she sees a shiver or two, but she isn't sure. "I think I'm going to sleep a little longer."

Jester nods slowly. "Okay… sorry for waking you up."

"It's no problem. I appreciate it, actually. I'm just tired," he says as he gets comfy and hugs his pillow.

He's snoring again before she even gets a chance to leave his little sleeping area.

She ends up trying to teach Nugget some new tricks without much success, but it's alright because he's adorable. Caleb watches her over the top of his book, offering pointers and putting a defensive hand on Frumpkin when Nugget gets a little too close.

She's about to tell Nugget to shake her hand for the twentieth time when they hear the creaking stairs of the tower.

A sleepy, disheveled firbolg emerges seconds later. He looks just as tired as he did when Jester talked to him, possibly even more so.

"Caduceus! You're awake!"

"Mr. Clay," Caleb greets with a glance up from his book. "Jester said you were coming down with something. Are you feeling better?"

Caduceus sniffles thickly, which seems to go to his throat and light the match for a painful coughing fit. He winces, a hand going to rub at his throat.

He sounds ten times worse. He looks ten times worse.

"Oh... Well, I think I'm just going to have to let this run its course. I'm feeling pretty unwell, to be honest." He punctuates the sentence with a quick sniff.

"Did you have a nice nap, at least?" Jester asks as she kneels down to pet Nugget.

A thoughtful sound. "I've had nicer naps. It's- it's hard to breathe, you know?"

She frowns. "You didn't get much sleep, huh?"

Caduceus makes a confirming noise. And he turns back into the kitchen. She can hear clanking as he gets his tea kettle and the creaking of the water pump as he fills it. Between each noise of movement is a sniffle or a cough.

Caleb closes his book without a word and makes his way into the kitchen as well.

"Mr. Clay, why don't you let me do that, hm?"

"Oh, it's no trouble. I'm not that bad off."

"Even so, wouldn't it do your body good to have as little physical activity as possible?"

Caleb is smart, and sweet, and very persuasive when he wants to be. Jester wonders if he's being nice because he wants to be or because of some weird guilt complex where he feels like he owes something to the group. Either way, it's a sweet gesture.

"Sure," Caduceus agrees. Jester wanders in, Nugget following behind, just as Caduceus is pulling up a chair and sitting down. He props his cheek on his palm and sniffs, mouth still open. Looks like he was being honest when he said it was hard to breathe. The clacking of Nugget's claws seems to clue him in when they're near. He offers a smile Jester's way. "Have I missed anything exciting?"

"Literally nothing."

"Ja, it's been pretty quiet here."

"That's nice, isn't it? Everyone gets a chance to rest their bones." Another sniffle.

He makes an alarmed noise, like a cat when you touch them without warning, when Jester feels his forehead. He relaxes once he realizes what's happening.

"Yeah," he says, reading her expression. "Fever's still there, but it's not-" he turns away and muffles a hacking cough into the bend of his arm. "It's not..." He clears his throat with a pained wince. "It's not bad."

"Just enough to make you feel like shit then?" Caleb asks as he sets a cup of tea in front of their friend.

Caduceus nods. "Pretty much, yeah." He takes a sip of the tea with a look of bliss. It's the first time today Jester's seen him swallow without being in pain. Brows furrow a bit and he smacks his tongue. "It looks like tea, it tastes like- like..."

"Hot water." Caleb finishes.

"Mm. Sorry, Mr. Caleb."

"I didn't expect you to be able to taste much. Don't worry."

Caduceus' sniffling gets more frequent as he sips the tea, and Jester can see a faint wetness at the base of his nostrils.

"I suppose I should go back upstairs after I'm done. I don't want anyone getting sick because of me."

Jester and Caleb exchange a look. It's a different look.

"You don't have to go, Caduceus! Won't you get lonely?"

He sniffs. "No one should suffer just because I didn't feel like being alone." Another sniff. "Oh, snf, I think I need to take care of...um…" His sentence fades off, apparently not feeling the need to finish it. He pulls a green kerchief from his left sleeve. He massages his nose in the fabric, scrunching it at the bridge quite adorably.

He doesn't sound any less stuffed up when he's done.

Jester rubs his back as he drinks his tea, and Caleb has found a seat at the table. Jester comes to the realization that it's that solitude that she loves, right here, with a sniffly firbolg and an awkward wizard.

-----

She knows that his sinuses have been impossibly stuffed. She's tried to help, of course, but nothing helps. Everyone can sense the frustration and misery from their favorite firbolg, even when he's being just as pleasant as ever. She can tell he's tired of not being able to breathe.

And then the headaches came along. He doesn’t say anything at first. She can see a quick wince at every sniffle. He's suddenly blowing his nose a lot less which in turn makes it harder for him to breathe. By bedtime, he's sitting upright in his bed, having propped himself up with blankets.

He looks miserable. His mouth open as he tries to get enough air, brows furrowed at the headache he has yet to admit, and eyes watery and puffy from congestion. He offers a tired smile, "Thank you, miss Jester. I'm sure I'll be alright after a good night's rest."

By the looks of him in the morning, that night's rest didn’t come.

"Oh, Cad…" Beau says, wincing as he finds his way into the kitchen.

Caduceus gives a congested sniff and a sleepier-than-usual smile. "Hey. Morning, Beau."

"You look like shit, buddy. What are you doing out of bed?"

He waves a hand dismissively. "A cold doesn’t require me to be bedridden, does it? There are things I want to do today."

Fjord frowns. "I'm sure those things can wait 'til tomorrow, right? I mean, not that we don’t appreciate your help, but no one wants you to work yourself sicker."

"That's alright. I'm not much for staying in… oh…" Everyone stands up straight on high alert when Caduceus sways dangerously to the side. It's Fjord who gets to him first, grabbing his arm and steadying him.

"Whoa, take it easy, big guy."

Caduceus blinks owlishly as he's lowered into a chair. "Jeez, sorry. I think my balance is a little off."

Fjord's brows knit together in concern. He presses the back of his hand to Caduceus' forehead and frowns deeply. "Okay, so," He looks up to the others and gestures his hand toward Caduceus, who's now massaging his head and face with a pained wince. "He's burning up. Maybe we should find a physician or something."

"Shit, I don’t know about that…" Beau says.

Caleb shakes his head. "Would anyone around here even have training in firbolg physiology?"

Fjord shrugs. "I don’t know! Shouldn’t we do someth-"

Caduceus shakes his head, eyes still squeezed shut as he kneads at his forehead. "The fever is normal," he mumbles.

"For firbolgs?"

"For- for me. I've ah, always run a little hot when I'm not feeling my best. It's not something to get worked up about," he clears his throat, opens his eyes, almost looking ashamed to admit that he's prone to fevers. It makes Jester want to watch him more closely. "And I'm not very comfortable going to a doctor. So, I'll just stay here and hope it'll run its course."

He ends up on their sofa. It's the longest piece of furniture they have, and still not quite long enough for him to fully stretch out. He doesn’t need to, anyway, because he stays propped up against the arm.

"You look like you haven't slept all night," Beau observes.

Caduceus hesitates and nods. "I was tossing and turning a bit, yeah."

-----

Jester runs her fingers through his hair as she hums a random tune. Caduceus' head is a warm weight in her lap, comforting and familiar. She doesn’t usually enjoy sitting still this long, but she can handle being still if it's what Caduceus needs to finally get some rest.

The quiet snoring pauses. The firbolg stirs, turning over and nuzzling her dress. Snoring continues.

Beau wanders out of her training room and chuckles a little at the sight. "Wow, he's really out."

"Shh! Don’t wake him!" Jester whispers loudly.

"Shit, sorry," she blows some hair out of her face and kneels down near his feet. "Did he sleep last night?"

"Not at all."

Caduceus' mouth hangs open as he's still unable to breathe through his nose. His nostrils are red and chafed, evidence of how much he's had to wipe his nose. Jester pets his head and sighs.

"He isn't getting better," she says quietly.

"He'll get there."

"I hope so."

Caduceus wakes with a cough. He rushes to sit up and cover his mouth. Jester rubs his back. The cough not only sounds painful now, but chesty and irritated.

He snuffles as the fit ends, and lets Jester guide his head back to her lap.

"Morning big guy," Beau greets. "You sleep good?"

Caduceus smiles. "Yeah, naps are great."

-----

Days pass and Caduceus spends most of them being headachy and stuffed.

Jester spends the night with him once when his fever is in the process of breaking and giving him the most trouble. She brings extra pillows and blankets and lets him cuddle with her until the worst of the shivers pass.

He wakes up damp in sweat but no longer achy and cold, and feeling a little more like a person again.

It's a relief to all of them that they don’t have to go searching for a firbolg-specializing doctor, and a relief to Caduceus that he can breathe through his nose.

"Are you feeling better?" Caleb asks at breakfast.

"Much better. I can think clearly again."

They eat breakfast together, and he's able to taste it for once.
