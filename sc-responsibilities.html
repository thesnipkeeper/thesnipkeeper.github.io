Title: Responsibilities
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Alexis has to be the responsible sibling and she is not a fan
Word count: 3.4k

Responsibilities

“I swear to God, if you fuck with my stuff, I’m disowning you,” the threat would be a lot scarier (or a lot more entertaining) if he doesn’t start hacking up a lung immediately after.

Alexis scoffs in exasperation. She has to wait for him to finish coughing before she can give a clever response. “Okay, David, I agreed to helping you so maybe don’t act like a bitch today.”

“Yes,” he says, clearing his throat. “But you’re under my orders.”

She rolls her eyes and steps away from the decoration she intended on improving, but if David wants the Jazzagals stage to look horrible, then fine.

“I don’t see how mom couldn’t have done this.”

David sighs, and then starts coughing again. “She said something about-” cough “-resting her vocal chords-” cough “And her body’s vocal chords. Whatever that means.”

It sounds like David’s the one who needs to resting his vocal chords, but Alexis keeps that to herself. If she acknowledges his cough one more time they’re actually going to fight. Thank goodness they’re the only two in the school auditorium because no one else could handle David in Decorating Mode. (Alexis has flashbacks of her tenth birthday party and the first time she witnessed Decorating David. Obnoxious. But the party was awesome, so). Well, there’s Patrick. But Patrick’s at some business conference, and isn’t supposed to come back into town until later that night.

David moves off to the stage steps and sits down to take some gulps from his water bottle, as if that would cure whatever gross situation he’s got going on.

He looks tired. He looks sick. He doesn’t complain one bit. It’s like he just wants to get this done as soon as possible so he’s able to complain without making it go longer.

“What do you want me to do with these lights?”

“Hang them on the-” cough “On the tree in front.” David stands up again and heads up the stage to help her.

Alexis has to admit, the stage looks amazing. She and David, when not at each other’s throats, are a powerful duo. The Christmas set is beautiful, complete with a tree, fake snow (“not too much, just enough to be tasteful”), and red and green all around.

David had a vision, and he may have succeeded.

She takes one end of the lights and David takes another. They maneuver through the various branches and position the lights evenly around the tree, but when it comes to the rest of Alexis’ end,

“Ugh, I can’t reach the top, David.”

David nods and immediately pulls one of the folding chairs on the stage over. “Here, let me...” Alexis lets him take the lights from her (she didn’t want to do it anyway) and watches him climb onto the chair. “Just need to- yep, like that, and-” He pulls up an arm to cough into, lights still in-hand.

“Do you want me to help?”

“No, I-” cough “I’ve got-” cough “-it.”

“David-”

“Alexis, just let me-” He’s still coughing as he tries to wrap the lights around the top of the tree. Alexis hovers below him, as if she’d actually be able to catch him if he falls.

This time, though, the coughing doesn’t stop after a while. This time it keeps going until he’s wheezing and out of breath, and still can’t stop. Alexis hears the last of his air leave him with another cough.

Suddenly, he’s stumbling off of the chair.

Alexis grabs his arm just in time to avoid his head and the rest of his upper body knocking into the hardwood stage as he hits the ground.

And he’s still coughing.

“Dav-? Oh my God...” Alexis rushes off and grabs the bottle of water he’s been nursing since they got there, quickly shoving it in his hands and kneeling next to him as he takes a drink.

They wait an agonizing few minutes for the fit to taper off, and by then Alexis realizes how gray he looks. This isn’t a cold, is it?

She frowns, watching her brother regain his breath and take sips of water with a truly miserable expression, and takes advantage to lay a hand on his forehead. David doesn’t even look at her.

“David!”

“Huh?”

“David, you have like, a really high fever.”

David looks at her as if she’s talking nonsense. “What? No, no that’s not right. I would know that.”

She scoffs. “Seriously, David. I think you might need a doctor.”

“What?!” That gets his attention. “Really?!”

“Yes, really!”

The two make the same exact noise of disgust as they step outside and find the parking lot covered in a thin blanket of fresh snow. David muffles a cough into his jacket sleeve.

“Oh my God. Nope. Just take me to the motel.”

“Come on.”

“Do you know how many people die from accidents in bad weather, Alexis?”

He continues talking about statistics as the two of them get into the car, but Alexis stopped paying attention to his nerd stuff years ago.

She starts the car, and sighs.

“Seriously, I’m not going to force you to go,” cough. coughcough. She continues, “but you freaked me out and David, you’re skin is super warm and I’m scared for you and Patrick isn’t here to be all sensible and know stuff, so-”

David sighs, nods reluctantly. It’s a true testament to how he’s feeling, how worried he is for his own health that he agrees, “Fine. Let’s go.” cough. sniffle. “But drive slow.”

Alexis does, mostly because David continually nags her. At some point, though, the nagging stops. And a soft, wheezing snore starts.

Alexis spares glance at her brother to find him fast asleep against the window, mouth open. She shakes her head. He definitely should have said no to their mother, or done something besides work when he’s obviously too sick.

It’s ten minutes before they finally show up to Elmdale Hospital. She shakes him awake, and he starts with a nasty cough. Somehow, he looks worse. Somehow, he feels warmer.

“C’mon, David,” she says a little softer.

David coughs again. “I changed me mind,” he says hoarsely. “Let’s go home.”

Alexis frowns. “Why though?”

“Well, what if that they tell me I’m dying or something?” even when miserable, he still manages to be dramatic.

“Then we’ll know and we can get your funeral arranged the way you want it before you go,” Alexis jokes.

But David looks terrified.

“Oh my God, it’ll be fine. Come on.”

They wander into an eerily-empty emergency room and talk to a nurse who’s far too cheery to be working there.

David’s in the middle of another coughing fit when they walk up to the counter, and she gives him a sympathetic look before handing the forms to Alexis.

She rubs his back uselessly as they sit down.

They manage to fill out the form pretty quickly, and Alexis hands it back to the woman at the front desk.

“Your brother?” She asks with a smile.

“Uh, yeah, how’d you know?”

“Well, I heard the way the two of you were talking to each other. I kind of figured that you weren’t a couple when you called him an asshole.”

Alexis chuckles. “Well, he was kind of being one. How am I supposed to know everything on this sheet?”

A hoarse, weak “I can hear you,” comes from the waiting area.

The woman smiles once more. “Someone will be out for you shortly. I hope you two have a good night.”

Alexis winces as a harsh, wet cough comes from behind her. “I don’t know about that, but thank you.”

All he can do is cough, and shiver, and Alexis doesn’t even complain when he lays his head on her shoulder even though ew, David, you’re gross.

Bzzzt

Alexis sees Patrick’s name on her phone screen and quickly unlocks it, managing without jostling her sick brother.

Patrick: About to head home. How’s the stage setting going?

Fuck, she forgot to text Patrick.

She starts to type out the whole story and how she and David ended up where they were, but gets a better idea.

Patrick receives a picture of David asleep and drooling on her coat, and the simple words: He has a bad cough, in the ER rn.

Patrick: What?? A cough? As in pneumonia??

Alexis: Don’t know yet :/ waiting to see a doctor.

Patrick: Fuck. He wasn’t feeling good when I left but I thought he was just getting a cold.

Alexis: Me too.

A nurse looks directly at them expectantly, and Alexis nudges her brother off of her shoulder.

“David?”

-----

“Pneumonia?!” David exclaims. And then starts to cough again.

The doctor just nods calmly. “Luckily we’ve caught you in your early stages so I can just give you a prescription for antibiotics and send you on your way, sound good?”

“Um-” He clears his throat and it does absolutely nothing. “Yeah, sure.” David turns to Alexis. “You didn’t tell mom and dad, did you?”

She scoffs. “I’m not stupid, David. I wouldn’t do that to you.”

Their parents have a long history of either overreacting or underreacting to family emergencies. When Alexis had her wisdom teeth out (“Remember that we love you if you don’t survive the anesthesia” “mom, what?!”), or when David fell off of the second story balcony when he was shitfaced (“People survive falls all the time” “okay, but he seriously broke his leg, dad”).

They quickly learned in their adulthood not to alert mom and dad of their various injuries and ailments. Just casually mention it, and pretend they’re crazy no matter how they react. That worked.

At some point, someone shows up and puts an oxygen mask on his face. He accepts it with obvious distaste.

David starts to get burnt out, truly burnt out, while they wait for the registration people, and the prescription, and the instructions and various examinations. His coughing is wet and chesty, and exhausted, as if he can’t manage to get enough air to cough productively.

“David, the mask,” Alexis reminds him when he sounds like he’s getting out of breath. He rolls his eyes and puts it back on his face. “Patrick texted me earlier.” David turns to her, brows raised expectantly (hopefully). “He said he was getting ready to leave, that was a couple hours ago. So like, I guess he’s home by now, r-?”

Bzzzt.

Patrick: snow is bad. I had to get a hotel room. Tell David I’m sorry.

Alexis frowns. David makes a questioning noise that just results in irritating his lungs.

“Uh, nevermind. He’s snowed in, David.”

David sighs and flops against the pillow.

“He says he’s sorry?” Wait- fuck, wait- those are tears. “Oh God, David, it’s fine. He’ll be home tomorrow!” She assures in a panic. “Don’t cry, fuck please don’t cry you know I don’t know what to do when-”

David pulls the mask down and his breath hitches in a sob. Another thing that he probably wouldn’t let happen if mom and dad were here.

“Ugh David...” And without thinking, Alexis finds herself hopping onto the edge of the bed and sitting next to him. She rubs his back rather awkwardly as he swipes at his eyes.

His breath hitches again, but this time he’s coughing again, so Alexis guides the mask back to his face until he finishes slipping it on.

And then they just sit.

He begins hacking again at one point and asks for a tissue.

“Don’t look,” he croaks.

Alexis is not about to watch her brother hock mucus into a tissue, so she turns until he assures that he’s done.

-----

David’s fast asleep by the time the hospital delivers the news that he can go. Alexis wakes him up and repeats the news. They leave with a prescription.

He falls asleep on the way home.

Alexis: mind if we borrow your apartment? I don’t think David’s ready to deal with mom and dad tonight.

Patrick: Go ahead. David has a key.

One thing Alexis didn’t think about was the stairs. She looks up at the top floor window where Patrick’s apartment is, and swears.

“Okay, so...”

David sighs, coughs. “Let’s just go. I’d rather die on my way up the stairs than go back to the motel right now.”

It takes ten minutes to climb three flights of stairs. David’s hacking breathlessly by the time they get to the top, so much so that he ends up sitting at the landing just to breathe.

Alexis waits. “We made it, though, at least, so that’s pretty cool.”

“Mm-” cough “-hmm.”

She bullies him into the bed. (“Nngh, germs,” “I don’t think Patrick cares, David”) and shoves the first dose of antibiotics down his throat.

“Wanna call Patrick before you go to sleep?” Alexis offers. He’s been so out of it she isn’t sure he’s looked at his phone once.

David furrows his brows, cogs in his feverish brain turning slowly. “No... no, better not. I’ll pro’y just end up crying again.”

“Mm, yeah, that’s fair.” Her throat tightens, and Alexis swallows hard. “Well, go to sleep, David. I’m going to go fuck up your  alphabetized movie collection.”

David’s eyes are already closed, but he still manages to mumble something about fighting her the moment his lungs are functional. And then he’s asleep.

Alexis quickly gets up and closes the curtains the separate the bed from the rest of the apartment. It’s more for her benefit, she thinks as she clears her throat and makes her way to Patrick’s couch.

She finds a couple of throw blankets and a pillow and sets them down next to the couch.

And she cries.

Head in hands, Alexis chokes out a sob and lets fall the tears she’s been holding back for the last couple of hours.

She’s not cut out for this.

David’s always been the one taking care of her, looking out for her, making sure she’s safe when she puts herself in impossible situations.

She’s never realized how terrifying it is to be responsible for someone. Who knows what state David would be in right now if she just said “okay” and took him to the motel to sleep it off?

She can’t forget how hot his skin was when she felt his fever, or how broken he looked when he found out Patrick couldn’t make it home tonight.

Patrick.

She should probably let him know that they’re back. She doubts that David had remembered to text him.

Alexis: at your place! David’s sleeping, im claiming your couch

Patrick: he ok? he hasn’t replied to any of my texts

Alexis: I don’t think he’s thought about his phone tbh. he’s fine.

Patrick: wbu?

Alexis swipes at her eyes and sniffles, trying to stop herself crying as if Patrick knows. Call it a premonition or whatever, because her phone starts to vibrate.

Patrick calling

She snuffles. “Hey.”

“Hey, everything okay?” Patrick’s voice is soft as if he’s also in the room with a sleeping David.

“No,” Alexis admits with another sniff. She chuckles despite herself. “Ugh, Patrick, I’m such a mess. He’s so sick, it freaked me out. Why did you have to be gone?” she tries to stay quiet, but with the volume of David’s wheezing snores, she doubts he’d be able to hear her anyway.

Patrick stays silent, she continues.

“I mean, I’ve been kidnapped like a million times but this just- I hated it. If I didn’t talk him into going to the hospital-” she stops herself and sighs.

“He’s alright now, though?” Patrick asks, worry at the edge of hsi soft tone.

“Yeah,” she sniffs. “Yeah, he’s got meds and stuff. The doctor say he’ll be fine.”

“Good. Good good...” Patrick clears his throat. “You know, this isn’t all on your shoulders, right? I mean chances are that I would’ve come home tomorrow and heard him coughing like that and dragged him to the hospital anyway.”

“Is that supposed to make me feel better?”

Patrick sighs. “I just mean... don’t feel like this is your job. But um, thank you for taking care of him tonight. I... I miss him.”

She can’t help a smile. She knows he misses her brother, she knows her brother misses him, and as disgusting as it is, she’s so happy they found each other. “Then get your ass over here so I don’t have to do this anymore.”

A chuckle. “I’m on it, I swear.”

Alexis is about to say something else when she hears a wet hacking from the other room.

“Oh my God, is that him?” Patrick asks, sounding rather terrified.

“Uh, yeah...” the coughing doesn’t stop. She can faintly see David struggling to sit up in bed as it gets more and more breathless. “Give me a sec, Patrick.”

“Sure?”

When she goes to check on him, he’s failed to sit up and is instead awkwardly hunched over as he tries to regain control of his lungs. David has a hand clamped over his mouth. She figures the one way she can help is by grabbing the tissue box from the living room and setting it within David’s reach.

He takes one without a word and presses it to his mouth as he keeps coughing.

Alexis sits next to him and starts rubbing his back uselessly. He’s warm. She should have gotten some fever reducer in him before she put him to bed.

Alexis frowns, but is relieved when her brother’s coughing starts to die down.

“Is he okay?” is the first thing Patrick asks when she brings the phone back to her ear.

“Yeah, yeah. He’s just-”

“Try getting him to sit up when he sleeps. It should help the cough a little bit.”

“Okay? Yeah, David, Patrick says- oh my God,” she rolls her eyes and hands the phone to her brother.

He looks confused, eyes glassy from fever and watery from the fit, but he puts the phone up to his ear with shaky hands. “Hey?”

Wow, he sounds awful. But he smiles.

Alexis absconds to the couch, accepting that she’s not going to get her phone back anytime soon, and listens to her brother talking more consciously than he has in hours.

They both wake to David’s coughing throughout the night. At some point she helps him reposition himself when his self-made pillow tower collapses behind him and he’s too uncoordinated to fix it.

“I’m sorry,” he rasps, so quiet she barely catches it.

“Don’t be sorry, David. I...” she shakes her head. “I think I might owe this to you?”

David makes a noise, his eyes already falling shut as Alexis nudges him to lie back.

“Don’t owe me a fucking thing.”

A scoff. “Yeah right, David.”

-----

She hardly sees her brother the next few days. Patrick’s been filling in for him at the store while he recovers.

Alexis gets screamed at for not telling her parents, but she takes it in a stride because she knows that David would be screaming much louder if she had told them.

She’s in the middle of deciding on her outfit for the day when the door opens.

“David? What are you doing here? Aren’t you like, in quarantine, or something?”

“Recovery, and I missed the motel, so I’m back.” David looks tired, still looks sick, but not as dead as the last time she saw him. He has a bag in one hand, which he throws on his bed, and a vase in the other.

He turns to cough into his arm before extending the vase out.

“Here, it’s...” he clears his throat, closes his eyes for a moment like whatever he’s trying to say refuses to come out. “Thanks for... everything. You shouldn’t have had to deal with all of that.”

Alexis raised an eyebrow but gladly takes the vase. The flowers will look adorable on her desk. “Well, as long as I never have to do that again...”

David’s quick to shake his head. “You won’t! You definitely, definitely won’t,” he says, emphasizing even move with his hands before he has to cough again. “The- the flowers were Patrick’s idea, to be honest.”

“What, really?”

David nods reluctantly. “Yeahhh, he just said I should do something for you because that wasn’t the easiest thing you’ve had to put up with or whatever.”

Alexis smiles. “Well then I guess I should thank you for... worrying about me. All those years.”

“Can we not acknowledge that conversation please? Or like, this one?”

Alexis snorts. “Absolutely, David.”
