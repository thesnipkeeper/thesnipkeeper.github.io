Title: Double Chocolate Truffle
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David and Patrick take atrip out of town, David insists that his cold isn't a problem until it becomes a problem
Word count: 2.3k

Double Chocolate Truffle

Patrick winces at the sound of yet another sneeze. He turns to David whose nose is buried in a tissue and is currently building up to a second sneeze.

He finally says what’s been hanging in the air all morning. “We don’t have to go if you’re not feeling good.”

Patrick’s still in the middle of throwing clothes in a bag, but David’s been fully packed for two days and his suitcases are stacked neatly next to the couch (“Do you really need all of this for a weekend trip?” “Do you know who you’re asking? Of course I do”). David shakes his head and tosses the tissue into the bin with an impressive aim.

“I told you, it’s just a cold.” He scrunches his nose and sniffs. His nose hasn’t stopped dripping, and it’s already starting to develop a pink hue that Patrick is going to be sure not to bring up. “We’ve been looking forward to this for a month. We haven’t had any time off. We’re going.”

“Yeah, but are you really going to enjoy this if you’re sick?”

“Um, absolutely.” David wraps an arm around his waist, pulls him close and smiles. “Any alone time with you that isn’t here will be amazing no matter what. And I really wish I could kiss you right now.” He releases Patrick with a groan before running the back of his hand against his nose.

Patrick chuckles and pulls him in for a kiss before he can process it. David recoils, eyes wide.

“Stop, you’re going to get sick too and then this entire trip is actually going to be ruined.”

Patrick rolls his eyes. “We were kissing yesterday, and the day before that. I think that if I was going to get sick, it would have happened already.” He goes to zip up his duffle bag. “Anyway, I would hope that kissing isn’t the worst thing that we’ll be doing this weekend.”

David bites back a smirk as his eyes go up to the ceiling for a moment. “Okay... you may have a point.”

They manage to get everything loaded into Patrick’s car without too much trouble, though David’s suitcases do become a bit like Tetris. Patrick was sure to bring the box of tissues from his apartment and put it in the car between the front seats, and David pointedly does not acknowledge the addition as he gets into the car with a wet sniffle.

And they’re off.

It’s clear he’s trying to keep conversations going and act like his usual eccentric self, but Patrick can tell he’s tired. His voice is developing a hint of congestion as he sinuses are probably beginning to get stuffed.

“Apparently this town is known for its cute little shops.” David sniffs and wipes his nose with the tissue that he’s kept balled up in his hand before going back to scrolling through his phone. “So we could maybe check them out tomorrow and-”

“Take notes for the apothecary?”

David chuckles. “Um, browse... but yes, I do in fact expect to get some... business ideas. There’s also an ice cream shop that sells a flavor called double chocolate truffle,” he informs with delight. “So, you know... we’ll make a little detour.”

“Right,” Patrick says fondly. “I think we can do that.”

The hotel that they’ve decided to stay at is small and homey. David’s the one who convinced him, although it didn’t take much. He’s right, they’ve needed time off for a while now. The store has been hectic, and with business waning as winter comes, they decided to take a weekend off

The first sneeze of the car ride nearly causes Patrick to jump, and by the way he’s holding his hand over his nose and scrambling for a tissue, David wasn’t expecting it either. He blows his nose and sniffles.

“Anyway, I think we should take tonight to just relax and enjoy our time away from home,” he says before quickly dabbing his nose with his sleeve.

That idea has never sounded better, Patrick decides, but for David instead of himself.

-----

They speak to a young hotel receptionist who cheerily informs them of everything they need to know about the place and what there is to do. She blesses David when he sneezes, and Patrick swears he’s never heard a more forced “thank you” in his life.

David gasps as they walk into the hotel room. “Oh my God, this is adorable,” he says, rubbing Patrick’s shoulders from behind him with excitement.

“It really is. You picked a great place, David.”

“I mean, of course I d-” David inhales quickly, and he has to twist away to aim a cough into his elbow. The forceful sound is enough to alarm Patrick just a little.

“Jeez, that sounds kind of rough,” Patrick says as he unloads their luggage at the foot of the bad.

David waves it off. “Just a little tickle in my throat, it’s nothing. Anyway, I think we should start thinking about dinner plans.”

Patrick can’t help but laugh. “David, it’s not even noon!”

“Okay, okay,” he clears his throat. “In an hour, then?”

“Fair enough.”

They unpack and then lie down to watch TV for a bit. Except lying down seems to irritate David’s cold, as he starts sniffling a little more frequently, and Patrick can tell one side of his nose is stuffed up by the way he keeps trying to blow it.

He presses a kiss to David’s temple, to which his fiancé makes a noise of protest that says something along the lines of “I told you I’m fine”. Still, he leans into Patrick with a sniffle.

They lie comfortably snuggled up for a while. David’s breath evens out and Patrick thinks he’s going to fall asleep.

Until, of course, he hears David’s stomach growl.

“Can we talk about our dinner plans now?”

“Yes.”

-----

They find a charming bar and grill in town and David excitedly talks about everything he wants to eat.

The place is nearly packed, but they’re seated quickly.

When it comes time to order their food, David has to clear his throat in order for the waitress to hear him and even then, his voice doesn’t lose the raspy edge.

And then he barely touches his meal.

“Something wrong with your sandwich?”

“Hm? Oh, no, I’m just not as hungry as I thought I was.” He takes a drink of water and winces. “And my throat kind of hurts, but it’s nothing. I’ll just take it back to the hotel and heat it up later.”

He looks tired, now that Patrick’s really looking at him. His facade is beginning to fade, and he’s even admitted to feeling too bad to eat. It doesn’t bode well.

He takes David’s hand as they walk out of the restaurant, and seeing his fiancé's face as the smile starts to fade, he sees that it’s starting to really hit David. The realization that he is sick on their first weekend off in so long.

“Hey, why don’t we look for that ice cream place you were talking about?”

David shakes his head. “I think I’m ready to go back for the night.”

Patrick frowns. “Okay. We could probably stop somewhere and get something for your throat, too, if you wanted?”

“I just... want to lie down, I think.”

“Hey...” Patrick twists in front of him before they break apart at the car. He lays his other hand on David’s shoulder and looks him in the eyes, smiling. “No moping! we’re going to have fun this weekend, okay? I’m going to do everything in my power to make sure we both enjoy it.”

David bites back a smile. His eyes light up, just a little. “Very ambitious of you, considering how hideous I am right now.”

“You’re beautiful.” Patrick leans in to kiss him, and this time David meets him in the middle with a grin.

-----

David falls asleep far earlier than he usually does. Patrick can’t help but chuckle when he hears snores come from his fiancé. Patrick’s always been the snorer in their relationship, but David’s cold has obviously changed things up a little.

Maybe this really is just a little cold. David might not be feeling great, but it won’t stop them from having fun together. They’ll just have to slow down a little bit.

At least, that’s what Patrick thinks.

He’s wrong.

David is no better in the morning, of course. Actually, he’s worse. Last night he was coughing a little bit, sneezing a little bit, had some trouble waking up in the middle of the night (and waking Patrick in the process, to which he reacted by cuddling David until they were both asleep once again). Now, his cough is constant and painful, his nose is running relentlessly and he seems to be too stuffed up to do anything about it. Blowing his nose takes a while and accomplishes nothing but a coughing fit and increasingly pink skin around his nostrils and upper lip.

And yet Patrick watches him sniffle through the beginning of his morning routine as if nothing’s wrong.

His cheeks are red, as well as his watery eyes. Patrick comes up from behind him and palms his forehead. An unhealthy heat meets his skin, and David doesn’t even bother to pull away and insist that he’s fine.

“David... you can’t go out like this.”

David sniffles, and then sniffles again. “It’s fine,” he says, waving his hands in a way to induce calmness in himself. He sounds so stuffed up and hoarse it almost hurts Patrick to hear him speak.

“We can always wait until tomorrow to go to the shops,” Patrick tries.

David groans, but with his sore throat it sounds a little more like a dying goat. He goes through a whole face journey until he gives a defeated, “I wanted to go today, though.”

“I know,” Patrick wraps his arms around David from behind and nuzzles his shoulder. “But you’re not going to have any fun with the way you’re feeling.”

David jerks suddenly, and he hears the unfortunate wetness in his breath as he starts to cough. Patrick stands up straight and rubs his back, making a sympathetic noise when David finishes the fit with a sneeze. He presses a tissue into his fiance's hand and presses a kiss to his cheek.

“Come back to bed,” he begs. “Let’s just have a lazy day. Isn’t that what we wanted anyway? To relax?”

David sighs and sniffles thickly against the flood of mucus trying to drip out. “I really don’t feel good,” he admits.

Patrick cups his face, feeling the heat radiate from David’s body. “Yeah, I think you have a fever.”

David makes a congested whining noise. He lets Patrick lead him back to bed. The nightstand on his side of the bed is already littered with tissues.

Poor David can’t go a minute without sneezing or coughing, and he readjusts himself restlessly. He isn’t particularly cuddly until he starts to shiver.

David makes an effort to turn away from Patrick when he sneezes, and he gives a loose-sounding sniffle. Patrick hands him a tissue before he has a chance to ask.

“Bless.”

“Thank you,” he breathes out before blowing his nose. He makes a miserable noise, shifting closer to Patrick to sap his body heat. 

Patrick doesn’t hear anything from him for a couple of minutes. He thinks David might have fallen asleep when he sniffles again. Except this sounds different, not quite cold-induced. Patrick moves to get a look at his fiance’s face, and sure enough, David is tearing up.

“David, it’s okay…”

He shakes his head and tries to scrub the tears from his cheeks, and all Patrick can think to do is pull him closer. 

“It’s all ruined.”

“Nothing’s ruined,” Patrick assures softly, pressing kiss after kiss to whatever part of David he can get to. “You’re just feeling extra shitty right now.” 

Eventually David wears himself out and does fall asleep. Patrick holds him until David ends up moving out of his grasp in his sleep. 

He takes the opportunity to get out of bed and grab his keys and wallet. Surely David will accuse him of heartlessly leaving him while he’s sick, but Patrick’s sure he’ll be forgiven when he comes back. He has ideas, after all.

-----

David’s still snoring softly when Patrick returns. He sets his things down, but keeps the rest. His hand is freezing, actually, from the cold paper bowl in his hand, but it’s a price he’s willing to pay for love. 

Patrick eases himself onto the edge of the bed and rubs David’s leg. He can’t help but smile at his fiance’s groggy state as he wakes.

“Hm?” his snuffles, brows furrowed, before those deep brown eyes finally open. Said eyes go directly to the jacket Patrick is still wearing. “Did you go somewhere?” he asks with a yawn.

“Yeah, I just went to pick up some stuff.”

“Mmm?” David sits up. “Like what?”

“Well… got some tylenol,” he sets the bottle on the nightstand by David’s side of the bed. David looks a bit relieved to see some sort of drug that can make him feel better. “And uh, just thought I’d stop somewhere else on my way back…”

David’s eyes positively light up at the bowl of ice cream Patrick sets in his hands. “Is this…?”

A nod. “Double chocolate truffle ice cream. We can go back for more tomorrow if you feel like it.”

David gasps, and of course has to turn away to cough. “What the fuck did I do to deserve you?”

Patrick chuckles. “I just thought it would help you feel a little better.” 

He leans forward to kiss David, and David complies with a grin and a gurgly hum. “I fucking love you.”

“I love you too.”

“Sorry, I was talking to the ice cream.” He laughs at Patrick’s confused expression. “Kidding! Obviously!”
