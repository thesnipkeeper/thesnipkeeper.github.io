Juno Steel and the Falling Planks

Juno liked to think that stealth was one of his strong suits on a heist. He’d learned from his previous profession how to stay in the shadows and not be seen.

But, sometimes things just didn’t happen that way. Sometimes one stumbles into a stack of planks and knocks the entire thing over and alerts the bad guys to one’s whole group.

Juno didn’t even really have a chance to feel guilty about it. There were other things on his mind, like the fact that he had yet to really regain his balance or get the world to completely stop spinning. How he managed to get back to the ship with the others could only be explained by pure adrenaline coursing through his system.

And now that that was over, and everyone was safely inside, he realized how truly awful he felt.

“What the hell, Steel?” It was Vespa, of course, back to blame him for something else or yell at him just because she felt like it.

Juno was too busy leaning against the nearest chair, trying to catch his breath to really pay her any mind.

“Vespa, please...” Buddy’s weak attempt to quell the strife. She wasn’t trying hard, likely because she was just as frustrated and just wasn’t showing it.

The floor was spinning below him, so Juno squeezed his eyes shut and took a deep breath. God, he... he hurt.

“You fucked us, Steel! What the hell was that about?”

“I lost my footing, okay?!” He hissed, eyes still closed.

“You lost your-” Vespa gave an exasperated laugh. “You almost got us killed and you just lost your footing?”

“Perhaps we’ll let this be a cautionary tale about being more careful in the future,” said Buddy.

Vespa continued, and her voice was getting louder. She was closer, Juno could tell because now her voice was making his head split open. He glanced up just to see her furious face still chewing him out. And honestly, if it wasn’t Vespa, he would agree with them. He fucked up. He ruined the mission. But damn, he just wanted her to stop yelling.

“Are you listening to me, Steel?” Vespa demanded.

“Unfortunately yes, I’m still hearing your shrill voice,” was Juno’s answer, and apparently it wasn’t the correct one. Juno was shoved, forcing him to stumble backward onto the floor. Through the twirling room, he got a quick glance at Vespa’s face. To her credit, she didn’t look like she intended to go at him that hard.

It was too late for either of them to take anything back, though, because Juno felt his stomach twist and turn. He leaned over on the ground just in time to vomit up his breakfast.

“What’s goin’ on out- Mistah Steel?!” from Rita, and a worried gasp from Nureyev.

Juno was too busy trying not to heave again to see what everyone’s reactions were, but he held up a hand to halt anyone coming near him. He didn’t care to be touched or, you know, punched at the moment.

“Juno,” Buddy said. Her voice was closer now. “Tell me that you’re not drunk,” doing her best impression of a disappointed mother.

“I’m not drunk,” Juno affirmed, and then growled out a, “Look, I’m sorry about the-” He paused, hand going to his mouth as he swallowed hard. Talking was hard when all he could really think about was the overwhelming nausea and dizziness.

“Enough of this. Let’s get you to the infirmary, hm?”

He wanted to keep pleading his case, or keep apologizing, or maybe just antagonize Vespa some more, but instead Juno just gave a wordless nod and let Peter and Buddy help him up.

“He’s burning up,” Peter murmured, as if he didn’t want Juno to hear.

Buddy responded with a simple sigh.

For once, the hard mattress and loud machines didn’t stop him. Juno fell asleep the moment he was in the infirmary bed.

And woke up to his stomach aching horribly. Juno groaned and shifted before he even opened his eyes. Whoever was in the room with him must’ve noticed the signs before he did, because there was the sound of quick movement as if rushing to get something.

Juno was powerless to stop the nausea.

“In here, in here!” Vespa said, shoving an emesis bin in front of him just in time to catch another wave of vomit. He got her hand a little in the process and earned a groan of disgust. Juno would’ve felt quite proud of himself had he not been puking his guts lout at that moment.

Eventually, he felt somewhat done, and accepted a tissue to wipe his face while Vespa went to work disinfecting the bin.

“Is it poison?” Was the first thing Juno thought to ask.

“Pois-? No, Steel, you have a stomach virus.” She threw the bin onto the table next to him.

Juno moaned and let his head fall back. “Even worse.”

She scoffed, chuckled. And then things got incredibly awkward. “Listen, I...”

Juno knew what was about to happen, and this time he almost felt lucky when his stomach cramped again. “Hold that thought,” he said, pushing off of the bed and willing the room not to spin as he did so. He snatched the bin and gagged uselessly into it.

“Damn it, Steel, I just cleaned-”

He cut her off with a productive heave that brought foul-tasting liquid hitting the bottom of the bin. Things were getting caught in the back of his throat and - ew - his nose. “Ugh...”

Vespa sighed. “Keep your head up when you puke and it won’t come out your nose.”

Just thinking about his predicament made him gag allover again, but this time he heeded her advice. He finished by wiping his face and blowing his nose into a new tissue.

Things were awkward once again. Juno didn’t hesitate to break the silence.

“So you were about to apologize for assaulting me while I’m horribly sick?”

Vespa growled, but regained her composure as she remembered her current duty as ship doctor. That was the best time to pick on her, after all.

Juno saved her the response. He didn’t have the energy to keep up the banter. “Maybe I should be apologizing too. You know, I knew I was coming down with something and I didn’t tell anyone.”

She huffed out through her nose as if she wanted to lecture him or yell at him, but instead just shoved her hands in her pockets. “Well- well, say something next time and you won’t get punched as hard.”

Juno snorted. “Sound advice.”
