As a Group

They’re waiting for a table at a nice steakhouse when Liam notices something. They’re missing one of their people. It’s too loud inside to say anything to the entire group, so he nudges Travis until the man leans in.

“Where’s Taliesin?” Taliesin usually loves going out at strange restaurants and ordering the absolute weirdest thing on the menu.

“Oh, he caught some kinda bug,” Travis says. “From the way he sounded on the phone, he’s probably gonna be down for the count for the rest of the weekend.”

Liam frowns. “He seemed fine this morning!”

“Right? Dunno, man, he got real sick real fast.”

Poor Taliesin. Liam can think of few worse things than getting sick at a hotel during a convention. He’d come down with his fair share of con plagues and was miserable and lonely every single time.

... shit, Taliesin must be bummed about missing out on dinner. Everyone’s out while he’s stuck in his room.

Liam huffs, and thinks about it for a good couple of minutes. Their table won’t be ready for another half hour, and he’s starving. If he leaves now, he could grab something and bring it back to the hotel.

He makes his decision. He nudges Travis again. “Hey, I’m just gonna go back to the hotel.”

“Are you sure?”

“Yeah, I’m kinda tired.” He really is, actually, now that he thinks about it. The last place he wants to be right now is a loud restaurant. Checking on Taliesin is just a plus, he supposes.

“I’ll bring something back for you, then,” Travis says, patting his back.

“Nah, that’s alright.”

Liam stops at a CVS with the intent of buying everything to cure his friend’s ailment. And then he realizes that he doesn’t know what his friend’s ailment actually is. He could spend a good amount of time guessing, or he could just call Tal and ask.

He’s pacing back and forth in the cold and flu aisle as the phone rings. Maybe Tal is sleeping, and maybe Liam is about to wake him up. Shit, maybe he should’ve thought this through.

“Hello?”

Travis was right. Taliesin sounds awful. His voice is tight and raspy, and slurred like he was definitely sleeping.

“Hey bud, heard you were pretty sick, and I happen to be at CVS right now if you want me to pick anything up for you.”

There’s a pause. “You’re... done eating already?”

“Nah, they’re busy and I didn’t feel like waiting.” He has his eye on a microwave pizza in the frozen aisle. Not as good at steak, but sounds pretty damn delicious when he’s already exhausted.

Taliesin hums. “I don’t really need anything, but thank you.”

“Are you sure? You don’t sound good.”

“I don’t feel good,” he says with a chuckle. “But I think I just need sleep. Like, a lot of sleep.”

“What are your symptoms, though? Because I’m staring at about two-hundred bottles of cold drugs guaranteed to help you get fucked up.”

“Oh, it’s not a cold.”

“What do you mean?”

“Stomach stuff, you know? Just a lot of throwing up and stuff. I’ll feel like shit for a while but I’ll be fine, really.”

“Aw, Tal...” Liam frowns. “I’m getting you like ten flavors of Gatorade.”

“Jesus,” he laughs. “Okay.”

“Glad we can come to an understanding.”

They end the call when Liam offers some well-wishes and promises to be there in a little while.

And he does indeed grab a shit ton of Gatorade, one of those little microwaveable containers of soup, and one of those little pizzas.

God, poor Taliesin. Hopefully he’ll be feeling better by the time they have to fly back home. Liam can’t imagine flying with a stomach bug.

His phone rings as he’s on his way out of the store, and he somehow manages to get his phone to his ear while also carrying the bags to the car.

“Where’d you go?” Marisha, and Liam can hear the bustle of the restaurant in the background.

“Just realized how tired I was, y’know?”

“You okay? You’re not getting what Taliesin has, are you?”

“It’s just been a long day,” he says reasonably. “Speaking of Taliesin, though, I’m on my way to bring him some stuff.”

“Aw shit, you beat us to it. Matt and I were going to head over tonight. Maybe we still will, though.”

“I’m sure he’d love it.” Taliesin loved company. As much as he likes to call himself a weirdo introvert, he enjoyed being around people, especially when he was having a rough time.

She hums. “Okay, when you see him, let him know we’re coming to bother him.”

-----

He stops by his room to change and grab a phone charger, and then makes his way to Taliesin’s just a floor above.

Taliesin looks like shit when he answers the door. His hair is a mess, and there’s dark circles and lines under his eyes that only happen whenever Taliesin works for multiple days straight. He’s leaning on the door frame like he doesn’t have the strength to stand on his own.

“I promised you Gatorade,” Liam said, holding up one of the bags. “and I brought it.”

Taliesin smiles, or tries to. “Thanks,” he goes to push off the door frame, and stumbles in the process. Liam’s heart skips a beat in panic before Taliesin regains his strength.

“Jesus, go sit down,” Liam urges as he all but lets himself into the room.

Taliesin does so with a self-deprecating chuckle. Liam glances at the bathroom on his way through the room. There’s towels on the floor, arranged in a way that looks like they’ve been napped on. He frowns, wondering if Taliesin’s been downplaying his illness to everyone who’s asked.

The bed is a mess of tangled covers, but Taliesin doesn’t seem to mind as he sits down and tries to cover himself a bit with a shudder. He’s already wearing flannel pants and a sweatshirt, he shouldn’t be cold.

“Have you had anything to eat or drink all day?” Liam asks, setting a couple bottles on the nightstand and the rest on the room’s desk.

Taliesin looks sheepish. “I’ve been... tired.” He crosses his arms over his chest, barely stifling another shiver as it courses through him.

Liam nods. “You should drink something, at least.”

“I’m...not sure that it’ll stay down.”

“Might be worth trying though?”

Taliesin shrugs and takes the closest Gatorade, cracking it open with less ease than was usually involved in opening a plastic bottle. He takes the smallest, most cautious sip and nods. “Not bad.”

Liam sighs. He looks the man over. “You should’ve told someone how sick you were.”

“Oh, I’ll live,” Taliesin says dismissively.

“Yeah, but... we care, y’know.”

A small, sleepy smile. “Yeah, I know. Sorry. Just didn’t want everyone to think they needed to worry about me when we’re so busy.”

“Well, first of all, we’re always busy. That’s no excuse.”

Taliesin chuckles.

“Second, Matt and Marisha wanted me to tell you that they’re going to come by and bug you too.”

Taliesin looks a bit relieved at that. “Alrighty.”

“Third,” Liam feels his forehead. “You’re burning up.”

“I know that, too. Well, I figured.” He takes another sip and sets it aside. Two sips isn’t enough to keep him hydrated, but it’s a start.

“I’ve got some ibuprofen back in my room if you want any.”

“That’s okay,” he says. “I try to let fever run their course.”

Maybe that’s why he always looks so damn miserable when he’s sick. Still, Liam can’t blame him.

Liam leaves the soup and Gatorade with Taliesin. He thought about staying until Matt and Marisha got there, but Taliesin was starting to fall asleep and Liam still wanted to eat his own dinner.

He makes the mini pizza for himself back at his own room, eats it, and almost immediately falls asleep.

Liam wakes an hour later with an overwhelming sense of nausea. He clamps a hand over his mouth, still half asleep as he stumbles to the bathroom.

He’s in there for a good while as his stomach tries to rid itself of his dinner and whatever else he’d had time to eat that day. He’s tempted just to stay there, getting up sounds like so much work, but in the end, the desire to be in bed wins.

His stomach should be empty, but it still feels unsettled and gross, and he can feel chills setting in as he makes his slow way back to bed.

I think there’s something going around.

He texts, and then follows it with a GIF of the virtual rag doll falling downstairs.
