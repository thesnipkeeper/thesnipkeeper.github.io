Word count: 1.3k

Always Here

Carmen had asked Player to look into an event that had some wildly expensive items up for auction. One of them happened to be a vase that the Countess had had her eye on for as long as anyone had known her. If she could get the thing before the Countess did, and return it to its rightful owner... (which wasn’t the auction, actually.) that would be the perfect day.

He’d told her that he’d get back to her, and that was over twelve hours ago. She woke up in the morning expecting a “call me when you can!” text from her hacker friend. Nothing. Not a peep.

Had he forgotten? But Player never forgot something like this, he had a better memory than probably anyone else on the team.

And then she began imagining the worse. If VILE had somehow gotten to him... kidnapped him to get back at her or use him or-

Player’s face appeared on her computer. Carmen’s heart jumped with delight as she breathed a sigh of relief. He wasn’t kidnapped, that was always great.

“Hey Red!”

Carmen dropped the bag of fruit she was about to pour into the blender. “Player, oh my God, I was worried something happened.”

“Oh,” he winced, looking guilty. “No, sorry, I just... overslept. And then I realized that I fell asleep last night before I looked into your auction. That’s my bad.” He turned his head to the side and gave a weak cough and sniffle.

Carmen frowned.

“Hey Player! Glad you’re not dead!” Zack said as he walked by, snatching a bag of chips off the counter in the process.

Carmen folded her arms and looked at her friend on the screen. “I wouldn’t be so sure about that. Are you doing okay, Player?”

Player scoffed and waved a hand, “Psh, it’s just a cold. Maybe my parents were right about staying up too late.” He sniffed again. He looked like someone who had overslept, but also like someone who could’ve used a few more hours of sleep. Certainly not someone who should be out of bed and staring at bright screens all day.

“Why don’t you get some rest? Just send me what you’ve got and we can handle everything else.”

Zack raised an eyebrow at her. “Can we? I mean, not that I don’t believe in us but uh, can we handle everything else?”

He had a point.

“No, Zack’s right,” Player said, voice sounding stuffier and more miserable by the second. He sniffled and swiped at his nose. “I’ve got to get you guys into this auction. And I’m fine, really. Like I said, it’s just a-” he stopped to clear his throat, and brought a hand to his mouth to cover it. “Uh... hold on...” he stood up and disappeared from the view of his camera.

Carmen raised an eyebrow. “Player...?”

“Ah jeez, he’s gonna barf!”

“Oh no...” she sighed, and then thought for a moment. “I don’t want to need him for this one...”

“... But we do,” Zack finished for her, looking equally as worried. “Maybe Ivy and I can learn hacking real quick, give the guy a sick day?”

“I’m not sure that’s how it works, bro,” Ivy said as she made her way over. She leaned against the counter, behind the computer and out of its view. Not that it mattered, Player was still gone and Carmen found herself worried about her oldest friend once again. “So Player’s sick, huh? Maybe we can do something to help.”

“You just said we can’t become hackers,” Zack stated.

“Not hacking, moron!” she scoffed. “I mean, he must want company or something, right?”

Carmen didn’t think about that. Did player ever get lonely when they weren’t on a mission together? Did he have any other friends?

He returned to his chair with a red, tired face and watery eyes. “So the auction-”

“Player,” she interrupted. “You’re way to sick for this.”

“What? No, I-”

“Dude, you just puked!”

Player narrowed his eyes at Zack as if he were revealing some big secret. And then he sighed. “I could really use a distraction right now, okay? Like, last night... could have been better and I feel like if I don’t have something to do today I’m gonna go insane and-”

“Okay okay,” Carmen said. “But we’re not making you work too much.”

“You can stay on here as long as you want, though!” Ivy offered.

Relief seemed to wash over his exhausted features. “Thanks.” another throat clearing. “So, it’s your classic rich people auction. Fancy clothes, gross wine. If you walk in there, you’re gonna have to look ready to spend a ridiculous amount of money.” He continued to talk about the auction, only interrupting himself once to blow his nose and again when he thought he might vomit again.

They all took notes and worked on the ideal plan to make it work. Shadowsan was more than ready to take on the Countess if he needed to, and Zack already had the getaway all figured out. This would work.

They were all feeling confident in their plan and abilities when Player made a choked nose. Before they knew it, the guy ducked out of view of the camera and began vomiting into the waste bin or whatever he’d managed to grab.

Zack made a noise of disgust, Ivy frowned, and Shadowsan, in traditional Shadowsan fashion, didn’t change his expression once as they were forced to listen to their friend relinquish the contents of his stomach.

Player reappeared wiping his mouth on the sleeve of his hoodie. He looked as if he were about to cry, whether out of misery or embarrassment.

“Player,” Shadowsan spoke before any of them could find words. His voice was thundering, serious. “Get some rest. We will be here when you wake up.”

Player looked guilty, and as if that was all he wanted to hear. “I- I’m sorry, guys. I’m kind of useless right now.”

“No, you’re not,” Carmen said quickly. “You’ve given us everything we need. Take a break, buddy.”

-----

The auction went better than they could have imagined. They snatched the vase, sent it on its way to a museum in its home country, angered Countess Cleo to the fullest extent, all in a matter of hours. It was glorious.

It was dark by the time they returned to home base. Carmen’s hair was in complete disarray and she was way too tired to do anything about it. The others flopped on the couch, lazily recounting their day’s adventure. Carmen opened her laptop and set it on the coffee table, perhaps a bit hopeful.

“Wonder how Player’s feelin’,” Ivy said, practically reading Carmen’s mind. “He didn’t sound so great earlier. I kinda thought he was gonna fall asleep right there at his desk.”

Carmen hummed. “I hope he got some real rest today.”

“Speak of the devil,” Ivy exclaimed when a familiar face appeared on the screen.

Player wasn’t at his desk, but on his phone sitting up in bed. His hair was a mess and he ran a hand down his face as if he’d just woken up. “Did it go okay?”

“It went great!” said Zack.

Player smiled. “Awesome!”

“How’re you doing?” Carmen asked.

“Mmm,” he hummed hoarsely. “I think I’ve felt better.”

Zack scoffed. “You’ve looked better, that’s for sure- ow!” As Ivy promptly elbowed him.

“Are you staying hydrated?” asked Shadowsan, brows furrowed as he waited for the answer.

Player sleepily held up a water bottle he’d had on his nightstand. Shadowsan nodded in approval.

“My parents think I have the flu,” he admitted with a sniffle. “I guess I’m not going to be doing much of anything for a while. I’m sorry, guys.”

Carmen shook her head. “Hey, you take care of yourself. We need you healthy again!”

He smiled. “Don’t be surprised if you all get more random calls. I think I’m gonna get bored lying in bed all day tomorrow.”

“We’ll be here,” Carmen promised.
