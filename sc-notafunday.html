Word count: 2.2k

Not a Fun Day

When David and Patrick learned that they had to pick up store items from a supplier on their day off, they decided to make it a fun day trip. A little adventure for the two of them.

David just wishes that he was in a better mood.

He winces as another cramp made itself known in his stomach. Breakfast really isn’t agreeing with him today. His stomach is killing him, has been since he left, and he only wishes that the pain would go away so he could actually have fun with his fiance.

“I was thinking we could stop at that one chinese place that you like after we pick up the candles?” Patrick says.

David bites back the whimper of pain as another cramp hits him, and he nods as casually as he possibly can even though chinese food is the last thing he wants right now. Any food is the last thing he wants right now.

“Sounds great!” His voice comes out a little strained, but Patrick is thankfully too focused on singing along to the Britney Spears song on the radio to notice.

David goes back to being miserable, subtly massaging his stomach with one hand. The pain is unbearable, and definitely putting him in a bad mood. It’s only a matter of time until Patrick notices.

But, he thinks, he’s capable of pretending for the day. If anything, Patrick should be able to have a fun day. And then David can go home and puke or, ugh, God... shit or whatever disgusting thing his stomach is going to force on him.

It becomes increasingly clear that he’s not going to be able to wait that long.

The gas cramps make their way lower in his stomach, causing him to wince at the sudden pain and pressure. He doubles over for a moment with a whine until the pain finally dissipates. For now. And it isn’t until he sits up and looks over that he realizes Patrick is looking at him with clear concern and surprise.

“David, you okay?”

“Yeah, I just, my stomach,” he says with a dismissive wave. “I’ll be fine.”

“Are you sure?”

“Yeah! Yeah.”

Patrick seems unsure. “Are you carsick?”

“Patrick, I’m fine!” he laughs as if his fiance is being completely irrational.

“Okay, okay...” Patrick keeps driving.

The pressure in David’s stomach gets worse. He bites his lip. This is far worse than he ever thought possible. He needs a bathroom. Soon.

“Actually,” he pipes up, a good few minutes after their previous conversation. “I might need to stop whenever we get to a gas station or whatever.”

Patrick nods. “Yeah, sure.  I think there’s one coming up in fifteen minutes. Is that good?”

No. Definitely not. “Yes, totally.”

The first few minutes go by relatively quickly. But then it becomes a struggle to avoid doing anything disgusting. Not to mention, his stomach is hurting worse by the minute, and he’s suddenly sweating a lot for some off reason.

“Actually,” he repeats, desperation edging his voice. “I really have to go. Like, now.”

Patrick seems to understand the urgency of the situation, but that doesn’t change his response. “We really can’t stop anywhere right now. I mean, unless you want to go in the woods.” He gestures toward the trees as they drive by.

David clenches his jaw. “No.”

“I’ll drive fast, I promise.”

“Please.”

Another few minutes, and he’s no longer trying to hide it. He’s shifting and whimpering as his gut roils. 

“Patrick,” he whines, even though he knows that his partner really can’t do anything. 

“I know, I know...”

David almost cries in relief when they pull into the parking lot of a dingy old gas station and he can finally rush inside. Patrick wanders into the store as well, browsing the snacks and drinks while David hurries to the bathroom. 

Whatever he ate at the cafe has made him incredibly sick. He doubles over on the toilet, wrapping both arms around his middle as he tries to rid his body of whatever horrible stuff is inside of him.

Worse, he feels a different kind of feeling in his stomach. No no no, he absolutely refuses to start puking as well. Not happening.

The door of the bathroom squeaks open, and David silently swears that someone else has to be in here with him until he hears,

“David?” Just Patrick, thank God.

“Mmm.”

“How are you doing, sweetheart?”

“Okay, new rule: you’re not allowed to call me ‘sweetheart’ while I’m on the toilet.”

Patrick chuckles. “That’s fair, I guess. But really, are you okay?”

David huffs. “I don’t- I don’t know,” he admits. “I don’t feel great.”

Patrick makes a sympathetic noise. “I’ll grab some stuff for the road that might help. Take your time.”

“Oh, I will!” David laughs rather humorlessly. 

-----

David feels rather weak and washed out by the time he returns to the car. He gives a heavy sigh as he buckles in. 

“Okay, you look terrible,” Patrick says. “I’ll call Jenna and tell her I’ll come by tomorrow instead.”

“No,” David draws out. As good as going home sounds right now... “We’re almost there. Can we just get this over with, please?”

“If you’re sure.”

“I’m not, but let’s go,” David says with a lazy hand wave.

Patrick starts the car without a word, and David lets his eyes slip shut as they drive off. He focuses on tamping down the threatening nausea that’s still trying to work its way up his throat. No, he decides. Not today, when things are already terrible.

He only has so much choice in the matter, however.

The feeling increases tenfold and David’s eyes shoot open as he gasps, “Patrick, pull over.”

Patrick does so without question while David struggles to get his seat belt off. 

He just manages to lean out of the car in time to bring up a sickening wave of vomit. The taste alone causes David to throw up again, and again. 

He isn’t sure how long he’s at it before he’s left desperately dry heaving. 

A sudden hand on his shoulder makes him jump, and he realizes that Patrick must have gotten out of the car a while ago. He feels a handful of napkins pushed into his hand and wastes no time making use of them to wipe the snot and vomit off of his face. He sniffs, and then regrets it when it burns his nose.

“You’re such a mess,” Patrick chuckles fondly.

David smiles despite himself. Only Patrick can say something like that to him without either getting snapped at or hurting David’s feelings. “I know... God...” his voice is wrecked thanks to all of the horrible puking. It’s only then that he looks down and sees that his shoe and pant leg took the brunt of the first wave. “Oh God!”

“Hey, it’s okay,” Patrick says immediately. He must have noticed earlier and been prepared. “We can clean it at home, I promise.”

David immediately takes his shoes off and lets Patrick put them in the backseat. There isn’t much he can do about his pants at the moment, he laments.

The rest of the ride to Jenna’s is constant misery. Patrick has to pull over a second time when David thinks he’s about to puke again, but thankfully it’s a false alarm. 

Patrick loads the candles into the car himself, wishes Jenna a good day, and finally they’re homeward. 

And then, of course, Patrick’s car sputters halfway there.

-----

David’s leaned out the door once again, dry-heaving miserably while Patrick swears at the engine under the hood. He hears another car, another familiar voice, but he’s too busy fighting for control over his digestive system. 

“Thanks for coming, Stevie,” Patrick says. “I don’t know what’s wrong with it.”

It’s a piece of junk, is what David would have said if he felt like talking.

“Not a problem, I needed an excuse to leave work for a little while. Where’s David?”

He hears their footsteps on the gravel as they both walk around the car to find him. He’s somehow shuddering now, which is fantastic when his sweater is still damp from when he was sweating.

“He’s not feeling too good.”

“Yeah, I can see that. You’re not gonna puke in my care, right, David?”

For some reason, his stomach chooses that moment to force him to gag up some awful yellow bile. Stevie steps back with a disgusted noise. 

“Don’t worry,” Patrick says. “I’ve got some bags from the gas station earlier. Just in case.”

David feels the back of his fiance’s hand on his forehead, brushing away his damp bangs from his now limp hair. 

“Okay, he has a fever.” It’s the same tone of voice Patrick uses when he’s upset about something uncontrollable. David supposes that this is one of those times. “David, are you okay to start heading home?”

David swallows thickly and nods. No, he’s not sure if he’s going to make it the whole way without puking, but he’s too determined to be back at Patrick’s apartment to worry about that.

-----

David finds himself in the backseat of Stevie’s car, leaning against his fiance who so lovingly decided to sit in the back with him despite how disgusting he is right now. The plastic bags from the gas station are in Patrick’s lap, an awful reminder that they mind actually have to be put to use if Stevie’s driving get any worse.

“I’m so sorry about all of this,” he says to both Patrick and Stevie, but mostly Patrick. Jesus, he must be really sick if he’s apologizing. He lifts his head to glance at his fiance before resting it on his shoulder once again. “This was supposed to be a fun day.”

“Hey, we can have plenty of fun days when you’re feeling better,” Patrick says softly, running a hand through David’s hair.

“Just don’t puke in my car,” Stevie reminds from the front.

David moans. He shudders against Patrick, eventually bullying him into wrapping an arm around him to keep him warm. Patrick somehow gets past David’s disgustingness enough to press a kiss to his head as he holds him.

-----

David never thought he’d be so relieved to see his boyfriend’s cramped apartment.

“Go on, go get into bed.” Patrick says, for some reason giving him another kiss. Ew! “I’ll make some ginger tea for us.”

David starts to head there, and stops. “Are you sure?” he asks with a wince. “I might be... infectious.”

“I’m sure,” Patrick laughs. “I mean, we did kiss this morning. And like, all day yesterday.”

“Oh God...”

“David, it’s fine. Go rest.”

David winces at a sudden feeling in his gut and silently swears. “Actually, I-” he gestures to the bathroom, and Patrick makes an ‘ah’ sound and leaves him alone.

Nothing solid makes it out of him. Hardly anything at all, really. His stomach doesn’t even hurt anymore, it just feels strained and exhausted just like the rest of him. David finishes up and makes sure to wash his hands as thoroughly as possible.

If he is contagious, he should be asking Patrick for a ride back to the motel. He should. He doesn’t want to. If Patrick doesn’t mind having him around, then David isn’t going to try to change his mind.

He stumbles a little bit when he comes out of the bathroom, having to hang onto the door frame for a moment. The room spins.

“David?” Patrick asks, panic in his voice.

David shakes his head and raises a hand to dismiss it. “Just a little lightheaded.”

“Are you still cold?”

He has to take a moment to assess his current state before he nods. He’s just shaky in general, and the shivering doesn’t help.

Suddenly, he’s given a stack of his comfiest clothes to change into, and then David’s being led to the bed he’s been waiting for all day. A cup of tea is pressed into his hand, and Patrick climbs into bed with his own cup, also in pajamas even though it’s far too early for him to be getting in bed.

“Drink carefully, so you don’t make yourself-”

“Yes, thank you, I know how to rehydrate,” he says before taking a sip.

Patrick laughs. “Okay then.” He takes his book from the nightstand and starts reading. Just like that.

David blinks. “What’re you doing?”

Patrick raises an eyebrow and gestures toward the book in his hand. “Reading?”

“Don’t you have something better to do?”

A chuckle. “What?”

“No, I don’t mean it like-” He sighs. “You can’t just stay in bed with me all day.”

Patrick shrugs. “I’m just hanging out for a little while.” He flips a page.

David bites back a smile. Despite how awful he feels, he is relieved not to have to spend all day alone, or worse - all day at the motel. Either way, he’s glad to have his fiance around.

He rests against Patrick once again, his cheek smushed against his shoulder, scanning through the book to try to figure out what it’s about. It doesn’t work.

Patrick takes a sip of his tea, David takes a sip of his own.

David groans. “We were going to get lunch.”

“We can get lunch literally any other day, David,” Patrick says, his voice so contagiously calm.

“Yeah,” David sighs. “But you haven’t eaten yet.”

Patrick stops mid-page flip. “Oh yeah, oops.”

David scoffs. “Can you like, go eat so I don’t feel any more guilty for ruining everything.”

Patrick laughs and folds the corner of his page the way that makes David cringe. “I can do that.”

“Nothing cooked, please! I can’t smell food right now.”

“Gotcha!”
