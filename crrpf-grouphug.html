Title: Group Hug
Fandom: Critical Role RPF
Summary: Matt is sick, his pals worry
Word count: 400

Group Hug

“Can I...” Matt pauses to rub his eyes. “Can I have a marching order?”

They all start to discuss where they would be, while Matt continues rubbing at his face and ending it with a yawn. 

Marisha catches his eye, mouthing a, “you okay?”

He nods. But she can tell when he’s lying. She narrows her eyes at him, but contributes her opinion to the marching order on the map.

He’s thinking weirdly slow tonight, she notices. She hasn’t talked to him since he showed up that afternoon to prepare for the game. In retrospect, he was acting weird then, too. Maybe he’s just tired. None of them have been getting as much sleep as they should be.

Matt is quick to sit down when the battle’s over with, and takes a big drink of his water.

“Matt?” Liam repeated after his question. “DM?” He chuckles when Matt looks up with wide eyes.

“Jeez pardon me. What was that?”

“How many doors are in the room?”

“Oh! just um... just the one you came through.” He massages his temple, and Marisha frowns, hoping he doesn’t have a migraine coming on.

The break comes as soon as they find themselves in a safe place after the battle. Matt skedaddles to the lounge and sits down on the couch, leaning his head back and closing his eyes. 

Marisha sits next to him, and he seems to know it’s her because his eyes stay closed. She lays a hand on his arm and runs it up and down gently.

“You feeling okay?” She asks quietly. Everyone else is in the kitchen area getting snacks and drinks.

A few seconds go by before he shakes his head.

“What’s wrong?”

“I’m not sure, I just don’t feel well...” he mutters. “Might be getting sick.”

Marisha frowns and lifts a hand to his face. He’s definitely warm. Way too warm. “Have you been feeling like this the whole game?”

Matt doesn’t answer and that’s enough for her to know that he’s been running a fever since the stream started and just hasn’t said anything. Because of course not.

She sighs and leans against him. “Sorry.”

“I’ll be fine,” he says, finally lifting his head. “Do you have any ibuprofen?”

“Yeah, yeah I’ll go get it.”

When Marisha returns, the rest of the group is flocked around him asking if he was okay. He has a newly-opened bottle of water in his hand now, and his head is on Taliesin’s shoulder as they discuss the game.

“Jesus, I would’ve gone easier on you if I knew you were sick,” Liam says. 

“Psh, not possible. Caleb knows too much.”

The group laughs, and soon enough Matt is medicated and ready to return to the table.

“Let’s go have a filler episode, shall we?” Travis says as they head back into the studio. Everyone agrees. 

They’re not about to make their DM work harder than he needs to.
