Title: Compliance
Fandom: Scorpion
Summary: Toby tricks Walter into resting
Word count: 1.5k

Compliance

Toby sort of enjoys being the resident doctor at Scorpion when everyone gets sick. It gives him an opportunity to be nice to his friends for once. Kind of. The group managed to catch a cold on a mission that he stayed behind on, which is great for him, not so great for the grumpy, sniffly geniuses (and Paige) in his midst.

“Is this really necessary?” Happy growls as he presses the thermometer into her ear. The mere fact that she isn’t punching him in the stomach is enough to signify that she’s not feeling good.

“No, not if you guys are okay with possibly having a foreign illness...”

Paige groans from her desk. Considering she had to survive an entire flight with the rest of the whiny people, Toby doesn’t blame her for being a little exasperated. “Do we have any cold medicine?”

“Next thing on my list!” he calls, right before the device beeps. He hums thoughtfully. “That would be a fever, my love. Not too awful, but take it easy, okay?” he doesn’t miss the small smile she has as he presses a kiss to her overly-warm cheek. “Your turn, Sly! You want a kiss too?”

“I’ll pass!”

Despite Sylvester’s own frustration with the situation, he lets Toby look him over just like Paige and Happy. No doubt that he’s already done it all himself, but it seems to offer him peace of mind.

His temperature is elevated, not quite as high as Happy’s, and his breathing sounds normal for that of someone with a cold. Toby blows him a kiss as he packs up his stuff. Sylvester rolls his eyes and instead leans over to look behind them.

“Hey, Walter,” he greets.

And suddenly Toby was alert. “Ah ah, Walt!” Toby calls, stopping Walter in his tracks. “Ha! You thought you were gonna get away with this! Get over here!”

Walter sighs. “Unnecessary. If you checked over everyone else, then there’s no point in-”

Toby gets to him before he can finish his tangent. Walter is clearly not at his best if he isn’t quick enough to flinch. Instead, he glares at Toby while they wait for the thermometer to beep.

“Your fever is higher than any of theirs,” Toby informs with his best know-it-all voice. “Which means bed for you! You’re body’s stressed.”

“I don’t have time to be sick right now,” Walter mutters, flipping through the papers in his hand in an attempt to ignore him.

“Tough.”

“A cold doesn’t require bed rest.” And he walks away.

“He’s not wrong,” Happy says with a shrug. “He’ll be fine.”

“Yeah, sure, but he’s going to make himself sicker for longer.”

“That’s his problem, not yours.”

Toby huffs. She might be right, but that doesn’t mean he’s not going to involve himself anyway.

-----

He’s definitely going to involve himself, Toby decides, when he hears his boss’ coughing fit from the loft. That’s the sound of someone who definitely hasn’t been resting. It’s time to bug his boss. Toby grabs his medical bag and starts up the stairs, leaving the other sniffly geniuses behind.

Predictably, Walter is focused on a project. Toby imagines that the gadget isn’t that important, a mere time-waster so he doesn’t have to go downstairs with the others. He’s sniffling repeatedly despite the box of tissues sitting next to him.

“How you feeling?”

Walter startles, which triggers a cough. “Toby, what’re you doing here?” He doesn’t waste any time before going back to his project.

“Oh, just wanted to come say hi. Hey, by the way, I picked up some meds if you want any.”

“No thanks. snf!”

Toby grabs a tissue from the box sitting right next to him and holds it in front of his face. Walter looks like he wants to refuse, but his runny nose clearly wins the fight when he accepts it.

“Back to my original question, how’re you feelings?”

“Sick, just like the others.”

“Well, the others took their medicine like good children, so they’re probably feeling a lot better than you are right now.”

Walter sighs, which sounds somewhere between horrible and disgusted as breath tries to gurgle through his clogged sinuses. He doesn’t even bother to do anything when Toby presses the inside of his wrist against his forehead.

“ ‘kay boss, your fever’s up. Maybe take a break for a while.”

“I’m busy.”

Toby looks over his shoulder to see a gadget that can only be described as the lovechild between a stapler and a slightly sadder stapler. “On an office gadget?”

“Mm.”

“Walt, what the hell is that thing?”

“It’s a remote controlled stapler.” He slid a piece of paper underneath, grabbed a handheld button in front of him, and pressed.

It stapled.

“Impressive. Exactly when is that going to be useful?”

“Well, it’s not fin- hhh Finished...”

“Mmhmmm.”

Walter sniffles thickly and runs the remains of the tissue (there’s an entire box, you idiot) under his nose. It doesn’t seem to help anything, as he ends up sneezing into the wad with a horrible squelch that has Toby cringing.

Toby does him the favor of grabbing a handful of clean tissues and pressing them into his less-snotty hand. Walter gives a nod of thanks before muffling a second sneeze into it. Toby simply sighs and starts rubbing his back.

“Why would I think you need medicine? You sound fantastic.”

Walter rolls his eyes. The poor guy truly does look awful. He’s looking more defeated by the second, and Toby knows that all he has to do is wear him down until he finally goes to bed.

“Do you have any coffee up here? I’m really craving a cup.” And he’s off, rifling through Walter’s cupboards like he owns the place.

“There’s coffee downstairs. In the kitchen. With everything else.” Walter’s annoyed tone might have meant something if he didn’t finish the sentence with a clogged snuffle.

“Oh yeah I know, I just thought I might as well make you a cup of tea too, huh? For your sore throat or whatever.”

He looks back smugly just in time to see his friend’s brows furrow, as if he’s about to ask how he knew about his sore throat. Of course Walter doesn’t ask, because his illness-gunked brain seems to remind him that oh yeah, Toby reads people for a living.

Toby doesn’t actually expect to find tea in Walter’s cupboard, and yet there’s a box of herbal tea sitting in the back with the other stuff that he probably doesn’t bother with. He has a kettle, but it’s usually used for experiments rather than tea. Still, Toby fills it and sets it on the stove. He crosses his arms, leans against the counter, and watches his friend try to handle his runny nose with the sleeve of his shirt.

“Okay, you have tissues right there, do you realize that Sylvester would be borderline-homicidal right now if he saw you doing that?”

Walter glares and pointedly takes a tissue. “Why are you here, Toby?”

“Told you. Wanted to check on you and then make coffee.”

“You’re not making coffee.”

“I wanted to check on you and then make you tea. Because you’re sick and stubborn and clearly suffering from some blow to your ego because you’re sicker than the others even though you’re all infected with the same stupid cold.”

“My ego is fine.”

Toby sighs loudly. “Walt, you don’t like what this cold is doing to your brain. I get it. Happy’s the same way.” Walter’s lips form a thin line. Until he has to open his mouth to breathe, that is. “But if you don’t take care of yourself instead of pushing yourself, you’re not going to make things any better. Honey?”

Walter huffs. And nods.

Toby finds the honey, a feat to which he makes a victorious noise. He has a nice cup of tea made before long, and surprisingly, when he turns around, Walter is sitting on the couch.

“Are you happy now?” He mutters as Toby carefully hands him the hot mug.

“You bet I am. How about some medicine too while you’re feeling compliant? It’ll boost efficiency.”

“...Okay.”

Toby squeezes his friend’s shoulder before going downstairs to grab the medicine. Paige stares, wide-eyed.

“Don’t tell me you actually convinced him to take medicine?” She said, amazed.

“Oh, Paige, I’ve got him sitting down and sipping honeyed tea.”

“Yeah right,” Happy scoffs from her station. “That’s impossible.”

“Not when you’re me!”

She rolls her eyes and gives a fond chuckle.

When he goes back upstairs, Walter is looking rather adorable. He’s unbuttoned his shirt to reveal an old college t-shirt underneath, and he’s snuffling between sips of tea.

Toby grabs the box of tissues on his way to the couch and sets it down next to him. He doesn’t bother reading the dosage on the box of meds before handing a couple to his friend.

“Thanks,” Walter says simply, without even looking at him. And sure, he’s not fantastic at expressing himself, but the simple ‘thanks’ is enough for Toby to know that he’s succeeded in his endeavor.
