Title: So in Love
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Alexis has her reasons for staying at work, and those reasons are Ted
Word count: 800

So in Love

Alexis: r u coming or what

David: yes omg calm down

Alexis: david hurry up

David rolled his eyes at his phone and stuffed it in his pocket. He found the bottle, grabbed the car keys, and went off. He told Alexis to stay home like a normal person but of course she wasn’t going to listen. Since when did she ever take his advice?

David wasn’t even surprised that she looked completely normal when he walked into the vet clinic. Alexis was great at looking good all the time. Well, he liked to think that they both were.

“David!”

There it was. She may have looked fine but she sounded horrible.

“Did you bring- yes thank you!” Alexis squealed as David set the bottle on the counter, as if he’d brought her a prize and not cold and flu medication.

He looked toward the exam room and saw that Ted was busy with a patient. He leaned forward and kept his voice low as he said, “Just tell him you’re sick so he’ll end you home.” Why didn’t she do that in the first place? Why did she even go into work at all, he wondered. She was clearly not feeling well that morning.

Alexis growled, and then snuffled thickly. She tried to open the medicine as subtly as possible, side-eyeing the exam room in case Ted came out. “I can’t.”

“He’s not that much of a monster,” David laughed. Well, he thought he was funny, even if Alexis wasn’t laughing.

“No, I just...” a sigh. “I like being here, okay? Leave it alone!”

Oh. Oh.

Was she really so in love with him that she’d rather spend the day miserable at work? Damn.

“Okay, but like,” he rolled his eyes. He shouldn’t care. She was the one making the stupid decision. “Will you just like, drink water and stuff?”

Alexis looked like she was about to make a joke about how ‘awww he cared’ before she turned away to sneeze. David cringed and recoiled.

“Bless you!” Came from the other room.

“Ugh,” she grumbled. “He’s so nice.”

David huffed. “Okay, I’m leaving. But just for the record, if you die, I’m going to be very obnoxious at your funeral.”

“Bye, David.”

-----

David was in the middle of filing his nails when his dad walked in (without so much as knocking, of course).

“Where’s your sister?”

David raised an eyebrow before he answered. “She’s... at the clinic, you know, where she’s been all day.”

“Oh,” it was clear that the gears in Johnny’s head were turning slowly. David continued filing his nails. “She went to work in her condition?”

“I mean, it’s not like she has tuberculosis.” Probably not, anyway, although she did keep him up all of last night coughing.

“Yeah, but still, I would’ve thought she’d jump at the chance to stay home from work.”

“I mean,” David couldn’t hold back a chuckle. “Under normal circumstances, like, sure. But maybe there’s something at work that she’d rather, you know, be doing.”

“Oh.” Johnny’s eyes widened. “Ohhh.”

“Mhmm.”

“Huh. I didn’t realize that the two of them...”

“Oh, they’re not.”

Johnny nodded slowly, looked like he was about to say something before David’s phone started to buzz.

He picked up faster than he’d like to admit when he saw Ted’s name on the screen.

“Hello...?”

“Hey David. So, I think you should come pick up your sister? I think she’s getting sick, and I mean, I don’t even know why she came into work today.” The concern in his voice would be adorable if David wasn’t so jealous of his sister having such a cute relationship with her ex.

“Oh, um, okay, she wants me to pick her up?”

Ted’s voice got quieter. “See, that’s the thing. I... haven’t actually sent her home yet... I wouldn’t usually just ask you but it’s really busy here and I don’t want her walking in the cold when she’s already not feeling well, and-”

“No, it’s fine.” David went to find the keys. “I’m on my way.”

-----

Alexis looked even worse when he got there. Her makeup had clearly worn off, which was concerning in and of itself. She and Ted were both behind the counter, an open first aid kit sitting atop it while Ted seemed to be putting a thermometer in her ear.

She gasped when she noticed David. “Wha- you called my brother?”

“I wanted to make sure you had a ride home!”

She was pouting like a kid, but David knew that his sister was soaking up the attention just like she always had.

“Yep, that’s a fever!” Ted informed in his usual cheery demeanor. “Go home and rest, Alexis.”

Alexis sniffled and whined. “What about you, though?”

“The clinic will survive without you for a couple of days,” he said so kindly that David wanted to hate him.

She sighed dramatically. “Fine, snf!”

Once the two of them were in the car, David couldn’t help sending a knowing look his sister’s way.

Alexis growled. “What, David? Why are you looking at me like that?”

He shrugged innocently and started the car. “No reason.”
