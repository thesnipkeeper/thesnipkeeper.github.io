The Foggy Job

His entire body still aches from the job that they’d done just a couple days earlier. He longs to sleep in, but then hears the others already awake and realizes that he already did sleep in.

Eliot should have known the moment he woke up. He’s more tired than usual, snapping at Hardison and Parker when they don’t deserve it (as opposed to snapping at them when they do deserve it). Sophie tries to make conversation with him over breakfast and he just brushes her off. He’s not in the mood to talk.

He’s also not particularly hungry, so he skips his usual cereal and sticks to a cup of coffee instead.

It’s when he’s in a basement hallway, waiting for Parker to bring the mark’s thugs in his direction that he realizes something is actually wrong. The tiredness, the fact that the coffee is sitting in his gut like an achy reminder that it’s done him no good today... it’s no fluke.

He doesn’t have time to think about it further before he’s getting punched in the gut. He nearly vomits right then and there, but rage wins out in the end and he punches the guy in the face, knees him in the stomach, punches him again. One down, two to go.

Nausea tries to force itself up his throat once again, so he gets the second guy down. The quicker these two are out, the quicker he can deal with this.

“You good, Eliot?” Nate asks over the comms, without any real concern. He checks in with them, but with the confidence that they’re all far too good at what they do.

Good... good? His stomach’s cramping, he’s gonna puke anytime now. But the fight, yeah he’s got that.

“Just peachy,” Eliot growls before taking the third guy down.

“Parker, how about you?”

He doesn’t pay attention to Parker’s response as he looks over the three men on the ground. Well, two on the ground and one shoved into a nearby storage bin. They all seem pretty fucking unconscious.

Eliot enjoys the quiet for a moment while adrenaline continues to pump through him. He takes a deep breath, and immediately vomits into a nearby trash bin.

It’s nasty. Coffee doesn’t taste nearly as good coming back up.

Just as soon as it happens, there’s a cacophony of voices in his ear.

“Ew, what the hell?” Hardison.

“Eliot, God...” Sophie.

“What was that? Are you dying?” ... Parker.

“I’m fine.” He growls, and wipes his mouth uselessly. “Let’s get outta here.”

-----

They all settle in for a drink as soon as the job is done. Soon the client, a younger man who’s been caring for his father since high school, would be by, and he would receive enough money to pay his bills, get the care and supplies they haven’t been able to afford. Ten times over.

Tonight, they’ll relax. Or the others will, at least.

Eliot passes on drinks. Usually a beer is the perfect end to a day like this, but tonight he’s having trouble keeping his eyes open. There’s still an unsettled feeling in his stomach, waiting to screw him over like a brutal predator, threatening him. His head hurts, which really doesn’t do anything to help the nausea.

“Eliot!”

Eliot stops massaging his temples to look up and find everyone staring at him with raised eyebrows. “What? What do you want?”

Hardison chuckles. “I said your name like five times. What’s with you tonight, man?”

If only he knew. “I’m tired, Hardison. Jesus.”

“Grumpy, too,” Parker comments.

Nate nods, sighs, and huffs out a quick laugh. “I think we could all use a good night’s sleep.”

“Mind if I stay here, Nate?” Hardison asks before downing the last of his beer.

“Yeah, me too,” says Parker. “It’s really late, you guys. We’re coming back tomorrow, anyway.”

Nate waves them off. “Do what you want. I’m going to sleep as soon as I get upstairs.”

Eliot’s secretly thankful that it’s happening; he’s not sure that he could stay awake for the ride home.

-----

They end up in the living room (except for Sophie, who’s either sleeping in the guest room or Nate’s room). The three of them have done this before, it’s nothing new. Eliot takes the floor, Parker usually gets the couch. Hardison just ends up passing out after a night of messing with his computer stuff.

Eliot’s often the first to fall asleep. He’ll listen to Parker and Hardison’s quiet conversations and then pass out where he lay.

Tonight, however...

His stomach hurts again. The nausea that’s been threatening him since they got back is surfacing. Part of him just wants to puke everything up right away and get it over with, but the other part of him wants to ignore the fact that it’s happening at all.

He shudders.

Hardison looks up from his laptop, wide-eyed as if he’s surprised to see that Eliot’s still awake. “Are you cold?”

“Little bit,” Eliot admits with a huff. “Why does Nate keep it so damn-”

“Uh, not Nate,” Hardison interrupts. “Me. And it’s a perfect seventy-five degrees in here, thank you very much.”

Eliot scoffs. “Then why-?” Oh. Well, shit. “Shit.”

Hardison raises an eyebrow. “What?” He demands.

“Think I’m gettin’ sick.” Maybe he should have figured that out a lot earlier. But damn it, he had better things to do today. He should’ve just gone home...

“I mean, yeah.”

Eliot glares. “What’s that supposed to mean?”

Hardison blinks, then shrugs and goes back to his dumb computer. “You’re right, you’re right. Healthy people puke all the time.”

Eliot’s ready to gripe some more, but instead he just wraps his blanket tighter around himself and shivers again. A fever. It’s been a while since he’s had one of those. Hell, it’s been a while since he’s been sick at all. His body usually has better things to do, like, y’know, fight bad guys.

Something’s hurled his way, and he flinches until he realizes that it’s Hardison’s blanket. Eliot nods his thanks and bundles himself up.

-----

He wakes up. Kind of. He never truly fell asleep, instead lying with his eyes closed, listening to Parker snore and Hardison tapping on his keyboard, wishing that he could fall asleep.

He’s groggy at first, trying to figure out why he’s suddenly wide awake. And then his stomach cramps.

He’s going to barf. Like, now.

Eliot throws the blankets off and rushes away. Hardison says something that he doesn’t hear, because he’s too damn busy making it to the bathroom before he pukes all over the place.

-----

He’s not sure how long he’s been in there. Five minutes? An hour? When there’s a knock on the door.

“Eliot, you good?”

“I...” He hasn’t realized how foggy everything is until he tries to form a sentence that’ll at least show Hardison that he hasn’t choked to death. “I’m...” He’s tired. His brain isn’t working properly. “I feel weird.”

He thinks back to the last time that he was this sick. It was some kind of flu he’d picked up on the plane on the way to a job. He’d forgotten to mention to them how much illnesses like this really take him down. He doesn’t get sick often, but when he does, it’s... well, it’s bad.

As much as he wants to hide and tell everyone he’s fine, Hardison’s concern isn’t misplaced.

“A- alright, man, do you need anything? Water or- or somethin’?”

Eliot nods, then realizes that Hardison can’t see him. “Water,” he rasps. To wash the horrible taste out of his mouth, if anything. He feels washed out and drained beyond belief. The idea of going back to bed is a mere fantasy, hopefully water will... make things less horrible.

A knock on the door. “Can I come in?”

Eliot lazily reaches up and turns the knob, letting the door swing open by himself. A cup of water appears on the floor next to him, and he snatches it up and drinks greedily before he can think to do anything else.

“Easy, man...” Hardison warns, but it’s way too late.

Eliot drains the whole cup. He sighs, hands it to Hardison to refill it, and then leaves the newly filled cup next to him.

Hardison sits on the edge of the tub. He’s staring.

“What?” Eliot growls. He doesn’t mean to sound quite as grumpy as he does, but his raspy voice certainly doesn’t help.

“Nothing,” Hardison shrugs, clears his throat. “You just don’t look too good.”

“Well, I don’t feel too good, either.” Especially now. He should’ve known that chugging water was a bad idea. He did know that chugging water was a bad idea. And yet... “Shit...” a hand goes to his mouth until he can get over the toilet again. Eliot chokes up a mouthful of the water he’d just drank. It’s better than dry heaving, he decides, so it’s not so bad.

There’s a hand on his back as he coughs up water a second time. He doesn’t try to shrug it off, it’s not nearly as annoying as his own body right now.

“You’ve got a fever! Dude, maybe we should take you to a doctor or something.”

“They ain’t gonna do anything,” he says with a sigh. “If I can handle a stab wound or a...” he pauses, swallows thickly, “... a gunshot, I can handle this.” God, he’s so tired.

Hardison doesn’t have an argument for that. They don’t do well with hospitals, none of them do. They either involve bad memories, or require a fake identity and medical history that just makes it a chore. Others might not expect that Eliot’s the one to take the doctor role of the group when it comes to injuries, or that one time that Parker ate bad Chinese food and ended up in the fetal position on Nate’s couch for two days.

“Sure,” Hardison agrees. “Are you good?”

“I’m... good.”

Hardison gives him a pat on the back, lingers, and leaves.

He’s not good.

Some time passes before Eliot decides that he’s done throwing up. He decides that he’s going to stand up, get back to bed (well, “bed”) and sleep this off. Maybe he’s the one who ate something bad this time, although that didn’t usually come with a fever and chills this intense.

Not a problem, he’s dealt with worse.

He goes to pull himself up, except his vision darkens the moment he puts any weight on his legs. Eliot lowers himself back to the floor, shudders, and decides to fall asleep right there. Good enough.

-----

Eliot’s vaguely aware of a cold hand on his neck. He’d smack it away if he wasn’t so tired.

“...burning up, Nate.”

“... get him to the couch?”

He grunts, tries to protest as he’s hoisted up. Someone - Hardison and Parker, he thinks - has each of his arms over their shoulders. He’s being dragged. He tries to help, but trying to use his legs seems to cause more problems.

He huffs as he’s dropped onto the couch. His stomach doesn’t agree with all of the sudden movement, but he can’t bring himself to care because he’s so damn comfortable right now. Has Nate’s couch always been this comfortable? Damn.

“I knew I shouldn’t have just left him...” Hardison grumbles.

“Well, it’s not your fault,” Nate says. He lays a hand on Eliot’s forehead and gives a heavy sigh. “Let’s keep an eye on him tonight.”

Eliot can’t resist the couch any longer. He falls asleep.

-----

He’s sticky, sweaty. Eliot shoves off whatever blanket is stifling him and sits up. He rubs his eyes, and only then notices his surroundings. The coffee table is cluttered with medicine and cleaning supplies and various unopened bottles of water and Gatorade. He furrows his brows, but doesn’t have time to think about it much more before Parker walks in.

“Oh hey, you’re awake. Eliot’s awake!” She yells to the rest of the house and worsens a headache he’s just realizing exists.

He then notices the bucket in her hands when she sets it down next to him. He raises an eyebrow, smelling cleaning solution. Oh.

“Wha- did I...?”

“Hurl? Like twice since we found you dead in the bathroom last night.”

“I don’t...” he sighs, drags a tired hand down his face. “I don’t... remember.”

“Yeah, I mean, you were pretty out of it.”

He doesn’t feel much better, but at least he’s more.. aware. If that’s a good thing at all. “How long was I sleeping?”

“Umm,” she screws up her face in thought. “Well, it’s four now, so-”

“In the afternoon?”

“Yeah?”

“You owe me a new shirt, Eliot!” Are Hardison’s first words when he shows up.

Parker snorts. “You puked all over him last night.” She’s giggling, because of course she is.

“Probably did your shirt a favor,” Eliot mumbles. “Where’s Nate and Sophie?”

“Client meeting,” they answer in unison.

“So,” Hardison plops down next to him on the couch. Eliot groans. “How’re you feelin’?”

“Horrible.”

“And what did we learn?”

“Excuse me?” Eliot asks, glaring pointedly.

“Well, when your friend asks, ‘are you good’ and you lie, then...”

“Hardison.”

“Fine, fine. Good to see you like, conscious, man.”

“Thanks.”
