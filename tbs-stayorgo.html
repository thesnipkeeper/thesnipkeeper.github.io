Stay or Go

He'd texted her to let her know he was coming. It still felt invasive when he walked into her house.

He wasn't sure what to expect when Sam called him to check on Joan, but finding her in her home office doing paperwork was not what he thought would happen when Sam described the state that Joan was in.

Maybe he should have expected it. They had dated, after all.

"Hi there," he knocked on the frame of the door.

Her cheeks were beet red. It was the first thing that he noticed. The second thing was the blanket wrapped around her shoulders, then the half-full cup of tea on her desk next to a box of tissues that looked like it'd traveled with her throughout the house.

Joan looked up and sniffed lightly. "Hi." With just the one word he heard how congested she was, how her throat was definitely sore. It made him want to wrap her up in a hug and never let her go, but they weren't there yet. Maybe never would be. Probably never would be. She sat up from her dependent lean against her desk. "So Sam put you up to this?"

"She did," he smiled. "But I would have come on my own had I known you were sick."

They hadn't seen much of each other the past few days. They'd been working on their own projects and really hadn't had time to touch base if it wasn't absolutely necessary. Sam was even out of town, and she knew that Joan was sick. Looking at her now, had Owen been that oblivious?

Joan cleared her throat and then winced. She took a sip of her tea, and it didn't look like it helped. "It's really not that bad. And for the record, I didn't think Sam would send you to my rescue because I mentioned a little cold."

This was not a little cold. Owen decided that now wasn't the time to point that out.

"Well, hopefully one of these things can help!" He held up a grocery bag from the pharmacy. It was full with every kind of medicine for every kind of symptom. It was the same treatment she received when she used to get sick as his girlfriend.

She smiled, and then had to turn away to cough. "You didn't have to."

"You know how Sam is. She'll stop everything to make sure that you're alright." he said, as if using their mutual friend and coworker as an excuse was going to do him any good.

She hummed. Coughed again, this one sounded like it came from deep in her chest.

"Tell you what, you'll get this," gesturing to the bag, "If you stop your work for a few seconds and sit down."

"I am sitting."

"You're sitting, but you're not relaxing."

Joan didn't look like she wanted to argue further, nor did she seem against the idea. She just looked sick and exhausted and she was probably desperate to do something to get her mind off of it, like work.

She nodded toward the living room, and they both made their way over and sat down. Well, Owen watched her sit down at first, and then joined her when he realized he was standing there awkwardly.

"Take your pick!" He announced as he placed the stuff in front of her.

Joan laughed as she took the bag and started to pull everything out.

"Tylenol should be fine for now," she sniffed, and pawed at her nose with her sleeve (a very un-Joan thing to do; she really must not have felt good).

She took the medicine, turned on the TV, and the two of them sat. It was uncomfortable at first, for Owen - specifically - because Joan kept talking to him and asking him what he wanted to watch all while he was wondering whether she was waiting for him to leave.

But if Joan wanted him gone, it wouldn't be a hint that he'd have to pick up. It would be a 'you can go now, Green'.

"The kitchen's well-stocked for once if you're hungry," she said after a while. "I haven't really wanted anything the past couple of days. Of course I get sick the week I finally go grocery shopping for real food."

Owen frowned. "You haven't eaten? At all?"

"I've eaten, just not anything that can't be microwaved in under two minutes." She smiled, as if that would do anything to wipe off the worry he had painted on his face.

"Well, I'm going to cook something for both of us, then." Owen stood up decidedly, and then turned around with less confidence. "If… that's alright, I mean."

Joan smiled once again. "Yes, of course it is."
