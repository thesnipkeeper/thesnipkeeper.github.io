Title: Talks Machina
Fandom: Critical Role RPF
Summary: Taliesin isn't the one who's nice and warm
Word count: 900

Talks Machina

They'd all been working ridiculously hard. Between all of the new shows, Critical Role itself, and the rapidly-growing fanbase… yeah, there was a lot to do. It wasn’t out of the ordinary to walk into the lounge and find one or two members of the cast and crew asleep on the sofa.

Matt had been working his hardest to keep everyone happy and healthy during all of the hard work. He was a Godsend, really. It would have been easy for everyone to get worn out if he wasn't around to keep spirits high.

Today it was Matt who was having trouble keeping up his mood.

He wasn't grumpy (Taliesin wasn't sure he'd ever seen his friend grumpy), but he was more dismissive than usual. Less talkative, finding quieter places to get work done. Matt was just as sweet to everyone as always, with a little more yawning thrown in. 

So work was finally getting to him, then. It shouldn't have been a surprise.

Taliesin had to admire his ability to work no matter how exhausted he was. Matt spent the whole day helping everyone and answering questions when Marisha wasn't around, and no one else seemed to notice how tired he was today because he was just that good at hiding it.

He was just as helpful when Taliesin went to him with a question, and proceeded to ask how he was doing. That fucking precious guy. 

He always found himself as a guest on Talks, not that Taliesin minded, really, but he definitely didn’t mind when he found out that Matt was the other guest tonight. Talks was usually considered work, but he had a feeling that sitting in front of a camera and drinking would be considered a break for Matt.

"You wanna take a nap before Talks starts?" Taliesin was only half-joking. Matt obviously hadn't been sleeping the past week.

"Hm? Oh psh! Naw, I'm good."

"Fine fine, I tried," Taliesin chuckled. 

The two of them ended up in the kitchen to get some final non-alcoholic drinks before the stream. 

“Since when do you drink tea?” 

Matt looked up from his cup with a shrug. “It’s a tea day, y’know?”

“Everyday is a tea day for me.”

Matt snorted. “God, that was bad.”

-----

Brian made some terrible jokes and then asked some questions just like every Tuesday. Taliesin was in the middle of answering a question about Caduceus’ motives when Matt casually leaned against him. It was little gestures like that that reminded him how much he loved Matt and his friendship with him. 

Matt knew that physical contact was Taliesin’s favorite part of relationships. The two of them were always snuggling and hugging and maybe... holding hands every once in a while. To be able to do it so shamelessly on stream warmed Taliesin’s heart, chasing away any on-camera jitters he had.

He finished the question while wrapping an arm around Matt’s shoulders and pulling him closer. Matt’s head was immediately on his shoulder, his full upper body leaning against him. It was nice, close, warm…

Taliesin furrowed his brows while Matt and Brian were cracking jokes about Brian’s possible career as a horror novelist. Matt was really warm. It would have been comforting had it not been such a concern. 

“Wow, you’re nice and warm,” Matt chuckled, snuggling closer.

“Wha- thank you?” He bit back the I’m warm?!.. It probably wasn’t a good idea to make a big deal about it on the show. Still, he found himself offering as much body heat as possible to a burning-and-somehow-still-shivering Matt.

It all made sense now. The sleepiness, the fucking tea. Matt was sick. 

He stayed cuddled against Taliesin for the rest of the show.

“Don’t forget to love each other, and is it Thursday yet?”

The minute the stream cut out, Taliesin pulled away from him to get a look at his face. “Why are you so warm?” he didn’t give Matt a chance to answer before he started feeling his face with the back of his hand. Matt closed his eyes and sighed as if he’d been found out. “Matt…”

“Oh shit…” Brian said. “Buddy, you okay?”

“I’m fine, you guys,” he chuckled at first like they were being ridiculous. He finally got serious after some intense looks from both Brian and Taliesin. “I- I’m probably coming down with something, but it’s just a fever,” he admitted. He was still shaking and shivering, so Taliesin grabbed the blanket on the back of the couch and wrapped his friend in it, earning no complaints.

“Is it? Just a fever, I mean.”

Matt sighed.

“You’ve been feeling like this all day, haven’t you? That’s why you’ve been acting so strange,” Taliesin frowned. He tried tamping down the frustration. “Why didn’t you say something?”

But of course Matt didn’t say anything. He never did. He spent so much time trying to solve other people’s problems that he constantly forgot about his own. Taliesin and Marisha had to count on each other to remind him to eat some days when he got deep into the DM planning zone.

Both of them had been too busy to truly check on him. 

“I should have, I’m sorry.” And he did sound sorry, to his credit, but God damn. 

“I’ll get some meds from the first aid kit,” Brian said. “You want drugs, right?”

Matt chuckled. “Sure.” He hesitated before leaning against Taliesin once again, stifling a painful-sound cough as he did. “I’m sorry,” he repeated.

“No, it’s… it’s okay. We’ve all been busy,” Taliesin pressed a kiss to his friend’s head and sighed. “You’re the one who’s going home with Marisha, though.”

Marisha had a track record of bullying (with love) Matt into taking care of himself. Anytime he was sick, she could be trusted to keep him in bed and smother him with affection until he was well again.

“She’s not gonna let me get anything done…”

Taliesin smirked. “I know.”
