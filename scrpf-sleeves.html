Sleeves

He's a mess. He knows he's a mess, everyone else does too. But this is a meeting that couldn't be skipped, especially when you're the one conducting it.

The meeting goes about as well as one can go when he's constantly interrupting himself with sneezes and sniffles. And of course everyone has to bless him. The meeting would be finished so much earlier if he wasn't dealing with this stupid cold.

When the meeting's over, he snuffles desperately. He needs to blow his nose, needed to for a good while, but that's one thing his coworkers did not need to witness. He scans the room for a box of tissues, coming up empty.

Dan sneezes, which then prompts a cough.

"Oh God..." He mumbles, before coughing again.

"Bless you."

He turns around to see Noah who's seemed to linger in the doorway on his way out.

"Ugh," Dan scrubs at his nose with his sleeve. It's not particularly sanitary or dignified, but it's just Noah watching him. "Thanks. Snf! Sorry, I've been fighting this cold all day."

Noah nods, smiles. "And it looks like it's winning!"

"Yeahhh, I-" Dan sneezes, getting his sweater sleeve to his nose just in time to save his friend from germs. "Fuck!" He sniffles wetly.

Noah makes a sympathetic sound and moves to lay a hand on his arm, unbelievably sweet just like everything else he does. "Dan, you really don't sound good. Why don't you go home before you get the whole staff sick?"

"I would love to," Dan breathes out congestion evident in his voice. "But I can't just go home and do nothing because of a cold. I'll be careful."

"Mm'kay, might wanna start being careful by changing out of the snotty sweater."

Dan takes a look at his sleeves. They're damp and certainly snotty. He grimaces and nods. "Right. Good idea." He stops to think, and then just pulls the sweater over his head, much to Noah’s surprise. He has a T-shirt on, and that would be just fine until he can grab another sweater from his car.

-----

After a long day of trying very hard not to infect any of his coworkers, Dan is thankful to be at home where he can let loose a little bit. Where he doesn't have to worry about spreading germs.

That's the nice thing about living alone. Even though living alone when you're sick is possibly the worst thing in the world.

He sneezes and startles Redmond who was peacefully asleep on his lap until Dan had to rudely awaken him.

Dan sniffles and wipes his nose on the back of his hand, and then his palm. "Sorry, Red."

Redmond seems to accept the apology, as he lays back down with a simple huff.

The two of them remain comfortable on the couch together, Dan leaned against the arm with a pillow he dragged down from his bedroom. He hasn't yet decided whether he'll be sleeping here or make the trek upstairs, but right now he just wants to watch TV until he's no longer paying attention to the awful cold he's afflicted with.

He sneezes again, and this time Red doesn't even react. He must have gotten used to it by now. Dan smiles and pets his head.

"You're a good boy, Red," he says between sniffles. He could probably have done with picking up some tissues and maybe medicine to conk him out on his way home from work, but he can't be bothered to do it now. For right now, his sweatshirt sleeve will do just fine, and having a warm, cuddly dog is sort of like medicine, right?

He doesn't realize he's dozing until the phone wakes him up. Noah's video calling him? Dan picks up before he remembers how truly awful he must look up close on a camera.

Noah's smile is the first thing he sees. "Hey, how're you feeling."

"Ugh," is Dan's answer as he scrubs at his nose with the rough fabric of his hoodie. "I haven't felt this gross in years. I really hope I didn't give it to anyone today."

Noah hisses in sympathy. "Yeah, we'll find out." A few seconds go by that Dan spends pressing his sleeve to his nose before Noah speaks again. "I didn't really want anything, just wanted to see how your cold was treating you."

"Terribly, but thank you."

"Do you need anything?"

Dan blinks. "What?"

"Do you... Need anything?" He repeats. "I could pick up some stuff and bring it over?"

Dan sighs stuffily. "You don't have to do that. Snf! I'm so contagious right now."

"Have you eaten dinner yet?"

He thinks for a moment, and cringes. "To be honest? No I have not. Or... Lunch. Oh God, I should-"

"Has your cold put you off pizza?"

"Oh God, never. But Noah-"

"What do you need? Besides pizza, I mean."

Dan opens his mouth to tell Noah he shouldn't come over to his germ-filled house and watch him blow his nose all night. But instead, he finds himself smiling and saying, "Tissues would be nice."

Noah nods. "Gotcha. Very important. Anything else?"

"Cold medicine?"

"Understood!" He says cheerily. "See you in a bit!"

Dan grins. "See you." He ends the call and immediately sneezes into the collar of his sweatshirt. Ugh. If Noah wasn’t already infected by him this morning, he’s definitely going to be by the time he leaves.

“Sounds like we’re going to have a visitor tonight,” he says to Redmond, petting the sleepy dog. “Thank fuck.”

Dan texts him to tell him that the door’s unlocked, because he doesn’t feel like getting up and letting Noah in, especially when it would mean disturbing the adorable animal asleep on his lap.

God, he’s excited to have another person around.

-----

“Knock knock!” Noah calls as he walks in the door.

Dan’s suddenly alert, his hazy attention on the TV broken. Noah’s holding a pizza, kicking the door shut expertly with his foot. There’s a plastic bag hanging from his arm as he makes his way over to the living area.

He sets the pizza down on the coffee table, and Redmond is suddenly very interested in Noah’s presence as well.

With a chuckle, he ruffles Red’s fur. “Sorry buddy, no pizza for you.” That’s when Noah finally turns to Dan and gets a good look at him. “Wow, you look...”

“I know,” Dan sighs miserably. He snuffles, his nose choosing this moment to be so stuffed up he can’t stop it from running. He sits up and pulls any piled blankets away to make room for Noah to sit.

“Good thing I got the good stuff, huh?” He says, pulling out a bottle of cold medicine.

Dan gasps. “Oh my God, I love you.” Fuck.

Noah just chuckles, brows furrowing a bit in confusion. “Well gee, I love you too, Dan.”

Thankfully, the pizza and Redmond’s determination to get to it is enough to distract them both from what just happened. Maybe Dan can chalk it up to delirium later.

They eat, and talk, and Noah pointedly opens the box of tissues he bought when Dan’s been struggling to eat pizza and sniffle at the same time.

“Sorry,” Dan says as he finishes blowing his nose. “I did warn you.”

“You did,” Noah agrees with a nod. “But um, you- you aren’t going to work tomorrow, are you?”

Dan shakes his head quickly. “It was super irresponsible of me this morning, so no. My dad actually bullied me home an hour early.”

“Good. Hopefully you haven’t quite made it to patient zero status, then.”

Dan scoffs, and then breaks into a coughing fit that signifies exactly how much he really is in patient zero status.

-----

Dan wakes to a cold dog nose on his face. Redmond has decided it’s time for him to get up, then.

Dan groans as he sits up, only to realize he’s on his couch. His muscles ache just as much as one would expect from spending fifteen hours on the same couch. His nose decides to run the moment he’s upright, and he drags his sleeve across it until the gross feeling isn’t as present.

That’s when he registers the sound of movement behind him in the kitchen, and the faint sound of music. Dan looks over the back of the couch and immediately smiles.

“Oh.”

Noah looks up. He’s in the same T-shirt he was in last night, but he seems to have borrowed a pair of sweats. He smiles.

“Good morning!”

Dan nods, intent on wishing him a good morning as well, and ends up sneezing instead. The goop inside his head shifts and he’s forced to find a tissue and disappear behind the couch once again.

“Bless you!”

“Thanks, ugh...” He sniffles and finally decides to wander over to the kitchen. He’s careful to steer clear of any open food because he’d hate to infect anything Noah plans on eating. “Are you making pancakes?”

Noah chuckles, a bit sheepishly. “I hope that’s okay.”

“It’s more than okay.” He clears his throat. “Um, sorry for falling asleep on you last night.”

“You needed it. It was pretty late, anyway.”

It was. And he had also just taken the medicine Noah graciously provided and spent the rest of the night more out of it than he already was.

“Where did you sleep?” Dan asks as he drags a tired hand down his face. He takes a seat at the island, propping his chin on his palm.

“Guest bedroom?”

“Oh, yeah that makes sense.” A sniffle. “Sorry, I’m... foggy. Thanks for staying so late last night, I- it was nice to have someone around.”

Noah stops his cooking and looks at him, offering possibly the warmest smile in the world. “Well, it sucks to be alone when you’re sick. I know you like having people around.”

Dan smiles. “Yeah. Still, you may have doomed yourself.”

“Oh, probably,” Noah says honestly. He flips a pancake.

The two of them eat breakfast together. It feels like last night, almost, but something’s different. Dan’s cold-gunked brain doesn’t figure it out right away.

But as he finds himself getting lost in Noah’s presence and realizes he hasn’t heard a word he’s been says, he finally figures it out.

He has a crush.

Fuck.

-----

It’s a couple days later when Dan’s cold finally lets up a little bit. He still has a cough, and his sinuses still feel full, but he’s not sneezing allover everything and infecting everyone in his general vicinity, so that’s nice.

But that’s also the day he gets a text from Noah.

The plague has found me, followed by an adorably staged selfie of Noah in his hotel room with a tissue covering the lower half of his face.

Dan has to bite back a smile, because the fact that he shared germs with one of his poor colleagues is not, in any way, adorable.

He knows what he has to do.

He buys tissues, and cold medicine, and a pizza. He makes his way to Noah’s floor of the hotel and knocks on the door with his elbow.

Noah opens the door, eyes wide with a surprised smile. His nose is just starting to turn pink, and his eyes are watery and swollen. He looks miserable and still manages to greet Dan with a cheery, “Hey!”

“I thought I should probably return the favor,” Dan says, nodding toward the pizza. He lets Noah lead him inside and close the door behind him. “Especially considering it’s my fault you’re sick. The only thing worse than being sick alone is being sick alone in a hotel room.” He speaks from experience.

Noah chuckles, which seems to trigger a cough that sounds downright painful. “Guess you don’t have to worry about catching it at least.” Brown eyes find Dan’s own and they look so impossibly kind. “Thanks, man.”

Dan nods. “I’m going to make sure to keep my germs to myself next time.”

“Good. But it’s not going to matter much if I’m over at your house again.”

Dan tries not to let his heart flutter at the idea of Noah spending another night at his home. At a loss of how to respond, he’s thankful that Noah ends up distracted by a sneeze.

Dan opens the pizza box and quickly takes a slice for himself. He can finally sort of taste things again, and that in itself is exciting.

He laughs at a terrible joke in a commercial and ends up coughing because of it. Noah immediately looks at him with concern.

“You’re still sick.”

“It’s just a cough,” Dan says, clearing his throat.

Noah frowns. “You didn’t have to come all the way here if you-”

“I’m fine,” Dan promises, laying a hand on his arm to quiet him. He immediately pulls it away and finds a very interesting spot on the pattern of the bedspread to pay attention to.

As if an adorable repeat of just a few days ago, Noah falls asleep in the middle of an episode of Househunters. Dan smiles. He should probably leave.

At least, that’s what his thinks before he ends up falling asleep as well.

The two of them wake up snuggled together, and that’s just the beginning of the time they begin to spend with each other.
