Title: Repulsion
Fandom: Blake Holsey High
Summary: Marshall and Corrine have their first kiss and then bad things happen
Word count: 2k

Repulsion

Marshall’s pretty sure he’s been sent on another plane of existence, because he feels otherworldly.

Lucas narrows his eyes at him from his side of the room. Marshall’s been humming for the past half hour.

“You’re way too happy to be doing math homework,” he says. “What’s up with you.”

Marshall just grins and shrugs. “I just had a really good day today, y’know?”

Lucas’ eyes get even narrower. “...Sure?”

“Corinne kissed me.”

Lucas goes from suspicious to surprise in a heartbeat. “Seriously?”

“Yup.”

“Dude...”

“I know.”

His first kiss.

Corinne kissed him. She actually kissed him.

-----

Marshall wanders into science club just as usual. He sits down, discusses the subject with his friends, and makes bad science puns with everyone. As usual.

But halfway through, his eyelids start to feel heavy. And then he feels weak. Moving takes more effort than it should, even picking up a pencil is hard work.

By the time they’re done with their discussion, he hasn’t even paid attention. Something about the transfer of energy or whatever.

He hasn’t realized how spaced out he is until Josie says something.

"Are you okay?"

"What? Yeah, why?"

Josie raises an eyebrow and scrutinizes him. "You look like you're about to pass out," she says, putting her hand on her hip.

Lucas nods and joins them. “Yeah, now that I think about it, you’ve been really out of it since you got here. Like, really out of it.”

Marshall scoffs, but he has a feeling that it doesn’t have as much effect as usual. “Guys, I’m fine.”

Before he even notices, Professor Z has joined the group as well, along with Corinne who’s now giving him a look of concern that would be adorable and flattering if he wasn’t so dead set on convincing his friends that nothing is wrong with him.

Z is about to say something when Marshall stands up. And immediately stumbles. The room spins around him and he’s not even aware of how he stays on his feet until he looks on either side of him and finds Z and Corinne on either side of him, effectively holding him up.

He groans and rubs his head as they help him into a nearby chair in lieu of the stool at the desk.

“What was that you were saying about being ‘fine’?” Corinne asks.

But the room is still spinning, and he suddenly feels very not good, because his stomach is turning just as much as the room is.

Marshall moans, folding over in the chair and carefully bringing a hand to his mouth. “I don’t feel so good...”

“Oh boy,” Z mumbles. “Lucas, get the- yes, thank you.”

Suddenly there’s a waste basket in his lap and he hugs it. He’s uncertain of what’s about to happen, but he’d rather not risk it and hork everywhere in front of his friends. And Marshall sits there like that for a few long seconds. Nothing happens, but at least he’s not as dizzy as long as he doesn’t move wildly anytime soon.

Z clears his throat. “Why don’t you give Marshall some privacy? He doesn’t exactly need an audience...” he says before he manages to shuffle out everyone but Lucas.

“I can help him back to our room when he’s ready,” Lucas says, moving back to his friend.

Professor Z nods. “Alright, that sounds good.”

And really, Marshall is glad he decided to stay instead of Corinne, because he isn’t sure he could puke in front of his girlfriend.

“You okay?” Lucas asks.

Marshall swallows hard. “Don’t know.” He no longer feels in immediate danger of throwing up. Actually, he feels a lot better. “I think I’m okay,” he says, carefully setting down the waste bin he didn’t have to use.

“Alright...” Z says slowly. “Still, maybe you should go see the nurse.”

Marshall’s already shaking his head before Professor Z can even finish the sentence. “I can’t, I have to study. We have a history test tomorrow and that’s my worst subject.”

He goes to stand, and both Z and Lucas are there to make sure he doesn’t face plant.

“Marshall, you should really-”

But he’s already got Lucas leading him out of the room. “See ya, Professor Z!”

The last thing he hears before they walk out of the classroom is Z sighing and, “See you two tomorrow.”

-----

“So what was up with that today?”

They’ve been studying quietly for a while, but it’s about the time that they get restless and in need for a break. It’s only fitting that Lucas breaks the silence with the question that he’s probably been dying to ask for a while.

Marshall shrugs. “Dunno. I was fine for a second and then I was so dizzy I thought I was gonna puke.”

“So are you sick?”

“I don’t think so? I don’t feel sick.”

Lucas chews the inside of his lip as he stares, like he wants to say something else, and Marshall pretends not to notice.

Eventually, though, he gets tired of it.

“Okay, what?” Marshall asks with a bit of an exasperated chuckled.

Lucas shakes his head and turns his attention to the book open in front of him on his bed. “Nothing!” He says. “As long as you’re not dying or whatever it’s fine!”

“Well, I’m obviously not dying.”

“Then you’re fine!” Lucas says as if he’s trying to convince himself.

“Yes, exactly!”

-----

Science class the next day, Marshall takes his seat next to Corinne, giving her a reassuring smile as she gives him a look that says are you okay?

He is. He’s fine. He’s felt fine ever since the close call in Science club yesterday.

But now...

It’s weird, he’s suddenly feeling lightheaded, and dizzy, and...”

“Marshall?” Corinne whispers, but apparently loud enough to interrupt Mr. Z as he’s no longer lecturing. She grabs his arm, and he blinks foggily.

That same feeling from yesterday is back, but worse.

“I- Corrine-” he tries to warn her, but it’s too late.

Corinne gasps as he vomits onto her lap. The rest of the class groans in discuss, but he can’t bring himself to care right now when his vision is going foggy and dark.

Suddenly he’s being eased onto the floor, a safe distance away from the puddle of puke he’s probably going to have to clean. Professor Z kneels in front of him and looks him over while Josie sets the waste bin at his side. Marshall lets his eyes slip shut, but stops himself from completely passing out.

“Dizzy,” he mutters. Even with his eyes closed, he can still feel the terrible vertigo.

“I can see that,” Z says before feeling his forehead. He hums thoughtfully. “Well, no fever...”

“But he’s obviously sick!” Josie. “I mean, look at him!”

“I don’t get it. He was totally fine this morning.” Lucas.

Marshall can hear Z giving the rest of the class quick instructions on their next assignment and dismissing them. The room couldn’t empty fast enough.

“Should I get the nurse?” Corinne asks, quiet and shaky.

Z hums uncertainly. “I’m... not sure. Marshall, are you still with us?”

“Mhm. Yeah.”

“Can you open your eyes?”

“Rather not.”

Z chuckles a bit. “Well, at least warn us, do you feel like you’re going to throw up again?”

He’s not sure. He just knows that he feels horribly dizzy and tired and uncomfortable. The nausea comes and goes at weird times.

“What weird times? What do you mean by that?”

And apparently he explained all of that out loud.

“Dunno.”

Z sighs out, “Alright, same as yesterday, folks. You guys don’t want to be late for your next class. Let them know that Marshall isn’t feeling well. And that... Corinne needs some time to change.”

They agree reluctantly, and Marshall hears the last set of footsteps leave before the classroom door closes.

After a few seconds, he carefully opens his eyes. Z is sitting a bit more comfortably next to him with all the patience in the world.

“Ugh...” Marshall groans at the vomit on the ground. “I’m sorry. I didn’t... I didn’t feel it coming at all.” He carefully sits up against the desk behind him and clears his throat of the gross in his mouth.

“Do you have any idea what’s making you sick?”

“No idea, Mr. Z. I just... I felt really dizzy and weird all of a sudden.” He blinks. “But I feel a little better now.”

Marshall drags the sleeve of his blazer across his mouth, and goes to do so with his nose as well before a handkerchief hangs in front of his face.

"Here, save your uniform," Z says kindly.

"Ah, thanks." Marshall makes use of it as Z stands up. "Sorry about the mess. Give me a sec and I'll-"

"No no, you just nearly fainted. I can't in good conscience let you do anything right now."

Marshall nods. "Sure." He massages his head. Throwing up has caused a subtle pressure. "I can't believe I totally puked on her, Mr. Z."

Z has the audacity to chuckle. "I think she'll forgive you, Marshall."

-----

"Maybe it's something in the science room that's making him sick?" Lucas suggests. "Maybe we have some sort of pathogen that only he's vulnerable to."

Josie scoffs. "I mean, that's possible, but-"

"He's fine once everyone leaves," Z says with a frown.

Needless to say, he asked Marshall to sit out today's science club. There's no doubt in Z's mind that he's in his room studying instead of resting, but that's probably as good as it's going to get.

"Yeah, that's true..." Lucas mutters. "Yesterday he started to feel better almost immediately after you guys left."

Corinne shakes her head. "That doesn't make any sense, though. Why would we make any difference?"

Lucas' eyes go wide with supposed realization. He looks to Corinne.

"Didn't... No, never mind."

"What? Lucas, what were you going to say?" She pushes.

He shakes his head. "It's just, didn't you say that you got shocked the other day?"

Corinne huffs. "Yeah..." She admits. "I accidentally touched a wire when I was working on my project. It wasn't a big deal, though."

Z has to keep himself from lecturing anyone on safety, because this obviously isn't the time. Still, a lesson to have at a later date.

"You..." Lucas clears his throat. "I mean, you and Marshall- maybe something happened when you um..."

Corinne's eyes go wide. "He told you?!"

"Well, of course he told me! I'm his roommate!"

Corinne groans in frustration. "What does that have to do with anything."

Z shakes his head, "I'm sorry, what are we talking about here?"

Josie crosses her arms and smirks. "Corinne and Marshall had their first kiss yesterday."

Oh. Oh, that is not what he was expecting. "Ah. Well, it's possible that, when you were shocked..."

Lucas nods. "You turned into repulsive magnets!"

Corinne scoffs. "What?"

"Marshall is repulsed by you," Lucas continues. He clears his throat at the threatening look she gives him. "I mean that when you kissed him, you somehow turned him into a repulsing magnet. He's only getting sick when he's near you, Corinne!"

Corinne looks like she wants to argue, but the facts line up. "He only threw up after I touched him yesterday," she admits. "And he was acting totally fine until he sat down."

Josie drags a hand down her face. "This is crazy. What are we going to do?"

-----

Marshall looks up from his textbook and watches his friends file in. “Guys? What are you all doing in my room?”

Lucas is holding a strange device, and Marshall hardly has time to react when Josie hands him a bucket. “Wha-”

Corinne walks in.

His stomach turns, and not in the fun way it should when he sees his girlfriend.

“Oh...” he groans.

“Just trust us, Marshall,” Lucas says before clipping something on his finger. “You’re allergic to Corinne.”

Corinne takes his other hand. He wants to pull away because he’s in danger of puking like, now. But Corinne seems confident in whatever is happening.

“Wha-? AH!”

The electricity courses through him, to Corinne, and back to him.

As Lucas unclips the thing off of his hand, Marshall realizes he feels a lot less dizzy.

“What just happened?” He demands.

“Well,” Josie clears her throat. “Being around Corinne was kind of making you sick?”

“...O...kay.”

“But you’re fine now?”

“Yeah, I think so?”

Corinne breathes out a sigh of relief. “Thank goodness. I’m not sure I could-” she clears her throat. “Anyway, see you tomorrow?”

Marshall blinks. “Sure.”
