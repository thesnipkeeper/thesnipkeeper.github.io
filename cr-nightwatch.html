Title: Night Watch
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: The gang keeps an eye on Caduceus
Word count: 1.1k

Night Watch

Caduceus wakes in the back of the cart, muttering sleepily. Jester doesn’t really pay him any mind, only watches as his brows furrow and he slowly sits up straighter. He’s been so tired all day, she hasn’t wanted to bother him, so instead she just scoots next to him and presses against his side.

He smiles and gives her a small nuzzle of affection. The smile disappears, and his eyes widen.

“Oh, oh no...”

He’s tense all of a sudden.

“What’s wrong, Caduceus?” Jester asks with a hint of panic.

He’s breathing weird. “I don’t feel so good. I think-”

“Fjord, stop that cart!”

“What? Why?”

The others turn to her with a raised eyebrow until they see Caduceus with a hand pressed to his mouth.

“Caduceus is about to puke!”

Fjord swears and stops the horses.

Thankfully, it’s not hard for a tall guy like Caduceus to get out by himself. She keeps hold of his arm to stop him losing his balance as he jumps down.

She winces the moment that they hear a cough, followed by a wave of vomit hitting the ground. He didn’t get very far, apparently.

“That sounds bad,” Beau says. “He sick?”

“I don’t know, maybe. I mean, he’s been pretty quiet today.”

Caleb hums in agreement. Nott frowns.

“Do you have anything to help, Jester? You are the other cleric, after all.”

Jester huffs at Nott and turns back to their firbolg friend. He’s wiping at his mouth with a kerchief that’s far too pretty to be abused. His usually bright eyes are foggy and red-rimmed. He also looks incredibly tired.

Jester gives him a sympathetic smile as he climbs back onto the cart. He sits down next to her and doesn’t even bother to react when she presses a hand to his forehead.

“Oh, Caduceus,” she frowns. “You’re really warm.”

“Am I?” he replaces her hand with his own, and belatedly realizes that that won’t help much. For someone who knows exactly what’s going on around him at all times, he’s not very good at realizing that he’s sick, apparently. “Huh.”

Beau winces. “Yeah dude, you don’t look so good.”

“I must’ve caught something back in town...” he states plainly, eyes fuzzy with exhaustion. “That’s not... very good.”

“It’s okay,” Jester promises. “We’ll take care of you.”

He nods. “I... appreciate it.”

They keep an eye on him for the rest of the ride, partially just to avoid getting puked on. Eventually, it gets dark and they decide that it’s best to bed down for the night.

Just like always, Caleb sets up his dome as they decide on watch shifts.

“I’ll take first,” Jester announces.

“Me too,” says Beau. “This place is giving me the creeps.”

“Alright,” Fjord says. “Guess I’ll take second.”

Yasha raises a hand. “I’ll take third.”

-----

Caduceus crashes the moment that he lies down. Granted, the guy’s always been good at that, like a superpower.

Beau hates seeing him sick. It’s weird, if she’s being honest, to see someone usually so put together, so miserable.

“You said he had a fever,” she says after a few minutes of comfortable night time silence. “Was it bad?”

Jester shrugged. “I mean, I don’t know how warm firbolgs usually are, you know? But he definitely felt hotter than normal.”

“So he’s like, actually sick then. Not just motion sick or whatever,” Beau sighs. “Poor guy.”

“I know...” Jester has a deep frown as she glances at their firbolg friend sleeping fitfully.

Cad is, of course, still snoring. Beau chuckles.

They sit quietly for a good while. Eventually, the two of them end up leaning into each other for warmth. They’re tired, waiting eagerly to be able to wake Fjord for the next watch.

A sound catches both of their attention.

Caduceus grunts and sits up from his bedroll.

“Caduceus?” Jester says.

“Everything okay?” Beau asks.

Caduceus wordlessly crawls outside of the dome, which has both of them immediately on alert, trying to ask him where he’s going and to wait.

And then they hear puking.

Beau sighs. “I’m gonna go sit with him.” Caduceus strikes her as the type of person who doesn’t particularly like being in a situation like this alone. So she goes and kneels down to where he’s gagging on the grass, laying a hand on his back.

She always forgets how thin he is. He wears so much baggy, flow-y stuff, it does well to hide how bony he is under it all. He can’t take being sick like this for very long. Beau knows, realistically, that that’s not a big concern as long as they’ve got Jester. It’s just a bug.

“How you doing, Deuces?” she asks after his body gives him a few minutes of a break.

Cad nods. “I think that I’m done. For- for now.”

“Wanna go back in the dome?”

Another nod. They do so.

He lies down, Beau goes back to sitting with Jester. It doesn’t look like Caduceus goes back to sleep immediately this time. He tosses and turns for a good few minutes until Jester pokes Fjord awake for his watch.

“Caduceus threw up a little while ago,” Beau informs him as she gets up. “Keep an eye on him.”

Fjord nods, sending a worried glance Cad’s way. “Got it.”

-----

Things are almost eerily silent, save for the sounds of his friends sleeping. Fjord’s bored, if he’s being honest. But he sits.

Listens.

Taps his foot.

Caduceus gives a shaky breath. He doesn’t sound asleep, Fjord thinks.

“Deuces?” Fjord whispers.

“Hm?”

“You alright?”

“Cold...”

Fjord gets up, (he needed a reason to get up and do something, anyway) and he makes his way to Caduceus’ spot on the edge of the dome. He does smell a faint sour smell of vomit that tells him Cad didn’t make it too far out of the dome before he threw up.

“Need another blanket?”

Caduceus shudders, and Fjord can actually see him shaking under his bedding. He’s never seen their firbolg friend look so... distressed. “Can’t sleep,” he finally laments.

“Hey, Deuces,” Fjord says. “Mind if I sit with you, then? I’m kinda bored.”

Caduceus gives a smile and nods. “Please.”

-----

The woods are less spooky in the morning.

Jester wakes before anyone else, which isn’t much of a surprise. She scans the outside sleepily as the birds chirp, until she remembers.

Caduceus. Is he okay?

Her gaze immediately snaps to his sleeping area. Jester smiles.

Caduceus is sound asleep, head in Yasha’s lap as she runs her fingers through his hair. His face is red and weary, like he’s had a pretty rough night, but they both look comfortable.

“Hi,” Jester whispers to Yasha with a smile she doesn’t bother to hide.

Yasha looks up, having just noticed that someone else is awake. “Hey, good morning.”

“Caduceus looks comfy!”

Yasha nods. “I am as well. He’s... very warm.”

Jester smiles sadly. He’s in for a rough couple of days, she thinks. But they’ll make it okay
