Mind Over Matter

“Where’s Klein?”

Michelle chuckled. “That’s a good question. He’s usually here before any of us. I don’t think that he’s ever been late in his life.”

Speak of the devil, in walked Klein with a cup of coffee and the most “don’t talk to me” expression they’d ever seen.

“Look who decided to show up!” Michelle called, and it seemed to get the slightest smile out of him. “Where have you been?”

Klein sighed. Just like that, Toby saw a flash of Klein’s morning. A buzzing alarm clock, fumbling out of bed and nearly falling over from moving so fast. He saw Alvin cough, and sneeze, taking his temperature while also digging through his bathroom drawer for an old bottle of cold medicine. The last thing Toby saw was their boss looking at the reading on the thermometer and throwing his head back with a groan.

“Overslept,” answered Klein. Which wasn’t technically a lie, Toby supposed.

Now that he knew the man was sick, Toby could see the soreness around Klein’s nostrils, the watery eyes. He wasn’t the only one who noticed, Michelle seemed to see it as well.

The watched him walk off to his office.

“He’s feeling pretty lousy,” Toby informed as he crossed his arms over his chest.

“Yeah, I don’t need to be a telepath to figure that one out. Wait, you read him?”

Toby grinned and shrugged. “It just happened.”

She chuckled and shook her head. “Just make sure he never finds out you did that.”

“I don’t plan on telling him!”

The two of them sat back and prepared themselves for a day with a grumpy, sick boss.

Dev had been going through files on an old hard drive all day. He’d finally found something worth it.

Klein’s office was open, but the lights were dimmer. Toby wasn’t sure that he was in there until they walked in and found him at his desk, looking exhausted for someone who’d done nothing but sit at a desk all morning. There was a box of tissues within reach, but that was the only sign there would be that he was sick.

He sat up straight when he noticed the two of them.

“Dev found a lead,” Michelle said. “A name and address.”

“On that old hard drive? Nice work, go uh, follow it.”

“Well,”

Klein raised an eyebrow.

“The address doesn’t exist anymore. It’s a strip mall now.”

“And the name?”

“She’s long dead.”

He sighed before giving a quick sniffle. “Interesting. Alright. No relatives?”

“None,” she confirmed.

“And unfortunately, I can’t read dead people!” The joke didn’t seem to amuse either of them, so Toby shut his mouth. Klein looked so tired.

“This case just keeps getting better...” He rubbed his nose, sniffed again. “Why don’t you interview the-” a sneeze cut him off. He managed to pull a tissue just in time to cover it. He blew his nose immediately before tossing it and giving his nose an experimental sniffle.

I’d certainly kill someone to be in bed right now, Klein’s thoughts said.

“You know,” Toby said. “We can handle this, right Michelle?”

“Oh, definitely.”

“Why don’t you go home and get some rest?”

“Excuse me?”

She nodded in agreement. “Before you get the entire office sick. Do I have to remind you of the flu epidemic last year?”

Klein groaned. “You certainly do not. snf! Alright, you two have a good point. Go find that killer.”
