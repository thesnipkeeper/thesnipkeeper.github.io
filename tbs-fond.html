Title: Fond
Fandom: Bright Sessions
Summary: Joan and Owen have to cancel date night
Word count: 1k

Fond

“Your tea, miss,” Owen presented grandly as he handed the mug to her. “Careful, it’s hot.”

Joan giggled. “Why, thank you, sir.” She held it, inhaling the steam in hopes that it would help her breathe. Probably unlikely, but she had to try. She missed breathing through her nose. “What a time to get sick, hm? This isn’t exactly what I had in mind for our date night.”

They’d both been so busy that planning dates was complicated. And of course Joan woke up with a cold the day that they were supposed to go on their first real date in two months.

Owen kissed the top of her head before sitting next to her. “I think we’ll manage. There’s nothing wrong with a little night in, right?”

“Except every night is a night in for us,” Joan said with a hoarse laugh. Her breath caught, and she quickly handed the tea over to Owen to cover a cough. “Excuse me,” she shook her head, which did nothing to clear it.

He was careful handing back the tea, and then wrapped an arm around her shoulders. She was warm, she was also shivering. She hadn’t mentioned chills to him; Joan was never one to complain about how she was feeling if she didn’t have to. Other people’s problems were always more fun to deal with than her own.

He was warm, comfortable. She leaned into him as she sipped the tea, delighted to find that it calmed the cough that had been annoying her all day.

“This is nice, too,” Owen said after a while. “I mean-! Not that I like that you’re sick, but we don’t get much of a chance to just sit and do nothing.”

Joan hummed in agreement. “No, I get what you mean.” She set down the empty mug and snuggled into him even further until she was able to successfully sap all of his warmth. Even during their quiet nights, they often still had work to do. Paperwork to fill out, e-mails to send (God, so many e-mails). But tonight they were able to relax guilt-free.

She ended up with her head in his lap, nearly asleep to the sound of the random movie they’d chosen to watch. She could stay there all night, enjoying Owen and the calm that always came with him. Her nose had other plans.

Joan sat up, Owen’s throw blanket falling off of her shoulders, scrubbing her nose with an annoyed, childish sound.

“Going to sneeze?” Owen asked with a hint of amusement in his voice.

Joan sneezed into her cupped hands and groaned. She nodded her thanks at the tissue box held in front of her and went to work cleaning her nose.

“God, I’m not getting any sleep tonight, am I?”

Owen pressed a kiss to her temple and rubbed her back as she pulled another tissue from the box. “I have some cold medicine you can take. The powerful stuff, you know?”

“Oh,” Joan said, delighted. “That sounds amazing.” She got comfortable against him once again, once she wasn’t actively dripping snot.

A few minutes of silence went by before Owen hummed thoughtfully. “We should look into creating Atypical cold meds.” They could never keep work out of their conversations, but Joan didn’t mind. Their work was important to the two of them.

“That’d be great,” she agreed. “It’s not fun when I have to tell all of my patients that they need to be careful what kind of medications they take.”

“I can imagine.”

“Mm.” And things were quiet again. For a little while.

She was wide awake when Owen suddenly jerked with a powerful sneeze, and another. He blinked woozily and sniffed.

“Uh oh,” she said, sitting up to get a good look at him. His eyes were a little watery. “You caught my cold.”

Owen chuckled and shook his head, waving dismissively. “No no, it can’t happen that fast.”

“It can if we caught it from the same place. And did you know that a person is most contagious before symptoms even appear?”

“Alright, well...” his eyes went cloudy before he sneezed again, expertly shielded in his elbow.

“Bless you,” she didn’t mean to sound as fond as she did. “We’re such a mess, hm?” She pressed herself into his side, and he readily cuddled her as he continued sniffling. The sneezing must’ve activated a runny nose.

“Date night indeed!”

-----

Joan was right.

She couldn’t sleep. It wasn’t the fact that she couldn’t breathe through her nose, or that she couldn’t get warm despite the blankets Owen had piled on top of her. It was the stupid cough.

Owen wasn’t getting any sleep either, of course. And if he was just starting to get sick, he almost needed the rest more than she did.

“Y’alright?” Came the groggy voice.

Joan was halfway out of the covers, one leg swung over the edge of the bed. She was already freezing, so she grabbed a couple of the blankets to take with her. “I’m fine, go back to sleep,” she whispered.

“No... Joan,” Owen sat up, now fully awake (though a little groggy). He squinted at her in the dim light. “Where... where’re you going?”

She sighed, cleared her throat. “Just the couch.”

“Can’t sleep?”

“No, and neither can you if I’m in here.” She coughed again, as if her body was trying to prove the point of how disruptive she was.

Owen gave a drawn-out, sleepy whine and went to latch onto her arm. “Don’t go.”

She chuckled. “Owen, you need sleep.”

“Need you.”

God damn, he was adorable when he was tired. “Owen...” she groaned.

“Here...” she watched as he took her pillows and stacked them up. He patted them as invitingly as a half-asleep Owen could.

“It’s not going to stop me from coughing,” but she was letting him pull her back into bed and snuggle against her even as she said it.

“Neither is the couch,” Owen sniffled, sounding horribly stuffed up already. They were in for a rough week.

“Can’t argue with that,” she smiled. She maneuvered in his arms and kissed him, on the lips this time as there was no reason not to. Owen hummed happily. “We’re taking a sick day tomorrow, right?”

“Please,” he said, rather miserably.
