Title: Like Before
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Not a lot has changed between the twins
Word count: 400

Like Before

Vex is woken by a click of her door. Her first instinct is to reach for the knife kept under her pillow for intruders.

The gangly figure with long hair makes her relax once more.

She doesn’t say a thing at first, and neither does Vax as he pulls back the covers of her bed and climbs in.

"What do you think you're doing?" It's very possible that he's drunk and doesn’t realize which room he walked into, and Vex truly hopes that's all it is.

As children, they had a habit of climbing into each other's beds when they had nightmares or bad nights. She knew how bad her brother's nightmares could get.

Vax gives a light cough as he pulls the blankets tightly around him.

…Or when they were sick. They were impossible to keep apart when one of them didn’t feel well.

"Cold." Is Vax's only explanation.

Vex only sighs. They're getting too old for this. They are too old for this. But she wraps an arm around her brother and lets him sink into her hold, shivering pitifully, teeth chattering as he tries to get warm.

Vex brings a hand to his forehead. He's definitely running a fever. She considers going to wake Pike, but thinks better of it. There's no reason to bother Pike at this hour, and right now Vax just wants comfort.

They both fall asleep before long. Hopefully it's just an out-of-the-blue fever from exhaustion or… or something, and Vax will feel better in the morning.

That hope is crushed when Vex wakes to her brother in the process of coughing up his own lungs.

He doesn't say anything as she sits up and signifies that she's awake. He's too busy gaining control of his lungs.

Vex doesn't like the flush in his cheeks or the ominous rattle from his chest.

She presses the back of her hand to his forehead. "What the hell are you coming down with?" she mutters with a frown. "Should I be worried?"

Vax shakes his head. "No, no..." he clears his throat, it does nothing to improve his voice. "I'll be alright."

"You'd better be."

Vax chuckles - or tries to - but it only triggers another coughing fit. "Don't worry about me, dear sister."

"Ugh. Just don't die or something. We need you around."

"Mm'kay," he mumbles sleepily. "Let me stay here?"

She sighs heavily. "Yeah, alright."
