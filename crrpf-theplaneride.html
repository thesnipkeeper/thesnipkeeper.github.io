The Plane Ride

He should have been more careful the moment he felt a cold coming on. Gotten more sleep, eaten real food. Self-care always eluded him before con, and during con, and... Well, after. 

It was easy to get caught up in stuff. 

"Are you sure you're good to fly?" 

They had arrived at the airport early. Very early. Taliesin felt a little gross when he got up that morning. Leave it to him to come down with something before they even leave the convention.

Since then his scratchy throat has turn painfully sore and his slightly runny nose has developed a steady trickle that requires him to keep tissues nearby at all times. 

Needless to say, he’s not looking forward to the plane ride. "It's just a cold," he says, even as he's pawing at his runny nose, trying to get it to cooperate. 

Matt doesn't look convinced and Taliesin doesn’t blame him. He knows how awful his voice sounds between the congestion and the sore throat. 

His body is already starting to ache and he’s a little chillier than normal. He sniffles desperately a few times when he feels liquid at the base of his nostrils. Ugh, he can never stop messing with it because it just keeps running. 

Sniffling is a mistake, he realizes. A wet itch makes itself known in his sinuses. He doesn’t have a chance to grab a tissue, so he’s forced to steeple his hands and hope for the best.

The sneeze is loud and embarrassingly wet, and definitely gets the attention of a few people seated near them. He really hopes that none of them are gonna be on the same plane as him, because he must look and sound ridiculously contagious.

More pressing matters, though: the entire bottom half of his face is covered in goop and he can’t remove his hands without showing everyone in the crowded airport. He snuffles before, “Hey, Matt- oh, thank you...” Leave it to his best friend to have a tissue ready when he needs it.

He does his best to blow his nose as quietly as possible, resulting in a gurgle that tells anyone within earshot how stuffed up he is. Matt hands him another, and rubs his back as he cleans up the residual mess. 

“God,” Taliesin looks around self-consciously. “I must look like a walking plague rat right now.”

“So do half the people here.” Now that Matt mentions it, it is flu season, and Taliesin can see several other sick people as he looks around. Now he hopes they’re on his plane so he alone can’t be blamed if someone else ends up sick. “But don’t worry about that,” Matt says, waving dismissively. “Just try to stay comfortable on the plane.”

“snf! Easy for you to say. You can still breathe through your nose. snf!”

Matt chuckles. “You’re right, how can I be so insensitive? Us nose-breathers just can’t think about anyone else.”

Taliesin scoffs, and pulls up one of the less damp tissues to his nose when it blows a bit of wetness onto his upper lip. 

This is gonna be fun, he thinks bitterly. 

He and Matt get on the plane and find their seats, Taliesin taking the window seat so he’s not in many people’s lines of sight.

He gets a dirty look from an old woman at the first sneeze after the plane takes off, and that’s when he realizes how long this ride is going to be.

“Did you take anything earlier?” Matt asks.

Fuck. “No,” Taliesin sighs. “I completely forgot.” He really should have. Tylenol might not have helped with the sneezing or the unending runny nose, but if what he feels coming on is a fever...

Matt hums sympathetically. “Just a couple hours, right?”

“Yeah,” Taliesin says, biting back a shiver. “Just a couple of hours. 

His body aches. No matter what position he sits in, he can’t get comfortable. His legs hurt like he’d been exercising the entire weekend. Sure, he did a little more walking than normal because of the convention, but this isn’t an ache from too much moving.

A bone-deep chill adds to everything else a half hour into the plane ride. He shudders, and he doesn’t realize he’s drawn closer to Matt until his friend is wrapping an arm around him and letting him cuddle closer.

“Are you cold?” Matt asks quietly.

Taliesin nods with a huff. Whatever cold he’s coming down with is going to get worse before this ride is over, not better. He really should have remembered that Tylenol.

He closes his eyes and tries to relax against Matt’s shoulder, despite the fact that he can’t breathe out of one of his nostrils. Matt absently rubs his arm, and eventually rests his head on Taliesin’s. It’s nice.

Until he has to sneeze, because of course.

Matt feels the quick, deep inhale and knows to let him sit up. He pulls his hoodie sleeve over his nose and explodes with a sneeze, and then another. The second one is harsh enough to irritate his scratchy throat further.

“Snf snf! Sorry...” 

Matt doesn’t say a thing, instead looking at him with worry and pulling him back to him until they’ve returned to the previous position. 

-----

“Tal, wake up, we’ve landed.”

Taliesin is shaken awake, and that’s when he realizes that he fell asleep on Matt’s shoulder. Wasn’t the first time, won’t be the last.

He moans, rubbing his eyes. Everyone’s grabbing their things and slowly shuffling toward the door, but Matt patiently waits for him to wake up completely. 

He goes to inhale, and finds that his sinuses are full and breathing just results in a sickening gurgle. 

“Oh God,” He says as Matt grabs their carry-on. “Feel gross... snrfl...” Punctuating it with a wet snuffle.

Matt frowns. “Yeah, you don’t look too good. Do you wanna make a medicine stop on the way home?”

Taliesin shakes his head. “I want to go back to sleep as soon as possible,” he admits with a sheepish chuckle. He stands still for a while, long enough for his head to adjust to the new orientation, and followed Matt into the airport. 

It feels good to be back in his hometown, but it’ll feel better to be home. Except, he’ll probably have to quarantine himself so his roommates don’t get sick. Maybe they should stop somewhere to get disinfectant and hand sanitizer and... ugh.

Marisha picks them up, having come home a couple days before to keep the studio running. She frowns the minute Taliesin gets in.

“Did something happen? You look tired.”

Taliesin nods. “Just caught something. Snf!”

He’s seen Matt and Marisha have their weird, silent conversations. The two of them will look at each other and make various facial expressions until something happens. 

“You wanna come stay with us tonight?” Matt finally says, turning to the backseat where Taliesin is trying his best to wipe his nose without getting too much more snot on his sleeve. “We can sleep off the plane ride and then have a little movie night if you’re feeling up for it.”

“Honestly? That sounds amazing.”
