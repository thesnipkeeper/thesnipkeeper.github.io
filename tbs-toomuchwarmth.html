Fandom: Bright Sessions
Summary: Owen's body forces him to rest before Joan can
Word count: 1k

Too Much Warmth

Owen had been a little out of it since they got home from work. Joan just assumed he’d been busy, perhaps overdone it on the coffee earlier and was suffering the crash.

They picked up dinner and ate it on the couch in front of the TV like a true, worn out couple at the end of the day. Joan was ravenous after having skipped lunch that day, and she thought that Owen had too.

“Owen?”

He was staring dazedly at the TV while poking at his salad with a fork. The container was still full, she’d only seen him take a couple of bites. Hearing his name had him alert and turning to her. “Hm?”

“You’re not eating much,” she said. “Is it that bad?”

“The...? Oh, no the food’s fine, I’m just not very hungry.” He set the salad down and yawned.

Joan frowned. “Are you feeling alright?” she asked, laying a hand on his back. He had yet to change out of his work clothes, instead simply removing his jacket.

“Yeah!” He nodded quickly. “I feel fine, just tired. Think I might head to bed early, actually.”

“Okay! I’ll put the rest in the fridge.” She bit back the concern as he stood up. It wasn’t like him to go to bed so early, it certainly wasn’t like Owen to be this wiped out, to not eat...

He gave her a quick kiss and disappeared down the hallway. Joan listened to him get in pajamas and brush his teeth while she continued to eat her dinner.

-----

Owen was fast asleep in a tangle of blankets when she finally went to bed. She joined him and fell asleep, enjoying his warmth.

-----

Too much warmth.

Joan woke to being smothered in heat. It took her a moment to realize that it was the person curled around her, Owen holding her close with his face pressed into the back of her neck. He was hot, uncomfortably so.

So then, why was he shuddering?

Joan struggled to turn around to face him, earning a sad whimper as she disturbed him.

“Owen,” she said quietly. “Are you awake?”

“Mm,” he responded. A few seconds late, his eyes opened, though he still managed to look half asleep. “Hm?”

“Hey,” she reached up to push some auburn hair out of his face. “You feel a little warm,” said Joan, letting her hand settle on his forehead. She was met with burning skin. He was still shaking. His brows furrowed as if trying to process what she was talking about. “I think you’re running a fever.”

He swallowed, closed his eyes for a moment, and nodded. “Y-yeah, me too.”

Joan frowned, and then threw the covers back and made her way to the ensuite. She returned with a thermometer, along with a bottle of tylenol and a bottle of water.

“Temp first?” She held the thermometer to him and he gave a sleepy nod, turning his head.

He gasped at first when she pressed it into his ear, if nothing else was an indication of a fever, that would have been. She held it there, using her other hand to run through his hair. He hummed contently like a sleepy cat, leaning into her until the device beeped.

Joan’s eyes widened. “103.4.”

He raised an eyebrow. “Excuse me?”

“Owen, you have a temperature of 103.” She didn’t mean to use a scolding tone, as if it was his fault he ran so hot when he was sick.

Owen looked confused, inconvenienced. “Well...” he muttered. “I... I guess I should get some sleep.”

“Agreed.”

She handed him the water and two pills, and he swallowed it without hesitation before sighing.

“I’m sorry,” he said with the most pitiful, miserable voice.

Joan couldn’t help but chuckle. “For what?” she asked as the two of them got comfortable in be once again.

“Ruining our weekend.” 

Joan hummed thoughtfully. “Yes, I’m sure you did this on purpose just so we can’t go out on a Saturday, didn’t you?” Owen huffed. She smiled and pressed a kiss to his forehead. “Let’s get some rest.”

“Mhm.”

-----

She was in the middle of making some breakfast when she heard footsteps padding down the hallway. A tired, rumpled Owen emerged, pajamas wrinkled and hair adorably everywhere. His face was still red, but he didn’t look in immediate danger of keeling over.

“Good morning,” she hummed, greeting him with a kiss on the cheek.

His offered a tired smile. “Good morning.” Owen took a seat at one of the stools at the counter and slumped forward as if he couldn’t be upright a second longer.

“I’m making some oatmeal if you’d like some?”

“Maybe a slice of toast?”

“Ah, a delicacy, coming right up.” She smiled just to get him to smile again. “Feeling any better?” she asked, reached across the lay a hand on his forehead. Still warm.

“Oh, wonderful,” he laughed, and then cleared his throat. “I’m just... a bit groggy and tired and feverish.”

Joan nodded as she pushed the toast down. “I’ll politely remind you that this has happened before.”

Owen whined childishly and dropped his head on the counter.

Whenever it was a high fever along with much smaller symptoms, they could chalk it up to his working too hard. Stress did a number on one’s body, Joan was always quick to remind anyone of that. And Owen’s body certainly took hits back when they were dating before... everything.

It was like his immune system would let him get something small like a cold, and then crank up the heat to force him to rest for once.

“We’ll have a relaxing day,” Joan promised. “And perhaps a week, if I end up with this as well.”

He gasped (which triggered a cough) and looked up at her. “We kissed.”

She couldn’t bite back a smile at his realization. “We did, yes. That’s a thing that people tend to do when they’re a couple.”

“What’s that you’ve always said? A person’s most contagious before symptoms...?”

She nodded. “Yes, but we’ll see, okay?” Joan pushed the toast toward him. “Why don’t we just get through today?”

He nodded, and then let her kiss him.
