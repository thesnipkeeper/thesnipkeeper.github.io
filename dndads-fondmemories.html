Fandom: Dungeons and Daddies
Summary: Henry is - unsurprisingly - an emotional sick person
Word count: 1.1k

Fond Memories

Out of nowhere, Henry vomited.

It wasn’t until they were trying to find a place for him to sit down and rest afterward that they realized their friend really hadn’t eaten anything that day.

“I’m sure it’s nothing,” Henry reasoned, even as he was scrubbing his mouth on the back of his hand and sitting quite still to avoid jostling his stomach further. “I probably ate something weird. There’s a lot of weird food out here, you know...”

Darryl nodded. “Sure, buddy, sure. But your face is a little red. Do you mind if I...” He lifted a hand in a questioning manner, and Henry tilted his head for easy access, not seeming to have the energy to do much else.

Darryl laid his palm on his friend’s forehead just like he always did with Grant. Henry was really warm. “Okay,” Darryl said. “That’s uh, that’s a fever.”

Henry furrowed his brows. “Are you sure? I mean, I might’ve just... I don’t know, accidentally eaten meat or something?”

“It could be...” Ron gasped. “What if it’s some kind of alien fantasy plague? Are we all going to die?”

Glenn took a few steps back. “Shit, then let's just leave him here and get outta here.”

“Okay,” Henry said, annoyed. “It’s nothing. It’s nothing! Just an upset tummy, that’s all.”

“We’ll rest here for the night, how about that,” Darryl offered.

 -----

There was less rest than they’d hoped for.

Henry woke them all up when he had to scramble a safe distance away from their campsite to throw up again. Darryl tiredly made his way over to him and rubbed his back as he gagged.

He leaned on a nearby tree for support until he couldn’t stay standing anymore. Henry knelt down on the grass in defeat, taking a moment to catch his breath before the next bout.

“You uh, you don’t have to stay with me, Darryl...” he said hoarsely before spitting into the grass. “We don’t exactly know how long this is going to last, right?”

“I mean, look,” Darryl sighed. “I can go if you want me to, but I’ve gotta be honest, buddy, you’re not looking too good.” Even at night, Darryl could see a sickly sheen of sweat on his friend’s skin. After a couple seconds of silence, he asked, “Do you want me to go?”

A sniff. Aw shit, Henry was crying. “No...” he said, voice breaking miserably. “I feel horrible.”

Darryl, lacking anything else to say, just continued to rub his back as another painful heave took over his body. He couldn’t have much left at this point, could he? But that didn’t stop his stomach from trying to get rid of something that wasn’t there.

 -----

Things weren’t much better in the morning.

Henry was tired, out of it, and his fever was back and higher than before. They couldn’t travel like this.

“I’m so sorry, guys...” his voice was raspy and painful to listen to.

Even Glenn looked worried. “Hey man, not your fault.”

“If we die of the plague, we die together,” Ron agreed.

They took the day off to form a plan, discuss what they knew about their kids’ situation. Darryl thought it’d be best to get at least a little productivity out of the downtime

And, as it turned out, Henry was incredibly cuddly when he was sick. He was constantly glued to someone’s side, head on their shoulder or their lap as they talked. He was down, anyone could see that. Henry, someone who was usually determined to look on the bright side of things, had yet to smile genuinely that day.

Their conversations became less about their current situation in the Forgotten Realms and more about their kids. Memories, each of their quirks... that was when Henry got teary-eyed.

He’d been somewhat cuddled against Glenn, but the man instinctively recoiled at the sight of Henry crying.

“Shit dude,” he said. “Are you okay?”

Henry nodded, sniffled, scrubbed at his eyes. “I just miss them so much...”

Oh... fuck. Now Darryl wanted to cry. “Aw Henry, we’ll... we’ll find them. We will.”

Henry sniffed. “When Lark and Sparrow are sick, I make them vegetable noodle soup.” He laughed tearfully, smiling for the first time at the thought of his kids. “But it’s impossible to keep those boys in bed, they can’t sit still for very long... and they only ever eat the noodles.”

Darryl chuckled. “Yeah, Grant used to be the same way when he was younger.” These days, Grant would just accept his fate and take the opportunity to play those online games with his school friends. He never talked to his family much when he wasn’t feeling good, and Darryl supposed that he was the same way sometimes. Couldn’t really blame the kid.

Henry sighed. “I love those little guys so much.”

Glenn awkwardly rubbed his back. The sight brought up a question that Darryl realized had been on his mind for a while.

“What about you, Glenn? What happens when Nick’s sick?”

“Oh,” Glenn scoffs. “We always get sick at the same time, so obviously we order pizza and watch horror movies until we can’t stay awake any longer.”

“Pizza?” Henry sobered for that, sitting up to look at Glenn with the most horrified look. “That’s... that’s horrible food to give to a sick kid.”

“Is it? He likes it.”

Darryl shrugged. “Glenn has a point, I guess. Sometimes it’s just about them being comfortable and happy until they’re not sick anymore.”

“Yeah, man. Nick hates being sick, I’ve gotta do something.”

“Grant’s never in the best mood, either,” Darryl agreed. “What about you, Ron?”

“Hm? Oh, Terry Jr. uh... well... I don’t really...”

Oh, he felt bad for asking. “Right. Um,” Darryl cleared his throat. “How’re you feeling, Henry?”

“Tired,” he replied. “Sad.”

“Still feel uh, pukey?”

Henry thought for a moment and nodded. Glenn moved away, or was about to, but it was Henry who got up with a hand on his stomach. He stumbled a bit, lightheaded and probably feverish.

Darryl took the initiative to go with him.

Henry gagged up a small stream of vomit, nothing but bile and what little water they’d gotten him to drink. And he started to cry again. Darryl swore.

“You’ll feel better in no time, buddy. I promise.” He rubbed his friend’s back between his shoulder blades. Henry was already so skinny, Darryl wanted desperately to sit him down and get some real food into him one of these days, not that vegan-veggie-whatever, but maybe he’d save that until after they were home.

Henry half choked, half sobbed. “What if we can’t find them?” So there was more on his mind than being sick, then.

Darryl bit the inside of his mouth to fight his own worry. 

“We’ll find them,” he promised.
