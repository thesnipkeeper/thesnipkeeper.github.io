Fandom: Magicians
Summary: Margo and Eliot find Quentin in a rough state
Word count: 1k

Green Cocktail

"Where is he?"

"I have an idea."

They exchange eye rolls.

This is just like Quentin Coldwater to abandon a party when it's getting good. To confine himself to his room with his stupid textbooks to do stupid studying when everyone else is downstairs drinking their evening away. They justified it easily as they do every time they feel like partying.

A way to celebrate not getting the shit beat out of them at the most recent competition. (The nature kids lost the game just as they do at life every day).

Margo downs her drink with a heavy sigh, because she really could have enjoyed her drink had it not been for Quentin and his loserness. She wanders upstairs until she reaches his room. The door's closed, but not locked or warded. So either he's in there, or he's an idiot. Both are possible.

Margo lets herself in and takes in the empty room. No Quentin, but the lamp is on.

"Q," Margo calls. She spots the light on from under the door of the ensuite and figures she should announce her presence. There's no privacy in this cottage, never will be for as long as she's there.

She expects a 'fuck off, Margo' or something along those lines, but instead she's met with the wrenching sound of vomiting.

She instantly makes a face, and then pushes the door open.

Poor, sweet, simple Quentin is hunched over the toilet seat with a string of drool hanging out of his mouth and slowly falling into the bowl. The contents of the toilet are an unfortunate green, courtesy of the cocktail Eliot shoved into his hand the second he returned to the cottage that evening.

"Oh honey, you really can't hold your liquor, huh?"

She kneels down and rubs his back, doesn't bother to cringe at how soaked through his shirt is. She stopped being grossed out by this stuff a long time ago, around the time she and Eliot became friends.

"It's not the alcohol!" he grumbles breathlessly, grabbing a wad of paper to clean his face. It doesn't do much. "I had one stupid drink."

"Okay, alright, no need to get all pissy."

She strokes his back as he flushes the grossness away and slumps against the toilet, not quite ready to let go. He still looks seconds away from being sick again. But as he relaxes a bit, Margo notices that he's shaking under her hand.

He's in that position for a good few minutes before Margo decides that something needs to be done.

"I'm dragging you to bed before you pass out in the toilet bowl and I have to use Meyer's Water Breathing on you to keep you from drowning in the worst way possible."

"Well, not the worst way possible."

"Shut it, douchebag. Help me get you up." She tugs at his arm, and he shakes his head.

"I... can't." Quentin drags a hand down his face, looking so miserable and exhausted that Margo almost feels true sympathy instead of pity.

"What do you mean?"

"Dizzy."

"Fine," she sits down once again, but this time making herself comfortable. "Guess we'll just be here, then."

"The hell is taking you guys so long?" says the mildly-slurred speech of a drunk Eliot. "Are you two fucking without m- oh. Gross. I even made that drink weak just for you, Q. C'mon."

Quentin groans.

Margo snorts. "You wanna help me get him to bed before he falls asleep in here?"

"Guys, I don't know..."

"Trust me, you'll be better off. We'll grab The Bucket and everything."

The Bucket is designated for situations similar to this one, but that usually involves a fuck ton of alcohol or drugs or two week-old leftovers. And it’s usually Eliot.

He lets himself get dragged to his feet. They have to stop a couple of times when he makes noises like he’s going to puke again. False alarms, thank God.

“Listen Q, I’m real attached to these shoes, so if you-”

“I’m not gonna throw up on your shoes, Margo.”

“Mmm hm. That’s what El said after that bottle of tequila at the after-finals party last year.”

Eliot huffs. “That was not my fault.”

“I mean, it kinda was, honey.”

The essentially drop Q onto his bed and watch as he curls up like a miserable puppy, trying desperately to get his blankets to cooperate. Eliot, adorably, helps and tucks him in. He gives a kiss on the head, and another on the cheek. Margo’s sure it started as a joke but the look on Eliot’s face is genuine fondness that the alcohol hasn’t helped to hide.

Quentin cringes. “Stop, you smell like booze.” but he still has that cute little smile on his face as his eyes close.

Eliot giggles like an idiot, and then flops into bed with Q. Quentin gives a loud, dramatic groan.

“Mm’kay, I guess I’m getting The Bucket then.” And Margo does retrieve it, albeit with some annoyance. Eliot’s clearly drunk to the point of uselessness.

Well, maybe not completely useless.

He’s wrapped himself around Quentin in an incredibly snug spooning session while Quentin shudders violently under the covers. He looks bad, and Eliot looks like he plans to be here for the rest of the night.

“It’s not like you to give up a night of partying,” she says quietly, setting The Bucket on the floor below Quentin’s head. The last thing any of them are going to want to do tonight is clean up puke.

“He’s nice and warm, though.”

“What, like a fever?”

“Probably.”

“Well, damn.”

She’s intent on leaving the boys to their moment before Eliot starts to sit up. His brows furrow for a moment, his mouth opens and then closes again.

Shit. Margo knows that look.

The Bucket is put to use as she dives for it and gets it into Eliot’s hands in time for the man to not vomit allover Quentin’s bed.

Eliot breathes, rasps as he tries to regain control of his stomach. Margo lays a hand on his forehead, and then the other on Q’s. They’re both about the same temperature. Warm as fuck.

“Of course it’s you two,” she mutters. “Of course.”
