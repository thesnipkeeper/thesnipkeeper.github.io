Title: New Kid (Schitt's Creek crossover)
Fandom: Blake Holsey High
Summary: The new transfer student is obnoxious and kind of interesting
Word count: 4.1k

New Kid

When one of the sister schools was suddenly shut down, Blake Holsey ended up with a few of its students. They were... preppier, definitely. Richer. Snootier.

From what they’d heard, the school excelled in performing arts and was in the process of raising a generation of writers and artists. So of course coming to Blake Holsey High of all places was a dramatic change for most of the transfer students.

“David,” Professor Z pointed to the front. “You know the answer?”

He nodded and immediately gave the exact answer that Z was looking for, with an added flourish.

The new transfer student in their class, David Rose, was strange, to say the least. He was clearly smart and diligent, but there was tension whenever he spoke in class.

It made sense, no one was going to accept the new kid immediately.

He ate lunch alone rather contently with a fork in one hand and book in the other, despite the weird looks he received from the popular kids.

He had glasses, but had only worn them a couple of times, usually when focused studying was involved. He was a good studier, surprisingly. Most of these kids expected everyone else to do the work for them, but David had been working hard. It was admirable how serious he was about his education, even though he didn’t always act like it.

David dug through most of his food with distaste (that was fair, the cafeteria food was awful and definitely not as good as some of the fancier schools) and then went for dessert in lieu of anything else. Admirably, he started eating one careful, neat bite at a time until he didn’t think anyone was looking. And devoured the thing.

“Hey guys,” Marshall unknowingly stopped all conversations going on around him. Josie huffed, apparently in the middle of a heated debate with Lucas about whether teleportation was in their near-future. “Maybe we should invite the new kid over.”

He’s hardly taken his eyes off of David since they sat down so it wasn’t hard for everyone to follow his gaze.

“Why?” Lucas asked without batting an eye. “He looks pretty comfortable by himself.”

“Well maybe he looks that way, but no one likes being alone, do they?”

“I like being alone,” Josie said, pointedly and unhelpfully.

“Maybe Marshall’s right.”

“Corinne, that sentence is a slippery slope. Trust me, I share a room with him. If you- Marshall! What are you-?!”

He was already heading toward the new kid’s table. There was no backing out now.

David looked up from his book, having shoved his tray away from him in favor of something that looked strangely like a romance novel. He raised an eyebrow and Marshall wasn’t sure if the smile he offered was sincere or not.

“Um, hello?” he said, shaking his head.

Right. He needed to say something. He was just standing there like an idiot.

“Oh! Uh, hi, I’m Marshall.”

David glanced at his outstretched hand, and didn’t make a move to shake it. “David,” he said, not unkindly.

Marshall nodded and shoved his hands in his pockets. Wow, he was nervous.

“Did you...want something?”

“Yeah! Well, I mean- I was just wondering if you wanted to sit with my friends and I?” He gestured over to the table, where everyone suddenly pretended like they hadn’t been watching the whole encounter. Heck, they might as well have been scarfing down popcorn with their amount of interest.

David looked past him and then quickly back down at his book. “No, but thanks.”

“Oh, okay! That’s totally fine, I just thought I’d...” David wasn’t paying attention anymore. Marshall returned to his friends with an air of defeat, nearly shot by the look of pity on all of their faces.

“Dude...” Vaughn mumbled. “That was uh, rough.”

Lucas hissed in sympathy as Marshall sat down. “Well, screw him, right? Good luck to him making any friends here when he acts like that.”

Marshall sighed. So that was that, huh? So much for being nice. He wasn’t sure why--of all bad social interactions he had at school--that was the one to keep him down the rest of the day. He just... he really thought they could bring someone else into their group, but apparently he was very wrong. So Marshall went about his day with a cloud above his head.

He didn’t intend to mope, but he and everyone else knew he was anyway.

The strange this was, though, that no one said anything. No one teased him or said "cheer up, Marshall, jeez" and he wasn't sure why. Why were they letting him get away with being a buzzkill? It didn’t matter, he’d get over it eventually, but for now he’d just be a little disappointed of having been rejected so badly.

When nighttime came, he caught Lucas glancing at him in the mirror as they were brushing their teeth.

“What?” Marshall asked, except with the mouthful of toothpaste it came out more like ‘whah?’.

Lucas shrugged. “Nothin’,” and went back to brushing his teeth.

“Wha’ever weirdo,” Marshall scoffed before spitting and rinsing his mouth.

He went to bed, but sleep didn’t come easily. He was still suffering the embarrassment at lunch. Maybe David was as unpleasant and antisocial as everyone thought he was, but the thought still didn’t sit right, even after being directly rejected by the guy.

Or maybe he just liked to eat alone. And Marshall was overthinking the whole thing.

-----

"Each of you are being assigned a partner. You'll get a subject to study along with the guidelines of the assignment," Z explained as he worked through the classroom and handed out papers.

Marshall loved the assigned subjects. They gave him an opportunity to study something new, to broaden his horizons.

Ecosystems, the paper read. Marshall W. And-

No.

This wasn't happening.

"David?" Marshall whispered, just loud enough for Lucas to want to look over his shoulder.

"Have fun with that," he patted Marshall's back and returned to looking over his own assignment.

Marshall could sense everyone's relief that they hadn't been partnered with David Rose, most difficult person to interact with in general, let alone for a school project.

He spared a glance at David's seat at the front of the classroom and caught David looking back at him just as uneasily before turning away the moment they made eye contact.

"Well, here we go..."

-----

Marshall waited on the courtyard patio, tapping his foot anxiously. David was late. It was possible David wasn't showing up at all. Maybe he gave up on having Marshall as a partner altogether and decided to just wing his part of the project.

He'd been sitting there for a half hour. He'd begun writing down notes for ideas he had on the project and anything else to keep his mind off of the waiting. In fact, Marshall was just starting to accept that his partner wasn’t showing up.

And then a "Um, excuse me!" got his attention. David pushed at a bully, one of the bigger students who had been having fun with the newcomers. He didn't budge. Should he do some-? "Move!" David said, practically stomping his foot in a tantrum. It would have been funny had Marshall not been through all of that before.

What was funny was how entitled David was acting toward the guy. As if it was going to do anything.

The bully shoved him, David blinking in surprise and suddenly losing the irritation on his face in place for fear. No one was doing anything. Why wasn’t anyone doing anything?

Marshall stood up before he even realized what he was doing, and marched right over to them.

"Hey!" He shouted, earning the bully's attention. "Leave him alone!"

The bully looked at him and scoffed. "Back off, Wheeler."

Instead of looking thankful, David just stared, brows furrowed and eyes wide like he was wondering what in the world Marshall was doing. Marshall shrugged right before he was pushed out of the way. The bully gave a hearty laugh and walked away.

"You two are lucky I have to get to football practice."

They each breathed a sigh of relief, shoulders falling as the guy walked away.

David shoved past him to the table Marshall had been camped at.

"What? No, thank you?" Marshall chuckled.

The look David gave him shut him up real fast.

"You didn't have to do that," he muttered as he sat down, shoving some of Marshall's things out of the way to make room for his own impeccably-organized binder. All of his supplies were devoid of color, all either black or white (with the occasional gray thrown in). He opened his binder and arranged the pencil next to it just so.

Marshall blinked. What was with this guy? "Look, fine, don't thank me, but you shouldn't be antagonizing people twice your size."

David glanced up from fixing the order of his pencil, highlighter, and pen for the third time. "Excuse me? I thought we had project to work on."

"Oh, really? That would have been nice to start about, hmm-" Marshall checked his watch. "Thirty minutes ago."

David scoffed as if he'd been personally wronged, laying a hand over his heart in earnest. "I had things to do. Sorry I made you wait a whole thirty minutes for a project that will ultimately mean nothing in our high school education."

"What things were so important?"

David cleared his throat, closing his eyes for a moment. "I um, accidentally napped and overslept."

Marshall massaged the bridge of his nose. He couldn't believe that he actually tried to befriend this kid. Maybe David Rose was just as unpleasant as everyone assumed. He took a deep breath through his nose before seating himself in front of his project partner.

David raised an expectant eyebrow.

"Sorry, I just didn't see myself confronting a bully today."

"Like I said-"

"I know, I know." Marshall unclipped his binder and pulled out the page of notes he'd written down. "Take a look at these and tell me what you think, okay?"

David accepted the papers slowly, looking at him with confusion. "Okay, did I say something to piss you off or are you just like this, like, all the time?" he asked, waving his hand wildly.

Marshall huffed and shook his head. "Never mind. Just meet me after class on Monday and we'll go over it." He began to pack up his things.

David was still holding the papers when he blinked and shook his head rapidly as if he was trying to understand something. "Okay," he said, giving an awkward chuckle. "Marshall, was it?"

"You know my name," Marshall sighed.

David nodded slowly, "Right. Um, well, let me give you these before you go." He opened his binder (black) and took two papers from a folder before sliding them to Marshall's side of the table, biting his lip as if stifling a smug smile.

Marshall raised an eyebrow at him and looked at the papers. "What are these?"

"Those are my notes that I wrote after science class yesterday. You're welcome."

Marshall swallowed. "I-"

Right. Out of all the things he didn't know about David Rose, the one thing Marshall did know was that he wasn’t a slacker when it came to his education. School was important to him whether he said it or not.

"See you Monday?"

"Yeah. But please don't be late."

"Honestly, I wouldn’t dare after this."

-----

Lucas made various impressed sounds as he read. "These are like, ten times better than any of my notes for anything."

Marshall gave a long groan as he flopped in the edge of his bed, buried his head in his hands, and nodded. "They're even color-coded and the lists are all alphabetical."

Lucas looked at him over his glasses.

"What?"

He averted his eyes back to the papers. "Nothing."

"Hey, quit doing that!" Marshall laughed. "What? What is it?"

"It's just…" Lucas sighed and set the papers down. He threw his hands up with a massive shrug. "You're being super weird. About this guy, I mean."

"Weird?" Marshall scoffed.

"Yeah Marshall, weird. I'm just saying that you've never complained about someone like this before. Or asked anyone to our table."

He narrowed his eyes. "What are you saying?"

Lucas didn't flinch, instead a sly smile stretching across his lips. 'I think you like him."

Marshall opened his mouth to protest, because how could Lucas know anything about who he liked? It wasn't as if they talked about those things often. But… instead, he found himself sighing and going back to groaning on his bed.

-----

He was late again.

Marshal huffed, checked his watch, rolled his eyes.

After their conversation before, David should-

Well, after their conversation yesterday, maybe David didn’t want to talk to him again.

Marshall stared at the overly-organized page of notes. He should really… apologize. Even though he didn't really do anything wrong.

He closed his notebook. David wasn't coming. They could probably manage to get the project done without these meetings. They were both capable individuals.

Marshall startled when a pile of books dropped on the other side of the table. Over them was David, looking a bit frazzled.

"Dude," he deadpanned.

David nodded as if he knew exactly what was coming. "Hi, yes, I know, I'm very late. My brain isn't exactly working on all cylinders today." He cleared his throat and sat down, scrambling through his things until he found the page of notes Marshall had given him. "These were surprisingly good, so I guess I should say thank you."

"I've been sitting here for nearly an hour waiting for you to show up. You're lucky I had studying to do or I would have left!"

David rolled his eyes, and Marshall had to admit that he looked tired, like he needed that accidental nap that made him late last time. "Mhm, yeah, I'm irresponsible and all that. Can we just finish this, p-please?" He got a strange look on his face and began digging through his bag. David pulled a tissue out of a packet and pressed it to his face before releasing a pair of forceful sneezes. He made an ugh noise and rubbing his nose.

"Uh, bless you?" Marshall said as he watched him then pull out a bottle of hand sanitizer and squeeze out a liberal amount.

"Mmm," was David's only acknowledgment.

It was then that Marshall realized that his nose was a little pink, his eyes sunken and watery. He sniffled like he was either suffering from allergies or at the beginnings of a nasty cold.

And by the exhaustion exuding from him, it was probably the latter.

David sniffed and immediately started coughing. He yanked his arm up to cover it, face getting redder with the effort.

"Jeez. You sick?"

"Am I sick?" He repeated, dramatic hand gestures to go with it. "Yes, because no one at this school can keep their germs to themselves, and now I'm sick. My first month here." He went back to tending to his nose with a grumpy, exasperated huff.

Marshall sighed. "Look… the project isn't due until next week. If you want to-"

"Uh uh, nope. We're doing this now."

So they did it. And surprisingly, it worked out well.

And Marshall tried very hard not to be worried as David seemed to get more and more miserable throughout their meeting. He'd emptied one plastic packet of tissues and started on a second. He sniffled and sneezed through the whole thing, obviously trying not to for Marshall's benefit. He'd hide his face sometimes as he sniffled, embarrassed by his own running nose but wanting to let Marshall finish his thought before dealing with it.

David looked very ready to be done when they'd finally finished planning everything out.

They said their goodbyes, and that was that.

-----

"He's not so bad," Marshall said as he returned to the dorm room.

Lucas looked up, having been in the middle of gnawing on his pencil. "Huh?"

"David. He's an entitled piece of crap, but he's kind of cool."

"Okay, Heart Eyes."

Marshall scoffed. "That is not what-"

"Mhm."

-----

"I trust that all of you have already started on your projects. I know it's a ways away, but you should be putting effort into it now," Professor Z said as he passed out worksheets.

The mention of the project had Marshall looking to the front of the class.

No David.

Now that he thought about it, he hadn't seen David all day.

Marshall caught Z just as he was setting papers on his and Lucas' desk. "Professor Z, where's David?"

Z looked up to David's usual seat as well. "Ah, he's not feeling well. He sent me a very um, dramatic email this morning."

Marshall took amazing notes that period. He attempted David's technique of alphabetizing the things that Z listed off, and organizing his thoughts so it'd be easier to understand them later. He even ended up switching between blue and black pen, for which Lucas gave a judging scoff.

They still looked awful, but at least they had all of the important points from class.

The bell rang, and Marshall leaned over to his friend. "Wanna come with me to drop off my notes to David?"

Lucas froze, just for a second, as if trying to comprehend what had just been said. "No," he answered decidedly.

"Right. Fine. I guess I'll… go by myself then." Dang it.

Lucas chuckled and patted his back. "Good luck."

-----

Marshall knocked on the door, tapping his foot anxiously. David's dorm was one of the few single-bed rooms in the school, and his parents probably paid a lot to get it for him. Did he get lonely, Marshall thought, isolating himself all hours of the day?

His heart was beating out of his chest by the time the door opened, revealing a very sick David Rose.

David blinked and leaned against the doorframe. "Um… hi, what are you doing at my room?"

Marshall kept himself from wincing at the sound of his voice. It was thick with congestion so much so that pronouncing M's seemed to take an effort. David's nose was red and tender-looking now, his eyes puffy and his lips chapped. He didn't seem to be able to breathe through his nose at all, and Marshall's heart twisted with sympathy.

He swallowed and nodded. "I uh, I just thought I'd drop off my notes from science class. Since you weren't there."

David accepted them slowly, almost looking suspicious. "Thank you…?" He cleared his throat and changed his footing. "You wouldn't happen to have notes from our other classes, would you?" Of course someone would do a favor for the guy and he'd ask for a hundred more.

"Not… really. To be honest, I didn't notice you were missing until science, so um…"

David scoffed. "Well, that makes me feel very noticed, thank you."'

"…. Sorry."

"I'd invite you in but," he sniffled. "The whole room is like one of those petri dishes we made in class last week."

"Oh, I don't mind that." Marshall swore inwardly at himself. Could he sound any more desperate?

David only looked slightly caught off-guard by the response before nodding and giving a little chuckle. "Oh, well…"

"I- I mean! I'll need my notes back, so I can study… later…"

"Right. Right, I'll just type them up now, then. Not a problem." David bit his lip like he always seemed to do when he wanted to hide a smile. He stepped aside and held the door open.

The room was impossibly clean and organized. Marshall wasn't sure what else he should have expected, really. All of the decorations were black or white, the bedspread a combination of the two. David liked things in their place, and he liked things to fit his preferences just right.

"Wow," was all he could think to say.

"Thank you very much, I tried my best," as if David knew exactly what he meant. He grabbed a tissue from the box on his desk and pressed it to his nose, using the other hand to set the notes in front of him.

Marshall sat on the bed, feeling much more shy than he'd felt in a long time. He watched David manage to type with one hand.

David glanced over and realized Marshall was looking at him. He scoffed and wiped his nose before tossing the tissue in the overflowing bin next to the desk. "My nose won't stop running, it's obnoxious. Sorry if I look totally disgusting, but it's not like I was expecting you."

"No!" Marshall shook his head. "You look fine!" Idiot.

David glanced up, bit back another smile, and went back to typing. He snuffled through it, and blew his nose the moment he handed the notes back.

"Ugh."

"You- you know, the nurse might have some cold medicine. She needs your parents' permission to give it to you, but I mean-" Marshall shrugged and shoved his hands in his pockets, feeling ridiculous. He had his notes. He should just go.

The edge of David's lip curled up in a small smile. "I might ask about that, thanks." Was that the first sincere thing that had come out of his mouth? "But really, you should go before you catch the plague."

-----

Lucas turned a page in his comic book. "There's a movie night in the gym next weekend."

"We never go to the movie nights," Marshall mumbled. "They always play bad movies, remember?"

"I'm just saying, you could go with someone else." He flipped another page, and added, "Like David."

Marshall didn't like that smirk on his friend's face one bit.

He rolled his eyes. "Lucas, shut up. You know I can't just-"

"Why not?"

"Because- well, because." Because David was better than him in every way. Because David was a wealthy prep kid and Marshall was there on scholarship trying to scrape by and save money for his future at the same time.

"Whatever, man."

-----

David recovered in a few days, returning to classes with a lingering cough and a sense of frustration from it. He continued to correct students in the middle of lectures and decide that he was the best, and this time it was almost entertaining rather than annoying.

The bell rang signaling the end of science class, and Lucas elbowed him. "Go." he urged.

David was still packing his things, putting everything in their rightful place.

Marshall froze. And stayed frozen until Lucas physically shoved him closer. Even Z blinked in surprise.

"Hey uh…" He shoved his hands in his pockets once again.

David stared at him expectantly.

"I just remembered I have something important to get from my office," Z said, shoving his things in his bag faster than ever.

"I have something to um get from your office as well!" Lucas and Z both skedaddled out of the room until it was just the two of them.

"…Yes?" David said.

Marshall cleared his throat. "So there's this movie night in the gym once a month," he started.

David nodded. "Right. I hear they only play like, B-movies."

"Yeah," Marshall laughed nervously. "They're always completely awful. But um, do you want to go? I- with me, I mean."

David's brows raised. "Oh! Like a, like a date?" he asked lowly, as if it was a secret to keep from the empty room.

"Yeah. Yeah, like a date." He swallowed and forced himself not to vomit out of nervousness in front of his crush.

"Is there going to be food?"

"Pizza?"

David grinned. "I'd like that a lot, then." He leaned in, hesitated, and then kissed Marshall's cheek. "We should finish our project."

Marshall's entire being short-circuited. Heat rushed to his cheeks. "Right. The project. Let's do that."

-----

David didn't bother asking for permission before slipping into the sixth chair at their lunch table. His plate was piled with food--it was sloppy joe day--and Vaughn made an impressed face as he glanced at it next to him.

David took a bite of his tater tots and then looked up to find everyone looking at them. "Sorry, should I have asked-"

"No!" Everyone answered in unison.

"No, you're very welcome to sit here!" Said Corinne.

"It's just…" Josie cleared her throat. "We didn't think you wanted to after…"

"Ah," David said with realization. "Well, things are a little different now, thank you very much."

Marshall hadn't told anyone besides Lucas.

But everyone certainly knew when David reached across the table and squeezed his hand before going back to his lunch.
