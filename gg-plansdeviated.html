Plans Deviated

Lorelai wakes to the other side of the bed shifting rudely, and resists the urge to groan into her pillow. What is this, the fifth time? She longs for sleep, to be able to stay asleep, but the universe and her partner both refuse to give that to her tonight.

"Luke," she mutters into the bed, not bothering to open her eyes.

"Sorry, go back to sleep," is his only response before disappearing into the ensuite for the hundredth time.

Lorelai decides that she can only be woken up so many times in the night before she's awake for good, so she sits up with a yawn, flips on the bedside lamp and grabs her book from the nightstand. It's not a good book by any means, but it keeps her occupied and entertained, even when she forgets what she read the day before.

She continues to yawn and read and yawn and wonder if whatever is on TV is better than this book and yawn. It's three in the morning, there's probably some real weird stuff on at this time. Should she risk it?

Before she has a chance to abandon the poor book in her lap, though, Luke emerges somehow looking even more tired than she feels.

"Hey," Lorelai says with another yawn. "You okay?"

Luke gives a quick nod as he makes his way back to bed. Is he pale, or is that just the incredibly unflattering lamp light?

"Yeah, I'm fine," he answers shortly.

Lorelai looks him over with a frown. Now that she's not just feeling bad for herself because she's been woken up a million times, she realizes that he's also been awake a million times for some reason.

"Mm'kay babe, you've been in and out of the bathroom all night. You're obviously not fine."

Luke sighs. "I think I ate something weird. Can we just go back to sleep, please?" He's already sinking down into the blankets and getting comfortable.

She wants to keep reading the terrible book, but if Luke wants to sleep, they can sleep. For a while. She goes to snuggle against him and recoils more violently than necessary. He's warm, and not 'under several layers of blankets for too long' warm but unnaturally warm.

He goes to question her violent reaction with annoyance, but before he can say anything,

"Honey, you're burning up!" She says with a gasp. She can feel the subtle shivers as she's so close to him, now that she's paying attention. She turns the lamp on once again, much to his dismay, and sits up to get a better look at him.

"What?" He pulls himself up too, and looks at her like she's grown a second head.

Lorelai lays a hand against his forehead, and then his neck. He recoils when she goes for the cheek, but she doesn't need to, anyway. "You have a fever. That's a fever."

"Really?"

"Yes!"

He furrows his brows as if trying to assess how he currently feels, but before he can come to some conclusion, he swears and darts off to the bathroom again, hands already at the waistband of his pajamas.

Lorelai frowns in sympathy and pulls out her book once again.

-----

Lorelai lets him sleep in.

She calls Caesar to inform him of the situation as soon as she's downstairs, and then makes herself some breakfast.

She's just in the process of tipping the rest of the blessed coffee into her mouth when she hears footsteps upstairs, and the bathroom door closes. She expects to hear Luke return to bed a couple minutes later. Instead, the unmistakable sound of heaving and gagging. She swears and makes her way upstairs, letting herself into the bathroom to find her partner knelt in front of the toilet, looking way too weak and small for as big of a man as he was.

"Oh hon..." She coos as she kneels next to him and lays a hand on his back. He's not as warm now, but instead his shirt is soaked through with sweat. His hair looks damp, and he looks all sorts of miserable.

Luke tries to catch his breath and only heaves again. There's nothing left in his stomach, but it doesn't seem to care. She can hear his stomach gurgling angrily from where she is, and it's complaining about something that he shouldn't be punished for!

He flushes the yuck away and sits back against the tub. He leans his head back, eyes closed for a moment as he regains his bearings.

"Call Rory," he mutters after a few seconds of silence.

"Wha- oh God..." Lorelai groans. "I totally forgot!"

"So did I until I woke up," he sighs. "Great timing, huh?"

"It’s fine, babe, I'll just tell her to stay with Lane or something."

The original plan was for Rory to stay with them for the weekend. The three of them were going to go out together, do a bunch of fun things and make use of the little time she had in Stars Hollow before she had to go back to being an incredible reporter.

Lorelai shakes her head. She’ll explain things to Rory later, right now Luke needs her. So, she sits next to him against the tub. He huffs out through his nose tiredly, and lets her pull his head onto her shoulder. She’ll have to shove him into the shower later, and their sheets are probably gross and damp from sweat.

She rubs his arm with one hand and uses the other to smooth away his sweaty hair.

Sitting on the bathroom floor is possibly the most uncomfortable position one can be in, and yet Lorelai finds herself feeling sleepy. Luke is a heavy, comforting weight against her, breaths slow and even. Apparently he’s not so uncomfortable, either.

His stomach continues to make deep, unhappy noises, as if it hasn’t made him suffer enough already. She really, really hopes that it’s just settling or something, but that’s proven not to be the case when Luke wakes from his sleep with a grumble.

“Mm’kay Lorelai,” he mutters hoarsely. He sits up, hand going to his belly. “You’ve gotta leave.”

She nods. “Gotcha.” They stand up together and Luke is already shoving her out the door as she says, “Holler if you need anything!” The door closes.

Lorelai sighs heavily and grabs her phone on the way back downstairs.

“Hey!” Is Rory’s delightful greeting after the first ring. “I’m almost there, I swear. There was an insane line at the car rental place so-”

“Rory, I- wait, why are you renting a car? You were going to call us to pick you up!”

“Sorry! I didn’t rent a car, I called Lane and she picked me up!” she defends, chuckling with no true apology in her tone. “I just thought that you guys shouldn’t have to drive all the way to the airport, especially with Luke being as sick as he is.”

Lorelai blinks, scoffs. “How did you-” she stops. “Ah, Lane.”

“Caesar, to Lane, to me,” Rory explains as if Lorelai should have made that connection already.

She should have, she doesn’t know why she’s still underestimating the gossip speed of Stars Hollow.

“How’s he feeling?”

“He’s... not feeling great,” she answers lamely. “Pretty sure he was on the toilet basically all night... ah, and he’s probably going to kill me for telling you that.”

Rory makes a disgusted noise. “Thanks for that.”

Lorelai laughs, and then remembers her reason for calling in the first place. “Oh, right, I was going to tell you... you might wanna stay somewhere else, babe. We don’t want you to catch anything, especially when you’re only here for a few days.”

“Lane and Zach have been gracious enough to offer to let me sleep at their place if I want to, but we’ll see.” She doesn’t sound as disappointed as she should, Lorelai decides with a huff.

They say goodbye, and Paul Anka chooses that moment to nudge her leg in a plea for breakfast.

-----

Lorelai and Paul Anka both rush to the foyer at the knock at the door. He barks happily at the sight of Rory, jumping up to her lap until she gives him sufficient pets.

“Rory!” Lorelai scolds, while still smiling. “I thought we were meeting in the park!”

“I know, but I just... I wanted to be home first, okay? Just for a little while.”

She can’t bring herself to say no, not when she hasn’t seen her kid in months an she and Luke have been using this day to get them through the week up until he got sick. She wraps Rory in the most suffocating hug she can manage until her daughter makes a noise of annoyance and pushes her away.

“Michel’s got the weekend shift, I’m all yours.”

“I’d like to walk in the door first,” she says, dragging her bags into the foyer.

Lorelai scoffs. “I mean sure, that’s fine I guess.”

They make their way into the living room and get settled on the couch. Lorelai asks about work, and sparks a big long story about a guy in an office who chastised her choice of flats over high heels. She can’t be more proud when Rory says that she informed him that he should be wearing heels as well, very loudly in the middle of the busy office.

The thumping of someone coming down the stairs gets their attention from the giggle fest they were having about what kind of heels her coworker would look best in.

Luke is in jeans and a T-shirt, not having gotten his cap on quite yet. His hair is still damp from a shower, and Lorelai can already smell the body wash of hers that he swears he doesn’t use. It shouldn’t surprise her that he’s not in pajamas. She’s known him long enough that she shouldn’t expect the guy to stay in bed all day. He’s pale, looks exhausted, but his face lights up the minute he sees who else is sitting on the couch.

“Rory!” Luke exclaims, a mix of surprise and delight. He hesitates when she gets up to hug him, but he’s clearly just as happy to see her as Lorelai was, wrapping her in a hug. “I thought you-”

Lorelai rolls her eyes. “She’s a disobedient child, Luke. I don’t know who raised her like that.”

Rory laughs and goes back to her spot on the couch, while Luke takes the chair and looks like he’s longed to sit since he came downstairs. “I just really wanted to come to the house, okay? And now you guys can’t kick me out, or you’d be rude hosts.”

“Ah, manipulation. Nice,” Lorelai says with a nod of approval. “Now I know who raised you like that.”

Luke scoffs. “It’s nice to see you, Rory,” he says, pointedly ignoring Lorelai’s comments. “I promise, I didn’t plan on getting sick this weekend.”

Rory sighs dramatically, leaning against her mom and putting her feet on the coffee table in the process. “It’s fine, Luke. Gastrointestinal distress happens to the best of us.”

“And with that lovely note,” he huffs and stands up. Lorelai watches him waver for just a second before he catches his balance on the back of the chair, and wonders if he’s dizzy or if his legs are just shaky. “I’m going to call Caesar and make sure everything’s alright.”

They wait to talk until his voice echoes from the kitchen, explaining the cold banana ban to Caesar once again. There’s some clanking, water running. Sounds like he’s making tea.

“Wow, he looks terrible,” Rory says. “I don’t know if I’ve ever seen Luke that sick.”

“You weren’t around for the great flu epidemic of ‘05. Granted, I was also very sick. Maybe sicker.” She groans at Rory’s raised eyebrow. “Okay definitely sicker, which is not fair because he’s always so together, why am I the one who gets all dead when I’m sick?”

That’s how Luke has always been. He gets sick, and goes on with his day. A bad cold never stopped him from cleaning out the gutters or fixing the porch, nor will a stomach flu stop him from tinkering with the sink like it sounds like he’s doing now. Lorelai, on the other hand, always finds herself tragically draped across the couch at the first sniffle. And he fondly teases her every time, before looking after her.

She still remembers the horrible flu that had gone around Stars Hollow when she and Luke first started dating. They both got sick so suddenly that it was impossible to determine where it came from or who infected who first. They were each conked out with fevers and coughs and enough snot to drown themselves.

It was still somehow a nice few days together.

“God damn it...” Luke mutters, followed by the clank of something metal falling to the floor.

“You all good, babe?” she calls.

“Yeah, ‘ts fine!”

She scoffs and turns to her daughter. “He’s fixing the faucet. He got maybe two hours of sleep last night and he’s fixing the faucet.”

Rory gives a shrug. “That’s Luke.”

“What kind of heels would he look good in? I’m thinking stilettos.”

She screws up her face in thought and takes a few seconds before, “Wedges.”

“Really? He seems like a wedge guy to you?”

-----

Luke watches the lack of drip on the faucet with a triumphant sigh of relief. The relief only lasts for a moment, however, before he realizes that he has no idea what to do with himself now. He really doesn’t feel that bad, besides of course the constant dull nag of nausea in his gut and the headache. The smell of Lorelai’s coffee in the coffeemaker isn’t helping any.

Tea! Damn it, he was making tea before he got sidetracked. Luke goes back to where he’d been before the faucet taunted him with that drip and fills the kettle. He sets it on the stove and huffs.

God, he’s tired. Part of him knows that he needs rest, that he should just go back to bed and relax or whatever sick people are supposed to do, but the mere idea has his skin crawling. He’s not good at just lying there. He needs to do something, and the tap is already fixed.

He desperately wants to go to the diner and work, but he’ll get kicked out by half the patrons the moment he steps foot inside.

Luke gasps quickly at the cold hand on his neck, and realizes that he’s been staring at nothing for a good couple of minutes.

“You’re kinda hot,” says Lorelai before filling her coffee cup to the brim. She leans against the counter and takes a sip. It’s a wonder how the woman never seems to burn her tongue, as if she’s built up a specific immunity to hot coffee.

“Well, thank you. That’s nice of you to say.”

Lorelai snorts. “Yeah, that fever makes you sexier than ever, babe.” She glances at the stove, and back at him. “So, what’cha doin’?”

“Tea,” he answers simply.

“Ah,” she nods. There’s a long few seconds that Luke forgets to notice before, “You uh, gonna turn the stove on?”

“What? Oh...” Luke looks up at the dials and sure enough, he never bothered to turn on a burner. He growls low under his breath, shaking his head at himself as he finally flips on the heat and hopes he can get his tea made any time within the next century. “Jesus Christ, what is with me today?”

Lorelai laughs a bit and sets her coffee aside so she can wrap her arms around him. It’s uncomfortable in away, because his skin feels overly-sensitive to every touch and he’s not sure whether he’s hot or cold, but he finds himself turning to embrace her more easily. It’s warm, but a good kind of warmth.

“You’re just a little out of it! It happens to the best of us, especially when we accidentally take twice the recommended Benadryl and decide to watch Willy Wonka upside down.”

Luke hums, remembering his partner with her legs over the top of the couch, amazed at the chocolate river scene. “And you’re never taking Benadryl again if I can help it.” He sighs. “I just need to wake up, that’s all.”

“You’re not gonna listen if I tell you to go back to bed, are you?”

“Nope.” He presses a kiss to the top of her head before they slowly pull away from each other, and he finds himself missing her warmth.

“Fair enough. Want me to make you some toast? Or... pop tarts? Rory always liked the strawberry ones when she wasn’t feeling good, granted she always puked them up again, but...”

“It was always worth it,” says the second Gilmore as she wanders in. “Strawberry pop tarts are an experience.”

“They certainly are when you’re cleaning strawberry vomit off the floor.”

“You could have said no to the pop tarts, mom!”

“Yeah but you were so cute and sad and your doe eyes were working overtime whenever you were sick.”

“Can we stop talking about food,” Luke begs the moment that one isn’t talking over the other. “Or vomit. Or strawberry pop tarts.”

Lorelai chuckles. “Sorry, hon. I’ll just make some toast and you can decide if you want it.” She kisses his cheek and drops a slice of bread into the toaster.

“What do you two have planned today?” He makes an attempt to change the subject from anything food related. He might not be in immediate danger of vomiting, but that doesn’t mean he wants to hear about any of it.

“Andrew has a few books set aside for me, so I need to go pick those up.”

“The bookstore? Boring! I thought we were gonna go shopping and eat a ton of-” Lorelai stops herself. “Air.”

Luke scoffs.

Rory nods. “Good save.”

“Thank you.”

“Going to the bookstore is still technically shopping, just for books instead of more clothes you don’t need.”

“Hey missy, you’re one to judge!”

Rory gives a long groan, tilting her head back for full dramatic effect. “Please just take me to the bookstore and then we can do whatever you want.”

“Fine!” She says with delight. “Whatever I want, so-”

“No. Stop.”

“-If I wanted to, like, take a trip to Disney World and-”

“Oh my God, mom,” but the two of them are laughing hysterically, excited to be together again.

The two of them always double the amount of insanity in the room, but its worth it, because Luke loves watching the love of his life so damn happy to be with her daughter.

The phone starts to ring and Lorelai groans. “Okay, give me a sec. Watch Luke’s toast!”

“I’ll protect it with my life!” Rory calls back.

He waits until Lorelai’s out of earshot to mutter, “I don’t want the toast. Do you want the toast?” As if on cue, the toast pops up and both of them look at it strangely.

“Not especially,” she admits.

Lorelai stomps back into the kitchen, wearing an expression that can’t mean anything good. “I don’t believe this!” she says, throwing her hands up in the air.

Rory’s already spreading peanut butter over the slice of toast that she didn’t want. “What’s wrong?”

A growl. “They need me at work. There’s this whole crisis with the laundry and Michel is on the verge of a panic attack and if I don’t get there I think he might actually explode and that can’t be good for our rating.” She’s already draping her jacket over her arm and pulling them each into a hug, sighing sadly at her daughter. “I’m sorry.”

“It’s okay! I’ll be in town all weekend.”

“But your books, too!” Lorelai laments.

“Mom, it’s fine. I can walk, or I can get them tomorrow. I don’t need-”

“I’ll drive you,” Luke volunteers before he even realizes it. The two women look at him with identical raised eyebrows, but Lorelai gives in first. He just wants to get out of the house. He fears that he will lose his mind if he doesn’t find something to do, and if that something is just taking Rory to pick up her books, that’s fine for now.

“Whatever you two decide, I’ve gotta go rescue Michel. Save some toast for me!” She offers each of them a kiss on the cheek.

“Nope, all mine!” Rory says as her mother walks away, continuing to enjoy the abandoned breakfast.

And then it’s just the two of them. Rory finishes her toast while Luke makes tea for the both of him. He sets down in front of her, interrupting her with a finger and an “ah!” before she says anything.

“Drink the tea. Tea’s good for you. Keeps you... healthy and stuff.”

She huffs, but complies. “Doesn’t look like it’s doing you any good,” Rory takes a sip as he pulls a chair out and sits across from her.

“Yeah well, that’s my problem.” He tries to ignore her scrutiny, he really does, but she’s got that signature Gilmore stare that says she’s got something to say.

“Really though, you don’t look good. I can walk into town, you know.”

He shakes his head immediately. “It’s freezing out, and I don’t mind.” He sighs and drinks the tea. “And I really need to do something today.”

“Right,” Rory nods. “Mom always says that you’re bad at sick days.”

He narrows his eyes. “She does, huh?"

"Mhm, and I'm inclined to agree. I mean, c'mon, being sick is like the one time you can lay in bed all day with no shame?"

Luke grunts. Rory gives a knowing nod.

"Aaand that's what you're afraid of."

"Just let me drive you to the damn bookstore, Rory. Let me feel like a person."

She shrugs and tosses the last bit of toast in her mouth. “If you insist.”

Luke has never truly noticed how bumpy his truck was until today. He swallows, ignoring the nausea that's growing from the winding roads of Stars Hollow that he swears weren't here before this. Surely, he’s not going to throw up because of a simple drive into town. He can’t imagine he’d have anything left to throw up anyway, right?

Still, Luke brings a hand to his sour stomach with a frown as he parks the truck by the bookstore. The light of the morning exacerbates his headache, and he suddenly feels ten times more exhausted now that they’re not at home.

Worst of all, Rory is giving him the same look that her mother does when she’s silently worrying.

“I’ll be back,” she says as she climbs out of the truck. She adjusts the bag on her shoulder, gaze staying on him for a moment.

“Alright!”

She looks like she’s about to ask him one of those questions he hates, like ‘are you okay?’ or ‘why do you look like a zombie?’, but she just returns his acknowledging smile and nod and heads into the bookstore.

Luke sighs. And he finds himself shivering. Really? Chills? Now? Jesus Christ, the universe hates him. That’s the only explanation. He’s wearing a coat, for God’s sake. He shouldn’t be cold. Said coat is scratchy against his sensitive skin, and he sort of wishes he’d taken some tylenol before they left.

Luke doesn’t realize that she’s out of the bookstore (or that he was falling asleep) until he startles at the truck does opening. Rory doesn’t notice his flinch, thank goodness, as she gets in and sets the bag of brand new books in her lap.

“It went okay?”

“My books purchase?” Rory asks, a chuckle and a raised eyebrow. “Yeah, it went fine. We can go home now. Thanks for bringing me, Luke.”

Go home? They’ve hardly been out for ten minutes. And then he’ll be back to where he started, hating himself and the whole world for making him feel useless and having nothing to think about but how miserable he feels and how he can’t do anything about it.

And to his surprise, with a glance over at Rory, she doesn’t look eager to go home, either.

“You wanna get coffee, don’t you.” It’s not a question. He can see it.

“... yeah, yes... I really do,” she admits. “But you don’t have to-”

“No, c’mon,” he unbuckles his seat belt. “Let’s go to the diner. It’ll give me a chance to see how Caesar’s doing on his power trip. I should’ve known better than to make you drink tea.”

“It was good tea,” Rory tries.

“But you’re a coffee junkie just like Lorelai.”

“Always.”

They start their walk to the diner, and Luke does his best to hide the shivers that overtake him as the cold hits him. He zips his coat the rest of the way, fully aware that he looks ridiculous. And God, did his muscles always ache this much?

Caesar is predictably disappointed when they walk in, until Luke explains that he’s not there to work and the diner is still under his control. His employee breaths a sigh of relief and pours Rory’s coffee.

And, for whatever reason, whether it be the change in temperature from outside to in, the smell of food and grease and coffee, or the sight of food and grease and coffee, the nausea hits him tenfold.

Luke swallows convulsively. There’s no God damn way he’s letting himself throw up. Not here, not anywhere. He’s fine, he’s been fine since they left.

He stifles a wet burp into a gloved fist as Rory returns with her paper coffee cup and nods toward the door, signaling that she’s ready to go. Suddenly, home is the only place he wants to be right now. Home is warm, and home is where he won’t have spectators when he spends the next however long puking his brains out.

“Caesar’s going to be crushed when you come back,” Rory says. She wears a pleasant smile, directed at the coffee in her hands as she takes a sip. Luke shoves his hands in his pockets and tries not to shudder violently.

His stomach gurgles menacingly, and there’s a familiar sour taste in the back of his mouth that doesn’t mean anything good. He has to at least make it the drive back before something bad happens, he has enough control over his body for that, right?

“Luke?”

“Hm?”

Rory’s scrutinizing him with a deep frown, brows knitted together with concern. She stops walks and waves a hand toward herself. “Come closer for a minute?”

He raises an eyebrow, but takes an extra step closer. Rory’s removing her glove for some-

Aw, damn.

Her hand is on his forehead before he knows it. It’s not as cold as it could be after holding the hot beverage, but it still feels freezing against his skin. If his minor flinch at the feeling, or the glances from the patrons in the ice cream shopper, weren’t humiliating enough, Rory gasps as she pulls her hand away.

“You’re burning up!” she says, using the exact exasperated tone that Lorelai does.

He scoffs and continues walking until she’s forced to as well, giving a dismissive wave. The faster they get to the truck, the faster they get home, and the less likely he is to vomit in front of the entire town. “It’s not that bad.”

“Yes it is!” she argues.

“Nah, it just seemed like it ‘cause it’s co-” he stops.

His stomach does a sickening flip and he suddenly knows that it’s too late for him.

“Luke...?” Rory asks with caution. “Are you okay...?”

He shakes his head a swallows convulsively.

“Are you going to throw up?”

He nods, brings a hand to his mouth.

“Like right now?”

Luke gags. Nothing comes up the first time, thank God, but he can feel whatever could possibly be in his stomach preparing to eject itself.

“Oh God, okay, uh...” Rory looks around in a panic and he finds himself getting dragged by the arm to the alley behind the bookstore.

The smell of the dumpsters helps speed up the process, as Luke gags again and this time slime drips through his fingers until he has the sense to remove his hand and brace himself against a brick wall.

The tea. The damn tea comes up, along with bile and the single saltine Lorelai talked him into eating early, early that morning. He heaves, and spits into the horrible puddle below.

Rory pats his back, and he realizes belatedly that her coffee is missing. She must’ve thrown it away when she was helping him avoid being the town spectacle. She hands him a napkin from her coat pocket.

“Jesus Christ,” he mutters as he stands up and wipes his face. He gives his nose a quick blow and sniffs, still feeling the post-vomiting sting inside his nostrils.

“I’ll drive home. C’mon, keys.”

He has no choice but to put them in her waiting hand. He’s in no position to even try to argue that he’s fit to drive.

-----

“Do we have to tell your mom about this?” he sighs out. “Because I’ll be hearing about it for the next decade.”

“I won’t tell,” she says as she hangs her coat on the rack and kicks her shoes off. “But that doesn’t mean that rest of the town won’t.”

“Of course,” Luke mutters.

He’s half asleep on the couch, covered in an old crochet throw Rory bullied him into taking when he started shivering. The coffee table is littered with medicines and water bottles and a lukewarm mug of tea. The TV is playing at a volume low enough for him to fall in and out of sleep to.

He feels supremely useless.

Rory’s doing work in the kitchen with some horrible music playing. Luke and Lorelai have both teased her for her recent horrible music taste, but that doesn’t stop her.

And that’s the rather calm state that the house is in when Lorelai runs through the door to the downstairs bathroom and vomits.

-----

He runs a hand up and down her back as she throws up.

They’ve managed to move their misery to upstairs, but not before kicking Rory out for the rest of the day.

Lorelai heaves, but there’s nothing really left to come up. Apparently most of her breakfast was evacuated as soon as she got home. So, Luke is left sitting next to her on their bathroom floor, swallowing back his own nausea.

Lorelai sniffles, and then again. He’s ready to grab a tissue or a wad of toilet paper for her to wipe her face with, and then he realizes that those are sad, Lorelai’s-trying-not-to-cry sniffs.

“Sweetheart, what’s wrong?”

She shakes her head, dragging her sleeve across her face in lieu of anything made for the purpose, and Luke can hardly judge her when he’s in the same state. She leans against him until the two of them are sitting cuddled against the wall, a true pitiful sight to anyone who would’ve found them.

Lorelai’s shuddering, so he pulls a towel from the nearest rack and helps her wrap herself in it. It’s not going to help her get warm if she has a fever, he knows that, but maybe it offers some kind of comfort.

She rubs some fresh tears from her cheek and gives a watery chuckle, shaking her head at herself like she can’t believe this is happening.

“I was just excited to see Rory, that’s all.”

He sighs. “Yeah, me too.”

“And now you’re in here when watching me puke is probably the last thing you want to be doing right now.”

“I’ll be okay,” he promises. If he’s being honest, this is the happiest he’s been all day. He should probably feel guilty for that, but it’s just nice to feel somewhat useful.

She sniffs again. “We probably should have anticipated this, right? I mean, when’s the last time only one of us has gotten sick.”

He hums in thought. “I seem to remember a particular moment involving some two week old Chinese leftovers that you swore were still good.”

“Luke,” she says, low and warning. “Don’t you dare bring that up right now. I will puke on you.”

“You did then, too.”

“Oh my- I swear to God...” she groans, already looking more nauseated.

He chuckles. “If it makes you feel any better... I puked in the bookstore alley today.”

Lorelai gives a single, loud unapologetic laugh. “Excuse me?”

“Mhm. The only reason it wasn’t allover the sidewalk was because Rory had the sense to drag me away.”

“God, you wouldn’t have heard the end of it from anyone.”

“Tell me about it.”

Lorelai hums contently, still a bit tearful.

They sit in silence for a few minutes, and Luke dreads whenever they have to get up off of this floor. God, he’s getting old. Is his foot asleep, or has he lost feeling in his entire leg?

“Luke,” she says, voice breaking. She sniffs liquidly and scrubs at her eyes again, and he feels terrible for not noticing that she’d begun to cry in what he thought was a comfortable silence. “You don’t have to stay in here.”

He scoffs. “Really? You begged me to sleep in here with you after the Chinese incident.”

She makes an attempt to laugh, and it just comes out as a snort that shoots watery liquid onto her upper lip. “Yeah, but you’re really sick, too. You should be in bed.”

The idea of leaving her side doesn’t please him one bit. Not after a day of trying not to feel useless, and failing spectacularly.

“I’d rather stay with you, if that’s okay.”

She smiles, hums happily, and snuggles into him. “That’s fine with me.”

“I thought so.” He kisses her head, and they hunker down for a nice and miserable night together.

-----

Rory’s banishment doesn’t last long, for several reasons.

One of those reason being that Lorelai has been mopey and sad and tearful since they had to send her daughter away on the weekend that they were supposed to spend together. Luke can’t take it anymore, he can’t do anything to make her feel better.

They’d also failed to grab anything liquid from the kitchen before they sequestered themselves upstairs, and the two of them are beginning to get thirsty, and rather testy about which of them has to go downstairs to get water or ginger ale.

So, they call Rory. Or rather, Luke calls Rory while Lorelai is in the bathroom, attempting to brush the taste of vomit out of her mouth with minimal success.

There’s what sounds like an intense game of scrabble going on with Lane and Zack’s kids in the background, and it fades as Rory realizes it’s him on the phone and steps away.

“Your mom misses you,” he says, fully aware that his voice sounds downright pathetic. “And...” he sighs, shakes his head at himself. “We could use some help. Stairs are... difficult.”

Rory agrees in a heartbeat. He can already hear her telling the kids that she has to go, and that yes, ‘qua’ is an acceptable word, sorry Steve.

“I’ll grab some stuff from Doose’s,” she says.

They discuss the potential shopping list, among other things as she puts her coat on. He hears her ask Lane for a ride, who readily agrees.

“Thanks, Rory.” He’s in the middle of hanging up when Lorelai comes back to bed, looking ten shades paler.

Her brows knit together as she looks at him. “Rory? You called Rory?” There’s an excitement in her voice that shouldn’t be there after throwing up for the past few hours, and yet... “She’s coming?”

“Yup.”

Lorelai climbs under the blankets and curls up against her pillows, shuddering from a fever that won’t quit. Neither of them have been able to keep any kind of fever reducer down quite yet.

“I missed her.”

“It’s been a handful of hours.”

“I still missed her.”

Luke supposes that shouldn’t surprise him.

-----

“You two are a sorry sight.”

“We know,” Lorelai groans as she sits up. She makes grabby hands for the Doose’s bag. “Give it here, what’d you get me?”

“Something you’ll probably throw up later,” Rory says. She sits on the edge of the bed and watches her mom pull various things out the bag. “Are you guys sure you didn’t eat some old Chinese leftovers or something?”

Luke snorts. He finds himself curious enough to help Lorelai dig through the groceries, in search for Gatorade or something drinkable after Lorelai’s complained of dying of thirst for the past hour.

“I’m never going to hear the end of the leftovers incident, am I?”

“Nope,” Luke and Rory say in hilarious unison.
