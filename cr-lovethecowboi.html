Title: Love the Cow Boi
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Caduceus isn't feeling well and Jester is going to do everything in her power to make him feel better
Word count: 1.4k

Love the Cow Boi

When Jester comes to the kitchen late at night for a glass of milk, the last thing she expects to see is someone else awake, let alone Caduceus.

"Hello!" she greets, and immediately feels guilty when she sees him jump out of startlement.

Caduceus turns to look at her and offers his trademark smile, though understandably weary seeing as though it's the middle of the night. A candle on the table provides a soft light throughout the room, and Jester can see a half-full cup of steaming tea sitting in front of her fellow-cleric.

"Oh, hey. Couldn’t sleep?"

Jester shakes her head. "I'm just not tired, and Caleb's snoring super loud. I thought it was you at first, but guess not!"

Caduceus chuckles. He takes a small sip of his tea and sets it back on the table. Jester gets her milk and sits next to him.

"You couldn’t sleep either, huh?"

He shakes his head and then looks as if he's about to pick up his teacup again before changing his mind. "Not at all," he answers rather exasperated at the thought.

Jester makes a sympathetic noise and leans against his arm. Being closer, she notices there's something different about the tea. She leans in a bit and finds that it smells… spicier than usual.

"That's a different tea," she comments before taking a sip of her milk.

A nod. "It's ginger. It's good for settling your stomach."

Jester frowns. "…Oh… is it helping?" the motion of the boat has taken a while for most of them to get used to, but there are still days when it'll make them feel a little woozy and tired. She knows Caduceus isn't a big fan of living on a boat, maybe it's because it makes him sick.

Caduceus pauses. "I'm not… sure." his brows furrow as he thinks about it. "I'll be thankful for just couple hours of sleep, to be honest." Jester notices that he's almost hesitant to drink his tea as he lifts it.

Jester takes a good look at him in the dim light, taking in his uncomfortable expression and weird shifting. He looks different, and he even smells different. The room is silent save for the sounds of them sipping their respective drinks, until there's a distinct gurgle.

"Was that your belly?" Jester asks, half amused and half concerned. She's pretty sure no one's stomach is supposed to make those kinds of noises.

Caduceus is noticeably embarrassed. A hand goes to his stomach, unguarded by his armor. Jester has enjoyed seeing him in casual outfits on the boat. He wears the prettiest, most interesting stuff during the day, and a simple loose shirt and leggings at night.

"Ah… yeah, sorry."

Jester eyes him worriedly as he takes a much tinier sip of his tea. It's hard to tell in the candlelight, but he looks flushed, and his eyes are a bit red as if they'd been watery and rubbed. His hair is stringy as if it's very oily or damp.

His belly makes another unhappy sound that has Caduceus wincing at first, and then suddenly he's wrapping his arms around his middle and doubling over, eyes squeezed shut.

"Caduceus! Are you alright?"

His eyes open, though he doesn’t look in much less discomfort. He lets out an involuntary moan. "I guess the tea didn’t help. I don’t feel very well, I might go lie down again…" he doesn’t make any move to stand.

Jester rubs his back. It's sweaty and hot through his clothes, and she only feels more sympathy for him. He must be pretty uncomfortable. Hopefully his seasickness will fade with time.

She helps him to his room - or rather - follows him because she's worried and he doesn’t seem to mind. He gets into bed and doesn’t protest when she lets herself into his room.

"When did this start? You seemed okay at dinner!"

He nodded. "It came on fast, but I think these things usually do. I was almost asleep when it started bothering me the most."

The much brighter lantern in his room gave her a better look at him. Jester felt his forehead and everything unfortunately made sense.

"This isn't seasickness is it, Caduceus?"

A flash of embarrassment came over his face. "Oh, no I don’t think so. I felt worse after I threw up. That doesn’t usually happen when I'm seasick. Weird, right?"

Jester's face fell, her heart dropping. Guilt and sadness and sympathy. Of course Caduceus didn’t feel the need to tell anyone he wasn’t feeling good. He's lived alone for long enough, had to take care of himself. She notices a bucket next to his bed, it's glistening inside like it's been rinsed out recently.

"Caduceus…"

"Hm?"

"I'm sorry you're feeling sick."

"It happens, and it won't last forever..." he folds over once again, going from his relaxed position back to being curled up in pain. A stifled whine of discomfort rises from his throat.

She watches with a pang of concern and rubs his arm. It's warm, and she wonders if she didn’t notice earlier or if his temperature is just rising that quickly. "Do you want me to stay with you tonight?"

He smiles wearily. "I really appreciate the sentiment, but there's no reason for you to be bothered by this as well." He burps a bit and then blushes. "And I'm not very good company right now."

Jester knows one thing about Caduceus - he doesn’t lie. He believes he should be doing this on his own and he doesn’t see a reason for her to be around.

"I'll leave if you really want me to." Caduceus looks at her a bit pitifully. "Do you want me to?"

"Not especially."

"That's all you had to say! It's no fun being sick all night when you're alone, I know that for sure."

With that, Caduceus leans down and bumps his warm forehead against her shoulder. She takes it as a thanks.

She falls asleep a bit. She's unsure if he sleeps at all, because she wakes up a couple times to him shifting uncomfortably, and then vomiting. Jester tries to stay awake after that, and sometimes helps him get out of bed before he throws up again.

They're both sleeping uncomfortably by morning, only woken by Fjord knocking on the door.

"Deuces?"

Jester sits up groggily and looks at the still-sleeping firbolg next to her. He's curled up, not looking particularly rested. She frowns and quietly opens the door before Fjord can get too loud.

The half-orc definitely didn't expect to see her come out of Caduceus' room.

"Ah... Hey, uh, Jester."

"Shh!" She shoves a finger in his face, nearly touching his lips, before quietly closing the door behind her. "Don't wake him! Caduceus is very sick. He needs sleep!"

Fjord raises an eyebrow and looks like he's processing the fact that their zen cleric is actually capable of coming down ill.

"He's been throwing up all night and if you wake him now I will throw you overboard."

Fjord raises his hands defensively. "I was just coming to see why he wasn't up yet. And uh, now I know..." He turns to start walking away, but stops himself and looks back at her. "Is he gonna be alright? I mean, it's not some magical sea plague or something, right?"

"I don't think so, Fjord, I think he just picked up a bug at our last stop." The last stop where he was consuming food from all sorts of unsanitary sources.

"Ah, okay. Well..." Fjord gives an awkward wave and walks away. Jester smiles. What a dweeb.

She leaves to have some breakfast and when she returns to Caduceus' room, he's awake and looking awfully miserable.

"I brought you a banana," Jester says. "You know, in case you felt like eating."

Caduceus gives a smile, but it's not quite as comforting as his usual.

"That's nice of you. I'm not sure I feel like eating anything right now, though."

"Understandable since you threw up like five times last night!"

Caduceus pales.

"Sorry, I'll try not to talk about that. How are you feeling?"

He furrows his brows and thinks for a moment, sitting up further on the bed. "Tired. Really tired. I think I might go back to sleep soon."

"That's probably a good idea." Jester perches on the edge of the bed and palms his forehead. He leans into it with a satisfied hum, which makes sense because he's still too warm. "You'll get some rest, then?"

"Mhm. Definitely." he hardly looks like he can keep his eyes open now. Jester smiles, kisses each of his cheeks and then his forehead, earning another happy noise from him.

"Feel better, okay?"

"Mhm…" Caduceus mumbles as he snuggles back into bed.
