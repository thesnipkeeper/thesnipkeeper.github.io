Road Trip!

Road trips were supposed to be fun. You were supposed to take too long to get to your destination because you're stopping to eat and rest at different restaurants.

It was a chance to enjoy yourself before you arrived somewhere where you had work to do. 

That was Marisha, Matt, and Taliesin's idea of fun.

Road trips weren't fun when someone was afflicted with a stomach bug.

They stopped at a cute little family restaurant on their way to the convention, with worn carpet and a faded sign on the front.

They sold burgers, fries, and various crime-against-humanity sandwiches. Marisha lived for it, it reminded Matt of his hometown, and Taliesin wasn't picky when it came to food.

At least, he usually wasn't. 

Matt and Marisha watched with raised eyebrows as he ordered fries and a water. They didn't think too much of it, they knew he didn't sleep well the night before so it was possible that he was just really tired.

The unfinished plate of fries ended up getting pushed toward Marisha.

“Are you feeling okay?” Matt asked as he took a bite of his overly-cooked burger.

Taliesin smiled. “Yeah! Yeah, just really tired, you know? I’m ready for this weekend to be over.”

Marisha made a sympathetic noise. They could all understand that. All the traveling and conventions could be too much sometimes, and Taliesin especially wore a lot of hats.

Cut to their car parked on the side of the road as they try not to watch their friend vomit into the grass. It was the third time they had to stop.

They had to pull into a gas station once more, and Taliesin nearly ran into a display in his rush to get to the bathroom. Matt bought a Sprite and a Gatorade and waited outside the door for Taliesin.

He was in the backseat trying to sleep, had been for a couple hours. They hadn’t had to pull over in a while, so they took that as a good sign, but he was still clearly miserable. He shifted every once in a while with a stifled whimper as it jostled his sore insides. Marisha tried to drive smoothly, but that wasn’t always easy.

“Getting kinda hungry,” She said quietly as to not disturb their friend.

Matt nodded. “Let’s stop somewhere.”

They pulled into the drive through while Matt reached back, gently shaking Taliesin’s knee. 

“Mm?” 

“We’re stopping for supper. Want anything, buddy?”

Taliesin huffed out a laugh. Yeah, that was fair. “No.”

“Are you sure? You haven’t really eaten anything.”

“Not anything that hasn’t come back up, no,” he ran a hand down his face and sighed. 

Marisha ordered him something anyway. He took one bite, to his credit, before handing it back.

“I hate this,” he said with a sigh. “Not being able to eat is the worst.”

“I know,” Marisha said sympathetically. “Remember when I had that bug a couple months ago? Everything I ate and drank came right back up. For like, two days.”

“Thanks for that. Makes me feel a lot better,” Taliesin deadpanned, although he couldn’t hold back a laugh.

Matt chuckled. “I had to take you to the ER, though, so can we not compare that time to now?”

Taliesin scoffed. “You’re gonna have to fight me to get me into the ER.”

“Let’s not think about that!” Matt was obviously getting freaked out, which made them laugh even more. Marisha patted his shoulder. 

“Everything’s gonna be fine, babe, don’t worry.”

They made it to the hotel in one piece, although Taliesin had to make frequent trips to the bathroom. He couldn’t possibly have anything left to vomit, and sometimes he didn’t. 

He was curled up in bed, and they could tell his stomach was bothering him, even when he didn’t say anything. He got up, hand on his stomach, and returned without having made a sound.

“You okay?” Matt asked, brows knitted together in concern. 

Taliesin sighed as he got comfortable in his bed once again. “Yeah, I just… I keep feeling like I’m gonna throw up.”

“Ugh,” Marisha said. “That’s the worst.”

Taliesin nodded in agreement. He cuddled himself tightly under the blanket. He was almost asleep, shivering and waiting for the cramps to pass.

Until he heard movement on the other side of the room, and suddenly the weight of two bodies in his bed.

“You guys are gonna get sick,” he sing-songed (badly) into his pillow with eyes still closed.

“That’s okay,” Matt mumbled with his face smushed against Taliesin’s back. “Think you needed this.”
