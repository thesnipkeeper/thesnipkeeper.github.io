On Return

Runaan let out a light gasp as Ethari came up behind him an wrapped his arms around his waist.

“I think it’s time for you to rest, my love.” He muttered, pressing a kiss to Runaan’s overly-warm cheek.

Runaan leaned into the contact, but made a noise of reluctance that told Ethari he hadn’t quite gotten through.

“You know that I can’t,” he replied tiredly, and continued to clean his weapons. "Not yet."

Ethari sighed and rested his forehead between Runaan’s shoulder blades. “Can’t blame me for trying.” He released his grip on his husband with some reluctance, and went to sharpen his own equipment. Busywork to keep him from bothering Runaan and still keep an eye on him in their home workshop, but it was enough for right now.

Runaan wasn’t the type to come home from a mission and do nothing. He was always wired and restless, buzzing about and doing things (sometimes irritating Ethari and Rayla in the process, but they knew to expect it at that point). Ethari would finally bully him into bed and effectively hold him down cuddle him until Runaan passed out and got the rest he needed.

Runaan had returned from the latest mission that morning, only this time he was being supported by one of the others in his group, a younger man who looked like he didn’t expect to be supporting his leader’s weight today.

Ethari gasped. “What happened? Where are you hurt?” He took Runaan’s weight on himself, nodding a thanks to the young man who made a quick exit.

Runaan found Ethari’s hands as they were jumbling around, looking for blood or injury of any kind. “I’m not hurt.” and that was all he managed before he tore from his husband’s grasp and vomited on the ground.

He begun to feel under the weather right before their journey home began. Runaan was feverish and definitely exhausted.

He took a moment to gather his bearings and drink the medicine Ethari gave him - a tonic that was usually reserved for Rayla when she wasn’t feeling well, but Ethari begged, insisting it would give him peace of mind if Runaan were to drink it as well.

Runaan was up and walking around after an hour of not-so-restful rest, though obviously still feeling sick and lightheaded. He went to his things and started to care for his weapons as he did whenever he returned from a mission.

And that was what Ethari was forced to deal with at this very moment. A stubborn, sick assassin who decided that a small break and a drink of juice was enough to cure him.

“I’m glad Rayla’s not here to see this,” said Ethari, trying to sound as casual as possible. “You’re a terrible influence. I have a hard enough time getting her to rest as it is.” It was meant to be a joke, but Runaan didn’t seem to think it was very funny.

He silently laid the bowblade on their beat up workshop table and turned around.

“Oh, Runaan... I was only kid- love?”

One glance at his husband’s face as he walked past told him that he wasn’t angry, he may not have even been paying attention to what Ethari had said, because soon he was vomiting in the yard once again.

He fell to his knees this time, and Ethari rushed to kneel behind him and run gentle circles on his back as he shook with each violent heave.

Ethari offered gentle, comforting words.

Runaan took a moment to catch his breath. Rarely did he look as out of sorts as he did right now, eyes wide and rimmed with tears from the force of throwing up.

“Turn this way?” Ethari said, gently guiding Runaan’s chin until he was facing him. “There we are...” He dabbed Runaan’s chin as the other continued to breathe heavily. “Hold still?” His heart warmed just a little when Runaan all but allowed him to press the cloth against his nose and wipe all of the gunk that threatened to spill out. He supposed to came with raising a child that these things no longer bothered him.

Runaan seemed to finally regain his stubbornness. His eyebrows knitted together, and he looked down with a noise of annoyance. Not at Ethari, but at himself, as if he had any choice in what was happening to his body right now.

“I know, love,” Ethari cupped his face, running a thumb along his cheekbone. “Now, please do me the favor of going to bed?”

Not exactly feeling like speaking, Runaan just nodded and allowed Ethari to help him to his feet.

-----

Ethari smiled fondly as he pulled the quilts over his husband. Runaan looked close to falling asleep already; Ethari’s job was nearly done.

“I can barely recall the last time you were ill. It’s been so long.”

Runaan huffed a sleepy chuckle. “Well, I certainly can. The last thing I’m going to do is remind you of that day.”

Ethari raised an eyebrow. “What are you...?” He stopped to think, and then gave a chuckle himself. “Ah.” Rayla’s tenth birthday. Runaan spent the whole day pushing through to make it the best birthday Rayla’d ever had. It ended with Runaan’s cold being ten times worse than it would have been.

But she was so happy.

He spent the day fretting over his husband’s health, but at the end of the day, not even Ethari could blame him for it when their godchild wouldn’t stop talking about how much fun their day was.

Runaan nodded, and then his brows quickly furrowed. “Where is Rayla?”

“She’s playing with some friends, she’ll be back soon. I think they wanted to finish the fort they were building inside of that old fallen tree.”

Runaan huffed with annoyance. “I told her to be careful with that thing. It’s rotted, it could-”

“She’ll be fine, Runaan,” Ethari said with a soft smile. Rayla was perfectly capable of taking care of herself, especially in the woods. She’d been trained by the best, after all. “I’m only glad I managed to get you into bed before her return. Perhaps I’ll be able to convince her to rest next time she’s sick as well.”

“You’re capable of many things, my dear, but I’m not sure getting that child to sit still is one of them.” Runaan shifted, turning on his side and snaking an arm over his abdomen.

“Stomach feeling tender?”

A reluctant nod. Runaan’s eyes had slipped shut. He’d look comfortable if it weren’t for the way his brows were knitted together in discomfort. "Been throwing up all day. I think it's finally had enough."

“I’ll get something to help. Don’t you go anywhere,” Ethari’s husband managed a tiny smile at the joke, and that was enough to please him.

He went about filling an enchanted warmer with water and sealing it. It did wonders for various injuries that his husband came home with, this time it was only illness instead.

Runaan accepted it gratefully, and the look of relief on his face as he pressed it against his belly eased a bit more of Ethari’s concern.

“You get some sleep, alright? I’ll tell Rayla not to bother you.” Ethari laid the back of his hand on Runaan’s forehead and frowned at the warmth that met his skin. With any luck, this nap would do some good in his recovery.

“Thank you, Ethari,” Runaan muttered, nearly asleep already. “I’m sorry that I’m so impossible.”

“I’m not sure impossible would be the right word. I won, didn’t I?”
