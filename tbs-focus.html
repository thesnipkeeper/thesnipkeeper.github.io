Focus

Dr. Joan Bright sipped her coffee and sighed. Finally, the caffeine fix that she’d been waiting for. After the morning she’d had, she was lucky to have made it to the office in one piece (and with matching shoes), and she was grateful that she took Sarah’s suggestion to buy a coffeemaker for the place. It came in useful on (rare) mornings like this when she overslept and didn’t have time to consume the substance she spent so much time telling her patients to avoid.

Luckily, no one arrived on time on Sundays. She was able to make her coffee, and get herself together before the first patient showed up.

So, she was as together as she was going to be when Caleb knocked on the door. God, why was she so tired? Wasn’t the caffeine going to do anything? As if this day couldn’t get any- no, not the time to think like that.

“Come in, Caleb,” she said, and cleared her throat of the disuse.

“Hey, Dr. Bright.” He sat down.

They talked about school, about Adam. Caleb mentioned a boy in one of his classes that he found to be particularly annoying.

Joan hated that she was having trouble paying attention. She was his therapist. She cleared her throat again and shook her head as if to clear that, too. She could only hope that Caleb wouldn’t notice and ask her about it, but he seemed a little unfocused himself, having trailed off from his rant.

“Caleb?” She had to try it twice when her voice died the first time. “Are you alright?”

“I-” He furrowed his brows as if to figure out the answer to that himself. "Sorry Dr. Bright, I think I must be getting sick or something? I’m like, really tired and stuff.”

Joan was quiet for a moment, bit her lip, and sighed. “Actually, I think that might be me.” Maybe se

Caleb stared at her in confusion for a good while before his face lit up in realization. “Oh shit,” he said, and then shook his head. “Sorry, sorry... Jesus, you must feel horrible then if all I’m feeling is-”

“This,” Joan interrupted. “This isn’t about me,” she insisted. “Apologies for making it hard for you to focus, Caleb. I didn’t think that through.”

Caleb chuckled. “Nah, it’s fine I just, I guess I didn’t know that you could get sick. I mean, you’re Dr. Bright.”

Joan couldn’t help but smile. “Yes, well, we’re all mortal.” Most of us, anyway. “Anyway, the boy in your class?”

Caleb rubbed his eyes and nodded. “Uh yeah, yeah. He’s an asshole.”

“Caleb...”

-----

Joan’s throat was sore by the time the session was over. Caleb seemed to be able to tell. He also seemed eager to get out of there, and she couldn’t blame him.

“See you next week,” Caleb said as the two of them stood up.

“Have a good day, Caleb,” wow, her voice did not sound good. That wasn’t... fantastic for her profession. She winced. Maybe another coffee was in order.

“Feel better?” he said hesitantly, as if he wasn’t sure that was what he was supposed to say.

Joan smiled. “Thank you, and again, I’m sorry for-”

He shook his head. “Don’t worry about it, it’s happened before. Actually Adam had a cold a couple weeks ago and it was such a weird feeling.”

The session was technically over, but she couldn’t help but let her curiosities get the better of her, even when she was eager to go home. “Interesting... does it feel different depending on who it is?”

“Yeah, kinda. I mean, it’s like everyone’s got their own unique feelings, but when they’re sick, it gets a little more... cloudy? Sad, or tired or something, I don’t know.”

She nodded. “Huh. Well, I’ll see you never week?”

“Yeah!”

He left, and Joan sighed. And sniffled. Ah, a runny nose. Fantastic.

“Dr. Bright,” Sarah said, getting her attention.

“Hm?”

“I was thinking about picking up some tea if...?” She smiled and held her hands out to let Joan finish the idea for her.

Joan chuckled. “That would be great.”
