Syrup and Spices

Sewing was usually the only thing that calmed him and helped him wind down. It helped with the day-to-day anxiety that came with his unspoken position at the school, the anxiety that he was usually forced to tamp down to appear calm in front of the others.

Today, all he wanted was a nap.

The sewing needed done. Sumi’s dress had a tear in it, and she’d begged him with everything in her to make it look good again. He didn’t know what she did to wear her clothes out so quickly, but she was the reason that he had such a large array of blinding, brightly-colored fabrics.

Cora needed a coat. She didn’t think that she did, Kade didn’t think she felt the cold like the others did, but winter was coming and she was definitely going to need something to wrap herself in after swimming in cold water. Actually, the school needed a lot of coats.

He sniffled. That was about all the noise he’d made the past couple of hours, as his nose was running like a waterfall. Having to look down at the sewing machine certainly didn’t help anything. He dragged his sleeve along the underside of his nose with another, much more watery sniffle as drips threatened to come out.

For goodness’ sake, he just needed to finish this one seem and he could-

Kade sneezed. He had the sense to turn away from his current project and press his face into his shoulder, but that didn’t stop the result from being... messy. Kade swore.

Accepting defeat and leaving the candy-pink dress abandoned under the machine’s needle for the moment, he stood up and found the tissue box on his nightstand. It was feeling particularly light, after a night of trying to tame the runny nose no better than he was now.

He cleaned his face, blew his nose, squeezed a generous amount of hand sanitizer on his skin. It had become an unfortunate routine. His nose and upper lip were sore from all of the abuse.

He gave an experimental sniff, found that his airways were clear enough for now, and went back to sewing.

The sneezing and sniffling wasn’t... so bad. He could handle it. He was handling it. It was the cough that really messed with him.

He wasn’t coughing that much - not yet, anyway - but when the coughs did surface, he was forced to clutch his chest and try to find a way to do it that wouldn’t make it feel like all of the air was getting sucked out of him. In all honesty, he hadn’t taken off his binder just because he was tired, and it seemed like unnecessary work when he was in the middle of something.

But the most recent coughing fit caused a new ache in his chest that made him rethink his selective laziness.

“Hey, are you dying?”

“Jesus!” Kade laid a hand over his heart at the sight of Sumi standing in his room as if she belonged there. How long had she been there before she bothered to say anything? He huffed, brows furrowed. “Why don’t you knock like everyone else?”

“What’s the point of that?”

They’d had that conversation about five times already, and Kade never won in the end. The nonsense girl was never going to understand the concept of privacy.

“What’re you doing here? Your dress isn’t done yet.”

Sumi wore a dress that was similar to the one he was working on, but with different blinding colors and patterns on the skirt.

“You didn’t answer my question!” She accused.

“Am I dying?” Kade repeated, rather deadpan. “Probably not, but I’ll get back to you on that.”

“You’d tell me, right? If you were dying? Because-”

“Sumi, I’m not dying.”

“Oh, okay! Then why do you sound like you’re dying?” She asked questions so innocently that one couldn’t help but answer. Others had ulterior motives when they spoke, but Sumi was incapable of having ulterior motives because she was too busy speaking too frankly for anyone to misinterpret her.

Kade sniffed. His nose was running again, and his sleeve was getting a little too damp to be useful. “I’m a little sick. Am I keeping you up?”

“No! I was about to eat the rest of my cookie stash.”

The girl already ate an extra dessert at every meal, she didn’t really need a cookie stash.

“You’re sick, but you’re not dying,” Sumi concluded. She strolled in and sat on his bed as if he’d invited her in. “Maybe you have spicy blood.”

Kade blinked.

“You know, cinnamon-y or cayenne-y blood, or-”

“A cold,” Kade sighed out with a defeated sniffle. “And you really shouldn’t be in here, you’re gonna catch it. My room is probably covered in germs.” It definitely was. All he’d done today was sneeze.

Sumi paid that no mind. Instead, she crossed her legs on his bed and narrowed her eyes at him. “You look like you hurt.”

He... he did. And just like that, Kade’s brain was out of the sewing-zone. He was suddenly aware of how much his chest hurt, and his head. God, his head was killing him. His throat was a little scratchy, part of the reason why he hadn’t gotten around to eating or drinking much that day.

“Yeah,” he sighed, shoulders falling.

Sumi frowned. “You should go to sleep, right? That’s what you’re supposed to do when you’re sick.”

“Just yesterday, you told me that you would melt into a puddle of syrup if the pink and orange dress wasn’t fixed.”

“I wasn’t being serious!” She defended. “You know you don’t have to do all that right now,” she lazily flailed her arm to the stack of clothes and fabrics in his to-do pile. “You’re just doing it because you want to torture yourself like a weirdo.”

Kade blinked. She was... kind of right. That may have been the reason he hadn’t bothered to take off his binder yet. He put chores ahead of his health just because he could. It was an experience like no other when Sumi made you realize something about yourself, an experience that seemed to happen a little too often.

“Okay,” he said. “Okay, I’m done for the night.” He flipped off the light on his sewing table, which usually wasn’t done until midnight at least. “... thanks, Sumi.” He added, quietly.

“For what?”

Kade sniffed. “Being you, I guess.”

“Oh,” she laughed. “You’re welcome! Also, you have snot on your face.”

Kade blushed fiercely. Gross. He pressed the abused sleeve against his nose, and was met with an abundance of watery slime that he hadn’t managed to sniffle up.

“God...” he mumbled, still sniffling as he fixed his predicament. The shirt was going in the laundry in a few minutes, anyway, and Sumi was the last person someone could be embarrassed around, so he used the sleeve the best he could. He needed to preserve the tissues, this cold wasn’t going away any time soon.

“Alright,” he said with a thick sniffle. “You’ve actually gotta leave.” He poked at her until she reluctantly pulled herself off of his bed. “I don’t want you to catch this.”

“Okay, okay...” she gave a childish pout. “Goodnight, Kade!”

“ ‘Night, Sumi.”

He smiled. Plenty of ridiculous things came out of that girl’s mouth, but it was only fitting that plenty of sweet things did as well.
