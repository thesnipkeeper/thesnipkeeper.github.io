Title: Molly and Caduceus
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly thanks Caduceus for giving him life, and a cold.
Word count: 1.3k

Molly and Caduceus

What started out as a simple bout of the sniffles turned into a full-blown, miserable cold. Caduceus no longer had the energy or the motivation to do anything but lie in bed and sneeze.

The others would come in and bring him food, sometimes keep him company.

He snuffled his way through a bowl of soup Nott had made, and fell asleep on Jester's shoulder at some point as she sat in his bed and read her book to him. It felt nice, having people around when he wasn't well. It was a luxury he didn't have living alone at the temple.

Caduceus was getting a bit restless by evening time.

-----

"Go on, sit down," Mollymauk pulled out a chair for him before taking his arm and leading him to it. He sat down slowly and made a noise as Molly draped a blanket around his shoulders, pulling it tightly around him. "I've heard you like tea, so I thought you might enjoy one of my favorites."

Caduceus blinked. Molly was filling a kettle and setting it on the stove, humming contently as his tail flicked about with a mind of its own. Caduceus hadn't done a lot of talking, his throat already hurt and speaking made it much worse. Thankfully, Mollymauk didn’t ask him any questions that required something more than a nod or a shake of the head.

He sniffed liquidly and ran his sleeve under his nose. It seemed to get Mollymauk's attention, because suddenly bright red eyes were looking back at him. A fang peeked out as the tiefling bit his lip, eyebrows knitted together in what Caduceus could only interpret as concern. The look faded away into Molly's usual knowing expression, and he turned around again.

His mind was a fog of sickness and fever, so Caduceus wasn’t sure exactly how much time passed before a steaming teacup was placed in front of him. It wasn’t one of his own. It lacked the wear and tear enough for him to deduce this was a new cup. He leaned in and made an attempt to inhale the steam, but it only resulted in a congested gurgle from his bunged sinuses. The nagging headache intensified for a moment.

"I'm sorry," he rasped, wincing and rubbing at his temples. "I don’t know if I'll be able to taste anything but hot water."

Molly hummed. "Just… just take a sip and tell me how it is. Trust me." He leaned over the back of Caduceus' chair and watched as the firbolg took a careful sip.

At first, it truly didn’t taste like anything. He was so congested, he felt like he was sipping on slightly-flavored water as opposed to tea. And then his eyes widened. An aftertaste of spice and sweetness filled what senses he had left, and he smiled with delight.

"Mint," He said with a grin.

"Mm-hm!" Mollymauk leaned on the table. "It's simple, I know."

"But it's perfect," Another sip. "Thank you, Mr. Mollymauk."

Molly grinned. "You're sounding better already."

-----

Caduceus loved days like this. Everything was quiet and everyone was working on their own little thing and finding ways to relax before they ended up with another job from the Bright Queen. He was seated in his garden, giving it some much-needed TLC after he'd been too sick to work on it. Yasha had really helped, but the garden was too big these days for one person to take total care of.

He watched as water cascaded from the watering can and soaked into the soil of his vegetables. He really, really loved days like this.

"Feeling better?"

He nearly dropped the can at the sudden voice. Molly had an amused grin on his face as he stepped in, red eyes glowing in the low light.

"Much better," Caduceus smiled. "The last of it was gone this morning."

"That's nice to hear. I know everyone's been missing you around since you've been bedridden." He sniffed. "Need a hand?"

"Oh, no but thank you. I don't mind the company, though," he said honestly.

Molly smiled, nodded, and found a seat on a nearby bench as Caduceus watered the remaining plants.

"I've been meaning to speak to you about something," Mollymauk began.

"Oh?"

"Mm." He smiled less-extravagantly, as if he was deep in thought. "I..." He chuckled. "Thank you for... You know."

Caduceus raised an eyebrow. He set his watering can to the side and turned his full attention to Mollymauk. He wasn't sure what Molly was trying to say, but it was obviously important to him. "I'm not sure I understand."

Molly sighed, and for once he looked embarrassed. "Thank you for reviving me."

"Oh," Caduceus cracked another smile. "Well, I don't think it was your time yet."

They'd been very pointedly avoiding having this conversation. Caduceus could sense the slight jealousy when Mollymauk awoke to all of his friends having someone else. He understood, and he gave Molly time to adjust to their new situation, their new setting, and didn’t take it personally when Molly was cold toward him for a while.

"Right. I don't... I don't think so, e-" a few quick intakes of breath before Molly whipped to the side away from Caduceus and sneezed, and then again. He recovered with a liquid sniff and produced a colorful handkerchief, scrubbing at his nose with a harshness born of annoyance.

Caduceus knew those sounds well. Those were the sounds he'd been making for most of the week. "You caught my cold. Sorry about that." he could see a redd-ish tinge to Mollymauk's nostrils now that his attention was truly drawn.

Molly waved him off with a jewelry-ridden hand and a sniff. "Just some sneezes. Nothing to worry about."

Probably not true, but Caduceus nodded and didn’t argue. He continued in his garden as Molly talked to him. Mollymauk joked about their house and the Xhorhas government loving them so much, and then began to tell Caduceus about his time in the circus.

He'd never pictured Yasha working at a circus, but Caduceus had begun to learn that people could truly surprise.

"Of course she wasn’t normally a performer, but we needed a fill-in every now and then, and she was always willing to…" His voice wavered, and. Caduceus looked up to see the tiefling pawing at his nose, eyes squeezed shut. "heh-schoo!"

"Bless," Caduceus offered.

Molly gave him a watery-eyed glare as he recovered. "Not a word. I'm fine."

Caduceus shrugged and went back to his gardening, and Mollymauk went back to his story.

The sounds coming from the garden bench were becoming more and more miserable over just a few minutes. Mollymauk seemed to wait until he was absolutely desperate before blowing his nose, and even then it didn't sound like it did much good.

He snuffled, and then sneezed again, and Caduceus started to notice the little winces after sniffles and swallows.

"A healing spell might be able to ease the headache and sore throat," he didn't bother to look back at Mollymauk. It was a casual enough offer.

When he did look up, Molly narrowed his eyes. "How did you-? No, never mind. I've heard about your little perceptive abilities."

"Yeah, I'm told it's creepy. Shall I?"

"Oh, don't waste your spells on me." He chuckled - Caduceus wasn't sure what was so funny. He was obviously feeling terrible and yet he was refusing help. "It's strange, you know. Being ill is a good thing to remind you that you're alive."

"Ah, that it is." Caduceus pulled the last of the weeds and cleared his throat. "Still, it might be best if you were resting as to not give this reminder a head start."

Molly hummed in acknowledgement. He rested his chin on his palm with a tired smile. "I'll head to bed in a bit. Just allow me to enjoy a little bit of... Living."

"Will do, Mr. Mollymauk." He brushed his hands on his gardening apron. "I'll start on some soup in the meantime."
