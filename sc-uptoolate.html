Up Too Late

David’s sneeze from the other side of the store ends in a pained groan.

“Oh God, that one was- okay that one was really painful.”

He chuckles, but when Patrick finally looks up from his work, David’s face is pinched in discomfort. His smile falters slightly.

“You okay?”

“Hm?” David asks, turning to him as he rubs his nose. “Oh, snf! Yeah, there’s just like, all sorts of pressure going on in my head right now. Physically and mentally," he adds with a chuckle.

Patrick scoffs. “Maybe we should pick up some decongestants before we go to my place.”

David sniffles thickly. “No, I’ve made it this far without drugs. I’m not giving in.”

“Okay, but I think this whole thing you’ve got about going without medicine is kind of ridiculous.”

He shakes his head “It’s too late to change my mind now.”

Patrick laughs fondly. “Whatever you say.”

David’s been fighting a cold for a couple of days and has stubbornly refused to take anything for it besides the occasional cough drop or tylenol. He claimed to have spent his whole childhood and pre-Schitt’s Creek adulthood medicating as much as possible whenever he got sick. Like a crazy person, he wants to run through this whole cold without doing the same.

Patrick knows why David’s doing this, but he can’t help but question his decision when he hears how miserable David is. The sore throat, sputtering coughs and the inability to breathe through his nose could all be helped if he just took some sort of medicine.

But he can picture David, alone in New York, downing a million different things that probably shouldn’t go together on the day of a gallery-opening, and making it all the way through until the meds wear off. David’s told him the story before, and it ended with him passing out on his way to greet a VIP guest.

The rattle of a pill bottle gets his attention, and he finds David swallowing a tylenol with a more intense look of pain.

David’s cold has generally just been sneezing and coughing (and lots of snot), but Patrick can’t help but wonder if he’s feeling worse. He isn’t sure why a cold would choose two days in to start getting bad, it’s not like David’s been having a great time up until now.

He’s been slower today, a little more tired and a lot less talkative. Patrick’s seen him when he’s really sick, and this is starting to remind him of it, even when David himself doesn’t seem to notice.

“Ah ah,” Patrick steps in his eye line when he’s deciding on a sweater for the day.

David’s hand is pressed to his pink nose to stem the flow. He sighs. “Wha-? Seriously? Patrick, I’m fine...”

“Then prove it,” Patrick says innocently, tapping the thermometer against David’s shoulder with each word.

David rolls his eyes. He turns it on and slips it into his mouth, the same routine they’ve gone through that past couple of days. Patrick wants to make sure he’s not lying because the store’s been so busy for the holidays.

The reading shows no signs of a fever, at least not a significant one, and David flaunts it proudly before handing it back and choosing one of his heavier sweaters.

Patrick doesn’t feel the need to say anything more until David coughs and promptly cries out in pain.

“David?!” Patrick rushes to the stockroom where David’s been reorganizing, and finds him with tears in his eyes.

David waves dismissively while he rubs a spot under his ear. He slowly makes his way to sit down on a nearby stool and Patrick nearly has a heart attack when he sees his boyfriend’s balance falter.

He rushes over in case he needs to make a rescue, but David sits down without a problem. He’s wide-eyed as if he’s not seeing straight.

“What’s wrong?” Patrick asks, kneeling next to him and laying a hand on his back. He realizes then that David feels warmer than usual.

David closes his eyes for a moment while Patrick feels his forehead and neck.

“Dizzy,” David says. “Super dizzy. Ugh.” He winces and opens his eyes. “My ear hurts.”

Patrick frowns. “You feel like you’re running a temp, too. Maybe we should close up early and head to the doctor.”

David sniffles, and then seems to regret it when he winces. “No doctor. I’ll just head home and like, sleep it off...”

“Okay, well first of all, I’m not letting you walk home when it’s like one degree outside," he chuckled. He cups David's cheek, stroking gently with his thumb as if that could make all the pain in his head go away. "Do you want to rest in here until we close?"

David huffs, looking conflicted. "I... Yeah, that's probably a good idea." His hand goes back to his ear. It's not hard to tell that it's bothering him, and Patrick bites his lip with worry.

The two of them have had their share of colds during their relationship, but this is the first time that one of them might need a doctor. Patrick suddenly feels responsible for the health of his boyfriend, and he's got to admit to being a little freaked out. An ear infection isn't dangerous, but David's hurting…

The front door jingles and they look back to see a customer walking in. Patrick curses. "Take a nap or something?" he says, giving David a quick kiss.

He's already getting settled on their futon and pulling his phone out. "I'll be watching something terrible on Netflix."

Patrick can hear a few scattered coughs and sneezes here and there, often followed by groans. The sound of David’s show cuts out after a while. He must have fallen asleep.

Soon, it’s five o’ clock.

They’re finally done.

Patrick locks the door and the register and then heads back, excited to deliver the news that the store is officially closed for the day. He frowns.

David hasn’t lied down, and his phone is abandoned on a nearby table. His head is leaned back a bit and his eyes are closed. He looks tense.

“Honey?” Patrick tries.

“Mm.” His chest is slowly rising and falling, mouth open as he breathes the same way he has been the past couple of days without a functioning nose. He’s breathing through the pain, Patrick realizes. He opens his eyes and sighs tiredly. “I think I’d like to go to the doctor.”

“Sure.”

-----

It’s dark out by the time they return to Patrick’s apartment. David refuses to go to the motel (not that Patrick wanted that, anyway) for fear that he would be quarantined by his family. Or worse - cared for by his family.

“I don’t mean to be that guy,” says David as they walk in, laying the prescription bag on the counter. “But we skipped dinner, and...”

“Yes yes,” Patrick chuckles. “I have a frozen pizza that we can heat up.”

“Oh thank God. Have I told you how much I appreciate you?”

He shrugs playfully and wraps an arm around David’s waist. “I can always hear it again.” He goes to pull David in for a kiss, but bumps his ear in the process and immediately earns a yelp. Patrick hisses in sympathy. “Sorry!”

David whines. “I think I’ll just wait for that pizza.”

“Wait-”

“Hmm?”

“The doctor said to take your first dose as soon as you get home,” he informs, nudging the prescription in David’s direction.

David makes a face. “Fine.” He snatches it with distaste and quickly tears open the bag, reads the bottle, and dry-swallows one of the pills. He makes a show of going to the couch and stubbornly sitting.

“Maybe you can lie down while you wait,” suggests Patrick as he tries to read the oversimplified instructions on the back of the pizza box.

“Laying down hurts.” He clears his throat. “Where’s the-?”

“Oh, I put it in your bag.”

David nods and digs into his bag until Patrick hears a bottle of pills rattling. David swallows two. He must still be hurting. Patrick rarely sees him this subdued.

-----

David doesn’t eat nearly as much as Patrick expects. They usually have to have predetermined portions for the both of them to avoid arguments over who gets the last slice, but this time David eats two and then ignored the rest of the pizza entirely. Patrick is floored.

David leans on his shoulder, stiffly at first, and gets more comfortable as he realizes what movements will hurt his ear and which ones won’t. He snuffles thickly and shifts after a while, a small pained whimper escaping.

“Is the tylenol helping?”

“Little bit. Stop moving.”

A scoff. But Patrick stops moving. The medicine has seemed to take away some of the fever. Maybe he can get one more dose of pain reliever in him before they go to bed.

David suddenly sits up. His face falls and Patrick has seen this enough the past two days to know what’s coming. He reaches for a tissue and keeps it ready while David doubles over with a truly impressive sneeze, followed by a whimper.

“Oh, honey...” Patrick can’t help but coo in sympathy.

David accepts the tissue and does a thorough job of wiping his nose rather than blowing it. He coughs into the thin paper before he can stop himself.

“How the fuck am I supposed to sleep tonight when I can't cough without being in excruciating pain?” he mumbles.

Patrick shifts until he has an arm around David’s shoulders, lightly playing at his hair. He presses a kiss to his temple. “We’ll figure it out.”

They don’t.

David can’t be horizontal for more than a minute before the pain starts rushing to his ear. He’s also complaining of a headache and he can’t stop coughing, and Patrick can tell he’s exhausted because he’s starting to get grumpy - and not in a cute way.

“I just want to sleep,” he groans for the fourth time.

Patrick sighs. “Oh my God, it’s like looking after a toddler,” David scoffs. “Here, sit up, just let me...”

David complies, though while huffing impatiently, while Patrick organizes their pillows behind them until they’re stacked up against the back.

“Mm’kay, lay back?”

David does. His neck is met with pillows, the fluffiest one at the top of the stack so his head is nicely supported. He makes a noise of satisfaction. “Oh. This is nice. Thank you.”

“It might help your coughing too.”

“Well, if my coughing is so disruptive, why don’t you just send me back to the-”

“Nope. We’re not doing this. Go to sleep.”

David doesn’t.

While the pillows do help his cough, and the pain in his ear, he’s still horribly congested and suffering the worst sinus headache of his life. He shifts over and over again, trying to find the right spot.

Eventually, Patrick gets tired of waiting for him to get comfortable, with all the awkward tossing and turning, and starts to fall asleep.

He forced back to the conscious world to heavy movement.

Patrick growls. “Mmph, what’re ‘ou doing?”

“N-nothing. Go back to sleep, honey.”

Patrick doesn’t let the quick, comforting squeeze on his arm lull him back to sleep, because it sounds like something’s wrong. He sits up, propping himself on his elbow as he watches David pull a blanket and couple of pillows off of the bed.

“David,” Patrick says more sharply. “Where are you going?”

David clears his throat and snuffles. Patrick can hear the rustle of him wiping his nose on his sleeve. “Just the couch. Having some trouble sleeping, so I figured I shouldn’t keep you awake with all my... issues.”

“You okay?”

David takes a soft tone, “I’m fine. Go back to sleep.”

He frowns, but lets his head fall back onto his pillow. There’s no way he can go back to sleep. He can hear David sniffling continuously while he’s getting situated. If he were in bed, Patrick could just reach over and get him a tissue.

David coughs, the sound quickly muffled like he’s stuff his face in a pillow to keep from making noise.

Patrick opens his eyes again. His boyfriend is on the couch, leaning against the arm, face illuminated by his phone that’s probably playing a youtube video of some kind. His idle face is hinted with pain; the tylenol has probably begun to wear off by now, and it’s too early for another dose.

Patrick can’t bring himself to go back to sleep when David is obviously miserable.

He barely thinks about it before he gets out of bed and heads to the couch.

David looks up, but doesn’t say anything when Patrick nudges his legs and lets him set his feet on Patrick’s thighs.

“I guess if you’re gonna be awake all night, so will I.”

David huffs out a laugh, and of course coughs in the process. He must be tired if he’s not wildly arguing against it.

“I can’t believe I’m being the reasonable one, but I feel like I should point out that we have work tomorrow and one of us should be fully-rested.”

“Naw, I’m not gonna be able to sleep if I know you can’t.” He steals a bit of David’s blanket to cover himself up. “Anyway, it’s been a while since I’ve stayed up too late.”

David sighs. “I’m so tired, Patrick,” his voice breaks at the end.

Patrick rubs his leg. “I’m sure you’ll crash eventually.”

A nod. “Can we... talk for a while? I need something to distract me. My ear...”

“Sure, David.”
