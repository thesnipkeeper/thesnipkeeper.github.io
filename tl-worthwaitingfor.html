Worth Waiting For

He buttoned his  cardigan as he walked in.

"Oh, hey Trent! Have a good one!" Keely said as she passed him in the hallway.

He smiled and nodded. "You as well." He breathed out rather shakily and pressed his hand to his bag. Shit. He forgot his water bottle. He gave a sigh, shook his head at himself and his own incompetence, and kept walking.

His nose started to run as he walked. He wiped at it with his sleeve when no one was around and really hoped that the sore throat that was forming wouldn't turn into a cough because he hated fucking coughs. The most disruptive symptom in his opinion, and it had no business involving him.

He regained his composure before putting himself in the doorway of a familiar office.

"Is Ted around?"

Beard looked up from his book, only for a second, before looking back down. "He's in a meeting with Rebecca. Something about uniforms. Won't be long."

"Right," Trent nodded. He took a seat at Ted's desk and sighed.

He was still getting used to being there, not as a reporter but as Ted Lasso's boyfriend. What a strange feeling to be welcomed just like any player's significant other. Right now, he just wanted to see Ted. To go home with him and do nothing comfortably while Ted rambled on about God knew what.

He rested his forehead in his palm and huffed out a sigh.

"Y'okay, Trent?"

Beard was looking at him over his book with the same expression he always wore when he was pretending not to be reading someone.

"Not feeling my best today, actually," he admitted. "Must be coming down with something." As if on cue, the chills that he'd been experiencing hit him harder and he gave an involuntary shudder. Dignity be damned, he wrapped his arms around himself like a child.

Beard set his book down and stood up. Trent was too busy focusing on not actively chattering his teeth to pay attention to the coach. How long was Beard gone? He wasn't sure, but the man returned with a cup of tea in one of Ted's favorite mugs.

Lacking words, Trent nodded his thanks. Beard nodded back at him, and returned to his book.

The tea was good. It was really good. Something herbal, he couldn't place the exact flavor but it tasted like the good kind. Trent nursed the cup for the next few minutes. It didn't soothe the chills, but it did distract him from them.

The steam did make his nose run, though. He grabbed a tissue from the box on Ted's desk and pressed it to his nose. The plan was to keep it there. Forever. Because his nose wasn't going to stop leaking any time soon. It had other ideas, as evidenced by the deep itch in his sinuses that grew until he gave a truly horrendous sneeze into the flimsy paper.

And then another. 

And another.

Mucus dislodged itself from his sinuses, filling the tissue with slime. He kept it pressed to his nose while he reached for another and quickly swapped them out to blow his nose.

"Bless you," said Beard. "Sounds like a hell of a cold." And then, "if it's a cold, that is."

"It's questionable," muttered Trent as he pinched the excess from his nostrils with a snf!

Beard hummed in acknowledgement.

"Trent Crimm? Who let a wild guy like you in here?"

Trent felt relief rush over him at the sound of that voice. He stood up with a kind of dorky smile that could only be brought out of him by Ted Lasso and let himself get wrapped in a warm hug.

Ted went to kiss him, and Trent pulled away, painful as it may have been to do so. It earned a look of confusion, Ted's eyes wide and brows going up in concern.

"What's wrong? Why do you look like you've been runnin' laps with the team for the last hour? Why- why're you so warm?" He laid his hands on each side of Trent's face and frowned deeply. "You're sick!"

Trent sighed, nodded. "I'm afraid so. We can reschedule, I just wanted to see you before I went home, I suppose."

"You come over, we'll order soup or somethin' that's  good for sick people, I'll get you all comfy and we'll see if you still wanna go home. Sound good?"

Trent saw the smallest smile on Beard's face out of the corner of his eye.

"Ted, I'll get you sick."

"Your germs don't concern me, sir. C'mon, come to my place and relax because I know that's not what you're gonna do at home."

He was right. Trent would get bored in five minutes and start doing work. If there was no work to be done, he'd make some.

"Alright," he said, unable to bite back a smile. This time, Ted leaned in and he didn't resist the kiss.

"Jeez, you're hot. Well, I mean temperature-wise but your body is very attractive as well." Beard snorted from his desk. "Let me grab my stuff and we'll be out of here." And Ted was out.

Trent cleared his throat. "Thank you," he said. "For the tea."

"No problem. Get some rest, Trent."

"It sounds like I'll have to."

Beard chuckled.

Ted returned, and brought him home.

Trent was asleep on the man's couch an hour later.
