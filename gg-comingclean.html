Coming Clean

“I’m so Sorry, Sookie,” Lorelai muttered helplessly as she ran a wad of paper towels over the puddle on the floor. The coffee, the poor abandoned wasted coffee was absorbed and tossed into the trash. She continued cleaning just so she wouldn’t have to stand up and face everyone else in the kitchen quite yet. “My hand just slipped! I don’t know what’s wrong with me today!”

“It happens!” Sookie was busy getting things prepped for lunch. She could hardly care about such a thing, but Lorelai still felt bad. She knew her friend liked a clean kitchen, but their relationship seemed to offer some grace. “You do seem kind of scrambled, though. Is everything okay?”

“Yeah,” Lorelai sighed as she stood up. She brushed the wrinkles out of her clothes and tossed the last wad of paper towels into the trash. “I dunno. It’s just one of those days, you know? Like, I slept weird and my alarm didn’t go off, and my pop tarts weren’t toasted all the way through and- ugh.”

Sookie nodded as if anything she was saying made any sense. “It’s okay,” she said, voice soft and understanding. “You can always hide out in here!”

“I’d love to, but we’ve got so many check-ins today, I can’t leave Michel at the front by himself.” Nor would Michel let her leave him to do all the work by himself. “The only thing getting me through today is our date tonight.”

“Oh, you and Michel have a date?” Sookie giggled.

Lorelai scoffed. “With Luke. We’re going to that new steakhouse outside of town and I am so ready to gorge myself on smoked meat you have no idea.”

“Well, I hope you have a good time!” She said, rinsing a handful of carrots before beginning to chop rather seamlessly. “And you have something else to look forward to, too! Rory’s coming for lunch, remember?”

“Oh my God,” Lorelai leaned her head back in relief at the thought. She hadn’t seen her daughter in a week, and Rory was the one who had to fit her mom into her schedule. “You’re right. Okay, I can get through this.”

She couldn’t. There was no way. It wasn’t even ten in the morning and she’d already been screamed at by an old couple, she somehow got coffee on her skirt, and one of the cleaning staff were so hungover they could hardly do their job. She sent them home for the day, told them never to come back to work like that again, and took over the extra rooms that needed to be finished before the next guests checked in.

She didn’t realize how long she’d been at it until she heard a familiar voice from the lobby.

“Mom? It’s me, your starving child!”

“Rory!” Lorelai ran down the stairs and enveloped her daughter in a hug, earning an oof from the kid. “You have no idea how good it is to see you.”

“Well, that’s flattering,” Rory said, voice muffled by her mother’s shoulder. She pulled away before Lorelai actually wanted to let her go, which was never. She never wanted to let her go ever again. “But is lunch ready yet? Because all I’ve eaten this week is ramen and pretzels and I think my organs are about to fail if I don’t eat real food.”

“That’s not good, I’d like your organs to function.” She wrapped an arm around Rory as they went to the dining room. It was later, the guests had already had their lunch. There were a couple of people playing cards or drinking coffee, but the rest of the place was theirs.

Sookie made stir-fry, and of course it was the most amazing stir-fry on the entire planet. Lorelai watched with pride as her daughter scarfed it down like there was no tomorrow.

“How’s school going?”

“Good!” Rory said with a mouthful of food. “Paris only made one other person cry at the paper today.”

“Oh wow, she must be going soft.”

“How’s your day been, though?”

“Well, it started off with a cold pop tart and spilled coffee. And I was basically told by a sweet little grandmother that I’m Satan.”

“Ah.” Rory nodded with a raised eyebrow. “So, not a good day, then, huh?”

“Nope,” she said, popping the ‘p’.

Rory hummed sympathetically. “I’m sorry, mom.”

And just like that, all of the stress of the day hit her in one go. Maybe the excitement of spending time with Rory sort of mixed with all of her other emotions, or maybe she was just that stressed. Whatever it was, she found herself tearing up.

“Mom?” Rory looked panicked. She reached across the table a laid a hand on Lorelai’s arm, rubbing gently. Jesus, the kid was too good at comforting her mother like that was her job and not the other way around.

“God, I’m sorry...” she sniffed, rubbing the tears out of her eyes. Not now, not in the damn dining room in front of guests and employees. “I don’t know why I’m crying.”

“It’s okay, sometimes bad days do that, right?”

Lorelai gave a watery chuckle and nodded. She tried to get a hold of herself, especially when Sookie emerged from the kitchen with two pieces of coffee cake.

“Tell me how it is!” Sookie whispered excitedly, squeezing Rory’s shoulder. She glanced at Lorelai and frowned immediately. “Oh no, what’s wrong? Was it the stir-fry?”

Lorelai snorted. “No, Sookie, the stir-fry was amazing. I’m fine, it’s just getting to be a long day.”

The coffee cake was amazing. The tears were gone. Lorelai had come to learn rather quickly that her best friend’s cooking could solve most problems in her life.

Rory checked her watch and stood, giving her mom a quick hug. “I need to go, I’m sorry.” she was the one to elongate the hug now, and Lorelai loved it. “I’ve got a paper due tomorrow and I still need to finish it.”

“Fine, fine, leave me for your education or whatever.” She smiled.

“Have a good time on your date! Give me details! About the food, I mean, please.”

“Absolutely I will.” Her phone started to ring, the cell phone that Michel constantly scolded her for having on her person. Rory wandered off to talk to him, mostly answering questions about school that he pretended not to be asking.

“You’ve got Lorelai,” she said, absently scratching at the coffee stain on her skirt.

“Lorelai, hey,”

It felt so nice to hear Luke’s voice. “Hi babe,” she hummed. “What’s up?”

He cleared his throat. “I’ve gotta cancel our date tonight. Something... something came up.”

Lorelai wondered if it was anatomically possible for her heart to drop. Because it did. She felt like crying allover again, but she swallowed the lump in her throat. “Oh no, is everything okay?”

That got the attention of both Rory and Michel.

“Yeah, I just... I can’t tonight. I’m sorry, I’ll make it up to you, okay?”

“It’s fine, Luke, stuff comes up.”

Rory looked furious, and Michel looked whatever his version of ‘protectively furious’ was as well.

“Thanks and... I’m sorry.”He sighed for moment before giving a rushed, “I need to go, I’ll call you later, okay?”

“Sure.”

The moment the call ended, Lorelai sobbed.

“Luke canceled?” Rory demanded.

“Something came up, apparently,” she said with a humorless laugh. “Jesus, I need to stop crying today.”

“Rather rude to cancel on a date last minute like that,” Michel mumbled.

“Mom...”

“I’m okay,” Lorelai insisted, sniffling in a way that said otherwise. “I just... I need to get back to work. There’s a couple more rooms that need to be finished.”

“Their check-ins aren’t until tomorrow,” Michel reasoned with a worried frown.

“Better to get them done now, right?”

She gave her daughter one last hug and went back upstairs to clean and maybe cry a little. Jesus Christ, she wanted this day to be over with.

There were very few times in Rory Gilmore’s life when she’d been furious with Luke.

Luke was a fixed point in the town, was a part of her routine since she was a kid.

Things change, of course, when he was dating her mom. Because now he was even more involved in their lives, and normally Rory loved it. She loved getting to spend time with him and see a side of him she didn’t get to see from just eating at the diner every day of her life. It was clear that he enjoyed getting to spend time with her as well, he’d come out of his shell.

Today, however, Rory was prepared to murder the man. Anyone who made her mom cry deserved a piece of her mind. Her paper could wait.

She marched into the diner.

“Rory, hey sweetie!” Babette was fun to talk to, but not at the moment. Rory had a murder to commit.

“Hi! Sorry, Babette, I can’t talk right now.”

“Oh! A woman on a mission!”

She had no idea.

Caesar was behind the counter taking a complicated order from Kirk.

“Where’s Luke?” Rory asked with a hint of irritation.

Caesar barely looked up from the notepad. “Hm? Oh, he’s upstairs.”

So, Rory went upstairs. She knocked on Luke’s apartment door without a second thought. When there was no immediate answer, she knocked again. And Luke had the sense to answer that time.

His eyes widened, clearly not having expected anyone, especially her. “Rory, hi, what-”

“You’re a jerk.”

Luke blinked. “What?”

“Mom’s having the worst day ever, and all that was getting her through it was your date tonight, and then you cancel on her? Because something came up? What does that even mean?”

“Rory-”

“No! You don’t get to defend yourself! Mom’s cried like three times today and one of those was because of you!”

Luke’s face fell immediately at that. “Oh no...” he said. “I didn’t meant to-”

“Then why did you cancel your date?!”

“I couldn’t go,” he said, keeping his voice steady, which just upset her more.

“Why not?”

“Rory...”

She held out her arms for an explanation, any kind of explanation. He gave in with a heavy sigh, shoulders falling.

“I’m sick, okay? I must’ve caught something, because all I’ve done today is puke.”

Rory’s arms fell to her side. “Oh...” He did look tired, and it was definitely not normal for Luke Danes to be wearing pajamas in the middle of the day.

“Look, I didn’t mean to upset Lorelai. I didn’t realize...”

“You should’ve just told her that, Luke.”

“Well,” another sigh. “I know that now.”

Rory shifted uncomfortable. “I’m... sorry for yelling at you, then.”

He chuckled. “Nah, I think I needed it.”

“I’m sure it’s fine,” Sookie said as she expertly kneaded some bread dough.

Lorelai had been sulking in the kitchen. The inn was at a lull. All of the guests were out doing things and the staff took the opportunity to relax before the dinner rush. Someone else had cleaned the rooms she was going to work on, as it turned out, which was great but it also meant that she had nothing to do. “I know, it’s just... the way he said it, you know?”

Sookie nodded. “I’m sorry, sweetie. It sucks. You can come over to our place for dinner, if you want! I’m making pork chops!”

Pork chops did sound good. Before Lorelai had a chance to think about it more, though, her phone started to ring. She almost didn’t want to pick up this time. It couldn’t be good news.

“Hi,” Luke.

“Oh, hey Luke,” she said. Sookie glanced her way with wide eyes.

“I kind of lied to you earlier, and I think I need to come clean...”

Lorelai frowned. “O...kay?” she wasn't sure that she was ready for whatever this was going to be.

“Something didn’t come up, exactly, I...” he sighed heavily. “I caught some kind of stomach bug and I’m pretty sick.”

Lorelai blinked. That wasn’t what she was expecting. At all. “Oh.”

“I know I upset you, and I’m really sorry, sweetheart. I... I’m not used to- to this, but Rory kind of gave me a piece of her mind.”

“Wait, Rory what?”

He laughed. “She showed up and yelled at me a little.”

She bit back a smile. God, she loved her kid. “Luke, I uh,” a chuckle. “I didn’t tell her to do that.”

“No, I know, but I deserved it. I should’ve just been honest with you.”

“Honesty... honesty is appreciated. So be honest, do you want me to come over and take care of you?”

“God, no. I’m a mess right now.”

Lorelai snorted. “I’ll call you tonight, then, to make sure you’re okay.”

She could hear the smile in his voice as he said a simple, “Alright. Love you.”

She found herself smiling unabashedly as they ended the call.

“What was that all about?” asked Sookie.

“Everything’s fine,” she said.
