Title: Together
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's got a cold, and it turns out Patrick does too
Word count: 800

Together

“Alright, I’m heading to the store.” Patrick pulls him in for a kiss, an amazing one that has David leaning in and making a delighted noise until- David gasps, and then promptly coughs when it crackles in his chest.

He weakly shoves at Patrick, who looks a mix of surprised and amused.

“Don’t kiss me! You’ll get my germs!”

Patrick chuckles, but there’s something in his expression that David can’t quite decipher. He’s only joking, he doesn’t want Patrick to stop kissing him ever.

“Bye, David. Get some rest, okay?” He says it in the sweetest way that has David’s heart fluttering ridiculously.

He bites back a smile of his own. “Love you.”

“Love you too.”

He sneezes the moment Patrick walks out the door, and he’s then reminded how terrible he actually feels without his fiance there to keep him distracted from his plague.

Patrick’s been the sweetest David has ever seen him, and only for a little cold. David didn’t even have to ask before Patrick told him that he could take care of the store on his own today.

The only thing about being able to stay home from work when he’s sick, David thinks, is that he’s alone. And bored.

He watches Netflix for a while. It’s hard to pay attention to what’s going when he constantly has to cough or sneeze or blow his nose.

He makes some tea for himself, and it doesn’t taste quite as good as when Patrick makes it, but it’s enough to keep him from coughing for a bit. David finally enjoys his show in peace for a little while.

Netflix gives him a prompt asking him if he’s still watching, and by that time his stomach is growling.

Oh God, how did he not notice that he’s starving?

And incredibly alone right now.

-----

“Oh hey David!” Twyla greets as he walks in. “How are you feeling?”

David wants to question the inflection on the ‘you’ but honestly, he’s too tired and hungry.

“Um, I’ve been better but, has Patrick gotten lunch yet?”

“Nope!”

So David proceeds to order lunch for the both of them. Twyla slides a stack of napkins his way when his nose starts running, and he’s never felt more thankful for her in his life.

One of the napkins is still pressed to his dripping nose as he enters the store. No Patrick. He sets the bag on the counter, gives his nose one last wipe before tossing the soggy paper. He squeezes some santizer on his hands from the bottle at the register and scans the place for his fiance.

“Um, hello?”

A sneeze from the back room. Patrick. David could identify that sneeze anywhere. He gets a bad feeling, and that feeling is only confirmed when Patrick emerges from the back room.

Wide-eyed and...sick. Very obviously sick.

“What are you doing here? You’re supposed to be resting!” Patrick smiles, pretends like nothing's wrong as he wraps David in a hug and kisses his cheek. Far more affection than David was expecting. In fact, more than usual.

“Um. hi,” David says, giving a confused smile. He rests his arms on Patrick’s shoulders. In such close proximity, he can spot the moisture at the base of his fiance’s nostrils, the red tint that says he’s been blowing his nose all day. “I brought lunch?”

“Oh, thanks but I... already ate.” Patrick clears his throat.

“You didn’t though, ‘cause I asked Twyla.” He raises an eyebrow, waiting for Patrick to admit it. Instead, Patrick pulls away and swipes at his nose when he thinks David isn’t looking.

“Right. Yeah, I’m just not very hungry.”

David groans, and ends up coughing as well. “So are we just going to pretend you didn’t catch my cold, or...?”

Patrick sighs, and then it’s his turn to start coughing. Except it sounds much more painful, coming from his throat rather than his chest. “It came on fast.”

David frowns. “Did you look this bad this morning and I’m just that oblivious?”

“It was just a sore throat this morning!” Patrick says. “Now it’s everything else and a... much worse sore throat.”

David coos sympathetically and pulls him in for another hug. It’s adorable that Patrick doesn’t resist in the slightest. He’s usually against PDA at work, but he seems to be quite cuddly today. David could get used to this.

“So are you really not hungry or are you just afraid to eat because of your throat?” He asks, unpacking their food.

Patrick’s hesitance to answer says it all.

“Mm’kay, then let’s put this food away and go get soup at the cafe instead. And then we’ll come back and finish the work day like the two professional individuals we are. And then we’ll go home and load up on cold medicine.”

“You don’t have to stay, David. I gave you the day off.”

David shakes his head and wraps an arm around Patrick. “I’d rather be here for us to be miserable together.”

Patrick smiles and kisses him just as nicely as he had that morning.
