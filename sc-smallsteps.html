Small Steps

They had an unfortunate start to the day when David threw up in the bed.

Patrick stirred awake, groggy at first until he realized what had woken him up. “David...?”

“Oh my God. Oh my God!” David clamped a hand over his mouth in shock and embarrassment rather than because of nausea. He looked more freaked out about the mess than the fact that he’d just vomited, and only left the scene when he started gagging again and had to run to the bathroom before the next bout.

Patrick got the floor clean in no time (and the one blanket that was in the line of fire). He tried telling David that it really wasn’t that big of a deal, but it was difficult when he was on the other side of a closed door.

“There’s no reason for you to come to work if you’re this sick,” Patrick said to the door. 

The toilet flushed, but it didn’t sound like David was making any attempt to get off of the floor. “I’m fine!” but the words didn’t really mean anything when they came out in a miserable croak.

David was still throwing up when he left for work, promising that he’d be there as soon as possible and Patrick should just head out without him. Against his better judgment, Patrick did.

-----

Patrick was in the middle of going through their books for the month when the doorbell rang. He looked up, ready to greet their first customer of the day, only for his smile to fade.

“David, what are you doing here?”

David didn’t look sick. David looked amazing. He was skilled at hiding signs of illness or exhaustion. Patrick remembered a couple of occasions when he’d let his boyfriend do him up when he hadn’t gotten enough sleep the night before and looked a little rough for working with customers.

“Hi,” David gave a small smile before moving past him to put his bag in the back. He averted his eyes strangely. Did Patrick have something in his teeth? Or perhaps he just knew that he shouldn’t be there.

“David...” Patrick caught his arm before he could walk away. David still wouldn’t look up at him no matter how hard Patrick tried to be in his line of sight. “Hey, go home.”

David shook his head. “Mm’m. I’m here for the day. Now, if you’ll excuse me, I need to get to work.”

Patrick sighed.

-----

Stevie entered the store, almost sneakily when she saw that Patrick was at the register and David was nowhere to be seen.

“So I saw David at the cafe this morning,” she said quietly, leaning on the counter. “He ordered ginger tea. And then he refused to talk to me. What the fuck.”

Patrick nodded and gave a heavy sigh. He glanced toward the back hallway to make sure David was still organizing in the back room before he said, "David threw up last night. He... Didn't quite make it to the bathroom?"

Stevie's eyes widened. "Ohhh, that must be killing him."

"Yeah." Another sigh. “He hasn’t looked at me all day. And I don’t think he’s as okay as-”

His sentence was cut off when David darted from one end of the hallway to the other, haphazardly making it into the store’s bathroom and closing the door, seconds before they heard the sound of vomiting.

“Fuuuuck,” Stevie muttered. “This again.”

“What? What do you mean, ‘this’? Is there something wrong with him?” Did David have some sort of health condition he didn’t know about? Should he be worried?

Stevie scoffed. “I mean, a lot of things. But he does this thing when he gets sick where he pretends that nothing is wrong until it gets so bad that he physically can’t keep doing it. It’s stupid.”

Patrick frowned. “Why?”

“Something to do with people treating him like shit in New York, probably.”

Patrick grimaced inwardly. He hated hearing about David’s time in New York. He hated when David made such casual references to people abusing his friendship in the past. David called himself damaged goods, and though Patrick didn’t agree with him one bit, he was beginning to realize why David thought that about himself. It was because others had been telling him that relentlessly for years.

Stevie left before David came out of the bathroom. He looked pale and exhausted, adjusting his sweater until his sleeves were symmetrical and free of folds and creases.

He cleared his throat and smiled.

“Feeling okay?” Patrick tried, as casually as possible.

“Fantastic,” David replied. And he went back to work as if he hadn’t just spent ten minutes throwing up.

Patrick thought about what Stevie said. David needed to know that he was loved. That he was cared about and no one was going to get after him for not being his best.

“It’s okay to be sick, you know.” The words came out before Patrick could stop them.

David looked up with alarm. The frown that had been on his face all day deepened. “Look,” he sighed out, taking a few steps closer to Patrick. “What happened this morning? Was humiliating. And I think I just need a moment to... try to pretend it didn’t happen.”

Patrick blinked. “David, it’s fine.”

“It’s not though,” David grimaced. “I don’t know how you’re ever going to think of me as attractive ever again, and I-”

Patrick tried to hold in the chuckle, he really did. And he thought he’d gotten away with it until David looked back at him with wide eyes and a furrowed brow.

“Um, excuse me, I’m kind of pouring my heart out right now.”

“I’m sorry,” Patrick said with an unrelenting smile. “It’s just that, there’s nothing that can keep me from being attracted to you. Especially not a little puke.”

David shook his head. “That’s- that’s not-”

“David,” Patrick said, setting his hands on David’s shoulders. He tried to ignore the warmth he felt radiating from his boyfriend’s body. “Go home, my home, I mean, and get some rest.”

David’s eyes darted around the room as he seemed to be weighing his options. “I don’t feel good,” he finally admitted. Just hearing him acknowledge it gave Patrick a sense of relief.

“I know.”

“But I can’t do that. Not yet.”

“Okay, small steps?”

David nodded, biting his lip.

Patrick kissed his cheek. “We can work on it. In the meantime, at least go in the back and rest? Take some fever reducer, you feel warm.”

“I can do that,” David decided. He cleared his throat and stopped before he full turned around. “You talked to Stevie, didn’t you?”

Patrick swallowed. “Maybe.”

-----

The next time something like that happened was when David had the flu.

He’d dismissed it as a cold right up until he went to stand up and passed out in the middle of the cafe. When Patrick went to help him up, he felt the unmistakable heat of a fever rolling off of him.

Patrick knew that David thought little of himself as a person. He knew that it was something he was working on, especially now that they were together.

But now, thinking as he felt his sleeping boyfriend’s forehead, Patrick realized that David’s weird thing about being sick wasn’t just a result of habits created when he was surrounded by awful, selfish, judgmental people.

He was hurting himself.

It was as if he didn’t think he deserved to take care of himself when he was sick.

Patrick sniffled, rubbed the tears from his eyes.

“ ‘trick? What’s wrong?” David shifted, eyes opening in thin slits, just enough to see Patrick without having to deal with the daylight that was still pouring through the curtains.

“Nothing,” Patrick laughed despite himself. He wiped the wetness from his cheeks and went to brush the hair away from David’s forehead.

David yawned and sat up, slowly as he still seemed to be dizzy. He got a look at Patrick’s face and frowned, suddenly looking more lucid than he had all day. “Not nothing. You’re crying...” He reached up to cup Patrick’s face.

Patrick crumbled. Fuck... he was supposed to be comforting David.

“David,” he said, laying his hand over David’s. “I love you so much. You know that, right?”

David chuckles, his breath crackling a bit until he had to pull away for a weak cough. “Of course.”

“I don’t want you to think that you don’t deserve to take care of yourself.”

David frowned. “I...I’m not trying to. Not like, actively.” He sniffled. “It’s just... I’m so used to feeling like I have to ignore my body when I’m sick, you know? I lived alone in New York, and I worked alone, and I couldn’t exactly expect anyone to help me if I decided to stay in bed...”

Patrick knew he must look pathetic, crying for seemingly no reason.

“I hate having to worry about you like this, David.” His voice was barely above a whisper.

“I’m sorry.” David’s as well.

Patrick shook his head. He could feel David spiraling already. He didn’t want him to think that he was upset, or that he thought David was a burden. He leaned forward until their foreheads bumped together. He needed the closeness, so did David.

“Small steps,” Patrick said. David nodded against him. “Next time you’re not feeling good, will you just tell me?”

David gave a watery chuckle and pulled away to cough again before, “I can do that.” He snuffled, nose running, and ran the back of his hand under his nose. “I’m- I think I need a tissue...”

Patrick chuckled and pulled one from the box for each of them. “God, we’re such a mess.”

-----

Patrick had noticed he was acting a little off all day. David had been tired, quiet, and slow-moving. He figured he just didn’t sleep well the night before, dismissed it as such until David made a noise. What couldn’t be dismissed, however, was the fact that he skipped breakfast.

When Patrick looked up, his boyfriend’s face was pinched in pain.

Patrick was about to ask what was wrong when David cut him off.

“I don’t feel very well,” he breathed out. “My throat’s like, really sore.”

Patrick went into worried-boyfriend mode immediately. “Feel like you’re getting sick?”

David nodded. “It’s not a big deal,” he said quickly as Patrick approached him. “But- but you told me to tell you, I’m just holding up my end of the deal.” He held his hands out in a “whatever” manner, even though his face said that he was feeling particularly sheepish and vulnerable.

“Thank you, David.” Patrick grinned. “Do you need anything?”

David swallowed, grimaced. “Um. You don’t have to-”

“Please?”

He giggled hoarsely. “...Tea, I guess?” he shrugged.

Patrick smiled, nodded, kiss him. “I’ll be back!”

-----

He nodded a greeting toward Stevie who was seated at the counter eating lunch, promptly asked Twyla for a tea.

“Heyyyy,” Stevie said between bites. “Tea in the middle of the day, huh? Pretty risky.” She was always giving him crap for his hesitance about caffeine. He just laughed.

“Actually it’s for David. He’s got a sore throat.”

Stevie blinked. “And he told you he has a sore throat?”

Patrick smiled. He probably shouldn’t be happy about such a small victory, but he couldn’t help it. He definitely shouldn’t be happy about his boyfriend being sick, though, so maybe he should tone it down.

“Nice. I’m proud of you two.” It was as genuine as Stevie could get, which was good enough for Patrick. She went back to her sandwich.

He thanked Twyla for the tea, said goodbye to her and Stevie, and returned to the store.

David was at the counter tiredly rubbing his face. He definitely didn’t look like he was feeling well at all. He perked up as Patrick set the tea in front of him.

“Thanks,” he smiled.

“Why don’t you go home?” Patrick said softly.

David made a refusing nose as he sipped the drink. “Small steps. For right now, I’ll take the tea.”

Patrick nodded. It wasn’t exactly what he wanted. He wanted David to be able to take a sick day guilt free.

But it was a start.
