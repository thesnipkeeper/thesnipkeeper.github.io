Unprepared

It’s late, the diner’s practically dead now that the dinner rush is gone. That means that Luke can grab some of his own dinner before he kicks out the remaining customers and closes up for the night.

Luke certainly doesn’t expect the first thing he sees when he walks upstairs to be his nephew, sitting on the kitchen floor as if that was where he’s meant to be.

“Hey,” he says, biting back a confused grin as he observes the sight.

Jess looks up, sits up a bit off of the cupboards that are supporting his back. Luke needs to get around him to grab a plate for his sandwich, but he almost feels the need to keep his distance like the kid is a wild animal.

“What uh... what’re you doing on the floor, dare I ask?”

“Nothing,” Jess says.

“Alright. Let me try that again. What the hell are you doing on the floor, Jess?”

Jess huffs out through his nose, closes his eyes, and rests his head against the cupboard behind him. “I dunno. Sat down, got comfortable...”

Luke blinks. “O...kay.” He steps around the teenager, leans over him, and grabs a plate and then his sandwich from the fridge. He’s in the process of opening a beer when he realizes that Jess hasn’t move a muscle in a good few minutes.

The kid almost looks like he’s asleep. On the kitchen floor, of all places. Luke takes a sip of his beer, a bite of his sandwich, and leans against the counter to just... observe. It’d be entertaining if he wasn’t so God damn confused right now.

“Jess,” he says after a while.

“Hm?” Jess doesn’t bother to open his eyes or turn his head.

“What’re you doing?”

“Nothing. Leave me alone.”

Luke sighs heavily before finally asking, “You okay?”

“ ‘m fine.”

“Yeah? Why is your face red?”

Jess shrugs.

“Jess, I’m not gonna just let you sleep on the kitchen floor. Go to bed if you’re that tired.”

Jess has the audacity to look annoyed at Luke for even questioning him when he finally opens his eyes for a moment. “I’m fi-” He sits up a bit more, and then hunches over, a hand going to his mouth.

Luke doesn’t have the chance to ask another question before the kid’s throwing up.

Vomit, in his hand, down the front of his shirt, on his jeans, and all over the floor. Luke swears and tosses his dinner on the counter. He rushes to grab Jess, pull him up an get him over the sink. If anything, it’s just so they don’t have an even bigger mess to clean up. It seems to be a good course of action, as Jess gags again and brings a wave of chunky vomit that proves he hasn’t had a chance to digest the last thing he ate.

Luke looks away with a wince while he continues to hold the poor kid upright. He can feel a burgeoning fever, heat soaking through Jess’ T-shirt. Luke sighs and rubs his back.

“Get it all up, kid.”

Jess does, because he throws up a third time, and a fourth immediately after. He hardly has a chance to get a breath in before heaves each time.

He stays hunched over the since as he waits to see if there’s more on its way. Jess spits uselessly, doing absolutely nothing to get rid of the drool dripping from his mouth. Luke rubs his back in wide circles in the meantime.

They wait a while. He catches his breath, tries to get the bits of grossness out of his mouth. Eventually, Luke hands him a paper towel, and then a cup of water to rinse out his mouth more thoroughly.

“Alright...” Luke sighs. “Go get in pajamas, get to bed.”

Weirdly enough, Jess does exactly what he’s told without complaint. That should feel better than it does, shouldn’t it?

With Jess in the process of getting out of his vomit-soaked clothes (“Throw it in a pile, I’ll start the laundry in a sec”, “Mhm...”), Luke goes to work cleaning the mess on the floor. It occurs to him that he hasn’t cleaned his kitchen floor in a good while, so maybe this is a good thing? Either way, he starts to wipe and scrub and spray until its gone.

Only then does he look over at Jess, who’s curled into a ball on top of his covers. His jaw is tight, he has his arms wrapped around his stomach while a wince is etched into his brows. Luke has never seen the poor kid so miserable (save for the night of the car accident, but that was a different sort of miserable, Luke supposes).

Jess gives a pained moan when Luke is putting the cleaning stuff away.

“Y’okay?” He asks.

Jess sits up, legs over the edge of his bed, and then doubles over. Luke can tell he’s trying hard not to make any more sounds of complaint, as if puking all over the floor didn’t already get all of his attention. He starts to stand on shaky legs, and Luke can’t help but tense as he watches the guy try to gain his balance.

“Jess-”

“Leave me alone,” is all Jess says as he walks past him and shuts the door behind him.

Luke winces. Did he eat something bad, or is it just some bug he caught? Luke thinks, for a terrifying moment, about all of those stories he’s heard of appendicitis mistaken for stomach flu and food poisoning. God, what is he has to take Jess to the ER? What is he supposed to do if his nephew’s appendix bursts on his watch?

Jess is in the bathroom for a while. Luke turns on the TV and cranks the volume to give him some semblance of privacy in the wide open apartment.

He ignores Luke when he finally emerges, looking like a zombie, and goes to bed. He’s asleep in seconds, and Luke knows that because of the blaring snores from Jess’ side of the room.

-----

Jess’ bed is empty when he wakes up. At first he thinks the kid sneaked out somewhere, but the deadbolt is still locked. Luke’s groggy mind remembers the evening before, and with that he remembers that he spent a good amount of time cleaning puke off of his kitchen floor.

He spots the closed bathroom door, and a flash of irrational fear that his nephew somehow died of a burst appendix or some unknown illness in the middle of the night overwhelms him for just one second before he pushes it open.

Jess is asleep. His head pillowed on a bunched towel he seemed to manage to yank down from the rod, and his huge hoodie wrapped around him (and doing a terrible job at keeping him warm if those shivers are any indication). He’s pale, looking every kind of horrible.

There’s a streak of vomit on the floor and toilet seat where the kid must not have been able to get over it fast enough, and one of the blankets from Jess’ bed sitting in a heap in the corner.

Luke sighs.

“Jess. Jess, wake up.”

“Go ‘way.” He hardly makes an effort to open his mouth as he says it, let alone his eyes. Instead, he breathes deeply and curls up tighter. “Just fell asleep.”

Oh, Jesus.

“Yeah well... you’ll sleep a lot better in your bed.”

Jess groans, long and hoarse. “No.”

“Christ, Jess. C’mon, work with me here.”

Jess reluctantly sits up and lets Luke pull him to his feet. The moment that he’s upright, however, he lunges for the toilet once again, giving an unproductive and painful-sounding heave. Luke keeps him steady as his stomach continues to try to bring something up that’s not there.

A trickle of discolored liquid comes up after a good couple of minutes of misery. It looks like Jess may have made an attempt to drink water.

Luke sighs heavily and waits for the kid to wipe his mouth and flush the toilet before they continue their way to his bed. Possibly the longest it’s ever taken to get to the other side of his apartment.

Jess is still overly warm, Luke realizes as he helps him sit on the edge of his bed. He also realizes that he doesn’t own a thermometer. Why would he? He’s never needed one, really. He hardly gets sick, and if it was ever bad enough that he had a fever, he didn’t see the point in checking it.

He lays a hand on Jess’ forehead for a quick second before the teenager pulls away with a weak growl.

Luke huffs setting his hands on his hips. For once, his exasperation is aimed toward the situation and not Jess himself. “How long were you throwing up last night?”

“Off and on,” Jess shrugs. His words slur together as he speaks, and he practically looks asleep already as he gets situated in bed. “ ‘m going back to sleep.”

“Need me to grab your other blanket?”

“Oh, shit...” he says. “I- It needs to be washed. I totally forgot.”

“Gotcha,” Luke nods. “Get some sleep, I’ll call the school and let them know you’re not gonna be there.”

“I’m sure they’ll be thrilled,” he mutters sleepily as his eyes begin to slip shut.

“And I’m gonna put a bucket over here, and you’re cleaning up your own vomit if you don’t make it this time.”

“Understood.”

“Jess.”

Jess opens his eyes again with the most annoyed expression he can muster in his state.

“You gonna be okay by yourself?”

“Yes,” he scoffs. “Jesus Christ, I’m not five. Can I please go to sleep now?”

“Fine, fine!”

Luke cleans, and cleans and cleans. He makes sure Jess has water, but maybe he should grab some ginger ale from Doose’s later? Something to worry about at another point in the day. He washes his hands even better than he ever has before and decides to have Caesar do all of the cooking today.

What an insane couple of days these are turning out to be. He’s never really thought about what he’d do if Jess got sick while he was staying in Stars Hollow. Granted, he didn’t really have a chance to think about any of it when Liz sent him there. Jess has had a couple of colds here and there, but those were always a reason to ignore him, as he was even grumpier than usual.

And he falls into the rhythm of work.

Kirk wants soggier toast and a different kind of salt for his eggs, and Luke glares at him until he changes his mind on both fronts.

He clears a table that harbored three whining children and two parents who couldn’t have cared less that their annoying little pre-schooler dumped three ounces of syrup on the table, and the chair, and the floor.

He makes small talk with the sweet older ladies who comes for breakfast every Monday and Thursday morning and are some of the few people who laugh at his deadpan jokes and sarcasm. They make sure to tell him how handsome he his, and ask if he’s got a girl yet. He replies with a joke to veil the fact that his love life is the last thing that he wants to talk about, and they laugh and order their usuals.

It’s later in the morning, the early birds’ breakfast rush having calmed down when Lorelai Gilmore walks in. Luke is taking advantage of the lull in customers to properly mop the floor and get rid of the syrup-y stickiness he’s been unable to get up with a wet rag.

“Gooood morning!” Lorelai appears right next to him, effectively getting in the way of the stupid syrup spot. He nudges her back a few steps and continues to mop.

“Morning, Lorelai.”

She looks down to the patch of tile that he’s vigorously scrubbing. “What happened here?”

“Syrup. Kids,” he answers simply. “How much of a backlash do you think I would get if I banned children altogether?”

“Oh, I’m sure no one would care,” she waves a hand dismissively. “Just like no one cares when you don’t put up Thanksgiving decorations or when no one cared when you stopped serving fruit as a side.”

Luke winces at the memory. Hardly anyone ever ordered the fruit, so he took it off the menu. That seemed to have gotten around town and suddenly everyone was showing up asking for fruit cups and getting annoyed when he confirmed that he no longer served them. This town is full of crazy people.

“What’ll it be?”

“Well,” she folds her hands together in an attempt to look cute. It works, but he wouldn’t tell her that lest her ego get too big for the diner. “I was hoping that you could make me some of those pancakes... you know, the ones with blueberries and chocolate chips?” She thinks for a moment, and adds, “And a side of fresh fruit, please.”

He scoffs. “I can offer you blueberry pancakes or chocolate chip pancakes.”

Lorelai gives a long, complaining groan. “C’mooon, Luke! I need special sustenance today! My mom’s group is having their luncheon at the inn and half of the ladies are going to try to ask me personal questions with thinly-veiled judgment.”

“Pick one, Lorelai,” he says as he sets the mop back into the bucket and looks at her, giving her the look that urges her to make up her mind. Sometimes it works, sometimes it doesn’t.

Lorelai huffs, crossing her arms. “Fine. Blueberry.”

He nods. “I’ll get Caesar right on it.” He gestures toward a table and finally gets her to sit down.

Then she stands up. “Wha- no, you have to make them! They’re not the same if you’re not making them!”

“Jesus Christ...”

“Your pancakes are always better!”

“Don’t let Caesar hear you saying that,” he mutters, failing to hide his amusement.

“Luke, please!”

“Sorry,” he sighs out, before rolling the mop bucket to the back by the stairs to be dealt with when he’s done with her nonsense. “I’m not cooking today.”

“What does that mean?”

“It means that Caesar’s gonna make your breakfast, and you’re gonna like it.”

He calls the order back to his employee, who waves from his station in a vague acknowledgment of what he’s been told. When he turns back to Lorelai, she’s looking at him with a raised eyebrow as if waiting for further explanation.

“Jess is sick,” Luke says. “I didn’t think it’d be very hygienic to be handling food today, alright?”

“Sick, huh? You sure he’s not faking?”

“Yup.”

“Okay, but are you sure sure? He’s sneaky, Luke. You know that.”

“I’m sure.” He starts to clear another table. Thankfully, this one is not covered in syrup, but in salt instead.

“How sure? I know you want to trust him, but-”

“I’m ‘found-him-asleep-on-the-bathroom-floor-this-morning’ sure, okay?” He barks it a little more annoyed than he meant to. Jess is always going to be a sensitive subject between the two of them, he’s accepted that.

Her mouth closes, and she nods, wearing an small smile. “Ah.”

Luke finishes scrubbing the table. He’s on his way to get the coffee Lorelai didn’t have to ask for when he hears the thumping of someone coming down the stairs, followed by a startled sound and a crash.

Jess mumbles swears as he picks himself off the floor. “Thanks for that,” he growls, glaring at Luke and then the mop bucket whose wheel he tripped over.

“Jesus, sorry,” Luke goes to help him up, but Jess smacks his arm away and pulls himself up, looking rather weak and shaky as he does. He grabs the book that fell out of his pocket and momentarily checks it for any mop water-related damage.

Jess huffs and brushes himself off. He’s dressed, and his hair looks a little messier than usual, but he at least appears to have gotten some sleep as he’s not the blob of illness-induced exhaustion that Luke’s dealt with the past twelve hours.

“Where are you going?” Luke demands.

“Gonna find somewhere to read,” Jess replies as if that’s obvious.

Luke stares uselessly. “Shouldn’t you be... resting, or something?”

“I’m fine,” Jess scoffs.

“Have you eaten? Or drank anything?”

“I’m fine,” he repeats, and walks out.

Luke is left with a splash of mop water to clean up and a feeling of concern and ineptitude at being concerned. He can’t be too frustrated with a sick teenager, though, because he knows that he’d do the exact same thing. He can’t just expect Jess to be okay with lying in bed all day, because he wouldn’t be either.

“Oh!” Luke remembers Lorelai’s coffee that’s been sitting un-delivered on the counter.

She nods her gratitude. “You’re right, he looked pale,” she says as she takes a sip.

“Did he?” He did. Luke’s trying not to think about it.

-----

A few hours pass and Luke once again finds himself worrying about the teenager in his charge. This isn’t right, it’s not like him to worry about Jess. Jess disappears, he comes back, that’s what happens every day of Luke’s life. Any yet...

He shakes his head at his own ridiculousness and continues to scrub the counter with mediocre effort. It takes him a while to realize that the counter doesn’t even need scrubbing and he was actually in the middle of drying dishes. With a huff, Luke grabs a clean towel and starts working on the stacks of mugs and glasses in front of him.

He doesn’t bother to look up when the door jingles until he hears a familiar pair of voices.

“Jesus Christ, Gilmore, ease off a little!”

Rory groans and pokes his back until he moves further inside. “No! No, because you’re sick.”

“I’m not that sick!” Jess makes the classic mistake of laughing at a woman when she’s trying to be serious, specifically a Gilmore woman. Yet, he’s allowing himself to get herded into the diner like a sheep.

Rory huffs. “First of all, you stopped mid-conversation because you thought that you were going to throw up, so yes, you are that sick.” Jess rolls his eyes and chuckles, but he doesn’t have a chance to speak before she continues. “Second, you’re probably spreading your germs allover town or something! You’re supposed to stay home and keep whatever plague you have to yourself!”

Jess is still laughing, but he sinks into a nearby chair and sets his book on the table, where the bookmark is much deeper in than it had been when he left. “Fine, you win. What do you want from me?”

“I want you to-” Rory stops, brows knitting as if she really doesn’t know what she's going to say next. “I want you to- to be more conscientious.”

Jess raises an eyebrow, an amused smirk painted on his face. “Conscientious? Really? I was innocently reading in the middle of the park before you dragged me back here. That’s not very conscientious of you, Rory.”

“Well!” She groans and folds her arms over her chest. “You look terrible!”

He does, he really does. But Luke isn’t about to get involved in whatever argument is happening right now. Instead, he takes the last of the clean mugs and starts to dry it while enjoying the show.

Jess rolls his eyes. “No one’s forcing you to look at me.”

If Rory is anything like Lorelai (and she absolutely is), she’s not going to give up just because he responds humorously. If anything-

“Jess, you almost vomited in the park!”

“So?”

“So?! So!” Rory flails her arms helplessly. She’s still in her school uniform, probably just spotted him on her way home and tried to talk to him before the signature Gilmore overreaction began. After a few seconds of silence and frustrated gesticulating, paired with Jess’ unwavering grin, Rory sighs. “Just- just stay here!”

A scoff. “You’re not in charge of where-” he stops.

Luke drops the towel. After the past day, he knows that look.

Jess brings a hand to his mouth, stifling a wet burp into his fist that indicates something worse on its way up. To his credit, his walk to the bathroom is calm and slow despite his situation.

Rory looks worried, but Luke makes his way around the counter and stops her before she tries to say anything.

“I’ve got this, Rory,” he promises, giving her shoulder a quick squeeze.

She nods, looks unsure at Jess’ book left on the table and then at Luke, and walks out with a small wave.

Apparently Jess didn’t have a chance to close the bathroom door behind him, so it’s a good thing the diner is empty. Luke leans against the frame and stares at the sight of his nephew spitting into the toilet.

Luke chuckles. “What was that all about?”

“That family should be institutionalized,” Jess mutters, without making an effort to stand just yet.

“You don’t have to tell me twice,” says Luke. “But she was just worried about you. You do look kinda... pale. And... y’know, nauseous.”

“I’m always pale.”

“You look more pale.”

“And the correct word is ‘nauseated’.”

“Jesus Christ.”

Jess wipes his nose and mouth with a wad of toilet paper and flushes all of it away. He pulls himself up onto shaky legs. Was he that unsteady a few minutes ago? He did sit down as soon as he walked in. Jess turns to leave, but of course Luke is still in the doorway.

“Um, excuse me?”

“She was also right, you should be here. Upstairs, I mean. Get your plague out of my diner.” He lets the kid pass, and follows him out through the hallway as Jess gives a signature teenager scoff.

“Fine, whatever.” He goes to grab his book, and Luke doesn’t miss the moment he loses balance for just a second, wavering unsteadily. He grabs the back of a nearby chair as if to lean over it while he picks up the half-read book. “I’m gone.”

“Good,” Luke says, swallowing his concern. “Drink some water or something.”

“Yup!” Jess calls as he starts his way up the stairs.

Luke only hears half as many footsteps on the stairs as there should be. He raises and eyebrow and is about to go look what’s going on before the remaining steps are finally taken. Jess couldn’t make it up the steps without taking a rest.

Luke decides to find something to clean.

-----

“Help ya find something, Luke?”

Taylor’s gratingly-cheery voice is the last thing that Luke wants to hear. And he hates that the answer to that question isn’t a ‘no’.

“Actually uh,” he shoves a hand in his pocket and waves uselessly with the other. “I’m lookin’ for the ginger ale. I don’t see it any...” he trails off as Taylor nods and pulls a cluster of bottles off of the shelf in front of him. Right in front of him. God, first he misses that and then he needs Taylor’s help to find it? Doubly humiliating. He sighs heavily as he takes it. “Thank you.”

Taylor looks smug. “Happy to help!”

He cranes his neck and takes a glance at the rest of the items in Luke’s basket before Luke has a chance to turn and hide it from view. Luke’s already gathered some signature upset-stomach items like sports drinks and saltines. He also grabbed a small bunch of bananas when he saw them, remembering that they’re supposed to be gentle on one’s stomach.

“Feeling rough, are we?” Taylor asks, inadvertently leading Luke to the register to check out. Luke was really hoping to get to the register while Dean was there, but apparently he was too late.

“Not me, Jess is a little sick.”

“Right,” he nods and punches in the weight for the bananas.

Luke can’t stand when he uses that long, drawn out tone, as if he’s trying so hard not to say something. He finds a way to make one want to know what he’s thinking. Probably because no one would listen to him talk otherwise.

And Luke, unfortunately, gives in with a heavy sigh. “What?”

Taylor shrugs innocently, raising his eyebrows in a way that says ‘what could I possibly know?’. “You know rebellious teenagers and their drinking habits. Feign illness in the midst of a hangover.”

Oh, Jesus Christ. “And you’d know hangovers wouldn’t you, Taylor?”

Taylor sputters, looking up wide-eyed in the middle of scanning a bottle of Gatorade. “Excuse me?”

“Well, you’re clearly the expert,” he mutters idly as he digs through his wallet.

“I’ve never had a drink in my life, I’ll have you know!”

An easily disproved statement like that, coming from Taylor Doose, is a gift to anyone. But perhaps Luke would toss that one to Lorelai later and let her torture the man herself. She gets more delight out of being around insufferable people.

“You never faked sick when you had a hangover?”

“Absolutely not!”

Luke hums and nods.

“But that Jess is a liar and a- a hoodlum!”

Yes, both of those things are true. But Luke scoffs and shakes his head anyway, because Taylor’s looking for a reason to get upset just like always.

Taylor hasn’t told him the total he’s owed yet, so Luke looks at the numbers on the small black screen and lays the money on the counter before snatching his bags.

“He’s a kid with a fever and a stomachache. See ya, Taylor.”

“Luke! You can’t just- we have a system here!” Taylor is stammering in fury that someone would walk away from him, which just makes it that much more fun.

-----

Jess is asleep when he finally goes up to the apartment. There’s no sign that he’s eaten anything, but there’s a cup of water next to his bed that’s half-empty. Maybe the kid actually listened.

Luke slips his shoes off and unbuttons his shirt, relieved to finally be done with work and the rest of the events of the day.

He puts the groceries away and then starts pulling things out of the fridge and cupboard. Soup ingredients, a soup recipe that he prides himself on. Rory Gilmore ate this soup when she refused to eat anything else when she was thirteen, and Luke is confident enough to think that Jess will at least give it a try.

And if he doesn’t, there’s always saltines and Gatorade.

The first pan he gets from the cupboard makes a loud clanking noise against another. Luke freezes, he hears Jess stir, and he continues.

Cooking shouldn’t be as calming for him right now as it is. Perhaps it’s because he hasn’t gotten to cook all day, or that he’s not dealing with snarky customers.

It’s not long before the kitchen smells amazing, so much so that Luke remembers he hasn’t eaten dinner yet.

“What’cha doing?”

“Gah! Jeez...”

Apparently he’s been so focused on the cooking process that he forgets there’s another human in his apartment.

He finds an amused-looking Jess in a wrinkled t-shirt and sweatpants. His hair looks all kinds of gross, having been damp with sweat off and on all day. He looks more rested than before, maybe he’s on the mend and Luke won’t have to worry about his caretaker skills much longer.

“Soup,” he gestures to the pot on the stove. “For dinner.”

“Ah,” Jess nods and takes a seat at the table, propping his chin on his palm. He’s not looking as rested in that position. “What a cliche.”

“Yeah, well... I figured we should have a dinner that isn’t half grease, something you might actually be able to digest. How’re you feeling? Any better?”

“Well, I’ve been asleep for... what time is it?”

“Seven fifteen.”

“Jesus, I’ve been asleep for three hours...” he sighs.

He doesn’t seem thrilled about that realization at all, which doesn’t make a lot of sense to Luke. He’s sick, he hardly slept last night, of course he’s going to sleep all day.

“You know,” Luke starts, keeping his attention on stirring the soup and not on Jess’ preemptive eye-roll. “It’s probably a good thing Rory forced you back here when she did. You weren’t looking too good. Rest is generally important when you’re sick.”

Jess scoffs. “Whatever.”

Luke grabs a couple of bowls and starts ladling the soup.

“I’m not hungry.”

“Just try a little bit, okay?”

“Once again, I’m not five.”

“Just eat the damn soup, Jess.”

Jess does, in fact, eat the damn soup.

The room is silent save for their slurping. Jess eats more than Luke expects him to given the day that he’s had. He doesn’t seem particularly upset about eating, either, until he pushes the bowl away with a new grimace.

“Shit...” Jess mutters, arm pressing into his stomach as he leans forward.

Luke stops eating. “Y’okay?”

“I’m fine,” he grumbles with annoyance. “I’m going back to bed.”

“Well, put your bowl in the sink- Jess! Your dish-!” but Jess walks off, completely ignoring him in the process. Luke decides not to bother him about it, and rinses both bowls.

It’s only a few minutes later, when Luke is finished with the dishes and the leftover soup is in the fridge, that he glances over to his nephew’s side of the apartment. Despite the fact that it’s been silent over there since Jess abruptly excused himself, he’s awake, and curled into a ball tighter than Luke thought possible.

He has both arms wrapped around his belly, and his knees pulled up as if to help add pressure. His eyes are close, face blank save for the way his brows are knitted together.

Luke doesn’t try to be quiet as he scoots the designated bucket closer toward Jess’ bed. It smells like disinfectant, he must’ve thoroughly cleaned it earlier.

Jess’ eyes open. “What?” he asks, trying hard to get rid of the pained edge in his voice.

Luke shrugs and states, “You’re gonna puke again.”

Jess does a horribly fake scoff. “No I’m n-”

Those are all the words he gets out before he pulls a hand to his mouth and tries to sit up, an urgent, strangled noise in the back of his throat. Luke shoves the bucket in his lap and pats his back, really trying to ignore the heat coming off of him. He’d definitely force some kind of meds in him if he thought Jess could keep it down.

Well, there goes the soup.

He doesn’t even bother to shove Luke away when he sits down next to his nephew and makes sure any clothing or bedding is out of the line of fire. He must be feeling awful if he’s not telling Luke to screw off.

That’s not comforting.

Luke swallows over the anxious lump in his throat that’s been there since Jess got bullied back to the diner. This isn’t just a quick bout of food poisoning, it’s an actual illness that maybe he’s supposed to do something about?

“Maybe we should go to the hospital,” Luke accidentally says out loud.

“What?” Jess bites, and then chuckles and scrubs saliva off of his chin. He’s certainly in fine form. “No. It’s just a bug.”

“Does your stomach hurt?”

“Of course my stomach hurts, you dick.”

Luke lets that slide. “Well, it could be... I dunno, appendicitis or something.”

“It’s not.”

“Do you know that for sure?”

“Yes!” Jess rasps, pushing his hoarse throat to the limits.

“No you don’t, Jess! How the hell-”

“I don’t have an appendix!”

Luke stops. He closes his mouth, breathes in, and nods. “Ah.” He clears his throat, feeling supremely stupid. “When-?”

“When I was nine. I puked up blood in the middle of school and they called an ambulance.”

Liz never told him anything about that. She never mentioned much about Jess at all, did she? How bad did appendicitis have to be for someone to start vomiting blood? 

“Jesus Christ.”

Jess laughed in agreement. “So can you get off my case right now and let me suffer in peace?”

“I dunno, Jess. You haven’t been able to keep anything down.”

“Oh my God, Luke. I can take care of myself. Just go away.”

And Luke knows that that’s true. He’s been doing it for ages. Liz probably didn’t even notice that he was sick until his appendix burst, and Jess probably didn’t feel the need to tell her.

So, he stands up. “There’s Gatorade in the fridge, saltines on the counter. Try to keep something down if you feel like you might be able to, alright?”

“Thanks,” Jess mutters dryly, still bent over the bucket.

This time, Luke finds himself waking up every time Jess moves between his bed and the bathroom. He thinks about asking if he’s okay, if he needs anything, maybe reminding him to drink something. But he knows that Jess wouldn’t enjoy being bombarded with unhelpfully helpful questions in the middle of the night, so he stays in bed.

Until the footsteps to the bathroom are followed by a CRASH, and then a pained swear.

Luke’s out of bed before he realizes it, darting over and opening the door.

“Jess-? What-”

Jess is on the floor, rubbing his shoulder. The shower curtain and rod are now on the bottom of the tub. He must’ve fallen and taken the curtain with him.

Jess doesn’t acknowledge his presence before he scrambles over the toilet and heaves fruitlessly. God, he looks terrible. Was he this pale before?

Luke waits until he’s done before he pushes the kid's bangs away and not-so-gently presses his palm against Jess’ forehead.

That’s a fever. A real, proper fever that requires attention. His cheeks are red, and he’s not sweating, and he has yet to even glance at Luke’s direction. He’s so out of it. How much has he thrown up tonight? Three? Four times?

“Just lost my balance,” he rasps.

“Let’s get you back to bed,” Luke says, helping hoist the sick teenager up to his feet. Jess is absolutely no help this time. In fact, he’s fighting against it.

“No, leave me alone...” he slurs. At least he’s still aware enough to argue a little bit.

“You should be in bed, kid.”

“No, I-” he huffs, stiffening in Luke’s grip as much as he can, making himself harder to move. “I can’t... Go away.”

Luke lets go real quick. “Oh.” Jesus, his stomach just wants to torture him in every way. “I’ll- I’ll fix the curtain... tomorrow. Don’t worry about it.”

It’s still dark out, and he’s supposed to be awake in an hour, anyway, so it makes sense that he’d just stay up and start work a little early, right? He’s never been this tired in the morning, but then again, it’s not often that he’s woken up every two hours.

Luke yawns and calls out, “If you need anything, I’ll be downstairs!”

“Whatever!”

-----

“So, how’s Jess?”

He can tell that Lorelai is asking more out of obligation than anything else, and he can’t blame her for not caring about the punk kid. Those are the same types of question he asks people out of obligation even though he’s never really given a crap about people’s kids (the younger Gilmore excluded, of course).

“He’s uh, sick.”

Lorelai scoffs into her coffee cup. “Be a little more vague, there.”

Luke sighs, scrubbing a particularly tough spot of dried ketchup on the counter. Disgusting. How did he miss that the million other times he’s cleaned the counter yesterday?

“Well, he’s not himself, that’s for sure. Not like there’s much I can do, right?”

“Yeah, I’m gonna guess he’s not much for coddling.” She takes another sip of her coffee.

He hums in agreement.

The moment feels eerily similar to yesterday, except now he’s in a bad enough mood that he’d probably punt a kid for spilling syrup on the table, and his parents along with him.

“You okay?”

“Yeah,” Luke clears his throat. “Just pretty tired. Didn’t sleep great, got up earlier than I needed to.”

“Earlier? Jesus, like 4AM?” she laughs, clearly thinking she’s funny until he gives her a look that confirms it for her. “Jesus,” she repeats. “Why the hell would you get out of bed at four in the morning?”

“I was already up. Jess kinda crashed into the shower curtain, scared me half to death. Not easy to fall asleep after your heart jumps outta your chest.”

Lorelai frowns. “Ah, jeez...” She looks down at her coffee cup as she tries to think of something else to say. “Why don’t you go take a nap or something? I’m sure Caesar can handle things.”

A nap does sound good. And it’s not as if he’s very functional there at the diner when he’s sleep deprived. But... he can’t sleep in the middle of the day. It’s a literal impossibility for him, always has been. Accidental naps, sure, but he’s never been able to rationalize going to sleep when there’s something else he could be doing. Like scrubbing gross ketchup globs off of the counter.

“I’ll take a break after the lunch rush,” he says with a shrug.

She nods, and silently holds out her empty coffee cup until he fills it. “Good enough.”

-----

He’s completely exhausted by the time he takes his lunch break. He’s not particularly hungry, just longing for sleep that he may or may not end up having. A nap is sounding quite nice now.

His limbs are tired as he climbs the stairs and lets himself into the apartment.

“This is just getting sad,” is the first thing he says, shoulders falling in a heavy sigh.

Jess, seated on the kitchen floor, this time against the refrigerator instead of the cupboard, opens his eyes and minutely turns his head to face his uncle. “ ‘sup.”

“Sup,” Luke replies with a scoff. He walks to the kitchen until he’s a mere foot away from Jess, and looks down at him with his hands on his hips and a deep sigh. “You okay?” he tries to sound insincere, even though there’s dark circles under Jess’ eyes and he looks as if he can’t hold his head up.

“Yeah, ‘m good.” Jess doesn’t even bother with a joking response. “Wanted some Gatorade, got lightheaded, needed to sit down.”

“And... how long ago was that?”

“I have no idea.”

Luke swears under his breath. A sour smell hits him, and he sees a damp streak down the front of Jess’ shirt. God, he’s a mess.

When he helps Jess to his feet, he finds that the fever is still going strong, probably part of the reason that the kid is so weak. He tries not to think about the fact that Jess also hasn’t been able to keep food or water down the past couple of days.

Jess wavers on the way to his bed, not a simple stumble but looking as if he’s seconds away from passing out. Luke manages to take most of his weight, he’s not a real heavy person, after all.

Jess lies back and closes his eyes, looking horribly sick and exhausted. Luke somehow misses the snarky brat of a teenager.

“I think you’re dehydrated,” he says.

Even in his miserable state, though, Jess manages a “Mmm,” that can easily be interpreted as “No kidding, moron.”

He grabs a bottle of Gatorade and cracks it open before nudging his half-asleep nephew with it. The cold bottle to his burning skin causes Jess to gasp before he takes it. He starts gulping like there’s no tomorrow.

“Small sips, Jess.”

“I’m thirsty.”

“Yeah, but if you drink it like that, you’re just gonna puke it up again.”

Jess heeds his warning.

Luke doesn’t like this. He doesn’t like that he’s in charge of another person’s wellbeing. Who knew that Jess would actually need his help with something? He wasn’t prepared for this.

So, he calls Lorelai.

“Luke, calm down. What’s going on?”

“He just-” he huffs out through his nose. “He’s so pale and he practically blacked out when he was walking four feet to his bed. He’s yarfed anything he’s had to eat or drink and... I don’t know what to do, Lorelai! No one said that I had to- to do this kind of thing! But he’s- he’s real sick, and what if he... I dunno, dies or some-”

“Luke! Luke, take a breath.”

Luke tries.

“He’s not going to die just because he’s still throwing up, I promise. That’s just the stomach flu, it happens. Jesus, how long have you been freaking out about this?”

A while. Too long. He should have called her sooner. He doesn’t have the chance to actually answer before she continues. It’s probably for the best, anything he tries to say wouldn’t be real sentences.

“I’ll be over in a few minutes. Just sit down for a bit, okay?”

Luke finds himself doing so, eyes wide and a little wild.

There’s always been something about Lorelai’s voice that calms him in any crisis. She’s the only person that can say the words “calm down” to him, and it actually works.

So he sits. And then he paces. He checks the clock.

He finds himself breathing a sigh of relief when he hears someone coming up the stairs.

Lorelai gives a courtesy knock before letting herself in.

 

“I have arrived!” Lorelai sing-songs.

He goes to thank her about a million times, but - almost as if on cue - there’s a groan from Jess’ side of the room and then the patter of liquid hitting plastic. Luke winces and rushes over.

“I told you to take small sips,” he says unhelpfully.

Jess moans and heaves again. “Screw off.”

“Hey Jess.”

Jess looks up from his hunched position, and furrows his brows at Lorelai before turning back to Luke. “What’s she doing here?”

“Ah, so kind.”

Luke sighs. “I- I called her.”

“Why?”

He isn’t quite sure how to answer without sounding like an idiot. ‘I was worried about you and I have no idea what the hell I’m doing’ didn’t seem quite appropriate. God, maybe this was a mistake.

“He’s helping me with some financial stuff,” Lorelai chimes in. Luke sends a thankful glance her way. “Future inn things and stuff. I see that you’re feeling fantastic!”

Jess rolls his eyes, and doesn’t even bother to be embarrassed as Luke takes the bucket from him. “Yeah.”

“The way that Rory described it, it sounded like you were on death’s door. You definitely look it.”

Jess ignores her and lies down. He’s shivering faintly, and Luke has a feeling that he’s waiting for them to leave his ‘room’ so he can cover up without looking pathetic. So, they do. Luke leaves to rinse the bucket, and Lorelai follows him.

“Hey,” she says, leaning against the bathroom door. “Has he been peeing?”

“Excuse me?”

“Has he peed, Luke?”

“How would I know that?”

She shrugs. “Well I don’t know! You two share an apartment the size of a grape, I thought maybe you’d know.”

He sighs and shakes his head. He sprays the bucket with some kind of bleach stuff that happened to be under the sink the first time he ended up cleaning this thing.

“He might be dehydrated.”

“I know that!” he snaps.

Lorelai recoils a bit, but doesn’t bother to say anything before he raises his hands in apology.

“Sorry. Sorry, I-”

“-You’re worried, because your kid is sick and you don’t know what to do,” she says with a smile.

“He’s not my kid,” Luke argues, even though he knows she’s right. “But- I don’t know, I’m freaking out just a little bit, okay?”

She nods, and follows him out, staying in the kitchen while he returns the bucket to Jess’ bedside. Jess is asleep, snoring softly. He’s probably exhausted after being awake the whole night before.

Lorelai is going through his fridge and cupboards when he gets back, pulling out honey and lemon juice and a variety of other things. He doesn’t question it at the moment.

“Do I need to take him to the hospital?” He asks in a hushed tone. The very last thing he ever wants to do is go to the hospital for any reason, but he’s ready to drag Jess’ ass down there if he has to.

“I think he’ll be fine if we can get some fluids in him. Did he chug that Gatorade?”

Luke scoffs. “Told him not to.”

“Mhmm, been there before.” She grabs a glass and a spoon and starts mixing things together like she’s done this a million times. Maybe she has.

“What’re you doing?”

“I used to make this stuff for Rory when she was sick. It’s like a homemade electrolyte drink.”

“It’s gonna... help?”

“It should. Here,” she nudges him with a glass of cloudy, lemon-y liquid. “Take that to him, see if he’ll drink.”

Just his return wakes Jess. Luke manages to persuade his nephew to take a few sips, nagging him about small sips as the teenager grumpily drinks. He set it aside after just a few small sips, but that’s enough for Luke.

“Alright, go back to sleep. Get some rest.” He pats Jess’ arm.

Jess mutters something and has no trouble complying. Luke’s going to be so happy when the kid goes back to staying out too late and complaining about the number of trash cans the diner has.

Lorelai’s snacking on pillaged food. She’s got that look in her eyes that she gets right before she sm- yep, there’s that smile.

“You’re a cute uncle. All concerned and stuff.”

He rolls his eyes.

“You also look like you need a nap. And food. Have you eaten much today? I found some mustard pretzels in your cupboard if you want any.” She holds the bag out for him, and he scoffs and shakes his head.

“Nah, I’m not hungry. And I don’t nap.”

“Right, well-”

Luke hunches over just a bit as a cramp makes itself known in his belly. He lays a hand over it and winces.

“Uh oh. Luke?”

“Sorry,” he breathes out, straightening up. “Maybe I am hungry.”

She scoffs. “Dunno if that’s it, but go for it.”

“What does that mean?”

“Nothing. Proceed.” She gestures with a single pretzel in her grasp.

What is she-?

His stomach cramps again.

Oh.
