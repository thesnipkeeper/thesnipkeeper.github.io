Maybe Too Much

She’s been listening to her boss sneeze all morning. Detective Juno Steel is sick, and he has yet to admit it!

Mr. Steel sniffles disgustingly. He doesn’t have any tissues, he’s been using his shirt instead. Gross.

Rita’s thought of all of the different ways to try to help. She offered to make tea, he growled at her like a grump. She offered to go pick up soup, and once again got growled at.

Rita sighs heavily in hopes that it will get his attention, but he ignores her. Well, that isn’t exactly much different than usual.

He sneezes for the millionth time, managing to cover with his sleeve-covered hand. He doesn’t pull his hand down, however, and instead sniffles desperately. Rita can only imagine the snotty situation that he’s dealing with.

“Boss, do you need a tissue?” She asks with slight amusement.

Juno glares at her. “I’m fine.” but he has yet to pull his hand away from his face. He gives a particularly soupy snuffle and sighs. “Okay,” he says quietly. “Fine, yeah, I’ve kinda got a situation here.”

Rita tries not to feel too triumphant, but she doesn’t hide it from her features as she gets up and grabs a box from the office’s tiny bathroom. It’s cheap, generic tissues that she picked up one time when getting supplies for the office, but he doesn’t seem to mind.

Juno wastes no time using his non-snotty hand to pull out a- wow, a lot of tissues and press one to his face. There’s a gurgly sound as he empties his nose into the thin paper. Rita visibly cringes.

He’s still dabbing at his nostrils when she asks, “Don’t you think that you should go home and rest, boss? I mean, you’re sneezin’ everywhere, and you obviously aren’t feelin’ good.”

“I’m fine, Rita. It’s just a cold.”

“A very snotty cold, Mistah Steel.”

He doesn’t deny it. “Well, if it’s bothering you, then just leave.”

Rita doesn’t bat an eye. She’s been snapped at by her boss so many times it doesn’t have an effect, and she can see the guilt cross his features immediately afterward.

“Sorry,” Mr. Steel says with a sigh and a sniff. “Sorry, I...”

“It’s okay, boss, you don’t feel good.”

“Look, I can’t leave right now,” he pauses to run his sleeve under his nose, and then remembers the tissues and uses those instead. “snf! We’ve got research to do if we want to finish this job and, you know, get paid. snf snf!”

“Okay fine,” Rita agrees. “But if you’re gonna be here, at least let me help.”

“Well, you do work here so you should be help-”

“I’m talkin’ about your cold, Mistah Steel! Let me get you some medicine or somethin’! Or- oh! Oh! What about some of that vapo-stuff that you breathe in, or cough drops. Do you have a cough?”

“Just a sore throat right now.” He runs a hand through his hair, looking tiredly at the stack of files he’d been rifling through.

“Aww, boss...” RIta frowns. He really isn’t feeling well. “I’ll get you some medicine. Wait here!”

“I’m not going anywhere, Rita.”

-----

It isn’t until she watches her boss down the little cup of cold meds that Rita realizes she’s never actually seen Mr. Steel take anything before. Liquor, sure, but he doesn’t seem to take anything no matter how much pain he’s in. Rita has thoughts on it, theories, but none that make her happy, so she doesn’t say anything.

They continue their work like normal after that.

Until she hears a giggle from the other side of the room where Mr. Steel’s desk is. She almost wonders if someone else has gotten in, because she can’t imagine her boss giggling. And yet, when she looks up, he has a stupid smile on his face. It’s adorable, Rita decides, and not at all weird as long as he’s happy!

“What’s so funny, boss?”

“Oh, it’s just something in these files. Someone wrote in the logs that a lady fell off a roof by accident and lived.”

“...what’s funny about that, boss?”

“She lived! It’s crazy how that kind of stuff happens. And yet people can die just as easily from an infected paper cut. Life is crazy, Rita.”

He certainly looks and sounds like he feels better, but Rita still can’t help but feel like something’s off.

“Are you feelin’ alright, Mistah Steel?”

He shrugs. “Feel great. Whatever meds you got me are really working.”

“Boss...?” Rita raises an eyebrow and looks him over. She wanders to his desk to take a closer look, and immediately gasps to see the half-empty bottle of cold medicine sitting next to him. “How much of that did you take, Mistah Steel?!”

“Enough,” Juno replies, giving a little sniff. “My head doesn’t hurt anymore. I can’t even feel my head. Do I have a head?”

Rita sighs heavily and hooks a hand under his arm. “C’mon, boss, I think you need to lie down.”

“What? No, I feel great, Rita. I’m trying to work.”

“Yeah sure, boss, but I think you’ve been reading the same page for an hour. C’mon.”

It’s concerning how easily she’s able to guide him from his desk to the old futon. He mumbles something too himself as she sits him down and drapes a blanket over him (a blanket that’s just as old as the futon, to be honest).

“Rita, ‘m fine...” his eyes are already closed as he says this. He clumsily lies down, hugging a throw pillow with a sad sniffle.

“Sure, boss. You just have a nice little nap and I’ll finish the research, okay?”

“Mm’kay.”

She leaves to get some water, and he’s already snoring congestedly when she comes back. Rita frowns, watching her boss in a rather drug-induced sleep. At least he felt better?

-----

She’s good on her word. Rita finishes the research, takes detailed notes for Mr. Steel, and writes down possible culprits to the murder. (the second victim’s cat is particularly suspicious, she decides). She doesn’t realize how much time has passed until the snoring stops and is replaced by a moan.

“Rita?” Juno sits up and winces. He drags hand down his face before seeing where he is. “Oh...”

Rita rushes to him. “Mistah Steel, you’re awake!”

“Yeah, I think so, anyway. Snrf!” He sounds more congested now, snottiness returned. Just drags his sleeve across his nose once more. “Ugh, snf! I think the meds wore off.” He blinks blearily, before catching Rita’s worried stare. “What is it?”

“Boss... you took a lot of that stuff.”

“Yeah, maybe a little too much.”

“Maybe? Maybe too much?! What am I supposed to do if you die here, boss?!” She starts to cry, and suddenly Juno looks more awake than he has all day.

“Whoa okay, Rita, I-!” he coughs into his sleeve. “I took a little too much cold medicine, what’s the big deal?”

The big deal is that Rita’s sure he’d do it again if he felt the need to. “You know I don’t like it when you do that, boss,” she says quietly. “It’s like you were trying to hurt yourself and feel better at the same time.”

He sighs. And he’s quiet for a good few seconds while Rita waits for him to respond. She crosses her arms over her chest, doing her best to look stern.

“I mean... yeah, I guess I was.” He rubs his eyes. “Sorry for scaring you, I guess.”

“No, you’re not,” Rita argues. “At least, not really! But- just don’t do that again, boss, at least not when I’m around.”

Juno nods. “Alright. Alright, I can do that.”

She plops next to him on the futon and wraps him in a hug, earning an ‘oof’ in response. “You’re my favorite boss, boss.”

“I’m your only boss.” and then a, “Uh, Rita...”

“It’s okay, I ain’t afraid of germs, Mistah Steel.”

A huff. “No, Rita, I-” he sneezes, half onto her shoulder before he breaks free from the hug and sneezes again into his abused sleeve. Juno groans and swears under his breath as he rubs his nose.

“Oh! Jeez, boss, you could’ve said somethin’!”

He sighs. “Can I please have some tissues.”

“Yeah! Yeah, I’ve got it.”

He presses one to his face the moment Rita retrieves the box and blows his nose once again. He looks worse than he had before he chugged all that medicine. “I don’t suppose you’re gonna let me take any more of that stuff, huh?”

Well, it’s like he’s reading her mind. “Nope!”

“Fantastic.” He sniffles. “I really am sorry, Rita.”

She nods. “I know, boss.”
