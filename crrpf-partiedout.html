Title: Partied Out
Fandom: Critical Role RPF
Summary: Taliesin hides out during a party and Liam gives him some good drugs
Word count: 1.1k

Partied Out

Taliesin blows his nose for the thousandth time, wincing at the clogged gurgle that indicates just how stuffed up and full of gross he is. No one seems to care when they're too busy drinking and laughing and being able to breathe through their noses. 

"Well, you sound fantastic." Liam patted him on the back. 

"Ha. Yeah." He snuffles and takes another drink of punch. "Uh, Liam-" 

"Hm?" 

“You wouldn’t happen to have any painkillers with you, would you? My head is killing me.” It’s a sinus headache instead of the usual stress headaches Taliesin is prone to. He can feel the pressure in his head whenever he sneezes, reminding him how stuffed up he is.

Liam’s eyes briefly knit together in worry. “Oh! I don’t have some personally,” he leans in closure. “But I know where to find some of the good stuff.”

“What?”

Liam chuckles. “Follow me.”

So Taliesin does. Liam leads him into the kitchen/break room area.

“Sit down, I’ve got this.”

Taliesin does, but raises an eyebrow in confusion. “I mean, I just need...”

“Yeah, you want to go back to the busy party when you’re feeling like shit?” 

“Ohhh.”

Liam chuckles. He opens a cupboard, the one above the sink, and pulls out a first aid kit. “You looked pretty done. I figured you could use some time-wasting excuse to get out of there for a while.”

“I’m not trying to...” Taliesin sighs. “I’m just tired. And I... don’t feel well.”

Liam pulls a bottle out of the container and inspects it, while waving his other hand dismissively. “Naw, I think everyone understands. We all knew you were coming down with something, anyway.”

Taliesin sniffles and runs the inside of his wrist against his nose. He usually loves their little office parties, but having a miserable cold certainly dampened things. It’s hard to enjoy spending time with your friends when you’re constantly worrying that your nose is visibly runny.

He sniffles again, harder this time, and winces. Liam is standing in front of him with a bottle of ibuprofen and a cup of water just as he brings his hand to his head.

“Aw buddy... here.” 

Liam sits next to him and rubs his back as he takes two pills. 

“And now we wait for it to kick in.”

Taliesin gives a yawn. “Yeah.” He leans his head back against the couch and closes his eyes, hoping to fall asleep just until the pain reliever works.

Liam stays there. Taliesin has a feeling that he wanted to get out of there for a little while as well. 

He leans his head on Liam’s shoulder with a heavy sigh, and lets himself be guided further down until his head is in his friend’s lap. 

Liam begins running his fingers through his hair. He forgets how comfortable Liam is with touch. The two of them are probably the cuddliest of the group, it’s a wonder they don’t do this more often.

“This okay?”

“Mm. Amazing,” Taliesin answers with a snuffle. “Might get snot on you, though.”

Liam gives a hearty laugh. “I have kids. I know how that goes.”

Taliesin falls asleep to Liam’s hands gently carding through his hair.

He wakes to cool fingers on his temple.

“Hm...” eyes drifting open, he realizes that he must have fallen asleep on Liam. “Fuck, sorry,” he says as he looks up at his friend. “Didn’t mean to pin you down.”

Liam chuckles. “I didn’t expect you to fall asleep. I must be better at that stuff than I thought.”

Taliesin gives a sleepy smile before he sees Laura knelt in front of him. She pressed her palm to his forehead, thankfully warmer than her fingers, and nicer on his over-sensitive skin.

“You’re kind of warm,” she says with a frown.

“Yeah, I know,” he sighs out. “At least the headache is gone.”

Liam snorts. 

-----

The first thing that greets Taliesin as he walks in the studio a couple days later is a loud sneeze. He recognizes it immediately.

His suspicions are confirmed when he walks into the lounge and sees Liam with watery eyes and a tissue steepled over his mouth and nose. He looks like he’s going to say something before he sneezes again, this time more forcefully.

“Ugh, God,” he mumbles to as he swipes the tissue on the underside of his nose. His nostrils are already pink, face a little more flushed than usual.

“Sorry.”

“Okay, well, there’s no way to prove that this is your cold. A lot of people are sick this week.” Liam coughs into the collar of his hoodie.

Taliesin frowns. “Yeah but… still. I’m gonna make you some tea.”

Liam chuckles. “That’s the most mom thing I’ve heard come out of you.”

“Really? You sure? Even when I had to remind everyone to bring a jacket the day we went to the farmer’s market?”

“Okay. Fair point. You’re just perpetually in mom mode, then.”

“Absolutely!” Taliesin fills the tea kettle, his - technically - since he was the only one around the office who drank tea religiously. Liam likes flora flavors, he remembers, so he makes a hibiscus tea in lieu of the green tea that Liam claims “tastes like grass”.

Liam sniffles his way into the kitchen and leans on the counter. “You don’t have to do this just because you’re feeling guilty, you know.”

Taliesin chuckles. “Yes I do. Here,” he pours the hot water over the herbal tea bag. “Don’t drink it until its steeped.”

“Aye aye, sir.” Liam moves closer, and seems to hesitate for a second. Taliesin isn’t sure what to expect until the guy presses a kiss to his temple. “Thanks, buddy.”

Something in his stomach flutters with happiness. With a warmth that makes him want to give Liam a hug and take care of him. He and Liam have never been the closest of friends. It had nothing to do with how they felt about each other, they just had very different personalities that didn’t always go together.

But as he watches his sniffly friend go against his instructions and drink the tea before it’s steeped, he realizes that they have one thing in common. They’re both rather… cuddly people. They like physical contact.

It’s funny how little time the two of them spend together, but he wouldn’t mind spending a little more if it meant getting more contact like that.

“You didn’t listen to me.”

“I wanted to try it!”

“But it’s not done, Liam! That’s the whole point of steeping tea!”

Liam sticks out a tongue at him and promptly has to turn away to sneeze.

Yeah, maybe they’re closer friends than Taliesin thinks they are.
