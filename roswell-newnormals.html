Fandom: Roswell New Mexico
Summary: Michael notices something off about Alex
Word count: 800

New Normals

Michael wakes to the sight of his sleeping boyfriend.

He’s beautiful.

Michael will never tire of waking up to see that face, striped with the small bit of sunlight that shines through the curtains. Alex wakes up at the same time every morning, his internal clock permanently set for six-thirty in the morning.

Michael waits.

And waits.

And before he knows it, it’s almost time for him to get out of bed and start the day himself while Alex is still fast asleep at seven.

Alex finally wakes up and immediately that adorable, beautifully sleepy smile tugs at his lips as he looks at Micheal.

“Hey,” Michael says as he finds himself smiling back. “Good morning, sunshine.”

“Morn-” Alex’s voice gets caught, he turns away to cough. “Morning. Jeez.”

Michael’s smile disappears. Any differentiation from the norm puts him on high alert these days. “Whoa, you okay?”

“Yeah,” Alex nods, clearing his throat. “Yeah, just a tickle. I probably didn’t drink enough water yesterday with everything that was going on at the lab.” It isn’t like him to forget to drink or eat. Those things are just as hardwired into his brain as anything else. He insists that routines aren’t as important to him as they really are. Michael is in no place to talk when it comes to one’s past controlling current habits.

“Alright, well how about we get some water and breakfast into us before we have to continue the insane chaos that is our lives.”

Alex grins. “Sounds good to me.”

Michael is trying not to pay attention. He knows that Alex worries about him constantly looking for a reason for this not to work out. That he doubts every little thing. So he doesn’t want to make it obvious that he’s watching Alex.

He’s moving slower.

Michael watches him button his shirt wrong, sigh, and rebutton it with more aggression. He watches him play with his breakfast for a long time, as if the bowl of cereal is going to get any better the soggier it gets.

He feels even more ridiculous when he thinks that not only does Alex smell different, but the kiss the two of them share before they go in their respective ways tastes different. Michael feels stupid, and he also never thought he'd be close enough with someone to notice something like that.

The concern is forgotten as he gets into the groove of work once again. He doesn’t give any of it a second thought until Maria calls him.

“What’s up, De Luca?”

“I think you should go home and be with your boyfriend.”

Michael pauses, stammers, cogs in his brain turning as he tries to figure out whether he should know what that means. Finally, he settles on a very intelligent, “What?”

“Okay, I ran into Kyle at the Crash Down and he mentioned that Alex went home early and that he wasn’t looking too hot.”

“He didn’t…” Michael took the phone from his ear for a second to make sure he didn’t have any notifications. “He didn’t text me.”

“He probably didn’t think of it.”

So Michael packs up. Walt doesn’t even ask for specifics when Michael tells him he has to leave for the day, and Michael pretends like the man can’t see right through him when it comes to his relationship.

He finds Alex fast asleep on the couch. He looks miserable. Still beautiful, though. 

Michael sighs and sits on the edge by his legs. The weight of another person seems to be enough to wake him, and he does so slowly and groggily.

“Hey,” Michael greets softly. He brings his hand to Alex’s cheek and yeah, it’s warm. Like really warm. How did he get so warm so quickly?

Alex smiles. He sits up, opens his mouth to speak, and triggers a cough instead. Michael winces at the wet, chesty sound. By the way Alex’s brows knit together, he’s willing to bet that it hurts his throat, too.

“I knew something was weird this morning,” Michael says, half to himself, but of course Alex is paying attention.

“You did, huh?”

“You smelled different.”

Alex smirks, that annoying, precious, loving smirk. “I didn’t think it was going to be this bad. But I… feel terrible.”

“You sound terrible.”

“And smell it too, apparently.”

Michael scoffs. “I said different!”

He tries to laugh and ends up coughing again. “I’m gonna go to bed.”

Michael nods and he’s aware that he looks weird as soon as he does it. He feels awkward.

“Michael?”

He looks back at Alex and kisses him. Alex is surprised, but he smiles as soon as the surprise wears off and suddenly he’s wrapping Michael in a hug.

“I like this,” he muffles into Michael’s shoulder. He doesn’t have to be specific, because Michael is thinking the exact same thing.

Being together. Living together.

“Me too.”
