Title: College AU
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Molly rescues Caleb from himself
Word count: 1.2k

College AU

"Please come rescue your dying boyfriend from the library. He's coughing all over the place."

Mollymauk raised an eyebrow at the text. Mostly because he didn’t, in fact, have a boyfriend, dying or otherwise. But he had a feeling he knew who Beau was referring to. 

Molly never really stepped foot in the library (studying wasn’t exactly his thing), but he made it there quickly enough to hear the terrible, wet coughs coming from one end. Beau was at the front desk as usual, feet on the counter as she played a game on her phone. 

“I tried telling him to go back to his dorm, but he won’t fucking listen to me.”

Molly giggled. “You think he’s going to listen to me?”

“Well-! Okay, but...” It was obvious that Beau knew he had a point. “Nott and Jester are still in class. Fjord is on the other side of campus. I figured you wouldn’t be busy.”

She figured right. Still, ouch. They both winced as another fit of coughs sounded from the far end of the library, where most of the study tables were.

“I can try,” he shrugged. “But you and I both know…”

“Yeah yeah, he’s a stubborn asshole. Just like the rest of us.”

Mollymauk nodded in agreement and then made his way over there. He passed various students who looked irked to have to be listening to the obvious plague rat. And then he caught sight of the long reddish hair, the figure hunched over a table, shaking with another hacking cough.

Molly caused him to jump in surprise as he laid a hand on Caleb’s back, but he relaxed once he realized who it was.  With as private as he was, it was surprising how much physical contact Caleb didn’t mind.

“Surprise,” Molly said, quietly enough so other people in the library wouldn’t get any more disturbed than they already were.

“Mollymauk…” Caleb breathed out. His voice was coarse. Molly wondered how long he’d been coughing to sound that bad off. 

“You need to be resting, and somehow the responsibility fell on me to convince you.” Molly perched on the edge of the table. "So. C'mon."

To his credit, Caleb tried to laugh, but he ended up hunched over, sputtering wetly. Molly gave his back a gentle pat as he regained his ability to breathe. 

"Oh, Caleb…"

"I need to study."

"Mm, and what's Nott going to say when she finds out you haven't been taking care of yourself? You'll worry her, Caleb."

He seemed to think about it for a bit. He and Nott had an interesting friendship, managing to get a dorm together despite not being family. Molly was pretty sure they sometimes shared a bed on particularly bad nights, and yet they didn't seem to be romantic

Caleb coughed. Again.

"And do you want to spread your germs to all of these poor, stressed students?" Molly said even quieter, gesturing to the various tables around them.

Caleb sighed. 

"So if anything, go study in your room?" 

He looked like he wanted to argue, but he obviously didn't have any more fight in him. Maybe Mollymauk finally wore him down, although, his worsening health probably had more to do with it but Molly was more than happy to take credit.

Caleb stood, muffling a weak cough into his sleeve. Molly gathered up his friend's things before he had a chance.

Molly wrapped an arm around Caleb's shoulder and led him out of the library, winking at Beau on their way out. 

"See ya, Caleb," Beau said, ignoring Molly completely.

They made it to Caleb and Nott's dorm room with only a few stops for Caleb's lungs to try to jump out of his chest. The poor thing sounded spent. Molly remembered hearing in a casual conversation that Caleb had a cold, but that was a week ago. Whatever this was, it probably wasn't a cold anymore. 

"So," Molly set Caleb's things on the nearest desk as they walked in. The room was certainly cluttered, but not as much of a mess as he expected from the room of Caleb and Nott. "Have you thought of making an appointment with campus medical?"

Caleb chuckled. "No." He didn't try to argue that it was just a cold, that he was fine. 

Molly wasn't going to push it. That was a job for Nott, he decided happily. 

Caleb exhaled heavily before his breath caught in his throat and he needed to sit down. His bed - predictably - was the bottom bunk. 

He began taking his coat off and coughing toward the ground. Not that Molly minded, it was his room after all. 

Still, Caleb cleared his throat and offered a sheepish "Sorry. That was rude."

Molly laughed. "Doesn't bother me any. Do you have any… well, anything? Medicine, tea, cough drops…? Are you going to be alright here?"

"I'm perfectly fine being alone," Caleb's brows furrowed, voice rising in offense. "I'm not going to break, Mollymauk. It's a cough."

Well, obviously he overstepped.

"Right, of course." 

The frustration on Caleb's face faded. Caleb closed his eyes and sighed. "Sorry, I'm… not feeling very well."

He looked and sounded so defeated, so tired. Of course Caleb would be a grumpy sick person, Molly thought fondly. He couldn't be upset by his attitude when he was obviously feeling so poorly, when he admitted it.

Molly smiled warmly and plopped into Caleb's desk chair. "Do you mind if I keep you company until Nott gets back from class? I have nothing to do, I'm so bored."

"Not going to be any more exciting here, but alright." Caleb inhaled suddenly, and reached to grab a tissue before coughing into it. It sounded rather productive. Molly wondered how much gunk was sitting in his respiratory system.

It wasn’t long before Nott had returned to the dorm room.

“Caleb! You’re alive!”

There was an oof as she tackled him into a hug. He reciprocated quicker than he would have anyone else, wrapping his arms around her. Only, he had to pull away when he started coughing. Molly saw the frown on Nott’s face.

“That doesn’t sound good…”

“No, it does not,” Mollymauk agreed. He patted her shoulder as he stood up. “Take good care of him.”

Molly had a relatively uneventful evening. He tried studying, but that had never been his strong suit. He tried watching a movie, but sitting still was boring. He ended up going to bed early for once in his life. And waking up to a knocking on his door.

A little goblin woman was there in the doorway when he answered it. She smiled a little sheepishly. “So… I have a seminar to go to and Caleb… well, I don’t want to leave him alone.”

“I’d be happy to babysit,” Molly said with a chuckle. 

Not breathed out a sigh of relief. “Thank you!”

-----

“What’s this…?” Molly wasn’t met with any resistance as he lifted Caleb’s wrist for better view. A neon strap hung around it, looking alarmingly like…

“Ah,” Caleb sputtered into his kerchief for a moment. “Took a trip to the emergency room last night. I was keeping Nott awake, and no medicine was helping...”

Molly frowned. “I had no idea. What… what did they say?”

“Bronchitis. Not pneumonia.”

“Thank goodness.” 

Caleb hummed in agreement, and Molly couldn’t help a gasp of surprise when he felt a weight on his shoulder. Caleb was resting against him, completely comfortable. He was warm, obviously running a low-grade fever, and his breath rattled with each inhale.

Molly didn’t have the heart to move when he felt the redhead fall asleep. He probably needed it if he spent the night in the ER. 

Molly pressed a kiss to the top of his head and tried not to smile too much.
