Safety

David contentedly cuddled up to his boyfriend. There was hardly enough room for the two of them in his tiny bed, but that was alright. Patrick was all he needed right now. Well, Patrick and cough medicine. The TV in his and Alexis’ room was tiny, and they hardly used it when they had their phones and computer to watch things on, but Patrick didn’t mind.

He was having trouble following the plot of the movie Patrick had brought over. He felt like he would have trouble following it even without the high fever, but that was alright.

David ruined their cuddly moment when he had to sit up to cough away from Patrick. Patrick blessedly supported him so he didn’t fucking fall off the bed like a tragic consumption victim. Patrick helped him settle back against him, lightly scratching at the nape of his neck (Very lightly after David had snapped at him about his skin being sensitive right now. Which it was).

“I should be letting you rest,” he said in the sweetest tone that David never deserved.

“Nooo,” David whined hoarsely, wrapping around his boyfriend as tightly as he could. “I am resting.”

“Yeah, but I meant sleep. You haven’t slept all day.”

“Trust me,” he muttered. “If I could have fallen asleep by now, I would have!” It wasn’t exactly easy to sleep when everything ached and he was coughing every ten seconds.

“Can you guys be quiet, please? I’m trying to study,” Alexis snapped from her spot at the table. She did indeed look busy with all of her

“OhmyGod, shut up,” David snapped back. “I could literally be dying.” He coughed, both out of necessity and dramatic effect.

“Um, if you died, would you be quiet then?”

Patrick just chuckled. He found the two of them entertaining for some reason. Still, David loved the way he could hear his voice with his head on his chest. That was nice. He could stay there all day. He just might.

David sighed. He turned away from his boyfriend to cough again. The medicine did nothing. Maybe he should double the dose next time. Patrick probably wouldn’t approve, but it was nothing he hadn’t done before.

“David,” Patrick said softly. It was then that David realized his eye had slipped shut. “Get some sleep.”

David whined as Patrick started to get up. And suddenly his nice, warm pillow was gone. “Noo, don’t go,” though he could hardly keep his eyes open anymore.

“I’ll be back tonight, I promise.”

The last thing David heard wass a conversation between Patrick and Alexis, and the last thing he felt was a soft kiss to his temple before he fell asleep.

-----

He woke up to someone entering the motel room. With a fuzzy scan around the room, he saw that Alexis was now sitting cross-legged on her bed flipping through a magazine. So the intruder wasn’t her. She looked up and smiled.

“Patriiick!” Alexis sing-songed. “You’re back!”

“And I brought dinner!” he said, holding up bags of takeout.

David imagined it would have smelled amazing if he had an appetite for anything.

“For her too?” He mumbled, still cuddling his pillow. He wasn’t quite ready to wake up yet. “She’s not the one dying.”

“You’re not dying, and Alexis has so graciously let me stay here today and I wanted to thank her.”

David’s eyes had closed again, but he could tell that he wasn’t going back to sleep anytime soon. Apparently his body thought that was enough rest for the day. Instead, he listened to Patrick give Alexis her food and thank her.

He asked something about taking David’s temperature, and that was when David realized that that might be a good idea. With further analysis of how he felt, it was possible that his fever went up.

He felt a weight on the edge of the mattress, and then a hand on his arm that was still clutching the pillow. “Hey David,” came Patrick’s buttery voice.

“Mm,” he replied. “Not hungry.”

A chuckle. “Yeah, I kind of figured. It’ll be in the fridge when you want it.” The hand went from his arm to his forehead, and Patrick made a discontented sound. “You feel a little warmer than you did earlier.”

“Mhm.”

Patrick gave a fond huff through his nose. Not quite a laugh, but enough to be adorable. “Want me to leave you alone so you can go back to sleep?”

“Can’t sleep anymore,” David admitted. “Hurts.”

“What hurts?”

Everything. “Head. Arms. Legs. Throat. Chest.”

“Okay, okay,” the hand went to his hair, which felt incredibly nice. “I’m gonna go grab your thermometer.”

“Nightstand.”

“Wha- oh!” The drawer to his and Alexis’ nightstand opened, and David finally opened his eyes. “You keep it close, huh?”

“Ugh,” scoffed Alexis. “He does it like, every day.”

Patrick blinked, and David really wished he could go back to sleep like, right now. There was a reason he didn’t come out about everything to Patrick all at once. He had to give him reasons to keep him around first! Patrick didn’t need to know about his stupid compulsions. But, Alexis existed.

“Really? You take your temp every day?”

David sighed and finally worked up the will to turn over so he was on his back. “Would that scare you away?” He asked with a wince. Suddenly, Alexis turned to him with a clearly guilty expression on her face, seeming to finally realize that she shouldn’t have outed him.

Patrick chuckled. “Nope! Open up.”

David obeyed, slipping the thermometer under his tongue.

“And while we wait for that, I’ll go get you a glass of water and maybe you can take some medicine.”

David nodded and tried not to enjoy the coddling too much. He kept the thermometer in his mouth as he watched Patrick grab a cup and head to their bathroom to fill it up.

Alexis caught his eyes long enough to mouth a ‘sorry’.

Normally this would have been when David blamed her for ruining his relationship. But the thing was, he thought, that he wasn’t mad this time, somehow. Maybe because he felt a certain security in Patrick that he had never felt before. He could be himself without worrying about Patrick deciding that he was too much, or worse: trying to fix him. Also, Alexis would have never apologized before.

The thermometer beeped just as Patrick was returning from the bathroom. David sleepily pulled it out and squinted at the reading.

“Fuck,” he breathed out.

And normally, seeing numbers like that would have triggered a panic attack, but this time the panic was quickly tamped down by the loving hand stroking his arm as Patrick took the thermometer to see the numbers himself.

He made a sympathetic sound and leaned down to press a kiss to David’s forehead.

David hummed contentedly. He felt safer in that moment than he ever had.
