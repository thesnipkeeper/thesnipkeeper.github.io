Unfinished Shelf

In retrospect, he should have known better than to stay up until 3AM the night before he’s supposed to help his friends with their new studio.

It wasn't entirely his fault. Another friend called a bit last-minute and asked if he could have a set of minis done in record time. He couldn't refuse. A new painting job sounded like fun! And he loves that group. Everyone there is so sweet, and having new projects to work on is always a treat.

If only he hadn't been busy all week, he would have been able to get the minis done on time without sacrificing a good night’s sleep.

Oh well.

He’s glad he could stream the whole eight hours it took to finish them, even when he is super tired by the end.

Unfortunately he (finally) goes to bed with a bit of a tickle in his throat, and wakes up feeling like death.

That's what you get for not sleeping, Nox tells himself as he gets prepared for his day. He thinks about telling his friends that he’s not feeling well and can’t help today, but they have so little help anyway and he already made the commitment.

He’ll just show up, help, leave a little early.

Nox takes some medicine and packs some tissues when he feels his nose start to run. It’ll be a long day, but it’ll be worth it.

Danny greets him and lets him in the moment he shows up.

“Thank you so much,” he says. “We wanted to get this done before the stream tomorrow, and we only have so many hands.”

Nox smiles. “I’m happy to help!”

He leads Nox to the many boxes of shelves that they’re currently trying to put together, and Nox gets to work.

Reading instructions with his head down causes his nose to drip. He snuffles desperately and turns his head up, trying not to look too weird. In the end, he has to pull out a tissue from his pocket and wipe his nose.

And that would be that if the tissue didn’t tickle his nose.

“Wow, bless you!” Art calls from the other side of the studio where he and Ryan are currently trying to figure out their own instructions.

“Thanks,” Nox says with a sniffle.

“Getting a cold?” Danny asks as he passes by with a box in his arms.

He can’t exactly lie, can he? It’s not going to help anything. “Yeah,” he shrugs. “It’s nothing. I’ll make sure to wash my hands and stuff, don’t worry!”

Danny chuckles. “We’re not worried.” and he continues with his box.

It’s not nothing, Nox realizes, when his nose is dripping relentlessly and is now so clogged he can’t sniffle. He’s forced to have a tissue in-hand at all times if he doesn’t want snot dripping onto his friends’ new shelves. The work isn’t exactly something he can do without looking down, which just makes it that much worse.

His body is starting to ache as well. Fuck. Nox can feel the subtle symptoms of a low-grade fever starting to take hold, and now he’s thinking he should probably tell his friends he needs to go home.

But...

He sighs. He wants to help. Fabbi has told him that he’s hopeless when it comes to helping people, but he did tell them that he would. He’ll just finish building and head out.

“Runny nose?”

Nox doesn’t realize that he’s been sniffling wetly and continuously for that past couple of minutes until he looks up and sees Danny setting a box of tissues next to him. His own supply is a damp wad in his pocket at this point.

“Thank you,” be breathes out in relief.

Danny nods and then lowers himself to Nox’s spot on the floor, sitting cross-legged. “You don’t have to do this if you’re not feeling good, you know,” he says, just quietly enough so the others can’t hear. “I mean, we appreciate the help, but you have literally no obligation.”

Nox nods. “Oh, I’m aware, I just want to help.”

Danny goes to say something, and then his mouth closes with a frown. “Hey guys!” He yells, getting Art and Ryan’s attention. “Let’s take a break!”

“We’re almost done!” Art says back.

“Fine, Nox and I will take a break,” he says, jokingly childish.

He nods at Nox and then stands, urging Nox to follow as he makes his way to their tiny kitchenette area.

“I’m fine,” Nox says.

“Here, sit down, I want to see something.”

Nox isn’t sure whose idea it was to buy a thermometer for the studio’s first aid kit, but he’s thankful to see it as Danny holds the device out to him.

“If you have a fever, I’m sending you home,” Danny explains.

Nox can’t help but chuckle, only for it to turn into a cough, before he takes it and slips it in his mouth.

The reading isn’t great, it is a fever, but it could be much worse.

Danny angles his wrist so he can see it as well, and sighs, offering a sweet smile. “Thank you for your help today.”

“Well...” Nox sniffles. “Sorry about this.”

Danny laughs. “Don’t fucking apologize. Guys, Nox isn’t feeling great, so he’s going to head out.”

Nox waves to the others as they say their goodbyes.

“Sorry,” he says again once they’re outside.

Danny just shakes his head and pats Nox on the back. “Feel better, buddy.”

-----

It’s a couple days later before Nox finally feels more human than snot monster again. Danny messages him asking for his opinion on their new campaign. He asks for a video call, to which Nox quickly agrees to after spending all day painting and fixating.

When he sees his friend’s face on the screen, however, he’s a less excited.

Danny’s nose is unmistakably pink, and his eyes look watery.

“Hey, alright so I was thinking of making a dragon encounter, but would that be too much?”

The two of them immediately fall into a discussion about D&D and all the possibilities for Danny’s next campaign stream, but there’s no ignoring the sniffles and small coughs through his phone’s speaker.

Danny pauses, pulls the camera away from his face, sneezes, and continues.

“Ohhh...” Nox says, accidentally interrupting his friend in the middle of the explanation for his new idea. “Looks like you caught my cold...”

Danny clears his throat, suddenly looking sheepish. “Yeah, I mean, maybe a little.”

“I owe you an apology, then.”

He shakes his head with a sniffle and laughs. “No way! Dude-”

“No, I shouldn’t have come over when I was sick. I knew that-”

“Nox,” Danny says. “It’s fine. We share germs over here all the time.”

“Which means... the others are bound to catch it as well.” Nox sighs.

“Well, yeah, probably,” he admits. Danny scrunches his nose with another sniffle and the scrubs it on the back of his hand. He laughs when Nox is speechlessly guilty. “Again, not a big deal, my dude.”

Not a big deal? He’s the one to blame for their office pandemic!

“That’s what happens with family, though,” Danny adds.

Nox smalls, huffing out a small laugh. “Ah, family. Yes. That’s pretty nice.”

Danny nods. “I think so! I should probably go take some dayquil or something, thanks for your help again. Bye!”

Nox blinks. A scoff.

He owes Danny and the others a lunch for the trouble he’s caused whether they think so or not. Or maybe he can finally finish that fucking shelf.

He isn’t sure exactly what he’s supposed to learn from this outside of carry hand sanitizer and stay home when he’s sick, but he thinks it might have something to do with friendship.

Nox closes his laptop and decides to head to order food to have sent to the studio. It’s the least he can do.
