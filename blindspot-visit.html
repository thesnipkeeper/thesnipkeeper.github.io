Title: Visit
Fandom: Blindspot
Summary: Patterson goes to Rich's when he doesn't show up for work
Word count: 500

Visit

Patterson didn't make it a habit of going to Rich's home. It was often gross and he always made it weird and... Well, she'd been there plenty of times. They used it as their base back when they were two of the blind mice. 

Patterson wasn't proud to admit that she had a key to Rich's apartment. 

She was the one chosen to go grab him when he was an hour late for work, and she wasn't sure if she was worried about him or pissed at him. Why wouldn't he show up to work when his entire life out of prison depends on it? 

Patterson knocked, waited, and immediately let herself in. She... Really hoped she wasn't about to find Rich's dead body. 

The house was quiet, curtains still drawn. "Rich?" She called. 

She stepped over a pile of clothes in the hallway and headed for what she knew was his bedroom. 

Rich was asleep. 

Rich Dotcom, the extraordinary hacker who was given a second chance to prove himself by working at the FBI, fucking overslept. 

"Are you serious?!" 

Rich woke violently, sitting up as soon as he saw her. As the blankets slid off of hia shoulder, she realized he wasn't wearing a shirt. Patterson hoped to God that he at least had pants on. 

"What are you doing in my apartment?!" 

"Rich, it's ten-thirty. I was scared you were dead or something, but no you're just sleeping in. What the hell?" 

"I'm sorry, okay? It's not my fault!" To his credit, he immediately got out of bed (even more to his credit, he was wearing underwear). He dug into a very disorganized drawer and pulled on a pair of pants. "Look, I didn't sleep great last night and I must've turned off my alarm, and- ugh." 

"You realize how important this is, right? This isn't a job where you can just slack off." 

Rich sighed. "I know. I'm just-" he leaned over to grab a shirt, and stumbled back when he stood. Patterson rushed to catch him before he could fall to the floor.

Rich made a strangled noise and clamped a hand over his mouth as she helped him keep his balance. He squeezed his eyes shut for a moment before swallowing hard.

“You’re sick,” she deduced.

He nodded once the nausea passed. “Ugh. I’ve been puking all night. I went to bed like, an hour ago.”

Patterson sighed. Of course this would happen to him. “Alright. I’ll let them know you’re not coming in today.” She nodded toward the bed, urging him to sit down before he fell over.

He raised an eyebrow. “You would do that?”

“I mean, I’m an agent. What are they gonna do, call me a liar? I’m looking at you right now and I know that you’re not gonna be any help today. Actually, you’d probably be ridiculously annoying, if the last time you were sick was anything to go by.”

“You can always count on that! I’m known for being exceptionally annoying.”

Patterson rolled her eyes. “Don’t die, okay? There’s no guarantee that you’ll be found if you fall and hit your head.”

“Wow I feel better already.”

A scoff. “See you later, Rich!”
