Sound Sleep

They tried getting separate rooms once, the first time the three of them traveled together. It didn’t work out.

They all ended up in the same room anyway.

That was how it was after that, whenever Orym, Dorian and Fearne stayed at an inn. They asked for one room for all three of them, ignored the raised eyebrow of the innkeeper, and went on their way. It was a good way to keep each other in check, and be there if one of them needed something.

Things were no different now.

Their current room had two beds. One for Fearne, and one for Orym and Dorian. They were pretty good at sharing a bed, seeing as though Dorian was a sound sleeper and Orym was, well, small. (Fearne was not small, and she was quite unpleasant to share a bed with.)

Or, Dorian was usually a sound sleeper.

Tonight, he tossed and turned, making sleeping rather difficult for the halfling on the other side.

“Dorian!” Orym whispered. “Dorian, you’re moving too much.”

Dorian didn’t wake. It didn’t register until Orym heard a faint whimper that his friend might be having a nightmare. He shouldn’t have been surprised.

Orym sat up and shook Dorian awake as gently as he could.

Dorian gasped. “I- Orym? I-”

Orym was prepared to comfort him, to offer some quiet words to help him get back to sleep. He didn’t expect Dorian to turn to the side and vomit over the edge of the bed.

“Fuck,” Dorian rasped out, staring at the puddle on the floor. “I’m sorry, I don’t-”

“No, it’s okay. Easy to clean up.”

Dorian didn’t look too reassured. In fact, he looked horrified with himself.

“I- I can-” his breath quickened. “I can do it, I just...”

This wasn’t a post-dream panic attack. This was... something different altogether.

“Really, I can take care of it,” Orym insisted, setting a hand on his friend’s shoulder to keep him from trying to get up. Orym had a feeling that the man wouldn’t be able to stay on his feet for very long right now. He frowned, taking the same hand and laying it on Dorian’s forehead. “Are you always this warm?”

“What?” Dorian’s focus was scattered.

“Your temperature feels high. I think you might have a fever?”

“Oh,” Dorian sighed. “That would explain the...” he couldn’t finish the sentence, so Orym just nodded.

“Seems it would. I’ll be right back.”

There really wasn’t that much to clean up, thankfully. But Dorian watched with a combination of guilt and panic the entire time that Orym worked at it. There was clearly something about this that Orym hadn’t gotten around to learning.

“M-maybe I should-” Dorian swallowed, looking less like he was about to vomit again and more like he wanted to cry. “I should get a different room. I’d hate to get you two sick.”

“I don’t think either of us care,” Orym said honestly. “Are... are you alright? Aside from the fever, I mean, you seem a little...” a little what, exactly?

Dorian shook his head in the same dismissive manner that he always did when he was avoiding a conversation or ten. “I’m okay. I’m... okay.”

Orym wasn’t convinced, but he couldn’t justify prying, so they went back to sleep. Dorian stayed on the far edge of the bed for the rest of the night.

-----

Dorian woke to a freezing hand on his forehead.

“He’s very warm,” Fearne hummed, before carding her fingers through his hair. It was nice until she hit a knot, sending a surge of pain through Dorian’s body. The fever that he apparently had made every pain worse than it needed to be.

Dorian grunted and sat up, immediately regretting it when the room spun.

“Oh, I’m sorry,” Fearne hummed. “I was trying to be gentle. It didn’t work.”

“It didn’t,” Dorian agreed with a chuckle. He noticed that his friends were fully dressed and the sun shined into the wind in such a way that it must’ve been far later than their usual wake-up time. “What...”

“We let you sleep in,” said Orym, looking up from sharpening his sword. “There’s no harm in spending an extra day or two around here, right?

Dorian sighed. “No, guys, that’s not necessary.” Even as he said it, he couldn’t miss the fact that his speech was slurred with sleep and fever and there wasn’t a damn thing he could do about it.

“I think it is,” Fearne shrugged. “It’d be pretty bad if you died on the road. What would we do with your body?”

Orym choked on nothing. “And we’re worried about you.”

‘That too,” Fearne agreed easily.

Dorian nodded. "Well... Thank you. I'll try to get better quickly."

Orym patted his leg and smiled. "Take your time, buddy."
