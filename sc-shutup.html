Title: Shut Up
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David and Alexis hide out while their parents throw a party
Word count: 700

Shut Up

He can hear the bustle of excitement just downstairs. Laughter, clinking glasses... He's missing out on it all, and he couldn't care less. He already told his parents he was too sick to go to their obnoxious party. He'd planned to stay in his room and read all night, it must've just been karma that he woke up with the flu this morning. Reading is out of the question. He can't stop sneezing or coughing long enough to focus.

He hasn't slept all day, either, despite the fact that he's exhausted. His body just wants him to lie there and feel miserable, apparently.

He knows he's a pathetic sight. His bed is absolutely littered with tissues. Any other time he wouldn't think of letting gross snotty tissues just exist in his bed, but he's far too tired to care today.

David feels an itch in his chest. With an eyeroll, he sits up just enough to cough properly and clear his airways for a few seconds, before flopping back onto his pillow.

David’s eyes are closed. He’s not asleep, but he hopes to fall into blissful unconsciousness soon as to serve as an escape from how awful he feels.

Sleep, please take him...

He is nearly asleep when a sudden weight on his bed startles him. David tries to yelp, but his voice fails him.

He sits up, groans, and lies back down, this time draping his arm over his eyes.

"What are you doing in my bed?" He asks hoarsely. "You're supposed to be downstairs."

Instead of answering, Alexis gives a truly pathetic whine, followed by an equally-pathetic cough.

"You gave me your stupid flu," she says with a sniffle.

David shakes his head against his pillow. "Don't try to put this on me. We probably got sick at the same time." He coughs, sniffs. "That still doesn't tell me why you're here in my bed."

"Can I stay in here today?"

"No."

He can't see her, but he knows that she's pouting. "Please, David? I don't want to be alone like this all day."

"But I absolutely do." He has to pull his arm off his face when a tickle makes itself known in the back of his nose. He only has a chance to inhale and press his sleeve to his nose before he sneezes. "Tsch-uh! Ugh, God..." He moans, reaching for a tissue. David hesitates to blow his nose in front of his sister for just a second before he realizes that he has no choice. There's too much gross on his upper lip to be shy.

Alexis doesn't make a comment, likely wrapped up in her own misery. She curls up near the foot of his bed like a gangly cat.

"I don't think I can go back to my room."

"Oh my God..." David mutters. And she's probably telling the truth if she has what he has. He had to work up the motivation just to get up to pee this morning. Moving is hard, moving is exhausting and dizzying.

And it's not like they haven't done this before. When they were both sick as kids, Adelina would put them in the same bed so it was easier to keep an eye on them. Alexis loved it, David despised it. Mostly.

"Fine," he hisses. "But stay on your side."

Alexis makes a noise of delight, dampened by sickness, before she climbs under the covers. She snuggles up to him before he can even try to resist, her bony chin digging into his shoulder.

"No no," he says, pushing at her rather weakly. "Your side is over there. Go, move."

Alexis huffs and does so. "I can't believe I'm missing the party."

"Oh no, you're missing one of the million parties mom and dad throw every year."

"Ugh, shut up, David." The bed shakes as she's racked with a cough that has David wincing.

"Um, you can't tell me to shut up in my room."

"...Shut up."

David grabs a nearby pillow and whacks her with it without even looking.

"Hey!"

"Just go to sleep."

Alexis doesn't respond for a few seconds, and he thinks maybe she's taking his advice and-

"Tshew!" Or she had to sneeze. "David, do you have any tissues?"

He wordlessly grabs the box from his nightstand and sets it between them in the bed. Giving up some of his tissues is a small price to pay to not have a bed covered in his sister's snot.

"snf! Thadks."

She sounds just as miserable as he feels.

"Seriously," he sighs. "Get some sleep."
