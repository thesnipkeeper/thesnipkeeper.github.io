Title: Date
Fandom: Magicians
Summary: Eliot talks Quentin into going out and then regrets it
Word count: 1.7k

Date

"Come on, Quentin. It's been too long."

He had a point. They had been talking about going on a real date for weeks. Spending time together usually consisted of studying in the physical cabin and listening to Eliot ramble about something that happened at class.

As a rule, Quentin preferred to stay put, where there weren't people or scary situations. But... The restaurant that Eliot had been talking about sounded really good.

Quentin sighed, setting his book down. "Fine, you win! Let's go do so-"

Eliot cut him off with a relieved sigh. "Thank God. I'll help you pick out an outfit."

Quentin blinked. "What's wrong with what I'm wearing?"

Without a word, Eliot just looked him over and chuckled. "Funny."

-----

Eliot was full of energy and had no problem taking the lead in their conversations over dinner. Quentin, never usually one for restaurants or… doing things, took his menu recommendation and got pasta, while Eliot ordered something he was afraid to ask what it was.

"You're right," Quentin said. "We should do this more often."

Eliot just smiled.

-----

"That was amazing," Quentin said as they exited the restaurant. It'd been too long since he had a meal that good.

"See? What'd I tell you. Trust my amazingly good taste." Eliot said with a smirk.

They wandered into a nearby bookstore, browsing the shelves and having light conversation. Eliot got quieter as time went on, though, and it had Quentin wondering if he said something wrong. "Would you... want to get coffee after this? There's a cafe across the street that has really good lattes."

He looked over when his question was met with silence. Eliot was spaced out staring at the open book in his hands, but obviously not reading anything. Quentin laid a hand on his shoulder, earning a light jump off startlement in return. Eliot blinked dazedly at him.

"El? You okay?"

Eliot swallowed hard, Adam's apple bobbing up and down before he gave a little smile. "Of course I am. You said you wanted to get coffee? I'm dying for a double-shot espresso right now."

Quentin believed it. There was a strange exhaustion to his voice that hadn't been there before.

He watched Eliot take a second sip of his drink, frowning worriedly. It was the second sip in about ten minutes.

"Something's wrong and you're not telling me," he stated plainly.

Eliot's eyes widened a bit, realization that he'd been found out. He sighed, setting his mug on the saucer. His voice lowered before, "It's nothing." He continued at Quentin's incredulous look. "Dinner just isn't sitting well. That's all."

Quentin felt guilty for feeling so relieved. He thought that he'd done something wrong, or that something terrible had happened.

"Oh... Sorry."

Eliot giggled. "Yes, thanks for that. On that note, do you want the rest of my latte?"

Quentin accepted it and continued on the second drink when he finished his own. They discussed classes for a while, and their friends, before Eliot looked to be in a little more discomfort.

"Maybe we should head back," Quentin tried, looking at his partner with a raised eyebrow. Eliot swallowed and placed a hand on his distended stomach.

"Sure," he nodded. And then something that completely caught Quentin off-guard. Eliot burped.

Quentin blinked, and then giggled at Eliot's blush as he rushed to cover his mouth. "I don't think I've ever heard you-"

"Oh God."

It kept happening and Eliot was trying too hard to hide it. Quentin could tell his stomach was in discomfort. He had to admit that he'd never seen Eliot in physical discomfort. At this point they've all seen each other broken emotionally, but this was different. And a little more comical.

Eliot looked so tired as they walked into the door. He sighed and pulled Quentin close, pressing a kiss to his hair.

"I'm heading to bed, but it's been a fantastic night. Thank you, Q."

"Oh… okay. Well, I'll be up later."

-----

Quentin expected him to be asleep when he went up to Eliot's room. The poor man looked so exhausted, and he wasn’t even sure why. Going out and doing stuff usually seemed to fuel Eliot, not drain him. So, Quentin opened the door and found Eliot curled up in bed reading a book about elemental spells.

"You're still awake?"

Eliot looked up and immediately sat up in his bed, keeping his blankets tucked around him. He smiled sleepily and opened his arms for Quentin to join him. Quentin eased into Eliot's hold with a grin, which disappeared when he realized that Eliot was shuddering.

He laid a head on Quentin's shoulder, nuzzling lazily.

"Q, I feel… really weird."

"Weird? Weird how?" A pang of anxiety rose up in Quentin's stomach. He started to think of all of the possibilities, all the evil magic things that could be making Eliot feel 'weird'.

He hummed under his breath in thought, which morphed into a whimper. "Stomach is hurting like a bitch. I dunno, I just feel shitty. And cold."

Quentin laid a hand on his forehead. "You're not super warm…"

Eliot nuzzled his hand. "It's probably fine."

Probably.

They cuddled for a while, read for a while, before Eliot's book was abandoned on the nightstand and he was curled up. A miserable whine escaped him as he squirmed, both hands pressed into his belly in an attempt to ease the discomfort.

"El?" Quentin cooed, running his fingers through Eliot's hair comfortingly gentle.

Eliot whimpered. "Hate this."

"Was it dinner?"

Eliot stopped, thought about it, and scoffed. "No way. That place wouldn’t betray me like this."

"Oookay…" he had his doubts.

Eliot offered a dramatic ugh before cuddling Quentin a little harder. He was covering himself up, and began shivering more aggressively. He whimpered and nuzzled into Quentin's shoulder as the latter ran his fingers through Eliot's curly hair.

Soon, they were both asleep.

-----

Quentin woke to the sound of the squeaky bathroom door. He blinked at the figure walking toward the bed, but he was completely awake when it all but fell on the bed.

Eliot immediately curled up in a pile of blankets that looked like they'd been abandoned pretty quickly. He seemed to realize that he probably disturbed Quentin, as he looked up guiltily.

His eyes were red-rimmed, face crimson from the strain of vomiting. His nose was running just a little, and he rubbed at his face to clean it up.

"Sorry."

"Time is it?"

"Um… midnight?"

Quentin wasn’t sure what to say. It was the middle of the night, his brain wasn’t working so great. Finally, "Need anything?"

Eliot giggled hoarsely before climbing up and making an attempt to get in bed properly. "A digestive system that doesn’t hate me," He said as Quentin helped him get situated in bed. He nudged Q's hand away when he went to grab the blankets. "No covers."

Quentin  nodded. He noticed the sheen of sweat on Eliot's skin. Whatever chills he was experiencing must have gone away, and been replaced with… well, sweat.

"I should've ordered the same thing so I could suffer with you," Quentin joked, running his fingers through damp hair.

Eliot huffed out a laugh against his pillow. "I don’t think you want to be puking and shitting out your internal organs." his eyes were already falling shut. "But you're right."

Quentin waited until he heard Eliot's breath even out, slow steady breaths. Despite Eliot's earlier protests, he grabbed a quilt from the end of the bed and draped it over him. He'd get cold later.

"Fuck fuck fuck…"

Was what Quentin woke up to next. It still wasn’t morning, he realized with distaste. He groaned and rubbed his eyes before searching for the source of the noise. A weak yet acidy smell hit him and he looked down to find Eliot scrubbing the floor. Quentin was up in a second.

"Fuck. You okay?"

"I'm fantastic, thanks."

"El, hey, let me-"

"No."

"Seriously! You're sick, you don’t need to-"

"No! I-" Eliot sighed, closing his eyes. They were watery and he looked like he'd been holding back tears for a while. The way he moved hinted at the fatigue in his infected body. "You should probably just go to your own room. You… don’t need to deal with this."

Quentin sighed. "Okay, I… I swear you're the most dramatic person ever." He grabbed Eliot's wrists and looked into tired, red-rimmed eyes. "Eliot. Go lie down, okay?"

Eliot gave a stubborn look like he was going to argue, but he just pulled himself up. He removed his shirt, throwing it in the general direction of his hamper, and Quentin only then noticed the patch of vomit down the front.

"I haven't had food poisoning since… ugh, high school."

"You're lucky. My immune system was shot for a while during college, and the smallest weird foods would make me feel like shit."

Suddenly, the door flew open.

"What the fuck are you two doing in here? I'm trying to sleep!" She looked down at where Quentin was, cleaning up the last bit of mess, and then at Eliot who was snuggling a pillow to his abdomen and looked like death. "What…"

"Eliot's sick," Quentin offered.

Margo's face softened as she rushed to her friend. "Poor thing, was it that place I told you not to go to?"

Quentin chuckled, and could practically hear Eliot's piercing expression at her.

She looked down at Quentin. "And you're…"

"Just cleaning."

Margo rolled her eyes, and with a flick of her wrist, the floor was clean and dry like nothing had happened. Quentin blinked, and the groaned in frustration at himself. He should have done that a long time ago.

"I had to master it when Eliot became a raging alcoholic."

"Now, that's just insulting." Argued a tired Eliot. "I can hold my liquor just fine."

"The stain on my satin dress says otherwise, honey."

A soft whine came from Eliot, who just nuzzled her and cuddled harder.

Quentin chuckled, taking in the scene. Two of his best friends, or his boyfriend and girlfriend, together. Eliot had started with resting against her shoulder and was now using her stomach as a pillow, looking exceptionally comfortable. Margo ran her fingers through his mess of hair, other hand held in Eliot's like he was telling her not to leave.

Quentin loved times like this. Maybe not when Eliot was violently ill, but everything else was perfect.
