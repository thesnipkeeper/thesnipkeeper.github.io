Title: Professional
Fandom: Schitt's Creek RPF
Summary: Dan's just trying to be responsible, damn it
Word count: 1.2k

Professional

Dan tries to ignore the persistent gurgling in his gut. He’s not hungry, not even close. In fact, he hasn’t eaten all day, because he’s afraid that if he does...

He should have seen this coming. He passed out the minute his head hit the pillow last night, and he thought it was because he’d been working too hard. He decided that the headache he’d been suffering was just from stress.

But a nagging worry in the back of his mind is telling him that he’s probably getting sick. He’s been hearing a few people talk about a stomach bug going around, and he hasn’t thought about it until now. He’s usually so careful!

A cup of coffee sits in front of him, still hot and steaming and what he would crave on any other day. He has no intention on drinking it, he just wanted to buy something to be polite while he was having a meeting here. The very idea of drinking coffee right now begins to turn the unsettled feeling into full-blown nausea, and-

Dan shakes his head. Don’t think too much about it, he decides.

So fine, he’s probably coming down with something. That just means that he has to hold out until this meeting is over, and then he can go home and... deal with this.

“Hey!”

Dan finds himself smiling for the first time that day at the sight of his friend. Noah’s bundled up in a coat and scarf, face red from the cold, with one of those adorable smiles that often has Dan weak at the knees.

“Hi,” Dan greets happily. “Find the place okay?”

“Being totally honest? I used google maps. Your directions were uh, confusing.”

Dan snorts. “Sorry, I’ve been a little out of it today.”

“But this is nice!” He gestures to the cafe, and sounds genuine enough.

“Yeah, I love it here. I come here to do work sometimes.”

Noah unzips his coat and drapes it on the back of the chair opposite of Dan. “I’m gonna get a coffee. You want a scone or anything?”

Oh God no, Dan thinks. He forces a smile and shakes his head, “I’m fine.”

He takes the few minutes Noah spends in line and ordering to gather his bearings. They’re here to talk about schedules and other work-related things. (and to catch up, because they’ve missed each other and neither of them are willing to admit it). He needs to be awake and somewhat professional. He only has to last through the meeting, and then he can spend the rest of the day wallowing in his own misery.

Noah returns with his coffee and cute smile. Dan realizes that he’s looking at him expectantly.

“What?”

A chuckle. “You weren’t kidding when you said you were out of it, huh? I asked if you were going to drink your coffee.”

“Oh,” Dan laughs awkwardly and nudges the cup with hid finger. “I changed my mind.” it’s a lame excuse, and he doesn’t blame Noah for the strange look he’s giving him.

“Are you okay? You seem pretty tired.”

Dan’s stomach cramps horribly for a few seconds and he tries not to wince. “I’m- I’m good.” he sighs. “Just didn’t get a lot of sleep last night.” It’s a lie. He got plenty of sleep last night. But whatever virus is developing in his system has suck every bit of energy he has. It’s not even noon.

Noah makes a sympathetic sound before taking a sip of his coffee. “I guess I know how that is.”

The small talk fades to what they’re here to discuss in the first place. Dan struggles to keep himself focused on the work at hand and not the worsening ache in his belly. He finds himself constantly staring off while Noah is talking.

Another cramp makes itself known, and Dan doesn’t realize he’s made a noise in response until he sees Noah looking worriedly at him. Dan shifts, hoping to ease the pressure in his stomach.

“Are you sure you’re okay?” Noah asks, setting down the folder they were looking through together.

“I-” He can’t exactly lie now. It’s obvious that something is wrong and Noah wouldn’t believe him for a second if he tried to come up with another excuse. And his stomach is killing him. “I think I’m coming down with something,” he sighs out. “My stomach’s been bothering me all morning.”

Noah gasps. “Dan, you should have told me. We didn’t have to do this today!”

“I thought I would be okay, but...” Another, more insistent cramp causes nausea to spike for a quick moment. Dan swallows hard. “Yeah, maybe we should finish this later. Sorry for dragging you out here.”

He needs to get out of here, fast. Fuuuccck. He thought he had a little more time before he actually threw up.

“Hey,” Noah gently grasps his arm as he stands up. “Do you need a ride home?”

Dan shook his head quickly. “I think I- oh God...” he whimpers quietly, determined not to get anyone else’s attention.

Noah seems to get the idea, and his face turns soft. “Okay, okay... bathroom? I’ll get your stuff together.”

“Thank you,” he breathes out.

-----

When Dan emerges from the bathroom, Noah is sitting at the table with his coat on and Dan’s bag hanging off of his shoulder. His gaze finds Dan’s and he immediately goes to him.

Dan feels shaky and washed out and... gross. After ten minutes of throwing up, he wants nothing more than to go home.

“Hi,” Noah says so sweetly. “How you feeling?”

“Horrible,” Dan whispers, smiling so no one knows his situation.

Noah rubs his back. “Let’s go.”

-----

“I was trying to be professional at our meeting today,” Dan laments. He slumps against the tub and huffs. He’s since shed his sweater and glasses.

Noah has the audacity to chuckle. “You were doing great until you started puking.”

He gets Dan comfortable on the couch and encourages him to sip some gatorade so sweetly it's like he hasn't just witnessed him throwing up a disgusting amount.

He shutters, which seems to urge Noah to feel his forehead. He makes a dissatisfied noise.

"You're running a fever."

"Oh God," Dan laments. "This is the last thing I need."

Noah lays a hand on his arm and rubs gently. "You'll be fine, Dan. I'm here, for one thing!" He's joking, but Dan has to admit...

He's right. Things are much less terrible with Noah around

 He can't help but feel incredibly thankful nto have a friend like him.
