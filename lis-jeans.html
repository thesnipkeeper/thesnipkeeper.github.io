Title: Jeans
Fandom: Life is Strange
Summary: Max is sick and cuddly
Word count: 1.6k

Jeans

The sound of forks hitting plates and coffee pouring into mugs calmed Max more than anything else. She watched the steam rise from her own fresh cup of coffee, and she couldn’t help but capture it on her camera. Satisfied with her photo, she took a cautionary sip of the precious liquid and sighed.

Max wanted this moment to last forever.

"Even brought your camera to the diner. Figures."

There was no sight weirder than Chloe in a waitress uniform. Even with the makeup and blue hair, she looked like Joyce, standing there, coffee pot in-hand.

"I'm always prepared!"

Chloe scoffed, plopping into the booth and lazily topping off Max's coffee to the brim.

Max had been there every Sunday morning since Chloe started work at the diner. She was really getting used to the unlimited coffee refills, the bacon, the pancakes… oh, and seeing Chloe, of course. That was always nice.

Sunday was Max's favorite day.

But Sundays couldn’t last forever. Max had been tired since she woke up, and just feeling generally... off. She didn’t even indulge in the blessed bacon today, instead ordering toast with a couple of those little jelly packets. She hoped the coffee would help.

"I have to go, I'm studying with Alyssa soon," Max informed sadly.

Chloe blinked. "Seriously? You're ditching me for school?"

"Yup!" She went to stand up, and nearly stumbled back into the booth before grabbing onto the back to regain her balance.

Max thought she just needed some caffeine and breakfast in her system, but now she wasn't sure.

"Oh shit," Chloe shot to her feet "Uh, Max, you don't look so hot."

Max blinked blearily. "I don't feel so hot," she admitted, letting Chloe push her back into the seat. Chloe's hand was on her forehead before she could even try to pull away.

"Holy fuck, you are burning up, though..." Max wasn't sure how she felt when Chloe's voice got all serious like that. On the one hand, it was weird to hear her tone anything but the douchiness that it usually harbored. On the other hand, serious Chloe was so much more sweet and sincere.

If she really did have a fever, then Max could no longer dismiss her headache as caffeine withdrawal.

"Are you gonna be okay heading back to Blackwell?"

Max nodded, even though she felt like death. It was just a short bus ride back, she could handle that.

"Yeah, I'm good. I'll text you later, okay?"

Chloe still had worry painted all over her face, but she pushed it away as she said, "You'd fuckin' better."

Standing up and walking really made her realize how tired she was. It was like wading in a pool, a pool full of molasses, that is. Her limbs felt heavy, her brain was working at half speed. She hopped onto the bus and found herself pulling her jacket tighter as a wave of chills hit her. 

This was weird, Max decided. This was really weird. 

The bus ride was beyond unpleasant. All of the bumps and turns just made her feel worse, and the person sitting in front of her of course decided to open their window.

When she stepped out, she looked longingly at the school and wished she could somehow teleport so she wouldn't have to drag herself to the library.

But, unfortunately things didn't work like that.

-----

Alyssa practically bullied her back to the dorms the moment she started coughing.

Max had a feeling that what she was coming down with wasn’t going to be pleasant at all.

“I might grab something from the vending machines,” Alyssa said as Max sat on her bed. She insisted on walking her back, and Max was too tired to refuse (and, maybe it was a good idea). “Do you want me to get you anything?”

“Not unless the vending machines have twelve-ounce bottles of cold medicine,” Max said with a chuckle, that just turned into a cough at the end.

Alyssa laughed. “Well, take care of yourself, okay? I heard that there’s some kind of flu going around. A few people have had to go to the infirmary.”

“Whoa, really?”

She nodded, eyebrows turning up in concern. “So, be fucking careful, okay?”

They said their goodbyes, and Max started pulling off her shoes and jacket the moment Alyssa was gone. She got comfortable in bed, or as comfortable as one could be in bed with jeans on. She definitely didn’t have the energy to change clothes, the jeans would do for now.

Max was still freezing. Now at least she knew that it was chills and that her room wasn’t actually a million degrees below zero. She probably had ibuprofen around there somewhere, but she wasn’t sure that she’d be able to get out of bed now.

She didn’t think about it for too long before she fell into an uneasy sleep.

-----

“Max?”

Max groaned. Whoever was in her room could find someone else to bug. She didn’t feel like helping anyone with their project or... or socializing in any capacity.

“Mad Max, wake up, it’s me.”

Oh, wait.

Despite how much she desperately wanted to stay asleep, Max forced her eyes open. She smiled at the sight of her friend, still in her diner uniform.

Chloe chuckled and plopped onto the bed. “Morning sunshine.”

“What’re you doing here?” Max dragged a hand down her face. She wasn’t shivering anymore, instead she felt gross and hot all over.

“Just here to make sure that you don’t die on me, hippie.” Chloe automatically started helping her escape from the blanket cage that she’d created for herself, before pulling a few stray pieces of hair out of Max’s face. “You okay?” she asked, voice gentle.

Anyone else and she might have put on a brave face and told them it was nothing, but it wasn’t anyone else. It was Chloe. Max shook her head.

“Well, I’m about to change that. I told mom you were getting sick and she sent me with food. She didn’t even tell me I couldn’t sneak in.”

“If anyone catches you here, you’re going to be busted,” Max rasped.

“If I get caught. Look...” she took a paper bag that seemingly came out of nowhere (or maybe Max just didn’t notice it until now). “In here we’ve got soup, waffles, and I did you the favor or bringing meds. The good kind that they’re not gonna give you here.”

“Thanks, Chloe.” Max curled up. “But I’m not very hungry right now.”

“But you have a fridge!”

Max chuckled sleepily. “That I do.”

Chloe did manage to put all the food in the fridge for later, after rearranging some things and judging Max’s choice of soda.

“So do you want me to fuck off and leave you to sleep?”

Realistically, if Max did have the flu, she should probably be telling everyone to fuck off. But her loneliness and misery definitely won, in the end.

“Can you stay? Just... just for a little while.”

Chloe chuckled. “Yeah, sure.” She kicked her shoes off and climbed onto the bed, immediately getting comfortable.

Max snuggled up against her, and soon had to turn her face into her pillow to muffle a cough.

Chloe made a noise in the back of her throat that sounded suspiciously like concern. "You’re really not feeling good, huh?”

She could feel her whole respiratory system getting more gunked up by the second. It was only a matter of time before she stopped being able to breathe through her nose. Until then, she had the aches and pains in all of her muscles to worry about, and the fact that she probably couldn’t get out of bed without blacking out, and...

“Hm’m,” Max confirmed.

“Are we going to talk about why you’re still in your clothes?”

“Lot of work to take off.”

Chloe scoffed. “Fair enough, I guess.”

They were both comfortable, nearly asleep, when Max coughed again, this time it turned into a fit that she couldn’t stop, gasping for breath and trying to clear the tickle from her throat. Chloe swore and helped her sit up, still holding her.

“Sit tight,” she said, getting up. She grabbed a bottle of water off of Max’s desk. It’d been sitting there for a couple days, and definitely tasted like plastic, but Max needed something to make her throat less scratchy, so she drank it.

Chloe sat on the edge of the bed as Max sipped the water, still coughing every once in a while.

“God, Max, you can’t do anything halfway, can you?”

Max huffed out a small laugh. She didn’t really feel like talking anymore. Or... being awake. It was just nice having Chloe around.

“Hey so, I have a good idea. Why don’t you get fucked up with this stuff?” Chloe snorted, handing her the bottle of medicine. She was right. It was the strong stuff. “Just don’t take too much or I’ll be responsible.”

Max raised an eyebrow.

“Just kidding, the more you take the better you’ll feel.”

Of course Chloe would have that outlook on drugs. But Max did indeed intend on taking enough to make her forget about how bad she felt.

Chloe helped her measure out a generous dose, and laughed when she struggled to swallow it all without gagging.

They talked for a bit, Chloe more than Max, obviously, until the medicine started to kick in.

She slid back down under the covers. She was still wearing her jeans, but that would be fine until she had the energy or motivation to deal with it.

Chloe chuckled fondly and got back to cuddling her.

She felt a kiss pressed to her forehead as she drifted into a peaceful sleep.
