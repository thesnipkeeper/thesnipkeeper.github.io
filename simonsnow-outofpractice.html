Out of Practice

They weren’t used to this, they weren’t good at this.

Baz had never been in a real relationship, and honestly, neither had Simon. Not really.

They didn’t know what it was supposed to be like, what they were supposed to do with each other now that they weren’t saving the world and/or trying to kill each other.

They’d lived together their entire lives, but never as a couple.

Their relationship was in an odd place that neither of them could define. They were afraid to cross boundaries that shouldn't have been there in the first place.

... right? Those boundaries... Baz never saw them in other people’s relationships.

Nothing had happened, but that was just the thing, wasn't it? Nothing. Nothing bad, nothing good. They were just... together. Stuck?

All Baz knew was that his boyfriend was depressed, and he was absolutely no help. most useless boyfriend in the world. He had no idea what the hell he was doing, and that became abundantly clear the morning that Simon wouldn’t get out of bed.

Was he getting worse? Was this how things were going to be?

Baz leaned against the doorway of the other bedroom and observed the lump of tangled blankets in the middle of Snow’s bed, leathery red wings lying limp to the side.

“Missed breakfast,” he said. “Bunce was here for some reason, she ate your share of the eggs.”

“Mm.”

Baz did his best to swallow back his worry. Simon had had bad days, plenty of them, but this was the first time that he refused to get out of bed. The least he was usually capable of doing was curling up on the couch like a tragedy.

“It’s nearly noon. Do you plan on joining me for lunch, at least?” He had no idea what his lunch plans were, but Baz was willing to say anything just to see that Simon was still sort of alive.

“Baz,” it was barely audible under the blankets. “Stop.”

Baz scoffed. “Stop? Stop what?” He walked in further, taking a seat on the edge of the bed, the side Simon had his back turned to. He nudged one of the wings out of the way as he sat, only to be met with a whine of protest. “Snow, get up.”

“Stop,” Snow muttered, must enunciated this time. “Please... just... leave me alone for a few minutes.”

Baz frowned. He tried to brush away the immense wave of hurt that hit him. So Simon just didn’t want him around anymore? Was that it?

He stood slowly, wordlessly, and began to leave.

The sudden whimper from behind had him turning around, only to see Simon curling into a ball. That sound he was making was pain. Baz had known him long enough to identify it.

“Simon?”

Before he knew it, Simon was out of bed with a hand over his mouth and shoving past Baz to the bathroom.

Baz winced at the sound of gagging, and then liquid hitting liquid.

He only felt a little guilty for the relief that Simon wasn’t getting more depressed, or angry with Baz, just sick. But the relief only lasted for a moment before he realized that he had no idea how to care for a sick boyfriend.

He’d dealt with sick Simon when the two of them shared a room and hated each other. He ignored it and tried to pretend like he didn’t care.

This, now, was his responsibility.

He stepped carefully into the bathroom and knelt down next to where Simon was leaned over the toilet. He was still heaving rather uselessly, hardly able to get a breath in.

Baz laid a hand on his back, finding him to be burning up beneath the fabric of his shirt. Snow tensed, and made another whining sound.

“D- don’t touch me,” he said breathlessly, moving away from Baz’s hand.

Baz pulled back, and moved away a bit. He was... he was being smothering, wasn’t he? God, he was terrible at this. “Sorry.”

Simon sniffed, grabbed a handle of toilet tissue to wipe his face, and flushed his stomach contents away. He leaned back against the wall, face red, as he finally caught his breath.

“Do you-?” Baz cleared his throat. “Do you want me to leave, or...?”

Simon shook his head.

“Alright.” Didn’t want to be alone, but didn’t want to be touched. Baz could work with that.

Simon huffed, curling in on himself with his arms wrapped around his stomach. “Hurts...”

“What hurts, Snow?”

“... Everything,” he said, the smallest smile on his face as Baz rolled his eyes at his dramatics.

“It occurs to me now that waking you up by talking about food probably didn’t do any good.”

Simon winced. “No, it didn’t.”

-----

Simon made an urgent sound in the back of his throat right before he leaned over the edge of the couch and vomited into the bucket. A bucket that he swore he wouldn’t need, Baz might add.

Baz swore. “You’ve really caught something.”

“It’s not fair,” Simon rasped, before spitting.

‘What isn’t fair?”

“You can’t get sick. I have to suffer alone.”

Baz couldn’t help but scoff. “Sorry, you’re saying that you’d rather I be dragged down with you?”

Simon gave a sleepy smile. “Maybe.” He moved back - slowly, like he was afraid of what his stomach was going to do if he moved too suddenly. “One of the many reasons I knew you were a vampire.”

Baz raised an eyebrow. “That’s ridiculous.”

“Is it? We lived together all that time and you never got sick. Remember that stomach flu outbreak? You were literally the only one left standing.”

Baz couldn’t forget that winter. The entire school - even the faculty - was completely out of commission. They initiated a lock down of all of the facilities just to sanitize the place. Which meant, of course, that Baz was stuck dragging Snow to the bathroom before he made a mess of the rug in their room.

Had he been human, he most definitely would have gotten sick.

“Alright, well now you know.” He grabbed the bucket, careful not to look at the contents. He might be immune to illness, but that didn’t mean his gag reflex was missing. “I’m a vampire.”

The joke was lost on Simon, he was too busy frowning and going wide-eyed. “Baz, I can-” He sat up quicker than he should have.

“Ah ah,” Baz set a hand on his chest and gently pushed him back against the arm of the sofa. “I can do it.”

He got the bucket cleaned out and sanitized. He still needed to text Bunce and let her know that she may begin projectile vomiting in the near future, but for now his boyfriend was a ball of misery and wings on the couch.

What now?

“Should I be making you drink water or something?”

Simon chuckled, shrugged. “No idea.”

“Well,” Baz sat on the edge of the couch by his legs. “I suppose we’ll figure it out.”
