Title: What's a CVS
Fandom: Sleep Hollow
Summary: Abbie teaches Crane how to have the flu in the 21st century
Word count: 2.3k

What's a CVS

When an officer is sick and doesn’t want to take off work, that isn’t a problem. There are times when the precinct needs all hands on deck, times when a simple cold doesn’t seem like a big deal.

Cops work through a lot of pain and discomfort.

And as Abbie watches her partner cough up a lung in the passenger seat of her car outside of a witness’ home, she realizes one thing.

Ichabod isn’t a cop.

He mentioned getting a cold that morning when Abbie picked him up at the cabin. Ichabod didn’t look... great, but Abbie didn’t think anything of it. In retrospect, she should have seen how tired he was, how he didn’t bother complaining about the prices when they stopped for coffee, how he ordered tea instead of coffee.

At the time it just seemed like a normal case of the sniffles. He certainly wasn’t making a big deal about it.

The hacking gets persistent enough that it sounds like he’s having trouble getting a breath in. Abbie sighs sadly and reaches to rub his back as he convulses with each irritated cough.

“Take it easy, Crane,” she says softly.

He nods, looks up. His eyes are watery from the force of the fit, and there’s a glint of moisture on the edge of his nostrils as he hasn’t been able to tend to it.

“My apologies, leftenant.” She winces at the hoarse edge of his voice that hadn’t been there before. A hand goes to his chest, patting it as if it would help. “I don’t know where that came from.”

Abbie frowns. She wasn’t against bringing him to question their suspect’s family, he’s been a big help on the case. But now she’s wondering if her judgment was a bit skewed by the ‘suck it up’ mentality she’s lived with for so long.

Her judgment was definitely skewed.

He’s still clearing his throat and getting his breath back. His nose is nearing ‘drippy’ territory and she feels the need to bring it to his attention. As miserable as he must feel, she doesn’t want snot getting on her car, nor does he want it on his clothes.

“Might wanna wipe your nose,” Abbie offers.

His eyes widen momentarily before returning to hooded exhaustion. “Oh...oh dear...” Ichabod gives a wet sniffle and fumbles for his handkerchief (which must be a little worse for wear by now). He presses it to the underside of his nose and sighs. Abbie can tell he’s already done with this cold and he hasn’t even had it for a full day. “Apologies twice over.”

Abbie chuckles, pulling her keys from the ignition. “Listen, why don’t you stay in here while I go talk to them, huh? Give you a moment to rest.”

“Rest,” Ichabod repeats with a huff. “No, I’m alright, thank you. Allow me a moment to gather my bearings and I’ll accompany you.” His sentence, of course, is punctuated with a sneeze. By the groan that follows, it hurts his throat and causes a whole new set of problems from beneath that handkerchief.

“I’m good.” She pats his shoulder and gets out of the car. “I’ll be back soon.”

Ichabod simply nods and continues tending to his nose, muffling a couple of itchy coughs into the fabric as well.

Abbie tries not to feel guilty for bringing him out here. He’s a grown man, he said he would come with her.

She shakes her head and knocks on the door of the little house.

-----

When Abbie returns to her car, she finds the both adorable and pathetic sight of her partner fast asleep.

His coat is draped over him, Abbie sees a red tint on his cheeks. It could be her imagination, but his nose looks to have taken on a tender pink tone since she’d gone inside the house just a half hour ago, like he’s been rubbing it raw out of frustration.

She puts her key in the ignition and is about to turn the car on when she hears a shuddery breath from the sick man beside her. Ichabod is shivering.

Abbie frowns and finds herself laying the back of her hand against his temple. His eyes flutter open, making a congested questioning sound.

“Fever?” he asks, voice rough from sleep.

“Yup,” she sighs out. “Seems that way.”

His eyebrows knit together as he sits up, moving to adjust himself for the ride home. Ichabod drags a tired hand down his face with a harsh sniff. “How did it go?”

Abbie hums negatively. “They haven’t talked to him in years. I don’t think they even knew he was in town.”

“Another dead end, then.”

“Yup.”

Ichabod coughs, not quite quick enough to cover it. He finds his handkerchief from the pile of coat on his lap and presses it to his mouth to muffle a few more.

“Jesus, you really don’t sound good.”

“Well, if it helps, leftenant, I feel just as well as I sound.”

Abbie scoffs. “We’ll stop at CVS and get you some stuff before I take you home.”

“Home? Leftenant, I don’t know what a ‘CVS’ is, but we don’t need to go there, nor my home. Have you forgotten that we have a murder to solve?”

“You’re not gonna be any help with whatever plague you’ve got, Crane. It’ll be easier if you just try to get better without working.”

At his silence, she glanced over at him and found him staring with wide eyes.

“Plague, you say?”

“Not- that was a joke. There’s no plague.” Usually he’s sharper than this, she thinks fondly, but this cold seems to be gumming up his sarcasm detector.

He just nods and goes back to leaning against the window.

“And CVS is the place where we’re going to be buying you some medicine. And uh, tissues.”

“Leftenant,” Ichabod says. “That’s not necessary. I appreciate it, I do, but I’m capable of working just fine.”

“Big talk coming from a man who’s shivering under his coat and slurring words like he’s drunk.”

The corner of his mouth quirks up in a small smile. “I suppose you’re right. Still, I’d like to help.”

Abbie sighs heavily. “We’ll go over the files at your place, then.”

Ichabod makes an approving sound.

“After we stop at CVS.”

A groan.

-----

Abbie fills a basket with cough drops, cold medicine, tissues, and a thermometer.

She grabs a bottle of water from the cooler as she stands in line. Crane will gripe, but he'll be thankful to have something to drink before they reach the cabin.

"Looks like someone's not feeling good," is the first thing the cashier says as she starts scanning each item.

Abbie nods. "Yeah, my friend is coming down with something."

"Oh, that's too bad. I know the flu virus is pretty rough this year."

Abbie swears inwardly. She hadn't even considered the flu, but now that she thinks about it she remembers how many people at the precinct have called in sick. It's not like Ichabod has developed an immunity to any strain.

She smiles and takes one plastic bag in each hand. She should have caught on earlier, left him at home to rest the minute he said that he wasn't feeling a hundred percent.

She returns to the car. Ichabod is shivering, looking like he's trying desperately to go back to sleep.

He's feeling worse by the minute, it seems. The sooner she gets him home the better. Or, maybe, his ability to hide how he's feeling is getting worse by the minute.

Either way, he digs through the bags and asks questions about each item. The curious tone has taken a tired edge, as he asks whether the medicine will really help. It's hopeful, sort of makes Abbie realize how awful he really feels to be so willing to just take modern medicine as long as he won't feel like this.

"You'll either feel better or be too high to notice you're sick," she says with a chuckle.

"Ah, well that sounds pleasant."

"Oh, it is."

-----

Ichabod is completely out of it by the time they make it to the cabin. He’s sleeping, or trying to, and Abbie has to shake him awake. She can feel his body heat seeping through the fabric of his shirt.

He wakes with a snort.

“Hey, Crane, we’re here.”

Ichabod just nods and clumsily unbuckles his seat belt. He wanders in, not unlike a zombie, and allows Abbie to steer him toward the couch without argument.

“I feel a little... light headed.”

“Yeah.” Abbie sets the bags on the coffee table and begins pulling things out. She starts with the thermometer. “I think you’ve got the flu.”

Crane clears his throat, wincing in pain. His brows knit together in confusion. “Sorry, the ‘flu’?”

“Influenza. It’ll make you wish you were dead for a little while, but you’ll be fine,” she mumbles, reading the instructions on the package.

“So that’s what you call it. Things like this were quite deadly in my day.”

“In your day people still thought bleeding was an effective treatment.”

Ichabod chuckles, which tapers off into a weak cough. “Yes, well...what’s this?” he inspects the thing handed to him.

“So that’s a thermometer, and it’s gonna tell me how bad your fever is.”

Ichabod looks at her worriedly. “Leftenant, am I in danger?”

This must be scary for him. Being properly sick for the first time since he woke up, being told that he has something that killed regularly in his era. All the attention seems to be freaking him out instead of making him feel better.

“No,” Abbie says. “God, no. You’ll be fine, Crane. It’s just helpful to know what your body temperature is at. Helps tell you how sick you are.”

He looks unsure, still twisting the device around in his hand.

“Everyone gets the flu. Half the precinct has it, so I’ll probably be in the same shape as you in a few days.”

“I truly hope not,” Ichabod sighs out. “This is most unpleasant. How do I...?”

“Press the button right there, and then keep it under your tongue until it beeps.”

Already having gotten the hang of using modern technology, Crane follows her instructions easily enough. She has to admit that seeing the large, often pretentious guy huddled under a blanket with a thermometer in his mouth is a comical (adorable) sight. She bites back a smile.

It beeps and he pulls it out slowly, squinting at the screen. “...102.9?”

Abbie looks over his shoulder and nods before taking it and setting it on the coffee table with the rest of the stuff. “Yeah, this is definitely the flu.”

-----

Crane holds the file with one hand while the other has his kerchief (“use the damn tissues””I refuse.”) in the other, perpetually by his face in case of any unfortunate coughs or sneezes.

He’s shivering, she can see it, but she hopes the medicine will help.

Crane has a thing that he does when he’s really tired. He’ll stare off into space for a long while, like his brain can’t focus anymore. She sees it now, but worsened by the fever.

“Hey,” Abbie says, setting a hand on his shoulder to get his attention. “Why don’t you go to bed? I can finish going through these.”

Crane shakes his head and snuffles. “I’ll be alright for a little while longer. I just... need a moment.”

“Crane, you look like shit.”

“Mmm,” he hums in agreement.

“Come on,” Abbie stands up and tugs on his arm until he does as well. He looks dizzy, and she really hopes that he doesn’t lose his balance because she definitely wouldn’t be able to catch him. “You look like you’re about to pass out.”

Ichabod makes another agreeing noise, but he stops on their way to his bedroom. “Leftenant... I don’t want to leave you with all of the work.”

“It’s my job,” she assures.

She manages to get him into bed. Abbie makes sure that everything is on the nightstand and within his reach.

He nods appreciatively, looking as comfortable as he can be while being miserably sick.

“Alright, feel better, Crane. I’m gonna finish going through those files.”

“Leftenant-!”

Abbie stops on her way out, and turns around to see him looking hesitant.

“I...” He sighs, closing his eyes for a moment. “I wouldn’t mind if you wanted to... stay.”

“In here?”

Ichabod nods.

Abbie’s face lights up in realization. “You don’t want to be alone.” It’s not a question. It’d explain why he’s stayed with her all day without complaining about how he was feeling.

“I really do not,” he breathes out. “This is... I’m a bit nervous about this. Of course, I don’t expect you to-”

Abbie smiles. “I’ll grab my work and meet you back in here.”

He seems to let out a sigh of relief at not having to admit that he’s scared. Abbie gathers all of the files and anything else she’ll need and returns to his room.

“Scooch,” she says.

Ichabod chooses one side of the bed and moves over. Abbie gets comfortable, careful not to sit on top of the blankets he’s huddled in. She sits cross-legged and sets the stack of files on the spot in front of her.

Ichabod smiles, ridiculously adorable and sleepy. “I appreciate this, leftenant.”

Abbie chuckles. “No problem. Get some sleep, alright? You’re gonna be fine, I promise.”

He’s snoring within minutes.

A couple hours later he wakes himself with a coughing fit and Abbie helps him take another dose of medicine. He’s too miserable to go right back to sleep as he waits for the new dose to kick in. They chat for a while.

Things are nice. And Abbie only hopes she remembers how nice things are when she wakes up with the same flu the next morning.
