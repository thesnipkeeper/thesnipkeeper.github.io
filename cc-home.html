Title: Home
Fandom: Camp Camp
Summary: David and Max realize that they're a family
Word count: 3k

Home

"This kid at school threw up everywhere." Max laughed. "It was disgusting. We had to have class in another room just so they could clean it."

Max always had fun stories from things that happened at school. Some of them because of his shenanigans, some of them not. David didn't think anything of it.

David helped Max do his homework and talked to him more about school as he made dinner. "Peas or carrots for our vegetable?"

Max scoffed. "Neither."

David turned around, a can in each hand. "Peas," he tried to sound stern, "or carrots." Max just laughed at his attempt.

"Carrots, I guess. They're not as weird."

"Weird? There's nothing wrong with peas! They're good for you!"

He could hear Max roll his eyes. "Whatever you say, camp man."

They'd seemed to have gotten comfortable with each other since Max moved in with him months ago. His parents hadn't put up much of a fight, even more of a reason for David to take him home. He worked so much with kids every summer for years that David was confident in having a kid. Especially Max. He knew how Max worked, and vice versa, though they never mentioned it.

David plated both their food. Max would just eat the chicken and rice if he let him scoop his own. He'd leave the poor carrots to fend for themselves. (Max gave him a look when he said this.)

"It sounds like Nikki and Neil are coming back this summer!" David said between bites of chicken. "Are you excited?"

"Fuck yeah. We're gonna fuck shit up."

"Max, language!"

Max stared at him challengingly for a few seconds before huffing. "Fine, whatever. It's gonna be fun, okay?"

He loved seeing Max excited. Excited for something besides weekends and pizza day at school. He spent so much time watching the kid hate everything and be upset.

David didn't even need to ask Max to help with dishes. He wordlessly pulled a chair up to the counter and hopped up with the drying cloth in his hands. It would never happen again if he told Max how proud he was.

Later that night they watched TV, after dinner when things were starting to get quiet and wind down. It was a good time. Max spent most of it flipping through channels while David read from a book about the trees in their area.

Finally, Max seemed to have gotten tired of using the remote and stopped at a random cartoon. He curled up on his end of the sofa, resting his head on the arm.

David heard him make a noise, though he wasn't sure what kind. A grunt, maybe?

"What was that?"

"Hm?" Max lifted his head, looking as if David was disturbing his nap. "What was what?"

"Oh, I thought you might have said something! Oops."

Max huffed out a half-hearted laugh and laid back down. He was often tired on school days. They seemed to drain him completely, especially today.

It was nine at night when it happened. David was thinking about going to bed; he had to wake up early to finish his work and make Max's lunch.

"Dav... David..." David thought Max was asleep until now. He slowly sat up and turned to him, an uncharacteristically worried expression painted on his face. "I don't..."

Max cut himself off abruptly to clamp a hand over his mouth. His eyes widened in panic. Like most kids, he didn't know what was happening or what to do.

David did. He shot up off of the couch and scooped Max up. The bathroom was the first door in the hallway, they could make it.

They didn't. Max made a strangled cough behind his hand, and David barely had time to put him down before vomit was spraying through his fingers and onto the floor. He panted, a few seconds before his breathe caught again.

David winced as a fresh wave of vomit splashed against the floor. There was tonight's undigested dinner.

Max coughed, gagged and heaved a few more times before he was able to bring up something less substantial.

David rubbed his back. He could feel what might have been the beginnings of a fever.

"Do you feel like you're done, buddy?"

Max took a few seconds before he nodded weakly. "...sorry," he croaked. "The floor."

"That's what linoleum is for, isn't it? I'll have this cleaned up in no time!"

"I... Oh fuck..." Max was overwhelmed. He looked down at his hand, and then his shirt, both covered in puke. He didn't know what to do with himself.

"Why don't we get you cleaned up first?"

He helped Max wash his hands, and then remove his shirt without getting anything in his hair. Then, David deposited him on his bed with pajamas and a clean shirt. He told Max he could get dressed while he cleaned up the hallway, and Max tiredly agreed.

Max emerged from his bedroom clad in his PJs. At first David thought he was wanting to talk to him, but he watched as Max rushed into the bathroom, one hand over his mouth and the other over his stomach.

David finished cleaning up, during which he had to listen to the kid throw up more of his dinner. Finally, he was there.

"Done?" David asked, brushing sweaty bangs away from his face.

Max shook his head. "Stomach still hurts..." Then a mumbled "fuck" under his breath.

So, David pulled Max up, careful not to jostle him too much, and sat him on the edge of the tub. Max didn't see how that was any better at first, but then David plopped down next to him.

Max leaned into his side and David wrapped an arm around him.

"This sucks," he muffled into David's shirt.

This was among one of the first times Max wasn't reluctant to show some kind affection. He only let himself do that stuff when he wasn't feeling himself. Sometimes if he had a bad day at school, or was thinking too much, he would find a way to stay in the same room as his new dad. David wondered if they were really getting to be family.

He stroked Max's arm comfortingly. Max squirmed, whimpering and whining as he wrapped an arm around his stomach.

He seemed to have fallen asleep against David until he suddenly made a panicked noise and sat up. David helped him get over the toilet in time, and rubbed Max's back as he threw up.

David pressed the back of his hand to Max's temple. Max didn't even complain.

David hummed thoughtfully. "Feels like you've got a fever."

"No shit," came the choked reply.

David spent the next day tending to his sick kid. Max finally started to keep food down by evening time, though he was still nauseated and threw up when he overdid it.

The day after that, Max's fever was gone and he started to eat without David having to beg him. Still, he was obviously not feeling a hundred percent. He stayed glued to David's side as the counselor worked on writing, and fell asleep at some point.

David was snapped out of his focus when his phone vibrated.

"Gwen!" Max stirred, and David had to remind himself to be quiet. "Hi!" he said, a little more hushed but not any less excited.

"Hey, I'm in town. Wanna hang before I leave? I wanna see you guys."

David loved hearing it. He loved that Gwen still thought about him when they weren't at camp. They hadn't seen each other since David took Max in. David frowned, looking down at the small body curled up against him. Max still looked sick, and he'd thrown up once that morning. Not to mention he himself was tired from taking care of a sick kid for the past two days.

"Probably shouldn't. Max isn't feeling good and I'd hate for you to catch something."

"What can I bring?"

David tried not to smile, or get excited to see her. He couldn't, Max was still sick.

"Really, Gwen. You don't have to."

"You don't want me to come over?"

Oh man, she was good.

"Of course I do! But-"

The kid stirred again before sitting up and rubbing sleep from his eyes. "Gwen coming over?"

David pulled the phone away. "Not today, kiddo."

"Why not?"

There was a chuckle on the other end. "Sounds like its two against one. What do you guys want for dinner?"

The phone was snatched out of David's hand before he could answer. "Coffee," Max said into the phone. David rolled his eyes, took the phone back, and turned on speakerphone.

Gwen was laughing. "Nice try, twerp. How about soup and sandwiches? I'm by the deli."

Max thought for a moment. "That works."

-----

The three found themselves eating at the table. Max was still in PJs, legs happily swinging from the chair as he ate his sandwich. He ate like he was starving. David nibbled on his own sandwich, but breakfast seemed to have filled him up.

Wait, did he eat breakfast?

"Max, slow down!"

"No can do, camp man. I took eating for granted in my lifetime but," he took a bite. "No more!"

David shook his head fondly.

"Wow," was all Gwen had to say as she bit into her own lunch. She looked up, and her and David's eyes met. He blushed and quickly looked away.

Gwen smirked at him. Thankfully, Max was too preoccupied to notice.

"Things look like they're going well for you two. Good for you guys."

"Yeah! Max is doing really well in school, and-"

"Daviiiid..."

"Aww, are you embarrassed about being smart?" David cooed.

"A smart ass, maybe," Gwen mumbled.

They spent the day watching movies while David worked on his project. Max seemed tired (he wasn't making fun of the movie nearly as much as usual), but was obviously feeling better, and David was relieved he didn't have to rush the kid to the bathroom yet again. He felt awful admitting it, but even a sick Max wore him out. Possibly more, for some reason, because he was so exhausted.

It was around nine when David closed his computer for the night, and ten when Max went to bed. The little guy must have been really tired if he didn't need to be told to go to bed, but good for him for getting the rest he needed.

"You don't mind if I stay here tonight, do you?"

David blinked. "I already thought you were!" He got comfortable against the couch. "Still warning you, though. Max has been sick, and the apartment hasn't been completely sterilized, so..."

"Psh. I'll be fine."

David shrugged.

It was eleven when David realized something was wrong. But what?

He shifted uncomfortably. He hadn't finished his sandwich from earlier, and the soup went tragically uneaten. He must have been so tired he wasn't even hungry.

Yeah, that was probably it.

"I'll get you some blankets from the closet," he said as he stood up. He was moving slower than usual, feeling slightly lightheaded. Maybe he should have eaten more for lunch, or eaten dinner at all.

Gwen frowned at him as he returned. "Are you... Okay?"

"Yes? Why wouldn't I be?"

"I dunno, you just look kinda pale. And tired."

"I am tired," he said with a sigh. He plopped down next to her. "Exhausted, actually. I'm not so sure why."

He could feel Gwen scrutinizing him and couldn't for the life of him figure out why. Usually when Gwen was giving him that look it was because he was missing something. But what could he possibly be missing?

"David," she said, almost frustrated with him. Almost. "Didn't you say Max is sick?"

He nodded. "For the past couple days. Why?"

The look she gave him was deadpan. David took her silence to think about what she was saying.

"...oh. Uh oh."

Gwen chuckled fondly. "You're an idiot." She palmed his forehead and shrugged. "No fever, maybe I'm wrong."

She wasn't.

What little food he'd eaten that day wasn't sitting well, and knowing why seemed to make it feel so much worse.

"Well, I'd better get a good night's sleep shouldn't I? You too! You have a drive tomorrow!"

Gwen smiled and gave him a casual hug as she made her 'bed'.

"I do hope I'm wrong, David. Sleep well, dude."

She really wasn't.

David went to bed, only to find Max curled up fast asleep. He started sleeping in David's room the night he didn't feel good and apparently he hadn't changed his mind quite yet. David smiled fondly and climbed under the covers. He should really brush his teeth and wash his face and everything else, but right now he just wanted to sleep.

Sleep didn't last long. David woke with a horrible stomach cramp and a pitiful whimper. He soon remembered that he wasn't alone and needed to stay quiet if he wanted Max to get enough rest to get better. Oh, but his stomach was hurting so bad...

It gave an ominous gurgle and David's eyes widened in horror. He needed the bathroom.

David rushed in as fast as he could while still being quiet, fumbling with the waistband on his pajama bottoms. He didn't remember Max having diarrhea. Why did he have to?

David wrapped both arms around his middle, folding over to put some comforting pressure on his upset stomach. It hurt, and it felt awful, and David couldn't help the miserable tears welling up in his eyes. It had been a while since he'd been this sick.

He wasn't sure how long he waited before his lower intestines seemed settled. David cleaned up, flushed, and washed his hands vigorously.

He just needed a good rest and he'd feel better tomorrow. The past two sleepless nights just left him a little worn, that was all.

That wasn't all. An hour of sleep before David was scrambling out of bed to get to the toilet in time. He braced himself against the wall and threw up whatever was still inside of him. His stomachache didn't go away, so David just sat on the edge of the tub, where he'd put Max a couple nights ago, and waited for the next round.

He was still so tired. David was tempted to curl up on the floor and go to sleep until his stomach woke him up again. Before he gave in, though, there was a knock at the door.

"David?"

David closed his eyes. Max. "You're supposed to be asleep, buddy." His voice was wrecked from vomiting, his throat sore.

"Yeah well... you woke me up."

David sighed, feeling a pang of guilt. He should've been more careful getting out of bed, but it was an emergency...

"Sorry, Max. Go back to sleep, okay?"

Much to his relief, footsteps faded away from the door. He swallowed and looked back down at the contents of the bowl, staring at the half digested bits of sandwich. His stomach gurgled but gave no other sign it was going to force anything up anytime soon. He almost wanted it to, at least it'd be over with. With a miserable sigh, he rubbed at his face and buried his head in his hands.

The door clicked open. Before David could tell whoever it was that it was occupied, he saw Max padding in with a little bottle of Gatorade.

David couldn't help a small smile. It was from the pack he'd bought Max when he was having trouble keeping food down.

-----

He'd heard somewhere that water could be bad for you if didn't have electrolytes, so David decided a run to the store was in order. The problem? He couldn't just leave Max by himself in the middle of the night.

Max wasn't sleeping, anyway. The poor guy was too shivery and achey to get comfortable, and had refused to do anything besides watch TV with a bucket next to him.

"How do you feel about a trip to the store?" David asked from the kitchen. They were both exhausted, but they also both knew they wouldn't be sleeping anytime soon. He craned his neck to look to Max for a response.

The kid looked a little taken aback by the question. "Now?"

David shrugged. "Maybe! We can always go tomorrow."

Max looked to the TV, and then gave a nervous glance at the bucket next to him. "I'm not gonna change out of pajamas."

"Honestly?" David said with a chuckle. "Neither am I."

-----

"No Pedialyte."

"Max-"

"That stuff tastes like shit, David."

David already had an armful of the stuff. He gave a defeated sigh and started putting it back.

"How about..." He thought for a moment. "Gatorade?"

Max shrugged. "Sure."

David grabbed an 8 pack of cute little bottles. Max rolled his eyes but agreed that yeah, that flavor was pretty good. He rubbed his eyes and yawned, and David knew it was finally time for bed.

-----

 "Max..." He breathed out as the kid passed the bottle to him. "Thank you."

David hadn't realized how thirsty he was. Maybe he was feeling better, or maybe he was just getting dehydrated.

Max didn't say anything, but didn't leave, either. He stood for a little while as David sipped the drink, before finally sitting down next to him on the tub. He leaned into David's side just as he'd done a few days ago.

A pause. "Sorry."

David capped the drink and sat it next to him.

"It's not your fault! It happens, families share germs sometimes." He realized a little too late that it was his first time referring to them as family. He liked that term.

Max didn't even act fazed by the word, as if he already knew it was accurate. "Yeah, I guess. But... Why'd you let me sleep in your fucking bed? I probably got germs everywhere."

"Because I wanted you to be comfortable! And it was easier to keep an eye on you."

They sat in there for a little while longer before David decided he was done. Though he didn't feel like it, David knew their guest would have to use the restroom at some point, so...

"What are you doing?" Max asked, exasperated. "Are you cleaning?!"

David just shrugged as he pulled out their disinfectant and cleaning rag. "We don't want Gwen getting sick, do we?"

"You're so weird."

Still, Max helped him clean.

They returned to bed not too long after, both immediately falling asleep.
