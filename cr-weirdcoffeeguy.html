Weird Coffee Guy

Mollymauk had seen the customer every Thursday evening for a month now. He was a redhead, long hair that was usually left hanging around his face, clothes that looked clean but also old and worn like he hadn't bought new ones in years. 

His name was Caleb. And he was fucking cute.

Business usually came to a lull on Thursday evenings until it was time to close. Molly found himself absently cleaning and hoping that Caleb would return for the fifth week in a row.

He was mopping in the back when Caduceus poked his head in.

"Hey, uh, Mister Caleb is here. You should probably serve him since you usually make his order." Molly didn't believe that grin on his boss' face for one second. Caduceus knew. He wasn't sure how, he was good at hiding these things! But Caduceus definitely knew.

Caleb stood up straight, fidgeting with his keys as he stared at the menu. Molly wasn't sure why he always insisted on looking at the menu. He ordered the same drink every time he came - coffee with two pumps of caramel and a dash of milk. It sounded… okay, at best, but Caleb must've liked it, though why someone would come to a tea shop specifically for coffee was beyond him.

"Medium roast with two pumps of caramel and a dash of milk, please," Caleb said.

"Fantastic! I'll get that started and bring it out to you."

A flicker of a smile appeared on his face. "Thank you."

Caleb had a specific table he liked to sit at every Thursday. It was by a window, small and only seated two people. Today, it was occupied by someone having an important-looking meeting.

Caleb looked lost for a second. And then he chose a different table with some hesitance. It was… Interesting watching him, but then Molly realized he hadn't started on the man's drink.

The bass-y voice from behind him acted as a devil on his shoulder, except there was no angel on the other. 

"We have some leftover cakes. You should offer him one. Be a shame if baked goods went to waste, huh?"

"You think you're quite sneaky, don't you?"

Caduceus shrugged innocently. "No, not at all. I'm just trying to help."

Molly scoffed and finished up the drink. He walked to Caleb's table with coffee in one hand and a plate of cake in the other.

Caleb's mouth seemed to open as if to say 'I didn't order that', but Molly quickly explained, "On the house! If you want it, that is. Caduceus likes to get them eaten before the end of the night."

"Oh," Caleb nodded, eyes on the cake and coffee instead of Molly. "Thank you."

Molly tried not to watch him eat it from behind the counter. It was weird and creepy and a stupid crush, but Caleb was so adorable. He ate just as methodically as he did anything else.

He returned the plate exactly ten minutes before closing time, the same time he left every night.

"How was the cake?" Caduceus asked as he added the dishes to the bin. 

"It was very good, thank you." Caleb smiled and he was gone.

----- 

Caleb was a mysterious person, that was for certain. It was hard not to be curious about what a man like that did for a living.

The sixth week Molly couldn't stop the excited smile when he saw his favorite customer walk in. 

"Medium roast with-?"

Caleb shook his head. "Hot tea, please."

Molly blinked. "Really? You're sure?"

"Ah, yes. Very sure." Snf. His voice sounded thick, like clogged sinuses. He sniffled periodically as he counted out the correct change, pawing at his reddened nose every once in a while.

The poor guy had obviously come down with a cold, and a miserable one by the looks of it. Molly wondered how hard of a decision it was to change his order up. 

He brought the tea to Caleb’s table as the man was rubbing his nose in a handkerchief. 

“D- danke,” he muttered, hastily stuffing the kerchief in his pocket with a sniffle. 

“I brought a little honey as well. It’s good for sore throats, you know.” Molly smiled. 

He was trying to be nice, but a deep blush made itself known on Caleb’s cheeks that had nothing to do with illness. He averted his eyes and cleared his throat.

“Is it that obvious?”

Molly chuckled. “Caduceus was actually the one who suggested it. He’s… he sees into people’s souls, to be completely honest.” The firbolg owner offered a tiny grin their way before going back to work. Caleb continued to stare at his tea and blush. Did Molly cross a line? He was beginning to worry. “Well, feel better, Caleb.” 

Molly quickly retreated behind the counter to wallow in self-pity. He went into the backroom and slid to the floor with a dramatic groan. Caduceus watched with amusement painted all over his face.

“I’m usually so good at this,” Molly lamented. “What am I doing wrong?”

Caduceus hummed thoughtfully. “Not everyone’s brains work at top speed when they’re sick. Maybe Mister Caleb just isn’t feeling well enough to realize that you’re in love.”

The word sent shivers down Molly’s spine. “Alright, well, I’m not in love thank you very much.”

“Right. Of course.” 

He hated when Caduceus did that. Pretended not to be right all the time. Molly scoffed. He wasn’t the type of person to fall in love. It was just a crush because Caleb was hot, that was it. 

A harsh sneeze sounded from the cafe. Molly frowned. He must’ve been feeling awful. 

Caduceus mirrored his frown. “Sounds like just the beginning of a cold for him. Hope he feels better.”

Molly hummed in agreement. He hadn’t bothered to get off of the floor quite yet. “Me too.”

-----

Despite not being in love, Molly felt his heart skip a beat when the tired redhead walked into the tea shop for the seventh week in a row. Caleb didn’t look sick any more, thank goodness, and he ordered his usual drink.

Mollymauk decided that he had nothing to lose. So he wrote his number on Caleb’s coffee cup before he brought it to him.

“So here’s a question,” Molly said as he set it down on Caleb’s usual table. “What do you do while you’re here every week? I’m curious.”

“Ah, um…” Caleb pulled out a notebook, a nice one, from his bag. “I write sometimes.”

“You’re a writer!” Molly exclaimed in surprise. 

“I’m not very good, but it’s a nice way to relax.”

Molly tried not to stare as he waited for Caleb to see the number on his cup. It didn’t take very long. The redhead looked to the counter with raised eyebrows, and Molly did his best, charming smile. 

Caleb took the cup with him when he left that night.

Molly had hope.

-----

No phone call the entire week. Molly certainly wasn’t waiting by the phone, though. He had things to do besides wait for someone hot to call him. He was never this worried before when they didn’t call him, but…

Ugh, maybe Caduceus was right.

Fucking love. 

He waited for the doorbell to chime that eighth week. 

They went well into the evening without a Caleb showing up, until it was closing time and there had been no sign of him.

Caduceus patted Molly on the shoulder. Neither of them had to say anything.

Molly went home pretending like his heart hadn’t shattered into a million pieces. His roommate Yasha and her girlfriend were watching a movie when he walked in the door.

“Hey,” Yasha smiled. “How was work?”

“I bet you spent all night talking to that guy instead of actually working,” Beau said.

Molly sighed. “He never showed.”

The kind smile on Yasha’s face, as well as the smirk on Beau’s, both disappeared.

“Shit, sorry Molly.”

Molly waved a hand dismissively. “It’s alright. My irresistible self has been rejected a couple of times before.”

Beau gagged.

“Maybe he was just busy this week?”

“Maybe.”

-----

On the ninth week, the bell chimed, and a tired redhead walked in. 

Molly nearly gasped. Caduceus followed his gaze and then smiled knowingly. 

As Caleb got closer to the counter, Molly realized he didn’t look well. He didn’t look sick this time, but very, very beaten up. Various bruises covered his face, along with some cuts on his forehead and cheek. With even more inspecting, Molly realized his wrist was in a cast under his coat sleeve.

“Caleb, what happened?” 

Caleb smiled sheepishly. “Ah… there was a bit of an accident.” He was silent for a good few awkward seconds before, “I apologize for not calling you. I was… I wasn’t in good shape, but I’m alright now. I just wanted to speak in person.” He cleared his throat. “Also, I’d like my usual order if that’s alright.”

Molly wanted to cry with relief. But it didn’t make him any less worried about why it looked like someone had taken a fist to Caleb’s face.

“I’m just glad you’re alright,” Molly said. “I’ll start on your coffee.”

Caleb’s smile got a little brighter. “Danke. Would… would you mind joining me? If it’s alright with Mister Clay, that is.”

Molly looked back at his boss and earned the biggest approving nod.

“I think I can handle things up here just fine.”

-----

They went on a date the next day. Molly spent way too long finding and outfit and fixing his hair, long enough that Yasha actually ended up checking on him to make sure he was still sane. 

He suggested the bar that Yasha worked as a bouncer at. It was nice enough, and Caleb said he’d been there before and loved it. So Molly found a booth near the back by a window, and waited. And waited. By the fifteen minute mark, he was starting to get worried or wonder if he’d been stood up. 

Yasha gave him a questioning glance, and he just shrugged.

When Caleb finally showed up, he looked frazzled. 

“Sorry I’m late,” he breathed out.

Molly was just relieved to see him at all. “Is everything alright?”

Caleb hesitated before nodding. “J-ja. Just a mishap with… my car. But it was taken care of.”

Molly didn’t believe him. Between looking beat up the week before, and this, he had to know what was going on. Of course a date wasn’t the perfect time to question it.

“If I might be so nosy, where do you live, Caleb? Anywhere near the tea shop?”

Caleb took a sip of his beer, a Zemnian brand Molly didn’t even know the bar carried. “I ah… live with my friend Nott and her family on the northern side of town.”

“Not too far, then! I live a block away from the shop.”

“Must be nice being so close to work.”

“Oh, it is. I walk there every day and it’s so pretty.”

Their conversations were pleasant enough to make them both want to go out again. Same bar next week, they decided. Molly would… ease him into wanting to do a variety of things, but right now he was just happy to be going out with the guy he had his eye on for two months.

Molly decided he might have to admit to being in love.
