Lack of Biscuits

Something was off. Something had been off ever since Rebecca settled into her office that morning.

Was there a meeting she’d forgotten about? Or perhaps she had an errand that she was going to run before she come to work?

She couldn’t pinpoint it, so she shook her head and drank her tea and tried to get some kind of work done. It didn’t taste quite right.

It wasn’t until Higgins arrived to get her approval on something that she decided to acknowledge it.

“This morning feels strange,” she muttered as she signed the paper.

“Really? How so?”

“I don’t know... it feels as if I’m missing out on something. We didn’t have anything scheduled this morning, did we?”

Higgins shook his head, and then looked as if he had something to say. Rebecca waited as patiently as she could before she decided to go back to her work. She couldn’t wait for him to speak forever, she learned that a long time ago.

Finally, though, “Well, perhaps it’s because Coach Lasso hasn’t brought biscuits yet,” he said, glancing about his desk for the little pink box that was usually sitting in front of her at this time of morning.

And for fuck’s sake, he was right. She said as much. “Oh my God, you’re right. Have I really let myself become this accustomed to sugary snacks first thing in the morning that I feel wrong without them?” Rebecca laughed at the idea.

Higgins shrugged, and cleared his throat. It went unsaid that it wasn’t the biscuits she’d grown accustomed to, but Ted’s company that had become a pleasant part of her routine.

It was also strange that he hadn’t shown up.

“Have you seen him this morning?”

“No, I don’t think I have,” he answered after thinking about it for a moment. “Do you suppose he’s late?”

Before Rebecca had a chance to speculate, a familiar accented voice made itself known from the doorway.

“Mornin’, boss! Ah, and Higgins!” Ted still had his jacket on and his bag over his shoulder. Apparently, he’d come straight up there before even tossing his things in his own office.

“We were just wondering what happened to you,” she said as he walked in. She noticed there was a specific lack of pink boxes in his hands and took another sip of tea. She didn’t need the extra calories this morning, anyway.

He smiled and nodded. “Yeah, I uh, slept in.”

“Ah,” Higgins said. “Accidentally set your alarm wrong again?”

“Didn’t hear it, actually!” Ted replied with a hearty laugh. “Those darn things ought to be a little more obnoxious, you know? Especially when you’re not in the same room and...” He cleared his throat. “Anyway, I wanted to apologize. In my rush to get to work, I completely forgot your biscuits.” He breathed out, which sounded like a sigh but he was still smiling, because of course he was.

His hair looked a bit off from its usual style, and she was pretty sure he’d missed a button on his shirt, and Rebecca supposed that was to be expected from everyone on the occasional rough morning. A disheveled Ted Lasso was always more adorable than he had any right to be.

“Oh, that’s alright. I didn’t even notice,” Rebecca chuckled while Higgins gave her the side-eye.

He nodded again. “Now I’ve gotta apologize to beard for standing him up,” he said with a wince. “It was my turn to buy the coffee. Well! Have a good day, you two!”

Higgins offered a wave, while Rebecca wished him the same as Ted left. They looked at each other as the sound of his footsteps faded away.

“Didn’t notice, eh?” Higgins said rather smugly.

She huffed. “Shut up.”

-----

Ted had an apology on his lips before he entered the office. “I’m so sorry I-” and he was promptly cut off when a coffee cup was shoved at him. “Ya didn’t have to,” he said to Beard, who was already back to putting his feet on his desk and reading his book. “It was my turn.”

“Figured you’d need it. Not like you to sleep in this late.”

No, Ted supposed it wasn’t. Not that he didn’t have his lazy mornings, or that he didn’t occasionally lose track of time trying to blot a breakfast stain off of his shirt, but it was rare for him to be this late for work. Even more rare for him to show up and immediately want to go back home and go to bed. That wasn’t an option, though! He had a job to do and people relying on him, and he wasn’t about to leave all that for a dumb nap.

“Yeah.” He sipped the coffee. Any other morning like this, he would’ve chugged the stuff, but today he found himself wincing with each sip. His stomach gurgled unhappily at the foreign liquid, and he sat at his desk and subtly laid a hand on his belly, as if it would ease the discomfort. “Rough night, couldn’t sleep. You know how it is, sometimes you’re just lyin’ there and you start thinkin’ and suddenly-”

Beard nodded wisely. “Even your thoughts have thoughts.”

Ted chuckled and pointed at him with pride. “Exactly!”

The truth was that he had suffered a rough night, and he had struggled to fall asleep after he lied down. What he didn’t mention to Beard was that all that took place on the bathroom floor when he started puking up his dinner.

His stomach felt full and crampy when he’d gotten home from the pub that night. He assumed that it was just indigestion from eating or drinking too fast, until those cramps turned sharp and painful and had him curled up in the fetal position on the couch, begging his digestive system to let him barf just to ease the pain.

He got his wish eventually. He burped and saliva suddenly flooded his mouth, warning him to get to the bathroom before it was too late.

And the bathroom was where he stayed all night. He hardly had anything in his stomach after a while, and that didn’t stop it from trying to expel something that wasn’t there.

A gurgle lower in his gut made itself known after a while, and that needed to be dealt with.  Ted had reached a point that night where his stomach cramped and he didn’t know which end it was going to come out of.

He’d just fallen asleep twenty minutes beforr when he heard his alarm go off in the bedroom. He went back to sleep, and woke up with just enough time to shower and haphazardly get dressed.

The coffee he nursed at the moment reminded his stomach what last night was like. Ted drank it anyway. Beard got it for him, after all, and when he had stood him up!

“You eat breakfast?” Beard asked, turning a page in his book.

“Nah, I was in too big of a rush to-” a paper bag was tossed on his desk before he could finish, and in it was a muffin. Banana nut, his favorite. “Aww, bud, you don’t hafta do that!”

“Most important meal of the day!”

“You’ve got that right. Don’t know what I did to deserve a friend like you.”

Beard just smiled and continued to read.

-----

Ted drank every last drop of coffee and ate the whole muffin. It would have been rude not to, and part of him hoped that maybe he just needed some food in his stomach and caffeine in his veins to fix him up. He made a point to ignore the angry gurgles that came from his belly as he swallowed the last bite with great difficulty.

He pressed a hand to it and resisted the urge to tell it to shush, you’re too darn loud!

It complained louder.

“Y’okay, coach?” Beard asked, glancing up. “Look like you’re hurtin’.”

“I’m fantastic! Yup, fit as a fiddle over here. Is everyone here?” He swallowed hard to stave off the impending nausea. Not right now, absotively not. They had shit to do! Important shit!

Beard hummed thoughtfully and looked out the window. “Yup, think so. You ready?”

He wanted a nap. Would anyone notice if he curled up in the corner of the room with his jacket and let Beard do all of the coaching? Probably, but it didn’t stop him from fantasizing about it as he went out to greet everyone and tell them what they were working on today.

He made it through a slightly-confusing motivational speech and followed the team out to the field. (Dragged behind them would have probably described it more accurately.) He was exhausted, and his muscles had ached since he left the office. Muffins didn’t make your body hurt, last he knew, but he did spend the night on the floor, and that never did anything good for someone his age.

Beard, bless him, could tell that Ted was not up to coaching today. He patted him on the back and took the reins as much as he needed to, which was really just repeating their plan for the day and sending them off to it.

That coffee and muffin was not sitting well in his stomach. In fact, they felt as if they were actively fighting each other in there. The gurgling felt closer to the surface, and he burped and tasted something sour in the back of his mouth.

Ted swallowed. Saliva flooded his mouth once again. He made a noise. He didn’t mean to, but he was never good at not drawing attention to himself and golly, his stomach hurt. Coach Beard heard the strangled whine and raised an eyebrow at him.

He refused to let it happen. This was practice, training, they were busy, his body was going to have to wait until later! There was no reason for this, anyway. He did all his puking last night, unless he had some kind of bug or something, but he didn’t feel that bad.

... Okay, so he did feel that bad. His entire body ached, and all he’d done was stand there! There was no reason for him to feel worse than he had that morning!

“Coach Lasso!” Ted turned around and saw Rebecca in the window of her office.

“Y- yeah, boss?” he called back, feeling cold sweat on the back of his neck as he swallowed again.

“Do you mind meeting me after you’re done? I wanted to talk about next week’s game!”

“Sure, boss!”

Sam jogged his way, looking as excited and eager to do his best as ever. “Hey coach, what if we-?”

Ted held up a hand to cut him off, turned to the side.

And puked.

Not just once, no of course not. It was a good three times in a row while the whole field went silent.

He felt Beard’s hand on his shoulder as if the man were afraid he was going to fall forward and wanted to be ready for it. He heard Rebecca’s window close. Fair. That probably wasn’t the sight or sound she needed outside of her office.

When he finally felt... well, not done, but unlikely to barf again at the moment, Lasso stiffly straightened himself and tried to clean his mouth and mustache with the sleeve of his jacket. Beard nudged him and handed him a half-crumpled but clean napkin from their usual coffee shop. He nodded his thanks and tried to clean his face. Oh God, his nose was running too. Gross, he’d have to do some more thorough ‘stache cleaning later.

“... coach?”

Ted cleared his throat and spat some foul-tasting residue.

“Sorry Sam, what were you saying?”

Sam didn’t answer, instead he blinked and furrowed his brow until his gaze caught sight of something behind Ted.

It was pretty darn rare to see Rebecca anywhere near the pitch. ‘You’re going home,” she said as she walked up, keeping her distance from the puddle of vomit that was currently seeping into the grass. “Right now, Lasso, go on.” She made a shooing motion with both hands.

He chuckled and waved it off. “I’m fine, it was just an upset stomach.” The bout had made him feel shaky and unsteady, and he was tempted to just take a seat right there and do the rest of his coaching from the ground.

“That’s why you were late this morning,” Beard said, mostly to himself in realization. “You’re sick. Last time you were that late was the all-you-can-eat taco incident.”

Ted huffed. “Now, you promised you’d never bring that up again.”

“Ted,” Rebecca said his name in a way that demanded his attention. “Get out of here before you infect the entire club.”

He wanted to protest. But he couldn’t form any kind of sentence that would convince her (no one could) and he... yeah, he really wanted to go home and go to bed. And Beard was giving him the wide-eyed look that urged him.

He sighed, shoulders falling in defeat. “Alright, fine, y’all win.”

-----

It didn’t occur to Rebecca until she was already at the door that she’d never actually been to Coach Lasso’s place before. She never had a reason to until now, where she stood uncomfortably and debated whether she should just leave.

She knocked, and waited, taking a deep breath and fidgeting restlessly.

She wasn’t sure if she’d be here had she not felt so guilty. Guilty for what, exactly? It wasn’t as if Ted’s illness was her fault.

But perhaps she’d pressured him too much that he didn’t feel comfortable taking a sick day, or maybe he was just another moronic man who didn’t know when to quit.

... Alright, she couldn’t only blame the men. She’d do the exact same thing.

Still, though.

He’d just... looked so sick when he left the club. And sad, really sad. And horribly quiet. None of those things were a good look on Ted Lasso. She watched the way he zipped up his jacket completely as he walked out the door with his stuff, shuddering a bit before the cool air from outside had a chance to hit him.

And there she was, carrying a paper cup and some over-the-counter things that were meant to help with ailments like this. Rebecca wasn’t an expert, but she wanted to do something nice for him. For some reason.

The door opened and Ted looked far worse than he had earlier. Either he was worse, or his appearance at work was a charade. She was willing to bet on the former. But his face lit up when he saw her, though his brows knitted in delighted confusion.

“Boss! What’re you doin’ here?”

That was a damn good question, wasn’t it.

“Hi,” she smiled lamely. “I, er-”

“Not that I’m not happy to see ya! Come on in!” He chuckled, and seemed to realize what a mess he was, because he immediately went to work fixing his hair.

“Well, I... had a feeling that you probably didn’t have much in the way of medicines and the like, being a man living alone and all.”

He made a clicking noise and pointed at her with a nod. “You’d be absolutely correct about that. But jeez, you didn’t hafta...” Ted trailed off as they entered the living room. He rubbed the back of his neck with a chuckle and shoved some blankets aside on the sofa where he’d clearly built a nest for the day. “Jeez, sorry. I haven’t been the tidiest today. On account of, y’know, everything. Here, sit down!”

Rebecca hadn’t planned to stay, but those sorts of plans were often thwarted by the man’s genuine and pure kindness. He also looked far too exhausted to stay standing for too long no matter how hard he tried to hide it. In the end, she sat on a chair just so he’d sit down as well. He used his foot to nudge aside a small waste bin that looked like it’d been pulled from the bathroom, clearing his throat in embarrassment.

“I also brought tea, and- no, hold on,” she said patiently, as the genuine smile on his face faded into a forced sort of smile. “It’s mint, and it’s good for stomach aches.”

“Ah,” he nodded, and accepted the cup. He took a sip immediately, because the man wasn’t so impolite as to refuse a gift. He made a ‘not bad’ sort of face and took another sip. “That’s... not nearly as bad as the other stuff you’ve got in this country.”

She couldn’t help but laugh. “Thank goodness.”

She began to feel an awkward silence creeping up, but Ted Lasso refused to let it happen.

“I hope the uh, grass is okay.” A chuckle. “Who knew I was that bad off, huh? Crazy how that can just... happen.”

“Ted.”

“Hm?” he feigned innocence. Something he was quite good at.

“You were sick far before you came to work this morning. Don’t lie to me. Come on.”

He sighed and massaged his forehead with a wince, as if this was the last conversation he wanted to have during her visit. Well, tough.

“Look, I might’ve felt a little icky last night, but I figured it was... y’know, all outta my system by then.”

She gave an exasperated huff. “Take a fucking sick day next time, Lasso. Jesus. We don’t need the whole team vomiting on the field.”

He smiled sheepishly and nodded. “Understood, boss.”

Rebecca nodded in approval and watched the American take another sip of tea. He was mildly tolerating it, so credit to him for that. His facade was beginning to fade away the longer he sat there, and she could see the exhaustion and misery showing through. It made it easier to notice the flush on his cheeks.

“Anyway, there’s some fever reducer in here, as well as some other things. I... I grabbed a lot of things off of the shelf, not all of it may be applicable to your condition.” The store clerk had looked at her as if she were patient zero for just about everything. “But for next time. You’re bound to get a cold or flu while you’re here.”

He set the tea aside and sifted through the items. “Jeez, look at all this stuff... I’m gonna be the healthiest person on earth.”

She snorted. “We can only hope. I need you around, after all.”

He smiled at her, eyes crinkling at the edges and absolutely sparkling with that Lasso happiness despite his illness. Oh God... it was time to go. She stood up, earning a frown from the man that almost had her sitting back down again and just staying there forever.

“I’ve got some work that needs finished at home, but it’s nice to see that you’re still alive and- not well, I suppose, but alive.”

“Thanks, boss! I’ll be back in no time, promise.”

“I expect it.”

“And I’ll bring extras to make up for the lost biscuits!”

Thank God. “I expect that as well,” she replied with a chuckle.
