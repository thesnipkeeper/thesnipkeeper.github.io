Title: Point Taken
Fandom: Bright Sessions
Summary: Sam forces love onto her friend
Word count: 1.5k

Point Taken

Sam can tell something's off the moment she sees Joan that morning.

A grumpy Joan Bright is certainly nothing new, but this is an exhausted, stressed kind of grumpy. Sam wonders if she got any sleep last night, or if work itself has already taken a tax on her.

She almost dreads their working together today. They're supposed to be going through files, organizing and re-grouping and researching. The AM has an insane amount of papers to go through, and Joan - information hungry as she is - has clearly wanted to read all of it.

Sam has her cup of tea, and Joan's coffee, in tow as she heads down the hallway to her friend's office. She stops just outside of the doorway when she hears...

Coughing. A wet, productive-sounding cough, the kind that-

Joan sniffles thickly.

Yeah, the kind that comes with a particularly nasty head cold.

Sam frowns in sympathy. No wonder she's been so out of sorts today.

Joan has a tissue pressed to her nose when Sam enters, knocking on the door frame.

"Hey," she greets kindly.

Joan tosses the tissue as if it never existed and returns a smile. "You look ready for the most boring work of your life."

"Oh, you know I am. Here, I brought coffee." She sets the paper cup down next to a tissue box and a pile of cough drops that they're also pretending don't exist, apparently.

Joan breathes a sigh of relief. "Thank you, I was just about to get something-" to sooth her throat? Keep her awake?

God, she looks so tired, Sam would give her a hug of she thought it would help. It wouldn't. Joan doesn't work like that.

Joan clears her throat out of necessity when her voice catches. It seems to ignite something in his respiratory system and causes her to cough. She barely manages to turn away to cover the cough, and gets what looks like a slightly-damp tissue to muffle it. She swallows and winces afterward, which pretty much confirms for Sam that her throat is sore.

"You're really not feeling great, are you?" Sam asks.

Joan sniffs, and chuckles. "I'll admit, I've been better."

"Oh, Joan..." Sam frowns. "Maybe you should just go home?"

"I'm good here, thank you," Joan snaps so quickly and bitterly Sam nearly recoils.

"O-of course, I wasn't trying to- I just-"

"I know, Sam, but I'm not going home because of a cold. Of course, I'm not going to make you stay in my office if you're worried about germs."

"I'm not worried about catching it, Joan. I'm just worried about you."

Joan smiles, just slightly. "I'm alright, but thank you." The coughing fit that follows says otherwise.

She works with her chin propped on her palm until she inevitably had to wipe her nose again. Sam catches her eyes slipping shut at one point when she’s supposed to be reading.

“Did you sleep last night?”

Joan widens her eyes, shakes her head a bit as if to wake up fully. She gives a liquid sniff and grabs another tissue. “Ugh,” she says as she wipes her nose. “No, not really.”

Sam gives a sympathetic hum as she looks her friend over.

Joan raises an eyebrow under the scrutiny. “What?”

“Oh,” Sam sits up. “I guess I’ve just never seen you sick before.”

“I am human, you know,” she says with a bit of a laugh. It seems to cause her nose to run again, because she hurriedly shields her face and sniffles.

“Dunno, sometimes I think that you’re more superhuman than I am. But today...”

“Ah,” Joan nods. “I see. It makes you feel better to see me suffering.”

Sam scoffs. “That’s not what I meant!”

Joan looks like she’s going to make another smug remark, but instead she rushes to grab a tissue and sneezes once, twice... and... yep, there’s the third.

“Bless you!”

“Snf! Thank you.”

-----

Sam runs into her again in the break room. Her cold hasn’t gotten any better, and in fact seems to have gotten much worse. She looks exhausted, sick, half-asleep at the table with a cup of coffee that she’s clearly forcing herself to drink.

Her cheeks have taken a pink color to them, the same hue that her nose was before it became bright like it is now. It looks sore, she’s clearly done nothing but blow her nose all day.

Sam sets her cup under the coffee maker and starts it before turning around to face Joan. She leans against the counter as casually as possible, but Joan hardly notices her presence anyway.

Shes leaning on the table, coffee cup in one hand while the other is supporting her head.

“How’re you feeling?” Sam asks, even though she already knows the answer.

Joan sniffs and sits up straight, taking another sip of her coffee before she finally lets her eyes meet Sam’s. “Oh... I-” She must not have used her voice in a while, because she breaks off in a coughing fit that causes her to abandon her coffee too muffle it into her elbow. She clears her throat afterward, and Sam doesn’t have to ask to know her throat is killing her. “Not great,” Joan concludes.

“Yeah...” Sam says with a wince. “Yeah, you don’t look great.”

Joan is not in as good of spirits as she had when they were together earlier. Sam wishes she knew how to fix it, to make her feel better... it’s not as if she’s ever been much good taking care of people.

“Um, should you,” she clears her own throat. “Should you be drinking coffee? When you’re sick? I mean, how many cups have you had?”

“Not enough,” is Joan’s first reply, and then a hint of a joking smile before she continues. God, her voice sounds horrible. “No, I can almost guarantee that I should not be drinking coffee right now, and yet...” she gestures to the half-empty cup in front of her.

Sam chuckles. “I guess I can’t judge.”

Joan smiles, goes back to her coffee. There’s a stack of napkins next to her that she’s likely pillaged, though Sam can’t imagine why she’d use those unless she’s out of tissues.

“Have you taken anything?”

Joan sighs immediately. “Sam, it’s just a cold, really...”

“I know, but just humor me for a second. Please?”

Another sigh, though this one’s more of a congested huff. “No, I haven’t. I don’t want to risk anything making me... groggy.”

“Groggier than you are right now?”

Joan glances up at her, back to her coffee. “Point taken.”

“Seriously, go home. Take a nap. Get some of that really strong cold medicine and drug yourself out for the rest of the day.”

Joan muffles a quick cough into her sleeve.

“You know you want to.”

“Fine, yes. A nap might... help.” Help, possibly, but she’s clearly deep in the throes of a miserable cold already. It isn’t going away any time soon. “I’ll talk to Owen.”

Sam nods approvingly. Owen is the last person who’s going to let her stay at work in her state.

-----

Sam knocks.

The person who answers... she thinks it’s Joan, but it’s Joan in sweats and a t-shirt. She looks even worse than she had earlier, if that’s even possibly.

“Hey!”

Joan smiles sleepily. “Hello. Thank you for...” she gestures to the grocery bag in Sam’s hands.

“Of course!” She’d texted Joan earlier and pretty much wore her down until she divulged her usually sick-person essentials. “Can I come in?”

“Yes, yeah, but um, I can’t promise how germ-free my apartment is right now.”

“That’s okay, I did work with you all day, so...”

Joan sighs, sniffs. “Right. Sorry, then, if you catch this.” She takes a step back and closes the door behind Sam.

“I’m not too worried.” Sam’s already pulling things out of the bag. “I know you said you didn’t care, but I got you the nice aloe tissues anyway? Your nose looks sore.”

Joan’s hand went to cover her face self-consciously for a moment. “Oh, Sam, you didn’t have to- I mean, all I asked for was medicine and-”

Sam takes a plastic package and holds it up. “And a thermometer, which... it alarms me that you didn’t already own, to be honest.”

Joan shrugs. “It’s not as if I’ve spent a lot of time in my own home the past few years.”

Right. The woman who’s essentially been obsessed with either work or her family drama for the past few years (if her brother being held against his will could be considered ‘drama’).

“But you needed tissues, right? And I thought maybe some of your favorite snacks would make you feel better, but I’m now realizing that you probably can’t taste...”

“Thank you, Sam.” Joan smiles sweetly. “I... thank you. It’s been a while since someone’s...”

“Taken care of you?”

She nods.

“Well, you’ve got me.”

“And you’ll have me when you inevitably catch this.”

Sam snorted. “I hope you know that I’m holding you to that.”
