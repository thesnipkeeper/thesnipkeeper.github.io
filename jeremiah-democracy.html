Title: Democracy
Fandom: Jeremiah
Summary: Jeremiah and Kurdy return to find out that the Great Leader isn't as impervious as they thought
Word count: 1.2k

Democracy

“They did say that he was going to meet us in here, right?”

Kurdy sighed heavily and leaned back in his chair. “’s far as I know. What’s taking him so long?”

They’d been waiting for Markus for a good forty-five minutes. They could have had time to shower or get something to eat instead of spending it stuck in the debriefing room waiting for the great overlord to arrive.

Jeremiah mirrored his friend, folding his hands over his stomach and sinking down into the chair. May as well try to get some rest. Who knew how long they’d be waiting for him.

“Sorry I’m late.” The sudden voice caused both of them to jump in surprise, each having been about to fall asleep.

“Oh, look who decided to show up,” Jeremiah muttered, sitting up once again.

Kurdy leaned almost all the way onto the table while Markus took a seat in front of them. “Do you know how long we’ve been here, Markus?”

Markus sighed and nodded. “I said that I’m sorry. Let’s just get down to business, shall we?”

Well, it seemed that today he was less eager to entertain them. Not that it mattered. Jeremiah thought that getting down to business sounded like a great idea, at least then he and Kurdy could get some hot showers and a few hours of sleep.

“Penville was just as boring as you said it was gonna be,” Kurdy said.

They began their hopefully short discussion about the uneventful trip they had to visit a group that had little resources to spare. It was nice, he and Kurdy had agreed, that they got to have one trip where they didn’t have to worry about getting themselves or someone else out of a hostage situation.

Markus massaged his temple, looking more tired than he usually did.

Jeremiah didn’t think anything of it. They all had their moments where they were running on fumes. A good sleeping schedule was hard to come by those days.

Kurdy continued his story about the little kid that had convinced them to play ball with him and his friends. Until Markus interrupted him with a sudden itchy cough.

“Excuse me,” he blinked dazedly and cleared his throat. “Con- conti-” but he coughed again, this time harsher. Markus pulled up a piece of scrap fabric-turned-kerchief and pressed it to his mouth to muffle the fit. He sniffled afterwards, wiping at his nose with the abused close.

He’d been blinded by his eagerness to get this over with and get things done, but now that Jeremiah was really looking, he realized that Markus’ face was red, his eyes were watery and his nose had a sore, pink hue to it.

“You okay?” Jeremiah finally asked.

Markus nodded as his gave another water sniffle. “The last team before you to return brought a cold with them. It’s,” he paused to clear his throat. “It’s been making its rounds.”

Kurdy hissed in sympathy. “No wonder everyone’s been so pissed today.”

Markus hummed in agreement.

“We’ll finish this up, then,” Jeremiah said, and he could swear he saw a rush of relief across Markus’ face. Maybe he was just as eager to shower and go to bed as they were. Granted, he had the sniffles and they’d been trekking in the mud for a week, but... “They don’t have a lot to offer in terms of skills or resources, but they’re sweet people and I think that they’d be willing to help with anything if we asked.”

Markus smiled. “Good, good. We’ll keep in touch with them, perhaps we can help them down the li- hhh- line-”

“You okay, man?” Kurdy asked.

Markus nodded slowly, eyes going cloudy before he pressed the kerchief to his nose and gave an impressive sneeze. And another.

And another. Jesus.

Jeremiah couldn’t help but chuckle. It was weird seeing the man so out of sorts, and a little sad. “Bless you, as they used to say.”

Markus, face still buried in the cloth, gave his nose a quick blow before clearing his throat again. “I’ll give you your next assignments tomorrow. Snf! Get something to eat and get some rest.”

Jeremiah raised an eyebrow. “I think the same goes for you.”

A chuckle. “It’s a good thought, but I’ve got a lot of work to do.” He stood up and gave them a nod. “Good work,” before walking off.

“I’m ready for dinner,” Kurdy said, and Jeremiah couldn’t help but agree.

-----

The two of them were high on a good night’s sleep and having been able to finally bathe. They arrived at breakfast in good spirits, only to see Markus sitting at a table in the corner, among the crowd, with an uneaten bowl of food in front of him.

“Shit,” Kurdy said. “He looks awful.”

If just his appearance wasn’t enough, the wet cough that echoed throughout the cafeteria certainly would have been.

Jeremiah was determined not to make it his problem. Markus was a capable adult man, he didn’t need someone telling him to rest.

Still, Jeremiah somehow found himself gravitating toward Markus’ table.

Markus watched him sit down with a raised eyebrow and a tense pose that waited for Jeremiah to say something he didn’t like.

“How much sleep did you get last night?”

Markus laughed. “Not enough.” His voice sounded like he’d been gargling nails. Jeremiah could imagine that he’d spent most of the night coughing.

“That’s what I thought. You look bad, man. You shouldn’t be working yourself to death.”

Markus rolled his eyes. He looked down at his breakfast and lightly pushed it away.

“And you’re also wasting food, which is a huge offense, in my opinion.”

A sigh. “Yeah, I know. I really thought that I would be hungry once it was sitting in front of me.” He cleared his throat and winced. “It turns out that swallowing hurts.”

Jeremiah hummed sympathetically and made it a point to enjoy his own breakfast. “Go back to bed,” he muttered before he took a bite. Maybe Markus wouldn’t hear.

Markus laughed yet again. Or maybe he would hear, because apparently he hears everything. “I appreciate that you care,” he paused to wipe his nose, this time on his sleeve. Must’ve run out of kerchiefs. “But that’s not possible. Especially not right now.”

“Markus, you’re working yourself to the bone and you’re making yourself sicker. You’re no use to anyone here if you’re dead.”

The corner of Markus’ mouth turned up in a small smile, with a look that Jeremiah couldn’t quite decipher. “It’s funny, that’s almost word-for-word exactly what Erin said to me this morning.”

Jeremiah scoffed. “Well, there you go. You’re outvoted. You like democracy, right?”

Another sniffle. His nose was red, looked impossibly sore from the abuse it likely suffered in the past day. “I don’t think democracy really applies to my personal choices, but I see your point.” He paused in thought for a moment before getting up. “I’ll let Erin know that she’s in charge today. You’ll be reporting to her later.”

Jeremiah blinked. “Wait, really?” Did that actually work?

“Yeah, you’re right,” Markus said. “If I want to take care of other people, I need to take care of myself, too.”

“Wow,” how did he manage to make everything sound so smart, even when Jeremiah was the one who said it? “Okay, have a good day, then. Oh, hey Markus-”

He managed to get Markus’ attention before walked away. Jeremiah pushed the bowl toward him.

Markus sighed at the bowl as if it’d done something offensive, and took it.

“Eating is part of taking care of yourself, right?”

Markus nodded. “I suppose that it is.”
