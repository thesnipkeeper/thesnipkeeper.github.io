Over the Years

This wasn't the first time Taliesin's crashed on the Mercer's couch, it certainly wouldn't be the last.

Matt was practically asleep already as he dragged himself into the bedroom. He hadn't talked much all night, a sign of how exhausted he must have been after a day of talking to fans and getting his picture taken.

They returned from the convention late that night and all promptly went to bed.

Taliesin didn't need to ask if he could stay before he haphazardly pulled a pillow and blanket out of the linen closet and fell on the couch to regret sleeping there in the morning.

But morning didn't come before he woke up.

The sound of the bedroom door opening, footsteps. Taliesin opened his eyes and tried to get his groggy vision to focus on the figure in the hallway. It was Matt, he realized, probably just taking a piss of something.

Taliesin closed his eyes again, practically asleep already.

A cough. A wet, chesty cough. For a moment, Taliesin was worried that Matt was throwing up, but as he listened more intently, he knew that wasn't exactly the case. He could hear the creak of the medicine cabinet and a variety of other sounds that weren't his friend just taking a middle-of-the-night piss. A faint beep.  A thermometer?

Taliesin bit his lip. They always thought that they'd escaped the con crud, and it always managed to sneak up on at least one of them. Apparently Matt was the unlucky one this time.

Matt wandered out into the living room/dining room and flipped on one of the dimmer wall lights in the kitchen. He rubbed his eyes and looked to where Taliesin was definitely not asleep. He offered a smile, and Taliesin sat up.

"Did I wake you up?" Matt asked quietly, voice sounding rough and unused.

Taliesin yawned and ran a hand down his face. “Kinda. You feeling okay?”

Matt sighed, eyes going back and forth like he was hesitating to answer. “I’m good, throat’s just a little scratchy.”

“That cough sounded nasty, though…”

Matt chuckled, which only caused him to cough once more. Taliesin could hear the gunk in his chest, wished he could do more to help. Instead, he continued to let himself wake up as Matt filled a glass with water. 

“Yeah,” Matt wandered into the living room and took a seat. “Think I’m coming down with something.”

“Fucking con crud,” Taliesin muttered.

“Mmhm.” He sipped his water and sighed. “I’ve been coughing off and on all night, so I think I’ll just go try to work on stuff until I finally crash.”

Taliesin winced. None of them had really gotten enough sleep over the weekend, with the convention and travel and everything else going on. The last thing Matt needed was to stay up all night and work, especially when he was already getting sick.

“Wanna watch some TV?” Taliesin offered.

Matt’s eyebrows raised. “I can’t stay, I’ll keep you up.”

“I don’t care about that, you should know that by now. I’d… I’d rather you relax and keep me up than work all night.” And it was the truth. Taliesin could just go home the next morning (or technically, later that day) and sleep. He had nothing to do. And… “Honestly, I kind of just want to cuddle with you. It’s been a long weekend.”

Matt smiled and shook his head fondly before moving from a chair to the couch that Taliesin had claimed as his bed for the night. Matt immediately and shamelessly leaned against his shoulder while they flipped through to find something to watch. They settled on a bad sitcom neither of them had seen, and then they were content.

Until Matt started coughing, that is. The change in position must have irritated something in his chest, because suddenly he had to sit up and turn away while the horrible coughs tore out of him. Taliesin, feeling useless, patted his back until the fit was over. 

“Sorry,” Matt said, eyes watery. He nodded a thanks and accepted the water Taliesin pressed into his hand and took a couple of careful sips. He gave a shaky breath as if he was afraid anything would jump start another coughing fit.

Taliesin kissed the side of his head as he laid back down. “Sure you’re okay?” he asked softly.

Matt nodded against him. It wasn’t long before Matt’s breathing started to even out, and he was asleep. Taliesin didn’t dare move and wake his friend, especially when Matt’s initial plan was to stay awake all night. Eventually, he found himself falling asleep as well.

-----

“D’awww.” 

Taliesin was the first to wake, blinking blearily and becoming more and more aware of the weight against his leg, which just so happened to be Matt, who was still sleeping soundly. He found the source of the voice to be Marisha, watching them from the kitchen with a smile.

“Shh!” 

She pointed to her husband and stuck out her lower lip. “I would never wake him!”

The body against him shifted as Matt woke. “Mmph?”

“Shit. Hey Matt, good morning.”

Matt immediately coughed, only sort of managing to turn away from Taliesin. Not that Taliesin care, really. He rubbed his friend’s back as he gradually woke up. 

“Heeeyy. Morning.” Oh God, he sounded horrible, like he was equal-parts stuffed up and sore and tired. Taliesin and Marisha shared a wince as Matt yawned and rubbed the sleep from his eyes. 

“How’re you feeling?”

Matt chuckled and wasted no time answering that question. “Like shit.”

“Fair,” Taliesin nodded.

Marisha moved quickly from the kitchen to the living room. “Aw no, are you getting sick?” she asked, sitting on the arm of the couch and pressing a hand to his forehead. By the look on her face, Taliesin had a feeling that the result wasn’t great.

Matt nodded reluctantly. “Sorry for falling asleep on you, Tal, I-” whatever needless apology it was going to be was cut off when he pulled his shirt collar up to cover another awful cough. Marisha bit her lip. 

“Being completely honest? That was sort of my plan all along.”

Matt’s glare didn’t do much when his eyes were red and watery. 

-----

Marisha was the only one of the three of them who had stuff to do that day. She had to be at the studio to help build the set for the next show they were doing. Taliesin was perfectly fine staying, even when Matt swore he would be fine alone.

That was the thing about Matt. Sure, he’d survive on his own while he was sick, but he’d hate it. He liked being around others just as much as Taliesin did when he wasn’t feeling good. It was how things always were, ever since they met.

When one of them got sick, the other was at their side simply keeping them company.

“Bless you!”

“Ugh, God…” Matt covered his face with one hand and pulled a tissue with another. He blew his nose rather enthusiastically before throwing it in the overfilled bin next to the couch. “Thanks, Tal…”

Taliesin was in the middle of snooping through the kitchen while Matt was leaned over his computer with a tissue constantly pressed to his nose. He couldn’t have been getting that much work done between the coughing and sneezing, but he did look adorable trying to do it while wrapped in a blanket. 

“What… should we have for lunch?”

“Oh, yeah…” Matt sniffled and cleared the gunk from his throat. “I should have warned you. We haven’t gone shopping in… a while.”

He laughed. “Mmm, I see that now. Maybe I’ll pick something up.”

“We can go to that deli with the really good soup?”

Taliesin blinked. He hadn’t expected a ‘we’. or for Matt to want to go anywhere today.

-----

Matt ran the back of his hand against his sleeve with a thick snuffle. God, did he feel awful. And yeah, he probably shouldn’t be leaving the house when he was uber contagious but he just needed to go out and do something, and most of all, he didn’t want Taliesin to leave him alone.

They decided to make a quick trip to CVS on the way back home when Matt admitted that he didn’t have any cold medicine. Taliesin lightly pushed him when he went to take out his wallet, and then gave him a kiss on the forehead as they got into the car. 

And they made their journey home.

He struggled to eat with his stuffed nose, but wow was it nice having something to eat. He wasn’t initially hungry at all when Taliesin mentioned it, but he was thankful that he did. Matt could feel himself getting better already.

-----

Matt winced at the sound of a sneeze as he walked into the studio. He knew that sneeze, and he knew that it wasn’t just an innocent sneeze. 

Just as he suspected, as he was making his way into the lounge, Taliesin was in the middle of a sneezing fit. It was followed by a cough that sounded all too familiar. 

“Sorry, buddy…” Matt said.

Taliesin looked up with watery eyes and just laughed as he wiped his nose. “The price of friendship?”

Matt scoffed. “Sure.”

Taliesin didn't need to go home, Matt was aware, but he watched his friend sneeze and cough his way through the day. Matt felt guilty that he got to spend the cold at home being coddled and Taliesin had to spend it at work.

He got his friend a packet of tissues when he went to grab lunch, at Taliesin's request because he was running dangerously low. 

Honestly, the sneezing and coughing were the only things that alerted anyone that Taliesin was sick. He never mentioned it or complained, just simply did his work with hand sanitizer nearby.

But it was near the end of the day that his energy was obviously waning. Taliesin began to give one-word replies and though he tried to smile and laugh, it was pretty obvious that he wasn't feeling good. 

"Hey," Matt laid a hand on Taliesin's back as he was writing an email. He'd been quietly dealing with a runny nose for the past half hour. "How are you feeling?"

Taliesin hummed and cleared his throat. He winced. "My head hurts a little."

"You know you can go home early, right?"

He nodded, clearing his throat. "I'm just waiting for the Tylenol to kick in… what?"

Matt didn't realize that the face he was making was so obvious, but Taliesin was looking at him expectantly like he definitely had something to say. 

"Nothing, just um… feeling a little guilty for giving this to you," he admitted.

Taliesin gave a hoarse laugh. "You say that as if this hasn't happened a million times before."

It was true. They'd passed endless bugs back and forth over the years, even more so when Marisha was added to the mix.

"Yeah well… I hope you feel better. You can always come hang out with us tonight if you want."

Taliesin smiled. "As nice as that sounds, I think I just want to go home and sleep. Thanks, Matt." 

Matt leaned down and pressed a kiss to his forehead. "Love you."

"Love you too, buddy."
