Sketchy Establishments

Magnus has never had to worry about food poisoning.

His magical immune system always warded off bacteria as long as his magic wasn’t completely depleted. When he did ever feel a certain ache in his stomach that signified oncoming illness, all he had to do was drink a potion.

Now that he’s mundane, however, he forgets to take things into account like maybe eating food at a sketchy-looking restaurant wasn’t a good idea.

And maybe that’s the reason his stomach is cramping enough to make an 800+ year-old man cry out in pain.

He whimpers, pressing a hand into his stomach and hoping it’ll do something to relieve the pressure. It doesn’t, of course. Hopefully this will all go away after a while and he can just... avoid that restaurant for eternity.

Alas, things only get worse from there. He feels the nausea creeping up, and Magnus reluctantly gets out of the comfort of his bed and drags his tired body to the bathroom.

He leans over the toilet, bracing himself with a hand on the wall, and gags uselessly. The sick feeling is there lurking, but it’s not enough to make him bring up the stuff irritating his stomach. In contracting his stomach to induce something, all he manages is a wet burp.

Magnus sighs. How long is this going to go on for? Can’t he just make it stop somehow? He could make himself vomit, but he’d really prefer not to.

Just when he thinks he’s just going to have to spend the rest of the afternoon waiting for his stomach to finish struggling to digest his lunch, he... feels something a little lower.

Right. That’s another fun aspect of mundane illness, Magnus thinks with a tired eye roll as he fumbles with untying his pants and sits down.

He doubles over, every movement causing pain in his lower stomach. Magnus is free to whimper and whine and feel sorry for himself. He’s completely alone until Alec gets home, after all.

Alec. Ohhhh no.

Alec could not see him like this. Not yet.

After a while, though, Magnus is far too miserable to think about anything else but his aching belly and what he’d do to that establishment if he still had his magic.

It isn’t long before he’s finally finished. For now, anyway. His stomach still hurts and feels impossibly full, but he can just take this opportunity to lie down and hope for death.

Alec gets home, tired enough not to question Magnus’ lack of affection. He certainly doesn’t feel like cuddling right now, and Alec looks as if he’s moments away from dozing on the couch.

Magnus feels about the same. He’s exhausted. In a perfect world, the two of them would go to bed extra early and snuggle and wake up for an early breakfast...

Not tonight.

-----

“Magnus, are you... okay?”

“Yes!”

“Okay, it’s just that you’ve um, been in there a while and-”

Magnus swears under his breath. “I’m fine!”

“Alright.”

He still feels sick. He sort of just wants to curl up in the corner and wait for all of this to end. Instead, he feels an entirely different kind of unsettle in his stomach seconds before he grabs the trash can and vomits.

Finally, he thinks. That’s what he’s been waiting for since this whole thing started.

There’s quick footsteps and a more insistent knock on the door.

“Magnus?!”

“I’m-” he spits into the bin. “I’m fine, Alec!”

Alec seems to take the hint that he doesn’t exactly want an audience for this, as he hears reluctant footsteps back out to the living room.

Magnus emerges after a while, feeling thoroughly washed out. He must look awful, too, if Alec’s face is anything to go by.

“By the Angel, Magnus...” He immediately stands up and guides him to the couch. Magnus doesn’t have the energy to fight the concern anymore. “Have you been throwing up this whole time?”

Magnus growls. “Not exactly.” He waits for a moment of disgust or awkwardness, but Alec’s expression doesn’t change at his confession of... intestinal distress.

“Ah,” is all Alec says. “Do you think you’re coming down with a bug?”

“I ordered lunch from the chinese restaurant down the road,” Magnus informs with a sigh.

“Oh Magnus...” Alec says, his voice a mix between pity and amusement. Magnus can’t blame him, it was a pretty funny and stupid decision. “Why would you do that to yourself?”

“In my defense, it was delicious until it tore up my insides.”

Alec chuckles and squeezes his shoulder. “Need anything?”

Magnus just admitted one of the grossest personal situations and Alec doesn’t even bat an eye? He must be pretending not to care for Magnus’ benefit, which makes him feel even more self-conscious wondering if Alec is secretly completely unattracted to him now.

“No,” Magnus says. “I’m just... tired.”

Alec hums thoughtfully, looking him over. “When’s the last time you had something to drink?”

Oh.

“Um...”

Alec’s up and off to the kitchen before he finishes admitting that he hasn’t bothered to drink anything since this whole thing started. He returns with a glass of water and a kiss on the forehead.

Magnus finds himself leaning away, even if the affection is the one thing he’s spent the day longing for, he can’t imagine Alec ever wants to be near him again.

Alec pauses, brows furrowed, and sets the water on the coffee table.

“What’s wrong?”

“You’re really not disgusted?”

Alec blinks. “...Because you’re sick?” he asks slowly.

“Yeah, because I’m sick.”

He frowns and sits down next to Magnus. This time Magnus doesn’t lean away. “Am I supposed to be?”

“Honestly? Maybe? This is kind of humiliating.”

Alec chuckles. “Magnus, it’s not that big of a deal. It happens to everyone. Welcome to being human.”

Magnus groans and buries his head in his hands before his boyfriend wraps an arm around him. He ends up leaning into Alec’s side with a heavy sigh.

“Hey,” Alec presses a kiss to his temple and the puts the water in his hand. “Drink some water, okay? I think you’re a little dehydrated.”

Magnus does, taking small sips just as Alec instructs.

He’s learning more and more about being human every day.
