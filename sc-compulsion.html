Compulsion

David grimaces, laying a hand on his slightly-distended stomach. “Uck. I think I ate too much.”

They went to the cafe for lunch, and it was just like any other lunch, but he feels bloated, like he must have overdone it. 

“Yeah right,” Alexis says with a scoff. “You ate what you always eat. You’re fine.”

“Um, then why does my stomach hurt so bad?” he doesn’t bother to sit up to look at her, instead just turning his head and raising his eyebrows expectantly.

“How am I supposed to know that?” Alexis scoffs, and goes back to painting her nails while her dying brother lies in misery.

David rolls his eyes and turns over. He curls up and wraps both arms around his middle as if that will stop the aching. It doesn’t of course, but it’s enough of a placebo for him to convince himself  that he’s feeling better.

The cramps go from dull to sharp and insistent, and that’s when David begins to think he might have a problem. 

Alexis is watching a movie on her computer, headphones in, so he lets himself whimper a little bit. This must be what period cramps are like, he thinks, your insides constricting over and over against your consent.

“Alexis?” David whines. She can’t hear him. “Alexis!”

With a sigh, she removes her earbuds and looks at him with the must bored expression he’s ever seen her muster. “What?”

“Can you get me some tylenol or something? My head hurts...”

Alexis shrugs. “Get it yourself, I’m busy.” And the headphones go back in.

David groans. He would get them himself, he honestly would, but he’s dying! And also very cold for some reason. 

Oh no. 

He has a bad feeling, not just a physical one. 

Where did he put his thermometer? 

His family (mom and Alexis, mostly) made fun of him for taking his temperature regularly. It’s more of a compulsion than anything else, really, his go-to when he’s feeling different. 

Stevie found it in his room one night when they were drinking together, and cracked jokes about it for a solid five minutes.

“Seriously? You’re this much of a control freak that you need to keep track of your body temperature?”

“Okay, first of all, I’m not a control freak, I’m just the only person who knows how to do anything right ever.”

Stevie rolled her eyes, chuckled, and took another sip of her beer.

“Second, it’s normal for one to keep track of one’s vitals.”

“Normal for who?” Stevie asked with a scoff. “Why do you need to keep this thing right by your bed? What-”

“I just need to, okay? I need to know, and I need to have it close. Can we drop it?” David cut her off. He spoke quickly, somehow hoping he’d talk so fast she wouldn’t hear him and she’d just ignore it.

But Stevie was his best friend. She was used to listening to his rapid rambles, even when drunk.

Her face fell, suddenly looking much more sober. A wave of understanding washed over her expression. “Yeah.” she held her hands up in surrender (one empty and one holding a bottle of mediocre beer). “Dropping it.”

David huffed. “Good.”

No no no... where is it? It was in his nightstand last he knew. Where could it have gone? He dug around in the drawer despite the fact it only held a few items. David tries to keep his breath steady, but it’s hard when he’s starting to feel worse by the second. He’ll feel so much better once he just finds it, he tells himself.

The last place he looks is his toiletries container in the bathroom.

Oh thank God. His reflection shows him someone who’s red-faced and breathing heavily, so he looks away before he has to worry about his appearance as well.

David turns it on, slides it under his tongue, and waits. He’s done this countless times before. He sits on the edge of the tub, unable to stay standing another second. He could pass out at any moment, after all. 

It’s probably nothing, David tells himself. He supposedly has a habit of blowing things out of proportion, so this is probably one of those things people tell him about.

He ate too much. He’ll take the rest home next time.

The thermometer beeps and David wastes no time looking at the numbers.

He can’t breathe.

That’s the highest it’s been in years. He can’t... no no no, he can’t breathe. Surely it’s another symptom.

He could be dying right here in this motel bathroom and no one would ever know until Alexis finished her stupid movie.

David covers his mouth to stifle the sob. He isn't even sure why it upsets him so much, can't put his finger on it. All he knows is that there's some sort of virus in his body right now and he can't do anything about it. Oh, that's why he's upset. 

His stomach twists again, reminding him that the worst is yet to come. 

He can't leave. What if he has to throw up? 

Alexis' laptop closes. She gets off the bed and he can hear her put shoes on and grab her jacket. "I'm heading out!" She calls to no one in particular, and then leaves.

So now he's alone. He tries to stay calm but… what if something happens and Alexis isn't there to- actually, what could she do anyway? He laughs to himself.

More than likely his parents are in the next room, but he can't bring himself to ask them for help. He's always just… locked himself away when he's sick. When he was a child he would dread getting sick just because it was so boring and lonely. 

So he just sits on the bathroom floor and waits for the inevitable as he staves off the panic rising by the second. 

It's cold in here, he finds himself shivering despite how hot his skin is. Every shiver reminds him that it's a result of germs in his body and he really wants to be sick now. He's being ridiculous, but he can't stop.

David winces as his stomach gives a sickening gurgle. It's audibly complaining now as if he can do anything about it. He sighs.

Going back to bed would be nice. It's cold and uncomfortable in here… but he needs to stay. Something bad could happen at any moment. 

He presses his arms into his crampy stomach and moans. What if it's appendicitis or e coli or… oh God, maybe he should be going to the hospital.

Davids just starting to consider going to bed, of course that's when his stomach finally decides to rebel. 

Nonono…

He positions himself over the toilet and gags uselessly, and then again. The third time ia more productive, bringing up the half-digested sandwich he ate earlier. He can't take in a breath before he's heaving again, and he can't stop himself from panicking this time. 

He needs help. He needs a ride to the hospital or- or something…

He needs Stevie. Does he? Yeah, that's probably the safest option. Who knows what could happen if he’s left alone.

But he’s throwing up again, and before he knows it, he’s crying. Choking sobs between the heaving. He tries his best to hold it together as his stomach contracts. It’s no use. This is who he is. Pathetic, lonely, and worst of all, disgusting.

The most recent wave of vomiting has left him breathing heavily, throat and stomach both sore. His nose is running, David realizes with horror, and there’s drool all over his chin. He grabs a handful of toilet paper to clean himself up with. Gross.

“David, you here?”

Who the fuck…?

“Stevie?” Oh God, his voice sounds terrible. It breaks halfway through, and he knows it’s a mixture of vomiting and crying. There’s a knock on the door and he panics. “No! Don’t come in here, I’m disgusting.” 

“Oh, sorry. Are you taking a shit or something?”

David pauses. “No.”

“Then can I come in?”

A sigh, more dramatic than usual. “Fine.” 

He struggles to get close enough so he can reach up and open the door. It’s not locked, but he’d prefer to be the one to initiate this. Predictably enough, Stevie chuckles once she gets a view of him.

“Jesus.”

“What do you need?” Despite all of the puking he’s done in the past hour or so, he still feels full, not nearly done. It’s like his stomach is trying to push everything out as efficiently as possible. 

Stevie leans against the doorway, watching him as he backs away from the toilet and sits against the wall. He feels utterly spent.

“Nothing. Well, I ran into Alexis on her way out, and she mentioned that you were complaining about a stomach ache like a baby.” Stevie snorts. “So I figured I’d come check on you after I got off work.” She slides down to the floor next to him, and there’s more sympathy on her face than he’s ever seen. “The flu is going around town.”

“Flu,” David repeats, suddenly feeling short of breath once again. “Like, stomach flu?”

“Mm. It’s pretty nasty. How’re you holding up?”

There’s no way to answer that without sounding like a pathetic bitch. Good thing he’s never cared about being a pathetic bitch.

So instead of an ‘I’m fine’, David just whines and curls in on himself. “I feel really awful,” he whines. He scrubs at his cheeks, still damp from tears, and Stevie’s brows knit in concern.

“Oh my God, have you been crying?”

David scoffs. “No! ...okay, yes. Maybe I just really hate this and I haven’t been this sick in years and my stomach still hurts and… what? Why are you looking at me like that?”

He hates the look of pity on her face. Or maybe that’s just amusement. Instead of acknowledging his bitching, she asks, “Are you gonna puke anytime in the near future?”

David sighs, loud and long. “I don’t know. Probably not… I don’t know.” He certainly feels like it’s… possible. Eventually. 

“Hm, okay, well what we’re gonna do is get you in bed so you can be tragic and comfortable at the same time.”

David’s already shaking his head when she says the word. “No, I’m not about to throw up in my bed.”

“I’ll get you a bucket, idiot. C’mon, get up.”

-----

She somehow manages to get him in bed. He’s started shivering again, and Stevie actually stops him when he reaches for his thermometer. So he just sits back and lets her… take care of him? No, that’s not what this is. But whatever it is, he likes it, even if she’s sitting three feet away.

“You’re not used to this, are you?” 

The question shouldn’t be as much of a surprise as it is. David finds himself thinking long and hard about the answer. “Well, my family isn’t exactly the most nurturing.”

Stevie scoffs as if that’s an understatement.

“I’ll give you the rundown, then. Sip this,” she pulls out the Sprite she stole bought from the vending machine. “Don’t eat anything until you actually feel like it, and just… rest, or whatever.”

“Gee, thank you.”

“I’m just saying! What did you do when you were sick? You know, back when you were stupid rich?”

David shrugs, and then slides further under the covers so he can wrap his arms around his belly without looking pathetic. “I’d stay in my room. Sometimes someone would check on me. Never mom, though. Sometimes dad, never mom. She doesn’t do sick people.”

“Mm. Like you, then.” 

He rolls his eyes. “Okay, yes, maybe we’re a little similar in that respect.” A new cramp makes itself known in his sickly stomach, and he can’t help but whimper until it passes. 

“Well, I’m sorry that you had such a shitty time when you were sick.”

“Me too.”

He isn’t sure exactly how it happens, but somehow his head ends up in her lap, and she’s running her fingers through his hair (completely ruining it, but he decides it’s fine). 

“Gross, I can hear your stomach,” Stevie says with a chuckle.

David whines. “I know, I hate it.”

A few seconds go by with nothing by silence. He wonders if she’s thinking about leaving him to fend for himself, but instead… 

“Do you want me to…y’know.” She gestures vaguely towards his body.

“I don’t know what that means. Are you asking to give me a handjob right now? Because the answer’s no.”

“Oh my God, David. Do you want me to rub your stomach?”

“...Oh. Would- does that help?” His stomach gurgles again, and he tries not to wince or draw attention to it.

Stevie shrugs. “It might.”

“You know what? I’m too weak for critical thinking. Go right ahead.” He pulls up his shirt, just enough so she can decide on her own if she wants contact with his skin. Honestly, the idea of belly rubs has always been appealing, but he’s never really been around someone when he needed it.

Countless memories of drinking too much or food poisoning, ending up alone in his apartment to deal with all of it. He likes having someone around to at least offer.

Stevie’s hands are hesitant at first, slowly pulling up his shirt and splaying a palm above his belly button. “Oh…”

“What?”

“Nothing,” Stevie says, clearing her throat.

“Not nothing! You can’t just do that!”

“You’ve just… you look different than the last time I’ve seen you with your shirt off.”

David lifts his head to specifically look at her. “Did you just call me fat?”

“No! I mean, it’s nice,” he believes her, because there’s a slight smile tugging at her lips that she’s trying to hide.

“Oh.”

Admittedly, he has gained a little bit of chub since they moved into town. Less to do and not nearly as healthy food. He doesn’t mind much as long as his clothes still fit. It’s actually nice being a little… soft.

The belly rub is the most pleasant thing he’s experienced all day.

His stomach might be gurgling angrily, and his head might still hurt and he’s still shivering and tempted to get his thermometer again… but somehow, Stevie’s hands put him to sleep.
