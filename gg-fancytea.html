Fancy Tea

Lorelai was really looking forward to some pancakes and eggs, and ungodly amount of coffee for breakfast. She managed to get dressed and showered in time that morning to make it to Luke’s, she managed to snatch a good table before anyone else.

She was ready to enjoy her breakfast, to have a good day.

So she could do without the chorus of dying sounds coming from behind the counter.

“Hey Luke, do you mind not making that sound while I’m trying to eat?”

He glared at her, coffee pot in one hand and the napkin he’d been coughing into in the other. He looked tired. Luke always looked tired (despite how much he preached about the importance of sleep) but this was a different kind of tired.

“Apologies, Lorelai,” he deadpanned before making his way over to her to refill her coffee cup.

“Wow, third refill and you haven’t even said anything judgmental and rude yet? How’s that cold?”

The hacking soundtrack had been part of the diner for a good week now. Lorelai realized that she spent so much time there these days that she was able to track the progress of the poor guy’s cold. He wasn’t so bad off at first, just a few sniffles here and there, some small coughs. The cold was hardly a cold at all. But the small coughs became long fits of breathless wheezing as he held onto a nearby table for support. Coughs always seemed to linger with him when he was sick, but this was particularly bad.

“Give me time,” he said. “It’s still early.”

Lorelai laughed. “From Luke Danes? That’s unheard of.”

Luke rolled his eyes, and looked like he was about to make some genius comeback before his breath caught. Lorelai knew what was coming, so she averted her attention to her precious cup of coffee while he buried his face in the sleeve of his flannel and coughed into it.

He eventually disappeared back behind the counter and drank from a water bottle he’d apparently been keeping back there for this purpose. It didn’t seem to do much, but hopefully it’d help his throat a little bit.

God, he looked terrible.

“Luke,” Lorelai nodded him back over, and he reluctantly wandered to her with an impatient (yet incredibly tired) look. “Hey, you okay?”

His eyebrows raised. He probably wasn’t expecting that. They were occasionally capable of genuine conversation, especially when the other one wasn’t having a good day. “Yeah, I’m fine,” he dismissed. “Just this cold, it’s sticking around a little longer than planned.”

“Overstaying its welcome,” Lorelai agreed. “But you’re coughing a lot. Have you seen a doctor?”

Luke chuckled. “No, it’s a cold. I don’t need a doctor to tell me to rest and drink plenty of fluids.”

“Dunno, maybe you do. So far I’ve only seen you do one of those things.”

He sighed, not bothering to defend himself. “Well, I’ll be fine.”

She hummed, nodded, and looked him over. “You didn’t sleep great last night, huh?”

He cleared his throat. “No I did not.”

“Aw, Luke...”

“No, don’t-” he winced at the tone in her voice, and was probably about to beg her never to use it on him ever again before he started to cough. The second of a glimpse she got at his face before he hid it in his arm once again was that of exhaustion and annoyance and misery that he was trying so hard to hide.

Lorelai watched with sympathy as he grabbed onto one of the empty chairs at her table and coughed, getting desperate breaths in between that weren’t really breaths at all but wheezes that couldn’t have been getting that much air. His face got red, redder than it had already been. He pulled on the back of the chair as if he were going to sit down.

He collapsed.

The coughing stopped.

Lorelai gasped and shot up from her chair. Everyone else went quiet, a few more gasps. And then sighs of relief as Luke groaned and sat up with a hand to his head.

“Oh my God,” Lorelai gasped, this time in relief.

Luke winced and looked around the diner to find everyone staring at him. “Damn it,” he said gruffly. “Did I pass out?”

“Just for a few seconds,” she said, pulling a piece of hair behind her ear as she knelt down next to him. “Jesus, Luke. No-!” She grabbed his arm as he tried to use the other to pull himself up. “Don’t stand up yet, you idiot, you’ll just black out again.”

Luke sighed, rubbing his head. “Yeah, you’re probably right...”

“You need me to call a doctor?” Ms. Patty asked Luke, who shook his head.

“No, thanks Patty.”

Lorelai scoffed. That was a conversation for a few minutes in the future when Luke wasn’t still confused about the fact that he passed out in the middle of his diner.

He looked so out of it. Lorelai feared a concussion until she realized how red his face was.

She tossed his cap to the side and laid the back of her hand on his forehead, with absolutely no resistance from Luke as he was still trying to catch his breath and gather his bearings.

“Luke, you’re burning up,” she hissed under her breath. The man preached about health all day and then worked with a fever that practically burnt her skin. What an idiot.

“Jesus...” he muttered, as if it was some kind of big inconvenience.

“Okay, okay,” she said to herself as she stood up. “Diner’s closed, everyone out!” Lorelai announced.

And, to her horror, Luke didn’t argue one bit. He just stayed sitting on the floor, coughing weakly into the collar of his shirt as she ushered the concerned townspeople out of the place.

“Think you can get into a chair?”

He thought for a moment, processing the question, and then nodded.

“Alright,” she patted his back and offered a hand as he pulled himself up. He ended up in the chair he was trying to sit in before, still coughing and wheezing. He blinked slowly and kept his eyes on the table. “Dizzy?” she asked.

Luke gave a careful nod. “Very.”

“Mm’kay, see, that’s something you could have acknowledged before you fainted.”

“I didn’t faint,” he argued weakly.

“You one-hundred percent fainted, sweetie. There’s a whole diner that can confirm that for you.”

He huffed. Coughed again.

“You got a thermometer? Like, one that you don’t impale a chicken breast with?”

“Uh,” he thought about it. “Yeah, upstairs, in the bathroom cabinet. I think it still works.”

“Cool, gotcha, okay. Don’t pass out again while I’m gone.” She squeezed his shoulder, trying not to let his smoldering body heat concern her too much as she went up to Luke’s apartment.

The thermometer was in the bathroom cabinet, behind about a thousand expired medicines and box of band aids that may have been from the dark ages. She pushed the button on the device and was delighted to hear it beep and turn it on the way that they’re supposed to.

Luke was doubled over in a coughing fit when she returned. He hardly had a chance to take a breath, and for a moment she was afraid he was going to puke. So, she said,

“You’re not gonna be sick, right? Because I don’t feel like cleaning up vomit today.”

By the unsure look on his face, it wouldn’t be the first time he’s coughed himself sick this week, but he shook his head.

“I’ll be sure to warn you,” he deadpanned. He sounded tired, his words slurring together like he’d downed ten beers while she was gone. “Look, you really don’t have to-”

“Sh.”

“I’ll just go upstairs and sleep this off.”

Lorelai sat down and pulled the chair closer to him until their knees were practically touching, effectively locking him in place lest he try to stand up. “I mean, you can try...” she said as she pushed a button on the thermometer. “But I doubt you’re gonna get much sleep with that cough.”

He sighed out through his nose and nodded admittedly. If anything else wasn’t a sign that he hadn’t been sleeping, that certainly was.

“Let’s just take your temp. Give me this, okay? Peace of mind or whatever.”

“Fine.”

“I’m guessing by the delving mission I had to go on to find this thing that you haven’t bothered to check your temperature since you’ve been sick.”

Luke muffled a deep, horrible cough into his arm. “I don’t see the point. Not like it’s gonna help any.”

“God, men...” she muttered, before holding it out to him. “Under your tongue until it beeps.”

Luke did so (and Lorelai had to admit that a compliant Luke was both concerning and very cute). He had to try three separate times, because his lungs demanded his attention and he had to pull the thing out of his mouth to cough, twisting away from Lorelai each time.

He was so tired of the stupid thermometer by the time it beeped that he just handed it off to her and started coughing again. Lorelai patted his back lamely as she read the number.

She stopped patting his back, and stared at him. It took him a bit to look up and see her expression, but when he did, his brows furrowed in confusion.

“What?” he asked, a sharpness on the ‘t’ as if he expected her answer to be ridiculous.

"Okay, Luke... you're about to hate me more than you ever have."

His eyes widened in realization, and then narrowed. "Don't you dare."

"But we're going to the hospital. Right now.” She was already grabbing her purse, and then picking up his discarded hat off the floor.

He chuckled, which came out as more of a wheeze. “No we are not.”

“You have a fever of 103, and- yeah yeah, I know - but a fever like that proves that this is not a cold.” She stood up, and grabbed his arm to pull him to his feet as well. “C’mon, we’re going now.”

He tried to protest, that she had to be to work and could drive himself! Lorelai didn’t trust the man who could hardly walk on his own to drive all the way to the hospital, especially when his respiratory system had given up on the world.

Still, Luke allowed her to shove him into her car.

All of the movement seemed to have irritated his lungs, because he began to cough immediately, desperate breaths in between. Lorelai reached across and pulled some napkins out of the glove compartment when it was clear he was actually coughing something up. Gross.

"Hang in there," she said softly, patting his leg.

"I'm fine," he sputtered out between coughs.

"Clearly." And they were on their way.

-----

“Luke.”

“Hm?”

“You’re causing an earthquake.”

“Oh.” His knee stopped bouncing. He cleared his throat and pulled himself to sit straighter in the chair. “Sorry. I hate hospitals.”

“I know.”

His eyes darted around the room like a wild animal. “We should just go.”

“You already gave them your name, they can track you down.”

“Do they do that?”

“Do you really want to find out?”

He huffed, which just sparked another fit from his lungs and got the attention of everyone else in the waiting room. Lorelai rubbed his back, feeling his muscles convulse with every cough. He could hardly get a breath in without coughing, and the worst part was that he didn’t sound much worse than he had the last couple of days. How long had Luke needed to see a doctor?

“Luke Danes?”

He stood up, still coughing, and glanced back at the door as if he were ready to run the other way. Lorelai corralled him in the direction of the nurse.

“You want me to come with you?”

His eyes were still wide with an adorable childlike fear, and he nodded minutely.

So, Lorelai did.

The last time she was at the emergency room was when Rory broke her wrist, and that was... a bad night. The time before that was when Rory had food poisoning. She was so weak and tired that Lorelai completely freaked out and forced her kid into the car. It turned out to be nothing.

She’d never accompanied a grown man to the ER, and that was turning out to be a different experience.

Luke coughed, and coughed, sitting on the edge of the bed with his shoulders hunched. The nurse had told him that he could lie down, but when he tried that he just ended up coughing more.

“Do I even have to say it?” Lorelai sighed out.

Even with watery eyes, he glared at her. “I don’t know what you’re about to say but I’d rather that you- you didn’t-” he broke off coughing again.

“You should’ve gotten checked out sooner.”

A groan, more coughing.

“I mean, it probably wouldn’t be so bad if-”

“Quit nagging me, please,” he rasped. “I’m really not in the mood.”

“Obviously,” Lorelai rolled her eyes.

They waited a long time before the doctor came and looked him over. He listened to Luke’s lungs, took his temperature, all the normal doctor things before the diagnosis was given.

Luke was left with several prescriptions and an intense scowl on his face.

“Pneumonia,” he scoffed as soon as they got in the car. “How in the hell?”

“Mm, I dunno. By not going to the doctor, maybe?”

He ignored her, fiddling with the brightly-colored paper bracelet on his wrist as some coughs bubbled up from his chest. She was surprised he hadn’t demanded to cut it off the moment they walked out of that building.

Lorelai sighed heavily as she started the car. She was tired, hospital ordeals were always exhausting. And if she was tired, Luke was probably ready to collapse by now.

“Pharmacy, then we get you to bed.”

“God, I hate pharmacies.”

“Those too, huh?”

Luke didn’t answer.

“So, do you have a humidifier?”

“No,” he said with a tone that implied that was the most ridiculous question on the planet.

“Mm’kay, I’ll bring ours over later.”

“Lorelai...”

“What?” She was ready for him to complain again. Instead,

“You don’t have to do all this.”

She chuckled. “Trust me, I’d much rather be at work bossing around florists right now, but I want to do this.” Plenty of other people would have been able to. Despite his prickly personality, Luke had plenty of people he could rely on. Just... probably not many he could tolerate. “Look, it’s fine. You’d do the same for me, or for Rory, right? Actually, you did after my dad’s heart attack. Remember?”

He nodded. “Yeah, I...” a cough. “Thanks.”

“No problem, Luke.” The moment was a little too sincere for her in the middle of the day, so she decided that she needed to hear herself talk. “Okay, we should start making the mental shopping list now. Do you have tylenol or anything at your place?”

“Uhh...”

“I’m gonna take that as a no. Tylenol, maybe some tea? Do you have tea?”

Luke gave a wheezing breath and cleared his throat. “I have tea.”

“No, like, the fancy herbal kind that’s good for-”

“Yes, I have the fancy herbal tea.”

She glanced at him, wondering if he was pulling her leg, but the look on his face was embarrassment fair and simple. “Huh. You really haven’t been feeling good.”

He didn’t answer, he also didn’t deny it. The fact that he wasn’t in great shape was abundantly clear the moment he passed out in the middle of the diner anyway.

Lorelai made sure that their trip to the pharmacy was the shortest one in existence, and probably for the best as the other customers were starting to get annoyed with Luke’s constant coughing. The poor man could hardly tell the pharmacist his name and date of birth without hacking up a lung.

They walked out with antibiotics and an inhaler that Luke rolled his eyes at. Lorelai could tell that he was going to be a fantastic patient.

The climb up to his apartment was even more of an adventure. The wheezes got louder with each step, and he had a white-knuckled grip on the railing.

“Okay, okay,” Lorelai said, rubbing his arm as he coughed and tried to catch his breath at the same time. He sounded dangerously close to coughing himself sick, and that was the last thing either of them needed to deal with today. “Take a break,” she took grabbed hold of his arm and gently pulled him down. “C’mon, sit.”

He looked impossibly annoyed with himself.

Lorelai laid a hand on his back, and that probably did nothing to help. “You okay? Do you need that inhaler thing?”

He shook his head, but his was still sputtering with chesty coughs. After a couple minutes of wheezing, still unable to get a full breath in, he nodded and held a hand out in a pleading gesture.

“Okay, alright...” She tore open the pharmacy bag and managed to put the thing together just the way the pharmacist had instructed. She probably knew how to do it better than Luke did, since the guy couldn’t stop coughing long enough to pay attention.

Luke coughed harshly as he took it from her.

“Deep breath, and hold it,” she said softly.

He did so. And they sat (relatively) silent as the coughing tapered off and his airways gave him a break.

Luke looked so tired of today. “This is your fault,” he muttered hoarsely, before pulling himself up back onto the railing and starting his slow ascent to his apartment.

Lorelai scoffed, laughed. “My fault! How do you figure that?”

“You took me to,” wheeze. “the doctor. None of this,” wheeze. “would have been a problem if I stayed in,” wheeze. “blissful ignorance.”

She snorted. “Yeah, ‘cause you would’ve been dead. Soupy phlegm, totally filling your lungs. Do you like lung soup, Luke? ‘cause that’s what was gonna happen.”

He growled, took another step. “Please, never say lung soup ag- again-” he broke off into a coughing fit, but thankfully this one didn’t sound like he was in danger of passing out.

“Take it easy, you can chew me out when we’re up these stairs.”

A huff, and he continued.

Luke gravitated toward his bed the moment they walked in. Lorelai couldn’t blame him.

She set the pharmacy stuff on the table to be looked over later, and began snooping through the cupboards in search of fancy herbal tea. Luke was in the process of coughing and trying to get his shoes off at the same time. When it came to the rest of his clothes, he seemed concerned with modesty at first and then soon gave up.

Lorelai looked away as he shoved his jeans off of himself, busying herself with finding his mugs and filling on with water.

“I’m assuming you don’t have a tea kettle,” she said, even as she stuck the mug in the microwave.

“You’re assuming correctly,” Luke replied with a wheezy chuckle. He was in sweats and a T-shirt when Lorelai looked back at him. He looked comfortable and rather handsome, although he looked rather handsome in anything.

“So,” she read the instructions on the box of tea that was already half gone. “If you need Rory and I to help out at the diner tomorrow...”

“Naw, that’s alright.”

“Luke,” she huffed and turned back to him. “You’re not seriously about to tell me that you’re going to work tomorrow.”

“No, I’m not,” he said. “I’ll stay closed tomorrow.”

Lorelai breathed a sigh of relief. She thought she was going to have to fight the guy. She might actually be able to take him, though, in his weakened state.

“Good,” she said pointedly. “Something that you should’ve done a week ago.”

“Yeah, well...” a sudden, harsh cough, this time muffled into a tissue. “Town’s gotta eat.”

“The town can suck it up and eat anywhere else so you don’t die of pneumonia.”

He made a noise of agreement.

The microwave beeped, and Lorelai dipped the tea bag in. The box had a specific steeping time, but she doubted that Luke paid attention to the stuff. She brought the mug to him and sat next to him on the edge of the bed.

“What?” he muttered, even while nodding his thanks for the tea. Lorelai, admittedly, was staring.

He looked pale and tired and sick, his face turned a horrible color during the particularly intense coughing fits and it terrified her every time. She was scared to leave him alone, as her imagination ran wild with the thought of the guy dying alone up here in his dreary apartment. She didn’t say any of that.

“You just look bad.”

He rolled his eyes. “Thanks.”

“No problem. Don’t you wanna lie down?”

He shook his head and bobbed the tea bag up and down. “Makes me cough more. I’ll get situated in a minute.” Luke looked down at the tea for a moment, and then back up to Lorelai. “Thanks. For all this. I don’t know if I’ve said that yet.”

Lorelai couldn’t help but smile. “You did, but I’ll take it again.” She squeezed his shoulder, hand coming in contact with the fever that radiated from him, even through the fabric of his shirt. “Why don’t I stack your pillows, make it easier for you to sleep?”

She didn’t give him a chance to answer before she was arranging his pillows properly, the way she used to do for little Rory when she had a cough and couldn’t sleep at night.

Luke was practically asleep where he sat, eyes drifting closed every so often as he held the warm mug in his hands.

“So... are you good? For now, I mean? Because I will be back tonight, Luke Danes. You won’t be able to get rid of me after this whole debacle.”

He gave a sigh (which was barely a sigh when his breath crackled in his airways). “I’m good. But you don’t have to-”

“Shut up. I’ll be by after work with food.”

“Wha- I have food.”

“Not Sookie’s sick day soup!” Lorelai argued. She stood, and frowned at the guy who was seconds away from falling asleep and ending up with a lap full of tea.

He chuckled as she gently took it from him and set it on the nightstand. And Lorelai only hesitated for a moment before pressing a quick kiss to his forehead. He didn’t flinch one bit.

“Get under the covers you’re shivering,” she instructed, and he began to with a nod. “Don’t forget to take another dose of tylenol in a few hours! Get some sleep!”

“Thanks, mother,” he muttered, not unkindly, as he got comfortable.

Lorelai laughed and left the apartment.

She would be back later, force food into him, and then announce that she was sleeping on his couch tonight.

He’d argue, call her a crazy person, and tell her where the extra blankets were.

Neither of them would get any sleep with his ridiculous coughing, and they’d stay up all night chatting sleepily.

But right now, Lorelai was just going to go to work and think about today.
