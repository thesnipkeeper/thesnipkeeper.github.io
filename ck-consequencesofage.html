Consequences of Age

A soft knock on the door of the ensuite he’d shut himself in early that morning. Daniel lifts his head, blinking the fuzz from his vision as he slowly realizes that he’d fallen asleep seated in the corner where the bathtub and wall meet. He sniffs, scrubs his face, accomplishes nothing in the process.

“Mm?” He lacks real words at the moment, and probably for the best.

“It’s me, babe. I bring water and tylenol. Can I come in?”

“Mm’hm.” He drags a hand down his face once again, muffling a cough into it in the process. He’s probably covered in germs, he thinks, and so is the bathroom. He and Amanda are far beyond caring about germs now, though, which is good because his wife’s presence is the only thing to make him happy today.

The door squeaks open and Amanda enters with care. “How are we feeling?” she asks, and then before he can answer, “Were you sleeping?”

“Apparently,” and there is where what he already knew is confirmed, that his voice is nonexistent. His throat stings, itches, makes him cough on his poor wife as she’s handing him the water bottle. She doesn’t acknowledge it.

She makes a sympathetic sound as she kneels down next to him, pressing the back of her hand to his forehead and temple. “You feel hotter than you did.”

He huffs and shakes his head. “I don’t get it. The kids didn’t have it this bad.”

She smiles, a small chuckle. “I know, babe, I know. Guess you’re just unlucky.” And getting old went unsaid, thankfully.

He sniffs. His head feels heavy and full of gunk. His entire body feels heavy and full of gunk.

And he can’t help but be a little bitter about this flu ruining his plans for his day. He was actually going to get some stuff done at work before he woke up coughing (which then turned into puking, and then more puking).

Amanda had to help him to the bathroom because he was too damn dizzy to get out of bed himself, and now he’s too damn dizzy to move his head.

“You want the pills?”

“If they’ll stay down,” he says tragically.

“We can try.” She presses them into his hand and then kisses his cheek.

He makes a lame effort to move away, making a noise somewhere between a growl and a whine that can’t be discernible as either because he’s so hoarse. “Don’t do that, you’re gonna catch it.”

“If I was going to catch it, I would’ve by now, my love,” she says, sounding a little too smug for his liking. “Take your medicine and let me put you to bed.”

-----

Someone has to be blamed, Johnny decides, and that someone is Miguel. He spent all of last week talking about his poor sick girlfriend and how much he missed her and how he took care of her when she came back to school still sort of sick.

Miguel never once caught whatever bug Sam had. Of course he didn’t, he’s a healthy young person after all.

Johnny is neither healthy nor young.

The kid’s a carrier, plain and simple.

Johnny blows a flood of gunk out of his nose and tosses the useless tissue in the waste bin. He shudders and zips his hoodie the last two inches that were left to be zipped, making him look like the biggest loser on the planet. Jesus he's cold.

He wants nothing more right now than to be at home, drinking beer until he doesn’t care about how sick he is anymore. Maybe he’d even pass out until he was healthy again.

But what message would that send to the pussies that he was trying to train up? Let your weakness control you? Hell no.

As much as he would hate to admit it, colds and flus have hit him harder the older he gets. To be knocked on your ass from a simple case of the sniffles is beyond humiliating for a grown man.

Johnny hacks, doesn’t bother to cover it because it’s his own damn office and his students are the reason he’s sick anyway. He sniffs. His nose isn’t running, but it’s still clogged to hell and no amount of blowing can do anything about that.

He’s going to spend the whole class struggling to pronounce his consonants. Struggling to make his voice sound normal at all with his sore throat.

He’s decided not to acknowledge the unsettled feeling in his stomach. That’s a problem for another time.

“Sensei?”

Johnny’s heart jumps out of his chest. “Jesus, Miguel.”

Miguel starts to chuckle and thinks better of it. “I said ‘Sensei’ like three times before you finally heard me.” He notices the state of the room immediately, covered in used tissues, cough drops, and a bottle of cold medicine that Johnny may or may not have been sipping from. “You’re sick?”

Johnny’s done very little that day except sit and stare off and think about how shitty he feels. He can’t even begin to deny his illness.

“Yes,” he answers. “And it is your fault.”

“Mine? Why?”

“You’re a plague rat, that’s why. Bringing your damn germs into my dojo and-”

“But I haven’t been-!” Miguel stops himself. “Oh.”

Johnny scoffs, and then coughs horribly. “Yeah, oh.”

Miguel winces, and to his credit offers a careful, “Sorry, Sensei.”

He rolls his eyes. “Nothing we can do about it now.”

“Okay but-” Miguel pauses at the glare Johnny gives him, and then decides to continue. “If you do have what Sam had, then you should definitely be-”

“If you say ‘in bed’, you’re going to be today’s practice dummy.”

His mouth snaps shut, nods.

“Good. Go get ready for class.”

Miguel does so, and Johnny lets out a horrendous sneeze the second he’s out of the office.

It’s going to be a long day.

-----

Daniel tries to go to sleep. He’s tired, he knows he’s tired, and yet his body can’t grant him the simple mercy of being unconscious while he’s afflicted with this plague of a flu. He tosses and turns, coughs a whole lot now that he’s horizontal.

He sends a few e-mails, and tries to make one phone call before he realizes that using his voice is not a productive use of his energy.

He’d convinced Amanda that she could go to work, and now he’s regretting it. Not because he’s so sick that he needs someone looking after him every second, but because he’s bored. He’s not used to sitting and getting nothing done. It used to drive her crazy how he’d run around and work no matter how sick he was. She was the one who showed him the appeal of staying still and letting his body rest long enough to recover.

Doesn’t make said resting any less of a struggle.

He should have asked Amanda to send some things her way, but her day is full of meetings anyway. She doesn’t need her poor pathetic husband bugging her about whatever ridiculous request he has.

He sits up, and when that doesn’t immediately make the room spin, continues to get up until he’s on his feet.

He needs to do something.

-----

He really thinks that he’ll be okay. Sure, his vision’s swimming a little and he has to hang onto the wall and anything else to keep himself upright. Sure every movement makes him want to puke, and sure, he can’t really breathe without coughing or sniffling and disgusting half his students.

And it is okay, up until he has to run out of training mid-sentence to avoid painting the floor with his stomach’s contents.

“Sensei?”

Johnny groans at the voice on the other side of the door. He’s been hunched over the toilet for a half hour now, and he’s listened to Miguel continue the class for him like the Godsend he is.

He pulls himself to his feet with as much strength as he can muster, which - unfortunately - isn’t much, and opens the door to be face-to-face with the one to blame for all of this.

Miguel swallows when he gets a look at him. Johnny isn’t a fan of receiving such a pitying, guilty look from one of his own students.

“Sensei...” he begins, looking as if he’s rehearsed this. “I think you should go home. We can take care of things here, and I can lock up if you need me to.” He fidgets slightly and then stops himself and continues to hold his ground.

Johnny leans heavily against the door frame and sighs. He feels washed out and exhausted. He feels like he’s been run over by a semi, like he could pass out at any moment. This flu is no joke, apparently, because Johnny finds himself nodding.

“Fine, yeah,” he swallows against the sour taste of vomit in his mouth. His breath must smell like shit. “I’m heading home. Lock up, don’t do anything I wouldn’t do.”

Miguel breathes a sigh of relief and nods.

Johnny coughs harshly as the cold air hits his lungs, and then he doesn’t stop coughing until he’s been sitting in his care for five minutes waiting for it to warm up.

The desire to go home and pass out on the couch is strong until he remembers that he has nothing for being sick. Not Tylenol, not a single cough drop, and no hard liquor. All of that should be solved before he gets home and he’s too sick to get up again.

So Johnny finds himself in the middle of CVS, comparing cold and flu meds. He’s already found his liquor and a bottle of the kind of cough syrup that could probably get him just as drunk. He feels irritation creep up when someone dares to occupy the same aisle as him.

He doesn’t look up, just keeps reading the labels as he listens to the intruder give a cough that’s almost as impressive as his own. The poor guy must have it just as bad. Johnny can’t help but think the other man should be at home in bed until he remembers that oh, yeah, he’s here too.

“Anything I can help you with?” Johnny recognizes that as the store clerk he simply grunted at when he was asked that same question on arrival.

“No, thanks. I think I’ve got it.”

Wait a minute-

“Larusso?”

There stands Daniel Larusso, in sweats and a hoodie, nose red and chafed as he runs what looks like a well-used tissue under it. He looks just as bad as Johnny feels, maybe even worse.

“Johnny,” he acknowledges tiredly, in a voice that’s clogged with congestion. “You too, huh?”

Johnny nods. “No thanks to your kid.”

“My kid?” Daniel spits in offense. “What-?”

“Miguel.”

“Ah. Yeah. Well,” he was probably going to say something snarky, but he ends up coughing instead. His face is pinched in pain as the coughs scrape against his throat, and Johnny almost winces in sympathy. “Jesus... I should go.” He scans the shelves for only a second before grabbing a specific box of flu medicine.

“Is-” Johnny clears his throat out of necessity than anything else, lest he start coughing to. “Is that the good stuff?”

Daniel glances at the box and then at the stuff in Johnny’s basket. “Probably not as good as what you’ve got there, but I’d say it works.”

Johnny grabs one for himself.

They end up at the checkout at the same time, Larusso with his lotion-y tissues and Johnny with his get-too-wasted-to-feel-sick pack. They’re both coughing and sniffling enough to sound like plague victims. No doubt the whole store is going to get sanitized after they leave, and Johnny wouldn’t even blame them.

The cashier gives him his total, and just as he’s about to hand the man a wad of cash he’d scrounged up for this purpose, Larusso leans over him and swipes his card.

Johnny stares at him, brows furrowed in confusion.

“For my kid, I guess,” Daniel shrugs. “Take care of yourself, Johnny.”

Johnny scoffs. “Thanks. You too, Larusso.”
