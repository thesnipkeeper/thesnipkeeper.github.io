Title: Progress
Fandom: Scorpion
Summary: Walter's getting a cold and the others make it easier for him to admit
Word count: 1k

Progress

Toby was so preoccupied trying to keep his fiancée from falling to death that he almost didn’t notice the way that Walter’s voice broke as he spoke to the others over the comms. Or the liquid at the base of his nostrils.

“Walt,” Toby said, getting his friend’s attention.

Walter looked up expectantly. “What-?”

“You’ve got a little...” Toby pointed to his own nose.

Walter averted his eyes and scrubbed the moisture away with his sleeve in a way that would have Sylvester screaming in horror. And just like that, he was back in the game.

But Toby saw the signs. It was his job to see the signs, right? Signs like the fact that Walter’s voice was starting to sound thicker, or his sniffles that were becoming more frequent. He gave the occasional cough and would wince afterward. He was getting sick.

It wasn’t important, but it was something Toby couldn’t help but see. The others had to have noticed as well, they weren’t that dense.

It wasn’t until after everything was done and everyone was safe that Toby decided to mention it.

“You sound like you’re getting a cold.” It was casual as ever, but Walter had to have known that he’d been made a long time ago.

Walter sniffled and dragged his sleeve under his nose once again as the two of them made their way down the stairs. “Yeah, it’s- it’s annoying.”

And that was all that they spoke about it before they got to the others. Toby gave his girl a big hug, and tried to ignore the serial throat clearing from his friend behind him.

-----

Paige and Happy looked at the others as they sat.

“It’s nice to just be able to sit after hanging a hundred feet in the air,” Paige mused.

Happy made a noise of agreement, folding her arms and leaning against the inside of the ambulance. Both their attention was drawn to Walter, who was muffling a sneeze into his elbow. Paige winced.

“He doesn’t sound good.”

“Eh, he’ll be fine. Colds never deter him.”

It was true, the few times that Walter had been sick since Paige started work at Scorpion had been mild. Or, he just worked through them enough that they seemed mild.

Walter sneezed again, which in turn made him cough. Poor guy.

-----

Paige really didn’t think that Walter would be volunteering to do anything when he was sick, and yet he pulled up a chair to help her the moment that she agreed. She decided to ignore how delighted it made her feel.

They worked for a bit, discussing aspects of the wedding. It was clear that their mutual goal was to take the burden off of Toby and Happy when really none of them (except for maybe Paige) knew what they were doing.

“Are you and Happy, uh, okay?”

Paige smiled. Asking about other people was new territory for him, and yet he didn’t let his hesitance get the better of him. “Yeah, we’re fine.”

“Good.” Walter nodded. “Good. Conflict is bad for efficiency.” He went back to looking through his file of Dude of Honor duties, giving a sniffle as he did so. Paige had almost forgotten that he had a cold.

She couldn’t help but chuckle. “Can’t disagree, but we could also say that it’s bad for friendships?” ish, she added in her head with a smile.

He nodded. “That too.” another sniffle, this one thicker.

Paige wordlessly took the tissue box on her desk and set it next to him, watching him with sympathy as he cleared his throat and pulled one out.

“Thanks. Sorry, I promise that I’ll keep my germs to myself.”

“I hope you tell Sylvester that, because he might show up in hazmat suit tomorrow.”

Walter’s chuckle was muffled through the tissue he had clamped to his nose. “Again,” he added before turning away from her and ridding his sinuses of the gunk clogging it. “Ugh...” he threw it in the nearest bin and squirted on some sanitizer.

“You know, we can do this tomorrow. Why don’t you get some rest?”

Walter paused, and shook his head with a sniff. His nose was starting to turn pink. “Going to bed earlier isn’t necessary. I’ll just sleep a little later tomorrow.”

“Okay...” Paige said. “Well, I appreciate the help, anyway.”

And she continued to appreciate it until he was sniffling non-stop. She was suddenly very glad that she refilled the office’s tissue supply, because he was going to need a lot of them. They were discussing seating when he pulled another tissue and blew his nose. She didn’t miss the groan he gave before tossing it.

“I think it would be a good idea to- to...” he trailed off.

“Gotta sneeze?” Paige asked, both amused and sympathetic.

He nodded slowly and pressed the back of his hand against his nostrils, which did nothing to stifle the multiple sneezes.

“Okay, Walter,” Paige sighed. “I think it’s time to put you to bed.”

Walter shook his head as he tended to his cold. “I’m perfectly capable of helping despite the cold. It’s more efficient for us to work on this together.”

“You know what I think, though? I think it’s inefficient for you to be helping while you’re sick. C’mon,” she poked at him. “Off to bed.”

“I want to help.”

And Paige saw the desperation. He’d been working so hard on communication and friendship, and he didn’t want to let the cold sabotage any progress they’d made in their friendship. She had to make sure that he knew going to bed wasn’t going to undo anything.

“You’re as bad as Ralph, sometimes,” she said in faux-exasperation. “I can never get him to rest when he’s sick. He always has some kind of project. We can work while you’re in bed. At least then Toby won’t blame me for working you to the bone.”

Walter smiled. “Understood.”

He fell asleep minutes after he got in bed. She made sure there was water and tylenol on his nightstand. By the gurgly snores coming from him, he wasn’t in for much of a better day in the morning, but at least he was resting.
