Title: Coast is Clear
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Nott accuses Molly of Poking at Caleb and then finds out something new
Word count: 400

Coast is Clear

Caleb stood silently next to Nott and Molly, looking through Frumpkin's eyes, hearing through his ears, looking to make sure the coast was clear ahead. His hand, clamped tightly on Nott's shoulder, felt quite warm - almost stiflingly so in the heat of Nicodranas.

Molly leaned over and looked at Caleb's eyes, and then turned to Nott.

"He's been very quiet today," Molly said as casually as ever, even leaning on Caleb's shoulder and earning an annoyed grunt from the blind wizard.

Nott shrugged. "He gets that way, you know."

The truth was that Caleb had been coming down with a cold for the better part of a day. He hadn't said as much, but he didn't deny anything when Nott mentioned his runny nose, nor did he try to hide when he blew his nose in his old, wrinkled kerchief as they got ready for the day. He'd ordered a hot drink at the tavern that morning, and Molly was right, he was talking much less because - Nott knew - his throat was sore and his scratchy voice would give him away.

Molly hummed thoughtfully and stood up straight before giving the wizard a quick glance. Nott hadn't realized what he was doing until he had already pulled a colorfully-patterned kerchief from his coat. She looked up and saw a shiny pool beneath Caleb's nostrils. While he could probably feel it, it was the last of his priorities while he was in Frumpkin.

"What are you doing?" Nott asked, almost accusingly.

Molly ignored her and began gently dabbing at Caleb's nose and upper lip.

The wizard jumped, gasped, blindly batting at Molly's hand.

Molly retracted his hand with a smirk, folding his kerchief over the damp area. "There we go. Much better," he gently picked up Caleb's hand and pressed the handkerchief into his palm before closing his fingers around it. Caleb, only furrowing his brow, was obviously focused on other things, likely wouldn't acknowledge what was happening until he came back.

"Don't just go poking at him!"

"I'm not."

Nott scoffed. The nerve. She hated when the others messed with Caleb while he couldn't see or hear.

The hand on Nott's shoulder gripped harder as Caleb's eyes came back with a gasp. "Coast is clear," he breathed out congestedly. He gave a momentary glance at the cloth in his hand and looked at Molly. "What..."

"Your nose was running," Was all Molly offered, and Nott was ready for Caleb to chew him out.

But instead, "Ah." as the wizard pressed the cloth to his nostrils and wiped away the excess liquid Molly hadn't gotten to.

Molly hummed before pressing a kiss to Caleb's temple. "I think you're catching a cold, my dear."

"Mm. It's not so bad. Just some sniffles, really." Nott's eyes widened. The last person she expected to admit it to was Mollymauk. "What are we doing standing around? Let's go."
