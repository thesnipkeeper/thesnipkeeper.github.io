Title: Warm and Hot
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David's got a sinus infection
Word count: 1.3k

Warm and Hot

“Coming to a town barbecue would be a lot nicer if I could taste anything,” says David with a deep snuffle.

“We didn’t have to come,” Patrick reasons.

“Yes, but it’s still a barbecue, so-” David stops to clear his throat. He’s had a cold for a week now. They thought he was getting better until he woke up with a newly-stuffed nose.

Patrick chuckles. “You just want to grab the food and go, don’t you?”

“Absolutely. I have no plans to socialize with the townsfolk.”

Around them are people from Schitt’s Creek and Elmdale talking and laughing and eating. Every once in a while they have to dodge an excited child running across the park.

There’s various people using cheap dollar store fans to cool themselves off. It’s hot, even in the shade, but everyone obviously wants to take advantage of the summer. The annual BBQ wasn’t going to be postponed because of a heat wave.

David muffles a sneeze into the sleeve of his sweater, recovering with a liquid sniff.

“David, maybe you should take that off. I’m kinda afraid you’re going to get heatstroke.”

David shakes his head. “I’m fine. I’ll stay in the shade.”

Patrick sighs.

They eventually find the rest of the Roses, looking significantly out of place in the middle of a small town event. They take a seat at their picnic table.

“Well, you’re looking better!” Mr. Rose says to David.

“Yeah,” David clears his throat. “Well, I spent enough time trying to make myself look better this morning, so thank you. Good to know I’ve looked terrible all week.”

Mr. Rose raises an eyebrow, and Patrick just smiles and runs a hand down David’s back. “David’s still not feeling great. We’ll probably just stay long enough to eat.”

“Ah, going home after this?”

“Nope. We have the store to open.”

Patrick nods, and sighs. He never imagined dealing with a sick David could be so tiring. It isn’t even that he’s had to take care of him, it’s just been an annoying cold so he’s been perfectly capable of functioning. But David’s been grumpy, tired. Patrick knows it’s because he’s spent half the week unable to eat anything without pain because of his sore throat, and the other half trying and failing to breathe through his nose (with sneezes scattered through both halves).

David had finally started acting like his old self yesterday, obviously feeling well enough to criticize Patrick’s choice of lotion placement.

And then this morning happened. Patrick woke to his fiance blowing his nose in the bathroom, sounding more stuffed up than he should have been. A cold relapse?

They get their food and eat in relative silence. At least David’s sore throat is gone, Patrick thinks. The only thing worse than sick David is hangry David.

He snuffles his way through a burger, and then a hotdog. He yawns at one point, and Patrick notices something a little concerning.

“David,”

“Hm?”

“Are you... shivering?”

David goes to answer, but has to clear his throat again. “I’m just a little cold,” he says with a reluctant nod.

“Honey, it’s way too hot out here for you to be feeling a little cold. And you’re wearing a sweater.”

David shrugs, but in the way Patrick knows he’s aware of what’s going on. Patrick sighs and lays a hand on David’s forehead before he can pull away. It’s dry, and warm. Not how someone should feel in the middle of the summer heat.

“You feel warm,” Patrick realizes with a frown.

“It’s just a cold, though,” David sniffles again.

“Uh huh, okay David.”

-----

It’s not just a cold. It’s a sinus infection. The doctor in Elmdale prescribes antibiotics and recommends a decongestant as well. David is very Unhappy with the situation.

“Why does this happen to me? What did I do to deserve this sort of targeted suffering?” David mutters as they walk through the pharmacy parking lot. He still manages to flail his hands as he talks, so at least he’s well enough for that.

But Patrick sees the exhaustion on his face, in the way he moves. He realizes that David’s been sick for a week and has still worked every single day.

“Why don’t I drive you to my apartment? You can take the day off.”

David raises an eyebrow, staring momentarily over the top of the car before they get in. “I- you don’t have to do that,” he says, shaking his head.

“I think a fever is a good reason to skip out on work for a day,” Patrick shrugs.

“Well, if you insist. I guess I’ll take a day off to... recover from this deadly illness,” he smirks.

Patrick returns the smile before giving him a quick kiss, only to have his chin gently nudged away.

David’s eyes are wide. “Are you crazy? We already have one plague victim,” he says as he gestures to himself. “Do you want this too?”

“We’ve been sharing a bed for the past week, so I think if I was going to get sick, I already would have by now.”

David blinks. “So you choose to kiss me? When I have snot and phlegm and...” he shakes his head again. “Nevermind.”

-----

It’s two hours before closing when Patrick realizes that something’s wrong.

There’s a scratchiness in the back of his throat that no amount of tea can soothe away, and a heavy feeling in his limbs. He looks at the boxes of merchandise that should be unpacked, but the idea of merely carrying out into the store sounds like so much work. He’ll save that work for later, Patrick decides. It’s not often that he chooses to be lazy, but he thinks he’s earned it for today.

The sleepiness he’s been feeling all day makes a lot of sense now. He’s getting David’s cold.

Patrick drags himself through the rest of the day and locks the store the moment the clock strikes five.

He muffles a painful cough into the collar of his shirt on his way out the door.

His nose is starting to run by the time he reaches his apartment.

“Heyyy!” David greets from the couch, where a Sandra Bullock movie is playing. “How was work?”

“It was good,” Patrick says as he throws his keys on the table. “A little slow since it’s so hot out, you know? No one wants to leave their houses today.” He clears his throat, and inwardly swears when he feels a tickle in his nose.

He didn’t plan on hiding this cold from David, but he just wanted a little more time before it was obvious.

Not gonna happen, his body decides, and Patrick sneezes.

David’s head pops up over the back of the couch. “What was that?”

Patrick rubs his nose just quick enough to remove the liquid building up at the base of it. “Nothing,” he shakes his head.

“No,” David stands, making his way over. “No, that was a sneeze. You sneezed.”

Patrick sighs. “I... yeah, it was a sneeze.” And as if things couldn’t get any worse, his nose is running again. Patrick finds himself snuffling up the liquid that’s threatening to drip out.

David nods, making an ‘mhm’ sound. “You sound exactly like I did three days ago.” He takes Patrick’s arm. “Come on, get your PJs on and join my blanket nest.”

Patrick chooses a pair of soft flannel bottoms David bought him for Christmas and joins his fiance on the couch. He finds himself being wrapped in a blanket until they’re cuddled against each other. David’s warm and still shivering, but he looks less tired.

“We should close the store tomorrow, right?”

Patrick chuckles. “We can’t do that, David. As much as I’d love to, a cold isn’t enough for us to stay home from work when we’re running our own business.”

David nods, clears his throat. “It was worth a try.”
