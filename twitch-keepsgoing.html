Title: Keeps Going
Fandom: Twitch
Summary: Skyler somehow manages to stay energetic no matter what
Word count: 700

Keeps Going

It isn't abnormal for Sky to lose his balance. He's always moving non-stop, after all. But he isn't laughing this time as he stumbles for a second, having to lean on a nearby building.

"Whoa, you good?" She chuckles and turns the camera away from him.

“Yeah, mhmm,” Skyler nods. “Just got a little lightheaded for a second. Whew!” he shakes his head as if to clear it and then continues following her.

“Really? Are you alright?”

“Yeah! I’m fine!”

She doesn’t think much of it.

-----

And then he says he’s not very hungry when they go to the pizza place chat voted on.

Trae doesn’t want to mention it on camera, even when she catches him losing focus randomly. He’s out of it.

He takes a sip of his water and clears his throat while Trae answers a question from the chatroom, and she doesn’t miss the wince. A headache? Maybe that’s why he’s being different.

Skyler inhales quickly and turns away to cough. It sounds... bad.

Oh. He’s not feeling good.

They end when they’re at a restaurant getting dessert. And the moment the stream ends, Skyler’s demeanor changes.

Trae puts her stream stuff away while Skyler grabs a napkin and blows his nose.

She hums sympathetically. “Yeah, I figured you weren’t feeling great.”

Skyler nods. “I think I’m getting a cold or something. It didn’t start until we were already streaming.”

Without a second thought, Trae reaches across the table and presses the back of her hand to his forehead. He grins.

“D’awww,” he says with a laugh. “That was cute.”

Trae nods before going back to her ice cream. “Mm, you’re warm,” she says around the spoon in her mouth. “Do you want some ibuprofen? I have some in my bag.”

“You just carry drugs around, huh?”

“I’m a woman, my gut tries to kill me at least once a month!”

“Wow, that’s dramatic.” He giggles childishly. “Naw, I’m good.” He snuffles, running the back of his hand under his nose, and clears his throat.

“Okay, well, thanks for sticking with me today.”

-----

“Sky, take the medicine.” Trae shoves a bottle at him. “Take it!”

“I’m fine.”

“Um, no. You’re shivering,” she says pointedly. “And you’ve been complaining about feeling achy for the last ten minutes. That’s a fever, Skyler. These will help.”

Skyler stares wide-eyed from behind his glasses for a moment before slowly taking the bottle.

“Never pegged you for the mother hen type, Trae,” he mutters as he shakes out a couple of pills.

Trae laughs. “Oh my God, I’m really not.”

Skyler falls asleep on their way to drop him off.

-----

Trae sits at her desk and sees a notification for Skyler’s stream. She opens it without thinking. She’d completely forgotten he was sick until his face appears and...

oh wow, he looks awful.

He sounds awful. His voice is hoarse and thick with congestion, his nose is pink, and he sounds tired.

But somehow he manages to put on a good show for the stream regardless.

-----

The next time he’s sick on stream may be her fault. She insists that they have to try the new restaurant that’s in the same area they’re exploring.

Of course it’s only after they’ve gorged themselves that he lays a hand on his stomach and says, “That sandwich isn’t sitting right.”

“Oh no, are you gonna barf?”

“God, I hope not.”

He asks for a bathroom in the next store they’ve wandered in.

Trae does her best to keep the chat busy. She hides her concern as the amount of time he’s been gone exceeds five minutes, and then ten...

She’s tempted to text him and ask if he’s dead when he finally appears.

His hair is damp, his face is red, but he still smiles.

“We good?”

“Yeah! Let’s go. What’d they vote on? What’s next?”

Trae turns the camera slightly and gives him a look that amounts to “Are you okay?”

He smiles and shrugs. Stomach ache, he mouths. I’ll be fine.

Trae raises an eyebrow and nods before talking to the camera once again.

It will never stop amazing her how he can keep going when he’s not feeling good.

-----

Skyler throws up two minutes after they end the stream. “Oh God. I’m never eating there again, Trae.”

“Yeahhh, sorry.”

And it’ll never stop amazing her how fast he can crumble afterward.
