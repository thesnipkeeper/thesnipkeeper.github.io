Title: Undone Progress
Fandom: To the Bone
Summary: Eli's reminded that she's in a safe place
Word count: 1k

Undone Progress

Eli wasn’t a stranger to feeling bad for no reason. Her body would decide to shut down on her at the most random times, even when it was used to running on empty.

She didn’t question it when she woke up feeling like crap. It wasn’t a normal kind of feeling like crap, at least not her normal, but who knew what could be happening, now?

She nearly fell asleep at breakfast, took a bite or two and realized that she just couldn’t eat anymore. It seemed to concern everyone after the progress she’d been making.

A heavy exhaustion slowly fell on her.

She slurred her way through her turn in group that morning.

“Are you doing okay, Eli? You seem a little out of it.” Their therapist asked it in her usual therapist tone, but her brows were knitted together in worry.

“Yeah, I’m just...” Eli dragged a tired hand down her face. “Kinda tired this morning.”

Karen frowned and nodded. “It’s alright. We all have those days.”

Eli shrugged.

“Luke, why don’t you tell me how you’re doing today?”

Luke went on some long ramble about his plans for his next outing or something. Eli lost track, because she felt as if she couldn’t hold her head up anymore, let alone stay conscious. She let her body slump against the arm of the couch.

-----

Cold fingers against her cheek woke her abruptly.

“She’s hot!”

“Jesus, Luke.”

“Oh, shut up. She’s got a temperature.”

“Okay, alright,” Karen’s calm voice contrasted with everyone else who sounded like they’d just gotten done laughing about how Eli fell asleep in the middle of their group therapy.

She was vaguely aware of Luke, his arm loosely around her waist as he carefully walked her up the stairs. It was sweet, or would have been if she wasn’t so desperate to get to her bed and sleep for eternity.

“There we go, just a few more.”

“Feel like shit.”

He chuckled. “That’s quite obvious."

"No, Luke, I-" she stopped dead in her tracks and clamped a hand over her mouth.

"Oh, shit."

Luke's reaction was almost comical. Almost. It was hard to find anything funny when she was about to puke what little she'd managed to get down at breakfast.

This was not happening.

Not after she'd made so much progress.

Luke looked at her as she took another step up the stairs. "Are you-?" He stopped himself.

They kept going. Eli made it all the way up the stairs, to the room, to her bed, all while holding on to her stomach contents. The nausea subsided once she was in bed and lying still, very still.

Luke sat on the edge of the bed with caution, careful not to jostle her.

"Do you want me to get you anything?" He asked, lowering his voice to a near-whisper.

She shook her head against the pillow. Eli kept her eyes squeezed shut, as if the light could be what may push her over the edge and made her puke.

"Do you want me to leave you alone?"

No, she didn't.

But she would never admit it. She would never admit that she was scared one stupid illness was going to undo everything she'd worked so hard for. What if this was what broke her brain allover again?

"I'm just gonna sleep," she mumbled.

"Alright, well shout if you need anything."

She listened to his footsteps as he descended the stairs and only then did Eli let herself whimper. She curled up into a ball, and wished that she'd gotten under the covers before getting into bed.

Too late, was her last thought before she drifted into sleep once again.

-----

"Eli?"

She felt ten times worse than she had when she fell asleep. She huffed and curled tighter, shivering, only to notice that she was under a blanket.

"Eli."

That was Dr. Beckham's voice.

Eli finally opened her eyes. The light wasn't nearly as assaulting as she thought it would be. It looked like the curtains had been pulled shut. 

"Hey," he smiled. "Sorry to wake you, but Lobo and I wanted to make sure we didn't have to be worried about you."

Eli furrowed her brows in confusion. He and Lobo stood there as if they were expecting something.

So Dr. Beckham continued. 

"Is it okay if Lobo takes your temperature?"

"Oh," Eli sat up, slowly, her muscles crying at every movement. The nausea had lessened and instead become a vague nagging in her middle that warned her not to do anything crazy anytime soon. "Yeah. Sure."

She sat still, no fight left in her as Lobo pressed the thermometer in her ear. It beeped, and Lobo read the numbers and nodded.

"103.1."

"Ever the overachiever, huh Eli?" said Dr. Beckham.

"Guess so." She dragged a hand down her face.

"Do you feel nauseated at all?"

She thought about it, and nodded. "Little bit."

"Is that freaking you out?"

It wasn't just the nausea. It was being sick in general that caused the surge of anxiety through her. She never thought that there would be a day where she'd get nervous about not being able to eat.

But saying all of that sounded like a lot of work. She went with a simple,

"Kind of."

"Yeah," he nodded. "I figured. Luke said something about you when I talked to him. Apparently you fell asleep during the group this morning."

"Apparently."

"By the short sentences, I'm going to assume you want us to leave you alone now."

"Excellent deduction, Dr. Beck."

He snorted. "Alright. Feel better, Eli. Let someone know if you need anything." He nodded at her and then at Lobo and went on his way.

Lobo hummed. "Luke will probably be up here to bother you in a little bit. Get some rest."

"Well, I was trying."

Lobo just chuckled and followed Dr. Beckham out the door.

Eli fell asleep, but less miserable this time.

It wouldn't be long before she woke up again, this time to Luke singing show tunes in the hallway on his way to bug her.

For once, she felt safe. She felt comfortable. She felt cared for.
