Fandom: Kevin from Work

Word count: 600

Project

“Audrey and I were goi’g to stay late to work odn a project today...” Kevin laments with a thick sniffle. “Mbaybe I cad still...” He makes a startled noise that mixes horribly with his congestion as he’s shoved back onto the bed.

“Nope. No way,” Roxie doesn’t even look like she had to look up from her phone to do it.

“I’b ndot actually that sick, though.”

Roxie scoffs. “I had to like, pick you up off of the floor this morning, Kev.”

“Just got a little lightheaded. Snf!” He runs the back of his hand against his nose. He cringes at the wet streak on his skin and promptly wipes it on his pajamas. Roxie doesn’t bat an eye.

She throws the tissue box his way without looking up from her phone.

He mumbles a thanks and grabs a handful before blowing his nose. “Why are you still here, anyway?”

“Um, excuse me, this is my room. And also I care about you or whatever.”

Kevin chuckles, but that only strikes the match for a coughing fit that has him doubled over. It scrapes his sore throat, making him groan in pain.

“God, I feel horrible. I can’t believe I forgot to get a flu shot.” His eyes widen. “Wait, Roxie-”

“What? Oh, naw I’m good. I got one.”

He raises an eyebrow. “Really?”

“Uh, yeah? I don’t wanna get sick, gross.”

He sits back against the pillows, trying to go back to sleep, but all he can think about is Audrey. About- about how she’s stuck doing extra work because he’s not there to help her. Yeah, that’s it. It’s not fair for her.

He looks at the time. He could still...

Kevin sighs, sneezes, and then coughs. He’s a mess, he knows he is. He presses a tissue to his face to mop up the unfortunate situation underneath, and the uses his other hand to promptly throw the blankets back.

“Kevin-! Where are you going?”

He squeezes his eyes shut for a moment as the rush of vertigo passes, and he continues to get up. “Bathroom,” he snaps. “Is that okay with you?”

Roxie rolls her eyes and sits back.

He has to lean on the wall for support because the room is still spinning, but all-in-all, Kevin figures he could be much sicker. The runny nose and consumption-esque cough can be remedied.

He searches the bathroom until he finds flu medicine and cough drops, downs the medicine, grabs the cough drops...

Kevin isn’t sure how he manages to get dressed so quickly, maybe it’s his drive, or maybe it’s love (gross, okay Kevin calm down). But he seems to look somewhat presentable despite the shadowed eyes and the pink nose. He ignores the dizziness this time as he grabs his keys, just as Roxie is wandering out of the room.

“Bye Roxie thanks for helping me out!”

“Wha-? Kevin!”

But he’s already out the door.

-----

“Audrey,” Roxie laughs as her brother is led in by the woman he’s spent so long pining after. “Thanks for um...”

“Yeah,” Audrey nods. “He threw up on the way here. I think he’s a little delirious, actually?” She rests her palm across his forehead once he gets settled on the couch. “Anyway... Kevin, take care of yourself, okay?”

Kevin’s already half asleep. He’ll be freaking out when he realizes that Audrey personally drove him home from work.

“Ugh,” Kevin curls up and pulls the throw blanket over himself with a shiver. “I don’t feel good.”

“Imagine that, idiot. How fucking dare you make me be the responsible sibling,” she mumbles as she pulls off his shoes, and then drags one of his other blankets in to drape over him.

“Thanks, Rox.”

“Fuck you, Kevin. Feel better.”
