Drops

“Ah ah ah, sit still!”

“I am.”

“No you’re not, Wiggly McGee.”

“Ugh, please don’t call me that ever again.”

It was certainly not what Paige expected to hear when she and Ralph walked into the garage that morning. The voices were coming from the living area, and when they finally approached, she saw two of her coworkers, one lying down and the other with a bottle in his hand.

“What’s going on?”

Toby and Walter turned to her, Walter like a deer in headlights and Toby wearing his Exasperated Doctor face.

“I’m trying to put drops in Walter’s ear and he’s being uncooperative.

"Drops? For what?"

"Our boy came down with acute otitis media."

Paige raised an eyebrow. She was used to simply crossing her arms and waiting for an explanation.

"Middle ear infection. He had a dizzy spell last night before I left and we ended up taking a trip to the urgent care together. Now give me your ear, O’Brien.”

Walter grunted, but did so, essentially letting Toby turn his head himself. He winced, giving a startled his as Toby put the drops in.

“Is Walter going to be okay?” Ralph asked.

“He’ll be fine as long as he keeps taking his antibiotics without a fight.” That last word was aimed pointedly at his boss. “Now lie still for five minutes and then let it drain.”

Walter gave a minute nod against the pillow. He must have been sick if he wasn’t rambling about the localities of antibiotics, Paige thought.

Now that she did think about it, he seemed tired the day before. A bit more irritable and antisocial than usual, especially with Toby. They all chalked it up to a rough day. Apparently that wasn’t all. She sat on the couch by his feet and rubbed his leg with a sympathetic smile.

She was thankful when Toby took Ralph off with the promise of showing him a cool trick that would make kids at school love him.

“You could’ve said something yesterday.”

“I should have,” he admitted. “I didn’t think it was important.”

She noticed the flush in his cheeks and furrowed her brow before laying the back of her hand against his face just like she did with Ralph. He was warm, but by the shy look he gave her, he was already aware.

“Get some rest today, alright?” She said.

“I can tr-”

Of course Cabe had to walk in at that moment. “Look alive, everyone! We’ve got a new case!”

Paige sighed. Toby groaned from the other side of the garage. Of course they had a new case.
