Tattle

Rory thought she recognized that sneeze. It reminded her of the time her mom spilled perfume on the living room carpet. It reminded her of allergy season.

She knew exactly why when she walked into English class and saw Mr. Medina with his face buried in a tissue. The trash can under his desk was usually hidden, but the overflowing catastrophe made it and the millions of used tissues visible.

He lowered the cloth from his face and sniffed before huffing out through his mouth. His nose was cherry red.

Rory frowned in sympathy and went to her desk.

The discussion of Harper Lee’s short career as an author was occasionally put on pause when Mr. Medina had to cough, or sneeze. This time, he raised a hand as a student finished telling him their thoughts on what Boo Radley represents.

He chuckled sheepishly “Hold on...” He pulled a tissue out of his pocket, turned around a bit, and blew his nose. Everyone stayed awkwardly silent as they waited for him to be done. “Whew, sorry, guys. This cold is not giving me a break.”

He had them fill out worksheets about symbolism and representation in fiction near the end of class. Rory finished and perfected hers before everyone else, of course, and by then Mr. Medina was slumped over his desk with his head in his hand.

Jeez, he looked... bad. Just bad. Rory couldn’t believe he even came to school today looking the way he did. Was he just that dedicated?

Lorelai hadn’t mentioned anything about Max being sick, and that could only mean that he hadn’t told her.

She didn’t mention it to him after class was over. It had gotten easier to separate Mr. Medina and Max, and Rory had no reason to talk to Mr. Medina about how he was feeling when he clearly just wanted the day to be over.

-----

Lorelai looked up from her work when the inn’s door jingled, signaling the arrival of a certain someone.

“Hey sweetie, how was school?”

“It was fine,” she said.

Rory made her way behind the desk and set her backpack down, already prepared to help out. It was usually her way of procrastinating homework, Lorelai found, because of course her weird kid would be the one to procrastinate work by doing work. Nevertheless, she gave her a stack of papers that needed filing.

“Have you talked to Max today?”

“No, actually we’ve both been so busy that it’s been a couple of days. Why? Did he ask about me?” she poked Rory and waggled her eyebrows.

“Ew, no. And it would be super weird if he did.” She rolled her eyes. “He didn’t seem like he was feeling good today.”

Lorelai frowned. “What do you mean?”

“It just looked like a really bad cold. But like, really bad. He lost his voice by the end of class.”

That was what she got for being busy. This was why she liked to keep a thriving non-work life most of the time, but with all of the stupid events and conventions that were going on that week... well, she forgot to call her boyfriend.

He could have called her, though. That was always an option. And it felt better to put some of the blame on him than to accept that she was completely oblivious.

“Jeez, he should’ve told me.”

“Honestly, with how he looked today, I’m surprised he was even conscious long enough to make it through the school day. He probably went to bed the second he got home.”

Lorelai sighed and added to Rory’s filing pile, receiving no protests in the process. “Okay, well, I know what I’m doing tonight.”

-----

She gathered as much sick person supplies as she could find at Dose’s. Soup (canned. The man was already sick, she didn’t have to torture him with her attempt at cooking.), cold meds, cough drops, and - from experience with Rory’s last cold - a lot of tissues. Four boxes, which would hopefully last him a day or two.

She felt confident in her endeavor. She didn’t hesitate one bit when she knocked on the door to his apartment.

There was shifting on the other side, the creak of his couch and the rustling of blankets. And then footsteps of sock feet.

Max opened the door. He sighed, but a tired and relieved smile came over his face.

“Rory told you, huh?”

“Yup, she sure did.”

He widened the door to let her in, giving a chuckle that immediately turned into a cough. He rushed to get his elbow over his face in time to cover.

“It’s just a cold.” He said, swiping at his nose.

Lorelai reached to press her wrist to his forehead and frowned. “Sure, a bad one. You’ve got a temp, Max. And you sound like you’ve got a clothespin over your nose, by the way. Here,” she pulled one of the many boxes of tissues out of the bag, tore it open, and handed it to him.

He sniffled and gave a hoarse laugh. “Thanks, I- I ran out.”

“I figured you might."

She gave him some privacy to deal with his stuffed nose and went to work emptying the bag of its contents. He watched over the tissue in amazement as she kept pulling stuff out.

"Are we starting a pharmacy in my apartment?" He asked with a final snf! Before tossing the tissue.

"It's worth considering. Never too late to start making money on the side."

"Sure, sure."

She held up the can of soup. "Have you eaten?"

"No," he admitted. "I've been too busy sleeping and wallowing in self-pity."

"Huh, so Rory was right."

"What?"

"Nothing."

"Also, I'm not that hungry."

"Well, tough, 'cause I'm gonna cook this."

"You mean heat it up?"

"I sure do. Do you wanna go lie down again?"

Max had a pretty comfy looking nest built for himself on the couch. Used tissues were scattered about, and a half-full mug sat abandoned on the coffee table.

He muffled a cough into his arm as he sat down. Max looked like he could fall asleep again right then and there, but he also seemed to be anticipating the idea of soup.

She prepared it the best she knew how and set it on the stove to “cook”. When Lorelai returned, she found him blowing his nose pretty disgustingly.

“Sexy,” she said as she perched herself on the arm of the couch.

He laughed. “Thanks. Snf! That is my current goal."

She leaned on him and pressed a kiss to his head. “You could’ve just told me.”

“Lorelai, my brain has been so gummed up with snot the past two days…”

“Ew, okay, stop.”

“Yeah, that’s what I thought. Give me a break.”

“I guess I didn’t call you, either.”

“Eh,” a shrug, and a sniff. “You’ve been busy.”

“Well, sure, but…” she sighed. “That’s not how things should be.” She leaned further and kissed his forehead. God, he was warm. It was kind of nice. She sank down to the actual couch and snuggled him, humming contently.

“Lorelai.”

“Hm?”

“I think the soup’s boiling over.”

“Damn it!”
