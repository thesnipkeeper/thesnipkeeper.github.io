Title: Last Man Standing
Fandom: Critical Role RPF
Summary: The group's been passing around a cold, and Taliesin must be getting it too
Word count: 3k

Last Man Standing

They have a habit of passing bugs around. If one of them has a cold, the rest of them are almost guaranteed to come down with it in a matter of days. That's part of the reason why they try to stay home whenever they're sick, as to not tempt fate.

Yet...Cold and flu season is taking a toll on all of them.

Tissue boxes are scattered around the studio, and there's not a moment where a cough or sniffle can't be heard. The cast and crew alike are suffering from it simply because it's not bad enough to take off work. The cold is more of a nuisance than anything else.

That's what Taliesin's heard, anyway.

The cold has been merciful on him - that is to say - he's the last man standing. Everyone's accused him of witchcraft and being a vampire and he proudly doesn’t deny either, even if he just figures it's just because he hasn’t spent as much time at the studio as everyone else. He's been recording non-stop for a week, going from one recording studio to another. Having a sudden influx of work is great, but it's putting a strain on him. He supposes he should be thankful since it's the reason he's not snotty and gross right now.

-----

He's first met with the sight of Matt blowing his nose into what looks like a well-used handkerchief. Taliesin knew he wasn’t in good shape when he talked to Matt on the phone earlier that day. He sounded like his sinuses were filled with cement, the poor guy was not having a good time.

Marisha, however is coughing a worrying amount. Everyone watches her a little closely when she gets a cough ever since her 'death sneeze' that was ultimately caused by hard coughing fits, and from the sounds of the miserable hacking coming from the other side of the studio, it's fair.

"Still got that cold?"

Matt's groan is enough of an answer. He stuffs his kerchief in his pocket and then pulls out a bottle of purell to scrub his hands. Taliesin isn't sure what the point of that is since literally the entire studio is already sick.

"I don’t know how you're not sick," Matt says with amazement. "Seriously, what demon did you make a pact with?"

"You don’t know. Uka'toa maybe."

Matt snorts. "Ha. Ha."

"Marisha sounds rough."

Matt frowns at the sound of another cough from his wife. "Yeah, we don’t think it's like last time, though. It just went to her chest a little more than everyone else. Snf." be brings the kerchief back up to wipe away the stray moisture making its way down his upper lip. "We're already stocking up on cough drops and meds to get her through the game tomorrow."

"And nasal spray for you?"

Matt's expression turns deadpan. "Yes. And nasal spray."

-----

Thursday's game session was still planned despite everyone being down with the same cold. It's not like they haven't done it before. Tons of post-con plagues have made their way through the group, and they always powered through. Taliesin considers it a reward for getting through a week and a half of back-to-back recording sessions and odd jobs at the studio.

He walks out of his last job and heads home with the knowledge that he's finally done for a few days, he can finally rest, and he can go play D&D with his friends tomorrow night.

Matt calls before he goes to bed. "Hey, snf! can you bring your other notes tomorrow?"

"Yeah, sure. What are you planning?"

"It's funny that you think you can squeeze stuff out of me after all these years." Matt punctuates his smugness with a watery sniffle. "I just need to check up on something."

"Fair enough," Taliesin pulls back his blankets, prepared to have a good night's rest after a long day - well - long week. "Are you guys feeling any better?"

"Yeah, so much better. I mean, I know I don’t sound it, but I feel like a new man! I'm hoping it'll be about gone by tomorrow."

Taliesin smiles. "Good."

"I still don’t know how you're not sick," Matt grumbles. Taliesin hears him pull away from the phone to give his nose a quick blow. "But I'm glad you don’t have to deal with this. Slightly resentful, maybe, but glad."

Taliesin chuckles, "See you tomorrow, buddy."

-----

It was bound to happen, but why did it have to happen on Thursday of all days? Taliesin contemplates this as he tries to work up the motivation to get out of bed that morning.

He's got a stuffy nose, maybe a bit of a scratchy throat, but that's not the worst of it. His entire body aches, his head, his limbs, his face. Taliesin shivers and wishes he didn’t have to get out of the warm cocoon of his blankets. Oh well, it was bound to happen. Can't be surrounded by sick people and not expect to get sick.

He coughs painfully and wishes it wasn’t winter break. Normally he'd text one of his roommates and ask them to deliver meds to his room as the group of them often did, but everyone's visiting their families and Taliesin's the only one whose family lives in LA.

He allows himself a moment to wallow in self-pity because wow does he feel awful. And then he goes back to sleep. It's not hard to fall asleep right away, the fever has already sapped energy out of him.

Taliesin remembers waking up occasionally to take a sip of water from his nightstand or check the time. He goes back to sleep each time, hoping to sleep off this cold before tonight.

Riiing riiing

Taliesin wakes from a not-so-restful sleep. He groggily grabs his phone and slides the answer button without bothering to look at the caller ID.

"Hello?" He sounds tired, but other than that not nearly as bad as everyone else has.

"Hey, are you on your way?" Matt's voice says.

Taliesin pulls the phone away, checks the time, and his eyes widen. He should be on his way, and yet he's still unshowered and in his PJs.

"Oh fuck. Sorry, I kind of slept all day." and a cough gives him away before he can stop it.

Matt sighs. "Guess you didn’t quite escape it, huh? Sorry, buddy."

"Yeah… Jesus I don’t know how you guys dealt with this. I feel so…" he goes to sit up and suddenly the room is spinning and dark spots are flashing in his vision. "Ohhh God…"

"Tal? You okay?"

"Lightheaded."

Matt makes a sympathetic sound. "Probably the congestion, huh?"

"I don’t…" Taliesin leans against the wall and gives the room a moment to sit still. "I don’t know… I think I'm running a fever."

"You think so?"

"I dunno, maybe." he doesn’t have time or energy to actually check his temp. "Listen Matt, I don’t really want to miss tonight's game, but I don’t know if I can…"

He can hear Matt's frown. "Hey, we've got lots of stuff here to keep you comfortable if that's what you're worried about."

Taliesin sighs. He doesn’t want to inconvenience Matt by making him play Caduceus, and this is the one thing he's been looking forward to through all of his busy days. His reward. He'll regret missing it, right?

"I don’t know how attentive of a player I'll be, but…"

"I'll get your tea ready," he sounds too sweet for Taliesin to refuse.

-----

Taliesin shows up and wishes he hadn't.

"Are you upset or something?" Laura asks. Apparently he's not being as good of a conversation partner as usual.

"No, no no… sorry, I just don’t feel great. I think I finally caught everyone's cold."

"Aw, sorry. Well I think Matt's planning to end early tonight so we're not staying up late."

That's the best thing Taliesin's heard all day. He isn't sure he could stay up as late as they usually do.

Now he's sure he's running a fever. He's too achy and cold not to be.

"Laura," he says lowly. "Can you- do I feel warm to you?"

Laura's eyebrow raises at the out-of-nowhere question, and she presses the back of her hand to his forehead. A frown. "A little bit, yeah. Do you want some meds? We have so many fucking meds."

"Please."

Laura hands him a bottle of Tylenol and a bag of cough drops. She also ends up giving him the option of cold medicine but warns that it might make him drowsy. He passes/

He pulls his jacket tighter around himself, ultimately zipping it up to block out any cold air. Everyone else is in shorts and t-shirts, but he's freezing. He tries not to whimper when every movement causes him to ache, and he ends up taking a couple Tylenol and hoping it helps.

He isn't paying attention to the game at all. There are a few times where Matt has to say his name to get his attention, and it makes him even more embarrassed when everyone's giving him weird looks. He tries to apologize but the group's already moved on to something else and he doesn’t feel like trying again.

He's too out of it.

At one point, he considers napping on the table. He wouldn’t, of course, but it's a nice thought. He feels colder, more tired and more… awful. Moving takes all of his strength. Just getting to the lobby couch during their break is a lot of work.

"Ugh," he says. "I feel awful." he leans his head back and closes his eyes, ignoring the fact that he's going to have to get back up in just a few minutes.

The Tylenol did nothing. He feels worse, and he suspects that his fever is no longer just a little temp. He shifts so he's lying horizontal, his entire body feels like jelly as he melts into the sofa.

Liam chuckles. "Welcome to our world all of this week."

If they all really felt this awful, why did they come to work? Taliesin could hardly get out of bed and that was just to play a game for a couple hours.

He begins falling asleep, ignoring the fact that he probably won't be able to get up again.

-----

Taliesin experiences a rude awakening when face is patted. He whimpers as a hand comes into contact with sensitive, feverish skin. Why are they trying to hurt him?

He's too tired to pay attention to more than bits of conversation.

"He's not very..."

"I think we should-"

"...time does the urgent care...?"

"... probably closed."

"...'ra, can you get the…?"

Taliesin wishes they would be quiet; he's trying to sleep. He does drift away for a quick second before a hand caresses his cheek, cool and gentle this time.

"Stay with us, buddy. Can you talk to me for a second?"

Taliesin whimpers. His body aches, he's so cold, and he just wants to go back to sleep.

"Tal," he feels the couch dip next to him, doesn't bother opening his eyes. "You've got a pretty high fever. We're gonna take your temp and see if it warrants a hospital visit, okay?"

Hospital? Fuck no, he wants to sleep. Still, he feels the same gentle hand rubbing his arm as it rests on his stomach, and it quickly eases the desire to argue. He feels awful. Maybe the hospital can help.

Wait.

"The game," he barely manages a whisper.

Matt chuckles. "I think we're done for the night. Caduceus is kinda out of commission."

Taliesin finally brings himself to open his eyes. Bright lights hammer at his head, and the crowd of people make him want to sink further into the couch.

Laura jogs up to them, handing Matt the thermometer they kept at the studio.

"His fever wasn’t that bad earlier…" she worries her lip while Matt figures out the thermometer. "I'm sorry, Tal. I should have noticed."

"I think we all should have…" Marisha's voice from… somewhere. "He kept saying he didn’t feel good."

He wants to insist that he's fine, to tell everyone it's not their job to look out for him. He could have stayed home if he wanted to! Matt presses the thermometer in his ear before he has a chance to say anything.

Taliesin vaguely remembers them investing in a thermometer to put in the first aid kit after one of them ended up really sick at the studio. Marisha? Marisha had a stomach bug of some kind, and Matt was starting to get nervous when she started running a temp. He drove to the nearest drug store and bought one for them.

Matt had looked so freaked out. Near tears and-

Shit. The look on his face right now is too familiar, except Matt is trying harder to hold it back. He's too good at pretending to be level-headed.

 "Jus' a cold…" Taliesin finally says. Maybe if they believe him he can go back to sleep.

There's a distant beeping before the pressure in his ear disappears. Matt frowns at the reading, wide-eyed. Taliesin knows that look, the trying-not-to-panic look that so few people catch onto.

"We're going to the hospital. Travis, can you help me get him…"

Taliesin is vaguely aware of being manhandled by both of them until he ends up in Matt and Marisha's car. He can't keep his eyes open any longer after the dizzying trip, and he nearly melts as Marisha climbs into the back seat with him and rests his head on her lap. She cards her fingers through his hair and pets him, occasionally pressing her hand to his forehead to feel his fever.

"Sorry," he manages. "Shouldn’t have to do this."

"Oh sh, you'd do the same. You did." she brushes her fingers through his hair. The car is silent for a little while longer before she adds, "I'm sorry we didn’t notice. We figured it wasn’t a big deal since… y'know."

Taliesin shakes his head against her. He can only hope she knows what it means because he can't muster any more words.

-----

He fidgets with the bright bracelet they put on him when he arrived.

"Have you been out of the country in the past six months?"

Taliesin takes too long thinking about the question. "Australia."

"Right." Matt writes something on the sheet and continues to fill things out. Taliesin trusts him to know most of his information anyway.

He shudders as a cold gust from the door hits his feverish skin. Marisha wraps an arm around him. She's not the warmest person but just the contact makes him feel better.

He feels himself drift away on her shoulder, feels Matt's knee bouncing anxiously as they wait for his name to be called.

"Taliesin?"

-----

The triage nurse gets serious when she takes his temperature. Taliesin is too out of it to notice until he hears the worried voices of his friends asking questions.

The flu.

He has the flu.

A pretty nasty one, apparently.

The doctor delivers the news with some sympathy, seeing the three of them obviously exhausted and shaken up from having to come to the hospital at all. After being poked to death by several inexperienced nurses, he leans back and lets the IV do its thing. It's got something to bring the fever down, and it should hydrate him to boot.

He just wants to go home. Even when he's able to leave, he has a prescription

Marisha and Matt are seated next to the bed, Marisha with her head on Matt's shoulder. They're pretty sure she's fallen asleep. She hasn’t slept well with the lingering cough.

Matt looks worse than Taliesin feels. "Thought you just had-"

"I know," Taliesin says. He sighs heavily. He's pretty sleepy. "I know, me too… not your fault."

Matt doesn’t look so sure.

"Matt…" it's as scolding of a voice as Taliesin can muster while being feverish and sick. "You're doing that overthinking thing."

A scoff. "No I'm not."

A slurred, "Yeah, you are," comes from the head rested on his shoulder.

Matt just sighs. "Just… please slap us next time you're this sick and we're not paying attention?"

Taliesin chuckles. He shifts on the bed, careful not to dislodge his IV, and turns to get a better look at the two of them. They're fucking adorable.

Time goes by. "Is the medicine working?"

Taliesin nods softly against the pillow. His eyes have slipped shut. He thinks he might be able to finally get some sleep before the sun rises.

Of course that's when the doctor walks in to check his IV.

"Looks like you're free to go, Taliesin," he says with a smile that makes Taliesin want to punch him in the face.

Matt nudges Marisha awake and then helps Taliesin get his jacket on. His arm is sore, probably will be for a week, but at least he's not dizzy anymore.

They head to the pharmacy at five in the morning, as you do, and Matt and Marisha start grabbing tissues, meds, and various flu-friendly foods that can be microwaved while they wait for the prescription. They're discussing what would be best for him at home.

He isn't sure what he says in his daze of sickness (something about keeping everything near him because no one's home to bring him things if he needs them to) but they're both giving him weird looks.

"You're home alone?" Marisha asks while he groggily goes through the motions of picking up his prescription and the like.

He shrugs. "Holidays. Snf."

They head out to the car with way too many bags for a 5AM pharmacy trip and the medicine that will be a chore to take for the next week, and Matt makes a decision.

"You're coming home with us."

"No I'm not," he quickly argues. And then Marisha gives him The Look. He must be dumb enough to continue. "I'm not giving you two the flu. You were already sick for-"

"Nope, you're coming home. We'll pick up some things on the way there and then you can sleep in front of the TV all day, alright?"

That sounds really nice. Being snuggled under blankets, with the added comfort of someone being there if he needs it.

"…Fine." he sighs out dramatically, smile on his face all the same.
