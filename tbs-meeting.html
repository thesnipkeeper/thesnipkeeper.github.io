Meeting

Joan had gone to bed a couple hours earlier the night before. She was so tired when she got home that she could hardly keep her eyes open. It was strange to be that tired so soon after coming home from work, but not a complete surprise. It’d been a busy week at the AM, after all.

Despite that, she woke up groggy, as if she hadn’t gone to sleep at all. Perhaps it was sleeping too much that did it. No matter the reason, she ignored the tired ache in her limbs and started getting ready for work.

She made her bed the same as every morning, and pulled out today’s outfit, setting it neatly on top.

There was a strange taste in her mouth that she couldn’t exactly pinpoint, so Joan was eager to brush her teeth. She usually waited until after breakfast, but she wasn’t particularly hungry, so apparently breakfast wasn't happening. She could always dig into her snack stash at work if she ended up wanting something to eat.

She brushed her teeth thoroughly to rid herself of the weird taste.

Joan barely had time to get the toothbrush out of her mouth before she threw up.

She gasped at the mess in the sink, and the sight of toothpaste and vomit together just made her vomit again. She gripped the edge of the sink and spat, sniffled.

Only then did she risk looking at herself in the mirror.

“Fuck.”

-----

“Joan, you’re here!” Owen said from the doorway of her office.

“I am, yes...?” She sat up straighter from her slumped position. She wasn’t really doing work, just going over the papers on her desk and trying very hard to ignore the building nausea in her stomach. She pulled her arm from where it’d been wrapped protectively around her middle as Owen walked in.

“I just didn’t see you come in.”

Joan nodded. “I got here a little early, to... go over everything before the meeting.” The last thing she wanted right now was company.

“Oh, I see.” He smiled kindly, which faded as he looked her over in the moment of silence. “Are you alright?” His voice went up at the end the way it always did when he was unabashedly concerned for someone. For her.

There was no sense lying. She sighed. “No,” she answered quietly. Owen frowned, eyes wide with worry, so she gave a self-deprecating chuckle to lighten the mood. “My stomach’s a little upset? I sort of... threw up in the bathroom sink this morning.”

Owen frowned. “Joan...”

“Sorry, too much information!” She laughed again, despite wanting to cry at the absurdity of it all. Of course she’d come down with some miserable bug the day of their big meeting, where they introduce the employees to new policies and where they were supposed to look professional.

“So the answer to my question is no, then. You’re not alright.”

She rolled her eyes and nodded. “I’ll survive, Owen, really.”

“Of course you will, I know you,” he scoffed.

If there was anything she was known for, it was working through any sort of illness or injury. During her previous time with the AM, she had an unfortunate session with an Atypical teenager that ended with Joan suffering from horrible vertigo for the rest of the day.

“Not to mention, we both need to be here today.”

His lips formed a thin line as he thought of what else to say, because he knew that she was right. This was the big meeting, the one that mattered, the one that would steer their employees in the right direction.

“Please let Sam or I know if you need anything?”

“I will.” She wouldn’t need anything, She’d done this before, it was easy enough. “But,” she stood up, causing a new surge of cramps in her gut. Joan resisted the urge to double over in pain. “If you’ll excuse me...”

“Wait, Joan, we should probably go over the meeting together so that we both-”

She tried not to sound as desperate as she was when she said, “Owen, it’s kind of an emergency.”

“O-Oh! Right, of course.”

Today was going to be... awful.

-----

The muscles in her abdomen ached, her throat was sore. She was sure that she’d have to re-tie her hair before she got out of there. And... and make herself look like she hadn’t spent half the morning vomiting and sweating (and maybe crying a little).

Joan wasn’t sure how long she spent knelt in front of the toilet, but it was long enough for Sam to come looking for her.

“Joan?”

Joan sighed out through her nose and gave a rasped, “I’m here.”

“Are- are you okay? Owen said that you were pretty sick...?”

“I-” she couldn’t exactly claim to be fine in her current position. “Yeah. I... I am.”

Her stomach gurgled as if reminding her that she wasn’t done yet. A cramp had her folding in two with both arms wrapped around her middle. She didn’t realize that she was whimpering in pain until it already happened.

“Joan?” Sam said again, but this time more panicked.

Joan wanted to respond, but instead she found herself heaving into the toilet for the thousandth time. A trickle of bile fell into the water below, but that didn’t stop her stomach from contracting again. She gagged breathlessly, unproductive. It left her breathing hard, tears in her eyes from the force of it, nose dripping.

“Give me a few minutes?” Her voice was ruined. How was she supposed to talk in front of people like this?

“Sure,” said Sam, sounding unsure. “Um, you have your phone, right? You can text me if...?”

Joan nodded, and then realized she couldn’t see her. “Yes, thank you.”

Sam left. Joan waited for the sound of the door closing before she allowed herself to cry.

-----

No one could make fun of her for keeping mouthwash in her office ever again, she decided.

Joan had straightened her clothes and fixed her hair in the bathroom, but she was still finding wrinkles and things buttoned wrong, and she was pretty sure that her ability to dress properly had been absent that morning.

“How do I look?”

Sam looked her over and chuckled. “Amazing as always.”

Joan cracked a smile. “Well, thank you, Samantha. But I don’t look...?”

“Sick?”

Joan sighed and nodded.

“No, you look fine. Seriously.”

“Alright,” she breathed out. “Alright.” Just then, she shuddered. She kept a sweater in her office for times like this (well, not exactly like this, but). She slipped it on, knowing all the while that it wasn’t going to help if what she was experiencing was fever chills.

“Do you want some ibuprofen or something?” Sam offered.

She did. It could probably fix the aches and chills. “Honestly? I don’t think that I could keep it down right now,” said Joan, sheepishly.

Sam frowned in sympathy. “Well... I guess you guys should get this over with quick, then.”

“I don’t know exactly how quick it’s going to be if we’re going over our entire patient ethics.”

“Well, shit.”

The two of them laughed. They were interrupted by a knock on the door.

“Come in!”

“Joan, Sam,” Owen nodded to each of them. “Ready?”

Joan buttoned her sweater and smiled. “Yup!” And intended on ignoring the violent oncoming bout of chills the best she could.

-----

“Dr. Bright, if you’ll join me up here,” Owen gestured her up to the front of the conference room with an expression that was almost apologetic. Owen had explained the technicalities, the policies and their changes. This was her part of the meeting, the psychology, the emotional parts.

She’d become increasingly cold by the time it was her turn to speak. Just as expected, the sweater did very little, yet she could feel the heat in her cheeks that she hoped no one else could notice. She’d also become increasingly lightheaded, and only figured that out after she stood up and subtly used the table to support herself.

Owen was watching her carefully, which was the last thing she wanted in front of a group of people who were all supposed to respect them as employers. She didn’t need anyone knowing how well she and Owen actually knew each other.

She held her chin high, cleared her throat, and began as normal.

“Agent Green touched on this, but I’ll be expanding on the patient ethics a bit more.” Joan folded her hands in front of her and pretended like her legs weren’t threatening to give out on her. “Our mission is to show people that they aren’t their ability, but that it is a part of them. We’ll never single them out only for what they can do.” She shifted her weight from one foot to the other, closed her eyes for a moment as the room started to darken. “A patient should never feel like they’re being judged, or that they’re nothing but their ability and any trouble it might cause. Show them that they’re more than...”

Joan trailed off, bringing a hand to her head. She breathed out heavily. She just had to get through this and she could sit down, or even retreat to her office.

Owen cleared his throat. “Dr. Bright?”

She shook her head to clear it. “Sorry, um. As I was saying, show the patients that they’re more than their a- abilities...”

That was the last thing she got out before her legs finally gave up on her.

“Joan!” and Owen and Sam’s panicked voices were the last thing she heard.

-----

Joan woke to whispered voices and the sound of footsteps on linoleum.

Her head ached, in more ways than one. It felt like an exhaustion headache at first, and then there was another surface layer that felt bruised, and-

Oh. Oh God.

She opened her eyes to find her friends immediately alert.

“Joan, hey!” Sam said, relieved smile on her face.

Owen still looked horrified, wrecked with anxiety.

“The meeting-”

“-ended the moment that you fainted,” Owen finished.

“How long have I been out?”

“Not long,” Sam promised. “You just got here a couple minutes ago. But Joan...”

“You had a fever of 103.”

Joan stared at the two of them. No wonder they seemed so worried. “I...” she sighed sadly. “I’m sorry, I had no idea.”

“I think that you had some idea,” Owen’s voice was soft, but still that scolding tone he managed so well.

“Alright,” she admitted. “Maybe I did.”

This whole day had gone so wrong. The one day that needed to go perfectly, and Joan got sick, fainted in front of her employees, and terrified her closest friends.

“Oh, oh no, Joan...” Sam’s hand laid atop hers. Joan realized at the sympathetic tone in her voice that she was tearing up. Yes, let’s add crying at work to the list of terrible things. “Seriously, everything’s fine, it worked out!”

“God,” Joan sniffed and scrubbed at her face. She pulled herself up the best she could, because lying down made her feel even more useless and she just needed to be sitting up right now for fuck’s sake... “Sorry, just,” she gave a teary chuckle. “Today couldn’t have gone more wrong, could it?”

“And yet, it’s all okay in the end,” Sam said cheerily, now holding Joan’s hand in hers. “Because you two make an awesome team.”

Owen hummed. “I think it’s the three of us, actually.”

Sam shrugged, smiled proudly. “Okay, fair enough.”

Owen got called away, which was for the best, Joan decided, because she would much rather be crying in front of one person instead of two.

She also had one less person to make her stay in bed.

“Whoa, wait where are you going?” Sam said quickly, grabbing onto Joan’s shoulders as she tried to hop off.

“I can’t just stay here for the rest of the day, can I?”

“Joan, you’re not any better just because you’re conscious now, you-”

Joan ignored her, got to her feet, and immediately regretted it when her vision began to go dark once again. Sam helped her back onto the bed with an exasperated huff.

“Joan.”

“I...” Joan growled. “I don’t want to stay here.”

“I know, I know, but they’re going to give you some medicine and electrolyte solution, okay? And then you can leave after your body processes that a little?” She stated it as a question, but Joan knew that it wasn’t.

And she didn’t have the energy to argue.

Sam’s voice was gentle and sweet as she said, “I can sit with you for a while if that’s alright? And then maybe you’ll go home and rest?”

Joan nodded. “I’d like that.”

Sam smiled brightly.
