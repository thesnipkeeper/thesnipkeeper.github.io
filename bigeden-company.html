Title: Company
Fandom: Big Eden
Summary: Communication has never been their strongsuit
Word count: 1.5k

Company

The thing is that Henry knows well that Pike hates social gatherings. Specifically large social gatherings at which a thousand of the lovely townsfolk will come up and pat him on the back and ask him how he’s doing.

And he won’t lie, it’s entertaining to watch the large man get so awkward around people he’s grown up with. It’s entertaining up until Henry realizes that the man is truly miserable when he’s forced to sit among a crowd for more than fifteen minutes.

He insists, insists that Pike doesn’t have to come with him to the spring picnic. Especially today. He’s not sure what it is, maybe just a bad day for the man, but Pike’s acted off since he woke up that morning (after Henry, which was strange in of itself). It seems like going to a big picnic is the last thing that he would enjoy on a day where he’s not feeling with it.

“Look, I’m not even going to be there that long, I’m just going to say hi to a few people, eat, and leave.”

“No, I can-” Pike swallows. “I want to go. With you, if that’s okay.”

Henry chuckles. “Of course it’s okay, I just don’t want you to feel like you have to go.”

“I don’t,” says Pike, rather quickly. And then he smiles adorably, “Just want to be around you today.”

Henry returns the smile, a warm feeling flooding over him. Of course he wants to be with Pike today, just like he does every day until the end of time. He shrugs, “Okay, let’s go, then,” he goes to kiss Pike, but the other man is already turning around to get dressed.

-----

“Hey Pike, ya made it!” Jim is the first to pat him on the back the second the walk onto the park grounds. He laughs jovially, excited to see his friend.

Pike doesn’t look the least bit comfortable. They find a table and sit down, bow their heads to bless the meal, and then stand to get in line for a plate of food.

Henry hears his partner take a deep, shaky breath behind him and turns around. “You doing okay?” he asks quietly and casually enough.

Pike nods without making eye contact. “Yup.”

“Aaaalright.” He doesn’t look okay. He looks anxious. He looks exactly how Henry thought he would if Pike came with him. The reach the potluck line and luck out on three different kinds of potatoes.

“Now, don’t you go eating all the cheese potatoes, Pike!” Jim laughs.

Pike rolls his eyes. “I’ll try not to.”

Being ahead of him in line, Henry doesn’t really see Pike’s plate until they’re seated.

“You must not be very hungry?” It’s a question. Pike’s anxiety may get the better of him at times, but Henry also recalls him skipping breakfast to do some maintenance work in the store first thing in the morning. He’s a big guy, and a big eater. He should be hungry.

Pike shrugs. “I dunno.”

Henry looks between their two plates and almost feels self-conscious of the amount of food he’d piled on for himself. He watches Pike push around what little food is on his own plate and frowns, doesn’t say anything else.

They eat, they chat, and Henry is glad to have his partner there to share it with. Pike doesn’t look like he’s enjoying it necessarily, but he does smile and laugh, and eventually eats the food on his plate with each tiny bite.

He wraps an arm around Henry as they talk to Jim and the gang, warm and casual. It’s nice until Henry registers the heat coming off of him. The man is wearing a long-sleeved shirt, but it’s not as hot of a day as it could be. Pike doesn’t look like he’s sweating, either. Henry turns to him and opens his mouth to ask-

“Get up there ‘n dance, you two!” Jim practically pushes them in as the music starts and other couples lead each other in.

Henry and Pike wander in. They don’t have much choice now, in the center of it all, so they hold onto each other and go with the music. Pike, as Henry learned not too long after they started dating, is a pretty good dancer.

Tonight, though, he’s stiff, anxious, and clearly ready to get out of there.

They lean into each other, and Henry immediately leans back, and feels guilty for alarming the other man. “Okay, do you have a fever?” he asks accusingly. Pike stammers, and Henry’s hand is on his face and neck before he can work up an answer. “You’re too warm! Jesus, you’re wearing flannel! We’re going-”

Pike tries to say something else, and then presses his mouth shut. He brings a hand to his mouth, stifling something that’s between a gag and a hiccup. Henry swears and leads him away from the music, the people, all things that nobody would want to be near when they’re sick.

Pike gags again once they’re out of the main crowd, and this time he has to pull his hand away and vomit into the grass. Henry winces and looks away.

“Pike...” he mutters, half out of sympathy and half out of exasperation as he rubs his back. He feels Pike’s muscles tense when he heaves once again.

Jim, for once, doesn’t have anything to say. He shows up with a bottle of water and a sympathetic smile, and squeezes Pike’s shoulder as they make their way out of the picnic. It’s time to go home.

-----

Henry sits cross-legged on the bathroom floor. It’s not where he expected to end up today, but right now he’d rather be here with the man he loves than anywhere else.

Pike’s hunched over the toilet, looking smaller than he ever had. He shudders. He’s out of his flannel now, in a worn T-shirt that he usually wears on his days off. His hair is in a ponytail, Henry had pulled it back as gently as he could to keep it out of the way.

He hasn’t said a thing since they got there, only camped in the bathroom and nodded slightly when Henry asked if he wanted company.

Pike’s stomach gurgles angrily, and he curls forward even further as his breath catches in pain. He stays in that position for a few seconds before he lurches forward and gags, bringing up what has to be the last of what’s in him. He heaves, nothing comes up. He heaves again, nothing.

Henry frowns. “I’ll go get you some water.”

“Wait-”

Henry stops. Pike’s voice is torn up from an hour of throwing up, the poor man looks miserable beyond anything he’s seen before. His eyes are watery and Henry isn’t sure if it’s from the force of vomiting or because he’s crying.

“What?”

“Don’t-” Pike clears his throat but it does little to improve his voice. “Don’t leave me. Please.”

Henry’s heart tightens. His never heard Pike like this. “Pike I’m... I’m just going to the kitchen.”

But if Pike wasn’t crying before, he looks like he could now.

So Henry wordlessly nods, sits back down, and takes Pike’s hand as he backs away to the wall. It isn’t long before Pike’s head is in his lap.

He’s not asleep, he has to be way too uncomfortable to fall asleep. He shivers even though he’s still burning up and Henry almost wonders if he should call the town doctor or something. And then he remembers that Pike’s a grown man who hasn’t even been sick for a day and he’ll be fine.

They sit there for a while before Henry speaks again. “I think you need to be in bed.”

Pike doesn’t say anything, but lets Henry help him to his feet.

“Let’s get you some water, okay?” he intends on going to the kitchen himself but is now not surprised when Pike follows him in and stands there for the few seconds it takes for Henry to fill a glass and turn back toward the bedroom.

Henry sets a bucket next to the bed before the two of them climb in. Pike covers up immediately and lets Henry wrap an arm around him.

“I’d really love to know what happened today.”

“What d’ you mean?”

“You were obviously getting sick and I can’t help but wonder why the hell you wanted to go to that stupid picnic.”

Pike chuckles tiredly. “It’s ridiculous...” Henry sort of feels bad bringing it up now, when he seems like he could fall asleep at any moment.

“Pike...” Henry breathes out. “I don’t like whatever that was. C’mon, give me something.”

“I-” Pike gives a heavy sigh, clears his throat. “I just wanted to be with you.” he ends it with an attempt at a shrug.

Henry blinks. “Jesus, I could have stayed here. You really think I would’ve wanted to leave you here sick alone?”

“No, but-”

“Pike, you could’ve told me.”

Pike nods. “Yeah. I know.”

Henry sighs, presses a kiss to his partner’s forehead. “Just... communicate next time.”

Pike huffs out a sleepy chuckle. “I will.”
