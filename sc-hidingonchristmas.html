Title: Hiding on Christmas
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: The boys get sick and happily decided to spend Christmas at home
Word count: 1k

Hiding on Christmas

Poor David had been struck down with a nasty cold. Well, he’d been coming down with it for a while, Patrick thought. He had been complaining of a scratchy throat a couple days before, but they both chalked it up to the weather, in complete denial that either of them could get sick during Christmas time.

It wasn’t a huge deal, they could still head to Patrick’s parents’ home. David would just be sneezing a little more than usual.

They were getting ready to head out when David mumbled something about needing to sit down for a moment. When Patrick squeezed his shoulder and asked him if he was alright, heat was radiating off of his boyfriend’s body.

He already felt a little guilty for dragging David along when he had a run-of-the-mill cold, but a fever was too much.

Patrick lit the fireplace and wrapped David in the warmest blanket he owned. It was an adorable sight, seeing his love grumpily paw at his nose while the Mariah Carey Christmas album played in the background.

Patrick grinned, and then realized he had no idea what his mother had said on the other line.

“Are you sure you two are alright?”

“Yeah mom, David’ll be fine.” He ignored the scoff that came from the couch. “We just didn’t think it’d be a great idea to come and... share it. And I don’t really think he’s up to traveling, you know?”

Marcy Brewer made a sympathetic noise. Patrick felt something then. His mother was such a nurturing person, and to know that David was so much a part of the family now that she was worrying about him getting sick warmed his heart.

“I’m sure you’re not far behind.”

Patrick chuckled. She had a point. He and David were kissing (among other things) around the same time David started to feel symptoms. “Yeah, well,” He smiled. He couldn’t bring himself to be bothered by it. “We’ll manage. Love you mom. Merry Christmas.”

She giggled. “Merry Christmas. Tell David I said I hope he feels better.”

“Will do.” Patrick ended the call and turned back toward his boyfriend, who was looking significantly more grumpy.

“This is why my family hasn’t celebrated Christmas the last few years,” David muttered. “It’s too much pressure to put on one day, and we’re always setting ourselves up for disappointment when something like... well, like this happens.” He gestured aggressively to himself, eyebrows knitted together and eyes wide in his usual Exasperated David Rose expression. Except this time it involved sunken eyes and a red nose. His hair was messier than usual, unstyled and pieces hanging in his eyes.

“I know,” Patrick sighed, perching himself on the back of the couch. “Technically it’s two days, though. Christmas is tomorrow.”

“Well then both of them are ruined.”

“They’re not ruined, David. We can have our own Christmas here. Do you want anything?”

“I think you know what I want,” David smiled.

Patrick thought he did at first, but David’s expression lacked the usual lewdness when he was flirting with him. Finally, it hit him. “I’m not driving all the way to Elmdale to buy you coffee, David.”

“Well, it’s not my fault the cafe is closed today.”

“Because people want to spend time with their families! And I’m not sure that you’re supposed to be drinking coffee when you’re sick.”

“Ridiculous.”

Patrick chuckled. He ran his fingers through David’s hair, petting his head like when he’s trying to calm a cat. He half-expected David to start purring as he lean into Patrick’s hand. “How about some hot chocolate? And later we can eat that pie mom and dad are never going to get.”

David sighed. “I guess that’s fine. Not that I’m going to be able to taste anything,” he gave a congested sniffle for effect.

Patrick snorted. “You’ll live.”

He went to the kitchen and made David’s favorite hot chocolate recipe. He whisked the milk and cocoa powder on the stove. He was stirring in the sugar when a miserable cough made him frown.

He finished in record time. Patrick poured the cocoa into the two mugs and brought them to the living room.

David was fast asleep, snoring softly and nose running down his upper lip. He did mention that he had trouble sleeping the night before with his stuffed nose. He just finally crashed.

Patrick smiled and put the cocoa in the fridge. They could heat it up later.

-----

Christmas Eve was fantastic. Even with David feeling awful, he seemed to enjoy it, and being able to eat the food they didn’t get to bring to the Brewers’ home definitely made him feel better.

David went to sleep a little easier that night with a full belly and an exhausting day of eating and watching movies.

“You know we have to make sure my parents don’t know we’re still in town, right? That means that we can’t risk running into them,” David had said before he fell asleep.

“Guess we’ll have to stay here all of tomorrow, then.”

“Oh well,” David giggled sleepily.

Patrick woke David with a sneeze.

He sniffled, finding his nose stuffed and throat sore.

David made a sleepy, sympathetic sound and cuddled him closer. “Bless.”

Patrick couldn’t help but chuckle. “Merry Christmas, huh?”

“Mhmm.”

Their day consisted of arguing over the placement of the tissue box and the best movie to watch while they drank hot chocolate.

David fell asleep against Patrick’s shoulder shortly after lunch, and was given a rude awakening by way of a sneeze from his boyfriend.

“Jesus...”

“Sorry. Sleep good?”

“Mmm, not really, but at least you're comfy." He made a happy noise and pressed his face into the space of Patrick's neck, snuffling into his ear without a concern for spreading germs. "And so warm."

Patrick sighed, and then coughed, not bothering to cover it completely. "I think it's time to medicate ourselves," he said decidedly.

"Nooo," David argued, holding him tighter. "Just a few more minutes."

He smiled, feeling the warm, comfortable weight of his boyfriend against him. He didn't particularly want to get up. In fact, he saw a nap in his near future. "I suppose it can wait."
