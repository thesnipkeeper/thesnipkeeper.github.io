At the Top of the List

"Caleb."

Caleb dreamt of someone calling his name.

"Caleb."

His eyes shot open. This wasn't a dream. He caught a glimpse of glowing, green eyes in his face and gasped at the figure standing on the side of his bed, pulling himself up against the headboard.

"Calm down!" The figure whispered, sparing a glance toward Nott, who was curled up next to Caleb, snoring softly. "It's me, it's Fjord."

Caleb breathed out a calming sigh, panic dissipating as Fjord waited patiently for him to wake up and process what was happening. Alarms didn’t work for when his friends were inside the perimeter, he reminded himself, so of course he was susceptible to… rude awakenings.

"What is it? What's wrong?" he mumbled, rubbing his face. It was 2:30 in the morning, he'd only been asleep for a little over an hour. Nott warned him not to stay up too late reading, even if they didn’t have anywhere urgent to be in the morning. As usual, he should have listened to Nott.

"Look, uh… it's Molly. He's ill and- well, he's asking for you."

Anxiety rose in his chest. Molly had seemed rather unwell the night before. He only had one drink after their return to the inn (strange), and he was the first to retire for the night (even stranger). Caleb hoped that it was nothing, he was simply tired from the events of the day, but - apparently - it wasn’t. "He's ill? You should wake Jester right away. She can help."

"No, no Caleb," a large hand clapped his shoulder, and Fjord pointedly said, "He's asking for you."

Caleb averted his eyes. Recently, his and Molly's relationship had... changed. For the better, Caleb hoped. He liked to think that no one else could tell, but their group wasn't exactly known for keeping to themselves when it came to each other's business. There was no confusion or awkwardness from Fjord, only apparent concern for Molly's well-being. That in itself was enough to know that everyone was aware of the two of them.

Caleb nodded wordlessly, threw his covers back, made sure Nott was still asleep, and followed Fjord down the hall.

"He woke me up and said that he felt 'wrong', whatever that means," the half-orc explained in a hushed voice. He turned the handle to his room and led Caleb in.

A the candle in front of the window had just recently been lit, as evidenced by the melted wax that had yet to drip down the side. The room was about the same as his and Nott's, perhaps a bit bigger as the two of them didn’t mind being close together as much as the others did. What really caught Caleb's eye, however, was a shivering Mollymauk on the left side of the single bed. He took shaky breaths in and out, and as Caleb walked further into the room, he saw that those breaths weren't teeth chattering from cold, but rather… was Mollymauk crying?

An exhale from the tiefling was accompanied by a soft, barely-stifled whimper as if he was trying so hard not to cry. He was sitting up in bed, a blanket around his shoulders evenly enough that he couldn’t have covered himself that well in his current state. Molly's hair was messy and damp, and his skin had a flushed quality to it that - now that Caleb thought about it - was there the night before.

Caleb glanced back at Fjord, who was standing near the doorway with his arms crossed and a worried expression, before sitting on the edge of the bed and taking one of Molly's hands between his. It was damp with tears and probably mucus as well, wet streaks all over the tiefling's lavender hand. Caleb frowned. A sign that this had been going on for a little while, then.

Molly's teary gaze went from his hand to Caleb's eyes, and he covered his face with the other. He took a shuddery breath and huffed out a laugh despite himself. "Don’t look at me right now. I'm disgusting."

Caleb cracked a smile. "You ask me in here and then tell me not to look at you? What is that all about?" But Molly's smile had already faded, and the tears were returning, and… Caleb had no idea what to do. He'd never learned how to fix these kinds of situations. So, he leaned closer, and pulled Molly into his arms until the tiefling was crying into his shoulder.

"Oh…okay, it's alright." As Caleb turned to look at Fjord, the two of them exchanged looks of concern. "I hope you don’t mind if I stay here for a little bit."

Fjord gestured widely. "Please. Let me know if you need anything."

He rubbed Mollymauk's back, thankful to hear the crying dissipate, but displeased to feel a fever through the fabric of Molly's shirt. "You may be able to stay in our room for the rest of the night."

"I'll take the floor and hope Nott doesn’t fuckin' maim me when she wakes up and sees me there."

Caleb snorted, because that was an absolutely warranted concern.

There was a click of the door shutting quietly behind them, and then they were alone.

Caleb reluctantly pulled Molly away to look at his face. It was wet and blotchy, and Caleb noticed his nose running more than what was normal for a crying fit. He clumsily brushed some curls out of Molly's face and pulled him in, pressing a kiss to his overheated forehead.

"You're running a temperature," he informed grimly. "Fjord said you were ill?"

Molly nodded as he rubbed at his eyes with a sniffle. "I've never felt this awful, Caleb. I'm so sorry. Everything hurts and I'm tired and I can't sleep and I wanted…"

"…Me?"

Another sniffle as Molly gave the smallest smile. He reached for Caleb's hand and struggled until they finally had their fingers entangled comfortably.

"Jester could help. She is a cleric, after all."

"Caleb… just- just… be here, alright?"

"I can do that." Caleb caught sight of an abandoned purple handkerchief on the nightstand and make quick use of it, wiping up Molly's face despite the tiefling's protests. He batted at Caleb's hand weakly and made a sound of disgust when the wizard began wiping at his nose.

"Eugh," he said, snatching the kerchief from Caleb's grip. "Really?"

Caleb shrugged, seemingly unaware that touching someone else's bodily fluids was something he should be concerned about. "It needed to be done. Don’t you feel a little better now?"

"Not really, no." He started to chuckle and ended up coughing instead. It sounded painful, and Caleb could hear it deep in Molly's chest. He tried not to worry too quickly. Illness was a normal thing, people got sick and got better, no cause for concern. He clamped the kerchief over his mouth and let out two more breathless coughs.

"Have you slept yet?" Caleb asked. He raised a hand to feel Molly's forehead, and then hesitated. Maybe he was doing too much touching. Maybe Molly didn’t want to be touched anymore. Is that why he stopped him just a few seconds ago? Caleb should've noticed sooner as to not make him uncomfortable.

Caleb's thoughts were interrupted when Molly's hand left his other, and grabbed his wrist, pressing the back of Caleb's hand to his forehead with a soft smile. "I've tried," he answered while Caleb's hand traveled down to his cheek, then his neck. He let out a shaky sigh, and gave a desperate, exhausted look. "I feel so awful, Caleb."

He hummed thoughtfully.

Caleb climbed onto what was Fjord's side of the bed and covered both of them up. Molly was quick to press his body to Caleb's, shivering incessantly, letting out soft whines at every movement.

"What's wrong?"

"Achy," Molly answered miserably. "Everything hurts." He took a deep, gurgly breath and rushed to get his abused kerchief in front of his mouth before coughing. It was muffled, but Caleb heard the unmistakable sound of mucus being brought up from his chest. He wiped his mouth and shuddered, pressing his forehead to Caleb's chest.

Molly didn't fall asleep easily. He spent a good amount of time tossing and turning, muscles too achy to let him sit still. Finally, though, Caleb heard even breathing and soft snoring. Molly was finally asleep, and with that, he could sleep as well.

-----

"Molly! You're here!" Jester's announcement turned all of their heads to a disheveled Mollymauk. "I thought you would sleep all day."

His clothes were wrinkled, hair a mess and gait lazy and lacking its usual grace. And yet, Molly offered his usual smile as he plopped into the chair next to Caleb. His demeanor wasn’t very different from how he acted after not getting enough sleep, as no one seemed to say anything.

"Oh," Molly yawned and ran a hand through his hair in an attempt to fix it. "That would be very nice, wouldn’t it? But we have things to do." A pair of red eyes caught Caleb's as the wizard stared at him. Was he going to pretend that he was alright? Perhaps he was truly feeling better after last night. Fjord seemed to be giving him a similar look.

Everyone's conversations continued around them, giving Caleb a chance to lean in. "How uh- how are you feeling?"

Molly cleared his throat, sitting up and leaning in closer as well. "You left me," he whispered, hand over his heart in faux-offense. His voice still had a hoarse, congested tinge to it, but not nearly as bad as before, probably because he had been crying.

"I was letting you get the extra rest that you needed after last night." he ignored the eyeroll. "How is your fever?"

Molly shrugged. "Gone for now, I think. I woke up drenched in sweat. It was awful."

Caleb nodded, relieved to hear that the fever had subsided. Maybe he really was getting better that quickly. Still… while the rest were ordering breakfast, Molly stayed relatively silent as he drank a mug of water Caleb had pushed in his direction.

"Bacon?" Caleb offered.

"No, thank you."

"You should eat something."

Molly sighed. "I-"

"Caleb's right!" Jester chimed in. "Breakfast is the most important meal of the day, Molly. If you don’t eat, then you could die!"

"Yeah, something like that," Beau agreed.

"Alright alright!" Molly raised his hands in surrender and broke off a piece of bacon from Caleb's plate. It was only a bite, but everyone else seemed satisfied that their harassment had worked. Caleb watched as he chewed and swallowed, making a face as if it was the most unpleasant thing he had ever done.

The two of the followed behind the rest of the group on their way to run errands. Molly moved slower than usual, but his carefree attitude remained.

They joined Nott and Beau at the blacksmith. Molly attempted to flirt with the halfling man for a discount (it didn’t work) (it never really worked). He joked around with Nott, the two of them messing around with people around the city. Molly would distract people while Nott would pick their pockets, and it went on for a painfully long time until they reached Pumat Sol's store.

The six of the always looked for excuses to go to Pumat's. He was one of the more pleasant people in town, and he always had something good to buy.

The large firbolg offered a jolly greeting, and Molly successfully worked his charm to get a discount on Fjord's armor enchantment.

"Thanks, Molly," Fjord said, patting him on the back on their way out of the store. Molly stumbled for a quick second and then smiled.

"Anytime!"

Caleb caught a quick wince from him. Molly's hand went to his head, and he wavered dangerously, enough for Caleb to prepare to catch him.

Molly met his wide eyes as he steadied himself. "I'm fine! I'm fine, just a little lightheaded," followed by a cough muffled into a kerchief Caleb realized had been wadded in his hand for a while. "I… oh dear," he blinked hard, supporting himself against the wall of a building. Everyone had walked ahead of them, probably for the best if he was trying to hide the fact that he was ill.

Caleb wasn’t sure what to say, so he waited patiently while the tiefling regained his balance. Eventually, Molly did. He stood up straight and shook his head.

"Whew, okay, let's go catch up with the others."

Caleb caught his arm before he could walk off. Molly's eyes widened.

"…Yes?"

Wordlessly, he placed his palm on Molly's forehead. The tiefling sighed heavily and closed his eyes as though he'd been found out.

"Your fever is back."

"Yeah…"

"We should return to the tavern, then."

Molly was already shaking his head when he started his sentence. "No no. No one actually has the luxury of staying in bed when they're sick, Caleb, not really, and especially not us. We have things to do, money to make, and though I'm not at my best, I'm not going to drop dead because of a little temperature." His breath caught on the last word, and he muffled a quick sneeze into his handkerchief. The purple fabric was darker in places, like it'd been used quite a bit while Caleb hadn't noticed.

Caleb frowned, looking over his… friend. Molly walked off, and he had no choice but to follow.

"Look at this, Molly!" Jester exclaimed. She felt the fabric of a dress and held it out for Molly to feel as well.

"Oh wow…" Molly said, running his jeweled fingers across the skirt of the dress. "That's very nice. Are you going to get it?"

"I should, shouldn’t I?" It wasn’t a question. Jester was already looking for the clerk and asking about the dress.

Caleb shifted uncomfortably. Many rich, fancy-looking people wandered about the store. He usually avoided walking into establishments like this, as he would usually just be kicked out anyway. For once, though, he was relatively clean. Their adventure in the mud a couple days before forced him to bathe just to function normally (and also because Molly told him to, and actually made it sound good). He felt the need to follow Molly into the store, to keep an eye on him despite how much he didn’t belong.

He spent a few more minutes standing near his friends, practically hiding behind them. The three of them were just waiting for Fjord, Nott, and Beau. Nobody seemed to be paying him any mind, but that didn’t seem to matter for his anxiety, because the place was packed. So many things could happen-

"Caleb?"

Caleb jumped at the hand on his arm, instinctively recoiling. Molly quickly retracted it.

"Whoa, okay, sorry. I didn’t mean to frighten you."

Just Molly. It was just Molly. Caleb shook his head. "It's alright. I should have been paying attention."

Molly gave him a look. It was a look that had been appearing on his face more and more the closer he and Caleb had gotten. Caleb wasn’t sure how he felt about the genuine concern coming from him, he almost missed Molly's constant mask of teasing and flirting.

The tiefling's brows knit in concern, which is rather funny considering he was the one burning with fever just the night before. "You look like you want to dart out of here, love. Why don’t we go out and wait for Jester?"

Caleb allowed himself to be led away to a weathered wooden table next to a food stand. Molly quickly sat down and huffed out a sigh. He certainly didn’t look like someone who had the flu. Maybe he was telling the truth; maybe he was feeling better.

But something in the back of Caleb's mind nagged that there was still something wrong. He didn’t know what it was; Molly seemed fine, after all, but he needed to keep an eye out for anything.

"How are you feeling?"

The question seemed to catch Molly off-guard, but he regained his smile before Caleb could pinpoint his expression. "I'm fine."

"Your fever?"

Molly cleared his throat. "Nothing I haven't dealt with before." His smile softened to that of something genuine. "Honestly. There's no need for you to waste all of your worry on me!"

Caleb really wanted to believe him. He couldn’t.

"Mollymauk, why are you lying to me?" he didn’t mean for it to sound so accusatory, but it got a reaction out of the tiefling. Molly tensed, averting eye contact for once. "I get up in the middle of the night to find you sick as a dog and you expect me to believe that everything is fine now?"

Molly laughed, and then sniffled, and upon further inspection Caleb realized that he was crying.

Shit.

He hovered awkwardly as he watched Molly struggle not to cry. Lips quivering, rubbing at his eyes to stop the tears.

The façade was gone.

"I'm sorry… I'm sorry, I just- I didn’t want anyone to make a big deal out of all of this," he took Caleb's hand, leaning in and nuzzling it. Hot to the touch. He was burning up. And with the fever, Caleb could imagine the achiness came with it. "This always happens," he said with a self-deprecating chuckle, wiping tears out of his eyes. "I'd be alright if this fucking fever hadn't come back."

Caleb made a questioning sound.

"They always make me… ugh," he gestured lazily to his current state. "And I get dizzy and… honestly? I'm not sure I can make it back to the tavern at this point."

Caleb sighed. "You should have asked Jester for some medicine, at least."

Molly nodded reluctantly, sniffling. "I know. It's too late now, though."

The moment they caught sight of the others, Caleb spoke to Fjord. The half-orc nodded in understanding and didn’t hesitate before holding his arms out to Molly.

Molly looked up at Fjord guiltily.

"I wondered if this was gonna happen," Fjord said as he scooped him up. Molly didn’t fight it, he didn’t mind being carried. In fact, he really seemed to be enjoying it, snuggling into Fjord's chest and wrapping his arms around his neck. Fjord didn’t mind. The half-orc didn’t say anything else, as the others were watching with confusion.

"Oh no, Molly, are you not feeling good?" Jester reached up and pressed the back of her hand against his forehead. She frowned. "Oh, Molly… you should be in bed."

Molly started to shiver during their walk, despite the weather being unchanging. He'd pulled his arms from around Fjord's neck in order to wrap them around himself. Caleb started to remove his coat when Jester nudged him.

"Your cloak has dirt all over it, Caleb. Here," she removed her own and reached up to drape it over Molly. He gave a shuddery thanks.

"He's burnin' up," Fjord said, looking down at the man in his arms. "Jester, can you fix him?"

Jester thought for a moment. "Well, I hope so. I think I have some medicine that can bring his fever down."

"What about a healing spell?" Nott asked.

"I don't think healing spells will help with something like this. Illness is a lot different than a stab wound or broken bone. It's not an injury, it's an infection." She paused. "But we can still help him feel better."

-----

Molly's eyes fluttered open when Fjord started leaning down to deposit him in the bed. He allowed Fjord and Caleb to cover him, snuggling into the bed as they did so with a little cough muffled into the blanket.

"Jester is doing what she can, alright Molly?"

"Bless her heart."

"Couldn’t have said it better myself. Feel better." Fjord smoothed the tiefling's hair back lovingly and earned an appreciative noise in response. He walked out with a nod to Caleb.

And for the time being, they were alone.

"Don’t do that again," Caleb said.

Red eyes that had drifted closed opened wide again. "I won't. Promise." He reached out until Caleb took his hand, and then pulled the wizard over until he was sitting on the edge of the bed. "I… I'm afraid I have a confession to make, Caleb."

Caleb raised an eyebrow.

"The um… the crying…"

"Yes, very normal to be upset when you're feverish."

"No, I mean -  yes, it was the fever but…" Molly sighed. "At the carnival, I had Yasha and others who attached themselves until I was well again. When I woke last night feeling exactly the same, I- I wanted someone to be close to."

"I'm going to point out that you were sharing a bed with someone already," Caleb tried, desperately hiding his blush.

"Yes, and Fjord was an absolute sweetheart. But I wanted you."

Caleb smiled. "Ah." He cleared his throat. "Have you had many fevers in your two years of… existing?"

A tiny nostalgic smile tugged at Molly's lips. He pulled the blankets from his lap to his shoulders with a shudder before answering, "I came down with quite a few fevers at the carnival. Turns out that waking up in a grave isn't overly gentle on one's body!" He chuckled, and then had to muffled a cough into his hand. "Yasha was- she was always there to make sure I was alright. I wasn’t in a good place back then, so terrified and sickly…" he cleared his throat and sank down into bed before hugging his pillow.

Caleb had never given much thought to Molly and Yasha's relationship. Molly seemed to be thinking on it quite a bit now.
