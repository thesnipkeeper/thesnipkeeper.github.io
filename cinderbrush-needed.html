Title: Needed
Fandom: Cinderbrush
Summary: Cameron is glad to be needed
Word count: 1.1k

Needed

"Wow, you look fucked up, man," is the first thing Cameron says when his friend lets him in. And in his defense, Jamie does.

Without makeup or glasses, there's nothing to hide the dark circles under their eyes. They're wearing an old pair of leggings and a hoodie that nearly goes down to their knees.

Jamie doesn't even try to act offended. They just nod and let Cameron in. "I feel fucked up. Come on in, it's freezing out there." They lay a hand on Cameron's back to lead him into his living room, and then promptly flop down onto the sofa that looks like it's been their home for most of the morning.

Cameron doesn’t sit. He wrings his hands, looking from his shoes to Jamie. He swallows a lump in his throat, willing himself not to cry.

Jamie's face falls. "Uh oh. What happened?"

Cameron clears his throat. "Sasha and I broke up."

Jamie stands up the moment he's finished speaking, wrapping him in a warm hug. And suddenly Cameron finds himself crying into his friend's neck, hot tears that he's spent so long holding back pouring down his face and soaking into Jamie's sweatshirt. He latches on so tight, he's needed this.

Jamie's gentle shushing and sweet words die down with his own sobbing, and eventually Cameron finds himself being led to the couch.

"You can stay here all day if you want to," they keep their voice low as Cameron scrubs at his face with his sleeve. "I'm not great company right now, but… maybe you shouldn’t be alone today."

Cameron growls in frustration. "I don't- I don't know what I want right now. I don't- ugh."

"Okay, it's okay…"

Jamie's hand makes its way down his back, and they sigh. A few seconds go by, "Do you wanna talk about it?"

"No."

"Fair enough." They clear their throat and make a noise of discomfort.

Cameron rubs the fog out of his vision with a sniffle and finally notices the true state of Jamie's living room.  A bottle of ibuprofen and a glass of water sit on the coffee table. They're both sitting on a pile of blankets, and there's a pillow against the armrest.

And Jamie is squeezing their eyes shut, hand leaving Cameron's back to massage their head.

"Jesus," Cameron gives a watery chuckle. "Exactly how fucked did you get at the party last night?"

"No more than usual!" Jamie grinds out. "I'm fine. It has to be almost out of my system by now."

Cameron winces. "Yeesh. Stomach?"

They nod. "Maybe a little too much to drink." They make a face and lay a hand on their stomach, and Cameron gets a bad feeling.

Jamie definitely isn't known for excessive hangovers or comedowns, especially hours after a party. Cameron remembers seeing them sip from a cup of something while negotiating the price of their um...wares, but he never noticed Jamie actually taking anything. They're smarter than to mix drugs with alcohol anyway.

"What-?"

Jamie doesn't bother to move away when Cameron presses a hand to their forehead, but they do look at him with a raised eyebrow.

"You're warm," Cameron states, awkwardly pulling his hand back down to his lap. "You're sick, Jamie. That's probably why you feel like shit." He must look so ridiculous trying to sound logical when his face is still damp and red from crying.

"Ah." Jamie runs a hand down their face with a resigned sigh. "I guess that makes sense. Threw up twice this morning and I didn't think I had that much to drink."

Cameron smiles. He doesn't realize that he's started to stare off until he feels a hand on his arm. Jamie looks at him intently.

"Hey, let's watch a movie or something." It's not a question. Jamie turns on the TV and finds something horribly-rated on Netflix that neither of them have seen.

Jamie curls up on their side of the couch. Cameron knows that look well, it's the look that they feel gross but are too tired to do anything about it. He vividly remembers caring for a very out of it Jamie after drinking a shit ton of alcohol. They'd had an issue with their dad at the time, and had chosen to drown their sorrows. It ended with Cameron spending the night to make sure his friend didn't choke on their own vomit.

Just as Cameron expected, Jamie makes a sound that's somewhere between a whine and a grunt, now kneading their hand into their soft stomach.

"I'll be right back. You don't have to-" they press the back of their hand to their mouth and swallow. "-to pause it."

The couple in the movie kiss after having very little resolution to the issues that caused their conflict in the first place, and that's just when Jamie wanders back in and flops on the couch.

They don't bother to say anything before adjusting Cameron just right so his legs will make the optimal pillow. Cameron covers them with a nearby throw.

"Definitely not a hangover," they mumble.

Cameron runs his hands through their hair in the way that practically has them purring.

"Wait," Jamie sits up, propping themselves on an arm as they face Cameron. "Is this right? I should be comforting you."

Cameron fails to bite back a smile. "Honestly? I think this is helping more than you know."

Jamie chuckles, and that's all it takes to get them to lie back down. "You know I fucking love you, right?"

"Not nearly as much as I love you, bro." Cameron's hands go back to Jamie's hair, untangling parts as they probably haven't bothered to run a comb through it since their digestive adventure this morning.

A scoff. "Gross. Watch the movie."

They decide to put on a second movie, and neither of them pay attention to it. Cameron's mind is elsewhere; it will be for a while, and Jamie is too sick to appreciate the poorly-written script.

Jamie's melted under his touch, and Cameron can feel their body heat soaking through the fabric of his sweatpants. He's helped Jamie through various hangovers and bad highs, but illness is a new one. He kind of likes his friend being sober enough to be self-aware.

Their breath evens out after a while, and they're fast asleep when Cameron looks down. He smiles. Jamie's neutral face is incredibly adorable, though he'd never tell them that for fear they'd murder him in a creative, witchcraft-y way.

Having someone asleep against him… it's nice.

He's not alone, Jamie's presence reminds him that.

And most of all, he's needed.
