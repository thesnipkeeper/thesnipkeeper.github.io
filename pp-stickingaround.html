Sticking Around

“I did tell you not to buy from vendors in that area,” Peter said as he ran his hand up and down Juno’s back. “But why would you listen to me? I’m just the one who knows about all of this, so-”

“Nureyev.” Juno huffed and swallowed thickly. “I’m really not in the mood. And I was hungry, okay? Can you blame me?”

“Well, I’m not the one blaming anyone. You’re the one who’s been vomiting for the last hour, after all.” He patted Juno’s back while the ex-detective growled at him.

And promptly heaved. Peter did warn him, this was his doing. If Juno wanted food, he could have just waited like the rest of them. Still, he wasn’t going anywhere. His love was miserably sick (even if it was his own doing) and Peter planned on sticking around.

Snapping him out of his thoughts, Juno groaned. This time his brows were pinched together in discomfort, and a hand went to his belly.

“You’ve gotta leave.”

“What? Juno, I didn’t mean anything-”

“No, Nureyev, you need to leave, like now.”

Peter put it together when Juno started to stand up.

“Ah. Alright, then. I’ll-” he cleared his throat. “I’ll go. Let me know if you need anything?”

“I- yeah, just...” Juno made a shooing motion and Peter wasted no more time giving his love some privacy.

He wandered out into the rather small common room of the ship, finding Rita watching some kind of stream and Buddy shuffling through papers while pretending that she wasn’t also watching the stream.

“Where is our lovely in-house doctor?” Peter asked.

“She’s in the infirmary doing inventory, I think,” Buddy said without looking up. “Not that it’s any of my business, but what are you looking for her for?” It wasn’t any of her business, but Buddy was of course going to ask anyway. Peter knew that both he and Juno didn’t seem to get along with Vespa, but damn if she wasn’t one of the best doctors he knew.

Vespa was an obnoxious, angry, unpleasant person, but she was also an amazing doctor.

“It seems that Juno’s gotten a bit of food poisoning.”

Buddy chuckled. “You did warn him.”

“I did,” Peter sighed out. “And yet...”

“Vespa might have some sort of medicine.”

Vespa was in comfortable clothes, and looked to be doing inventory just for something to do than actual necessity. She looked up as he entered, and before she could say anything,

“Juno’s sick, I thought you could help him.”

Her expression changed from annoyed assassin to... well, annoyed doctor, and she put a hand on her hip, setting down the monitor that she’d been working on.

“Okay, elaborate, Ransom. What kind of sick?”

“Well, he ate some strange food on our most recent stop.”

“He did, huh? He throwing up? Diarrhea?”

Peter nodded.

“Any fever?”

Peter thought for a moment. “I recall him having chills near the beginning.”

She nodded, hummed thoughtfully. “Okay, and I’m assuming that you just want him to stop puking, then.”

“Well, yes, I thought that was obvious.”

Vespa looked like she desperately wanted to smack the snark off of his face, but instead she swallowed it and said, “I have an antiemetic that could help, but really, food poisoning goes away fast if you just, you know, get it all out.”

“So, what you’re saying is that there’s nothing you can do?”

“Basically.”

Peter sighed.

“Look,” Vespa leaned against the counter, resigned. “Just keep him comfortable and hydrated. Come get me if you need to, but he’s probably just gonna be puking and shitting for the day and then it’ll be over with.”

He couldn’t help but wince.

“Oh, here,” she opened a cabinet and threw a bottle of something purple. “Electrolyte solution. Tell him to go easy on the plain water until he can keep food down.”

He nodded, inspecting the bottle of unappetizing liquid. “Thank you, Vespa. Really.”

“Whatever.”

Juno was curled up in his bed when Peter returned. All of the blankets had been kicked and shoved to the other side, leaving nothing but a miserable detective in the middle.

“Feeling better?” Peter already knew the answer.

“I’m never eating anything ever again.”

“Well,” Peter lowered himself onto the bed, careful not to jostle it too much. “That’s one way to go about things. Do you feel up to drinking anything?” He waved the bottle in Juno’s face.

Juno just narrowed his eyes. “What the hell is that?”

“Electrolyte solution from Vespa.”

“If it’s from Vespa, then it’s definitely poisoned.”

“Now now, do you really think that you could be any more poisoned?”

Juno snorted. “Fair point. I’ll... I’ll try some later.”

“Alright.” Peter set it aside and laid down to face Juno.

Juno chuckled tiredly, looking at him with a fair too adorable air of misery. “You do not have to stick around for this.”

“I’ve nothing else to do,” Peter reasoned.

“There’s definitely something better to do than watch me barf, but I’m not gonna refuse the company.”

“Good,” Peter leaned over and pressed a kiss to his cheek. “Then I guess I’m sticking around.”
