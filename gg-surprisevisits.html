Surprise Visits

“That’s it, get it all up,” Lorelai murmured as she ran a hand up and down his back.

Luke sniffled, which did absolutely nothing, and spat into the water below. “I told you that place was no good.”

“I know you did, babe,” Lorelai sighed. “I’m sorry, I know to listen to you next time. And I’d give you a kiss right now but I’m not going to do that.”

Luke lurched forward and heaved as bile came up in a trickle. “It’s the thought that counts.”

“Exactly.”

This wasn’t exactly how they’d planned to spend their night. The new bathroom tile was nice and all, but not really great for sitting on for hours.

And Lorelai couldn’t help but feel a little guilty for begging her boyfriend to take her to the new restaurant just outside of town. But what was she supposed to do when they advertised all those fun flavored burgers and sandwiches? Not go?

And now they knew that it was not a great choice, and they never had to go there again.

Luke gagged breathlessly and brought up another bit of liquid.

“You can’t have much left in ya,” it was her attempt at comfort, and it seemed to work on the guy, because he nodded as saliva trickled off of his chin. “Wanna go to bed?”

Luke coughed, sniffled. “Not... not quite yet.”

She rubbed his back lovingly. “Gotcha.”

“You can go to bed, Lorelai,” he sighed out, flushing the toilet and grabbing a handful of paper to clean his face. “I don’t wanna keep you up.”

“Mhm, mhm, well thank you, but it’s eight o’ clock. I was more asking for you because, you know, you’re sick and all.”

“Ah,” and then, “It’s only eight? Jesus...”

She chuckled. “I’m sure you’ll feel better soon.”

“Anyone home?!”

“And apparently Rory’s here!” Lorelai glanced at her boyfriend and then called, “Up here!”

Luke was beyond caring about an audience, and it wasn’t the first time Rory had seen him sick.

Rory made her way up the stairs and wandered in, rather confused to find the bathroom light to be the only one on. “Aw, Luke...” she said. “What happened?”

Luke sighed heavily. “Your mom chose the restaurant. That’s what happened.”

“Ah,” Rory nodded. “Yeah, that sounds right.”

“And now I know,” Lorelai promised. “C’mon, babe. Let’s get you off the floor before you become one with it.”’

Luke gave little resistance (and little help) as Lorelai pulled him to his feet and led him to bed. He didn’t even try to get under the covers, he probably felt too sweaty for it anyway.

“So what’s with the surprise visit?” She asked as she took a bucket from under the bathroom sink and set it next to the bed.

“I just needed some time away from school,” Rory shrugged. “People were getting to me.”

“People, huh? Anyone specific?”

“No, just people in general. There’s too many of them.”

“Agreed.”

Luke tried his best to sit up against his pillow. He was still a bit weak and shaky. “Sorry, Rory. I’m sure this isn’t what you wanted to come home to.”

She scoffed. “It’s not like it’s your fault, Luke!” She and Luke both turned in Lorelai’s general direction.

Lorelai did her best to be offended, with a girly gasp and a hand over her heart. “I’m feeling a lot of blame right now and I don’t much care for it!”

“That’s okay, mom,” Rory laughed as she wrapped her in a hug. “I’m going down to study. I won’t be too loud, promise!”

Lorelai chuckled as her daughter left. She sat on the bed and laid the back of her hand against Luke’s cheek. “You want me to leave you alone for a while?”

A scoff. “Never.”
