Title: Happy New Year?
Fandom: Critical Role
Summary: Fjord and Caduceus have an interesting new year
Word count: 2.5k

Happy New Year?

The New Year’s Eve party was a success, and more fun than they’d all had together in a long time.

The living room of Fjord and Caduceus’ apartment was full of conversation and giggles and all of their friend’s having a good time, and the kitchen was full of amazing food brought by everyone. Fjord had originally been against having any sort of party at their place, but Jester had convinced him (“And your apartment is bigger, you guys. C’mon” it really wasn’t, but it was somehow the cleanest.). It was actually pretty nice to have everyone over at once.

Caduceus seemed less than thrilled. He was fine with the idea of having the party there, but now he was clearly over it.

The firbolg stood up from the couch where he and Caleb had been chatting, and went to the kitchen with his empty glass. Fjord followed. He could use a break from Beau’s drunken rants, anyway.

Caduceus was in the process of refilling his glass with tap water and gulping it down.

“Hey,” Fjord greeted as he walked in. Their kitchen was a small strip compared to the rest of the place, and they often had to coordinate because the two of them really couldn’t do stuff in there at the same time. He leaned against the counter while he watched his roommate drain the glass. “You regretting the whole party thing?” He asked with a half-chuckle.

Caduceus smiled and offered a chuckle of his own. “Nah, of course not. I’ve had a lot of fun tonight.”

Fjord nodded. “But you’re not havin’ fun anymore.”

The firbolg made a dismissive sound and shrugged. “I’m just getting tired, that’s all. If it’s alright with you all, I might head to bed early.”

“Want us to keep it down?”

“Oh, I won’t have a problem falling asleep,” Caduceus said with a smile. He really did sleep like the dead, Fjord liked to joke that it was because he grew up in a cemetery, when in reality it was probably because he had about a million family members under one roof.

“Happy new year, buddy.” Fjord backed up to let his large friend out of the kitchen, patting him on the back as he did so. He scooped up some more chips and dip for himself while he listened to Caduceus say his goodnights to everyone.

 

Some had left, but Beau and Jester ended up crashing in the living room. Fjord had been so exhausted by that point that he just threw a couple blankets and pillows at them and went to bed.

Now, they were in the dining room silently sipping coffee. Jester looked delightfully excited for the first day of the new year and was enjoying a leftover pastry from last night’s spread, while Beau looked half-dead, or at least like she wished that she was. Fjord didn’t hold back his laughter as he wandered in to grab.

“Shut the fuck up,” Beau muttered before he had thought of something snarky to say.

“Didn’t say a thing,” Fjord said innocently. “Has Caduceus gotten up yet?”

Jester shook her head. “We haven’t seen him, but maybe he got up before we did.”

He nodded and began making coffee. And then with a glance, realized that Jester was looking at him with a frown.

“What’s wrong?”

“Dunno,” Jester shrugged, averting her eyes as if she hadn’t just looked like a sad puppy. “It’s just like... Caduceus was acting a little weird last night. Like, even before he started getting tired.”

Fjord nodded. “I think the party was a little more overwhelming than he was expecting.”

“No, I don’t think that’s it.” She said. “I mean, how many times has he been with us at bars when we’re super wasted?”

Beau made a noise of agreement. “That’s true. He knew what he was getting into.”

“Okay... then what do you think, Jester?”

Another shrug. “I don’t know. I’ve just never seen Caduceus not eat when there was food around!”

Fjord was going to grab his cup from under the coffee maker when he paused. Had Caduceus really not eaten anything last night? He didn’t remember seeing him eat, but he also wasn’t paying much attention. Caduceus was a big guy, he was thin, but it was the combination of his firbolg metabolism and his diet that made it hard to eat what everyone else did, and when everyone else did.

“Huh.”

“Maybe he was upset,” Jester said, and then took another bite of her breakfast.

They didn’t have a chance to discuss it anymore before the subject of their conversation wandered in. He didn’t look any less tired than he had the night before, in fact... yeah, he didn’t look to be in higher spirits than he had been.

“Morning, Deuces.” Fjord lifted his coffee in a greeting before taking a sip. “Sleep good?”

Caduceus offered a lazy smile and chuckle as he passed Fjord into the kitchen.

“That good, huh?” Beau laughed.

“Are you okay, Caduceus?” Jester asked.

An awful lot of questions for someone when they’d just gotten out of bed, but he answered both of theirs with a nod and a hum. He filled another glass with water the way he had last night. Except instead of drinking it, he made a noise with his brows furrowed and set it down.

“Somethin’ wrong?”

Caduceus answered his question by puking in the sink. Fjord took a step back in surprise while Beau and Jester made hushed noises of disgust. Poor hungover Beau especially couldn’t have enjoyed witnessing that.

“Aw jeez,” Fjord said, as if that was helpful at all.

Caduceus was still leaning over the sink, mouth open while watery drool fell into the basin. His nose was running too, and his eyes were glassy, though Fjord wasn’t sure if they were like that before or after he threw up.

 

Caduceus had caught a nasty bug, that was clear. The poor guy spent the rest of the day going from bed to the bathroom and back. No wonder he hadn’t eaten anything, Fjord thought. He must not have been feeling good last night.

Fjord was at a loss of what to do. Admittedly, he wasn’t usually the caring roommate. Of course he was there for Caduceus, but he wasn’t the mother-hen type like Caduceus was. He wasn’t sure if he should be trying to help, or giving Cad privacy.

The answer seemed pretty clear later the afternoon. Caduceus had come out of his bedroom looking sick, and rushed into the bathroom just like he’d done a million other times that day. Except he didn’t emerge. The sounds of awful gagging stopped, but no firbolg came out to go back to bed.

He frowned. It was pretty weird to check on one’s roommate while they were in the bathroom, right?

His thoughts changed when he heard a bang! and the clattering of something that must’ve been knocked over or spilled. Fjord was up right away, running and stopping at the bathroom door.

“Are you okay?” he asked, willing the panic to leave his voice.

“Mm, yeah,” Caduceus said. A pause, and then, “...no. I’m not sure. I...” he didn’t seem to be able to get his words together.

“Do you... need help?” What a weird fuckin’ question. Good thing Caduceus didn’t mind weird fuckin’ questions.

“I think so, yeah.”

Fjord let himself into the bathroom and was greeted by the sight of his roommate on the floor, rubbing his head like he’d just hit it on something. A couple of bottles that were on the sink we now on the floor, along with a container of cotton swabs.

“Deuces, what happened?”

“Got dizzy,” Caduceus muttered, his eyes closed as he kept a hand pressed to the back of his head. “I think I have a fever. I don’t... I don’t know. Don’t feel well.” He was a little scrambled, which was to be expected of him when he wasn’t at his best.

Fjord knelt in front of him and laid a gentle hand on his friend’s forehead. “Yeah, I think so.” Fjord sighed. “C’mon, I’ll help you to bed.”

“I’m heavy,” Caduceus said, as if Fjord needed a reminder.

Fjord chuckled. “We’ll figure it out.”

Poor Caduceus hardly had any strength left in him. Fjord was keeping him up, helping him keep his balance when he needed to pause a couple of times to close his eyes and will the room to stop spinning. He was sicker than Fjord had thought. Stomach bugs just made you puke and shit a lot, right?

Unless you were already a seven-foot-tall firbolg who never ate enough and had skipped meals beforehand. No wonder he was dizzy. He hadn’t eaten, and he now couldn’t keep anything down if he tried. Fjord was pretty sure that all he’d been puking up was water and bile.

Fjord felt his forehead once again as Caduceus sat back in bed, eyes closed. “This would be a good time to have a thermometer,” he muttered. But since when did people just buy thermometers?

“Oh, yeah,” Caduceus agreed with a tired chuckle. “Little too late now, huh?”

Fjord scoffed. “Yup. Well, happy New Year, Duecey.”

 

Fjord made sure that Caduceus had everything he needed. Water, thermometer, bucket.

Caduceus was asleep before Fjord even left his room. The poor guy needed as much rest as he could get. Fjord had a feeling that his night wasn’t going to be particularly peaceful, best that Caduceus get some sleep while he can.

Fjord found himself to be rather exhausted as well. It wasn’t even nine, he had time to get things done before bed. But he lacked the energy. Perhaps it was the day before - the party - that wore him out. Either way, he went to bed, falling asleep to the muffled sound of his roommate’s snoring.

 

The first thing Fjord noticed when he woke was that it wasn’t morning, not even close. The second thing that he noticed was that he was seconds away from vomiting.

He didn’t notice fast enough.

Vomit dripped from behind his hand, seeping through his fingers. It was allover, the bed, the floor, himself. He made a disgusted groan as he assessed the situation, and then hurried to the bathroom.

Nothing more came up, maybe it was a fluke, or maybe his body just wanted to elongate the torture. No matter how tired he was, Fjord knew he needed to take advantage of the moment to clean his room.

The washer and dryer were right near Caduceus’ room, and Fjord felt kinda guilty for doing laundry when the man was asleep, but he didn’t have much choice. He poured in the detergent and was about to close the lid when his roommate’s door opened.

“Fjord?” Caduceus said, voice rough with sickness and sleep. “Everything alright?”

“Oh, yeah, I-” Fjord winced. His own voice sounded even worse. He cleared his throat, only to find that it was sore from vomiting just a bit ago. “Think I uh, caught your bug. Made a little bit of a mess.”

Caduceus blinked slowly and offered a sympathetic smile. He leaned against the door frame, nodded. “Sorry about that.”

Fjord scoffed. “Not your fault.”

He went to close the washer before Caduceus made a noise. He looked up questioningly.

“Don’t forget your shirt,” he said, gesturing toward the giant fucking puke stain down Fjord’s front. How did he forget about that?

“Shit, thanks,” Fjord breathed out before pulling his shirt off, somehow avoiding to get the mess anywhere else in the process. He was finally able to start the laundry and hopefully go back to bed. “Sorry for waking you, I was tryin’ to be quiet.” He swallowed hard, feeling nausea slowly rising. Fjord was careful to breathe through his nose.

“I was already awake,” Caduceus shrugged. “Kind of. It’s been a weird night.” He sounded tired.

“Anything I can do?”

“Not for me, no,” he shook his head. “One thing that you can do for yourself, however, is get to the bathroom before you make another mess.”

Fjord raised an eyebrow, and it took him far too long to realize how nauseated he was. He was too focused on making sure Caduceus was alright to notice that he needed to throw up now.

Caduceus just smiled tiredly and stepped back as Fjord rushed into the bathroom.

 

The next morning was groggy, and came at eleven in the morning. Neither of them had slept well the night before and neither of them were particularly energetic.

Fjord made a single slice of toast for breakfast, of which he ate half. Caduceus didn’t try to eat, and Fjord didn’t blame him. He was having trouble even keeping water down, and Fjord tried not to be worried when the firbolg was so dizzy that he couldn’t get out of bed.

Fjord puked up his toast in the kitchen sink. So much for that.

He winced, hearing his roommate heave into a bucket in the other room. The two of them were useless right now.

So, Fjord called Jester.

 

Fjord was incapable of staying in bed all day. He needed to be doing something. Today something was just watching TV. Whatever. Any way of keeping his mind off of how he was feeling.

Jester let herself into the apartment with a quick courtesy knock. It appeared that she came prepared.

“Hello~!” she announced herself. “I’m here to save the day.”

Fjord couldn’t help but smile. “Sorry to have bothered you, Jess. But...” he sighed. “Thank God you’re here.”

“Omigosh Fjord you guys can call me anytime you need help, okay?” She set a hand on his shoulder, smiling before scanning the apartment. “Is Caduceus in his room?”

Fjord nodded. “He’s been kind of sleeping all day. I’m a little worried about him? He’s havin’ trouble keeping anything down. I mean, we both are, obviously, but I think he’s getting dehydrated.”

Jester frowned. “Poor Caduceus...” she said. “But I brought some things that should help you guys feel better.”

He listened as Jester knocked on Caduceus’ door. The low, quiet voice of Fjord’s roommate made itself known, and Jester spoke softly as she entered. Caduceus was in good hands.

 

It was a couple days later, a couple of miserable days later in which Fjord and Caduceus did little but vomit and sleep. One night, Caduceus even joined Fjord in his bed when they were both dealing with unrelenting chills at the same time.

Jester had given them a powerful electrolyte solution to keep them hydrated, and it was essentially their saving grace.

Fjord was in the process of nibbling on a slice of toast once again when his roommate wandered out.

Caduceus, if possible, looked even thinner than he had a few days ago. He seemed tired - of course he was - but he looked to be feeling better.

“Looks like we’re both on the mend, huh?” Fjord asked.

Caduceus smiled. “Seems that way.” Instead of getting something from the kitchen, he simply plopped onto the couch next to Fjord and sighed heavily. “Happy New Year,” he said with a low chuckle.

“So happy,” Fjord scoffed.
