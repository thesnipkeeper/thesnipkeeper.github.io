Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: David comes down with the flu on the day of a big event at the apothecary
Word count: 1.7k

Not So Alone

“Of course this has to happen today,” David laments. His head falls onto Patrick’s shoulder. He’s wrapped in the duvet from their bed, but he still can’t stop shivering. “Just don’t forget to put up the signs, and-”

Stevie’s brows furrow. “I’m starting to get the sense that you don’t have faith in me.”

“We absolutely have faith in you,” Patrick says before turning to look down at him. “Right, David?”

David Sighs. He’s not in any state to try to be funny or start one of his and Stevie’s little bantering adventures. He’s hardly staying awake right now.

“Right,” he breathes out before giving a stuffy sniffle.

The store is supposed to host a day-long crafting workshop. They have various teachers coming in to teach various things, and if today goes correctly, the “students” will be completely wine-drunk by the ending wood staining class.

And it’s not that he doesn’t have faith in Stevie. She runs her own company, for God’s sake. It’s that - and he’d never admit this - he doesn’t want her to have to do it all by herself. The two of them will do many things for each other (usually reluctantly) but he knows she doesn’t want to. He knows she hates having to fake nice and tell people what to do.

“Um,” David lifts his head off of Patrick’s shoulder and looks at his husband. “Why don’t you go with her?” He asks quietly.

“Why?” they both ask in unison. Patrick’s expression is simply confused while Stevie’s is downright offended.

“It’s a lot of work, and if something goes wrong, it’s going to reflect badly on the store!”

Stevie scoffs. “I’m sorry, you think I need supervision? I’ve done this before, David.”

“Um, no, I-” his voice breaks and he turns away to cough. The chesty sound is enough for both Stevie and Patrick to wince. “I think that having two people would just ensure that the event runs more efficiently.”

Stevie and Patrick exchange looks, and Stevie shrugs. “I don’t care, sure.”

Patrick sighs and nods, “You’re probably right. Are you sure that you’re going to be okay here?”

“Literally all I plan doing today is sleeping.”

David hums happily as his husband pulls him forward and presses a kiss to his forehead.

“I guess I’ll get ready, then. I’ll meet you there, Stevie?”

“Yup! See you, David.”

David puts on the most dramatic groan.

-----

After an hour alone, he’s regretting sending his husband off.

Patrick left him with the TV remote, a cup of water, and various medicines on the coffee table within reach. David doesn’t have any intention of getting off of this couch until his husband returns.

He hasn’t had breakfast, and he doesn’t even want breakfast, which is a little scary. Instead of worrying about it too much, David takes a couple tylenol and turns on HGTV until he falls asleep.

He isn’t asleep for long before his phone starts blaring at him.

David takes the time to groan loudly before answering.

“Hello?” he winces. His voice does not sound great.

His husband’s voice would be comforting to hear if he didn’t sound so urgent. “Hi, where are the extra tablecloths?”

David clears his throat, takes a moment to think. “They’re in the-”

“-Because we looked through the blue bin in the back and we couldn’t find them, so-”

Fuck. Everything important for today was supposed to be in the blue bin.

“Ummm.” He drags a tired hand down his face. “Did you look at the gray bin? The one underneath the shelf on the left wall?”

“Oh... I forgot about that one. Thank you, David.” His voice is more distant as he tells Stevie where the gray bin is, and then comes back. “How are you feeling?”

David sniffles experimentally, and then swallows. Fuck. “Stuffed,” he says. “My throat hurts.” And his head and his entire body.

Patrick makes a sympathetic noise, and there’s a shifting as he switches the phone to his other ear. From the sounds of it, he probably has it cradled between his shoulder and ear as he unfolds tablecloths.

“Just text me any time you want me to come home, okay?”

Despite how horrible he feels, David can’t help but smile. “Okay. I guess I should let you get back to work, though.”

“I love you.”

“I love you too.”

-----

David starts to shiver again as the medicine wears off and the chills come back. His throat stings more and more each second, enough for him to dread swallowing and have saliva pooling in his mouth because of it. Part of him wants to call Patrick home, but the other part knows Patrick wouldn’t actually be able to do anything.

He’s going to feel horrible whether his husband is with him or not.

So David just pulls the duvet up to his neck and shudders miserably. He muffles a sneeze into the fluff and whines as it scrapes his throat. Of course that sets off a fit of coughs that make everything even worse. David’s finally forced to pull an arm out of his cocoon and get a tissue to deal with the mucus coming from both his mouth and nose.

Ugh.

There’s no going back to sleep after that.

Every part of his body is complaining. Moving hurts. His skin is so overly sensitive that he whimpers when the glass of water he tries to drink touches his arm.

Maybe it is time to call Patrick. Being coddled is the only thing that’s going to fix him right now.

Coincidentally, his phone starts to ring before he has a chance to consider it more. Stevie, this time.

“Do you have the number of the quilting instructor?”

David pulls the phone away to check the time. The cogs in his brain turn slowly as he goes through the schedule he had memorized for today. “I’ll text it to you. Is... she not there?”

“Nope,” Stevie says, popping the ‘p’. “But the knitting instructor said that they could help out if we need them to.”

David groans, which then turns into a cough. “It’s weird that she’s not there yet...” he mutters. “She’s usually on time... um, are they sure?”

“Yeah, they seem totally down.”

“Okay,” David yawns. He’s never been more thankful to have people in his life who are more laid back than him. He would be freaking out right now if he had the energy. “Give them a... bottle of wine or something before they leave as an extra thank you. But! But call Susan first.”

“Got it. Thanks, David.”

He has a moment to listen to the background chatter. It’s loud, but it sounds like everyone is having a good time. He can pinpoint the sound of his husband’s laughter for a second.

“Is everything okay over there?”

“Yes, everything’s great. Are you like, eating and stuff? Like, you’re not dying, right?”

David takes a moment to consider. “I don’t think so...? But the day is still young.”

A scoff. “Great. See you later. Feel better or whatever.”

“You’re so kind. Goodbye.”

-----

David wakes up from a measly ten-minute nap, not feeling like he ever actually fell asleep in the first place. It’s the pain in his throat that wakes him, along with the aches in his arms and legs. Oh, and a new discomfort that seems to have made itself known.

In his stomach. He can’t bother to be anything but done with this whole thing. David rolls his eyes as he sits up, muscles crying in pain. He takes another few sips of water, hoping that it’ll calm his stomach and soothe his throat. It does neither of those things.

He decides to just sit and watch TV with a tissue pressed to his nose and hope the almost-nausea goes away on its own.

It’s a good thought until he ends up dashing to the downstairs bathroom with a hand over his mouth.

David only has a moment to whimper before he’s heaving into the toilet. Heat rushes to his face with the effort as the second gag brings of a rush of something sour mixed with the water he’d drank.

His stomach gurgles, but shows no intention of torturing him anymore. David blows his nose and slumps against the wall. He should go back to the couch, but right now he’s just going to regain some energy.

“Fuuuck,” he whines. This is bad. Not just because he’s miserable, but because he knows what he has to do now.

He takes a few minutes to rest before he gets sick of sitting on the (very nice marble) tile floor. David pulls himself to his feet with shaky limbs and slowly makes his way to the couch, supporting himself against the wall and whatever furniture is in the vicinity.

He flops down, coughs, and dials Patrick’s number.

He was trying to avoid this, things need to be done, and to be done right, but...

“Hey David, everything alright?”

David drags a hand down his face. “I want you to come home,” he mumbles. “I threw up. I’m miserable. I miss you. Please come home”

There’s a pause before he hears the jingling of keys and a whispered conversation between Patrick and Stevie before, “I’m leaving now, see you in a few minutes.” There’s something fond in his voice that pushes the thoughts of guilt out of David’s mind.

He smiles and lies back down, content to know that his husband will be there and make things a little less horrible.

-----

There’s a knock at the door, and David is comfortably clung to his husband. At the risk of inviting a home invader, Patrick calls, “Come in!”

Not a home invader, just Stevie with a pharmacy bag.

“Hey, so I brought some soup from the cafe, and I took a bottle of wine for myself.”

Patrick, bless him, is careful not to move too much and jostle David as he accepts the things from her. “Thanks so much, Stevie.”

David’s borderline-alcoholic friend leaves and he clings to Patrick tighter.

“I’m going to have to pour this in bowls, David.”

“Nngh. Stay here.”

“Mhmm, but we both need to eat.” He moves out from under him, but offers a kiss on his head that makes everything okay.
