Molly Meets the Gang

It was days like this when he really wished he had a car, Mollymauk decided as he watched the rain pour with a grim frown. This truly wasn’t fair.

Why have a car when he can walk everywhere and get a ride or take the bus everywhere else? He was on tour with his performing group for weeks at a time, anyway. Living in downtown had its advantages; everything was so close and handy.

So when Mollymauk went to pour himself a bowl of sugary-as-shit cereal on his night off and found that they were out of milk, he had no choice to make a trip to the store! The grocery store was a mere fifteen-minute walk away and he'd been unusually tired all day; he could probably use the exercise. And he really, really wanted some late-night cereal.

Who had time to check the weather forecast these days?

He'd been working up the courage to leave the store, standing between the outside doors and the inside doors, for a few minutes now as he watched the rain come down. The few lamps in the parking lot were the only sources of light, and he could still tell how wet it was outside.

He huffed, gathered his bearings (and his milk) and stepped outside. Molly was immediately pelted with cold, heavy rain that soaked his clothes and hair. He had no choice but to just set his sight on the wet sidewalk in front of him and continue home. The quicker he made it home, the quicker he could get into warm clothes and snuggle in for an early night of television and cereal. Yasha wasn’t home tonight, he could watch anything without the two of them arguing over the definition of 'trash TV'.

He could only walk a few minutes before the wind picked up, blowing rain into his face and making it impossible for him to move on any longer. He took shelter under the awning of a liquor store and shivered.

There was the sound of a car door up the block. A man looking nearly as tired as Mollymauk himself with a long, worn-out coat and an umbrella made his way up to the door. Mollymauk backed away in an attempt to look like he wasn’t completely lost as the man shook off his umbrella under the awning.

The two exchanged quick, awkward eye contact and nods of acknowledgment as one often does with strangers. Except the man didn’t continue or go inside the store like Molly expected him to. Instead, he stopped and turned around. Molly was afraid he was going to be mugged or something for a terrifying second, but the man just furrowed his brows and took a step toward him, eyes quickly looking him over and seeming to stick to his peacock tattoo for an extra second.

Molly was about to tell him off before the man finally got words out.

"Does- does your name happen to be Mollymauk, by any chance?" his voice was so quiet it was almost unheard amongst the loud weather, and his eyes quickly averted after he spoke as he pulled a piece of red hair behind his ear.

It admittedly caught Molly off-guard. Who was this guy and why did he know who he was? Still, he put on a smile and made sure to show his fangs a bit. He was too tired to deal with creeps tonight.

"I suppose that would depend on who was asking."

The man cleared his throat, looking thoroughly uncomfortable. "You live with Yasha, ja? I'm a friend of Beauregard's."

Molly scanned the figure in front of him until his sleepy mind put two-and-two together. "You must be Caleb."

A small, awkward smile. "That's me."

Molly outstretched his hand and offered a smile. He'd heard of Caleb Widogast. Yasha described him as a weird-but-sweet man who enjoyed his solitude. He lived with Beau's other friend, the goblin woman. Molly had been hoping for Yasha to introduce him to the rest of the gang when he returned, only for Yasha and Beau to have gone on vacation the day after he returned.

He received surprisingly-firm handshake, and then took his own away to wipe some wetness from his nose. The rain had caused it to go cold and runny.

Caleb stood silently for a few uncomfortable seconds before, "Are you going in as well?" he gestured to the door.

"Hm? Oh, no. I'm just waiting out the storm so I can head home."

He raised an eyebrow and then nodded, seemingly deciding there was nothing left to say before he went inside. Molly watched him through the store window. Caleb ignored the clerk's greeting and went straight to one aisle, picking a bottle in one swift motion and paying for it without any extra words.

A weird man indeed.

He seemed startled by Mollymauk as he exited the store. Molly just waved half-heartedly.

"You know, this rain is supposed to go on for a while," Caleb's eyes went to his feet, and then back to Mollymauk. "It's very late, but we are having pizza and movies at our house tonight and you're free to join us." he cleared his throat. "Or… would you prefer a ride home?"

The offer warmed his heart and filled him with relief. But he was cold and wet and his clothes and cereal were at home. Still, Molly chose interesting social situations over his own solitude any day. It was going to be lonely at home without Yasha around.

He held up his milk. "Could I borrow your fridge, then?"

Caleb smiled.

-----

Molly nodded appreciatively when Caleb turned on the heat in his car despite the fact that he looked quite warm himself. He sniffled, hoping for feeling to come back in his nose, and cleared his throat of the tickle caused by the damp air.

"Eyy, you made it back!" Molly heard a voice cheer. He made contact with bright yellow eyes that were now narrowing at him. "Who the fuck is that?"

Caleb turned to him as if he'd forgotten that he needed to be introduced. "This is Yasha's roommate, Mollymauk. We ran into each other, I invited him over."

He introduced himself to Nott and Yeza, the goblin woman he'd heard about and the timid halfling man he hadn't. Molly cleared his throat and hoped the warm, dry air would do him some good.

"Could I bother you for some dry clothes?" Molly asked. He removed his shoes in the foyer and winced when he felt how soaked his socks were. His coat was nearly sopping, and he almost felt guilty putting anywhere in the doorway.

"Yeah, Caleb, look at him! He looks like a drowned rat."

Caleb chuckled. "Of course."

Caleb's clothes were clean, but worn. Molly caught sight of plenty of torn seams as he slipped on the borrowed clothes. Still, the long-sleeved t-shirt Caleb had offered him was warm, and the flannel bottoms even warmer. He draped his wet clothes over the shower door and then grabbed a tissue from the box on the sink to deal with his runny nose.

He was finally comfy and warm, and in the company of interesting new people.

He talked to Nott and Yeza, even managing to make them laugh. It wasn’t hard for Molly to make friends, honestly. People either loved him or hated him and he was alright with either.

Caleb took his milk to their kitchen and returned with a plate of pizza, and the four of them sat down for a truly sappy movie.

One of the character's was having a poorly-executed crying fit when an entirely different fit made itself known in Mollymauk's nose. He sniffed, and scrubbed at it in a sideways motion with the back of his hand. It did nothing but exacerbate the brewing tickle.

"Hhhh- shiheuw!" He gave one quick inhale before using his hand to shield his new friends from the spray. He could feel the other's glances toward him as he recovered with a sniff, and just started to lower his hand before- "Oh come o- hih…. Hih-Shuew!" he sniffed again, more thickly this time, making it clear to everyone how much loose fluid was trapped in his sinuses. "Fuck… pardon me. I don’t know where that came from. Snf!"

Caleb made a noise of acknowledgement, but Nott was the first to speak.

"Bless you." She almost sounded impressed. "Are you getting a cold?"

Molly shook his head, giving an extra sniff and paw at his nose before going back to the movie. "No no, it's just the rain."

"You look quite dry now, though," Yeza said, leaning over past Caleb and Nott. "Are you sure you're not catching cold?"

"Getting sick from rain is nothing but an old wives' tale," Caleb muttered. Molly hadn't even realized that he was paying attention to their conversation.

"Maybe so," Yeza said. "But walking around in cold rain certainly can't help your immune system."

"I'm alright," Molly insisted, adding a smile to tell them he's appreciative of their concern.

-----

"Hih-SCHuew!" It was the sixth sneeze in the past hour. His nose was now suffering from a near-constant trickle. Caleb had brought the tissue box from the bathroom into the living room. Molly's eyes were watery and itchy, and no amount of throat-clearing would rid him of the nagging soreness in the back of his throat.

No one had said anything, but he was receiving looks of worry from Nott and Yeza. The look from Caleb wasn’t worry, but instead a furrowed brow as if he were trying to solve a difficult puzzle. Molly found him so interesting, and would have told him so if he didn’t cough when he tried to. He had to make quick action to raise the crook of his arm to his face to shield everyone from his germs.

Yes, okay, so maybe he was catching a cold.

It made sense in retrospect. He spent most of the day strangely tired. He'd assumed it was from all of the travel of the circus, except he'd gotten home days ago and he never experienced tiredness like this after work before.

"Perhaps we should cut this short and let Mr. Mollymauk get some rest," Caleb said, and Nott turned off the TV.

Yeza was yawning as badly as Molly had been, which made him feel less guilty.

"I can drive you home now, if you'd like."

Molly was so comfortable here, and it was so late, and he was so tired, and…

"Or you could stay here," Nott offered. "We just have the couch, no guest room, but we have a fuck ton of extra blankets and pillows."

That sounded wonderful.

-----

Molly sniffled miserably as he watched Caleb make up the couch. Nott was dragging blankets downstairs, while Yeza was stocking the coffee table with tissues, medicine and water. He couldn’t believe all of the work these people were putting in for him just because he knew the person dating their friend. It was touching, and made him love them even more.

Soon, Nott and Yeza had gone upstairs for the night, leaving Caleb. He furrowed his brows at Molly's shivering. He'd managed to get warm until now. As if he needed any more proof that he was ill, his body decided to bring on a fever.

"Do you have everything you need for tonight?" He asked as he rearranged the things on the coffee table.

"More than enough. Thank you. Honestly. Snf! I do hope you don’t come down with this as well, though."

Caleb shrugged. "It'll probably happen. That's alright."

Oh. Well, he was realistic.

"Help yourself to anything in the kitchen. Except for the whiskey. That's Nott's and she will kill you if you touch it." Caleb had a fond smile on his face. "Feel better, Ja? Goodnight, Mr. Mollymauk. It was nice to meet you."

Molly couldn’t help the smile that grew on his lips, genuine and impossible to push away. He watched Caleb ascend the staircase, looked at the pile of cold supplies next to him, and felt something warm in the pit of his stomach.

Had he found a family?
