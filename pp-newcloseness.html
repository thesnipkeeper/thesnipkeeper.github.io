New Closeness

They’re lying in bed when Juno hears a gurgle. He can’t help but chuckle as he turns around in Nureyev’s arms.

“Was that your stomach?” Juno asks, almost impressed.

Peter blushes. “I- perhaps.”

“Wow.”

But Peter isn’t exactly joking about it, or anything really. He looks uncomfortable, now that Juno takes a moment to notice.

Juno frowns. “Are you... okay?”

“I’m fine,” Peter says, strained. “Just... ate something strange earlier.”

“Nureyev, we all ate the same thing today.”

“Mm. We did, didn’t we. That’s... concerning.”

Juno sits up and looks at his boyfriend. Stomach problems weren’t exactly a cause for concern, but Peter’s reaction to it makes him think that something’s more wrong than he’s willing to admit.

“I’m fine, Juno. Just a little queasy. Let’s go to sleep, shall we?”

Juno raises an eyebrow, but lies back down and lets himself be wrapped in Peter’s arms once again. They fall asleep easily.

 

When Juno wakes up, he’s cold. And still rather tired. It doesn’t feel like morning, and with a glance at the clock, he sees that it’s not in fact morning, but the middle of the night. He yawns and drags a hand down his face, and then realizes...

He’s alone. Nureyev’s gone. The rest of the room is empty.

He tamps down the hurt that he feels, low self-esteem telling him that Peter sneaked out the moment he had a chance, but that doesn’t sound like him.

So, Juno gets up. He could use some water, anyway, he tells himself. And if he just happens to pass Nureyev’s room on the way, well...

The door is slightly ajar. Juno doesn’t feel quite as invasive pushing it open just a little bit.

Peter isn’t asleep. He’s in bed, but he’s shaking violently and looking ten shades paler than he had earlier. There’s a trash bin next to his bed with what looks like vomit having dripped down one of the sides.

Juno swears, which gets Peter’s attention immediately.

“Juno...” he says, voice painful. “I...”

“You’re sick,” Juno says, mostly to himself. “Fuck, you’re really sick.”

Peter shakes his head. He pushes his arm against the bed in an attempt to pull himself up to a sitting position, but his arm gives out and leaves him lying there. He huffs. Still manages to look annoyed when he’s horribly ill.

“I’m okay,” Peter’s voice is calm and steady, despite his state. “It’s just a bug, Juno. Don’t worry, please.”

“I- Nureyev... do you want me to stay?”

“No.” Peter chuckles. “You don’t need to see me looking any worse than I do right now.”

Juno frowns, looking him over. “You’re sure?”

A nod. “Go back to bed, Juno. I’ll be alright.”

Juno isn’t sure about that. He can’t exactly argue, though. Peter’s a grown adult and if he doesn’t want Juno with him, well... Juno will just...

“Okay... I guess I’ll...” he gestures to the door.

Peter smiles.

“See you tomorrow?”

Another nod. Peter looks like maybe he’s falling asleep, so Juno exits quietly and this time shuts the door behind him so no one else feels the need to bother the guy.

He tries to ignore the feeling of being pushed away. This isn’t time for him to think about himself! Peter’s the one who’s suffering.

Juno goes back to bed, and doesn’t sleep much that night.

 

The bustle of everyone else getting ready for the day is what wakes him up next. He takes his time getting dressed and making his hair look right before he braves the kitchen during breakfast time.

Just as expected, everyone’s chatting and/or arguing. Rita offers the same cheerful morning greeting that Juno’s heard for years.

“Where’s Mr. Ransom?” She asks, which gets everyone else’s attention. “Weren’t you two together last night?”

Juno clears his throat loudly. “He’s in his room. Wasn’t feeling great.” They’re too focused on getting breakfast to question his being weird, and those who might notice are gracious enough not to bring it up.

Juno chooses coffee over food. He drinks it while listening to Rita tell him all about the movie that she and the big guy watched the night before. Her voice fades out when Nureyev walks into the room. By that time, Vespa and Buddy are already doing planning or work, and Juno wonders if Peter purposefully planned his emergence for when there are less people around.

More importantly, though, Nureyev looks-

“Mr. Ransom, you look terrible!” Well, no one gets things out in the open like Rita does.

Peter nods. “I’m aware.”

“When Juno said you weren’t feelin’ great, I didn’t know that he meant- I mean, no offense, Mr. Ransom, but you kinda look like you’re dyin’!”

“You kind of do, actually,” Juno agrees. He narrows his eyes at Peter, who looks like he hasn’t gotten any sleep in the past year and is sporting some old pajamas he wouldn’t usually be seen in outside of his room. Without makeup, dark circles under his eyes are very present.

“Rough night,” Nureyev admits. “I just need some water and things should be a little-” he doesn’t finish his sentence before he clamps a hand over his mouth and rushes to the sink. Rita yelps as he vomits.

Juno’s out of his seat, rushing to Peter’s side. He looks unsteady, Juno tells himself. That’s all, he just wants to keep Peter from falling over. So, he lays a hand on the man’s back and runs it up and down as Nureyev heaves. Not much comes up. He can’t have much left.

“Should I get Ms. Vespa? I should get her right now!”

That sounds like a good idea to Juno, but Peter looks as if that’s the last thing that he wants. “Wait Rita, maybe not yet...”

“Really?”

“Really,” Peter affirms. “I... alright, perhaps getting out of bed was a mistake.”

Juno sighs. “You think?” he grabs a nearby towel and hands it to Peter, who scrubs his face and sighs. “Okay, if you don’t want to, I completely understand, but come to my room? Let me... keep an eye on you. You’re making me nervous.”

Peter turns to him with a tired, guilty look before averting his eyes. He nods. “Please.”

 

The moment that Peter is comfortable in his bed, Juno plops down, making the nauseated man groan.

“A little more careful, please...?”

“Oh, sorry. My bad.”

“Juno.”

“Hmm?”

“You’re upset.”

“What gave you that idea?”

Peter stares at him for a good few seconds before Juno sighs.

“You essentially told me to fuck off last night.”

“Oh, Juno...” Peter sighs, but Juno shakes his head and continues.

“No, I know that you didn’t mean- I’m just saying.” Wow, he’s really not good at these kinds of conversations. But, a year or two ago, he would’ve never considered having one in the first place, so Juno chooses to see this as an improvement on his part. “You... you could’ve asked me for help or something.”

“I’m sorry, is Juno Steel lecturing me on asking for help?”

Juno chuckles. “Well, fair point. I- I’m gonna get you some water or something.”

“Wait...” Peter catches his wrist. “Stay here, sit with me for a moment.”

Juno lower back onto the bed, letting himself be pulled into whatever position Peter wants him in for optimal comfort. Peter lays his head on Juno’s chest and gives a content sigh. He’s also effectively locked Juno in for whatever conversation that they’re... supposed to have.

Juno dreads it. His heart is pounding and Peter can definitely tell.

And yet, they lie there. No talking, no conversations at all. Just the two of them.

“I enjoy having you around all the time, Juno,” Peter says, after a long, long silent moment. “Having people close is new for me.”

Juno swallows, nods. “Yeah, me too.”

The moment is ruined not too long after that when Peter sits up and carefully clamps a hand over his mouth.

Juno’s quick to act. “Okay, alright, bathroom?” 

Nureyev nods quickly.

Juno wastes no time helping the taller man to the bathroom, muttering assurances the whole way. Peter heaves painfully the moment that he’s over the toilet, Juno winces.

“Want uh, privacy?” 

A pause, and a nod as Peter drags the back of his hand across his mouth.

Juno closes the door behind him and only then realizes that his heart is still beating like he’s just run a marathon. There’s a knock on the door. 

Rita? RIta, the one who dispels his anxiety on some days and, well, causes it on other days.

“Is Mr. Ransom okay?” she quickly asks. “Also, I brought water and crackers and stuff because I figured his tummy was pretty sick and-” She shoves all of the stuff at him at once, and then looks at him with a raised eyebrow. “Are you okay, Mr. Steel?”

Juno lets out a long, calming breath and nods. “I’m okay. Thank you, Rita. I think we’ll both be fine.”
