Parabatai Vibe

Jace knew something was wrong with his parabatai before he even laid eyes on Alec. 

There was a certain feeling that one got when their parabatai was under stress, physical or emotional. It wasn't that he'd feel what Alex was feeling, exactly, but instead a nagging sense of dread that refused to go away. One that demanded he do something or suffer the unsettled feeling until it was solved itself. He knew that Alec felt the same, like times when he ended up knocking on Jace's bedroom in the middle of the night when he just happened to be in the middle of a nasty post-nightmare panic attack. 

They were always there for each other, even when they didn't know what was wrong.

Jace felt it the moment he woke. So, he wasn't as surprised as he should have been when Alec emerged from his office with a flushed face and tired features. 

"Wow, what's up with you?"

Alec shook his head, moving past him. "Nothing."

"Hey," Jace caught his arm before he could walk away, and Alex reluctantly turned to him with a long sigh. "It's not nothing. I can feel it, Alec. What's wrong?"

Alec closed his eyes for a moment, like he was cursing that this had happened. "I don't feel well, okay? Can we drop it, please? We have work to do."

Jace frowned. "Don't feel well, how?"

"Like, I'm sick, Jace. Drop it." And he walked away.

A sigh. Jace had fought countless demons, sparred with many skilled fighters, but a sick Alec was one of the toughest things to deal with. 

His plan was to ignore it completely. If Alec wanted to be a stubborn asshole, that wasn’t Jace’s problem. 

Except, apparently it was.

Jace found himself completely distracted by the bond, snapping at people and not paying attention to the various briefings and meetings. This was the worst that Alec’s illness has ever affected him. He wasn’t sure why, but he knew one thing--he was going to put an end to it. If anything to give himself a little relief. 

The feeling kept getting stronger, which could only mean that Alec was getting worse.

The first minute of free time Jace had was spent heading to his brother’s office, nearly knocking people down in the process. He didn’t bother to knock before he entered.

“Jace, what-?” But Alec didn’t get to finish his sentence, because he was doubled over in a fit of horrible coughs before he could. God, it was bad. Jace couldn’t help but wince. 

Jace sighed and closed the door behind him. The last thing Alec would want was the whole institute listening to him while he hacked up a lung.

When Alec was finished, his eyes were watery and he reached for a bottle of water on his desk with a sniffle. His movement was lazy, lacking Alec’s usual air of purpose. 

“Look,” Jace started as Alec took careful sips of water. “I can’t get any work done while you’re stressed like this. Please, go home, take care of yourself. Or just- just take a nap so I don’t have to deal with this.”

Alec raised an eyebrow, mouth open as if he wanted to argue, but instead he just gave a sigh that crackled as it came out. His chest sounded so full of gunk, Jace wondered how he didn’t hear it before. “I’m sorry.”

“But not sorry enough to stop working, I’m guessing?”

Alec didn’t have to answer. The apologetic look on his face remained. 

Jace rolled his eyes. “Is Magnus home?”

“What?”

“Magnus, your boyfriend.” It was Alec’s turn to roll his eyes. Jace continued. “Is he home?”

“I don’t- probably? But-” Alec inhaled quickly and turned away to muffle another wet cough into his sleeve. 

Jace just nodded and walked off. 

“Jace! What are you doing!”

He smirked, and didn’t reply.

If Alec wanted to be a stubborn asshole, Jace would just play the game. And also be a stubborn asshole.

------

Alec could feel the crackle in his chest as he inhaled for another cough. He pulled a tissue from the box on his desk, and he was glad he did, because the hacking brought up enough mucus that he felt equal parts relieved and disgusted.

This was awful.

He’d heard various others around the institute coughing, but he hadn’t really paid it any mind until he woke up with a horrible cough. 

His head pounded, there was an ache deep in his bones. There were times that no number of layers were enough to chase away the chills he felt, and then times that those layers became so stiflingly hot that he couldn’t get them off fast enough.

He didn’t want Jace to interfere. But that was obviously too much to ask. He shouldn’t have expected any different.

And he was sweating again. Alec moaned, which just ignited the ever-present itch in his throat and threw him into yet another fight with his own lungs. He simultaneously tried to stop coughing while also unzipping his jacket and yanking it off. Alec unbuttoned his collar, hoping to cool off just a little.

He rolled his eyes. 

Whatever Jace was planning had better not interfere with his work more than this illness already was. 

That was certainly too much to ask for. Alec wasn’t going to kid himself. He wasn’t sure exactly what Jace was planning, but at the mention of Magnus, it couldn’t be anything good. Alec tried not to worry about it too much. It wasn’t as if anyone could just force him to stop working, not his brother, or the love of his life...

Fuck, he was tired.

Alec rested his chin on his hand just for a moment. The papers he was filling out weren’t particularly time-sensitive. He could just take a moment…

Somehow, his head ended up in his folded arms on the desk.

And he was asleep.

-----

Alec woke to soft words and an insatiable tickle in his chest.

He moved away from the body that stood next to him and aimed a cough at the floor, and then another, before grabbing a tissue and spitting the loose mucus into it.

“You poor thing,” Alec felt a hand on his forehead, parts of it cooler than others from the rings on Magnus’ fingers. “You’re burning up.”

Alec couldn’t help but lean into the touch as his coughing dissipated. 

“Thanks for coming, Magnus.” 

“It was no problem.”

Alec looked past his boyfriend to see his parabatai, immediately giving him a watery-eyed glare. With Jace’s eyes on them, he nudged Magnus’ hand away and coughed again.

“I’m fine.”

“Oh, yes. Do you always fall asleep at your desk when you’re fine?” Well, he had a point. Magnus smirked. “I’ll handle things from here. Thank you, Jace.”

Alec wanted to say that he really was okay, that Jace was just being selfish because their bond was bugging him, but he ended up coughing before he could tell Magnus any of that.

This fit went on until he couldn’t get a breath in. Still, his airways struggled to rid themselves of the itch. Alec felt a bit of panic rise as he tried to breathe, each time only triggering another cough. Magnus’ gentle words were the only thing reminding him not to pass out.

“Okay, alright, take it easy… that’s it…”

At first he thought maybe Magnus had used some sort of magic, but no… focusing on his words helped him to get through the fit. Alec felt significantly weaker when it was all over. He gave a shaky breath and rubbed away the tears that had built up in his eyes. 

He accepted the water Magnus pressed into his hands. 

His chest and stomach both ached. He was still so tired.

Alec took a moment to drink some water and gather his bearings. He thought over his current situation, how little work he’d gotten done today, and the fact that he felt awful.

“I want to go home,” he finally said.

Magnus smiled. “I can help with that.”

------

“I thought you were going to stay in bed,” came the soft yet scolding voice from behind him. 

Alec finished pouring the steaming water over a tea bag, watching it bleed color. 

“Was out of tea,” Alec said with a shrug, and then a cough that he had to aim away from the kitchen utensils. Magnus snaked his arms around his waist from behind, head bumping between his shoulder blades. 

“I could have gotten you more tea, Alexander.” 

Alec smiled and twisted around in Magnus’ arms, mug in-hand. “Lying down makes it worse,” he admitted.

This was his third cup of tea, it was the only thing that soothed his cough, loosened the gunk in his chest and gave him a few moments of peace.

Magnus reached up to smooth the damp hair away from his forehead, satisfied to find that his fever was gone. For now. It would come and go, and Magnus was there whenever it did.

“Moving around makes it worse, too. I can tell,” Magnus observed, humming thoughtfully. He pressed an ear to Alec’s chest, and Alec couldn’t help but chuckle. Credit where credit was due, Magnus seemed to know what he was doing.

Still, Alec struggled to twist out of his grip in time to avoid coughing on his boyfriend. He swallowed and moaned. “Shit, sorry...”

“Oh darling,” Magnus frowned. “You don’t want to go back to bed, why don’t we watch a movie?”

Cuddling wasn’t really in the cards tonight. Even when Alec’s chills returned and he could really use Magnus’ body heat, he’d just end up coughing on him and that was definitely not something he wanted to do.

Instead, Alec found himself curled up on one end of the sofa, knees up as he drank his tea and tried desperately not to let the tickle in his throat disturb the movie. Even though Magnus had draped two blankets over him, he still shivered. Alec wanted to cry with how miserable he felt.

He missed work. He wasn’t sure if he just felt worse than he had that morning, or if he had less to distract him from the illness plaguing him.

Alec was thrown into a particularly long coughing fit, during which he could tell Magnus was trying not to pay too much attention to for his benefit. His throat was starting to hurt from the force of it, and his head ached far worse than it had before.

He was bothering Magnus, wasn’t he? Alec would probably be annoyed too if someone just continuously coughed with no end to it in sight. Well- not Izzy or Jace, they’d gotten sick plenty of times and Alec was never annoyed with them. But anyone else... he was just being a bother.

“Alec? Alexander, sweetheart, what’s wrong?”

For fuck’s sake, now he was crying?

“Sorry, I’m sorry,” Alec shook his head, wiping the tears away. “I’m fine, I don’t know why-” A hand against his forehead silenced him.

Magnus frowned, worry in his eyes. “That fever might have something to do with it...” he mumbled.

“I just need a second.” Alec shrunk away and coughed, curling further in on himself as much as his long limbs would allow.

Magnus looked reluctant, but nodded and gave him a little space.

Alec wasn’t even sure why he felt so tearful all of a sudden. He just... the aches and the never-ending itch in his chest, the inability to get comfortable as the fever began to make his skin overly-sensitive... it was too much

Magnus shouldn’t have to deal with this.

“Alexander,” came a quieter, almost cautious voice.

He looked up at Magnus, who almost looked apologetic for having said anything.

“Whatever is going through your head right now isn’t true, alright?” Alec was about to ask how he knew when Magnus continued. “And I don’t know about you, but I’m exhausted. What do you say I make you a potion that will absolutely guarantee a good night’s sleep and we go to bed?”

Alec swallowed thickly. “Are you sure?”

“Yes, darling, of course I’m sure.”

-----

It was still dark outside when Alec woke with a coughing fit. The potion Magnus had given him last night must have worn off, because suddenly he couldn’t stop coughing.

The body next to him shifted. Fuck. Alec clamped a hand over his mouth to muffle the sound, and headed for the bathroom. If anything, he could at least give Magnus more sleep before his body began to fuck things up for both of them again.

He closed the door as quietly as possible and proceeded to hack up a lung. The bathroom was colder, Alec realized. He should have brought a blanket with him. He wrapped his arms around himself, shuddering and trying to get his airways under control.

He thought he’d gotten away before he made too much noise and woke Magnus. The knocking on the door told him he was wrong.

“Are you okay?”

“Yeah,” Alec croaked out between coughs. “Sorry, go back to sleep.”

“Are you throwing up?”

Oh, oops. “No, I just didn’t want-” cough. “-didn’t want to wake you up.”

“Little too late for that,” the end of the sentence drew out a bit as if Magnus was in the middle of a yawn. “Will you come back to bed, love?”

Alec did. Neither of them went back to sleep; he was still coughing every five seconds. 

But Magnus wrapped his arms around him, chasing away the shivers more effectively in any blanket, holding him when the fits wracked his body. 

And Alec decided that this was okay.
