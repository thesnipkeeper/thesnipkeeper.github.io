Title: Latched
Fandom: Schitt's Creek
Summary: Stevie finds out how cuddly Alexis Rose can truly be, and she's somehow okay with it
Word count: 1.1k

Latched

Stevie doesn’t expect anyone to be home when she enters room seven. Mr. Rose is working at the desk, the other three should be at work as well. So she can’t help but gasp when she enters and finds another human being.

Alexis doesn’t even move from her bed as Stevie tentatively takes a step inside.

“Sorry, I didn’t think anyone would be home.”

Alexis shakes her head against her pillow and goes back to scrolling through her phone. “It’s fine.”

She’s acting strange, but Stevie decides not to ask. Asking questions when it comes to the Rose family is a slippery slope.

Lacking anything else to say, Stevie just lifts the stack of towels in her hand with a questioning expression. Alexis nods, offering a tiny smile.

So, Stevie sets the towels in the bathroom, lingering a few more seconds as she wonders if she should say something. It’s not exactly like Alexis to just stay in bed all day. She constantly buzzes with so much energy, it’s a wonder she’s ever capable of sitting still.

“Um,” Stevie closes her eyes. Fuck. She’s actually doing this. “Are you... okay?”

Alexis sighs. “Don’t feel good.”

“Oh. Sorry.”

The way she’s wrapped up under the blankets suddenly makes a little more sense. And now that Stevie’s paying attention, she can see subtle shivers coming from Alexis. Has Alexis ever been cold?

“It’s fine, I’m just,” A sniffle. “I hate this. But like, I don’t feel like doing anything else.”

Stevie stands awkwardly a couple feet away as Alexis sits up in bed. She keeps her blanket pulled up to her neck, teeth chattering slightly. Her movements are slow and lazy as opposed to the bounciness Stevie was used to seeing.

“Oh God,” Stevie gasps. “You look awful.”

“Ugh. I know.” Alexis coughs miserably, and Stevie can’t help but wince. “I feel awful, and David said he’d pick up medicine on his way home from work, but...”

But that’s not for a few hours.

Stevie sighs. She’s going to regret this. “Can I...do something?” she asks, flapping her arms in a lazy shrug.

Alexis seems to think on it for a moment. “...don’t think so.” another sniffle.

“Alright.” Stevie nods and turns to leave.

“Unless-!”

Fuck.

“Unless you want to like,” she gives a shrug, the way she does when she thinks she’s looking casual and confident but is actually radiating nervousness. She pulls a piece of hair behind her ear. “Like, stay here for a little bit?”

“Uhh.”

Alexis’ eyes widen. “No, forget it. It was stupid,” she chuckles, shaking her head. “I’m just bored, and nothing to distract me from-”

“Sure.” Fuuuuck.

“What?”

“I’ll... stick around. For a while, I mean. Anything for an excuse to get out of work for a while.” It’s impossibly slow at the motel today. The only reason she even bothered to bring towels here was because there was nothing else to do.

Alexis’ “Yay!” is much hoarser and more painful to listen to than usual. She scoots to one side of her bed and pats the empty space. “Come here. snf!” Stevie can’t bring herself to refuse.

-----

David goes to unlock the door, only to find it’s already opened. He clutches the pharmacy bag, hoping that his sister hasn’t been abducted again.

“So I wasn’t sure what kind you needed so I just grabbed three different- oh.” He finally takes in the sight before him.

His sister, fast asleep. On Stevie’s shoulder. He sucks back a smile as she glares at him.

“Not a word,” Stevie hisses.

David shrugs innocently. “I don’t know what you’re talking about. This is a totally normal occurrence.” Well, it’s not a lie. Alexis is known for being extra cuddly when she’s not feeling well. He usually takes the opportunity to abscond as to not get all of her germs, but she seems to have found someone else to latch onto.

And if David knows one thing about Stevie, it’s that she wouldn’t have simply agreed to something like this if she really didn’t want to.

“Um,” he clears his throat. Alexis’ cheeks have taken on a shade of pink that signifies a higher fever. “Is she okay, though?”

Stevie nods. “She’s-”

“Can you guys keep it down? I’m trying to sleep,” comes the grumble from the blankets.

David presses his lips in a straight line that morphs into a smile as he looks up at his friend. Stevie glares once more.

-----

The next time Alexis is sick, it’s not quite as adorable. And yes, Stevie decides, she’s allowed to consider her adorable without it being...weird.

It’s late. She’s about to close up the lobby for the night when she gets a text.

Alexis: can you bring me some gatorade or sprite or something? plz plz? im super sick and my stomach hurts :(

Stevie blinks. She knew that Mr. and Mrs. Rose were out on some sort of spa trip, and David spends more time at Patrick’s than anywhere else these days. It didn’t occur to her until now that Alexis Rose, clingiest person on the planet is alone.

She’s tired, spent the whole day working for Mr. Rose. Which must be why she doesn’t bother to argue or ask questions before she grabs some sprite out of the fridge in the back.

When she arrives, Alexis is sitting on her bed, clutching the bathroom’s trash bin with a miserable expression. Her face is red and her hair is tangled and damp. Stevie can’t help but wince.

“Hey,” she announces herself.

Alexis seems to sigh out of relief at the sight of her. “Stevie, thank God. I thought I was going to die here.”

“Jeez, how long have you been sick?”

Alexis growls, taking the can and opening it. She stares at it for a second or two and promptly sets it aside. “I felt gross earlier, but I thought it was just because I didn’t sleep great last night?” She swallows hard. “Then I started throwing up.”

Stevie frowns. “Are you... okay? I can call David…”

“Nooo, I’m fine.” She waves a hand dismissively. “Totally fine.”

“Mm’kay. Those words would have like, a little more merit if you didn’t sound like you were dying.”

Alexis sighs, and then quickly scrambles to get over the bin. A weak burp is all that comes up at first, followed by a stream of vomit. Luckily for her, Stevie’s never been grossed out easily.

A pitiful whine comes out as she tries to catch her breath, only for an inhale to catch in her throat.

Stevie rubs her back as she heaves, feeling a pang of sympathy.

When Alexis is finally done, she has tears in her eyes from the exertion and she looks so impossibly worn out.

Fuck, Stevie thinks, right before she says, “Um. If you- if you need me to stay...”

“Please?” it’s so desperate and broken that Stevie finds herself unable to even think about leaving.
